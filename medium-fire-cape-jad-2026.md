# I Died 6 Times Before Getting My OSRS Fire Cape — Here's What I Changed

If you've ever attempted the Fight Caves in Old School RuneScape, you know the feeling: 90 minutes of grinding through 62 waves, heart pounding — and then you miss one prayer switch, and it's over.

I died six times before I got my first Fire Cape. Each failure cost me millions in supplies. But every death taught me something. Here's what I learned.

## The #1 Mistake

Players overthink Jad. He has two attacks and two prayer switches. That's it. But 90% fail because they panic.

**The secret:** Only focus on prayer switching. Nothing else matters.

## Budget Setup That Works (~5M GP)

You don't need expensive gear. Use a Toxic Blowpipe with Amethyst darts, Blessed D'hide body and chaps, and an Amulet of Fury. Total cost: about 5M. Bring 8 Saradomin Brews, 4 Super Restores, and fill the rest with Prayer Potions.

## Three Rules That Saved My Attempts

**1. Kill bats first.** Tz-Kih bats drain your prayer. Kill them immediately on every wave.

**2. Use Italy Rock.** The safespot makes the entire fight easier. Hug the west side — melee enemies get stuck behind it.

**3. Protect from Magic after Wave 30.** From Wave 31 onward, keep Protect from Magic on permanently. The mages hit hard and often.

## Beating Jad

Wave 63 arrives. Here's your script:

Listen for the sound cues. Jad's magic attack has a distinct "whoosh" charging sound that starts before the fireball appears. Learn this sound — it is more reliable than watching the animation.

One action per Jad attack. Attack Jad once, wait for his next attack, switch prayer if needed, then attack again. Never rush.

When Healers spawn, tag each one once, then line them up behind Jad. Only one can attack you at a time.

If you miss a prayer switch, do not panic. Eat a Brew — it heals 16 HP. You can survive one hit.

## Final Advice

The Fire Cape is one of the most rewarding achievements in OSRS. It is not about having the best gear. It is about staying calm, listening for the sound cues, and trusting the strategy.

I failed six times. On attempt number 7, I walked out with the cape. You will too.
