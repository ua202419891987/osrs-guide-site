F2P Methods (No Membership Needed)

1. Cowhide Collection — 150K–200K GP/hour

Requirements: None (F2P accessible) Location: Lumbridge Swamp / North of Al Kharid This is the classic. Kill cows, collect cowhides, bank them, sell on GE. - Each cowhide = ~140 GP (GE mid-price) - ~20 cows per inventory - ~7 trips per hour = ~140 hides = ~20K GP raw The trick: Tan them into soft leather using the Al Kharid tanner (cost: 1 GP per hide). Soft leather sells for ~170–190 GP each. Your profit nearly doubles. Verdict: Boring but reliable. Perfect for total beginners.

2. Iron Ore Mining (F2P) — 100K–150K GP/hour

Requirements: 15 Mining Location: Barbarian Village / Rimmington Iron ore = always in demand (smithers buy it in bulk). - ~300 iron ore per hour (F2P worlds are crowded) - ~100 GP per ore = ~30K raw The upgrade: Join a busy world (WW/US), mine only when rocks respawn instantly. Don't waste time waiting. Verdict: Decent starter method. Switch to members ASAP for real GP.

Low-Level P2P Methods (Sub-50 Stats)

3. Flax Picking → Bow String Making — 300K–400K GP/hour

Requirements: 10 Crafting (to spin), access to Seers' Village Location: Taverley / Seers' Village Pick flax north of Seers' Village → spin into bowstrings at the spinning wheel in Seers' Village. - 1 flax = ~90 GP - 1 bowstring = ~1,000 GP - ~300 bowstrings/hour = ~300K GP Why this works in 2026: Magic shortbows and longbows are still trained by thousands of accounts daily. Bowstrings have consistent demand. Verdict: Best low-stat AFK money maker. Watch a show while doing it.

4. Winelaw (Cooking Wine) — 250K–350K GP/hour

Requirements: 35 Cooking, started Romeo & Juliet quest Location: Any bank + your inventory Buy cooking wine from the Draynor Village Wine Merchant (25 GP each), sell on GE (~700 GP each). - ~500 wine/hour (if you world-hop quickly) - Margin = ~650 GP per wine Risk: This method is trade-limited if you buy too much from one NPC. Spread purchases across multiple worlds. Verdict: Fast GP if you understand GE limits. Not AFK.

Mid-Game Methods (50–80 Stats)

5. Slayer (General) — 400K–800K GP/hour

Requirements: 50 Combat, Slayer unlocked Location: Varies by assignment Slayer is the best兼顾 (combat training + money) method in the game. You don't pick the monsters — your Slayer master does. High-GP tasks in 2026: - Nechryael (need 80 Slayer): ~1.2M GP/hour (ancient shards + pure essence) - Gargoyles (75 Slayer): ~600K–800K GP/hour (granite maul + only 1 death per task) - Dust Devils (65 Slayer): ~400K–600K GP/hour (iron ore + herbs) Pro tip: Use a Slayer helmet (i), cannon (where allowed), and understanding of your block list = massive GP boost. Verdict: The best long-term money method. You train combat while making bank.

6. Wintertodt (Firemaking + Construction) — 500K–700K GP/hour

Requirements: 50 Firemaking (recommended 70+), 30 Construction helps Location: Wintertodt Camp (Great Kourend) Wintertodt is a minigame where you chop wood, light fires, and survive. Rewards are shockingly good in 2026: - Reward rolls: 4–6 items per kill (depending on points) - High-value loot: Grimy herbs, uncut gems, pure essence, ore packs, tome of fire - Bonus: Huge Construction XP from fixing braziers Real GP/hour: ~600K GP (conservative). With lucky tome/ornate pool rolls, it can spike to 1M+. Verdict: Best mid-game AFK method. Also trains Firemaking to 99 passively.

7. Flipping (GE) — 1M–5M+ GP/hour (Skill Dependent)

Requirements: 5M–10M+ starting GP, understanding of GE mechanics Location: Grand Exchange (VARROCK) Flipping = buy items below mid-price, sell above mid-price. It's merchanting, not skilling. Best flipping items in 2026 (stable margin): - Rune items (platebody, 2h sword, scimitar) - Herbs (grimy snapdragon, grimy ranarr) - Resources (iron ore, pure essence in bulk) - High-end gear (bandos pieces, abyssal whip during crashes) The skill curve: Flipping has a steep learning curve. You will lose GP at first. Start with 1–2 item types and learn their price patterns. Verdict: Highest GP/hour in the game if you're good. But it's not "passive" — you need to understand the market.

End-Game Methods (80+ Stats / Maxed)

8. Zulrah — 1.5M–2.5M GP/hour

Requirements: 75 Magic, 75 Ranged, 70 Defence, started Regicide Location: Zulrah shrine (Eastern Zeah) Zulrah is a solo boss that drops high-value unique items (tanzinite mutagen, magic fang, serpentine visage) plus consistent stackables (snake hides, pure essence, death runes). - With uniques: 2M–4M GP/hour (RNG dependent) - Without uniques (dry): ~800K–1.2M GP/hour The catch: You need to learn the rotation (4 attack patterns). Dying = lost supplies. Use a guide. Verdict: Best boss for consistent high GP. Learn the rotation and it becomes semi-AFK.

9. Vorkath — 1.8M–3M GP/hour

Requirements: 80+ Ranged, 78+ Magic, started Dragon Slayer II Location: Vorkath's lair (Ungaeld) Vorkath = the most consistent high-GP boss in OSRS. No RNG rotation — same fight every time. - Drops: Dragon bones, Wrath runes, Superior dragon bones, gramt portions - Uniques: Dragon claw (3.5M GP), Vorkath's head (50K GP), Skeletal visage (10M+ GP) GP/hour: ~2M GP (conservative). With a visage drop, it spikes to 10M+ in one kill. Verdict: The "money printer" of OSRS. Learn it once, print GP forever.

10. Chambers of Xeric (Raid) — 2M–5M GP/hour (Team Dependent)

Requirements: 80+ Combat stats, knowledge of raid mechanics Location: Mount Quidamortem (Zeah) CoX is the most popular raid in OSRS. Loot is split among team members (3–5 players). 2026 meta loot: Twisted bow (1.2B GP), Ancestral robes, Kodai insignia, Dragon claws - Solo/duo CoX: 3M–6M GP/hour (high skill requirement) - 5-man CoX: 1.5M–2.5M GP/hour (more relaxed) Verdict: Best end-game group content. Also the most fun. Find a raid team and learn the rooms.

11. Nightmare (Solo/Group) — 2M–4M GP/hour

Requirements: 85+ Combat stats, 80+ Ranged/Magic Location: Morytania (after SotE quest) The Nightmare drops Inquisitor's armor, Nightmare staff, and Eldritch orb — all high-value in 2026. - Solo: 2M–3M GP/hour (high difficulty) - Group (5–10 players): 1M–2M GP/hour (more chill) Verdict: Great alternative to raids if you want boss variety. Drops are less volatile than CoX.

12. AFK Farming Runs (Herbs + Trees) — 600K–1.2M GP/hour (Passive)

Requirements: 65+ Farming (higher = more profit) Location: Farming patches across Gielinor Farming runs take ~10 minutes every 80–90 minutes. You plant herbs (ranarr, snapdragon, toadflax) and trees (magic, yew, palm). - Per run: ~200K–400K GP profit - Per hour (amortized): ~600K–1.2M GP/hour Why this is #12: It's not active GP/hour — it's passive. You do a run, log out, come back 90 minutes later. But the GP adds up fast if you're consistent. Verdict: Every serious account should be doing farming runs. It's free GP for almost zero effort.

The 2026 Meta: What's Changed?

Three things have shifted the money-making landscape in the past 12 months: 1. GE tax (0.5%) — Every trade over 25 GP now has a small tax. It doesn't kill flipping, but it makes sub-100 GP margin flips not worth it. 2. Botting bans (Jagex is cracking down) — Gold farms are being banned faster. This has increased demand for legitimately farmed supplies (herbs, ores, logs). Prices are up ~10–20% on many commodities. 3. New content (Varlamore + Sailing) — New skills = new supplies. If you're an early adopter of new content, you can sometimes flip new items for massive margins (until the market stabilizes).
