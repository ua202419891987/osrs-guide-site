1. Summer Sweep-Up Impact on Skills — Overview

The Summer Sweep-Up 2026 was one of the largest game balance updates in OSRS history, touching 14 out of 23 skills with significant changes. Released on June 17, 2026, this update fundamentally altered how efficiently you can train certain skills, which methods are now optimal, and what the "best" route is for reaching 99 in each skill.

If you've been following pre-2026 training guides, many of them are now outdated or suboptimal. This guide exists to fix that — giving you the most current, accurate training roadmaps based on post-Sweep-Up mechanics.

📊 Which Skills Were Affected Most?

🔴 Major Overhaul (Training Method Changed Significantly)

  • Hunter — Complete overhaul of tracking mechanics and new creatures added
  • Agility — New shortcut courses and redesigned existing courses
  • Thieving — Blackjack mechanic reworked, new NPC targets
  • Mining — Powerleveling rebalanced, new gem rocks
  • Smithing — Blast Furnace adjusted, new smithing paths
  • Fishing — New fishing spots and spot rotation changes
  • Woodcutting — New trees and rebalanced respawn timers

🟡 Moderate Changes (Some Methods Better/Worse)

  • Combat Skills (Attack/Strength/Defence/Hitpoints) — Training through Slayer now slightly different due to monster changes
  • Magic — Spellbook adjustments and new utility spells
  • Runecraft — Ourania Altar tweaked, new combination runes
  • Herblore — Potion secondary ingredients adjusted
  • Farming — Growth cycles and new herb patches

🟢 Minimal or No Changes

  • Prayer, Construction, Slayer, Firemaking, Cooking, Crafting, Fletching, Smithing (low-level) — Largely unchanged

The Sweep-Up wasn't just cosmetic — it shifted the meta for efficient training. Here's what changed in broad strokes:

2. Skills With Major Changes

These skills had their training methods fundamentally altered. If you trained them before June 2026, the old guides are wrong. Here's exactly what changed and what to do now.

🏹 Hunter — Complete Overhaul

Hunter received the most dramatic changes in the Sweep-Up. The entire tracking system was reworked, and several new creatures were added to the game.

What's New in Hunter

Mangrove Sifaka (Level 43+): A new monkey-style creature found in the Mangrove Swamp (southern Karamja). Requires 43 Hunter, gives ~32,000 XP/h. This bridges the gap between Swamp Lizards and Chinchompas, making the 43-53 grind much less painful than before.

Sunlight Moth (Level 80+): A new butterfly that gives ~95,000 XP/h at 90+ Hunter. It's the new "endgame" Hunter method, rivaling Chinchompas for speed but with different mechanics (more active, less click-intensive than Chins).

Tracking Rework: Box trapping now has a slightly higher success rate at all levels. The "fail rate" was reduced by approximately 15% across the board, making all box trapping methods faster.

💡 New Best Hunter Route (Post-Sweep-Up)

Levels 1-9: Crimson Swifts (faster than before) → 9-19: Copper Longtails → 19-29: Prickly Spurs → 29-43: Swamp Lizards (more XP/h) → 43-53: NEW Mangrove Sifaka → 53-80: Chinchompas (use Grey if on a budget, Red for speed) → 80-99: Chinchompas OR Sunlight Moths (your choice based on GP vs time preference).

NEW: Oo'glog Agility Course (Level 50+)

Located in the Oo'glog area (unlocked after partially completing As a First Resort quest). Requires 50 Agility, gives ~48,000 XP/h. This course is a game-changer for the 50-70 Agility range, which was previously a slog through Seers' or Canifis.

NEW: TzHaar Agility Course (Level 85+)

Located in the TzHaar City (accessed via the rope trench in Mor Ul Rek). Requires 85 Agility, gives ~72,000 XP/h. This is now the fastest Agility training method in the game, slightly beating Ardougne at high levels. The course also has a chance to drop Onyx bolt tips, making it slightly profitable.

🗡️ Thieving — Blackjack Rework & New Targets

The blackjack mechanic was reworked to be less click-intensive, and several new NPC types were added for mid-to-high level Thieving training.

NEW: Pickpocket Dwarves (Level 65+)

Dwarves in the Dwarven Mine now drop gems when pickpocketed successfully. At 65-80 Thieving, this method gives ~62,000 XP/h plus ~200K GP/h from gem drops. This makes the 65-80 Thieving grind actually profitable for the first time.

NEW: Pickpocket Elves (Level 80+)

Elves in Prifddinas (after Song of the Elves quest) can now be pickpocketed. At 80-99 Thieving, this gives ~90,000 XP/h, making it the new fastest Thieving method. However, it requires completion of the hardest quest in the game, so it's endgame-only.

⚠️ Blackjack Change Warning

The blackjack minigame was made less click-intensive (you no longer need to right-click "Knock-out" every time), but the XP/h only increased by ~12-14%. It's still a viable method, but Pyramid Plunder and the new Dwarf/Elf methods are now competitive or better depending on your level.

Blast Furnace was slightly nerfed (mostly to discourage goldfarmers from abusing it). However, early Smithing (Bronze through Iron) got a big boost, making the first 30 levels much less painful. The new GSM Utility method (smelting Gold/Silver/Mithril utility items) provides a mid-speed, mid-cost alternative to Blast Furnace for players who don't want to deal with the BF minigame.

4. Skills That Stayed the Same

These skills were largely untouched by the Summer Sweep-Up. Your pre-2026 training guides for these skills are still accurate and can be used without worry.

✅ Skills With No Meaningful Changes

  • Prayer — All methods unchanged. Gilded Altar still 350% XP,Chaos Altar still 350% XP. Your pre-Sweep-Up Prayer guide is 100% valid.
  • Construction — POH building costs and XP rates unchanged. Optimal construction training (Oak larders) still the same.
  • Slayer — No new Slayer monsters were added, and no Slayer master assignments were changed. The skill itself is untouched (though some individual monsters were tweaked cosmetically).
  • Firemaking — Burning logs gives the same XP. Wintertodt is unchanged.
  • Cooking — Cooking ranges and burn rates unchanged. The Cooking guild is still the best place to cook.
  • Crafting — All leather/glass/spinning methods unchanged. The fastest crafting methods (Linum irit, etc.) are still the same.
  • Fletching — Fletching bows/darts/bolts unchanged. The optimal route (Oak shorts at low levels, Redwood longs at high levels) is still optimal.
💡 What This Means for You

If you already trained these skills before the Sweep-Up, you didn't miss out on anything. If you're planning to train them now, you can safely use any guide written before June 2026 — the information is still 100% correct.

5. New Training Methods That Didn't Exist Before

The Summer Sweep-Up introduced several brand-new training methods that didn't exist in the game before. Here's where to find them and how to use them.

🆕 Mangrove Sifaka Hunting (Hunter 43+)

Location: Mangrove Swamp, southern Karamja (south of the Kharazi Jungle)

Requirements: 43 Hunter, partial Jungle Potion quest (to access the area)

XP/h: ~32,000 at 43 Hunter, scaling up to ~38,000 at level 53

How to do it: Set box traps near the mangrove trees. The Sifaka monkeys have a 5-tick (3-second) trap setting time. This method is less click-intensive than Chinchompas and provides a good bridge between Swamp Lizards and Chins.

Profit: Sifaka fur sells for ~500 GP each on the GE. At ~120 fur per hour, this method is ~60K GP/h profit on top of XP.

🆕 Oo'glog Agility Course (Agility 50+)

Location: Oo'glog, south of the Feldip Hills

Requirements: 50 Agility, partial As a First Resort quest

XP/h: ~48,000 at 50 Agility, scaling up to ~55,000 at level 70

How to do it: Complete the 6-obstacle course. The course has a lower fail rate than Seers' Village (only ~5% fail rate at 50 Agility vs ~15% at Seers'). This makes it the best 50-70 Agility method by a significant margin.

🆕 Pickpocket Dwarves (Thieving 65+)

Location: Dwarven Mine (Falador entrance or Ice Mountain entrance)

Requirements: 65 Thieving

XP/h: ~62,000 at 65 Thieving, scaling up to ~70,000 at level 80

Profit: Dwarves drop gems (Sapphire through Diamond). At ~200K GP/h profit, this is one of the few Thieving methods that makes money.

🆕 Sunlight Moth Catching (Hunter 80+)

Location: Sunlight Meadow, south of Shilo Village

Requirements: 80 Hunter, Shilo Village quest

XP/h: ~95,000 at 90 Hunter, rivaling Chinchompas

How it differs from Chins: Sunlight Moths require you to chase them (they move every 2-3 catches), making it more active than Chinchompa box trapping. Some players prefer this for variety; others find Chins more AFK. Try both and see which you prefer.

🆕 TzHaar Agility Course (Agility 85+)

Location: TzHaar City (Mor Ul Rek, accessed via the rope trench in the TzHaar area)

Requirements: 85 Agility

XP/h: ~72,000 at 85 Agility, scaling up to ~78,000 at level 99

Unique feature: This course has a chance to drop Onyx bolt tips (1/200 chances per lap). At current GE prices, this makes the course ~50-100K GP/h profit on top of being the fastest Agility method.

🆕 Gem Rocks Mining (Mining 45+)

Location: Shilo Village mine (requires Shilo Village quest) or the Gem Mine (requires Family Crest quest)

Requirements: 45 Mining

XP/h: ~30,000 at 45 Mining, scaling up to ~40,000 at level 99

Profit: ~150-300K GP/h depending on luck with Diamonds. This is now the most profitable mining method for mid-level players.

7. FAQ — Summer Sweep-Up Training Questions

Do I need to re-train skills I already trained before the Sweep-Up?

No. The Sweep-Up changed training methods, not your existing skill levels. Your 99 Strength is still 99 Strength. You don't need to do anything except update your future training plans if you plan to train more skills.

Which skill got the biggest buff in the Sweep-Up?

Hunter got the biggest overall buff. The addition of Mangrove Sifaka (43-53) and Sunlight Moths (80-99)缩短了 the most painful parts of Hunter training. Combined with the tracking rework (less fail rate), Hunter training is now ~30-40% faster overall.

Which skill got nerfed the most?

Smithing got the most significant nerf. Blast Furnace XP rates were reduced by 3-6% across all bar types. It's not a massive nerf, but it was enough to make Smithing less attractive as an early-game skill priority.

Are the new training methods worth using, or are old methods still fine?

The new methods are significantly better in most cases. For example, the Oo'glog Agility course (50-70) is ~30% faster than the old best method (Seers' Village). The Mangrove Sifaka (Hunter 43-53) is ~60% faster than the old method (continuing Swamp Lizards to 53). Use the new methods — they're strictly better.

How much did the Sweep-Up change the "optimal" quest order?

Surprisingly little. Most quests don't interact with the changed skills in ways that alter quest priority. The one exception is Swan Song (unlocks Mining Guild), which is now slightly more valuable because the Mining Guild changes make high-level mining more profitable. But overall, your quest order doesn't need major changes.

I'm a new player starting after the Sweep-Up. Do I need to worry about any of this?

Nope! You're actually in the best position — you can use this guide (written post-Sweep-Up) to plan your training from the start, avoiding all the obsolete methods. Consider yourself lucky — pre-Sweep-Up players had to figure out the changes through trial and error.

Will there be another Sweep-Up-style update that changes everything again?

Jagex hasn't announced a specific date for the next Sweep-Up, but based on the 2025 and 2026 updates, they seem to be doing these large balance passes roughly annually. The next one might be in mid-2027. When it happens, we'll update this guide again. For now, the information here is current as of June 2026.
