OSRS Optimal Leveling Guide 2026: Maximize XP/Hour for Every Skill

Published: June 9, 2026 By OSRS Guru Last Updated: June 9, 2026

Every OSRS player eventually faces the same question: "What is the absolute fastest way to get from level 1 to 99?" Whether you are a main account grinder, an Ironman with limited resources, or a UIM looking to minimize time wasted, understanding XP per hour (XP/hr) benchmarks is the foundation of efficient progression. This comprehensive guide breaks down the optimal training methods for every skill in OSRS as of 2026, backed by real XP/hr data, cost analysis, and strategic insights to help you choose the best path for your account. For a broader overview of how all skills fit into your account progression, check out our OSRS Skills Overview for Beginners before diving into the specific methods below.

Efficiency in OSRS is not just about raw XP/hr — it is about balancing speed, cost, enjoyability, and account goals. A method that yields 300k XP/hr might cost 10 GP per XP, while a slightly slower method at 250k XP/hr might actually make you profit. Throughout this guide, we will examine the current meta for each skill, highlight the absolute fastest methods regardless of cost, and provide alternatives that offer a better balance of speed and economy. If you are following a structured path, our OSRS Skills Progression Path 2026 pairs perfectly with the data in this guide to create a cohesive leveling plan.

One of the most powerful yet often overlooked tools for accelerating your leveling is strategic questing. Many quests offer massive XP rewards that can instantly boost skills by 10, 20, or even 50+ levels worth of experience. By planning your quest order intelligently, you can skip some of the slowest early-level grinds entirely. Additionally, we will cover multi-skill optimization strategies — methods that train two or even three skills simultaneously, effectively doubling or tripling your effective XP/hr. Whether you are pushing for max cape or just want to reach the content you actually enjoy playing, the data and strategies in this guide will save you dozens or even hundreds of hours.

Understanding XP/Hour: How to Read the Data

Before diving into skill-specific methods, it is important to understand what XP/hr numbers actually mean in practice. The figures cited in this guide represent realistic, sustained XP rates achievable by an experienced player with appropriate gear and game knowledge. Your personal results may vary based on your account's gear, stats, latency, and how optimally you play. AFK methods naturally have lower XP/hr but allow you to do other things simultaneously, which has its own value.

The XP Table: What the Numbers Mean

Total XP required for level 99 is 13,034,431. At 100k XP/hr, that is approximately 130 hours of gameplay. At 200k XP/hr, it drops to 65 hours. At 400k XP/hr, just 32.5 hours. The difference between "good" and "optimal" methods can therefore mean saving 50–100+ hours per 99. When evaluating methods, always consider not just the raw XP number but also: required quest prerequisites, gear investment, GP cost per XP, and how interactive (click-intensive) the method is.

Factors That Affect Your Personal XP/hr

Several factors can significantly impact your actual XP rates versus theoretical maximums. Having maxed gear (e.g., full Inquisitor for melee, completed Tyrannosaurus Rex tasks for superior accuracy) can improve rates by 10–30%. Using the right prayers (Piety, Rigour, Augury) is essential for optimal combat XP. For non-combat skills, having unlocked relevant content (e.g., Farming patches via quest completion) can be the difference between a viable and non-viable method. Always check our skill-specific guides for prerequisite checklists before committing to a training plan.

Optimal Combat Training (Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints)

Melee combat remains one of the most straightforward skills to optimize because the XP/hr scaling is predictable and well-documented. The golden rule of optimal melee training is simple: train Attack, Strength, and Defence together through Slayer rather than grinding them in isolation. However, if you need to boost a specific stat quickly (e.g., getting 70 Attack for a quest requirement), there are targeted methods that maximize XP/hr.

Optimal Attack & Strength Training (Levels 1–99)

For pure XP/hr at low levels (1–40), nothing beats controlled combat in multi-combat areas. From 1–10, train on cows or goblins. From 10–30, Al-Kharid Warriors offer excellent XP with low risk. From 30–60, Hill Giants (in the Edgeville Dungeon) provide consistent ~25k–35k XP/hr with decent drops. From 60–70, Moss Giants or Experiment Cave (after Creature of Fenkenstrain) offer ~40k–50k XP/hr. At 70+, the real powerhouse methods open up: Slayer tasks with optimum gear yield 60k–120k+ XP/hr depending on the task, while dedicated bossing (e.g., Nightmare or Hueycoatl) can exceed 200k–300k+ XP/hr when accounting for both offensive and defensive XP.

Defence Training: The Often-Ignored Skill

Defence is best trained alongside Attack and Strength since you take damage in all melee encounters. However, if you need to train Defence in isolation (e.g., reaching 75 for Barrows gloves on an account with high Attack/Strength), the optimal method is Sand Crabs or Rock Crabs. These low-defence NPCs have massive HP pools and never deal damage that cannot be easily healed, allowing for essentially AFK Defence training at ~40k–60k XP/hr. At higher levels (70+ Defence), choosing to use a defender or shield strategically while on Slayer tasks maintains Defence XP at competitive rates without sacrificing overall efficiency.

Hitpoints Training Optimization

Hitpoints XP is passively gained whenever you deal or take damage in combat. To actively maximize HP XP, you want encounters where you deal damage rapidly and consistently. The optimal methods mirror those for melee: check our complete Hitpoints training guide for detailed breakdowns, but the short version is: NMZ (Nightmare Zone) with Dharok's or Absorption potions can yield 70k–100k+ HP XP/hr while also training your chosen combat style. Alternatively, high-intensity bossing (e.g., Muspah or Vardorvis) trains HP at 80k–150k+ XP/hr as a secondary benefit.

Optimal Ranged & Magic Training

Ranged: Fastest Methods by Level Range

Ranged has some of the most distinct XP/hr tiers in OSRS. From 1–40, Chuck/gnomish firemaking... only joking — from 1–40 Ranged, monsters like Cows (with a shortbow) or Al-Kharid Warriors are serviceable at ~20k–30k XP/hr. The real game-changer unlocks at 40 Ranged with the ability to use Red Chinchompas, which revolutionize Ranged training. From 40–70 Ranged, Red Chins at Ape Atoll or the Cortestis Dungeon yield 80k–120k XP/hr. From 70–99, Black Chinchompas at the Ape Atoll multi-combat zone or Hydra (with a crossbow) can reach 150k–250k+ XP/hr, making Ranged one of the fastest combat skills to 99 when budget permits.

Magic: High Alchemy vs. Combat Spells

Magic training splits into two categories: combat Magic (offensive spells for bossing/PvE) and utility Magic (High Alchemy for GP, teleportation for efficiency). For pure XP/hr, the optimal path is: 1–40 at rock crabs or sand crabs using strike/bolt spells (~25k–40k XP/hr). At 40+, starting to use splashing on a multi-combat NPC is an option, but the truly optimal method is combat Magic through Slayer or bossing. At 55 Magic, our Magic training guide details how to use High Alchemy for profit while training, but for raw XP, bursting/barraging Slayer tasks is king. Bursting Dust Devils (74 Magic) yields 150k–200k+ XP/hr; barraging Maniacal Monkeys (75 Magic) can exceed 300k XP/hr, making Magic potentially the fastest combat skill in the game.

Hybrid Combat Training: The NMZ Method

Nightmare Zone (NMZ) deserves special mention as one of the most efficient multi-combat training methods in OSRS. By properly configuring your overloads, absorption potions, and prayers, you can AFK-train Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, and Ranged (if using a blowpipe) simultaneously. A well-optimized NMZ session yields ~70k–100k+ XP/hr in your primary combat style while also passively training Hitpoints. This is the gold standard for players who want to minimize active playtime while still making meaningful progress across multiple combat skills.

Slayer: The Most Efficient Long-Term Path

Slayer is widely considered the most efficient way to train combat skills in OSRS because it combines XP across Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, Hitpoints, and Prayer (via Gilded Altar or Ectofuntus after tasks) while also generating profit. A high-efficiency Slayer setup with optimum gear, cannon (where allowed), and proper quest unlocks can yield 80k–130k+ combined combat XP/hr along with 50k–200k+ GP/hr profit depending on task RNG.

Optimal Slayer Unlocks and Block List Strategy

To maximize Slayer efficiency, you must complete key quests that unlock superior enemies, better gear, and expansion areas. Our complete Slayer 1–99 guide covers the full unlock order, but the critical upgrades are: unlocking the Bone Crusher (Morytania Hard Diary) to passively train Prayer, purchasing the Slayer Helmet blueprint (50 Slayer + 50 Defence), and completing the Kourend & Kebos questline to access superior Slayer masters like Konar (who assigns tasks in diverse locations with Brimstone-chest rewards). Proper block list management — blocking slow tasks like Cave Bugs or Turoths while prioritizing fast tasks like Dust Devils, Nechryaels, or Abyssal Demons — is the single biggest factor in sustained Slayer XP/hr.

Cannon vs. No Cannon: When Each Is Optimal

The Dwarf Multicannon is a massive XP/hr multiplier for ranged Slayer tasks, effectively doubling or tripling your kills per hour on multi-combat NPCs. However, cannonballs are expensive (~200–300 GP each, with hundreds used per hour), so the cannon is optimal when the GP/time tradeoff makes sense. For main accounts with excess GP, always use a cannon on ranged-compatible tasks. For Ironmen or budget players, the cannon is still recommended for key power-leveling phases (e.g., getting 70 Ranged efficiently) but should be skipped on tasks where the profit from drops already exceeds cannonball costs. Learning when to deploy the cannon is a core skill for any efficiency-focused player.

Optimal Production Skill Training (Crafting, Herblore, Smithing, Fletching, Runecrafting)

Crafting: Fastest to 99 in 2026

Crafting is one of the most expensive but also most customizable skills in terms of speed. The historically optimal method — blowing glass (molten glass) — remains extremely fast at higher levels (~220k–280k+ XP/hr at 87+ Crafting) but requires significant GP investment. Alternative fast methods include making dragonhide bodies (profit-positive at high Crafting with adequate supply) or creating gems/ jewelry. Our 1–99 Crafting guide provides a detailed cost-benefit analysis, but the TL;DR is: if you want the absolute fastest 99, budget 80–120M GP and use molten glass from 87–99 for a sub-10-hour 99. For a slower but profit-positive approach, green dragonhide bodies from 66–99 can actually generate profit if you have the hides available (ideal for Ironmen).

Herblore: The Expensive but Essential Skill

Herblore is notoriously expensive, but 2026 has seen some shifts in meta due to new herbs and potion recipes. The optimal Herb training method is making the highest-tier potions you can afford at each level bracket. From 1–38, use guam tar or marrentill potions (cheap but slow). From 38–78, ranarr potions (battlemage potions, prayer potions) offer the best XP/GP ratio. From 78–99, making super combat potions, divine super combat potions, or bastion potions at ~300k–450k XP/hr is the gold standard. Total cost for 99 Herblore in 2026 ranges from 90M–140M GP depending on market fluctuations. Plan accordingly or use our money-making guide to fund this grind.

Fletching: The AFK-Friendly Speed Run

Fletching is among the fastest and most AFK-friendly 99s in the game. The optimal method: string maple longbows from 50–70 (~180k XP/hr), then string yew longbows from 70–85 (~220k XP/hr), then string magic longbows from 85–99 (~280k–350k+ XP/hr). With the right setup, Fletching 99 can be achieved in 8–12 hours of /played time, much of which can be AFK. This makes it an ideal candidate for your first or early 99s, giving you a sense of progress without requiring intense focus. Many players knock out 99 Fletching in a single weekend session.

Runecrafting: The (Still) Slow Grind

Runecrafting has a reputation as the slowest and most tedious skill, but 2026 optimizations have improved the situation. The optimal method depends on your Agility level and quest progress. From 1–23, create air runes (slow but cheap). From 23–44, craft cosmic runes via the ZMI altar (~30k–45k XP/hr with ~70 Agility). From 44–77, the ZMI altar remains optimal, scaling up to ~55k–70k XP/hr at higher Agility. At 77+, blood runes at the Blood Altar offer ~80k–120k XP/hr with profit potential of 1.5M–2.5M GP/hr. While still slow compared to other skills, the profit potential at high Runecrafting makes the end-game grind more palatable. Note: 99 Runecrafting is a requirement for the Quest Cape and various endgame activities.

Smithing: The Blast Furnace Advantage

Smithing has two distinct optimal pathways: the expensive way (superheating items or smithing cannons) and the efficient way (Blast Furnace). Blast Furnace, unlocked after The Giant Dwarf quest, allows for dramatically faster Smithing XP when managed correctly. With 60 Smithing and proper coal/ore management, you can achieve 100k–150k+ XP/hr at Blast Furnace. From 70 Smithing, smithing cannonballs or high-tier bars at BF can extend the efficiency further. The total cost for 99 Smithing via Blast Furnace is moderate (30M–60M GP), making it one of the more accessible "expensive" production skills.

Optimal Gathering & Support Skills

Mining: 3-Tick and Blast Mining Methods

Mining has evolved significantly since the introduction of Blast Mine and optimized 3-tick mining techniques. The optimal methods by level: 1–30, copper/tin (or Doric's Quest for instant 30). 30–55, iron ore (3-tick with circumstances) at ~40k–60k XP/hr. 55–72, granite at the Blast Mine (~60k–80k XP/hr). 72–99, Amethyst mining (~80k–110k XP/hr) or continued Blast Mine optimization. Notably, our 1–99 Mining guide details how to combine Mining with passive GP generation, as high-level Mining methods (runite, amethyst) are profit-positive. Motherlode Mine remains a popular AFK alternative at ~25k–40k XP/hr but is NOT optimal for raw XP/hr.

Farming: The ROI King of OSRS

Farming is unique among skills because it generates massive profit while training. The optimal Farming strategy is running a fixed route of herb and tree patches every ~80 minutes (the growth cycle time for most farms). A max-efficiency Farming run with 91+ Farming yields 200k–400k+ XP per run while generating 1M–3M+ GP per run. This makes Farming effectively "free" 99 if you have the initial seed investment. Our 1–99 Farming guide for profit walks through the exact patch route and seed types to maximize both XP and profit. Farming is strongly recommended as an early priority skill because the passive GP generation funds other expensive 99s.

Agility: Rooftop Courses and the Grace Period

Agility is widely considered the most tedious skill to train, but it is also one of the most impactful for overall account efficiency (unlocking shortcuts that save seconds or minutes per trip). The optimal Agility path is straightforward: progress through rooftop courses in order of level requirement, from Draynor (10 Agility) to Ardougne (90 Agility). The best AFK-ish method is the Falador course (same as other courses, ~12k–18k XP/hr early, ~50k–65k XP/hr at high levels). A 2026 update improved the Sepulchre (70 Agility) to ~90k–120k XP/hr with decent loot, making it the new gold standard for efficient Agility training. Expect 20–35 hours of /played for 99 Agility via optimal methods.

Thieving: Pyramid Plunder and Ardougne Knights

Thieving has two optimal pathways depending on your account goals. For pure XP/hr, Ardougne Knights (95 Thieving) yield ~200k–280k+ XP/hr, making Thieving potentially the fastest skill in the game at maximum efficiency. However, this method is click-intensive and requires 95 Thieving. The more balanced method is Pyramid Plunder (rooms 1–4 at 57 Thieving scaling up), which yields ~90k–130k XP/hr while also generating substantial GP and unique loot (Pharaoh's Sceptre). Many players combine Pyramid Plunder with Wintertodt grinding for Firemaking for multi-skill efficiency.

Quest XP Reward Strategy: Free Levels

Quests are the single most underutilized tool for accelerating leveling. Many players treat questing as something to "get out of the way" rather than a strategic resource. In reality, completing the right quests at the right time can instantly boost skills by the equivalent of 20–50+ hours of grinding. A well-optimized quest order can get you to 50+ in multiple skills before you ever manually train them.

The Big XP Quests You Should Prioritize

Some quests stand out for their massive XP rewards. Here are the highest-value targets: Waterfall Quest (2x 13,750 XP in Attack and Strength — enough for level 30+ instantly), Tree Gnome Village (11,450 XP in Attack and Defence), Fight Arena (43,740 XP in Attack and Strength combined), Dragon Slayer I (18,650 XP in Strength and Defence), The Grand Tree (18,400 XP in Attack and Agility), and Monkey Madness I (35,000 XP in Attack and Defence). Completing just these quests before manual training can save you 15–25 hours of early-game grinding. Check our beginner questing guide for the optimal order.

Lamp and Book Rewards: Where to Spend Them

Many quests and diaries reward XP lamps or books that let you choose which skill to boost. The optimal strategy for these is almost always to put them into your slowest, most tedious skills (typically Agility, Runecrafting, or Construction) rather than skills you actively enjoy or that train passively through other content. A Tome of Fire or equivalent XP book spent on Runecrafting can save you 3–5 hours of grinding. Plan your lamp usage carefully — once spent, it cannot be changed. Prioritize lamps from our complete skill training guide recommendations to maximize value.

Quest Point Rewards and Diary Bonuses

Beyond direct XP rewards, quests unlock content that enables optimal training methods. For example, Elemental Workshop I & II unlock the ability to craft elemental shields and armor; Horror from the Deep unlocks the Berserker ring (massive melee DPS boost); The Fremennik Trials unlocks the Fremennik gloves (cheap early Defence training gear). Completing all F2P quests before becoming a member is a classic efficiency move: it gives you ~15–25 quest points and several free skill levels without spending a penny. For members, following a quest optimilization spreadsheet (like the one linked in our guides) ensures you never waste a lamp or miss an unlock.

Multi-Skill Optimization: Train Two at Once

The most efficient OSRS players do not train skills in isolation — they find methods that train two or even three skills simultaneously. This section covers the best multi-skill training methods that effectively double or triple your XP/hr efficiency.

NMZ: Up to 5 Skills at Once

Nightmare Zone deserve the top spot. By using Dharok's set with Absorption potions, you can AFK-train Attack/Strength/Defence (your chosen combat style), Hitpoints (from taking damage), and Ranged (if using a blowpipe) all at the same time. A well-configured NMZ session yields ~80k XP/hr in your primary combat style plus ~60k–80k HP XP/hr. This is the closest thing OSRS has to "passive max cape training." The initial setup cost (overloads, potions) is moderate, but the time savings are enormous.

Slayer + Prayer: The Bone Crusher Method

By completing the hard Morytania Diary and purchasing a Bone Crusher (or its upgraded version, the Bone Crusher 9000 from Fortis Colosseum), you can automatically bury bones dropped by monsters you kill. When assigned Slayer tasks, this means every kill passively trains Prayer at ~50k–200k XP/hr depending on the task. Combining Slayer with passive Prayer training effectively eliminates the need for dedicated Prayer training (which is normally one of the most expensive grinds). This is arguably the single highest-value efficiency unlock in the game for mid-game accounts.

Farming + Herblore: The Self-Sustaining Loop

Farming and Herblore have a natural synergy: Farming grows herbs that are used in Herblore. By training Farming optimally (running herb patches every ~80 minutes), you generate a steady supply of grimy herbs that can be cleaned (1 XP per herb cleaned, adding up) and eventually turned into potions. While the XP rates from cleaning your own herbs are modest, the GP savings are substantial — growing your own herbs for Herblore training can reduce the total 99 cost by 30–50M GP. For Ironmen, this loop is absolutely essential, as our Farming guide explains in detail.

Agility + Thieving: Pyramid Plunder

Pyramid Plunder deserves mention as a method that trains Thieving (primary) and Agility (secondary, from the jumping and climbing obstacles within the pyramid). While the Agility XP is modest (~10k–20k XP/hr secondary), it adds up over a long Thieving grind. More importantly, Pyramid Plunder generates substantial GP and unique loot, making it one of the most enjoyable multi-skill methods. Many players alternate Pyramid Plunder sessions with Agility rooftop courses to break up the monotony.

Final Tips for Efficient Leveling

  • Prioritize quests before grinding: Completing high-XP quests before manual training can save you 20–50+ hours total across all skills. Always check quest rewards before spending XP on a skill from level 1.
  • Use multi-skill methods whenever possible: NMZ, Slayer with Bone Crusher, and Farming + Herblore loops effectively double or triple your XP efficiency. Never train a skill in isolation if a multi-skill alternative exists.
  • Balance speed with cost: The fastest method is not always the best method. If you drain your bank account for one 99 and cannot afford the next, you have actually slowed down your overall progression. Maintain a GP buffer at all times.
  • Track your XP/hr with a tracker plugin: Use RuneLite's XP tracker widget to monitor your real-world XP rates. You may discover that your "optimal" method is underperforming due to inefficient clicking or gear choices.
  • Take advantage of bonus XP events: Jagex occasionally runs bonus XP weekends or other events. Plan your longest grinds to coincide with these events for 1.5x–2x XP efficiency.
  • Don't neglect enjoyable content: The optimal method that makes you hate the game is NOT optimal in the long run. If you burn out after 50 hours of barraging, you have lost more time than you saved. Mix high-efficiency grinds with fun content.
  • Join an efficiency Discord or community: The OSRS efficiency community (efficiency discord, 〜) constantly discovers new methods and refines XP/hr data. Staying connected to these communities ensures you always have the latest meta information.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the single fastest skill to 99 in OSRS?

Cooking is the fastest skill to 99, achievable in 4–8 hours by cooking high-level fish (sharks, manta rays, or anglerfish) at a cooking range with Hosidius kitchen eligibility (5% burn rate reduction). Fletching is the second-fastest at 8–12 hours (stringing magic longbows). These two skills are popular early 99s because they build momentum and confidence for longer grinds. However, "fastest" does not always mean "most efficient" — Cooking and Fletching provide limited account progression benefits compared to combat or production skills.

How much GP do I need to train all skills optimally to 99?

The total cost for 99ing all 23 skills using optimal (fastest) methods is approximately 600M–900M GP as of 2026 prices. The most expensive skills are Construction (120M–180M), Herblore (90M–140M), Pact (90M–130M), and Crafting (80M–120M). The cheapest skills are Farming (profit-positive), Slayer (profit-positive), and Mining (profit-positive at high levels). Most players fund their expensive 99s through bossing, high-level Slayer, or dedicated money-making methods rather than trying to front-load all the GP at once.

Is Slayer actually the most efficient way to train combat?

Yes, for the vast majority of accounts, Slayer is the most efficient combat training path. The combination of combat XP, profit, quest unlocks, and access to superior monsters makes Slayer objectively superior to grinding one NPC repeatedly. The only exception is if you need a specific combat level quickly (e.g., for a quest requirement) and cannot wait for Slayer RNG — in that case, targeted NMZ or sandbox crab training is faster for that specific stat. However, long-term, no method beats Slayer for well-rounded combat progression.

What is the best way to use quest lamps and books?

Always spend quest lamps and XP books on your slowest, most tedious skills — typically Agility, Runecrafting, Construction, or Firemaking. Never spend them on combat skills (which train passively through Slayer) or Production skills you enjoy (which you can train while making GP). A single large XP lamp spent on Runecrafting can save you 5–10 hours of grinding. Plan your lamp usage by listing your most dreaded skills and allocating lamps to them first. Our questing guide includes a lamp optimization spreadsheet.

How do I balance efficiency with fun in OSRS?

The key to sustainable efficiency is alternating high-intensity grinds with enjoyable content. A good rule of thumb: grind a skill for 2–3 hours, then do something fun (bossing, PvP, Raids, questing) for 1–2 hours. This prevents burnout while still maintaining progress. Also, remember that "optimal" is account-dependent — an Ironman's optimal path looks very different from a main account's. Do not blindly follow a spreadsheet if it makes you miserable. The most efficient player is the one who keeps playing consistently over months and years.

Are there any shortcuts to 99 that do not involve grinding?

Not really — OSRS is designed around long-term progression. However, you can significantly accelerate your 99s through: (1) quest XP rewards (free 20–50+ levels across skills), (2) Lamp/book optimization (strategic allocation to slow skills), (3) multi-skill methods (NMZ, Bone Crusher, etc.), and (4) efficient money-making to fund fast but expensive training methods. There are no "cheats" or shortcuts that bypass the fundamental time investment, but smart planning can easily cut 200–500+ hours off your journey to max cape.
