OSRS Prayer Training Guide for Beginners 2026

Prayer is the single most impactful skill in OSRS — it can make you nearly invincible against any monster. This complete beginner guide covers everything from your first bone buried to level 99, including essential prayers you need at every stage of the game.

1. Why Prayer Is the #1 Skill for Beginners

No other skill in OSRS changes your combat effectiveness as dramatically as Prayer does. At just level 43 Prayer, you unlock all three Protect Prayers that reduce incoming damage by 100% from a specific attack style.

Here's what that means practically:

🛡️ With Protect From Melee Active (Level 43)

  • A Hill Giant hits you for 0 damage on every melee attack
  • You can fight monsters 30+ levels above you without eating food
  • Boss fights go from impossible to completely manageable
  • PvP opponents using Melee become harmless
  • Your GP per hour skyrockets because you spend nothing on food

The same applies to Protect From Missiles (Ranged) and Protect From Magic. Once you have all three, you can choose which attack style to nullify before every fight.

Beyond protection, Prayer gives you massive stat boosts: +15% Strength (Piety), +23% Ranged strength (Rigour), and +25% Magic damage (Augury). These turn average DPS into elite-tier damage output.

💡 The Golden Rule of Prayer

Every single combat encounter in OSRS becomes easier with Prayer. The only question is whether you've leveled it high enough yet. Make Prayer training a priority from day one — it pays off more than any other skill investment.

The Lumbridge Chapel altar is free and restores full prayer instantly. Always stop by before heading into combat. Members should build a Gilded altar in their Player-Owned House (POH) — it gives 350% XP per bone vs 100% at a regular altar.

3. 5 Essential Prayers Every Beginner Needs

You don't need to memorize all 26 prayers right now. Focus on these 5 first — they cover 90% of what a beginner encounters:

① Protect From Melee (Level 43)

What it does: Reduces incoming melee damage by 100%. Monsters hitting you with melee attacks deal zero damage.

When to use: Fighting Hill Giants, Moss Giants, any melee-based Slayer task, Barrows brothers (melee ones), most bosses' melee phases.

Training priority: Get to 43 Prayer as soon as possible. This single prayer changes everything about PvM.

② Protect From Missiles (Level 40)

What it does: Reduces incoming ranged damage by 100%. Ranged attacks from monsters and players deal zero damage.

When to use: Fighting rangers in wilderness, monsters that use ranged attacks (some dragons, certain slayer creatures), PvP when opponent switches to bow.

③ Protect From Magic (Level 43)

What it does: Reduces incoming magic damage by 100%. Magic attacks from monsters and players deal zero damage.

When to use: Fighting mages, monsters with magic attacks (many bosses), Wilderness PKers casting spells. Also reduces damage from some special attacks like Vorkath's breath.

④ Incredible Reflexes (Level 34)

What it does: Increases your Ranged attack speed by 15%. You shoot arrows faster.

When to use: Any time you're training Ranged or using Ranged in combat. Stacks with other prayers. One of the best early-game offensive prayers because it noticeably increases kill speed even at low levels.

⑤ Ultimate Strength (Level 31)

What it does: Increases your Strength level by 15%. At 50 Strength, this adds +7 levels (57 effective). Each level increases max hit by roughly 2-3 damage.

When to use: Every melee fight. Combine with Super Attack potion for maximum effectiveness. This is the bread-and-butter prayer for melee training.

🎯 Prayer Priority Order for New Accounts

  1. Level 1–31: Bury bones while doing other activities → Unlock Ultimate Strength
  2. Level 31–34: Continue burying → Unlock Incredible Reflexes
  3. Level 34–40: Bury bigger bones → Unlock Protect From Missiles
  4. Level 40–43: Final push → Unlock Protect From Melee AND Protect From Magic
  5. Level 43+: Congratulations! You have all three Protect Prayers!

④ F2P Training Strategy

The smart approach for F2P players: don't specifically grind Prayer. Instead, pick up bones from monsters you're already killing for combat training or money making, and bury them between fights.

🦴 F2P Bone Collection Route

While training Combat at Chickens/Cows (levels 1–10) → collect and bury regular bones naturally. Move to Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon) at level 30+ → they drop Big Bones which give 15 XP each. By the time you reach level 50 combat, you'll naturally be at 37–43 Prayer without ever "grinding" it.

⑥ Gilded Altar Setup Guide

🏠 How to Access a Gilded Altar

Option A (Build Your Own): Complete the Construction quest/tutorial (requires 500K GP to start house). Build a Chapel at level 45 Construction (~400K GP materials). Add two Incense Burners (Marigolds from farming or bought on GE). Light them with clean marigold. Result: 350% XP per bone.

Option B (Use Someone Else's): Go to World 330 (official Gilded Altar host world). Walk into someone's POH through the Rimmington portal. Most hosts accept tips but don't require them. Look for "Gilded Altar" in chat or follow the crowd to the house with the glowing altar.

Efficiency tip: Use Ectophial teleport to Port Phasmatys → walk south to Canifis bank → use POH portal. Or use the Kharyrll portal in your own POH for direct Canifis access. Bank-chew-bank cycle takes about 50 seconds per inventory.

⑦ 🏆 Method 2: Chaos Altar (Wilderness — Risky but Free)

The Chaos Altar in level 38 Wilderness offers 350% XP (same as Gilded Altar) but costs nothing — no incense, no construction needed. Just bring unnoted bones and offer them there.

The risk: You're in the Wilderness. PKers can attack you at any time. If you die, you lose ALL your unnoted bones (unless protected by Protect Item prayer holding 4 items).

⚠️ Chaos Altar Safety Rules
  • Only bring what you can afford to lose (max 200K GP worth of bones per trip)
  • Wear lightweight gear (no armor needed — just boots of lightness)
  • Keep Protect Item prayer ON at all times
  • Watch the minimap for white dots (other players approaching)
  • If attacked, run south immediately — don't try to fight
  • Bring a Teleport to House tablet or Ectophial for instant escape

6. Prayer Flicking Basics

Prayer flicking is an advanced technique where you quickly toggle a prayer on and off so that its effect applies for just one game tick (0.6 seconds) but only drains prayer points for that single tick instead of continuously. Master this and your prayer points last 3–5x longer.

⑧ How Prayer Flicking Works

  1. Activate a prayer — It starts draining 1 PP per tick
  2. Immediately turn it OFF — The effect stays for the current tick
  3. Turn it back ON just before the next tick processes
  4. Repeat rapidly — This is "flicking"

The result: Instead of draining 1 PP per second, you drain maybe 0.2–0.3 PP per second, extending your prayer duration massively.

🎯 When to Use Prayer Flicking

  • Boss fights — Sarachnis, Zulrah, Vorkath: extend protect prayers to last the whole fight
  • Slayer tasks with low-damage monsters — Flick protect prayers only when the monster actually attacks
  • PvP — Flick Protect prayers based on your opponent's weapon switch
  • Don't bother flicking: Regular training (Hill Giants, etc.) — Not worth the APM effort

⑨ Getting Started with Flicking

Don't try to learn perfect flicking on day one. Start simple:

Step 1: Learn the Timing

Go to Lumbridge and find a chicken. Activate Protect From Melee, then practice clicking the prayer icon ON-OFF-ON-OFF rhythmically. Watch your prayer point counter — it should drain much slower than leaving the prayer permanently on.

Step 2: Try It In Real Combat

Go fight a Hill Giant with Protect From Melee. Leave it on until the giant attacks, then flick it off right after the attack animation finishes. Turn it back on just before the next attack comes. You'll take minimal damage while saving tons of prayer points.

Step 3: Gradual Improvement

After 2–3 hours of practice, muscle memory kicks in and flicking becomes automatic. Don't stress about being perfect — even imperfect flicking saves 50%+ prayer points compared to not flicking at all.

8. Prayer Training FAQs

What's the fastest way to 43 Prayer as a F2P player?

Buy Babydragon bones from the Grand Exchange (~800 GP each) and bury them. At 30 XP each, you need about 650 bones to reach level 43. Total cost: ~520K GP. This takes about 1 hour of straight clicking. Alternatively, fight Hill Giants for Big Bones (free but slower, ~3–4 hours).

Should I bury bones or use an altar?

Always use an altar if possible. Burying gives 100% base XP. A regular church altar gives 125% XP. A Gilded Altar with burners lit gives 350% XP. That's 3.5x faster for the exact same bones. Even if you pay tips to use someone else's Gilded Altar, it's still far more efficient than burying.

Is prayer flicking allowed? Will I get banned?

Yes, prayer flicking is 100% allowed by Jagex. It's considered a legitimate skill mechanic. Many top players use it extensively in bossing and PvP. However, using automated software (bots/macros) to flick for you IS bannable. Manual clicking only.

What bones should I use at the Gilded Altar for 99 Prayer?

It depends on your budget:

  • Budget (~10M GP total): Dragon Bones — 72→300 XP = 52,000 bones needed. Takes about 17–20 hours at Gilded Altar.
  • Medium (~28M GP total): Superior Dragon Bones — 175→729 XP = 22,000 bones needed. Takes about 10–12 hours.
  • Speed (~80M GP total): Amphyte Bones — 260→1083 XP = 15,000 bones needed. Takes about 6–8 hours.

I keep running out of prayer points in fights. What am I doing wrong?

Two possibilities: (1) Your prayer level is too low for the number of prayers you're trying to maintain simultaneously. Deactivate non-essential prayers. (2) You're not bringing enough prayer potions. For fights longer than 5 minutes, always bring 2–4 Super Restores. Consider a Prayer Flask for extended trips (holds 6 doses equivalent).
