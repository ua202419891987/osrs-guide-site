🎯 Why Dailies Pay: 47M-129M GP/Month

Most OSRS players approach money making incorrectly. They log in, grind a method for 3 hours, make 500k GP, and then log off feeling like they "earned" their GP. But this approach has a fundamental flaw: it doesn't compound.

Daily and weekly routines are different. They're based on the same principle as compound interest in finance: small, consistent actions that build on each other over time. A herb run that takes 5 minutes and earns 200k GP is infinitely more efficient than grinding Green Dragons for 3 hours — and the herb run gets better as your Farming level increases, while Green Dragons stay the same.

19.8M to 53.7M GP per month for 24 minutes of play per day. That's 1.24M - 3.36M GP per hour of active play — better than most active bossing methods, with zero risk and almost no effort.

The key insight: daily routines don't compete with active money making — they complement it. Even if you only have 30 minutes to play, spending 15 of those minutes on your daily routine means you're earning GP two ways at once: passive (from the routine) and active (from whatever you do with the remaining time).

Mindset Shift: Treat your dailies like brushing your teeth. Do them every day, at the same time, without thinking about whether you "feel like it." The GP adds up faster than you think, and the habit is more valuable than any single boss kill.

🗺️ Optimizing the Herb Run Route

The most time-efficient herb run route (for maximum profit per minute) is:

  1. Teleport to Ardougne: Use Ardougne cloak (from Ardougne Diary) or Mythical cape to reach Ardougne Farming patch.
  2. Ardougne patch: Harvest, compost, plant new seed, pay farmer (if desired).
  3. Teleport to Catherby: Use Catherby teleport (POH, Spellbook, or Achievement Diary reward).
  4. Catherby patch: Repeat harvest/plant cycle.
  5. Teleport to Falador: Use Falador teleport.
  6. Falador patch (south): Harvest/plant.
  7. Use Explorer's Ring (4) or teleport: To reach the Trollheim patch (requires My Arms Big Adventure).
  8. Trollheim patch: Harvest/plant. This patch never gets diseased.
  9. Teleport to Morytania: Use Morytania legs (from Morytania Diary) or Ectophial.
  10. Morytania patch (south of Canifis): Harvest/plant.
  11. Port Phasmatys patch (if unlocked): Requires Ghosts Ahoy and Morytania Diary.
  12. Harmony Island patch (if unlocked): Requires Great Brain Robbery.
  13. Kourend patch (Farming Guild): Use Xerus teleport or Arceuus teleport.

With all patches unlocked, a full herb run covers 7-9 patches and takes 8-10 minutes (mostly teleport time). The profit per run ranges from 80k GP (low-level herbs, no protection) to 400k+ GP (high-level herbs with ultracompost and farmer payment).

🛡️ Disease Protection — To Pay or Not to Pay?

Each herb patch can be protected by paying a farmer with a specific item. If you don't pay, there's a chance the patch will become diseased (reducing yield to 0). Here's when to pay and when to risk it:

  • Always pay for Ranarr, Snapdragon, and Torstol: These seeds are too expensive to risk. The farmer payment (usually 1-3 of a specific herb or item) costs far less than re-buying the seeds.
  • Use Ultracompost always: Ultracompost (made from Supercompost + Volcanic ash) reduces disease chance to nearly zero AND increases average yield by 1-2 herbs. At ≈1k GP per bucket, it pays for itself with one extra herb harvested.
  • The Trollheim patch never diseases: This patch (unlocked after My Arms Big Adventure) is the only completely safe patch. Always plant your most valuable seeds here, even without payment.

🐦 Birdhouse Runs: 120K/run (Mechanics + Profit)

Birdhouse runs are a Hunter training method that also generates consistent GP. They were introduced with the Fossil Island update and remain one of the best passive income sources for mid-game accounts.

🔧 How Birdhouse Runs Work

  1. Craft birdhouses: Use a knife on logs to make a birdhouse frame, then use a clockwork mechanism (made with a clockwork from The Digsite quest) on the frame. Each birdhouse requires 1 log of the appropriate type and 1 clockwork mechanism.
  2. Place birdhouses: Go to Fossil Island, dig 4 spaces (using a spade on Fossil Island soil), and place a birdhouse in each space. The birdhouse will fill with bird nests over 50 minutes.
  3. Collect after 50 minutes: Return to Fossil Island, right-click "empty" on each birdhouse. You'll receive 10-20 bird nests per house (40-80 nests total), containing herbs, seeds, and jewelry.
  4. Bank the loot: Herbs and seeds can be sold on the GE or used for your own Farming/Herblore training.

⚡ Optimizing Birdhouse Runs

  • Use a Seed box: The Seed box (from Fort Forinthry or purchased on GE) lets you store 500 of each seed type. This saves bank space and makes it easier to manage your birdhouse loot.
  • Combine with herb runs: Both have similar grow times (50 minutes for birdhouses, 80-90 for herbs). Do them in the same session to minimize login frequency.
  • Use Fossil Island teleport: After The Dig Site quest, you can use the Digsite pendant to teleport directly to Fossil Island. Alternatively, use the House on the Hill teleport (Varlamore) and run south.
  • Always use Teak or Mahogany: The log cost is negligible compared to the profit difference. At 80 Hunter, Mahogany birdhouses earn ≈120k per run vs. ≈40k for regular — that's an extra 80k GP per run for 6k GP in logs.

👑 Managing Miscellania: 425K/day (Kingdom Setup)

Managing Miscellania is a daily passive income activity unlocked after completing The Fremennik Trials quest. You appoint yourself ruler of Miscellania (and later Etceteria), invest up to 75k GP per day, and assign workers to collect resources. After 24 hours, you collect the resources and sell them on the GE for profit.

🔧 How Kingdom Management Works

  1. Invest GP: Talk to the Advisor in Miscellania castle and invest up to 75k GP per day (1.2M GP if you've also unlocked Etceteria and manage both kingdoms).
  2. Assign Workers: You have 15 workers (or 25 if managing both kingdoms). Assign them to collect specific resources. The allocation determines what you receive when you collect.
  3. Wait 24 hours: Workers collect resources over time. The approval rating (shown in the management interface) must stay above 75% for maximum efficiency. If it drops below 75%, you receive fewer resources.
  4. Collect resources: Return after 24 hours (or up to 7 days later — the game stores up to 7 days of resources) and collect your loot. Bank it, sell it, or use it for your own skilling.

  • Daily Cost: 75k GP (investment)

Kingdom Pro Tip: You can "bank" up to 7 days of kingdom resources. If you're going on vacation or won't play for a few days, invest 75k GP per day and don't collect until day 7. You'll receive 7 days' worth of resources all at once. This is great for Ironmen who need large resource batches.

📅 Weekly Tasks: 1.6M/week (Tears, Circus, Penguins)

In addition to daily routines, several weekly activities generate meaningful GP. These take 10-30 minutes per week and should be part of every money-making routine.

💧 Tears of Guthix (Weekly)

Tears of Guthix is a minigame that gives XP in your lowest skill. While not a direct money maker, the XP reward saves you hours of training time (which = money saved on training costs). It's also a quest requirement for While Guthix Sleeps and provides a nice XP lump sum once per week.

  • Requirements: Tears of Guthix quest completed, 43 Prayer (recommended for protection)
  • Time: 5-10 minutes per week
  • Reward: XP in lowest skill (≈15k-30k XP depending on quest points and stats)
  • GP Value: Indirect — saves training costs. Estimated value: ≈50k-200k GP per week (depending on what skill is lowered)

🎪 The Circus (Weekly)

The Circus is a recurring weekly event (every Monday reset) that offers various minigames. The GP reward is modest (≈50k-150k GP per week depending on performance) but it only takes 10-15 minutes.

  • Requirements: None (members only)
  • Time: 10-15 minutes per week
  • GP Reward: ≈50k-150k GP per week
  • Notes: The Circus also gives XP in Agility, Firemaking, Magic, and Thieving. The GP is a nice bonus but not the main draw.

🐧 Penguin Hide and Seek (Weekly)

Penguin Hide and Seek is a weekly Distraction & Diversion where you find 10 "disguised" penguins across Gielinor. Each penguin found gives 1-2 points (depending on type), and points can be redeemed for XP or GP.

  • Requirements: None (members only), Cold War quest started
  • Time: 20-40 minutes per week (to find all 10)
  • GP Reward: Redeem points for GP at ≈1,500 GP per point. 10 points = ≈15,000 GP (not significant). Better to redeem for XP.
  • Notes: Penguin points are more valuable as XP than GP. Only redeem for GP if you have all 99s or don't need the XP.

📜 Clue Scroll Stacking (Weekly/Monthly)

Clue scrolls are a money-making method that many players treat as a weekly activity. By "stacking" clues (doing easy/medium/hard/master sequentially), you can complete 1-2 full clue scrolls per week. High-level clues (Elite/Master) can contain valuable rares (3rd age items worth 10M-2B GP).

  • Requirements: Varies by clue tier (combat for hards, 80+ for masters)
  • Time: 30-60 minutes per clue (master)
  • GP Reward: Highly variable. Easy/Medium clues: 20k-100k GP. Hard: 50k-300k GP. Master: 100k-500k GP + rare chance at 10M-2B GP items.
  • Notes: Clue stacking prevents "clue overflow" (getting a new clue while you have an active one). Do your easiest clues first, then work up to master.

❓ FAQ + Seasonal Opportunities (Monthly Tips)

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I really need to do my dailies every single day? What if I miss a day?

A: You don't need to be perfect. Missing 1-2 days per week won't ruin your progress. But the compounding effect is real — doing your dailies 7 days a week earns 4x more than doing them once a week. If you can't log in daily, at minimum do your herb runs and birdhouse runs 3-4 times per week. Kingdom can be collected once every 7 days without penalty.

Q: What's the single most valuable daily routine for a new member (first week in members)?

A: Herb runs. Even at 32 Farming (Ranarr), your first herb run will earn 80k-150k GP. Do 3 runs per day and you've made 240k-450k GP on day one. Combine with battlestaves (unlocks at 54 Crafting / 66 Magic after The Hand in the Sand) for an additional 130k GP per day.

Q: How do I find time for dailies if I only have 1 hour to play per day?

A: Spend the first 15 minutes on dailies, then spend the remaining 45 minutes on active money making (bossing, Slayer, etc.). This way you earn GP two ways simultaneously. Over a month, those 15-minute daily routines will generate 20M-50M GP — which is probably more than your active play earned in that same month.

Q: Are there any "seasonal" money-making opportunities I should know about?

A: Yes! OSRS has several seasonal events that offer unique money-making opportunities:

  • Halloween Event (October): Candy tokens can be alched or used for XP. Usually worth 50k-200k GP if you complete all tasks.
  • Christmas Event (December): Christmas crackers (if obtained) contain 3rd age items worth 10M-2B GP. The event itself gives small GP rewards.
  • Easter Event (March/April): Easter eggs can sometimes be sold for GP. Usually modest (50k-100k GP).
  • Double XP Weekends (3-4 per year): Not a direct money maker, but the free XP saves you massive training costs. Plan your most expensive training (Prayer, Herblore, Construction) for these weekends.

Q: Should I cancel my daily routine to do League seasons or temporary content?

A: League seasons (which occur roughly annually) offer unique rewards and cosmetics that can never be obtained again. If a League is active, prioritize it over daily routines — the rewards are time-limited. For other temporary content (e.g., D&D rotations), keep up your dailies unless the temporary content offers unique rewards you can't get otherwise.

💡 Monthly Money Opportunities (Lesser-Known Methods)

  • Sell Pressure (GE Manipulation): Some players "sell pressure" items with high alch values when the GE price is below alch value. This is advanced and risky, but can generate 5M-20M GP per month if you understand the market.
  • Quest Speed-Running Services: If you're skilled at questing, you can offer questing services on the OSRS forums or Discord for 1M-5M GP per quest. This is against Jagex's real-world trading policy if you accept real money, but GP-for-GP services are generally tolerated.
  • TEAMS (Temp Entity Assistance): Helping other players with bosses they can't solo (e.g., helping with Chambers of Xeric, Theatre of Blood) can earn you a "tip" of 100k-1M GP per raid. Again, check Jagex's current policy on paid services.

Final Thought: The beauty of daily and weekly routines is that they turn OSRS from a "grind" into a habit. You log in, do your routines, and you've made meaningful progress toward your GP goals — even if you only have 15 minutes to play. Consistency beats intensity every time. A player who does their dailies every day for a year will be richer than a player who grinds for 12 hours one day and then doesn't play for a week.
