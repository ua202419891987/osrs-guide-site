Understanding Skill Dependencies & Prerequisites

What Are Skill Dependencies?

Skill dependencies represent situations where training one skill efficiently requires having trained another skill first. Understanding dependencies prevents wasteful progression and ensures you're never locked out of content due to missing prerequisites. Smart dependency planning compresses your overall progression timeline and maximizes early-game efficiency.

Combat-Based Dependencies

Combat skills form an interconnected web of dependencies. Prayer dramatically improves combat efficiency through offensive prayer boosts (Piety for melee, Eagle Eye for ranged, Mystic Might for magic). Higher Hitpoints significantly improve bossing capabilities. Magic enables access to high-level teleportation spells, utility spells, and combat interactions. Most combat-focused players should reach 70+ Attack, Strength, and Defence before investing heavily in Prayer and Magic, as these support skills provide multiplicative benefits at higher base stats.

Production-Based Dependencies

Production skills have surprisingly few hard dependencies but benefit from supporting skills. Cooking depends on Fishing for raw food supply. Smithing requires Mining for ore acquisition. Herblore requires Farming for herb supply in late-game content. Magic enables access to high-level teleportation spells required for efficient training. Most production skills can train independently but improve dramatically with supporting gathering skills.

Quest & Content Prerequisites

Many quests and content pieces require specific skill levels. Waterfall Quest requires 30 Attack and 30 Strength minimum. Monkey Madness requires 70 Attack and Strength minimum. Grand Tree Quest requires 60 Woodcutting. Lunar Diplomacy requires 40 Prayer, 40 Magic, 60 Defence, 65 Herblore, and 75 Crafting. Planning your progression around major quests ensures you don't waste time on locked content.

Early, Mid, and Late-Game Progression Phases

Early-Game Phase (0-40 Total Level)

Your early-game phase should focus entirely on establishing combat capability. Target 40 Attack, 40 Strength, and 40 Defence through synchronized training at goblins or other low-level monsters. Simultaneously train Cooking to 30-40 for healing food production. This foundation typically requires 8-12 hours and opens access to basic slayer content. Early-game players should complete the tutorial island and reach level 1-5 in several skills, then focus entirely on combat. Money-making is not a priority in early-game; your focus is establishing foundational capabilities.

Early-Mid-Game Phase (40-70 Total Level)

Progress your combat stats to 70 Attack, 70 Strength, and 70 Defence through slayer tasks. Slayer provides the ideal middle-ground between combat progression and profit generation. Reach 70 Cooking for reliable food supply. Reach 60 Agility for improved travel efficiency. Reach 40 Magic for basic utility spells. This phase typically requires 30-50 hours and generates your first meaningful profit through slayer tasks (300k-800k gp/hour). Many intermediate players stop at these levels and pursue specific goals from this foundation.

Mid-Game Phase (70-85 Total Level)

Diversify your skill progression during mid-game. Push combat stats toward 90+ through slayer. Develop production skills: reach 70+ Herblore for elite potion creation, 70+ Crafting for jewelry production, 70+ Fishing for consistent gathering. Reach 70+ Prayer for access to elite prayer buffs. This phase typically requires 60-100 hours and generates significantly improved profit (1-2m+ gp/hour through production or bossing). Mid-game players access dangerous content that provides exceptional profit opportunities.

Late-Game Phase (85-99 Total Level)

Pursue your personal 99s during late-game. Whether you're chasing combat-stat perfection, production-skill mastery, or completionist goals, late-game is where specialization determines your OSRS identity. Reach 99 in your chosen skills for maximum efficiency in preferred activities. Many late-game players generate 2-5m+ gp/hour through combination of high-level bossing, elite production, and specialized activities. Late-game progression is entirely self-directed based on personal goals and preferences.

Milestone Checklist

  • Level 40 Milestone: Basic combat proficiency reached, slayer access unlocked, first meaningful content available
  • Level 70 Milestone: Mid-level boss access, slayer profitability achieved, production skill options available
  • Level 80 Milestone: High-level slayer profitable, elite production viable, diverse playstyle options available
  • Level 90 Milestone: Boss-level combat capability, elite production profit secured, specialized content access unlocked
  • Level 99 Milestone: Mastery in chosen skills, maximum content access, legacy achievement unlocked

Playstyle-Specific Progression Paths

The Casual Completionist Path

Casual completionists pursue balanced progression with emphasis on achievement diaries and quest requirements. Progression order: 1) Reach 40 Combat Stats, 2) Train Support Skills (60+ Agility, 50+ Prayer), 3) Pursue Achievement Diaries requirements, 4) Level support skills to 99 for unlocked content, 5) Optional specialized skills. This path typically generates 400k-1.5m gp/hour through diverse activities. Casual players enjoy content variety and long-term engagement without pressure toward specific monetary goals. Time investment: 300+ hours for significant achievement diary progress.

The Hardcore Money-Maker Path

Hardcore money-makers optimize every hour for maximum gold production. Progression order: 1) Reach 70 Combat Stats minimum through efficient slayer, 2) Train Herblore to 70+ for potion creation, 3) Train Crafting to 80+ for jewelry production, 4) Reach 77+ Runecrafting for blood rune profitability, 5) Achieve 99 in highest-ROI skills. This path generates 2-5m+ gp/hour at high levels through elite production and bossing. Money-making focused players accumulate billions of gp over 500+ hours. Time investment: Highly variable based on skill selection, but typically 200-400 hours to reach 2m+ gp/hour generation.

The PvP Combat Specialist Path

PvP specialists focus on combat prowess and wilderness survival. Progression order: 1) Reach 70+ Combat Stats for basic competency, 2) Train Prayer to 70+ for offensive boosts, 3) Reach 94+ Magic for elite combat spells, 4) Achieve 99 Attack, Strength, and Defence for maximum DPS, 5) Develop specialized wilderness skills (thieving for supplies, construction for housing buffs). This path generates exceptional combat effectiveness and wilderness dominance. PvP specialists prioritize skill optimization over profit generation. Time investment: 400+ hours for competitive late-game PvP capability.

The Boss Raider Path

Boss raiders focus on accessing high-level bosses and elite bossing content. Progression order: 1) Reach 75+ Combat Stats for mid-level boss access, 2) Train Prayer to 70+ for combat buffs, 3) Reach 80+ Defence for survival capability, 4) Train Magic to 80+ for boss-specific requirements, 5) Achieve 90+ Attack, Strength, and Prayer for elite boss mechanics. This path generates 2-5m+ gp/hour through specialized bossing. Boss raiders experience the most engaging and challenging OSRS content. Time investment: 300+ hours for serious bossing viability.

The AFK-Friendly Path

AFK players prioritize minimal-attention skills that can train while monitoring secondary activities. Progression order: 1) Reach 70 Combat Stats through slayer (minimal AFK), 2) Train Woodcutting and Fishing to 99 for AFK gathering, 3) Train Fletching to 99 for AFK arrow-shaft production, 4) Train Cooking and Smithing concurrently, 5) Optional AFK production skills. This path generates 400k-1.2m gp/hour through AFK activities. AFK players can maintain OSRS progression while working, studying, or managing other responsibilities. Time investment: 600+ hours for comprehensive AFK skill development, but time is highly parallelizable with other activities.

Return on Investment Analysis: Time vs. Benefits

Understanding Skill ROI

Skill ROI compares the time investment required to train a skill against the benefit gained (profit generation, quest access, or content unlocks). High-ROI skills provide significant benefits relative to time investment. Low-ROI skills require extensive time investment for minimal practical benefit. Strategic skill selection prioritizes high-ROI skills during early-mid game, saving low-ROI grinds for late-game specialization.

Exceptional ROI Skills (Early-Game Priority)

These skills provide maximum benefit relative to training time: Cooking (40-hour grind, enables food production supporting all activities), Attack (60-hour grind to 70, enables slayer access generating 500k+gp/hour), Hitpoints (trains passively with combat, critical for survival), Prayer (50-hour grind to 70, provides 100%+ combat effectiveness improvement). These core skills should be trained immediately; their benefits compound over 1000+ hours of endgame gameplay.

Good ROI Skills (Mid-Game Focus)

Good ROI skills provide solid benefits: Fishing (120-hour grind to 99, generates 800k+ gp/hour and provides healing food), Defence (60-hour grind to 70, essential for bossing), Magic (100-hour grind to 80+, enables quest access and boss content), Slayer (200-hour grind to 70, generates 1m+ gp/hour). These mid-game skills deserve training emphasis as benefits scale significantly.

Mediocre ROI Skills (Optional Specialization)

Mediocre ROI skills provide moderate benefits relative to time investment: Agility (50-hour grind provides quality-of-life rather than direct profit), Thieving (80-hour grind for moderate profit), Herblore (100-hour grind to 70, required for elite bossing). These skills merit training when they support your chosen playstyle but don't justify early priority.

Low ROI Skills (Late-Game Only)

Low ROI skills require extensive time for minimal practical benefit: Firemaking (8-hour grind to 99, provides minimal in-game benefit), Early Runecrafting (200-hour grind to 77, generates moderate profit but requires absolute highest-level training). These skills deserve training only during late-game specialization when you've maximized core skills.

ROI Calculation Example

  • Cooking to 99: 6 hours time investment × 0 gp/hour profit = 0 gp value, +infinite utility value. ROI: Exceptional (utility-based, not profit-based)
  • Crafting to 99: 150 hours time investment × 2m gp/hour at 99 = 300m gp value. Breakeven at endgame: 75 hours (half time investment). ROI: Very Good
  • Fishing to 99: 120 hours time investment × 800k gp/hour at 99 = 96m gp value. Breakeven at endgame: 120 hours (100% of time investment). ROI: Good
  • Firemaking to 99: 8 hours time investment × 0 gp/hour profit = 0 gp value, minimal utility. ROI: Poor

Common Progression Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Mistake 1: Training Skills in Random Order

Many new players train whichever skill interests them at the moment, resulting in chaotic progression with significant time waste. Consequence: Reaching high levels in low-priority skills, missing critical early progression, inefficient content access. Solution: Plan your first 100 hours entirely focused on combat stats and core support skills. Establish your foundation before exploring specialized skills.

Mistake 2: Ignoring Prayer Too Long

Many players delay Prayer training until much later, missing tremendous combat efficiency improvements available through mid-game bossing. Consequence: Dramatically slower slayer progression, missed bossing opportunities, 50%+ reduced damage output during mid-game content. Solution: Reach Prayer 70+ by level 85 total. Prayer represents the best single damage improvement available and should be prioritized alongside combat stats.

Mistake 3: Over-Investing in Low-ROI Skills

Players often grind low-ROI skills like Firemaking, Runecrafting early game, wasting time that could develop core skills. Consequence: Extended grind times for minimal benefit, reduced early-game profitability, delayed access to higher-level content. Solution: Identify high-ROI targets (combat stats, prayer, core gathering), grind those first, save specialization skills for late-game.

Mistake 4: Not Planning for Quest Requirements

Many players encounter quest requirements mid-game and must interrupt primary grinding for prerequisite skills. Consequence: Unexpected grinds disrupting progression flow, frustration and potential player abandonment. Solution: Research major quest requirements before beginning progression planning. Front-load quests requirements into your early progression plan.

Mistake 5: Ignoring Your Playstyle

New players often train skills they think they should train rather than skills aligned with their actual playstyle preferences. Consequence: Grinding skills you don't enjoy, reduced gameplay satisfaction, potential player burnout. Solution: Honestly assess whether you prefer active gameplay (bossing, slayer) or AFK progression (gathering, production). Align your skill selection toward your preferences.

Mistake 6: Rushing to 99s Too Early

Ambitious players often pursue level 99 in skills prematurely, delaying access to content that requires more balanced development. Consequence: Hyper-specialized characters lacking versatility, unnecessary extreme grinds in low-priority skills. Solution: Develop balanced core skills to 70-85 range before pursuing specialized 99s. Versatility enables superior content access than specialization in early-mid game.

Mistake 7: Not Using Available Resources

Many new players underutilize existing guides, training methods, and community knowledge, resulting in inefficient training and suboptimal progression. Consequence: Training at outdated or suboptimal methods, significantly extended training times, missed gold-making opportunities. Solution: Research current best-practice training methods for each skill. Consult community resources before beginning long grinds.

Final Strategic Tips for Optimal Progression

  • Create a progression roadmap: Write down your first five 99s before starting grinding. This clarity prevents mid-game direction changes that waste time.
  • Balance active and AFK: Combine engaging content (slayer, bossing) with AFK skills (woodcutting, fishing) to prevent burnout during long grinds.
  • Leverage early progression efficiently: Your first 100 hours dramatically impact your long-term progression efficiency. Don't waste them on low-priority skills.
  • Join a community: Experienced players provide invaluable guidance, motivation, and content partnership. Community engagement dramatically improves progression satisfaction.
  • Monitor progress regularly: Track your progression milestones and celebrate achievements. Progress visibility provides motivation during long grinds.
  • Adjust based on happiness: If a skill grind becomes unbearable, pause and pursue different content. Enjoyment matters more than theoretical optimization.
  • Consider group activities early: Dungeoneering, Chambers of Xeric, and group bosses provide social progression while advancing core skills and generating profit.

Monthly Checkpoint System

The most effective progression trackers use monthly checkpoints rather than daily goals. Each month, assess your progress across three dimensions: combat readiness, wealth generation, and quality-of-life improvements. Set specific numeric targets for each — for example, Month 1 targets might include 50+ combat stats, 500K GP bank value, and completion of 10 key quests. Month 3 targets escalate to 70+ combat, 5M GP, and access to Barrows or God Wars Dungeon. This checkpoint system prevents the common trap of training aimlessly while providing clear direction without the rigidity of daily schedules that most players abandon within weeks. Players who track monthly checkpoints report 30% higher goal completion rates than those using ad-hoc planning methods.

Quest-Gated Skill Requirements

Many high-level training methods are locked behind quest requirements, making quest completion a parallel progression track alongside skill training. For example, accessing the Pyromancer outfit for Wintertodt requires Lost City, while the Bone Crusher from the Morytania diary needs multiple prerequisite quests. Plan your questing timeline alongside your skill progression to avoid bottlenecks where you reach the required level but cannot access the optimal training method because of missing quest completions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the absolute fastest path to late-game viability?

The fastest path prioritizes combat stats and prayer: reach 70 Attack, Strength, Defence, and Prayer through slayer tasks (50-70 hours), then push to 85+ combat stats while training production skills (100-150 hours). By 150 total hours, you're viable for mid-level bossing and elite slayer content. This fast-track path sacrifices specialization for rapid access to endgame content.

Should I pursue 99s early or spread skills evenly?

Spread skills to 70-85 range first, then specialize. Balanced development enables access to diverse content and better profitability compared to hyper-specialized builds. Once you reach 1,500+ total level, pursue specialized 99s aligned with your goals. Early specialization limits playstyle flexibility and reduces content access.

How do I know which playstyle is right for me?

Try different activities during early-mid game: combat slayer (PvE), bossing (skill challenges), production activities (AFK profit), PvP wilderness (competitive). Notice which activities you return to repeatedly. Your playstyle preference determines optimal skill progression; specializing in uncongenial activities leads to burnout.

Is Prayer training expensive?

Yes, Prayer training typically costs 20-50m gp from bones (expensive) or 50-100m+ gp from elite bones. However, Prayer provides multiplicative combat efficiency improvements that generate this investment back through improved slayer profitability within 50-100 hours. Prayer investment is expensive but excellent long-term value.

Can I skip any skills entirely?

Yes, approximately 8-10 skills are optional. You can complete 99% of OSRS content without maxing Firemaking, Runecrafting, or Construction. However, skipping combat skills (Attack, Defence, Hitpoints) or support skills (Prayer, Magic) significantly limits content access. Most skipped skills are low-ROI specializations trainable only in late-game.

What if I've already trained in suboptimal order?

Don't reset or restart; accept the past and optimize future progression. If you've over-invested in low-ROI skills, simply prioritize high-ROI skills going forward. Your accumulated experience provides advantages offsetting less-than-optimal early progression. Many successful players had suboptimal early progressions but optimized afterward.

How often should I update my progression plan?

Review your progression plan every 50-100 hours, adjusting based on actual playstyle preferences, discovered preferences, and early success measurements. Plans should guide direction while remaining flexible enough to accommodate preference changes and discovered interests during gameplay.
