1. Why Herb Runs Are the Best Passive Money Maker in OSRS

Herb runs sit in a unique sweet spot in OSRS: they require almost no active attention, have low skill requirements, and deliver consistent, repeatable profit that scales with your account progression. No other money-making method gives you 1M-3M GP per day for just 15-25 minutes of work.

Here is why herb runs outperform most other money makers:

💰 Why Herb Runs Win

  • True passive income: Plant once, come back 80 minutes later — the game does the work while you quest, PvM, or skill
  • Low requirements: Start making profit from level 32 Farming (Ranarr) — reachable in a single day of questing
  • Scales with patches: Start with 5 patches, unlock all 9 over time — each new patch adds 15-20% more profit
  • No competition: Herb patches are instanced per player — you never compete for resources
  • Consistent GE prices: Herbs maintain stable prices due to high demand from Herblore training
  • Combines with birdhouse runs: Do herb runs alongside birdhouse runs for maximum passive GP/hour
💡 The 80-Minute Rule

All herbs take exactly 80 minutes to fully grow (4 growth stages at 20 minutes each). Set a timer on your phone or use RuneLite's timers plugin to never miss a harvest. Three runs per day at 5 minutes each = 15 minutes of work for 2.5M-3M GP.

📊 The Verdict

For low-risk, high-consistency runs, Toadflax is the smart choice. The seeds are cheap enough that even a full crop failure (rare with ultracompost) costs only 2,500 GP per patch.

4. Optimal Herb Run Route

A well-planned route saves 2-3 minutes per run. With 3-4 runs per day, that is 6-12 minutes saved daily — which adds up to hours over a month. Here is the most efficient route starting from a bank:

Step 1: Catherby (Bank teleport)

Use Camelot Teleport or Catherby teleport. Run north to the patch. This is the closest patch to a bank, making it the ideal starting point. Deposit herbs, withdraw seeds, compost, and secateurs.

Step 2: Ardougne (Ardougne Cape / Teleport)

Teleport to Ardougne using an Ardougne cloak, Ardougne teleport, or Skills necklace to the Fishing Guild. Run south-west past the monastery to the patch.

Step 3: Hosidius (Xeric's Talisman)

Use Xeric's Talisman to teleport to Hosidius (or use a skills necklace to Woodcutting Guild and run south). The patch is west of the Hosidius kitchen, near the plough field.

Step 4: Farming Guild (Skills Necklace)

Use a Skills Necklace to teleport to the Farming Guild. The herb patch is on the first floor. Run up the stairs immediately after entering.

Step 5: Falador / Port Sarim (Explorer's Ring / Falador Teleport)

Teleport to Falador using Explorer's Ring 2+ or a Falador teleport. Run south past the Port Sarim jail to the patch south of the fishing shop.

Step 6: Trollheim (Trollheim Teleport)

Use the Trollheim teleport on the Standard spellbook. Run north past the herb patch area. Requires My Arm's Big Adventure quest to be completed.

Step 7: Weiss (Weiss Teleport / Icy Basalt)

Use Weiss teleport (Icy Basalt requires 64 Magic and the Making Friends with My Arm quest) or use the Trollheim teleport and run north-east through the cave.

Step 8: Canifis (Ectophial / Kharyrll Portal)

Use an Ectophial to teleport to Port Phasmatys, then run east to Canifis. Alternatively, use a Kharyrll portal in your POH. The patch is east of the Canifis bank.

Step 9: Harmony Island (Harmony Island Teleport)

Use the Harmony Island teleport if available (The Great Brain Robbery required). This patch adds the least convenience but every patch counts for maximum profit.

⏱️ Route Time: 5-7 Minutes

A full 9-patch run takes roughly 5-7 minutes with optimal teleports. Without the late-game patches (Weiss, Harmony Island), expect 4-5 minutes for 7 patches. The entire run fits comfortably between Slayer tasks, quests, or during a POH break.

8. Compost & Magic Secateurs Guide

Two items make the difference between a mediocre herb run and an exceptional one. Using ultracompost and Magic Secateurs is non-negotiable for serious profit.

🌱 Ultracompost: The Single Best Investment

Ultracompost costs roughly 1,000 GP per bucket on the GE. Each herb patch needs one bucket. Here is what it does:

💰 Ultracompost Math

One bucket of ultracompost costs ~1,000 GP but increases your average yield from 5 to 8-9 herbs per patch. With Snapdragon at 13,500 GP each, that is 40,500-54,000 GP of extra herbs for only 1,000 GP investment. The ROI is 4,000-5,000%. Never skip ultracompost.

✂️ Magic Secateurs: Free 10% Yield Boost

Obtained from the Fairytale I - Growing Pains quest, the Magic Secateurs provide a +10% boost to the number of herbs you harvest from each patch. This is a passive effect — they just need to be in your inventory or on your tool belt.

How to Get Magic Secateurs

Complete Fairytale I - Growing Pains. Requirements: 17,000 Farming XP (from any source), 49 Thieving, 40 Farming, 10 Herblore. The quest takes ~30 minutes and rewards you with Magic Secateurs plus 17,000 Farming XP. This is the most impactful quest for herb run profitability in the entire game.

10. Herb Run Profit FAQs

What is the most profitable herb to farm in OSRS in 2026?

Snapdragon is the most profitable overall at 80K GP per patch (720K GP for 9 patches). However, if you factor in seed cost risk, Toadflax offers the best profit-to-risk ratio with seeds costing only 2,500 GP each. For players under level 62, Ranarr (level 32) is the clear winner at 74K GP per patch.

How many herb patches can I access with minimal questing?

You can access 5 patches with zero quest requirements: Falador, Catherby, Ardougne, Canifis, and Hosidius (requires 65% Kourend favour but no quests). Adding Trollheim (My Arm's Big Adventure) and the Farming Guild (65 Farming) brings you to 7 patches. The final two patches (Weiss and Harmony Island) require 2 additional quests but are lower priority.

Is ultracompost really worth the cost for herb runs?

Absolutely yes. Ultracompost costs ~1,000 GP per bucket but increases your average yield from 5 herbs to 8-9 herbs per patch. With Snapdragon at 13,500 GP each, that extra 3-4 herbs per patch represents 40,500-54,000 GP of additional value per patch, for only 1,000 GP investment. The return on investment is approximately 4,000-5,000%. There is no better investment in your herb run setup.

Can I lose money on herb runs?

It is very difficult to lose money with proper setup. The only way to lose GP is if multiple patches die (disease) and your yield drops below 4 herbs per patch. With ultracompost, disease chance drops to 3-5%. Even if one patch dies completely, the other 8 patches easily cover the loss. Toadflax is mathematically impossible to lose money on at current prices — even 1 herb harvested covers the seed cost.

Should I combine herb runs with birdhouse runs?

Yes, this is the optimal strategy. Birdhouses take exactly 50 minutes to fill (similar to herbs at 80 minutes) and are located on Fossil Island. The teleport route from Fossil Island pairs perfectly with herb runs. See our complete birdhouse runs guide for the full synergy strategy.
