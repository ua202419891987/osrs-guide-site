Quest Overview & Lore

The Golem (referred to by many players as the "Goraik quest" due to the golem's ruinous nature) is a classic members-only quest set in the Kharidian Desert. It tells the story of a dormant clay golem in the abandoned city of Uzer and the magical force that awakens it. The quest is part of the desert quest series and connects loosely to the broader desert narrative that includes Icthlarin's Little Helper and Contact!

The golem itself was built centuries ago by the ancient mage Vizier as a guardian for the city of Uzer. After a great calamity destroyed the city, the golem fell into a deep slumber — until you, the player, arrive to rekindle its magic and uncover what truly happened to Uzer. Along the way you will explore desert lore, solve a multi-step puzzle, and face off against a powerful demon summoner.

Step-by-Step Walkthrough

This walkthrough is written to be clear and easy to follow. It includes all steps needed to complete the quest from start to finish. For puzzle solutions (spoilers), see the Puzzle Solutions section.

Step 1: Starting the Quest — Talk to the Golem in Uzer

  • Travel to the Kharidian Desert. The easiest way is to use a Desert Amulet 1 (from Easy Desert Diary) or walk through the Shantay Pass (bring 5 GP or a Shantay Pass permit).
  • Head to the ruined city of Uzer (south-east of the Shantay Pass, marked on the world map).
  • Inside the ruins, go to the northern building. You will find a dormant clay golem in the centre of the room.
  • Right-click "Search" the golem or talk to it (it is dormant at first). You will receive a scroll that starts the quest.
  • Read the scroll. It mentions the Vizier's journal and gives you a clue to search the bookshelves in the building.

Step 2: Find the Vizier's Journal

  • Search the bookshelves in the same room as the golem. You will find Vizier's journal (Volume 1).
  • Read the journal. It explains that the golem needs a special clay mixture and a magic spell to reawaken.
  • The journal also mentions that the recipe for the clay is hidden in the library of the Desert Phoenix (a building in the desert).
  • Head to the Desert Phoenix (a building north-east of the Shantay Pass, near Al Kharid).
  • Search the bookshelves there to find Vizier's journal (Volume 2) and a clay recipe scroll.
💡 Navigation Tip: If you have completed the Grand Tree quest or have a Spirit Tree unlocked, you can use the Spirit Tree network to get close to the desert quickly.

Step 3: Prepare the Special Clay

  • The clay recipe requires: 4 buckets of water + soft clay (made by using water on clay).
  • Buy or collect 4 buckets of water (can be from any water source).
  • Buy or mine clay (mining spot in the Dwarven Mine or Rimmington). Use the clay on a water source (fountain or sink) to make soft clay.
  • Use the 4 buckets of water on the soft clay while standing near the golem in Uzer. This creates enchanted clay mixture.
  • Use the enchanted clay mixture on the dormant golem. The golem will partially awaken and ask for a magic spell to fully activate.

Step 4: Learn the Activation Spell

  • Return to the Desert Phoenix library and search the bookshelves again. You will find a spell scroll containing the activation incantation.
  • The scroll requires you to have level 33 Magic to read (or use a magic spell book). If your Magic is below 33, train it now using our magic training guide.
  • Read the scroll. It teaches you the "Golem Awakening" spell (cosmetic; in practice you just need to use the scroll on the golem).
  • Return to Uzer and use the spell scroll on the partially awakened golem. The golem will fully awaken!

Step 5: The Golem's Request — Defeat the Demon Summoner

  • Now that the golem is awake, it tells you that a demon summoner is threatening the ruins of Uzer. You must defeat the summoner to restore peace.
  • The golem gives you a demon-bane amulet (+10% damage vs. demons). Equip it!
  • Go to the south chamber of the Uzer ruins. You will find the Demon Summoner (a level 75 NPC).
  • Preparation: Equip your best combat gear, pray Protect from Magic (the summoner uses magic attacks), and eat food when below 50 HP.
  • Defeat the Demon Summoner. The fight is straightforward — the summoner has 75 HP and uses magic attacks that max 12 damage.
  • After defeating the summoner, search the chest in the south chamber to obtain the Vizier's key.

Step 6: Unlock the Golem's True Potential

  • Return to the awakened golem and use the Vizier's key on it. The golem will unlock its full power and thank you.
  • The golem will now serve as a follower NPC (cosmetic; it follows you around Uzer for the rest of the quest).
  • Talk to the golem one final time to complete the quest.
  • Congratulations! You have finished The Golem quest. Claim your rewards from the quest reward interface.
⚠️ Combat Warning: The Demon Summoner can hit hard on low-defence accounts. If you are below Combat level 50, consider bringing a friend or using range/magic to safespot him.

Puzzle Solutions (Spoilers)

The Golem quest has two main puzzles: the clay preparation puzzle and the spell scroll puzzle. Here are the full solutions.

Puzzle 1: The Clay Mixture Recipe

Objective: Create the enchanted clay mixture to partially awaken the golem.

Solution (Step-by-Step):

  1. Obtain 4 buckets of water (fill buckets from any water source).
  2. Obtain 1 soft clay (use clay on water source to make soft clay).
  3. Use the 4 buckets of water on the soft clay while standing next to the golem in Uzer.
  4. The game will prompt you: "Mix the water into the clay?" Click Yes.
  5. You now have enchanted clay mixture. Use it on the dormant golem.

Common Mistake: Trying to use regular clay or buckets of water separately on the golem. You MUST mix them first!

Puzzle 2: The Spell Scroll (Magic Requirement)

Objective: Obtain and use the spell scroll to fully awaken the golem.

Solution (Step-by-Step):

  1. Go to the Desert Phoenix library (north-east of Shantay Pass).
  2. Search the bookshelves (not the desk). You may need to search multiple times.
  3. After 2-3 searches, you will find the spell scroll.
  4. Read the scroll (requires 33 Magic; if below 33, train Magic first).
  5. Return to Uzer and use the spell scroll on the partially awakened golem.
  6. The golem fully awakens and the quest progresses.

Tip: If you cannot find the scroll, make sure you have read Vizier's journal (Volume 2) first — it gives a clue about which bookshelf to search.

Puzzle 3: The Demon Summoner Fight (Combat Puzzle)

Objective: Defeat the Demon Summoner in the south chamber of Uzer.

Solution (Combat Strategy):

  1. Equip the demon-bane amulet given by the golem (+10% damage vs. demons).
  2. Pray Protect from Magic (the summoner's main attack style).
  3. Attack with your strongest style (melee recommended; the summoner has low defence).
  4. Eat food when your HP drops below 50. The summoner hits up to 12 damage per hit.
  5. After defeating the summoner, search the chest in the south chamber for the Vizier's key.
💡 Puzzle Tip: If you are struggling with the demon fight, consider using ranging or maging from a distance. The summoner cannot ranged or mage you back if you stand behind the ruined walls in the south chamber.

Post-Quest Content & Unlocks

After completing The Golem, there are several pieces of content that become available or easier to access. Here is what you can do next.

Desert Diary (Easy)

  • Completing The Golem is a requirement for the Easy Desert Diary.
  • Finish the rest of the Easy Desert Diary to earn the Desert Amulet 1, which gives:
    • Desert heat resistance (1 hour longer before taking damage)
    • Pharaoh's Sceptre (if you have completed Icthlarin's Little Helper)
    • Access to Nardah teleport (very useful for desert travel)

Continue the Desert Quest Series

The Golem is part of the broader desert narrative. After finishing it, consider doing these quests next:

  • Icthlarin's Little Helper — unlocks the desert pantheon lore and gives early desert access.
  • Contact! — mid-level desert quest with decent rewards (15K XP lamps).
  • Desert Treasure — the grand finale of the desert series (unlocks Ancient Magicks).
  • The Tale of the Muspah — not desert-specific but thematically similar ancient lore.

Farm Desert Herbs (After Quest)

Now that you have unlocked Uzer, you can farm desert herbs in the ruin patches. Uzer has a herb patch that is rarely used by other players, making it a good spot for Farming training.

Slayer Tasks in the Desert

After the quest, you can receive Slayer tasks in the desert area from the Desert Slayer Master (once unlocked). The desert has several good Rush/Easy Slayer monsters including:

  • Desert Lizards (Slayer level 22+) — good for low-level Slayer.
  • Crocodiles (Slayer level 1+) — found near the River Elid.
  • Vultures (Slayer level 30+) — decent drops for mid-level players.

Ironman-Specific Tips

Ironman accounts have unique considerations for The Golem quest. Since you cannot trade with other players or use the Grand Exchange, you will need to gather all quest items yourself. Here are tips for Ironmen.

Ironman Preparation Checklist

  1. Waterskins: You must craft your own waterskins. Buy empty skins from the Desert Shop (Shantay Pass) and fill them at a water source. Bring 6 full waterskins.
  2. Buckets: Buy from the General Store in Al Kharid or Port Sarim. You need 4 buckets.
  3. Clay: Mine clay in the Dwarven Mine (Edgeville Dungeon) or Rimmington mine. You need 1 clay to make soft clay.
  4. Soft Clay: Use the clay on a water source (fountain in a POH or a sink in a city). You need 1 soft clay.
  5. Food: Cook your own lobsters or swordfish. Train Hitpoints by fighting cows or algae to get combat stats up before the quest.
  6. Gear: Smith your own Rune armour or get Dragon gear from The Lost City and Dragon Slayer quests.

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Even experienced players sometimes make mistakes in The Golem quest. Here are the most common pitfalls and how to avoid them.

Mistake 1: Not Bringing Enough Waterskins

The Mistake: Running out of water in the desert and taking heat damage (or dying).
The Fix: Always bring at least 6 full waterskins (4-dose). Drink BEFORE your character starts taking damage.

Mistake 2: Forgetting to Read Both Journal Volumes

The Mistake: Only reading Volume 1 and wondering why the quest does not progress.
The Fix: Search the bookshelves in BOTH Uzer AND the Desert Phoenix. You need both journal volumes to progress.

Mistake 3: Using Regular Clay Instead of Soft Clay

The Mistake: Trying to use regular clay with water buckets and failing the puzzle.
The Fix: You MUST turn the clay into soft clay first (use clay on water source). Then use the water buckets on the soft clay.

Mistake 4: Underestimating the Demon Summoner

The Mistake: Going into the fight with low HP / bad gear and dying.
The Fix: The Demon Summoner is level 75 and hits up to 12 damage. Bring 10+ food and pray Protect from Magic.

Mistake 5: Not Having 33 Magic for the Spell Scroll

The Mistake: Getting to the spell scroll step and realising you cannot read it.
The Fix: Train Magic to 33 before starting the quest. Use Wind Strike / Wind Bolt on cows or Al-Kharid warriors to get 33 Magic quickly.

⚠️ Final Warning: The Golem quest is not especially hard, but the desert environment can be deadly if you are unprepared. Always bring waterskins, food, and an emergency teleport.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I do The Golem quest on my F2P account?

A: No, The Golem is a members-only quest. You need a membership (or a bond) to start and complete it. See our F2P to P2P guide if you need help transitioning.

Q: What is the fastest way to get to Uzer?

A: The fastest way is to use a Desert Amulet 1 (teleport to Nardah) and run south-east. Alternatively, use an Amulet of Glory (teleport to Al Kharid) and run south through the Shantay Pass.

Q: Do I need to complete other desert quests before The Golem?

A: No, The Golem has no hard prerequisites. However, completing Icthlarin's Little Helper first makes desert navigation much easier and provides useful lore context.

Q: Is the Demon-Bane Amulet worth keeping?

A: Yes! The amulet gives +10% damage vs. demons, which is very useful for early Slayer tasks (Lesser Demons, Greater Demons, Black Demons). It also has a +3 Prayer bonus, making it a decent low-level Prayer amulet.

Q: How long does The Golem quest take?

A: Approximately 45-75 minutes for most players. If you are following this guide closely and have the required items prepared, you can finish in under an hour.

Q: Can I boost my Magic level to read the spell scroll?

A: Yes, you can use a Magic potion or Wizard's mind bomb to temporarily boost your Magic level to 33. However, it is easier to just train Magic to 33 first.

Q: What should I do after finishing The Golem?

A: A natural next step is to continue the desert quest series. We recommend Icthlarin's Little Helper (if not yet done) followed by Contact! and eventually Desert Treasure. Check our Quest Cape Roadmap 2026 for a full quest order.

Q: Are there any missable rewards in The Golem?

A: No, all rewards are permanent. However, if you do not pick up the Demon-Bane Amulet after the golem gives it to you, you can get another one by talking to the golem again in Uzer.

Final Tips for Goraik Quest Success

  • Prepare all items before leaving for the desert — nothing is worse than running out of waterskins halfway through!
  • Read both journal volumes carefully; the clues are all there.
  • Use the demon-bane amulet in the final fight — it makes a noticeable difference.
  • Consider completing Icthlarin's Little Helper first for desert teleport access.
  • If you are a low-level Ironman, farm your own food and supplies before starting; do not rely on the GE.
  • After the quest, do the Easy Desert Diary to get the Desert Amulet 1 — it is one of the most useful early-game items.
