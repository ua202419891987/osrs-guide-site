🥇 #1 — Doom of Mokhaiotl (Delve 1–16): 15M–23M GP/hr

Req. Sailing Req. Combat GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
90+ 95+ Ranged, 95+ Magic, 90+ Defence 15M–23M 🔴 Extreme Focus ✅ Yes

Doom of Mokhaiotl is the highest GP/hr activity in all of OSRS right now. It's a deep-delve Sailing boss encounter where you descend through 16 increasingly difficult waves, each with unique mechanics and escalating rewards.

How It Works

You sail to the Mokhaiotl Depths (unlocked via a Sailing-only sea route south of Fossil Island) and descend through Delve levels 1–16. Each wave throws a new combination of enemies, environmental hazards, and boss mechanics at you. Loot accumulates per wave, and the deeper you go, the more valuable the drops become.

Key Requirements

  • 90+ Sailing — required to navigate the Mokhaiotl Depths currents
  • 95+ Ranged — primary DPS style;Bow of Faerdhinen + Crystal Armor or better
  • 95+ Magic — needed for specific wave mechanics and freezing enemies
  • 90+ Defence — you will take damage even with perfect prayer
  • Rigour + Augury — non-negotiable for DPS checks
  • Sloop with Cannons, Wind Catcher, and Crystal Extractor facilities

Profit Breakdown

Delve Depth Avg. GP/hr Notable Drops
Delve 1–5 (Entry)3M–5MBasic resources, alchables
Delve 6–10 (Mid)7M–10MOnyx bolts, dragon items, noted supplies
Delve 11–14 (Deep)12M–16MRare uniques, Sailing-specific drops
Delve 15–16 (Max)18M–23MMega-rares, pet chance, unique Sailing rewards
⚠️ Reality Check: Doom of Mokhaiotl Delve 16 is harder than 500+ ToA Expert mode. Most players farming this profitably are near-maxed with hundreds of hours of practice. If you can consistently clear Delve 10, you're already in the top 5% of OSRS PvMers. Don't be discouraged — start at Delve 1 and work your way up.

🥈 #2 — Yama (Duo Contracts): 14M–19M GP/hr

Req. Sailing Req. Combat GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
85+ 99 Magic, 90+ Defence, 77 Prayer 14M–19M 🔴 High Focus ⚠️ Need duo

Yama is a Sailing-exclusive sea boss accessed via contract missions from the Port Sarim Sailor's Guild. You and a partner take a contract, sail to Yama's lair, and fight a multi-phase boss with mechanics that require coordination between both players.

Why Duo?

Yama's mechanics are explicitly designed for two players. One player handles crowd control and adds (typically the Magic user), while the other focuses on single-target DPS (Ranged or Melee). Soloing is technically possible but drops GP/hr to ~6M–8M — not worth it.

Key Requirements

  • 85+ Sailing — required to reach Yama's deep-water lair
  • 99 Magic — primary style;Sanguinesti Staff or Shadow required
  • Access to a reliable duo partner — this is the real bottleneck
  • Sloop with Cannons facility

Contract System

Contracts are obtained from the Sailor's Guild. Each contract guarantees 1–3 kills before needing to return. Higher-tier contracts (unlocked at 90+ Sailing) offer better loot multipliers. Stack contracts whenever possible to minimize travel time.

🤝 Finding a Partner: Join the "OSRS Sailing" Discord or the We Do Raids server. The Yama LFG channel is active 24/7. Look for partners with similar gear — mismatched gear levels mean one person carries, which kills GP/hr for both.

🥉 #3 — Fortis Colosseum (Wave 12): 11M–16M GP/hr

While not strictly a Sailing-only activity, the Fortis Colosseum is accessed via a Sailing route through Varlamore waters, and 80+ Sailing significantly reduces travel time between runs. The Colosseum's Wave 12 completion is one of the most consistent high-end money makers in the game.

Req. Sailing Req. Combat GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
80+ (travel shortcut) Near-maxed combat, all 3 styles 11M–16M 🔴 Extreme ✅ Yes

Key benefit: no rare-drop dependency. Colosseum profit comes from consistent wave rewards and the guaranteed chest at Wave 12. Unlike raids where you can go dry for 100+ hours, Colosseum pays out reliably every completion.

#4 — Theatre of Blood (Hard Mode): 9M–14M GP/hr

ToB Hard Mode is a land-based raid, but Sailing speed dramatically improves your reset efficiency. Players with 85+ Sailing can use the Sloop teleport network to reach ToB (via the Meiyerditch sea route) in under 30 seconds, compared to the 2+ minutes using conventional teleports. Over hundreds of raids, this adds up to millions of extra GP.

For a full ToB strategy breakdown, see our Boss Guides section.

#5 — Cutting Raw Marlin: 3M–10.5M GP/hr

Req. Sailing Other Skills GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
82 Chum Spreader (boat upgrade) 3M–10.5M 🟡 Semi-AFK ✅ Excellent

This is the best mid-game Sailing money maker and arguably the best skilling profit method in OSRS right now. You catch Raw Marlin via Deep Sea Trawling (or buy them on the GE if you're a main), then use the Chum Spreader boat facility to cut them into Marlin Fillets, which sell for a massive premium.

How the Profit Scales

Sailing Level GP/hr Efficiency
82–85~3MLearning the rhythm, occasional mistakes
85–90~5M–7MConsistent cuts, good Chum Spreader uptime
90–95~8M–10MNear-perfect technique
95–99~10.5MMax efficiency, zero wasted ticks
🎯 Why This Is So Good: At 82 Sailing (which you can reach in ~25–30 hours of semi-AFK Salvaging), you unlock a money maker that out-earns Vorkath and Zulrah without any combat gear, supplies, or death risk. For Ironmen, this is your ticket to funding Construction, Fletching, and other buyable skills.

Setup & Inventory

  • Sloop with Chum Spreader facility installed (~150K GP)
  • 2 Angler crewmates + 1 Navigator (increases catch speed)
  • Raw Marlin — either caught yourself via Deep Sea Trawling or bought from GE
  • Profit per Marlin: Buy raw for ~1.2K → cut into fillets → sell for ~3.5K each → ~2.3K profit per fish

#6 — Chambers of Xeric: 7M–9M GP/hr

Like ToB, CoX benefits from Sailing's fast travel network. The Mount Quidamortem sea route (75+ Sailing) cuts travel time by 60% compared to the Xeric's Talisman teleport. Over 1,000+ raids, that time savings translates directly into extra GP. For a detailed CoX strategy, see our Chambers of Xeric Loot Guide.

#7 — Frost Dragons (On-Task): ~4M GP/hr

Req. Sailing Req. Combat GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
87 90+ Magic, 80+ Prayer, Slayer task ~4M 🟡 Medium ✅ Yes

Frost Dragons are Sailing-exclusive Slayer creatures found on icebergs in the far northern sea. They're unlocked at 87 Sailing and assigned by Duradel, Nieve, and Konar. They drop Frost Dragon Bones (noted, ~25K each) with a 100% drop rate, making them one of the most consistent profit-per-kill monsters in OSRS.

Why They're So Consistent

  • 100% bone drop rate — no RNG dependency
  • ~160 kills per hour on-task with max Mage → ~4M GP/hr guaranteed
  • Also drops Frost Dragonhide (~8K each), noted Magic logs, and Rune items
  • Off-task kills drop GP/hr to ~2.5M — still decent but noticeably worse

#8 — Deep Sea Trawling: 2.3M–3.3M GP/hr

Req. Sailing Other Skills GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
50 69–91 Fishing 2.3M–3.3M 🟢 Semi-AFK ✅ Excellent

Deep Sea Trawling is the hybrid training + profit method. You deploy your Trawling Net from your Sloop, sail through rich fishing waters, and haul in fish while gaining Sailing XP. The star of the show is Raw Marlin, which sells for 1.2K each and can be further processed for even more profit (see #5 Cutting Raw Marlin).

Profit by Fishing Level

76–82~1.8M~30KMore Marlins, Swordfish
82–91~2.5M~35KConsistent Marlin, Shark

With 2 Angler crewmates + a Navigator, your catch rate increases by ~15%. Add the Trawling Net facility (50K GP, level 50) to your Sloop for maximum efficiency.

#9 — Shipwreck Salvaging: 200K–400K GP/hr (AFK)

Req. Sailing Other Skills GP/hr Intensity Ironman?
15+ None 200K–400K 🟢 Full AFK (3–4 min) ✅ Best method

Shipwreck Salvaging is not the highest GP/hr, but it's the most accessible and sustainable Sailing money maker. The 3–4 minute AFK window means you can do this while working, studying, or watching shows — and you'll earn ~20M+ GP on the way to 99 without even paying attention.

Use Salvager crewmates to boost loot by 5–15% per crew member. A full Sloop (level 50+) with 3 Salvagers + 2 Lookouts maximizes both XP and profit.

#10 — Coral Farming: 400K per Run (Passive Income)

Req. Sailing Other Skills GP/run Time per Run Ironman?
15 (access) 28–77 Farming ~400K 5–7 minutes ✅ Yes

Coral Farming is the Sailing equivalent of Herb Runs. You plant coral spores at underwater farming patches (accessible only by boat), wait for them to grow (similar to farming timers), then harvest and sell the coral. Different coral types unlock at different Farming levels:

Coral Type Farming Level Grow Time GP per Patch
Brain Coral2840 min~40K
Fire Coral4560 min~65K
Staghorn Coral6280 min~90K
Pillar Coral7790 min~140K

There are currently 4 underwater coral patches (Fossil Island, Hosidius Reef, Piscatoris Cove, Varlamore Coast). A full run at 77+ Farming takes 5–7 minutes and nets ~400K GP. Do this between Slayer tasks or bossing for passive income that adds up fast. At 3 runs per day, that's ~1.2M GP/day for 15 minutes of work — or ~36M/month in true passive income.

🌿 Combine with Herb Runs: Since coral patches are on the same timer mechanic as herb patches, do them together. Herb run → Coral run → back to whatever you were doing. At high Farming, a combined herb + coral run can net 600K–800K per run. For more farming profit methods, see our Money Making Guides.

💰 The Bottom Line

  1. Sailing is now the best money-making skill in OSRS. Doom of Mokhaiotl (23M/hr) out-earns everything else in the game.
  2. You don't need max stats to profit. Raw Marlin cutting at 82 Sailing earns 3M–10.5M/hr with zero combat requirements.
  3. AFK money exists. Dragon Hook Salvaging at 87+ Sailing nets ~400K/hr with 3–4 minute AFK windows.
  4. Passive income stacks. Coral Farming (400K/run) + Herb Runs = 600K–800K per combined run, multiple times per day.
  5. Training to 99 pays for itself. You'll earn 20M+ GP just from Salvaging on the road to 99 — Sailing costs practically nothing to max.
