🎟️ Can You Play OSRS Members for Free? Bond Farming Guide 2026

Yes — you can keep OSRS membership active forever without spending real money. This guide breaks down exactly how bonds work, the fastest F2P money-makers to afford your first bond, early-member methods that pay back in days, and the long-term routines that keep you bonded indefinitely.

The question every free-to-play OSRS player asks eventually: "Do I really have to pay $12 a month for membership?" The answer is no. Jagex built a system — bonds — that lets you buy membership with in-game gold. But farming 5+ million GP every two weeks is not trivial, especially when you're starting from zero. This guide walks you through the entire journey: understanding bond mechanics, grinding your first bond as a F2P player, transitioning to faster member methods, and building a sustainable cycle that keeps you bonded for life. For the broader membership overview, see our complete F2P-to-P2P transition guide.

① 🎟️ What Are Bonds & How Do They Work?

Before you start grinding, you need to understand the instrument you're grinding for. Bonds are a Jagex-sanctioned item that lets players trade membership for in-game gold — effectively letting the market set the price of subscription time.

1.1 📜 Bond Basics — Tradeable vs Untradeable

A bond is an item purchased with real money from Jagex (currently around $8 each). When bought from the official site, it arrives in your bond pouch in a tradeable state. Tradeable bonds can be sold on the Grand Exchange, gifted to other players, or traded in person. Once a bond becomes untradeable — which happens the moment it's removed from the pouch or after being traded once — it can only be redeemed by the holder.

For bond farmers, what matters is the Grand Exchange price. As of June 2026, a bond trades for roughly 5.2 million GP. That price fluctuates based on supply and demand, but it has stayed in the 4.8M–5.6M range for most of the past year. You buy a tradeable bond off the GE, click it, and redeem it for 14 days of membership.

  • Bond (tradeable): ~5.2M GP on the GE — can be sold or gifted
  • Bond (untradeable): The version you hold after purchase — redeem for 14 days membership
  • Bond pouch: Holds bonds safely; bonds in the pouch are not lost on death

1.2 💰 What a Bond Actually Gives You

Redeeming one bond grants 14 days of members access on a normal account. That means you need roughly 2 bonds per month — about 10.4M GP — to stay permanently bonded. Here's what that membership unlocks for your GP farming:

🎯 The Bond Math — What You Need to Sustain

To stay bonded permanently, you need to farm ~5.2M GP every 14 days. That's roughly 370K GP per day. If you play 2 hours a day, you need a method that averages 185K GP/hour. That is very achievable — but only after you're already a member. The hard part is the first bond, which you must farm in F2P.

1.3 🔄 The Bond Lifecycle for a Farmer

Your journey has three phases. Phase 1: grind your first bond entirely in F2P (slow, 1–3 weeks). Phase 2: redeem that bond and immediately switch to fast early-member money-makers to farm the next bond before the 14 days expire. Phase 3: once you have mid-level stats (70+ combat, 50+ Slayer), you enter the sustained phase where one bond takes a few days, not weeks. This guide covers all three phases in detail.

② 💰 F2P Money Making for Your First Bond

This is the hardest phase. F2P money-makers cap out around 100K–250K GP/hour, meaning your first 5.2M GP bond takes 20–50 hours of focused grinding. The key is picking methods that scale with your stats and don't require capital to start.

If you're brand new with level 1 stats, start here. These methods need no quests, no levels, and no gear — just time.

💡 Rotate Methods to Avoid Burnout

F2P grinding is tedious. Don't do one method for 8 hours straight — rotate between 2–3 methods across a session. Do cowhide tanning while watching a video, then switch to clay mining when you want to be active. Variety keeps you sane through the 20+ hour grind.

2.2 🏹 Combat-Based F2P Money Makers

Once you have 40+ combat stats, F2P combat methods open up. These are more engaging than shop-scraping and give combat XP alongside GP.

  • Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon): 100K–200K GP/hour. Drop big bones (~350 GP each) and limpwurt roots. Requires brass key to enter. Best F2P combat GP for low-level players.
  • Flesh Crawlers (Stronghold of Security): 50K–100K GP/hour plus excellent combat XP. Drop herbs, runes, and iron ore. Great for training strength while profiting.
  • Ogress Warriors (Corsair Cove): 150K–250K GP/hour. Drop rune med helms, nature runes, and gems. Requires completion of the Dragon Slayer I quest access to Corsair Cove. Best mid-level F2P money-maker.
  • Greater Demons (Wilderness Volcano): 200K–300K GP/hour but high risk — you're in the Wilderness. Only attempt with minimal gear you can afford to lose.

2.3 🎯 High-Level F2P: Your Pre-Bond Power Grind

At 70+ combat, F2P money-making becomes genuinely decent. Two methods dominate:

📊 Top 2 High-Level F2P Methods

1. Ogress Warriors/Shamans — 200K–280K GP/hour. Consistent drops, safe location (Corsair Cove has a bank nearby). This is the single best F2P bond-farming method for most players.

2. F2P Boss — Obor (Hill Giant Boss) — 250K–400K GP/hour with good RNG. Requires a giant key (1/128 drop from hill giants). Obor drops Bryophyta's essence and rune items. You need 60+ combat and decent food (lobsters or better).

⚠️ Don't Buy Gold from Third Parties

Farming your first bond is slow. You will be tempted to buy GP from gold-selling sites. Don't. Jagex tracks RWT (real-world trading) and bans accounts caught buying or selling gold. A banned account loses everything — all your stats, your bond money, your progress. Farm it legitimately. It's slower, but it's safe.

③ ⚡ Early Member Methods — Quick Payback

You've redeemed your first bond. The clock is ticking — you have 14 days to farm the next 5.2M GP before membership expires. The good news: member methods are 5–10× faster than F2P. The bad news: you start with low member stats, so you must pick methods with low requirements.

3.1 🌿 Wintertodt — The Beginner Goldmine

Wintertodt is the single best early-member money-maker. It requires only 50 Firemaking (achievable in a few hours) and zero combat stats. It's a skilling boss where you chop bruma roots and feed a brazier. The supply crates you earn contain herbs, seeds, logs, gems, and ore — all sellable on the GE.

  • GP/hour: 200K–400K (highly RNG-dependent; some crates are worth 50K, others 500K)
  • Requirements: 50 Firemaking, warm clothing (clue boots, gloves of silence, etc.)
  • XP/hour: 40K–80K Firemaking XP — you're training a skill while making GP
  • Bonus: Also gives Woodcutting, Construction, and Fletching XP passively

🔥 Wintertodt Strategy — Maximize Crate Value

The supply crates scale with your Firemaking level. At 99 Firemaking, a full Wintertodt grind can yield 5M+ GP in crates alone. Even at level 50–70, expect 250K–350K GP/hour. Tips: bring a rune axe (or better), wear warm clothing with the lowest weight possible, and always cut bruma roots — don't just stand at the brazier. Open crates at the Grand Exchange so you can immediately sell the contents.

3.2 🦀 Sand Crabs + Herb Drops (Combat + GP)

If you'd rather train combat than skill, Sand Crabs on Zeah's southern coast give consistent combat XP and small but steady GP from herb and seed drops. It's not a primary money-maker (50K–80K GP/hour), but you're training the stats you need for better methods while earning something. Pair this with Wintertodt for a balanced early-member routine.

3.3 🐉 Green Dragons — The Classic Bond Farmer

Once your combat stats hit 50+ (Attack, Strength, Defence all 50+), Green Dragons in the Wilderness become available. Each kill drops dragon bones (~1,600 GP) and green dragonhide (~1,400 GP) — roughly 3,000 GP per kill, and you can kill 150–200 per hour.

  • GP/hour: 400K–600K (higher with a cannon, but cannonballs cost GP)
  • Requirements: 50+ combat stats, anti-dragon shield (free from Duke Horacio after Dragon Slayer I)
  • Location: Wilderness — north of Edgeville or the Wilderness Slayer Cave
  • Risk: PKers patrol this area. Wear cheap gear (rune chainbody, not platebody — less lost on death), bring a one-click teleport (Amulet of glory or Varrock teleport), and never bring more than you can afford to lose.
💡 Green Dragon Survival Tip

Use the Wilderness Slayer Cave entrance (requires Wilderness Slayer tasks) — it's a single-combat area where PKers can't attack you while you're fighting a dragon. Alternatively, use the Chaos Temple altar nearby to bank bones quickly. Always keep "Protect Item" prayer on if you're risking anything valuable.

3.4 📜 Chaos Altar Prayer Training (Save GP)

While not a direct money-maker, the Chaos Altar in the Wilderness (level 38) gives 3.5× prayer XP per bone — the same as a Gilded Altar but with a 50% chance to not consume the bone. This halves your prayer training costs. If you're farming bonds, every GP saved is GP earned. Train Prayer here instead of buying a Gilded Altar host.

④ 🔄 Sustained Bond Income — Long-Term Methods

Once you're past the early-member phase — 70+ combat, 50+ Slayer, a few quest unlocks — bond farming shifts from a scramble to a routine. These methods reliably produce 500K–2M GP/hour, meaning a full bond takes 3–8 hours of focused grinding instead of 20+.

4.1 🐍 Zulrah — The Bond Farmer's Best Friend

Zulrah is a solo boss that requires 100+ combat and completion of the Regicide quest. It's the gold standard for sustainable bond income because it has consistent drops, no entry cost (no supplies consumed beyond food/potions), and a learning curve that rewards practice.

  • GP/hour (learning): 500K–800K (2-3 kills per trip)
  • GP/hour (experienced): 1.5M–2.5M (10+ kills per trip)
  • Requirements: 100+ combat, 80+ Ranged/Magic recommended, Regicide quest, anti-venom
  • Unique drops: Tanzanite fang (~3M), Magic fang (~1.5M), Zulrah's scales (consistent 50K+ per kill)

🐍 Zulrah Learning Curve

Zulrah has 4 rotation patterns. Don't try to memorize them all at once — learn one rotation, get comfortable, then add the next. Use the Zulrah Helper plugin in RuneLite (it overlays the current rotation on your screen). Most players reach 1M+ GP/hour within 50 kills. The first 20 kills will be slow and you'll die — that's normal. Persevere; this is the most important boss a bond farmer can learn.

4.2 🐉 Vorkath — High-Value Solo Boss

Vorkath (post-Dragon Slayer II) is another solo boss optimized for bond farming. It has a slower kill rate than Zulrah but higher per-kill value due to consistent dragon bone and hide drops plus the chance at the Dragonbone necklace (~2M) and Vorkath's head (for assembler).

  • GP/hour (with Dragonhunter Lance): 1.5M–2.5M
  • GP/hour (with Whip/standard gear): 800K–1.2M
  • Requirements: Dragon Slayer II quest, 80+ melee stats recommended
  • Per kill: ~40K GP in consistent drops (bones + hide) plus rare uniques

4.3 🏰 Barrows — Low-Requirement Consistent GP

If you don't have the stats for Zulrah or Vorkath yet, Barrows is the bridge. It requires 70+ combat stats and completion of Priest in Peril + Nature Spirit. Each run takes 5–8 minutes and yields 100K–300K GP depending on RNG.

  • GP/hour: 500K–1M (average ~700K)
  • Requirements: 70+ combat, 43 Prayer (Protect from Melee), spade, any food
  • Key drops: Barrows armour pieces (150K–4M each), Bolt racks, blood runes
  • Why it works for bonds: Low requirements, no consumable cost beyond food, and you can do it with a magic shortbow and rune armour.

4.4 🧹 Slayer — The Passive Income Engine

Slayer isn't the fastest GP/hour, but it's the most sustainable because you're training a skill while earning. High-level Slayer tasks unlock money-makers that fund bonds indefinitely:

Once you hit 75+ Slayer, a single task of Gargoyles or Brutal Black Dragons can pay for half a bond. Two tasks a day (4–5 hours of play) keeps you bonded with GP to spare. This is the phase where bond farming stops feeling like a grind and starts feeling like just... playing the game.

4.5 🌾 Farming Runs — Passive GP Between Sessions

Farming is the secret weapon of bond farmers. Herb runs take 5 minutes and can be done every 80 minutes. With 70+ Farming and magic secateurs, a single herb run of 8 patches yields 200K–400K GP in ranarr/snapdragon herbs. Do 4 runs a day (20 minutes total) and that's 800K–1.6M GP of nearly passive income. Pair farming runs with birdhouse runs (Hunter) for even more passive GP.

⑤ ⏳ Time vs Money Analysis — Is Grinding Worth It?

Let's be honest: bond farming is a time tax. Before you commit to the cycle, you should understand the economics — both in game-time and real-world equivalent value.

5.1 📊 The Real Cost of Bond Farming

A bond costs ~5.2M GP and gives 14 days of membership. Jagex sells bonds for ~$8 each (real money). If you farm 5.2M GP in 10 hours, your "hourly wage" is $0.80/hour. If you farm it in 5 hours (mid-level bossing), you're earning $1.60/hour. Compare this to minimum wage in most countries ($7–$15/hour) and the math is brutal.

5.2 🧠 When Bond Farming Makes Sense

Despite the low "wage," bond farming is the right choice for many players. Here's when it's worth it:

  • You're a student or in a low-income situation where $12/month genuinely matters. Farming 10 hours to save $12 is rational when you have more time than money.
  • You enjoy the grind. If you find Wintertodt relaxing or Zulrah engaging, the GP is a byproduct of fun — not "work."
  • You're building an account anyway. Early-game players need to grind stats regardless. Bond farming methods (Wintertodt, Slayer, Barrows) double as training. The GP isn't "wasted time" — it's the natural output of playing the game.
  • You want the satisfaction of a self-sustaining account. Many players find pride in never having spent real money on membership. That's a valid motivation.

5.3 💡 When Paying Real Money Makes More Sense

Conversely, paying $11/month for Premier Club (the cheapest annual rate) is smarter if:

  • You have a job and your free time is limited. If you only play 5 hours a week, spending 3 of those hours farming bonds means you're "playing" but not progressing.
  • You're at endgame where your time is better spent on raids, achievements, or maxing. At high levels, the opportunity cost of bond farming is huge — you could be doing CoX learning raids instead.
  • You want to support Jagex. Subscription revenue funds OSRS development. If you love the game and can afford it, paying directly keeps the game alive.

⚖️ The Hybrid Approach — Best of Both Worlds

Many players use a hybrid model: pay for membership during their progression grind (when stats are low and bond farming is inefficient), then switch to bonds once they reach mid-level (70+ combat, 50+ Slayer) where farming is fast. This gives you the best of both — you don't waste 30 hours F2P grinding, but you also don't pay forever once your account can sustain itself.

⑥ 🛡️ Risk Management — Market Fluctuations

Bond prices are not fixed. They rise and fall with the in-game economy, and if you're not prepared, a price spike can leave you unable to rebond in time. Here's how to manage the risks.

6.1 📈 Why Bond Prices Fluctuate

Bond prices are driven by supply and demand. Supply comes from players buying bonds with real money and selling them for GP. Demand comes from players like you — who want membership without paying cash. Prices spike when:

  • New content drops — Players return to try updates, increasing demand for bonds and GP simultaneously.
  • Double XP-style events — OSRS doesn't have DXP, but leagues and temporary events drive players to buy bonds for GP to buy supplies.
  • Real-world economic factors — When the British pound weakens (bonds are priced in GBP on the Jagex store), fewer bonds are bought, reducing supply.
  • Account bans waves — When Jagex bans gold farmers, the supply of GP drops, and bond prices can temporarily rise as GP becomes scarcer.

6.2 🛡️ How to Protect Yourself from Price Spikes

💡 The Buffer Rule

Always keep a 1–2M GP buffer beyond your bond cost. If the bond price rises from 5.2M to 5.8M during your farming period, the buffer covers the difference. Never farm exactly 5.2M and rebond with zero GP left — you're one price spike away from losing membership.

  • Buy bonds early — As soon as you hit 5.2M GP, buy the bond. Don't wait. Prices only go up over the long term.
  • Track the price — Check the GE bond price daily. If it's trending up, prioritize farming over training.
  • Keep 2 bonds in your pouch — Once you're stable, always have 2 bonds banked. This gives you a 28-day buffer where you don't need to farm at all — useful for exams, vacations, or burnout.
  • Diversify income — Don't rely on one method. If Zulrah gets nerfed or Barrows drops crash, you need backup methods.

6.3 ⚠️ Account Security — Protect Your Bond Investment

If your account gets hacked, you lose everything — your bonds, your GP, your stats. Bond farming is worthless if you don't secure your account. This is non-negotiable.

⚠️ Mandatory Security Checklist

1. Enable Authenticator — Jagex's 2FA. Use the Jagex Authenticator app, not SMS. SMS can be SIM-swapped.
2. Use a unique password — Not reused from any other site. Use a password manager.
3. Set a Bank PIN — A 4-digit PIN that delays access to your bank by 3 days if changed. This is your last line of defense.
4. Keep bonds in the pouch — Bonds in your pouch are safe on death. Never carry bonds in your inventory in the Wilderness.
5. Only use RuneLite from runelite.net — Fake clients steal credentials.

⑦ ❓ FAQs — Bond Farming & Free Membership

Q: How much GP do I need for a bond in 2026?

A: As of June 2026, an OSRS bond costs approximately 5.2 million GP on the Grand Exchange. This fluctuates between 4.8M and 5.6M depending on market conditions. Always check the current GE price before starting a farming session — the price can shift 100K–300K in a week.

Q: How long does it take to farm a bond as a F2P player?

A: For a fresh F2P account with low stats, expect 20–40 hours of grinding to farm 5.2M GP. With 70+ combat using Ogress Warriors and Obor, it drops to 16–20 hours. This is the hardest phase — once you're a member, farming speeds up dramatically.

Q: Can I farm a bond in 14 days as a new member?

A: Yes, if you play 3+ hours a day. Wintertodt (200K–400K GP/hour) plus Green Dragons (400K–600K GP/hour) can net you 5.2M GP in 8–12 days of focused play. The key is to start farming immediately upon redeeming your first bond — don't spend the first week exploring. For the roadmap, see our first 10 things to do as a new member.

Q: Is it better to buy membership with real money or farm bonds?

A: It depends on your situation. If you have a job and limited playtime, paying $11/month (Premier Club annual rate) is more efficient. If you're a student with more time than money, or if you enjoy the grind, bonds are viable. The hybrid approach — pay during low-level progression, switch to bonds at mid-level — is often the best balance.

Q: What happens if my membership expires while I have member items?

A: Your member items stay in your bank — they're not deleted. But you can't use them until you're a member again. You also can't enter member areas or use member skills. Your F2P skills and items remain fully accessible. Nothing is lost; you're just locked out of member content until you rebond.

Q: Can Ironman accounts use bonds for membership?

A: Yes. Ironmen can redeem bonds for membership just like normal accounts. However, Ironmen cannot buy bonds from the Grand Exchange (they can't use the GE for trading). They must either buy bonds with real money from Jagex, or farm the GP value through in-game methods and have a normal-account friend gift them a bond. For details, see our Ironman membership guide.

Q: Do bonds go on sale or get cheaper?

A: Jagex occasionally runs promotions where bonds give bonus items or extra membership days. The GP price on the Grand Exchange is entirely market-driven and never goes "on sale" directly. Long-term, bond prices tend to rise over years as the game economy inflates — so buying early is always better.

🎯 Final Tips for Bond Farmers

Bond farming is a marathon, not a sprint. The players who succeed are the ones who treat it as a system: farm efficiently, maintain a GP buffer, secure their account, and don't burn out. Start in F2P with Ogress Warriors or Wintertodt prep, transition to member methods immediately upon redeeming your first bond, and aim for the Zulrah/Slayer phase where one bond takes an afternoon instead of a week.

Remember that the goal isn't just "free membership" — it's building an account that sustains itself. Every hour you spend grinding is also an hour of XP, quest progress, and skill training. By the time you're farming bonds with Zulrah at 1.5M GP/hour, you've also got a 100+ combat account with 50+ Slayer. The bond is the byproduct; the account is the real reward. Keep your bonds in your pouch, your authenticator on, and your buffer stocked — and you'll never pay real money for OSRS again.
