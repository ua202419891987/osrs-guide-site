1. What Is Slayer & Why Start Early（什么是Slayer及为何早开始）

Slayer is one of the most unique skills in OSRS. Unlike other skills where you train by repeating the same action (fishing shrimp, burning logs), Slayer sends you on varied combat assignments to kill specific monsters that you would never encounter during normal gameplay. Each task is different, each monster has unique mechanics, and the variety keeps the grind fresh even after hundreds of hours.

🎯 Why Slayer Is the #1 Skill for Long-Term Accounts（为何Slayer是长期账号第一技能）

Most skills in OSRS give you one type of reward. Mining gives Mining XP. Cooking gives Cooking XP. Slayer is different — it gives you combat XP, GP, rare drops, and Slayer XP all at once. Here's why starting Slayer early changes your entire OSRS experience:

🛡️ Slayer Unlocks the Entire Game（Slayer解锁整个游戏）

  • Access to boss content: Every major boss in OSRS requires a Slayer level or being on a Slayer task to fight. From KBD (level 1) to Vorkath (level 80+) to Grotesque Guardians (level 75), Slayer is your gatekeeper to the best PvM content.
  • Unique drops: Slayer monsters have exclusive drop tables with items you cannot obtain anywhere else. Whips from Abyssal Demons, Dragon boots from Dagannoths, Granite Mauls from Gargoyles — these are often the best GP sources for mid-level players.
  • Combat training with purpose: Instead of mindlessly clicking on cows for Attack XP, you're killing Banshees for Slayer XP while also gaining Melee/Ranged/Magic XP. The dual-purpose nature makes every hour of gameplay feel productive.
  • Slayer points economy: Completing tasks earns Slayer points that you can spend on unlocking new monsters, buying Broad bolts/arrows, or skipping unwanted tasks. The point system creates a progression loop that rewards consistency.

② ⏰ When Should You Start Slayer?（何时应该开始Slayer？）

The optimal time to start Slayer depends on your account type and goals, but here are the general recommendations:

Your Situation（你的情况）Recommended Start（推荐开始）Why（原因）
Complete Beginner (F2P)（纯新手·F2P）Level 15–20 CombatTurael is available immediately; tasks are very easy and teach combat basics
New Member Account（新会员账号）Level 30–40 CombatVannaka becomes available; tasks start dropping useful loot worth keeping
Returning Player (Pre-EOC)（回归玩家）Level 50+ CombatYou likely have the stats already; jump straight to Chaeldar or Duradel
Ironman Account（铁人账号）Level 20 Combat (Turael)Slayer is the most efficient way to obtain rare drops for untradeable upgrades

The short answer: you can start Slayer at literally any Combat level. Turael assigns tasks to players with Combat level 1. The only question is whether you're ready for the combat difficulty of the tasks you'll receive. If you're struggling to kill Hill Giants at Combat 30, you might want to do some general combat training first before taking on Slayer tasks.

💡 The Slayer Progression Mindset（Slayer进阶心态）

Don't treat Slayer as a "skill you train to 99." Treat it as your primary way of playing OSRS. Every time you log in, pick up a Slayer task. Over time, your Combat levels, Slayer level, bank wealth, and game knowledge all grow together. This is the most efficient and enjoyable way to play OSRS long-term.

2. Choosing Your First Slayer Master（选择你的第一位Slayer大师）

Slayer Masters are NPCs who assign you tasks. Each master has different requirements, assigns different monsters, and offers different rewards. Choosing the right master for your current Combat level is critical — too low and tasks are boring/low XP; too high and you'll die repeatedly.

④ 🗺️ All 7 Slayer Masters — Complete Comparison（全部7位Slayer大师·完整对比）

Here is every Slayer Master in the game, ranked by the recommended Combat level to start using them:

① Turael — The Starter Master（新手大师）

Location: Burthorpe (easily accessible from tutorial area)
Combat Requirement: None (Level 0+)
Quest Requirement: None
Task Length: Very Short (10–20 monsters)
Difficulty: Very Easy
Slayer Points per Task: 0 (none)
Best For: Absolute beginners learning the combat system; players who want very short tasks

Pros: No requirements; tasks are trivially easy; located in Burthorpe right next to a bank and lodge. Cons: Awards zero Slayer points (so you can't buy unlocks); tasks are very short (less engaging); monsters assigned are extremely low-level (low GP and XP).

Verdict: Use Turael for your very first 1–3 tasks to learn how Slayer works. After that, switch to Mazchna or Vannaka.

② Mazchna — The Early Game Master（前期大师）

Location: Canifis (after Ghosts Ah! Woo! quest)
Combat Requirement: 20+
Quest Requirement: Ghosts Ah! Woo! (F2P accessible with membership)
Task Length: Short (20–40 monsters)
Difficulty: Easy
Slayer Points per Task: 1 (2 on task #10, #50, #100, etc.)
Best For: Players with Combat 20–50 who want consistent short tasks

Pros: Low requirement; awards Slayer points (slowly); tasks are manageable for low-level accounts; located near a bank in Canifis. Cons: Tasks are shorter than ideal for efficient training; lower XP rates than Vannaka.

Verdict: Solid choice for Combat 20–40 players who find Vannaka's tasks too difficult. Once you're comfortable at Combat 40+, move to Vannaka.

③ Vannaka — The Best All-Around Master (Recommended)（最佳全能大师·推荐）

Location: Edgeville Dungeon (wilderness entrance side) OR Corner of the Chaos Temple in Level 38 Wilderness (risky!)
Combat Requirement: 40+
Quest Requirement: None (just Combat 40)
Task Length: Medium (40–80 monsters)
Difficulty: Medium
Slayer Points per Task: 2 (4 on task #10, #50, #100, etc.)
Best For: Players with Combat 40–70; the "sweet spot" for most mid-game players

Pros: No quest requirements (just hit Combat 40); good task variety; decent Slayer points; tasks are long enough to be engaging but not overwhelming; located near Edgeville bank for easy looting. Cons: Edgeville Dungeon location means occasionally running past higher-level monsters; some tasks can be dangerous for under-geared players.

Verdict: Vannaka is the single best Slayer Master for players progressing through the mid game. Stay with Vannaka from Combat 40 all the way to Combat 70+ before considering Chaeldar or Duradel.

④ Sumona — The Conditional Choice（条件选择）

Location: Pollnivneach (requires The Feud quest)
Combat Requirement: 35+
Quest Requirement: The Feud (required to access Pollnivneach)
Task Length: Medium (similar to Vannaka)
Difficulty: Medium
Slayer Points per Task: 3 (5 on task #10, #50, #100, etc.)
Best For: Players who have completed The Feud and want slightly better points rate than Vannaka

Pros: Higher Slayer points than Vannaka; assigns some unique tasks. Cons: Requires The Feud quest (not trivial for new players); location is less convenient than Edgeville; not a massive upgrade over Vannaka.

Verdict: Only use Sumona if you've already completed The Feud and prefer the Pollnivneach location. Otherwise, Vannaka is superior for most players.

⑤ Chaeldar — The Mid-Game Transition（中期过渡大师）

Location: Zanaris (Lost City quest required)
Combat Requirement: 70+
Quest Requirement: Lost City
Task Length: Long (80–150 monsters)
Difficulty: Medium-Hard
Slayer Points per Task: 4 (8 on task #10, #50, #100, etc.)
Best For: Players with Combat 70–90 who want longer tasks and better XP

Pros: High Slayer points; longer tasks (better efficiency); assigns higher-level monsters with better drops. Cons: Requires Lost City quest (can be challenging for newer players); Zanaris location requires a Dramen Staff or completion of Fairytale Part II; tasks can be difficult for under-geared Combat 70 accounts.

Verdict: Switch to Chaeldar once you're consistently comfortable with Combat 70+ gear and have completed Lost City. The higher Slayer points rate makes unlocking new content much faster.

⑥ Konvou — The Varlamore Master（Varlamore大师）

Location: Valamore (after Children of the Sun quest)

Pros: Modern content with unique rewards; assigns tasks in the Varlamore region (convenient if you're already there). Cons: Requires Varlamore questline (endgame content); tasks can be very challenging; less proven than the classic masters.

Verdict: Only relevant if you've already progressed through the Varlamore storyline. Otherwise, stick to Chaeldar or Duradel.

⑦ Duradel — The Endgame Master（后期大师）

Location: Shilo Village (Shilo Village quest required)
Combat Requirement: 100+

Verdict: Duradel is the ultimate Slayer Master. Once you have Combat 90+ with good gear, switching to Duradel is a massive upgrade. The extra Slayer points alone justify the switch.

3. Unlocking Each Master (Quest Requirements)（解锁每位大师·任务要求）

Several Slayer Masters require quest completion before you can access them. Here's the exact quest order you should follow to unlock all masters as efficiently as possible.

⑦ 📋 Quests That Unlock Slayer Masters（解锁Slayer大师的任务）

① Ghosts Ah! Woo! — Unlocks Mazchna（解锁Mazchna）

Requirements: None (F2P accessible, but you need membership to use Slayer points)
Length: Short (~10 minutes)
Rewards: Access to Mazchna; 600 GP; 600 Crafting XP
Why do it: Mazchna is only useful for Combat 20–40 players, and by the time you reach Combat 40 you'll want to be at Vannaka anyway. This quest is low priority unless you're doing a F2P Slayer grind.

② The Feud — Unlocks Sumona（解锁Sumona）

Requirements: None (though 30 Thieving helps)
Length: Medium (~30–45 minutes)
Rewards: Access to Pollnivneach; 15,000 Thieving XP; ability to use magic carpets
Why do it: The Feud is actually a very useful quest for multiple reasons (Thieving XP, carpet transport). However, Sumona herself is not a major upgrade over Vannaka. Do this quest early anyway for the transport benefits, but don't feel pressured to switch to Sumona immediately.

③ Lost City — Unlocks Chaeldar（解锁Chaeldar）

Requirements: 31 Crafting, 36 Woodcutting, ability to defeat a level 63 Ent (magic-resistant)
Length: Medium (~45–60 minutes)
Rewards: Access to Zanaris; 3 Quest Points; ability to wield Dragon Longsword
Why do it: Lost City is a milestone quest. It unlocks the Fairy Ring network (massive QoL improvement) and gives access to Chaeldar. Highly recommended once you have Combat 60+ stats. The Ent fight requires Magic attacks (it has high Melee defence), so bring Air Strike or Wind Bolt runes.

④ Shilo Village — Unlocks Duradel（解锁Duradel）

Requirements: 32 Prayer, 20 Crafting, 20 Smithing, ability to defeat threats up to level 100
Length: Long (~60–90 minutes)
Rewards: Access to Shilo Village (bank, mine, fishing spots); 8,000 XP in 3 skills; 2,000 GP; access to Duradel
Why do it: Shilo Village is one of the most important quests for Slayer players. Duradel is the best master in the game, and Shilo Village itself has a convenient bank. Do this quest as soon as your Combat stats allow (around level 60–70 Combat with reasonable gear).

⑤ Children of the Sun — Unlocks Konvou (Varlamore)（解锁Konvou）

Requirements: Completion of Varlamore Part I questline (multiple quests)
Length: Very Long (several hours across multiple quests)
Rewards: Access to Valamore region; Konvou Slayer Master; new content
Why do it: Only relevant for accounts that have progressed into the Varlamore endgame region. If you're a new player in 2026, this is not a priority until you've already completed the major mainland quests.

⑨ ⚔️ Task-by-Task Strategy Guide（逐任务策略指南）

Task 1–3: The Tutorial Phase（教程阶段）

Your first few tasks will be extremely easy (Cows, Chickens, Spiders). Use these tasks to:

  • Learn how to read your Slayer task (check the Quest tab → Scroll down to "Current Task")
  • Practice the combat cycle: Attack → eat food when health is low → loot drops → next monster
  • Start your first Slayer-specific item collection: Earmuffs (from Witch's House quest or buy from Slayer equipment shop)
  • Bank valuable drops (feathers, cowhides, Big Bones) for GP

Task 4–6: The Gear Check（装备检验阶段）

Around task 4–6, you'll start getting assignments that require actual gear. This is where many beginners struggle. Make sure you have:

  • Steel armor set (full helm, platebody, platelegs) or Black armor if you can afford it
  • Steel scimitar (faster attack speed than longsword) or better
  • Shield (any steel/kite shield for extra defence)
  • Food: 10–15 Lobsters or 20+ Tuna
  • Anti-dragon shield (if assigned Dragons — buy from Oziach in Edgeville after Dragon Slayer quest)

Task 7–10: The Progression Phase（进阶阶段）

Tasks 7–10 will start assigning mid-level monsters (Moss Giants, Demons, etc.). These tasks can take 30–60 minutes each. Key tips:

  • Use Protect from Melee prayer if you have level 43 Prayer — it makes these tasks trivial
  • Bring a teleport (Tablet or runes) in case you get low on food and can't finish the task in one trip
  • Loot everything — even "junk" drops like Iron arrows and Steel plateskirts add up over time
  • Note valuable drops with Magic notepaper (if you have it) or bank frequently

⑩ 🔄 Should You Skip or Stay on a Task?（跳过还是继续任务？）

As a beginner, you have very limited Slayer points, so skipping tasks costs you dearly. Here's the decision framework:

💡 Turael Skip Trick (Advanced)（Turael跳过技巧·高级）

Once you have 15+ Slayer points, you can use Turael to "skip" tasks for free. Get a task from your main master (e.g., Vannaka), then talk to Turael and ask him to assign you an "easier task." He will overwrite your current task with a Turael-level task. This effectively gives you a free skip. Use this strategically when you get a truly awful task (e.g., Suicide Squad in the Wilderness).

6. Block & Skip List for Beginners（初学者屏蔽与跳过列表）

One of the most important mechanics in Slayer is the ability to block certain tasks (permanently remove them from your task pool) and skip tasks (temporarily reject a task, costing Slayer points). Managing your block and skip list effectively is the difference between enjoyable Slayer and a miserable grind.

🚫 Tasks You Should BLOCK Immediately（应立即屏蔽的任务）

Blocking a task costs 100 Slayer points and removes that monster from your task pool permanently (until you unblock it). You can have up to 5 blocked tasks at once (more if you spend points to increase the limit). Here are the first blocks every player should get:

① TzHaar (Jad) — Block at Slayer Level 1（立即屏蔽TzHaar·Jad）

Why block: TzHaar tasks require fighting in the Fight Caves (wave-based minigame). This is an endgame boss fight, not a Slayer task for beginners. You will almost certainly die, waste time, and get zero loot. Block this immediately.
Exception: Only unblock TzHaar when you're specifically going for the Fire Cape and want to use Slayer points to get Fight Caves attempts.

② Bronze/Spiritual Creatures (Low Priority)（Spiritual Creatures·低优先级）

Why block: Spiritual Creatures (assigned by some masters) are located in the God Wars Dungeon, which requires 60 Agility to access and has extremely high-level monsters. As a beginner, you cannot efficiently do this task. Block it until you're at endgame.

③ Suqahs — Block After First Couple Encounters（Suqah·前几次遭遇后屏蔽）

Why block: Suqahs are located on Lunar Isle and require Fremennik Diary partial completion or Lunar Diplomacy quest progress to access efficiently. They're also not very profitable for beginners. Block to save time.

④ Jungle Horrors / Cramps — Block If You Dislike Them（Jungle Horrors·不喜则屏蔽）

Why block: These tasks are assigned frequently and involve running around collecting dinosaurs or fighting in inconvenient locations. Not "bad" tasks, but if you find them annoying, block them. Your Slayer time should be enjoyable.

⑤ Anything in the Wilderness（荒野中的任何任务）

Why block: Any task that requires going into the Wilderness (PKers can attack you) should be blocked if you're not comfortable PvPing. This includes certain Skeleton assignments, Green Dragons, and Revs tasks. Your safety is more important than the GP from these tasks.

🔄 When to Skip vs. When to Stay（何时跳过vs何时继续）

Skipping a task costs 2–5 Slayer points (depending on your current master). As a beginner with few points, skip sparingly. Here's the framework:

Your Slayer Points（你的Slayer点数）Skip Behavior（跳过行为）Example（示例）
0–30 pointsNever skip (always stay)You need points to buy the Slayer Helmet; skipping wastes progress
30–100 pointsSkip only if task is dangerousWilderness tasks, high-level bosses you can't kill yet
100–300 pointsSkip moderately annoying tasksTasks that are tedious but not dangerous
300+ pointsSkip freely（自由跳过）You have enough points for all unlocks; optimize for enjoyment

🎯 Slayer Unlocks Priority (How to Spend Your Points)（Slayer解锁优先级·如何花费点数）

Slayer points can be spent on various unlocks. Here's the order of priority for beginners:

  1. Slayer Helmet (400 points): Combines all Slayer protection items into one. This is the #1 priority.
  2. Second Slot (100 points): Allows a 6th blocked task. Quality of life upgrade.
  3. Broad Bolts/Arrows (300 points each): Required to kill certain Slayer monsters with Ranged.
  4. Ring of Wealth (250 points): Teleport to Grand Exchange.
  5. Herb Sack (350 points): Stores herbs; useful if you're also training Herblore.
  6. Extra Block Slots (100 points each): Each additional slot lets you block one more monster type.
💡 The 400-Point Milestone（400点里程碑）

Your absolute #1 goal as a new Slayer player is to accumulate 400 Slayer points as quickly as possible. That's roughly 200 tasks with Turael (0 points) — so don't use Turael for long. Switch to Vannaka (2 points/task) as soon as you hit Combat 40. At 2 points per task, you need about 200 tasks to reach 400 points. That sounds like a lot, but by the time you're at Duradel (5 points/task), you'll be earning points much faster. Stay focused on the Slayer Helmet — it transforms the skill.

7. Slayer FAQs for Beginners（初学者常见问题）

What Combat level should I be before starting Slayer?（开始Slayer前应该到什么战斗等级？）

You can start Slayer at Combat level 1 with Turael. However, the tasks become meaningful (useful loot, decent XP) at around Combat level 30–40 when you unlock Vannaka. If you're a pure account (intentionally keeping Combat low), you can still do Slayer — just stick to Turael and focus on tasks that don't raise Combat levels you want to keep low.

How many Slayer points do I need for all important unlocks?（所有重要解锁需要多少Slayer点数？）

Here's the approximate total: Slayer Helmet (400) + Broad Bolts (300) + Broad Arrows (300) + Ring of Wealth (250) + Herb Sack (350) + Extra Block Slot (100) = 1,700 points. At 5 points per task (Duradel), that's about 340 tasks. It's a long-term investment, but every unlock makes Slayer more enjoyable and efficient.

Can I do Slayer on an Ironman account?（Ironman账号能做Slayer吗？）

Absolutely — Slayer is even MORE important on Ironman because many rare drops (Dragon items, herbs, seeds) are most efficiently obtained through Slayer. The same master progression applies. The only difference is that you can't buy Slayer equipment from the GE, so you'll need to obtain them through other means (quests, monster drops, or the Slayer Equipment shop using points).

What should I do if I get a task I can't complete?（收到无法完成的任务怎么办？）

First, check if you have the required Slayer level. If you don't, the master won't assign that task to you (the game prevents this). If you physically can't complete the task (not enough food, can't access the location), use your Turael skip (free) or spend points to skip it. Don't struggle through a task that isn't fun — Slayer is a long-term skill, and enjoyment matters.

Is Slayer worth it for F2P players?（F2P玩家值得做Slayer吗？）

Slayer is technically available in F2P, but it's extremely limited. There's only one F2P Slayer Master (Turael, essentially), and the task pool is tiny (mostly cows, spiders, giants). F2P Slayer is mainly useful for getting a few early tasks done while you save up for membership. Once you have membership, the entire skill opens up.

How long does it take to get 99 Slayer?（99 Slayer需要多长时间？）

At efficient rates (Duradel, good gear, no skipping), 99 Slayer takes approximately 150–200 hours. At beginner rates (Vannaka, budget gear, skipping some tasks), it can take 300–400 hours. The good news is that Slayer doesn't feel like a grind because every task is different. Most players naturally reach 99 Slayer over 1–2 years of casual play.

Should I use Melee, Ranged, or Magic for Slayer tasks?（Slayer任务用近战、远程还是魔法？）

Melee is the default for 80% of Slayer tasks because it's the most GP-efficient and doesn't consume expensive ammo. Ranged is excellent for tasks where you can safe-spot (Dust Devils, some dragons). Magic is rarely the best choice for general Slayer (expensive runes), but it's essential for certain monsters (Susceptible to Magic attacks). As a beginner, focus on building a good Melee setup first, then add Ranged as a secondary option.
