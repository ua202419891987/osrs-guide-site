💡 Quick Verdict — F2P Leveling 2026

Maxing every F2P skill to 99 takes 1,500–2,500 hours. Don't do it. The smart play: get all skills to 50–60 (takes 40–80 hours), complete Dragon Slayer, bank 1M+ GP, then go members. F2P XP rates cap at ~50K XP/hour; members methods hit 200K–400K+. Every hour you spend grinding 70–99 Mining in F2P is an hour you could have spent getting 70–90 in members. This guide shows the optimal F2P path — read the Leveling Order section for the exact checkpoint plan.

Free-to-Play OSRS isn't just a demo — it's a complete game with seven trainable skills, dozens of quests, and a surprising amount of depth. Many players spend months (or even years) as F2P before ever buying membership. The key is knowing what to train, where to train it, and when to stop. This guide covers every F2P skill with the best training methods, XP rates, and a recommended leveling order that maximizes your efficiency. ① 🎯 Why Level a F2P Account First?（为什么先练F2P账户？）

Before you rush to buy a bond or subscription, here's why spending time in F2P is actually smart:

1.1 💰 Learn the Game Without Wasting Membership Days（在不浪费会员天数的情况下学习游戏）

Membership costs money (or bonds worth ~5.2M GP). Every day you spend as a member trying to figure out where Lumbridge Castle is, you're burning real-world value. F2P lets you learn the core mechanics — movement, questing, banking, Grand Exchange trading, and basic combat — for free. When you finally go members, you'll know exactly what you're doing.

② ⚔️ Combat Leveling (Attack/Strength/Defence/Ranged/Magic)（战斗技能升级（攻击/力量/防御/远程/魔法））

Combat is where most F2P players spend their time. Your combat level is the single most visible stat, and it determines what you can kill, what quests you can complete, and whether PKers in the Wilderness laugh at you or run away.

💡 Ranged Budget Tip

Use iron arrows for all training up to level 70. The DPS difference between iron and adamant arrows is tiny in F2P (you don't have Ava's device to retrieve arrows), and iron arrows cost 5 GP each vs. 80 GP for adamant. Save your GP for gear upgrades.

2.3 ✨ Magic — F2P Training Methods（魔法 — F2P训练方法）

Magic is expensive to train in F2P (runes aren't cheap), but it unlocks teleports, high-alching, and powerful combat spells. Two main paths:

Path 1 — Combat Magic (costs GP, faster XP):

  • 1–25: Cast Wind Strike on cows/chickens (mind rune + air rune, ~2 GP per cast)
  • 25–45: Cast Fire Strike on Hill Giants (chaos rune + fire rune, ~60 GP per cast)
  • 45–55: Cast Fire Bolt on Moss Giants (chaos rune + fire rune, ~60 GP per cast)
  • 55+: High Alchemy (generates profit! See below) OR Fire Blast on Ogress Warriors

Path 2 — High Alchemy (earns GP, slower XP):

  • Buy items below alch price on the GE (common picks: magic longbows, adamant platebodies, rune items)
  • Cast High Alchemy while running between training spots
  • XP: 65 XP per cast. GP: 50–200 GP profit per cast
  • This method can earn you 100K–300K GP/hour while training Magic
⚠️ Don't Splash in F2P

Splashing (wearing full armour to hit '-65 magic attack' and attacking rats) is a members-only strategy — it requires specific gear that doesn't exist in F2P. You can't AFK Magic in F2P the way members can. Read our F2P combat training guide for more combat-specific methods.

3.2 🍳 Cooking（烹饪）

Cooking is the fastest F2P 99 and the cheapest buyable skill.

  • 1–15: Cook shrimp (Draynor or Lumbridge range)
  • 15–30: Cook trout (from your fly fishing)
  • 30–58: Cook salmon (better XP than trout)
  • 58–74: Cook tuna (burns less at Lumbridge castle range)
  • 74–99: Cook swordfish → cook sharks at 80+ if you buy them
💡 Always Cook at Lumbridge Castle Range

The Lumbridge Castle cooking range has the lowest burn rate in F2P. It's on the ground floor, right next to the bank chest. Use it for every single fish you cook — there's no reason to cook anywhere else in F2P.

3.6 🎨 Crafting（制造）

Crafting in F2P has limited options, but you can still reach 99.

  • 1–8: Leather gloves (buy leather from GE)
  • 8–27: Gold amulets (buy gold bars, use necklace mould)
  • 27–46: Holy symbols (silver bars, holy symbol mould)
  • 46–54: Tiaras (silver bars, tiara mould)
  • 54–70: Cut emeralds → emerald amulets
  • 70–99: Cut diamonds → diamond amulets (members item, can't wear but can sell)

F2P crafting is slow and expensive (3–8 GP/XP). Unless you're a completionist, getting 50+ crafting before members is enough — members unlocks battlestaves, dragonhide armour, and glassblowing, all of which are faster and profitable.

💡 F2P Runecrafting Reality Check

Getting 99 Runecrafting in F2P takes 300–600 hours. Members get access to the Abyss, Guardians of the Rift, and Ourania Altar, which massively increase XP rates. If you want 99 Runecrafting, go members. 44 Runecrafting (for nature runes + profit) is a much more reasonable F2P goal.

④ 🗺️ Best F2P Training Spots Map（最佳F2P训练地点地图）

Knowing where to train is half the battle. Here's a quick reference of the most important F2P locations:

4.1 🔥 Stronghold of Security (Barbarian Village)（安全堡垒（野蛮人村））

The Stronghold of Security is the single most important F2P training location. It has four floors, each with aggressive monsters scaling in difficulty:

  • Level 1 — Minotaurs (level 12): Good for levels 15–30, drop iron arrows
  • Level 2 — Flesh Crawlers (level 28/35/41): Best AFK spot for levels 40–70, aggressive, great XP
  • Level 3 — Giant Spiders (level 50): Pure XP for levels 55–99, no drops but fast kills
  • Level 4 — Ankous (level 75/82): For levels 70+, good drops (death runes, law runes)

Each floor also gives a reward at the center chest: 1K, 5K, 10K, and the coveted boots (Fancy or Fighting boots). These boots are BIS F2P footwear. Also, if you haven't already, claim your 10K GP from the Stronghold of Security — just navigate to the end of level 4 and collect from the chest. For a step-by-step guide, check our first week progression guide.

4.2 🦎 Corsair Cove (Best All-Round F2P Zone)（海盗湾（最佳全能F2P区域））

Corsair Cove is an underrated F2P area southwest of Rimmington. It has:

  • Ogress Warriors (level 82): Best endgame F2P combat/Ranged training (30K–50K XP/hour, alchable drops worth 80K–120K GP/hour)
  • Maple trees: Behind the gate (requires Dragon Slayer) — 3 maple trees, rarely crowded
  • Lobster/swordfish fishing: On the pier
  • Bank deposit box: Right next to the fishing spots

4.3 ⛰️ Crandor (Dragon Slayer Island)（克兰多尔（屠龙者岛））

Unlocked after the Dragon Slayer quest, Crandor has:

  • Moss Giants (3 spawns, safe spots available)
  • Lesser Demons (for high-level melee/Ranged training)
  • 2 gold rocks for mining

It's a quiet island — perfect for solo training without competition. Access via the Karamja volcano shortcut (after completing Dragon Slayer) or by boat from Port Sarim.

⑤ 📋 F2P Leveling Order — What to Train First（F2P升级顺序 — 先练什么）

You have 12 skills and limited time. Here's the optimal order to level them for maximum efficiency.

5.1 🥇 Phase 1: The First 10 Hours (New Account)（第一阶段：前10小时（新账户））

Your priorities in the first 10 hours:

  1. Do the Stronghold of Security — Grab the 10K GP reward and boots. This takes 15 minutes and funds your early game.
  2. Start Waterfall Quest (members) or Cook's Assistant (F2P) — If you're already members, Waterfall Quest gives 13,750 Attack + Strength XP. In F2P, do Cook's Assistant, Imp Catcher, and Witch's Potion.
  3. Train melee to 30/30/30 — Kill cows in Lumbridge, collect hides for GP. Takes 2–3 hours.
  4. Buy full mithril + rune scimitar (when 40 Attack) — Use cowhide profits.
  5. Train Fishing to 20 (fly fishing) — For food self-sufficiency.

5.2 🥈 Phase 2: The Mid-Game Foundation (10–40 Hours)（第二阶段：中期基础（10-40小时））

  1. Melee to 50/50/50 — Hill Giants then Flesh Crawlers. This is your main grind.
  2. Ranged to 40 — For Dragon Slayer preparation.
  3. Magic to 37 — Falador Teleport is a huge mobility upgrade.
  4. Fishing & Cooking to 40 — Lobster fishing at Karamja for food and small profit.
  5. Woodcutting to 30 — Willows at Draynor for AFK breaks.
  6. Start Dragon Slayer — Aim to complete it by the end of this phase.

5.3 🥉 Phase 3: F2P Endgame (40–80+ Hours)（第三阶段：F2P后期（40-80+小时））

  1. All combat stats to 60+ — Ogress Warriors or Giant Spiders.
  2. Prayer to 40+ — Big bones at Wilderness altar or bury at GE.
  3. All skilling skills to 50+ — Pick 2–3 you enjoy, don't grind all of them.
  4. Bank 1M+ GP — Through Ogress alchables, yew logs, swordfish, or high alching.
  5. Complete all F2P quests — 22 quests for the Quest Point cape (members only) but still worth doing.

At this point, you should seriously consider going members. F2P XP rates cap at around 50K XP/hour, while members methods can hit 100K–300K+ XP/hour. If you're spending 10+ hours on a single level, that's the signal to upgrade.

⑥ 🚪 When to Switch to Members（何时转为付费会员）

This is the million-GP question: when does F2P stop being worth it? Here's how to know.

6.1 📉 Signs F2P Is Becoming Inefficient（F2P变得低效的迹象）

You should strongly consider going members when:

  • Your next level takes 5+ hours — Members methods could cut this to 1–2 hours
  • You've done all 22 F2P quests — There's nothing else to unlock
  • You have 1M+ GP and can afford members gear — Going members with no GP is painful
  • You're grinding skills above level 60 — Members XP rates pull ahead dramatically
  • You're bored of killing the same 5 monsters — Members has 100+ slayer monsters, bosses, raids
  • You want to try a members-only skill — Slayer, Agility, Thieving, Farming, Herblore, Construction won't level in F2P

6.2 💡 The Sweet Spot: Level 50s Across the Board（最佳平衡点：全技能50级）

After coaching hundreds of new players, here's the consensus: get all F2P skills to 50+, complete Dragon Slayer, and then go members. This takes roughly 40–60 hours of playtime and gives you:

  • Enough combat stats to handle most members content
  • A solid understanding of game mechanics
  • 500K–2M GP for starting members gear
  • All 22 F2P quests completed (and the QP to start Recipe for Disaster)

Going members earlier (e.g., at level 3) isn't wrong — you'll just spend your first week doing things you could have done for free in F2P. Going members later (e.g., 70+ all stats) means you wasted 100+ hours on slow XP. Level 50s is the Goldilocks zone.

⑦ ❓ FAQ — F2P Leveling Guide（常见问题 — F2P升级指南）

Q: What's the fastest F2P skill to 99?

A: Cooking is by far the fastest F2P 99. You can buy raw fish from the GE and cook at Lumbridge Castle range, reaching 200K–300K XP/hour at high levels. From level 1, a 99 Cooking grind costs about 2–4M GP and takes 40–60 hours. Firemaking and Prayer are also fast but more expensive.

Q: Should I train Prayer in F2P or wait for members?

A: Wait for members if possible. The Gilded Altar (3.5× XP) is members-only and cuts the grind by two-thirds. In F2P, your best option is burying big bones (15 XP) at the GE or running them to the Wilderness altar (2× XP but PKer risk). Getting 43 Prayer in F2P takes ~4 hours of big bone grinding; in members it takes ~1 hour at a Gilded Altar.

Q: Where should I train combat for the best XP in F2P?

A: For pure XP, Giant Spiders in the Stronghold of Security (Level 3) give the best rates. They're aggressive, have 50 HP, and constantly respawn. For a mix of XP and GP, Ogress Warriors at Corsair Cove are unbeatable — 30K–45K XP/hour plus 80K–120K GP/hour in alchable drops. For a dedicated combat guide, see our F2P combat training guide.

Q: Can I make good GP in F2P?

A: Yes, but nowhere near members rates. The best F2P money-makers are: Ogress Warriors (80K–120K GP/hour via alchables), high alching while skilling (100K–200K GP/hour), yew log cutting (60K–80K GP/hour), and swordfish fishing (80K–120K GP/hour). A dedicated F2P player can earn 500K–1M GP/day. For comparison, members can earn 2M–5M GP/hour with high-level PvM. Check our F2P money-making guide for a full breakdown.

Q: What's the best F2P quest to do first?

A: Start with Cook's Assistant (300 Cooking XP, 1 minute), then Imp Catcher (875 Magic XP), then Witch's Potion (325 Magic XP). These three take under 10 minutes total and boost your Magic level. Next, do The Knight's Sword (12,725 Smithing XP — skips you from level 1 to 29 Smithing instantly). Then focus on unlocking Dragon Slayer. For the full quest order, see our F2P quests before membership guide.

Q: Is it possible to enjoy OSRS without ever buying membership?

A: Absolutely. Many "F2P purists" have accounts with hundreds of millions of XP and maxed F2P skills. F2P has a dedicated PvP community (Edgeville wilderness, clan wars), its own economy, and dedicated worlds. That said, roughly 80% of OSRS content is members-only. If you find yourself enjoying F2P enough to play 50+ hours, you'll almost certainly enjoy members even more.

Q: What's the best F2P melee weapon at each level bracket?

A: 1–10: Iron scimitar (buy from the F2P shop in Al Kharid, 96 GP). 10–20: Black scimitar (300 GP). 20–30: Mithril scimitar (1,200 GP). 30–40: Adamant scimitar (3,600 GP). 40–60+: Rune scimitar (32,000 GP — skip the rune 2h sword, scimitar's 4-tick speed is always better). After Dragon Slayer, the rune platebody (+10 Defence) is a massive upgrade. For crush training (vs. certain bosses), Viggora's chainmace is the best option if you have 60 Attack and access to the Wilderness.

Q: How much GP should I have before going members?

A: 1M GP is the minimum for a comfortable members start — enough for basic gear (rune scimitar, rune armour, amulet of power), some food, and a few teleports. 2–5M GP lets you buy initial Slayer gear and maybe a Bond (5.2M GP). If you have less than 500K GP, consider staying F2P a bit longer to build your bank — members money-makers are faster, but you need some starting capital.

Q: Can I complete Dragon Slayer at combat level 40?

A: Yes, but it's tough. The Dungeon has level 40-60 monsters, and the final fight against Elvarg (level 83 dragon) hits hard. If your combat stats are 40/40/40 with 40 Defence, bring: rune scimitar, rune armour, 10+ lobsters, and an amulet of power. If you're struggling, level Attack to 50 first — the extra max hit makes a big difference. Most players complete Dragon Slayer at 50-60 combat stats for a much easier time.

Q: What's the most efficient F2P Magic training method for low GP?

A: If you're on a tight budget, your only free Magic training option is splashing — but F2P doesn't have the gear required for effective splashing (full dragonhide doesn't exist in F2P). Instead: (1) Cast Wind Strike on cows for 1-15 Magic (costs almost nothing — air and mind runes are everywhere), (2) Steal from the duel arena or do the Demon Slayer quest for rune rewards, (3) At 31 Magic, buy fire runes in bulk and cast Fire Strike on Hill Giants (drops law runes worth ~200 GP each). The most GP-efficient path: combine Magic training with High Alchemy once you reach 55 Magic — buy items below alch value and profit while training.

⑧ 💡 Final Tips for F2P Leveling（F2P升级最终建议）

After spending hundreds of hours in both F2P and members, here's what I wish someone had told me:

  • Don't try to max everything in F2P. Getting 50+ in all skills is the smart play. Members training is 2–5× faster for almost every skill. Every hour you spend grinding 70–99 Mining in F2P is an hour you could have spent getting 70–90 Mining in members.
  • Balance combat and skilling. Many new players go all-in on combat and neglect fishing/cooking, then get stuck because they can't afford food. Train fishing and cooking alongside combat so you're always self-sufficient.
  • Use the Grand Exchange wisely. Check buy/sell margins before you flip items. In F2P, margins on common items (ores, logs, fish) are tight — 5–10%. Don't undercut yourself.
  • Do your F2P quests. They unlock critical areas (Corsair Cove, Crandor, Asgarnian Ice Dungeon) and give XP rewards that skip early grinding. The Knight's Sword alone saves 3 hours of Smithing training.
  • Join a clan chat. Even in F2P, you can join clan chats. "OSRS Advice" and "Reddit CC" are active F2P-friendly chats where you can ask questions and find groups.
  • Protect your account. Set up 2FA on your email and OSRS account, use a bank PIN, and never share your password. For a complete security walkthrough, read our account security guide.
🎯 The F2P Leveling Mindset

F2P OSRS has a beauty that members has lost: simplicity. No chaos altar rushing, no 0-time training, no efficiency-scape. You kill cows, you catch fish, you chop trees. The grind is slower, but the satisfaction is real. Treat F2P as your tutorial — learn the game, build your bank, complete Dragon Slayer. Then, when you finally sail to a members world, you'll appreciate every teleport, every new weapon, and every quest reward that much more.
