OSRS How to Get Graceful Outfit — Full Guide 2026

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 12 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
🏃 Gear Unlock — Updated June 2026

The Graceful Outfit is the most impactful untradeable gear set in OSRS. Full Graceful reduces your run energy drain by 30% and increases run energy restoration by 30% — affecting everything from questing and herb runs to bossing and skilling. This guide covers exactly how to get all 6 pieces as fast as possible, the best rooftop courses for Marks of Grace, recoloring options, and how to optimize your Graceful grind in 2026.

1. What Makes the Graceful Outfit Essential

The full Graceful Outfit provides three stacking benefits:

  • 30% reduced run energy drain — You can run for 30% longer before needing to walk. This is the main benefit and affects literally every activity that involves movement (which is almost everything).
  • 30% increased run energy restoration — Your run energy recovers 30% faster while walking or standing still. Combined with high Agility levels, this means you almost never walk.
  • 25 kg total weight reduction — The set weighs -25 kg total (each piece has negative weight). Lower weight = slower run drain. Graceful alone drops your weight below 0 kg in most setups.

If there's one grind every OSRS account should prioritize, it's Graceful. The QOL improvement from full Graceful is more noticeable than almost any gear upgrade.

2. Marks of Grace — How They Work

Marks of Grace spawn randomly on Rooftop Agility Courses as you complete laps. Key mechanics:

  • Marks spawn on the course tiles themselves (not in your inventory)
  • You must manually pick them up — they appear as glowing golden marks on the ground
  • Marks despawn after 10 minutes if not collected
  • Spawn rate increases with course tier (higher level = more marks per hour, generally)
  • After level 80, mark spawn rates DECREASE on higher courses (Rellekka, Ardougne give fewer marks than Seers' Village)
  • You cannot buy Marks of Grace — they're untradeable

7. Advanced Tips & RuneLite Plugins

  1. RuneLite "Agility" plugin — Highlights the next obstacle on rooftop courses with a green box, reducing misclicks. Absolutely essential.
  2. RuneLite "Mark of Grace" indicator — The built-in Agility plugin flashes your screen and plays a sound when a Mark appears. With this, you'll never miss a Mark.
  3. Screen Markers — Use RuneLite's Screen Markers to draw boxes where you need to click on each course. After 10 minutes, this becomes muscle memory.
  4. Combine with alching — During the long straightaways on Seers' and Ardougne courses, you can High Alch between obstacles. This adds 50K-70K Magic XP per hour at zero cost to your Agility training.
  5. Turn on game sounds — Both Marks of Grace and each course obstacle completion have distinct sound cues. With sounds on, you can watch shows or videos and react to audio cues for clicks.

8. Common Mistakes During the Graceful Grind

  1. Leaving Seers' Village too early. Seers' Village gives the most Marks per hour. Once you unlock it at 60, stay there until you have all 260 marks. Don't jump to Pollnivneach at 70 — your marks rate will drop.
  2. Skipping Canifis entirely. While Seers' is best, Canifis (40-60) is the second-best marks course. If you specifically want Graceful early, do Canifis from 40 to 60 instead of Falador. You'll arrive at Seers' with 120-160 marks already collected.
  3. Not picking up Marks immediately. Marks despawn after 10 minutes. If you notice one at the start of a lap, grab it before continuing. They're easy to miss if you're on autopilot.
  4. Buying recolors before the full set. The set bonus (30% run restoration) only activates with all 6 pieces. Don't spend Marks on recolors until you own the full set — a partial Graceful set is significantly worse than the complete one.
  5. Training without weight-reducing gear. Boots of Lightness, Spottier Cape, and Penance Gloves all reduce weight (and therefore decrease run drain). Wear them before you have Graceful pieces to make the process faster.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I lose my Graceful Outfit and how do I reclaim it?

A: Graceful is reclaimable if lost on death below level 20 Wilderness. If you die above level 20 Wilderness or in PvP while skulled, Graceful is permanently lost. If you lose it in a safe death (most PvM), it appears in your gravestone or at Death's Office with no retrieval fee (untradeable). If permanently lost, you must re-earn all 260 Marks of Grace — there is no buy-back option.

Q: Is the full Graceful set worth it for PvM or just skilling?

A: Graceful is primarily a skilling and questing set. In PvM, you typically want combat stats from your gear. However, Graceful is excellent for activities that involve running between locations: herb runs, birdhouse runs, questing, clue scrolls, and any boss that requires movement between phases (e.g., Zulrah before you have teleports, Muspah, Akkha puzzle room). Many players keep Graceful as their default "running around" gear set.

Q: What is the fastest possible time to get full Graceful?

A: Roughly 6-8 hours from a fresh account. This requires rushing 60 Agility via the optimal XP route (skipping Canifis for Falador at 50), then doing ~200 Marks at Seers' Village. Players who specifically speedrun Graceful often do: 1-60 in 5 hours via Gnome Stronghold → Draynor → Al Kharid → Varrock → Falador, then 3-4 hours at Seers' Village for the remaining 120+ marks. With Hard Kandarin Diary teleport, shave off another 30-45 minutes.

🏃 Graceful Is Your Forever Gear

No single item set in OSRS impacts your day-to-day gameplay as much as the Graceful Outfit. From the moment you equip all 6 pieces, run energy stops being a constant frustration. Questing becomes faster. Herb runs become smoother. You'll wonder how you ever played without it.

The 8-10 hour grind to 260 Marks of Grace might feel long, but you'll use Graceful for thousands of hours afterward. It's the best time investment you can make on any account. Now get to those rooftops. 🏃‍♂️
