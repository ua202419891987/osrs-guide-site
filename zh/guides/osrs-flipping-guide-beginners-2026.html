🎯 Why Flipping Pays: 7% Daily ROI (Zero Requirements)（为什么倒卖赚钱——7%日收益，零要求）

While PvM requires gear and combat stats, skilling requires level grinding, and merching requires deep market knowledge — flipping requires nothing except GP and patience. You can start flipping with 10K GP on a level 3 account fresh off Tutorial Island.

Here's what makes flipping unique:

  • Zero requirements. No combat levels, no quests, no skill levels needed. A brand new account with 10K can start flipping.
  • True passive income. Place your buy and sell offers, then go train Slayer, do quests, or log out. Your offers continue working in the background.
  • Scales infinitely. 100K becomes 200K becomes 500K becomes 1M. Every GP you make from flipping becomes capital for the next, larger flip cycle.
  • Works while you sleep. Overnight flips on slow-moving items can earn 200K-500K+ while you're completely offline.
  • Complements every other activity. Unlike bossing which requires 100% of your attention, flipping occupies zero game time — just the 2 minutes it takes to manage your GE slots between activities.

📊 How GE Flipping Works: Spread + 4h Buy Limit（GE倒卖原理：价差+4小时限购）

To flip successfully, you need to understand three core concepts: the buy-sell spread, the buy limit, and the 4-hour timer. These are the pillars of every flip you'll ever make.

③ 📐 The Buy-Sell Spread (Your Profit)（买卖价差——你的利润）

Every item on the Grand Exchange has two prices at any given moment:

  • Instabuy Price: The price impatient buyers pay to get an item immediately. This is always HIGHER than the GE guide price.
  • Instasell Price: The price impatient sellers accept to sell an item immediately. This is always LOWER than the GE guide price.
  • Margin = Instabuy Price minus Instasell Price. This is your flipping profit per item (minus the 1% GE tax on the sell).

Visualizing the Spread: If Zulrah scales have an instasell price of 240 GP and an instabuy price of 260 GP, the margin is 20 GP per scale. You buy scales at 240 GP — someone who needs scales NOW pays you 260 GP. You pocket 20 GP per scale. Buy 10,000 scales and you've made 200K. Minus 1% GE tax on the sell side (~2.6K) = ~197K net profit on one flip cycle. That's the entire business model.

④ ⏱️ The 4-Hour Buy Limit — Capacity Cap（4小时限购——容量上限）

Every tradeable item in OSRS has a buy limit — the maximum quantity you can purchase from the Grand Exchange every 4 hours. This timer counts from your first purchase of that item, not from when the offer completes. Examples:

Diversification isn't just about risk — it's about capacity.

⑤ 📊 GE Tax — The 1% You Must Account For（GE税——必须计算的1%）

Since 2022, OSRS applies a 1% tax on all Grand Exchange sales, capped at 5M GP per item. This means when you sell an item for 10,000 GP, you receive 9,900 GP (minus 100 GP tax). When calculating your margin, always subtract 1% of the sell price to get your real net profit. A 20 GP margin on a 260 GP item is actually 20 - 2.6 = 17.4 GP net profit per item after tax.

The Flipping Math at Each Scale: 100K capital flipping nature runes at 12 GP margin (after tax): 100K / 110 GP per rune = 909 runes per cycle, 909 x 12 = 10.9K profit per cycle, 3-4 cycles per day = 33K-44K profit per day. 500K capital: 4,545 runes x 12 = 54.5K per cycle, 3-4 cycles = 160K-218K/day. 2M capital: reaches the 25K buy limit, 25,000 x 12 = 300K per cycle, 3-4 cycles = 900K-1.2M per day. This is how flipping scales — it's pure multiplication.

🔍 How to Find Items to Flip (3 Methods)（如何找倒卖物品——3种方法）

Finding profitable items to flip is the most important skill in this game. The margins from last month are useless today — you need to check current margins before every flip session. Here's how to do it systematically:

⑥ 1️⃣ Method: Manual Margin Check (Most Accurate)（方法一：手动价差检查——最准确）

  1. Pick a candidate item. Start with high-volume items: runes, ores, bars, food, potions, ammunition, common PvM supplies.
  2. Buy 1 at +20%. Open the GE, select the item, set price to 20% above the displayed guide price, buy 1. Note the exact price it buys at.
  3. Sell that 1 at -20%. Now sell that same 1 item at 20% below the guide price. Note the exact price it sells at.
  4. Calculate the margin. Buy price minus sell price minus 1% of sell price (tax) = your net margin per item.
  5. Evaluate. If the margin is at least 1% of the item's guide price, the item is worth considering. A 200 GP item with a 2 GP margin meets this threshold.
  6. Scale up. Place your bulk buy offer at the instasell price you discovered. When filled, list them at the instabuy price.

⑦ 2️⃣ Method: Use Price Tracking Tools（方法二：使用价格追踪工具）

  • OSRS Wiki Prices — The wiki tracks 7-day and 30-day price trends for every tradeable item. Look for items where the daily volume is high (1M+ traded per day) and the price trend is stable or slightly rising.
  • GE Tracker / RuneLite GE Plugin — Third-party tools that show real-time margins for thousands of items. The RuneLite "Flipping Utilities" plugin is the best free option — it shows active margins, volume, and ROI as percentage directly in your GE interface.
  • GE History Tab — In-game, right-click any item in the GE interface and select "History." This shows the price graph. Avoid items that are sharply rising or falling — they're being manipulated. You want a stable line with small daily fluctuations.

⑧ 3️⃣ Method: Follow Content Cycles（方法三：跟随内容周期）

OSRS item prices follow predictable patterns tied to game content. Learning these patterns lets you anticipate margin changes before they happen:

  • Weekday vs Weekend: PvM and Skilling supplies rise Friday-Sunday (more players actively playing), dip Monday-Wednesday. Buy midweek, sell on the weekend.
  • New Boss/Content Release Day: Items related to the new content spike in the hours after release. If a new boss requires dragonbane weapons, expect Dragon Hunter Crossbows and Lance to spike.
  • Double XP / Bonus Events: Supplies that give XP (bones, herbs for Herblore, bars for Smithing) spike during bonus XP events. Buy 2 weeks before, sell during the event.
  • League Starts: Items used in Leagues spike as players prep. Items sold during Leagues (main game rares) dip as players liquidate for League supplies.

📋 Step-by-Step Strategy: 100K → 1M GP（逐步策略：10万变100万）

Here is the exact process to follow. This is the same strategy used by experienced flippers — just scaled down for smaller capital.

⑨ 📋 The 5-Step Flip Cycle（5步倒卖周期）

  1. Step 1: Margin Check. Test 5-10 candidate items using the 1-item buy-sell test described in Section 3. Pick the 2-3 items with the best margin-to-price ratio (at least 2-3% ROI).
  2. Step 2: Calculate Your Quantity. Divide your available GP by the instasell price. This is how many you can afford. BUT — never exceed the 4-hour buy limit, and never put more than 25% of your total GP into one item. Example: 100K GP, Nature Runes instasell at 105 GP = 952 runes max. Plus you want to split capital across 2-3 items, so buy ~400 nature runes + allocate the rest elsewhere.
  3. Step 3: Place Buy Offers. Enter your buy offers at the instasell price for your target quantities. Use separate GE slots for different items.
  4. Step 4: Wait (and Do Something Else). Go train a skill, do a Slayer task, complete a quest, or log out. Your offers fill in the background. High-volume items like runes and cannonballs typically fill within 15-60 minutes.
  5. Step 5: Sell. When your buy offers fill, immediately list the items at the instabuy price (from your margin check). The sell offers usually fill within 15-60 minutes for high-volume items. Collect your profit.

🎯 Your First Flip — Walkthrough（你的第一次倒卖——完整演示）

You have exactly 100K GP. Here's what you do:

  1. Margin check 5 items: Cannnonballs (instabuy 198, instasell 190, margin 8), Nature Runes (instabuy 118, instasell 110, margin 8), Fire Runes (instabuy 6, instasell 5, margin 1), Coal (instabuy 198, instasell 185, margin 13), Iron Ore (instabuy 72, instasell 65, margin 7).
  2. Pick the best 3: Coal has the best margin (13 GP, 7% ROI), Nature Runes are solid (8 GP, 7.3% ROI), and Iron Ore is decent (7 GP, 10.8% ROI but lower total value).
  3. Split capital: 35K on Coal (35,000 / 185 = 189 coal), 35K on Nature Runes (35,000 / 110 = 318 nature runes), 30K on Iron Ore (30,000 / 65 = 461 iron ore).
  4. Place 3 buy offers at instasell prices. Wait for them to fill (typically 30-60 minutes).
  5. List for sale at instabuy prices: Coal at 198, Nature Runes at 118, Iron Ore at 72.
  6. Calculate profit: Coal: 189 x 13 = 2,457 GP (minus 1% tax on sale = ~370 GP tax, net 2,087). Nature Runes: 318 x 8 = 2,544 GP (minus ~375 tax, net 2,169). Iron Ore: 461 x 7 = 3,227 GP (minus ~330 tax, net 2,897). Total net profit from one cycle: ~7,153 GP.
  7. Repeat 3-4 times per day: ~21K-28K GP profit per day from 100K capital. That's 20-28% daily ROI.

Important Reality Check: Your first few flips will be small. 7K profit from 100K feels underwhelming. But 100K becomes 128K in one day, which becomes 164K, which becomes 210K... it compounds. A 20% daily ROI on flipping means 100K becomes 1M in roughly 13 days, 10M in about 26 days. This is the power of consistent flipping — it's not about big wins, it's about never losing and always growing.

📈 Scaling Strategy for 1M-10M Flippers（1M-10M倒卖者扩展策略）

At this capital level, you need to balance high-volume consumables with mid-value items:

  • 70% of capital in high-volume consumables: Zulrah scales, Dragon Bones, Death Runes, Gold Ore, Sharks. These fill fast and sell fast — your "bread and butter" flips that generate consistent profit even when markets are slow.
  • 20% of capital in medium-volume items: Prayer Potions, Super Restores, Ranarr Weeds. Slightly slower to fill but higher margins per item.
  • 10% of capital in low-volume items: Abyssal Whips, Berserker Rings, Barrows items. These can sit in your GE slots for 4-12+ hours. The margins are large (50K-350K per flip) but the turnover is slow. Only use these in slots you won't need for other flips.

The 70-20-10 Capital Allocation Rule: 70% fast-turnover (fills in under 1 hour), 20% medium-turnover (fills in 1-4 hours), 10% slow-turnover (fills in 4-12+ hours). This ensures you always have GP coming back to reinvest, even if your slow flips haven't sold yet. Never tie up more than 10% of your flipping bank in items that take longer than 4 hours to turn over.

⚠️ 6 Common Mistakes That Cost GP（6个常见错误）

Every new flipper makes these mistakes. Learn from them in advance and save yourself the GP:

1️⃣ Mistake: Overinvesting in One Item（错误一：过度投资单一物品）

The Problem: You find a great margin on Dragon Bones and dump your entire 5M flipping bank into them. The margin collapses overnight and you're stuck holding 1,500 dragon bones that are now worth 50 GP less each — a 75K loss plus whatever you lose selling at the lower price.

The Fix: Never put more than 25% of your flipping bank into a single item. If you have 5M, split it across at least 3-4 different items in different categories (runes + ores + supplies + PvM drops). Margins collapse, but they never collapse on every item simultaneously.

2️⃣ Mistake: Flipping Low-Volume Items（错误二：倒卖低交易量物品）

The Problem: You try to flip an Armadyl Chestplate with 5M. You can't even afford one. Low-volume, high-value items require 50M+ capital just to buy and sell one unit. Your 5M sits uselessly in your GE slot while you wait for a price you can never reach.

The Fix: Only flip items where you can afford at least 10 units. If an item costs 1M, you need 10M+ in your flipping bank before touching it. Stick to items where your capital lets you buy in bulk.

3️⃣ Mistake: Ignoring GE Tax（错误三：忽略GE税）

The Problem: You calculate a 10 GP margin on an item and buy 25,000 units, expecting 250K profit. But the 1% tax on your 1,500,000 GP sale costs 15K, reducing your net profit to 235K. Worse — if your margin was only 5 GP, the tax could eat 30% of your profit on a 200 GP item.

The Fix: Always subtract 1% of the sell price from your margin calculation BEFORE you place your buy order. Use this formula: Net Margin = (Sell Price x 0.99) - Buy Price. If the result is negative or under 1% ROI, skip that item.

4️⃣ Mistake: Canceling Offers Too Quickly（错误四：过早取消订单）

The Problem: Your buy offer for 5,000 coal hasn't filled in 30 minutes, so you cancel it and try a different price. Now you've wasted 30 minutes and a GE slot, and you need to restart the process.

The Fix: High-volume items (runes, ores, cannonballs) should fill within 1 hour at the correct instasell price. If your offer hasn't filled after 90 minutes, the margin has shifted — cancel, re-do the margin check, and place a new offer at the updated price. But DO NOT cancel within the first hour — slow fills are normal during off-peak hours (2AM-8AM game time).

5️⃣ Mistake: Panic Selling During Dip（错误五：恐慌性抛售）

The Problem: You bought 10,000 Zulrah scales at 240 GP each. The next day, the instasell price drops to 225 GP. You panic and sell everything at 225 GP, locking in a 150K loss.

The Fix: Zulrah scales always recover. They're one of the most volume-traded items in the game. Unless there's a permanent game update that changes the item (e.g., a new BiS magic weapon replaces scales), temporary price dips reverse within 24-72 hours. Hold your position. The only time you should sell at a loss is if the item has been permanently devalued by a game update.

6️⃣ Mistake: Flipping After Game Updates（错误六：游戏更新后倒卖）

The Problem: A major game update drops. You see Bandos items dropped 10% and think "great time to buy." But the update introduced Torva as a direct upgrade to Bandos, permanently reducing its value. Your "discount" was actually a legitimate market correction.

The Fix: Never flip in the first 2 hours after a game update. Let the market settle. Read the patch notes first. Understand WHY something moved before you decide it's a buying opportunity. Wait at least 24 hours after major updates before making large flip decisions.

❓ FAQ + Advanced Tips（常见问题+进阶技巧）

❓ Q: How much GP to start flipping?（Q：需要多少本金？）

A: You can start with as little as 10K-50K GP. Items like fire runes, feathers, and eye of newt cost 2-5 GP each with buy limits in the thousands. Your first cycle will only profit 1K-3K GP, but that's how every flipper starts. The key is to simply start. 10K becomes 13K becomes 17K... compounding does the rest.

❓ Q: How fast do GE offers fill?（Q：GE订单多久成交？）

A: High-volume items (runes, ores, cannonballs) typically fill in 15-60 minutes at the correct price during peak hours. Medium-volume items (potions, food, herbs) fill in 1-3 hours. Low-volume items (boss unique drops, Barrows equipment) can take 4-24 hours. During off-peak hours (2AM-10AM game time), expect everything to fill 2-3x slower due to fewer players online.

❓ Q: Can I make money while training?（Q：边练技能边赚钱？）

A: Absolutely — and this is the biggest advantage of flipping. Place your buy offers, go do a Slayer task or quest for an hour, come back to collect filled offers, list them for sale, go do another activity. Efficient players maintain 4-6 active GE slots with flip offers while bossing, skilling, or questing. This is how wealthy players make GP even when they're not actively "money making."

❓ Q: What's the best item to flip?（Q：最佳倒卖物品是什么？）

A: Zulrah Scales for accounts with 1M+ capital. They have the perfect combination: high volume (traded millions per day), high buy limit (20,000 per 4 hours), high enough per-item value for substantial profit per cycle (17 GP margin x 20,000 = 340K per cycle), and they scale beautifully as your capital grows. For sub-1M accounts, Nature Runes are the best all-around flip item.

❓ Q: What are "limit stacking" & "overnight"?（Q：什么是限购叠加和隔夜倒卖？）

A: Two advanced techniques:

  • Limit Order Stacking: Place buy orders for the same item at multiple price points below market. Buy 1,000 coal at 180 GP, another 1,000 at 175 GP, another 1,000 at 170 GP. When the market dips, your lower orders fill automatically. When it recovers, you profit more. This takes advantage of natural price fluctuations without requiring you to be online.
  • Overnight Flipping: Before logging off, place buy offers for 4-6 different items at slightly below current instasell prices. During the night, fewer players are online — sellers get impatient and sell lower, buyers pay premiums for instant purchases. When you log in the next morning, your orders have often filled at better-than-normal prices. List them for sale at above-market prices and let them sell during the day.

❓ Q: How to avoid losing GP on flips?（Q：如何避免倒卖亏损？）

A: Follow these five safety rules:

  1. Always margin check before buying in bulk. Never assume yesterday's margins still apply.
  2. Never put more than 25% of your flipping bank in one item. Diversification protects you from margin collapses.
  3. Don't flip items that are being manipulated. If an item's price chart looks like a spike, stay away. You're looking at the work of a merchant clan, and you'll be left holding the bag.
  4. Account for GE tax in every calculation. A 1% tax seems small, but on a 500K flip, it's 5K lost.
  5. Set a stop-loss rule. If an item drops more than 10% in a day and you're holding it, sell immediately. Take the L and flip something else. The biggest flips are the ones where you didn't lose money.
