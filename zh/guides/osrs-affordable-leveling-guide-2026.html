OSRS Affordable Leveling Guide 2026: 1–99 on a Budget (Low Cost Methods)

Published: June 9, 2026 By OSRS Guru Last Updated: June 9, 2026

Not every OSRS player has millions of GP to spend on 99s. Whether you are a new F2P player, a low-GP member, an Ironman with limited resources, or simply someone who prefers to earn their levels the old-fashioned way, this guide is for you. We will cover the most cost-effective and even profit-making methods for every skill in OSRS as of 2026. You do not need maxed gear, billions in the bank, or expensive consumables to reach 99 in every skill — you just need the right methods and a bit of patience. If you are just starting out, our New Player Guide 2026 pairs perfectly with this budget approach to get you oriented.

Training on a budget requires a different mindset from efficiency-maxing. Instead of asking "what is the fastest XP/hr?", you ask "what is the best XP/hr I can get for free or profit?" Sometimes the answer is a method that makes you GP while training; other times it is a slower but nearly free method. This guide prioritizes methods that cost 0 GP or actually generate profit, with notes on when spending a small amount of GP is worth the time savings. We will also highlight F2P-compatible methods for each skill, since many budget players start their journey in F2P. For a broader look at how to build your account from scratch, our skills progression path guide provides a roadmap that works on any budget.

A major theme of budget leveling is using quests and Slayer to unlock better methods before you spend a single GP on training. Many of the best budget methods are locked behind quest requirements — for example, completing key quests unlocks access to better training spots, superior gear, and XP lamps that can save you hours of grinding. Similarly, Slayer is the backbone of budget combat training because it generates profit while leveling your combat stats. By the end of this guide, you will have a complete understanding of how to reach 99 in every skill while spending minimal GP — and in many cases, while making a profit. Let's dive in.

Budget Leveling Philosophy: XP vs. GP
（预算升级理念：XP vs GP）

The core principle of budget leveling is understanding the trade-off between time and GP. In OSRS, GP can buy you faster XP — but if you do not have GP, you can still get the same XP by investing more time. The most efficient budget players are those who know when to spend GP (because the time savings are worth it) and when to stick to free methods (because the GP cost is not justified by the XP gain).

The "Free" Methods vs. "Cheap" Methods
（"免费"方法与"廉价"方法）

Throughout this guide, we categorize methods into three tiers: Free (costs 0 GP, often profit-making), Cheap (costs some GP but less than 1 GP/XP), and Expensive (costs more than 1 GP/XP, not recommended for budget players). For example, training Attack on Hill Giants is "Free" because giants drop valuable loot; training Magic by buying runes and splashing is "Expensive" and should be avoided on a budget. Always default to Free methods first, then Cheap methods only if the XP/hr improvement justifies the cost given your current GP balance.

When Spending GP Is Worth It
（何时值得花费GP）

Even budget players should spend GP in a few strategic areas: teleportation (saving time walking), quest requirements (unlocking better methods), and key gear upgrades (like a Dragon Scimitar, which dramatically improves melee XP/hr). A good rule of thumb: spend GP on items that improve your XP/hr across multiple skills (like gear), but avoid spending GP on consumables that only help one training session (like buying runes for Magic training when you could use combat spells instead). For more on earning the GP you do need, our money-making guide for beginners is essential reading.

F2P-Compatible Training Methods
（F2P兼容训练方法）

Many players start their OSRS journey in F2P and want to make meaningful progress before becoming a member. The good news is that almost every F2P skill has a viable (if slow) training method that costs 0 GP. This section covers the best F2P methods for each skill so you can make progress even without a membership.

F2P Combat: Where to Train
（F2P战斗：训练地点）

In F2P, melee combat is trained most efficiently at cows (levels 1–10), Al-Kharid Warriors (10–30), and Moss Giants in the Wilderness (30–50+ for brave players). Ranged in F2P is best trained on cows with a shortbow or at the Ranged training area in Lumbridge. Magic in F2P is slow but doable: use combat spells on low-level NPCs, or use our cheap Magic guide methods with the free runes from quests. Prayer in F2P is trained by burying bones from cows or goblins — slow but free.

F2P Production & Gathering
（F2P生产与采集）

F2P production skills are limited but trainable. Cooking is trained by catching shrimp in Lumbridge and cooking them immediately (almost 0 GP cost). Fishing pairs perfectly with Cooking in F2P — shrimp, sardines, and herring are all catchable and cookable without membership. Mining in F2P focuses on copper/tin (1–15), iron (15–40+), and coal (30+). Smithing in F2P uses the ores you mine to smith bronze/iron/steel items. Woodcutting in F2P is willow-only (level 30+), which is slow but AFK and free. Firemaking in F2P burns the logs you cut. All of these skills are 100% F2P-compatible and cost 0 GP if you gather your own materials.

Transitioning from F2P to P2P on a Budget
（从F2P到P2P的预算过渡）

The most budget-friendly way to transition to P2P is by earning enough GP in F2P to buy a Bond (currently ~14–17M GP depending on GE prices). F2P money-making methods include mining iron (1.2M–1.8M GP/hour at high Mining), catching lobsters (800k–1.2M GP/hour at high Fishing), and killing Hill Giants for limpwurt roots and giant keys. With ~10–15 hours of focused F2P money-making, you can afford your first Bond and unlock P2P permanently through continued Bond farming. Our F2P to P2P Bond guide (if available) or general money-making guide covers this in more detail.

Budget Combat Training (Melee, Ranged, Magic)
（预算战斗训练(近战、远程、魔法)）

Melee on a Budget: Crabs, Giants, and Slayer
（预算近战：螃蟹、巨人和杀戮者）

Melee is the most budget-friendly combat style because it requires almost no GP to train efficiently. From 1–40, train on cows or goblins (free, ~15k–25k XP/hr). From 40–60, Hill Giants in Edgeville Dungeon (free, ~25k–40k XP/hr, with decent loot). From 60–99, the absolute best budget method is Slayer — it trains Attack, Strength, and Defence simultaneously while generating profit. If you are not yet at 50+ combat for Slayer, Sand Crabs (Hosidius) offer free, AFK melee training at ~40k–60k XP/hr with 0 GP cost. A Dragon Scimitar (~120k GP from the Monkey Madness I quest reward) is the only gear upgrade worth buying; everything else can be obtained through questing or monster drops.

Ranged on a Budget: Cannons Are Optional
（预算远程：大炮可选）

Ranged can be expensive if you use expensive ammo, but budget methods exist. From 1–40, train on cows with a shortbow and bronze/iron arrows (cheap, ~15k–25k XP/hr). From 40–70, the budget method is Red Chinchompas — expensive upfront but extremely fast (120k+ XP/hr). If you cannot afford Chins, train Ranged through Slayer using cheap arrows or bolts dropped by monsters. From 70–99, Black Chinchompas are the fastest method but very expensive; a budget alternative is continuing Slayer with Ranged (using a rune crossbow with bolts obtained from Slayer drops). With patience, Ranged 99 is achievable on 0 GP through Slayer alone.

Magic on a Budget: Combat Spells > High Alchemy
（预算魔法：战斗法术>高炼金）

Magic is notoriously difficult to train on a budget because runes are expensive to buy. The solution: use combat spells that require minimal rune consumption, or use monster drops to train. From 1–40, use the cheap Magic training methods from our guide: curse spells on cows/goblins (almost free if you buy runes in bulk), or use the Magic training arena (unlocked after Witch's House quest) for free XP. From 40–99, the budget method is Slayer with Magic (using spells like Fire Blast or Fire Wave on monsters weak to Magic). Completing Dream Mentor unlocks Vryes and other content that helps Magic training. With smart questing and Slayer, Magic 99 is achievable with less than 5M GP invested.

Prayer: The One Expensive Skill (and How to Minimize Cost)
（祈祷：唯一昂贵的技能(及如何最小化成本)）

Prayer is the only skill where "budget" training is genuinely difficult, because XP requires bones (or Ectofuntus tears). The budget approach: (1) Bone Crusher 9000 from Fortis Colosseum (passively trains Prayer while on Slayer tasks), (2) Ectofuntus (4x XP per bone, requires teleporting bones to Ectofuntus but is nearly free), and (3) Burying dragon bones from Slayer tasks (0 GP cost since you get the bones for free). With Bone Crusher 9000, you can reach 70+ Prayer with 0 GP spent. For 99 Prayer on a budget, Ectofuntus with dragon bones (purchased in bulk when prices dip) is the most cost-effective method at ~4–6 GP/XP.

Slayer: The Budget Player's Best Friend
（杀戮者：预算玩家的最佳伙伴）

If you take only one thing away from this guide, let it be this: Slayer is the single best skill for budget players. It trains Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, Hitpoints, and Prayer (with Bone Crusher) simultaneously while generating profit. A well-optimized Slayer setup costs almost 0 GP to maintain and can generate 50k–500k+ GP/hour depending on task RNG. This section explains how to Slayer on a budget.

Getting Started with Slayer (Low GP, Low Requirements)
（开始杀戮者(低GP、低要求)）

To start Slayer, you need 50 GP for a Slayer cape or 25 GP per task (Turael/Guiar AP). The absolute cheapest way to begin: do Turael tasks (Burthorpe) for easy tasks that require no gear. These tasks are slow XP but generate some profit and help you learn the mechanics. At 20+ Combat, switch to Mazchna (Canifis) for better tasks. At 50+ Combat, Krystilia (Wilderness) offers high-risk but high-reward tasks (only recommended if you have some GP for gear). The key budget Slayer rule: never skip a task unless it is genuinely impossible for your gear level — skipping costs GP you do not have.

Budget Slayer Gear Progression
（预算杀戮者装备进展）

You do not need millions of GP for Slayer gear. The budget progression: (1) Dragon Scimitar (120k GP, or free from Monkey Madness I reward), (2) Rune armor set (200k–400k GP total, or obtained from F2P drops), (3) Dragon defender (obtained from the Warrior's Guild, 0 GP), (4) Barrows gloves (requires quest grind but 0 GP), and (5) Abyssal Whip (1.5M–2.5M GP, the first major GP investment most budget players make). With just a Dragon Scimitar, rune armor, and a Dragon defender, you can complete 80% of Slayer tasks efficiently. Save your GP for an Abyssal Whip before spending on luxury items.

Using the Cannon on a Budget
（预算下使用大炮）

The Dwarf Multicannon costs ~600k–800k GP (plus ~200–300 GP per cannonball). On a budget, is it worth it? The answer: yes, but only on ranged-optimized tasks. The cannon effectively doubles your kill rate, which means twice the XP/hr and twice the loot (profit). If you cannot afford the initial cannon cost, skip it — you will still make progress, just slower. Prioritize buying a cannon after you have 2–3M GP banked from early Slayer tasks. The cannon pays for itself within 10–20 tasks if used correctly.

Budget Production Skill Training
（预算生产技能训练）

Crafting on a Budget: Quests and Cheap Methods
（预算工艺：任务与廉价方法）

Crafting is notoriously expensive, but budget methods exist if you are patient. The best budget approach: (1) Complete quests with Crafting XP rewards (e.g., Shilo Village gives 6,000 Crafting XP, Between a Rock gives 5,000 Crafting XP), (2) Train Crafting by making items from monster drops (e.g., crafting dragonhide into armor using dragonhides from bosses/Slayer), and (3) Use the Crafting guide budget methods like spinning flax into bowstrings (profit-making at high Crafting) or cutting gems from ores you mine yourself. With smart questing and using drops, Crafting 99 is achievable with less than 20M GP invested (compared to 80M–120M for optimal methods).

Herblore on a Budget: Farming + Cleaning Herbs
（预算草药：种植+清理草药）

Herblore is the most expensive skill in the game — but only if you buy all your ingredients. The budget approach: grow your own herbs through Farming and clean them yourself (1 XP per herb, adds up over time). By farming herbs profitably, you can supply 50–80% of your Herblore needs for free. For the remaining herbs and secondary ingredients, buy them in bulk when GE prices dip. A budget Herblore 99 costs 40M–70M GP (half the cost of optimal methods) if you grow your own herbs. This requires more time (running farm runs every ~80 minutes) but saves massive amounts of GP.

Smithing on a Budget: Blast Furnace and Giant Dwarf
（预算锻造：高炉与巨人矮人）

Smithing is actually profit-positive at high levels if you use the Blast Furnace correctly. The budget method: (1) Complete The Giant Dwarf quest (unlocks Blast Furnace), (2) Mine your own ores (0 GP cost), and (3) Smelt bars at Blast Furnace (costs 2,500 GP per hour for the foreman, negligible). From 30–60 Smithing, smelting steel bars at BF is profit-positive. From 60–99, smelting addy/cannonballs at BF generates 100k–300k GP/hour while training Smithing. A Smithing 99 on a budget can be profit-positive overall if you mine your own ores.

Fletching and Runecrafting on a Budget
（预算制箭与符文制作）

Fletching is naturally budget-friendly because it is fast and the materials (logs + feathers) are cheap. The budget method: cut your own logs (0 GP cost) and buy feathers in bulk (~5–10 GP each). From 1–99 Fletching, total cost is 5M–15M GP — very affordable compared to other production skills. Runecrafting on a budget is slow but free: use the Ourania Altar (ZMI) (unlocked after Enter the Abyss miniquest) to craft runes for profit. At 77+ Runecrafting, crafting blood runes generates 1.5M–2.5M GP/hour while training — making Runecrafting one of the few skills that pays for itself.

Budget Gathering & Support Skills
（预算采集与支援技能）

Mining: Iron, Amethyst, and Profit
（采矿：铁、紫水晶与利润）

Mining is naturally budget-friendly because it generates profit from the ores you mine. The optimal budget method: (1) 3-tick iron mining (30–55 Mining, ~40k–60k XP/hr, 400k–800k GP/hr profit), (2) Granite at Blast Mine (55–72 Mining, ~60k–80k XP/hr, decent profit), and (3) Amethyst mining (92–99 Mining, ~80k–110k XP/hr, 800k–1.2M GP/hr profit). With Mining, you can actually fund your other 99s through the GP you generate. Our 1–99 Mining guide covers the exact 3-tick method and Blast Mine optimization for maximum profit.

Farming: The Ultimate Budget Skill
（种植：终极预算技能）

Farming is unique because it is profit-positive at all levels if you run the right crops. The budget method: run herb and tree patches every ~80 minutes. Even with minimal initial investment (10–20M GP for seeds), a single farming run at 91+ Farming generates 1M–3M GP and 200k–400k XP. Over time, Farming pays for itself many times over. This makes Farming the #1 recommended skill for budget players — it generates the GP you need for other skills. Prioritize Farming early in your account progression.

Agility, Thieving, and Firemaking on a Budget
（预算敏捷、盗窃与生火）

These support skills cost 0 GP to train but are slow. Agility is trained by running rooftop courses (0 GP cost, ~12k–65k XP/hr depending on level). Thieving is trained at Pyramid Plunder (57 Thieving+) or Ardougne Knights (95 Thieving+), both of which generate profit while training. Firemaking is trained by burning logs from your Woodcutting (0 GP cost, ~30k–60k XP/hr). None of these skills require any GP investment, making them ideal for budget players. The trade-off is time: Agility 99 takes 20–35 hours, Thieving 99 takes 15–25 hours, Firemaking 99 takes 10–20 hours.

Woodcutting and Fishing: AFK Profit
（伐木与钓鱼：AFK盈利）

Woodcutting and Fishing are both AFK-friendly and profit-positive, making them ideal for budget players who want to multitask. Woodcutting: willow (30–60, slow but AFK), yew (60–99, ~800k–1.2M GP/hr profit). Fishing: lobsters (40–80, ~800k–1.2M GP/hr profit), monkfish (80–99, ~1M–1.5M GP/hr profit). These skills are perfect for background grinds while watching Netflix or working. They will not get you 99 quickly, but they will generate the GP you need for other skills with almost 0 active effort.

Using Quests to Skip Expensive Grinds
（利用任务跳过昂贵训练）

Quests are the single most powerful tool for budget players because they provide massive XP rewards for 0 GP (or very low GP for quest requirements). By completing the right quests at the right time, you can skip the most expensive early-level grinds entirely. This section highlights the quests that give the best XP-to-effort ratio for budget players.

The Must-Do Quests for Budget Players
（预算玩家必做任务）

These quests provide massive XP rewards and should be completed as early as possible: Waterfall Quest (2x 13,750 XP in Attack & Strength — enough for level 30+ instantly, 0 GP cost), Tree Gnome Village (11,450 XP in Attack & Defence, 0 GP cost), Fight Arena (43,740 XP in Attack & Strength combined, requires minor GP for food), Dragon Slayer I (18,650 XP in Strength & Defence, 0 GP cost), Monkey Madness I (35,000 XP in Attack & Defence, unlocks Dragon Scimitar — essential budget gear). Completing just these quests before manual training saves you 15–25 hours of grinding and gives you a Dragon Scimitar for free. Check our beginner questing guide for the optimal order.

Using Lamps and Books Strategically
（战略性使用灯与书）

Many quests and diaries reward XP lamps or books that let you choose which skill to boost. For budget players, the optimal strategy is to put these into the most expensive skills (typically Prayer, Construction, or Herblore). A single large XP lamp spent on Prayer can save you 5–10M GP. Plan your lamp usage carefully: before spending, check whether the skill has a free or cheap training method available. If it does, spend the lamp on a skill that does NOT have a cheap method. This maximizes the value of each lamp.

Quest Requirements as a "Budget Wall"
（任务要求作为"预算门槛"）

Some of the best budget training methods are locked behind quest requirements. For example, Blast Furnace (Smithing) requires The Giant Dwarf; Bone Crusher 9000 (Prayer) requires Fortis Colosseum unlocks; Slayer requires multiple quests to access superior masters. Budget players should prioritize quest requirements early — not because the quests themselves are expensive, but because they unlock content that saves you GP in the long run. A 5-hour quest grind can unlock a method that saves you 50M GP over the course of 99ing a skill. This is the essence of budget efficiency.

Earning Bonds: F2P to P2P on a Budget
（赚取债券：F2P到P2P的预算方案）

For many budget players, the ultimate challenge is affording membership without spending real money. OSRS allows you to buy Bonds (membership items) with in-game GP, effectively letting you play P2P for free if you can generate enough GP. This section covers the most effective methods for earning Bonds in 2026.

F2P Bond Farming Methods
（F2P债券赚取方法）

The most reliable F2P method for earning Bonds is mining iron (1.2M–1.8M GP/hour at 40+ Mining) or fishing lobsters (800k–1.2M GP/hour at 40+ Fishing). With current Bond prices at ~14–17M GP, you need approximately 10–15 hours of focused F2P money-making to afford your first 14-day Bond. After becoming a member, continue Bond farming through member money-making methods like bossing, high-level Slayer, or Farming runs. Many players maintain permanent P2P status through Bond farming alone.

P2P Bond Farming: The Best Methods
（P2P债券赚取：最佳方法）

Once you have P2P, the best Bond farming methods are: (1) Slayer (80+) — 100k–500k+ GP/hour profit, (2) Farming herb runs — 1M–3M GP per run, (3) Bossing (Vorkath, Muspah, Hueycoatl) — 2M–5M+ GP/hour with good gear, and (4) High-level Skilling (Runecrafting blood runes, Mining amethyst) — 1M–2.5M GP/hour. With 2–3 hours of focused Bond farming per day, you can easily maintain permanent membership. The key is to treat Bond farming as a scheduled activity rather than relying on random drops.

Saving GP for Bonds: Tips for Consistency
（为债券节省GP：持续技巧）

To reliably afford Bonds, you need to be disciplined about GP management. Tips: (1) Always keep 5–10M GP in the bank as a Bond buffer, (2) Do not spend GP on expensive training methods until you have a 30-day Bond buffer, (3) Use our complete skill training guide budget methods to minimize GP spent on 99s, and (4) Consider using a credit card for Bonds if you can afford the real-money cost — the time saved from not Bond farming can be used for actual skilling. For many players, buying Bonds with real money is the most efficient use of their time.

Final Tips for Budget Leveling

  • Prioritize profit-making skills first: Farming, Mining, Runecrafting, and Slayer generate GP while training. Get these to high levels early so they can fund your expensive 99s (Construction, Herblore, Prayer).
  • Use quests to skip grinds: Every quest XP reward is GP saved. Complete all high-XP quests before manually training skills. Our questing guide has the optimal order.
  • Never buy GP with real money: It violates Jagex's rules and risks your account. With patience and the methods in this guide, you can 99 every skill on 0 real-money investment.
  • Join a clan or community: Budget players benefit enormously from clan support — free gear loans, advice on cheap methods, and group content that generates profit. The OSRS community is generous to newcomers who show genuine effort.
  • Track your GP/hour on every activity: Use RuneLite's GP tracker to see which activities are actually profit-positive. You may discover that your "efficient" method is actually losing you GP.
  • Be patient: Budget 99s take longer, but they are more rewarding. A 99 you earned through effort and smart planning feels better than a 99 you bought with GP. Enjoy the journey.
  • Reinvest your profits wisely: When you earn a big GP drop (e.g., a rare drop from a boss), do not spend it on luxuries. Invest it in gear that improves your XP/hr or GP/hr, then continue the budget cycle.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I really reach 99 in every skill without spending GP?

Yes, but with caveats. Skills like Farming, Mining, Runecrafting, and Slayer are profit-positive and can be 99ed with 0 GP spent. Combat skills (Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic) can be 99ed through Slayer with minimal GP spent (just gear upgrades). The expensive skills (Construction, Herblore, Prayer, Crafting) require GP investment, but you can generate that GP through the profit-making skills. Total GP needed for all 23 99s on a budget: ~150M–300M GP, which is achievable through ~200–400 hours of focused money-making.

What is the cheapest skill to 99 in OSRS?

Farming is the cheapest (often profit-positive) skill to 99 if you grow your own herbs and trees. With an initial investment of 10–20M GP for seeds, each farming run generates profit and XP simultaneously. Over time, Farming pays for itself and then some. Other nearly-free 99s: Mining (if you mine your own ores), Runecrafting (if you use ZMI/Blood runes), and Slayer (which is profit-positive at high levels).

How do I earn my first 10M GP as a new player?

The most reliable methods for new players: (1) Mining iron (40+ Mining, 1.2M–1.8M GP/hour), (2) Fishing lobsters (40+ Fishing, 800k–1.2M GP/hour), (3) Killing Hill Giants (30+ Combat, 300k–600k GP/hour plus good drops), and (4) our beginner money-making guide methods. With 10–15 hours of focused money-making, you can afford your first major gear upgrade (Abyssal Whip, Dragon Scimitar) or save for a Bond.

Is F2P or P2P better for budget players?

P2P is objectively better for budget players because it unlocks profit-making methods that F2P cannot access (high-level Slayer, Farming runs, bossing). However, F2P is a fine starting point to learn the game and earn your first Bond. The optimal path: start in F2P, earn 14–17M GP for a Bond, become P2P, and then use P2P money-making methods to maintain permanent membership. Trying to 99 everything in F2P is possible but extremely slow and limited.

What gear do I need before starting budget combat training?

The minimum gear for budget combat: (1) Dragon Scimitar (free from Monkey Madness I or 120k GP), (2) Rune armor set (200k–400k GP or free from drops), (3) Dragon defender (0 GP, from Warrior's Guild), (4) Amulet of Glory (0 GP, from quest rewards or GE). With just these items, you can train melee to 99 and complete most Slayer tasks. Save your GP for an Abyssal Whip (1.5M–2.5M GP) as your first major upgrade.

How long does it take to 99 all skills on a budget?

On a budget, expect 1,500–3,000 hours of /played time for all 23 99s (compared to 800–1,200 hours for optimal methods). The difference is because budget methods are slower (lower XP/hr) and because you need to spend time money-making to fund the expensive skills. However, budget players often enjoy the game more because they experience more diverse content. If you play 2–3 hours per day, you can max in 1.5–3 years on a budget. The journey is the destination!
