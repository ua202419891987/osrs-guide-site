1. Day 0: Login & Assess (30 Minutes)

Before you start grinding, you need a baseline assessment. This 30-minute session will tell you exactly where you stand and what you need to prioritize.

🎯 Day 0 Checklist (Do This First)

  1. Log in to your account — If you can't remember your password, use the Jagex Account recovery system.
  2. Check your total level — Press the Skills tab (flashing lightning bolt icon) and look at your total level. This is your single most important metric.
  3. Check your Quest Points — Press the Quest List (scroll icon) and count how many quests you've completed. You need 280+ QP for endgame content.
  4. Check your bank — Open your bank and see how much GP you have. Also check for high-value items you might have forgotten about (Dragon armor, Rune items, etc.).
  5. Set your 7-day goal — Based on your assessment, pick ONE primary goal for the week (see below).

Pick ONE primary goal for the week. Trying to do everything will leave you burned out and with minimal progress. Here are good goals based on account type:

Focus: Days 1-5 (Quests + Gear). You need Barrows gear and 70+ Combat to start bossing. By Day 6, you should attempt your first Barrows run or God Wars Dungeon trip.

2. Day 1: Quest Foundations

Quests are the highest ROI activity in OSRS. A single day of questing can give you 20-30 combat levels worth of XP (through quest rewards) plus unlock gear, areas, and teleports.

Today's objective: Complete the 5 most important quests for a returning player. These quests are chosen because they unlock teleports, gear, and XP rewards that will accelerate your progress for the rest of the week.

📋 Day 1 Quest Order (Do in This Exact Order)

  1. Tree Gnome Village (Required for Spirit Tree network)
    • Requirements: None (can be done at any level)
    • Rewards: Access to Spirit Tree network (fast travel to 6+ locations)
    • Time: ~30 minutes
    • Why first: Spirit Trees save HOURS of travel time over your play session
  2. The Grand Tree (Unlocks Gnome Agility Course)
    • Requirements: Tree Gnome Village completed
    • Rewards: 18,400 Attack XP (brings 1 Atk to 37), access to Gnome Agility Course
    • Time: ~45 minutes
    • Why second: 18K free Attack XP is HUGE for low-level accounts
  3. Fairy Tale Part I: Growing Pains (Start Fairy Ring questline)
    • Requirements: 17 Farming (can be boosted), 40 Thieving (boost with Thieving pot)
    • Rewards: Access to Fairy Rings (best fast-travel in the game), 3,500 Farming XP
    • Time: ~1 hour
    • Why third: Fairy Rings let you teleport to 50+ locations. Essential for efficient play.
  4. Fairy Tale Part II: Cure a Queen (Complete Fairy Ring access)
    • Requirements: Part I completed, 49 Farming, 57 Thieving
    • Rewards: Full Fairy Ring access, ability to use Fairy Rings from any location (with a Fairy Ring teleport tab)
    • Time: ~1.5 hours
    • Why fourth: Completes the Fairy Ring unlock. You now have the best transportation in the game.
  5. Lost City (Required for Fairy Rings + Zanaris access)
    • Requirements: 31 Crafting, ability to defeat a Level 63 Ent (bring food)
    • Rewards: Access to Zanaris (useful for future quests), required for Fairy Tale Part III
    • Time: ~45 minutes
    • Why fifth: Completes the "Fairy Ring trifecta." You now have Spirit Trees + Fairy Rings + Zanaris access.

⏰ Day 1 Time Management

If you have 2 hours to play today, you can complete all 5 quests. If you have less time, prioritize in this order:

  1. Tree Gnome Village (must do — unlocks fast travel)
  2. The Grand Tree (must do — free 18K Attack XP)
  3. Fairy Tale Part I (should do — Fairy Rings are essential)
  4. Fairy Tale Part II (nice to have — completes Fairy Ring access)
  5. Lost City (can skip temporarily — do later when you have more time)

3. Day 2: Core Skills Push

Now that you have transportation unlocked (Day 1), it's time to train your core skills. The goal today is to get your combat stats to a level where you can start making money efficiently (Day 3) and upgrading your gear (Day 5).

Today's focus: Get your combat stats to 60+ Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, and Magic. This is the "mid-game threshold" where the game starts to open up.

⚔️ Optimal Skill Training Order for Returning Players

Train your skills in this order for maximum efficiency:

Step 1: Attack → 60 (If Not Already There)

Method: Train on Rock Crabs (Hosidius, Kourend) or Sand Crabs (Zeah). These monsters have low Defence and don't retaliate (you can AFK them).

Time: 1-2 hours to get from 40 to 60 Attack.

Why 60: Unlocks Dragon weaponry (Dragon Longsword, Dragon Dagger). These are massive damage upgrades over Rune weapons.

Step 2: Strength → 60 (if not already there)

Method: Same as Attack — Rock Crabs or Sand Crabs. Use a Strength potion (boosts Str by 5 levels) to speed up training.

Time: 1-2 hours from 40 to 60 Strength.

Why 60: Increases your max hit with melee weapons. At 60 Str, you'll hit 20-22 damage with a Dragon Longsword (vs. 15-17 at 40 Str).

Step 3: Defence → 60 (if not already there)

Method: Rock Crabs (again). Use Defence potions to boost if needed.

Time: 1-2 hours from 40 to 60 Defence.

Why 60: Unlocks the ability to wear Dragon armor (Dragon chainbody, Dragon platelegs). This gives you +40 Ranged Defence — you'll take way less damage from Ranged attacks.

Step 4: Prayer → 43 (MANDATORY)

Method: Bury Big Bones (buy from GE for ~150 GP each) OR use a Gilded Altar in someone's Player-Owned House (World 330 — official Gilded Altar host world).

Time: 1-2 hours to get from 1 to 43 Prayer (using Dragon Bones at Gilded Altar).

Why 43: Unlocks Protect Prayers (Protect from Melee, Protect from Missiles, Protect from Magic). These reduce incoming damage by 100%. Prayer is the single most impactful skill in OSRS.

4. Day 3: First 1M GP

You now have decent combat stats (60+) and Protect Prayers (43 Prayer). Today's goal is to make your first 1 million GP. This might sound daunting, but with 60+ combat and Protect Prayers, there are several reliable methods to generate income.

Why 1M GP matters: With 1M GP, you can buy Barrows gloves (2.5M sold for 1.2M), Dragon armor set (~800K), and still have GP left over for supplies (food, potions, teleport tabs). Essentially, 1M GP = you're no longer "poor" in OSRS.

💰 Top 4 Money Making Methods for Day 3

Pick the method that best matches your combat style preference and current gear:

Method 1: Slayer (BEST OVERALL)

  • Requirements: 50+ Combat, 40+ Prayer (you have this from Day 2!)
  • How to start: Talk to Nieve (Falador, behind the lodestone) or Konar (cats in the mountains).
  • GP/Hour: 300K-600K (depending on task and luck)
  • Why it's great: You get combat XP + Slayer XP + GP simultaneously. Slayer points can be spent on Slayer helmet (massive damage boost).
  • Best tasks for GP: Gargoyles (70 Slayer), Nechryaels (80 Slayer), Abyssal Demons (85 Slayer). If you don't have the Slayer level, do Hill Giants or Moss Giants (low requirement, ~200K GP/hour).

Method 2: Barrows Runs (Mid-Level Bossing)

  • Requirements: 70+ Combat, 43+ Prayer, Barrows armor set (buy from GE for ~1M total)
  • How to do: Enter the Barrows minigame (north of Mort'ton). Kill all 6 brothers, then loot the chest. Each run takes 5-8 minutes.
  • GP/Hour: 500K-1.2M (depending on Barrows drops)
  • Why it's great: Barrows brothers drop Barrows equipment (which sells for 100K-500K each). Even "bad" drops sell for 10K-30K GP (Bolt racks, Key halves).
  • Strategy: Use Protect from Melee for most brothers. Kill Ahrim and Karil from a distance with Ranged/Magic. Use Prayer potions to maintain Protect prayers.

Method 3: Herb Runs (Passive Income)

  • Requirements: 50+ Farming (can be boosted with Garden pie), GP for seeds (~50K investment)
  • How to do: Plant herbs at 5 main herb patches (Falador, Catherby, Ardougne, Port Phasmatys, Trollheim). Harvest after 80 minutes.
  • GP/Hour: 200K-400K (passive — you only spend 10 minutes actually planting/harvesting)
  • Why it's great: Low time investment, consistent income, and you can do it while doing other activities (Slayer, bossing, etc.).
  • Best herbs to plant: Ranarr Weed (used for Prayer potions — always in demand), Snapdragon (used for Super Restore — high value), Torchlight (new herb in 2026 — high price).

Method 4: Giant Babydragon Farming (Low Effort)

  • Requirements: 60+ Combat, access to Mythic monsters (complete Lost City quest from Day 1)
  • How to do: Kill Giant Babydragons in the Mythicita dungeon. They drop Babydragon bones (30 Prayer XP each, sell for ~800 GP) and Green dragonhide (used for crafting).
  • GP/Hour: 250K-400K
  • Why it's great: Very low effort — Babydragons have low Defence and don't hit hard. You can AFK for 5+ minutes at a time.

🎯 Day 3 Action Plan (Step-by-Step)

  1. Morning (1 hour): Do a Slayer task. Even if you don't make 1M GP, you'll make 200K-400K GP plus get combat XP.
  2. Afternoon (1 hour): Plant herb seeds at all 5 patches. This is a 10-minute activity that pays off 80 minutes later.
  3. Evening (1 hour): Attempt Barrows runs. Even 2-3 runs will generate 200K-600K GP. Watch a YouTube guide first if you've never done Barrows.
  4. Before bed: Harvest your herbs. Depending on your seeds, this should give you 100K-300K GP.

Total Day 3 Income (Realistic): 600K-1.2M GP. If you hit 1M GP, congratulations — you've achieved today's goal!

💡 What to Do With Your First 1M GP

Don't blow it all on cosmetic items or overpriced gear. Spend your first 1M GP on gear that generates more GP:

  • Dragon armor set (~600K total) — Makes you 2x more survivable in combat
  • Dragon weapon (Dragon Longsword ~50K, Dragon Dagger ~20K) — Increases your DPS
  • Supply stock (300K) — Prayer potions, food, teleport tabs. Having supplies lets you train longer without banking.

5. Day 4: Diary Rewards

Achievement Diaries (also called "Diaries") are sets of tasks tied to specific regions of Gielinor. Completing a Diary unlocks rewards that range from convenient teleports to stat boots to exclusive gear. For returning players, Diaries are the fastest way to improve your account's quality of life.

Today's goal: Complete the Easy and Medium tiers of the 3 most impactful Diaries. This should take 2-3 hours but will save you hundreds of hours of travel time over your OSRS career.

🏆 Top 3 Diaries to Complete First

① Kandarin Diary (Seers' Village)

Why it's #1: The Kandarin Diary (Easy+Medium) unlocks:

  • Teleport to Camelot (spell) — One of the most useful teleports in the game (puts you in the middle of multiple training areas)
  • Seers' Village teleport (unlocked by completing Medium) — Teleports you directly to Seers' Village
  • Flax spinning — NPCs in Seers' Village will spin flax into bowstring for you (saves time if you do Farming)
  • Requirement: 31 Agility (for Easy), 65 Magic (for Medium — to cast Camelot Teleport)
  • Time to complete: ~1 hour

② Desert Diary (Kalphite area / Sophanem)

Why it's #2: The Desert Diary (Easy+Medium) unlocks:

  • Nardah teleport — Teleports you to Nardah (Desert city). From Nardah, you can quickly access Desert Quarry (Mining), Sophanem (Agility Pyramid), and Kalphite Lair (Slayer).
  • Pharaoh's Sceptre (reward for Medium) — A teleport item that gives you fast access to Pyramid Plunder (Thieving training) and other Desert areas.
  • Requirement: 10 Thieving (for Easy), 49 Thieving (for Medium)
  • Time to complete: ~45 minutes

③ Fremennik Diary (Rellekka)

Why it's #3: The Fremennik Diary (Easy+Medium) unlocks:

  • Rellekka teleport — Teleports you to Rellekka (Fremennik capital). From there, you can access Fremennik Slayer Dungeon, Neitiznot (banking), and Waterbirth Island (Dagannoth Kings).
  • Fremennik sea boots (reward) — Gives +4 Defence bonus when worn. Small but helpful for mid-level players.
  • Requirement: 20 Combat (for Easy), 40 Slayer (for Medium — to kill Fremennik Slayer monsters)
  • Time to complete: ~1.5 hours

7. Day 6: Boss Introduction

Today's the day you've been working toward — your first boss kill. Bossing is the most exciting and rewarding part of OSRS. Bosses drop rare loot worth millions of GP, give massive XP, and provide a sense of accomplishment that training skills can't match.

Today's goal: Successfully complete your first boss run and walk away with at least one valuable drop. We'll start with the easiest entry-level bosses and work our way up.

👹 Best First Bosses for Returning Players

① Barrows (Entry-Level Boss Group)

Why it's the best first boss: Barrows is low risk, high reward. The 6 Barrows brothers are not very difficult (you can kill them with 60 Combat + Barrows gear), and they drop Barrows equipment that sells for 100K-500K GP each.

Requirements: 70+ Combat, 43+ Prayer, Barrows armor set (you should have this from Day 5).

Strategy:

  1. Enter the Barrows minigame (north of Mort'ton, use Mort'ton teleport scroll).
  2. Kill all 6 brothers (use Protect from Melee for melee brothers, kill Ahrim/Karil from distance).
  3. Loot the treasure chest (requires 80% completion of the tunnels).
  4. Repeat — Each run takes 5-8 minutes. In 1 hour, you can do 8-12 runs.

Expected loot: 1-3 Barrows items per hour (worth 100K-500K each) + Bolt racks (sell for 2K-5K GP each).

② Giant Mole (Entry-Level Solo Boss)

Why it's great: The Giant Mole is a solo boss located in the Falador Dungeon. It has a simple attack pattern (burrows underground, pops up elsewhere) and drops valuable loot (Mole skin, Mole claw) used for gloves and boots.

Requirements: 60+ Combat, any mid-tier gear (Dragon armor + Abyssal Whip works fine).

Strategy: Use Melee (whip + defender). When the Mole burrows, follow the falling dirt particles to find where it resurfaces. Kill it 10-15 times to get your first Mole drops.

Expected loot: Mole skin (used for Mole slippers — +4 Strength) and Mole claw (used for Mole gloves — +3 Attack). These sell for 50K-100K GP each.

③ God Wars Dungeon (GWD) — Entry-Level Group Boss

Why it's next: GWD is the first "real" boss most players attempt. It requires teamwork (can be done solo, but easier with others) and drops high-tier loot (Godswords, Bandos armor, Armadyl armor).

Requirements: 80+ Combat, Barrows gear or better, 70+ Prayer (for extended trips).

Strategy: Join a pub GWD team (use the OSRS Discord or in-game clanchat). 3-5 players can easily clear GWD bosses. Kill General Graardor (Bandos) for Bandos armor (melee BIS) or Kree'arra (Armadyl) for Armadyl armor (Ranged BIS).

Expected loot: Bandos chestplate (~10M GP), Bandos tassets (~15M GP), Godsword shards (combine to make a Godsword — worth 50M+ GP).

🎯 Day 6 Bossing Plan (Step-by-Step)

  1. Hour 1: Do 5-10 Barrows runs. Learn the mechanics, get comfortable with bossing, and hopefully get your first Barrows drop.
  2. Hour 2: Kill the Giant Mole 10-15 times. It's a low-stress boss that helps you practice movement and positioning.
  3. Hour 3: Attempt God Wars Dungeon. Find a team (use World 73 — official GWD world) and kill one GWD boss. Even if you don't get a drop, the experience of raiding with a group is valuable.

🎁 Day 6 Rewards Summary

By the end of Day 6, you should have:

  • 1-3 Barrows items (sell for 300K-1.5M GP total)
  • Experience with 3 different boss types (Barrows, Giant Mole, GWD)
  • Confidence to attempt harder bosses (Zulrah, Vorkath) in the future
  • A taste of the "bossing lifestyle" — the adrenaline, the loot, the community
⚠️ Bossing Can Be Frustrating — That's Normal

Your first few boss kills will probably be slow and clumsy. You might die. You might waste supplies. You might not get a drop for hours. That's okay. Bossing is a learned skill. The more you do it, the better you get. Don't give up — your first rare drop will make it all worth it.

Why: Medium Diaries unlock incredible quality-of-life improvements (teleports, gear upgrades, XP lamps). Completing all Medium Diaries is a flag that you're a mid-to-high-level player.

Why: Blood Moon Rises is the capstone Grandmaster quest of the Elder God Wars storyline. Completing it unlocks endgame content and gives you serious bragging rights.

Requirements: 280+ Quest Points, 80+ in most skills, completion of all previous Elder God Wars quests.

💡 Join a Clan for Long-Term Motivation

Playing OSRS solo can get lonely. Joining an active clan gives you:

  • People to boss with (Group bossing is way more fun than solo)
  • Advice from experienced players (ask them for help with quests, gear setups, etc.)
  • A sense of community (clan events, competitions, friendship)
  • Access to clan wars (PvP practice)

Look for clans recruiting in the OSRS Clan Recruitment forum or ask in the OSRS subreddit.

🏆 Day 7 Celebration

You've completed the 7-Day Fast Track! Take a moment to celebrate your progress. OSRS is a game — it's meant to be enjoyed. Here are some fun ways to celebrate:

  • Buy something cosmetic (cosmetic override, holiday item) that you've always wanted.
  • Show off your stats — Take a screenshot of your level 40+ stats and post it on /r/2007scape with the tag "[Returning Player]." The community is supportive and will congratulate you.
  • Help a newer player — Pay it forward. Help someone else with a quest or a boss. OSRS has a great community — be part of it.
  • Take a break — OSRS is meant to be played at your own pace. If you're feeling burned out, take a few days off. Your account will be there when you come back.
