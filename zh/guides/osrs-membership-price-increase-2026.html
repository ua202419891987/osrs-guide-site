🚨 OSRS Membership Price Increase 2026 — Full Breakdown & Community Reaction

1. 🚨 What Changed in March 2026

On March 18, 2026, Jagex announced a global price adjustment for all RuneScape and Old School RuneScape memberships. This was the first major price change since 2022, and the reaction was immediate.

The official reason given by Jagex:

📢 Jagex Statement (March 2026)

"Due to rising operational costs, inflation, and our continued investment in content development, we are adjusting membership pricing to ensure we can keep delivering the high-quality updates our players expect."

The update also mentioned increased costs for servers, anti-cheat infrastructure, and the upcoming Sailing skill development.

For OSRS players, the change was particularly painful because many had already been frustrated by the dual membership requirement (needing both RS3 and OSRS membership to access both games).

💡 Did You Know?

The last major price increase was in 2022 (+$2/month). The 2026 hike is the largest percentage increase in RuneScape's 20+ year history.

4. 🤔 Is Membership Still Worth It in 2026?

The answer depends entirely on how much you play and what you value. Let's break it down:

① ✅ Yes, It's Still Worth It If...

✔️ You play 10+ hours per week

At $14.99/month, that's $3.75 per week for a game with 20+ skills, 150+ bosses, and thousands of hours of content. Compared to WoW ($14.99) or FFXIV ($12.99), OSRS is still competitive.

✔️ You care about the upcoming Sailing skill

Sailing is still planned for late 2026/early 2027. If you're excited about new content, membership is the only way to access it.

✔️ You PvM seriously (Bossing, Raids)

Members content is where the real money is. A single Twisted Bow charge (if you're lucky) pays for 100+ months of membership.

② ❌ No, Skip It If...

✘ You only play casually (2-3 hours/week)

At this play rate, you're paying $1.50+ per hour — not great value. Consider buying bonds with GP instead (if you can farm ~5.2M GP/2 weeks).

✘ You're frustrated with Jagex's direction

If you're unhappy with MTX, the Sailing delay, or the price hike — vote with your wallet. Many players cancelled and switched to F2P bossing (with limitations).

② 👥 Shared Membership (Family Plan)

Jagex introduced a Shared Membership option in late 2025:

  • Up to 3 accounts can share one membership
  • Cost: $19.99/month (vs $14.99 × 3 = $44.97)
  • All accounts must be on the same Jagex account
  • Best for: Players with multiple ironmen or a main + alt
💡 Money-Saving Tip

If you have 2+ accounts, the Shared Membership plan saves you $10/month vs. buying separate subscriptions. This partially offsets the price hike.

③ ⏸️ Take a Break — Use the Grandfather Window

6. ❌ How to Cancel & Get a Refund

Unhappy with the price hike? Here's how to cancel and possibly get a refund:

① 🖥️ Cancel on Jagex Website

Step 1: Log in to your Jagex Account

Go to account.jagex.com → "Payment Settings" → "Manage Subscription".

Step 2: Cancel the subscription

Click "Cancel Subscription". Your membership will remain active until the end of the current billing period.

Step 3: Request a refund (if eligible)

Jagex's refund policy (2026):

  • Within 14 days of purchase: Full refund (EU/UK consumer law)
  • After 14 days: Prorated refund if you haven't used the membership
  • Price hike refunds: Jagex offered a 1-month free extension to existing subscribers in March 2026 (no longer available)

② 📱 Cancel on Mobile (iOS/Android)

If you subscribed via the App Store or Google Play:

  • iOS: Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions → RuneScape → Cancel
  • Android: Google Play Store → Menu → Subscriptions → RuneScape → Cancel

Note: App store subscriptions are not eligible for Jagex direct refunds. You must cancel through the app store.

③ 🔄 Resubscribe Later?

If you cancel now and resubscribe later:

  • Your character and progress are never deleted (unless inactive for 5+ years)
  • You'll pay the new rate ($14.99/month) when you resubscribe
  • All your items, quest progress, and stats remain intact

7. ❓ FAQs — OSRS Membership Price Increase 2026

Q: Will the price increase again in 2027?

A: Jagex hasn't announced future increases, but given the 2026 hike was the first in 4 years, another increase before 2029 is unlikely. That said, inflation happens — lock in a 12-month sub if you're worried.

Q: Are bonds affected by the price increase too?

A: Yes. Bond prices in USD went from $6.99 to $9.99, and in-game GP prices rose from ~4.5M to ~5.2M GP per bond.

Q: Can I still play OSRS for free after the price hike?

A: Absolutely. F2P remains free, and many players are returning to F2P content (free-to-play bosses like Obor and Bryophyta) to avoid paying.

Q: Is there a way to get membership cheaper (VPN, regional pricing)?

A: Jagex detects VPNs and may ban accounts for regional price arbitrage. Don't risk it. The only legitimate discount is the 12-month prepay ($10.99/month vs $14.99/month).

Q: Did Jagex compensate existing subscribers?

A: Yes — existing annual subscribers kept the old rate for 3 months (March→June 2026). New subscribers from March 18 onward paid the new rate immediately.

🎯 Final Verdict

The 2026 OSRS membership price hike sucks — there's no sugarcoating it. A 25% increase is steep. But if you play regularly, it's still one of the best gaming values available. If you're a casual player, consider bonding with GP or taking a break until the Sailing skill launches (which might justify the new price).

Our recommendation: If you're on the fence, cancel and play F2P for a month. See how much you miss members content. If you come back, you'll appreciate it more — and you'll have saved $14.99.
