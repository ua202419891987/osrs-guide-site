OSRS Diary Priority Order — Which 10 Diaries to Complete FIRST (Max Impact)

Not all Achievement Diaries are created equal. Some unlock game-changing rewards that save you hundreds of hours. Others give cosmetic flakes you'll never use. This data-driven guide ranks every diary by reward value per hour invested — so you never waste time on low-impact tasks again.

1. Why Diary Order Matters (Not All Diaries Are Equal)

Achievement Diaries are among the most rewarding content in OSRS — but only if you prioritize the right ones. Completing all 10 regions' diaries takes 200+ hours for the average player. Doing them in the wrong order means missing out on powerful rewards when they'd help you most.

Consider this: the Varlamore Easy Diary takes 45 minutes to complete and gives you a teleport that saves 30 seconds per trip to one of the most trafficked areas in the game. Over a year of playing, that's 10+ hours saved. Meanwhile, the Karamja Elite Diary might take you 15 hours and gives you... the ability to notes certain items. Useful? Sure. Game-changing? Not even close.

🎯 The Core Principle: Reward per Hour Invested

Every diary has two numbers that matter:

  • Time Cost: How many hours to complete (including skill training time)
  • Reward Value: How much this reward improves your account (measured in GP saved, time saved, or power gained per hour of gameplay)

The diaries in this guide are ranked by Reward Value ÷ Time Cost. The higher the ratio, the sooner you should do it.

② Why Beginners Should NOT Start with Hard Diaries

It's tempting to look at the flashy Elite diary rewards and want them immediately. Don't. Here's why that's a mistake:

ReasonExplanation
Skill RequirementsElite diaries need 70+ in multiple skills. Getting those skills just for the diary is inefficient — get them naturally through gameplay first
Opportunity CostAn Easy diary takes 1 hour and gives teleports/utility. An Elite takes 20+ hours and gives niche benefits. Do the Easy one first, feel the benefit immediately
Compound BenefitsMany diary rewards stack. Having 5 Easy diary rewards active simultaneously is stronger than having 1 Elite reward alone
Quest BlockingSome diaries require quests you haven't done. Doing diaries forces you to do quests in an inefficient order
💡 The Smart Progression Path

Complete all Easy diaries first (they're fast and give universal utility). Then do Medium diaries in regions where you already have the skills. Leave Hard/Elite diaries until you naturally meet the requirements through normal gameplay. This guide shows you exactly which ones to prioritize within each tier.

2. The Scoring System — How We Ranked Diaries

To create an objective ranking, we scored each diary across four dimensions. Here's the methodology so you understand why certain diaries rank higher than others.

③ Dimension 1: Reward Value (40% weight)

How impactful are the rewards in daily gameplay? We categorized rewards into tiers:

TierExample RewardsImpact Score
S-Tier解锁新 teleport / 双倍掉落 / 永久 stat boost10/10
A-Tier更快的 teleport / 减少道具重量 / 常规的 utility 提升7–9/10
B-Tier区域特定的小福利 / cosmetic / 偶尔有用的 ability4–6/10
C-Tier几乎不使用的 flavor reward1–3/10

④ Dimension 2: Time Required (30% weight)

How many hours from "decide to start" to "claim reward"? This includes:

  • Time to reach required skill levels (if you don't already have them)
  • Time to complete prerequisite quests
  • Time to actually do the tasks

Important: Our time estimates assume you have reasonable mid-game stats (60 combat, 50 in most skills). If you're below that, add more time. If you're above that, subtract time.

⑤ Dimension 3: Difficulty (20% weight)

Some tasks are annoying even if you have the levels. Examples:

  • Low difficulty: "Enter this location" / "Equip this item" / "Pickpocket X times"
  • Medium difficulty: "Kill X without taking damage" / "Complete raid in time limit"
  • High difficulty: "Get 100% drop rate item" / "PvP related tasks" / "Coordination-heavy boss mechanics"

⑥ Dimension 4: Long-term Utility (10% weight)

Does this reward stay useful at high levels, or does it become obsolete? Examples:

  • High utility: Teleport items (useful forever), XP lamps (always valuable), unlocks that remove gameplay friction
  • Low utility: Rewards that only matter at low levels (e.g., +1 hidden prayer bonus from a mediocre helm)

📊 Final Score Formula

Total Score = (Reward Value × 0.4) + ((10 - Time Score) × 0.3) + ((10 - Difficulty Score) × 0.2) + (Utility Score × 0.1)

Higher score = do it sooner. The Top 10 below all score above 7.5/10.

3. Top 10 Diaries — Detailed Breakdown

These are the diaries with the highest reward-to-time ratio. Complete these first and you'll feel the benefits immediately.

⑦ 🥇 #1: Varlamore Easy Diary

Difficulty: Easy | Estimated Time: 45–90 minutes | Score: 9.2/10

Why It's #1

Varlamore is the newest region (released 2024), and its Easy diary is absurdly fast while giving best-in-slot teleport access to one of the most active areas in the game. The rewards fundamentally improve your ability to train and make money in Varlamore.

Core Rewards

RewardImpact
Varlamore Easy Diary reward — each diary completed in the Varlamore diary series grants the 6% XP increase to Blood Moon and Eclipse armor piecesPermanent 6% XP boost to the best mid-game armor sets
Ability to note cam torum hitsQuality-of-life for dungeon runs
200%Teleport to Quetzal (unlocked after completing several tasks)Fast access to entire region
2 free teleports per day on the Quetzal whistleReliable fast travel

Requirements

  • Quests: Children of the Sun (unlocks Varlamore access)
  • Skills: 10 Agility, 15 Mining, 20 Woodcutting (all very low)
  • Combat: None required for Easy

Optimal Completion Route

  1. Start at the Quetzal pen in Civitas illa Fortis — this is your hub
  2. Task: Mine 3 limestone in the Varlamore limestone quarry (ideal time to train Mining if low level)
  3. Task: Cross the bridge to the West — use the Varlamore transport system to efficient route between tasks
  4. Task: Pickpocket a Civitas illa Fortis citizen (need 10 Thieving, safe in safe zone)
  5. Task: Kill a Varlamore-specific enemy (check task list in-game for exact requirements)
  6. Claim reward from Achievement Diary NPC in Civitas illa Fortis

Pro tip: Do this diary immediately after finishing Children of the Sun quest. You'll already be in Varlamore, and the low skill requirements mean you can finish it in one sitting.

⑧ 🥈 #2: Kandarin Medium (and Hard when ready)

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 3–5 hours | Score: 8.8/10

Why It's #2

The Kandarin diaries are among the most impactful in the game. The Medium reward gives you the Camelot Teleport spell (faster than regular teleport) and access to the Coal Bag (holds 27 coal for Smithing). The Hard reward gives you the Glory (Charged) permanently equipped effect and unlocks the Crystal tools benefits.

  • Quests: Camelot (unlocks teleport), King's Ransom (for Hard)

  • Combat: Kill enemies up to level 110 (for Hard tasks)

Optimal Route

  1. Base: Seers' Village bank — most tasks are within running distance
  2. Group A (North): Tasks around Camelot and Catherby — do these together
  3. Group B (South): Tasks around Ardougne — use Charter Ship or teleport
  4. Efficiency note: Combine this with Ardougne Diary tasks since they share locations

⑨ 🥉 #3: Ardougne Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 2–4 hours | Score: 8.5/10

Why It's #3

Ardougne is one of the most frequently visited cities in OSRS (bank, quests, PvM). The Medium diary gives you the Ardougne Cloak 2 which provides:

  • Daily teleport to Ardougne (useful forever)
  • Ecumenical key shard benefit — increases drop rate of keys in God Wars Dungeon
  • 10% chance to save a charge on Aquifer augments (Smithing benefit)
  • Ability to note items at the Ardougne monastery

Requirements

  • Quests: Plague City, Biohazard, Underground Pass
  • Skills: 50 Thieving, 46 Crafting, 42 Ranged
  • Combat: Kill a dark beast (level 182) — needs 60 Slayer

Optimal Route

  1. Start: Ardougne north bank — your primary hub
  2. Thieving tasks: Pickpocket Ardougne knights (great for training Thieving simultaneously)
  3. Ranging tasks: Shoot from the Ardougne tower — bring your own bow/arrows
  4. Combat tasks: Kill required enemies — can often be done during Slayer tasks
  5. Combine with: Kandarin diary since you'll be in the same region

⑩ #4: Falador Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 2–3 hours | Score: 8.2/10

Why It's #4

Falador is the hub for questing and early PvM. The Medium rewards include:

  • Falador Shield 2 — replaces Explorer's Ring for some tasks, provides free teleports
  • Access to the marketplace — noted items and special shops
  • Ability to note.
  • Increased chance at certain skilling rewards

Requirements

  • Quests: Recruitment Drive, The Lost Tribe
  • Skills: 42 Attack, 42 Strength, 42 Defense (for some tasks), 40 Prayer
  • Combat: Kill a moss giant (level 42) — easy at this stage

#5: Wilderness Easy Diary

Difficulty: Easy | Estimated Time: 30–60 minutes | Score: 8.0/10

Why It's #5

Despite the scary name, the Wilderness Easy diary is incredibly fast and gives you the ability to teleport from level 30 Wilderness (normally impossible below level 20). This is a huge safety net for wilderness activities.

Core Rewards

  • Wilderness sword 1 — provides various teleports including to the Abyss
  • Ability to craft lava runes at a reduced risk (one of the best Runecraft methods)

Safety Warning

⚠️ Wilderness Safety

While doing this diary, you'll be in level 1–20 Wilderness. PKers can attack you. Only bring what you can afford to lose. Skip this diary until you have 43 Prayer (Protect from Magic recommended for escape). Better yet, do it on a low-combat pure account to minimize risk.

Difficulty: Easy | Estimated Time: 20–40 minutes | Score: 7.9/10

Why It's #6

This is the fastest diary in the game. You can complete it in under 30 minutes with starting stats. The reward (Explorer's Ring 1) gives you daily rune essence and low-level teleport.

#7: Kourend & Kebos Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 4–6 hours | Score: 7.8/10

Why It's #7

Kourend is packed with high-value content (Slayer, Raids, farming patches). The Medium diary rewards include the Xeric's Talisman teleport upgrades which provide fast access to every major area in Kourend.

Core Rewards

  • 2 daily teleports to any of the above locations

#8: Desert Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 3–5 hours | Score: 7.6/10

Why It's #8

The Desert diary gives you the Desert Amulet 2 which provides:

  • Pharaoh's sceptre teleport charges — fast access to Pyramid Plunder (best Thieving training)
  • Antonis pass — free passage through the desert (saves buying passes)
  • Ability to note desert items at the Shantay Pass

Requirements

  • Skills: 50 Thieving, 49 Magic, 40 Ranged
  • Combat: Kill a Scorpia (level 147) for Hard — skip until later

#9: Morytania Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 3–5 hours | Score: 7.4/10

Why It's #9

Morytania is essential for Slayer and Barrows. The Medium diary gives you Morytania Legs 2 which provides:

  • Ability to teleport to the Morytania bank
  • More frequent drop ofrochemical nature something from certain enemies
  • Nechryael and abyssal demon update — improved Slayer efficiency

#10: Western Provinces Medium Diary

Difficulty: Medium | Estimated Time: 4–6 hours | Score: 7.2/10

Why It's #10

Western Provinces (also called "Seers' Village area") gives you access to tree running efficiency and faster banking in the western region. The Medium reward includes:

  • Western banner 2 — teleport to hunting area, note items
  • Access to additional teleport options
  • Improved drop rates for certain activities

4. Optimal Completion Route (Efficiency Path)

Now that you know which diaries to prioritize, here's the most efficient order to complete them — grouped by geography to minimize travel time.

Phase 1: Easy Diaries (Week 1)

📍 Route: Lumbridge → Draynor → Varrock → Falador

  1. Lumbridge/Draynor Easy (30 mins) — Start here because it's fastest
  2. Varrock Easy (45 mins) — Use Lumbridge teleport, walk to Varrock
  3. Falador Easy (30 mins) — Teleport to Falador, complete tasks
  4. Wilderness Easy (45 mins) — Only if you have 43 Prayer. Otherwise skip for now

Total time: ~2.5 hours | Rewards unlocked: Explorer's Ring 1, Varrock Armor 1, Falador Shield 1, Wilderness Sword 1

Phase 2: Medium Diaries — Southern Region (Week 2–3)

📍 Route: Ardougne → Kandarin → Falador

  1. Ardougne Medium (3 hours) — Base in Ardougne north bank
  2. Kandarin Medium (4 hours) — Tasks overlap with Ardougne area
  3. Falador Medium (2.5 hours) — Shorter than others, do while skills are fresh

Total time: ~9.5 hours | Rewards unlocked: Ardougne Cloak 2, Kandarin headgear 2, Falador Shield 2

Phase 3: Medium Diaries — Remote Regions (Week 4–5)

📍 Route: Kourend → Desert → Morytania → Western

  1. Kourend & Kebos Medium (5 hours) — Req: 50+ stats. Do this before Desert
  2. Desert Medium (4 hours) — Combine with Pyramid Plunder training
  3. Morytania Medium (4 hours) — Do during Slayer tasks in the region
  4. Western Provinces Medium (5 hours) — Do last since it's least impactful

Total time: ~18 hours | Rewards unlocked: Xeric's Talisman, Desert Amulet 2, Morytania Legs 2, Western Banner 2

Phase 4: Varlamore (Anytime after Children of the Sun)

Varlamore Easy (1 hour) — Do this as soon as you unlock Varlamore. It's short, fast, and gives immediate teleport benefits.

💡 Pro Tip: Combine With Other Activities

Don't "grind" diaries in isolation. Instead:

  • Thieving training? Do pickpocket tasks simultaneously
  • Slayer tasks? Kill the required enemies during your task
  • Farming runs? Complete farming-related tasks between patches
  • Quest grinding? Many diary tasks are completed automatically by quest requirements

This "passive completion" approach means you'll finish 50% of most diaries without ever specifically working on them.

7. Diary Priority FAQ

Can I complete diaries in any order, or do I need to do Easy before Medium?

You do NOT need to complete Easy before Medium. However, the requirements for Medium diaries assume you've done the quests that are also required for Easy diaries. In practice, you'll naturally complete Easy first because the skill requirements are lower. But if you already have 60 stats, you can skip straight to Medium.

What's the single most impactful diary reward in the entire game?

Opinions vary, but most players agree it's the Kandarin Hard Diary reward: the ability to have a permanently charged Glory (unlimited Edgeville teleports). This saves an enormous amount of time for any account that banks frequently. Runner-up: Desert Medium for Pharaoh's Sceptre teleport (Pyramid Plunder access).

I'm a low-combat pure (account with 1 Defense). Which diaries should I prioritize?

As a pure, your priorities are different:

  • Wilderness Easy — Very safe for pures (low combat level = less PK risk)
  • Varrock Easy — Unlocks access to low-level money making
  • Lumbridge/Draynor Easy — Explorer's Ring is useful at any combat level
  • Skip: Any diary requiring 40+ Defense or high Hitpoints

How long does it take to complete ALL diaries (Easy through Elite)?

For an account with already-high stats (90 Combat, 80 in most skills): ~120–150 hours of focused gameplay. For a mid-level account (60 Combat, 50 stats): ~200–250 hours (because you'll need to train skills). Don't rush it. Complete diaries passively while training other skills.

Do diary rewards stack? (e.g., if I have both Ardougne Cloak and Kandarin Headgear)

Yes and no. Teleport abilities stack (you can have multiple teleport items). XP boost effects usually don't stack (if two rewards give +2% XP in the same skill, only the highest applies). Unique effects always stack (e.g., coal bag from Kandarin + noting ability from Ardougne = both work).

I completed a diary but didn't get the reward. What's wrong?

You need to claim the reward from the specific NPC. Each region has a designated "Diary Reward NPC" (e.g., in Ardougne, it's the squire in the palace). Talk to them after completing all tasks in a tier. The game does not automatically give you the reward.
