1. 📅 What Happened (July 1–4, 2026)（事件经过：7月1-4日）

Between July 1 and July 4, 2026, the OSRS automation landscape changed overnight. In roughly 72 hours:

  • PowBot — paused operations and went offline（暂停运营并下线）
  • TRiBot — one of the oldest commercial clients, shut down（历史最久的商业客户端之一，宣告关停）
  • Storm — paused / discontinued（暂停 / 停止服务）
  • Inubot — paused / discontinued（暂停 / 停止服务）
  • DreamBot — the only major client still running as of July 14, 2026（唯一仍在运行的大型客户端）

The speed and coordination of the takedowns caught the botting community off guard. On Reddit and the official #gameupdate Discord, legit players mostly celebrated while botters scrambled for alternatives.

💡 Why This Matters to You（为什么这事与你有关）

Even if you've never touched a bot, this event changes the Grand Exchange, world populations, and the value of certain items. Read on — it affects every account.

3. 🔍 Why It Probably Happened（可能原因）

Jagex has not issued an official statement naming these clients. Based on community reports and historical patterns, the most-cited theories are:

① Legal Pressure (C&D Letters)（法律施压：停终函）

Jagex has a history of sending cease-and-desist letters to commercial bot developers. A coordinated wave in late June 2026 would explain the synchronized shutdowns.

② Payment Processors Pulled Support（支付渠道停止支持）

Several bot services rely on the same payment processors. If those processors restricted bot-related transactions under policy or pressure, the clients lost revenue and shut down.

③ Voluntary Exit（主动退出）

Some developers may have chosen to close shop rather than fight, especially after Jagex's prior legal wins in similar cases.

⚠️ Speculation vs. Facts（猜测归猜测）

None of the above is confirmed by Jagex. The fact is the clients are down. The why is community inference.

4. 👥 What It Means for Legit Players（对正常玩家意味着什么）

If you play legit, this is mostly good news — just watch for market swings:

① Cleaner Economy（更干净的经济）

With fewer bots, raw materials and bot-farmed drops will decrease. Prices on previously botted items (certain herbs, bones, raw materials) may rise.

② Grand Exchange Volatility（大交易所价格波动）

Expect short-term turbulence. Rare and bottleneck items may spike; previously bot-farmed goods may pump then dump. If you flip, watch the spreads.

③ Less Crowded Worlds（拥挤世界竞争减少）

Popular training worlds will be less packed. Means faster skilling and smoother boss rotations for everyone playing legit.

5. ⚠️ What It Means for Botters（对脚本玩家意味着什么）

For anyone running automation, the situation is straightforward:

  • Sunk costs: License fees and subscription time are gone.
  • Lost farms: Accounts tied to dead clients may be abandoned or banned.
  • No safe revival: "New mirrors" of dead clients are almost always malware or account-theft traps.
  • DreamBot risk: As the last one standing, it's a bigger target — don't assume it'll survive forever.

7. ❓ FAQ — Bot Client Shutdown 2026（常见问题）

Has Jagex officially confirmed the bot client shutdowns?（Jagex 官方确认了吗？）

No official statement names these clients. The shutdowns themselves are visible facts in the botting community, widely discussed on Reddit and the official Discord in early July.

Will prices stay high after the shutdowns?（关停后价格会一直高吗？）

Previously bot-farmed items may spike short-term. Within weeks, legit supply usually stabilizes the market. Don't treat any spike as permanent.

Is it safe to use DreamBot?（用 DreamBot 安全吗？）

Any third-party automation violates Jagex rules and risks permanent bans. We don't recommend it. This article mentions it only to report the factual landscape.

Can I get a refund for a bot client?（能拿回脚本客户端退款吗？）

Paused or shut-down clients typically don't refund. Beware anyone promising "chargeback assistance" — common scam pattern.
