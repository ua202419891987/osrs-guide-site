Ironman mode is OSRS's self-imposed challenge mode: no trading, no Grand Exchange, no picking up other players' drops. Everything you use, you earn yourself. This fundamental restriction changes the value of membership dramatically. For a normal account, membership is about unlocking content. For an Ironman, membership is about survival — because without member skills like Herblore, Farming, and Slayer, your account hits a wall it cannot pass. This guide explains exactly why Ironmen need membership more than regular accounts, which skills to prioritize, and how to keep your Ironman bonded without access to the GE. For the broader membership picture, start with our complete F2P-to-P2P transition guide.

① 🛡️ What Is Ironman Mode? Quick Overview

Ironman mode is an account type that restricts interaction with other players to force self-sufficiency. Created in 2014 at the community's request, it has become one of the most popular ways to play OSRS — over 15% of active accounts are Ironman variants.

1.1 🔒 Core Restrictions

All Ironman accounts share these limitations:

  • No trading — Cannot trade items or GP with other players (except via the Shield of Arrav/Heroes Quest partners, and certain NPC exchanges).
  • No Grand Exchange — Cannot buy or sell items on the GE. Every item must be obtained through PvM, skilling, or shops.
  • No picking up other players' drops — If another player kills a monster, you cannot loot the drop. You only see drops from your own kills.
  • No player-owned house portals from others — You can't use another player's POH facilities (altars, jewelry boxes) unless they're on a house party world and have them open.
  • Restricted shops — Some shops have reduced stock or higher prices for Ironmen to prevent shop-running as a replacement for the GE.

1.2 🎭 The Three Ironman Variants

TypeAbbreviationKey Mechanic
IronmanIMStandard restrictions above. Death is normal — you keep 3 items and lose the rest ( retrievable via gravestone).
Hardcore IronmanHCIMOne life. If you die, your HCIM status is lost and the account becomes a standard Ironman. Your HCIM lives are banked at certain total levels.
Ultimate IronmanUIMNo bank access at all. You cannot use the bank — everything you own must be carried, stored in POH, or in inventory. The purest form of self-sufficiency.
💡 Which Should You Play?

If you're new to Ironman, start with a standard Ironman. HCIM adds permadeath pressure that can be stressful for learning. UIM is a puzzle-box challenge for experienced players who already know the game inside out. Standard IM lets you learn Ironman mechanics without catastrophic penalties.

② 🚪 Can You Play Ironman Without Membership?

Technically, yes. Practically, it's a miserable experience that 95% of players abandon within a week. Here's why.

2.1 🚧 The F2P Ironman Wall

A F2P Ironman can train the 15 free skills, complete the 20 F2P quests, and kill F2P monsters. But they hit a hard progression wall almost immediately:

  • No Herblore — Can't make potions. No stamina potions, no prayer potions, no super combats. You fight everything with base stats only.
  • No Slayer — Can't train Slayer, which means no access to Slayer-locked monsters, no Slayer helms, no point-based unlocks. This locks off huge mid-game content.
  • No Agility — Can't restore run energy faster, can't access shortcuts, can't get graceful outfit. Every run across the map is slow.
  • No Farming — Can't grow herbs, so your potion supply is entirely dependent on monster drops (which are slow and unreliable in F2P).
  • No Crafting above glass/jewelry basics — Can't make dragonhide armour, can't make amulets of glory, can't craft battlestaves.
  • No Fletching — Can't make arrows, bolts, or darts. Ranged training is brutally slow without fletched ammo.

2.2 📊 What a F2P Ironman Can Actually Do

A F2P Ironman's realistic endgame is: 99 in all F2P combat stats, 99 in F2P gathering skills (Woodcutting, Fishing, Mining), completion of all F2P quests, and killing the F2P bosses (Obor and Bryophyta). That's maybe 200 hours of content — compared to the thousands of hours member Ironman content offers. Most F2P Ironmen either buy membership or quit.

🚪 The F2P Ironman Verdict

You can create an Ironman account without membership, but you'll experience maybe 5% of what Ironman mode offers. It's a demo, not the full game. If you're serious about Ironman, membership is effectively mandatory — either pay for it, or farm a bond (see section ⑤). For the standard membership comparison, read our F2P vs P2P comparison.

③ 💪 Why Ironmen Need Membership More Than Regular Accounts

Here's the counterintuitive truth: membership matters more for an Ironman than for a normal account. A normal account without membership can still buy everything on the GE and experience a decent chunk of content. An Ironman without membership can't buy anything — and the items they need are locked behind member skills.

3.1 🧪 The Potion Problem

On a normal account, if you need prayer potions, you buy them. On an Ironman, you must make them — which requires Herblore (member skill), ranarr herbs (from Farming, a member skill, or Slayer drops), and Snape grass (from Hobgoblins or Farming). Without membership, your Ironman has zero access to potions. This means:

  • No prayer potions → can't do bosses that drain prayer
  • No super combat potions → 15–20% weaker melee than a normal account
  • No stamina potions → constant run-energy management
  • No anti-venom → can't do Zulrah or other venom-heavy content
  • No saradomin brews → can't tank high-hit bosses

Potions are the lifeblood of mid- and end-game PvM. Without them, your Ironman is locked out of Zulrah, Vorkath, God Wars Dungeon, Chambers of Xeric, and Theatre of Blood. Membership isn't optional — it's the gatekeeper.

3.2 🌾 The Gear Problem

On a normal account, you buy gear. On an Ironman, you craft, smith, or earn it. Critical mid-game gear is member-locked:

3.3 🗺️ The Content Problem

Beyond gear and potions, membership unlocks the actual gameplay that makes Ironman mode rewarding. The 8 member skills, 100+ member quests, all bosses, all minigames, the entire Zeah continent, Prifddinas, Morytania, the Wilderness bosses — all of it is member-only. A F2P Ironman experiences a tiny fraction of what makes the mode fun. The journey from level 1 to maxed on a member Ironman is a 2,000+ hour adventure. On a F2P Ironman, it's a 200-hour dead end.

④ ⭐ Top 5 Member Skills Every Ironman Needs

If you're starting a member Ironman, these are the skills to prioritize. They're not optional — they're the infrastructure your entire account runs on.

4.1 🧪 #1: Herblore — The Potion Lifeline

Herblore is the #1 priority for every Ironman. Without it, you have no potions, and without potions, you can't do mid-game PvM. Target milestones:

  • Level 38: Prayer potions — required for almost all bosses
  • Level 50: Super energy/stamina — needed for run-heavy content
  • Level 69–72: Super combats / Ranging potions — massive damage boost
  • Level 81: Saradomin brews — tanking potions for raids
  • Level 87: Super combat potions — the goal for most of mid-game
  • Level 90+: Overloads (CoX) — endgame raiding

🌱 How Ironmen Train Herblore

Ironmen get herbs from three sources: Farming runs (the primary source — 8 herb patches every 80 minutes), Slayer drops (monsters drop herbs as uncommon drops), and boss drops (Zulrah, Cerberus, and others drop noted herbs). Always clean your own herbs (grants XP) and make potions in bulk. Save every herb — even guam and marrentill are useful for low-level potions and stamina ingredients.

4.2 🌾 #2: Farming — The Self-Sufficient Engine

Farming is how Ironmen sustainably produce herbs, food, and materials. It's the skill that makes the difference between "constantly out of potions" and "comfortable supply." Key targets:

  • Level 32: Ranarr herbs — for prayer potions. Plant these in every patch.
  • Level 38: Toadflax — for agility potions and saradomin brews later.
  • Level 62: Snapdragon — for super restores (better than prayer potions).
  • Level 85: Spirit trees — passive GP and a teleport network.

Do herb runs every 80 minutes alongside birdhouse runs (Hunter). With 8 patches (including the disease-free Hosidius and Troll Stronghold patches), a single run at 70+ Farming with magic secateurs yields 200K–400K GP equivalent in herbs. This is how Ironmen never run out of potions.

4.3 🏠 #3: Construction — Quality of Life King

Construction is the skill that makes an Ironman's life tolerable. A well-built POH replaces half the teleport network and gives you an altar for prayer training. Priority builds:

💡 Construction Materials for Ironmen

Ironmen can't buy planks on the GE. You must chop logs and mill them into planks at the Sawmill (Varrock) or with the Plank Make spell (Lunar spellbook, 86 Magic). For early Construction, use regular logs and oak logs. For high-level builds, you'll need thousands of mahogany logs — get them from the Woodcutting Guild on Zeah or from Miscellania (Managing Miscellania kingdom management).

4.4 🧹 #4: Slayer — The Content Unlock

Slayer is the skill that gates most Ironman PvM content. It unlocks:

  • Gargoyles (75 Slayer): Consistent GP and granite maul drops
  • Cerberus (91 Slayer): Primordial/eternal crystals — BiS boots
  • Hydras (95 Slayer): Alchemical hydra — top-tier money and uniques
  • Kraken (87 Slayer): Kraken tentacle for toxic staff
  • Slayer helmet (i): BiS head slot for most combat, requires 55 Crafting + Enchanted gem

Train Slayer from the start. Every combat hour should be a Slayer task. This ensures that when you hit 85+ combat, you also have 50+ Slayer — opening up the money-makers that fund your bonds and gear.

4.5 🏃 #5: Agility — The Quality-of-Life Skill

Agility is tedious but essential. It grants run energy restoration (at higher levels, you effectively never run out) and unlocks shortcuts that save enormous time. The Graceful outfit (purchased with Marks of Grace from rooftop courses) reduces weight by 25kg — critical for activities where you carry supplies. Target level 60 (Varrock rooftop for full Graceful) and eventually 90+ for the Ardougne cloak 4 and advanced shortcuts.

⑤ 💰 How to Afford Bonds as an Ironman

Ironmen can't use the Grand Exchange — so how do they buy bonds? This is one of the most common questions from new Ironman players. The answer has a few layers.

5.1 🎟️ The Bond Purchase Problem

Ironmen cannot buy bonds from the Grand Exchange. They also cannot trade with other players. So the standard "farm GP and buy a bond on the GE" strategy does not work. Ironmen have three options:

  • Option A: Buy bonds with real money from Jagex. This is the simplest. An Ironman buys a bond from the Jagex store with cash, redeems it on their Ironman account. No GP needed.
  • Option B: Farm GP on a normal account, buy a bond, and transfer it. Ironmen can receive bonds as gifts. If you have a normal (main) account, farm 5.2M GP there, buy a bond on the GE, and trade the bond directly to your Ironman. Bonds are one of the few items that can be traded to Ironmen.
  • Option C: Use the "Bond Gifting" system. A friend or clanmate can gift a bond to your Ironman. This is allowed by Jagex and is a common way for established players to help newer Ironmen.
⚠️ Don't Break Ironman Rules with Bonds

Bonds are tradeable to Ironmen because Jagex explicitly allows it — bonds are an out-of-game item. However, you cannot use a bond transfer as a way to funnel GP or items to your Ironman. Only the bond itself can be traded. Any attempt to circumvent Ironman trading restrictions (e.g., "drop trading" items, using a middleman to transfer gear) will result in your Ironman status being revoked and potential account bans. Play it clean.

5.3 💡 The Main-Account Strategy

Most serious Ironmen also have a normal account (a "main"). The main farms GP through standard money-makers (Zulrah, Vorkath, raids) and buys bonds to gift to the Ironman. This is the most efficient setup because:

  • Your main can use the GE and access all money-makers at full efficiency
  • Your Ironman never needs to worry about bond farming — they just play Ironman content
  • Both accounts progress simultaneously

If you don't want a main, the simplest path is to pay for your Ironman's membership with real money. At $11/month (Premier Club), it's a small cost for thousands of hours of gameplay. For the full bond farming breakdown for normal accounts, see our bond farming guide.

⑥ 💀 HCIM & UIM: Special Considerations

Hardcore Ironman and Ultimate Ironman add layers of complexity to the membership question. Here's what each variant needs to consider.

6.1 💀 Hardcore Ironman (HCIM) — Death Is Permanent

HCIM accounts have one life. If you die an unsafe death, your HCIM status is permanently lost and the account downgrades to a standard Ironman. This changes the membership calculus:

  • Membership is even more critical — Without member skills (Herblore for potions, Agility for run energy, Prayer via gilded altar), HCIM deaths are far more likely. A HCIM without super combat potions or prayer potions is a HCIM that will die.
  • Banked lives at total level milestones — HCIM earn extra lives at 600, 1200, 1800, and 1920 total level. Reaching these milestones safely requires member skills to train efficiently.
  • Avoid the Wilderness — Unless you're confident. PKers are the #1 cause of HCIM death. Green dragons, Chaos altar, and Wilderness bosses are death traps for new HCIM. Wait until you're comfortable before risking your status.
  • Always carry a teleport — A one-click teleport (Ectophial, Amulet of glory) is mandatory at all times. Many HCIM deaths come from being caught without an escape.

🛡️ HCIM Safety Protocol

Before any boss or dangerous activity: (1) Check the wiki for the boss's max hit — ensure your HP + food can survive it. (2) Bring 2 teleport options in case one fails. (3) Turn on "Protect Item" prayer if risking gear. (4) Never do content you don't understand — watch a guide first. A HCIM death is permanent. Treat every dangerous activity with respect.

6.2 🎒 Ultimate Ironman (UIM) — No Bank, No Problem?

UIM cannot use the bank. Everything they own must be carried, stored in their POH (with a Costume Room and Storage), or dropped at a safe location (a "drop party" for later retrieval). This creates unique challenges that membership-specifically addresses:

  • POH is essential — A UIM's POH is their bank. Construction is arguably more important for UIM than any other Ironman variant. Every storage unit (armour case, magic wardrobe, toy box, bookcase) holds items that would otherwise clutter your inventory.
  • Fossil Island note vault — Member content that gives UIM a bank-like storage for fossils and certain items.
  • Looting bag — A member item (drop from Wilderness monsters) that lets UIM carry 28 extra items — but items inside are lost on death. Critical for PvM trips.
  • Seed box and herb sack — Member items that let UIM carry more herbs and seeds without occupying inventory slots.
  • No bank means no bond buffer — UIM can't store GP in a bank. If they're farming bond value, they must do it on a main account and gift the bond.
💡 UIM Without Membership Is Unplayable

A F2P UIM is essentially unplayable. Without a POH for storage, without member items like the looting bag, and without member skills to sustain yourself, a F2P UIM can barely function. If you're considering UIM, membership is not optional — it's the foundation the entire mode is built on. Start as a member from day one.

For a normal account, you can argue about whether membership is "worth it" — F2P has decent content. For an Ironman, the ROI is off the charts. You're paying $11/month for access to 90% of the game's content, all of the self-sufficient mechanics (Herblore, Farming, Slayer, Construction), and the entire progression curve from level 1 to maxed. Without membership, Ironman mode is a 200-hour demo. With membership, it's a 2,000-hour adventure.

💰 The Bond Math for Ironmen

If you have a normal account that farms 5.2M GP per 14 days (see our bond farming guide), your Ironman's membership is effectively free. The main does the grinding; the Ironman receives the bond as a gift. This is the most common setup for serious Ironman players and makes the cost-benefit analysis trivially positive — you're paying zero dollars for thousands of hours of content.

7.3 🎯 When to Start Membership on an Ironman

Start membership immediately when you create the Ironman. Do not try to "prepare" in F2P first — it's a waste of time. The F2P Ironman experience is so limited that there's no meaningful prep you can do. Create the account, become a member, and start training with full access to all skills and content from minute one. The only exception is if you want to do the F2P quests first for quest points — but even then, you can do those quests as a member.

⑧ ❓ FAQs — Ironman Membership

Q: Can I create an Ironman account without membership?

A: Yes. You can create and play an Ironman in F2P. But the experience is extremely limited — no Herblore, no Slayer, no member skills, only 2 bosses. Most F2P Ironmen either buy membership or quit within a week. If you're serious about Ironman mode, start with membership from day one.

Q: Can Ironmen buy bonds from the Grand Exchange?

A: No. Ironmen cannot use the Grand Exchange at all. To get bonds, Ironmen must either (1) buy them with real money from Jagex and redeem them, (2) receive them as a gift from another player (bonds are one of the few items tradeable to Ironmen), or (3) farm GP on a separate normal account, buy a bond there, and trade it to the Ironman.

Q: What's the first member skill I should train on an Ironman?

A: Herblore and Farming together. Start doing herb runs as soon as you have the Farming level for ranarrs (level 32). Clean and use every herb you get from Slayer and bossing. Without potions, you're locked out of mid-game PvM. Second priority is Slayer — train it alongside combat so you unlock money-makers and gear-locked monsters. See section ④ for the full priority list.

Q: Can a HCIM die in F2P?

A: Yes — HCIM can die anywhere, including F2P. An unsafe death in F2P (e.g., killed by a hill giant while low on food) will downgrade your HCIM to a standard Ironman permanently. This is another reason to start membership immediately: member skills and potions make survival much easier, and the content is more rewarding for the risk you take.

Q: Can UIM use the bank at all?

A: No. UIM have zero bank access — not even one slot. All storage must be done through the POH (Costume Room, armour case, etc.), the Fossil Island note vault, the looting bag ( Wilderness-only storage that's lost on death), and item-specific containers like the seed box and herb sack. This is what makes UIM the hardest mode in OSRS — and why membership (with its POH and storage items) is absolutely mandatory.

Q: Is it worth paying real money for Ironman membership, or should I grind bonds on a main?

A: If you have the time to maintain a normal account that farms 5.2M GP per 14 days, do that — your Ironman membership is then free. If you'd rather focus purely on the Ironman, paying $11/month (Premier Club) is a small cost for the content you get. Most Ironman players use the main-account strategy, but there's no wrong answer — it depends on whether you enjoy the bond farming grind on your main.

🎯 Final Tips for Ironman Membership

Ironman mode is the most rewarding way to play OSRS — but only with membership. Without it, you're playing a demo. With it, you're embarking on a 2,000-hour journey of genuine self-sufficiency where every potion, every piece of gear, and every level feels earned. The five skills to prioritize are Herblore, Farming, Construction, Slayer, and Agility — these form the infrastructure that makes everything else possible.

If you're playing HCIM, treat every dangerous activity with respect — one death ends your Hardcore status permanently. If you're playing UIM, invest in your POH early; it's your only storage. And regardless of variant, don't try to farm bonds on the Ironman itself — use a main account or pay with real money. Your Ironman's time is better spent progressing, not grinding GP it can't even spend.
