2. Getting Your First 10K GP (15 Minutes)

Before you can do anything with the Grand Exchange, you need starter capital. Here's exactly how to get your first 10,000 GP in under 15 minutes with zero requirements. Once you have starter cash, the world opens up — including better methods covered in the First 5M GP Members Guide 2026.

📍 Stronghold of Security — 10,000 GP Free

Located beneath Barbarian Village (north of Falador, west of Edgeville). Enter the ladder at the center of the village. Complete four levels of simple maze/combat obstacles — each gives a reward totaling 10,000 GP. Also gives Fancy Boots and an emote unlock. Takes 10–15 minutes, absolutely no combat or skills required.

💡 Starter Path

Create account → Run to Barbarian Village (2 min walk from Lumbridge) → Complete Stronghold of Security (15 min) → 10K GP → Use this to start shop arbitrage or buy basic gear.

📍 Alternative: Pickpocket Men in Lumbridge

Men in Lumbridge (near the castle) can be pickpocketed at level 1 Thieving. Each success gives 1–6 GP. Slow (~2K GP/h) but requires zero effort and zero travel. Not recommended as a primary method, but useful if you're already in Lumbridge.

3. Collection Methods — Gather Items from the World

These methods involve collecting items from the game world and selling them on the Grand Exchange. They require zero skills — just time and inventory space.

🐄 Cowhide Collecting (Lumbridge) — 40K–60K GP/hr

The classic starting money maker. Cows east of Lumbridge Castle drop Cowhides worth 150–200 GP each on the GE, plus Bones (80–120 GP) and Raw Beef. Walk to the cow field, kill cows (level 2, harmless), collect hides, and bank at Lumbridge Castle basement. Sell at the Grand Exchange.

💡 Efficiency Tip

Many players in the cow field kill cows and don't pick up the hides (limited inventory space while training combat). Walk around the field picking up ground hides — you can fill an inventory in 2–3 minutes without fighting. This is faster than killing cows yourself.

🍌 Banana Picking (Karamja) — 20K–30K GP/hr

The most peaceful zero-requirement method. Go to Port Sarim, buy a one-way ticket to Karamja for 30 GP. Walk south to Musa Point banana plantation. Pick bananas from the trees (they respawn quickly). Sell on the Grand Exchange for ~100–150 GP each. Zero risk, fully AFK.

Use this method only if you want zero-risk AFK income or are watching Netflix. It's the slowest zero-req method but completely peaceful.

🌾 Flax Picking (Seers' Village) — 25K–40K GP/hr

Pick flax from the flax field south of Seers' Village bank. Each flax sells for ~50–80 GP on the GE. With 500+ flax picked per hour, that's 25K–40K GP/hr. Requires no skills — just run between the field and the Seers' bank. This is a members-only method but requires no membership-specific skills to access the area.

4. Pickup Methods — Loot What Others Leave Behind

These methods involve picking up items that other players drop or leave on the ground. They require zero skills and often earn more than collection methods.

⚔️ Wilderness Ditch Looting (Edgeville) — 50K–200K+ GP/hr

Stand at the Wilderness Ditch just north of Edgeville (level 1 Wilderness). PvP players fight in the Wilderness and drop items when they die. You collect what falls out. During peak hours (weekend evenings US/UK time), this earns 100K–200K+ GP/hr. Normal days: 50K–100K GP/hr.

⚠️ Safety Rules for Ditch Looting
  • DO NOT cross the ditch into deeper Wilderness — stay on Edgeville side
  • DO NOT bring anything valuable — you can be attacked
  • DO watch the minimap — run south toward ditch if you see a white dot approaching
  • DO bank frequently at Edgeville bank just south of the ditch

🦴 Wilderness Bone Yard — 80K–120K GP/hr

The Bone Yard is in level 20–25 Wilderness (north of the Graveyard of Shadows). Bones are scattered on the ground — pick them up and sell on the GE. Big Bones (~300–400 GP each) are the main prize. The "empty inventory" method: bring nothing except clothes, run from Edgeville, pick up bones until full, teleport out. If a PKer kills you, you lose nothing. For more Wilderness money-making methods, see the Wilderness Money Making Guide 2026.

💀 Graveyard of Shadows (Level 15 Wilderness)

Between Edgeville and the Bone Yard, the Graveyard of Shadows has scattered bones and occasionally valuable items from PKer deaths. It's at level 15 Wilderness — safer than the Bone Yard but with less density of bones. Good if you want medium risk with medium reward.

5. Exchange Methods — Buy Low, Sell High (Arbitrage)

Once you have 10K–50K GP starter capital, exchange methods become your most scalable zero-requirement income source. These require market knowledge but no skills. For a complete guide to GE trading, see the Grand Exchange Flipping Guide 2026.

🔄 Shop-to-GE Arbitrage — 30K–100K+ GP/hr

Buy items from NPC shops at fixed low prices, then sell them on the Grand Exchange for profit. Shop stocks respawn in 1–2 minutes, so this is repeatable indefinitely. With 10K GP, you can do 10–15 trips before needing to sell.

📈 Grand Exchange Flipping — 10K–50K+ GP/hr (scales with capital)

Once you have 50K–100K GP in capital, start flipping items on the GE. No skills needed — just market knowledge. Buy at 1–5% below market, sell at 1–5% above. Start with high-volume items: runes (Fire, Air, Nature), feathers, and prayer potions.

6. Zero-to-Bond Complete Roadmap

A bond costs ~12.8M GP (June 2026). Here's the fastest path from zero to your first membership bond using only zero-requirement methods. For a broader bond strategy after membership, check the Bond Farming Strategy Guide 2026.

Step 1: 0 → 10K GP (15 minutes)
Complete Stronghold of Security in Barbarian Village. Your starter capital.
Step 2: 10K → 500K GP (8–12 hours)
Cowhide collecting in Lumbridge. At 50K GP/h, this takes ~10 hours. Bank hides and sell in bulk on GE.
Step 3: 500K → 5M GP (40–60 hours)
Option A (Safer): Cowhides + Shop Arbitrage combined for ~70K GP/h.
Option B (Faster): Wilderness Bone Yard at 100K GP/h — cuts time in half but requires Wilderness awareness.
Step 4: 5M → 12.8M GP (Bond Purchase)
At this point you have enough capital for effective GE flipping. Combine flipping with your best active method. With 5M GP capital, you can make 100K–300K GP/h flipping mid-volume items.

⏱️ Total Time Estimate

Fastest path (Bone Yard + flipping): ~60–80 hours
Safest path (Cowhide + arbitrage): ~100–130 hours
Recommended hybrid: Cowhide to 500K → Shop arbitrage + flipping → ~80–100 hours.

Remember: if you can afford $7.99, buy membership directly. One month of P2P earning easily covers 4+ future bonds.

7. Zero Requirement Money Making FAQs

Can I really make enough for a bond with zero stats?

Yes, but it takes time. A bond costs ~12.8M GP. At the fastest zero-req method (Wilderness Bone Yard, ~100K GP/h), that's ~128 hours. Most players find it more efficient to buy one month of membership with real money ($7.99) and then sustain bonds through P2P money makers (which earn 500K–3M GP/h easily).

What's the fastest zero-req method?

Wilderness Ditch Looting during peak hours can reach 200K+ GP/h, but it's inconsistent. The most consistent high-GP method is the Wilderness Bone Yard at ~80K–120K GP/h. For consistency without Wilderness risk, Cowhide collecting (~50K GP/h) is the most reliable starter method.

Do I need to worry about bots affecting prices?

Bots affect cowhide prices and some other markets, but the methods in this guide remain profitable. Cowhides fluctuate between 150–200 GP — even at 150 GP, the method is worth doing. Shop arbitrage is immune to bots because shops have fixed prices and limited stock that bots cannot deplete.

Can I do these methods as an Ironman?

Mostly no. Ironmen cannot trade on the Grand Exchange, so cowhide selling, shop arbitrage, and GE flipping don't work. However, Stronghold of Security works (gives 10K GP that ironmen can use for shop purchases), and the Bone Yard can be used for Prayer training. For ironman-specific money, see Ironman Beginner Guide 2026.

What should I spend my first GP on?

Priority order: (1) A Fire Staff (~1K GP) for free fire runes. (2) Amulet of Power (~2K GP) for combat. (3) Starting GE flipping capital (keep 50K GP+ liquid). (4) Bond for membership (12.8M GP) — membership unlocks 10x better money makers.

Are there one-time money makers I missed?

Stronghold of Security (10K GP) is the only notable one-time reward with zero requirements. Other one-time rewards (Prince Ali Rescue's ~10K GP, Vampyre Slayer's ~4.5K GP) require quest completions. All methods in this guide are repeatable indefinitely — you can do them as much as you want.

How much GP can I make flipping with zero stats?

With 50K GP capital: ~500–2,000 GP per flip cycle (5–15 minutes per item type). With 500K GP: ~5K–25K GP per cycle. With 5M GP: ~50K–250K GP per cycle. Flipping scales linearly with your available capital — more GP = more profit per cycle.

What's the best method for a brand new F2P account?

Three-phase approach: (1) Stronghold of Security (10K GP, 15 min). (2) Cowhide collecting until 100K GP (~2 hours). (3) Switch to shop arbitrage (Eye of Newt at Port Sarim, Ball of Wool at general stores). This three-phase approach is the most efficient start for any new account.

Is Wilderness looting safe for a brand new account?

Ditch looting (level 1 Wilderness) is relatively safe if you stay on the Edgeville side and watch the minimap. The Bone Yard (level 20–25 Wilderness) is riskier. Always use the "empty inventory" method — bring nothing you can't afford to lose. With practice, you'll rarely die.

What if the GE prices change significantly?

GE prices fluctuate with supply and demand. Always check live prices at prices.runescape.wiki before selling. The methods in this guide have remained profitable for years — prices may shift but the fundamental arbitrage opportunities persist. Shop arbitrage is particularly stable because NPC shop prices are fixed.

8. Final Tips — Scale Your Income

  • Always do Stronghold of Security first. 10K GP in 15 minutes is the fastest GP/hr you'll ever make. Do it before anything else.
  • Graduate from zero-req methods. These are starter methods, not endgame. Once you have 500K+ GP, invest in stats and quests to unlock P2P money makers earning 500K+ GP/hr.
  • Diversify your income. Don't rely on one method. Have one active method (cowhide/Bone Yard) and one passive method (GE flipping/arbitrage) running simultaneously.
  • Use efficient routes. The difference between 40K and 60K GP/hr in cowhide collecting is often route optimization. Plan your bank → field → GE loop to minimize walking.
  • Watch the clock on Wilderness methods. Weekend evenings (US/UK timezones) are peak PvP hours — this is when ditch looting earns 200K+ GP/hr. Off-peak hours are safer for Bone Yard runs.
  • Track your GP/hr. Use RuneLite's XP Tracker plugin to time your money making sessions. If a method drops below your target GP/hr, switch immediately.
  • The bond is the goal, not the grind. Once you have membership, your earning potential jumps 10x. Every zero-req hour is an investment in unlocking that potential.
