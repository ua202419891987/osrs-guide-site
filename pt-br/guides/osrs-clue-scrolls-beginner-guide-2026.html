🗺️ 1. What Are Clue Scrolls?

A Clue Scroll is a random drop from almost any monster in OSRS. When you get one, you "read" it to start a Treasure Trail — a sequence of 1–5 steps (depending on clue difficulty) that ends with a reward casket.

The reward casket contains random loot — ranging from worthless items (100 GP) to extremely rare valuables (hundreds of millions GP). This makes Clue Scrolls exciting — every casket is a gamble.

🎁 Why Clue Scrolls Are Worth Doing

  • Fun: Solving riddles and emote clues feels like a mini-game
  • Profit potential: One casket could pay for your membership
  • Unique cosmetics: Many Clue rewards are untradeable cosmetic overrides you can't get anywhere else
  • Low requirement: Beginner clues require almost no stats
  • Passive income: You get clues while doing Slayer, bossing, or training

💡 Start with Beginner & Easy Clues

Beginner and Easy clues are perfect for new players. They don't require high stats, the steps are simple (talk to an NPC, kill a low-level monster, do an emote in a location), and the rewards are still decent. Save Medium+ clues until you have 70+ Combat.

② You Can Only Have One Clue at a Time (Per Tier)

OSRS has a limit: you can only hold ONE clue scroll per difficulty tier at a time. If you get a second Beginner clue while you already have one, it won't drop. This prevents inventory clutter but also means you should complete your clues promptly to keep getting more drops.

How to Complete a Clue Scroll — The 3 Step Types

Every clue step is one of three types:

  • ① Talk to NPC: The clue says "Talk to [NPC name] in [location]." Go there, right-click the NPC → "Talk-to." They'll give you the next step or the final casket.
  • ② Kill a monster: The clue says "Kill [monster name] in [location]." Go there, kill the monster. The next step drops automatically.
  • ③ Do an emote: The clue says "Equip [item], go to [location], do [emote]." This is the tricky one — see Section 5 for all emote answers.

② Beginner Clue: Kill Steps

Beginner clues ask you to kill very low-level monsters:

  • Goblin (anywhere) — 2–11 Combat
  • Cow (Lumbridge) — 2 Combat
  • Chicken (Lumbridge) — 1 Combat
  • Man/Woman (Lumbridge, Varrock) — 2 Combat
  • Al-Kharid Warrior — 9–20 Combat

These are trivial at any combat level. Just go to the location and kill the specified number of monsters (usually 1–5). The next clue step drops automatically.

③ Beginner Clue: Emote Steps (Simplified)

Beginner emote clues are very easy. Example:

Example Beginner Emote Clue

Clue text: "Equip a iron dagger, go to the Lumbridge cows field, do the 'wave' emote."

Solution:

  1. Equip an iron dagger (wield it, not in inventory)
  2. Go to the Lumbridge cow field (west of Lumbridge castle)
  3. Open the Emotes tab (smiley face icon)
  4. Click "Wave" emote
  5. A Reward casket (Beginner) appears! Pick it up.

💡 How to Read Emote Clue Coordinates

Some emote clues give coordinates (e.g., "05° 25' South, 12° 45' East"). These correspond to the World Map coordinates. Open your map (hotkey: `) and look at the bottom-right corner — it shows your current coordinates. Move your character to the exact coordinates listed, then do the emote with the required items equipped.

🕺 5. Emote Clues — Every Location & Answer

Emote clues are the most confusing part for beginners. Here's the exact process:

Step 1: Read the Clue Carefully

The clue text tells you exactly what to do. Example: "Equip a maple longbow and a studded body, go to the building north of the forge in Falador, and do a bow."

Step 2: Equip the Required Items

You must wield or wear the items (not just have them in inventory). Right-click the item → "Wield" (weapons) or "Wear" (armor). The clue won't complete if items are in your inventory.

Step 3: Go to the Exact Location

Use the map to navigate. Some clues say "near [landmark]" — get as close as possible to that landmark. If the clue gives coordinates, use the World Map coordinates display.

Step 4: Do the Emote

Open the Emotes tab (smiley face icon in the game interface). Click the required emote. Important: You must be standing in the right spot AND have the right items equipped WHEN you do the emote.

Step 5: Pick Up the Casket

After doing the emote correctly, a Reward casket appears on the ground. Pick it up (it goes into your inventory). Right-click it → "Open" to see your rewards!

🎯 Pro Tip: Use the "Clue Scroll Solver" Website

If you're stuck on an emote clue, go to oldschool.runescape.wiki and search "Clue scroll emote locations." The Wiki has a complete list of every emote clue, exact coordinates, and required items. You can also use the "Clue Scroll Helper" plugin in RuneLite (recommended).

💎 The "Jackpot" Rewards (Rare Drops)

All Clue tiers have a chance to drop extremely valuable items:

  • 3rd Age equipment: Cosmetic armor sets worth 100M–2B GP (yes, billions)
  • Gilded armor: Cosmetic, worth ~10M–50M GP
  • Ornamental quivers: Cosmetic, worth ~5M–20M GP
  • Ring of nature: Useful teleport item, worth ~500K GP

The odds of getting a 3rd Age drop are about 1 in 10,000–100,000. Most players never see one. But the possibility keeps Clue Scrolls exciting!

⚡ 7. 5 Clue Scroll Tips for Beginners

① Drop Unwanted Clues (If They're Too Hard)

If you get a Medium+ clue and you're a beginner, you can drop it (right-click → "Drop"). This frees up your clue slot so you can get an easier clue. Warning: Dropped clues disappear forever — only drop if you're sure you can't complete it.

② Use the RuneLite Clue Scroll Plugin

If you play on RuneLite (the official OSRS launcher), enable the "Clue Scroll" plugin. It shows exactly where to go for emote clues on your map, tells you which items to equip, and even auto-steps through NPCdialog. This makes Clue Scrolls 10x easier.

③ Bank Your Casket if You're in Danger

If you're in the Wilderness (anywhere with a dark red map background) and you get a Clue Scroll drop, finish it quickly and bank the casket. If you die in the Wilderness with a casket in inventory, you lose it. Never risk a high-value casket in the Wilderness.

④ Do Clues While Doing Other Content

Clue Scrolls are passive income — you get them while training Slayer, bossing, or killing monsters for other reasons. Don't specifically "grind" clues (the drop rate is too low). Just always have an open clue slot and complete them as you get them.

⑤ Save High-Tier Clues for Later

If you get a Hard/Elite/Master clue but you're still a beginner, save it in your bank. These higher-tier clues give much better rewards, but they're also much harder to complete. Wait until you have 70+ Combat and better gear before attempting them.

❓ 8. FAQ

Can I get Clue Scrolls in F2P?

Yes! F2P players can get Beginner and Easy clues. However, some clue steps require membership (e.g., an NPC in a members-only area). If you're F2P and get a clue that requires membership, you'll have to drop it or upgrade to membership to complete it.

Do I need to do anything special to "activate" Clue Scrolls?

No. Just kill monsters. Clue Scrolls drop randomly. There's no way to increase the drop rate (except "Ring of wealth" for Medium+ clues, which gives a tiny boost).

What should I do if I can't find the NPC?

Use the World Map (hotkey: `) and search for the NPC name. The map shows NPC locations as blue dots. Alternatively, search the OSRS Wiki for the NPC name — the page will show the exact location with a map screenshot.

Can I get multiple caskets from one clue?

No. Each Clue Scroll gives exactly one reward casket. The number of steps (1–5) doesn't affect the number of rewards — it just makes the clue longer to complete.

Are Clue Scrolls worth doing for money?

Yes, but not as a primary money maker. Clue Scrolls are passive income — do them while doing other content. The expected value per hour is about 200K–400K GP for Beginner/Easy clues, which is decent but not as good as focused money makers like Slayer or bossing. The real value is the excitement of potentially getting a rare drop.
