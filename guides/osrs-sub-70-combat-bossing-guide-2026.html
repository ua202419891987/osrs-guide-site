⚔️ OSRS Sub-70 Combat Bossing Guide 2026 — Low Level Boss Strategies

You don't need 80+ combat to boss in OSRS. This guide covers every boss accessible at 40-70 combat stats, with exact requirements, budget gear setups, safe strategies, and GP per hour. Whether you're a mid-level account or an F2P player transitioning to members, here's how to start bossing early and profit.

📅 Updated: July 2026 — Sub-70 combat boss strategies · 12 min read

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Start bossing at 50 Combat with 40/40/40 stats and budget gear worth ~500K GP — no need to grind to 80+
  • 📌 Best entry bosses: Moss Guardian (50 Mining), Barrows (50 Combat + prayer), Giant Mole (60 Attack/Strength)
  • 📌 Giant Mole earns up to 800K GP/hr at 60+ melee stats — one of the most profitable low-combat bosses in OSRS
  • 📌 Barrows at 50-60 Combat: 200K-400K GP/hr with Iban's Blast + Protect from Melee — no magic level beyond 50 required
  • 📌 Don't wait to boss: the GP you earn from early bossing will fund your account faster than grinding on low-level monsters
💡 Quick Verdict — Sub-70 Combat Bossing 2026

You can start bossing at 50 Combat (40/40/40 stats) with budget gear worth ~500K GP. The best entry boss for low combat is Moss Guardian (requires 50 Mining, not combat stats), followed by Barrows (requires 50 Combat + prayer). If you have 60 Attack/Strength, Giant Mole is the most profitable low-combat boss (up to 800K GP/hour). Don't wait for 80 combat — the GP you earn from early bossing will fund your 80 combat grind faster than training on Hill Giants.

Most OSRS bossing guides assume you have 80+ combat stats, Barrows gear, and a bank of 10M+ GP. But the reality is: you can start bossing much earlier. With 50 Combat and 500K GP in gear, you can solo Barrows. With 60 Attack/Strength, you can kill Giant Mole and make 500K-800K GP per hour. This guide is for players who want to skip the "grind to 80 combat" phase and start earning boss loot early.

If you're coming from F2P and just bought membership, this guide will show you exactly which bosses you can do right now — without spending 100 hours grinding combat levels. For a complete members startup guide, also check our F2P-to-P2P transition guide.

① 🎯 What "Sub-70 Combat" Means — Definitions

"Sub-70 Combat" covers a wide range of account builds. Here's what you can expect at each combat bracket:

Combat BracketExample StatsBoss AccessGP/Hour Potential
40-50 Combat40/40/40, 43 PrayerBarrows (with prayer pots), Moss Guardian200K-400K
50-60 Combat50/50/50, 43-52 PrayerGiant Mole, Barrows (faster), King Black Dragon400K-800K
60-70 Combat60/60/60, 52-70 PrayerGod Wars Dungeon (entry), Zulrah (with 75 Magic), Vorkath (with 80 in all)800K-1.5M

Important: "Combat level" in OSRS is calculated as: (Attack + Strength + Defence + (Prayer / 2) + (Hitpoints / 3)) / 4 for melee. If you're a Strength pure (e.g., 50 Strength, 40 Attack, 40 Defence), your combat level is lower — and you can boss at even lower visible combat. This guide assumes balanced combat stats (roughly equal Attack/Strength/Defence).

💡 Don't Forget: 43 Prayer is More Important Than 70 Attack

Protect from Melee/Magic/Missiles (43 Prayer) is the single biggest combat upgrade for low-level bossing. With 43 Prayer, you take 0 damage from most boss attacks. Without it, you'll need to bring 10+ lobsters per kill and your DPS suffer. If you're 50 Combat but only 30 Prayer — train Prayer first. For the cheapest Prayer training methods, see our complete Prayer guide.

② ⚔️ Bosses Accessible at 40-50 Combat

At 40-50 Combat, your DPS is low — but with the right strategy, you can still kill several bosses. The key is: high defence + prayer rather than high DPS.

2.1 💀 Barrows (All 6 Brothers) — 50 Combat Minimum

Barrows is the best entry boss for low-combat accounts. The brothers have fixed combat levels (81-118), and with 43 Prayer + protect prayers, they hit 0. The loot is excellent: Barrows gear (worth 500K-2M GP per item), and the chance at the Barrows unique table.

RequirementDetails
Combat Stats50+ Attack/Strength/Defence (for Barrows gear) OR 40+ (with food rushing)
Prayer43+ (Protect from Melee/Magic/Missiles required)
GearRune scimitar, rune armour, amulet of power (~50K GP total)
Time per Run15-25 minutes (first time), 8-12 minutes (experienced)
GP/Hour200K-500K (depends on unique drops)

Strategy: Use the safespot at Ahrim's room (range him from behind the fence). For Dharok, use Protect from Melee and kill him quickly before his HP-drain effect kicks in. For a full walkthrough, see our Barrows complete guide.

2.2 🌿 Moss Guardian — 50 Mining Required (Not Combat!)

Moss Guardian is unique: it requires 50 Mining to access, not high combat stats. The boss has only 150 HP and hits very low (max 3). You can kill it at 40 Combat with 50 Mining and basic gear.

RequirementDetails
Combat Stats30+ (very low requirement)
Other Stats50 Mining (required to enter the boss area)
GearAny melee weapon (iron scimitar is enough)
GP/Hour100K-200K (drops: mossy keys, seeds, low-value herbs)

Moss Guardian is less profitable than Barrows but excellent for learning boss mechanics. It's located in the Moss Lair (Falador area, requires 50 Mining to enter the cave).

2.3 🐉 King Black Dragon (KBD) — 50 Combat Recommended

KBD is a Wilderness boss (level 48 Wilderness) with 255 HP. At 50 Combat, you'll need prayer pots and good food — but KBD drops dragon bones (13K GP each) and visage (4.5M GP). It's risky but high-reward.

⚠️ KBD = Wilderness Risk

KBD is in level 48 Wilderness. PKers with 78+ combat will attack you. Only bring gear you're willing to lose. A typical KBD trip: rune scimitar, rune armour, amulet of power, lobsters, 2-3 prayer potions. If you die, you lose ~200K GP. For a complete KBD guide, see our KBD boss guide.

③ ⚔️ Bosses Accessible at 50-60 Combat

At 50-60 Combat, your DPS becomes respectable. You can now kill bosses that drop 500K-2M GP per hour consistently.

3.1 🐀 Giant Mole — 60 Attack/Strength Recommended

Giant Mole is the most profitable low-combat boss. It has 240 HP, is underground (no PK risk), and drops Mole skin (20K GP) + Baby mole pet (50M GP). With 60/60/60 stats, you can kill Mole in 2-3 minutes.

RequirementDetails
Combat Stats60 Attack, 60 Strength, 50+ Defence (recommended)
Prayer43+ (Protect from Melee when Mole digs)
GearRune scimitar → Dragon scimitar (if affordable), rune platebody, amulet of power
Time per Kill2-4 minutes (depending on DPS)
GP/Hour500K-800K (consistent, no PK risk)

Strategy: Giant Mole spawns in the Falador Mole Lair (enter via the tunnel south of Falador). It has a digging attack that makes it disappear — use a spade to find the tunnel entrance and follow it. For a complete strategy guide, see our Giant Mole boss guide.

💡 Giant Mole is the Best Low-Combat Money Maker

If you have 60 Attack/Strength and 500K GP for basic gear, Giant Mole is the most consistent money maker in the game for your combat bracket. No PK risk, no special items required, and the profit is 500K-800K GP/hour. Compare that to Hill Giants (20K-40K GP/hour) — Mole is 10× more profitable.

3.2 💀 Barrows (Now Faster) — 50-60 Combat

With 50-60 Combat and better gear (Dragon scimitar), your Barrows trips drop from 15 minutes to 8-10 minutes. This pushes GP/hour to 400K-600K. If you got lucky with your first Barrows runs and have a Barrows weapon (e.g., Abyssal Whip from Barrows loot table — wait, Abyssal Whip is not from Barrows. Let me fix: if you got a Barrows unique like Karil's crossbow or Ahrim's staff, your DPS improves significantly).

3.3 🐉 Skeletal Wyvern — 55 Slayer Required

Skeletal Wyverns are not a "boss" in the traditional sense, but they drop the Dragon Cane (500K GP) and Wyvern Visage (4M GP). They require 55 Slayer to kill and have 140 HP. At 55 Combat, they're a decent low-level moneymaker.

④ ⚔️ Bosses Accessible at 60-70 Combat

At 60-70 Combat, you're entering "mid-game" bossing territory. Your DPS is now good enough to kill most entry-level bosses in under 2 minutes.

4.1 🐉 King Black Dragon (KBD) — Now Consistent

With 60-70 Combat, KBD kills drop to 3-5 minutes (from 6-8 minutes at 50 Combat). This makes KBD a consistent 400K-700K GP/hour method. The dragon bones (13K GP each) and occasional visage (4.5M GP) make it worth the Wilderness risk.

4.2 🌊 Zulrah — 75 Magic Required (Not Combat!)

Zulrah is a high-level boss that requires 75 Magic (for Magic Dart) and 70 Ranged or 75 Magic for the fight. But here's the thing: your combat stats don't matter for Zulrah — only your Magic/Ranged level and your ability to survive the mechanics. Some players kill Zulrah at 60 Combat (with 75 Magic and 70 Ranged). It's extremely difficult but possible.

Realistic recommendation: Don't attempt Zulrah before 70 Combat. The mechanics are punishing, and with low HP (say, 60 HP at 60 Combat), one mistake = death. For the complete Zulrah guide, see our Zulrah boss guide.

4.3 ⚔️ God Wars Dungeon (GWD) — 60+ Combat, 70+ Recommended

GWD is the first "raid-style" bossing content in OSRS. The entry requirement is 60 Combat, but the recommended stats are 70+ in your chosen style. GWD drops very valuable loot: Bandos armour (4M-8M GP per piece), Armadyl armour (3M-6M GP), and Supreme Overloads.

⑥ 🛡️ Safe Strategies for Low Combat Bossing

When your DPS is low, survival is more important than speed. Here are the essential survival strategies for low-combat bossing:

6.1 🛡️ Always Use Protective Prayers

With 43 Prayer, you unlock Protect from Melee, Protect from Magic, and Protect from Missiles. These prayers reduce the corresponding damage type to 0 (or very close to 0). Always use the correct protect prayer for the boss you're fighting:

  • Barrows brothers: Each brother uses a different style — use the matching protect prayer
  • Giant Mole: Protect from Melee when Mole is above ground; turn off prayer when Mole digs (it can't hit you)
  • KBD: Protect from Fire (always) — KBD's fire breath hits 25+ without protection
  • Zulrah: Switch protect prayers based on Zulrah's rotation (complex — watch a video guide first)

6.2 💉 Bring More Food Than You Think You Need

At 40-50 Combat, your max hit is low (10-14 with rune scimitar). Boss kills take 5-10 minutes. Bring at least 10-15 lobsters per trip (not just 5-8). If you run out of food, you die — and in the Wilderness (KBD), that means losing your gear.

6.3 🗺️ Learn Safespots (When Available)

Some bosses have safespots where they can't reach you:

  • Ahrim (Barrows): Safespot behind the fence in his room
  • Giant Mole: No safespot, but Mole digs and disappears regularly (ticking damage window)
  • KBD: No safespot — you must tank hits with prayer + food

6.4 💀 What to Do If You're About to Die

If your food is running low and the boss has 10-20 HP left: don't teleport yet. Eat your last food, land 2-3 more hits, then teleport only if you're about to die. If the boss dies, you get the loot. If you teleport early, you wasted the trip. For more combat survival tips, see our low combat survival guide.

⑧ ❓ FAQ — Sub-70 Combat Bossing

Q: Can I solo Barrows at 40 Combat?

A: Yes, but it's slow. At 40/40/40 stats with 43 Prayer, you can solo all 6 brothers. Each brother takes 2-4 minutes to kill (vs. 30-60 seconds at 80 Combat). Bring 15-20 lobsters per trip and expect 15-25 minutes per full run. The GP per hour is lower (200K-300K) due to the slow kill speed, but it's still profitable.

Q: What's the minimum GP I need to start bossing?

A: 150K GP. That buys rune scimitar (32K), rune platebody (42K), rune platelegs (38K), amulet of power (1.2K), and rune kiteshield (35K). Add 10K GP for lobsters and teleport tabs. With 150K GP and 43 Prayer, you can start killing Barrows brothers immediately.

Q: Is Giant Mole really 500K-800K GP/hour? How?

A: Giant Mole drops Mole skin (20K GP) every kill. At 60 Combat with Dragon scimitar, you kill Mole in 2-3 minutes. That's 20-30 kills per hour = 400K-600K GP from Mole skins alone. Add Baby mole pet chance (1/3,000 — 50M GP if you get it) and the rare Mole claw drop, and the expected value is 500K-800K GP/hour.

Q: Should I train Prayer before bossing?

A: Absolutely. 43 Prayer (Protect from X prayers) is the single most important upgrade for low-combat bossing. Without it, you'll need 10+ lobsters per brother at Barrows. With it, you need 2-3. The 4-hour grind to 43 Prayer (big bones at Wilderness altar) pays for itself in saved food costs within your first 5 Barrows runs.

Q: Can I boss in F2P?

A: No. There are no bosses in F2P OSRS. The closest thing is "Hill Giants" and "Moss Giants" — but they're regular monsters, not bosses (no special loot table). If you want to boss, you need membership. The good news: you can earn your first bond by killing Giant Mole (500K-800K GP/hour) if you have 60 Combat on a members account. For F2P bond strategies, see our F2P first bond guide.

Q: What if I die at KBD? Do I lose everything?

A: Yes — KBD is in the Wilderness (level 48). If you die, you drop all your equipped gear and inventory. That's why you should only bring "disposable" gear to KBD: rune scimitar, rune armour, lobsters, 2-3 prayer potions. Total risk: ~200K GP. If you die, you lose 200K GP — annoying, but not account-ruining. As you get richer, you can bring better gear — but always calculate: "Is the potential profit worth the risk?"

Q: How do I get to God Wars Dungeon at 60 Combat?

A: God Wars Dungeon is located north of Trollheim (members only). You need 60 Agility to use the shortcut, or 70 Agility + 75 Strength for the mountain path. At 60 Combat, you can't solo GWD bosses — but you can join a team in the God Wars Dungeon Friends Chat. Most teams welcome lower-level players as "prayer support" (someone who brings prayer pots and uses protective prayers while others do the DPS).

Q: What's the best low-combat boss for ranged/magic training?

A: For Ranged: Ogress Warriors (Corsair Cove, F2P/P2P) are not a boss, but they have 82 HP and drop alchables worth 80K-120K GP/hour. For Magic: Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon) have 28 HP and drop big bones (for Prayer) + limpwurt roots (sell for 2K GP). If you want a real boss for Ranged/Magic training at low combat, try Experiment #2 in the Iorwerth Dungeon (requires 40 Defence + 40 Attack, but you can safespot it with Ranged).

⑨ 💡 Final Tips for Sub-70 Combat Bossing

Bossing at low combat is all about efficiency over ego. You don't need to be the strongest player in the room — you just need to be smart about which bosses you attempt and how you gear for them.

  • 43 Prayer > 80 Attack. If you have to choose between training Prayer and training Attack — train Prayer first. The survivability upgrade is worth more than the DPS upgrade from 60→70 Attack.
  • Giant Mole before Barrows. If you have 60 Attack/Strength, do Giant Mole first. The GP per hour is higher, and the risk is lower. Use Mole profit to buy better gear for Barrows.
  • Don't solo KBD until 60 Combat. At 50 Combat, KBD kills take 8-10 minutes and you'll use 10+ lobsters per kill. At 60 Combat, kills drop to 4-6 minutes. The GP per hour nearly doubles.
  • Join a clan chat for team bosses. GWD and some Wilderness bosses are much easier with a team. "OSRS Advice" and "Reddit CC" are good places to find teammates.
  • Track your kills per hour. If you're getting less than 15 Barrows kills per hour (full runs), you're being too slow. Watch YouTube speedrun videos to learn faster routes.
  • Upgrade your weapon before your armour. A Dragon scimitar (120K GP) is a bigger DPS upgrade than Dragon platebody (150K GP). Always upgrade weapon first.
🎯 The Sub-70 Combat Bossing Mindset

Bossing at low combat is not about being the strongest — it's about being the smartest. You might kill Barrows brothers in 3 minutes while an 80 Combat player kills them in 45 seconds. But you know what? You're still getting the same loot. The GP per hour is lower, yes — but you're also learning boss mechanics, earning GP, and having fun. Don't let "I'm not 80 Combat yet" stop you from bossing. Start now, upgrade as you go, and enjoy the journey.
