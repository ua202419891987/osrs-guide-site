1. Achievement Diaries Overview

Achievement Diaries are sets of tasks grouped by region. Each region has 4 tiers: Easy, Medium, Hard, and Elite. This guide covers Easy and Medium tiers for all regions — the ones you should complete first for maximum account progression.

💡 How to Track Your Progress

Open your Achievement Diary interface by clicking the green trophy icon in your game UI (or searching "Diary" in the search bar). Select a region and tier to see:

  • ✅ Completed tasks (green checkmark)
  • ⏳ In-progress tasks (yellow circle)
  • 🔒 Locked tasks (red X — you don't meet requirements yet)

Use this interface to check off tasks as you complete them. The game automatically tracks most tasks — you don't need to claim completion for each one individually.

Should You Complete Easy Before Medium?

Technically: No. You can do Medium tasks without completing Easy.
Practically: Yes. The skill requirements for Medium diaries assume you've already done the quests required for Easy. In almost all cases, you'll naturally complete Easy first because the skill requirements are lower.

Exception: If you already have 70+ stats, you can skip Easy diaries and go straight to Medium. But you'll still need to do the quests required for Easy diary tasks.

Common Stuck Points & Solutions

Stuck: "I can't pickpocket successfully at level 10 Thieving."
Solution: Train Thieving to level 15 first (pickpocket 20+ men in Al Kharid). Or bring lots of food and keep trying — you'll succeed eventually.

Solution: Train Agility at Gnome Stronghold Course (level 1+) until you reach 10. Takes about 30 minutes.

⚠️ Wilderness Warning

All Wilderness diary tasks involve entering the Wilderness (PvP zone). PKers can attack you. Follow these safety rules:

  • Only bring what you can afford to lose
  • Wear 1-item setup (protect item prayer saves 1 extra item on death)
  • Bring a teleport tablet for emergency escape
  • Check the Wilderness level on screen — if it says "Level 1" you're safe-ish; "Level 30" you're in danger
  • Do this diary at World 330 (less PKers) or with a friend watching your back

Don't set aside "diary time." Instead, complete diary tasks passively while doing other activities:

Example 3: Farming Runs

7. FAQ & Troubleshooting

A task says "In Progress" but I already did it. What's wrong?

This is usually a timing issue. The game checks task completion when:

  • You log out and log back in
  • You enter a new area (zone change)
  • You talk to the diary NPC

Fix: Try all three. If it still doesn't update, check the exact task description — you might have misread it (e.g., "Kill a unicorn" vs "Kill a UNICORN in the Wilderness").

Can I complete diary tasks on different characters and claim the reward on one?

No. Diary completion is per character. Each of your OSRS accounts needs to complete the tasks individually. However, some tasks can be skipped if you've already done them on another account (the game sometimes recognizes previous completion, but this is inconsistent). Always assume you need to do everything from scratch on a new account.

What's the fastest way to reach the required stats for Medium diaries?

Use our skill training guides for each skill. Prioritize:

  • Attack/Strength/Defense: Train to 60 (all Medium diaries need 60 combat)
  • Magic: Train to 60 (many teleport/spell tasks)
  • Ranged: Train to 60 (ranged kill tasks)
  • Thieving: Train to 45 (pickpocket tasks)
  • Mining/Smithing: Train to 50 (ore/chance tasks)

I died in the Wilderness during a diary task. Can I get my items back?

Maybe. If you were killed by a PKer, your items drop on the ground. Other players can take them. If you were killed by a monster (NPC), your items stay on your gravestone for 2 hours. Go back to the exact location and click your gravestone to retrieve items. Warning: If another player walks over your gravestone, they can't pick up your items (only you can). But if you don't retrieve within 2 hours, items disappear forever.

Do I need to do the tasks in order, or can I skip around?

You can skip around freely. The tasks don't need to be completed in any specific order. The game tracks each task individually. This means you can do "the fun tasks" first and save "the annoying tasks" for later (or combine them with other activities).

How do I know which NPC gives the diary reward?

Each region has a "Diary Reward NPC". Common ones:

  • Lumbridge/Draynor: Ned (Draynor)
  • Varrock: Rat Burgiss (south of Varrock)
  • Falador: Sir Vyvin (White Knight Castle)
  • Kandarin: Elder Gnome (Seers' Village)
  • Ardougne: Ardougne Town Crier
  • Desert: Desert Town Crier (Shantay Pass)
  • Kourend: Kourend Town Crier

If you can't find the NPC, search "OSRS [Region] diary reward NPC" on Google — the OSRS Wiki has exact locations.

Can I complete diaries in Ironman Mode?

Yes! Ironman accounts can complete all the same diaries. The only difference is that you need to obtain required items yourself (can't buy from GE). This makes some diaries significantly harder (e.g., needing 70 Smithing for Hard diaries when you can't buy armor). Ironman-specific tip: Prioritize diaries that give teleport items and skilling boosts — these help you get uniques for the rest of your account.
