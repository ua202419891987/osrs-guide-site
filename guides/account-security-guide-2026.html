1. Why Account Security Matters

Old School RuneScape accounts are high-value targets for hackers. A maxed main with 2B+ bank is worth hundreds of dollars on the black market. Even mid-level accounts with 50M-100M GP are regularly targeted.

The Reality Check

Bottom line: 20 minutes of setup versus potentially thousands of hours lost. The math is simple.

2. Jagex Account — Upgrade NOW If You Haven't

In 2023, Jagex introduced Jagex Accounts — a modern authentication system that replaces the old username/password login. As of 2026, this is the single most important security upgrade you can make.

Why Jagex Accounts Are Superior

  • No more recovery questions — hackers can't social-engineer their way in
  • Backup codes — 10 one-time codes to recover access if you lose 2FA device
  • Multi-character support — up to 20 characters under one secure login
  • Login notifications — email alerts for new device logins
  • Mandatory 2FA — authenticator app is required, not optional
  • No username login — your login name is never exposed publicly

How to Upgrade

  1. Go to account settings on the RuneScape website
  2. Click "Upgrade to Jagex Account"
  3. Follow the migration wizard (takes <5 minutes)
  4. Set up your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator)
  5. Save your 10 backup codes in a safe place (NOT on your computer!)
  6. Verify your email address

Critical Warning

Write down your backup codes on paper. Store them somewhere physical — a notebook, a drawer, or a safe. If you lose your phone AND your backup codes, Jagex CANNOT recover your account. This is by design — the security is so strong that even Jagex can't bypass it.

3. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) Setup

If you're still on a legacy account (haven't upgraded to Jagex Account), you MUST enable the RuneScape Authenticator immediately.

Setup Instructions

  1. Download an authenticator app: Google Authenticator, Authy, or Microsoft Authenticator
  2. Log into your RuneScape account on the website
  3. Go to Account Settings → Authenticator
  4. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app
  5. Enter the 6-digit code to confirm
  6. Click "Enable"

What 2FA Does and Doesn't Do

ScenarioProtected?Notes

Someone steals your phoneNoUse screen lock + biometrics on phone
Your email is compromisedPartiallyHacker can disable 2FA through email
You click a phishing linkDependsFake login pages can capture 2FA codes too
Jagex Account + 2FA + Secure EmailYesLayered defense covers all angles

Key point: 2FA on your RuneScape account is useless if your email is insecure. A hacker who controls your email can disable your authenticator. See Section 6 for email security.

4. Bank PIN — Your Last Line of Defense

Even if a hacker somehow gets into your account, a Bank PIN prevents them from accessing your bank. This is your absolute last line of defense — do NOT skip it.

How to Set Your Bank PIN

  1. Talk to any banker in OSRS
  2. Select "I'd like to check my PIN settings"
  3. Set a 4-digit PIN (don't use your birth year, 1234, or other obvious numbers)
  4. Choose 7-day recovery delay (maximum security)

Bank PIN Settings — What the Delays Mean

SettingSecurityInconvenience
3-day recoveryLowMinimal — but hackers only need to wait 3 days
7-day recoveryMaximumIf you forget PIN, you wait a week. Worth it.

Always choose 7-day recovery. If you forget your PIN, waiting a week is annoying but losing your entire bank is devastating. The math is obvious.

Never Share Your PIN

Jagex moderators will NEVER ask for your bank PIN. Anyone asking for your PIN — in-game, on Discord, via email, anywhere — is a scammer. No exceptions.

5. Strong Password Strategy

A weak password is the easiest entry point for hackers. Here's what makes a password strong in 2026.

Password Rules

  • Minimum 16 characters — length beats complexity
  • Use a password manager (Bitwarden, 1Password, KeePass) — don't try to memorize unique passwords
  • Never reuse passwords — if one site gets breached, all accounts with that password are vulnerable
  • Avoid personal info — no birthdays, pet names, favorite sports teams
  • Passphrase method: 4-5 random words (e.g., "correct-horse-battery-staple-guitar") — easy to remember, hard to crack

What NOT to Use

Bad PasswordWhy It's BadCrack Time
password123Most common password everInstant
runescape99Easily guessable for OSRS playerSeconds
Your RSN + numbersHackers know your RSNMinutes
Same as email passwordOne breach = everything goneN/A

Use a password manager. It generates and stores unique, strong passwords for every account. You only need to remember one master password.

6. Email Security — The Most Overlooked Layer

Your email is the master key to your RuneScape account. If a hacker controls your email, they can disable your authenticator, change your password, and lock you out completely. This is the #1 attack vector for OSRS account theft.

Email Security Checklist

  1. Enable 2FA on your email — Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo all support it. This is NON-NEGOTIABLE.
  2. Use a unique password — your email password must be different from EVERY other password you use
  3. Check recovery email/phone — make sure these are up to date
  4. Review account activity — Gmail has "Last account activity" at the bottom. Check it regularly.
  5. Create a dedicated email — consider a separate email address used ONLY for RuneScape

Pro Tip: Dedicated RS Email

Create a Gmail account used exclusively for RuneScape. Never use it for anything else. Don't give it to friends, don't register on fan sites with it, don't use it for Discord. This single change eliminates 90%+ of potential attack vectors.

7. Account Recovery Configuration

For legacy accounts (non-Jagex Account), recovery questions are critical. For Jagex Accounts, backup codes serve this purpose.

Legacy Account Recovery Settings

  • Set recovery questions with answers ONLY you know
  • Do NOT use publicly available information (mother's maiden name, first pet, etc.)
  • Treat recovery answers like passwords — random, unique, stored in password manager
  • Keep your registered email current

Jagex Account Backup Codes

  • You receive 10 one-time backup codes when enabling 2FA on a Jagex Account
  • Each code can be used once to recover access
  • Store them offline — paper, not digital
  • If you use all 10, generate a new set immediately
  • Never share backup codes with anyone, ever

8. Phishing & Scam Awareness

Phishing is the #1 way OSRS accounts get hacked. Hackers create fake websites that look exactly like the RuneScape login page, tricking you into entering your credentials — including 2FA codes.

Common Phishing Tactics

TacticHow It WorksRed Flag
Fake Forum LinksLink to "rs-forums.runescape.com" or similarURL is NOT runescape.com
Double XP Scams"Double XP Weekend! Click here to activate!"Jagex never requires you to click external links for events
Fake StreamsFake Twitch/YouTube streams with "quitting" giveawaysAsks you to log in via external link
Discord PMsRandom DM with "check out this OSRS tool"Unsolicited DMs from strangers
Email PhishingFake Jagex emails about "suspicious activity"Check sender address — only @jagex.com is real

How to Never Get Phished

  1. Bookmark the real RuneScape site — never navigate to it through links
  2. Check every URL carefully — runescape.com vs runescape.co vs runescape-forums.com
  3. Never click links in Discord/email — even from "friends" (their account might be compromised)
  4. Jagex never asks for your password — any message asking for credentials is fake
  5. Use the official RuneLite launcher — only download from runelite.net

9. Linked Accounts — Steam, Google, Apple

Linking your OSRS account to Steam, Google, or Apple can be convenient, but it also creates additional attack surfaces. If your Steam account is compromised, your OSRS account might be too.

Best Practices for Linked Accounts

  • Only link accounts that are equally secured (2FA on Steam/Google/Apple too)
  • Review linked accounts in your Jagex Account settings regularly
  • Remove any linked accounts you no longer use
  • Steam Guard is mandatory if you link Steam — enable it

11. What To Do If You Get Hacked

If the worst happens, act immediately. Every minute counts.

Immediate Actions (Minutes 0-5)

  1. Change your email password first — this is how they'll maintain access
  2. Enable 2FA on your email if it wasn't already
  3. Change your RuneScape password
  4. End all active sessions from account settings
  5. Check linked accounts — hackers often link their own Steam/Google account

Short-Term Actions (Hours 0-24)

  1. Scan your computer for malware/keyloggers (Malwarebytes is free and effective)
  2. Check your email's "sent" folder — hackers may have used it to compromise other accounts
  3. Review bank transaction history on RuneScape website
  4. Report the hack to Jagex through the official support page

Important Reality Check

Jagex's Policy on Lost Items

Jagex does not return lost items from hacked accounts. Their policy is clear: account security is the player's responsibility. If your items are gone, they're gone. This is why prevention is everything.
