① How Pet Drop Rates Work

Pets are the rarest drops in OSRS. Every pet in the game follows a specific drop rate formula that depends on the activity. Understanding these formulas is the first step to efficient pet hunting.

① Skilling Pet Formula

Skilling pets follow a scaled drop rate based on how many actions you perform per hour of a given skill. The base formula is:

📐 Skilling Pet Drop Formula

Base Rate = (SKILL_XP_PER_ACTION × 25) / (REQUIRED_XP_FOR_99)

This base rate is then multiplied by a global skilling pet rate (~1/60,000 to ~1/200,000 depending on the skill). The final number is your effective chance per action.

  • Slower skills (Agility, Runecraft): Higher per-action rate — you do fewer actions per hour, so each action counts more.
  • Fast skills (Cooking, Fletching): Lower per-action rate — you do thousands of actions per hour, so each individual action is less likely.
  • Key insight: Over time, the expected hours to pet is surprisingly similar across all skills (~40-80 hours at 99).

💡 Pet Drop Rate Note

These rates are per kill, not per hour. A 1/3,000 boss you can kill in 2 minutes per kill (~30 kills/hour) will take roughly 100 hours on average to see the pet. A 1/1,000 boss at 10 kills/hour takes about the same time. Kill speed matters just as much as drop rate.

② Threshold Mechanics Explained

OSRS uses a threshold system for boss pets to prevent extreme dry streaks. This is one of the most important mechanics to understand for pet hunting.

② How Thresholds Work

📊 Threshold System — Step by Step

  • Base rate: Each boss has a base pet drop rate (e.g., 1/3,000 for Giant Mole).
  • Threshold value: Most bosses have a threshold of X kills (typically equal to the denominator of the base rate). For Giant Mole: threshold = 3,000.
  • Every threshold passed: Your effective drop rate doubles (the denominator is cut in half).
  • Example: Giant Mole pet rate is 1/3,000 base. After 3,000 kills without pet → rate becomes 1/1,500. After 6,000 kills → 1/750. After 9,000 kills → 1/375.

③ Skilling Pet — Detailed Breakdown

🐿️ Rocky (Thieving)

Source: Pickpocketing any NPC or looting chests. The rate scales with level — at level 99, the rate is roughly 1/58,000 actions. Best method: Pickpocketing Ardougne Knights (1-tick method) at level 99 is the fastest at ~400-500 pickpockets/hour.

  • Fastest: Ardougne Knights (1-tick) — ~500 actions/hour
  • Most profitable: Vyrewatch / Elves — slower but valuable drops
  • AFK option: Pyramid Plunder chests — 1 roll per chest opened

🐦 Heron (Fishing)

Source: Any fishing method rolls for the Heron. The rate scales with Fishing level. At 99, each catch has an ~1/116,000 chance. Best method: 3-tick Barbarian Fishing (if tick-perfect) or 2-tick Karambwan (slightly slower but less effort).

  • Fastest: 3-tick Barbarian Fishing (1.2K+ fish/hr)
  • AFK option: Karambwan / Monkfish / Anglerfish
  • Passive option: Tempoross reward pool (also rolls for pet)

🪵 Beaver (Woodcutting)

Source: Chopping any tree rolls for the Beaver. Rate at 99 is ~1/58,000 actions. Trees like redwood (fast afk) and teaks (3-tick) are the fastest methods. Best method: 3-tick teaks at Fossil Island for maximum actions/hour.

  • Fastest: 3-tick teaks (Fossil Island) — 1,500+ chops/hour
  • AFK option: Redwood trees — extremely afk, ~80 chops/hour
  • Forestry events: Also add extra rolls via event rewards

🌿 Tangleroot (Farming)

Source: Checking the health of any fully grown Farming patch (trees, fruit trees, herbs, allotments, hops, bushes, cacti, mushroom, calquat, redwood, celastrus, hesperornisform, etc.). Rate scales with Farming level. Best method: Regular tree runs + fruit tree runs + calquat runs + redwood + celastrus. Maximize number of checks per day.

  • Fastest: Multiple tree runs per day (4+ fruit trees, 5+ regular trees, calquat, celastrus, redwood)
  • Passive: Do your herb/allotment/belladonna runs — each check rolls for pet
  • Key tip: Tangleroot is the only skilling pet that resets daily (patch grow times limit checks)

🪨 Rock Golem (Mining)

Source: Mining any ore or rock (including Motherlode Mine pay-dirt, gem rocks, Volcanic Mine, shooting stars, and MLM sack). Rate at 99 is ~1/163,000 actions. Best method: 3-tick granite or 3-tick iron for maximum actions/hour.

  • Fastest: 3-tick granite (+ tick-perfect mining) — 2,000+ actions/hr
  • AFK option: Motherlode Mine (lower actions/hr but 1 roll per pay-dirt + sack roll)
  • Volcanic Mine: Also rolls for the pet (good XP + pet chance)

🐿️ Giant Squirrel (Agility)

Source: Completing any Agility course lap. Rate at 99 is ~1/22,000 laps. The Squirrel (Hallowed Sepulchre) is a separate pet with a different rate. Best method: Sepulchre gives both Squirrel and GP, rooftop courses are more laps/hour.

  • Fastest laps: Rellekka Rooftop / Ardougne Rooftop — 250+ laps/hour
  • Best value: Hallowed Sepulchre (Squirrel pet + GP + ring of endurance)
  • Passive: Some players get Giant Squirrel while training to 99 — estimated ~10-15% of maxed players have it

🌀 Rift Guardian (Runecraft)

Source: Crafting any rune. Rate scales with XP per action. At 99, the rate is ~1/1,200,000 XP worth of actions. Best method: Guardians of the Rift (GotR) — combines good XP + pet chance + rewards.

  • Fastest: Ourania/ZMI with huge rune pouch (bank-preset method)
  • Most popular: Guardians of the Rift — solid XP + profit + pet chance
  • Note: The Rift Guardian is the rarest skilling pet by XP rate, but the most common by player count due to Runecraft's slow nature

🔥 Phoenix (Firemaking)

Source: Burning any log. Rate at 99 is ~1/100,000 actions. You also get a pet roll from Wintertodt supply crates. Best method: Burning redwood logs (fastest burning speed) or Wintertodt (multiple rolls per kill via crates).

  • Fastest: Redwood logs — burn ~3,000+ logs/hour
  • Best overall: Wintertodt — supply crates give multi-roll + Wintertodt pet also rolls here
  • Cheapest: Normal logs (minimal cost, but slower)

🐹 Baby Chinchompa (Hunter)

Source: Catching any chinchompa — grey, red, black, or carnivorous. Rate at 99 is ~1/69,000 catches. Best method: Black chinchompas in Wilderness (high profit, risk of PvP) or red chins for safety.

  • Fastest: Black chinchompas in Wilderness (320+ catches/hour if uninterrupted)
  • Safer option: Red chins in Feldip Hills (250-280 catches/hour)
  • Passive: Birdhouse runs do NOT roll for this pet
💡 Skilling Pet Tip: Pre-99 vs Post-99

You can get skilling pets below level 99 — they just have a much lower rate. The rate scales linearly with your level (at level 50, roughly 50% of the level-99 rate). Most players go well past 99 before seeing most skilling pets. The average pet takes 5-15M XP past 99 to obtain.

💡 Important: Skotos Pet Rate

Skotos (from Skotizo) has a base rate of 1/65 — the best pet drop rate in the game by far. You need dark totems from Catacombs of Kourend to summon Skotizo. ⑥ Hardest Pets to Obtain

These pets require extreme dedication, high skill requirements, or absurd kill counts. Only the most persistent pet hunters obtain these.

⑥ The 5 Hardest Pets

💀 #1: Dagannoth Kings Pets (x3)

Rate: 1/5,000 each. Threshold: 5,000 each. Why it's hard: You need to kill all 3 kings simultaneously (tribrid). Each pet has a separate 1/5,000 rate. To get all 3, you'll likely pass 15,000+ combined kills. Plus, Rex is safespottable but Prime and Supreme require active tribrid. Estimated time: 300-500 hours for all three.

💀 #2: God Wars Dungeon Pets (x4)

Rate: 1/5,000 each (Bandos, Armadyl, Saradomin, Zamorak). Threshold: 5,000 each. Why it's hard: GWD requires kill count to enter, and each boss is in a different room. The 1/5,000 rate is the worst for non-Wilderness bosses. Estimated time: 200+ hours per pet.

💀 #3: Corporeal Beast (Pet Dark Core)

Rate: 1/5,000. Threshold: 1,000. Why it's hard: Corp has massive defense and requires either a team (for fast kills) or specific gear (spear + divine potions). Solo kills are ~7-10 minutes each. Mass worlds give ~50 kills/hour but less loot per person. Estimated time: 100+ hours solo.

💀 #4: Tangleroot (Farming)

Rate: ~1/7,500 checks. Threshold: None. Why it's hard: You're capped by grow times — you can only check trees/fruit trees once per several hours. While the per-action rate is better than many skilling pets, the time gate makes it the longest skilling pet to target. Estimated time: 80-150 hours of active tree runs over several weeks.

💀 #5: Rift Guardian (Runecraft)

Rate: ~1/1,200,000 XP worth of actions. Threshold: None. Why it's hard: Runecraft is the slowest skill in OSRS. Even at 80K XP/hr (ZMI/Soul Runes), you need ~15M XP past 99 for the average pet. That's 187+ hours of Runecraft. Most players never touch Runecraft past 99, making Rift Guardian extremely rare.

💡 Honorable Mention: Bloodhound (Clue Scrolls)

Rate: 1/1,000 from master clue scroll caskets. While not a "boss" or "skilling" pet, Bloodhound is one of the rarest pets in the game because master clues take ages to stack and complete. You'll likely have 1,000+ master clues done. Estimated time: 500+ hours of clue scroll grinding.

⑧ Strategies for Efficient Pet Hunting

Pet hunting is a marathon. Here are the most effective strategies to maximize your chances and minimize burnout.

⑧ Strategy 1: Boss Pet — Maximize Kills Per Hour

⚡ Kill Speed is Everything

Your chance at a boss pet is proportional to kills per hour, not drop rate alone. A 1/1,000 boss at 10 kills/hour (100 hours expected) is worse than a 1/3,000 boss at 60 kills/hour (50 hours expected). Strategy:

  • Upgrade gear: Each DPS upgrade reduces hours-to-pet. A twisted bow at Kree'arra cuts kill time in half.
  • Use max gear where possible: If you're pet hunting, GP efficiency doesn't matter — kills per hour matters.
  • Learn tick manipulation: 3-tick methods at skilling or 1-tick flicking at bosses can add 30-50% more kills/actions.
  • Bring the right inventory: Minimal food, maximum damage. Learn the boss well enough to prayer-flick or dodge.

⑧ Strategy 2: Skilling Pet — Stack Multiple Rolls

🔄 Action Count Over Everything

For skilling pets, the key is actions per hour (or XP per hour). Higher actions = more rolls = more pet chances:

  • 3-tick methods: Teaks (Woodcutting), Barbarian Fishing, Granite (Mining) — these can double your actions/hour.
  • Piety/Rigour/Augury: For skilling? No. But for bossing, yes — faster kills = more rolls.
  • Wintertodt crates: Open crates on your main to stack multiple rolls at once. You can bank hundreds and open in one go.
  • Tree runs: The limit is how many runs you can do per day. Do 2-3 tree runs daily. Use ultra-expensive compost + tree payment for guaranteed growth.

⑧ Strategy 3: The "No-Life" Method — Focus One Pet

If you truly want one specific pet, commit to nothing else. Studies of pet hunters show that focused grinding reduces emotional burnout compared to "I'll just do a few kills per day".

  • Set a daily KC goal: "I will do 500 Vorkath kills per week" — track it in a spreadsheet.
  • Watch/Listen to content: OSRS YT/Netflix/Podcasts — pet hunting is a second-screen activity.
  • Take breaks: Pet hunting is long. Take a week off if you're feeling burnt out. The pet will still be there.

⑧ Strategy 4: Use the Collection Log + Tracker

The Collection Log (accessible via the Quest tab > Collection Log) tracks your pet drops. Use Runelite's Pet Info plugin to:

  • Track your current KC for each boss
  • View your estimated pet chance (percentage based on KC)
  • See how many thresholds you've passed
  • Keep a "dry streak" tracker for motivation

⑧ Strategy 5: Ironman-Specific Advice

Ironmen pet hunt the same way as mains, but with some differences:

  • No buying supplies: You must gather everything yourself — this limits how many kills/hr you can sustain.
  • Skilling pets are more natural: Ironmen train skills longer (often past 99 for resources), so skilling pets come more passively.
  • Boss pets take longer: Lower DPS due to gear restrictions means fewer kills/hour.
  • Drop rate is the same: Ironmen have the same pet rates as mains — they just kill slower.
💡 The "Beast of Burden" Method

For bosses like GWD (1/5,000), bring a Beast of Burden (terrorbird/war tortoise/pack yak) filled with food. This triples your trip length. Longer trips = fewer banking trips = more kills per hour. At Bandos, a BoB with 28 food can mean 10 extra kills per trip.

Here's a recommended tracking system for dedicated pet hunters:

📋 Pet Hunting Spreadsheet

  • Column 1: Pet name + boss/skill
  • Column 2: Current KC / XP (update after each session)
  • Column 3: Base rate + threshold info
  • Column 4: Estimated % chance so far (use Wiki calculator)
  • Column 5: Kills today / kills this week (motivation tracker)
  • Column 6: Notable loot obtained (keeps the grind interesting)

Many pet hunters aim for 1 pet per month. A slower pace (200-500 kills/day) leads to less burnout than 1,000+ kills/day sprints.

💡 Collection Log Tip: Recoloring Pets

Some pets can be recolored by using specific items on them. For example, Kalphite Princess can be toggled between green and blue forms. Rift Guardian and Rock Golem can be recolored with the corresponding elemental/mineral items. Check the Wiki for each pet's recolor options!

⑩ Pet Hunting FAQ

Can I get multiple pets from the same boss?

No. Once you receive a pet from a boss, you cannot receive it again. If you roll the pet again, you'll get a pet dupe notification ("You have a funny feeling like you would have been followed...") but no additional pet drops. You can, however, get multiple different pets from the same area — like the Dagannoth Kings (3 separate pets) or GWD (4 separate pets).

Do thresholds carry over if I stop killing a boss?

Yes. Your threshold progress is permanent and tracked on your account. If you kill 2,000 Giant Mole in 2024 and come back in 2026, you'll still be 2,000 KC towards your first threshold. The rate improves and stays improved forever.

How many pets can I have following me?

One at a time. You can only have one pet following you out of your pet menu. However, you can store any number of pets in your Pet House (Menagerie) in your Player-Owned House. You can also fill your inventory with pet rocks for a funny screenshot, but only one pet can actively follow.

What's the best pet drop rate in the game?

Skotos (from Skotizo) at 1/65. The worst drop rate among boss pets is 1/5,000 (Corporeal Beast, GWD bosses, Dagannoth Kings). Among skilling pets, Tangleroot has the best per-action rate (~1/7,500 checks) but is time-gated by growth times, while Rift Guardian takes the most hours on average.

Can I get skilling pets at level 1?

Technically yes, practically no. The pet roll formula scales with your level. At level 1, your effective rate is roughly 1% of the level-99 rate (e.g., the Beaver at level 1 Woodcutting would be ~1/5,800,000 per chop). Getting a skilling pet before level 50 is extremely rare — only a handful of documented cases exist.

Do XP lamps / quest rewards give skilling pets?

No. Skilling pets are only rolled from actions — not from XP lamps, quest rewards, random events, or any other non-action XP sources. You must actually perform the skill to get the pet.

What happens if my pet is killed in the Wilderness?

If your pet is out and you die in PvP (Wilderness), your pet behaves like any other item — it drops to the ground and can be picked up by the PKer or you. Always insure your pet with Probita in Ardougne (or the Pet Insurance NPC). For a small fee (500K GP per pet), your pet is insured. If lost, you can reclaim it from Probita for 1M GP. This only covers once per insurance claim.

Can I trade pets?

No. All pets in OSRS are untradeable. They are permanently bound to your account upon reception. The only exception is that ironmen CANNOT drop-trade pets to their main account — the mechanic is intentionally disabled for ironmen.

Are pet rates affected by loot share / co-op?

In most group bosses, the pet is rolled individually for each player. For raids (CoX, ToB, ToA), the pet rate scales with your personal points/MVP status. More points = better pet chance. In mass worlds (Corp, GWD), the pet rolls per player with rates proportional to damage dealt.
