1. Sailing Quest Line Overview

Unlike skills like Slayer or Hunter, Sailing launched with a focused quest line rather than dozens of interconnected quests. As of the November 2025 release, there are 2 released quests and 1 confirmed upcoming quest:

2. Pandemonium — Entry Quest

The Pandemonium quest is your introduction to Sailing. It has no requirements — any account can start it immediately after the skill's release.

📍 How to Start

Head to Port Sarim and speak with Will and Anne at the docks. They will guide you through the introductory sequence, teaching you basic navigation and ship mechanics.

📋 Quest Steps

  1. Talk to Will and Anne at Port Sarim docks
  2. Learn basic navigation controls on a practice boat
  3. Complete your first short voyage
  4. Interact with your ship's facilities (Cargo Hold, Captain's Log)
  5. Return to Port Sarim to complete the quest

🎁 Rewards

RewardDetails
Starts you at level 6 SailingSkips the first 5 levels entirely — you go straight to the fun part
Raft — starter shipBasic vessel with Cargo Hold facility pre-installed
Captain's LogTracks all your voyages, charting progress, and quest progress
💡 After Pandemonium — Talk to Everyone

Once you complete Pandemonium, take a few minutes to talk to every NPC at Port Sarim docks:
• Shipwright — ship upgrades and repairs
• Notice Board — courier and bounty tasks
• Chartin' Charles — Sea Charting tutorial (in the Pandemonium cave area)
This will save you hours of confusion later.

3. Troubled Tortugans — Main Quest

Troubled Tortugans is the main story quest in the Sailing line. It involves helping a group of Tortugans (turtle-like humanoids) and unlocks access to the Great Conch area — a key location for mid-level Sailing content.

📋 Quest Details

DetailInformation
TypeMain quest
RequirementsLevel 45 Sailing (confirmed via OSRS Wiki)
LengthMedium — multiple stages
LocationGreat Conch and surrounding sea areas

🎁 Rewards

  • Great Conch access — a new sea area with its own content
  • Gryphon Slayer unlock — allows Slayer assignments for Gryphons (level 51 Slayer, 45 Sailing)
  • Sailing XP — significant quest completion reward
  • Shellbane Gryphon access — the Slayer boss variant becomes available

⚔️ Unlocked Content

Completing Troubled Tortugans gives you access to:

GryphonsSlayer monster (51 Slayer, 45 Sailing)Gryphon Feathers for Horn of Plenty
Shellbane GryphonSlayer bossDrops Belle's Folly (tarnished stab weapon)
Belle's FollyMid-game stab weapon (65 Attack)Can be polished at Giant's Foundry (70 Smithing)
💰 Belle's Folly — Worth the Quest

Belle's Folly is a tier gap-filler weapon requiring 65 Attack, sitting between the Dragon Sword (60 Attack) and the Zamorakian Hasta (70 Attack). The tarnished version drops from the Shellbane Gryphon — polish it at the Giant's Foundry polishing wheel with 70 Smithing and completion of the Sleeping Giants quest. For Ironmen, this is a particularly valuable mid-game upgrade. Even for main accounts, it's worth doing the quest just for the Slayer unlock.

4. Red Reef — Upcoming Sequel

Jagex released Red Reef on May 20, 2026 as the sequel to Troubled Tortugans. Here are the confirmed details from the official Jagex news post:

DetailInformation
StatusReleased ✅ (May 20, 2026)
TypeQuest sequel to Troubled Tortugans (Tortugan series #2)
RequirementsTroubled Tortugans completed, Level 52 Sailing, Level 48 Smithing, recommended 65+ combat
Start LocationElder Raley on the Great Conch (Summer Shore, south east house)
Rewards2 quest points, 15,000 Sailing XP, 5,000 Smithing XP, Sacred Grove access (Camphor Trees), Bosun's Bench Schematic

🔧 Key Unlock: Bosun's Bench

The Bosun's Bench is a new ship facility unlocked by Red Reef. It allows crew members to automatically repair your ship over time using repair kits from the cargo hold. Build requirements: Level 63 Sailing, Level 54 Construction, 6 Camphor Planks, 24 Adamantite Nails, 2 Lead Bars, and the Bosun's Bench Schematic.

⚠️ Important Note for Hardcore Ironmen

Ship sinking does NOT count as a dangerous death for HCIM. However, if your character dies while on the ship, that DOES count as a dangerous death.

5. Recommended Quest Order

Since there are only 2 released quests (plus 1 upcoming), the order is straightforward. However, there are good reasons to complete them in a specific sequence to maximize your account progression.

📋 Optimal Order

OrderQuestDo It ForWhen
1PandemoniumYour first ship, level 6 Sailing, unlocks all Sailing contentImmediately — as soon as you start Sailing
2Troubled TortugansGreat Conch access, Gryphon Slayer tasks, Belle's FollyLevel 45 Sailing (when you can access the area)
3Red Reef (released May 2026)Sacred Grove access, Bosun's Bench auto-repair, Sailing XPLevel 52 Sailing, Level 48 Smithing

🎯 Why This Order Works

The progression is intentionally designed by Jagex to follow your Sailing leveling path:

  • Pandemonium first — it teaches you the basics and gives you the essential tools (Raft + Captain's Log)
  • Troubled Tortugans at level 45+ — by this point you should have a Skiff or Sloop, upgraded hull, and enough navigation skill to handle the journey to the Great Conch
  • Red Reef — requires level 52 Sailing and 48 Smithing. Unlocks auto-repair (Bosun's Bench) at 63 Sailing.

6. Sailing Quest FAQs

Do I need to complete Pandemonium to train Sailing?

Yes. Pandemonium is mandatory — it's how you get your first ship (Raft) and Captain's Log. Without it, you have no way to sail. The quest takes about 10-15 minutes and has no requirements.

What level Sailing do I need for Troubled Tortugans?

Jagex has not published an exact Sailing level requirement, but community reports suggest it's accessible to players around level 45-50 Sailing with a Skiff or better ship. [DATA_UNVERIFIED] You'll also need a ship capable of navigating moderate hazards.

What is the Great Conch?

The Great Conch is a sea area unlocked after completing Troubled Tortugans. It contains new Sea Charting locations, access to Gryphon Slayer tasks, and the Shellbane Gryphon boss fight.

Is Belle's Folly worth getting?

Yes, particularly for Ironmen. Belle's Folly is a mid-game stab weapon that fills the gap between the Dragon Sword and the Zamorakian Hasta. It drops in a "tarnished" state and requires 70 Smithing + the Sleeping Giants quest to polish at Giant's Foundry.

When is Red Reef coming out?

Jagex has announced Red Reef as a 2026 update but has not given a specific release date. We will update this guide with confirmed details as soon as Jagex announces them.

Will there be more Sailing quests after Red Reef?

Jagex has only confirmed Red Reef as the next Sailing quest. Given that Sailing is an expansion-scale skill, it's reasonable to expect more quests in future updates, but we will only report what Jagex officially announces.
