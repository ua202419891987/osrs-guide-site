🔧 OSRS Summer Sweep-Up 2026 Complete Guide — Gear, Gauntlet & Meta Changes

The Summer Sweep-Up 2026 (June 4) is the largest QoL and balance update since Project Rebalance. It reworks 15+ weapons and armor pieces, overhauls Gauntlet preparation, improves Hunter and Agility training, adds 7 new shortcuts, and introduces new early-game equipment. This guide covers every change with confirmed numbers from Jagex.

📅 Update Guide · June 2026 · 18 min read · Data sourced from Jagex Official & OSRS Wiki

⚡ Quick Verdict

Biggest winners: Sanguinesti Staff (20% heal rate, +8 damage on heal, cheaper runes), Inquisitor set (standalone crush bonuses, no full set needed), Soulreaper Axe (max stacks 5→3 but each +10%), Gauntlet runners (T1 weapons free, 27% faster runs).

Only real nerf for PvM: Burning Claws spec energy 30%→35%. All other nerfs are PvP-only (Eclipse Atlatl attack speed, Soulflame Horn spec accuracy).

Important for returning players: Sailing was NOT added in this update — it was already released on November 19, 2025. This guide covers the separate Summer Sweep-Up rebalance. If you haven't played since before November 2025, read our Coming Back Guide first.

1. 🏹 Gauntlet & Corrupted Gauntlet Overhaul

The Gauntlet preparation phase received a complete rework to reduce RNG frustration and speed up entry times. These are among the most impactful changes in the update.

① Fixed Miniboss Room Layout

Previously, the three miniboss rooms were randomly placed, forcing exploration. Now they are fixed to North, East, South, and West — you always know where to go. The type of miniboss is still random (Bear, Beast, etc.), but the location never changes. Saves ~15-20 seconds per run.

② Miniboss Attack Range Reduced

All minibosses had their maximum attack range reduced from 16 tiles to 10 tiles. If you can't see them on screen, they can't hit you. Massively reduces random deaths for learners.

③ First Kill Guarantees Weapon Frame

The first non-miniboss enemy you kill is now guaranteed to drop a Weapon Frame. No more "bad RNG = restart" situations. Once you have 3 frames or have crafted weapons, no more frames will drop.

💡 New Gauntlet Strategy

Go T1 weapon (free!) + T1 armor, then immediately hunt the boss. Don't chase T3 gear unless you're going for a specific speedrun time. Check North first for the nearest miniboss. Resources stack now, so you can pick up everything without worry.

3. 📉 Nerfs & Adjustments

Only 3 items were nerfed, and 2 of them are PvP-only effects. PvM players are barely affected:

ItemNerfScopeImpact
Burning ClawsSpec energy: 30% → 35%PvM + PvPMinor. Still best-in-slot spec weapon for PvM. Get 2 specs per bar instead of 3.
Eclipse AtlatlAttack speed -1 tick unless wearing full Eclipse setPvP onlyForces PvP builds to commit to full set or use another weapon.
Soulflame HornSpec accuracy: +100% → +50%PvP onlyReduces one-shot potential. Still usable but no longer guaranteed hit.

① Burning Claws Spec Change — Detailed Analysis

From 30% to 35% spec energy means you now get 2 specs per 100% energy (with 30% remaining) instead of 3 specs per 90% energy. Over a boss fight, this translates to roughly 15-20% fewer spec opportunities per trip. However, the damage per spec is unchanged — Burning Claws remains the best spec weapon for most PvM scenarios. Dragon Claws and ZCB are viable alternatives if you prefer more frequent specs.

💡 PvM Takeaway

If you use Burning Claws for PvM, expect slightly fewer specs per trip but identical damage per spec. No need to sell — they're still top tier. For PvP players, the Eclipse Atlatl and Soulflame Horn changes are more significant and may require gear adjustments.

4. 🛡️ Inquisitor Set Complete Breakdown

The Inquisitor's Armor set received one of the most comprehensive reworks in the update. The key change: set effects were removed and applied to individual pieces, meaning you no longer need the full set to benefit.

PieceChangeEffect
Inquisitor's Great Helm+2 crush accuracy, +2 strengthAdditionally gives +0.5% crush damage and accuracy on its own (no set needed)
Inquisitor's Hauberk+4 crush accuracy (matches Oathplate)+1% crush damage and accuracy on its own (formerly +0.5%, doubled)
Inquisitor's PlateskirtRebalanced to match Oathplate+1% crush damage and accuracy on its own (formerly +0.5%, doubled)
Inquisitor's Mace+7 strength, +7 crush accuracyGuarantees +1 max hit in all setups, +2 in some. Now competitive with Scythe on crush-weak targets

🎯 What This Means for Your Gear

Previously, Inquisitor was dead content unless you had the full set + Mace. Now you can mix and match:

  • Inquisitor's Hauberk + Bandos Tassets + Inquisitor's Mace = crush bonus on chest and weapon
  • Inquisitor's Mace + any other armor = still gets crush bonus from the mace alone
  • Any single Inquisitor piece gives standalone crush benefits — no set required
This makes Inquisitor gear incredibly flexible for any crush-weak boss (Vyrewatch Sentinels, Tormented Demons, Tekton, etc.).

① CoX Prayer Scroll Reroll Mechanic

A new "reroll" mechanic was added for Arcane and Dextrous prayer scrolls in CoX. When you receive a scroll you already own, you can choose to reroll it. The reroll has a 1/x chance of hitting the unique table again (in which case the scroll is removed and replaced). If it fails, the scroll is simply deleted. Works for both normal and Challenge Mode CoX. This is a significant buff for Ironmen who have been hunting specific scrolls.

② Nightmare of Ashihama (Feedback Phase)

The dev team is considering larger changes to Nightmare, including moving Volatile and Eldritch orbs to other NPCs, making the Nightmare drop a generic orb charged by "essence" from other bosses, and increasing the Harmonised orb drop rate from 1/1,600 to ~1/533 at Phosani's. These are still in the feedback phase and not implemented yet.

9. ❓ Summer Sweep-Up FAQs

Is Summer Sweep-Up a separate update from Sailing?

Yes. Sailing was released as a standalone skill on November 19, 2025 — over six months before Summer Sweep-Up. The Sweep-Up is a separate QoL and balance update. If you quit before November 2025, you missed Sailing. If you quit before June 4, 2026, you missed the Sweep-Up gear rebalance. See our Coming Back Guide to catch up on everything.

Which gear buff matters most for me?

It depends on your level: If you're 70-90 combat, the Master Wand buff (+10% magic damage) is huge. If you're end-game, the Sanguinesti Staff buff and Soulreaper Axe rework are most impactful. If you do crush-weak bosses, Inquisitor rework is the biggest change since it no longer requires the full set.

Should I change my Gauntlet strategy?

Yes. Go for T1 weapon (free!) + T1 armor, then rush the boss. Don't aim for T3 armor anymore — it's intentionally less efficient now. Always check North first for the first miniboss. Resources stack, so pick up everything.

Did any items get nerfed that I should sell?

Only Burning Claws got a PvM-relevant nerf (spec energy 30%→35%). If you use them often, you might consider Dragon Claws or ZCB as alternatives. All other nerfs are PvP-only. Dinh's Bulwark dropped in price (~22M→15M) but this was more from market dynamics than a direct nerf. Inquisitor and Soulreaper Axe prices are rising post-buff.

Are the new Agility shortcuts worth training for?

If you have 70+ Agility, the "no crossbow needed" changes are amazing — permanently free up an inventory slot. The 7 new shortcuts are situational but nice QoL (especially Arceuus Library for book restoring at 62 Agility). Hallowed Sepulchre being accessible 5 levels earlier (77 for floor 4, 87 for floor 5) is a significant buff for mid-level Agility training.

Is Inquisitor worth buying now?

Yes, especially the Inquisitor's Mace (+7 strength, +7 crush accuracy) and Hauberk (+1% crush damage and accuracy standalone). You no longer need the full set. Even one piece gives crush bonuses. For crush-weak bosses (Tekton, Tormented Demons, Vyrewatch), Inquisitor is now meta. Price has been rising since the buff — buy sooner rather than later.

How did the Gauntlet meta change?

Average completion time dropped from ~6:30 to ~4:45 — a 27% increase in XP/hr. T1 weapons are free (0 shards). Miniboss locations are fixed (N/E/S/W). Resources stack. First kill guarantees a weapon frame. The new optimal strategy is T1 weapon + T1 armor rush.

Did Hunter training get faster or just more AFK?

Both. Deadfall traps now have 2 traps instead of 1, trigger in 4.8s instead of 9s, and wild kebbits are 2.5x easier to catch. Despite a 20% base XP reduction on some creatures, overall XP/hr is up ~60%. Early Hunter (level 1-53) is significantly faster since you can place 2 traps from level 1. The skill is both faster and more AFK.
