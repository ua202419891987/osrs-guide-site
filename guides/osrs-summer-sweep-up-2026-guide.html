OSRS Summer Sweep-Up 2026
Complete Guide

Every major change explained: Gauntlet QoL overhaul, 15+ gear buffs/nerfs, new Agility shortcuts, money making updates, and the new PvM meta. Updated June 11, 2026.

📅 June 11, 2026 ⏱️ 18 min read 📊 Update Guide

🔥 Why This Update Matters

Summer Sweep-Up 2026 (June 4) is the largest quality-of-life and game-balance update since Project Rebalance. It fundamentally changes how Gauntlet works, buffs multiple underused weapons, and adds 7 new Agility shortcuts that will save you hundreds of hours of run time. If you haven't logged in since June 4 — everything has changed.

1. Gauntlet & Corrupted Gauntlet — Complete Overhaul

The Gauntlet preparation phase has been completely reworked to reduce RNG frustration and speed up entry times. These are the biggest changes in the entire update.

1.1 Fixed Semi-Boss Room Layout

Previously, the three semi-boss rooms were randomly placed in any of the four cardinal directions, forcing you to explore the entire map every run. Now, the semi-boss rooms are fixed to North, East, South, West — you always know exactly where to go. The type of semi-boss is still random (Beast, Bear, etc.), but the location never changes.

Impact: Saves ~15-20 seconds per run. Over 1,000 Gauntlet runs, that's ~5 hours saved.

1.2 Semi-Boss Attack Range Nerfed

All semi-bosses had their maximum attack range reduced from 16 tiles to 10 tiles. Previously, you could get sniped from off-screen by a ranged or magic semi-boss while crossing the map. Now, if you can't see them on your screen, they can't hit you.

Impact: Massively reduces "random death" frustration. Much more forgiving for learners.

1.3 First Kill Guaranteed Weapon Frame

The first non-semi-boss enemy you kill in a Gauntlet run is now guaranteed to drop a Weapon Frame. Previously this was RNG-based and could force a restart if you got unlucky.

Impact: No more "bad RNG = restart" situations. Every run is now viable from the start.

1.6 Weapon Frame Drop Limit

If you already have 3 weapon frames or crafted weapons in your inventory, no more will drop. This prevents inventory clutter and wasted drops.

3. Gear Nerfs — What Got Hit

Only 3 items were nerfed, and all three are PvP-only effects — PvM players are completely unaffected:

ItemNerfScope

For PvM players: The only real nerf is Burning Claws costing 35% spec instead of 30%. This means you get 2 specs per 100% instead of 3 per 90%. Still the best spec weapon in the game.

4. Money Making Changes

4.1 Guardians of the Rift (GotR) — Abyssal Needle Buyable

You can now buy Abyssal Needles for 750 Abyssal Pearls from the reward shop. Previously, you had to get lucky with the drop. This is a huge buff for Ironmen and main accounts who want the Needle but have bad RNG.

Also: Abyssal Lanterns now work outside the Gauntlet (only Willow, Blisterwood, Magic, and Redwood lanterns have the external effect). This makes farming Catalytic runes much more AFK.

4.2 Tempoross — New Skip Options

Fish Barrels and Tackle Boxes can now be bought for 6,000 Spirit Flakes each. This saves inventory slots and allows for longer AFK sessions without banking.

4.3 Coral Farming — Life Cycle Extended

ChangeOldNew

Impact: At 99 Faring with Celestial Nets, Faring Potions, and Attas Seeds, your yield is slightly lower. But at low levels (50-80), you get significantly more coral per cycle. This makes training Faring via coral much less painful.

Also: Thieving caskets now have a 1/20 chance (down from 1/15) to give Coral Fragments — a slight nerf to Thieving GP/hr, but coral prices should stabilize.

4.4 Aerial Fishing — Pearl Costs Reduced

Impact: Getting the full Angler outfit now costs 200 pearls instead of 400. This is a ~20-hour time save for new Faring training accounts.

Also: Molch Pearl shop now sells Shark Bait — using Shark Bait reduces the fishing tick interval from 5 ticks to 4 ticks (~400 GP cost for +20% speed). This makes Aerial Fishing noticeably faster.

4.5 Heroic Clue Scrolls — Better Drop Rate

Drop rate improved from 1/1,400 to 1/1,050 (~25% increase). If you're killing bosses that drop Heroic Clues, you'll get ~33% more Clues per hour.

6. PvM Quality-of-Life Improvements

6.1 Phosani's Nightmare — SotE Rework

The Knight Waves training ground now preserves your progress when you leave. Previously, leaving = reset. Now you can do a few waves, leave, and come back later. This makes getting the full Knight Waves reward much less of a grind.

6.2 Tombs of Amascut (ToA)& Chambers of Xeric (CoX) — Scroll Re-Roll Idea

The devs are considering a mechanic to allow re-rolling the "Olm's Scroll" drop in Chambers of Xeric. Nothing is confirmed yet, but the fact that they're discussing it suggests a future update may address the frustration of getting useless scrolls.

7. Other Important Changes

7.1 Early-Game Run Energy QoL

Completing any quest now fully restores your Run Energy and clears all poison/disease effects. Additionally, multiple early-game monsters now drop 1-3 dose Energy Potions. Energy Potion dose restoration increased from 10% to 15% per dose.

Impact: Early game (levels 1-40) is now much less tedious. You'll rarely run out of run energy.

7.2 Hunter Skill Overhaul

8. New Meta — What to Use Now

⚔️ Best-in-Slot Changes (June 2026 Meta)

8.1 Ranged Meta

No changes to BiS ranged setup. Twisted Bow remains best for long-range; Dragon Hunter Crossbow is still best for dragons. The Venator Bow target-selection fix makes it slightly more appealing for multi-target scenarios (e.g., Demonic Gorillas, Slayer tasks).

8.2 Magic Meta

Sanguinesti Staff is now viable for high-level PvM. With the 20% heal rate, +8 damage on heal, and -33% rune cost, it's now competitive with the Trident of the Swamp for sustained fights. For bursting/barraging, the Ancient Sceptre variants (+10% magic damage) are now strictly better than before.

Recommended Magic Setup (post-update):

  • Long fights (Raid bosses, Nightmare): Sanguinesti Staff > Trident of the Swamp
  • Bursting/Barraging: Ancient Sceptre (any variant) > Master Wand > Trident
  • Budget magic (mid-game): Master Wand is now a legit option (was previously trash)

8.3 Melee Meta

Inquisitor's Armor is now much more flexible. You can mix Inquisitor pieces with Oathplate or Bandos and still get the crush bonus. The Inquisitor's Mace is now best-in-slot for all crush-weak bosses (not just some).

Soulreaper Axe is now a top-tier option for slow, high-damage weapons. With the 3-stack system (each stack = +10% damage), it rivals the Abyssal Tentacle for DPS — and doesn't degrade.

Ghrazi Rapier and Blade of Saeldor both got +1 max hit. They're now strictly better than the Abyssal Whip for their respective attack styles.

8.4 Gauntlet Meta (Most Important Change)

TheGauntlet meta has completely shifted toward speed-running:

StrategyOld MetaNew Meta (Post-Update)
Prep approachGet T3 armor + T2 weapon (slow, high-shard cost)T1 weapon (free!) + T1-2 armor, rush boss ASAP
Semi-boss roomsExplore entire map to find themGo directly North/East/South/West — fixed locations
Weapon Frame RNGCould get unlucky, forced restartFirst kill = guaranteed frame; no bad RNG runs

Result: Average Gauntlet completion time dropped from ~6:30 to ~4:45 for experienced players. This is a ~27% increase in XP/hr.

9. FAQ

Q: Is the Summer Sweep-Up safe to use on my main account?

A: Yes — this is an official Jagex update. All changes are server-side and affect every account. There's no risk of bans for using the new shortcuts or Gauntlet changes.

Q: Which gear buff matters most for my account?

A: It depends on your level. If you're 70-90 combat, the Master Wand buff is huge. If you're end-game, the Sanguinesti Staff buff and Soulreaper Axe rework are the most impactful. If you do a lot of crush-weak bosses, the Inquisitor's Armor rework is the biggest change.

Q: Should I change my Gauntlet strategy?

A: Yes. Go for T1 weapon (0 shards!) + T1 armor, then rush the boss. Don't try for T3 gear anymore — it's intentionally made less efficient. Also, always check North first for the first semi-boss (it's the closest from the starting room).

Q: Are the new Agility shortcuts worth training for?

A: If you have 70+ Agility, the "no crossbow needed" changes are amazing — you can permanently free up an inventory slot. The new shortcuts are situational but nice QoL (especially the Arceuus Library one for book restoring).

Q: Did they add Sailing yet?

A: Not in this update. Sailing is still in development. The Summer Sweep-Up is a separate QoL/balance update. Sailing will be released as a separate skill later in 2026.

Q: Where can I find more detailed guides for specific content?

A: Check out our other guides linked below. We have full walkthroughs for Gauntlet, all boss guides, and skill training guides — all updated for 2026.
