Skill Training Herblore Budget Updated 2026

Herblore is infamous for being one of the most expensive skills to train in OSRS. However, with careful potion selection and smart ingredient sourcing, you can significantly reduce the cost. This guide covers the cheapest potions to make at every level, full GP cost breakdowns, and special sections for Ironman players who must source herbs from monsters and farming.

Herblore Basics

Herblore works by combining a cleaned herb with a secondary ingredient in a vial of water to create a potion. XP is gained when the potion is completed. The cost of training depends entirely on which potions you make and the current Grand Exchange prices of ingredients.

  • Always use a Zahur (Nardah) to clean herbs in bulk — costs 200 GP per herb but saves significant time.
  • Use the Serum 207 method (level 3) for the very cheapest early XP.
  • Mixing unfinished potions and adding secondaries separately is the standard method.
  • Check real-time Grand Exchange prices before buying in bulk — herb prices fluctuate heavily.
  • Completing Plague's End unlocks the Priffddinas Herblore Shop for convenient supply.

Early Levels: 1–38

The early Herblore levels can be accomplished quickly with minimal spending. The key is choosing the cheapest potions that give adequate XP.

  1. Level 1–3: Make Guam Leaf potions (Attack potions). Only 3 XP needed to reach level 3. Use any spare Guam leaves you have.
  2. Level 3–12: Make Serum 207 (Ashes + clean tarromin + vial of water). This is the cheapest low-level Herblore option if ashes are cheap.
  3. Level 12–26: Strength Potions (Tarromin + Limpwurt Root). Tarromin is one of the cheapest herbs.
  4. Level 26–38: Energy Potions (Harralander + Chocolate Dust). Harralander is inexpensive and the secondary ingredient is cheap.

Mid Levels: 38–72

The mid-level range is where Herblore costs start to rise. Focus on potions with a good XP/GP ratio and try to make potions you will actually use in the game.

Best Low-Cost Mid Level Potions

  • Level 38: Prayer Potions — Ranarr weed + snape grass. Expensive but sell well, partially offsetting cost. ~1,100 GP/potion, 87.5 XP.
  • Level 45: Super Attack — Irit leaf + eye of newt. Moderate cost, good for combat. ~900 GP/potion, 100 XP.
  • Level 50: Superantipoison — Irit leaf + unicorn horn dust. Very cheap secondary. ~800 GP/potion, 106.3 XP.
  • Level 52: Super Energy — Avantoe + mort myre fungus. Good XP, moderate cost.
  • Level 55: Super Strength — Kwuarm + limpwurt root. Better XP rate, moderate cost.

High Levels: 72–99

The high-level range is where the cheapest-per-XP potions become available. Ranging potions are widely considered the best cost-per-XP potion in OSRS for regular play.

Ranging Potions (Level 72) — Best Value

  • Ingredients: Dwarf weed + wine of zamorak
  • XP per potion: 162.5
  • Cost per potion: ~500 – 800 GP (varies with GE prices)
  • Cost per XP: ~3–5 GP/XP — best in game for Members
  • Why cheap: Dwarf weed is one of the least demanded herbs; wine of zamorak is plentiful

Ironman Herb Sourcing Guide

Ironmen cannot buy herbs from the Grand Exchange and must obtain them through farming, monster drops, or activity rewards. Here are the best sources:

Best Herb Farming Patches

  1. Falador Patch — Accessible early, near Falador bank. Plant ranarr or toadflax seeds.
  2. Catherby Patch — One of the most used patches. Close to Camelot and the Catherby bank.
  3. Ardougne Patch — Requires Ardougne Cloak for faster access. Good mid-game option.
  4. Morytania Patch (Canafis) — Requires Priest in Peril. Post-Kourend patch also available.
  5. Hosidius Patch — Located in Hosidius, Kourend. Always disease-free (75% favour required).

Herblore Cost-Cutting Tips

  • Use Zahur in Nardah to clean grimy herbs — costs 200 GP but much faster than manual cleaning.
  • Make potions in bulk batches — right-click the vial and select "make all" to process a full inventory quickly.
  • Sell finished potions on the Grand Exchange to partially recoup costs — prayer, super restore, and ranging potions sell well.
  • The Herblore Cape perk gives a small chance to create an extra dose when mixing potions.
  • Botanist's Outfit (from the Tithe Farm minigame) gives 2.5% bonus Herblore XP — worth getting before the 99 grind.
  • Complete Plague's End to unlock 4-dose potion upgrading in Priffddinas — adds 1 extra dose to 3-dose potions using lergberry drops.
  • Farming patches daily dramatically reduces herb costs for ironmen — do your farm runs every 80 minutes.
