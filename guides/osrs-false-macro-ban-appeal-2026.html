🚨 OSRS False Macro Ban Appeal 2026

Banned for "macroing" but you were at the keyboard the whole time? This is the companion to our prevention guide: a step-by-step walkthrough of appealing a wrongful botting ban through Jagex Support, the evidence that actually gets accounts reinstated, the realistic timeline, and what to do if the first appeal is denied.

🎓 Level: Intermediate
📅 Updated: July 2026 — Account Recovery · 13 min read

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Act fast — the appeal option is usually available for a limited window (commonly ~14 days) after the ban.
  • 📌 Appeal through the official Jagex Support ban-appeal form, never via social-media DMs.
  • 📌 Prove you are human: account age, membership/payment history, and a honest description of your playstyle.
  • 📌 One well-evidenced appeal beats ten angry ones — stay calm and factual.

🚨 1. First Steps After the Ban

You just saw the message. Before you do anything else, slow down — panic leads to mistakes that can hurt your case.

① Read the exact ban reason

The message will state the category: macroing (botting), major/minor offence, or RWT (real-world trading). This guide is for macroing bans you believe are false. Note whether it says temporary or permanent.

② Note the date and the appeal deadline

The ban screen shows when the appeal option closes. Write it down. Jagex commonly opens the appeal for around 14 days; after that the button may disappear and recovery gets much harder.

③ Stop logging in repeatedly

Hammering the login or spamming attempts can look like ban evasion or further suspicious behavior. One calm check to read the message is enough.

④ Screenshot everything

Capture the ban message, the date, your account display name, and any recent activity logs you have. You will reference these later.

💡 Stay Calm, Stay Honest

Do not admit to anything you did not do, and do not exaggerate. Jagex reviewers can see account data you cannot. A calm, accurate, human-sounding appeal is far more persuasive than outrage or a manufactured story.

📋 2. What Evidence Jagex Values

The reviewer's job is to decide whether a human or a script was at the controls. They weigh signals that are hard for a bot operator to fake:

SignalWhy It HelpsHow to Surface It
Account age & longevityLong-lived accounts are rarely freshly scripted botsMention creation year; old accounts self-evident
Membership payment historyPersonally-paid subs prove a real, invested ownerReference transaction IDs / cardholder name
Consistent single ownerNo signs of the account being sold or sharedState you are the only user, same region
Plausible human playstyleDescribes breaks, variety, real-life contextExplain your actual routine honestly
No RWT / no account tradingRemoves the "mule/bought account" suspicionConfirm all GP earned in-game

The strongest single piece of evidence is usually a continuous, personally-funded membership history. A bot farm does not keep an account subscribed for years on one person's card. Gather those records before you write the appeal.

✍️ 3. Step-by-Step Appeal Process

Jagex handles ban appeals through its official support site (support.runescape.com), not through the game client or social media. Follow these steps in order:

StepActionDetail
1Open the appealUse the appeal link shown in your ban message, or go to the official support site and sign in with the banned account.
2Select the characterPick the specific profile that was banned (relevant for Ironman / group accounts).
3Choose "Appeal a ban"Do not use the generic "submit a ticket" route for a ban — use the dedicated appeal flow.
4Write the reasonPolite, factual, deny botting, explain your legit behavior (see Section 4).
5Attach evidenceUse the evidence/notes field; follow up by email ifneeded with payment proof.
6Submit & save the referenceNote the ticket/reference number and the submitted date.
7Wait for the emailResponses arrive by email — check spam folders.

🎯 What to Write in the Reason Box

  • State clearly: "I did not use any bot, macro, or automation software on this account."
  • Describe what you were actually doing (e.g., "fletching yew longbows while watching streams, 1–2 hours most evenings").
  • Mention your official client (RuneLite default hub) and that you used no auto-input plugins.
  • If you used a VPN, say so and explain why (privacy/travel) — honesty reads as human.
  • Keep it to a few sentences. Reviewers read volume; clarity wins.

📎 4. Evidence to Attach With Your Appeal

You may not be able to upload files directly in every appeal form. Where you can, attach; otherwise summarize in the text and follow up via the support email with proof.

① Membership / payment proof

A bank or card statement showing the membership charge. Redact the full card number — Jagex only needs to confirm the cardholder name and that the subscription is yours. Transaction IDs from the Jagex store also work.

② A plain description of your routine

Explain your real-life context: when you play, for how long, what you were training, and that you take normal breaks. This directly counters the "24/7 bot" fingerprint.

③ Your plugin list

If asked, list only official RuneLite default-hub plugins. Explicitly state you used no auto-clicker, auto-prayer, or third-party-hub plugin. See Prevention Guide → for the safe/risky breakdown.

④ VPN / network explanation (if applicable)

If a VPN or shared IP was involved, explain it was for privacy or travel — not to run scripts or rotate proxies. This preempts the network-flag concern.

⚠️ Don't Over-Attach

One or two solid proof points (payment history + honest routine) beat a wall of screenshots. Reviewers are trained to weigh a few high-signal items, not volume.

⏳ 5. Appeal Timeline & What to Expect

Timelines vary, but here is the typical shape for a 2026 appeal:

StageTypical TimeWhat Happens
SubmissionInstantYou get a confirmation and a reference number.
Initial reviewA few days to ~2 weeksA Game Integrity reviewer examines the account data and your note.
Decision emailWith the review"Upheld" (ban stays) or "Overturned" (account restored).
ReinstatementUsually 24–48h after overturnAccount unlocked; you can log in again.
  • If overturned: Celebrate, then immediately harden the account (Section 7).
  • If upheld: It is often treated as final — but you can submit a new ticket with fresh, stronger evidence (see escalation).
  • Don't spam: Submitting the identical appeal repeatedly can delay or muddy your case. Each follow-up should add something new.
💡 Manage Your Expectations

Jagex will not reveal the exact detection telemetry (that would help real botters). You will get a category, not a timestamped log. Focus your energy on proving "human owner," not on demanding the raw data.

📈 6. Escalation If the First Appeal Is Denied

A denial is not always the end — especially for a genuine false positive. Escalate the right way:

  • Gather stronger proof. Payment receipts, long membership history, and a detailed, believable routine are your best lever. A denial often means the first submission lacked high-signal evidence.
  • Open a new support ticket referencing your original appeal reference number, and attach the new evidence explicitly.
  • Use official channels only. The support site is the path that reaches reviewers. Public posts (including @JagexMods on social media) are for visibility at most, never the actual appeal route.
  • Stay factual and polite. Anger in a follow-up reduces the chance a human re-reads your case. A calm, evidence-led message gets re-reviewed.
  • Accept that some bans are final. If multiple evidenced appeals are upheld, Jagex has concluded the account violated the rules. At that point, focus on keeping any other accounts clean per the Prevention Guide.

🚫 Escalation Don'ts

  • Don't create a second account just to appeal the first — that can itself breach rules.
  • Don't threaten or demand; it weakens your credibility.
  • Don't claim "a friend used a bot on my account" — that still means the account broke the rules.

🔒 7. After Reinstatement: Lock Down Your Account

Getting the account back is only half the job. Make sure it can't be flagged again — by you or by a hijacker:

  • Change your password immediately and use a unique, strong one.
  • Enable the Jagex authenticator (or a linked authenticator app) so no one else can log in and generate weird behavior.
  • Remove any risky plugins and confirm you are on official RuneLite only.
  • Review where you log in from. If a VPN or shared IP was involved, switch to a consistent residential connection (see VPN Login Fix →).
  • Adopt the prevention habits from the companion guide so your behavioral fingerprint stays human.

🐛 8. Appeal FAQs

How long do I have to appeal a macro ban?

The in-game/web appeal option is typically open for around 14 days from the ban date, though this can vary. Appeal as soon as you have your evidence together — waiting until the last day risks missing the window and makes recovery much harder.

Can I appeal more than once?

Your initial appeal is the primary review. After a denial you can submit a new support ticket, but it should contain new, stronger evidence (payment history, detailed routine). Re-sending the identical appeal repeatedly does not help and can slow things down.

What if my appeal is denied?

Gather higher-signal proof — especially a long, personally-paid membership history — and open a follow-up ticket referencing your original reference number. Some bans are ultimately upheld as final, but many genuine false positives are overturned on a well-evidenced second look.

Will Jagex tell me exactly why I was flagged?

They will confirm the category (macroing) but generally will not share the underlying detection telemetry. Revealing that data would help real bot developers. Write your appeal to prove you are a human owner rather than demanding the raw logs.

I used a VPN — should I mention it in the appeal?

Yes, honestly. Explain it was for privacy or travel, not to run scripts or rotate proxies. Concealing it looks worse if they already see the network flag; explaining it calmly shows a real person with a normal reason.
