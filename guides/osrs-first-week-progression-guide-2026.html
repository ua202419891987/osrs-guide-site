OSRS First Week Progression Guide — Day-by-Day Roadmap (2026)

Beginner Roadmap Progression Updated 2026

Welcome to Gielinor, adventurer! Your first week in Old School RuneScape shapes everything that follows. This isn't just a list of things to do — it's your personal coach for the next seven days. Follow this day-by-day roadmap and you'll transform from a level 3 noob standing in Lumbridge to a combat-ready player with solid stats, finished quests, and a growing bank account. Let's get to work.

Before You Start — Mindset & Preparation

OSRS is a marathon, not a sprint. Your first week sets habits that will serve you for years. Before you even create your account, internalize these three golden rules that separate successful players from those who quit after two days.

🏆 The Three Golden Rules

  • Golden Rule #1: Always have a goal when you log in. Aimless play is the #1 cause of burnout. This guide provides those goals — stick to them and you'll see progress every single session.
  • Golden Rule #2: Quests give more XP per hour than any skill training. A single quest can give you more XP than three hours of grinding. Always prioritize quest rewards over mindless skilling.
  • Golden Rule #3: Membership multiplies everything. F2P is a tutorial. Member content gives you 10x the training methods, money makers, and fun. Aim to earn enough for a bond (7 days = ~1.3M GP, 14 days = ~2.3M GP) by the end of week 1 or 2.

⚙️ Account Setup Checklist

  • Create your account (or use an existing fresh account if you have one).
  • Choose your game mode: Normal (recommended for first account), Ironman (self-sufficient challenge), Hardcore Ironman (one life only — not recommended for first-timers).
  • Set your interface: Modern mode is recommended for beginners (cleaner UI, easier to read). Legacy mode is available if you prefer the old-school feel.
  • Pick your username wisely: You'll be stuck with it forever. Make it something you're proud to have above your head in crowded worlds.

Once your account is created, you'll spawn on Tutorial Island. Don't skip it! Even if you've played before, the tutorial teaches the absolute basics and gives you a small head start.

Day 0 — Tutorial Island & Setup (30-60 min)

📍 Tutorial Island Walkthrough

Tutorial Island is your introduction to Gielinor. Completing it is mandatory for new accounts, and it teaches you the fundamentals: movement, combat, skilling, questing, and the bank. Don't rush — read the dialogue. The information is genuinely useful.

  • Combat Instructor: Learn to attack, use magic, and range. You'll finish with level 3 in Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, and Hitpoints.
  • Mining & Smithing Instructor: Mine tin/copper, smelt bronze bars, smith a bronze dagger. Basic crafting loop.
  • Cooking Instructor: Cook shrimp without burning them. Cooking is essential for sustaining yourself in combat.
  • Fishing Instructor: Catch shrimp with a small net. Fishing + Cooking = free food forever.
  • Prayer Instructor: Bury bones to gain Prayer XP. Prayer levels unlock protection prayers that are crucial for survival.
  • Quest Instructor: Complete a mini-quest to learn the quest interface. Quests are the fastest XP in the game.
  • Bank Instructor: Learn to deposit and withdraw items. Your bank is where you store everything you can't carry.
  • Account Creation: Choose your character appearance (cosmetic only — doesn't affect gameplay at all).

⚙️ Settings to Change Immediately

  • Turn on "Accept Aid": Allows other players to heal you, teleport you, and help in co-op content. Essential for community play.
  • Set chat to "Hide" for public/friends: If you find global chat overwhelming, hide it. You can always turn it back on later.
  • Enable "Side panels": Keeps your inventory, equipment, prayer, and spellbook as side tabs. Much more efficient than the mobile-style overlay.
  • Set camera to resizable mode: Maximum view distance. Being able to see more of the world helps with navigation and awareness.
  • Turn off "Shift-click to drop": Unless you plan to power-level, this just makes dropping items annoying.

First Actions After Tutorial Island

You spawn in Lumbridge — the central hub for new players. Before you run off to adventure, do these three things:

  1. Check your bank: Located in Lumbridge Castle basement (down the trapdoor south of the castle) or upstairs in the kitchen area. Open it, get familiar with the interface.
  2. Open your world map: Press the M key. This is your best friend. Every location in the game is on this map — learn to read it.
  3. Familiarize yourself with movement: Click to walk, hold to run (drain run energy), use minimap to orient yourself. Simple but essential.

✅ End of Day 0 Status

  • Combat level: 3
  • All combat stats: level 3 (Attack/Strength/Defence/Ranged/Magic/Hitpoints)
  • All other stats: level 1
  • Bank: Empty (maybe a few starter items)
  • Location: Lumbridge, ready to start your journey
  • Time invested: 30-60 minutes

Day 1 — Quest Blitz & First Combat Levels (2-3 hours)

Day 1 is all about momentum. You're going to knock out six F2P quests in about an hour, then spend the rest of your time getting your first combat levels. By the end of today, you'll have 6 Quest Points, combat level ~12-14, and your first ~2k GP from drops.

🌅 Morning Session (1 hour) — Speed-Run F2P Quests

1. The Blood Pact 5 min

Start: Talk to Xenia south of Lumbridge Castle (she's right outside, near the river).

Walkthrough: She sends you into a small dungeon to kill three minions. Super easy, designed for level 3 players.

Rewards: +1 Attack, +1 Strength, +1 Defence, 300 XP in a combat stat of your choice, 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: Instant free combat levels. This is the fastest way to get to level 4+ in your chosen combat style.

2. Imp Catcher 10 min

Start: Wizard Mizgog on the top floor of the Wizard Tower (south of Draynor Village, south of Lumbridge).

Walkthrough: He asks you to find a missing bead. Talk to Wizard Traiborn (same tower, different floor) who gives you a location hint. The bead is actually just sitting in a chest in the tower basement — no combat required.

Rewards: +1 Magic level (if your Magic is low), Amulet of Accuracy (minor accuracy boost for melee).

Why do it: Unlocks the Amulet of Accuracy, which is a decent early-game amulet. Also gives Magic XP.

3. Restless Ghost 15 min

Start: Father Aereck in Lumbridge Church (east side of Lumbridge).

Walkthrough: He sends you to talk to Father Urhney in Lumbridge Swamp (south-west of Lumbridge, enter the small shack). Father Urhney gives you a Ghostspeak Amulet. Go back to the Lumbridge Church basement, talk to the ghost, find the ghost's remains in the Wizards' Tower basement, and bury them.

Rewards: 1,125 Prayer XP — this jumps your Prayer from level 1 to level 9! Also 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: 1,125 Prayer XP is massive at this stage. Level 9 Prayer gets you access to better protection prayers sooner.

4. Ernest the Chicken 20 min

Start: Veronica outside Draynor Manor (north-west of Draynor Village).

Walkthrough: Ernest has been turned into a chicken. Enter Draynor Manor, go to the basement, find the oil can, poison, and key. Use them on the machine to turn Ernest back. The basement has poisonous spiders — bring food or just run past them.

Rewards: 300 XP each in Attack, Strength, Defence, Prayer, and Woodcutting. That's 1,500 total XP! Also 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: The distribution of XP across multiple skills makes this amazing for overall account progression. You get Prayer XP without burying bones!

5. Cook's Assistant 5 min

Start: Cook in Lumbridge Castle kitchen (first floor).

Walkthrough: He needs milk, egg, and flour. Milk a cow at the Lumbridge cow field (north-east of castle), grab an egg from the Chicken coop (same area), and use a pot on the flour bin in the Lumbridge Castle basement. Bring all three to the Cook.

Rewards: +1 Cooking level (if your Cooking is level 1), 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: Quick and easy. Every Quest Point counts toward Dragon Slayer prerequisites.

6. Sheep Shearer 10 min

Start: Fred the Farmer north of Lumbridge (north of the cow field, follow the path north).

Walkthrough: Shear 20 sheep with shears (Fred gives you shears). Use a spinning wheel (Lumbridge Castle second floor or Falador) to turn wool into balls of wool. Bring 20 wool balls to Fred.

Rewards: +1 Crafting level, +1 Farming level, 60 Crafting XP, 500 Farming XP, 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: Gives Crafting and Farming XP, which are annoying to train early on. Also another Quest Point toward Dragon Slayer.

🌇 Afternoon Session (1-2 hours) — Combat Training

Now that you have some Quest Points and a few combat levels, it's time to actually fight things. Head to the Chicken coop north-east of Lumbridge Castle (just past the cow field).

🐔 Chicken Training Strategy

  • Set your attack style to "Attack" (top option on the combat tab). Train Attack to 10 first — this unlocks better weapons.
  • Collect feathers: Chickens drop feathers. Pick up EVERY feather — they sell for ~5-10 GP each on the Grand Exchange. 500 feathers = 2.5-5k GP.
  • Chickens are level 1: You'll kill them in 1-2 hits. Extremely fast XP at low levels.
  • Train Attack to 10: This unlocks the Iron scimitar (buy from Lev's General Store in Al Kharid for ~24 GP). The scimitar is the best F2P attack speed weapon.

Once Attack hits 10, switch your combat style to "Strength" (bottom option) and head to the Cow field east of Lumbridge. Cows are level 2, slightly tougher than chickens, but they drop cowhides — and cowhides are worth ~110-120 GP each at the Grand Exchange.

🐄 Cowhide Money Maker (Your First Serious Income)

  • Kill cows. Loot every cowhide.
  • Do NOT drop cowhides. They are your primary income source right now.
  • Bank cowhides at the Lumbridge Castle basement deposit box (or run to Al Kharid deposit box if you have the GP for the toll).
  • Sell cowhides on the Grand Exchange when you have 50-100. Don't sell for less than 100 GP each.
  • 100 cowhides = ~11,000 GP. That's your first real money!

✅ End of Day 1 Milestone

  • ✅ 6 Quest Points (out of 33 needed for Dragon Slayer)
  • ✅ Combat level ~12-14
  • ✅ Attack ~10, Strength ~8-10, Defence ~5-7
  • ✅ ~2,000 GP from feathers and cowhides
  • ✅ Iron scimitar equipped (or better if you bought one)
  • ✅ Understanding of basic combat and money making loops

Day 2 — Stronghold of Security & More Quests (2-3 hours)

Day 2 is about two things: getting your first serious GP stack from the Stronghold of Security, and pushing your combat levels higher. The Stronghold of Security (SoS) is a F2P minigame that gives you 17,000 GP, an emote, and outfit pieces. It's mandatory for every new account.

🌅 Morning Session (1 hour) — Stronghold of Security

🏰 Stronghold of Security Guide

Location: Barbarian Village (west of Varrock, use the spirit trees or walk). Look for a large hole in the ground with a ladder.

Overview: Four floors, each with a safety question and a reward. You must find the correct door on each floor by reading the riddles. Monsters on each floor scale with your combat level.

Floor 1 — "Hope" (Pest Control)

  • Monsters: Minotaurs (level 12, great for combat training after the quest).
  • Goal: Find the door with the word "Pest" (answer to the riddle).
  • Reward: 2,000 GP + Safety shoes (cosmetic).
  • Combat tip: Minotaurs are level 12 and drop iron arrows. They're actually the best combat training spot for levels 10-25.

Floor 2 — "Solitude" (Isolation)

  • Monsters: Flesh Crawlers (level 8-12, very weak).
  • Goal: Find the correct levers to Pull (not push). Read the riddle carefully.
  • Reward: Genie lamp (use on any skill level 10+ for XP) + Chameleon boots (cosmetic).
  • Note: Flesh Crawlers are AFKable. You can train here while watching a video.

Floor 3 — "Affection" (Barbarian skill)

  • Monsters: Catablepon (level 15-21, moderately tough).
  • Goal: Avoid ghosts (they drain prayer), follow the path carefully. Answer involves "Affection".
  • Reward: 5,000 GP + Fancy boots (cosmetic).

Floor 4 — "Temptation" (Self-control)

  • Monsters: Ankou (level 22-35, tough but rare). The floor is mostly a maze.
  • Goal: Navigate the box maze. Don't step on dangerous floor tiles (they damage you). Answer involves "Temptation".
  • Reward: 10,000 GP + Rainbow boots (cosmetic) + "Idea!" emote.

Total SoS Rewards: 17,000 GP + 4 boots (cosmetic) + Genie lamp + "Idea!" emote. This is HUGE for a new account!

🌇 Afternoon Session (1-2 hours) — Combat Training + Quests

After the Stronghold, you have 17,000 GP in your bank. That's a massive head start. Now use that Genie lamp on Woodcutting or Fishing (get a gathering skill to 10+ so you have options later).

⚔️ Combat Training: Minotaurs (SoS Floor 1)

Go back to Floor 1 of the Stronghold (or stay there after completing it). Minotaurs are level 12 and give excellent XP for your level range. They also drop iron arrows and iron scimitars.

  • Goal: Push Attack to 15-20, Strength to 12-15.
  • Strategy: Use "Attack" style to 15 (unlocks Steel scimitar), then "Strength" style to 12-15.
  • Food: Bring lobsters or swordfish if you have the GP, otherwise cook your own shrimp/anchovies.
  • Loot: Pick up iron arrows. They sell for ~5-10 GP each. 100 arrows = 500-1,000 GP.

📜 Quests: Romeo & Juliet + Doric's Quest

Romeo & Juliet (no requirements):

  • Start: Romeo in Varrock square (middle of Varrock, near the fountain).
  • Walkthrough: Talk to Romeo, then Juliet (second floor of a house in Varrock east side), then back to Romeo. Very simple.
  • Rewards: 1 Quest Point (that's it, but Quest Points count toward Dragon Slayer).

Doric's Quest (no requirements):

  • Start: Doric north of Falador (west of Edgeville, follow the path).
  • Walkthrough: He needs 6 clay, 4 copper ore, 2 iron ore. Mine them yourself (clay/copper near Doric, iron in Al Kharid mine) or buy from GE (cheaper).
  • Rewards: 1,300 Mining XP — this jumps Mining from level 1 to level 11! Also 1 Quest Point, and free use of Doric's anvils (minor convenience).
  • Why it's amazing: 1,300 Mining XP for a 5-minute quest is insane. You can now mine iron ore, which is a decent money maker.

✅ End of Day 2 Milestone

  • ✅ ~17,000 GP in bank (from Stronghold of Security)
  • ✅ 8-9 Quest Points
  • ✅ Combat level ~15-17
  • ✅ Attack ~15-20, Strength ~12-15, Defence ~8-10
  • ✅ Mining level 11+ (from Doric's Quest)
  • ✅ "Idea!" emote unlocked (bragging rights!)
  • ✅ Steel scimitar equipped (buy from Vigroy's shop in Falador for ~104 GP)

Day 3 — Money Focus & Dragon Slayer Prep (2-3 hours)

By Day 3, you should have a feel for the game. Today is about making real money and preparing for Dragon Slayer — the most important F2P quest. Dragon Slayer lets you wear a Rune platebody (the best F2P torso armor) and gives massive Strength and Defence XP.

🌅 Morning Session (1.5 hours) — Money Making

💰 Option A: Cowhides (If You Haven't Collected 200+ Yet)

Location: Cow field east of Lumbridge (east of River Lum).

Method: Kill cows, loot cowhides, bank at Lumbridge Castle basement deposit box.

  • 100 cowhides = ~11,000 GP (sell on GE).
  • Goal: Collect 200-300 cowhides = ~22,000-33,000 GP.
  • Time: ~1 hour for 200 hides.
  • Why it's good: Zero requirements. Cows are level 2, you can't fail. It's boring but reliable.

💰 Option B: Hill Giants (Better GP/HR at Your Level)

Location: Edgeville Dungeon (west of Edgeville bank, enter the dungeon entrance). You need a Brass Key to access the Hill Giant area. Buy a Brass Key from the Grand Exchange for ~500 GP (or get a friend to drop one).

Requirements: ~15+ Combat (20+ recommended for efficiency).

  • Hill Giants: Level 28, 35 HP. They drop Big Bones (Prayer XP), Limpwurt roots (herb, ~500-1,000 GP each), Nature runes (alchemy, ~200 GP each), and other valuable loot.
  • Banking: Edgeville bank is RIGHT THERE. Super convenient. Loot → bank → repeat.
  • Profit: ~30-50k GP per hour (depending on drops).
  • Strategy: Use "Strength" attack style (bottom option) to maximize damage. Bring food (lobsters recommended).
  • Prayer tip: Bury Big Bones for Prayer XP, or save them for a member altar (3.5x XP). Your choice.

Why Hill Giants are amazing: They're the best F2P money maker for low-to-mid combat levels. Plus you get Prayer XP from Big Bones and decent combat XP.

🌇 Afternoon Session (1-2 hours) — Dragon Slayer Prerequisite Quests

Dragon Slayer has NO skill requirements, but it DOES require 33 Quest Points. You should have ~8-10 by now. You need ~23-25 more. These three quests get you closer:

1. Pirate's Treasure 15 min

Requirements: None.

Start: Redbeard Frank in the Blue Moon Inn (Varrock, south side of the city, look for the inn sign).

Walkthrough: He wants rum. Buy rum from Port Sarim (Wydin's Food Store, ~40 GP). Give him the rum, follow the clues to find a treasure map, dig in the correct spot (use a spade).

Rewards: +2 Thieving (if Thieving is low), 1/4 of a Sword (cosmetic), ~10,000 GP worth of emeralds and rings, 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: Gives GP and a Quest Point. Also introduces you to the Pirate's Treasure questline (there's more in member content).

2. Vampire Slayer 20 min

Requirements: None.

Start: Morgan in Draynor Village (west of Lumbridge, north of the market area).

Walkthrough: He wants you to kill Count Draynor (a vampire). Get a stake and hammer (buy from Grand Exchange or find in Draynor manor basement). Kill the vampire (level 19, in the Draynor manor basement).

Rewards: 4,825 Attack XP — this is a HUGE jump. At level 15 Attack, this quest gets you to level 25+ Attack instantly.

Why it's essential: 4,825 Attack XP for a 20-minute quest is one of the best XP rates in the entire game. Do this as soon as possible.

3. Witch's Potion 10 min

Requirements: None.

Start: Hetty in Rimmington (south of Falador, use the Rimmington teleport or walk).

Walkthrough: She wants rat tails and onions. Get them (rats are everywhere in Rimmington, onions are in the Rimmington field). Bring them back, drink the potion.

Rewards: 311 Magic XP + 1,000 Hitpoints XP (Magic jumps to ~level 4, HP gets a boost). Also 1 Quest Point.

Why do it: Quick XP for Magic and HP. Every little bit helps.

🐉 Dragon Slayer Readiness Check

After today's quests, check your Dragon Slayer prerequisites:

  • 33 Quest Points: ✅ Should be at ~12-15 by now (need 18 more — keep questing!).
  • Ability to defeat Level 83 Vampire (Delrith): Need ~30+ combat with decent food. You're not there yet, and that's okay.
  • Combat level ~20-24: After今天's training, you should be in this range.

If not ready for Dragon Slayer yet: That's normal! Keep training combat on Hill Giants. Pushing to 30 Attack/Strength makes Dragon Slayer much easier.

✅ End of Day 3 Milestone

  • ✅ ~50-80k GP in bank (from cowhides/Hill Giants)
  • ✅ 12-15 Quest Points
  • ✅ Combat level ~20-24
  • ✅ Attack ~20-25 (from Vampire Slayer XP!), Strength ~15-18, Defence ~10-12
  • ✅ Closer to Dragon Slayer prerequisites
  • ✅ Understanding of F2P money making (cowhides, Hill Giants)

Day 4 — DRAGON SLAYER & Big Milestones (2-3 hours)

Today is the day. You're going to attempt Dragon Slayer — the hardest F2P quest and one of the most iconic quests in OSRS. Completing it is a rite of passage. The rewards are insane: 18,650 Strength XP, 18,650 Defence XP, the ability to wear a Rune platebody, and 2 Quest Points.

🐉 Dragon Slayer — Full Walkthrough

Requirements: 33 Quest Points (you should have ~15-20 by now — if not, do more F2P quests first!).

Step 1: Start the Quest

Talk to Oziach west of Edgeville (he's standing outside the building west of the Edgeville bank, near the wilderness entrance). He won't give you the quest yet — he tells you to go to the Champions' Guild first.

Step 2: Champions' Guild

The Champions' Guild is south of Varrock (south of the city, across the river, follow the path). Talk to the Guildmaster. He gives you Map Piece 1 and tells you to find the other two pieces.

Step 3: Map Piece 2 — Melzar's Maze

Location: Melzar's Maze is north of Rimmington (south of Falador, west of Port Sarim). It's a maze building with multiple rooms.

Walkthrough:

  • Enter the maze. You'll go through rooms with different monsters.
  • Zombie room: Kill the zombie, loot the Orange key.
  • Ghost room: Kill the ghost, loot the Yellow key.
  • Skeleton room: Kill the skeleton, loot the Blue key.
  • Wizard room: Kill the wizard, loot the Magenta key.
  • Demon room: Use all keys on the door. Kill the demon (level 36, dangerous!). Loot Map Piece 2.
  • Total time: ~15-20 minutes.

Step 4: Map Piece 3 — Dwarven Mines (Ice Mountain)

Location: The Oracle is above the Dwarven Mines (Ice Mountain, west of Falador, north of the Dwarven Mine entrance).

What to bring: Silk, Lobster pot, Unfired bowl, Nails (any type). Buy these from the Grand Exchange before you go.

Walkthrough:

  • Talk to the Oracle. He speaks in riddles. Write down his clues (or take a screenshot).
  • Read the blueprints in the Dwarven Mine (enter the mine, read the board on the wall).
  • The blueprints tell you which item to get and where to find it. Follow the clues, get the item, bring it back to the Oracle.
  • The Oracle gives you Map Piece 3.
  • Total time: ~10-15 minutes (if you read carefully).

Step 5: Combine Map Pieces & Talk to Klarense

Use the three map pieces together (inventory, right-click "Use"). You get a map. Now talk to Klarense in Port Sarim (south of Port Sarim, near the docks).

Klarense offers to sell you a ship for 2,000 GP. Try to negotiate — you might get it for 1,000-1,500 GP. Pay him, then board the ship.

Step 6: The Dragon — Elvarg

Once you sail to Crandor, you'll face Elvarg — a level 83 dragon. This is the hardest fight in F2P. Here's how to win:

⚔️ Elvarg Fight Strategy

  • Required gear: Anti-dragonfire shield (get from Oziach for FREE before the fight), best weapon (Mithril/Adamant scimitar + shield), best armor you can afford.
  • Required inventory: 15-20 lobsters (or swordfish if you're rich), Prayer potions (optional but helpful), teleport tab (just in case).
  • Combat style: Use "Strength" attack style (bottom option) for max damage. Activate "Protect from Magic" prayer if you have Prayer level 12+ (reduces dragonfire damage).
  • Dragonfire: Elvarg uses dragonfire which can hit 30-50 damage. The Anti-dragonfire shield reduces this to ~10-15. Protect from Magic prayer reduces it further.
  • Melee phase: After dragonfire, Elvarg fights with melee. She can hit 10-15 per hit. Eat food to stay above 20 HP at all times.
  • The fight: It takes ~3-5 minutes with 25-30 combat. Stay calm, eat when needed, and keep attacking. She WILL die.
  • If you die: It's okay! You respawn at Lumbridge. Retrieve your stuff from your grave (3 minutes). Try again with better gear/more food.

Step 7: Claim Your Reward

After killing Elvarg, return to Oziach in Edgeville. He congratulates you. Quest complete!

Rewards:

  • 18,650 Strength XP — This is ~2 full levels at level 30 Strength!
  • 18,650 Defence XP — Same massive boost for Defence.
  • Ability to wear Rune platebody (the best F2P torso armor, ~200 defence rating).
  • 2 Quest Points (now at ~17-22, almost there!).
  • Ability to buy/compose Dragon battleaxe and Dragon longsword from other players (member content, but unlocked by this quest).

🎉 Post-Dragon Slayer Celebration

You just completed the hardest F2P quest in the game! Take a screenshot. Tell your clan chat. This is a genuine achievement.

  • Buy a Rune platebody: Go to Scavvo's shop in the Champions' Guild (south of Varrock) or buy from the Grand Exchange (~20-25k GP). Equip it. You're now wearing the best F2P torso armor in the game.
  • Check your stats: Your Strength and Defence probably jumped 3-4 levels each from the quest rewards. You're significantly stronger now.
  • Combat level: Should be around 30-35. You're no longer a noob — you're a legitimate adventurer.

🌇 Afternoon — Skill Training (Optional)

If you have time left, start training a gathering skill. You'll need one at level 30+ for member money making.

  • Woodcutting: Cut oak trees (level 15) near Draynor bank. AFKable, decent money (oak logs ~20-30 GP each).
  • Fishing: Fish trout/salmon (level 20) at the Barbarian Village fishing spot. Good food source for future combat.
  • Mining: Mine iron ore (level 15) in Al Kharid. Iron ore ~100 GP each, fast respawn.

✅ End of Day 4 Milestone

  • ✅ DRAGON SLAYER COMPLETE 🐉
  • ✅ Combat level ~30-35
  • ✅ Rune platebody equipped (best F2P armor!)
  • ✅ ~17-22 Quest Points (need 11-16 more for 33 — almost there!)
  • ✅ Bank ~30-50k GP (if you haven't spent it all on gear)
  • ✅ Massive confidence boost. You can kill a level 83 dragon. You belong in Gielinor.

Day 5 — Skills, Money & Pre-Membership Prep (2-3 hours)

Day 5 is about preparing for membership. You're going to train a gathering skill to level 30+ (so you can make money as a member) and push your bank value higher. You're also going to face the reality check of membership costs — and that's okay.

🌅 Morning Session (1-2 hours) — Skill Training (Pick ONE Focus)

🌲 Option A: Woodcutting (Most Popular Choice)

Why Woodcutting: It's AFKable, the logs sell on the GE, and it unlocks Willow trees at level 30 (good money, ~50-80 GP per log).

  • Levels 1-15: Cut regular trees. Anywhere works (Lumbridge, Varrock, Draynor).
  • Levels 15-30: Cut oak trees. Best spot: Draynor Village (bank is close, 3 oak trees in one spot).
  • Levels 30-40: Cut willow trees (when you hit 30). Best spot: Draynor near the bank (5 willow trees).
  • Goal: 30-40 Woodcutting (unlocks Willow trees = good money).
  • XP rate: ~15-30k XP/hr depending on level and concentration.
  • Profit: Willow logs ~50-80 GP each. 1,000 willows = ~50-80k GP.
  • Tip: Drop the logs while training (don't bank them). You need the XP, not the logs. Once you hit 30, THEN bank willows for money.

🐟 Option B: Fishing

Why Fishing: It gives you free food (cook the fish for Cooking XP too), and higher-level fish sell on the GE.

  • Levels 1-15: Fish shrimp/anchovies (Lumbridge, Catherby, Draynor).
  • Levels 15-20: Fish trout (Barbarian Village, Shilo Village if member).
  • Levels 20-30: Fish trout/salmon (Barbarian Village). Salmon gives more XP and heals more HP.
  • Levels 30-40: Fish lobsters (Catherby, Port Sarim — member content, but you can fish tuna/swordfish in F2P at Catherby).
  • Goal: 30-40 Fishing (unlocks Lobster fishing = good food + money as member).
  • XP rate: ~10-20k XP/hr (low level) to ~30k+ XP/hr (level 30+).
  • Tip: Cook the fish on a fire/range for Cooking XP. Two birds with one stone.

⛏️ Option C: Mining

Why Mining: Iron ore is a decent F2P money maker, and level 30 Mining unlocks the Motherlode Mine (member content, best Mining XP in the game).

  • Levels 1-15: Mine tin/copper (anywhere, Lumbridge swamp mine).
  • Levels 15-30: Mine iron ore (Al Kharid mine, 3 rocks in a triangle, fast respawn). Drop the ore for XP (don't bank it).
  • Levels 30-40: Mine iron ore and BANK it (sell on GE for ~100 GP each). Or mine coal (level 30, slower but more GP).
  • Goal: 30+ Mining (unlocks Motherlode Mine once member).
  • XP rate: ~15-25k XP/hr (drop method for iron).
  • Profit: Iron ore ~100 GP each. 100 iron = ~10k GP (if you bank and sell).

🌇 Afternoon Session (1-2 hours) — Money Making Push

Now that you have better combat stats (thanks to Dragon Slayer), you can make serious money. Your goal today: earn another 50-100k GP.

💰 Best F2P Money Makers at Your Level

  • Hill Giants (continued): If they're still profitable for you (check GE prices), keep killing them. 40-60k GP/hr at this level.
  • Kill Men/Guards: In Edgeville or Varrock, kill Men (level 2) or Guards (level 21). They drop herbs (Limpwurt roots, Grimy guam, etc.) which are valuable. Low combat XP but decent GP if you get lucky drops.
  • Collect Flax: South of Seers' Village (member area, but the flax field is accessible from F2P). 100 flax = ~1,000 GP (sell on GE). It's click-intensive but requires no combat.
  • Continue cowhides: If cowhides are still ~100+ GP on GE, keep killing cows. It's boring but reliable.

💳 Membership Math — Reality Check

Let's talk about membership. A 14-day bond costs ~2.3 million GP on the Grand Exchange (as of 2026). You've been playing for 5 days and probably have ~80-150k GP in the bank. That's... not close.

And that's NORMAL. Almost no one can afford a bond within their first week of F2P. It typically takes 2-4 weeks of consistent F2P playing to afford a bond.

Your options:

  • Option 1: Buy a $7-8 USD bond. This is the cheapest option for most players. One month of membership is less than a Netflix subscription. If you can afford it, just buy it — your account progression will 10x.
  • Option 2: Keep playing F2P until you can afford a bond. It takes 2-4 weeks typically. Use the money making methods in this guide. Once you get your first bond, maintaining bonds via in-game GP is easier (you'll have member money makers).
  • Option 3: Wait for a bond price drop. Bond prices fluctuate based on GE supply/demand. Check the GE regularly — sometimes you can snipe a cheap bond.

My recommendation: If you can afford $7-8, just buy a bond. The member content is where OSRS truly shines. F2P is essentially an extended tutorial. But if you can't or don't want to spend money, that's fine too — F2P is still fun and the bond grind is a satisfying achievement.

By the end of today, you'll have a solid F2P foundation and a clear plan for your future — whether that's buying membership or staying F2P longer.

🌅 Morning Session (1-2 hours) — Push Key Stats Higher

⚔️ Combat Training Priorities

  • Attack to 40: This unlocks the Rune scimitar — the best F2P melee weapon. Train on Flesh Crawlers (SoS Floor 2, fully AFKable!) or Hill Giants (better drops).
  • Strength to 30-35: More damage = faster kills = faster everything. Train on Hill Giants or Moss Giants (member content, but you can fight Obor in F2P — a giant boss that drops Big Bones and decent loot).
  • Defence to 20-30: Survivability. More Defence = less food needed = longer training sessions. Train on anything that hits you (everything gives Defence XP if you use the "Defence" attack style).
  • Prayer to 20+: Bury Big Bones from Hill Giant sessions. Or, if you're feeling brave, use the Chaos Altar in member wilderness (3.5x Prayer XP). Prayer level 20+ unlocks better protection prayers.

Best AFK training spot: Flesh Crawlers on SoS Floor 2. They're level 8-12, weak, and spawn quickly. Set your attack style, turn on "Run" if you want, and watch a YouTube video while your character kills things. Check every 5-10 minutes to eat food and loot arrows.

🌇 Afternoon Session (1-2 hours) — Remaining F2P Quests & Exploration

📜 Remaining F2P Quests to Complete

You need 33 Quest Points for Dragon Slayer (you should have it already if you followed this guide, but if not, here's what's left):

  • Rune Mysteries: Unlocks the Runecrafting skill. Even if you stay F2P, do this quest — it's fast, gives no XP but unlocks an entire skill. Start at the Wizard's Tower (south of Draynor).
  • Goblin Diplomacy: Fun, quick, rewards a gold bar + cosmetic items. Start by talking to General Bentnoze or General Wartface in the Goblin Village (north of Falador, west of the city).
  • Prince Ali Rescue: Longer quest, involves sneaking and disguises. Rewards Thieving XP and unlocks the ability to use the Al Kharid desert gate without paying the 10 GP toll. Start at the Al Kharid Palace.
  • Shield of Arrav: Requires a partner (trade the certificates with another player). Rewards 600 GP and 1 Quest Point. Start in Varrock Blue Moon Inn.
  • Demon Slayer: Short quest, involves killing a demon (level 27). Rewards 3 Quest Points and Silverlight (a demon-slaying sword). Start by talking to Gypsy Aris in Varrock (tent in the city square).

After these quests, you should have 33+ Quest Points. If you already completed Dragon Slayer, these are just for the Quest Points and achievement diaries.

🗺️ Explore the F2P World

Take 30 minutes to explore. Visit places you haven't been. Talk to NPCs. Read lore. OSRS has a rich world — don't rush through it.

  • Falador: Falador Park (cenotaph, flower patches), Falador Castle (White Knights), Party Room (balloon animals and drop parties).
  • Draynor Village: Market (Vegetable seller, Crafting store), Wise Old Man's house (lore), Draynor Manor (spooky quest location).

  • Al Kharid: Desert gate (toll 10 GP, or complete Prince Ali Rescue to remove toll), Al Kharid Palace, Duel Arena (PvP minigame, safe).

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Every OSRS player makes mistakes in their first week. Learn from theirs (and mine) so you don't have to learn the hard way.

  • Don't spread yourself too thin. There are 23 skills in OSRS. You cannot train them all at once. Pick 1-2 skills to focus on, and ignore the rest for now. You have years to max everything.
  • Don't ignore quests. I said this in the Golden Rules and I'll say it again: quests give more XP per hour than any skill training. Every quest you complete makes future training 2x easier. If you're stuck, go do a quest.
  • Don't hoard junk items. Bank space is limited (available in F2P). If an item isn't useful, sell it on the GE, alch it (if you have the Magic level), or drop it. A cluttered bank is a cluttered mind.
  • Don't train Prayer only by burying bones. Burying bones is slow (XP rate ~10-20k/hr). The Chaos Altar in the Wilderness (member content) gives 3.5x XP per bone. One trip to Chaos Altar = 10 hours of burying. It's dangerous (PvP zone) but worth it.
  • Don't buy expensive gear too early. An Adamant scimitar (costs ~1,000 GP) is fine until 40 Attack. You don't need a Dragon scimitar (member content, expensive) at level 30. Save your money for membership — bonds are expensive.
  • Don't forget food. Always bring food when training combat. Lobsters (heals 12 HP) or Swordfish (heals 14 HP) are ideal. Nothing is more embarrassing than dying to a level 12 Minotaur because you forgot to bring food.
  • Don't skip Dragon Slayer. I don't care how hard it looks. Dragon Slayer is the gateway to the rest of the game. The Rune platebody (unlocked by this quest) is the best F2P torso armor. The XP rewards are massive. Do the quest.
  • Don't play alone. OSRS has a massive, friendly community. Join a clan. Ask questions in the help channel. Watch YouTube guides. Read the OSRS Wiki (it's amazing). You'll learn faster and have more fun.
  • Don't get discouraged. Your first week will feel slow. That's normal. OSRS is a game where progress compounds. Week 2 is faster than Week 1. Week 4 is faster than Week 2. Stick with it and you'll be amazed at how far you've come.
  • Don't forget to have fun. OSRS is a game. If you're not enjoying yourself, take a break. Try a different activity. Set a silly goal (like collecting every dye color). The best OSRS players are the ones who find joy in the grind.

Bonus: Pro Tips for Faster Progression

⚡ Efficiency Tips

  • Use the OSRS Wiki: wiki.gg/oldschool-runescape — it's the best game wiki ever made. Every item, NPC, quest, and skill has a dedicated page with exact numbers and strategies.
  • Install RuneLite (if on PC): RuneLite is a free, open-source OSRS client with quality-of-life plugins (entity hider, ground item indicators, tile markers, etc.). It's not cheating — Jagex officially supports it.
  • Learn to use the Grand Exchange: The GE is the economy hub. Learn to buy/sell items efficiently (undercut buy orders, instant sell offers). Don't be the person selling items at 50% of GE mid-price.
  • Join the OSRS Discord/Reddit: r/2007scape (Reddit) and the official OSRS Discord are great places to ask questions, share achievements, and find friends.
  • Watch YouTube guides: Channels like "OSRS Guide", "Echo", "Protoxx", and "Woox" make amazing content. Watch a 10-minute guide before starting a new activity — it'll save you hours of trial and error.
  • Set small, achievable goals: "Get 40 Attack" is better than "become a maxed account". Small goals give you dopamine hits and keep you motivated.
  • Take breaks: OSRS is designed to be played for years. You don't need to grind 12 hours a day. Play for an hour, then do something else. Consistency beats intensity.
