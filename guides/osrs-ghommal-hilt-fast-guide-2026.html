Get Ghommal's Hilt Fast — Quickest Path to +5% Damage Boost

Don't waste 20 hours reading every task description. This guide gives you the absolute minimum-effort route to Ghommal's Hilt 1 — the +5% damage boost that makes every future PvM session more efficient. Total time investment: 4–7 hours if you follow this exact route.

1. Why Ghommal's Hilt Is Worth Rushing

If you're reading this, you probably already know that Ghommal's Hilt gives +5% damage. But is it really worth spending 5–10 hours of gameplay to get? Let's do the math.

📊 The Damage Boost: Concrete Numbers

The +5% damage applies as a multiplier to your max hit and your average DPS. Here's what that looks like at different combat levels:

Your LevelMax Hit (No Hilt)Max Hit (With Hilt)DPS IncreaseTime Saved per Kill
60 Combat2021+5%~3 seconds
80 Combat3536.75 → 37+5%~5 seconds
100 Combat5052.5 → 53+5%~7 seconds
126 Combat7073.5 → 74+5%~10 seconds

At first glance, 3–10 seconds per kill doesn't sound like much. But consider:

  • Slayer: 200-kill task × 5 seconds saved = 16 minutes saved per task
  • Bossing: 30-kill per hour × 5 seconds = 2.5 extra kills per hour = +8% profit
  • Quest Bosses: Some quest bosses are HP-gated. +5% damage can mean 1 fewer kill attempt = hours saved over time

💰 ROI Calculation: Is It Worth 5 Hours of Grinding?

Assume you spend 5 hours getting the Hilt. After that, you gain 5% more loot per hour forever.

If you make 500K GP/hour at your current level: 5% = 25K extra GP/hour.
Break-even point: 5 hours × 500K = 2.5M GP "invested" in time.
Payback period: 2.5M ÷ 25K/hour = 100 hours of gameplay.

After 100 hours of PvM with the Hilt equipped, you've "earned back" the time spent getting it. After that, it's pure profit forever. For most players, 100 hours happens in the first 2–3 weeks of membership.

🎯 Who Should Rush This First

The Ghommal's Hilt 1 is most impactful for:

  • New members (first 30 days): Every bit of extra damage helps when your stats are still low
  • Slayer-focused players: The Hilt shines most during long Slayer sessions
  • Bossing newcomers: 5% faster boss kills = 5% less food used = more profit
  • UIM/Ironman: You can't buy damage potions as easily, so the Hilt's passive boost is even more valuable
💡 When NOT to Rush

If you're below 50 Combat, don't rush this yet. The tasks involving boss kills (Giant Mole, KBD, Barrows) will be frustrating and slow. Get to 60–70 Combat first, then come back. The Hilt is still worth getting, but the journey is much smoother with better stats.

2. The Fastest Path (Prioritized Task List)

You must complete all 35 Easy-tier tasks — there's no way to skip any of them. But you CAN complete multiple tasks simultaneously, and you CAN do them in the optimal order to minimize backtracking and wasted time.

This section gives you the prioritized task list — tasks are ordered by efficiency, not by task number. Tasks that can be batched together are grouped.

⚡ Batch 2: Quick-Deliberate Tasks (30–60 Minutes Total)

These 8 tasks are fast to complete if you do them deliberately. Do them all in one focused session:

🎯 Batch 2 Speedrun Route (Estimated 30–60 Minutes)

  1. Attack with all 3 styles (1 fight): Hill Giant, 2 minutes. Bring melee weapon + bow + staff.
  2. Slayer item usage: Get Banshee task from Mazchna (20 Combat needed), buy Earmuffs, kill 10 Banshees. 10 minutes.
  3. Turael task (1 consecutive): Talk to Turael (Burthorpe), get task, complete. 2–10 minutes.
  4. Mazchna task (1 consecutive): Talk to Mazchna (north of Canifis), get task, complete. 10–30 minutes.
  5. Kill 10 Slayer creatures: Completes during Tasks 3–4 above.
  6. Deal 50 ranged damage (boss): Giant Mole with ranged. 5 minutes (during Batch 3).
  7. Deal 50 magic damage (boss): Giant Mole with magic. 5 minutes (during Batch 3).
  8. Kill boss in Rune armor: Giant Mole in full Rune. 5 minutes (during Batch 3).

⚡ Batch 3: Boss Tasks (2–4 Hours Total)

These 10 tasks involve boss kills. This is the "core" of the grind. Recommended order for speed:

  1. Giant Mole (1 kill): Lowest requirements. Also completes: 50 ranged damage, 50 magic damage, Rune armor boss kill, 5 bosses total (if you do 5 Mole kills). Do 5 Mole kills in a row to batch multiple tasks.
  2. One Barrows brother (1 kill): Quick intro. Also progresses toward "Complete all 6 brothers."
  3. Full Barrows run (all 6 + chest): Now that you know the mechanics. Completes 2 tasks at once (all 6 brothers + open chest).
  4. KBD (1 kill): Requires Anti-dragon shield. Moderate difficulty.
  5. Skotizo (1 kill): Requires Catacombs access. Higher difficulty but good practice for future PvM.
  6. Kraken (1 kill): Requires 87 Slayer. If you don't have 87 Slayer yet, skip this task temporarily and come back later. It's the only task with a high level requirement.

⚡ Batch 4: The "Annoying" Tasks (1–2 Hours Total)

These are the tasks that require either gear you might not have, or a time investment that feels tedious:

  • Deal 1,000 damage to single monster: Fight a high-HP boss (Giant Mole over several attempts, or a Slayer monster in Catacombs). 10–20 minutes.
  • Deal 100 damage in 1 hit: Hardest Easy task. Options: (a) Dragon Warhammer special, (b) Granite Maul special (2 hits in 1 tick), (c) high Strength + Dragon 2h spec. If you can't do this yet, come back after getting 80+ Strength.
  • 5 consecutive Slayer tasks: Use Turael (easy tasks). 30–60 minutes. Do this last so you're comfortable with Slayer.
  • Kill boss without taking damage: Giant Mole + safe spot. 5–10 minutes once you know the safe spot location.
  • Complete Slayer task using only 1 style: Plan this during normal Slayer progression. Pick a task where the monsters are weak to one style (e.g., Turael assigns Rats → use only melee).

3. Step-by-Step Speedrun Plan

This is your hour-by-hour action plan. Follow this exactly and you'll have your Ghommal's Hilt in 4–6 hours.

🕐 Phase 1 (Hour 0–0.5): Setup & Natural Tasks

Goal: Trigger all "natural completion" tasks and set up your CA tracker.

  • Open Combat Achievements interface → track the first 6 tasks that aren't yet complete
  • Fight Rock Crabs (Rellekka) for 15 minutes → triggers: Kill 10/50/100 monsters, Kill with Melee (10), Deal 100 damage
  • Cast Wind Strike on Cows for 5 minutes → triggers: Cast 50 spells, Kill with Magic (1 + 10)
  • Shoot Maple Longbow at Cows for 5 minutes → triggers: Kill with Ranged (1 + 10)

Progress after Phase 1: ~15–18 tasks complete (natural ones).

🕐 Phase 2 (Hour 0.5–1.5): Slayer Introduction

Goal: Complete all Slayer-related tasks and the "3 styles in 1 fight" task.

  • Go to Turael (Burthorpe south) → get a task → complete it → Task 26 done
  • Go to Mazchna (north of Canifis, requires 20 Combat) → get a task → complete it → Task 27 done
  • During Mazchna task, if assigned Banshees: equip Earmuffs → Task 29 done
  • Fight a Hill Giant (Edgeville Dungeon): attack with melee → switch to bow → switch to staff → kill → Task 17 done
  • Eat 50 food if not yet done (buy cheap food from GE, eat in a safe area) → Task 10 done

Progress after Phase 2: ~22–25 tasks complete.

🕐 Phase 3 (Hour 1.5–4): Boss Grind

Goal: Complete all boss-related tasks. This is the longest phase.

  • Giant Mole (5 kills): Falador Mole Lair. Also completes: Task 19 (1 Mole), Task 31 (5 bosses), Task 32 (50 ranged damage), Task 33 (50 magic damage), Task 34 (no damage — use safe spot), Task 25 (Rune armor kill — wear full Rune). ~6–8 tasks completed in one session!
  • One Barrows brother: Enter Barrows, kill Ahhren (easiest). Task 20 done.
  • Full Barrows run: Kill all 6 brothers + loot chest. Tasks 24 + 35 done.
  • KBD (1 kill): Wilderness Lever (Edgeville) → KBD lair. Bring Anti-dragon shield! Task 21 done.

Progress after Phase 3: ~30–32 tasks complete.

🕐 Phase 4 (Hour 4–5): Cleanup & Annoying Tasks

Goal: Finish the last 3–5 remaining tasks.

  • Deal 1,000 damage (1 monster): Re-fight Giant Mole or a Catacombs Slayer monster. With Dragon Scimitar, ~200 damage per kill × 5 kills on same monster (let it heal) = 1,000 damage.
  • Deal 100 damage (1 hit): If you have Dragon Warhammer, use spec on a low-def monster. If not, train to 80 Strength first and try with Dragon 2h spec.
  • 5 consecutive Slayer tasks: Turael assignments. Each task is 5–20 kills. 5 tasks = 30–60 minutes.
  • Skotizo (1 kill): Catacombs of Kourend. Use 3 Dark totems on altar. Task 23 done.
  • Kraken (1 kill): Only if you have 87 Slayer. If not, skip and come back later (you can still claim the Hilt with 34/35 tasks complete? No — you need all 35. So you MUST eventually do Kraken. Consider getting 87 Slayer first or asking a high-level friend for help).

Progress after Phase 4: 35/35 tasks complete → claim Ghommal's Hilt 1!

⚠️ Important: Kraken Task (Task 22)

Task 22 (Kill Kraken) requires 87 Slayer to access the Kraken boss. If you're below 87 Slayer, you CANNOT complete this task yet. Options:

  1. Get 87 Slayer first: This takes 50–100 hours. Not a "fast" option.
  2. Use a Slayer potion (boost): A Slayer potion boosts your Slayer level by 1–5. If you're 82–86 Slayer, you can boost to 87 and attempt the Kraken. Risky but possible.
  3. Get help from a friend: A high-level friend can kill the Kraken for you while you're in the instance. The kill credit should go to you (test this on a different boss first to confirm).
  4. Skip Kraken for now and work on Medium tier: You can start Medium CA tasks even without finishing Easy. Come back to Kraken when you reach 87 Slayer naturally.

5. After Getting the Hilt — Usage & Upgrades

Congratulations! You've completed all 35 Easy Combat Achievements and claimed your Ghommal's Hilt 1. Now what?

🎯 How to Equip and Use Ghommal's Hilt 1

Ghommal's Hilt goes in the shield slot. This means:

  • If you're using a 2h weapon (Abyssal Whip, Dragon 2h, etc.), you can NOT wield the Hilt at the same time — unless you use the off-hand trick (some 2h weapons allow a shield in the off-hand slot, but most don't).
  • If you're using a 1h weapon + shield, replace your shield with the Hilt.
  • If you're using Ranged (2h bow), you can't use the Hilt simultaneously — unless you use crossbow (1h) + Hilt.
  • If you're using Magic (staff, 2h), same problem — no shield slot available.

The workaround: Use 1h weapons (Dragon Scimitar, Rune Crossbow, Trident of the Seas) so you always have a shield slot free for the Hilt. The +5% damage boost applies regardless of your weapon type.

⚔️ Best Weapon + Hilt Combinations

  • Melee: Dragon Scimitar (1h) + Ghommal's Hilt 1 → works perfectly
  • Ranged: Rune Crossbow (1h) + Ghommal's Hilt 1 → works perfectly
  • Magic: Iban's Blast (requires Iban's staff, 2h) → doesn't work. Use Trident of the Seas (1h) instead, or Smoke battlestaff (2h) → doesn't work. Actually, most magic weapons are 2h. The Hilt is slightly awkward for magic users.
  • Magic workaround: Use Ancient Magicks (requires Desert Treasure quest) with a 2h staff, and accept that you can't use the Hilt during magic fights. Switch to melee/ranged for the Hilt boost when possible.

🎨 Gilded Spear Upgrade (Cosmetic)

After getting the Hilt 1, talk to Ghommal in the Warrior's Guild (Burthorpe). He offers to upgrade your Hilt to the Gilded Spear cosmetic version for a fee (usually 100K–500K GP). The Gilded Spear has the same +5% damage boost but looks golden.

This is purely cosmetic. Only do it if you care about the appearance of your equipped items.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get Ghommal's Hilt in F2P (free-to-play)?

No. Combat Achievements are a members-only feature. You need membership to access the CA interface, the boss tasks, and the Slayer skill. The Hilt itself is also members-only (can't be equipped in F2P).

What's the single fastest task to complete?

"Kill a monster with melee only" (Task 11) — 10 seconds. Kill a Cow in Lumbridge with a sword. The longest task is "Complete 5 consecutive Slayer tasks" (Task 30) — 30–60 minutes if using Turael's short tasks.

Do I need to do the tasks on the same world?

No. Tasks complete on any world. You can do some tasks on World 330 (Gilded Altar world) and others on your preferred world. The progress is tied to your account, not the world.

Can I complete tasks in Deadman Mode or other game modes?

Combat Achievements are available in all game modes (Normal, Ironman, Hardcore Ironman, Ultimate Ironman, Deadman Mode). Progress is shared across your account in the same game mode.

What if I make a mistake and "fail" a task?

You can't fail a task permanently. All tasks track cumulative progress. If you die during a boss kill, you don't lose progress toward "Kill 5 bosses" — you just need to kill more bosses. The only task with a "fail" condition is Task 34 (Kill a boss without taking damage) — if you take damage, that specific attempt doesn't count, but you can try again immediately.

Is there a way to see which tasks I have left?

Yes. Open the Combat Achievements interface — uncompleted tasks are shown in red/grey. You can also use the "Filter by tier" option to show only Easy-tier tasks. Track up to 6 tasks at once by clicking the green "+" icon.

Should I use a specific combat style for the boss tasks?

For Easy-tier bosses (Giant Mole, one Barrows brother, KBD), melee is usually the fastest because your melee stats are likely higher than ranged/magic at this stage. But if you prefer ranged or magic, those work too — just make sure your accuracy is high enough to hit consistently.
