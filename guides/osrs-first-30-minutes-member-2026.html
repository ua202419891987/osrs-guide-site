🎯 Before You Log In

So you just became a member in OSRS — congratulations! The members world is massive compared to free-to-play, with thousands of new items, dozens of new skills, and hundreds of quests. It's easy to feel overwhelmed and waste your first hours just wandering around. This guide gives you an exact minute-by-minute plan for your first 30 minutes as a member so you maximize every second.

Before you log into the members world, take 5 minutes to prepare. The difference between a prepared player and an unprepared one is thousands of XP in that first half-hour. Here's your pre-login checklist.

① Set Up Your Equipment

Before logging into members, make sure you're wearing your best F2P gear. You should have completed Dragon Slayer before becoming a member (if you haven't, read our OSRS best quest order guide first). Equip your rune platebody, rune legs, rune scimitar, and amulet of strength. Fill your inventory with 4 pieces of food (salmon or trout) and leave the rest empty for quest items.

💡 Pro Tip: Bank all your F2P items except your combat gear before logging into members. Members worlds have separate item spawns and banks, but your inventory carries over. Having a clean inventory means less time banking and more time questing.

② Prepare Your Game Settings

Configure your settings for maximum efficiency. Turn on "Shift click to drop" in Controls settings — this speeds up dropping items enormously. Enable "One click" mode for skilling. Turn on XP drops so you can see your progress. Set your brightness to max so you can see in dark areas like dungeons. These small settings changes save you dozens of clicks in your first 30 minutes.

③ Set Your Destination

Your first target after logging in is the Baxtorian Falls area, where Waterfall Quest begins. The fastest route as a new member is: Grand Exchange teleport (everyone starts with this) → run north-east through Ardougne → reach the Tree Gnome Stronghold area → head east to Baxtorian Falls. Have this route in mind before you log in.

⚡ 30-Minute Goal Summary

In your first 30 minutes as a member, your goals are: 1) Complete Waterfall Quest (30 Attack/Strength XP) 2) Complete Tree Gnome Village (Spirit Trees + more XP) 3) Start The Grand Tree 4) Buy basic members gear 5) Set up your kingdom and farm run. Achieving all five puts you weeks ahead of players who wander aimlessly.

🏃 Minute 0-5: Rush Waterfall Quest

The moment you log into a members world, open your quest log and start Waterfall Quest. This is the single most important thing you can do in your OSRS first member steps. Waterfall Quest is a members-only quest that gives 27,500 total combat XP — enough to take your Attack and Strength from level 1 to level 30 — and it takes only 15 minutes.

① Getting to Baxtorian Falls

From the Grand Exchange, run east through the Ardougne farmland, then north through the gnome lands. The Baxtorian Falls are located east of the Tree Gnome Stronghold. The run takes about 2-3 minutes. You can also use the Ardougne teleport if you've completed the Ardougne easy diary, but as a new member you likely haven't. Just run — the path is safe and there are no aggressive monsters along the way.

Route: GE → East toward Ardougne → North past Ardougne zoo → Continue north through the fenced area → Cross the bridge → Baxtorian Falls is straight ahead. Look for the large waterfall and the house belonging to Almer (the quest starter).

② Starting the Quest

Talk to Almer, who is standing outside his house just west of the waterfall. He'll tell you about his son who went missing in the Waterfall Dungeon. Read the book on the table inside the house. Then head into the waterfall itself — the entrance is behind the waterfall, through a small cave opening. Be careful: there are level 50+ monsters inside, but you can navigate through without fighting if you follow the safe path.

🎯 Waterfall Dungeon Safe Route

Enter the waterfall → Take the southern path → Go through the narrow crevice (safe spot) → Turn west → Follow the wall to the end → You'll find a fire and a cutscene → Search the chest → Kill the level 40 moss giant (safe-spottable) → Take the key → Open the gate → Quest complete!

Total time: 10-12 minutes with a good route.
Reward: 13,750 Attack XP + 13,750 Strength XP = Level 30 Attack and Strength instantly.

③ Applying the XP Lamps

When you complete Waterfall Quest, you receive two XP lamps: one for Attack and one for Strength. Apply them both immediately. You'll go from level 1 to roughly level 30 in both skills. This single quest skip hours of early combat training. If you had 10 Attack from the tutorial, you'll reach level 33 or higher. Congratulations — you're now stronger than most F2P maxed players, and it's only been 15 minutes.

💡 Pro Tip: Don't leave Baxtorian Falls yet. The next quest (Tree Gnome Village) starts just a short run south-west at the Tree Gnome Stronghold. Always chain quests that are in the same area — this is the golden rule of efficient questing.

⚔️ Minute 5-10: Tree Gnome Village

With your new 30+ Attack and Strength, head south-west to the Tree Gnome Stronghold to start Tree Gnome Village. This quest gives you 11,450 Attack XP and unlocks Spirit Trees, which are one of the best free transportation methods in OSRS. If you're following our OSRS membership checklist, this is step two.

① Finding the Starting Point

The quest starts at the Grand Tree, which is the massive tree in the center of the Tree Gnome Stronghold. Climb up the ladder and talk to King Narnode Shareen on the top floor. He'll tell you about the gnome ambassador who has been captured and taken to the Tree Gnome Village maze. This is a straightforward quest — the maze is the main obstacle.

② Navigating the Maze

The Tree Gnome Village maze is located just west of the Stronghold. Enter the maze and follow this route: start by heading north, then east, then south through a series of tunnels. Watch out for level 55 Khazard soldiers — they hit hard for your level, so bring the food you prepared. The correct path through the maze requires trial and error if you don't use a guide, but with a guide open you can complete it in 5 minutes. The maze exit is in the south-east corner.

💡 Pro Tip: The maze has aggressive level 55 soldiers, but they're not too dangerous with your new stats. Use Protect from Melee if you have it. If you die, you'll respawn in Lumbridge — just run back. The quest items are all kept on death.

③ Rewards and Spirit Trees

Complete the quest by talking to the captured gnome ambassador and then reporting back to the King. You'll receive 11,450 Attack XP and access to Spirit Trees. Spirit Trees are magical trees that teleport you between major cities including Varrock, Falador, Port Sarim, Tree Gnome Stronghold, and the Tree Gnome Village. This transportation network is invaluable and completely free. Use a spirit tree by clicking on it and selecting your destination.

🧭 Minute 10-15: The Grand Tree

Still in the Tree Gnome Stronghold, immediately start The Grand Tree. This quest requires 25 Agility. If you don't have it yet, spend 15 minutes at the Gnome Stronghold agility course (the course is inside the stronghold itself). You should be able to reach 25 Agility quickly. The Grand Tree gives 18,400 Attack XP and unlocks Gnome Gliders, which are another major transportation network.

① Agility Training at Gnome Stronghold

If you haven't trained Agility yet, do it now. The Gnome Stronghold agility course is the best place for early Agility training. It consists of obstacles: log balance, net, branch, balance rope, and a hole you jump through. Complete laps until you reach 25 Agility. Each lap takes about 30 seconds and gives around 85 XP at low levels. You'll need about 15-20 laps to go from level 1 to level 25. Yes, this is a slight detour from the 30-minute goal, but The Grand Tree is worth the investment.

② Completing The Grand Tree

The Grand Tree quest sends you through the Grand Tree itself. You'll climb through multiple floors, solve puzzles, and fight a level 50 demon near the end. The demon can be safespotted with ranged or magic if you have it. Bring the food you prepared earlier. The quest takes about 20-25 minutes with the Agility training included.

The key reward is 18,400 Attack XP, which combined with Waterfall and Tree Gnome Village brings you to approximately level 40 Attack. At level 40 Attack, you can use the Dragon Scimitar — the best melee weapon until level 75 Attack. You'll need Monkey Madness I to actually wield it, but reaching level 40 is the first requirement.

🎯 Combat XP Tracker (First 30 Minutes)

Waterfall Quest: 13,750 Atk + 13,750 Str = 27,500 total XP → ~Level 30 Atk/Str
Tree Gnome Village: 11,450 Atk XP → ~Level 35 Atk
The Grand Tree: 18,400 Atk XP → ~Level 40 Atk
Total: 57,350 combat XP in 45-60 minutes from zero requirements. Unreal efficiency.

③ Gnome Gliders Unlock

After completing The Grand Tree, you unlock Gnome Gliders — piloted by the gnome navigators. Gliders can take you to the Grand Tree, Al Kharid, Karamja, Feldip Hills, and Digsite. While not as extensive as fairy rings, gliders are useful for reaching certain areas quickly. Combined with Spirit Trees, you now have a solid transportation network for the early game.

📦 Minute 15-20: Buy Member Gear

With your new combat stats, it's time to buy the essential members gear that will carry you through the early and mid-game. If you're wondering just became member OSRS what to do with your starting gold, this section has the answer.

① Essential Gear Shopping List

Head to the Grand Exchange and buy these items. If you have limited gold, prioritize in this order:

Item Estimated Cost Priority Why You Need It
Dragon Scimitar ~100K GP ★★★★★ Best weapon 40-75 Attack
Rune Scimitar ~20K GP ★★★★★ Best weapon until Dragon
Amulet of Glory ~10K GP ★★★★★ Best stats + teleports
Ring of Dueling ~2K GP ★★★★★ Teleport to Castle Wars/Duel Arena
Games Necklace ~1K GP ★★★★☆ Teleport to Barbarian Assault
Dragon Dagger (p++) ~30K GP ★★★★☆ Great special attack weapon

② How to Make Your First 100K GP

If you don't have enough gold for the items above, here are the fastest ways to make your first 100K as a new member:

Cowhides: Kill cows in Lumbridge or Falador, bank the hides, and sell them on the Grand Exchange. Cowhides sell for around 200 GP each, and you can kill 100+ per hour. This makes about 20K GP per hour.

Telegrabbing Wines of Zamorak: Use the Telekinetic Grab spell to steal Wines of Zamorak from the Chaos Temple (deep wilderness, level 12). Each wine sells for about 1K GP, and you can grab 60+ per hour. This makes 60K+ GP per hour with almost no requirements.

Stronghold of Security: Complete the Stronghold of Security beneath Barbarian Village. It gives 10K GP, a Fancy Boots, and a Fighting Boots item. The stronghold takes about 20 minutes and the gold is completely free.

💡 Pro Tip: Don't buy a Dragon Scimitar yet if you haven't completed Monkey Madness I — you can't wield it until that quest is done. Buy a Rune Scimitar for now, then upgrade to Dragon once you finish MM1. The Rune Scimitar costs only 20K and is the best weapon until you unlock the Dragon Scimitar.

📋 Minute 20-30: Set Up Daily Routine

The last 10 minutes of your first half-hour as a member should be spent setting up your daily routine. Daily activities in OSRS generate passive XP and gold that accumulate over weeks and months. If you're following our OSRS membership checklist, this step ensures long-term success.

① Kingdom Management Setup

Complete Throne of Miscellania and Royal Trouble as soon as you can. These quests unlock the Kingdom Management system, which generates passive resources for you. For now, just visit the kingdom and invest any spare gold into the coffer. You need 750K GP for maximum efficiency, but even 100K GP will start generating resources. Set your subjects to gather hardwood and herbs — these are the most profitable options for new players.

Quick Kingdom Setup: Travel to Miscellania via the Rellekka boat. Talk to the Advisor to manage your subjects. Allocate your workers based on what you want. Check back every 2-3 days to top up approval and collect resources. Approval rating drops by 1% per day, so visit regularly.

② Farming Runs Setup

Farming is one of the most important skills for ongoing income. Start by completing the Fairy Tale I quest (it's short and gives 1,500 Farming XP). Then plant your first crops. Here's your initial farming setup:

Allotments: Plant potatoes or sweetcorn in the Lumbridge, Falador, and Catherby allotment patches. These grow in 40-60 minutes and give decent XP and produce.

Herb Runs: Once you have 30 Farming, start planting Guam Marrentil or Tarromin seeds. Herb runs take 15 minutes per cycle and generate 50K-100K GP per run depending on your herb level. Do herb runs every 90 minutes.

Tree Patches: Plant oak trees in the Varrock, Falador, and Lumbridge tree patches. Trees take 3-4 hours to grow but give massive XP per check. Oak trees give 450 XP per check, which is excellent for early Farming levels.

③ Birdhouse Runs

Birdhouse runs are passive Hunter training that also generates bird nests, which are used in Herblore and sell for good gold. You need 5 Hunter to start. Build birdhouses on Fossil Island (requires Bone Voyage quest completion, but you can rush this). Each birdhouse run takes 2 minutes and generates 2-4 bird nests every 50 minutes. Bird nests sell for about 5K GP each on the Grand Exchange.

Quick Birdhouse Setup: Complete Bone Voyage → Teleport to Fossil Island → Build 4 birdhouses using the clockwork and logs → Fill with seeds → Check back in 50 minutes. Rinse and repeat. This is the most efficient passive Hunter XP in the game.

🎯 Your First Week as a Member

Your first 30 minutes are complete, but the work isn't done. Here's what to prioritize during your first week as a member to maintain momentum and set up your account for long-term success. These priorities build on everything you've accomplished in that first half-hour.

① Day 1-2: Complete the Gnome Quest Line

Finish what you started. Complete Monkey Madness I to unlock the Dragon Scimitar. After that, complete Fight Arena if you haven't already, then Lost City for the Dragon Dagger (p++). These quests give you the best early-game weapons and all the Attack XP you need for the foreseeable future. Your goal by day 2: wield Dragon Scimitar, have 40+ Strength, and be working toward 50 Attack.

② Day 3-4: Recipe for Disaster Prerequisites

Start working on the skill requirements for Recipe for Disaster. You need: 40 Cooking, 50 Firemaking, 31 Fishing, 20 Farming, 30 Woodcutting, and eventually 100+ Combat. Train these skills through your daily routines (farm runs, firemaking for Wintertodt) rather than grinding them directly. The RFD prerequisite skills are useful in their own right.

③ Day 5-7: Fairy Rings and Transportation

Complete Fairy Tale II (at least to the point of unlocking fairy rings). Fairy rings are the single most important transportation system in OSRS. Once unlocked, start using them for everything — Slayer tasks, clue scrolls, bossing, and general travel. The time savings are enormous. Also unlock the Ardougne cloak (Ardougne easy diary) for another free teleport.

④ Ongoing: Daily Routine

By the end of your first week, your daily routine should be:

Morning: Check Kingdom → Collect resources → Refill coffer.
Throughout day: Herb runs (every 90 min) → Birdhouse runs (every 50 min).
Evening: Complete quests or train combat for Slayer.
Before bed: Plant tree seeds → Allotment patches.

This daily routine generates 200K-500K GP per day passively, plus thousands of XP in Farming, Hunter, and Herblore. All of this comes from the setup you did in your first 30 minutes as a member.

💡 Pro Tip: The single biggest mistake new members make is wasting time. They spend hours reading guides, wandering around, and getting distracted by random content. Follow this first-week plan strictly and you'll be ahead of 90% of new members within 7 days of gameplay. Focus on quests and daily routines — everything else can wait.

❓ FAQ — First 30 Minutes as a Member

What should I do first when I become a member in OSRS in 2026?

Your first action as a member should be to complete Waterfall Quest. It requires no skills, takes 15 minutes, and gives 27,500 combat XP. After that, complete Tree Gnome Village and The Grand Tree for another 30K XP and transportation unlocks. Then buy your essential gear (Rune Scimitar, Amulet of Glory, Ring of Dueling) and set up your Kingdom management and farm runs.

How do I get 30 Attack and 30 Strength fast as a member?

Complete Waterfall Quest. It gives 13,750 Attack XP and 13,750 Strength XP — enough to take you from level 1 to level 30 in both skills. This takes about 15 minutes and requires no prior skills. There is no faster way in the entire game to get 30 Attack and Strength.

What gear should I buy first as a new member?

Buy: Rune Scimitar (~20K), Amulet of Glory (~10K), Ring of Dueling (~2K), Games Necklace (~1K), Magic Shortbow (~1K), and eventually Dragon Dagger (p++) (~30K) and Dragon Scimitar (~100K) after completing Monkey Madness I. Total cost: about 50K GP for the essentials, 160K GP for the full setup.

What's the biggest mistake new members make?

The biggest mistake is wasting time on inefficient activities. New members often wander around exploring, try to train skills from level 1 with slow methods, or get overwhelmed by the sheer amount of content. Instead, follow a structured plan: rush Waterfall Quest, complete key quests for XP, set up daily routines, and only then explore the new content you've unlocked.

How do I set up Kingdom Management as a new member?

First, complete Throne of Miscellania and Royal Trouble quests. Then travel to Miscellania by boat from Rellekka. Talk to the Advisor and allocate your subjects. For maximum profit, put 10 workers on hardwood and 5 on herbs. Invest at least 100K GP into the coffer (750K is ideal). Check your kingdom every 2-3 days to maintain 95%+ approval and collect resources.

Should I train skills or do quests first as a member?

Do quests first. Quests give massive XP rewards that skip early skill training entirely. For example, Waterfall Quest skips 15 hours of combat training. The Grand Tree gives Agility XP that skips the first few tedious levels. Only train skills when you need a specific level for a quest requirement. Quest-first is always the most efficient path.

How do I make money as a new member quickly?

The fastest early money-makers are: telegrabbing Wines of Zamorak (60K+/hr), killing cows for cowhides (20K/hr), and completing the Stronghold of Security (10K GP one-time). For passive income, set up Kingdom Management (100K+/day) and start farming herbs (50K-100K per run once you have the level). Birdhouse runs also generate 10K-20K per run from bird nests.

What transportation methods should I unlock first?

Unlock Spirit Trees (Tree Gnome Village quest), Gnome Gliders (The Grand Tree), Fairy Rings (Fairy Tale II partial completion), and the Ardougne Cloak (Ardougne easy diary). These four transportation methods cover 90% of the map and are all free to use. Also buy an Amulet of Glory for teleports to Edgeville, Draynor, and Karamja.

Is it worth becoming a member at a low level?

Absolutely. Becoming a member at level 3 with no skills is completely viable and recommended. Members quests like Waterfall Quest give massive XP that skips the early game entirely. You can go from level 3 to level 40 combat in under 2 hours as a member. It would take 50+ hours of F2P training to reach the same level. Always become a member as soon as you can afford it.

What should I NOT do in my first 30 minutes as a member?

Do NOT: wander around exploring randomly, train combat on goblins (you have quests for XP), try to complete quests without a guide, buy expensive gear before completing quests, ignore daily routines, or try to train members skills like Slayer from level 1. Stick to the plan: quest, gear up, set up dailies, then explore. This order saves you dozens of hours.
