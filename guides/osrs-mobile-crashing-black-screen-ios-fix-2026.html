🍎 OSRS Mobile Freezing & Black Screen on iPhone/iOS — Fix Guide 2026

Does Old School RuneScape freeze on your iPhone, get stuck on a black screen, or refuse to open after you tap the icon? This 2026 guide covers the real iOS-specific fixes: the 30-second force-restart, iOS version requirements, storage cleanup, clean reinstall, Jagex Account / Apple ID login problems, and Background App Refresh crashes.

📅 Updated: July 2026 — Troubleshooting Guide · 12 min read · Level: Novice

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Force-close + hard restart fixes most temporary freezes in under a minute.
  • 📌 OSRS Mobile needs iOS 13 or newer — older iPhones (iPhone 6s/7/8 on iOS 12) can't run the current app.
  • 📌 Keep 1–2 GB free; a full iPhone causes black screens and app freezes.
  • 📌 Login is via your Jagex account (email), not Game Center — a black screen at login is usually a Jagex SSO/token issue.
  • 📌 Toggle Background App Refresh if the app crashes when you return to it.

🧊 1. Why OSRS Mobile Freezes or Black-Screens on iOS

The OSRS Mobile client on iPhone and iPad is Jagex's official iOS app from the App Store. iOS is more locked-down than Android, so the failure modes are a little different — there are fewer "driver" problems, but iOS version limits, storage pressure, and background-app management cause most of the trouble.

On iPhone, crashes usually come from one of these:

🧩 The Common iOS Failure Modes

  • A frozen/temporary state — the app got stuck and just needs a force-close + restart.
  • Unsupported iOS version — the device is below the minimum the app supports.
  • Storage pressure — no room to write cache, so it hangs on a black screen.
  • Login/SSO stall — a cached Jagex token or a Game Center mismatch at the login screen.
  • Background termination — iOS killed the app in the background and it crashes on resume.

Your character, bank, and membership are stored on Jagex's servers, so none of these fixes can delete your progress — worst case you just log in again.

💡 Start Here

Always try Fix 1 (force-close + restart) first. On iOS it resolves the majority of "it just froze" reports and takes less than a minute.

🔄 2. Fix 1 — Force-Close & Hard Restart (The 30-Second Fix)

If the app is frozen, tapping the icon does nothing, or you see a black screen that never loads, the fastest fix is to completely shut the app down and restart the device.

Force-Close OSRS Mobile

  • iPhone X / 11 / 12 / 13 / 14 / 15 / iPad with Face ID: swipe up from the bottom and pause in the middle to open the App Switcher, then swipe OSRS Mobile upward to close it.
  • iPhone with Home button (SE 2nd/3rd gen, 8, 7, 6s): double-click Home, then swipe the app up.

Hard Restart the Device

  • Face ID iPhones: press & quickly release Volume Up, press & quickly release Volume Down, then hold the Side button until the Apple logo appears.
  • Home-button iPhones: hold Side (or Top) + Home together until the Apple logo appears.

After the restart, reopen OSRS Mobile. In most cases the freeze is gone. If it returns immediately, move to the storage and login fixes below.

📲 3. Fix 2 — Update to a Supported iOS Version (iOS 13+)

OSRS Mobile requires a modern iOS version. As of 2026 the app targets iOS 13 or newer. Devices stuck on iOS 12 or earlier — chiefly the iPhone 5s, iPhone 6/6 Plus, and iPhone 6s/6s Plus once they stop receiving updates — will see "This app requires iOS 13.0 or later" and cannot launch it.

🔧 Check & Update Your iOS Version

  1. Open Settings → General → About and read the Software Version.
  2. If it is below iOS 13, open Settings → General → Software Update.
  3. If an update is available, install it over Wi-Fi with the phone plugged in.
  4. If no update is offered, your device is too old for the current iOS and therefore cannot run the current app.
🪫 Too Old to Update?

If your iPhone can't reach iOS 13, the hardware itself is the limit — there is no setting to bypass it. Your account is safe; play on a newer iPhone/iPad or the desktop client (including the free RuneLite client on PC). Keep your Jagex account login handy so you can pick up exactly where you left off.

💾 4. Fix 3 — Free Up iPhone Storage

Even on iOS, OSRS Mobile keeps a local cache of game assets. When your iPhone is nearly full, the app cannot write temporary files and hangs on a black screen — often right as the world loads. Jagex recommends keeping about 1–2 GB free.

🚀 Free Space on iPhone

  • Open Settings → General → iPhone Storage to see what's using space.
  • Tap large apps and choose Offload App (keeps data) or Delete App for ones you don't use.
  • Enable Settings → Photos → Optimize iPhone Storage to shrink your library in the cloud.
  • Clear Safari cache (Settings → Safari → Clear History and Website Data) if needed.
  • After freeing space, force-close and reopen OSRS Mobile (Fix 1).

Unlike Android, iOS does not expose a separate "clear cache" button for third-party apps. Freeing system storage and reinstalling (Fix 4) are the iOS equivalents of an Android cache clear.

♻️ 5. Fix 4 — Reinstall OSRS Mobile Cleanly

If the app is corrupted — common after an interrupted App Store update or an iCloud restore — a clean reinstall is the reliable repair. Your account is server-side, so reinstalling never deletes your character, bank, or membership.

Clean Reinstall Steps

  1. Confirm you know your Jagex account email and password (you'll log in again).
  2. Long-press the OSRS Mobile icon → Remove App → Delete App.
  3. Open the App Store, search "Old School RuneScape" by Jagex, and reinstall.
  4. Open it and log in with your Jagex account.
  5. Let the cache fully download on stable Wi-Fi before playing.
🔐 Before You Delete

Make sure your Jagex account recovery options (email + 2FA) are working. If you previously used Game Center, ensure your account is migrated to a Jagex account first (see Fix 5) so the reinstall doesn't strand you at login.

🔐 6. Fix 5 — Jagex Account / Apple ID Login & Black Screen at Login

Modern OSRS Mobile logs in through a Jagex account (your email + password, with optional two-factor authentication). Legacy Game Center linking is no longer how the app authenticates. A black or blank screen that appears after the login/SSO screen — or a spinner that never finishes — is usually a stuck authentication token or a network/SSO hiccup, not a graphics problem.

Step 1 — Confirm Your Jagex Account Login

  • On a browser, go to the Jagex account site and log in with your email + password to confirm the credentials work.
  • If you previously played via Game Center, make sure that character has been imported/linked into a Jagex account — Jagex has required Jagex accounts for some time, and un-migrated characters can fail to load.
  • Verify your email is verified and 2FA is set up so re-login is smooth.

Step 2 — Clear the Stuck Login State on iPhone

  • Force-close OSRS Mobile (Fix 1).
  • Toggle Airplane Mode on for 10 seconds, then off, to reset the network handshake.
  • Open Settings → Safari → Clear History and Website Data (the SSO popup uses Safari's web view).
  • Reopen OSRS Mobile and log in again.
⚠️ "Apple ID" vs. "Jagex Account"

Your Apple ID is only used to download the app from the App Store — it is not your OSRS login. Logging in to the game uses your Jagex account email. If the black screen appears at the Jagex SSO screen, the problem is the Jagex account session, never the Apple ID.

🔄 7. Fix 6 — Background App Refresh & Resume Crashes

A very common iOS complaint: you switch to another app, then come back to OSRS Mobile and it is frozen or crashes to a black screen. This is iOS memory management — the system suspended or terminated the game in the background.

🎛️ Tune Background App Refresh

  • Open Settings → General → Background App Refresh.
  • For OSRS Mobile, you can either enable it (so iOS keeps the session warm) or disable it (so you always start fresh, avoiding a broken resume state).
  • If the app crashes on return, try the opposite of your current setting — many users fix resume crashes by turning it off and fully reopening the app each time.
  • Also disable Low Power Mode while playing; it aggressively suspends background apps.
💡 The Safest Habit

On iPhone, treat OSRS Mobile like a foreground-only app: when you're done, force-close it (Fix 1). When you return, open it fresh and log in. This avoids nearly all resume-related black screens regardless of the Background App Refresh setting.

❓ 9. iOS Crash FAQs

Will reinstalling delete my account, bank, or membership?

No. Deleting the app only removes the local program and its cache from your iPhone. Your character, bank, and membership are tied to your Jagex account on Jagex's servers. After reinstalling, just log in with your Jagex account email and password.

My iPhone can't update past iOS 12 — can I still play?

Not on that device. The current OSRS Mobile app requires iOS 13+. If your iPhone no longer receives iOS updates, the hardware is the limiting factor. Your account is safe — use a newer iPhone/iPad or the desktop client and log in with the same Jagex account.

The screen goes black right after I tap "Play" / after the login screen — what now?

If it's after login, it's an authentication/SSO stall (Fix 5): clear Safari website data, toggle Airplane Mode, and log in again. If it's before login, it's usually storage pressure (Fix 3) or a corrupted install (Fix 4). Free space first, then reinstall if needed.

I used Game Center before — why won't it log me in now?

Game Center linking is legacy. OSRS Mobile now authenticates through a Jagex account. If your character was never imported into a Jagex account, the login can fail or hang. Recover/link the character to a Jagex account on a browser first, then log in on the app with that Jagex account email.

Why does it crash every time I switch away and come back?

iOS suspended or terminated the app in the background. Toggle Background App Refresh (Fix 6) to the opposite setting, turn off Low Power Mode while playing, and get into the habit of force-closing the app when you're done so each session starts fresh.
