1. The Biggest Change: Sailing Skill

If you haven't played since early 2025 or earlier, the single biggest change waiting for you is Sailing — Old School RuneScape's 24th skill, officially released on November 19, 2025 after three years of community-driven design.

Sailing is a Utility skill that gives you command of your own ship. You can travel across the seas of Gielinor, discover new islands, fight sea monsters, and access exclusive resources. It weaves into almost every other skill in the game.

🎯 What You Need to Know as a Returning Player

QuestionAnswer
How do I start?Complete The Pandemonium quest at Port Sarim docks. No requirements needed — starts you off with a Raft and unlocks Sailing.
What are the 4 training methods?Port Tasks (steady XP), Shipwreck Salvaging (AFK at level 15), Sea Charting (exploration), Barracuda Trials (fastest XP at level 30).
What resources does it unlock?New trees (Jatoba at 40 WC, Ironwood at 80, Rosewood at 92), new ores (Lead at 25 Mining, Rubium at 48, Nickel at 74), new fish (Marlin heals 24 HP).
New Slayer monsters?Gryphons (51 Slayer), Aquanites (78), Frost Dragons (87) — plus Shellbane Gryphon boss.
Is Sailing required?It's optional but highly recommended. Unlocks transportation, equipment (Belle's Folly, Aquanite Hopper, new blowpipes), and hybrid training.
⛵ Pro Tip: Your First Sailing Steps

Don't delay starting Sailing. Even if you only do the Pandemonium quest for now (takes ~20 minutes), you'll unlock level 6 and the Raft. From there, Port Tasks at Port Sarim give steady XP while you re-learn the game. The passive wind mechanic gives 150-300 bonus XP every 60 seconds — click the wind icon when it appears.

🛠️ June 2026 Sailing Updates

In the June 17, 2026 game update, Jagex made several quality-of-life changes:

  • Bounty Task rewards now scale based on your contribution to kills
  • Cannon convenience — picking up loot no longer interrupts cannon firing
  • Item value adjustments — Dragon Keel Parts, Cannon Barrel, and other Sailing items had their GE values updated for Death's Coffer calculations

2. Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts

Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts launched in April 2026 and is the most recent League season. If you missed earlier Leagues (Twisted, Trailblazer, Reloaded, Raging Echoes), this one introduced a unique Demonic Pacts mechanic — special buffs and challenges tied to demonic-themed content.

🎮 Key Features

  • Demonic Pacts — temporary power boosts with trade-offs
  • New relic system — choose your path through demonic-themed upgrades
  • Exclusive rewards — Soulreaper Axe (o) cosmetic, Demonic Pacts Throne Scroll, and ornament kits for Trident of the Swamp, Trident of the Seas, and Iban Staff
  • Slayer Helmet cosmetic — unlockable through league tasks (1,000 Slayer points)

📋 Rewards Adjusted in June 2026 Update

The June 17 patch made several visual and functional tweaks to League rewards:

  • Soulreaper Axe (o) — axe head made larger to match its powerful design
  • Demonic Pacts Throne Scroll — now requires 0 Construction to apply (was 99)
  • Toxic Trident (e) (o) — color darkened for better differentiation
  • Trident of the Seas (o) — icon rotated to match base version
  • Dual-wield league trophies and Storm cruiser's coat — no longer create "tail" effect behind character
  • Trident decoration — now works both ways (use staff on pack OR pack on staff)
💡 League Still Active?

Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts was still running as of June 2026. If you haven't claimed your rewards yet, check the League interface — rewards earned during the league are permanent and usable on your main account even after the league ends.

3. Bank Tags & Inventory Management

Released on June 17, 2026, Bank Tags are now available on mobile and the Official Client. This feature lets you organize your bank into up to 20 custom categories — no more scrolling through 800 items to find your gear setup.

🏷️ How Bank Tags Work

Creating a Tag

Click the + button in the top-left of your bank interface. Type a name (up to 25 alphanumeric characters). Choose an item as your tag icon via the search box.

Adding Items

Drag items from your main bank onto the tag icon on the left sidebar. They remain in your main bank — the tag is just a filtered view.

Organizing

Drag items freely within a tag to reorder. You can even leave gaps or blank rows for visual separation. Use Shift + drag to quickly duplicate a tag.

FeatureDetails
Max tags20 custom categories
Tag name limit25 alphanumeric characters
CompatibilityMobile, Official Client, and existing third-party client tags unaffected
Potion StorageSeparate from tag system — potions need to be in main bank to appear in tags
Main bankCompletely unaffected — tags are just an alternative view
💡 Returning Player Bank Tip

Create your first 3 tags immediately: "Melee Gear", "Ranged Gear", and "Mage Gear". Add your equipment, ammo, and potions to each. This alone will save you hours of bank sorting every week.

4. Trouver System Rework (Wilderness Death Changes)

One of the most impactful changes for anyone who ventures into the Wilderness. The Trouver Parchment system was completely overhauled on June 17, 2026. The goal was simple: reduce the risk of permanently losing key untradeable items.

📋 Three Categories of Untradeables

CategoryExamplesDeath Below 20 WildyDeath Above 20 Wildy
No combat bonusGraceful outfitNever lostNever lost
Combat bonus (mid-tier)Fire cape, Ava's assembler, Defenders, Void Knight gear, Fighter hat, Halos, Penance gearDamaged — repair at PerduDamaged — repair at Perdu
High-tier (Trouver required)Infernal cape, Imbued heart, Tarn's locketDamaged — repair normallyMangled — costs 500K GP to repair

🛡️ Key Changes from Old System

  • Graceful never lost anymore — even if you die at 50+ Wilderness
  • Fire capes, defenders, void — now only get damaged, never lost
  • Trouver Parchment is now permanent — apply once and it stays forever
  • Ornament kits — PKers can still take tradeable ornament kits, you keep the damaged base item
  • Warning system coming — Jagex is developing a warning when you enter the Wildy with high-risk untradeables
⚠️ What This Means for You

If you were afraid of taking good gear into the Wilderness before, this update changes everything. Your Fire cape, Ava's assembler, and Defenders are safe from permanent loss. Equip them with confidence — the worst that happens is a repair bill. For Infernal and other high-tier items, use a Trouver Parchment once and it's permanent protection.

5. New Slayer Content: Frost Dragons

Frost Dragons were added as a new Slayer monster accessible at level 87 Sailing. In the June 17, 2026 update, two new Slayer unlocks were added (100 Slayer Points each):

Both unlocks can be toggled off at any time (no refund). The Frost Dragon task now offers a viable alternative to other high-level Slayer tasks, especially valuable for Ironmen collecting Frost Dragon bones and Dragon Sheets.

6. PvP Improvements & Deadman Events

PvP has seen several updates since early 2026:

⚔️ PvP Quality Improvements (Starting March 2026)

Ongoing balance changes and quality-of-life improvements for PvP content across the game, including:

  • Vesta's Spear (bh) — fixed a bug where switching weapons after the special attack didn't block ranged damage
  • Emir's Arena — now allows Ancient Warriors' weapons
  • Last Man Standing — major equipment overhaul (Dragon Crossbow, Dragon Claws, Berserker ring (i) added to Max/Med loadouts)
  • LMS defaults — new player presets now include 3 Saradomin brews, 2 Sanfew serums, 10 sharks; default spellbook set to Ancient Magicks

🏆 Deadman Events

  • Deadman: Annihilation (January 30 - February 20, 2026) — Season 2 of the revived Deadman Mode
  • Deadman All Stars Season 3 (June 2026) — Top players compete in the seasonal finale
💡 PvP Tip for Returning Players

If you haven't tried LMS (Last Man Standing) before, now is a great time. The June 17 update added Dragon Claws and Dragon Crossbow to the loot pool, and new players get improved default presets. It's a zero-risk way to learn PvP mechanics.

7. Grand Exchange Improvements

On June 15, 2026, Jagex published a developer blog outlining planned improvements to the Grand Exchange. The key proposal being discussed is transactions beyond max cash stack — currently capped at 2,147,483,647 GP (Integer MAX_VALUE). This would allow trading high-value items like Twisted Bow (now ~1.55B GP) and 3rd Age items more efficiently.

💡 What This Means

Currently, items worth over max cash must be traded manually (player-to-player with trust involved). If the GE cap is raised, it would make trading mega-rare items safer and more liquid. As of June 2026, this is still being developed — no release date confirmed.

8. Jagex Accounts & Security Changes

On June 12, 2026, Jagex announced the full transition to Jagex Accounts. This is a mandatory migration that improves account security with:

  • Two-factor authentication (2FA) built in
  • Single login for all your OSRS and RS3 characters
  • Improved password recovery system
  • Linked email verification

If you haven't migrated yet, you'll be prompted to do so on your next login. The process takes about 5 minutes — have your original account email ready.

9. Quick Catch-Up Roadmap (1 Week Plan)

Follow this plan to get up to speed fast:

📅 Day 1 — Account & Security Check
Migrate to Jagex Account (5 min). Check your bank — what items do you still have? What might have changed value? Check the GE for your old gear.
📅 Day 2 — Start Sailing
Go to Port Sarim docks. Complete The Pandemonium quest (~20 min, no requirements). You'll start at level 6 with a Raft. Do a few Port Tasks to learn the system.
📅 Day 3 — Organize Your Bank
Use the new Bank Tags to set up 3-5 tags for your gear setups. Set up your new tag system — it'll save you hours of frustration.
📅 Day 4 — Wilderness & Trouver
Review the new Trouver system. If you have an Infernal cape or other high-tier untradeables, apply a Trouver Parchment for permanent protection. Try the new Wilderness content with confidence.
📅 Day 5 — Slayer & Combat
Check your Slayer master. If you have 87 Sailing, try Frost Dragons. Review your gear against current prices (see our Gear Inflation Guide for what's trending).
📅 Day 6-7 — Explore & Settle In
Try the things you missed most. Check out the Sailing training methods. Browse the GE for new items. Join a clan. The game may have changed, but the core OSRS experience is as good as ever.

10. Returning Player FAQs

I quit before Sailing was announced. What did I miss?

Everything. Sailing is OSRS's 24th skill, released November 19, 2025. It's a utility skill that adds ship travel, new islands, 4 training methods, new resources (Jatoba logs, Lead ore, Marlin fish), 4 new Slayer monsters, and multiple new equipment pieces (Belle's Folly, Aquanite Hopper, Ironwood/Rosewood blowpipes). Start at Port Sarim with the Pandemonium quest.

Is my old gear still good?

Mostly yes, but check current prices. The OSRS economy has seen inflation — Twisted Bow is over 1.5B GP, Scythe of Vitur and Tumeken's Shadow are in the hundreds of millions. If you own any mega-rares, they've likely appreciated. Check our Gear Inflation Guide for detailed price trends.

What's the deal with Bank Tags?

Bank Tags are a new organization feature released June 17, 2026 for mobile and Official Client. You can create up to 20 custom tags to filter your bank items. Drag items onto a tag icon to create a filtered view. Your main bank is unaffected — tags are just alternative views.

Can I still lose my Fire Cape in the Wilderness?

No. The Trouver System Rework (June 17, 2026) changed this. Fire capes, Ava's assemblers, Defenders, Void Knight gear, and Halos now only get damaged on death — they are never permanently lost. You pay Perdu a small fee to repair them.

What happened to Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts?

It launched in April 2026 and was still active as of June 2026. The June 17 update adjusted several cosmetic rewards (Soulreaper Axe visual, Trident colors, Throne Scroll requirements). If you participated, check the reward interface for your unlocks.

Is there a new Slayer monster I should know about?

Frost Dragons (87 Sailing) are the newest addition. Two new Slayer unlocks were added in June 2026: "Chance of Heavy Frost" (increases task weight) and "I See Dragon" (increases task length), each costing 100 Slayer points.

Do I need a Jagex Account now?

Yes. Jagex announced full transition to Jagex Accounts on June 12, 2026. It's mandatory for continued access. The migration takes ~5 minutes and adds 2FA, linked email verification, and unified login for all your characters.

What's the best way to make money as a returning player?

Your best money maker depends on your stats. If you have access to bossing, Vorkath (~3-4M GP/h) and Zulrah (~2-3.5M GP/h) remain strong. For no-requirement methods, check our Money Making Zero Requirements guide. The GE Improvements blog also hints at future trading enhancements for high-value items.
