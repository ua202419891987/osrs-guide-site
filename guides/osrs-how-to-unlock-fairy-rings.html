OSRS How to Unlock Fairy Rings — Quick Guide 2026

  • 📌 Partial Access: Complete Lost City (30 min) — equip Dramen Staff to use fairy rings
  • 📌 Full Access: Complete Fairy Tale I + start Fairy Tale II — no staff required, frees weapon slot
  • 📌 Key Trick: You only need to START Part II, not finish it — saves 1-2 hours
  • 📌 Requirements: 31 Crafting, 36 WC (Lost City); 17 Farming, 29 Herblore (Fairy Tale I)
  • 📌 Top Codes: DKS (DKs), AIQ (Fight Caves), BJS (Fossil Island), CIR (Zulrah area)
🧚 Teleport Unlock — Updated June 2026

⚡ Fastest Fairy Ring Unlock: 5 Steps (2-3 Hours Total)

  1. Complete Lost City (30 min) → Get Dramen Staff, you can now use fairy rings (must equip staff)
  2. Complete A Fairy Tale Part I (45 min) → Get Magic Secateurs, fairy ring network expands
  3. Start (do NOT finish!) A Fairy Tale Part II (2 min) → From here on you can use fairy rings without equipping the staff ✅
  4. (Optional) Build fairy rings in your POH at 85 Construction → combined with jewelry box, this is the fastest teleport network in the game
  5. Memorize these key codes → DKS (Dagannoth Kings), AIQ (Fight Caves), BJS (Fossil Island), CIR (near Zulrah)

🎯 Key tip: Fairy Tale Part II does NOT need to be completed — just starting it removes the staff requirement!

Fairy rings are the most powerful free teleport network in OSRS — over 50 destinations across Gielinor using simple 3-letter codes. This guide walks you through unlocking them at both partial access (Dramen Staff required) and full access (no staff needed), plus a complete reference of the most useful fairy ring codes for bossing, skilling, and questing in 2026.

1. Why Fairy Rings Are Essential

Fairy rings provide instant, free teleportation to over 50 locations across the entire OSRS map. No runes required. No charges. No cooldowns. Just dial a 3-letter code and go. They're faster than walking, cheaper than teleport tablets, and reach destinations that no other teleport method can access. Here's what they unlock:

  • Bossing: Direct access near Dagannoth Kings (DKS), TzHaar Fight Caves (AIQ), Kalphite Queen (BKQ), God Wars Dungeon area (BKP)
  • Slayer: Near Slayer Tower (ALQ), Fremennik Slayer Dungeon (AJR), Kourend Catacombs (CIS)
  • Skilling: Piscatoris fishing (AKQ), Feldip Hills hunter (CIR), Fossil Island (BJS), Farming patches (various)
  • Clue Scrolls: Many hard, elite, and master clue steps are near fairy ring destinations
  • Questing: Numerous quest locations are fairy ring-adjacent

Fairy rings paired with a Player-Owned House (containing a fairy ring) create one of the fastest travel networks possible — house teleport → fairy ring → anywhere in Gielinor, all in under 10 seconds.

2. Step 1: Complete Lost City (Partial Access)

RequirementDetail
Skills Needed31 Crafting, 36 Woodcutting
CombatDefeat a level 101 Tree Spirit (melee only, no special attacks)
Items NeededAny axe, food (~10 lobsters or better), some combat gear
Time20–30 minutes

Lost City Walkthrough

  1. Start: Talk to the warrior near Lumbridge Swamp (east side, near Draynor Village). He tells you about Zanaris and the Dramen Tree.
  2. Find the Leprechaun: Head into Lumbridge Swamp. Find the tree stump in the center of the swamp (near where the dungeon entrance is). Search it to find a leprechaun. Ask about Zanaris.
  3. Cut the Dramen Tree: The leprechaun tells you to cut a specific tree nearby WITHOUT any weapon or shield equipped. Unequip everything (only bare hands/fists). Right-click the tree and select "Chop." A level 101 Tree Spirit attacks.
  4. Fight the Tree Spirit: Re-equip your weapon and armor. The Tree Spirit hits up to 12 damage and has roughly 85 HP. Protect from Melee prayer works. Kill it with melee (it's immune to ranged and magic). It drops the Dramen Branch.
  5. Craft the Dramen Staff: Take the branch to Lumbridge Castle bank. Use a knife on the branch (requires 31 Crafting) to create a Dramen Staff.
  6. Enter Zanaris: Bank all weapons and armor (Entrana's restrictions apply). Equip ONLY the Dramen Staff. Travel to Port Sarim, take the boat to Entrana. On Entrana, walk to the northwest corner and find the shed with the trapdoor. Enter it to reach Zanaris. Quest complete.

After Lost City, you can use fairy rings by equipping the Dramen Staff and clicking any fairy ring. You'll see the dial interface — enter a 3-letter code to teleport. This is "partial access" — functional but requires the staff in your weapon slot.

3. Step 2: A Fairy Tale Part I — Growing Pains

RequirementDetail
Skills Needed17 Farming, 29 Herblore (both boostable)
Prerequisite QuestsLost City, Nature Spirit
CombatDefeat a level 111 Tanglefoot boss (melee only, secateurs required)
Items NeededMagic secateurs (from Nature Spirit), various farming tools/items, food
Time45–60 minutes

The quest involves helping the Fairy Godfather cure a sick plant. Key sections: finding items to cure the Fairy Queen's sick plant (draining a bucket on a specific tree, using the proper farming cure), and fighting the Tanglefoot boss (level 111, melee only, must use Magic Secateurs to damage it). The Tanglefoot is easier than the Tree Spirit — just wear armor, use Protect from Melee, and attack with the secateurs.

🌱 Farming & Herblore Shortcut: You can boost both requirements. Use a Garden Pie (+3 Farming boost, buy on GE for ~3K GP) and a Botanical Pie (+4 Herblore boost, ~5K GP) to meet the requirements at level 14 Farming and 25 Herblore base. This saves 1-2 hours of skilling.

4. Step 3: Start A Fairy Tale Part II — Cure a Queen (Don't Finish!)

This is the key trick every OSRS veteran knows: you do NOT need to complete Fairy Tale Part II to get the full fairy ring benefit. Simply STARTING the quest is enough.

How to Start Fairy Tale Part II:

  1. Complete A Fairy Tale Part I.
  2. Talk to the Fairy Godfather in Zanaris (he's in the main fairy city area). Select the dialogue option to begin Part II.
  3. He assigns you the quest and gives you items. That's it — the quest is now started.
  4. From this moment forward, you can use fairy rings WITHOUT equipping the Dramen Staff. The staff becomes optional.

Why this works: The quest's storyline involves the Fairy Queen granting you permanent access to the fairy ring network. The game engine checks whether you've started Part II (not whether you've finished it). This is one of the most valuable early-game unlocks — freeing up your weapon slot while still using fairy rings.

Do I Ever Need to Finish Part II?

Only if you want the quest cape, the XP lamp reward, or access to the Fairy Resistance Hideout (used in A Kingdom Divided quest). For pure fairy ring functionality, starting Part II is sufficient. Many players leave Part II unfinished for years.

6. POH Fairy Ring — The Ultimate Setup

At 85 Construction (boostable from 80 with a +5 stew spice boost), you can build a fairy ring inside your Player-Owned House in the Superior Garden. Combined with an Ornate Jewellery Box and a Portal Nexus, your POH becomes the ultimate teleport hub:

  1. Teleport to your house (House tab or Con cape — 1 tick)
  2. Use Ornate Jewellery Box for any jewelry teleport (2 ticks)
  3. Use POH Fairy Ring for any fairy ring destination (3 ticks)
  4. Use Portal Nexus for any spellbook teleport (2 ticks)

A maxed POH is the single best account upgrade in OSRS — and the fairy ring is a key piece of that puzzle. Even at lower Construction levels, adding a fairy ring to your garden makes your house the center of your travel network.

7. Common Fairy Ring Mistakes

  1. Completing Fairy Tale Part II unnecessarily. The quest takes 1-2 hours and offers minimal rewards. Start it for the staff-free ring access and move on. You can always finish it later for the quest cape.
  2. Forgetting the Dramen Staff after dying. If you die and lose your Dramen Staff (above level 20 Wilderness), you lose fairy ring access until you get another. Keep a spare Dramen Branch in the bank — you can craft it into a staff in 2 seconds.
  3. Not knowing about the "last code" memory. After using a fairy ring once, you can right-click and select "Last Destination" to return to the most recently dialed code. This is extremely useful for repeated trips (e.g., birdhouse runs).
  4. Using fairy rings without a POH ring. Without a POH ring, you must walk to a fairy ring location to use one. The most convenient public fairy rings are: Edgeville (just east of the bank), Ardougne (north of the zoo), and the one inside Zanaris (using the Dramen Staff teleport). Plan your routes around these.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use fairy rings on F2P worlds?

A: No. Fairy rings are a members-only feature. Lost City, Fairy Tale quests, and the fairy ring network are all members content. However, once unlocked on a members account, fairy rings remain functional if you go F2P (you retain your membership benefits until your subscription expires). F2P-only accounts cannot access fairy rings at all.

Q: What happens if I lose my Dramen Staff?

A: If you've started Fairy Tale Part II, nothing — you don't need it. If you only have partial access (Lost City only), you must obtain another Dramen Branch. Return to the tree in Lumbridge Swamp, cut a new branch (the Tree Spirit does NOT respawn), and craft a new staff. Keep a spare Dramen Staff or Lunar Staff in your bank as backup.

Q: Is the Lunar Staff better than the Dramen Staff?

A: Functionally identical. The Lunar Staff (from Lunar Diplomacy quest) also works as a fairy ring access tool and can be equipped in the weapon slot. If you've completed Lunar Diplomacy, the Lunar Staff is slightly better because it has +3 Magic bonus compared to the Dramen Staff's zero bonuses. If you've started Fairy Tale Part II, neither staff is needed — you can use rings without any staff equipped.

🧚 Fairy Rings Are Your First Major Unlock

The 2-3 hours you spend unlocking full fairy ring access will pay off for the rest of your OSRS career. Free, instant teleportation to over 50 locations — including direct routes to three major bosses, multiple Slayer dungeons, and every corner of the map — is an account-changing upgrade.

Prioritize Lost City on day one of your account. Rush Fairy Tale Part I and start Part II as soon as you have the Farming and Herblore requirements. You'll wonder how you ever played without them. 🧚‍♂️
