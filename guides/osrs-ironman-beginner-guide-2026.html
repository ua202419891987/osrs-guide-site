OSRS Ironman Beginner Guide 2026 — Complete Starter Guide

Ironman mode is the ultimate way to experience Old School RuneScape — every item you use must be earned by your own hands. This guide takes you from Tutorial Island all the way to mid-game, with zero shortcuts and zero wasted effort.

📅 Created: June 2026 — Ironman Starter Guide · 18 min read

⚡ Quick Verdict: Is Ironman Mode Right for You?

Yes — if you want a fresh, meaningful challenge. Ironman mode strips away the Grand Exchange and trading, forcing you to gather, craft, and earn everything yourself. It transforms OSRS from a shopping simulator into a true adventure. Every level-up, every drop, every piece of gear feels earned.

Skip Ironman if: You hate grinding, want to play socially (group content is limited until late-game), or dislike managing multiple skills simultaneously. Ironman is slower by design — expect 3-5x longer to reach mid-game milestones compared to a normal account.

Our recommendation: Start a regular Ironman (not Hardcore) and give it 50 hours. If the mode clicks, you'll never go back. If it doesn't, you can always de-iron or start fresh.

1. What Is Ironman Mode? (Restrictions & Account Types)

Ironman mode is an official game mode where you cannot trade with other players, use the Grand Exchange, pick up items dropped by other players, or receive loot from monster kills that you didn't contribute to. Every arrow, potion, and piece of food must be earned by your own account.

① Ironman vs Hardcore vs Group Ironman — Which One?

Account TypeKey RestrictionDeath PenaltyBest For
Regular IronmanNo trading/GE, solo self-foundNormal (keep 3 items)First-time ironmen, learning the mode
Hardcore Ironman (HCIM)No trading/GE, solo self-foundOne life — die and you're demoted to regular IronmanExperienced players who enjoy risk
Ultimate Ironman (UIM)No trading/GE + no bankNormal (but no banking = huge inventory mgmt challenge)Masochists and ultra-experienced players
Group Ironman (GIM)Trading only within your group (2-5 players)NormalGroups of friends who want a shared challenge
💡 Which Mode Should You Pick?

Start with Regular Ironman. Hardcore Ironman sounds exciting, but one DC (disconnect) at Wintertodt can end months of progress. You can always "upgrade" to Hardcore later by creating a new account. Regular Ironman is the most fun per hour of effort.

② What Changes as an Ironman?

Every aspect of the game changes when you can't buy anything. Here are the most impactful differences:

  • No runes from shops? You must craft them, kill monsters for drops, or buy from NPC shops (limited stock).
  • No cheap food? You fish, cook, or farm everything yourself. Early-game food management is critical.
  • No dragon armor from GE? You must kill dragons for bones/hides, smith the bars, and craft the armor.
  • No skipping skills with GP? Every skill matters. You can't just buy 99 Construction on day one.
  • No loot share? You must solo every boss or get the drop yourself in group content (most drops are based on your own contribution).

2. Your First 10 Hours — Tutorial Island to 40 Combat

The first 10 hours of an Ironman account are critical. Do them right, and you set yourself up for a smooth early game. Do them wrong, and you'll waste dozens of hours backtracking.

③ Step 1: Tutorial Island — What to Do Differently

Tutorial Island is mostly the same for Ironmen, but there's one key difference: choose the Mining and Smithing route during the skills section, not Cooking. Early-game ironmen need bronze daggers and tools far more than extra cooking XP.

Tutorial Island Checklist

  • Pick Mining/Smithing path
  • Collect ALL items from the survival expert's shop (you can sell them later)
  • Complete all sections including the new Combat Academy for the Sword of Varrock
  • After the island, talk to the Mage of Zamorak south of Varrock to unlock the Rune Mysteries quest reward (free Air Talisman)

④ Step 2: Lumbridge & Draynor — Early Quests

⑤ Step 3: Early Combat Training — Chickens to Hill Giants

Now that you have some quest XP, it's time to train combat. Here's the zero-waste path:

Combat Progression for Ironmen

  1. Levels 1-10: Chickens in Lumbridge — collect feathers (huge for fishing later), bury bones for prayer
  2. Levels 10-20: Cows just north of Lumbridge — collect cowhides for crafting later (save them all)
  3. Levels 20-30: Goblins or Barbarian Village — strength training with the best weapon you can find
  4. Levels 30-40: Hill Giants in Edgeville Dungeon — they drop big bones (15 prayer XP), limpwurt roots, and giant keys for Obor
  5. Level 40+: Moss Giants or move to the Stronghold of Security for the Fighty Boots
💡 Pro Tip: Collect Everything

On an Ironman, everything is useful. Cowhides → leather armor. Bones → prayer XP. Feathers → fishing. Limpwurt roots → herblore. Even the cheap drops matter. Bank everything you can carry. You'll thank yourself later when you need 200 limpwurt roots for herblore.

3. Wintertodt Rush — The Ironman Starter Boss

Wintertodt is the single most important early-game activity for Ironmen. It provides massive rewards: crates full of resources, 99 Firemaking, and enough starting supplies to fuel your entire early game.

⑥ Why Wintertodt First?

Wintertodt scales damage based on your Hitpoints level. At 10 HP, the boss hits for 1-2 damage. At 40 HP, it hits for 6-8. The optimal Ironman strategy is to rush Wintertodt with 10-15 Hitpoints for nearly zero-cost Firemaking training.

⑦ Wintertodt Prep Guide

Before You Go

  • Complete Druidic Ritual (unlocks Herblore, needed for many quests)
  • Get 35 Woodcutting (cut oaks in Draynor — takes about 2 hours)
  • Get 50 Firemaking via normal logs — or just start Wintertodt at 50 FM for decent XP rates
  • Bring warm clothing — at least a clue hunter cloak or firemaking cape if you have one
  • Bring 4-6 food items (cakes from Lumbridge bakery work perfectly)

During Wintertodt

  • Stay above 30% health (the "brazier" health bar in the boss arena)
  • Focus on repairing braziers and chopping the roots that appear
  • Light the brazier when it goes out (use bruma herbs from the ground)
  • Open supply crates every 10-15 kills to manage inventory
  • Don't open all crates at once — store them in the bank and open in batches as you need supplies
💡 How Far Should You Go?

Option A (Recommended): Go to 99 Firemaking. Yes, it's 30-40 hours. But the crates will set your account up for weeks — seeds for farming, logs for fletching, gems for crafting, planks for construction. Most experienced Ironmen consider this the single best investment of early-game time.

Option B (Alternative): Stop at 90 Firemaking (~15 hours). You unlock the cape teleport and still get ~250 crates. Return to Wintertodt later when you need more supplies.

①② Herblore — The Ironman Wall

Herblore is the hardest skill to train on an Ironman because you can't buy herbs. Here's the early strategy:

Ironman Herblore Early Path

  1. Complete Druidic Ritual immediately — unlocks the skill
  2. Pick up every grimy herb you see from monster drops, Wintertodt crates, and farming
  3. Start Farming ASAP — Farming is how Ironmen get herbs consistently. Do herb runs every 80 minutes (guam, marrentill, tarromin, harralander)
  4. Clean all herbs — Clean herbs give herblore XP; use the cleanest for potions later
  5. Make Attack potions at level 3 — Use Guam + Eye of newt (easily bought from NPCs)
  6. Do quests for herb XP — Jungle Potion, Heroes Quest, and others give nice herblore rewards
💡 Farming Tip for Ironmen

Start farming at level 1 as soon as you have seeds. Plant guam seeds (from Wintertodt crates or monster drops) in the Falador and Lumbridge patches. Use compost from the compost bin near the Falador patch. Every herb run gives you 3-6 herbs that can be cleaned for herblore XP or used for potions. Farming is the engine that drives Ironman Herblore.

①③ Ranged & Magic — Ammo Strategy

Ranged and Magic are free-to-train on normal accounts, but Ironmen need to think about ammo:

  • Ranged: Fletch your own arrows (logs → arrow shafts → headless arrows → broad arrows at 55 slayer). Collect bronze/iron arrows from drops. Use a bone crossbow (from the Creature Creation minigame) for very cheap training ammo.
  • Magic: Curse/Weaken spells on monsters (they cost 1-3 runes per cast and give 29-58 XP). Use strike spells (Mind, Chaos, Death runes from drops/Wintertodt crates). For teleports: buy law runes from the Mage Guild (50 Magic required) or the Ali Morrisane shop.

6. Membership vs F2P — The Ironman Dilemma

Should you buy membership immediately, or grind F2P first? This is the most debated question in the Ironman community. Here's the honest answer:

①④ Argument for Buying Membership Immediately

Most experienced Ironmen agree: buy membership on day one if you can afford it. Here's why:

  • Wintertodt is 100x better with members (warm gloves from clue scrolls, better food, and the ability to teleport to the bank faster)
  • Quests — Members quests like Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, and The Grand Tree give massive combat XP that skip hours of F2P grinding
  • Agility — Graceful outfit from rooftop agility courses is the single best QoL upgrade in the game. F2P can't train agility at all.
  • Farming — Herb runs are members-only. Without them, herblore is nearly impossible.
  • Transportation — Fairy rings, gnome gliders, spirit trees — all members-only. These save dozens of hours.

①⑤ Argument for F2P First

If you genuinely cannot afford membership (no bond purchase), here's what you can do in F2P to prepare:

💡 Our Recommendation

If you can afford the $12/month, buy membership immediately. You'll progress 3-4x faster and have more fun. If you need to earn a bond with in-game GP, focus on getting 50+ all F2P stats first, then use the F2P money-making methods (killing Obor, crafting jewelry, or alching) to buy your first bond. 8. Ironman Beginner FAQs

Can I de-iron my account if I regret it?

Yes. You can visit Adam (the Ironman tutor) in Lumbridge to downgrade your account. Regular Ironmen can revert to a normal account. Hardcore Ironmen who die become regular Ironmen. You cannot re-enable Ironman once removed, so think carefully before de-ironing. Group Ironmen can leave their group but lose group storage access.

Should I start as Hardcore Ironman?

Only if you're an experienced player who accepts the risk. One disconnect at Wintertodt, one lag spike at a boss, and months of Hardcore status are gone. Most players recommend starting as a regular Ironman and creating a separate HCIM later if you want the challenge.

How long does it take to get 99 Firemaking at Wintertodt?

About 30-40 hours at 50+ FM, depending on your efficiency. Wintertodt gives 150K-200K XP/hour at 50+ FM. Higher HP levels mean more time eating food, which slightly reduces XP rates. At 10 HP, you can stay for 5+ kills without eating. At 40 HP, you need food every 2-3 kills.

What's the best way to get runes as an Ironman?

Several sources: (1) Buy from NPC shops — the Mage Guild (level 50 magic), Ali Morrisane's shop in Al Kharid, and the Runecrafting Guild. (2) Monster drops — chaos druids, hill giants, and later slayer monsters drop runes. (3) Guardians of the Rift minigame — best source of all runes, especially for mid-game. (4) Wintertodt crates — common source of basic runes. Rune packs (from shops) are your best early-game option for laws, natures, and cosmics.

Is farming really that important for Ironmen?

Yes. Farming is the primary source of herbs for Herblore on an Ironman. Without consistent herb runs, your Herblore will stall around level 40-50. Start farming as soon as you have seeds. Do herb runs every 80 minutes (guam, marrentill, tarromin, harralander at low levels). Each run takes 5 minutes and gives 3-6 herbs per patch.

What's the Ironman money-making meta in early game?

Ironmen can't use the Grand Exchange, but GP is still needed for shop items, runes, and construction. Best early methods: (1) Alching items from Wintertodt crates (yew/magic longbows fletched from logs). (2) Selling items to specialty stores (Rune Scimitars to the Warriors Guild, jewelry to the Rogue's Den). (3) Agility pyramid (50 Agility, 10K GP per lap). (4) H.A.M. members (pickpocketing for jewelry to sell). (5) Thieving in Ardougne (cakes to sell,珠宝 to alch).

Should I rush the Quest Cape as an Ironman?

It's a great long-term goal, but don't rush it early. Many quests require high skill levels, gear upgrades, and boss kills that are harder as an Ironman. Aim for 150-200 QP (for Recipe for Disaster gloves) before worrying about the cape. The quest cape is a solid mid-game target (600+ hours into an Ironman).

What bosses can I kill early as an Ironman?

Early Ironman bossing options: (1) Obor (Hill Giant boss) — requires giant keys from hill giants, level 40+ combat. Drops rune items and giant key pieces. (2) Bryophyta (Moss Giant boss) — same concept, drops nature runes and rune items. (3) Wintertodt (already covered). (4) Tempoross (Fishing boss) — good food and supply drops, requires 35 Fishing. (5) Scurrius (Rats boss in Varrock Sewers) — great for early combat XP and supplies.

How do I get prayer levels as an Ironman?

Prayer is harder for Ironmen since you can't buy bones. Early methods: (1) Bury bones from combat training. (2) Kill Hill Giants for big bones (15 XP each) in Edgeville Dungeon. (3) Complete the Rag and Bone Man quest for a bonus. (4) Use the Ectofuntus in Port Phasmatys (members, 200% XP per bone — 30 XP for big bones). (5) The Chaos Altar in Wilderness (members, 350% XP but PvP risk). (6) Passive XP from ensouled heads (Slayer). Aim for 43 Prayer (Protect from Melee) as your first major milestone.
