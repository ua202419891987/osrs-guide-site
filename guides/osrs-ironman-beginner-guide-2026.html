🛡️ OSRS Ironman Mode Beginner Guide 2026

Ironman Mode is a special game mode where you cannot trade with other players, use the Grand Exchange, or receive help from non-Ironman players. You must obtain EVERY item yourself. This makes the game much harder but also much more rewarding. This guide covers everything: how to start, Ironman restrictions, which type to choose (Standard/Hardcore/Ultimate), and a step-by-step progression roadmap.

🛡️ 1. What Is Ironman Mode? (Read This First)

Ironman Mode is a self-sufficient game mode. The core rule: You must obtain all items yourself. No trading, no GE, no help from main accounts.

This changes the entire game:

  • Want a Dragon scimitar? You must kill 100+ Cave Crawlers (Dragon slayer quest) and then kill a Dragon to get the drop (1/5,000 drop rate).
  • Want GP for membership? You must earn it yourself (no begging, no buying from GE).
  • Want food for combat? You must fish/cook it yourself (no buying lobsters from GE).

🎯 Is Ironman Mode for You?

Yes, if: You enjoy a challenge, you like the feeling of earning everything yourself, you want to experience ALL content (not just rush to end-game), you don't mind slow progress.

No, if: You want to rush to 99 in everything, you want to make GP quickly, you get frustrated by slow progress, you like trading/merchanting.

Ironman Mode is extremely popular — about 30% of active OSRS players are Ironmen. It's considered the "pure" way to play OSRS.

💡 Ironman = Play the Way OSRS Was Originally Designed

Before the Grand Exchange (2007), all OSRS players were effectively Ironmen — you had to earn/find everything yourself. Ironman Mode brings back that old-school feeling. If you want the "real" OSRS experience, play Ironman.

⚔️ 2. Ironman Types — Which One to Choose?

There are 3 types of Ironman Mode:

① Standard Ironman (Recommended for First Ironman)

FeatureDetails
Death penaltyNothing (you keep playing as Ironman)
DifficultyHard (but not extreme)
Best forFirst-time Ironman players
PopularityMost popular (90% of Ironmen)

Recommended: If this is your first Ironman, choose Standard. It's challenging but not punishing. You can die 100 times and still keep playing.

② Hardcore Ironman (High Risk, High Reward)

FeatureDetails
Death penaltyYOU DIE = IRONMAN STATUS REMOVED (become normal account)
DifficultyVery Hard (one mistake = lose everything)
Best forExperienced players who want extreme challenge
PopularityLow (only 5% of Ironmen)

Not recommended for beginners. One mistake (disconnect, lag, PKer) and your Hardcore status is gone. Only try this after you've completed a Standard Ironman.

③ Ultimate Ironman (Extreme Challenge)

FeatureDetails
Death penaltyAccount deleted permanently
Extra restrictionNo bank (only inventory + equipment)
DifficultyExtreme (only for pros)
Best forVeteran players who want the ultimate challenge
PopularityVery low (less than 1% of Ironmen)

DO NOT choose Ultimate for your first Ironman. It's extremely difficult (no bank = you can only carry 28 items at a time). Only try this if you're a OSRS veteran.

🎯 Recommendation: Start with Standard Ironman

Standard Ironman gives you the full Ironman experience without the extreme pressure of Hardcore/Ultimate. You can always make a Hardcore Ironman later after you learn the ropes. 99% of Ironman guides assume you're playing Standard Ironman.

🚫 3. Ironman Restrictions (What You CAN'T Do)

As an Ironman, these actions are blocked:

① No Grand Exchange (GE)

You cannot use the GE to buy/sell items. The GE clerk will say "I'm sorry, Ironmen aren't allowed to use the Grand Exchange." All items must be obtained via drops, skilling, or quest rewards.

② No Trading with Other Players

You cannot trade items with other players (right-click → "Trade with" is disabled). This includes both buying and selling. You also cannot receive items from other players (even as gifts).

③ No Receiving Help from Non-Ironmen

If a non-Ironman player tries to give you items (dropping them on the ground for you), you cannot pick them up. Only other Ironmen can drop items for you (and only if you're in a Ironman-only trading clan).

④ No Using "POH" Construction Services

You cannot hire NPCs to build things in your Player-Owned House (POH). You must earn the materials and build everything yourself. This makes Construction very slow for Ironmen.

⑤ No "Note" Unlocking via Other Players

Some items (like notes from the "Allotment" farming patch) can only be unnoted by other players. As an Ironman, you need to find alternative methods (use the "Bank note unpacking" service in certain locations).

💡 What You CAN Still Do as an Ironman

Despite the restrictions, Ironman is still very playable. You CAN: Use the Grand Exchange as a price checker (just can't buy/sell), trade with other Ironmen (in Ironman Clans), receive quest rewards (quests still give items), pick up items from monsters you kill (drops are still yours).

🎁 4. Why Play Ironman? (Benefits + Challenges)

Ironman Mode is challenging, but it has unique benefits that many players prefer over normal mode:

① Benefits of Ironman Mode

BenefitExplanation
Sense of accomplishmentEvery item you get, you earned it yourself. No buying max gear from GE.
No rushingYou can't just buy best gear, so you experience ALL content at the intended pace.
self-sufficiencyYou learn every skill and mechanic (because you have to).
Unique communityIronman Clans are very helpful and friendly (everyone is in the same boat).
PrestigeHaving a high-level Ironman is impressive (shows you're skilled).

② Challenges of Ironman Mode

ChallengeImpact
Slow progressionGetting to 99 in any skill takes 2–5x longer than normal mode.
GP scarcityEarning GP is hard (no GE flipping, no selling loot). You'll be poor for a long time.
GrindingSome items have very low drop rates (you might kill 1,000 monsters for one drop).
LonelinessYou can't trade or do cooperative content with non-Ironmen (need Ironman clan).

🎯 The #1 Ironman Benefit: You Can't Quit Because You're Bored of Grinding

In normal mode, if you get bored of grinding a skill, you can just buy the required items from GE and skip it. In Ironman, you have to grind it yourself. This forces you to engage with ALL content, which actually makes the game more fun and varied. You'll do quests you never would have done in normal mode, train skills you never would have trained, and see content you never would have seen.

🆕 5. How to Start an Ironman Account (Step by Step)

Creating an Ironman account is easy:

Step 1: Create a New Jagex Account

Go to account.jagex.com and create a new account. Don't use your main account — Ironman should be a separate account.

Step 2: Select "Ironman Mode" During Character Creation

After creating the account, launch the game. During character creation (choosing your character's appearance), there's an option at the bottom: "Play as Ironman?" Select "Yes, I want to play Ironman Mode."

Step 3: Choose Your Ironman Type

The game asks: "Which type of Ironman do you want to be?" Select "Standard Ironman" (recommended for first-timers).

Step 4: Start Playing!

You spawn in Tutorial Island (same as normal mode). Complete the tutorial, then you're in Lumbridge. Welcome to Ironman Mode!

💡 Join an Ironman Clan Immediately

Ironman is much easier (and more fun) with help from other Ironmen. Join an Ironman Clan (use the in-game Clan system — search for "Ironman" or "IM" clans). Clanmates can give you advice, donate low-value items, and help with tough content. Many Ironman Clans have Discord servers with lots of guides and helpful players.

🌱 6. Ironman Early Game (0–500 Total Level)

The first 50 hours of Ironman are the hardest. Here's your roadmap:

③ Phase 1: Tutorial Island + Lumbridge (0–20 Combat)

① Complete Tutorial Island

Do the full tutorial (30 minutes). It teaches you the basics. Pick up EVERY item (you'll need them later).

② Do "X Marks the Spot" Quest (First Quest)

Talk to Veos in Lumbridge (near the general store). This quest gives you 4,500 XP in any skill (use it on Prayer or Attack). This is the best starter quest in OSRS.

③ Train Combat on Cows/Chickens (to ~20 Combat)

Kill Cows (Lumbridge farm) or Chickens (near Lumbridge castle). Collect bones (bury them for Prayer XP) and cowhides (save them — you'll need them for Crafting later).

④ Phase 2: Questing + Skill Training (20–40 Combat)

🗺️ Early Game Priority List

  • Quests: Complete all F2P quests (about 15 quests, 30 minutes each). Quest rewards give XP and unlock content.
  • Combat: Train to 40 Attack/Strength/Defence (use cows, chickens, goblins).
  • Prayer: Bury bones to 13 Prayer (unlocks "Thick Skin" — +5% Defence).
  • GP: Sell cowhides to the Lumbridge General Store (not GE — you can't use GE). Each cowhide = ~100 GP. Collect 100+ cowhides for ~10K GP.

⑤ Phase 3: First Big Upgrade — Dragon Scimitar (40 Combat+)

The biggest early-game upgrade is the Dragon scimitar (60 Attack required, but you can get it earlier via "Monkey Madness I" quest). This weapon doubles your DPS and makes all combat content much easier.

How to get it as Ironman: Complete "Monkey Madness I" quest (requires 43 Prayer, 60 Agility — use boosts). This unlocks the ability to wield Dragon scimitar. Then kill Dragon monsters (e.g., Green dragons in the Wilderness — be careful!) until you get a Dragon scimitar drop (1/5,000 drop rate). This might take 10–20 hours of grinding.

⚔️ 7. Ironman Mid Game (500–1500 Total Level)

Once you have 43 Prayer and 70 Combat, the game opens up significantly:

⑥ Key Mid-Game Goals

① Get 70 Combat Stats (Attack/Strength/Defence/Ranged/Magic)

At 70 stats, you can use Abyssal whip (best melee weapon pre-75 Attack) and Rune crossbow (best ranged weapon pre-75 Ranged). These make Slayer much faster.

② Complete All "Diary" Easy + Medium Tasks

Achievement Diary rewards are even more important for Ironmen (because you can't buy the benefits from GE). Priority: Lumbridge & Draynor (Explorer's Ring), Varrock (Varrock Armor), Falador (Falador Shield).

③ Start Slayer (Vannaka, 40 Combat)

Slayer is the best way to train Combat and get loot simultaneously. As an Ironman, Slayer drops are your primary source of gear upgrades. Do Slayer tasks exclusively — don't grind Cannonballs or other boring methods.

④ Farming Herb Runs (Passive Income)

Farming is the best passive income for Ironmen (you can't use GE to buy herbs). Do herb runs daily — the herbs you grow are used for Prayer potions, Super restores, and other useful potions. Without Farming, you'll run out of potions constantly.

🎯 Mid-Game Ironman Milestone: Your First Bond (Member Length)

Most Ironmen earn their first Bond (14 days membership) by selling Cowhides, Leather, and other drops to NPCs. It takes about 20–30 hours of gameplay to earn enough GP for a Bond. After that, you can maintain membership indefinitely via in-game GP (if you play efficiently).

❓ 8. FAQ

Can I switch from Ironman back to normal mode?

Yes, but it's permanent. You can "de-iron" once (talk to the Ironman tutor in Lumbridge). After that, you can never go back to Ironman. Only de-iron if you're absolutely sure — most players regret it.

How long does it take to max an Ironman account?

About 1,500–2,500 hours (6–12 months of full-time playing). This is 2–3x longer than a normal account. But the journey is much more fun and varied.

Can Ironmen do Group Content (Raids, CoX, ToB)?

Yes! Ironmen can do group content with other Ironmen. Many Ironman Clans organize Raid groups. You can't do group content with non-Ironmen (because they could give you items), but Ironman-only groups are allowed.

Is Ironman Mode profitable (can I make GP)?

Yes, but slower than normal mode. You can't use GE to flip items, but you can still make GP from Slayer tasks, bossing, farming herb runs, and selling drops to NPCs. Most mid-game Ironmen make ~200K–500K GP per hour.

Should I start an Ironman as my first OSRS account?

No. Play normal mode first to learn the game. Ironman is much harder — if you struggle with normal mode, you'll struggle even more with Ironman. Recommended: Play normal mode to ~70 Combat, then start an Ironman as a secondary account.
