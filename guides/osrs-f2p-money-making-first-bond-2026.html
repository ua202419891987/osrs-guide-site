1. 💰 Why You Need 10M GP for Your First Bond

Before you start grinding, you need to understand the target. In 2026, a single OSRS bond costs ~10M GP (sometimes fluctuating between 9.5M and 10.5M depending on the market). That's a lot of GP for a F2P player starting from zero.

1.1 Bond Price History (2026 Edition)

OSRS bond prices have been on a steady upward trend since bonds were introduced in 2014. Here's the trajectory that matters for F2P players:

YearBond Price (GP)USD EquivalentHours to Farm (Best F2P Method)
2020~3.2M GP$6.99~6 hours (cowhides)
2022~4.5M GP$6.99~9 hours
2024~7.8M GP$6.99~16 hours
2026~10M GP$9.99~20-25 hours

The jump from 2024 to 2026 is the steepest in OSRS history. The March 2026 membership price hike (from $6.99 to $9.99 per bond) pushed in-game GP prices to a new all-time high. For a F2P player, this means you now need to grind 40-60% longer than you would have in 2024 to afford the same membership time.

📊 The Math: Why 10M GP Is the Magic Number

In July 2026, the bond price on the Grand Exchange typically ranges between 9,800,000 and 10,400,000 GP. To be safe, aim for 10.5M GP before you try to buy — this gives you a buffer against price spikes and ensures you can relist if your first offer doesn't fill immediately.

Translation: At 1.8M GP/hr (the best realistic F2P rate), you need 21 hours of focused grinding to hit 10.5M GP. That's roughly 3 weeks at 1 hour/day, or 1 week at 3 hours/day.

1.2 GP/USD Ratio Trend — Why Bonds Keep Getting More Expensive

The GP/USD ratio is the number of GP you need to buy $1 worth of bonds. In 2020, it was ~457,000 GP/USD. In 2026, it's ~1,010,000 GP/USD — more than doubled in 6 years.

Two main drivers:

  • USD price hikes: Jagex has raised bond prices from $6.99 to $9.99 (March 2026), and the GP price follows.
  • GP inflation: More GP enters the game than leaves it. Bosses, raids, and money making methods continuously inject GP. The GE tax (0.5%) helps but doesn't fully offset it.

The bottom line: if you're planning to bond-farm your way to membership, start now. The target will only get higher in 2027.

⚠️ Important

Bond prices fluctuate daily. Always check the live OSRS bond price tracker before you start grinding so you know exactly how much GP you need. Don't rely on outdated numbers from YouTube videos!

2. 🥇 Best F2P Method: Cowhides (1.8M-1.8M GP/hr)

If you search any OSRS forum for "best F2P money maker," cowhides will be the #1 answer. And for good reason — it's consistent, requires almost no stats, and the profit per hour is still the highest of any F2P method in 2026.

Verdict: This is the method you should start with. It's boring, but it works.

2.1 Location: Lumbridge East Cow Field

The optimal spot is the cow field east of Lumbridge, just across the bridge from the castle. Here's why:

  • Shortest banking route: The Lumbridge Castle bank is a ~30-second run from the cow field.
  • Low competition: Most new players go to the North Lumbridge cows (closer to the GE), but the East field is less crowded.
  • Respawn rate: Cows respawn fast enough that you rarely have to wait, even in a populated world.

Recommended world: Use a F2P world (not a members world where F2P cows are empty). Worlds 301-310 are typically good for F2P farming.

2.3 Banking Route & Efficiency Tips

The difference between 1.8M GP/hr and 1.8M GP/hr is almost entirely efficiency. Here's how to maximize your rate:

Step 1: Kill cows and loot hides only

Ignore beef, bones, and cowbells. Only pick up cowhides. One cowhide = ~140-150 GP (GE mid-price). Beef sells for 1 GP each — not worth your inventory slots.

Step 2: Bank when your inventory is full (28 hides)

Run north to Lumbridge Castle. Use the stairs to the 2nd floor (there's a bank up there). Deposit all hides. Repeat.

Step 3: Use the GE or sell to players

Once you have ~500+ hides, sell them on the Grand Exchange. Cowhides are always in demand (used for crafting training), so they usually sell within minutes.

💡 Pro Tip: Use Range Instead of Melee

If you have 30+ Ranged, using a maple shortbow or even oak shortbow with iron arrows is actually faster than melee. Cows have very low defense, and you can safespot them by standing behind the fence. This saves food and AFK time.

3. 🪨 Second Best: Essence Mining (1.8M-1.8M GP/hr)

If you can't stand killing cows (fair), essence mining is the next best F2P option. It's slower than cowhides, but it's AFK-friendly and requires zero combat stats.

Best for: New accounts with 1 Attack/Strength, or players who want to multitask while watching Netflix.

3.1 Varrock East Mine vs Draynor Village

There are two main F2P essence mining spots. Here's the comparison:

📍 Varrock East Mine (Recommended for F2P)

  • Requirements: 1 Mining (iron/essence = no level req)
  • Banking: Varrock East Bank is ~15 seconds away — very fast.
  • Competition: Moderate. Usually 2-5 people mining here.
  • GP/hr: ~1.8M-1.8M (depending on world population)

📍 Draynor Village (Members only — skip for F2P)

  • Note: Draynor is in a members area. F2P players cannot access it. Don't waste time running there.

Real F2P option: South Varrock mine (just south of the city). It has essence rocks and is even closer to the South Varrock bank. This is actually the best F2P essence spot in 2026.

3.2 Runecrafting for Extra Profit (If You Have 1+ RC)

Here's where essence mining gets interesting. Instead of selling raw essence on the GE, you can:

  1. Mine essence (or buy it on GE for ~5 GP each)
  2. Runecraft it into Air runes (1 RC required, F2P accessible at the Air Altar)
  3. Sell the Air runes for ~6-7 GP each

The math: 28 essence × ~6 GP = ~168 GP per inventory vs. selling essence directly at ~5 GP = ~140 GP. It's a small margin, but it adds up over time. The real money is in Nature runes (requires 44 RC), but that's members-only.

⚠️ Warning: essence Prices Fluctuate

Essence is used for Runecrafting training. When a new RC training method is released (or when bots flood the market), essence prices can crash. Always check the GE price before committing to a long essence mining session.

Essence mining is great for your first 1-2M GP (to buy basic gear for cowhide farming). Once you have ~1M GP, switch to cowhides for faster progress. Don't waste time essence-mining your way to 10M GP — it's too slow for the full grind.

4. 🐟 Fishing & Cooking Combo (1.8M-1.5M GP/hr)

Fishing is the classic "relaxed" money maker. In F2P, your options are limited compared to members, but there's still solid profit if you know where to fish.

Important note: True F2P lobster fishing is not accessible — Catherby is members-only. The best F2P fishing money maker is actually trout/salmon at Barbarian Village or swordfish at Musa Point (if you have 45+ Cooking and can cook them for higher value).

4.1 Best F2P Fishing Spots (2026)

🐟 Trout & Salmon — Barbarian Village (River Lum)

  • Requirements: 20 Fishing (trout), 30 Fishing (salmon)
  • Location: River Lum, just north of Barbarian Village (F2P accessible)
  • Banking: Edgeville Bank is a ~45 second run (not ideal, but manageable)
  • Profit: ~1.8M-1.8M GP/hr (raw fish sell slowly on GE)

🔥 Cooked Food — The Real Money (Musa Point)

  • Requirements: 40 Cooking (lobsters), 45 Cooking (swordfish)
  • Method: Fish raw swordfish/lobsters at Musa Point (Karamja), cook them on the nearby range, then sell cooked fish on GE.
  • Profit: Cooked lobsters (~150 GP each) and swordfish (~400 GP each) sell much faster than raw.
  • GP/hr: ~1.8M-1.5M (if you have the stats and banking setup)

Catch: Musa Point is on Karamja Island. To bank efficiently, you need to use the Musa Point Charter Ship (40 GP per trip to Port Sarim) or walk to the Draynor bank (very long). This method is only worth it if you have ~50 GP to spend on charter ships per inventory.

4.2 Food Value for Members Later

Even if the GP/hr isn't the absolute best, fishing/cooking has a dual benefit: the food you cook will be useful once you finally get membership. Lobsters and swordfish are staple foods forSlayer and bossing. By the time you afford your first bond, you'll already have a stack of food ready for members content.

This makes fishing/cooking the best "long game" F2P method — slower GP/hr, but you're training two skills (Fishing and Cooking) that will save you millions later.

5. 🗡️ Hill Giants Loot Run (1.5M-2M GP/hr)

If you have 30+ Combat stats (Attack, Strength, Defense), Hill Giants in the Edgeville Dungeon are the highest-GP/hr F2P method in the game. The loot per hour can exceed 2M GP if you get lucky with big-ticket drops.

Warning: This method requires fighting other players in Wilderness level 1-2 (Edgeville Dungeon is partially in the Wildy). If you die, you lose everything. Only do this if you're comfortable with PvP risk.

5.1 Edgeville Dungeon Route

The Edgeville Dungeon Hill Giants are the most accessible F2P giants. Here's the route:

  1. Enter Edgeville Dungeon (hole south of Edgeville, requires a brass key or 10 GP entrance fee).
  2. Run past the skeletons and zombies. The Hill Giants are in the northern section of the dungeon.
  3. Banking: Edgeville Bank is right outside the dungeon entrance — the fastest banking of any F2P combat method.

⚠️ Wilderness Risk — Read This

Edgeville Dungeon Hill Giants are in Wilderness level 1-2. A PKer (player killer) can attack you if they have a higher Combat level than you. If you die, you lose all untradable items and whatever you're carrying.

How to stay safe:

  • Only bring gear you can afford to lose.
  • Bank frequently (every 10-15 kills).
  • Watch the Wilderness level indicator — if it says "Level 1" you're relatively safe; "Level 2-3" is riskier.
  • If a PKer appears, run to the dungeon exit immediately. Don't try to fight them.

5.3 Time to 10M GP — Hill Giants Route

If you can sustain ~1.5M GP/hr at Hill Giants:

  • 10M GP target ÷ 1.5M GP/hr = ~17 hours
  • At 1 hour/day: 17 days
  • At 3 hours/day: 6 days

This is the fastest realistic route to your first bond in F2P. The risk is the only downside.

7. 💡 Pro Tips to Speed Up Bond Farming

You've got the methods. Now here's how to squeeze every last GP out of your F2P grind.

7.1 Use Yak Track Rewards (Free GP)

Jagex runs a seasonal event called Yak Track (similar to a battle pass). Even F2P players can complete the free track, which often includes:

  • GP rewards (50K-1.8M GP per track)
  • Free bonds (rare, but possible in premium track — F2P can't access premium without membership, but the free track sometimes gives bond pieces)
  • GP booster tokens (temporarily increase GP from skilling)

Action: Check the in-game Yak Track (button near the mini-map) every time you log in. Even 50K GP here and there adds up.

7.2 Daily Challenges for Extra XP (and GP)

F2P players have access to Daily Challenges (the "Challenges" tab in the game interface). Completing them gives:

  • Bonus XP (which saves you time training skills later)
  • Sometimes GP rewards directly
  • Progress toward Reward Books (which contain GP)

The key insight: Daily Challenges rotate through skills you're already training for money. If today's challenge is "Mine 50 essence," that's free XP on top of your essence mining grind. Double-dip whenever possible.

7.3 Don't Waste GP on "Upgrade" Gear

A common mistake: new F2P players spend their hard-earned GP on slightly better gear (e.g., upgrading from iron to steel scimitar). Don't do this.

The GP/hr difference between iron and steel gear is negligible (~5-10% faster killing). The steel scimitar costs ~5,000 GP — that's 1/2,000th of your 10M GP bond target. Save every GP for the bond. Only buy gear if it unlocks a new method (e.g., buying a maple shortbow to unlock ranged cowhiding).

7.4 Use the Grand Exchange Smartly

When you're ready to sell your loot, use the Grand Exchange correctly:

  • Check the instant-sell price: Right-click the item on GE and select "Guide Price" to see the current market value.
  • Under-cut by 1 GP: If the guide price is 150 GP, list yours at 149 GP. It will sell instantly while still maximizing profit.
  • Sell in bulk: Don't do 28-item trips to the GE. Bank your loot until you have 1,000+ cowhides, then sell them all at once. Less running, more profit.

🎯 The Ultimate F2P Bond Strategy (2026 Meta)

If you want the absolute fastest route to your first bond, here's the meta strategy that top F2P grinders use:

  1. Spend your first 2 hours on essence mining to get ~1M GP for basic gear.
  2. Grind cowhides until you have ~3M GP.
  3. Use that 3M GP to buy range gear (maple shortbow + iron arrows or better).
  4. Grind cowhides with range — it's ~30% faster than melee.
  5. Once you hit ~8M GP, switch to Hill Giants to finish the last 2M GP quickly.
  6. Buy your bond. Redeem it. Welcome to members.

Total time: ~18-22 hours. Total real-world time: 2-3 weeks for most players.

8. ❓ FAQs — F2P Money Making for Your First Bond

Q: Can I really afford a bond as a pure F2P player in 2026?

A: Yes, absolutely. It's harder than in 2020-2022 due to bond price inflation, but it's still possible. The key is consistency — 1 hour a day for 3 weeks will get you there. Thousands of players bond-farm their first membership every month. You can too.

Q: What's the absolute fastest F2P method if I have 60+ Combat stats?

A: Hill Giants in Edgeville Dungeon (Wilderness level 1-2). With 60+ Combat and decent gear, you can hit 700K-2M GP/hr. The risk is PKers, but if you're careful, this is the fastest route. If you want zero risk, cowhides with a maple shortbow is the safest high-GP/hr method (~1.5M-750K GP/hr).

Q: Should I buy a bond with real money instead of grinding for it?

A: That's a personal choice. A bond costs $9.99 USD in 2026. If you value your time at more than ~$0.50/hour (the effective "wage" of F2P bond farming), buying the bond directly is more economical. But many players enjoy the grind as part of the OSRS experience — the feeling of earning your first membership through pure gameplay is genuinely rewarding.

Q: Can I use the same strategy to afford a second bond (renewal)?

A: Yes, but once you have membership, you have much better money making methods available. Members money making methods like Flax picking (~350K GP/hr), Yew trees (~1.8M GP/hr), and Slayer (1M+ GP/hr at higher levels) make bond-farming trivial. Your first bond is the hardest — after that, membership pays for itself.

Q: I keep getting killed at Hill Giants. What should I do?

A: Stop doing Hill Giants and switch to cowhides. Cowhides are 100% safe — there's no PvP risk. Yes, the GP/hr is lower (~1.8M-700K vs ~700K-2M), but you won't lose your gear to a PKer. For many players, the consistent income of cowhides is better than the high-risk/high-reward of Hill Giants. There's no shame in playing it safe.

Q: Are there any F2P money making methods not covered in this guide?

A: A few minor ones: Green dragons (requires 60+ Combat and the F2P area near the Wilderness, very high risk), smithing iron bars (very slow GP/hr, ~100K/hr), and flipping items on the GE (requires capital and knowledge). None of these beat cowhides or Hill Giants for pure GP/hr. This guide covers the methods that actually work for 95% of F2P players.
