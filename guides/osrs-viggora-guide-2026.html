① 📖 Who Is Viggora? (Complete Lore Breakdown)

Viggora's story is deeply tied to the history of Gielinor. Understanding his lore helps you appreciate why he appears in multiple locations and why he's so hostile to players.

1.1 🏛️ The Second Age — Viggora as Zaros General

During the Second Age, Viggora was one of the most powerful human generals serving Zaros (the Empty Lord). Zaros had conquered much of Gielinor, and Viggora was instrumental in expanding his empire. He was known for his skill with a chainmace — a weapon type that combines the reach of a whip with the crushing force of a mace. After betraying Zaros to join Zamorak's rebellion, Viggora was cursed to become an invisible ghost, wandering Gielinor for eternity. The chainmace that bears his name is said to be a replica of his signature weapon, imbued with a fragment of his restless spirit.

1.2 ⚡ The Betrayal — Why Viggora Switched Sides

The turning point came when Viggora, along with other key figures (including Zamorak, then a mere general), plotted against Zaros. The reasons are debated among scholars:

  • Power hunger: Viggora wanted more authority than Zaros was willing to give
  • Fear: He sensed Zaros's growing instability and wanted to survive the coming fall
  • Manipulation: Some believe Zamorak manipulated Viggora into betraying Zaros

Whatever the reason, Viggora fought alongside Zamorak's forces during the Sentinel Rebellion. Together, they managed to weaken Zaros enough that he was eventually banished (though not killed — Zaros's fate remains one of OSRS's biggest mysteries).

1.3 👻 The Curse — Viggora's Spectral Existence

After Zaros's banishment, something went wrong for Viggora. Despite fighting on the winning side (Zamorak's), Viggora was cursed. The exact nature of the curse varies depending on which in-game source you read, but the result is consistent:

  • Viggora became a spectral entity (a ghostly, invisible figure)
  • He was bound to wander specific locations tied to his past
  • He cannot rest or move on until certain conditions are met (the core of the "Curse of the Empty Lord" quest)
  • He appears as a hostile NPC in multiple locations, attacking players who disturb his wandering
💡 Lore Tip: Why "Empty Lord"?

The "Empty Lord" is Zaros — after his banishment, he was erased from history, and most Gielinor inhabitants forgot he ever existed. Viggora's curse is poetic: he served the Empty Lord in life, and in death he's bound to an empty, wandering existence. The quest "Curse of the Empty Lord" ties these themes together beautifully. You can learn more in our Curse of the Empty Lord quest guide.

1.4 📜 Post-Rebellion — Viggora's Role in the Third Age

After the banishment of Zaros, Viggora's exact fate is... complicated. Some lore sources suggest he was further cursed by Zamorak (who feared Viggora's growing power even within the new regime). Others suggest Viggora cursed himself as penance for his betrayal. The truth is left deliberately ambiguous by Jagex, but the in-game evidence (Viggora's dialogue, item examine texts, and quest lore) points to:

  • Viggora was cursed to become invisible (a ghost that can only be seen under certain conditions)
  • He was bound to three specific locations tied to his life: Edgeville Dungeon (where he fell in battle), Slayer Tower (where he trained), and Rogues' Castle (where he died)
  • He can only be permanently freed if a player completes the "Curse of the Empty Lord" mini-quest and lifts the curse

2.2 🗺️ Step-by-Step Walkthrough

📋 Curse of the Empty Lord — Quick Steps

  1. Find Viggora in Edgeville Dungeon — Enter the dungeon, go to the swamp area (south-west part). Look for a translucent, shimmering NPC. Note: If you can't see him, make sure your brightness is turned up (in-game settings → brightness: HIGH).
  2. Speak to him — He'll talk about his curse and ask for help lifting it. He'll mention three Cursed Artefacts scattered across Gielinor.
  3. Collect 3 Cursed Artefacts — These are scattered across Gielinor:
    • Artefact 1: In the Edgeville Dungeon swamp (near Viggora's starting location) — search the swampy rocks
    • Artefact 2: In the Slayer Tower (top floor, near the spectral figures area) — search the bookshelf
    • Artefact 3: In Rogues' Castle (Wilderness, level 50+ woods) — search the castle's basement ⚠️ WILDERNESS RISK
  4. Return to Viggora — Bring all 3 artefacts back to him in Edgeville Dungeon.
  5. Defeat Viggora (Boss Fight) — He'll attack you! This is a mini-boss fight with unique mechanics:
    • He uses Magic attacks (Protect from Magic recommended)
    • He can turn invisible (use the "Echo" enchantment on boots or well-lit areas to track him)
    • He summons spectral minions at 50% HP (kill them first — they heal Viggora if left alive!)
    • He has ~80 HP (not a long fight, but can surprise you if undergeared)
  6. Complete the quest — After defeating him, he'll be freed from the curse. Reward: 2,500 Prayer XP + access to Viggora's chainmace (drops now available as a repeatable boss).

③ 🗺️ Where to Find Viggora (All 3 Locations)

Viggora appears as a roaming NPC in 3 main locations. You can find him in any of these spots, depending on where he's currently wandering. He respawns every ~2 hours at a random one of the three locations.

3.1 🏚️ Location 1: Edgeville Dungeon (Swamp Area) — START

This is where the "Curse of the Empty Lord" quest starts. Enter Edgeville Dungeon (the trapdoor in Edgeville), go south-west to the swampy area. Look for a translucent, shimmering figure.

  • Teleport: Use Edgeville teleport (Amulet of Glory or Edgeville home teleport)
  • Safe for F2P? Yes, Edgeville Dungeon is F2P-accessible (though Viggora himself is members-only content)
  • Visibility: Turn up your in-game brightness! Viggora is translucent and hard to see at default brightness.

3.2 💀 Location 2: Slayer Tower (Top Floor)

Viggora also wanders the Slayer Tower (top floor, near the spectral figures area). This is a members-only area and requires some Slayer progress to access efficiently.

  • Teleport: Slayer Ring (unlocked after 75 Slayer points) or Canifis teleport
  • Combat Advisable: 60+ combat recommended (surrounding monsters are aggressive)
  • Safe? Yes, no Wilderness risk here. But the Slayer Tower has aggressive monsters (Banshees, Infernal Mages).

3.3 🏰 Location 3: Rogues' Castle (Wilderness) — DANGEROUS

The most dangerous location — Rogues' Castle in the Wilderness (level 50+). Viggora can appear here as a wandering NPC. This is also where you'll find Artefact 3 during the quest.

  • WARNING: Wilderness PK risk is high here. Bring minimal gear. Consider bringing a Wilderness sword (1) for the teleport option.
  • Teleport: Wilderness Obelisks or Burning Amulet of Glory (dangerous!)
  • Safe Strategy: Go during off-peak hours (2AM-6AM EST). Fewer PKers are active.
💡 Pro Tip: Tracking Viggora's Location

Viggora roams between all 3 locations. There's no fixed schedule. Some players use the in-game "NPC Follower" plugin (RuneLite) to track his last known location. Others just check all 3 locations in this order: Edgeville → Slayer Tower → Rogues' Castle. If he's not at any of the three, he's either despawning or you missed him (check your chat log for "You find nothing").

④ ⚔️ Viggora's Chainmace — Complete Weapon Analysis 2026

Viggora's chainmace is a one-handed crush weapon that requires 60 Attack to wield. It was introduced as part of the Wilderness content updates and is directly tied to the lore of Viggora. This section covers its exact stats, how to obtain it, Wilderness vs non-Wilderness performance, GP/hr rankings for Wilderness bosses, and whether it's worth your GP in 2026. 💡 Quick Verdict

Viggora's chainmace is a Wilderness PvM specialist weapon — not a generalist. When charged with Revenant Ether and used inside the Wilderness against NPCs, the chainmace gains +50% accuracy and +50% damage. Outside the Wilderness (or uncharged), it's outclassed by the Zamorakian Hasta. If you farm Wilderness bosses or hunt revenants, buy it (~200K-500K GP). If you never enter the Wilderness, skip it.

4.3 🧮 What Does +50% Accuracy & Damage Mean in Practice?

OSRS damage calculation multiplies your effective attack roll and max hit. The chainmace's +50% accuracy and +50% damage modifiers stack multiplicatively, meaning the effective DPS increase is roughly 2.25× base DPS (1.5 × 1.5). Here's a practical breakdown:

  • At 80 Attack / 80 Strength with standard gear: ~6.5 DPS uncharged → ~14.6 DPS charged (vs NPCs)
  • At 99 Attack / 99 Strength with BiS gear: ~9.0 DPS uncharged → ~20.3 DPS charged
  • Add a Salve Amulet (e) against undead bosses (Vet'ion, Calvar'ion): the 20% bonus stacks multiplicatively, pushing DPS even higher (~27.4 DPS total)
  • Add Piety prayer: another 1.23× multiplier → ~25.0 DPS (no Salve) or ~33.7 DPS (with Salve (e))

This makes the chainmace the #1 DPS-per-GP weapon for Wilderness PvM — outperforming even the Ursine chainmace (its upgraded variant) in raw DPS against crush-weak targets, at a fraction of the cost.

6.2 🎮 Combat Strategy (Step-by-Step)

  • Start with Protect from Magic — Viggora's main attack is Magic-based (max hit ~15). This reduces damage taken by 30-40%.
  • Watch for invisible phase — He'll turn invisible at 75% HP. Use area-of-effect attacks or run around to find him. Tip: Turning up brightness helps spot his shimmer.
  • Kill minions FIRST — At 50% HP, he summons 2 spectral minions. They heal him for 10 HP per tick if left alive! Kill them before focusing Viggora again.
  • Bring a friend — Viggora is easier with 2 players (one tanks, one DPS). The minions phase is much easier with 2+ players.
  • Use a special attack weapon — Dragon Dagger (p++) spec weapon can burst him down during the minions phase.

Not really. Viggora has a teleport mechanic — if you try to safespot him, he'll teleport to you after 3-4 attacks. Your best bet is to fight him fairly with Protect from Magic and good food. Some players try to use the Multicombat caves near his spawn to safespot, but Jagex has patched most of these spots.

⑦ ❓ FAQs — Viggora OSRS (2026 Updated)

Q: Is "Vigora" the correct spelling?

A: No! The correct spelling is "Viggora" (with a "g" after the "i"). Many players misspell it as "Vigora," but the official in-game spelling is Viggora. This guide uses the correct spelling throughout. If you're searching for guides, use "Viggora OSRS" for accurate results.

Q: Can F2P players encounter Viggora?

A: No. Viggora is members-only content. However, you can read about his lore on the OSRS Wiki or watch YouTube guides if you're curious. F2P players can technically enter Edgeville Dungeon (where Viggora spawns), but they won't see him (members-only NPC).

Q: How hard is the "Curse of the Empty Lord" quest?

A: It's a mini-quest, so it's shorter and easier than full quests. Combat 40+ and 10 lobsters should be enough. The boss fight is the hardest part, but with Protect from Magic, it's very manageable. Expect to spend ~15-20 minutes on your first run.

Q: Can I get Viggora's chainmace without doing the quest?

A: No. You must complete "Curse of the Empty Lord" first. After that, Viggora becomes a repeatable boss (you can fight him once per day per account for a chance at the chainmace drop). The drop rate is 1/128 — be prepared for a long grind!

Q: Is Viggora's chainmace good for PvM (bossing)?

A: It's good ONLY in the Wilderness vs NPCs (thanks to the +50% bonus). Outside the Wilderness (or uncharged), it's outclassed by the Abyssal Whip, Dragon Scimitar, and Zamorakian Hasta. For general PvM (GWD, Slayer), skip the chainmace and use a Whip or Tentacle.

Q: Where is Viggora right now? (How to track him)

A: Viggora roams between 3 locations (Edgeville Dungeon, Slayer Tower, Rogues' Castle). There's no fixed schedule — you just have to check all 3 locations. Some players use the in-game "NPC Tracker" plugin (RuneLite) to see his last known location. If you can't find him, he might be in the process of despawning/respawning (~2 hour respawn timer).

Q: Can I safespot Viggora during the boss fight?

A: Not really. He can teleport to you if you try to safespot. Your best bet is to fight him fairly with Protect from Magic and good food. Some players try to use the Multicombat caves near his spawn, but Jagex has patched most safespots.

Q: What's the drop rate of Viggora's chainmace?

A: The drop rate is 1/128. On average, you'll need 128 kills (~32 hours of grinding) to get one. Some players get it in 10 kills; others take 300+. RNG is RNG. Consider just buying it from the GE for 200K-500K GP unless you enjoy the boss fight.

Q: Is Viggora's chainmace worth it for Ironmen?

A: Absolutely! Ironmen can't just buy the chainmace from the GE (well, they can, but it takes time to farm the GP). The chainmace is extremely valuable for Wilderness bossing on an Ironman account. The +50% bonus makes short work of Vet'ion, Calvar'ion, and other Wilderness bosses. If you're an Ironman, prioritize getting this weapon early.

🎯 Final Tips for Viggora Hunters

Viggora is one of OSRS's most atmospheric NPCs. His story ties into the larger Zarosian lore, and completing "Curse of the Empty Lord" gives you a cool weapon plus some great lore rewards. If you're hunting for the chainmace, be patient — it's a 1/128 drop rate, so you might need multiple kills. Bring a friend to make the fights faster, and always watch out for PKers when you're at Rogues' Castle!

Pro tip: If you're struggling to find Viggora, try world-hopping. Some worlds have him spawned more frequently due to RNG. Also, turn up your brightness — Viggora is translucent and hard to see at default settings!
