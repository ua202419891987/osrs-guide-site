OSRS Skills Overview for Beginners 2026

OSRS has 23 skills. That sounds like a lot — and it is. But you don't need to understand them all on day one. This guide breaks down EVERY skill into simple English, explains which ones matter most for beginners, and gives you a training priority order.

1. What Are Skills in OSRS?

In OSRS, skills are your character's abilities. Each skill has its own XP bar and level (1 to 99). The higher your level, the better you are at that activity.

Think of skills like subjects in school:

  • Combat skills = P.E. class (fighting, getting stronger)
  • Gathering skills = Collecting resources (mining ore, catching fish)
  • Production skills = Making things (cooking fish, smithing armor)
  • Support skills = Utility (running faster, picking pockets, traveling)

2. Combat Skills — Your Foundation

Combat skills let you fight monsters, complete quests, and survive in Gielinor. These are the FIRST skills you should train.

The 7 Combat Skills

SkillWhat It DoesBeginner Priority
AttackWield better weapons, hit more often🥇 Train first (to 40-60)
StrengthHit harder (higher max hit)🥇 Train alongside Attack
DefenceGet hit less, wear better armor🥈 Train after 40 Attack/Strength
HitpointsYour health. More = survive longer📈 Grows passively from combat
RangedShoot from distance (bows/crossbows)🥉 Train after melee (30+)

PrayerActivate blessings that boost combat🔴 Don't train until later (expensive)
💡 Prayer Is Different

Prayer isn't trained by "doing" it actively. You "train" it by burying bones or using them on a gilded altar. It's very expensive (millions of GP to 99). As a beginner, ignore Prayer until you have a steady income.

🍳 Cooking: The #1 Beginner Production Skill

Cooking is the first production skill you should train. Why? You need food to survive combat. Catch fish (Fishing) → cook them (Cooking) → eat them (survive fights). It's the perfect loop for beginners.

Fastest route to 99 Cooking F2P: Cooking guild (level 32 Cooking) → cook tunas → cook lobsters (level 40) → cook swordfish (level 45).

💰 The Gathering → Production Loop

The most newbie-friendly money-making method is: Gather raw materials → Process them (production skill) → Sell for profit. Examples:

  • Mine iron ore → Smith into iron bars → Sell bars (or make armor)
  • Catch lobsters → Cook them → Sell cooked lobsters
  • Cut yew logs → Fletch into yew longbows → Sell bows

Support skills don't directly make you stronger in combat, but they unlock convenience, travel, and access to better content.

⚡ Slayer: The Most Important Mid-Game Skill

Slayer is assigned by Slayer masters. You kill specific monsters you might not normally fight. Why it matters: Slayer unlocks access to new areas, drops (like the Abyssal Whip from Abyssal demons), and gradually pushes your combat stats up. Start Slayer as soon as you have ~50 in your main combat stats.

F2P is a great "training wheels" mode. You'll learn the basics of combat, a gathering skill (fishing or mining), and cooking — all for free. Once you feel comfortable with the game mechanics, then buy membership to explode your content options.

7. Which Skills Should YOU Train First? (Priority Order)

If you're staring at 23 skills and don't know where to start, follow this priority order for your first 2 weeks:

🥇 Priority 1: Combat Skills (Attack → Strength → Defence)

Your combat stats determine which content you can access. Train these first:

  • Target: 40 Attack + 40 Strength + 40 Defence (about 20-30 hours of gameplay)
  • Method: Kill cows → Al-Kharid warriors → Moss Giants
  • Result: You can wear Rune armor + wield Rune scimitar. You're now strong enough for most F2P content.

🥈 Priority 2: Cooking + Fishing (or Mining + Smithing)

Once your combat stats are decent, pick ONE gathering + ONE production skill pair:

  • Path A (recommended): Fishing (gather) + Cooking (produce) — food for combat + some GP from selling extra fish
  • Path B: Mining (gather) + Smithing (produce) — make armor for combat + sell extra bars

🥉 Priority 3: One Support Skill (Agility or Thieving)

After you have combat + one money-making skill pair, start Agility. Why? Agility lets you run longer (stamina) and unlocks shortcuts that save hours of running time over your OSRS career.

💡 Beginner Money-Making Strategy: The 3-Step Loop

The most reliable way for beginners to earn GP through skilling follows this pattern:

  1. Pick ONE gathering skill you enjoy (Fishing, Mining, or Woodcutting). Don't spread yourself thin — focus on one to reach profitable levels faster.
  2. Use the Grand Exchange to sell raw materials directly. Most beginners make more GP selling raw fish/ores/logs than processed versions, because high-level players buy them in bulk for fast XP.
  3. Reinvest GP into better tools (Rune pickaxe, Rune axe, Lobster pot) as soon as you can. Better tools = faster gathering = more GP/hr.

Example: Fish lobsters at 40 Fishing (Catherby or Fishing Guild), sell raw lobsters at GE for ~200K GP/hr. Buy a Rune axe with the profit, then switch to cutting yew logs (60 Woodcutting) for 300K+ GP/hr. You've just bootstrapped from one skill to another using nothing but your own GP.

Frequently Asked Questions — OSRS Skills

Q1: What's the maximum level for each skill in OSRS?

Every skill in OSRS has a maximum level of 99 (about 13,034,431 XP). However, some skills can be virtually levelled beyond 99 through "virtual levels" shown in certain RuneLite plugins. In practice, once you hit 99, there's nothing new to unlock — you've maxed that skill. The total level cap is 2,277 (23 skills × 99).

Q2: How long does it take to reach level 99 in a skill?

It varies dramatically by skill. Fast skills like Cooking or Fletching can take 30-50 hours using efficient methods. Slow skills like Runecrafting, Agility, or Slayer can take 200-500+ hours. The key factor is XP per hour: some skills max out at ~40K XP/hr, while others can reach 800K+ XP/hr with the right methods.

Q3: Which skills are the easiest to get to level 99?

The fastest and easiest 99s for beginners:

  • Cooking — Can reach 700K+ XP/hr making wines or cooking karambwans. Often the first 99 for most players.
  • Fletching — Stringing bows gives 600K+ XP/hr. Profitable to 99 if you buy materials wisely.
  • Firemaking — Wintertodt is 300K+ XP/hr and profitable for supplies.
  • Prayer — Very fast XP (500K+/hr) but extremely expensive (100M+ GP for 99).
Q4: Which skills are the most profitable for making GP?

Top money-making skills at high levels:

  • Slayer (95+) — Hydra (Alchemical Hydra): 3M-5M GP/hr
  • Farming — Herb runs (Ranarr, Snapdragon): 1M-2M GP/hr passive income
  • Runecrafting (77+) — Blood runes at Zeah: 400K-600K GP/hr, very AFK
  • Hunter (80+) — Black chinchompas: 800K-1.5M GP/hr (wilderness risk)
  • Thieving (91+) — Pyramid Plunder or pickpocketing Elves/Vyres: 1M-3M GP/hr
Q5: Which skills are the hardest or slowest to train?

The slowest skills by XP rate:

  • Runecrafting — Historically the slowest. Even with Zeah bloods, maxes ~40K XP/hr AFK or ~90K XP/hr with Lava runes (very click-intensive).
  • Agility — Maxes ~65K-75K XP/hr at Ardougne rooftop. Repetitive and requires constant clicking.
  • Slayer — Average 20K-40K XP/hr overall. Standard max cape takes 300-500 hours.
  • Mining — Fastest methods cap around 90K-110K XP/hr (3-tick granite), but that's very click-intensive. AFK methods are much slower (~30K XP/hr).
Q6: What's different about training skills as an Ironman?

Ironman accounts cannot trade with other players, which dramatically changes skilling:

  • You gather everything yourself. No buying ores, logs, herbs from the Grand Exchange. Every resource must be farmed, mined, or collected personally.
  • Herblore becomes MUCH harder — you need to farm all your own herbs and gather secondaries.
  • Prayer is trained by killing dragons for bones, then using them at an altar — no buying bones.
  • Crafting requires crafting your own jewelry, making it essential for teleportation.
  • Production skills feel more rewarding because every item you make is truly useful.
Q7: Should I train one skill to 99 at a time, or spread out?

For most players, spreading out your training is better because:

  • Burning out on one skill is very common. Switching skills keeps the game fresh.
  • Many skills benefit each other — Farming gives herbs for Herblore, Fishing gives fish for Cooking, Mining gives ores for Smithing.
  • Quest requirements often ask for multiple mid-level skills, not one high-level skill.

The exception: If you're close to a 99 (e.g., level 95+), it can be worth pushing through for the skill cape and the sense of achievement.

Q8: What are "AFK" skills and which are best for AFK training?

AFK (Away From Keyboard) training means you can train while doing other things — watching videos, working, or playing another account. Best AFK skills:

  • Fishing — Barbarian fishing or monkfish: click every 30-90 seconds
  • Woodcutting — Redwoods (90+): trees last 2-3 minutes before depleting
  • Combat (NMZ) — Nightmare Zone: 20 minutes of AFK combat with absorption potions
  • Magic — Splashing: literally 20 minutes of AFK clicking once
  • Cooking — Cooking an inventory takes ~60 seconds, very low attention
Q9: What do skillcapes do, and how do you get them?

When you reach level 99 in any skill, you can buy that skill's cape from a specific NPC for 99,000 GP. Skillcapes provide:

  • A perk unique to that skill (e.g., Cooking cape prevents burning food, Crafting cape gives unlimited teleports to the Crafting Guild).
  • The ability to perform the skillcape emote (a special animation).
  • +9 bonus in all defensive stats, making them decent hybrid capes.
  • The Max Cape (all 99s) combines all skillcape perks into one item.
Q10: Are there skills I should never train past a certain level?

No skill is truly "wasteful" to train, but some have diminishing returns:

  • Prayer above 77 (for Rigour/Augury) is expensive with marginal benefit. But 99 Prayer is still useful for the cape perk.
  • Runecrafting above 77 (blood runes) — beyond this, the XP rates don't improve dramatically and the GP gain is small per additional level.
  • Firemaking above 85 (max Wintertodt efficiency) — unless you want the cape, there's no content unlock.

That said, every 99 gives you a cape, a perk, and bragging rights. Nothing is truly wasted.

Q11: How do temporary boosts (potions, pies) work with skill requirements?

Temporary boosts are crucial for questing and unlocking content without fully training a skill:

  • Visible boosts (e.g., Attack potion, ranging potion): Raise your visible stat. You need the boosted level for combat, but NOT for quest requirements (quests check your base level).
  • Invisible boosts (e.g., Slayer helmet, Salve amulet): Apply a damage/accuracy multiplier without changing your visible level.
  • Skill requirements for quests can sometimes be bypassed with spicy stews (from Recipe for Disaster) which randomly boost or drain stats by up to ±5.
  • Construction tea, Fishing boosts (admiral pie), and Smithing boosts (dwarven stout) are examples of skill-specific items that can help you meet requirements early.
Q12: What's the fastest way to level combat skills as a beginner?

For brand new accounts, the optimal combat training path:

  1. Do Waterfall Quest immediately — free 13,675 Attack + Strength XP (level 1 → 30 in both).
  2. Train at Rock Crabs (Rellekka) or Ammonite Crabs (Fossil Island) — high HP, low Defence, and aggressive. Extremely AFK.
  3. At Attack/Strength 60+: Move to Nightmare Zone (NMZ) for AFK training or train through Slayer for long-term efficiency.
  4. Use the best scimitar you can wield at all times (Iron → Steel → Rune → Dragon). Scimitars have the best DPS until you get a whip.
Q13: What skill levels do I need to complete ALL quests?

The maximum skill requirement for the Quest Point Cape (all quests complete) is approximately:

  • 70 Cooking (for RFD — Recipe for Disaster finale)
  • 70 Smithing (for Song of the Elves)
  • 70 Construction (for Song of the Elves)
  • 69 Slayer (for Monkey Madness II)
  • 70 Herblore (for Song of the Elves)
  • Plus various other skills in the 50-70 range. No skill needs to be 99 for quests — 70 is the practical max for the Quest Cape.
Q14: Which skills should I train BEFORE buying membership?

If you're on the fence about membership, try to hit these F2P milestones first:

  • 40 Attack, Strength, Defence — Lets you wear Rune armor and test if you enjoy combat.
  • 40 Fishing + 40 Cooking — Test the gathering-production loop. If you enjoy it, members opens up Farming, Hunter, and much more.
  • All F2P quests complete — Especially Dragon Slayer. This gives you a taste of questing and unlocks your first major achievement.
  • Try skilling for a few hours — Mine some iron, chop some logs. If the core loop feels fun, membership makes it 10x better.
Q15: What's the most efficient order to max all 23 skills?

Efficient maxing (used by experienced players aiming for the Max Cape quickly) follows this logic:

  1. Agility first — Full Graceful outfit from rooftop courses. Faster run energy recovery benefits EVERYTHING.
  2. Combat through Slayer — Train combat stats through Slayer instead of pure combat grinding. Kill two birds with one stone.
  3. Farming runs daily — Start herb runs and tree runs immediately. Farming is time-gated (trees take hours), so start early.
  4. Gathering before Production — Fish before Cooking, Mine before Smithing, Woodcut before Fletching. Gather your own resources if you're on a budget.
  5. Expensive skills last — Prayer, Construction, and Herblore are money sinks. Build your bank first through profitable skills like Slayer and Farming.
  6. Max combats before Runecrafting — Runecrafting is the slowest skill. Leave it for last because by then you'll have access to the best methods (Zeah bloods, Wrath runes).

Realistically, most players don't follow a strict order — they train whatever feels fun. The above is for efficiency-focused players.

Final Tips — Skills Overview for Beginners

  • Train combat skills alongside skilling. Don't neglect Attack, Strength, Defence, and Prayer. Higher combat unlocks better content, which in turn makes skilling easier and more profitable.
  • Set milestones, not goals. "Get 99 Woodcutting" feels impossible. "Reach 50 Woodcutting this week" is achievable. Small wins keep you motivated.
  • Use the OSRS Wiki skill calculators. They show you exactly how many actions, hours, and GP each level requires. Planning ahead saves frustration.
  • Don't skill for GP early on. Low-level skilling (mining copper, fishing shrimp) earns pennies. Focus on leveling up first — higher levels unlock exponentially better money methods.
  • Mix AFK and active training. Do active methods (Zulrah, Raiding) when you're at the keyboard, and AFK methods (Ammonite Crabs, red chins) when multitasking. This is the secret to fast XP without burning out.
