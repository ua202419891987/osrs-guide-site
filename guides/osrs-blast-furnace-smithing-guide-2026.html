1. Blast Furnace Overview & How It Works

The Blast Furnace is located in Keldagrim, the dwarven capital beneath the Fremennik Province. It is a massive industrial furnace that smelts ore into bars at double the speed of a regular furnace. More importantly, it uses half the coal required for steel/mithril/adamant/rune bars, making it incredibly efficient for both XP and profit.

Why the Blast Furnace Is the Best Smithing Method

  • Fastest XP in the game: Gold bars with goldsmith gauntlets reach 140K-170K XP/hr. Compare to anvil training which maxes around 60K-80K XP/hr.
  • Half coal cost: Coal requirements are halved. Steel needs 1 coal instead of 2. Mithril needs 2 instead of 4. Adamant needs 3 instead of 6. Rune needs 4 instead of 8.
  • Iron bars need NO coal: At a regular furnace, iron ore requires coal to smelt into bars. At the Blast Furnace, iron ore smelts into iron bars with no coal — a massive cost saving.
  • Profit potential: Many bar types (gold, mithril, adamant, rune) can be profitable when bought from the Grand Exchange. You can train Smithing and make money at the same time.
  • Batch processing: You can load up to 254 ores on the conveyor belt at once, and they smelt automatically while you bank. This means very efficient, non-stop action.

3. How to Reach the Blast Furnace (Keldagrim)

Step-by-Step Route

  1. Complete The Giant Dwarf quest — this opens the entrance to Keldagrim via the tunnel near Rellekka.
  2. Fastest route: Minecart system. Use the minecart from the Grand Exchange (Falador or Dwarven Mine) to Keldagrim. The Dwarven Mine entrance is just south of Falador.
  3. Alternative route: Rellekka tunnel. Go to Rellekka (north of Camelot), run east to the Keldagrim entrance tunnel, walk through.
  4. Once in Keldagrim: Run north-west from the main square. The Blast Furnace building has a large chimney and is impossible to miss.
  5. Enter and pay the foreman (Nils the Foreman, right near the entrance). Pay 60K GP for 1 hour of access on the official Blast Furnace world.

Which World Should You Use?

  • Official Blast Furnace World (352): Fee is only 60K GP/hr. Higher population but most players are training, not griefing.
  • Other worlds: Fee is 72K GP/hr. Lower population. Only use if 352 is too crowded or you prefer privacy.

Tip: World 352 is almost always busy. This is actually good — more players means the conveyor belt stays supplied and the furnace keeps running. You can also trade bars with other players if you need to unnote items.

4. Core Mechanics — Conveyor Belt, Dispenser & Ice Gloves

The Blast Furnace Workflow

Here is exactly how the Blast Furnace operates, step by step:

Step-by-Step Blast Furnace Process

  1. Bank your ores at Keldagrim's bank (south of the furnace), or use the bank chest inside the Blast Furnace building if available.
  2. Walk up to the conveyor belt on the ground floor of the furnace. This is the main contraption with a hopper above it.
  3. Load ores onto the conveyor belt: If you're making iron bars, load only iron ore. If you're making steel/mithril/adamant/rune, load coal FIRST, then the primary ore. For example, steel = 1 coal stack + 1 iron ore stack on the belt (or load both at once).
  4. The ores travel up and enter the furnace. After a few seconds, completed bars drop into the bar dispenser at the bottom of the furnace (looks like a small opening with a grill).
  5. Equip ice gloves and click the dispenser to collect the hot bars. You can collect up to 10 bars at a time from the dispenser.
  6. Run to the bar collecting chest (near the furnace, on the same floor). Click the chest with bars in your inventory to deposit them. You gain Smithing XP when you deposit bars into the chest.
  7. Repeat. Run back to the bank, withdraw more ore, load the conveyor belt again.

Ice Gloves — Why They Are Mandatory

Hot bars come out of the dispenser at extremely high temperatures. Without ice gloves, picking up a hot bar deals 8-10 damage and you cannot stack them. With ice gloves equipped:

  • You take zero damage when collecting bars from the dispenser
  • You can collect multiple bars at once (up to 10 per dispenser click)
  • The ice gloves have unlimited charges for this specific use
  • Store them in the glove locker inside the Blast Furnace building (right next to the dispenser) so you can free up an inventory slot

The Conveyor Belt + Hopper System

There are two ways to load ores into the furnace:

  • Conveyor belt (ground floor): Click the belt to load ore from your inventory. This is faster and the primary method. You can queue ores as long as the hopper has space (up to 254 ores total).
  • Hopper (upstairs): Climb the stairs and use ore directly on the hopper. Slower but an alternative if the conveyor belt is crowded.

Most players use the conveyor belt exclusively. The hopper stores up to 254 ores total. Once the hopper has coal + primary ore, the furnace smelts automatically. You must keep the hopper stocked to maintain production.

6. Gold Smelting for Profit (Fastest XP in OSRS)

Gold bar smelting at the Blast Furnace with goldsmith gauntlets is the fastest Smithing XP in all of OSRS. At maximum efficiency, you can reach 140,000 to 170,000 XP per hour. Even the most expensive anvil methods (like rune platebodies) max out around 80K XP/hr. Gold at Blast Furnace more than doubles that.

Why Gold Is So Powerful

  • No coal required — gold ore goes straight into the furnace with no coal. This simplifies the inventory and speeds up the process.
  • Goldsmith gauntlets multiply XP by 2.5x: Normal gold bar XP is 22.5. With goldsmith gauntlets (Family Crest quest reward), it jumps to 56.2 XP per bar. You MUST have these.
  • Gold bars are generally profitable: Gold ore typically costs ~150-170 GP, and gold bars sell for ~260-330 GP. That's a ~100-150 GP profit per bar, on TOP of the massive XP.
  • Low attention: Since there's no coal management, you just load gold ore on the conveyor belt and collect gold bars. Very repetitive and brain-off friendly.

Setup for Gold Smelting

Gold Bar Blast Furnace Setup

  • Items needed: Ice gloves (stored in glove locker), goldsmith gauntlets, stamina potions, gold ore (noted or unnoted)
  • Optional: Graceful outfit, Ring of Endurance (for longer stamina)
  • Inventory: 28 gold ore per trip to the conveyor belt
  • Process: Bank → withdraw 28 gold ore → run to conveyor belt → load ore → run to dispenser → collect gold bars (with ice gloves) → run to chest → deposit bars for XP → repeat

XP rate: 140K-170K XP/hr depending on how efficiently you move. You can expect to process roughly 2,500-3,000 gold bars per hour.

7. Iron Bars — Cheapest Training (60K-70K XP/hr)

Iron bars are the budget option for Blast Furnace training. The key advantage: iron ore needs no coal at the Blast Furnace. A normal furnace requires 1 coal per iron bar, which makes iron bar smelting expensive at normal furnaces. At the Blast Furnace, it's just pure iron ore.

Iron Bar Stats

  • XP per bar: 12.5 (low, but you process many of them)
  • XP per hour: ~60,000 to 70,000 (with good stamina management)
  • Cost per bar: Iron ore (~100 GP) vs Iron bar (~150 GP) = loss of ~50 GP per bar
  • Cost per hour: ~250K-300K GP/hr loss (very cheap compared to other methods)

Who Should Use Iron Bars

  • Low-level accounts starting at 15 Smithing (minimum for iron bars)
  • Budget-limited players who want to train Smithing cheaply
  • Ironmen with stocked iron ore from Motherlode Mine or mining training
  • Use iron bars from 15 to 40 Smithing (then switch to gold bars with goldsmith gauntlets)

Iron Bar Method

  1. Buy or mine iron ore (bank at Keldagrim bank)
  2. Load iron ore onto the conveyor belt (one stack of 254 ores max per load)
  3. Wait for iron bars to appear at the dispenser
  4. Collect with ice gloves and deposit at chest for XP
  5. Repeat

Note: Iron bars are the fastest way to train Smithing on a budget. They are not the fastest overall (gold bars are faster), but they cost only ~250K-300K GP/hr to run compared to other methods that might cost millions per hour.

8. Coal Bag Strategy & How to Get It

The coal bag is one of the most impactful items you can bring to the Blast Furnace. It holds up to 27 coal in a dedicated slot, freeing up your inventory for more primary ores. This drastically reduces the number of trips you need to make to the bank.

How to Get the Coal Bag

  • Purchase from Prospector Percy at the Motherlode Mine for 100 golden nuggets
  • Golden nuggets are obtained by mining pay-dirt at the Motherlode Mine
  • The average rate is roughly 30-40 nuggets per hour, so expect ~3 hours of Motherlode Mine to get your coal bag
  • Absolutely worth it if you plan to do any coal-based bar smithing at the Blast Furnace

How the Coal Bag Changes Your Inventory

Result: The coal bag increases your ore-per-trip by 50-100%, meaning fewer bank runs and more XP per hour. For steel bars, you go from 14 trips to 28 trips worth of iron ore per run. It's a massive efficiency boost.

Coal Bag Usage Tips

  • Always fill the coal bag first before leaving the bank — it's easy to forget
  • The coal bag is equipped in the ammunition slot (yes, you wear it like an ammo pouch)
  • To empty the coal bag, right-click it and select "Empty" — the coal goes into your inventory
  • At the conveyor belt, empty your coal bag first, then add the primary ores. This ensures the furnace has coal + ore ratio correct
  • Not useful for gold bars (gold doesn't need coal). Only bring it for steel, mithril, adamant, or rune bars.

Stamina Potion Consumption Per Hour

  • With graceful outfit: ~2-3 stamina potions (8-12 doses) per hour
  • With graceful + Ring of Endurance: ~1-2 stamina potions (4-8 doses) per hour
  • Cost per hour in potions: ~30K-80K GP/hr depending on gear

Inventory Layout

Here is the recommended inventory setup for a smooth Blast Furnace session:

Inventory Setup

  • Equipment: Ice gloves (stored in glove locker near dispenser), Graceful outfit (worn), Coal bag (ammo slot, if applicable)
  • Inventory (4 empty slots reserved):
  • 24 ores (or 27 gold ore for gold bars)
  • 1 stamina potion (4 doses)
  • 1-2 super energy potions (backup)
  • 1 stamina potion in inventory, rest in bank

Why keep empty slots? When you collect bars from the dispenser, you need inventory space. Each click on the dispenser gives up to 10 bars. If your inventory is full of ore, you can't collect. Leave 4-5 empty slots for bars.

12. Ironman Blast Furnace Strategy

Smithing is a challenging but rewarding skill for Ironmen. The Blast Furnace is just as powerful for Ironmen as it is for main accounts, but you need to source your own ores.

Early Game (15-40) — Iron Bars

  • Source iron ore: Mine at the Mining Guild (requires 60 Mining) or Al Kharid mine. With Varrock Armor (medium+), you can mine iron faster.
  • Alternative: Motherlode Mine gives iron ore as pay-dirt. You'll also get golden nuggets for the coal bag.
  • Process: Smelt iron bars at Blast Furnace — no coal needed.
  • Xp rate: ~60K XP/hr once you have a comfortable supply of iron ore.

Mid Game (40-70) — Gold Bars

  • Source gold ore: The best source is the Arzinian Mine (Before the Shadow quest) — fastest gold mining in the game. Requires completing "Between a Rock..." quest. You can mine ~2,000 gold ore per hour here.
  • Alternative: Lava Maze dungeon gold rocks (Wilderness) or Motherlode Mine. The Arzinian Mine is far superior.
  • Goldsmith gauntlets: Complete Family Crest quest. This is mandatory — without them, gold bars give only 22.5 XP each.
  • Xp rate: ~140K-170K XP/hr with self-mined gold ore.

Late Game (70-99) — Gold Bars or Adamant/Rune Bars

  • Gold bars are still the fastest XP, but you'll need massive amounts of gold ore.
  • Adamant/Rune bars: Mine adamantite at the Mining Guild (requires 70+ Mining, respawns ~35 sec) or runite at the Wilderness mine (requires 85 Mining, very slow respawn). Not recommended as primary training — use them for profit instead.
  • Blast Furnace + Goldsmith gauntlets all the way to 99 is the standard Ironman meta. You'll need ~65,000 gold ore from 40-99. The Arzinian Mine produces about 2,000 ore/hr, so expect ~32-35 hours of mining plus ~25 hours of smelting.

Ironman Item Priorities

  • #1 Priority: Complete Family Crest quest for goldsmith gauntlets
  • #2 Priority: Coal bag from Motherlode Mine (100 nuggets)
  • #3 Priority: Graceful outfit (for stamina management)
  • #4 Priority: Ice gloves from Heroes' Quest
  • #5 Priority: Ring of Endurance from Sepulchre (for even better stamina)

15. Pro Tips for Maximum Efficiency

  1. Store Ice Gloves in the Glove Locker: The Blast Furnace building has a glove locker near the bar dispenser. Store your ice gloves there so you don't waste an inventory slot walking back and forth. You can retrieve them instantly when you need to collect bars.
  2. Pre-load the Conveyor Belt: When you arrive, immediately load a full stack of ore onto the conveyor belt before doing anything else. The furnace runs continuously as long as it has ore. Pre-loading ensures the furnace is working while you bank for more ore.
  3. Use a Coal Bag for Any Coal-Based Bar: For steel, mithril, adamant, and rune bars, the coal bag almost doubles your ore-per-trip efficiency. The 100 nugget investment pays for itself within 1-2 hours of training.
  4. Goldsmith Gauntlets Are Non-Negotiable: Without them, gold bars give 22.5 XP each. With them, 56.2 XP each (2.5x). Complete Family Crest before your first gold bar session. It's a ~30-minute quest for triple XP.
  5. Stamina Management Wins: The entire process is running. Bank → conveyor belt → dispenser → chest → bank. Use stamina potions proactively (not when you're already out of energy). Drink at 60-70% energy remaining.
  6. Keep the Bank Chest Open: Use the bank chest inside the Blast Furnace building rather than running all the way to the Keldagrim bank. It's much closer to the conveyor belt.
  7. Watch GE Margins Before Buying Bulk: Gold ore prices fluctuate. If gold ore is above 180 GP and gold bars are below 280 GP, consider mithril or adamant instead. Always check both margins before committing to a large buy order.
  8. Varrock Armor for Bonus XP: After completing the Varrock Diary, the Varrock Armor gives a +10-60% chance of smelting double bars (including at the Blast Furnace). The effect varies by tier: Easy (+10%), Medium (+10%), Hard (+20%), Elite (+60%). Elite Varrock Armor is incredible here.
  9. Ring of Forging for Iron: While smelting iron bars, the Ring of Forging gives a chance to smelt multiple iron bars from a single ore. Each ring lasts 140 charges. Great for Ironman accounts preserving their iron ore.
  10. Deposit Box Alternative: There is a deposit box near the Blast Furnace entrance (outside the building). Some players use this to quickly dump bars without running to the chest. However, this does NOT give XP. Only the chest gives Smithing XP. Do not use the deposit box by mistake.

16. FAQ — Blast Furnace Smithing

Q: Is Blast Furnace the fastest Smithing XP in OSRS?

Yes, by a large margin. Gold bars with goldsmith gauntlets at the Blast Furnace give ~140K-170K XP/hr. The next best methods (anvil platebodies) max out around 60K-80K XP/hr. Blast Furnace is roughly 2x to 2.5x faster than any alternative.

Q: What's the minimum Smithing level for Blast Furnace?

30 Smithing. This is the minimum requirement to use the Blast Furnace. However, you can't make many useful bars at 30 — steel bars are your only option until 40 (gold bars) or 50 (mithril). The Giant Dwarf quest is also required to access Keldagrim.

Q: Is Blast Furnace profitable? Can I make money while training?

Yes, most bar types are profitable at the Blast Furnace. Gold bars give ~+100-150 GP profit per bar AND the fastest XP. Mithril, adamant, and rune bars are also profitable. Iron bars are roughly break-even to slightly profitable. You can easily earn 300K-600K GP/hr while training, or up to 6M GP/hr with rune bars at 85+.

Q: Do I absolutely need ice gloves?

Yes, 100% required. Without ice gloves, you take ~8-10 damage every time you collect a hot bar and can only collect one bar at a time. With ice gloves, you take zero damage and can collect up to 10 bars per click. They cost ~5K GP on the GE and store in the glove locker inside the Blast Furnace. Buy them before your first session.

Q: How many gold bars per hour at Blast Furnace?

With good efficiency (stamina potions, graceful outfit, pre-loaded conveyors), expect 2,500 to 3,000 gold bars per hour. Each bar gives 56.2 XP with goldsmith gauntlets, for 140K-170K XP/hr. Very experienced players with perfect efficiency can push past 3,000 bars/hr.

Q: How long does 1-99 Smithing take at Blast Furnace?

Gold bars (40-99): ~84 hours of active smelting. Iron bars (15-40) + Gold bars (40-99): ~88 hours total. Alternative route (various bars): 100-120 hours. Compare this to anvil training which would take 200+ hours. Blast Furnace cuts the time in half.

Q: Should I use the coal bag for gold bars?

No. Gold ore does not require coal. The coal bag only helps for steel, mithril, adamant, and rune bars. For gold bars, leave the coal bag in the bank and fill your inventory with pure gold ore.

Q: What's better — gold bars or rune bars for training?

Gold bars: Best XP/hr (140K-170K), profitable, requires 40 Smithing, simple inventory (no coal). Rune bars: Lower XP/hr (~85K), MUCH higher profit (~6M GP/hr), requires 85 Smithing, harder to buy in bulk (GE limit). For most players, gold bars to 99 is the optimal strategy. Switch to rune bars at 85 if you want profit over speed.

Q: Can I use Blast Furnace on F2P?

No. The Blast Furnace is in Keldagrim, which requires The Giant Dwarf quest — a members-only quest. Additionally, the furnace fee, stamina potions, ice gloves, and goldsmith gauntlets are all members-only features. F2P players must use regular furnaces (anvils) for much slower training.

Q: Does Varrock Armor work at the Blast Furnace?

Yes. Varrock Armor's double-smelt chance applies at the Blast Furnace. Elite Varrock Armor gives a 60% chance to smelt double bars. This is extremely powerful — it effectively gives you a 60% XP boost on top of existing rates. Always wear your Varrock Armor when using the Blast Furnace.
