1. Hitpoints Overview & Unique Mechanics

Hitpoints (HP) is the only skill in OSRS you cannot directly train. Every 1 damage you deal to an enemy gives 1.33 Hitpoints XP. This means Hitpoints trains alongside your combat skills — it's the ultimate "passive" skill. If you're new to OSRS combat, start with our Combat Training Guide before reading this.

Why Hitpoints Matters

BenefitDetails
SurvivalEvery HP level = +1 maximum hitpoints. At 99, you have 99 base HP. For melee training, your HP scales with your combat stats.
Boss ViabilityHigher HP means you can survive longer at bosses without eating, increasing kills per trip. Essential for Slayer tasks.
PvP AdvantageHP level determines your combat level. Higher HP = higher combat = stronger in PvP brackets.
Quest RequirementsHP is not directly required, but many quests require combat levels that need high HP to achieve.
Hitpoints Cape (99)Doubles natural HP regeneration rate (2 HP/min → 4 HP/min). Excellent for AFK training. Combine with Magic for even faster regen.

The 1.33:1 Ratio

In OSRS, combat XP is distributed as follows:

Combat Skill UsedCombat XP per DamageHitpoints XP per DamageRatio
Attack, Strength, Defence (Melee)4 XP1.33 XP3:1
Ranged4 XP1.33 XP3:1
Ranged (Longrange style)2 Ranged + 2 Defence1.33 XP3:1
Magic (combat spells)2 Magic + 1.33 HP1.33 XP3:2
Magic (defensive casting)1.33 Magic + 1.33 Defence + 1.33 HP1.33 XP1:1:1

Key insight: Hitpoints gains XP at 1/3 the rate of your main combat skills. By the time you reach 99 Attack/Strength, you'll have ~13M Hitpoints XP (level 98-99).

2. XP Formula — The Math Behind Hitpoints

Understanding exactly how Hitpoints XP works helps you optimize your training:

The Formula

Hitpoints XP = Damage dealt × 1.33

This means:

  • A max hit of 25 gives 33.25 HP XP (plus 100 combat XP)
  • Higher max hits = faster HP training
  • Using better weapons and gear directly improves your HP XP/hr
  • Overkill damage (hitting 25 on a 10 HP enemy) still gives full XP

XP Distribution Settings

In your combat settings (the crossed-swords icon), you control XP distribution:

  • Accurate (Attack XP): 4 Attack XP + 1.33 HP XP per damage
  • Aggressive (Strength XP): 4 Strength XP + 1.33 HP XP per damage
  • Defensive (Defence XP): 4 Defence XP + 1.33 HP XP per damage
  • Controlled: 1.33 Attack + 1.33 Strength + 1.33 Defence + 1.33 HP per damage

Note: Controlled gives the same total combat XP (4 per damage) but spreads it across all three melee skills equally.

Gear Priority for Melee HP Training

Your goal is maximum DPS (damage per second) — more damage = more HP XP:

  1. Weapon: Best-in-slot for your level. Scimitar (Dragon > Rune > Adamant) or Whip at 70+
  2. Strength bonus gear: Berserker Helm/Neitiznot Helm, Fighter Torso, Dragon Boots, Fire Cape
  3. Amulet: Amulet of Strength (cheap) → Amulet of Fury → Amulet of Torture
  4. Ring: Berserker Ring (i) > Warrior Ring (i)
  5. Prayer: Use Piety (70 Prayer) for +23% Strength — massive DPS boost

7. Nightmare Zone — AFK Hitpoints Training

The Nightmare Zone (NMZ) is the best AFK Hitpoints training method in OSRS. You can train for 15-20 minutes with zero interaction (using absorption potions) or use Dharok's for max XP rates.

NMZ Setup for Max HP XP

AFK Method (Normal)

  • Gear: Your best melee gear (proselyte + whip, or full obsidian + berserker necklace)
  • Inventory: 4-6 overload potions, rest absorption potions
  • Prayer: None — use absorption potions only
  • Strategy: Drink overload, then absorption potions. Rock cake to 1 HP. Wait 15-20 minutes.
  • XP Rates: ~70K-80K combat XP/hr = ~23K-26K HP XP/hr (fully AFK)

Dharok's Method (Max XP)

  • Requirements: 70 Attack, 70 Strength, 70 Defence, Dharok's set
  • Strategy: Keep HP at 1 for maximum Dharok's damage bonus (max hit ~70-80 at high Strength)
  • Risk: You can still die if absorption runs out. Check every 5 minutes.
  • XP Rates: ~120K-140K combat XP/hr = ~40K-46K HP XP/hr

Recommended NMZ Bosses (Easy)

  • Count Draynor
  • King Roald
  • The Kendal
  • Me
  • Tree Spirit
  • Khazard Warlord
  • Bouncer

Avoid: Elvarg, Dagannoth Mother, Slagilith, Tanglefoot — they have mechanics that break AFK training.

8. Slayer — The Best Overall Method for HP XP

Slayer is widely considered the best way to train Hitpoints because you gain: Combat XP + HP XP + Slayer XP + loot/profit — all at the same time. You kill two (or three) birds with one stone.

Why Slayer > NMZ for HP

  • You need Slayer XP anyway for PvM progression (87 Slayer for Kraken, 93 for Smoke Devils, 95 for Hydra)
  • Slayer monsters have better drops that fund your gear upgrades
  • Bursting/barraging tasks (dust devils, nechryaels, smoke devils) give massive HP XP
  • Superior monsters (rare spawns) drop valuable items and give bonus Slayer XP

10. Ironman Hitpoints Strategy

Hitpoints on an Ironman works the same way — it's passive. Because Ironmen can't use NMZ for AFK training (no absorption pots without Herblore), the strategy differs.

Early Game (1-60)

  • Train on Sand Crabs / Ammonite Crabs — no supplies needed, safe death mechanics
  • Do all combat quests (Waterfall, Fight Arena, Vampyre Slayer, Grand Tree) for free XP
  • Use Fire Strike to safespot low-level monsters for Magic + HP XP

Mid Game (60-85)

  • Slayer is your best friend. Every task gives HP XP + resources + Slayer XP
  • Unlock Fossil Island for Ammonite Crabs (passive HP training when you need to AFK)
  • Bone Crossbow + Bone Bolts for cheap Ranged training on safe-spottable monsters
  • Get a Dragon Scimitar (Monkey Madness) ASAP for max melee DPS

Late Game (85-99)

  • Continue Slayer to 95+ — HP will naturally reach 99 before or around 99 Slayer
  • Burst/barrage dust devils and nechryaels for massive Magic + HP XP
  • Use Red Chinchompas on Maniacal Monkeys if you need to catch up on Ranged + HP
  • Bossing: Vorkath, Zulrah, Gauntlet all give combat + HP XP while generating valuable loot

Ironman Tip: Don't Splash

Splashing (casting combat spells with -65 Magic attack so you always miss) gives Magic XP but NO HP XP. Ironmen should burst/barrage for Magic XP instead — you get better XP rates AND HP XP.

13. Pro Tips for Efficient Hitpoints Training

  1. Train HP Through Slayer: It's the most efficient approach. You need Slayer for PvM anyway — don't waste time at NMZ or crabs unless you need to AFK.
  2. Don't Splash Magic: Splashing gives zero HP XP. If you want to train Magic, burst/barrage tasks give excellent Magic + HP + Slayer XP simultaneously.
  3. Chinchompas Are the Fastest HP XP: Red/Black chins at Maniacal Monkeys give 100K-200K HP XP/hr. If you're in a hurry (and have the gold), this is the way.
  4. Always Use Your Best Weapon: More damage = more HP XP. Upgrade your weapon before anything else. A Whip (6M GP) is worth every coin.
  5. Prayer Boosts DPS: Using Piety (70 Prayer) during Slayer tasks boosts your DPS by ~23%. That's 23% more HP XP per hour. Always use offensive prayers when you can.
  6. Strength > Attack for HP XP: Higher Strength level = higher max hit = faster kills = more HP XP. Prioritize Strength training over Attack for HP gains.
  7. Hitpoints Cape Stacking: At 99 HP, wear the cape + use Rapid Heal prayer for 8 HP/min passive regen. Excellent for AFK Slayer or skilling activities that deal minor damage.

14. FAQ

Q: How long does 1-99 Hitpoints take?

Since HP is passive, it depends on your combat training. Rough estimates: If training combat at 60K-100K XP/hr, expect 13M HP XP after ~150-200 hours of combat. Pure HP grinding (chins) can be done in ~65-130 hours.

Q: Will I hit 99 HP before 99 Attack/Strength/Defence?

If you train ALL three melee skills to 99, you'll have ~39M HP XP (far past 99). If you train only one combat style to 99 (e.g., 99 Ranged), 99 Ranged = ~13M combat XP = ~13M HP XP = about level 98-99 HP.

Q: What's the fastest way to train Hitpoints?

Black Chinchompas at Maniacal Monkeys (MM2 tunnels): 100K-200K HP XP/hr. This is the absolute fastest method but costs money (chins are expensive) and requires active play.

Q: What's the most AFK way to train Hitpoints?

Nightmare Zone with absorption potions: 20 minutes of zero-click AFK for ~25K HP XP/hr. Use Dharok's at 1 HP for more XP but less AFK (5-minute check).

Q: Is it worth getting 99 Hitpoints?

Yes. The Hitpoints Cape's 2x regen is surprisingly useful for AFK training and Slayer. Plus, 99 HP gives you 99 base HP — that extra survivability matters at endgame PvM and raids.

Q: How do Ironmen train Hitpoints?

Through Slayer primarily. Sand/Ammonite Crabs for AFK. Bursting Slayer tasks for fast Magic + HP. Chinchompas from hunting for Ranged + HP. HP will naturally reach 99 well before you finish your PvM grinds.

Q: Can I train Hitpoints on F2P?

Yes — all combat in F2P gives HP XP the same way. Flesh Crawlers (Stronghold of Security, level 2) and Moss Giants (Varrock Sewers) are popular F2P training spots. Expect much slower rates than P2P.

Q: Does the Hitpoints Cape effect stack with other regen items?

Yes! HP Cape (2x) + Rapid Heal prayer (2x) + Regen Bracelet (2x) = 8x natural regen = 8 HP/minute. Add a Hitpoints Cape for 4 HP base × 4 = very fast passive healing.
