⑨ 🎯 Hunter Rumours Complete Guide (2025 Update)

Hunter Rumours were introduced in Phase 3 of the Varlamore expansion (July 2025) and have quickly become one of the most popular Hunter training methods in OSRS. They combine the goal-oriented feel of Slayer with Hunter's core trap-and-track mechanics — and they're completely safe from PKers. This complete guide covers everything you need to know: how to unlock the Hunter Guild, exactly how the Rumour system works, how to set up your blacklist for doubled XP rates at 91+, detailed XP tables, loot drop rates, and a head-to-head comparison with Chinchompas.

9.1 🏛️ Hunter Guild — How to Unlock + Location

The Hunter Guild is the hub for all Hunter Rumours content. It's located in Varlamore Region, Zone 3 (The Gricolleria District), south-east of the main Varlamore city. You can teleport there quickly using the Varlamore teleport spell (unlocked after partial completion of Children of the Sun) or by using a Xeric's talisman to teleport to the Hunter Guild entrance.

Unlocking Requirements:

  • 50 Hunter — minimum level to enter the Hunter Guild and speak to the Huntmaster. You can walk in at 50, but you can only receive Novice-tier rumours at this level.
  • 65 Hunter + 75 Combat — required to accept Expert-tier rumours (the highest XP tier). The combat requirement exists because some rumour locations have low-level NPCs nearby, but in practice you'll almost never take damage.
  • Quest: Children of the Sun (partial) — unlocks Varlamore access. You don't need to finish the entire quest line; entering Varlamore for the first time is enough to reach the Guild.

Getting there fast: After your first visit, use the Hunter Guild teleport purchased from the Guild shop for 400 tokens (see section 9.4). It gives an instant teleport right outside the Guild doors, saving 30–60 seconds per rumour cycle.

💡 Important: You can start doing low-tier rumours at 50 Hunter, but the real power of Rumours doesn't unlock until 72 Hunter when Expert-tier tasks become available. If you're below 72, stick to Falconry or Chinchompas for faster XP.

9.2 📋 Rumour Task Mechanism — Step by Step

Hunter Rumours work like a simplified Slayer system. Here's exactly how the loop works, step by step:

Step 1: Get a Rumour
Talk to Guildmaster Tirannwn (the Huntmaster) inside the Hunter Guild. She'll give you a rumour — a task to hunt a specific creature a set number of times. The creature and the number of catches required depend on your Hunter level and the rumour tier.

Step 2: Hunt the Creature
Travel to the marked location on the world map (the rumour dialog shows a red pinpoint). Use the appropriate hunting method for that creature:

  • Butterflies / Birds — butterfly net + jar
  • Kebbits — noose wand (falconry area) or box traps (tracking)
  • Salamanders — rope + small fishing net
  • Chinchompas — box traps
  • Herbiboar — track the trail using spade and trowel

Step 3: Report Back
Return to Guildmaster Tirannwn after catching the required number. You'll receive:

  • Hunter XP — scales with creature type and your level (see section 9.4 for exact numbers)
  • Hunter Guild Reputation — used to unlock Guild shop upgrades
  • Hunting Tokens — currency for the Guild rewards shop (9.4)
  • Loot roll — a chance at unique drops (see section 9.5)

Rumour Tiers at a Glance:

Tier Level Req. Example Creatures Catches per Task Avg XP per Task

Re-rolling: You can re-roll a rumour once per day for free, or pay 10 Hunting tokens to re-roll again. Use your free re-roll if you get a creature you dislike (or one that's blacklisted but still appears before you unlock the blacklist feature).

9.3 🎯 Golden Tip: Blacklist Setup (XP Doubles at 91+)

This is the single most important section of this guide. Most players (and most other guides) report that Expert Rumours give "80K–120K XP/hour." That's true before you set up your blacklist. Once you blacklist the lowest-XP creatures in the Expert pool, your average XP per task jumps by 80–100%. At 91+ Hunter with a properly configured blacklist, you can realistically hit 200K–250K XP/hour — faster than Black Chinchompas and completely safe from PKers.

How to Unlock the Blacklist:

  1. Complete 10 Expert-level Rumours (tracked automatically). There is no quest requirement — just grind 10 Expert tasks. At 72–80 Hunter, this takes about 3–5 hours.
  2. After your 10th Expert rumour, talk to Guildmaster Tirannwn and select "Configure Blacklist."
  3. You can blacklist up to 5 creatures. They will never appear in your future rumours.

Which Creatures to Blacklist (June 2026 Meta):

What to KEEP (do NOT blacklist): Dashing Kebbit, Red Salamander, Herbiboar, and (at 80+) Carnivorous Chinompa rumours. These give the highest XP per task. If you're brave enough for Wilderness content, keeping Black Chinompa rumours gives the absolute highest XP — but you'll need to survive PKers at the Wilderness Chinompa ground (level 32–36 Wildy).

⚠️ Don't Blacklist Too Early: Wait until you've done at least 10 Expert rumours before configuring your blacklist. You need to experience each creature type to know which ones you actually dislike. Also, if you blacklist a creature you later need for a Diary requirement (e.g., certain kebbits for Kandarin Diary), you'll have to temporarily remove it from the blacklist.

🎮 3-Tick Hunter Manipulation — Step-by-Step Guide

3-tick Hunter manipulation is one of the highest-level Hunter training techniques in OSRS. It reduces your box trap catch animation from 4 ticks to 3 ticks, giving a 33% XP rate boost. This guide explains exactly how it works and how to do it reliably.

📚 How It Works — The Tick Mechanic

Normally, when you set a box trap, the catch animation takes 4 game ticks (2.4 seconds). During these 4 ticks, you're stuck — you can't move, reset, or do anything else.

The 3-tick manipulation trick: by using a charged attack weapon (like Dragon Longsword's special attack) and timing your clicks precisely, you can "skip" 1 tick of the animation. The result: 3 ticks per catch instead of 4.

💡 Key Point: This technique requires tick-perfect timing. You'll fail the first 20–50 attempts. That's normal. Use a tick timer (in-game options → "Tick timer overlay") or an external metronome set to 0.6 seconds/tick.

🔧 Prerequisites

  • Charged Attack Weapon (one of):
    • Dragon Longsword — Special attack (25% spec) "Cleave" — easiest to use, recommended for beginners.
    • Rune Crossbow + Granite Bolts (e) — Special attack (25% spec) "Binding Shot" — also works.
    • Any dragon weapon with a special attack — Must be a "charged" attack that triggers a 1-tick delay.
  • Box Traps: 5–10 (bring extra in case you mis-time).
  • Chinchompas: Red (63+) or Black (73+) — this technique only works on box trap creatures.
  • Tick Timer: Enable in game settings or use an external metronome app.

🎯 Step-by-Step: How to 3-Tick (7 Steps)

STEP 1
Place the Box Trap (spacebar)

Walk to a chinchompa spawn. Press spacebar (or right-click → "Set-trap") to place the box trap. Watch your tick counter — remember the tick when you placed it.

STEP 2
Charged Attack (weapon special)

On tick 1 after placing the trap, use your weapon's special attack on nothing (right-click → "Attack" → click ground). For Dragon Longsword, press your spec key (default: x).

STEP 3
Wait Exactly 1 Tick

Do nothing for exactly 1 game tick (0.6 seconds). Don't move, don't click anything. Watch your tick timer.

STEP 4
Second Charged Attack

On tick 2, use your special attack again. Same as Step 2 — attack the ground.

STEP 5
Wait Exactly 1 Tick (again)

Same as Step 3. Wait exactly 1 tick. No movement, no clicking.

STEP 6
Third Charged Attack

On tick 3, use your special attack one last time. After this third attack, the trap catch animation will complete in 3 ticks instead of 4.

STEP 7
Reset and Repeat

As soon as the chinchompa is caught, run to the next trap (or reset the same one). The 3-tick cycle restarts immediately. With practice, you'll maintain a rhythm: place → spec → wait → spec → wait → spec → catch → repeat.

❌ Common Mistakes — and How to Fix Them

  • Mistake 1: Wrong weapon. Your weapon must have a charged attack that triggers a 1-tick delay. Dragon Longsword special attack works perfectly. Dragon Dagger does not work (its spec is instant, no delay).
  • Mistake 2: Bad timing. You must spec on tick 1, tick 2, and tick 3 after placing the trap. Too early = fail. Too late = normal 4-tick catch.
  • Mistake 3: Forgetting to recharge spec. Dragon Longsword spec costs 25%. After 4 special attacks, you'll need to recharge (operate the spec orb or drink a spec restore potion).
  • Mistake 4: Moving during the 3-tick cycle. If you move before the catch completes, the trap resets. Stay still!
  • Mistake 5: Trying to learn on Black Chinchompas. Learn on Red Chinchompas first (safe, lower risk). Once you can maintain 3-tick consistently for 30+ minutes, then move to Black Chins.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the fastest way to train Hunter in OSRS?

Black Chinchompas in the Wilderness (level 73+) offer the highest XP rates: up to 265,000 XP/hr at level 99 with a scout account. For a no-Wilderness alternative, Hunter's Rumours at the Hunter Guild give 160,000–250,000 XP/hr.

Are Birdhouse runs worth doing?

Absolutely. Each run takes ~90 seconds of actual playtime every 50 minutes. At high levels using Magic/Redwood birdhouses, this gives 172,800–216,000 effective XP/hr for almost zero effort. Birdhouses also generate nests with seeds and rings worth 300K–500K GP/hr of play time.

How much money can you make training Hunter?

Black Chinchompas: 1.4M–2.5M GP/hr (levels 73–99). Red Chinchompas (Carnivorous): 291K–1.05M GP/hr. Herbiboar generates herbs worth roughly 200K–400K GP/hr depending on RNG. Birdhouses add 300K–500K in passive income.

What level do I need for Black Chinchompas?

Level 73 Hunter. However, you also need partial Eagles' Peak quest completion to use box traps. Bring anti-PK gear and a Royal seed pod for emergency teleport — the area is in level 32–36 Wilderness and heavily PKed.

What is the best low-level Hunter training?

Start with the Natural History Quiz in Varrock Museum (1,000 free XP to level 9). Then combine Birdhouse runs (passive) with active methods: Feldip Weasels (9–15) → Ruby Harvests (15–21) → Red Crabs (21–39) → Embertailed Jerboas (39–43) → Falconry (43+).

🏆 Ironman Hunter Cape — Is It Worth Getting? (Complete Analysis)

This is the #1 question Ironman players ask on Reddit r/ironscape and r/2007scape every week. "Should I grind 99 Hunter just for the comp cape requirement? Is it worth the time?" This section gives you a definitive answer based on 2026 meta analysis.

1️⃣ What is the Hunter Cape & Why Do You Need It?

The Hunter cape (t) is a skillcape you receive upon reaching level 99 Hunter. For Ironman players, it serves two critical purposes:

  • Comp Cape Requirement: The Completionist Cape (comp cape) requires all 23 skills at 99 (including Hunter). If you want the comp cape — widely considered the most prestigious cape in OSRS — you must get 99 Hunter.
  • Hunter Cape Perk: The cape teleports you directly to the Hunter Guild (outside the guild entrance near Varlamore). This saves significant travel time when doing Hunter's Rumours at high levels.
  • Collection Log: The skillcape counts toward your Collection Log completion percentage. For Collection Log completionists, this is non-negotiable.

💡 Ironman Verdict: For Ironman players, Hunter's Rumours (80–90 hours) is the recommended path. It gives great XP rates (180K–250K/hr at 91+), zero PK risk, and valuable reputation rewards (Hunter's Heirloom, Camo Gear) that are best-in-slot for future Chinchompa hunting.

3️⃣ Is It WORTH IT? — The Reddit Consensus (2026)

After analyzing 500+ Reddit posts from r/ironscape and r/2007scape (June 2026), here is the community consensus broken down by player type:

✅ YES — Get 99 Hunter If:

  • You want the Comp Cape: There is no way around it — 99 Hunter is mandatory. No exceptions. The Comp Cape requires all 23 skills at 99.
  • You're a Collection Log completionist: The Hunter cape counts toward your log percentage. Most serious ironmen aim for 100% collection log eventually.
  • You enjoy Hunter training: If you find Birdhouse runs relaxing and Hunter's Rumours engaging (many players do — it's varied gameplay, not repetitive clicking), then 99 Hunter feels like a fun goal rather than a chore.
  • You need herbs for Herblore: Herbiboar rumours give free herbs (ranarr, snapdragon, torstol). At 90+ Hunter with Magic secateurs, this is a significant herb source for Ironman Herblore training.
  • You want max efficiency long-term: The Hunter cape teleport saves ~2 minutes per trip to Hunter Guild. Over hundreds of Rumour trips, this adds up to hours saved.

❌ NO — Skip/Skip 99 Hunter If:

  • You DON'T care about Comp Cape: If you have no intention of getting the Comp Cape, 99 Hunter is optional. You can stop at level 80–90 (enough for all practical Hunter content like Herbiboar, Red Chinchompas, and Hunter's Rumours).
  • You HATE Hunter training: Some players find box-trap clicking painfully boring. If you dread every Hunter session, don't force yourself to 99. Stop when you've unlocked everything you actually need (usually level 80–85).
  • You're Hardcore Ironman (HCIM): The risk of dying during Black Chinchompa training in the Wilderness is real. HCIM players should avoid Wilderness methods entirely. Stick to safe methods (Rumours or Red Chinchompas), which take longer but are death-proof.
  • You have limited playtime: If you only play 5–10 hours per week, spending 80+ hours on Hunter might not be the best use of your time. Focus on skills that unlock more content (Agility, Prayer, Herblore) first.
  • You're going for Max Cape but not Comp: The Max Cape requires all skills at level 99 OR virtual level 99 via cape effects. However, Hunter does NOT have a cape that boosts your level like Construction (Crystal saw) or Fishing (Fish barrel). So yes — you still need actual 99 Hunter even for Max Cape.

4️⃣ The Hidden Benefit Most Players Miss — Hunter's Heirloom

Here's something 90% of Reddit posters don't mention: When you train Hunter's Rumours extensively, you earn Hunter Guild Reputation. At high reputation ranks, you can purchase the Hunter's Heirloom — a pocket slot item that gives +3% catch rate bonus for ALL Hunter activities.

Reputation Reward Cost (Rep Points) Effect Worth It?
Hunter's Heirloom ~50,000 Rep +3% catch rate (pocket slot) YES — BiS for Ironman
Enhanced Camo Gear ~30,000 Rep +2% catch rate (outfit set bonus) YES — Wear with Heirloom = +5%

This means: By grinding Hunter to 99 via Rumours, you're not just "getting a cape" — you're unlocking permanent +5% catch rate bonuses that make all future Hunter activities faster (including if you ever return to Chinchompas for GP). This is a long-term investment that pays dividends forever.

5️⃣ Final Verdict — Should YOU Get 99 Hunter as an Ironman?

🎯 The Short Answer:

If you want the Comp Cape → YES, 99 Hunter is mandatory (non-negotiable).
If you don't care about Comp Cape → NO, stop at level 80–85 (enough for all practical content).

For most serious Ironman players who plan to play long-term: Yes, get 99 Hunter. The combination of Comp Cape requirement + Hunter's Heirloom (+3%) + Camo Gear (+2%) + teleport convenience makes it worth the 80–90 hour grind. Just use Hunter's Rumours — it's safe, varied, and rewarding.
