③ 🌱 Levels 1-9: Starting Out (Copper Longtail)

The first 9 levels of Hunter are the slowest and most tedious. You can't use box traps yet, and you're stuck with bird snares and tracking. Fortunately, it's over quickly.

3.1 🐦 Method 1: Crimson Swifts (Levels 1–9)

Go to the Feldip Hills Hunter area (south of Yanille, use Fairy Ring AKS or run from Yanille bank). Buy a bird snare from the Hunter supply shop. Set it up near the Crimson Swift spawns and wait 5–10 seconds. Each Crimson Swift catch gives 34 XP.

  • XP/hour: 5K–8K
  • Time to level 9: ~20–30 minutes
  • GP: 0 (Crimson Swift parts have no value)

3.2 🗺️ Alternative: Varrock Museum Quiz (Skip Levels 1–9)

If you haven't done the Varrock Museum natural history quiz, do it now. Talking to Orlando Smith and answering all the exhibit questions gives 1,000 XP in Hunter and Slayer at level 1 — this jumps you straight from level 1 to level 9 in both skills. It takes 5 minutes and saves you 20–30 minutes of Crimson Swift grinding.

⚡ Do the Museum Quiz First — Every Single Time

Go to Varrock Museum (east of Varrock square). Find Orlando Smith on the ground floor. He'll give you a quiz on the exhibits — answer the questions (or use the Wiki quick-guide). At the end, you get a 1,000 XP lamp in Slayer and Hunter. This takes any account from level 1 to level 9 instantly. For ironmen, this is mandatory early-game content.

④ 🐦 Levels 9-43: Early Methods (Wagtail/Ruby Harvest/Ferret/Lizard)

Levels 9 through 43 are the "wilderness" of Hunter — no single method dominates, and you'll bounce between several activities. Here's the best route.

4.1 💍 Ring of Pursuit — Where to Buy (Levels 1-29 Essential)

If you're training Hunter on a fresh account (no stats, no GP), levels 9-29 can feel painfully slow. That's where the Ring of Pursuit comes in — it shrinks your tracking radius by 2 tiles, making Kebbit tracking (9-15) and Tropical Wagtail tracking significantly faster. Reddit players consistently ask: "Where do I buy this ring?" Here's the answer:

  • 🛒 Where to buy: Lowe's Archery Emporium in Varrock (south-west of the square, next to the axe shop). Price: 500 GP. You can also buy from the Hunter shop in Yanille (same price, closer to Feldip Hills).
  • 📊 When it matters most: Levels 9-29 (tracking methods). At 29+ (Swamp Lizards) and 43+ (Falconry), the ring no longer helps because those methods don't use tracking.
  • ⏱️ Worth it? If you're on a speedrun, skip the ring and just power through 9-29 normally — the time saved isn't worth a detour to Varrock. But for casual players, the ring makes the early grind much less painful.
  • 💡 Ironman tip: Buy 2 rings. One for your main, one for your Slayer/construction iron. Low level accounts can't teleport, so the ring saves you tons of running time.

4.2 🐤 Levels 9-15: Tropical Wagtail (Bird Snare)

Stay in the Feldip Hills and switch to Tropical Wagtails. They're one of the few early creatures with decent XP rates.

  • XP/hour: 15K–22K
  • GP: ~20K/hour (Wagtail feathers used in fishing)
  • Location: Southern Feldip Hills, north of the Hunter shop

4.2 🦋 Levels 15-29: Ruby Harvest (Butterfly Netting)

At level 15, switch to Ruby Harvests. You'll need a butterfly net and a butterfly jar (buy both from the Yanille Hunter shop). Ruby Harvests are the fastest XP from 15–29.

  • XP/hour: 25K–35K
  • GP: ~40K/hour (Ruby Harvests sell for Herblore secondaries — used in Super Defence potions)
  • Location: Piscatoris Hunter Area (north-west of the Falconry, near the Falconer Matthias)

Ruby Harvests are jarred in sets of 5. Once you fill a jar, bank it at the Piscatoris bank (if you've done Swan Song) or run south to the bank chest near the fishing colony.

4.3 🦎 Levels 29-43: Ferret / Swamp Lizard

At level 29, you unlock Swamp Lizards and Ferrets. Both use a noose wand. The best spot is in the Canifis Hunter Area (south of the Slayer Tower, west of Canifis).

  • 29–43: Swamp Lizards — ~30K–45K XP/hour, decent profit (~60K GP/hour from selling swamp lizard parts for fishing bait)
  • Alternative — Spined Larupia (31 Hunter): Decent XP but useless profit. Skip it.
  • Alternative — Horned Graahk (41 Hunter): Same as Spined Larupia. Skip it.

The Swamp Lizard's XP rate holds steady at 40K+ if you're quick with your wand. However, at level 43, a far better method unlocks.

⚠️ Skip Salamanders (Orange/Red) in This Range

Orange Salamanders (29 Hunter) and Red Salamanders (59 Hunter) are mediocre XP (30K–45K/hour) and require running to a remote spot north of the Observatory. Unless you're training on an ironman without access to nets/jars, Swamp Lizards are better XP and Ferrets are more convenient. Salamanders are only worth it at level 29 if you don't want to buy a butterfly net.

7.4 🎮 3-Tick Hunter Manipulation — Step-by-Step Guide

If you've spent any time on r/2007scape, you've seen the question: "How do you actually do 3-tick Hunter?" It's the second-most-asked Hunter question on Reddit (right after "where are Red Chins?"). This section gives you the full, hand-holding breakdown — no guessing, no vague Wiki wording. By the end, you'll know exactly what to click, when to click it, and how to practice without wasting hours.

📖 What Is 3-Tick Manipulation?

OSRS runs on a 0.6-second game tick. Every action in the game — mining, fishing, trapping — is aligned to this tick cycle. By default, when a box trap catches a chinchompa, the collapse animation lasts 4 ticks (2.4 seconds). During those 4 ticks, you're stuck waiting before you can reset the trap.

3-tick manipulation inserts a second action — called a "trigger item" interaction — at the exact moment the trap collapses. This interaction finishes on the very next tick, which resets the trap 1 tick faster than normal. Over a full hour, those saved 0.6-second slices add up to a 33%+ XP boost.

MethodNormal XP/hr3-Tick XP/hrBoost
Grey Chin (53)80K–110K110K–130K+33%
Red Chin (63)100K–140K140K–195K+39%
Black Chin (73)130K–180K180K–220K++35%

The catch: 3-tick Hunter is physically demanding. You're performing two precise actions every 1.8 seconds (not 2.4). Most players can only sustain it for 45–60 minutes before fatigue causes missed ticks — which defeats the purpose. Read the "When NOT to Use 3-Tick" section before committing.

🧰 What You Need

  • RuneLite (mandatory): The "Menu Entry Swapper" and "Tick Bar" plugins are essential. No other client has the menu-control precision you need.
  • Trigger item pair (choose one): You need two inventory items that create a "Use X on Y" interaction. The community-standard choice is Guam leaf + Volcanic tar (or any herb + tar). Why? Both are buyable from the GIEL (Grand Exchange) for ~10 GP each, so you can practice without burning GP.
  • Alternative trigger pairs: Flint + Bowstring, Chinchompa + Box Trap (if you're out of herbs), or Empty slot + Any item (dropping an item counts as an action). Herb + tar is preferred because the "Use" menu entry is consistent and doesn't require a target tile.
  • Weight-reducing gear: Same as normal chinchompa hunting — full Graceful, Boots of Lightness, Spottier Cape. You'll be running more because the rhythm is faster.

📈 Step-by-Step Setup (RuneLite)

⚙️ RuneLite Configuration

Step 1: Open RuneLite → Click the wrench icon (top-right) → Search "Menu Entry Swapper" → Enable it.

Step 2: Inside Menu Entry Swapper → Click "Configure" → Find "Withdraw-1" → Enable swap. This lets you withdraw 1 herb/tar from the bank in one click (useful for restocking quickly).

Step 3: Search for "Tick Bar" in the Plugin Hub → Install and enable. Set the bar position to "Above inventory" or "Below inventory" — wherever you can glance at it without looking away from your traps.

Step 4: Place your herb and tar in the first two inventory slots (top-left). This minimizes mouse travel distance when you need to trigger the interaction.

Step 5 (optional but recommended): Bind a mouse side-button (if you have one) to the "1" key. Even better, use RuneLite's "Shift-click" configuration to make left-clicking your herb automatically select "Use" mode.

🎯 The Rhythm — How to Actually Do It

Here's the exact click sequence. Memorize this loop — it becomes muscle memory after 200–300 chinchompas:

🥁 The 3-Tick Click Sequence

  1. Set your 5 traps in the normal diamond pattern. Watch the chinchompas roam near your traps.
  2. When a trap collapses (you'll see the "click" animation and hear the trap snap sound), don't reset it yet. Instead, immediately right-click your herb → select "Use", then left-click your tar (or click the ground if using drop-method). This is your trigger action.
  3. On the very next game tick (0.6 seconds later, the Tick Bar helps visualize this), left-click the collapsed trap to reset it. Because your trigger action just finished, the trap resets 1 tick faster than normal.
  4. Repeat for every trap collapse. The rhythm is: Collapse → Trigger → Reset → Move to next trap.

💡 Pro tip for timing: Don't try to "feel" the 0.6-second window. Instead, watch the Tick Bar plugin. When the bar fills by roughly 1/3, that's your cue to click the reset. After 30–40 chinchompas, your hands will naturally sync to the tick without you thinking about it.

🏋️ Where to Practice

Do not learn 3-tick on Red Chins or Black Chins. The pressure of wasting GP will make you tense, and tension kills your rhythm. Follow this progression:

⚠️ Fatigue Is the Real Enemy

3-tick Hunter is physically intensive. After 60–90 minutes, even experienced players see their mistake rate climb from ~2% to ~15%. Those missed ticks erase the XP advantage. Take a 5-minute break every hour. Your long-term XP/hour will be higher if you stay fresh.

⚠️ When NOT to Use 3-Tick

  • During peak PK hours (7–11 PM GMT+8): If you're doing Black Chinchompas, 3-tick requires your full attention on the trap timing. You'll be slower to notice a PKer on the minimap. Either use normal ticking during peak hours or stick to Red Chins.
  • On mobile: Touchscreen latency is ~100–200ms on most devices, which is too slow for consistent 3-tick timing. You'll miss ticks constantly and frustrate yourself. Use normal methods on mobile.
  • If you have wrist/RMS issues: The increased click rate (roughly 20% more clicks per minute) can aggravate repetitive strain. If your wrists hurt after normal hunting, 3-tick will make it worse. Your health is more important than a 99 cape.
  • If you're below level 63: Grey Chinchompas (53–63) give relatively low XP even with 3-tick. The time spent learning the manipulation is better invested in just getting to Red Chins normally. Learn 3-tick after 63 if you want to use it.

💡 3-Tick Pro Tips

  • Use the Tick Bar plugin: Set it to show a sound cue on every 3rd tick. This audio feedback trains your rhythm even when you're not looking at the screen.
  • Bind your trigger to a mouse side-button: If your mouse has extra buttons, bind one to "1" (select inventory slot 1) and the other to "2". This lets you trigger the herb+tar interaction without moving your mouse.
  • Rest for 30 seconds every 100–150 catches: This isn't just about fatigue — it also lets you check your inventory and make sure you haven't accidentally used all your trigger items. Nothing is more annoying than going for a 3-tick reset and realizing you're out of tar.
  • Disable RuneLite's "Shift-click to drop" if you use the drop-method as your trigger. Accidentally dropping your box traps costs you a bank run.
  • Watch a video once, then practice blind: Reading about 3-tick is one thing. Seeing it is another. Search YouTube for "OSRS 3-tick Hunter guide 2026" and watch the trap-collapse timing in real speed (not sped up). Then close the video and practice for 30 minutes without guidance.
🎯 Is 3-Tick Worth It for You?

If you're a speedrunner or efficiency-scape player: yes, absolutely. The 30–40% XP boost is real, and the GP/hr difference on Black Chins is massive (1.2M vs 800K). If you're a casual player who plays while watching Netflix: no. 3-tick requires focus, and you'll miss more ticks than you save. Stick to normal ticking and enjoy the chill grind.

6. Black chins are least crowded (and least PKed) during off-peak hours: 3AM–8AM EST on US worlds, or non-peak EU times.

⚠️ Black Salamander Wilderness Safety — What Reddit Players Need to Know

  • Safe spots: The Black Salamander area (north of Chaos Temple) has multiple world ranges. If you see a white dot on the minimap, just log out — you lose nothing valuable.
  • XP comparison: Black Salamanders give ~90K-110K XP/hour (slower than Black Chins), but they're stress-free compared to Chinchompa hunting. Good middle ground between Herbiboar and Black Chins.
  • GP comparison: Salamanders sell for ~1,200 GP each (GE price, July 2026). You'll earn ~300K-500K GP/hour — decent, but not as much as Chins.

⑨ 🎯 Hunter Rumours Complete Guide (2026 Update) — XP Rates, Blacklist & Strategy

Hunter Rumours were introduced in Phase 3 of the Varlamore expansion (July 2026) and have quickly become one of the most popular Hunter training methods. This guide covers everything Reddit players ask about most: how the system works, exact XP rates at every tier, how to set up your blacklist for 240K XP/hour, and whether Rumours are worth your time versus traditional methods.

9.1 📜 What Are Hunter Rumours?

Hunter Rumours work like a simplified Slayer system. Here's the loop:

  1. Get a Rumour: Talk to the Huntmaster in the Hunter Guild (Varlamore) to receive a rumour — a task to hunt a specific creature a set number of times.
  2. Hunt the creature: Travel to the marked location and hunt the creature using the appropriate method (box trap, net, tracking, etc.).
  3. Report back: Return to the Huntmaster. You'll receive Hunter XP, Hunter Guild Reputation points, and Hunting Tokens (used to buy guild rewards).

There are three tiers of Rumours, gated by Hunter level:

Expert72+Dashing Kebbit, Red Salamander, Black Salamander, Herbiboar8,000–15,000

Each tier also has a minimum number of creatures to catch per rumour (e.g., 5–12 for Novice, 8–18 for Adept, 10–25 for Expert). The higher your Hunter level within a tier, the more creatures you're assigned — and the better the XP.

Step 2: Configure Your Blacklist

After your 10th Expert rumour, talk to the Huntmaster and select "Configure Blacklist." You'll see a list of all Expert-tier creatures. You can blacklist up to 5 creatures — they will never appear in your future rumours.

⑫ 🎣 Drift Net Fishing — Dual Skill Training (44 Hunter + 47 Fishing)

Drift Net Fishing is a dual-skill training method introduced as part of the Fossil Island expansion. It lets you train both Hunter and Fishing at the same time by collecting drift nets on Fossil Island. If you need both skills trained, this method saves you considerable total playtime — but it comes with costs and trade-offs you need to understand before committing.

1. 📖 What Is Drift Net Fishing?

Drift Net Fishing requires 44 Hunter and 47 Fishing (you can train both simultaneously to reach these requirements). You'll be working on Fossil Island — specifically the Drift Net area accessible after completing the Bone Voyage quest. The core loop: fill your inventory with empty drift nets, run to the drift net fishing spot, lay your nets in the water, wait for fish to get caught, then collect the nets (which give both Hunter and Fishing XP, plus various fish).

Each net takes approximately 8–10 minutes to fill. You can lay up to 4 nets at once (more with higher Hunter), and you'll need to run back to the net storage to restock regularly. The method involves constant running, which makes weight-reducing gear essential.

3. 💰 Cost & Profit Analysis

Supply cost: Each empty drift net costs approximately 2,000 GP from the Fossil Island net shop. At higher levels you'll use 40–60 nets per hour, which adds up to 80K–120K GP/hour in supply costs.

Fish revenue: Nets return raw fish (mostly cod, bass, and the occasional shark at high Fishing levels). Selling these to the GE recovers part of your net costs. At 80H/80F you can expect to recover about 80K–120K GP/hour from fish sales.

Break-even point: Approximately every 50 minutes of efficient Drift Net Fishing, your fish sales cover your net costs and you earn a small profit of 30K–50K GP on top. This makes Drift Net roughly profit-neutral to slightly profitable — unlike most dual-skill methods which are purely costly.

Best for: Ironmen who need large quantities of raw fish (especially shark) for cooking training or food supplies. The off-GE value of the fish you collect is much higher for ironmen than the GE sale price listed above.

4. 🤔 Is Drift Net Worth Doing?

✅ Pros:

  • Trains two skills at once — if you need both Hunter and Fishing, this saves 30–40% total playtime versus training them separately.
  • Low risk — Fossil Island is a safe area (no Wilderness, no PKers).
  • Ironman powerhouse — excellent source of raw fish for Cooking training and shark supplies for trips.
  • Decent variety — the running/collecting loop breaks up the monotony of pure trap-clicking.

❌ Cons:

  • XP rates are modest — 40K–50K combined XP (both skills) at high levels. Dedicated single-skill methods (Chinchompas for Hunter, Barbarian Fishing for Fishing) give much higher XP/hour individually.
  • Not true AFK — you need to run between net spots and restock every 8–10 minutes. More active than the name suggests.
  • Nets are expensive — 2K GP per net adds up fast. If you're not an ironman, the constant GP drain can be annoying.
  • Requires 44H/47F to start — you can't use this as your primary 1–44 Hunter training. It's a mid-game method.

  • Wear full Graceful — weight reduction = faster running = more nets laid and collected per hour. This directly translates to higher XP/hour in both skills.
  • Bring Stamina potions (4-dose) — the constant running drains run energy fast. One inventory of Stamina potions lasts about 2 hours of non-stop Drift Net Fishing.

  • Bank your raw shark — if you're an ironman, raw shark from Drift Nets is one of the best off-GE food sources in the game. Don't drop them.
  • Don't buy nets from the GE — always buy empty drift nets from the Fossil Island net shop (2,000 GP each, no markup). The shop stocks 100 nets and restocks 10 every minute.
  • Combine with Bird House runs — while you're on Fossil Island for Drift Nets, do a Bird House run in the same trip. Two skills for the time investment of one.

⑬ ❓ FAQ — 1-99 Hunter Guide

Q: How long does 1-99 Hunter take?

A: 40–50 hours using the fastest methods (Museum skip → Falconry → Red/Black Chinchompas). Using balanced methods (Red Chins only from 63–99), expect 55–70 hours. Pure Bird House runs take 6–12 months of daily runs. Hunter is one of the fastest 99s in OSRS when trained actively.

Q: How much GP does 1-99 Hunter earn?

A: Using the profit-focused route (sell all chinchompas), you'll earn 80M–120M GP from 1–99. Using the speed route (Black Chins from 73), you'll earn 50M–80M GP but finish faster. Using Herbiboar from 80–99, you'll earn 40M–60M GP from herb drops alone. Full details are in our Hunter profit guide.

Q: Black Chinchompas — is the Wilderness risk worth it?

A: For speed-focused players: yes. Black Chins are 30–40% faster XP than Red Chins and earn 50% more GP/hour. But you will die occasionally. A typical 80–99 Black Chin grind includes 5–15 deaths to PKers, each costing you ~100K–200K in lost traps and a small stack of chins. If you can accept that, Black Chins are the best. If dying tilts you, stick to Red Chins or Herbiboar. Play during off-peak hours (early morning EST) and use total level worlds (1250+) to minimise encounters.

Q: What's better — Red Chinchompas or Herbiboar from 80-99?

A: Red Chins give better GP (500K–800K vs. 400K–600K per hour) and slightly better or equal XP (100K–140K vs. 120K–160K depending on speed). Herbiboar gives free Herblore XP and has a rare pet chance. Choose Red Chins if you want GP. Choose Herbiboar if you want a pet, hate competition for spots, or are an ironman who needs herbs. Many players do a mix: Red Chins 80–92 for profit, then Herbiboar 92–99 for the pet hunt.

Q: Do I need Graceful for Hunter?

A: Not mandatory but strongly recommended. Weight affects how long you can run, and Hunter involves constant running between traps. Full Graceful (+ the spottier cape at 66 Hunter) brings your weight close to 0 kg, letting you run almost indefinitely — especially with stamina potions. If you don't have Graceful, at minimum get Boots of Lightness and a Spotted Cape.

Q: How do bird house runs work?

A: Head to Fossil Island (requires Bone Voyage quest). There are 4 bird house spots: Verdant Valley (south side), Mushroom Meadow (east side), and two on the north-west island. At each spot, place 4 logs on the bird house space, add 10 hop seeds (or cheaper seeds — barley seeds cost 1 GP each), and wait 50 minutes. Come back, empty the house (you'll get bird nests, feathers, and raw bird meat), and reset. A run takes 90–120 seconds. For the full Fossil Island setup, read our Fossil Island quick guide.

Q: What are Hunter Rumours and should I do them?

A: Hunter Rumours are a 2026 addition from the Varlamore expansion. You talk to a Huntmaster who assigns you a specific creature to hunt. Completing rumours gives bonus XP, Hunter guild reputation, and a chance at the Hunter's heirloom (which gives +1 trap slot). Rumours are fun variety content and worth doing if you're bored of chinchompas, but they're not efficient XP compared to chins or falconry. The heirloom is worth grinding for if you plan to hunt a lot of chinchompas.

Q: Can I train Hunter on a level 3 skiller?

A: Yes. Hunter is a non-combat skill with zero combat requirements. All major methods (Falconry, Box Traps, Bird Houses, Herbiboar) are accessible to level 3 accounts. The only restriction: Black Chinchompas are in the Wilderness (level 32), but PKers don't care about your combat level — they'll kill you regardless. Level 3 skillers should stick to Red Chins or Herbiboar for the 80–99 stretch.

🦅 The Hunter Mindset

Hunter isn't a skill you can "zone out" on. It demands attention — you're always clicking, always resetting traps, always watching for PKers. And when you finally hit 99, you'll have a massive stack of GP and one of the rarest skill capes in the game.

  • Do the Varrock Museum quiz on every account. It's free, takes 5 minutes, and skips you to level 9. There's no reason not to.

  • Mix up your methods to avoid burnout. Do 2 hours of Red Chins, then a Herbiboar session, then some bird house runs. Variety keeps Hunter from becoming a chore.

  • For the pet hunters: Herbiboar has the Herbi pet (1/6,500). Chinchompas have a pet chance at 99 Hunter (1/94,000 for Black Chins). Bird houses also give the Beaver pet at a 1/70,000 rate. You can multi-task your pet hunt while training.
