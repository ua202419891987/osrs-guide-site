Sailing is a Utility skill — OSRS's 24th skill, released on November 19, 2025 after three years of community-driven design. It gives players command of their own ship, allowing travel across the seas of Gielinor to discover new islands, fight sea monsters, and access exclusive resources.

2. Getting Started: The Pandemonium Quest

Before you can train Sailing, you must complete The Pandemonium — the skill's introductory miniquest. It has no requirements and is designed to teach you the core mechanics.

📋 How to Start

Head to the Port Sarim docks and speak with Will and Anne. They will guide you through the quest, which involves:

  • Learning basic navigation controls
  • Your first short voyage
  • Interacting with your ship's facilities

🎁 Rewards

RewardDetails
Starts you at level 6 SailingYou skip the first 5 levels entirely
Raft (starter ship)Basic vessel with Cargo Hold facility
Captain's LogRecords your voyages, Sea Charting progress, and quests
⛵ Pro Tip: Talk to Everyone at Port Sarim

Before setting sail, talk to the Shipwright (ship upgrades), Notice Board (Port Tasks), and Chartin' Charles (Sea Charting tutor, found in Pandemonium cave area). Understanding all four training methods from the start saves you hours.

3. The Four Training Methods Overview

Jagex designed Sailing with four distinct training methods, each suited to different playstyles and intensity levels:

Shipwreck Salvaging15AFK-friendlyLow-effort training + materials

The most efficient path is to mix all four: use Port Tasks for steady progress, Shipwreck Salvaging when you want to AFK, Sea Charting as soon as you unlock new areas, and Barracuda Trials when you're ready for focused training.

🌬️ Passive Wind Mechanic

Every 60 seconds while sailing, a wind icon appears. Clicking it gives your ship a 12-second speed boost and awards 150-300 bonus XP. This passive mechanic can add up to 10,000-15,000 XP per hour — always watch for the wind icon.

4. Port Tasks (Courier & Bounty)

Port Tasks are your main source of consistent XP. Available immediately after completing The Pandemonium, these are jobs posted on Notice Boards at every major port. You can hold one task at a time initially, but carry more as your Sailing level increases.

📦 Courier Tasks (Cargo Transport)

Transport cargo from one port to another. Your ship needs a Cargo Hold (included with your Raft).

  1. Visit the Notice Board at any port to pick up a courier task
  2. Collect cargo from the Cargo Supplies box at the departure port
  3. Store it in your ship's Cargo Hold
  4. Sail to the destination port and deliver the cargo

Courier tasks reward Sailing XP + GP. Longer routes with heavier cargo give more XP.

🎯 Bounty Tasks (Naval Combat)

These require a Cannon facility on your ship. The Bronze Cannon unlocks at level 28 Sailing.

  1. Equip your ship with a Cannon (hire from Shipwright, then install in a facility slot)
  2. Stock up on cannonballs (crafted through Smithing — bronze through dragon tier)
  3. Accept a Bounty Task from the Notice Board
  4. Sail to the target area and engage sea monsters
  5. Collect the bounty item and return to port

Bounty tasks reward Sailing XP + GP + rare materials like Shark Paint (ship cosmetic).

💡 Estimated XP Rates (Port Tasks)

Based on community reports: 1-30: 15,000-30,000 XP/h (courier tasks). 30-50: 40,000-70,000 XP/h (mix of courier and early bounty tasks). XP rates improve significantly as you unlock better ships and upgrades.

5. Shipwreck Salvaging (AFK Method)

Unlocked at level 15 Sailing, Shipwreck Salvaging is the AFK-friendly training method — ideal for second-monitor play or when you want to train while doing other things.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. Build a Salvaging Hook facility on your ship (requires level 15 Sailing and materials)
  2. Sail to a Shipwreck Salvage zone (marked on your map)
  3. Stop your ship and activate the Salvaging Hook
  4. Items are pulled from the wreck into your Cargo Hold
  5. Return to port and use the Salvaging Station to sort and process your haul

Each salvaging action gives small amounts of Sailing XP. Processing at the Salvaging Station gives bonus XP for each item sorted. You can choose to:

  • Quick salvaging: Grab everything fast but return to port more often
  • Selective salvaging: Only keep high-value items, discard the rest for faster XP
💡 Estimated XP Rates (Salvaging)

Community estimates put Shipwreck Salvaging at 30,000-50,000 XP/h — lower than active methods, but the trade-off is you can do it with minimal attention while watching videos or browsing.

6. Sea Charting (Exploration)

Sea Charting is an exploration-based training method — you discover and map the seas of Gielinor using tools stored on your ship. It provides one-time XP rewards for each charting point discovered, with additional bonus XP for completing entire sea regions.

🔭 Charting Tools (store in your ship's cabin)

🏆 Bonus Rewards

Completing an entire sea region unlocks Rapids — permanent speed boosts for that area when using upgraded Tillers. Some regions also reward consumable buff drinks that can be stored in a Keg facility on your ship for group-wide effects.

Your Chartin' Charles (in the Pandemonium cave) teaches you the basics and tracks your overall charting progress.

7. Barracuda Trials (Fastest XP)

Unlocked at level 30 Sailing, Barracuda Trials are time-limited navigation challenges located near Tempoross Cove. These are the highest XP-per-hour method in Sailing, but they require focus and skill. The learning curve is often compared to the Hallowed Sepulchre — tough at first, but very rewarding once mastered.

🎮 How Trials Work

In each trial, you navigate your ship through a storm zone, delivering supplies and rum to a destination within a time limit. You face hazards, currents, and obstacles. Completing within the time limit is required for XP rewards — failing means you try again.

💡 Estimated XP Rates (Barracuda Trials)

Community consensus: 50-72: 80,000-120,000 XP/h (Gwenith Glide trial). 70-99: 150,000-200,000+ XP/h (requires Mahogany Hull or better, reached at level 65).

8. Ship Upgrades: Hull, Keel, Sails & Facilities

⛵ Priority Order: Hull First, Always

Teak Hull at level 31 is the single most impactful upgrade in early Sailing. It provides roughly 15% speed improvement, which translates to 5,000-10,000 extra XP per hour. Prioritize hull upgrades over everything else until you reach Mahogany Hull territory (level 65).

Unlike some skills, Sailing does not pay for itself. Total upgrade costs are estimated at 20-60 million GP, making it a "semi-buyable" skill — you don't directly buy XP, but you must invest heavily in ship upgrades, crew wages, and repair costs. Plan your GP accordingly.

11. Sailing FAQs

Is Sailing worth training in 2026?

Yes. Sailing unlocks transportation benefits, exclusive resources, new Slayer monsters, new equipment, and hybrid training methods for other skills. Most serious accounts benefit from at least level 70 Sailing for access to high-tier upgrade materials.

How long does 1-99 Sailing take?

Approximately 120-150 hours using optimal methods (Barracuda Trials with proper ship upgrades). Grinding purely with Port Tasks or Shipwreck Salvaging will take significantly longer — 200+ hours.

What's the fastest training method?

Barracuda Trials at Tempoross Cove, unlocked at level 30. Once you have a Mahogany Hull (65) and Upgraded Sails (45), you can reach 150,000-200,000+ XP per hour. The learning curve is steep (like Hallowed Sepulchre), but it's the fastest by a wide margin.

How much GP does 1-99 Sailing cost?

Estimates range from 20-60 million GP depending on how efficiently you source materials. You can reduce costs by gathering wood and ore yourself (via Woodcutting and Mining), but the required quantities are substantial. Ship upgrades, crew wages, and repair costs add up quickly.

What's the best AFK training method?

Shipwreck Salvaging, available from level 15. Build a Salvaging Hook at a Shipyard, sail to a salvage zone, and activate the hook. You check back periodically and return to port to process your haul. It gives 30,000-50,000 XP/h with minimal attention.

Can Ironmen train Sailing?

Yes. All Sailing content is available to Ironmen, but upgrading ships requires gathering materials yourself through Mining, Woodcutting, and other skills. The new Thieving targets (ore stall at 82, cannonball stall at 87) are especially valuable for Ironmen.

What happens if my ship is destroyed?

If your ship sinks at sea, it respawns at your last visited port. You lose any unsecured cargo. Repair costs depend on your ship's tier and damage level. Always carry a Repair Kit for emergency fixes and check your hull HP before long journeys.

Is Sailing required for any quests?

Yes. At minimum, you need to complete The Pandemonium to start training. Troubled Tortugans (main quest) unlocks access to the Great Conch area and requires a moderate Sailing level. A third quest, Red Reef, is planned for a future update.

What's the active wind mechanic?

Every 60 seconds while sailing, a wind icon appears on screen. Clicking it gives your ship a 12-second speed boost and 150-300 bonus Sailing XP. This adds up to 10,000-15,000 passive XP per hour — always keep an eye out for it.

🎯 Your Sailing Training Game Plan

Follow this roadmap for the fastest path to 99:

✅ Levels 1-6 (Pandemonium): Complete the introductory quest at Port Sarim. Starts you at level 6 with a Raft.
✅ Levels 6-30 (Port Tasks): Courier tasks at Port Sarim + nearby ports. Build a Salvaging Hook at 15. Buy a Skiff at 15.
✅ Levels 30-50 (Barracuda Trials + Bounty): Start Barracuda Trials at Tempoross Cove. Build a Bronze Cannon (28) for Bounty tasks. Upgrade to Teak Hull (31) — priority #1.
✅ Levels 50-72 (Gwenith Glide): Buy a Sloop (50). Upgrade sails to Upgraded Sails (45). Run Gwenith Glide trial for 80k-120k XP/h.
✅ Levels 72-99 (High-tier Trials): Mahogany Hull (65) → Master Rigging (80). Run highest available Barracuda Trials for 150k-200k+ XP/h. Complete Sea Charting regions as you explore new areas.

Remember: Click the wind icon every 60 seconds for free XP. Prioritize hull upgrades. Don't ignore Sea Charting — completing regions unlocks permanent speed boosts.
