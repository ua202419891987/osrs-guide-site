How to Use This Ladder

Each tier teaches specific skills that unlock the next tier. Don't skip tiers — the mechanics compound. A player who's killed Zulrah 100 times will learn Vorkath in 10 deaths. A player who's never bossed before will need 50 deaths. Follow the order.

Before You Start

  • ✅ 70+ in all combat stats
  • ✅ 43 Prayer (protection prayers minimum)
  • ✅ Barrows Gloves
  • ✅ Dragon Defender / Dragon Scimitar minimum
  • ✅ Rune Crossbow + Broad Bolts minimum
  • ✅ Iban's Staff minimum

Tier 1: Learning the Basics

Step 1: Barrows

Difficulty★☆☆☆☆
RequirementsPriest in Peril quest, 50+ Magic, 43 Prayer
GP/hr500K-1M (with Morytania Hard diary: 1.5M+)
What You LearnPrayer switching, inventory management, simple gear switches

Barrows is the perfect first boss. All six brothers use protection prayers, and you learn to switch between them. Kill all six brothers for maximum reward potential, use Iban's Blast or Trident of the Seas, and bring prayer potions. The Morytania Hard diary doubles your rune rewards — a massive boost.

Step 2: Sarachnis

Difficulty★★☆☆☆
Requirements65+ Melee, 43 Prayer, crush weapon (Cudgel or Leaf-Bladed Battleaxe)
GP/hr600K-1M

Sarachnis teaches movement mechanics. She spawns minions that web you in place, and you must move to avoid them. She also uses a rapid melee attack that you can pray-flick through. This is your first taste of simultaneous movement + prayer management — a skill you'll need for every boss after this.

Step 3: Crazy Archaeologist / Deranged Archaeologist

These wilderness/demi-bosses teach you to dodge projectiles while maintaining DPS. Crazy Arch (23 Wilderness) drops the Rune Crossbow and Odium/Malediction shards. Deranged Arch (Fossil Island) is safer but lower profit. Both teach the same core skill: move, attack, move, attack — the rhythm of all modern OSRS bossing.

Tier 2: Prayer & Movement

Step 4: Demonic Gorillas

Difficulty★★★☆☆
RequirementsMonkey Madness II, 75+ Melee/Range, Arclight
GP/hr2-3M (zenyte shard at 1/300)
What You LearnGear switching every 50 damage, prayer switching based on attack style, counting attacks

Demonic Gorillas are the best PvM teacher in the mid-game. They switch protection prayers after taking 50 damage, forcing you to swap combat styles. They also switch attack styles (melee/range/mage), forcing prayer switches. They teach you the core rhythm of high-level bossing: attack → count hits → switch gear → switch prayer → repeat. The zenyte shard drop (12M+) makes them very profitable while you learn.

Step 5: Grotesque Guardians

Requires 75 Slayer. Two-phase fight (Dusk/Dawn) that teaches multi-phase bossing. You manage two health bars, dodge falling debris, and handle adds. The granite dust and granite maul drops provide consistent profit. This is your first taste of what raiding feels like — multiple phases, environmental hazards, and target prioritization.

Tier 3: Full Mechanics

Step 6: Zulrah

Difficulty★★★★☆
RequirementsRegicide quest, 75+ Magic/Range, Trident of Swamp, Blowpipe
GP/hr1-2M (scales + uniques)
What You LearnRotation memorization, 8-way gear switches, venom management, positioning

Zulrah is a rite of passage. There are 4 rotations (3 green, 1 turquoise), and you need to know where to stand and what to pray for each phase. Use the Zulrah Helper plugin on RuneLite — it shows you the rotation on-screen. Budget 20-50 deaths before your first kill. After your first kill, the next 49 come much faster. Western Provinces Hard diary gives one free resurrect per day.

Step 7: Vorkath

Difficulty★★★★☆
RequirementsDragon Slayer II, 80+ Range, Elite Void, DHCB or Dragon Crossbow
GP/hr2-4M
What You LearnWoox walking, acid phase movement, spawn management, precise timing

Vorkath is your money printer. Consistent 130-160K per kill with no rare drop dependency. The acid phase teaches precision movement (Woox Walk: attack while walking to dodge fireballs). The spawn phase teaches target prioritization. Master Vorkath and you'll never be poor again.

Tier 4: Solo GWD & Gauntlet

Step 8: Solo God Wars (Bandos/Zamorak)

Solo Bandos teaches sustain mechanics. You need to manage health and prayer across long trips. The 6:0 or 9:0 ranged methods teach kiting — attacking while moving in a pattern. This skill appears in every raid. Start with Zamorak (easier), progress to Bandos.

Step 9: Corrupted Gauntlet

Difficulty★★★★★
RequirementsSong of the Elves, 80+ in all combats, 70+ Prayer (Rigour/Augury highly recommended)
GP/hr4-6M (enhanced seed = 120M+)
What You LearnPrep efficiency, multi-phase bossing, tornado dodging, prayer + movement synergy

Corrupted Gauntlet is the ultimate mid-game test. No gear required, no supplies consumed — pure skill. Master CG and you're ready for anything. Budget 50+ deaths to learn. Start with regular Gauntlet (easier), then move to Corrupted once you can complete regular Gauntlet consistently.

Step 10: Tombs of Amascut Entry Mode

Your first raid. Entry mode ToA introduces you to invocations (difficulty modifiers), multi-boss gauntlets, and the concept of supplies-per-raid management. Start at 0 invocations and add one at a time. The Thread of Elidinis and Lightbearer ring from entry mode are genuine upgrades. This is your graduation from the bossing ladder.

FAQ

How many KC before I move to the next boss?

50 kills minimum for each boss. At 50 KC, you've seen every mechanic multiple times and built muscle memory. Before 50 KC, you're still learning. After 100 KC, you're efficient. Move up at 50 KC.

What if I can't beat a boss?

Go back down one tier and get more practice. Can't beat Zulrah? Go back to Demonic Gorillas for another 50 KC. Can't beat CG? Go back to Zulrah and Vorkath. Every tier builds on the previous one.

Should I use RuneLite plugins?

Yes. Zulrah Helper, Vorkath Helper, and Gauntlet Helper plugins are not cheating — they're standard tools that every serious PvMer uses. You're learning boss mechanics, not memorizing rotation charts.

⭐ The 50-KC Rule

The biggest mistake mid-level players make is quitting a boss after 5-10 deaths. Every difficult boss in OSRS has a learning curve. Your first Zulrah kill might take 30 deaths. Your 50th kill will take 0 supplies. Commit to 50 kills before judging any boss.
