1. How the OSRS Economy Works

OSRS has a fully player-driven economy. Items are priced based on supply and demand — there's no fixed store price for most things. As a new player, you need to understand three key concepts:

  • The Grand Exchange (GE) — The central marketplace in Varrock. You put items up for sale/buy and the game matches you with other players automatically. Almost everything is traded here.
  • GE Price vs Shop Price — Store prices are usually much lower than GE prices. Selling to the GE is almost always better than selling to a shop.
  • Raw materials vs Processed goods — Raw ores, logs, and hides are worth money. Processed versions (bars, planks, leather) are often worth more — or sometimes less. Always check the GE before processing.

2. Your First 100k GP — Day 1 Methods

These methods need zero stats and zero GP to start. They're slow, but they're how every player starts:

🐄 Cowhide Tanning (Best Day 1 Method)

  1. Kill cows east of Lumbridge. Pick up cowhides (100% drop rate).
  2. Take cowhides to the Tanner in Al Kharid (just south of Lumbridge, 1 GP per hide to tan)
  3. Sell Soft Leather or Hard Leather on the Grand Exchange (~200–300 GP each)
  4. Profit: ~150–250 GP per hide, 100+ hides/hour = 15,000–25,000 GP/hr

🪨 Mining Copper/Tin Ore

Mine copper or tin ore at Varrock East/West Mine, sell at GE for ~100–150 GP each. Very low GP/hr but trains Mining simultaneously.

🌳 Cutting Logs in Draynor

Willows at Draynor Village bank (need 30 Woodcutting) sell for ~10–15 GP each. With 1,000 logs per hour, that's 10,000–15,000 GP/hr. Not exciting, but AFK and trains Woodcutting.

💡 Which Method Should You Pick?

0 combat stats: Cowhide tanning (15k–25k/hr) → fastest zero-req method. 10+ combat: Al-Kharid Warriors (20k–35k/hr). 33+ Magic: Wine of Zamorak (100k–200k/hr) — best F2P method if you have Magic. 40+ combat: Hill Giants or Ogress Warriors (50k–100k/hr). The rule is simple: always use the highest GP/hr method you qualify for.

🏆 Best F2P Method Under Level 50: Ogress Warriors

After completing Dragon Slayer I (which also gives you access to Corsair Cove), Ogress Warriors drop Rune items, coins, and gems. Bring food and Rune armor. 40,000–80,000 GP/hr at 50+ combat.

🌿 Herb Runs (Passive Income — #1 Recommended P2P Method)

Plant Ranarr/Toadflax seeds every 80 minutes. Each patch takes ~30 seconds to harvest. With 5+ patches (requires some quests), earn 100,000–300,000 GP/run passively. This is the best money-to-time ratio in the early game.

🌿 Herb Run Step-by-Step

  1. Buy seeds at GE: Ranarr (~15k GP each) if 50+ Farming, or Toadflax (~2k GP) if lower
  2. Get supercompost from GE (buy or make from high-level herbs)
  3. Visit 5 patches: Falador → Catherby → Ardougne → Port Phasmatys → Trollheim
  4. Rake patches, compost, plant seeds — takes ~3 minutes total for all 5 patches
  5. Wait 80 minutes (go train combat or do quests in the meantime)
  6. Harvest: 5 patches × ~10 herbs = 50 herbs per run. Ranarr = 2k GP each = 100k per run

🐦 Birdhouse Runs (Passive Hunter XP + GP)

Set up bird houses on Fossil Island every 50 minutes. Each run takes 2 minutes and earns 5,000–15,000 GP in bird nests + Hunter XP. Run it on a timer while doing other activities. Best combined with herb runs: alternate between herb runs (80 min) and birdhouse runs (50 min) for maximum passive income.

⚒️ Blast Furnace (Active, 300k–600k GP/hr)

Smelt bars at the Blast Furnace minigame (Keldagrim). Steel bars at 60 Smithing = ~600,000 GP/hr. Gold bars at 40 Smithing (with Goldsmith Gauntlets from Family Crest quest) = ~300,000 GP/hr but also massive Smithing XP. How to get there: Take minecart from Ice Mountain (near Falador) or teleport with Dwarven Helmet.

🐟 Fishing Lobsters / Monkfish (AFK)

Fishing Lobsters at Karamja or Monkfish at Piscatoris (Swan Song quest) earns 80,000–150,000 GP/hr while being almost fully AFK. Great while watching YouTube or studying. Tip: Monkfish heal more than Lobsters (16 HP vs 8 HP), making them better for both food and GP.

5. Using the Grand Exchange Smartly

The Grand Exchange (GE) is in northwestern Varrock. It's the beating heart of the OSRS economy. Here's how to use it effectively:

🔍 Always Check Prices Before Selling

Go to prices.runescape.wiki before listing anything. The GE "guide price" shown in-game is often outdated — the wiki shows live trading prices with 5-minute updates.

📊 Instant Buy vs Patient Sell

  • Buying: Offer 5–10% above GE price to buy instantly. Useful for gear upgrades.
  • Selling: List at 1 GP below GE price for fast sales. Or list at market price and wait 10–30 min.

💼 GE Slot Limits

Free players get 3 GE slots. Members get 8 slots. With only 3 slots, prioritize: 1 slot for selling bulk drops, 1 for buying consumables, 1 for buying gear.

📈 Beginner Flip: Cowhide → Hard Leather

Buy Cowhides at GE (~200 GP), tan them at Al Kharid (1 GP each), sell Hard Leather at GE (~250 GP). 50 GP profit per hide, 1,000+ hides/hour = 50,000 GP/hr passive arbitrage. Not exciting but genuinely profitable with zero risk.

6. Money Making Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Dropping bones and hides — Big Bones are worth 250+ GP each. Always bank them or bury for Prayer XP.
  • ❌ Selling to shops — General stores pay a fraction of GE price. Always sell valuable items at the GE.
  • ❌ Processing low-value items — Tanning leather adds value. Cutting gems usually doesn't (uncut sapphires ≈ cut sapphires in price early on). Always verify.
  • ❌ Buying expensive gear too early — Dragon Scimitar costs 50k. Worth buying at 60 Attack, not before. Train to the requirement first.
  • ❌ Ignoring seed drops — Ranarr seeds from Slayer can be worth 30,000–50,000 GP each. Check every seed before dropping.
  • ❌ Skipping quest rewards — Quests like Waterfall are free level 30 Attack/Strength. That's hours of training time saved.
  • ❌ Not using teleports — Walking between bank and training spot wastes 20–30% of your time. Buy teleport tabs — they pay for themselves.
  • ❌ Flipping without research — Only flip items you understand (cowhides, leather, basic ores). Never flip items you haven't tracked for at least a week.
  • ❌ Staying on low-GP methods after unlocking better ones — Every hour at 10k/hr when you could do 50k/hr = 40k lost forever. Switch immediately.

8. Money Making FAQs

What's the fastest way to make 1M GP as a new player?

With membership: herb runs + birdhouse runs (passive), Blast Furnace (active). Without membership: kill Ogress Warriors after Dragon Slayer I, or Wine of Zamorak with 33 Magic. 1M GP takes 2–3 days of casual play as a member, or 5–10 days F2P.

Is merching (flipping) a good way to make GP as a beginner?

Cowhide flipping is simple and profitable (20k–50k/hr). Advanced merching (buy low, sell high on large items) requires GE knowledge and 1M+ GP starting capital. Learn it after you have 1M+ GP to work with. Only flip items you've tracked for a week.

Can I make good GP in F2P? Is membership worth it?

Yes, but it's much slower. 100k–200k/hr is achievable F2P with Ogress Warriors or Wine of Zamorak. Members see 3–10x better rates. Buy a Bond (700k GP) as fast as possible — it's the best investment you'll ever make.

What's the first big purchase I should make?

Rune armor (full set ~100k GP) at level 40 Defence. This is a massive survivability upgrade that lets you train more efficiently. Before that: Rune Scimitar at 40 Attack (20k GP) — this single purchase doubles your kill speed.

How do I know if an item is worth keeping or dropping?

Rule: If you don't know what it is, CHECK THE GE PRICE. Right-click a GE Clerk → "Item Lookup" → search the item. Common "junk" that's valuable: Uncut gems (500–5,000 GP), Grimy herbs (200–2,000 GP), Seeds like Ranarr (~15,000 GP), Big Bones (~200 GP). When in doubt, bank it and check later.

What are "Bonds" and how do I get one?

Bonds give 14 days of membership. Buy from other players for ~700k–900k GP at the GE. This is how F2P players become members without spending real money. Get 700k GP in F2P → buy Bond → become member. Best investment ever.

How much GP should I keep as an "emergency fund"?

Always keep at least 50k–100k GP in your bank for gear repairs, teleport tabs, and emergency food. Don't spend every last coin on gear — having GP flexibility means you can take advantage of good deals on the GE.

Is High Alchemy really worth it for money making?

Absolutely yes. At Magic 55, High Alchemy converts items into GP at 60% of their GE value. Buy Yew Longbows (~4k GP), alch them for ~3,900 GP. Profit: ~300 GP per alch + 65 Magic XP. Best combined with Fletching: buy logs, fletch into bows, alch for profit.

What's the difference between GE "guide price" and actual trading price?

GE guide price (shown in-game) is an average that updates slowly (every few hours). The actual price you'll buy/sell at is usually within 1–5% of guide price. Always check prices.runescape.wiki for live prices (updated every 5 minutes) before listing anything valuable.

Should I bank everything or only valuable drops?

Bank everything worth 100+ GP. That includes: all hides, all bones (Big Bones especially), all gems (uncut), all herbs, all seeds. Drop only: items with 0 GP value (ashes, burnt food, regular bones if you don't want Prayer XP). Pro tip: With RuneLite, use the "Item Value" plugin to see GE prices on every drop.

How do I start a herb run? What quests do I need?

Minimum: 9 Farming + completion of Fairytale I — Growing Pains (unlocks Fairy Rings for fast travel between patches). Ideal: Complete My Arm's Big Adventure (unlocks Trollheim patch, which never gets diseased). Buy Ranarr seeds at GE, buy supercompost, plant at 5 patches. Takes ~3 minutes per run. Returns in 80 minutes.

Is killing Green Dragons in the Wilderness worth the risk?

Green Dragons drop Dragon Bones (~1,500 GP) and Green Dragonhide (~800 GP) = ~2,300 GP per kill. At 40–50 kills per hour = 100k–200k GP/hr. However: PKers (player killers) actively hunt dragon killers. Only do this with 3-iteming: Wear only 3 items (weapon + shield + amulet), protect the rest. If you die, you lose nothing.

What's the best way to make my FIRST Bond (700k GP)?

F2P route (recommended): Cowhides → Hill Giants (40+ combat) → Ogress Warriors (after Dragon Slayer I). Accumulate 700k GP over 3–5 days of casual play. Key: Don't spend GP on gear — wear the cheapest armor that works. Every GP goes toward the Bond. Once you have membership, your income rate jumps to 300k–600k/hr with Blast Furnace.

Final Tips — Money Making for Beginners

  • Don't ignore F2P money methods. Cowhides, runes from the Dark Wizards, and Essence mining are your bread and butter before membership. They're slow but guaranteed income.
  • Set a weekly GP goal. Even 50K GP/week adds up fast. Track your bank value on the OSRS Wiki's high scores to stay motivated.
  • Always check GE prices before selling. Sell in bulk, not one-by-one. The GE tax is lower on large trades and you get better offers.
  • Avoid scam methods. "Double your money" lures and trust trades will clean you out. If a money method sounds too good to be true, it is.
  • Reinvest your first 500K into a Membership Bond. This unlocks P2P, where money-making rates are 5–10× better. It's the single best investment a new player can make.
  • Always have a 50k–100k GP emergency fund. Don't spend every last coin on gear. Flexibility means you can grab good GE deals and teleport when needed.
  • Switch methods immediately when you unlock a better one. Every hour at 10k/hr when you could do 50k/hr = 40k GP lost forever.
