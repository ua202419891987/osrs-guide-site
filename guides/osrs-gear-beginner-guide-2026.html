OSRS Gear Guide for Beginners 2026

New players often waste millions on gear that barely outperforms cheaper alternatives. This guide shows you exactly which gear upgrades actually matter — and which ones to skip — at every level from 1 to 70+.

1. Gear Basics — Slots, Stats, and Set Bonuses

Your character has 11 equipment slots: Head, Cape, Neck, Weapon, Body, Shield, Legs, Gloves, Boots, Ring, and Ammo. Each item in a slot provides stat bonuses.

📊 Key Combat Stats to Understand

StatWhat It Does
Attack Bonus (Stab/Slash/Crush)Increases chance to hit with Melee
Defence BonusReduces chance of being hit
Strength BonusIncreases max damage with Melee
Ranged Attack / Ranged StrengthHit chance and max damage for Ranged
Magic Attack / Magic Damage %Hit chance and max hit for Magic
Prayer BonusSlows prayer point drain — very valuable
💡 Strength Bonus > Defence for New Players

At low levels, increasing your Strength Bonus (kills things faster) is more efficient than stacking Defence (reduces hits slightly). Kill speed = less food consumed = less GP spent.

Complete Animal Magnetism quest at 18 Ranged / 19 Ranged. Ava's Attractor (at 30 Ranged) and Ava's Accumulator (at 50 Ranged) auto-retrieve your spent ammo. Saves thousands of GP in arrow costs over time.

💡 Wearing Melee Armor for Magic = Big Penalty

Wearing full Rune armor while casting spells gives a massive negative Magic attack penalty. For Magic combat, always wear Magic-appropriate robes. This surprises many beginners.

5. Prayer & Utility Gear

Prayer gear is often overlooked by beginners, but it makes a huge difference in how long you can sustain combat. A few Prayer bonus points can double or triple your Prayer duration.

Prayer Bonus Items

ItemPrayer BonusHow to GetCost
Monk Robes (set)+8 totalFree from Edgeville Monastery (talk to Brother Celerity)Free
Holy Symbol+8Craft (Crafting lvl 16) or buy at GE~1,500 GP
Proselyte Armor (chest/legs)+8 / +6Complete Slug Menace quest (30 Prayer, 30 Crafting)Free
God Vestments (full set)+10 to +15God Wars Dungeon or buy from other playersVaries
Prayer Experience Cape+499 Prayer (buy from Skill Master)99k GP
💡 Prayer Bonus Stacking

You can wear Monk Robes + Holy Symbol simultaneously for +16 Prayer bonus total. This makes your Prayer points last roughly 2.5x longer than wearing no Prayer gear. Essential for bossing and long Slayer tasks.

Essential Utility Items

These items don't directly increase your combat stats, but they save you time and GP in the long run:

  • Ring of Wealth — Passively increases rare drop table chance. Buy at GE (~8,000 GP). Wear it whenever you're killing monsters that have drops on the rare table (most bosses and high-level monsters).
  • Ring of Recoil — Reflects 10% of damage back to attacker (up to 40 damage per ring). Cheap (~300 GP) and useful for low-level combat. When it "breaks" after 40 damage reflected, equip a new one.
  • Amulet of Strength (+10 Strength) → Amulet of Fury (+10 all bonuses, +8 Prayer) — Neck slot matters more than most players realize. Amulet of Glory is a good mid-game option (+10 to all bonuses, +2 Prayer, ~10,000 GP).
  • Explorer's Ring 2/3/4 — From Lumbridge & Draynor Diary. Provides free runes (Air, Water, Earth, Fire) daily. Explorer's Ring 4 gives 26 free runes of each type per day.
  • Burner / Incense Sticks — Give +2-6 Prayer bonus when lit. Requires 10-60 Prayer to use. Can be combined with Prayer armor for even longer prayer duration.

⛪ How to Get Proselyte Armor — Step by Step

Proselyte is the best Prayer armor for mid-game players. Here's exactly how to get it:

  1. Complete The Slug Menace quest (requires 30 Prayer, 30 Crafting, 30 Magic, and 5 Fishing). Start it by talking to Brother Maledict at the Paterdomus Temple.
  2. After the quest, buy Proselyte pieces from Sir Tiffy Cashien in Falador Park for ~30,000-40,000 GP per piece.
  3. Wear the full set (helm, chest, legs, boots, gloves if you have them) for +25 Prayer bonus total when combined with Holy Symbol and Ring of Recoil.

Knowing where to get your gear saves you millions of GP over time. Many beginners waste GP buying gear that they could farm for free or earn through quests.

💡 Stronghold of Security — Do It Early

The Stronghold gives you 10,000 GP (enough to buy your first few weapon upgrades) and the Fancy Boots (+3 HP). Each floor has monsters ~level 20-60. Bring food and a teleport rune just in case. The whole thing takes ~45 minutes.

💰 Buy at Grand Exchange (Recommended for Most Gear)

Most weapons and armor should be bought at the GE rather than crafted or ground for. Time spent crafting gear is almost always better spent on actual combat training. Exception: Crafting your own Dragonhide armor saves significant GP.

When to buy vs when to farm:

  • Buy at GE: Weapons (Scimitars, Bows, Staves), Amulets, Rings, Boots, Gloves
  • Farm for it: Rune Armor (drops from Hill Giants, etc.), Barrows (requires 70+ Defence), Dragon armor (rare drops)
  • Quest reward: Proselyte Armor, Dragon Scimitar (Monkey Madness I), Dragon Defender (Warriors' Guild)

⚔️ Drops from Monsters

Rune equipment drops from monsters like: Hill Giants (Rune Javelin tips), Black Knights (Rune Full Helm), and later Barrows brothers (Barrows armor pieces). Don't buy Rune items — kill for them or wait to buy only what you can't get as drops.

🗺️ Best Places to Farm Gear Drops (F2P)

  • Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon) — Drop Rune equipment, Big Bones. Requires ~30 Combat. Bring a Dwarf Multi-cannon for faster kills.
  • Black Knights (Black Knight Castle) — Drop Rune Full Helm, Rune Platelegs. Level 33, aggressive. Good for Strength training.
  • Moss Giants (South of Observatory) — Drop Rune 2H Sword, Rune Battleaxe. Level 42, need 50+ Combat to fight safely.

🗺️ Best Places to Farm Gear Drops (P2P)

  • Slayer Monsters — As you level Slayer, many monsters drop useful gear. Gargoyles (75 Slayer) drop Granite Maul; Abbysal Demons (85 Slayer) drop Abyssal Whip.
  • Barrows Brothers (70+ Combat) — Each brother drops their unique Barrows armor piece. Do Barrows when you have 70+ Defence for the best mid-game armor.
  • God Wars Dungeon (70+ Stats) — End-game gear. Don't attempt until you have ~80 in all combat stats and high-end gear yourself.

7. Common Gear Mistakes to Avoid

  • ❌ Buying Dragon gear before level 60 — Dragon Scimitar requires Monkey Madness I (long quest). Get Rune Scimitar at 40 instead, save 50k GP.
  • ❌ Using melee armor for Magic training — Heavy armor gives negative Magic attack. Always switch to robes when casting offensive spells.
  • ❌ Ignoring the Cape slot — Ava's Device goes in the Cape slot. After Mage Training Arena, Skillcapes have good stats too. Never leave it empty at high level.
  • ❌ Upgrading too fast — Barrows armor at level 40 Defence is overkill. Stay in Rune until 70 Defence if you don't have the GP to repair Barrows.
  • ❌ Forgetting Gloves and Boots — Barrows Gloves (Recipe for Disaster, endgame) are best-in-slot. But even Leather Gloves beat nothing. Always fill every slot.
  • ❌ Using wrong ammo type — Crossbows use Bolts, Bows use Arrows. Bronze arrows work in a Maple Shortbow. Match your ammo to your weapon.

8. Gear FAQs

When should I upgrade my armor?

Upgrade when you hit the next tier's Defence requirement: 20 for Mithril, 40 for Rune, 70 for Barrows. Don't spend GP on armor upgrades between these milestones — the difference is minimal.

Is Bandos/Armadyl worth it for beginners?

Not at all. Bandos (70 Defence) and Armadyl (70 Ranged + Defence) cost millions and require end-game bossing to obtain. Barrows is 20–30x cheaper and nearly as good for most content.

Should I repair Barrows armor?

Yes, but it's cheap. Repair at Bob in Lumbridge (NPC) or use an Armour Stand in your Player-Owned House. A full set in disrepair costs ~300,000 GP to repair — well worth the benefits.

What's the best weapon for training Strength?

Set your combat style to Aggressive (trains Strength). Use Scimitar for fast attack speed + decent Strength bonus. At 70 Attack, the Abyssal Whip is best — but it only trains Defence or Attack, not Strength. Use a Berserker necklace + Obsidian maul setup for Strength.

What goes in the cape slot for beginners?

Ava's Accumulator (from Animal Magnetism quest) is the best cape for Ranged — it auto-collects arrows. Fire Cape (from TzHaar Fight Cave) is the best melee cape (requires 70+ combat). God Cape (from Horror from the Deep quest) is the best Magic cape. Don't leave the cape slot empty — even a cheap cape adds Defence bonuses.

Should I buy cosmetics over stats?

Never. In OSRS, cosmetics (party hats, Santa hats, etc.) cost millions and provide zero stats. Every GP should go into combat efficiency until you have 10M+ GP in the bank. Cosmetics are for end-game flexing, not for beginners. Focus on stats, not looks.

What's the difference between "Rune armor" and "Rune equipment"?

Rune armor includes: Rune Platebody, Rune Platelegs, Rune Full Helm, Rune Kiteshield. This is the best F2P armor set. Rune equipment can also include: Rune Scimitar (weapon), Rune Boots (not the same as Dragon Boots), Rune Defender. Buy the full set (Platebody + Legs + Helm + Shield + Scimitar) for ~200k GP total.

How do I know if my gear is good enough for a boss?

Rule of thumb: If your combat level matches the boss's recommended level, Rune armor + Dragon Scimitar is usually sufficient for beginner bosses. For harder bosses (Barrows brothers), use Prayer potions + Protect-from-Melee to supplement your Defence. Gear matters less than Prayer usage in most early boss encounters.

What's Barrows armor and why is everyone talking about it?

Barrows armor is a set of 6 armor pieces dropped by the Barrows Brothers (a boss activity). Each piece costs 100k–300k GP and has special effects (e.g., Dharok's hits harder at low HP, Verac's ignores Defence). Barrows is the best mid-level armor — cheaper than Bandos and better than Rune. You can start doing Barrows at 43 Prayer + 50+ combat.

Should I use a shield or dual wield?

For most training, use a shield + weapon (defensive style). The shield adds Defence bonuses and you train Defence simultaneously. Dual wield (two weapons) gives slightly more DPS but only at high levels. Dragon Defender (off-hand slot, from Warrior's Guild) is the best off-hand item for melee — it gives +25% accuracy and +24% damage bonus.

How much should I spend on gear at each combat level?

Level 1–20: ~5k GP (iron/steel). Level 20–40: ~15k GP (mithril/adamant). Level 40–60: ~100k GP (rune set). Level 60–70: ~200k GP (dragon weapon + rune armor). Level 70–99: ~3–5M GP (barrows + dragon defender). Don't overspend early — save GP for food and supplies.

Should I use a shield or a 2-handed weapon?

Use a shield (or Dragon Defender) unless you have a specific reason not to. The Defence bonus from a shield or the +6 Strength bonus from a Dragon Defender makes a huge difference. Only use 2-handed weapons if the DPS gain outweighs the lost defence — which is rare for beginners.

What's the difference between Attack bonus and Strength bonus?

Attack bonus increases your accuracy (chance to hit), while Strength bonus increases your max hit (damage dealt). For training, Strength bonus is generally more important — hitting harder means fewer hits needed, which means less food consumed. Stack Strength first, then Attack.

Why can't I equip this item even though my level is high enough?

Check that you've completed the required quest. Many mid-level items require quests: Dragon Scimitar needs Monkey Madness I, Dragon Defender needs Warriors' Guild completion, and some Barrows pieces need specific combat levels. Also verify you're equipping it in the correct slot.
