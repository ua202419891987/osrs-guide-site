OSRS Sailing Phase 2 Prep Guide 2026: What's Confirmed & How to Prepare

Jagex has laid out a full 2026 roadmap for Sailing content updates. From the Boat Combat rework (already live) to The Red Reef quest, player-designed islands, and a massive new sea area — this guide covers every confirmed update and tells you how to prepare your account. Only official Jagex announcements are included.

Get Sailing to at least 70+ now. Most Phase 2 content (Red Reef quest, player island, new sea area) will likely require 50-80 Sailing. The Boat Combat rework is already live. Prioritize hull upgrades (Teak at 31, Mahogany at 65) and unlock the Sloop (50). If you're above 70 Sailing, you're well-positioned for everything coming in H2 2026.

⚓ Boat Combat Rework (Spring — Already Live)

Released in March 2026, the Boat Combat rework was the first major Sailing update of the year. It transformed ship-to-ship combat from a minigame-like system into a proper combat experience.

① What Changed

ChangeDetails
Traditional combat stats applyYour equipment and combat level now directly affect combat effectiveness on ships
Ranged gear affects cannonsRanged attack and Ranged Strength stats now correctly apply to ship cannons
New weapon hotpointsSloops received additional weapon mounting points for different weapon types
Enemy NPC shipsPatrolling enemy ships now appear at sea — attackable for loot
Dragon Cannon BarrelNew acquisition method added for this high-tier upgrade
New Port TasksProtect NPC ships from enemy attacks for rewards

② How This Affects You

If you haven't tried Boat Combat since March, return to it. The rework made naval combat viable for both training and profit. Your existing Ranged gear now powers your cannons, so gearing up is straightforward. The new Port Tasks offer a fresh way to earn Sailing XP and rewards.

💡 Dragon Cannon Barrel Update

With the new acquisition method added in the Boat Combat rework, obtaining a Dragon Cannon Barrel is now more accessible. Check the OSRS Wiki for current drop sources and methods.

📜 The Red Reef Quest (Summer 2026)

The Red Reef is the second quest in the Tortugan quest line, following the introductory Tortugan content from the Sailing launch. It is expected to release in Summer 2026.

① What's Confirmed

  • Part of the Tortugan quest line (Chapter 2)
  • Expands the lore of Gielinor's oceans
  • Expected to introduce new Sailing training methods
  • New quest rewards (specifics TBD by Jagex)

② Preparation Tips

Based on the first Tortugan quest requirements, expect The Red Reef to require:

  • Sailing level: Likely 50-70 (prepare accordingly)
  • Ship upgrades: At least a Skiff with Teak Hull (31) recommended
  • Combat stats: Moderate combat levels for potential naval combat encounters

🏝️ Player-Designed Island (Summer 2026)

Jagex is creating a new island based on community player designs. The community will have input on the island's design, content, and features through official polls and feedback channels.

① What We Know So Far

  • The island is being designed with community input
  • Expected to contain new skill resources, monsters, or a minigame
  • Will function as a unique hub for Sailing-related activities
  • Accessible via Sailing (ship travel required)

② How to Stay Informed

Follow the official OSRS Discord and Jagex news posts for polling opportunities. Community members can submit design ideas when Jagex opens the floor.

🚢 NPC Ship Combat Expansion (Summer 2026)

Building on the spring Boat Combat rework, Summer 2026 will introduce more NPC ships with unique encounters and dedicated drop tables.

① What's Coming

  • More NPC ships with unique encounter mechanics
  • Dedicated loot tables for each ship type
  • Naval combat becomes a viable money-making method
  • Additional Sailing training opportunities through combat

② Preparation

  • Invest in cannon upgrades (Bronze → Dragon tier)
  • Build a Sloop (level 50) for additional weapon hotpoints
  • Stockpile cannonballs — prices may rise when the update drops
  • Train Ranged — it now powers cannon damage
💡 Stock Up Before the Update

Cannonball prices historically rise before major Sailing combat updates. Consider buying steel bars or cannonballs in advance if you plan to engage with NPC ship combat. Also consider the Perildance bitter drink (from Sea Charting) for damage boosts.

🏁 Barracuda Trial & Quest Sequel (Fall 2026)

Fall 2026 brings two confirmed additions: a new Barracuda Trial challenge and a sequel to an existing quest line that incorporates Sailing mechanics.

① New Barracuda Trial

A new trial challenge will be added, described as a timed race or combat challenge in open seas. This will test players' Sailing skills and offer new rewards. The existing trials (Tempor Tantrum, Jubbly Jive, Gwenith Glide) remain active.

② Quest Sequel with Sailing Mechanics

Another existing storyline will receive a sequel that incorporates Sailing gameplay. The specific storyline has not been named by Jagex yet. This demonstrates Jagex's commitment to integrating Sailing with existing game content.

🌊 New Sea Area (Winter 2026 & Beyond)

The biggest Sailing expansion of 2026 arrives in Winter. Jagex has confirmed a major new sea area with substantial content.

① What to Expect

  • Entirely new sea region with unique biomes and hazards
  • New monsters, resources, and islands
  • Likely the highest-level Sailing content to date
  • Potential new bosses and activities

② Beyond 2026

Jagex has also teased a larger sea expansion beyond 2026, indicating that Sailing will continue to receive major content updates for the foreseeable future.

⛵ The Long View

Sailing is not a "train once and forget" skill. Jagex has committed to ongoing content updates, treating it as a foundational expansion to OSRS rather than a one-time skill release. Training it now pays dividends for years to come.

❓ Phase 2 FAQs

When is the Red Reef quest coming?

Jagex announced it for Summer 2026. As of June 2026, the exact release date has not been specified, but it's expected within the next few months.

What Sailing level do I need for Phase 2 content?

Based on announced content, aim for level 70+. The Red Reef quest, player island, and new sea area will likely require 50-80 Sailing. Higher levels give you day-one access to everything.

Is the Boat Combat rework worth coming back for?

Yes. The rework (live since March 2026) made naval combat much more engaging. Ranged gear now directly powers cannons, new NPC ships offer loot, and new Port Tasks provide additional training options. It's a significant improvement over the launch version.

What's the player-designed island about?

Jagex is creating an island based on community design input. Players will vote on its content, theme, and features. It's expected to include new skill resources, monsters, or a minigame.

Should I stockpile cannonballs?

Cannonball prices historically rise before combat-related updates. Stockpiling might be profitable, but only invest what you can afford to hold. The new NPC ship encounters coming in Summer 2026 will likely increase demand.

Will there be new Sailing bosses?

Not explicitly confirmed, but the Summer NPC ship expansion and Winter sea area are expected to introduce challenging encounters. The Barracuda Trial coming in Fall may also include combat elements.

Do I need Construction for Sailing content?

Yes. Construction is used to build ship components and facilities. Level 70+ Construction is recommended for Phase 2 readiness. The highest-tier upgrades require up to 87 Construction.

What about the new sea area in Winter?

The Winter 2026 sea expansion is described as the biggest Sailing update of the year. It will add a complete new sea region with new content, monsters, resources, and islands. Expect high-level requirements (likely 80+ Sailing).
