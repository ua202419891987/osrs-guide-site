3. Ship Combat Rework

Jagex has announced a ship combat system rework scheduled for mid-2026. This is one of the most impactful Sailing updates because it directly affects how you engage with sea monsters and other players.

⚔️ What's Changing

Jagex has confirmed the following goals for the rework:

  • Improved combat mechanics — more engaging ship-to-ship combat
  • Better balance — making different cannon tiers and ship builds more meaningful
  • New strategic options — more choices in how you approach naval combat

Exact details of the new combat system have not been published yet. Jagex has stated they will release a developer blog with specifics closer to the update date. This guide will be updated once that information is available.

7. Phase 2 FAQs

When is Sailing Phase 2 coming out?

Jagex has announced Wyrmscraig Island for Summer 2026, the ship combat rework for mid-2026, and other content throughout the year. No specific dates have been given. We update this guide as soon as Jagex announces release dates.

What Sailing level do I need for Phase 2 content?

Jagex hasn't specified exact level requirements, but based on the content type (new island, sea bosses, Slayer master), it's reasonable to expect requirements in the 50-80 Sailing range. Aim for level 60+ to be safe.

Will I need a new ship for Phase 2?

Not necessarily a new ship, but you will likely need upgraded components. A Mahogany Hull (65) with Upgraded Sails (45) should be sufficient for most Phase 2 content. Higher-tier ships may be recommended for sea bosses.

Is Wyrmscraig Island free or members-only?

Sailing itself is members-only, so all Sailing content — including Wyrmscraig Island — requires an active membership. There is no free-to-play Sailing content.

Will Phase 2 add new Sailing training methods?

Jagex has not confirmed new training methods specifically, but new islands, quests, and sea bosses will naturally provide additional XP sources. The current four training methods (Port Tasks, Salvaging, Charting, Trials) remain the core progression path.

Where can I follow Phase 2 development?

Follow the official OSRS News page and the OSRS Wiki Sailing page for the latest announcements. Jagex typically publishes developer blogs and Q&As before major updates.

Follow this checklist and you'll be ready for everything 2026 has in store:

✅ Step 1: Train Sailing to 60+. Focus on Teak Hull (31) and Upgraded Sails (45).

✅ Step 3: Install a Cannon (28+) and stockpile cannonballs.
