1. Cargo Routes — Courier Task Profits

The most straightforward way to earn GP with Sailing is completing Courier Tasks. These cargo transport contracts are available from Notice Boards at every port — no combat required.

📦 How Courier Tasks Work

  1. Visit a port's Notice Board and accept a courier task
  2. Collect cargo from the Cargo Supplies box at the departure port
  3. Store it in your ship's Cargo Hold (included with your Raft)
  4. Sail to the destination port and deliver the cargo

💰 Profit Factors

Courier task earnings depend on three factors:

FactorImpact on Profit
Route distanceLonger routes pay more GP per task
Cargo capacityLarger Cargo Hold = more cargo per trip = higher payout
Ship speedFaster hull and sails = more trips per hour
Port reputationHigher reputation unlocks premium contracts

⛵ Upgrade Your Cargo Hold Early

The single biggest profit upgrade for courier tasks is a larger Cargo Hold. A Skiff (level 15) with an upgraded hull can carry more cargo per trip, effectively doubling your GP per hour without sailing any faster.

2. Bounty Tasks — Naval Combat Rewards

For players who prefer combat, Bounty Tasks offer better rewards than courier work. These tasks require a Cannon facility on your ship and involve hunting sea monsters.

🎯 Requirements

RequirementDetails
Bronze CannonCrafted or purchased — requires level 28 Sailing
CannonballsSmelted through Smithing (bronze → dragon tier)
Repair KitEssential for emergency hull repairs at sea

💰 Bounty Rewards

  • Sailing XP — more XP than courier tasks per hour
  • GP — higher base payout than courier work
  • Shark Paint — rare ship cosmetic (untradeable, grey finish, single-use)
  • Rare materials — some bounties drop materials used in ship upgrades

3. Shipwreck Salvaging — Materials & Loot

Unlocked at level 15 Sailing, Shipwreck Salvaging is both an AFK training method and a source of shipbuilding materials. While the GP per hour is lower than active methods, the materials you gather can save you millions on ship upgrades.

⚙️ How It Works

  1. Build a Salvaging Hook facility at any Shipyard (requires level 15)
  2. Sail to a Shipwreck Salvage zone (marked on your nautical map)
  3. Stop your ship and activate the Salvaging Hook
  4. Items go straight into your Cargo Hold
  5. Return to port and use the Salvaging Station to sort your haul

💰 What You Can Find

Item TypeExamplesValue
Common woodOak planks, nails, ropeLow — use for upgrades
Ship componentsPartial hull parts, sail clothMedium — saves upgrade costs
Rare findsUncut gems, clue scrolls, antique chartsHigh — sell on GE

Pro tip: Process everything at the Salvaging Station before storing — sorted materials stack more efficiently and some give bonus XP.

4. Deep Sea Trawling — Fishing Profits

Deep Sea Trawling is a Sailing + Fishing hybrid method that requires a Trawling Net facility on your ship. It's unlocked at higher Sailing levels and offers access to exclusive fish not found anywhere else in Gielinor.

🐟 New Fish from Trawling

FishHealsRequirementsBest Use
Marlin24 HPHigh Fishing + SailingHighest healing food in OSRS
Swordtip SquidVariesLantern Fishing with HarpoonCut into bait + Herblore ingredients
Jumbo SquidVariesLantern Fishing with HarpoonStrong potion base

Marlin is especially notable — at 24 HP per bite, it surpasses Anglerfish (22 HP) as the best healing food in the game. This gives it strong Grand Exchange demand.

🧾 What Are Dragon Sheets?

Dragon Sheets are a new material dropped by Lava Strykewyrms and Frost Dragons. They are used to craft Dragon-tier keels and Dragon cannonballs — the highest tier of ship components. Demand is high because every endgame Sailing player needs them for their final ship upgrades.

7. Farming & Hunter — Passive Income

Sailing adds new passive income sources through Farming and Hunter that you can maintain alongside your voyages.

🌿 Coral Nurseries (Farming)

Sailing introduces Coral Nurseries — underwater farming patches where you grow coral that can be harvested for Herblore ingredients. Three coral types are available:

  • Elkhorn Coral — basic tier, low requirement
  • Pillar Coral — medium tier, moderate requirement
  • Umbral Coral — high tier, best Herblore ingredients

🌾 New Crops (Farming)

Three new crops can be grown in Hop Patches:

CropUsed ForProfit Potential
FlaxLinen rolls (sails)Steady demand for shipbuilding
HempCanvas (sails)Higher tier, better profit
CottonCotton sails (best tier)Best profit per patch

🦀 Crab Hunting (Hunter)

A new Hunter activity where you set traps with Fish Offcut bait. Once baited, crabs enter traps automatically — the success rate is consistent regardless of your Hunter level, making it a reliable source of crab meat and shells.

🌳 New Hardwood Trees

Rosewood, Ironwood, and Camphor trees can be planted in Hardwood Patches (like the ones on Fossil Island). The bank chest near the Fossil Island hardwood patch makes it a prime location for farming these valuable trees.

9. Sailing Money Making FAQs

Can you make GP while training Sailing?

Yes. Courier tasks and Bounty tasks both pay GP alongside XP. Shipwreck Salvaging gives materials that reduce upgrade costs. The most profitable training method for GP is Bounty Tasks (combat), while the best XP method is Barracuda Trials (which doesn't pay well).

What's the best money maker unlocked by Sailing?

For most players, Dragon Sheet farming from Lava Strykewyrms or Frost Dragons likely offers the highest GP per hour. These materials are in high demand for endgame ship upgrades. However, you need 60-87 Sailing and appropriate Slayer levels to access them.

Is Sailing profitable overall?

Sailing is a net cost skill. Ship upgrades, crew wages, and repairs cost an estimated 20-60 million GP to reach 99. However, courier and bounty tasks offset some costs along the way. Think of it like Construction — you spend GP to train, but you unlock profitable content as you level up.

What's the best money maker for low-level Sailing?

Courier tasks between Port Sarim and Draynor Village. They require no combat, no special facilities, and give steady GP and XP. Save the GP you earn for your first hull upgrade (Teak Hull at level 31).

Can Ironmen profit from Sailing?

Yes, and in some ways Sailing is better for Ironmen. The new Thieving stalls (82/87) give access to cannonballs and ship materials. Crab Hunting provides reliable food. Coral Nurseries supply Herblore ingredients. And the new hardwood trees can be planted at Fossil Island for construction materials.

Are Dragon Sheets tradeable?

Yes, Dragon Sheets are tradeable on the Grand Exchange. This means you can farm them for profit even if you don't need them for your own ship upgrades. They are dropped by Lava Strykewyrms (60 Sailing, 62 Slayer) and Frost Dragons (87 Sailing, 1 Slayer).

What's Deep Sea Trawling GP potential?

Deep Sea Trawling produces Marlin (24 HP food), which is the best healing food in OSRS. The demand from high-level PvMers is strong. While exact GP data depends on market prices, Marlin food holds premium value due to its superior healing.

💰 Your Sailing Profit Game Plan

Follow this roadmap to maximize GP as you train:

✅ Levels 6-30: Courier tasks only. Build a Salvaging Hook at 15 for free materials. Save GP for Teak Hull (31).
✅ Levels 30-60: Mix courier and bounty tasks. Install a Bronze Cannon (28). Farm Karamja ↔ Ardougne route.
✅ Levels 60-87: Upgrade to Mahogany Hull (65). Start Lava Strykewyrm farming (60). Deep Sea Trawling for Marlin.
✅ Levels 87-99: Frost Dragon farming at Grimstone Island. Dragon Sheets pay for your final ship upgrades.

Remember: Sailing costs money to train, but the resources and content you unlock are worth the investment. Farm Dragon Sheets to offset upgrade costs.
