OSRS 0 to 100K GP Challenge 2026 — Absolute Beginner Money Making

Learn how to go from zero gold to 100,000 GP in Old School RuneScape with no skills, no quests, and no prior experience. Step-by-step money making for brand new players.

💰 Starting from Zero — Right After Tutorial Island

You have just stepped off Tutorial Island. You have a bronze sword, a wooden shield, a few pieces of bread, and exactly 25 coins in your pocket. Your bank is empty. Your skills are all at level 1. And you want to make 100,000 GP. This guide is built specifically for this exact moment. We are going to take you from zero to 100K using only methods available to free-to-play players with zero prior progress. Every method here has been tested in 2026 and works with current OSRS Grand Exchange prices.

① Your Starting Items — What to Keep & What to Drop

After Tutorial Island, you will have: Bronze sword, Bronze axe, Bronze pickaxe, Wooden shield, Quest cape (for looks only), 25 coins, and a few pieces of bread. Here is what you should do immediately:

  • Keep the bronze axe: You will need it for woodcutting, one of your early money makers.
  • Keep the bronze pickaxe: Mining will be useful later for gathering iron ore.
  • Keep the bread: Free food for your first few combat trips.
  • Sell everything else? No — the bronze sword and shield are junk but keep them for starting combat.

The most important item you have is your Home Teleport. Use it strategically to save time. You also have access to minigame teleports (every 20 minutes). Use the Clan Wars and Castle Wars teleports for quick banking and HP restoration.

② First 5 Minutes — Your First Moneymaker

Here is exactly what you do in your first five minutes as a new player:

  • Step 1: Walk from Lumbridge to the cow field, located just north of Lumbridge Castle, east of the chicken coop.
  • Step 2: Kill cows. Lots of cows. They have 8 HP, hit 1 damage max, and your bread will keep you alive.
  • Step 3: Pick up all cowhides. These are the most valuable drop. Also pick up bones if you want prayer XP, but hides are the priority.
  • Step 4: Bank at the Lumbridge bank. Use Home Teleport to get there fast. Replenish food at the cow field (there is a respawning bread spawn in the Lumbridge kitchen).
  • Step 5: Repeat until you have a full inventory of cowhides. At ~200 GP each in 2026 prices, a full inventory of 28 cowhides is worth over 5,000 GP.

In 30 minutes of cow killing, you can easily make 10,000–15,000 GP. That is your entire starting bank multiplied by 500. Do not skip this step.

③ Avoid These Noob Traps — Don't Waste Your First GP

New players frequently waste their first gold on useless items. Here is what NOT to buy:

  • Don't buy a "full set" of bronze or iron armor: It costs 5–10K and provides almost no defense bonus over beginner gear. You will replace it within hours.
  • Don't buy a "rare" item from another player: Scammers love fresh players. If a trade seems too good to be true, it is a scam.
  • Don't buy membership with your first 100K: Bonds cost ~8M GP. You are nowhere near that. Focus on building your F2P bank first.
  • Don't gamble: Flower poker, dice, and other gambling games are rigged against you. You will lose everything.
  • Don't buy expensive tools: A bronze pickaxe and axe are free. You do not need rune tools at level 1.

Your first 100K is for necessities only: food, arrows, runes, and basic armor upgrades. Do not waste it on cosmetics or "starter packs" from other players.

🐄 Level 3 Money Making — No Skills Required

You can start making money at combat level 3 with literally no skills trained. These methods work with zero investment and are available to every new player immediately after Tutorial Island. The best part? You do not even need to level up to profit.

① Cowhides — The King of Early Game Money

We touched on this in the previous section, but cowhides deserve their own deep dive because they are the absolute best moneymaker for level 3 players. A cowhide sells for approximately 180–220 GP on the GE in 2026. Cows have only 8 HP and drop hides 100% of the time. With 28 inventory slots, that is 5,000–6,000 GP per trip. You can fill an inventory in 3–4 minutes. At 10+ trips per hour, you earn 50K–70K GP per hour at level 3 with zero requirements. That is insane for a brand new account. The best cow spots are:

  • Lumbridge Cow Field: Directly north of Lumbridge Castle. Easy bank trip, respawns are fast.
  • Falador Farm Cows: South-east of Falador, near the Port Sarim road. More cows packed together, slightly longer bank trip.
  • Burthorpe Cow Field: Accessible via the Games Necklace teleport. Good if you have some starter GP for a Games Necklace.

② Bones — Easy Passive Money

While collecting cowhides, you will also get cow bones. A stack of 28 cow bones sells for around 1,500–2,000 GP on the GE. Bones are not as valuable as hides, but they are free and stack up fast. If you are training Prayer anyway, bury them for XP. If you care more about GP, bank them and sell in bulk. Regular bones from goblins, chickens, and cows all have value. Chickens outside Lumbridge drop bones and raw chicken, which you can cook or sell. A full inventory of chicken drops takes 2 minutes and nets ~1K GP.

③ Steel Items from Drops

Once you have a few thousand GP, buy a steel scimitar from the GE (~100 gp). Then start killing creatures that drop steel items:

  • Minotaurs (Stronghold of Security): Found on floors 1 and 2 of the Stronghold of Security (Barbarian Village). They drop steel arrows, steel coins, and steel weapons. The steel arrow drops sell for 2–3 GP each, and a stack of 100 arrows is 200–300 GP per kill.
  • Al-Kharid Warriors: Outside the Al Kharid Palace. They drop steel swords, scimitars, and kiteshields. A steel scimitar drop is worth ~100 GP.
  • Flesh Crawlers (Stronghold of Security floor 2): While dangerous for level 3s, they drop high-value herbs and coins. Wait until combat level 20+ to farm these efficiently.

The Stronghold of Security also gives you a free 10K gp reward for completing all four floors. Do this on every new account. It is one-time but it gives you significant starting capital.

🎣 F2P Skilling Money — Level 1 to 30

Once you have your first 10–20K from cows and bones, you can start training skills that produce sellable items. These methods take a bit more effort but scale much better into the mid-game. Each skill can generate 20K–50K GP per hour at low levels.

① Cooking Fish — From Level 1 to 30

Cooking is the most profitable F2P skill to train at low levels. Here is the progression:

  • Level 1–5: Cook shrimp and anchovies. Raw shrimp can be bought from the GE for ~3 GP each. Cooked shrimp sell for ~8 GP each. Even with the burn rate, you profit 3–4 GP per fish. Burn 50% but profit on the rest.
  • Level 5–15: Switch to sardines and herring. Raw sardines cost ~5 GP, cooked sell for ~15 GP. Better margins.
  • Level 15–30: Trout and salmon. Raw trout costs ~30 GP, cooked sells for ~80 GP. At level 20 Cooking, your burn rate drops to 20%, making this very profitable. A full inventory of raw trout (28) costs ~840 GP and sells for ~2,240 GP — over 1,400 GP per trip. At 100+ fish per hour, that is 30K+ GP per hour.
  • Level 30+: Tuna and lobster. Cooked lobster sells for ~180 GP each. Raw lobster costs ~130 GP. Profit of ~50 GP each. This becomes your main F2P cooking money maker for levels 30–65.

Cooking is zero-risk — you stand at the GE bank, buy raw fish, cook on a range, and sell back instantly. No walking, no dying, no competition for resources.

② Wines (Cooking Wine) — Level 1 Cooking

Making jugs of wine is a niche but very profitable moneymaker that requires zero cooking level. Here is how it works: Buy jugs of water (~1 GP each) and grapes (~50 GP each) from the GE. Use the grapes on the jugs of water to make unfermented wine. Each wine sells for ~130 GP on the GE. Your cost is ~51 GP per wine, so profit is ~79 GP per wine. You can make 1,000 wines per hour easily. That is 79,000 GP per hour at level 1 Cooking with zero skills. The catch: You fail to make wine if your Cooking level is below 35, but you still get the supplies back. So at level 1, your success rate is about 50%. But even at 50% success, 500 successful wines at 79 GP profit each is nearly 40K GP per hour. Not bad for a level 1 skill.

Wine making is highly AFK — you just click grapes on jugs repeatedly. It is one of the most recommended methods for new players who want to watch Netflix while earning GP.

③ Firemaking Logs — Simple but Steady

Firemaking is not a great moneymaker on its own, but if you combine it with woodcutting, it can add value. Normal logs burn to ash, which sells for ~20 GP each on the GE. Regular logs cost ~5 GP each (or are free if you cut them). Burning 1,000 logs yields 1,000 ashes worth 20K GP. That is not amazing, but it is simple and AFK. Better: Oak logs cost ~10 GP each, and oak ashes sell for ~40 GP each. Burn 1,000 oak logs and you make 30K GP profit. This requires level 15 Firemaking. Willow logs (level 30) cost ~5 GP and willow ashes sell for ~30 GP each — 25K GP per 1,000 logs. Firemaking is the weakest money maker on this list, but it is also the simplest and can be done while watching TV or reading.

⚔️ Combat Drops — Level 10 to 40 Money Making

Once you have trained your combat stats to level 10–20, you can start killing monsters that drop valuable items. Combat-based money making scales extremely well because as your levels increase, your kills per hour go up and your food consumption goes down. By level 40 combat, you should be making 50K–100K GP per hour in F2P.

① Hill Giants — Limpwurt Roots & Big Bones

Hill Giants are located in the Edgeville Dungeon (level 17 Wilderness area, but the dungeon entrance is outside the Wilderness). They have 35 HP and hit up to 4 damage. Their notable drops include:

  • Big bones (100% drop): Sell for ~300 GP each on the GE.
  • Limpwurt roots (rare): Sell for ~700 GP each. Used in Herblore for strength potions.
  • Steel items: Steel full helms, steel kiteshields, and steel longswords are common and sell for 100–300 GP each.
  • Coins (common): 50–200 coin drops per kill.
  • Clue scrolls (medium): Worth 30–50K if completed.

At level 20 Attack/Strength with an iron scimitar, you can kill 40–50 Hill Giants per hour. That is 12K–15K GP per hour from big bones alone, plus 10–20K in limpwurt roots and steel drops. Total: 25K–35K GP per hour.

② Moss Giants — High Level F2P Money

Moss Giants are located in the Varrock Sewers (difficult to reach without rope) and in the Crandor island dungeon. They have 60 HP and hit up to 8 damage, so you need combat level 30+ with good food to farm them efficiently.

  • Big bones (100% drop): Same as Hill Giants, ~300 GP each.
  • Moss giant key (rare): Used for the Obor boss fight. Sells for ~10K gp.
  • Rune scimitar (very rare): If you get a rune scimitar drop, that is 15K GP instantly.
  • Coins: 100–500 coin drops are common.
  • Nature runes (rare): Worth ~200 GP each.

At level 40 Attack/Strength with a mithril or adamant weapon, Moss Giants earn 40K–60K GP per hour thanks to the higher value coin drops and the occasional moss giant key.

③ Flesh Crawlers — The Herblore Gold Mine

Flesh Crawlers are found on Floor 2 of the Stronghold of Security (level 35 Wilderness area, but protected by the Stronghold mechanics). They have 25 HP, hit up to 2 damage, and drop:

  • Herbs (common): Guam, marrentill, and tarromin. Each herb sells for 200–500 GP.
  • Coins (common): 10–50 coin drops per kill.
  • Steel arrows (common): 5–15 arrows per drop, worth 10–30 GP.
  • Essence (uncommon): Pure essence drops worth ~5 GP each.
  • Clue scrolls (medium): Same as Hill Giants.

Flesh Crawlers are the best AFK combat money maker in F2P. They have low HP and low damage, so you can kill hundreds per hour without banking. At level 20 Defence with iron armor, you can stay for 30+ minutes on one inventory of food. Earnings: 30K–50K GP per hour depending on herb drops.

📈 Reaching Your First 100K — Combining Methods

By now you have probably made 30K–50K from the methods above. Getting to 100K requires combining multiple approaches and optimizing your time. Here is the exact roadmap to hit 100K as fast as possible.

① Combining Methods for Maximum GP/Hour

Do not stick to one method. Combine them for the best results:

  • Phase 1 (0–10K): Cowhides. 30 minutes of cow killing gets you 10–15K GP. Spend none of it.
  • Phase 2 (10K–30K): Buy raw trout, cook it, sell cooked. Use your 10K to buy 300 raw trout. Cook them at the GE range. Sell for 80K worth of cooked trout. Net profit: ~20K. Total: 30K.
  • Phase 3 (30K–60K): Spend 10K on an iron scimitar, 5K on salmon/trout food, and go kill Hill Giants for 2 hours. Sell all big bones, limpwurt roots, and steel drops. Earn 30K+.
  • Phase 4 (60K–100K): Use 20K to buy adamant armor (platebody, platelegs, full helm). With addy gear and your iron scimitar, kill Moss Giants or Flesh Crawlers. The higher damage and defence let you profit 40K–60K per hour. One hour of Moss Giants gets you to 100K.

Total time to 100K: 4–5 hours for a brand new account. Experienced players can do it in 2–3 hours by skipping the less efficient methods.

② Grand Exchange Flipping Basics

Once you have 20K+ GP, you can start flipping items on the Grand Exchange. Flipping is buying items at a lower price and selling them at a higher price. Here is how to start:

  • Step 1: Pick high-volume items. Look for items with 100K+ daily trade volume. Examples: Law runes, Nature runes, Yew logs, Lobster, Dragon bones.
  • Step 2: Place a buy offer for 5% below the current market price. Wait. If it fills within 5 minutes, your price is too high. Adjust down by 1–2%.
  • Step 3: Once your buy order fills, sell the items for 1–3% above market price. The gap between buy and sell prices is your profit.
  • Step 4: Repeat. With 100K GP, you can make 2K–5K per flip cycle. Each cycle takes 5–15 minutes, so you can flip 4–12 times per hour for 10K–50K GP per hour.

Flipping requires patience and watching the market. Use the GE tracker websites and in-game price check to find good margins. Never flip items with low volume — you will get stuck holding them.

③ Smart Spending — Stretch Your First 100K

When you reach 100K GP, resist the urge to blow it all on one big item. Here is how to spend it wisely:

  • Buy a Rune Scimitar (~15K): The best F2P weapon. This is your top priority.
  • Buy Full Adamant Armor (~40K): Adamant platebody (~15K), platelegs (~10K), full helm (~5K), kiteshield (~10K).
  • Buy Amulet of Power or Strength (~10K): Huge DPS boost.
  • Buy Games Necklace + Ring of Dueling (~5K): Teleport items save time = more GP.
  • Keep 30K liquid GP: Always have capital for flipping, runes, arrows, and food.

Do not buy a dragon weapon (members only), and do not buy cosmetic items. Upgrade gear first, then save for membership bonds if that is your goal.

🎯 What to Buy After 100K — Best Upgrades

Congratulations! You have 100K GP in your bank. Now it is time to turn that 100K into a sustainable money-making machine. The items you buy at this stage determine how fast you can reach your next goal (500K, 1M, or a bond for membership). These are the optimal purchases, prioritized by impact on your profit per hour.

① Adamant Weapons — Your First Real Upgrade

Adamant weapons are the second-best F2P weapon tier below rune. They are a massive upgrade from iron and steel:

  • Adamant Scimitar (~1K GP): Best DPS. Faster than the longsword, better accuracy than the mace.
  • Adamant Longsword (~800 GP): Higher max hit, slower speed. Good for training Strength.
  • Adamant Battleaxe (~900 GP): High damage, slow speed. Situational use.
  • Adamant 2H Sword (~1.5K GP): Slow but high damage for aggressive training.

At 15K GP, the rune scimitar blows all of these away. Save for the rune scimitar first, then use adamant for your secondary weapon slot (for different attack styles).

② Rune Scimitar — The Goal Weapon

The Rune Scimitar is the best F2P melee weapon in OSRS (the rune 2H has higher max hit but is slower). At 15K GP on the GE, it is a steal for the DPS it provides. With a rune scimitar and 40 Attack, your kill speed doubles compared to an adamant weapon. This means your GP per hour from combat doubles too. You should buy a rune scimitar as soon as you reach 40 Attack and have 15K GP. It is the single best combat investment in F2P.

Special attack: The rune scimitar has no special attack (in F2P). But its raw DPS is unmatched. For comparison, the rune longsword has a special attack (slice and dice) but lower base DPS. Stick with the scimitar.

③ Armor Upgrades — What to Wear at 40 Defence

Once you have 40 Defence, here is the optimal F2P armor setup:

  • Rune Full Helm (~7K GP): High slash/stab/crush defence. Worth the cost.
  • Rune Platebody (~35K GP): The most expensive piece. Prioritize platelegs first if you are on a budget.
  • Rune Platelegs (~15K GP): Good defence for the price. Upgrade from adamant.
  • Rune Kiteshield (~10K GP): High defence for ranged and magic.
  • Amulet of Power (~5K GP): +6 to all attack and defence stats. Better than the amulet of strength for general use.
  • Amulet of Strength (~3K GP): +10 Strength bonus. Better for pure strength training.
  • Amulet of Defence (~2K GP): Only for tanking. Use Power or Strength instead.

Full rune armor (platebody, platelegs, full helm, kiteshield) costs about 65K GP total. If you have 100K, you can buy full rune plus a rune scimitar and amulet and still have 15K left over. That is a complete F2P combat setup.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How long does it take to make 100K GP as a new player?

A: Using the methods in this guide, a brand new player can reach 100K GP in 4–6 hours of active gameplay. If you optimize the route (cowhides → cooking → Hill Giants → Moss Giants), you can do it in 3–4 hours.

Q: Can I make 100K without any combat?

A: Yes. Cooking fish (trout, tuna, lobster) and making wines are both zero-combat money makers that can easily reach 100K GP. Cooking from level 1 to 30 and selling fish will earn 50K+ on its own.

Q: What is the fastest F2P money maker for beginners?

A: Cowhides are the fastest for absolute beginners (0 skills, 0 GP). Once you have 10K+ GP, cooking trout or making wines becomes faster. At combat level 20+, Hill Giants are the fastest.

Q: Should I buy membership with my first 100K?

A: No. A bond (14 days of membership) costs approximately 8M GP. You need 80x your current bank to afford a bond. Focus on building up to 500K–1M first, then decide if you want to save for a bond.

Q: What should I buy first with 100K GP?

A: Runite scimitar (~15K), full adamant armor (~40K), and an amulet of power (~5K). Keep the remaining 40K for food, teleport items, and GE flipping capital.

Q: Is GE flipping worth it for small amounts?

A: Yes, but the margins are tighter. With 20K GP, you can make 500–1K per flip. With 100K GP, you can make 2K–5K per flip. Flipping becomes really good at 500K+ GP where you can flip higher-value items.

Q: What is the Stronghold of Security reward?

A: Completing all four floors of the Stronghold of Security gives you 10,000 GP in total (2.5K per floor). You also get the "Fighting Boots" from the fourth floor, which are cosmetic. The 10K GP is a huge boost for new accounts.

Q: Are there any one-time money makers for new players?

A: Yes! The Stronghold of Security (10K GP), the Lumbridge/Draynor achievement diaries (reward cash and items), and quest rewards. Complete "Cook's Assistant," "The Restless Ghost," and "Rune Mysteries" for quick XP and small cash rewards.

Q: How do I avoid getting scammed when buying gear?

A: Only use the Grand Exchange. Never trade with other players in person for expensive items. If someone offers to "trim your armor," it is a scam. If someone offers a "free item giveaway" that requires you to drop items first, it is a scam. Use the GE and ignore random trade requests.

Q: Can I make 100K GP in one day?

A: Absolutely. With 2–3 hours of focus, you can hit 100K GP in a single play session. The fastest route: 30 min cowhides (15K) → 30 min cooking trout (20K) → 1 hour Hill Giants (30K) → 30 min Moss Giants (25K) → sell all loot on GE (10K). Total: 100K GP in ~3 hours.
