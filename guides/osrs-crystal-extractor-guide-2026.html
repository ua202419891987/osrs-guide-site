OSRS Crystal Extractor Guide 2026: Placement, Cost & Wind Mote Mechanics

The Crystal Extractor is one of the most misunderstood facilities in OSRS Sailing. Many players place it wrong, waste 2M GP, and miss out on passive XP. This guide covers everything confirmed by Jagex — correct placement per ship type, material costs, wind mote mechanics, and whether it's truly worth building.

⚡ Quick Verdict

Worth building if: You're level 73+ Sailing, actively running Barracuda Trials, have 2M+ GP to spare, and have completed Song of the Elves (for crystal shard profits). It saves ~12 hours on the 72-99 grind and generates ~10,000 crystal shards worth 100M+ GP.

Skip if: You're below level 70 Sailing, haven't completed the Gwenith Glide Swordfish rank (needed for the Heart of Ithell), or you only do Shipwreck Salvaging and don't care about maximum XP rates.

1. What Is the Crystal Extractor?

The Crystal Extractor is a boat facility in OSRS Sailing that periodically generates Wind Motes for use with a Wind Catcher or Gale Catcher. It was added as part of the Sailing skill, released on November 19, 2025, and is one of the most valuable facilities for mid-to-high level sailors.

Its primary purpose is passive XP generation — every 60 seconds, it produces one Wind Mote that you can collect for 600 Sailing XP. The Wind Motes can also be converted into Tailwind charges via a Wind/Gale Catcher facility, providing speed boosts during Barracuda Trials.

💎 What Makes It Unique

Unlike most Sailing facilities that serve a single purpose (cannons for combat, salvaging hooks for loot), the Crystal Extractor serves two roles simultaneously: it generates passive XP and produces speed-boosting resources for high-intensity training methods. This dual-purpose design makes it the most efficient facility slot investment in the game.

① Gwenith Glide Swordfish Rank

Before you can build the Crystal Extractor, you must complete the Swordfish rank of the Gwenith Glide Barracuda Trial. This trial is located at Tempoross Cove and becomes available at level 30 Sailing. The Swordfish rank is the second tier of the trial (after Unranked), and completing it rewards you with the Heart of Ithell — a one-time, untradeable item required to build the Crystal Extractor.

You can check your trial progress in the Barracuda Trials menu accessible from the Tempoross Cove notice board. If you've already completed the Shark or Marlin ranks, you have definitely unlocked the Heart of Ithell and can proceed directly to building.

💡 Level Planning

If you're planning to rush 99 Sailing efficiently, make sure you've completed the Gwenith Glide Swordfish rank before you hit level 73. This way you can build the Crystal Extractor immediately and start gaining passive XP from the moment you unlock it.

② Material Acquisition Tips

  • Ironwood Planks: You can gather ironwood logs yourself at level 80 Woodcutting from ironwood trees found on Sailing islands, then convert to planks using a Sawmill. This saves roughly 5,000 GP compared to buying the planks directly.
  • Nickel Bars: Mine nickel ore (requires 74 Mining) from nickel rocks on Sailing islands and smelt with copper at a furnace. Each bar requires 1 nickel ore and 1 copper ore.
  • Magic Stones: These are the most expensive component. They can only be obtained from the Grand Exchange or as rare drops from high-level activities. There is no cheaper alternative — budget for 2M GP minimum.
  • Heart of Ithell: Earned from the Gwenith Glide trial. If you haven't completed the Swordfish rank, prioritize that before buying any materials, as the heart is the non-negotiable core component.

4. How the Wind Mote System Works

The Crystal Extractor has a straightforward but easy-to-miss mechanic. Understanding it fully prevents frustration and lost XP:

③ Generation Cycle

  1. Timer: Every 63 seconds while you are on your ship, the Crystal Extractor generates one Wind Mote.
  2. Storage Limit: The extractor holds a maximum of 1 Wind Mote at a time. If you don't collect it within 63 seconds, the extractor sits idle and you lose potential XP.
  3. Manual Collection: You must manually click the extractor to collect the Wind Mote. This is not automatic.
  4. Cannot Stack: Wind Motes do not stack in your inventory. Each mote occupies one inventory slot.
  5. XP Reward: Each collected Wind Mote awards 250 Sailing XP (updated December 2025) — this does not scale with your Sailing level.

🎯 Optimal Collection Rhythm

During Barracuda Trials, collect your Wind Mote at the start before beginning the trial route. Then collect again immediately after finishing. During Port Tasks or travel, set a 60-second timer or use visual cues (the extractor has a subtle glow animation when a mote is ready).

④ Crystal Shard Bonus

If you have completed Song of the Elves, each Wind Mote collected has a chance to generate 1 Crystal Shard (approximately 1 in 6 chance). Crystal Shards are untradeable but can be used to charge crystal equipment or through the Prifddinas shop for GP, runes, and resources. This makes the Crystal Extractor particularly valuable for maintaining crystal gear.

This shard bonus is what transforms the Crystal Extractor from "nice to have" to "essential for efficient players" — it provides a steady stream of shards that would otherwise require picking up from the Prifddinas area.

5. Correct Placement Per Ship Type (Critical)

This is the most important section of this guide. The Crystal Extractor must be placed on a specific facility hotspot for each ship type. Place it wrong, and you'll need to demolish and rebuild — wasting the 2M GP investment.

⚠️ The #1 Mistake Players Make

Players place the Crystal Extractor on any available hotspot without thinking about accessibility. The core rule is: you must be able to reach the extractor from the helm position without moving your character away from the wheel. If your character has to take even one step away from the helm to collect a Wind Mote, the placement is wrong.

⑤ Sloop (Level 50+) — Recommended Placement

The Sloop is the most common ship for high-level Sailing training. The correct hotspot is the one located diagonally forward-left from the helm position.

  • Correct: Facility slot that is adjacent diagonally to the helm wheel position, allowing you to reach it without moving.
  • Wrong: Any slot behind the helm, on the opposite side, or at the far ends of the ship. These require walking, which disrupts your sailing rhythm.

⑥ Skiff (Level 15+) — Recommended Placement

The Skiff has fewer facility slots, making placement simpler but still error-prone. The correct hotspot is immediately to the right of the helm position.

  • Correct: The facility slot directly adjacent to the right of the helm wheel.
  • Wrong: Any slot on the left side or at the bow/stern of the skiff.

⑦ How to Test Your Placement

Step 1: Build the Extractor

Visit the Shipwright at any major port (Port Sarim is most convenient). Select "Build Equipment" and choose the Crystal Extractor from the facilities menu. Confirm placement on your chosen hotspot.

Step 2: Station at the Helm

Walk to the helm position (the wheel) and stay there. Do not move.

Step 3: Wait 60 Seconds

A Wind Mote will generate. Watch for the glow animation on the extractor.

Step 4: Attempt to Collect

Click the extractor. If your character collects the mote without moving from the helm position, your placement is perfect. If your character walks away from the helm to reach the extractor, the placement is wrong — demolish and rebuild on the correct hotspot.

💡 Salvaging-Only Alternative

If you primarily do Shipwreck Salvaging and rarely run Barracuda Trials, you can place the Crystal Extractor adjacent to your Salvaging Hook instead. This lets you collect Wind Motes while waiting for salvaging actions, converting downtime into 24k-36k passive XP per hour. The trade-off is you lose the Tailwind benefits during trials — choose based on your playstyle.

6. Wind Catcher & Gale Catcher Synergy

Wind Motes are not just for XP — they are the fuel for the Wind Catcher and Gale Catcher facilities, which provide speed boosts during Barracuda Trials.

⑧ How the Fuel System Works

FacilityUnlock LevelFuel UseEffect
Wind Catcher53 Sailing1 Wind Mote per chargeProvides a Tailwind speed boost for the next trial run
Gale Catcher79 Sailing1 Wind Mote per chargeStronger Tailwind — significantly higher speed boost

The synergy works as follows:

  1. Crystal Extractor generates Wind Motes every 60 seconds → you collect them
  2. Wind Motes are stored in your inventory or transferred to the Wind/Gale Catcher
  3. Before starting a Barracuda Trial, use the Wind Mote to charge your catcher
  4. The catcher provides a Tailwind buff for the trial, increasing ship speed
  5. Faster trial completions mean more XP per hour — creating a feedback loop

🎯 Optimal Trial Strategy

With both a Crystal Extractor and Gale Catcher installed (both require 73 Sailing and 67 Construction), you can maintain a steady supply of Wind Motes for every trial run. The Gale Catcher provides a stronger boost than the Wind Catcher, making trial completions 8-12 seconds faster per run. Over 88 hours of training, this translates to roughly 4-5 extra hours of saved time.

Based on all confirmed data, here is the definitive verdict:

✅ Build It Immediately If:

  • You are level 73+ Sailing and actively training toward 99
  • You regularly run Barracuda Trials for XP (Gwenith Glide or higher)
  • You have 2M+ GP available for the magic stone cost
  • You have completed Song of the Elves (for crystal shard profits)
  • You want to optimize your XP per hour and save real-world time

9. Crystal Extractor FAQs

What level do I need to build the Crystal Extractor?

73 Sailing and 67 Construction. Neither requirement can be boosted with potions, stews, or any other method. You also need the Heart of Ithell from the Gwenith Glide Swordfish rank.

How much does the Crystal Extractor cost?

Approximately 2.06M GP — the bulk of the cost comes from Magic Stones (~2M GP each, you need 2). Ironwood Planks (~10.5K) and Nickel Bars (~1.5K) are cheap by comparison. The Heart of Ithell is untradeable, earned from the trial.

What happens if I place the Crystal Extractor in the wrong spot?

You must demolish it and rebuild on the correct hotspot. There is no way to move a facility once built. This costs the full 2.06M GP again. Always test the placement before finalizing — stand at the helm, wait 60 seconds, see if you can collect without moving.

How much XP does the Crystal Extractor give per hour?

Theoretically 36,000 XP/h (one mote per 60 seconds x 600 XP). Realistically 24,000-30,000 XP/h depending on how often you remember to collect. It is not a primary training method — it's passive bonus XP on top of your main training activity.

Can I use the Crystal Extractor without a Wind Catcher?

Yes. The Crystal Extractor generates Wind Motes regardless of whether you have a Wind Catcher or Gale Catcher installed. The mote itself gives 600 XP upon collection. The Wind/Gale Catcher is an optional facility that consumes motes for speed boosts during trials.

Do I need Song of the Elves to benefit from the Crystal Extractor?

No, but it's highly recommended. Without Song of the Elves, you still get the 600 XP per Wind Mote and the Tailwind boost for trials. With Song of the Elves completed, you also get 1-2 crystal shards per collection — which adds 100M+ GP profit over the 73-99 grind.

Can Ironmen use the Crystal Extractor?

Yes. All materials must be gathered yourself (ironwood logs from Sailing islands, nickel ore from mining, magic stones as drops or from PvM). The Heart of Ithell is earned from the trial the same as any account. Ironmen benefit especially from the crystal shard generation, as it provides a steady source of shards for crystal equipment upkeep.

How does the Gale Catcher differ from the Wind Catcher?

The Gale Catcher (requires 73 Sailing) provides a stronger Tailwind buff than the Wind Catcher (requires 50 Sailing). Both consume 1 Wind Mote per charge. The Gale Catcher's stronger speed boost translates to faster trial completions and thus higher XP per hour. Both can be built, but the Gale Catcher is strictly better at the same level requirement.

Is the Crystal Extractor worth it if I'm only going for 99 Sailing?

If you're going for 99 Sailing and already have the requirements (73 Sailing, 67 Construction, Heart of Ithell, 2M GP), the Crystal Extractor is absolutely worth it. It saves ~12 hours of training time and generates ~100M+ GP in crystal shards. The only reason to skip is if you're on a tight budget and need the 2M GP for essential hull or sail upgrades first.

🎯 Your Crystal Extractor Game Plan

Follow these steps in order to build and use your Crystal Extractor correctly:

✅ Step 1: Reach level 73 Sailing. Focus on Barracuda Trials at Tempoross Cove for the fastest XP.
✅ Step 2: Complete the Gwenith Glide Swordfish rank (if you haven't already). This earns the Heart of Ithell.
✅ Step 3: Confirm you have 67 Construction. Level up if needed — it's worth the investment.
✅ Step 4: Gather or buy materials — 6 Ironwood Planks, 5 Nickel Bars, 2 Magic Stones, and the Heart of Ithell. Budget ~2.06M GP.
✅ Step 5: Visit the Shipwright and place the Crystal Extractor on the correct hotspot (forward-left for Sloop, right-adjacent for Skiff).
✅ Step 6: Test the placement immediately. Stand at the helm, wait 60 seconds, try to collect. If you can reach it from the helm — perfect. If not, demolish and rebuild on the correct spot.
✅ Step 7: Optionally build a Gale Catcher for stronger Tailwind boosts during Barracuda Trials. This maximizes the extractor's potential.

Remember: Collect every 60 seconds for maximum passive XP. Don't let the extractor sit idle — 600 XP per minute adds up to 36K XP/h that you would otherwise miss.
