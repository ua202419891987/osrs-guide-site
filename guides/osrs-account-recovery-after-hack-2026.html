OSRS Account Recovery After a Hack 2026

Your account is gone, the password doesn't work, and panic is setting in. Take a breath. This guide focuses purely on the post-breach recovery flow: how to get back in, what to tell Jagex, and — importantly — what to do if the account was banned while the hacker had it.

🛡️ Level: Novice
📅 Account Security Series · Part 2 of 2 · 12 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Act from a clean device — change your email password and run a malware scan before you start, so the attacker can't just take it back.
  • 📌 Use the official Jagex Support recovery flow at support.runescape.com / jgx.game — never pay "recovery services" that DM you; they're scammers.
  • 📌 Prove ownership with specifics — creation date, old passwords, historical transactions, and characters you recognise.
  • 📌 If it was banned after the hack: appeal through the official ban-appeal process, explain it was compromised, and include your recovery ticket reference.
  • 📌 Re-secure immediately — once back in, enable 2SV and set a unique password before doing anything else.

1. 🚨 First 60 Minutes: Contain the Damage

Before you fight to get the account back, stop the bleed. Most "I recovered it but lost it again" stories happen because the player reclaimed the account from an infected PC and the stealer immediately grabbed the new password. Spend your first hour on containment, not on recovery forms.

⏱️ The First-Hour Checklist

  1. Change your email password first. Every reset link and 2SV code routes through your email — if the attacker still controls it, recovery is pointless.
  2. Run a full malware scan on every device you've used to log in (Windows Defender / Malwarebytes). Remove anything it flags, especially "RuneLite" copies from unofficial sources.
  3. Log out everywhere you can and note the last time you successfully logged in — this timestamp is useful evidence later.
  4. Warn your friends in-game and on Discord. Hackers DM your contacts pretending to be you to spread the scam.
  5. Don't pay "recovery agents." Anyone offering to "get your account back for a fee" via DM is a secondary scammer piling on.
💡 Why a Clean Device Matters

If a keylogger is still running, anything you type during recovery — including your new Jagex password and 2SV backup codes — goes straight back to the attacker. Use a phone, a different computer, or a cleaned machine for the recovery steps below.

2. 🔑 Step-by-Step: Reclaim Your Account

The 2026 recovery path runs through the official Jagex Account support system. There is no secret "recovery question" anymore — ownership is re-established through a support ticket backed by evidence. Here's the flow.

Step 1 — Go to the official support site

Open support.runescape.com (or the jgx.game support portal) from your bookmark or by typing it yourself. Select the option for account access / "I can't log in" or "unauthorised access."

Step 2 — Choose "Unauthorised Access" (not just "forgot password")

Flagging the account as compromised triggers the right review queue. A plain password reset won't help if the attacker changed your email and 2SV.

Step 3 — Submit the recovery form with detail

Provide as much verifiable history as you can (see Section 3). The more specific and accurate your answers, the faster and more likely the recovery.

Step 4 — Secure your linked email

Confirm the recovery email on the Jagex Account is one you control and that you've already re-secured (Step 1 of containment).

Step 5 — Wait for the response, then act fast

Jagex replies by email. Follow the instructions exactly and respond within any stated window. If they need more proof, give it — don't argue.

⚠️ Avoid the "Recovery Service" Trap

You will see comments and DMs offering to recover the account for you, often citing "insider" access. These are scams that steal whatever money you send and sometimes your personal data. Only the official Jagex Support process can return your account — there is no shortcut.

3. 📝 What to Provide to Prove Ownership

Support staff can't take your word for it — they need signals that you're the original owner. Accurate, specific details dramatically raise your success rate. Guess conservatively: wrong answers look like an attacker guessing.

Information to ProvideWhy It HelpsExample
Approximate account creation date Hard for an attacker to know; anchors your claim "Created around June 2019, during the Twisted League"
Previous passwords you've used Only the real owner recalls old credentials List 1–2 passwords from before the hack
Historical membership / bond purchases Transaction records are strong proof Dates of bonds bought, payment method used
Characters & stats you recognise Demonstrates genuine familiarity "Main is 2,100 total; an IM called [name] at 1,800"
Original registration email Links the account to your identity The email used when you first signed up
Last successful login before the hack Helps pinpoint the breach window "I logged in fine on the 3rd, noticed the hack on the 5th"
✅ Quality Over Quantity

Three accurate, specific facts beat a long list of guesses. If you're unsure about a field, say so honestly — "I don't remember exactly" reads far more like a real owner than a confident wrong answer. Staff are trained to spot both.

4. 📧 Contacting Jagex Support the Right Way

Knowing where and how to contact Jagex saves days of bouncing between fake forms. Stick to official channels only.

① Use the support portal, not social media

Jagex does not recover accounts over Twitter/X, Reddit, or Discord DMs. Use the in-portal ticket system at the official support site. Social "mods" who DM you are impersonators.

② One ticket, one clear story

Open a single ticket and explain the situation once, calmly and completely. Opening many duplicate tickets slows the queue for everyone, including you.

③ Keep your reference number

Save the ticket / reference ID from your confirmation email. You'll need it if you must follow up or if a ban appeal references the recovery.

④ Be patient but persistent

Response times vary with volume — often several days. If you haven't heard back within the stated window, reply to the same ticket rather than starting over.

🔍 Check Your Spam Folder

Jagex replies arrive by email and are frequently filtered into spam or promotions tabs. Whitelist the support domain and check junk daily so you don't miss a recovery approval or a request for more information.

5. ⚖️ What If the Account Was Banned After Being Hacked

This is the scary one. Hackers often use a stolen account for rule-breaking — botting, real-world trading (RWT), or spamming — and the account gets banned while they control it. The good news: Jagex does have a process for compromised accounts that were penalised as a result.

ScenarioWhat to Do
Account banned, and you can still log in Open a ban appeal through the official appeal system. State clearly the account was compromised, give the breach window, and reference your recovery ticket.
Account banned and you cannot log in First complete the unauthorised-access recovery (Section 2). Once access is restored, submit the ban appeal from inside the account.
Ban appeal was denied Review the denial reason, then reply to the same appeal / ticket with additional ownership evidence. A single denial is not always final if new proof is supplied.
You bought gold / the account (RWT / account trading) Be honest in the appeal. Third-party trading and account selling break the rules regardless of the hack; recovery is not guaranteed and the ban may stand.

⑤ How to Frame the Appeal

When you appeal, lead with the compromise, not with outrage. A clear, factual note works best:

📄 Appeal Template (adapt to your case)

"My account was accessed without my permission between [dates]. During that window I did not play it, and any rule-breaking (e.g. RWT / botting) was done by the person who hijacked it, not me. I have already submitted an unauthorised-access recovery (reference [ID]) and re-secured my email. I can verify ownership with [creation date / old passwords / transaction history]. Please review the login records for that period."

🧾 Login Records Are Your Ally

Jagex can see where and when the account was accessed. A sudden login from a country you've never visited, or a burst of bot-like activity you didn't do, is exactly the pattern that supports a "this wasn't me" appeal. Point support toward that anomaly rather than just asserting it.

6. 🛡️ Once Recovered: Lock It Down

Getting the account back is only half the job — the attacker may try again. Before you train, trade, or boss, harden the account so this never repeats.

🎯 Do These the Moment You're Back In

  1. Set a brand-new unique password you've never used anywhere else.
  2. Enable 2-Step Verification with an authenticator app, and save the backup codes offline.
  3. Review connected devices / sessions and remove anything unfamiliar.
  4. Run a final malware scan on your main PC before logging in there again.
  5. Bookmark the real sites (oldschool.runescape.com, runelite.net) to avoid future phishing.

7. 💡 Recovery FAQs

How long does OSRS account recovery actually take?

It varies with support volume and how much proof you provide. Simple, well-documented cases can resolve in a few days; contested or evidence-light cases take longer, and appeals may need a couple of rounds. Submitting accurate details up front is the single biggest factor in speed.

Can I recover an account if I never set up 2SV or don't have the email?

Yes, recovery isn't strictly dependent on 2SV or the original email — it depends on proving you're the original owner through historical details (creation date, past passwords, transactions, characters). If you've lost access to the registration email, explain that in the ticket and lean harder on the other evidence columns.

Will Jagex refund bonds or items lost during the hack?

Jagex generally does not restore items, GP, or bonds lost to a hijack — account access is what they recover, not the wealth. This is why prevention (2SV) matters so much: once a hacker empties your bank, those items are almost never coming back.

My appeal was denied. Is that the end?

Not necessarily. If you have new, stronger ownership evidence (a transaction receipt, an older password, a precise creation window), reply to the same appeal or ticket with it. Persistent, polite, evidence-backed follow-up within the official system is the right move — not creating new accounts to re-argue the case.

Should I make a new account while waiting?

You can play on a separate account, but keep your recovery ticket alive on the original. Don't use the hacked account's name or email patterns for the new one, and don't let the hacker lure your new account into the same scam circles. Treat the new account with the same 2SV protection from day one.
