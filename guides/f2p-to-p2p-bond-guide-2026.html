1. Bond Basics — What You Need to Know

An Old School Bond is an in-game item that can be redeemed for 14 days of membership. Bonds are bought with real money and sold on the Grand Exchange for GP. As of 2026, a bond costs approximately 12M-15M GP (fluctuates with the market).

Key Facts

Bond Price (2026)~12M-15M GP (check GE for current price)
Membership Duration14 days per bond
StackingCan redeem multiple bonds for extended membership
TradeableYes — buy from GE, redeem to activate
F2P PurchaseBonds can be bought and redeemed in F2P worlds
Daily GP Target~900K-1.1M GP/day to sustain membership forever

The Math

Bond price (14M) / 14 days = 1M GP/day needed to sustain membership. In members worlds, this is trivial. The challenge is earning your first bond in F2P — everything after that is much easier.

Recommended F2P Money Maker: Ogress Warriors

Why Ogress Warriors?

Ogress Warriors in the Corsair Cove dungeon are the best AFK combat money maker in F2P. They drop rune items (full helm, battleaxe, med helm), alchable runes (nature, death, law), and noted resources. No competition for spawns because it's instanced-ish with multiple rooms.

Setup: 40+ Ranged with maple shortbow + adamant arrows, or 13+ Magic with fire strike. Bring food for the first trip, then bank when inventory is full of runes/items.

Average profit: 150K-250K/hr. Do 2 hours/day for ~10 days = first bond.

4. Earning Your First Bond — The Strategy

There are two approaches to earning your first bond. Pick based on your playstyle.

Strategy A: Ogress Grind (No Starting Cash Needed)

  1. Day 1-3: Do all F2P quests for free XP. Get 40+ Ranged at cows/chickens then hill giants.
  2. Day 3-7: Complete Dragon Slayer quest (32 QP needed first). Unlocks Corsair Cove dungeon.
  3. Day 7-21: Grind Ogress Warriors 1-2 hours/day. Bank all runes, alchable rune items, gems, noted resources.
  4. Day 21-28: Sell everything. Buy a bond from GE. Redeem it. You're now a member.

Total time to first bond: ~20-25 hours of gameplay (2-3 weeks casual).

Strategy B: High Alching + Flipping (If You Have Some Seed GP)

  1. Day 1-3: Get 55 Magic (fire strike → teleports → high alch). Costs ~200K in runes but can be done in 6-8 hours.
  2. Day 3-10: Buy items below alch price on GE, alch them for profit. Use F2P alch profit calculators to find margins.
  3. Day 10-14: Combine alching (300K/hr) with flipping (200K/day passive).

Total time to first bond: ~15-20 hours (1-2 weeks with market knowledge).

Warning: Bond Price Fluctuation

Bond prices spike 10-20% during major OSRS updates (new skills, Leagues, Deadman Mode). If you're close to affording a bond and there's an update announcement, buy immediately before the spike. Conversely, bonds are cheapest during content droughts.

5. 14-Day Acceleration Plan (Once You're a Member)

You have 14 days of membership. Your goal: use every hour efficiently to make the next bond easier. Don't wander around — follow this plan.

Days 4-7: Skill Foundation

  • Farming rush: Do Fairytale I (3.5K Farming XP), then Forgiveness of a Chaos Dwarf quest, then Garden of Tranquility. Get 32 Farming for ranarr herb runs. Each herb run = 100K+ profit in 5 minutes.
  • Agility to full Graceful: Rooftop agility courses from 1-60 (Canifis 40-60 for marks of grace). Full Graceful = ~10-12 hours. Wear it 90% of the time.
  • Slayer start: Begin training Slayer. It's slow at low levels but every level unlocks better tasks and profit later.

Days 8-14: Income Generation

  • Herb runs: Ranarr (32 Farming) or Toadflax (38 Farming). 5 runs/day = 500K-750K/day.
  • Birdhouse runs: Stack with herb runs. 50K/run, 5 runs/day = 250K/day.
  • Slayer: Train to 58+ for Cave Horrors (black mask drop = 1.5M). Each task is profit.
  • Daily battlestaves: Complete Varrock Medium Diary as soon as possible. 30 staves/day from Zaff = 60K-100K profit for 2 minutes of work.

By Day 14: You should have earned 10M-15M from herb runs, Slayer, and dailies alone — enough for your next bond with GP to spare.

7. Avoiding the Bond Treadmill Trap

The Bond Treadmill is when you spend so much time earning GP for your next bond that you never actually progress your account. You log in, grind for 14 days, buy a bond, repeat — never getting better gear or higher stats.

How to Escape the Treadmill

  1. Spend your first 14 days on account progression, not GP grinding. Quests, skills, diaries — these permanently increase your earning potential.
  2. Invest at least 50% of your extra GP into gear/skills. Buying a whip (3M) increases your Slayer GP/hr. Buying prayer levels for Piety (70 Prayer) increases your DPS everywhere. These are investments, not expenses.
  3. Use passive income for bond funding. Herb runs and Miscellania should cover 50-80% of a bond. Then you only need 2-5 hours of active money making per 14 days.
  4. Stack 2 bonds at once if possible. Having 28 days of membership removes the pressure. You can take days off from GP grinding to quest, skill, or explore.
  5. Consider paying for your first month with real money. $11 for 30 days of membership lets you skip the F2P bond grind entirely and jump straight to P2P money makers that earn 5-10x more GP/hr.

The Reality Check

At 1M GP/hr (easy in P2P), a 14M bond costs 14 hours of gameplay per 14 days. That's 1 hour/day. If you're playing 2+ hours/day, bond sustainability is trivial. The treadmill only traps players who spend every GP on cosmetics or die constantly at bosses.

9. FAQ

Q: How long does F2P to first bond realistically take?

About 25-35 hours of total gameplay, spread over 2-4 weeks for casual players. Dedicated players can do it in 1-2 weeks playing 3-4 hours/day. The grind tests your patience, but it's absolutely doable.

Q: Is it better to just pay for membership?

Honest answer: for your first month, yes. $11 for 30 days lets you skip the worst part (F2P bond grind) and jump straight to making 1M+/hr in P2P. After that, sustain with bonds indefinitely. If you absolutely can't spend real money, the F2P grind is viable — just slower.

Q: What's the fastest F2P money maker?

High alching items with good margins (300K-500K/hr) when you have starting cash. Ogress Warriors (200K/hr) if you don't. Telegrab Zamorak wines (200K-350K/hr) if you have 33 Magic and can compete with bots for spawns.

Q: Can I sustain bonds as a casual player (1 hour/day)?

Yes, but barely. One hour/day = 14 hours per bond cycle. You need to average 1M/hr — which means Vorkath/Zulrah or very efficient herb run stacking. At lower GP/hr rates, you'll be on the treadmill. Recommendation: stack 2 bonds at once (28 days) to reduce pressure.

Q: What if bond prices keep going up?

Bond prices in OSRS have historically trended upward (from 3M in 2015 to 12-15M in 2026), tracking inflation. Your GP/hr also increases as your account progresses. A 2026 Vorkath kill is worth more GP than a 2020 Vorkath kill. The ratio stays roughly balanced.

Q: Can Ironmen use bonds?

Ironmen can use bonds on another account and pay for membership, but they can't trade bonds on their Ironman. Ironmen can use bonds that were donated to them by a main account via the bond pouch system (right-click "Use" bond on Ironman).
