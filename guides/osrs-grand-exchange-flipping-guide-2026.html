  • Zero requirements. No combat level, no skill level, no quest completion needed. A level 3 account with 10K GP can start flipping immediately.
  • True passive income. Place buy offers, go do anything else (train Slayer, quest, log out), come back to collect filled orders. Your offers work 24/7 even when you are offline.

  • No RNG. Boss drops are random. Skilling profits fluctuate. Flipping profit is predictable — if your margin is 10 GP and you move 10,000 units, you make 100K GP minus tax. Simple math.

📊 How the Grand Exchange Works: Spread, Limits & Tax

To flip profitably, you must understand three core mechanics: the buy-sell spread, the 4-hour buy limit, and the 1% GE tax. These are the pillars of every single flip you will ever execute. Master these and you master the market.

③ 📐 The Buy-Sell Spread — Where Profit Lives

Every item on the Grand Exchange effectively has two prices at any given moment:

  • Instabuy Price (IB): The price impatient buyers pay to get an item immediately. This is always higher than the displayed GE guide price. When you see someone say "buying X for Y price instantly", Y is the instabuy price.
  • Instasell Price (IS): The price impatient sellers accept to sell an item immediately. This is always lower than the displayed GE guide price. When you see someone say "selling X for Y price instantly", Y is the instasell price.
  • Margin = Instabuy Price − Instasell Price. This difference is the gross profit per item before tax. Your job as a flipper is to buy at the instasell price and sell at the instabuy price.

Example: Zulrah scales have an instasell of 240 GP and an instabuy of 260 GP. The gross margin is 20 GP per scale. You buy 10,000 scales at 240 GP (total 2.4M). You sell those 10,000 scales at 260 GP (total 2.6M). Your gross profit is 200K GP. After 1% GE tax on the sale (2.6M × 1% = 26K), your net profit is 174K GP. That is one flip cycle. Do 3 cycles per day and you earn 522K GP from a single item.

④ ⏱️ The 4-Hour Buy Limit — Your Capacity Cap

Every tradeable item in OSRS has a buy limit — the maximum quantity you can purchase from the GE every 4 hours. The timer starts from your first purchase, not from when the offer completes. This means if you buy 5,000 cannonballs, you cannot buy more for 4 hours regardless of whether the offer filled immediately or took 2 hours.

⑤ 💰 The 1% GE Tax — Always Account for This

Since the 2022 game update, all GE sales incur a 1% tax, capped at 5M GP per item. This means when you sell an item for 10,000 GP, you receive 9,900 GP. The cap means items above 500M GP (like a Twisted Bow at 1.5B) only incur 5M tax instead of 15M, which actually helps high-end flippers.

The formula: Net Profit = (Sell Price × Quantity × 0.99) − (Buy Price × Quantity). For thin-margin items (under 2% ROI), the tax can consume 20-40% of your gross margin. Always calculate net profit before committing capital.

Tax Impact Example: You flip nature runes with an 8 GP gross margin. Sell price is 118 GP per rune. Tax per rune = 118 × 1% = 1.18 GP. Net margin = 8 − 1.18 = 6.82 GP. That is 14.75% of your gross margin eaten by tax. For items with 2 GP gross margins (like fire runes), the tax eats over 50% of your profit — you need to move enormous volumes to make it worthwhile.

📋 Your First Flip — Complete Step-by-Step Walkthrough

You have exactly 50K GP. Here is the exact 5-minute process that will earn you your first flipping profit. Follow these steps precisely — do not skip the margin check.

⑥ ① Prepare: Choose Your Target Items

Pick 3 items from the starter list that fit your capital: Fire Runes (~5 GP each), Pure Essence (~3 GP each), and Feathers (~2 GP each). These cost almost nothing per unit, have massive buy limits (25K-30K), and trade millions of units daily.

⑦ ② Margin Check Each Item

  1. Open the Grand Exchange and select Fire Runes.
  2. Set price to 20% above the displayed guide price. Buy 1 fire rune. Note the exact price it buys at — this is the instabuy price.
  3. Now sell that same fire rune at 20% below the guide price. Note the exact price it sells at — this is the instasell price.
  4. Calculate: Instabuy − Instasell = gross margin. Then subtract 1% of instabuy for tax. If the net margin is positive, the item is worth flipping.
  5. Repeat for pure essence and feathers. Write down all three margins.

⑧ ③ Allocate Your Capital

Split your 50K into three portions. Example: 20K on fire runes (20,000 / 5 = 4,000 runes), 15K on pure essence (15,000 / 3 = 5,000 essence), 15K on feathers (15,000 / 2 = 7,500 feathers). Place your buy offers at the instasell price you discovered.

⑨ ④ Wait (Do Something Else)

Your offers will fill in 15-60 minutes for these high-volume items. Go train combat, do a quest, or simply log out. The GE processes offers even when you are offline — your buy orders will be waiting when you return.

⑩ ⑤ Sell and Collect Profit

When your buy offers fill, withdraw the items. List them for sale at the instabuy price you noted earlier. They should sell within 15-45 minutes. Collect your GP. Calculate your net profit: (total sales revenue × 0.99) − total cost. Repeat this cycle 3-4 times per day.

🚀 Advanced Flipping Strategies for 5M+ Banks

Once your flipping bank exceeds 5M GP, you can deploy advanced strategies that generate significantly higher returns. These techniques require more patience and market awareness but can double or triple your daily profit compared to basic flipping.

⑤ 📉 Limit Order Stacking

Instead of placing one buy offer at the instasell price, place multiple buy offers at staggered prices below market. Example: You want to buy 10,000 coal at ~185 GP. Instead of one offer for 10,000 at 185 GP, place: 2,000 at 180 GP, 2,000 at 178 GP, 2,000 at 175 GP, 2,000 at 172 GP, and 2,000 at 170 GP. During natural market fluctuations, some of these lower offers will fill — giving you a better average buy price and wider margin when you sell.

⑥ 🌙 Overnight Flipping

Before logging off for the night, place buy offers for 4-6 items at 3-5% below the current instasell price. During off-peak hours (midnight to 6 AM game time), fewer players are online. Sellers who want quick GP are more likely to accept lower prices. When you log in the next morning, your orders will often have filled at below-market prices. List them for sale at normal instabuy prices and collect the extra margin.

⑦ 📅 Content Cycle Trading

OSRS item prices follow predictable patterns tied to game content. Learn these and you can anticipate margin changes:

  • Weekend spikes: PvM and skilling supplies rise Friday-Sunday when more players are active. Buy Wednesday, sell Friday evening.
  • New content releases: Items used in new content spike in the first 24 hours. If a new boss drops a new BiS weapon, the old BiS drops temporarily as players liquidate — buy the dip.
  • Double XP events: XP-giving supplies (bones, herbs, bars) spike during bonus XP events. Buy 2 weeks before, sell during the event.
  • League seasons: Main-game items dip during Leagues as players liquidate. Buy during League, sell after when players return to the main game.

📊 The 70-20-10 Rule for 5M+ Flippers

70% of capital in high-volume consumables (nature runes, Zulrah scales, dragon bones) — these are your safe, predictable earners with 1-2 hour turnover.

20% of capital in medium-volume items (barrows sets, godsword shards, zenyte jewelry) — these take 4-12 hours but deliver 50K-200K profit per flip.

10% of capital in low-volume, high-margin items (rares, cosmetic items, discontinued items) — these can take days to sell but margins of 5-20% are common.

This allocation ensures you always have GP flowing back while maximizing your total portfolio return.

⚠️ 7 Common Mistakes That Cost You GP

Every flipper makes mistakes. The smart ones learn from others rather than losing GP to discover these lessons personally. Here are the seven most costly errors and exactly how to avoid them:

1️⃣ Mistake: Overinvesting in a Single Item

The Problem: You find a great margin on dragon bones and dump your entire 2M flipping bank into them. The margin collapses overnight as a PKer clan floods the market with bones from the Chaos Altar. You are stuck holding 650 dragon bones now worth 200 GP less each — a 130K paper loss.

The Fix: Never put more than 25% of your flipping bank into any single item. With 2M capital, max 500K in one item. Split across 4+ items in different categories (runes + ores + food + PvM supplies). Margins collapse on individual items, never on everything at once.

2️⃣ Mistake: Flipping Low-Volume Items

The Problem: You try to flip a Bandos Chestplate with 5M GP. You cannot afford even one. Even if you could, only 50-200 trade per day — your offer might sit for 48+ hours. Your flipping bank is frozen in a GE slot doing nothing.

The Fix: Only flip items where you can afford 10+ units. If an item costs 500K, you need 5M+ before touching it. For items under 100K volume per day, the risk of being stuck is too high for beginners.

3️⃣ Mistake: Ignoring the GE Tax

The Problem: You calculate a 12 GP margin on coal and buy 10,000 units, expecting 120K profit. But the 1% tax on your 1.98M sale costs 19.8K. Your actual profit is 100.2K. On thin margins (under 5 GP), the tax can eat 30-50%+ of your profit.

The Fix: Always use this formula before buying: Net Margin = (Sell Price × 0.99) − Buy Price. If the result is under 1% of the sell price, the item is probably not worth your GE slot.

4️⃣ Mistake: Canceling Offers Too Early

The Problem: Your buy offer for 5,000 gold ore has not filled in 20 minutes. You panic, cancel, and try a higher price. Now you not only wasted 20 minutes but you are buying at a worse price, compressing your margin.

The Fix: High-volume items fill in 15-60 minutes during peak hours, 30-120 minutes during off-peak. Wait at least 90 minutes before canceling. If after 90 minutes your offer has not filled, the margin has shifted — cancel, re-margin check, and place a new offer at the updated instasell price.

5️⃣ Mistake: Panic Selling During a Dip

The Problem: You bought 5,000 Zulrah scales at 240 GP. The next day, the price drops to 228 GP. You panic and sell everything at 228 GP, locking in a 60K loss. Two days later, scales recover to 245 GP.

The Fix: High-volume items like Zulrah scales, dragon bones, and nature runes always recover from temporary dips within 24-72 hours. Unless a permanent game update has structurally devalued the item, hold your position. The only exception is if the dip exceeds 15% — then sell regardless and cut your losses.

6️⃣ Mistake: Flipping Right After Major Updates

The Problem: A big update drops. You see Bandos items dropped 8% and think "bargain!" But the update introduced Torva armor as a direct Bandos upgrade. That 8% "dip" was a permanent market correction. You are now holding depreciating assets.

The Fix: Never flip in the first 2 hours after any game update. Read the patch notes carefully. Understand why prices moved before committing capital. Wait 24 hours for the market to settle, then make informed decisions.

7️⃣ Mistake: Flipping Without a Stop-Loss Rule

The Problem: You hold 500K worth of death runes that have dropped 5% in value. You tell yourself "it will recover." It drops another 8%. Now you are down 65K. You could have sold at 5% loss, taken the 25K hit, and recovered that loss in 2 successful flips on other items.

The Fix: Set a hard stop-loss at 8-10% for every item you flip. If the instasell price drops more than 10% below your buy price, sell immediately. Take the small L, reinvest the capital, and earn it back on your next 2-3 flips. The biggest losses come from hoping a bad position will recover.

💡 The Golden Rule of Flipping: Your job is not to predict prices. Your job is to exploit the spread. You are not an investor betting on appreciation — you are a market maker providing liquidity. The moment you start holding items hoping for price increases, you have stopped flipping and started speculating. Those two activities require very different skills, and speculation is far riskier.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How much GP do I need to start flipping on the GE?

You can start with as little as 10K-50K GP. Fire runes, feathers, and pure essence cost 2-5 GP each with buy limits of 25,000-30,000. With 10K GP, you can buy 2,000 fire runes and flip them for ~1.8K profit per cycle. It is small, but it is real profit from a level 3 account. The key is to simply start — 10K becomes 12K becomes 15K, and compounding does the rest.

What is margin checking and how do I do it?

Margin checking is the process of testing an item's buy-sell spread. Step 1: Buy 1 unit of the item at 20% above the GE guide price (instant buy). Note the price. Step 2: Sell that 1 unit at 20% below the guide price (instant sell). Note the price. Step 3: Subtract sell price from buy price = gross margin. Step 4: Subtract 1% of the buy price (GE tax) = net margin. If net margin is at least 1% of the item's value, it is worth flipping. Always margin check before every bulk purchase — margins change constantly.

What are the best items for beginners to flip in 2026?

The top beginner items are: Fire Runes (1-3 GP margin, 25K buy limit), Cannonballs (3-8 GP margin, 10K limit), Nature Runes (5-15 GP margin, 25K limit), Coal (10-30 GP margin, 25K limit), and Pure Essence (1-2 GP margin, 30K limit). All have millions traded daily, very stable prices, and high buy limits. As your capital grows to 500K+, add Zulrah scales, dragon bones, and death runes to your rotation.

How does the 4-hour buy limit work in practice?

Every tradeable item has a maximum quantity you can buy every 4 hours. The timer starts from your first purchase of that specific item, not from when the offer finishes. Example: Cannonballs have a 10,000 buy limit. You buy 10,000 at 10:00 AM. The timer resets at 2:00 PM. If you buy 5,000 at 10:00 AM and another 5,000 at 10:30 AM, the timer still resets at 2:00 PM (4 hours after the first purchase). The key to scaling: use all 8 GE slots on different items to multiply your total capacity.

Can I lose money flipping on the Grand Exchange?

Yes, but it is preventable. The main ways beginners lose money: (1) buying at too high a price and getting stuck when the margin shrinks, (2) flipping items with very low daily volume (under 100K traded), (3) panic selling during temporary market dips, (4) not accounting for GE tax on thin margins, (5) overinvesting in a single item that crashes. Follow the rules in this guide — diversify, margin check every time, use stop-losses, and stick to high-volume items — and you will lose money on fewer than 5% of your flips.

How many GE slots should I use for maximum profit?

All 8 slots. Here is the optimal allocation: 6 slots for active flipping (3 buy offers + 3 sell offers), 1 slot for overnight/long-term hold, and 1 slot for personal purchases (gear, supplies, quest items). As your capital grows to 5M+, dedicate all 8 slots to flipping. Each additional slot increases your daily profit by 12-20% because you can diversify across more items and capture more buy limit capacity.

What is the best time of day to place offers?

For buy offers, the best time is late night to early morning (midnight-6AM GMT). Fewer players are online, so sellers are more likely to accept lower prices to get quick GP. For sell offers, the best time is peak hours (Friday evening-Sunday evening GMT). More players are actively buying, and impatient buyers pay premium prices for instant purchases. The weekend peak is the most profitable time for flipping — plan your cycles so you are selling during these hours.

Can I flip on an ironman or HCIM account?

No. Ironman accounts (including Hardcore and Ultimate Ironmen) cannot trade on the Grand Exchange or with other players. Flipping is a standard account activity only. If you are playing an ironman, your alternatives for consistent GP include: High Alchemy (high-level crafting or smithing items), Thieving (ardy knights at 55+ thieving), Slayer (alchable drops), and bossing (unique drops). Consider creating a standard alt account if you want to engage in GE flipping.

How does GE tax affect my flipping profit calculation?

The GE tax is 1% of the sale price, capped at 5M GP per item. For low-value items with thin margins, the tax impact is significant. Example: You flip fire runes with a 1 GP gross margin. The GE guide price is 5 GP. When you sell at 6 GP, the tax is 6 × 1% = 0.06 GP per rune. That 6% tax eats 6% of your margin. For high-value items like a Twisted Bow (1.5B), the 5M cap is a benefit — instead of paying 15M tax, you pay only 5M. Always calculate: Net Profit = (Sell Revenue × 0.99) − Total Cost.

How do I scale from 100K to 10M GP?

The scaling path follows your capital milestones: 100K → 500K: Flip fire runes, pure essence, feathers, and cowhide. Aim for 20-30% daily ROI. 500K → 2M: Move to nature runes, cannonballs, coal, and iron ore. Your ROI drops to 10-15% but absolute profit increases. 2M → 5M: Add Zulrah scales, death runes, gold ore, and dragon bones. ROI settles to 8-12% but you are earning 200K-500K per day. 5M → 10M: Introduce barrows items, zenyte jewelry, and godsword shards. ROI drops to 5-8% but daily profit hits 500K-1.5M. At each stage, reinvest all profit into the next tier of items. Never withdraw flipping profits for personal spending until you reach your target bank size.
