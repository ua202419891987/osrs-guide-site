OSRS Forestry Guide 2026 — Complete Woodcutting Update Guide

Forestry is the biggest Woodcutting update in OSRS history, released in June 2023. It transforms the skill from a solitary grind into a dynamic, group-based activity with random events, new rewards, and a social woodcutting experience. This comprehensive guide covers everything: how Forestry works, all 9 Forestry events, the Anima-infused Bark currency, shop rewards including the Lumberjack outfit and Felling Axe Handle, XP rates with and without Forestry, and the best woodcutting strategies for 2026.

Requirements & Getting Started with Forestry

Forestry is available to all members and free-to-play players (with limited events). There are no quest requirements to participate, though higher Woodcutting levels unlock better trees for faster training. Here is everything you need to know before you start.

Requirements Overview

How to Start Forestry

  1. Get a Forestry Kit — Speak to the Friendly Forester NPC found in Draynor Village (south of the bank), Seers' Village (near the pub), or Prifddinas. He will give you a Forestry Kit for free.
  2. Join a Forestry World — The official Forestry worlds are 388, 398, 434, 444, 487, and 510. World 444 is the most popular for members. These worlds have the highest player density for group bonuses.
  3. Chop trees with other players — Find a popular tree spot (oaks at Draynor for low levels, teaks at Prifddinas for high levels). The more players on the same tree, the faster you chop.
  4. Participate in Forestry events — Events spawn randomly while you chop. Complete them to earn Anima-infused Bark and bonus Woodcutting XP.
  5. Spend Bark at the shop — Return to the Friendly Forester to spend your Bark on rewards like the Felling Axe Handle, Log Basket, Lumberjack outfit, and more.
Pro Tip: Forestry Worlds Are Key

Forestry events and group bonuses only work in the designated Forestry worlds (388, 398, 434, 444, 487, 510). Playing on a non-Forestry world means you get no group bonus and no events. Always train on World 444 for the best experience.

Important Note: Woodcutting Guild

Forestry does not work inside the Woodcutting Guild. The group level bonus does not apply and Forestry events will not trigger. The Woodcutting Guild is still useful for its +7 invisible WC level boost and proximity to redwoods and a bank, but you will not get Forestry benefits there.

How Forestry Works — Core Mechanics

Forestry introduces three major changes to how Woodcutting works. Understanding these mechanics is essential to maximizing your XP and Bark earnings.

Group Woodcutting Level Bonus

When multiple players chop the same tree, everyone receives an invisible Woodcutting level boost. This bonus increases your chopping speed without affecting the XP per log.

Players on Same TreeInvisible WC Level BonusEffect on Chopping Speed
1 (Solo)+0Baseline speed
2+1Slightly faster
5+5Noticeably faster
10++10 (max)Maximum speed bonus

This bonus applies to all trees outside the Woodcutting Guild in Forestry worlds. The bonus caps at +10 levels with 10 or more players on the same tree.

Fixed Tree Timers

Trees now have a fixed lifetime rather than depleting after a set number of logs. Each tree type has a specific timer before it disappears and respawns:

  • Oak trees: ~30 second lifetime
  • Willow trees: ~30 second lifetime
  • Teak trees: ~30 second lifetime
  • Yew trees: ~114 second lifetime
  • Magic trees: ~234 second lifetime

This means the number of players chopping the same tree does not deplete it faster — everyone benefits equally.

Forestry Events

While chopping trees (oak or higher) in a Forestry world, events can spawn within 20 tiles of your position. Events give bonus Woodcutting XP and Anima-infused Bark. After the February 2024 update, events were adjusted so that:

  • You must chop at least 2 full trees before you can participate in a new event
  • You can only participate in events within 20 tiles of your position
  • Relogging has a 3-minute cooldown to prevent event farming

These restrictions prevent players from hopping between worlds to farm events, balancing the Bark economy.

Bark Earnings Rate (Post-Nerf)

After the February 2024 nerf, the average Bark earning rate is approximately 1,000 Bark per hour in a busy Forestry world. This means the 10,000 Bark needed for the Felling Axe Handle takes roughly 10 hours of active Forestry training to accumulate.

All 9 Forestry Events — Full Guide

There are 9 distinct Forestry events. Each has unique mechanics and rewards. Members have access to all 9 events; F2P players can only encounter 3 (Struggling Sapling, Rising Roots, and Friendly Ent).

Struggling Sapling

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree (oak or higher). A felled tree turns into a struggling sapling.

How to complete: Three resource piles appear nearby. Collect 3 mulch items from the piles and feed them to the sapling. The correct combination of mulch is random each event. If you give it the wrong mulch, it deals damage to the sapling. Using the RuneLite Forestry plugin will remember and display the correct combination.

Rewards: Large Woodcutting XP drop + Anima-infused Bark + 20 random leaves

Strategy tip: Take 3 of one type first to test, then move to a different pile if it rejects them. Enable public chat to see what other players have discovered.

Rising Roots

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. Roots erupt from the ground around the tree.

How to complete: Chop the roots with your axe. Some roots have a green tint (Anima-infused roots) — prioritise these as they give more XP and Bark. Clear enough roots to end the event.

Rewards: Woodcutting XP + Anima-infused Bark. Anima-infused roots give bonus rewards.

Strategy tip: Look for the green-tinted roots first. They stand out visually and are worth significantly more Bark.

Flowering Bush

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. 8 flowering bushes appear around your location.

How to complete: Click on bushes to find Strange Pollen. Only 2 of the 8 bushes have matching pollen that can cross-pollinate. Click one bush with Strange Pollen, then click the matching bush. The RuneLite plugin highlights which bushes match.

Rewards: Woodcutting XP + Anima-infused Bark

Strategy tip: If using RuneLite, the Forestry plugin highlights the correct pair automatically. Without plugins, try clicking different bushes systematically.

Enchantment Ritual

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. Coloured shape markings appear on the ground.

How to complete: Several tiles on the ground display coloured shapes. One tile is different from the others (different colour or different shape). Stand on the odd one out to complete the ritual.

Rewards: Woodcutting XP + Anima-infused Bark

Strategy tip: With the RuneLite Forestry plugin enabled, the correct tile is highlighted automatically. This is one of the fastest events to complete.

Friendly Ent

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. A friendly tree ent appears and speaks.

How to complete: The ent says a phrase that indicates how it wants to be trimmed. There are 4 different types of dialogue, each requiring a different trimming action (e.g., "back and sides" means trim the back and side branches). Read its dialogue and trim accordingly.

Rewards: Egg bird nest (used for Evil Chicken outfit) + Woodcutting XP + Bark

Strategy tip: Pay attention to the ent's exact words. Common phrases include "back and sides", "top only", "everywhere", or "just the front".

Pheasant Control

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. Pheasants appear guarding nests, along with the Freaky Forester NPC.

How to complete: Steal eggs from nests that are not guarded by a pheasant. If you try to steal from a guarded nest, the pheasant pecks you and you are stunned briefly. Give the stolen eggs to the Freaky Forester.

Rewards: Pheasant tail feathers + chance at Golden Egg (used for Pheasant pet transmog) + Bark

Strategy tip: Wait for the pheasants to move away from nests. Watch their patrol patterns and grab eggs when the coast is clear.

Poachers (Fox Event)

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. Poachers appear and set traps to catch a fox.

How to complete: Click on the fox traps to disarm them before the poachers catch the fox. Disarm enough traps to save the fox.

Rewards: Woodcutting XP + Anima-infused Bark + rare chance at Fox Whistle (fox pet transmog)

Strategy tip: Traps are scattered around the area. Move quickly and click each trap to disarm it. More players means faster trap disarming.

Beehive

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. An unfinished beehive appears.

How to complete: Add logs to the beehive to complete its construction. Each log added gives Construction XP as well as Woodcutting XP.

Rewards: Construction XP + Woodcutting XP + Bark

Strategy tip: This event consumes your logs. Many players skip this event because the rewards are lower than other events and it uses up inventory space.

Leprechaun

Trigger: Random while chopping any tree. A leprechaun appears.

How to complete: Talk to the leprechaun. He will offer to bank all logs in your inventory for free. After banking, he creates a rainbow on the ground. Stand on the rainbow tiles to receive bonus Woodcutting XP on your next log.

Rewards: Free banking service + temporary XP boost on next chopped log

Strategy tip: This event is excellent for extending your trips. Take advantage of the free banking to clear inventory space. The XP boost from standing on the rainbow is a nice bonus.

Event Priority Guide

Not all events are worth equal attention. Prioritise events that give Bark and XP efficiently:

  • High priority (do always): Struggling Sapling (massive XP), Enchantment Ritual (best Bark), Flowering Bush (good Bark), Rising Roots (good Bark)
  • Medium priority: Friendly Ent (unique egg nest), Poachers (fox whistle chance), Pheasant Control (golden egg chance)
  • Low priority: Leprechaun (only bank convenience), Beehive (consumes logs, low rewards)

Anima-infused Bark — The Only Currency

Anima-infused Bark (often shortened to Bark) is the sole currency used in Forestry. There are no other points, tokens, or currencies in the Forestry system.

How to Earn Bark

  • Primary source: Participating in Forestry events. Each event gives a variable amount of Bark based on your contribution.
  • Best event for Bark: The Enchantment Ritual event gives the most Bark per completion.
  • Average rate: Approximately 1,000 Bark per hour in a busy Forestry world (post-February 2024 balance changes).
  • Storage: Bark is automatically collected into your Forestry Kit. It can also be stored in the bank.

What Bark Buys

Bark is spent at the Friendly Forester shop, located in Draynor Village (south of the bank), Seers' Village (near the pub), Prifddinas, and Nemus Retreat. See the Forestry Shop section below for the full list of items and costs.

Bark Farming Strategy

The most efficient way to earn Bark is to train at a high-density Forestry world (World 444) at teak trees in Prifddinas. The high volume of events means you earn Bark passively while training. Focus on completing Enchantment Ritual and Struggling Sapling events for the best Bark-to-time ratio.

Forestry Kit & Setup

The Forestry Kit is a wearable item that stores tools and items needed for Forestry events. It is essential for participating in the activity.

Getting the Forestry Kit

Visit the Friendly Forester NPC and speak to him. He will give you a Forestry Kit for free. The kit is equipped in the cape slot or can be carried in your inventory.

What the Kit Does

  • Automatically collects Anima-infused Bark from events
  • Stores event-specific items (secateurs, rake, insect repellent, rope, butterfly net, bow and arrow, seeds)
  • Can be upgraded with a Log Brace and Sturdy Girdle to combine with a Log Basket for more inventory space

Equipped Items for Events

Your Forestry Kit should contain these items for event participation:

  • Secateurs — used for trimming the Friendly Ent and other interactions (bought from shop with Bark)
  • Rake — used for certain event mechanics
  • Insect Repellent — used for the Beehive event

Most event items are optional — many events only require clicking and do not need special items. The RuneLite Forestry plugin provides all the guidance you need.

RuneLite Plugins for Forestry

Install these plugins for the best Forestry experience:

  • Woodcutting plugin (built-in): Enable all Forestry event notifications and highlights
  • Tree Count (plugin hub): Shows how many players are on each tree so you can find the best grouping
  • Entity Hider: Hide other players to reduce visual clutter during events

Lumberjack Outfit — Exact XP Bonuses

The Lumberjack outfit is a skilling outfit that gives a passive XP boost when worn while Woodcutting. It can be obtained either from the Forestry shop (5,000 Bark) or from the Temple Trekking minigame. The pieces give the following bonuses:

Note: The Forestry Outfit available from the same shop is a cosmetic recolor of the Lumberjack outfit. It provides the same +2.5% XP bonus and requires you to already own the Lumberjack outfit before purchasing.

Smart Spending Order

Optimal Purchase Order for Efficiency

  1. Felling Axe Handle (10,000 Bark + logs): +10% XP per log is the single biggest boost to your training speed. Alternatively, you can sell the handle on the GE for ~2.6M GP.
  2. Log Basket (5,000 Bark + logs): Doubles your inventory capacity for logs, reducing bank trips significantly.
  3. Lumberjack Outfit (5,000 Bark): +2.5% XP bonus that stacks with all other XP modifiers.
  4. Twitchers Gloves (~5,000 Bark): Only after you have the essential upgrades above.
  5. Cosmetics: Forestry Outfit, Funky Shaped Log, etc. — only after functional upgrades.

Total Bark needed for functional upgrades: ~20,000 Bark + additional log requirements. At ~1,000 Bark/hr, this takes roughly 20 hours of Forestry training.

Felling Axe — Complete Guide

The Felling Axe is a two-handed woodcutting axe introduced with Forestry. It offers the best Woodcutting XP in the game but requires Forester's Rations as fuel.

What is the Felling Axe?

The Felling Axe is a two-handed weapon that provides +10% Woodcutting XP per log compared to a standard one-handed axe. However, it has a 20% chance to not produce a log (you still get the XP). It requires Forester's Rations as fuel — each ration is consumed per log cut.

How to Get a Felling Axe

There are two tiers of Felling Axe:

AxeHow to MakeSpeed vs Standard
Dragon Felling AxeUse a Felling Axe Handle on a Dragon AxeSame speed as Dragon Axe, +10% XP per log
Crystal Felling AxeUse a Felling Axe Handle on a Crystal Axe near a singing bowl (requires Song of the Elves)4.5% faster than Dragon Felling Axe, +10% XP per log

The Felling Axe Handle costs 10,000 Bark + 500 noted oak logs from the Friendly Forester shop. It is also tradeable on the Grand Exchange (approximately 2.6M GP).

Forester's Rations

Felling Axes require Forester's Rations as fuel. One ration is consumed per log cut. Rations are made by combining leaves (from the Struggling Sapling event) with cooked fish:

  • Each combination gives a certain number of rations based on the leaf tier and fish tier
  • Formula: (leaf tier) x (fish tier multiplier) = rations produced
  • Example: Magic leaves (tier 12) x Cooked shark (multiplier 3) = 36 rations
  • Rations can also be bought from the Grand Exchange

Without Forester's Rations, the Felling Axe cannot be used to chop trees.

Felling Axe vs Standard Axe — Worth It?

Yes, absolutely. At 80K XP/hr, +10% means an extra 8K XP/hr. Over the course of training 1-99 Woodcutting, this saves approximately 15-20 hours. The 10,000 Bark cost (~10 hours of training) is quickly recouped in saved training time. If you are serious about Woodcutting, the Felling Axe is the single best upgrade available.

The Bottom Line: Forestry is 50-80% Faster

In a busy Forestry world at teaks, you can achieve 80K-100K XP/hr. This is roughly 1.5x to 1.8x faster than traditional methods. The key factors are: (1) group level bonus from multiple players, (2) event XP bonuses from Forestry events, (3) the Felling Axe +10% XP boost, and (4) the Lumberjack outfit +2.5% XP boost.

If Woodcutting 99 is your goal, Forestry cuts the grind from ~325 hours to ~160 hours or less. This is a significant improvement, though not as extreme as the inflated rates sometimes claimed online.

Best Strategies for Efficient Woodcutting Training

Now that you understand the mechanics, here are the optimized strategies for every training scenario.

Max XP Strategy (Active, 80K-100K XP/hr)

Location: Prifddinas teak trees (requires Song of the Elves quest)
World: 444 (Forestry world)
Requirements: 35+ Woodcutting, Dragon/Crystal Felling Axe, Lumberjack outfit, Log Basket

  1. Equip your Felling Axe with Forester's Rations loaded
  2. Go to Prifddinas teaks — the area east of the bank has multiple teak trees
  3. Find a tree with 5+ players on it to maximise the group level bonus
  4. Drop teak logs (shift-click drop) for maximum XP — do not bank teaks as they are worth very little
  5. Watch for Forestry events and participate in every Struggling Sapling, Enchantment Ritual, and Flowering Bush event
  6. Re-stock Forester's Rations as needed

Expected results: 80K-100K XP/hr depending on your Woodcutting level and event frequency.

Semi-AFK Strategy (50K-65K XP/hr)

Location: Seers' Village yew trees or Prifddinas yews
Requirements: 60+ Woodcutting, any Felling Axe or standard Dragon Axe

  1. Chop yew trees at a Forestry world — they have a ~2 minute lifetime before respawning
  2. Use a Log Basket to hold additional logs, reducing bank trips
  3. Bank the yew logs or note them with the Leprechaun event when it spawns
  4. Participate in events only if they are right next to you

Expected results: 50K-60K XP/hr while requiring attention only every 60-120 seconds.

Pure AFK Strategy (50K-60K XP/hr)

Location: Redwood Trees (Woodcutting Guild, north-west area) or Blisterwood Trees (Darkmeyer)
Requirements: 90+ WC for redwoods, or Sins of the Father quest + 62+ WC for blisterwood

Forestry events do not apply inside the Woodcutting Guild or at blisterwood trees. These methods use standard Woodcutting mechanics without any Forestry benefits. They are the best options for players who want to train while watching videos or working.

  • Redwoods: Each tree lasts ~4 minutes. ~55K XP/hr.
  • Blisterwood: Tree never depletes. ~60K XP/hr. Requires quest completion.

Profit Strategy

Location: Yew trees (Seers' Village) or Magic trees (Myths' Guild / Seers')
Requirements: 60+ WC for yews, 75+ for magics

  • Chop yew or magic logs and bank them
  • Yew logs: ~800 GP each / Magic logs: ~1,200 GP each
  • Sell Felling Axe Handles on the GE (~2.6M GP each) — this is the main profit from Forestry
  • At 1,000 Bark/hr, you earn a Felling Axe Handle worth ~2.6M GP every ~10 hours
  • Total profit: ~200K-300K GP/hr from logs + Bark value combined

Ironman Strategy

Ironmen benefit greatly from Forestry because it provides access to the Lumberjack outfit without the randomness of Temple Trekking:

  • Lumberjack Outfit (5,000 Bark): Guaranteed drop from the shop — no RNG involved. This is the primary reason ironmen should do Forestry.
  • Log Basket (5,000 Bark + logs): Significantly extends Woodcutting trips for ironmen who need logs for Construction.
  • Lost bags from events: The Poachers event gives access to a fox whistle for transmog.
  • Note: Felling Axe Handle also requires 500 noted oak logs, which ironmen must collect themselves.

Forestry FAQs — Everything You Need to Know

Q: What level Woodcutting do I need for Forestry?

A: You need at least level 15 Woodcutting (oak trees) to trigger Forestry events. However, you can receive the Forestry Kit from the Friendly Forester at any level. The best XP rates come from teak trees (level 35+).

Q: What is the currency for Forestry rewards?

A: Anima-infused Bark (shortened to Bark) is the only currency. There are no Event Points, Timber Points, or other tokens. Bark is earned by participating in Forestry events and is spent at the Friendly Forester shop.

Q: Is Forestry better than 1-tick teaks for XP?

A: Forestry group bonuses combined with the Felling Axe can match or slightly exceed 1-tick teak methods with far less effort. With Forestry, you can achieve 80K-100K XP/hr at teaks without intense clicking. For most players, Forestry is the better option.

Q: What is the best Forestry world?

A: World 444 is the most popular Forestry world for members. Other Forestry worlds include 388, 398, 434, 487, and 510. World 301 is the recommended F2P Forestry world.

Q: How long does it take to save up for a Felling Axe Handle?

A: The Felling Axe Handle costs 10,000 Bark + 500 noted oak logs. At approximately 1,000 Bark per hour, this takes roughly 10 hours of active Forestry training. The handle can also be purchased from the Grand Exchange for ~2.6M GP.

Q: Does the Lumberjack Outfit stack with other XP boosts?

A: Yes. The Lumberjack outfit +2.5% XP bonus stacks multiplicatively with other XP modifiers including the Woodcutting cape (+10%), Clan avatar (+1-6%), and bonus XP events. The Felling Axe +10% XP per log bonus also stacks.

Q: Do Forestry events work in the Wilderness?

A: No. Forestry events do not trigger in the Wilderness. Forestry is designed for safe zones only. You can still chop trees in the Wilderness normally, but you will not receive any Forestry benefits.

Q: Can I do Forestry on my own (solo) and still get good XP?

A: Solo Forestry is possible but less efficient. Without the group level bonus, your chopping speed returns to normal rates. Events still trigger solo, but less frequently. You earn Bark at a reduced rate (~500-700 Bark/hr solo vs ~1,000 Bark/hr in groups).

Q: What happened in the February 2024 Forestry nerf?

A: Jagex adjusted Forestry to prevent event farming. Key changes: (1) you must chop 2 full trees before you can participate in a new event, (2) you can only join events within 20 tiles of your position, and (3) switching worlds has a 3-minute cooldown. Before this nerf, players could earn 5,000+ Bark per hour by hopping between events.

Q: Are any Forestry rewards tradeable on the Grand Exchange?

A: Yes. The Felling Axe Handle is tradeable (~2.6M GP). Pheasant tail feathers and golden eggs are also tradeable. The Lumberjack outfit, Log Basket, and Forestry Outfit are untradeable and must be earned through Forestry.

Q: Does the Log Basket work with the Forestry Kit?

A: Yes, but you need a Log Brace and Sturdy Girdle (both from the Friendly Forester shop) to combine the Log Basket with the Forestry Kit into a single worn item. This frees up inventory space and is a worthwhile quality-of-life upgrade.

Q: What is the quickest way to earn Bark?

A: The most efficient method is training teaks at Prifddinas on World 444 with a full group. The Enchantment Ritual event gives the most Bark per completion. Prioritise this event over others for faster Bark accumulation. The Struggling Sapling event is also excellent for Bark and gives massive bonus XP.
