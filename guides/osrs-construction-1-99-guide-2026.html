OSRS Construction Training Guide 1-99

Cheapest, Fastest, and Most AFK Methods for 2026

Updated: June 29, 2026 Skills

Quick Verdict

If you want the fastest possible XP and have deep pockets, spam Mahogany Tables (950k+ XP/hr) — but expect to burn through 150M+ GP. For the best balance of cost and speed, Oak Larders (450k XP/hr, ~30M GP total) remain the community favorite in 2026. On a budget? Oak Dungeon Doors (~300k XP/hr) are highly AFK and cost around 15M GP from 1-99. Mythical Cape Racks offer a viable mid-cost alternative with decent AFK time. Bottom line: set aside at least 15-30M GP and a few focused sessions to unlock the incredible utility of a maxed Player-Owned House.

🏰 Why Train Construction?

Construction is one of the most impactful skills in Old School RuneScape. A maxed Player-Owned House (POH) gives you access to teleport portals, a rejuvenating pool, an altar for prayer restoration, a fairy ring, a spirit tree network, and much more. These conveniences save you thousands of hours over your OSRS journey.

However, Construction is also one of the most expensive skills to train. Expect to spend anywhere from 15M GP (budget methods) to over 150M GP (fastest methods) to reach 99. There is no profitable way to train Construction — every method costs money. The key is choosing the method that best fits your budget and playstyle.

Hot Tip: Complete the Royal Trouble quest and keep your approval rating above 95% to collect free Mahogany Logs from Miscellania. This can reduce your costs by 15-20% over the course of training.

① Key Benefits of 99 Construction

  • Max Pool: Restores HP, Prayer, Spec, Run Energy, and cures poison/disease in one click.
  • Jewellery Box: Unlimited teleports to Edgeville, Varrock, Camelot, Ardougne, Watchtower, and more.
  • Portal Nexus: Direct teleports to every major spellbook teleport destination.
  • Fairy Ring + Spirit Tree: Combined access from your POH garden.
  • Occult Altar: Switch between standard, ancient, and lunar spellbooks instantly.

② What You Need Before Starting

  • GP: Minimum 10-15M (for Oak Larders to 99). Have 30M+ for comfort.
  • House Portal: You need a house in Rimmington or Pollnivneach (buy from Estate Agent).
  • Sawmill: Use the Sawmill Operator or your own Sawmill in your POH (unlocked at 35 Construction).
  • Teleports: Games Necklace (Barbarian Assault teleport) or Teleport to House tabs/runes.
  • Rings of Duelling: To bank at Castle Wars/Clan Wars and teleport back.

③ Equipment & Inventory Setup

  • Hammer — always in your inventory while building.
  • Saw — required to build most furniture.
  • Teleport runes or tabs for quick bank trips (or use the POH Portal Nexus).
  • Optional: Construct. Cape teleport after 99, but we are covering methods for everyone.

① Route Tiers Ranked

  • Beginner (0-50): Crude Wooden Chairs (1-33) then Oak Chairs (33-50). Minimal investment (~200k GP).
  • Budget (50-99): Oak Dungeon Doors (~15M GP total). Slow but very cheap.
  • Standard (50-99): Oak Larders (~30M GP total). The go-to for most players.
  • Rich (50-99): Mahogany Tables (~155M GP total). For those with cash to burn.

② Butler Usage Guide

To achieve peak XP rates, you need a butler. Here is how to use them effectively:

  • Build 3-4 Oak Larders at once, then have your butler fetch more planks.
  • Keep your bank organized: only have the required planks in your bank (un-noted).
  • Use the "remove and build" method — remove the built item and immediately build the next one.
  • With the Demon Butler (47 Construction), he can fetch 20 planks at a time vs. Rick's 12.

③ Hotspots & Layout Planning

Before mass-producing items, make sure your POH has the correct rooms and hotspots. For Oak Larders you need a Kitchen. For Oak Doors you need a Dungeon room. For Mahogany Tables you need a Dining Room or Aquarium room. Plan your layout in advance to avoid rebuilding rooms.

Oak Larders — The Community Standard

Oak Larders have been the go-to Construction training method for years. They offer an excellent balance of speed and cost, making them accessible to most mid-level players. You need 33 Construction to start and a Kitchen in your POH.

① Requirements

  • 33 Construction (boostable with a Cup of Tea or Spicy Stew).
  • A Kitchen in your Player-Owned House.
  • Oak Planks (8 per Larder).
  • Butler (Rick or Demon) to speed up bank trips.

② Step-by-Step Method

  • Step 1: Fill inventory with 8 Oak Planks (or let butler fetch them).
  • Step 2: Build Oak Larder on the Kitchen hotspot (requires 8 Oak Planks + Hammer + Saw).
  • Step 3: Remove the Larder (right-click or use the remove option).
  • Step 4: Immediately build another Larder on the same hotspot.
  • Step 5: Call your butler to bring more planks when you run out.
  • Repeat until 99. That is roughly 15,500 Oak Larders total.

③ Cost & XP Breakdown

  • Wood cost: ~260 GP per Oak Plank. Each Larder costs ~2,080 GP in planks.
  • XP per Larder: 480 XP (8 planks x 60 XP each).
  • XP per hour: ~450k with Demon Butler, ~400k with Rick.
  • Planks needed: ~124,000 for 1-99 (13,034,000 XP / 60 XP per plank).
  • Total GP cost: ~32M GP (at 260 GP per plank).

Mahogany Tables — Max XP per Hour

If you have a fat bank and want 99 Construction in a single day, Mahogany Tables are your ticket. At nearly 1M XP per hour, this is the fastest Construction training method by a wide margin. Expect to spend around 155M GP from 50 to 99.

① Requirements

  • 50 Construction to build Mahogany Dining Tables (or use Eagle Lecterns at lower levels).
  • Dining Room or Aquarium room in POH.
  • 6 Mahogany Planks per table.
  • Demon Butler strongly recommended (level 47 Construction required).

② Step-by-Step Method

  • Step 1: Load your bank with noted Mahogany Logs. Un-note at the Sawmill or use pre-cut planks.
  • Step 2: Have your butler fetch 20 Mahogany Planks per trip.
  • Step 3: Build a Mahogany Dining Table (6 planks). Remove it. Build again.
  • Step 4: Repeat on the same hotspot. Each table gives 840 XP.
  • Step 5: At 950k XP/hr, you will hit 99 in about 12-13 hours from level 50.

③ Cost & XP Breakdown

  • Plank cost: ~1,600 GP per Mahogany Plank.
  • Each table: 6 planks = ~9,600 GP per 840 XP.
  • XP per hour: ~950k (peak concentration), ~850k relaxed.
  • Total planks for 50-99: ~93,000 planks.
  • Total GP cost: ~150M GP (50-99).
Money-Saving Tip: If you own a Demon Butler, he can take noted Mahogany Logs to the Sawmill and bring back planks. This saves the step of pre-converting logs, though it reduces XP/hr slightly.

Oak Dungeon Doors — The AFK Method

For players who prefer a more relaxed training experience, Oak Dungeon Doors offer significantly more AFK time than Larders or Tables. Each door takes longer to build, giving you 15-25 seconds of AFK per cycle. The XP rate is lower but the GP cost is roughly half.

① Requirements

  • 74 Construction (boostable via Spicy Stew or Crystal Saw from the Hand in the Sand quest).
  • Dungeon room in POH (requires a Doorman NPC or build the room itself).
  • 10 Oak Planks per door.
  • Demon Butler recommended for efficient door building.

② Step-by-Step Method

  • Step 1: Build a Dungeon room in your POH with a door hotspot.
  • Step 2: Call butler to bring 20 Oak Planks (enough for 2 doors).
  • Step 3: Build an Oak Door (10 planks, 600 XP).
  • Step 4: Remove the door and build it again on the same hotspot.
  • Step 5: Each build cycle gives you ~15-20 seconds of down time — great for alt-tabbing.

③ Cost & XP Breakdown

  • Oak Plank cost: ~260 GP each. Each door = 10 planks = ~2,600 GP.
  • XP per door: 600 XP (10 x 60 XP).
  • XP per hour: ~300k relaxed, ~350k try-hard.
  • Total doors needed: ~21,700 for 1-99.
  • Total GP cost: ~56M GP... wait — 21,700 doors at 2,600 GP each sounds high.

Correction: Using this method from 74 to 99 (roughly 10M XP) requires ~16,700 doors at ~43M GP. Many players start with Oak Larders (until 74) then switch to Doors for the final stretch. Total from 1-99: ~15M GP at current prices.

Mythical Cape Rack — Budget Alternative

The Mythical Cape Rack was introduced as a solid mid-tier method. It requires the Dragon Slayer II quest and offers slightly better XP than Doors at a reasonable material cost.

① Requirements

  • 50 Construction (boostable).
  • Completed Dragon Slayer II quest.
  • A Costume Room in your POH with a Cape Rack hotspot.
  • 5 Teak Planks per Cape Rack.

② Step-by-Step Method

  • Step 1: Build a Costume Room in your POH. Install a Cape Rack.
  • Step 2: Have your butler bring Teak Planks from the bank.
  • Step 3: Build a Mythical Cape Rack (5 Teak Planks, 320 XP).
  • Step 4: Remove and rebuild. Each cycle takes about 5-8 seconds.
  • Step 5: XP rates reach ~380k/hr with good focus and a Demon Butler.

③ Cost & XP Breakdown

  • Teak Plank cost: ~500 GP each. Each rack = 5 planks = ~2,500 GP.
  • XP per rack: 320 XP (5 x 64 XP per Teak Plank).
  • XP per hour: ~380k.
  • Total planks for 50-99: ~40,700 planks.
  • Total GP cost: ~20M GP (50-99).
Quest Requirement: Dragon Slayer II is a Grandmaster quest with high skill requirements (70+ in multiple skills). If you have not completed it, stick with Oak Larders or Doors instead.

Player House Layout & Key Upgrades

A well-designed Player-Owned House is the single most valuable quality-of-life upgrade in OSRS. Here is the optimal layout and the most important upgrades to prioritize at each level.

① Essential Rooms (Build Order)

  • Portal Room (level 1): Start with your portal. Upgrade to a Portal Nexus later.
  • Garden (level 1): Place your Fairy Ring and Spirit Tree here.
  • Kitchen (level 5): Needed for Oak Larders training. Later add a larder for convenient food.
  • Workshop (level 15): For the Sawmill and other workbenches.
  • Dining Room (level 25): Bell-pull for butler. Upgrade to Mahogany Tables for training.
  • Costume Room (level 40): Cape Racks and armour cases.
  • Study (level 40): Portal Nexus, Lecterns for spellbook swapping.
  • Chapel (level 45): Altar for prayer restoration.
  • Achievement Gallery (level 75): Max Pool, Jewellery Box, Combat Ring.

② Key Upgrades by Level

  • Level 37: Gilded Altar — fastest Prayer training in the game (host gilded altars).
  • Level 45: Oak Lectern — switch spellbooks at home.
  • Level 65: Portal Nexus — all major teleports in one room.
  • Level 72: Teak Garden Bench — cheapest way to build a Fairy Ring.
  • Level 78: Jewellery Box (Opal) — start recharging jewellery at home.
  • Level 82: Ornate Jewellery Box — unlimited teleport jewellery.
  • Level 85: Max Pool (Ornate Rejuvenation Pool) — full restore in one click.
  • Level 90: Occult Altar (or go to 95 for the full Nexus).

③ Optimal End-Game Layout

For a maxed house (2D east-wing / west-wing format):

  • Portal Room → Portal Nexus (Study) + Jewellery Box (Achiev. Gallery) + Max Pool (Achiev. Gallery).
  • Gardens → Fairy Ring + Spirit Tree in Gilded Garden.
  • Chapel → Gilded Altar with burners for 350% prayer XP.
  • Menagerie → Place this at the edge to keep pets visible without clutter.

① Level 1-33: The Early Grind

Start with Crude Wooden Chairs in your Parlour until level 8, then switch to Wooden Bookcases until level 14. At level 14, build Oak Chairs. This phase costs about 200k GP and takes roughly 30 minutes. At level 33, you unlock Oak Larders — the real training begins.

② Level 33-74: The Middle Grind

Build Oak Larders exclusively from 33 to 74. This is roughly 4.5M XP and takes about 10 hours. You will go through approximately 75,000 Oak Planks at a cost of ~19.5M GP. Key milestones: Demon Butler at 47 (massive speed boost), and the Ability to build Oak Dungeon Doors at 74.

③ Level 74-99: The Final Stretch

From 74 to 99, switch to Oak Dungeon Doors for AFK training or stay on Oak Larders for speed. This phase requires about 8.5M XP. With Oak Doors, expect ~30 hours of semi-AFK gameplay. Total cost: ~15M GP. With Mahogany Tables, expect 9 hours at ~100M GP. Choose based on your budget and AFK needs.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the fastest way to 99 Construction in OSRS?

Mahogany Tables are the fastest method in 2026, offering ~950k XP/hr. You need 50 Construction and a healthy bank (~150M GP from 50-99).

Q: Is Construction still one of the most expensive skills in 2026?

Yes. Construction remains one of the costliest skills, ranking alongside Herblore and Prayer. Even the cheapest method (Oak Dungeon Doors) costs ~15M GP from 1-99.

Q: What should I build in my POH first?

Focus on building a Portal Room for teleports, then a Kitchen for training, then a Study for the Portal Nexus (level 65+). The Max Pool (Ornate Rejuvenation Pool) at level 85 is the single most important end-game upgrade.

Q: Can I make money with Construction?

No. Construction is a pure money-sink skill. You cannot profit from it directly. However, a maxed POH saves you huge amounts of money on teleport runes, food, prayer potions, and stamina potions over time.

Q: How much GP do I need for 99 Construction?

Minimum ~15M GP (Oak Dungeon Doors), standard ~30M GP (Oak Larders), premium ~150M+ GP (Mahogany Tables). Prices depend on current plank market rates.

Q: Is the Demon Butler worth it?

Yes. The Demon Butler (47 Construction) fetches 20 planks per trip compared to Rick's 12. This increases XP/hr by roughly 15-20% and is well worth the upfront cost of building the Servant's Quarters and paying the 10k fee.

Q: What is the best AFK Construction method?

Oak Dungeon Doors offer the most AFK time at 15-25 seconds per cycle. They require 74 Construction and a Dungeon room. The XP rate is ~300k/hr but requires minimal attention.

Q: Do I need Dragon Slayer II for the Myth Cape Rack method?

Yes. The Mythical Cape Rack is locked behind Dragon Slayer II, a Grandmaster quest. If you do not meet the requirements, stick with Oak Larders or Oak Doors.

Q: Can I boost my Construction level?

Yes. You can use a Cup of Tea (+3) or Spicy Stew (up to +6 random boost). The Crystal Saw (Hand in the Sand quest) gives an invisible +3 boost specifically for saw-related items.

Q: Is 99 Construction worth it in 2026?

Absolutely. The Max Pool, Ornate Jewellery Box, Portal Nexus, and Occult Altar are game-changing unlocks. Every experienced player considers 99 Construction a top priority for account progression.
