OSRS Stuck Progression Guide 2026: Break Through Mid-Game Plateaus

You have base 70s combat stats, Barrows gloves, a fire cape, and a dragon defender. Maybe 100-200 quest points. But you log in and don't know what to do next. This is the most common stage to feel stuck in OSRS. This guide gives you a clear framework to identify where you are, set achievable goals, and build momentum toward raids and endgame content.

⚡ Quick Verdict

Feeling stuck is not a problem with your account — it's a problem with your goals. The fastest way to get unstuck is to pick ONE concrete objective (like "complete Song of the Elves" or "reach 70 Prayer") and pursue it relentlessly until done. Having a single direction transforms aimless logging-in into purposeful progression.

1. How to Know If You're Stuck

Being stuck doesn't mean your account is bad. It means you've hit the mid-game transition — the point where early-game goals (Barrows gloves, fire cape) are done, but endgame goals (raids, inferno, max cape) feel impossibly far away. Here are the signs:

① Warning Signs You're Stuck

SymptomWhat It Looks Like
Login loopsYou log in, stand at the Grand Exchange for 10 minutes, log out. Repeat tomorrow.
Bank analysis paralysisYou keep looking at your bank trying to figure out what to sell or buy. You can't decide.
Method hoppingYou try one money-making method for 30 minutes, switch to another, then a skill, then back to a third money method. Nothing sticks.
Guide fatigueYou've read 20 guides but haven't acted on any of them. Information without execution.
Comparison anxietyYou see others doing Corrupted Gauntlet and ToA, but your stats/gear aren't "ready" so you don't start.
💡 The "Logging In With Purpose" Test

Here's a simple test: when you log in tomorrow, do you know exactly what you're going to do for the first 30 minutes? If the answer is no, you're stuck. The fix is to decide before you log in what you'll work on.

2. The OSRS Goal-Setting Framework

Most players fail at mid-game progression because they set goals that are too big ("get a quest cape," "save 200M for gear"). The solution is to break everything into three tiers:

② The Three-Tier Goal System

🎯 Tier 1: Your Big Goal (1-3 months)

This is your North Star — the thing you're ultimately working toward. Examples:

  • Complete Song of the Elves (requires 70 in 8 skills)
  • Reach 175+ Quest Points for Recipe for Disaster completion
  • Achieve 1,500 Total Level
  • Earn Quest Cape

Rule: Only pick ONE big goal at a time. Having too many big goals creates analysis paralysis.

📅 Tier 2: Weekly Milestones (7-10 days)

Break your big goal into weekly chunks. If your big goal is Song of the Elves:

  • Week 1: 70 Mining (from 50) — 8 hours at Motherlode Mine
  • Week 2: 70 Smithing (from 55) — Blast Furnace gold bars
  • Week 3: 70 Agility (from 58) — Seers' Village rooftop
  • And so on until all 8 skills are ready

✅ Tier 3: Today's Task (1 session)

What you'll do next time you log in. Be specific:

  • ❌ Bad: "Train Mining today"
  • ✅ Good: "Do 4 inventories at Motherlode Mine upper level — about 45 minutes — aim for 35,000 XP"
  • ❌ Bad: "Make some money"
  • ✅ Good: "Do 2 herb runs (5 minutes each) and then 1 hour of Barrows"

🎯 Example: Your First Week Unstuck Plan

Day 1: Pick ONE big goal (e.g., "Complete Song of the Elves within 6 weeks")
Day 1: Look at the skill requirements. Which is your lowest? (e.g., 50 Mining)
Day 2: Spend 1 hour at Motherlode Mine. Stop when the hour is up.
Day 3: Repeat. Do NOT check how much XP you need for 70 — just do the hour.
Day 7: Check progress. You've done 7 hours of Mining. That's 200k+ XP. At this rate, 70 Mining takes 3-4 weeks.

3. Quest Priority: The Path to Quest Cape

Quests are the single best way to give your account direction. Every major quest unlocks significant content, training methods, or money-making opportunities. Here's the priority order for a stuck mid-game player:

③ The Mid-Game Quest Roadmap

How to Approach Quest Grinds

Don't try to do all quest prerequisites in one go. Instead:

  1. Pick ONE quest from the priority list above
  2. Check the skill requirements on the OSRS Wiki
  3. Write down the 2-3 skills you need to train to meet the requirements
  4. Use the Quest Helper plugin on RuneLite for step-by-step guidance
  5. Do the quest in 1-2 sessions once you have the requirements
  6. Celebrate each quest completion — they're real achievements
💡 Quest XP Rewards Are OP

Many quests give massive XP rewards that can skip entire levels. Examples: Waterfall Quest (13,750 Attack & Strength XP), The Grand Tree (18,400 Attack XP), Tree Gnome Village (11,450 Attack XP). Use the OSRS Wiki's "Quest XP Rewards" page to see which quests can boost your skills with minimal effort.

4. Skill Roadmap: What to Train and Why

Training skills without purpose is one of the main reasons players feel stuck. Every skill you train should unlock something useful. Here's the mid-game priority:

④ Combat Skills: Always Prioritize

Ranged85+Most money-making bosses (Vorkath, Zulrah, CG) rely on Ranged. Get this up first.
Magic85+Trident of the Swamp unlocks effective magic DPS. Needed for Zulrah, CoX, ToA.
Melees80+ Attack/Strength/DefenceOsmumten's Fang (9M) at 82 Attack is the best mid-game melee weapon.

⑤ Utility Skills: The Unlocks That Matter

Slayer69+ → 85+69 for MM2. 85+ for profit (Abyssal Demons, Cave Kraken, Cerberus, Hydra).

🎯 The 6-Week Skill Plan

Here's a realistic 6-week plan for a stuck player (2-3 hours per day):

Week 1-2: Farming runs (daily, 5 min each). Use trees + fruit trees for fast XP. Go from 32 → 55 in two weeks with almost no effort. Also train Ranged to 85 via Slayer or NMZ.

Week 3-4: Focus on Prayer. Collect dragon bones (or buy them) and use the Chaos Altar (350% XP, Wilderness risk) or Gilded Altar. Get from 60 → 74 for Rigour.

Week 5: Buy Rigour. Train Magic to 85 through burst/barrage Slayer tasks or Stun/Alching. Your DPS will skyrocket with Rigour online.

Week 6: Construction push. Get POH upgrades — 50 Construction is cheap (~500K GP) for massive QoL. Set up portal rooms to Varrock, Falador, and Camelot.

💡 The 30-Minute Skill Rule

Dreading a skill? Commit to just 30 minutes of it. Set a timer. Do the most efficient method for exactly 30 minutes. When the timer goes off, stop — even if you're enjoying it. This builds consistency without burnout. The next day, you'll be more likely to do another 30 minutes. Over a month, 30 minutes/day = 15 hours of training.

5. Money Making Foundations

One reason players get stuck is they don't have GP for gear upgrades. Here's the money-making ladder for the mid-game player:

⑥ Tier 1: Passive Income (5-10 min/day, 1M+ GP/day)

Before you do anything else, set up these passive money-makers:

  • Herb runs: 5+ patches (Farming guild, Morytania, Trollheim, Weiss, Catherby, Ardougne, Hosidius, Fally). Plant ranarr/snapdragon. 3-5 minutes per run. 250k-400k profit per run. At 2 runs/day = 500k-800k GP/day.
  • Bird house runs: Fossil Island. 1 minute per run. 50k-150k profit per run. At 2 runs/day = 100k-300k GP/day.
  • Kingdom of Miscellania: Complete Throne of Miscellania + Royal Trouble. Put 750k in coffer. Set workers on maples + coal. Collect weekly for 150k-300k GP + resources.
  • Varrock Battle Staves (Medium diary): Buy 30 battlestaves from Zaff daily. Profit: ~60k GP/day for 30 seconds of work.

Total passive: 1M-1.5M+ GP/day for ~10 minutes of total daily effort. That's 30M+ GP/month for doing almost nothing.

⑦ Tier 2: Active Money Making (1-2 hours/session)

💰 The 10M GP Rule

Aim to have at least 10M GP in liquid cash at all times. This lets you buy supplies without stress, take advantage of GE price dips, and fund equipment upgrades. Any GP above 10M can go toward your next big purchase (Rigour at 15M, Fang at 9M, etc.). Below 10M, focus on rebuilding through your passive income stack.

7. Starting Your Boss Journey

Many stuck mid-game players are afraid to start bossing because they think their gear or stats aren't good enough. Here's the truth: your stats are probably fine. You need to build confidence through a progression ladder.

⑩ The Boss Confidence Ladder

Step 1: Easy Entry (No Death Cost, Low Gear Requirements)

  • Barrows: 50 Magic, Iban's staff, any melee weapon. You'll make 500k-1.5M/h with almost no risk.
  • Obor / Bryophyta: Giant keys from hill/moss giants. Simple mechanics, teaches basic prayer switching.
  • Sarachnis: 70+ combats, moderate gear. Teaches prayer flicking and movement.

Step 2: Medium Step Up (Moderate Gear, Learning Curve)

  • Vorkath: 80+ Ranged, DS2 complete, Rune crossbow + ruby/diamond bolts (e). Expect to die 10-20 times before first kill. After 50 kills, it becomes routine.
  • Zulrah: 85+ Ranged/Magic, Regicide complete. The hardest part is memorizing the rotation patterns. Use the Zulrah plugin on RuneLite.

Step 3: The Big Leagues (High Gear Requirements, High Reward)

  • Corrupted Gauntlet: 85+ combats, SotE complete. Zero gear required (everything is found inside). The ultimate test of OSRS PvM skill.
  • God Wars Dungeon (solo): 85+ combats, good gear. K'ril and Graardor are farmable with the right setup.
💡 Boss Prep Checklist

Before trying any new boss, do this prep work: (1) Watch a 10-minute guide video, (2) Read the OSRS Wiki page for gear/inventory setup, (3) Accept that you will die multiple times — it's learning, not failing, (4) Set a goal of 10-20 attempts before you judge whether the boss is "too hard." Most bosses click between attempts 10-30.

8. The Mindset Shift

This is the most important section of this guide. The difference between players who break through the mid-game and those who stay stuck forever is mindset — not stats, not gear, not playtime.

⑪ The 5 Mindset Shifts That Get You Unstuck

1. Stop Comparing, Start Focusing

You see a maxed player with a 500M bank and think "I'm so far behind." But that player might have started in 2013. They have 10,000+ hours. Compare yourself to where YOU were a week ago. Did you gain a level? Complete a quest? Learn a boss? That's progress. That's winning.

2. Done Is Better Than Perfect

You don't need the optimal gear setup, the perfect inventory, or max efficiency. You just need to start. If you have a Rune crossbow and 80 Ranged, you can start learning Vorkath. Your first 50 kills will be slow and expensive. Kills 51-100 will be better. Kills 100+ will be profitable. You can't get to kill 100 without starting at kill 1.

3. Small Progress Is Still Progress

One hour of Mining training = ~40,000 XP at Motherlode Mine. At that rate, 70 Mining takes about 20 hours. That sounds like a lot. But 20 hours spread over 3 weeks is just 1 hour per day. On day 21, you have 70 Mining and access to the Mining Guild, Amethyst mining, and runite ore. The math works — you just need to trust the process.

4. Quests Are Investments, Not Chores

Every quest you complete unlocks something that makes the rest of the game easier, faster, or more profitable. Fairy rings (Fairytale II) save hours of travel time. Dwarf Cannon (Dwarf Cannon) doubles Slayer XP. Kingdom of Miscellania gives weekly passive resources. Treat quests as unlocking permanent account upgrades.

5. You Are Exactly Where You Need to Be

OSRS is a 20-year-old game with thousands of hours of content. There is no "right" pace. Some players max in 6 months. Some take 10 years. Both are playing correctly. The only wrong way to play is to play in a way that makes you unhappy. If you're not having fun, change what you're doing — not your account, not your goals, just your activities.

🎯 Your Mantra for the Mid-Game

"I don't need to be maxed. I don't need to be rich. I just need to be a little better than I was yesterday."

One quest. One level. One boss kill. One new piece of gear. Compound these small wins over months, and you'll wake up one day realizing you're no longer stuck — you're doing the content you used to dream about.

9. Stuck Progression FAQs

I have base 70s but feel stuck. What should I do first?

Pick ONE big goal from this priority list: (1) Complete Recipe for Disaster for Barrows Gloves, (2) Complete Song of the Elves for Prifddinas access, (3) Reach 74 Prayer for Rigour. Commit to one goal and work on it exclusively for 2-4 weeks. Any of these three will transform your account.

Should I focus on Slayer or bossing?

Both. Alternate between them. Do a Slayer task → do 30-60 minutes of bossing → do another Slayer task. Slayer builds stats and unlocks boss tasks. Bossing builds GP and PvM skills. They complement each other perfectly. Don't choose one over the other — do both.

How much GP should I have before trying raids?

For ToA (Tombs of Amascut) entry mode: ~15-20M for a basic setup (Trident of the Swamp, Rune crossbow, whip, barrows armor, and a few potions). For CoX (Chambers of Xeric): ~20-30M minimum. Join a learning-oriented clan or "We Do Raids" Discord — they accept learners with moderate gear.

I keep dying to Vorkath/Zulrah. Am I doing something wrong?

Dying 10-30 times to learn a boss is completely normal. This is not failure — it's practice. The problem is likely that you're trying to do too many things at once. Focus on one mechanic at a time. First, just learn to survive. Then add attacking. Then optimize. Watch a guide for the specific phase/mechanic you're dying to.

Is it worth rushing 99 in a combat skill?

Not particularly. The difference between 85 and 99 in any combat skill is about 8-10% DPS. The time investment (300+ hours) is better spent on quests, gear upgrades, and learning bosses. Get to 85-90 in your primary combat skills, then focus on other account goals. Come back for 99 later if you want the cape.

I don't have much playtime. Can I still progress?

Absolutely. The key is consistency over intensity. If you can only play 30-45 minutes per day, that's still 3.5-5 hours per week. In a month, that's 15-20 hours — enough to complete a major quest, gain 3-5 skill levels, or learn a new boss. Use your time efficiently: do passive income runs first (herb/bird), then focus on ONE goal per session.

What's the #1 mistake stuck mid-game players make?

Hoarding GP. Players save up 50M+ and are afraid to spend it because they might need it later. The best upgrades (Rigour at 15M, Fang at 9M, Blowpipe at 3M, Berserker Ring(i) at 2M) directly increase your earning potential. Spend GP on upgrades that help you make more GP faster. The bank will grow organically from there.

I feel like I'm wasting time when I play OSRS. What should I do?

This is a sign of burnout or goal misalignment. Ask yourself: "Am I having fun?" If yes, you're not wasting time — games are for enjoyment. If no, change your activity. Try a boss you haven't done, start a minigame, join a clan, or take a break. OSRS should be fun. If it's not, something needs to change — not your account, but your approach.

How do I know when I've "beaten" the mid-game?

You've beaten the mid-game when you can answer "yes" to most of these:

  • Can you consistently kill Vorkath/Zulrah or complete Corrupted Gauntlet?
  • Do you have 100M+ total bank value?
  • Can you run entry-mode ToA with a team?
  • Do you have 200+ quest points?
  • Do you have Rigour unlocked?
  • Can you make 2M+/h consistently?
At this point, you're entering the late-game. The goals change from "how do I get stronger?" to "how do I push endgame content?"

🎯 Your 7-Day Unstuck Challenge

Do this for 7 days and you will feel unstuck:

✅ Day 1: Pick ONE big goal (quest, skill milestone, or boss). Write it down.
✅ Day 2: Do your first herb run. Takes 5 minutes, makes 300k GP. Set up the routine.
✅ Day 3: Spend 30 minutes on your goal. If quest is the goal, check requirements and start a prerequisite. If a skill, train for 30 minutes. If a boss, watch a guide video.
✅ Day 4: Repeat herb run. Spend another 30 minutes on goal.
✅ Day 5: Spend 30 minutes making money (Barrows, Vorkath, or Slayer depending on your stats).
✅ Day 6: Spend 30 minutes on goal again. Check progress.
✅ Day 7: Sunday reset. Plan next week. You've made measurable progress. Repeat.

Remember: Being stuck is temporary. Every player who's doing endgame content was once stuck exactly where you are. The difference is they picked ONE thing and kept working at it until it was done. You can do the same.
