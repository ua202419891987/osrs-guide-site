1. Farming 1-99 Overview: The Big Picture

Farming is unique among OSRS skills. Unlike Mining or Woodcutting where you actively grind for hours, Farming is a time-gated passive skill. You plant, wait for crops to grow, and harvest. The waiting makes it the most efficient skill to train alongside other activities — you can reach 99 Farming with as little as 30-40 hours of active play spread over 3-4 months.

Here is the core strategy at a glance:

🏆 The Two Pillars of Farming Training

  • Pillar 1: Tree & Fruit Tree Runs (XP Focus) — Fastest XP in the game. Plant the highest-level tree and fruit tree saplings you can. These cost GP but save enormous time. Do these daily.
  • Pillar 2: Herb Runs (Profit Focus) — Profitable at every level. Herbs earn 400K-900K GP per run while giving passive XP. Herb run profits fund your tree run costs.

This guide teaches you how to balance both pillars at every level bracket. At low levels, you focus on unlocking patches and questing. At mid levels, you build the herb-run-to-tree-run profit loop. At high levels, you add Tithe Farm for active training and Calquat trees for bonus XP.

⏱️ Daily Time Investment at 99

To reach 99 in 4 months: spend 10-15 minutes per day on tree + fruit tree runs, plus 5 minutes per herb run (3 runs/day = 15 minutes). Total: 25-30 minutes per day. You will hit 99 without ever feeling like you "trained" Farming.

3. Level 15-30: The Compost Transition

Once quests have brought you to level 15+, it is time to plant your first trees and set up your compost system. This level bracket is about building the infrastructure for profit later.

🧪 Setting Up Compost Bins

Compost bins are located near major farming patches (Falador, Catherby, Ardougne). Fill them with 30 watermelons, pineapples, or other organic items. After 2-3 hours, collect ultracompost. You will need this for every herb run going forward.

How to Make Ultracompost

Step 1: Add 15 watermelons (or similar) to a compost bin → wait 2 hours → collect supercompost.
Step 2: Add volcanic ash (from the GM/Digger site or GE) to 15 supercompost → creates ultracompost.
Step 3: Each bucket of ultracompost costs ~200 GP to make yourself vs ~1,000 GP on the GE.

💡 Ultracompost for Free

Collect watermelons from allotment runs or buy pineapples from the Catherby charter ship (2 GP each). Use volcanic ash (GE price ~200 GP each) to convert to ultracompost. Making your own saves 700-800 GP per bucket, which adds up to 6,300-7,200 GP per herb run.

4. Level 30-45: Herb Runs Begin — The Profit Engine Starts

Level 32 unlocks Ranarr, the first truly profitable herb. This is where Farming transforms from a cost to an income source. From this point forward, every herb run you do will generate GP.

💰 Ranarr Profit Math (Level 32+, 6 patches)

  • Seed cost: 6 × 22,000 GP = 132,000 GP
  • Ultracompost cost: 6 × 1,000 GP = 6,000 GP
  • Total cost per run: 138,000 GP
  • Average yield: 6 × 8.5 herbs = 51 herbs
  • Herb value: 51 × 12,000 GP = 612,000 GP
  • Profit per run: 474,000 GP
  • Farming XP per run: ~6,000 XP

At this stage, do 2-3 herb runs per day. The profit will fund your tree runs, making the entire training process effectively free. You also unlock Toadflax at level 38, which has an even better profit-to-risk ratio with very cheap seeds (2,500 GP each).

5. Level 45-60: Sustainable Profit Loop

This is where the magic happens. You now have access to multiple profitable herbs and can unlock the Farming Guild patch at level 65 soon. At level 54, Kwuarm becomes available, offering excellent profit with moderate seed costs.

🌲 Adding Fruit Trees to Your Routine

Fruit trees give massive XP for very little time investment. They take 16 hours to grow, so one run per day is sufficient:

🔄 The Profit Loop in Action

Daily routine at level 50:

2. Midday: 1 herb run → +550K GP, +6K XP
3. Evening: 1 herb run + fruit tree run → +550K GP, +42K XP

Your herb profit now covers tree run costs with GP left over. You are leveling Farming for free while building your bank.

🎯 Unlock the Farming Guild at Level 65

The Farming Guild adds a 9th herb patch and a 7th tree patch. Prioritise reaching level 65 through fruit trees and regular trees. Switch from Pineapple to Papaya fruit trees and start planting Willows (level 30) or Maples (level 45) for tree runs.

💡 Calquat Trees: The Hidden XP Gem

Calquat trees (level 72) grow in a single patch in the Tai Bwo Wannai area. They give 24,000 XP per tree for only 5,000 GP in seed cost. This is the most XP-efficient tree in the game when measured by XP per GP spent. Always keep a Calquat tree growing in the background.

7. Level 75-85: The Mid-Game Push

At level 75, you unlock Magic trees — the highest XP tree in the game at 27,000 XP each. Combined with Palm fruit trees (21,000 XP each) and Calquat (24,000 XP), a single daily run gives you nearly 300,000 XP.

🌾 Tithe Farm: Active Training Option

Tithe Farm, located in the Hosidius House of Great Kourend, offers active Farming training at 55,000-65,000 XP per hour. It is an excellent option when you want to grind Farming actively rather than passively:

Your daily routine at level 75+ should look like this:

Daily Routine (Level 75+)

Morning (10 min): Tree run (Magic × 5) + Fruit tree run (Palm × 6) + Calquat → 285,000 XP, cost: 580,000 GP
Throughout day (5 min each): 3 herb runs (Snapdragon × 9) → 36,000 XP, profit: 2.3M GP
Optional (1 hour): Tithe Farm → 60,000 XP, cost: 20,000 GP
Daily total: 381,000 XP from passive + 60,000 XP from active = 441,000 XP. Net GP: +1.7M GP

8. Level 85-99: The Endgame Grind

The final 14 levels require 6.5M XP. With the right daily routine, this takes 3-4 weeks of passive training, or about 8-10 hours of active Tithe Farm grinding. Here is your endgame strategy:

🌰 Torstol Herbs: High Risk, High Reward

At level 85, Torstol becomes available. While Torstol seeds cost 60,000 GP each, the herbs are valuable for Super Combat potions and Torstol potions. However, Torstol is not recommended as your primary herb due to high seed cost volatility. Stick with Snapdragon for consistent profit.

🏆 The Final Push: Active vs Passive

Two Paths to 99

Path A: Passive Only (Recommended)
Daily tree/herb runs for 3-4 weeks. Total active time: ~7 hours spread over a month. Net cost: 0 GP (herb profit covers trees). This is the stress-free approach that lets you focus on other content.

Path B: Active Grind
Tithe Farm for 8-10 hours of concentrated training. Cost: ~200,000 GP. This is for players who want 99 Farming NOW and are willing to spend a day grinding.

Path C: Hybrid (Best for Most)
Continue daily tree/herb runs + add 1-2 hours of Tithe Farm per week. Reach 99 in 2-3 weeks with minimal time investment.

🎉 The 99 Farming Cape

The Farming cape is one of the most useful skill capes in OSRS. It acts as an unlimited teleport to the Farming Guild, saving you from carrying a Skills Necklace for herb runs. Once you have 99, every herb run becomes faster and more convenient.

⚖️ The Optimal Balance

Do both, every day. Herb run profits should cover tree run costs. With Snapdragon at level 62+, three herb runs per day generate ~2.3M GP profit. Tree runs cost ~1M GP per day. That leaves ~1.3M GP of pure profit each day while gaining 430K+ Farming XP.

If you are low on GP, cut back on Magic trees and use Yews instead. The XP is lower but the cost is 80% less. Never skip herb runs — they are your profit engine.

12. Farming Profit FAQs

What is the fastest way to level Farming from 1-99 in OSRS?

The fastest method is daily tree runs + fruit tree runs. Magic trees (27,000 XP each × 5 patches), Palm trees (21,000 XP each × 6 patches), and Calquat (24,000 XP) give ~285,000 XP in 10-12 minutes per day. Adding 1 hour of Tithe Farm per week provides another 60K XP. At this rate, you can reach 99 in 3-4 months with only 30 hours of total active play. For pure active speed, Tithe Farm offers 65,000 XP per hour but requires continuous attention.

Can I make money while training Farming in OSRS?

Yes. Herb runs are profitable at every level from 32 onward. Ranarr (level 32) earns ~74K GP per patch, Snapdragon (level 62) earns ~80K GP per patch. With 9 patches, each herb run generates 500K-750K GP profit. Three runs per day = 1.5M-2.3M GP profit, which more than covers the cost of tree runs. You can reach 99 Farming while increasing your bank value, which is rare for any OSRS skill.

How much does it cost to reach 99 Farming?

The net cost depends entirely on your method. Tree-only training (Magic trees from 75-99) costs approximately 80M-120M GP. However, if you balance tree runs with herb runs, the herb profits cover the tree costs. With 3 Snapdragon runs per day, you generate more GP than Magic tree runs cost. The net cost can be zero or even negative (you make money) by prioritising herb runs alongside your tree runs.

What should I plant at each Farming level for profit?

Follow this level progression: Level 1-8: Quests only (no planting). Level 9-25: Guam/Marrentill for early XP. Level 26-31: Harralander. Level 32-37: Ranarr (first real profit). Level 38-53: Toadflax (best risk-adjusted profit). Level 54-61: Kwuarm. Level 62+: Snapdragon (maximum profit). Always pair herb runs with tree runs for the best XP.

Is Tithe Farm worth it for Farming training?

Tithe Farm is worth it for two reasons: (1) It is the fastest active training method at 55K-65K XP per hour, and (2) it offers excellent rewards including the Herb Sack, Seed Box, Auto-Weed, and Farming outfit (2.5% XP boost). However, as the primary training method to 99, it would require 85-100 hours of repetitive work. Most players prefer passive tree/herb runs and use Tithe Farm for its rewards plus a few hours of extra XP here and there.
