🔄 OSRS Coming Back 2026 Guide: What Changed While You Were Away

Novice

You logged in and feel like you're playing a different game. Sailing? Blood Moon Rises? The Fractured Archive? A bunch of new gear you've never seen? This guide covers EVERYTHING that changed between late 2025 and mid 2026 — the new skill, new quest, gear rebalance, new raid, and the exact checklist to get you caught up in one weekend.

🔄 Returning Player Series · 2026 Catch-up · 15 min read · Updated June 2026 · Data sourced from Jagex Official & OSRS Wiki

⚡ Quick Verdict

If you quit before November 2025: The biggest change is Sailing — OSRS's 24th skill, released November 19, 2025. It's a full utility skill with ships, exploration, new resources, and its own economy. You'll also find a new quest (Blood Moon Rises — Myreque finale), a major gear rebalance (Summer Sweep-Up 2026, June 4), the Bank Tags system (June 17), a reworked Trouver system for Wilderness items, and a fourth raid (The Fractured Archive) coming in late 2026.

Your old gear still works. Almost nothing you owned is obsolete. A few items got nerfed (Dinh's Bulwark, Dragon Claws), but the rest either stayed strong or got buffed. You can jump right back in.

2. ⛵ Sailing: The NEW Skill (Released Nov 19, 2025)

This is the biggest change since you left. OSRS's 24th skill, Sailing, launched on November 19, 2025 — the first new skill added to Old School RuneScape since its inception. Here's what you need to know:

① What Is Sailing?

Sailing is a utility skill that gives you your own ship. You sail between ports, fight sea monsters, discover new islands, salvage shipwrecks, run trade routes, and access exclusive resources, monsters, and equipment. It integrates with Construction (shipbuilding), Woodcutting (ironwood logs), Mining (nickel ore), and Fishing (marlin).

② How to Start Sailing

Go to Port Sarim docks and speak with Will and Anne. Complete the The Pandemonium miniquest (no requirements, ~30 minutes). You start at level 6 Sailing with a basic Raft. From there, you upgrade to a Skiff (level 15) and eventually a Sloop (level 50) and Galleon (level 75).

3. 🏆 Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts (April 15 - June 2026)

OSRS's sixth League, Demonic Pacts, ran from April 15, 2026 through June 2026. While the League itself has ended, its rewards remain relevant to returning players:

① What Was Leagues VI?

Demonic Pacts started players in Varlamore with a unique twist — you could form pacts with demonic entities for powerful combat bonuses. It introduced new relics, tasks, and the Bountiful Harvest echo mechanic. Players earned league points to spend in the reward shop.

② Permanent Rewards Available Now

The Leagues VI reward shop is still open. If you participated (even briefly), you can spend your league points on:

  • Soulreaper Axe (ornament kit) — Gives the axe a Yama-themed cosmetic look. The axe head was later enlarged based on player feedback.
  • Demonic Throne scroll — Cosmetic for your POH. No Construction level requirement to apply (changed after player feedback).
  • Storm Raider coat — Cosmetic override
  • Trident ornament kits — For Toxic Trident (e), Sanguinesti Staff, and Ibans Staff
  • League trophies — Based on your final rank (Dragon, Rune, Adamant, etc.)
🏆 Did You Miss It?

If you didn't play Leagues VI, don't worry — the core game didn't change. League rewards are purely cosmetic. The Soulreaper Axe itself (the weapon, not the ornament kit) was also independently buffed in Summer Sweep-Up 2026 (see Section 5).

6. 🏦 Bank Tags, Trouver System & More (June 17)

The June 17, 2026 update brought two major QoL improvements that every returning player should know about:

① Bank Tags System

Now available on both mobile and the official client, Bank Tags lets you organize your bank into custom categories. This feature was previously only available in third-party clients.

  • Create tags: Click the "+" icon in the top-left of your bank interface. Name it (up to 25 characters) and choose an icon.
  • Add items: Drag items from your main bank onto the tag icon. Items remain in your main bank — tags are just a filtered view.
  • Up to 20 tags: Create separate tags for bossing, clues, farming runs, Slayer, etc.
  • Copy tags: Shift-drag an item to quickly create a new tag based on that item.
  • Rearrangement: Drag tags to reorder. Use mini-menu to delete or rename.

Bank Tags are completely independent of any third-party client tag system. Changes to tags don't affect your main bank layout.

② Trouver System Rework

A major overhaul to how untradeable items behave in the Wilderness:

  • No combat bonus items (e.g., Graceful): Never lost on death, even above level 20 Wilderness. No parchment needed.
  • Partial combat bonus items (e.g., Fire Cape, Defenders, Ava's Assembler, Void): Now only get damaged on death instead of being lost. Repair at Perdu for a small fee.
  • High-value items (requires Trouver Parchment): Once parchment is applied, the item is permanently protected. Above 20 Wilderness, it gets "mangled" instead of lost — repair costs 500K GP. Ornament kits drop to the killer; the base item stays with you.

This is a huge change for anyone who avoided the Wilderness due to fear of losing untradeables. Your Fire Cape and Graceful are now safe.

③ Other June 17 Changes

  • Frost Dragon Slayer tasks: New unlocks available — "Chance of Heavy Frost" (100 Slayer points, increases task weight from 5 to 8) and "I See Dragon" (100 points, task size 180-240).
  • Bounty Tasks: Rewards now scale with contribution. Higher damage share = better loot.
  • LMS gear updates: Dragon crossbow replaces Rune crossbow in Max/Med setups. Zuriel's staff now auto-casts Ancient Magicks.

7. 🏛️ The Fractured Archive: Raids 4 (Coming Q4 2026)

Announced at the Winter Summit 2026, The Fractured Archive is OSRS's fourth raid, scheduled for release in late 2026. While not yet released, it's worth knowing about as a returning player so you can prepare:

① What We Know So Far

  • Theme: A cave system carved by Guthix's followers to hoard powerful artifacts and god-related treasures. Deep beneath Gielinor, the Archive contains knowledge and relics from the God Wars.
  • Size: Up to 8 players (larger than Theatre of Blood's 5-player cap)
  • Layout: Theatre of Blood-style branching paths, not Chambers of Xeric-style puzzle rooms. Players choose paths that affect which bosses and rewards they encounter.
  • Wipe system: Contains a wipe mechanic allowing teams to restart mid-raid (similar to ToB's death system).
  • Rewards: Unannounced new unique items. Expected to include new best-in-slot gear for specific combat styles.

② How to Prepare

The raid requires level 50 Sailing to access (confirmed in the First Look blog). Other likely requirements based on raid history:

  • Combat: 90+ combat stats recommended
  • Gear: Current endgame gear (Scythe, T-Bow, Shadow) will likely be strong. New raid may introduce new BIS items.
  • Sailboat: Unlocks at level 50 Sailing (the Sloop). Make sure you have your ship ready.
  • We Do Raids Discord: Join WDR to find teams once the raid launches. The 8-player format means team coordination will be essential.
🕐 Don't Rush — The Raid Isn't Out Yet

The Fractured Archive is scheduled for Q4 2026. You have several months to prepare. Priority #1: Get Sailing to level 50 at your own pace. Priority #2: Maintain your existing PvM gear. Don't sell your bank for "raid prep" — nobody knows the exact meta yet.

9. ❓ Returning Player FAQs

Do I NEED to train Sailing to enjoy the game?

No. Sailing is completely optional for existing content. You can ignore it and still do everything you did before you left — Vorkath, Zulrah, Corrupted Gauntlet, Theatre of Blood, Chambers of Xeric, Tombs of Amascut all work fine without Sailing. The only content that requires Sailing is The Fractured Archive (level 50, coming Q4 2026) and Sailing-specific content like Frost Dragons (level 87).

My gear is 6-12 months old. Is it obsolete?

Almost certainly not. OSRS rarely makes gear obsolete. Your Whip, Blowpipe, Trident, Barrows armor, and God d'hide all still work perfectly. Items that got better while you were away: Sanguinesti Staff (buffed), Soulreaper Axe (buffed), Inquisitor (buffed from dead content to viable). Items that got worse: Burning Claws (spec cost +5%), Dinh's Bulwark (price dropped ~32%).

What's the single most important new thing I should do first?

Complete The Pandemonium quest at Port Sarim docks (30 minutes). It unlocks Sailing (level 6 immediately) and starts your ship journey. Even if you don't plan to actively train Sailing, the passive quest rewards and future raid access make it worth doing immediately. Second priority: set up your Bank Tags — it saves hours of bank sorting time.

What is the Trouver System rework and why does it matter?

The Trouver System rework (June 17, 2026) changed how untradeable items work in the Wilderness. Previously, items like Fire Capes, Graceful, and Void could be permanently lost on death above level 20 Wilderness. Now: Graceful never drops, Fire Capes get damaged (not lost), and high-end items protected by Trouver Parchment are permanently safe. This removes a major barrier to Wilderness content for many players.

Are my existing quests still the same priority?

Yes. Recipe for Disaster (Barrows gloves), Dragon Slayer II (Vorkath), Song of the Elves (CG), and Sins of the Father (blood shards) are still top priority. Add The Blood Moon Rises (Myreque finale, June 30) to the list — it is a must-do if you have the prerequisites. The Pandemonium is quick and gives Sailing access.

What should I sell from my old bank?

Consider selling: Dinh's Bulwark (nerfed indirectly, dropping in price), Burning Claws (slight nerf, less dominant). Hold: Soulreaper Axe (post-buff price rising), Inquisitor gear (now meta for crush-weak bosses), Sanguinesti Staff (major DPS upgrade), Ancient Sceptre (now +10% magic damage). Keep everything else unless you need GP for new gear.

How long to get Sailing to 50 for The Fractured Archive?

About 12-18 hours of active training from level 6 (post-Pandemonium). Break it down: levels 6-30 via Port Tasks (~8 hours), then levels 30-50 via Barracuda Trials (~5-8 hours). With efficient routes and consistent play, you can reach 50 in a focused weekend. The raid isn't out until Q4 2026, so you have plenty of time.

What happened in the Summer Sweep-Up 2026 that I should care about?

70+ changes including: Inquisitor set is now viable without the full set (each piece gives standalone crush bonuses). Sanguinesti Staff heals more often (1/5 instead of 1/6) with bonus damage. Soulreaper Axe reworked to reach max stacks faster. Master Wand and Ancient Sceptre now have +10% magic damage. Hunter and Agility got significant improvements. Gauntlet prep is faster with stackable resources. See Section 5 above for the full breakdown.

Is the game dying or growing?

Growing. Sailing brought back thousands of returning players in November 2025. The 2026 roadmap is one of the most ambitious in OSRS history with The Fractured Archive (Raids 4, Q4 2026), the Myreque finale, Summer Sweep-Up rebalance, and ongoing Sailing content expansions. Player counts are stable to slightly increasing. Gielinor is more alive than ever.

What's the one thing I should NOT do as a returning player?

Don't sell your entire bank to buy new gear. Your old gear still works. Take a week to understand what changed before making large financial decisions. The most common mistake returning players make is panic-selling their gear at a loss, then buying new gear that drops in price a week later. Play for a week, read the news, then make decisions.
