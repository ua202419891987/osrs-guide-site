2. Sailing: The NEW Skill (Released Nov 2025)

This is the biggest change. OSRS's 24th skill, Sailing, launched on November 19, 2025. Here's what you need to know:

① What Is Sailing?

A Utility skill that gives you your own ship. You can travel between ports, fight sea monsters, discover new islands, salvage shipwrecks, and access exclusive resources, monsters, and equipment.

② How to Start Sailing

Go to Port Sarim docks and speak with Will and Anne. Complete the The Pandemonium miniquest (no requirements, ~30 minutes). You start at level 6 Sailing with a Raft.

③ Sailing Training (4 Methods)

④ Why Sailing Matters for Your Account

  • Level 50 required for future OSRS raid content raid entrance
  • Level 73 unlocks Crystal Extractor (200K-290K GP/hr passive income)
  • Level 87 unlocks Frost Dragons (2-3M GP/hr boss)
  • New resources: Jatoba logs (40 WC), Lead ore (25 Mining), Marlin fish (new best food, heals 24)
  • New equipment: Belle's Folly (mid-game stab weapon), Aquanite Hopper (ranged off-hand)
⛵ Returning Player Sailing Priority

Week 1: Complete The Pandemonium (30 min) → Level 15 for Skiff + Salvaging Hook → Level 30 for Barracuda Trials.
Week 2+: Grind to level 50 for future OSRS raid content access. Use Port Tasks and Barracuda Trials for fastest XP. Don't worry about 99 — just get to 50.

3. Blood Moon Rises: New Quest & Boss

Released in early 2026, the Blood Moon Rises quest concludes the Myreque questline and introduces:

① Quest Details

  • Requirements: 70+ combat stats, previous Myreque quests completed
  • Length: ~2-3 hours
  • Rewards: Access to Blood Moon boss, new equipment, quest XP

② Blood Moon Boss (1.5-2.5M GP/hr)

A mid-game solo boss designed as a bridge between Vorkath/Zulrah and Corrupted Gauntlet. It drops:

  • Blood Moon Platebody (~2M) — Mid-game melee body, between Fighter Torso and Bandos
  • Blood Moon Tassets (~1.5M) — Mid-game melee legs, between Obsidian and Bandos
  • Blood Moon Amulet (~3M) — +12 Strength bonus, budget alternative to Torture
🎯 Why This Changes Your Gear Path

If you left OSRS with a Fighter Torso and thought "I need 28M for Bandos" — things changed. Now you can get Blood Moon Platebody (2M) for stats close to Bandos. The gear progression path is: Fighter Torso → Blood Moon Platebody (2M) → Bandos Chestplate (30M). This means your 5M can now buy a full mid-game melee setup that was impossible before.

9. Returning Player FAQs

Do I NEED to train Sailing to enjoy the game?

No. Sailing is completely optional for existing content. You can ignore it and still do everything you did before you left. But if you want to access the future OSRS raid content raid, you need level 50 Sailing. If you want the new Frost Dragons boss, you need 87 Sailing. For everything else (Vorkath, Zulrah, CG, ToA, ToB, CoX), Sailing is irrelevant.

My gear is 6-12 months old. Is it obsolete?

Almost certainly not. OSRS rarely makes gear obsolete. Your Whip, Blowpipe, Trident, Barrows armor, and God d'hide all still work perfectly. The only items that got worse: Dinh's Bulwark (nerfed), Dragon Claws (outclassed by newer spec weapons). Everything else is either unchanged or slightly buffed.

What's the single most important new thing I should do first?

Complete The Pandemonium quest at Port Sarim docks (30 minutes). It unlocks Sailing (level 6 immediately) and starts your ship journey. Even if you don't plan to actively train Sailing, the passive quest rewards and future access to future OSRS raid content make it worth doing immediately.

How has PvP changed since I left?

Wilderness got a refresh with singles-plus zones. The revamped Wilderness boss system (Vet'ion, Callisto, Venenatis reworks from 2024-2025) is still active. PvP gear meta shifted slightly: Soulreaper Axe and Inquisitor Mace are now viable for PvP. LMS (Last Man Standing) still active. The main PvP change: LMS reward shop was updated.

Are my existing quests still the same priority?

Yes. Recipe for Disaster (Barrows gloves), Dragon Slayer II (Vorkath), Song of the Elves (CG), and Sins of the Father (blood shards) are still top priority. Add Blood Moon Rises to the list — it's now a must-do. The Pandemonium is quick and gives Sailing access.

What should I sell from my old bank?

Sell: Dinh's Bulwark (nerfed, dropping), Dragon Claws (outclassed), Ranger boots (crashing from clue buffs). Hold: Soulreaper Axe (rising post-buff), Inquisitor gear (rising post-buff), Zaryte Crossbow (rising post-buff). Keep everything else unless you need GP for new gear.

How long to get Sailing to 50 for the raid?

About 12-18 hours of active training from level 6 (post-Pandemonium). Use Port Tasks (levels 6-30, ~8 hours) then Barracuda Trials (levels 30-50, ~5-8 hours). With passive wind clicks and efficient routes, you can do it in a weekend if you focus.

Is the game dying or growing?

Growing. Sailing brought back thousands of returning players. The 2026 roadmap is one of the most ambitious in OSRS history with future OSRS raid content, League 6 planned, and more Sailing content. Player counts are stable to slightly increasing. Gielinor is more alive than ever.

What's the one thing I should NOT do as a returning player?

Don't sell your entire bank to buy new gear. Your old gear still works. Take a week to understand what changed before making large financial decisions. The most common mistake returning players make is panic-selling their gear at a loss, then buying new gear that drops in price a week later.
