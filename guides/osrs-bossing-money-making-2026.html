1. 🐉 Overview & Why Boss for GP in 2026

Bossing is the fastest route to millions per hour in OSRS — but only once your account can actually clear the content. Unlike skilling (steady, low-effort) or flipping (capital-dependent), bossing rewards mechanical skill, gear, and stats with the highest GP/hr ceilings in the game. The same hour that earns ~250K from a Ranarr herb run can earn 3–4M at Vorkath.

Every GP/hr figure in this guide is sourced from the OSRS Wiki Money Making Guide (MMG), a community-maintained table that auto-computes profit from cached Grand Exchange prices and documented drop rates. Figures are cached July 2026; because GE prices move daily, treat them as verified baselines, not guarantees — always re-check live prices before committing a grinder.

💡 The Bossing Golden Rule

Pick the hardest boss you can kill consistently and safely. A 4.1M/hr Vorkath rate means nothing if you die every third trip and burn supplies. Efficiency (kills/hr) matters more than the headline rate — a smooth 90-kph Kraken run beats a sloppy 15-kph Zulrah attempt.

3. 🌱 Entry Bosses — Your First Millions

🐀 Scurrius (~203K/hr)

Found in Varrock Sewers, requires only 60+ Combat and 43 Prayer. A great first "boss" for low accounts — safe, instanced, teaches prayer flicking and movement. Profit is low but it is the gentlest on-ramp to PvM.

🕷️ Sarachnis (~506K/hr)

A single-combat instanced boss requiring 70+ Melee. Drops the Sarachnis cudgel (a strong crush weapon) and consistent alchables. One of the best mid-game bossing introductions — see the Sarachnis Solo Guide 2026 for the full rotation.

🐙 Kraken (~770K–1.0M/hr)

The Magic-only Slayer boss (87 Slayer + task). Near-zero death risk and an excellent steady earner. Full mechanics and GP/hr breakdown in the Kraken Guide 2026.

💡 Vorkath vs Zulrah vs Cerberus

Vorkath is the most consistent (predictable 6-attack cycle, no rotation memorising). Zulrah pays similarly but demands learning rotations. Cerberus is Melee and Slayer-gated (91). Pick based on your best combat style and whether you have the Slayer level.

5. 🔥 High-End Bosses — Raids & Wilderness

Above the solo-boss tier sit raids and Wilderness bosses. Raids (ToA Expert ~4.4M/hr) need a coordinated team and strong gear. Wilderness bosses pay 3.7–4.4M/hr but are genuinely dangerous — a PKer can end your trip and take the loot.

⚠️ Wilderness Boss Warning

Vet'ion, Callisto, and Spindel live in the Wilderness. Bring only what you can afford to lose, use a Looting bag, and watch for PK teams. The GP/hr looks amazing until a Rev weapon hits you for 50s — then it is a net loss.

For the safest top-tier payday, Tombs of Amascut (Expert mode) at ~4.4M/hr with a skilled team is the better risk-adjusted choice over the Wildy bosses.

10. ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Myth-Busting)

❌ Myth 1: "Higher GP/hr boss = better for me."

Wrong framing. A 4.1M Vorkath rate assumes 90+ Ranged, Rigour, and a DHCB. If you cannot sustain the kills/hr, a 1.0M Kraken run you can do blindfolded beats it. Match the boss to your account.

❌ Myth 2: "Wilderness bosses are free money."

Dangerous. Vet'ion/Callisto/Spindel GP/hr ignores PK deaths. One revenge-PK with a Rev weapon can erase hours of profit. Treat Wildy bossing as a risk premium, not a baseline.

❌ Myth 3: "Only the unique drop matters."

Incomplete. Most boss GP/hr comes from the consistent alchables and supplies (rune drops, herbs, seeds). Uniques are the cherry on top — do not quit a profitable boss just because the rare has not dropped yet.

❌ Myth 4: "GE tax doesn't affect bossing."

False. Since 29 May 2025 the GE tax is 2% (capped at 5M per item), applied when you SELL loot. On a 4M/hr boss that is ~80K/hr in tax — already baked into the Wiki MMG figures, but worth knowing when you price your own sales.

11. ❓ FAQ

What is the best boss to start making money?

If you have 87 Slayer, the Kraken (~1.0M/hr, near-zero risk) is the gentlest profitable boss. Without Slayer, Sarachnis (~506K/hr at 70 Melee) or Scurrius (203K/hr) are the on-ramps. Build up to Vorkath/Zulrah once stats and gear allow.

Why does Vorkath show two different GP/hr numbers?

The OSRS Wiki lists ~2.77M/hr with a Toxic blowpipe and ~4.1M/hr with a Dragon hunter crossbow. The DHCB's +30% dragon bonus is that dramatic — weapon choice is the single biggest Vorkath variable.

Are Wilderness bosses worth the risk?

Only if you are comfortable losing your loadout and can escape PKers. The GP/hr is real (3.7–4.4M) but unhedged. For safer top-tier pay, learn Tombs of Amascut or push toward the delve bosses with a team.

How much GP/hr can I realistically expect?

Realistic = Wiki rate × your efficiency factor. A smooth, no-death runner hits 80–100% of the listed rate; a learning player hits 40–60%. Focus on kills/hr and supply cost, not the headline.

Do I need a team for the best GP/hr?

Raids (ToA, CoX, ToB) and Yama pay best in teams, but solo bosses like Vorkath, Zulrah, and Cerberus already hit 3–4M/hr solo. Teams help at the very top, not at the entry.

12. 🏁 Final Tips

  • Match the boss to your account — efficiency beats ambition. A clean Kraken run > a dead Zulrah run.
  • Track kills/hr — it is the variable you control; the loot table is not.
  • Bank the consistent drops — alchables and supplies are your floor; uniques are the bonus.
  • Respect the Wildy — only risk what you can replace.
  • Re-check live GE prices — Wiki MMG figures are cached; verify before a long grind.
  • Diversify your income: pair bossing with skilling, flipping, and farming runs so downtimes still earn.
