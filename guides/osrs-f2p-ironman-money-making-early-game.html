F2P Ironman mode is OSRS on hard mode — no Grand Exchange, no trading, no picking up other players' drops. Every GP must be earned through NPC shops or High Alchemy. This guide covers the most efficient early-game money making methods, from cowhide processing to gem jewelry alching.

1. The F2P Ironman Challenge

As an F2P Ironman, you cannot buy or sell items on the Grand Exchange. Every gold piece enters your pouch through one of two channels: selling items to NPC shops (at heavily discounted rates) or using High Alchemy (at fixed alch values). This fundamentally changes how you approach money making — you must prioritize items with high alch values and process raw materials yourself.

The key difference between a regular F2P player and an F2P Ironman is value-added processing. A regular player can sell raw cowhides on the GE for profit. An Ironman must tan the hides, craft them into items, and then alch those items — each processing step multiplies the final GP value. Understanding this chain is the secret to Ironman wealth.

2. Cowhide Processing — The Best Starting Method

Cowhides are the most accessible resource for a brand-new F2P Ironman. Cows are abundant near Lumbridge, drop hides 100% of the time, and can be processed into items with decent alch values.

Craft into hard leather bodies at level 28+. Each body alchs for 78 GP — with 100 hides, that's 7,800 GP. The hides themselves cost 0 GP to obtain, only 100 GP to tan.

6. High Alchemy — The Ironman Endgame

High Level Alchemy (55 Magic) is the single most important unlock for any F2P Ironman. It converts crafted items into GP at fixed rates, eliminating the need for NPC shops entirely. Here's the priority order for reaching 55 Magic:

  1. Train to 13 Magic — Fire Strike on chickens/cows. Collect feathers and cowhides while training.
  2. 13–37 Magic — Fire Strike on Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon). Collect Big Bones for Prayer and limpwurt roots for secondary income.
  3. 37–55 Magic — Fire Bolt or Crumble Undead. Crumble Undead on skeletons is efficient and safe.
  4. 55 Magic achieved — Now every crafted item is pure GP. Hard leather bodies give ~70-85 GP each. Emerald amulets give ~1,080-1,400 GP each.
🔮 Nature Rune Strategy: Before 44 Runecrafting, kill Dark Wizards south of Varrock for nature rune drops. They drop 2–4 nature runes per kill and are easily safe-spotted. Stockpile 500+ nature runes before you hit 55 Magic so you can immediately start alching your crafted items.

7. Advanced Tips for F2P Ironmen

  • Always process raw materials before selling/alching. A raw cowhide sells for 1 GP to an NPC. A hard leather body alchs for 78 GP. That's a 78x multiplier from processing.
  • Get 28 Crafting as your first major milestone. Hard leather bodies are your bread-and-butter alch item. The XP from making them also pushes you toward 40 Crafting for gems.
  • Mine gold ore at the Crafting Guild. You need 40 Crafting to enter, but once inside, the gold rocks are close to the Falador bank. This is the fastest gold ore source for F2P.
  • Don't underestimate bones. Burying bones while killing cows gives Prayer XP. 43 Prayer (Protect from Melee) is a massive combat upgrade that you'll want before doing any serious PvM.
  • Save all gems. Sapphires and emeralds from random events and mining should be hoarded, never sold to NPCs. Once you can cut them, they multiply your alch values by 5x–10x over plain gold jewelry.

8. Common Mistakes

  1. Selling raw materials to general stores. A cowhide sells for 1 GP. A hard leather body alchs for 78 GP. Every raw material you sell instead of processing is GP thrown away.
  2. Skipping Rune Mysteries. Runecrafting is a slow skill, but being able to craft your own runes means you never run out of elemental runes for Magic training.
  3. Ignoring Crafting until late game. Crafting is THE Ironman money skill. Every level from 1–40 directly increases your GP/hour. Get it done early.
  4. Not banking bones. Prayer is expensive to train. Every bone you leave on the ground is lost XP. Bury them — 43 Prayer is essential.
  5. Using High Alchemy on low-value items. Each nature rune costs you time to obtain. Alch items worth 400+ GP minimum to make the nature rune investment worthwhile.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best money making method for a new F2P Ironman?

A: Killing cows for cowhides and processing them into hard leather bodies for high alching is the best early-game method. Cows are near Lumbridge, cowhides are free, tanning costs 1 GP each, and hard leather bodies alch for ~70-85 GP. This method requires zero starting GP and generates 20K-30K GP per hour at low levels.

Q: How do F2P Ironmen get nature runes for High Alchemy?

A: F2P Ironmen obtain nature runes through two main methods: killing Dark Wizards south of Varrock (they drop nature runes commonly) or crafting nature runes at the Nature Altar (requires Rune Mysteries and 44 Runecrafting). Dark Wizards are the fastest source early on — they're easily safe-spotted and also drop other useful runes.

Q: How do I get gold bars as an F2P Ironman?

A: Gold ore can be mined at the Crafting Guild (requires 40 Crafting and a brown apron) or in the Al Kharid mine. Smelt gold ore into bars at the Al Kharid furnace. Gold bars craft into rings (alch ~100-110 GP), necklaces (alch ~130-140 GP), and amulets (alch ~400-220 GP). Adding gems multiplies these values dramatically — a sapphire amulet alchs for ~850-950 GP.

Q: What is the best F2P method for training Crafting as an Ironman?

A: Kill cows for cowhides, tan them in Al Kharid, and craft the leather into the highest-level item you can make. At level 28, hard leather bodies give 35 XP each and are the best XP per hide. Alternatively, mine clay in the Varrock south-west mine, soften it with water, and craft clay items at a pottery wheel. This gives consistent XP and the items alch for decent values (bowls alch for 6 GP, pie dishes for 9 GP).

🛡️ The Ironman Grind Is Worth It

F2P Ironman is slow, methodical, and deeply satisfying. Every GP you earn represents resources you gathered and processed yourself. The early game is the hardest part — once you hit 55 Magic and 40 Crafting, your money-making loop closes and GP becomes a renewable resource rather than a constant struggle.

Embrace the grind. That hard leather body you just alched? You made it from scratch. That's the Ironman way.
