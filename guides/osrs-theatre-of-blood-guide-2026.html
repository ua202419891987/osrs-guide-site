OSRS Theatre of Blood Guide 2026 — Complete Raid Walkthrough & Scythe Farming

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 18 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
👹 Boss Guide — Updated June 2026

The Theatre of Blood is the ultimate test of OSRS PvM skill — 6 lethal rooms, culminating in the legendary Verzik Vitur boss fight, all for a chance at the 1.3B GP Scythe of Vitur. This is the most comprehensive ToB guide available, covering every room mechanic, role assignment, gear setup from budget to max, tick-perfect techniques, and how to recover from wipes.

Bring a full gear switch for all 3 combat styles. ToB rooms require specific styles — no single-style approach works. Inventory: 6-8 Saradomin Brews, 3-4 Super Restores, 1 Sanfew Serum, Stamina Potion, Ice Barrage runes, and the rest filled with Anglerfish/Sharks.

3. Room 1 — Maiden of Sugadinti 🩸

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ | Style: Melee + Magic

Maiden summons blood spawns (Nylocas Matomenos) that crawl toward her. If they reach her, they heal her AND increase her damage. One player (Mage role) must freeze the blood spawns with Ice Barrage. The other 3 players DPS Maiden with Melee. At 70%, 50%, and 30% HP, Maiden spawns a wave of Nylocas — the mage must be ready to freeze each wave. Tip: Stand on the north wall to minimize blood trail damage. Blood trails are the #1 supply drain — move immediately when one spawns under you.

4. Room 2 — Pestilent Bloat 💀

Difficulty: ⭐⭐ | Style: Melee

Bloat patrols the room. When his eyes glow, he charges forward — hide behind a pillar immediately. If Bloat hits you during a charge, you take 40+ damage and he flings you across the room. Between charges, run in and DPS with Melee. Bloat drops meat on the floor — if he walks over it, he heals. Tick-perfect technique: Stand behind the pillar, move out exactly when Bloat turns away, get 2-3 hits, then run back. Experienced teams kill Bloat before the 3rd pillar falls.

5. Room 3 — Nylocas Vasilias 🕷️

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Style: ALL 3 styles

The hardest coordination room. Small Nylocas spawn in 3 colors: Green = Melee only. White = Ranged only. Blue = Magic only. Players split into roles — each player handles one color. Kill ALL small Nylocas before the timer runs out. Then the Vasilias boss spawns — it changes color and protection prayer every few attacks. Match your style to its color. Tick-perfect: Stand 2 tiles from the boss spawn point when the timer hits zero — you get 2 extra hits before it activates. Bring a crossbow + staff switch for your assigned color's boss phase.

6. Room 4 — Sotetseg 🔮

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐ | Style: Ranged + Melee

Sotetseg has two devastating mechanics: the shadow ball and the death ball. During shadow ball, the team must stand on the CORRECT tile indicated by a brief flash on the floor — one wrong tile = 50+ damage to the whole team. The Maze runner (one player) must memorize the correct path through the shadow maze after Sotetseg's HP drops to 66% and 33%. Other 3 players follow the maze runner. Death ball: Sotetseg charges a massive dark orb — everyone must stand together to split the damage (70+ damage SOLO, ~20 each in a group of 4). Death ball without stacking = dead player. Tick-perfect: The shadow ball shows the correct tile for exactly 2 ticks. The maze runner must count tiles during the maze reveal — use RuneLite's ToB plugin to mark the path.

7. Room 5 — Xarpus ⚰️

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Style: All 3 styles

Phase 1 (Exhumeds): Xarpus spawns 3 exhumeds (skeletons). Kill them one at a time with Melee. DO NOT let exhumeds reach Xarpus — each one heals her for 50+ HP.

Phase 2 (Ranged): Xarpus climbs onto a ledge. Everyone stands at the edges and attacks with Ranged. Xarpus spits poison pools — move away immediately. This phase is a DPS race.

Phase 3 (Screech): Xarpus drops to the floor. Before each attack, she screeches — anyone standing still when the screech hits takes 80+ damage. You MUST be moving when the screech hits. Attack on the move (click to attack between steps). This is the #1 wipe cause at Xarpus. Tick-perfect technique: Use the "walk-and-attack" rhythm — click to move, click Xarpus, click to move. With practice, you maintain full DPS while never standing still. Fang excels here because its attack speed syncs naturally with the walk-and-hit rhythm.

8. Room 6 — Verzik Vitur 🦇 (The Final Boss)

Difficulty: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ | Style: All 3 styles — Hardest PvM in OSRS

Phase 1 (Pillar): Verzik stays on her throne. Hide behind pillars to avoid her ranged attack. When she shoots the pillar, run to the next one. DPS with Ranged during the brief gaps between pillar shots. DO NOT stand in the open.

Phase 2 (Electric): Verzik walks around the room. Attack with Melee during safe windows. She summons electric balls that orbit the room — dodge them or take 30+ damage. When Verzik raises both arms, she summons Nylocas — kill them fast or they heal her. At 35% HP, she enrages — electric balls speed up significantly.

Phase 3 (Tornado) — THE HARDEST MECHANIC IN OSRS: Verzik attacks with ranged bombs (rapid 20+ damage), melee slams (if you're close), and yellow lightning attacks that target every player. NEVER stand under Verzik — her melee slam hits through prayer. When she summons tornadoes, run. Don't attack. Just run. The tornadoes track players and deal massive damage. Dodge yellow lightning while running from tornadoes — this is the single hardest mechanical sequence in the game. At 20% HP, Verzik summons a green ball that bounces between players — whoever it hits takes heavy damage. Spread out. Dump all Dragon Claws specs at <10% HP.

🧠 Verzik P3 Survival Checklist: 1) Never stand under her. 2) During tornadoes: SURVIVE > DPS. 3) Yellow lightning has a brief floor indicator — move instantly. 4) Green ball: call out who it's targeting. 5) Brew to full HP when tornadoes spawn. 6) Use your dying teammate's supplies if they die — don't let loot go to waste.

11. Common Mistakes That Wipe Teams

  1. Standing still at Xarpus P3. You WILL die. The screech is unforgiving.
  2. Not stacking for Sotetseg death ball. Split damage or someone dies. Stack tightly.
  3. Missing the maze path at Sotetseg. If the maze runner gets lost, the whole team takes heavy damage. Everyone should watch the path reveal — not just the runner.
  4. Attacking during Verzik P3 tornadoes. Survival > DPS. The #1 cause of Verzik deaths.
  5. Not freezing blood spawns at Maiden. Every spawn that reaches Maiden heals her 20+ HP. Miss 3 spawns = 60+ HP healed = potential wipe from enrage damage.
  6. Bringing the wrong gear. You need ALL 3 combat styles. Showing up with only Melee gear is an instant kick from any team.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I learn ToB before ToA?

A: No — learn ToA first. ToA's invocation system lets you scale difficulty gradually. ToB has no scalable difficulty — every room is punishing from the start. Get comfortable at 150+ invocation ToA, then try ToB Entry Mode. See our ToA Solo Beginner Guide.

Q: How many KC to get consistent at ToB?

A: 50-100 KC. Expect to wipe 20+ times before your first completion. By 50 KC, you'll clear consistently with your team. By 100 KC, you'll be carrying learners.

Q: Is Scythe required for ToB?

A: No — but it helps. Fang is perfectly viable at every room. Scythe is BIS and noticeably faster but not required. Many teams prefer players who have proven mechanics over max gear.

🎭 The Ultimate OSRS Achievement

The Theatre of Blood isn't a raid — it's a rite of passage. Every death teaches you something. Every clear is earned. The first time you watch Verzik fall while your team is screaming in Discord is a core OSRS memory. Start with Entry Mode, find patient teammates, and embrace the wipes. The Scythe is waiting.
