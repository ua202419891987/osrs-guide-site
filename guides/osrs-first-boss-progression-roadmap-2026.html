Most new players think bossing is only for high-level accounts. Wrong. You can start bossing at Combat Level 30 — and the loot will fund your entire account progression. This roadmap reveals the 10 bosses every beginner must beat, in the exact order that maximizes profit and minimizes deaths.

1. Why Bossing Matters Even at Low Levels

Most new players make the same mistake: they spend their first 100 hours killing cows, chickens, and Hill Giants for pennies. Meanwhile, smart beginners are bossing at Combat Level 30 and making 200K–500K GP per hour — enough to buy all the gear they need for faster progression.

💰 The Math: Bossing vs. Regular Training

ActivityCombat Level RequiredGP/HourXP/Hour
Cows/Chickens3+5K–10K5K–10K
Hill Giants30+20K–40K15K–25K
Giant Mole (F2P)30+150K–300K20K–30K
Obor (F2P)35+100K–250K18K–28K
Bryophyta (F2P)40+80K–200K15K–25K

The Conclusion: Even the easiest bosses pay 5–10x more than regular monsters. And you get better combat XP because bosses have higher HP and give more XP per kill.

🎯 What This Roadmap Will Give You

By following this 10-boss progression in order, you will:

  • Make 5–10M GP total from boss drops (enough to buy full Dragon gear, membership, and your first house)
  • Reach Combat Level 70 naturally through bossing (faster than any other method)
  • Learn real PvM mechanics that prepare you for endgame content like Raids and Inferno
  • Get rare drops that could be worth 10M–50M GP (pet drops, unique unlocks)
  • Have fun — bossing is the most exciting part of OSRS, and you can start way earlier than you think
💡 Important: Be Realistic About Your First Kills

Your first kill of any boss will probably take 10–15 minutes and feel overwhelming. That's normal. By the 10th kill, you'll have the mechanics down and be killing in 2–3 minutes. By the 50th kill, you'll be efficient enough to make bank. Don't quit after one bad attempt.

3. Boss 1: Giant Mole (F2P Accessible, Combat 30+)

The Giant Mole is the perfect first boss. It's located under Falador Park, accessible to F2P players, and drops loot worth 150K–300K GP per kill. Best of all, the fight is simple: the Mole only uses melee attacks, so Protect from Melee makes you nearly invincible.

📍 Location & Requirements

How to Get There (F2P & P2P)

Step 1: Go to Falador Park (east of Falador city). Look for the spade icon on the ground near the center of the park.

Step 2: Use a Spade on the dirt patch to dig into the Mole's underground lair. If you don't have a spade, buy one at the Falador General Store for 2 GP.

Step 3: You'll appear in a large underground cavern. The Giant Mole is in the northeastern chamber. Follow the tunnel east, then north.

Requirements: Combat Level 30+ (recommended), 43 Prayer for Protect from Melee (highly recommended but not required), 200K+ GP for gear.

🧠 Fight Strategy — Step by Step

Phase 1: Digging Phase (Mole Underground)

  1. The Mole starts underground. You'll see dirt mounds moving across the cavern floor.
  2. Don't attack yet. Wait for the Mole to pop up out of the ground (it emerges randomly).
  3. When the Mole emerges, immediately activate Protect from Melee (if you have 43 Prayer).
  4. Turn on Ultimate Strength (if Prayer 31+) and Accurate attack style for best accuracy.

Phase 2: Surface Combat (Mole Above Ground)

  1. Attack the Mole with your scimitar. With Rune Scimitar and Protect from Melee, you'll take 0–2 damage per hit.
  2. The Mole has 240 HP. At 30 HP, it'll deal ~15 damage per hit without protection. With protection, 0 damage.
  3. Every 30–40 seconds, the Mole will burrow underground again. You'll see a message: "The mole buries itself..."
  4. When it burrows, step away and wait for it to re-emerge. Don't follow the dirt mounds — you can't hit it underground.
  5. Repeat until the Mole dies (usually 4–6 surface phases).
⚠️ Common Mistake: Panic Eating

With Protect from Melee active, the Mole hits you for 0 damage. You don't need to eat food unless you mess up and deactivate the prayer. Save your food for emergencies, and only bring 5–10 lobsters per trip (the rest of your inventory should be lobsters for healing if you do get hit).

🎯 Goals for This Boss

  • Kill the Giant Mole 20–30 times to get comfortable with boss mechanics
  • Save up 500K–1M GP from drops to fund your next boss (Obor/Bryophyta gear)
  • If you get a Mole Skin or Mole Claws drop, congratulations! That's 100K–150K GP instantly
  • Once you can kill the Mole in under 3 minutes consistently, move to Boss #2 (Obor)

4. Boss 2: Obor (F2P, Combat 35+)

Obor is the F2P giant boss located in the Edgeville Dungeon. He's slightly harder than the Giant Mole but drops better loot, including Big Bones (good for Prayer training) and Rune items.

📍 Location & How to Access

Getting to Obor (F2P & P2P)

Step 1: Go to Edgeville (north of Varrock). Enter the Edgeville Dungeon (dungeon entrance is in the southern building, south of the bank).

Step 2: Navigate through the dungeon (follow the path east, then south). You'll eventually reach a locked gate with a goblin named Obor behind it.

Step 3: To fight Obor, you need to pay him 50 Rune Essence (buy from GE for ~50 GP each = 2.5K GP entry fee). Right-click Obor and select "Pay-essence".

Step 4: The gate opens. Enter and fight Obor. You can only fight him once per payment (50 essence = 1 attempt).

⚔️ Gear Setup (Same as Giant Mole, + Better Weapon)

Use the same gear as Giant Mole, but upgrade to a Rune 2h Sword (60K GP) or Dragon Longsword (80K GP, members) if you can afford it. Obor has higher defense than the Mole, so accuracy matters more.

🧠 Fight Strategy

Obor's Attacks

  • Melee (Club): Obor swings his giant club. With Protect from Melee, this hits 0.
  • Rock Throw (Ranged): Obor throws a boulder. Protect from Missiles reduces this to 0.
  • Earthquake (Special): Every 30 seconds, Obor stomps and deals 10–20 damage to everyone in the room. This cannot be prayer-protected. Eat food when you see him stomp.

Strategy: Keep Protect from Melee ON most of the fight (Obor uses melee 70% of the time). When he starts winding up a rock throw (you'll see the animation), quickly switch to Protect from Missiles. Switch back to Melee protection after the rock lands.

Earthquake Warning: When Obor raises both arms above his head, that's the earthquake. Eat 2–3 lobsters immediately (or 1 swordfish). The damage is unavoidable but predictable.

5. Boss 3: Bryophyta (F2P, Combat 40+)

Bryophyta is the F2P nature boss located in the Falador Dungeon. She's the easiest of the three F2P bosses but has a unique drop (Bryophyta's Essence) that's always worth 50K–100K GP.

📍 Location & Access

Location: Falador Dungeon (enter from the west side of Falador, near the farming patch). Walk south through the dungeon; Bryophyta is in the southeastern chamber.

Requirements: 40 Combat (recommended), 40 Prayer (Protect from Missiles helps a lot). No entry fee.

🧠 Fight Strategy

Bryophyta's Attack Patterns

  • Magic Attacks (Green Spells): Bryophyta casts nature magic. Protect from Magic reduces this to 0.
  • Melee (Root Whip): If you stand too close, she'll whip you with roots. Protect from Melee for this.
  • Summon Roots (Special): Bryophyta summons 3–4 small roots that attack you. Kill them quickly (they have 10 HP each) or they'll overwhelm you.

Strategy: Use Protect from Magic as your default (she uses magic 60% of the time). When she summons roots, kill them first (they die in 1–2 hits with Rune Scimitar). Switch to Protect from Melee only if she starts whipping you at close range.

💎 Loot

Bryophyta always drops Bryophyta's Essence (50K–100K GP). She also drops nature runes, coins, and rare green d'hide items. Expected profit: 80K–200K per kill.

12. Common Beginner Mistakes & How to Avoid

❌ Mistake 1: Not Using Protect Prayers

The Error: Many new players don't realize that Protect from Melee/Missiles/Magic reduces damage by 100%. They try to tank boss hits with food and die quickly.

The Fix: Always activate the correct Protect prayer before engaging a boss. If you don't have 43 Prayer yet, prioritize getting it (train Prayer by burying bones from boss kills).

❌ Mistake 2: Bringing the Wrong Food

The Error: Bringing 20 shrimp (heals 3 HP each) to a boss fight. You'll run out of food in 2 minutes.

The Fix: Use Lobsters (12 HP) or better. For Bosses 1–5, 10–15 Lobsters is enough. For Bosses 6–10, bring 5–10 Swordfish or Monkfish.

❌ Mistake 3: Not Learning the Mechanics

The Error: Trying to brute-force a boss without understanding its special attacks. You'll die repeatedly and waste supplies.

The Fix: Read the strategy section for each boss before fighting it. Watch a YouTube video of the fight (search "OSRS [Boss Name] beginner guide"). The first kill is always the hardest — after that, muscle memory takes over.

❌ Mistake 4: Getting Discouraged After 1 Death

The Error: Dying once and thinking "I'm not ready for this boss." Then quitting bossing entirely.

The Fix: Death is part of learning. Even pros die sometimes. The important thing is to learn from each death. Why did you die? Did you run out of food? Forget to activate Protect prayer? Get surrounded by adds? Fix that one thing and try again.

13. After Level 70: What's Next?

Congratulations! If you've followed this roadmap and reached Combat Level 70, you've accomplished what 80% of OSRS players never do — you've become a competent PvMer who can make millions from bossing.

🎯 Recommended Next Steps

Step 1: Max Your Combat Stats to 75+

Now that you have good gear and bossing experience, train your combat stats to 75 Attack / 75 Strength / 75 Defence / 75 Ranged / 75 Magic. This unlocks the best weapons (Abyssal Tentacle, Blowpipe, Trident) and makes all bosses easier.

Step 2: Tackle Mid-Game Bosses

With 70+ Combat, you're ready for:

  • God Wars Dungeon (GWD): 4 bosses, each dropping unique gear worth 1M–10M GP
  • Corporeal Beast: Drops the Spirit Shield (30M GP) and Onyx bolts
  • Kraken: Easier than Zulrah, drops the Kraken Tentacle (200K GP)
  • Scorpia (Wilderness): Drops the Scorpia's Offspring pet and Venomant nexus

Step 3: Join a Clan

Bossing is more fun with friends. Join an OSRS clan (search "OSRS Clan Recruitment" on Reddit or the official forums). Clanmates can help you with tough bosses, give you free gear, and teach you advanced techniques like prayer flicking and gear switching.

14. Quick Reference Table & FAQ

What's the absolute minimum Combat Level to start bossing?

Answer: Combat Level 30. That's when you can effectively fight the Giant Mole (Boss #1). You need 43 Prayer for Protect from Melee to make the fight safe, but you can do it at 30 Combat with food tanking if you're careful.

Do I need membership to follow this roadmap?

Answer: No! The first 3 bosses (Giant Mole, Obor, Bryophyta) are all F2P-accessible. You can make 2–3M GP from these three bosses alone, which is enough to buy membership for 2–3 months. Then you can tackle the member bosses (Barrows onward).

How much GP do I need to start bossing?

Answer: 200K–300K GP for basic Rune gear. If you don't have that, kill Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon) for 2–3 hours — they drop Rune items worth 10K–50K each. You'll have 200K in no time.

What if I keep dying to the same boss?

Answer: Take a break and go back to an easier boss. Kill the Giant Mole 50 times to get comfortable with boss mechanics, then try Obor again. If you're still struggling, watch a YouTube tutorial or ask for help in the OSRS subreddit (/r/ironscape is especially helpful for new players).

How long does it take to complete this entire roadmap?

Answer: If you play 2–3 hours per day, you can complete the first 5 bosses in 2–3 weeks. Bosses 6–10 will take another 3–4 weeks as the fights get harder. Total time: 5–7 weeks of casual play. But remember — the goal isn't to "finish" bossing, it's to enjoy the journey and make GP along the way.
