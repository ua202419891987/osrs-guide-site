F2P gives you 15 skills. Membership unlocks 8 more completely new skills and expands 7 existing ones — taking you from 15 to 23+ skills with vastly more depth. This guide breaks down every exclusive skill, what it does, whether it's worth paying for, and which ones you should train first.

Let's be blunt: F2P OSRS is a demo. A generous demo — 15 skills, dozens of quests, the Grand Exchange, and all of Asgarnia and Misthalin — but a demo nonetheless. Membership unlocks the real game. And the single biggest unlock? Skills. Eight brand-new skills that simply don't exist in F2P, plus seven F2P skills that get massive expansions making them feel completely different. If you're on the fence about that $14.99/month subscription, this guide will show you exactly what those skills deliver. ① 🎯 Introduction — Why Skills Matter in OSRS

Skills define everything you can do in Old School RuneScape. Your combat level gates which bosses you can fight. Your skilling levels determine how you make GP. Your quest requirements force you to train skills you'd otherwise ignore. In F2P, you have a flat 15-skill experience. In members, that jumps to 23+ skills — and the depth multiplies exponentially.

1.1 📊 F2P vs Members: The Skill Count Reality

Here's the cold, hard numbers. F2P gives you exactly 15 skills. Membership adds 8 completely new skills and dramatically expands the remaining 7 — effectively giving you 15+ new dimensions of gameplay. And it's not just about quantity. The depth of a members skill like Slayer (with 100+ tasks, 30+ unique monsters, and unlockable bosses) dwarfs anything in F2P.

The F2P skill cap is technically 99 just like members. But here's the catch: F2P training methods stop being viable around level 50–60 for most skills. A F2P player grinding to 99 Woodcutting with yew trees earns ~30K XP/hour. A member at the Woodcutting Guild with redwoods earns ~70K XP/hour — and that's before tick manipulation. Membership isn't just about more skills. It's about better training for every skill.

1.2 💡 The Psychology of More Skills

There's a reason Jagex designed OSRS this way. F2P gives you just enough to get hooked — you can train combat, do some questing, make a bit of GP. But you'll hit walls. You'll see members running past you with graceful outfits and 99 capes. You'll watch YouTube videos of boss fights you can't access. The skill gap creates a powerful pull toward membership, and honestly? It works because the content on the other side is genuinely worth it.

② 🔑 The 15 Exclusive Skills Breakdown

Let's go through every single members-exclusive skill. For each one, I'll explain what it does, why it's valuable, how it affects your gameplay experience, and whether it alone justifies your subscription. I've ranked them roughly by impact — the skills at the top are the ones you'll feel most immediately upon upgrading.

2.1 🏃 Agility — The Movement Skill

What it does: Agility determines how fast your run energy regenerates and lets you use shortcuts across the map. At higher levels, you unlock rooftop courses that reward Marks of Grace — which you exchange for the Graceful Outfit, the single most important weight-reducing gear in OSRS.

Why it's valuable: Without Agility, you walk. A lot. Your run energy drains to zero in about 60 seconds of running, and in F2P it takes 7.5 seconds per 1% to regenerate. With 70 Agility, that regen time drops by more than half. With the full Graceful outfit (weight reduction: -25 kg), you can run almost indefinitely during questing, herb runs, and clue scrolls. Agility is the quality-of-life skill that makes every other activity feel smoother.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1: Gnome Stronghold course — first taste of rooftop running
  • Level 10: Draynor Village rooftop — start earning Marks of Grace
  • Level 30: Varrock rooftop — solid XP and marks
  • Level 50: Falador rooftop — the sweet spot for XP/hour
  • Level 70: Pollnivneach course + full Graceful set achievable
  • Level 90: Ardougne rooftop — best XP before 99
💡 Agility Is Always Your First Members Skill

Almost every veteran player will tell you: grind Agility first. It's boring, yes. But 70 Agility with full Graceful transforms how the game feels. Running doesn't feel like punishment anymore. You can complete quests in half the time. Herb runs become smooth 5-minute affairs instead of 10-minute slogs. Train it to 50 minimum before doing anything else.

2.2 🌿 Herblore — Potions That Change Everything

What it does: Herblore lets you clean herbs and mix them with secondary ingredients to create potions. These potions boost your combat stats, restore prayer points, cure poison, and provide dozens of other effects. In F2P, you have zero stat-boosting potions. In members, you have over 40.

Why it's valuable: Potions are the difference between struggling at a boss and farming it efficiently. A Super Attack potion (level 45 Herblore) boosts your Attack by 5–19 levels for 5 minutes. Super Strength (level 55) does the same for Strength. Super Combat potions (level 90) boost Attack, Strength, and Defence simultaneously. Prayer potions (level 38) restore your prayer points during boss fights — without them, you'd have to leave and restock constantly.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 3: Attack potion — your first stat boost
  • Level 15: Strength potion — massive early-game DPS increase
  • Level 38: Prayer potion — essential for every boss and Slayer task
  • Level 45: Super Attack — nearly double the boost of regular Attack pots
  • Level 52: Super Energy — run energy in a bottle
  • Level 55: Super Strength — the DPS backbone until Super Combats
  • Level 69: Antifire — required for dragon tasks and Dragon Slayer II
  • Level 90: Super Combat — one potion, three stat boosts

💰 Herblore Is Also a Top-Tier Money Maker

Making unfinished potions at the Grand Exchange can earn 200K–500K GP/hour with zero requirements. Cleaning herbs profits 100K–300K/hour. At high levels, making Super Combat potions or Stamina potions can profit 1M+ GP/hour. Herblore isn't just essential — it's profitable. For a full guide, see our low-cost Herblore 1–99 guide.

2.3 🕵️ Thieving — Free GP at Every Level

What it does: Thieving lets you pickpocket NPCs, steal from stalls, and loot chests. Every successful pickpocket gives you coins or items. At higher levels, you can steal from valuable targets like Ardougne Knights (50K+ GP/hour) and Elves (potential for Enhanced Crystal Teleport Seeds worth 2M+ GP).

Why it's valuable: Thieving is the only skill that generates raw GP from level 1 with zero investment. No gear, no supplies, no setup. Just click an NPC and gold appears. At level 55+, Ardougne Knights drop 50 GP per pickpocket at a rate of 200+ pickpockets per hour — that's 250K+ GP/hour with zero risk. At 85+, Elves can drop Enhanced Crystal Teleport Seeds worth millions. And at 91+, Pyramid Plunder is one of the fastest 99s in the game.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1–20: Men/Women in Lumbridge → Tea stall in Varrock
  • Level 25–38: Fruit stalls in Hosidius — zero risk GP
  • Level 38–55: Master Farmers — ranarr seeds (30K GP each)
  • Level 55–91: Ardougne Knights — 250K+ GP/hour
  • Level 85+: Elves — Crystal teleport seeds
  • Level 91: Pyramid Plunder — fastest XP to 99
💡 Thieving Is Your Emergency GP Fund

Blew your bank on gear? No problem. Spend 2 hours at Ardougne Knights and you'll have 500K+ GP in pure coins. Thieving requires literally zero startup capital — it's the ultimate fallback money-maker for broke players.

2.4 🎨 Crafting — Jewellery, Armour, and Millions

What it does: Crafting exists in F2P but is gutted — you can make pottery and basic leather. In members, Crafting explodes into one of the most versatile skills in the game. You'll craft dragonhide armour (the best ranged armour until Armadyl), cut gems for jewellery, make amulets with teleport charges, blow molten glass into light orbs and lanterns, and craft jewellery that sells for massive profit.

Why it's valuable: Members Crafting isn't just about making things — it's about utility. Games necklaces teleport you to Barbarian Assault and Corporeal Beast. Rings of dueling take you to Castle Wars and Ferox Enclave. Amulets of glory teleport to Draynor, Karamja, and Edgeville. Crafting is your access pass to the teleport network. At higher levels, crafting dragonhide bodies and battlestaves generates 300K–500K GP/hour while training.

Key members-only unlocks:

  • Level 7: Gold jewellery — rings, necklaces, amulets (unenchanted)
  • Level 23: Tiara moulding — essential for Runecrafting
  • Level 40: Ruby jewellery — Digsite pendants, games necklaces
  • Level 57: Green dragonhide bodies — 200K+ GP/hour
  • Level 63: Blue dragonhide bodies — 350K+ GP/hour
  • Level 71: Red dragonhide bodies — 500K+ GP/hour
  • Level 87: Fury amulet — BiS until Zenytes

2.5 🏹 Fletching — Ranged Combat's Best Friend

What it does: Fletching is completely members-only. You turn logs into unstrung bows, add bowstrings, and create ranged weapons from shortbows to magic longbows. At higher levels, you fletch dragon darts and dragon arrows — the best ammunition in the game.

Why it's valuable: Ranged is one of the strongest combat styles in OSRS, and Fletching is how you supply it. Making your own arrows and bows saves millions over buying from the GE. At level 95, fletching dragon darts can profit 500K–1M+ GP/hour (though it's click-intensive). Even at low levels, fletching yew longbows (level 70) and stringing them makes 100K–150K GP/hour — solid for a skill that requires only a knife and logs.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1–10: Arrow shafts → shortbows
  • Level 20–40: Oak bows → willow bows
  • Level 50–70: Maple bows → yew longbows begin
  • Level 80–85: Magic longbows — best fletching XP
  • Level 85: Dragon bolts — major money-maker
  • Level 90–95: Dragon arrows → dragon darts

2.6 ⚔️ Slayer — The Skill That Defines Members PvM

What it does: Slayer assigns you tasks to kill specific monsters. You can only damage these monsters while on a Slayer task. As your Slayer level rises, you unlock new monsters with unique drops — many of which are the best money-makers in the entire game.

Why it's valuable: Slayer is the single highest-GP skill in OSRS long-term. At level 75, Gargoyles drop granite mauls and cash — 500K+ GP/hour. At level 80, Nechryaels drop rune items and seeds — 600K+ GP/hour. At level 85, Abyssal Demons drop the Abyssal Whip — 700K+ GP/hour (with whip drops). At level 93, Smoke Devils and their boss variant drop the Occult Necklace. At level 95, Hydras drop the Hydra's Claw worth 60M+ GP. No other skill delivers this kind of consistent, combat-trained profit.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1–30: Turael tasks — basic monsters, low GP
  • Level 50–60: Bloodvelds, Jellies — 100K–200K GP/hour
  • Level 65: Dust Devils — 250K GP/hour, decent XP
  • Level 70: Kurasks — 350K+ GP/hour
  • Level 75: Gargoyles — The first "real" Slayer money-maker
  • Level 80: Nechryaels — 600K+ GP/hour in Catacombs
  • Level 85: Abyssal Demons — Whip drops and consistent profit
  • Level 87: Cave Kraken → Kraken boss — trident drops
  • Level 91: Cerberus — Primordial crystal (30M+)
  • Level 93: Smoke Devils — Occult necklace
  • Level 95: Alchemical Hydra — Hydra's Claw (60M+)

📊 Slayer: The Long-Term Investment

Slayer starts slow — your first 50 levels feel underwhelming. But the payoff curve is exponential. A player with 95 Slayer doing Hydra tasks earns more GP in 10 hours than a F2P player earns in 200 hours of Hill Giants. Slayer also trains your melee, ranged, and magic simultaneously — it's the most efficient combat training method in the game. For the full strategy, see our Slayer beginner-to-master guide.

2.7 🦅 Hunter — Implings, Chinchompas, and Profit

What it does: Hunter is a gathering skill where you track, trap, and catch creatures across Gielinor. The rewards include chinchompas (ranged weapons that hit multiple targets), impling jars (containing rare loot), and salamanders (hybrid weapons).

Why it's valuable: Hunter has two main draws. First, red chinchompas (level 63) are the fastest ranged XP in the game — throw them at Maniacal Monkeys and you'll earn 400K–700K Ranged XP/hour. They also sell for ~1,200 GP each and you can catch 200–300 per hour. Second, implings — specifically Magpie (level 65) and Ninja (level 74) — drop valuable items like dragon daggers, rune items, and noted ore. Dragon implings (level 83+) can drop dragonstone jewellery, glories, and even the rare Dragonstone Chest worth millions.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1–19: Crimson swifts → copper longtails (birds)
  • Level 19–29: Ruby harvests → tropical wagtails (butterflies & birds)
  • Level 29–47: Swamp lizards → orange salamanders
  • Level 47–63: Orange salamanders → red salamanders
  • Level 63: Red chinchompas — the holy grail of Hunter
  • Level 73: Black chinchompas — even better, but Wilderness risk
  • Level 80+: Herbiboar — 130K+ Hunter XP/hour + herbs

2.8 🏠 Construction — Your Player-Owned House

What it does: Construction lets you build and upgrade your own house. Starting with a bare-bones shack, you can expand it into a mansion with a portal nexus (teleports to 30+ locations), a gilded altar (3.5× Prayer XP), a jewellery box (infinite teleport jewellery), a pool of rejuvenation (restores HP, prayer, run energy, and spec), and a spirit tree/fairy ring combo.

Why it's valuable: A maxed Player-Owned House (POH) is the single largest quality-of-life upgrade in OSRS. With one house tab, you can restore all stats, teleport to any location in the game, and then return to your task. It centralizes all teleportation into one menu. Players with a maxed POH complete activities 20–30% faster than those without — that adds up to hundreds of saved hours over an account's lifetime.

Key unlocks:

  • Level 1: Buy a house (1,000 GP from any Estate Agent)
  • Level 47: Mounted Glory — infinite Edgeville/Draynor teleports
  • Level 50: Portal chamber — 3 teleport portals
  • Level 67: Gilded altar — 3.5× Prayer XP
  • Level 72: Ornate jewellery box — infinite teleports for 15+ items
  • Level 80: Ornate pool — restore HP, prayer, run, spec, and venom
  • Level 82: Max portal nexus — 30+ teleport destinations
  • Level 90+: Full nexus, fairy ring + spirit tree combo
💡 Construction: Expensive but Worth Every GP

Getting to 83 Construction (max POH nexus + pool) costs 15M–25M GP depending on method. That sounds steep, but consider this: a maxed POH saves you roughly 2–3 minutes per activity — herb runs, boss trips, clue scrolls, questing. Over 1,000 activities, that's 2,000–3,000 minutes saved. Time is money, and Construction buys you time in bulk.

2.9 🌾 Farming — Herbs, Trees, and Passive Income

What it does: Farming is the art of planting seeds in patches across Gielinor, waiting for them to grow, and harvesting the results. Crops include herbs (for Herblore), trees (for XP), fruit trees (for food and protection payments), and special crops like seaweed and cactus spines.

Why it's valuable: Herb runs are the single best passive income source in OSRS. A single ranarr weed run (level 32 Farming, 5 patches) takes 5 minutes and nets 100K–150K GP profit. Do that 5 times a day and you're earning 500K–750K GP for 25 minutes of work — all while training Farming. At level 38, toadflax herbs give similar profit with less risk. At level 85, torstol runs can profit 200K+ per run. Farming is the skill that prints GP while you sleep (metaphorically — the crops grow in real time).

Key unlocks:

  • Level 15: Oak trees — first decent tree XP
  • Level 27: Apple trees — cheap fruit tree XP
  • Level 32: Ranarr weed — 100K+ GP per herb run
  • Level 38: Toadflax — reliable mid-game profit
  • Level 55: Mahogany trees — best XP until redwoods
  • Level 72: Magic trees — 13,000+ XP per tree
  • Level 85: Torstol herbs — top-tier herb run profit
  • Level 90: Redwood trees — best tree XP in the game

2.10 🔮 Magic — From Utility to Annihilation

What it does (members expansion): In F2P, Magic caps out functionally around level 59 (Fire Blast). Members Magic goes to 99 and unlocks an arsenal that includes teleport spells to 20+ locations, Ancient Magicks (ice barrage, blood barrage), Lunar spells (NPC contact, vengeance, plank make), and the Arceuus spellbook (thralls, reanimation, shadow veil).

Why it's valuable: Members Magic transforms from a basic combat/support skill into the most versatile tool in the game. Ice Barrage freezes enemies for 20 seconds — essential for almost every boss and PvP. Blood Barrage heals you for 25% of damage dealt — making God Wars trips last 3× longer. High Alchemy turns items into pure GP. Lunar spells let you contact slayer masters remotely, cast vengeance (reflects 75% of damage), and superheat ores. The Arceuus spellbook adds thralls (summoned damage-dealers) that are meta for endgame PvM. Simply put: a members account without 94+ Magic is playing with one hand tied behind its back.

Key members-only unlocks:

  • Level 45: Camelot Teleport — access to Catherby/Seers
  • Level 50: Iban's Blast — early-game bossing spell (Underground Pass quest)
  • Level 60: Charge + God spells — 30 max hit at 60 Magic
  • Level 66: Vulnerability — reduces enemy Defence by 15%
  • Level 75: Fire Wave with Tome of Fire — 40+ max hit
  • Level 78: Teleport to House — instantly access your POH
  • Level 82: Ice Blitz (Ancient Magicks) — freezing and damage
  • Level 87: House Teleport + Recharge Dragonstone
  • Level 94: Ice Barrage — freeze enemies for 20 seconds
  • Level 96: Spellbook Swap — swap between all spellbooks anywhere

2.11 ⛏️ Mining & Smithing — Members Expansion

What changes from F2P: F2P Mining stops being interesting around level 40 (gold ore). Members Mining adds the Motherlode Mine (passive, low-effort training), gem rocks (profitable), blast mining (fastest XP), and access to runite ore at level 85. Members Smithing adds the Blast Furnace (50% fewer coal needed, faster bars), cannonball making (200K+ GP/hour with zero effort), and the ability to smith dragonfire shields, godsword blades, and more at high levels.

Why it's valuable: The Blast Furnace alone makes members Smithing 2–3× more profitable than F2P. Making mithril, adamant, or rune bars at the Blast Furnace earns 500K–1M+ GP/hour. The Motherlode Mine gives you consistent, semi-AFK Mining XP with a chance at the Coal Bag and Gem Bag — two items that increase efficiency in other skills. For the full breakdown, check our Mining 1–99 guide.

🔨 Blast Furnace: The Members Smithing Multiplier

Location: Keldagrim (start The Giant Dwarf quest to access). At the Blast Furnace, making steel bars earns 400K–600K GP/hour. Mithril bars: 700K–1M GP/hour. Rune bars: 1.2M+ GP/hour. You'll need a coal bag (Motherlode Mine reward) and ice gloves (Heroes' Quest) for maximum efficiency, but even without them it's better GP than any F2P Smithing method.

2.12 🪵 Woodcutting & Fishing — Expanded Gathering

What changes from F2P: F2P Woodcutting tops out at yew trees (level 60). Members adds magic trees (level 75), redwood trees (level 90, 70K+ XP/hour), the Woodcutting Guild with a bank nearby, and blisterwood trees for AFK training. F2P Fishing caps at lobster/swordfish. Members adds monkfish (level 62, heal 16, best budget food), sharks (level 76, heal 20), anglerfish (level 82, heal 3–22 and overheal), and minigames like Tempoross and aerial fishing.

Why it's valuable: Redwoods (level 90 Woodcutting) are one of the most AFK activities in OSRS — you click once every 4–5 minutes and earn 70K XP/hour. Anglerfish (level 82 Fishing) are essential for bossing because they overheal you above your max HP. And the Woodcutting Guild's invisible +7 level boost makes every tree faster to chop. These members-exclusive expansions turn gathering skills from tedious grinds into genuinely rewarding activities.

2.13 🌀 Runecrafting & Crafting — The Crafting Economy

What changes from F2P: F2P Runecrafting is painful — you run air runes at 5K XP/hour until your fingers bleed. Members Runecrafting unlocks the Ourania Altar (ZMI), the Abyss (2–3× more runes per trip), Guardians of the Rift (the Runecrafting minigame that's actually fun), blood rune crafting at level 77 (500K+ GP/hour), and wrath runes at level 95. The difference between F2P and members Runecrafting is night and day — members turns a miserable grind into a profitable, varied skill.

Why it's valuable: Blood rune crafting at level 77 through the Arceuus library or the True Blood Altar is one of the most consistent AFK money-makers in OSRS. You craft blood runes worth 200+ GP each at a rate of 2,500+ per hour — that's 500K GP/hour while getting decent Runecrafting XP. Guardians of the Rift at any level provides runes for your own use plus the Raiments of the Eye outfit (60% more runes crafted). Members Runecrafting isn't just bearable — it's good. ⑤ 🔗 Skill Synergy — Best Combinations

OSRS skills aren't isolated silos. They feed into each other in ways that multiply your efficiency. Learning these synergies is what separates a 500-hour maxed account from a 2,000-hour one.

5.1 🌿 The Holy Trinity — Farming + Herblore + Magic

This is the most important skill triangle in OSRS. Farming grows herbs. Herblore turns those herbs into potions. Magic provides the teleports to reach every herb patch in under 60 seconds. A player with 50+ Farming, 45+ Herblore, and 50+ Magic can complete a full herb run in 5 minutes and earn 100K–200K GP — five times a day. That's passive, reliable income that funds every other skill.

5.2 ⚔️ The Combat Loop — Slayer + All Combat + Herblore

Slayer assigns you monsters to kill. Those monsters drop herbs, seeds, runes, and alchable items. You use potions (Herblore) to kill them faster. The GP from Slayer buys better gear. Better gear means faster kills. Faster kills mean more Slayer XP and more GP. This feedback loop is the core gameplay cycle of OSRS membership — and it doesn't exist in F2P at all.

5.3 🏠 The Infrastructure Stack — Construction + Crafting + Magic

Construction builds the house. Crafting makes the teleport jewellery (mount it in the house). Magic provides the teleport spells (add them to the portal nexus). Together, you create a teleportation hub that can send you anywhere in the game in under 10 seconds. This stack is what makes herb runs, clue scrolls, and questing efficient. Build your POH infrastructure early and every other activity becomes faster.

5.4 🎯 The Range Powerhouse — Fletching + Ranged + Hunter

Hunter catches chinchompas. Fletching makes bows and ammunition. Ranged uses both. A player with 70+ Fletching, 63+ Hunter, and 70+ Ranged can supply their own ranged training from scratch — chinchompas for multi-target XP, magic shortbows for single-target, and broad bolts for Slayer.

5.5 🌊 The Gathering Foundation — Mining + Smithing + Woodcutting + Fishing

These four gathering/production skills form the backbone of the OSRS economy. Members expansions (Motherlode Mine, Blast Furnace, Woodcutting Guild, monkfish/anglerfish) turn these from tedious F2P grinds into profitable, useful activities. The synergy is straightforward: gather resources → process them → sell on the GE → fund other skills.

💡 The Synergy Mindset

When you plan your training, always ask: "What other skill does this benefit?" Training Hunter? It benefits Ranged (chinchompas) and Construction (implings drop planks). Training Agility? It benefits Farming (faster herb runs), Runecrafting (faster altar runs), and everything else. Seek out synergies — they multiply your account's growth exponentially.

⑥ ❓ FAQs — OSRS Members Exclusive Skills

Q: Can I train members skills in F2P worlds at all?

A: No. All 8 fully exclusive skills (Agility, Herblore, Thieving, Fletching, Slayer, Hunter, Construction, Farming) are completely locked in F2P worlds. You cannot even see your level in these skills without membership. The members expansions to F2P skills (like Blast Furnace, Motherlode Mine, etc.) are also locked. The moment your membership expires, you lose access to all of it.

Q: Which members skill makes the most GP?

A: Slayer, by a massive margin. At 95+ Slayer, Alchemical Hydra alone can generate 2M–3M GP/hour when you factor in Hydra's Claw drops. Even at 75 Slayer, Gargoyles reliably produce 500K+ GP/hour. Runner-up is Farming through herb runs — a maxed herb run (9 patches, torstol) nets 200K–300K GP in 5 minutes, and you can do it every 80 minutes.

Q: What if I only have 30 minutes a day — is membership still worth it?

A: Yes, but you need to be strategic. Focus on Farming (herb runs take 5 minutes each — do 2 per day), Slayer (tasks are 30–60 minutes each), and Agility (start a session, run rooftops for 30 minutes, log off). Avoid buyable skills like Construction and Herblore until you have enough GP — they require longer sessions to be efficient. With 30 minutes a day, you'll still progress meaningfully in members skills, just slower than a full-time player.

Q: Can I sustain membership through bonds using skills alone?

A: Absolutely. A bond costs ~5.2M GP (June 2026) and gives 14 days. At 75+ Slayer, you can earn that in 9–10 hours. Spread across two weeks, that's roughly 40 minutes per day. Many skills have bond-sustaining methods: Thieving (Ardougne Knights, 18 hours/bond), Hunter (red chinchompas, 15 hours/bond), Runecrafting (blood runes, 10 hours/bond), Farming (herb runs, 17 hours spread over 2 weeks). Once you have 2–3 income skills at mid-level, bonds become self-sustaining.

Q: Is Agility really that important? Can I skip it?

A: You can technically skip any skill, but you'll regret skipping Agility. Without it, your run energy drains in 60 seconds and takes over a minute to regenerate. Every herb run, every quest, every clue scroll takes 2–3× longer because you're walking half the time. The Graceful outfit (-25 kg weight) alone makes Agility worth it. Get at least 50 Agility — it's 8–12 hours of boring rooftops for hundreds of hours of smoother gameplay.

Q: What's the cheapest members skill to train to 99?

A: Thieving, by far. It requires zero supplies, zero gear, and generates pure GP as you train. From level 55–91 at Ardougne Knights, you'll earn 15M–20M GP on the way to 99. Alternatively, Hunter at red chinchompas (level 63–99) earns 30M–50M GP. Both skills are completely self-funding and profitable. On the flip side, Construction is the most expensive — expect to spend 80M–120M GP for 99.

🏁 Final Tips — Making the Most of Your 15 Skills

Membership skills are the core of the OSRS experience. F2P gives you a flat, limited version of the game. Membership gives you depth, variety, and progression that can sustain thousands of hours of gameplay. The 15 exclusive or expanded skills are each worth exploring in their own right — but the real magic happens when they work together.

Here's the bottom line: train Agility first (it makes everything else feel better), start Slayer early (it's your long-term GP engine), do herb runs daily (they fund your entire account), and build your POH (it centralizes every teleport in the game). Do those four things and the other 11 skills will fall into place naturally.

And if you're still unsure about the $14.99/month? Remember: 10 hours of members Slayer earns you a bond. After that first bond, you can play for free indefinitely. The subscription is a bridge — once you cross it, the skills pay for themselves.
