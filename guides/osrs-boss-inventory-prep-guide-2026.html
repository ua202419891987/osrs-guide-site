1. General Inventory Preparation Rules

Every boss inventory follows the same core principles. Master these rules, and you'll never show up to a fight missing essential items.

The Six Pillars of a Good Inventory

1. Weapon Switches

Every inventory needs at minimum: main weapon, special attack weapon, and any off-style switch. For magic/ranged hybrid bosses like Zulrah, you need a full switch for each style. For pure melee bosses like Sarachnis, you only need your weapon + defender.

2. Food & Healing

Bring 12-16 pieces of your best food. Use Karambwans for combo-eating (eat Karambwan + regular food in one tick for instant 40+ HP). For raids, use Saradomin Brews + Super Restores instead of regular food.

3. Potions

Minimum combat potions: Super Combat (melee), Ranging (ranged), or Magic (magic) potion. Bring 1-2 Stamina potions for movement-heavy bosses. Prayer potions or Super Restores for sustain.

4. Teleports

Always bring a Teleport to House tablet. For Wilderness bosses, bring a one-click teleport. For raids, bring a full Rune Pouch with key teleports.

5. Special Attack Weapon

Dragon Warhammer (DWH), Bandos Godsword (BGS), Bone Dagger, or Voidwaker. Special attacks reduce boss defence or deal burst damage. Always have at least one.

6. Utility Items

Rune Pouch, Holy Wrench, Ring of Suffering (i) or Ring of Life, emergency Antipoison/Antivenom. These prevent deaths from mechanics you might forget about.

Potion Quantities: The Formulas

Food (General Rule)

Boss Max Hit x 3 + 10 = Minimum Food HP Needed. If a boss hits 30, bring at least 100 HP worth of food (5 Manta Rays or equivalent). Add 50% more while learning.

Prayer Potions

1 Super Restore per 3-5 Kills (conservative). With 70 Prayer and Holy Wrench, each Super Restore dose restores ~25 prayer points. One 4-dose potion gives 100 prayer points. If you burn 20 prayer points per kill, you get 5 kills per potion. Adjust based on your prayer bonus.

Brew:Restore Ratio

Always 1:1. Each Saradomin Brew dose reduces your stats by ~10%. Each Super Restore dose restores your stats by ~25%. One Restore per Brew ensures you never lose DPS from stat drain. This is the raid-standard ratio.

💡 The 10-Minute Rule

Most boss kills take 1-3 minutes. If your combat potion lasts 5 minutes per dose, one 4-dose potion covers 20 minutes of combat — roughly 6-10 kills for mid-game bosses. A single dose per kill is usually sufficient if you sip between kills or during downtime phases.

6. Run & Prayer Management

Run energy and prayer points are the two resources most beginners mismanage. Proper management directly translates into longer trips and fewer deaths.

Run Energy Conservation

Most boss fights drain run energy fast if you're running between phases. Here's how to optimize:

  • Preserve run energy: Walk during safe phases (between boss attacks, during transitions). Only run when you need to dodge or reposition.
  • Stamina potion usage: Sip a Stamina potion dose when you hit 60% run energy, not 20%. This keeps you in the "green" zone where energy regenerates faster.
  • Boot swap: For movement-heavy bosses (Mole, Zulrah, Verzik P3), wear Graceful boots or Boots of Lightness to reduce energy drain.
  • POH pool: Build a Rejuvenation Pool in your POH. It restores run energy + prayer + health + special attack instantly. A single Teleport to House + teleport back saves 5-10 minutes per trip on some bosses.

Prayer Conservation Priority List

  1. Always have a Holy Wrench in your ammo slot — it's free, requires no inventory space, and adds 2-4 prayer points per potion dose.
  2. Use Protect Prayer exclusively — don't leave offensive prayers on unless you have excess prayer points. Turn off Piety/Rigour/Augury during boss downtime.
  3. Turn off prayers during special attacks — many boss special attacks (Vorkath fire bomb, Zulrah jad phase) have a window where prayers don't matter. Toggle off to save points.
  4. Use Super Restores over regular Prayer potions. They restore 10-15 more prayer points per dose at higher Prayer levels and also fix stat drain.

Death Mechanics & Item Recovery (July 2026)

As of July 2026, OSRS death mechanics work as follows:

  • Safe deaths (most bosses): Your items appear at your deathpile for 60 minutes. Other players cannot see or loot them. You must retrieve them by walking back to your death location or paying Death's coffer.
  • Unsafe deaths (Wilderness): Items appear to other players after 1 minute. Protect Item prayer saves 4 items. Always bank valuables before entering Wilderness.
  • Raid deaths (CoX, ToA, ToB): Your items stay inside the raid instance. You can re-enter to retrieve them or use the chest outside.
  • Death's coffer cost: If you can't reach your deathpile, pay Death (in Lumbridge graveyard) to retrieve items. Cost is based on item values.
💡 Inventory Safety Checklist

Before every boss trip, mentally check: Do I have a Teleport to House? Is my Rune Pouch filled? Do I have 1-2 emergency Karambwans? Is my Ring of Suffering equipped? If the answer to any is "no," spend 10 more seconds at the bank. It saves you millions in death fees.

8. Inventory Preparation FAQs

How much food should I bring to a boss fight?

For beginner bosses, bring 12-16 Monkfish or better food. For mid-game bosses, bring 12-16 Manta Rays with Karambwans for combo-eating. For endgame raids, bring 4-6 Saradomin Brews with 4-6 Super Restores. A general rule: bring enough food to survive 4-5 hits at your boss's max hit. Scale up as you learn.

What potions do I need for bossing in OSRS?

The essential potion loadout includes: Super Combat potion or Divine Super Combat (melee), Ranging potion (ranged), Magic potion (magic), Prayer potions or Super Restores (2-6 depending on trip length), Stamina potions (1-3), and Antivenom+ if the boss uses poison. For raids, use Saradomin Brews as your primary healing with Super Restores to recover stats.

How do I set up gear switches for multi-style bosses?

Arrange your inventory so switches are grouped by style. For Zulrah, have your Magic switch (Trident, Ahrim's top/bottom, Occult, Tormented) on the left and Ranged switch (Blowpipe, God d'hide, Anguish) on the right. Place your teleport and special attack weapon in the same position every time. Use F-keys to switch between inventory and equipment tabs. Practice the muscle memory at a bank before taking it to a boss.

How many prayer potions should I bring per trip?

For short trips (1-5 kills): 2-3 Prayer potions. For medium trips (10-20 kills): 4-6 Super Restores. For extended trips (30+ kills): bring a Prayer Flask (6 doses) plus 4-6 Super Restores. Factor in your prayer bonus: higher prayer bonus means fewer potions needed. A Holy Wrench saves roughly 1 potion per 10 kills. Budget 1 Super Restore per 3-5 boss kills for most content.

What emergency items should I always bring bossing?

Always bring a Teleport to House tablet (instant escape), a Rune Pouch with key teleport runes (Camelot, Falador, Varrock), and an emergency Stamina potion. For Wilderness bosses, bring a Teleport to House tablet + one-click teleport item. For raids, bring a Ranging potion and a Stamina potion in addition to your combat potions. Extra Super Restores serve as emergency prayer + stat restore.
