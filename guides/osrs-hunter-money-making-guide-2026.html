🏹 Money Making — Updated July 2026

Hunter is one of OSRS's most lucrative skilling moneymakers. Black chinchompas in the Wilderness can earn 1.8M-6M GP per hour, red chinchompas offer safe profits up to 2M GP/hr, and Herbiboar provides valuable herbs for Ironmen. This guide covers every profitable Hunter method with level requirements, trap counts, gear setups, and PK survival tips for 2026.

1. Hunter Level vs Trap Count

Your Hunter level directly determines how many traps you can set simultaneously. More traps = faster catches = more GP per hour. This relationship is the foundation of Hunter money making:

Hunter LevelMax Traps (Safe)Max Traps (Wilderness)Key Unlock

🏕️ Wilderness Bonus: Being in the Wilderness grants +1 extra trap across ALL level brackets. At level 80+ in the Wilderness, you can set 6 traps simultaneously — the maximum possible in OSRS. This is the primary reason black chinchompas (Wilderness) are so much more profitable than red chinchompas (safe zone).

2. Black Chinchompas — Highest Profit (1M-3M+ GP/hr)

Black chinchompas are the most profitable Hunter method in OSRS, caught at level 73+ in the Wilderness (level 30-32, north of the Lava Maze). The PK risk is real, but the profit potential is unmatched for a skilling activity. Current GE price (2026): ~2,000-4,000 GP each.

Anti-PK Setup for Black Chins:

  • Bring only box traps. Fill your entire inventory with box traps. No armor, no weapons, no valuables. If you die, PKers get nothing of value.
  • One-click teleport. Ring of Dueling or Ardougne Cloak — both work below level 30 Wilderness. Teleport instantly if you see a white dot.
  • Bank frequently. Every 100-400 chinchompas, run south to bank. If you die, you only lose what you caught since your last bank trip.
  • Hop worlds at the first sign of PKers. If you see ANY player (not just skulled ones), hop worlds immediately. Most PKers scout unskulled.
  • Avoid peak hours. Weekday mornings have the fewest PKers. Friday/Saturday evenings are the most dangerous.

3. Red Chinchompas — Safe Profit (1.8M-1.8M GP/hr)

Red chinchompas are caught in the Feldip Hills (south of Yanille) at level 63+ Hunter. This is a completely safe, non-Wilderness zone. While less profitable than black chins, red chins offer reliable income with zero PK risk. Current GE price (2026): ~1,000-1,700 GP each.

Private Hunting Ground: Completing the Western Provinces Hard Diary unlocks a private red chinchompa hunting area with zero player competition. This is a game-changer — no one can steal your traps or compete for chinchompas. The diary requires various skills and quests but is well worth the effort for serious hunters.

4. Herbiboar — Passive Herb Income (1.8M-1.5M GP/hr)

Herbiboar is a unique Hunter activity on Fossil Island (requires 80 Hunter and 31 Herblore). Instead of stackable items, it provides grimy herbs — making it especially valuable for Ironmen who need herbs for Herblore training.

Herbiboar involves tracking the creature through Mushroom Meadow by following its footprints, dung, and other trail signs. Each successful track leads to a catch, which rewards herbs and Hunter XP. The process is more interactive than chinchompa trapping, making it a popular alternative for players who find box trapping monotonous.

PKers gain nothing from killing you except chinchompas — and if you bank frequently, even those are safe. The only thing a PKer can take is the chinchompas in your inventory.

7. Advanced Hunter Money Making Tips

  • Prioritize 80 Hunter above all else. The 5th trap (or 6th in Wilderness) is the single biggest productivity increase in Hunter. Every level from 1-80 should be aimed at unlocking the next trap tier.
  • Complete Western Provinces Hard Diary. The private red chinchompa hunting ground eliminates player competition entirely. Your catch rate increases by 20-30% simply because no one else is stealing your traps or scaring chinchompas.
  • Sell chinchompas in batches, not all at once. Dumping 5,000 red chinchompas on the GE simultaneously crashes the price for your own sale. Sell 500-1,000 at a time.
  • Chinchompa prices fluctuate with PvM meta. When new ranged-weak bosses release, chinchompa demand spikes. Monitor OSRS news and stockpile during low-demand periods to sell during peaks.
  • Use the Hunter skillcape perk. At 99 Hunter, the skillcape summons a falcon that passively helps catch implings. This makes magpie/ninja impling hunting significantly more profitable post-99.

8. Common Mistakes

  1. Bringing valuables to black chin hunting. This is the #1 mistake. PKers at black chins are common. If you bring your graceful outfit, rune pouch, or cash stack, you will lose them eventually. Bring ONLY box traps.
  2. Not banking frequently at black chins. If you stay for 2 hours and catch 2,000 chinchompas, a single PK death wipes all your profit. Bank every 15-20 minutes.
  3. Staying at black chins with a white dot visible. If you see ANY player dot on the minimap, teleport or log out. Don't wait to see if they're friendly — they're not.
  4. Using the wrong tier box trap. You need box traps (not bird snares) for chinchompas. Ensure you have the correct trap type in your inventory.
  5. Ignoring Herbiboar as an Ironman. Herbiboar provides a steady stream of ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol herbs that are otherwise difficult to farm in bulk. It's arguably more valuable for Ironmen than chinchompas.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How much GP per hour can I make with Hunter?

A: Black Chinchompas (73+ Hunter): 1.8M-6M+ GP/hr but with Wilderness PK risk. Red Chinchompas (63+ Hunter): 2M-2M GP/hr safely. Herbiboar (80+ Hunter): 1.8M-1.5M GP/hr plus Herblore XP. Dragon Implings (83+ Hunter): 1M-3M+ GP/hr but extremely rare spawns.

Q: What Hunter level gives 5 traps?

A: Level 80 Hunter unlocks the 5th trap. Additionally, being in the Wilderness grants +1 extra trap, allowing 6 traps at level 80+ in the Wilderness. This is the maximum possible and is why black chinchompas (Wilderness) are more profitable than red chinchompas despite similar individual catch rates.

Q: Is hunting black chinchompas worth the PK risk?

A: Yes — with proper precautions. Bring only box traps (no gear), use a one-click teleport, bank every 15-20 minutes, and log out at the first sign of any player. Black chins earn 2-3x more GP/hr than red chins, with GE prices typically ranging from ~2,000-4,000 GP each. If you die, you lose only the chinchompas caught since your last bank trip — box traps are ~40-80 GP each.

Q: What is the best Hunter method for Ironmen?

A: Herbiboar (80+ Hunter) provides ~100-140 grimy herbs per hour including ranarr, snapdragon, and torstol — invaluable for Herblore training. Red chinchompas are the second-best option for ranged training ammunition. Black chinchompas are risky but viable if you need ranged ammo for bossing.

🏹 Hunt Your Way to Wealth

Hunter transforms from a slow skilling grind into one of OSRS's best moneymakers at higher levels. The key is getting to level 80 for that 5th trap — every level before that is an investment. Whether you brave the Wilderness for black chinchompas or play it safe with red chins in Feldip Hills, Hunter delivers some of the most consistent skilling GP in the game.

Set your traps. Watch your minimap. And watch your cash stack grow.
