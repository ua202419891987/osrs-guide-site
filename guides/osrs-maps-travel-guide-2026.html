OSRS Maps & Fast Travel Guide 2026

Gielinor (the OSRS world) is HUGE. The F2P map alone is bigger than most modern RPGs' entire worlds. The members' map is 10x larger. You WILL get lost. This guide teaches you how to navigate efficiently and unlock fast travel methods.

1. World Map Basics — How to Not Get Lost

The OSRS world is called Gielinor. The world map is your best friend. Here's how to use it:

How to Open the World Map

  • Method 1: Click the minimap (top-right corner of game screen)
  • Method 2: Click the globe icon next to the minimap
  • Method 3 (RuneLite): Install the "World Map" plugin for an enhanced map experience

How to Set a Destination and Follow the Arrow

🗺️ Setting a Waypoint (Step-by-Step)

  1. Open the world map
  2. Find your destination in the left sidebar (or click directly on the map)
  3. Right-click the location name → "Add marker"
  4. Close the map
  5. A green arrow appears on your screen — follow it!
  6. When you arrive, the arrow disappears automatically
💡 The Green Arrow Is Your Best Friend

New players often try to navigate by looking at the minimap and guessing which direction to go. This leads to getting lost 50% of the time. Always set a marker and follow the green arrow. It's foolproof.

2. F2P Map — Key Locations You Must Know

As a F2P player, you only have access to Misthalin (the starting continent). Here are the locations you'll visit constantly:

📍 Detailed Routes Between Key Locations

🗺️ Lumbridge → Varrock (2-3 minutes walking)

Route: Exit Lumbridge through the north gate. Follow the dirt road north past the Cow Field (you'll see cows on your right). Continue north until you see the Varrock south gate (a large stone gate with two guards). Enter the city.

Landmarks: Cow field (right side), River Lum (cross the bridge), Wheat field, Varrock south gate.

Running energy used: ~30-40% (starting from Lumbridge castle with full energy).

🗺️ Varrock → Falador (4-5 minutes walking)

Route: Exit Varrock through the west gate. Follow the road west and slightly south. You'll pass through the skill area (where players are cutting trees and mining). Continue west until you see the white stone walls of Falador. Enter through the east gate.

Landmarks: Varrock west gate, Skill area (trees/mining spots), Falador east gate (white walls).

Alternative: Use the Varrock Teleport (level 25 Magic) then run west to Falador.

🗺️ Lumbridge → Draynor Village (2 minutes walking)

Route: Exit Lumbridge through the south gate. Cross the small bridge over the River Lum. Continue south until you reach Draynor Village (you'll see willow trees along the river). The village has a market area with NPCs.

Landmarks: Lumbridge south gate, Small bridge, Willow trees (good for Woodcutting), Draynor market.

Why go here: Willow trees for Woodcutting training (level 30+), Draynor Manor quest (started in the manor north of the village).

🗺️ Port Sarim → Karamja (Boat, 30 GP)

Route: Go to Port Sarim (southwest of Falador). Find the boat to Musa Point (look for the sinking boat icon on the map). Pay the ship captain 30 GP. You'll arrive at Musa Point (Karamja Island).

What's on Karamja: Musa Point (dock area), Karamja Dungeon (entrance south of Musa Point — has Lesser Demons for Ranged training), Banana trees (east of dungeon — can pick bananas for Cooking training).

Return trip: Pay another 30 GP to return to Port Sarim. Or walk to Musa Point and pay the cart driver to take you to Shilo Village (members only).

💡 Pro Tip: Use the Map Marker for Long Trips

For routes longer than 3 minutes (like Lumbridge → Port Sarim), always set a marker on the world map. The green arrow will guide you and prevent getting lost. This is especially important in areas with multiple paths (like the forest south of Varrock).

📍 Memory Trick: The "Varrock-Lumbridge-Falador Triangle"

These three towns form a triangle in central Misthalin. Most F2P content happens inside this triangle. If you can navigate between these three, you can handle 90% of F2P content.

3. Walking & Running — Stamina Management

You have two movement speeds:

  • Walking: Slow. Uses no run energy. Use for short distances.
  • Running: Fast (2x speed). Uses run energy. Toggle with the running shoe icon (bottom-right of game screen).

Run Energy — How It Works

FactorEffect on Run Energy
WalkingRestores 0.5% per game tick (0.3 seconds)
RunningDrains 0.7% per game tick (varies with weight)
Carrying weightMore weight = faster energy drain
Agility levelHigher Agility = slower drain + faster restore
Stamina potions (members)Restores 20% + slows drain by 70% for 2 minutes

📊 Practical Energy Management Strategies

🚶 Short Trips (Under 30 seconds)

Strategy: Walk, don't run. Running for 20 seconds drains ~15% energy — not worth it for short distances.

Examples: Lumbridge castle → Lumbridge swamp (walk), Varrock east bank → Varrock west bank (walk).

🏃 Medium Trips (30 seconds - 2 minutes)

Strategy: Run, but only until you reach ~40% energy. Then walk the rest to let it restore. Or run the whole way if you have a musician nearby at the destination.

Examples: Lumbridge → Varrock (run, uses ~50% energy), Varrock → Falador (run, uses ~70% energy).

Musician locations: Lumbridge (near general store), Varrock (near east bank), Falador (near west bank), Draynor (near market). Stand near a musician to restore energy to 100% instantly.

🏃♂️ Long Trips (Over 2 minutes)

Strategy (F2P): Run until ~20% energy, then walk. Or walk the entire way (boring but saves energy for when you need it).

Strategy (P2P): Use Stamina potions (Level 26 Herblore). Each dose restores 20% energy and reduces drain by 70% for 2 minutes. This lets you run continuously for 5+ minutes.

Examples: Lumbridge → Port Sarim (walk, ~4 minutes), Varrock → Al-Kharid (run + walk, ~3 minutes).

⚖️ Weight Management — Why It Matters

Your carry weight directly affects run energy drain. The heavier you are, the faster your energy drains while running.

How to check weight: Hover over your inventory (bottom-right corner shows weight in kg).

Weight reduction: ( Members only) Runecrafting pouches reduce weight. Boots of Lightness (obtained in the Temple of Ikov quest) reduce weight. Penance gloves (Horror from the Deep quest) reduce weight.

F2P tip: Drop heavy items before running long distances. For example, if you have 20 iron ore (each weighs 1 kg), drop them, run to the bank, then come back for them.

💡 Pro Tip: Drop Heavy Items Before Running Long Distances

If you're carrying 20 iron ore (heavy!) and need to run to the bank, your run energy will drain in 30 seconds. Drop the ore, run to the bank, then come back for it (or teleport). Weight matters more than you think in F2P.

4. Teleport Spells — Magic Fast Travel

Once you have some Magic levels and runes, teleport spells are the fastest way to travel. Each spell teleports you to a specific location.

F2P Teleport Spells (Available Without Quests)

SpellMagic LevelRunes NeededDestination
Varrock Teleport253 Air + 1 Fire + 1 LawVarrock (south entrance)
Lumbridge Teleport313 Air + 1 Earth + 1 LawLumbridge Castle courtyard
Falador Teleport373 Air + 1 Water + 1 LawFalador city square
Camelot Teleport (members)455 Air + 1 LawSeers' Village (members area)

📖 How to Get Teleport Spells (F2P)

📍 Step 1: Buy the Required Runes

Where to buy: Varrock rune shop (northwest Varrock, near the west bank). The shop is called "Runes Emporium". You can also buy runes from the Grand Exchange (Varrock market area).

Cost for one Varrock Teleport: 3 Air runes (~15 GP) + 1 Fire rune (~5 GP) + 1 Law rune (~200 GP) = ~220 GP per teleport.

Budget tip: Buy Air runes in bulk (1,000+) to reduce cost per teleport. Law runes are the expensive part — save them for important trips.

📍 Step 2: Open Your Spellbook

How: Click the Magic tab (looks like a star, located near your Inventory tab). This opens your Spellbook.

If you don't see teleport spells: You might be on the Ancient Magicks or Lunar Spellbook. To switch back to the Standard Spellbook: Go to the Wizard's Tower (south of Draynor Village), talk to Wizard Sedridor, and select "Change spellbook to Standard."

📍 Step 3: Cast the Teleport Spell

How: Click the teleport spell in your Spellbook. Your character will glow briefly (chanting animation). After ~3 seconds, you'll teleport to the destination.

Important: You must have the required runes in your inventory (or in your equipped weapon if it provides runes, like a staff of air). If you don't have enough runes, the spell will be grayed out.

Interruption: If you take damage while casting, the teleport is interrupted and you lose the runes. Always ensure you're safe before teleporting.

📍 Step 4: Use a Staff to Save Inventory Space

Best staves for F2P:

  • Staff of Air (provides unlimited Air runes) — Buy from Zaff's Staff Shop in Varrock (200 GP).
  • Staff of Water (provides unlimited Water runes) — Buy from Zaff's Staff Shop (200 GP).
  • Staff of Earth (provides unlimited Earth runes) — Buy from Zaff's Staff Shop (200 GP).
  • Staff of Fire (provides unlimited Fire runes) — Buy from Zaff's Staff Shop (200 GP).

Why use a staff: If you have a Staff of Air equipped, you don't need to carry Air runes. This saves inventory space for food, potions, etc.

⚠️ Teleport Interruptions

If you take damage while casting a teleport, the spell is interrupted and you lose the runes. Always ensure you're safe before teleporting. Also, you can't teleport in the Wilderness (PvP area) above level 20 — keep that in mind!

✨ How to Use Teleport Spells

  1. Open your Spellbook (tab near Inventory)
  2. Click the teleport spell you want to cast
  3. Auto-cast will activate (your character glows briefly)
  4. Wait ~3 seconds → you arrive at the destination!
  5. Note: You need the required runes in your inventory or equipped weapon (staff that provides runes)

5. Lodestones — The Free Fast Travel Network (Members)

Important: Lodestones are a members-only feature. But since most players eventually become members, it's worth knowing about them early.

Lodestones are teleportation network points scattered across the members' world. Once you activate a lodestone (click it once), you can teleport to it from anywhere (except the Wilderness) for FREE.

📍 How to Use Lodestones

  1. Be a member
  2. Walk to a lodestone (they look like glowing pillars)
  3. Click it → "Activate lodestone"
  4. Now open your spellbook → "Lodestone Network" → click any activated lodestone → teleport!

F2P alternative: In F2P, your fast travel options are limited to: walking/running, teleport spells (Magic), and the boat to Karamja (Port Sarim → Musa Point, costs 30 GP each way).

6. Fairy Rings — Members' Best Fast Travel

Fairy rings are a network of teleportation rings hidden across Gielinor. They form the most comprehensive fast travel system in the game — but you need to complete the "Fairy Tale Part II" quest to use them.

🧚 Fairy Rings: The Long-Term Goal

You can't use fairy rings as a day-one player. But they're SO good that you should work toward them. Once unlocked, you can teleport to 50+ locations across the world instantly. This is a mid-game unlock (after ~10-15 quests completed).

8. Navigation Tips & Tricks

🧭 10 Navigation Rules Every Beginner Should Know

  1. Always set a map marker before traveling somewhere new. Follow the green arrow.
  2. Memorize the Varrock-Lumbridge-Falador triangle. These three towns cover 90% of F2P content.
  3. Unlock teleport spells early. Varrock Teleport (level 25 Magic) is your best friend. Save up runes to cast it.
  4. Don't run everywhere. Running drains energy. Walk for short trips; run for long ones.
  5. Use the "Run" toggle wisely. Turn it off when exploring (you want to notice things) and on when traveling long distances.
  6. Mark your bank on the map. You'll be going back constantly. Know exactly where your nearest bank is at all times.
  7. Don't enter the Wilderness (PvP area) by accident. The Wilderness starts north of Edgeville. Players can attack you there. Avoid it until you're ready.
  8. Use boats for island access. Port Sarim has a boat to Karamja (Musa Point). It costs 30 GP each way but gives access to new training areas.
  9. Download RuneLite's "World Map" plugin. It shows quest starts, skilling spots, and teleport locations directly on the map. Game-changing.
  10. Pay attention to landmarks. The big castle in Lumbridge, the massive church in Varrock, the white castle in Falador — these help you orient yourself.

11. Maps & Travel FAQs

How do I open the world map?

Click the map icon in the top-right of your game window (or press M on your keyboard). You can zoom in/out and click anywhere to set a destination marker (red X) that shows on your minimap.

What's the fastest way to travel between cities in F2P?

Varrock Teleport (Level 25 Magic) is the best F2P teleport. It takes you directly to Varrock's center. For other cities: Lumbridge Home Teleport (free, 30-min cooldown), Walk/run between nearby cities (Lumbridge↔Varrock in 2 min, Varrock↔Falador in 5 min). Members unlock Lodestones, Fairy Rings, Spirit Trees which make travel instant.

What are Lodestones and how do I unlock them?

Lodestones are free teleport locations (members only). You unlock them by clicking on them once in the game world. After that, you can teleport to any unlocked Lodestone from the Lodestone Network (click the Lodestone icon in your spellbook). Must-have Lodestones: Varrock, Lumbridge, Falador, Ardougne, Catherby.

What are Fairy Rings and how do I use them?

Fairy Rings are a teleport network unlocked by completing Lost City quest + Fairytale I quest. You use a Dramen Staff or Lunar Staff to activate them. Each ring has a 3-letter code (e.g., AJR for Rellekka, DKS for TzHaar). This is the best travel system in the game.

How do I restore run energy quickly?

Walking restores run energy 3× faster than standing still. Musician NPCs (found near Lumbridge, Varrock, Falador, and other cities) restore your run energy to 100% instantly when you stand near them. Energy potions (Level 26 Herblore) restore 20% energy per dose. Explorers' Ring (4) from the Lumbridge & Draynor Diary restores 100% energy 3× per day.

What's the minimap and how do I use it?

The minimap is the small circular map in the top-right corner of your screen. It shows your immediate surroundings (within ~30 tiles). Yellow dots = NPCs, White dots = other players, Red dots = monsters, Blue dots = items on the ground. Click on the minimap to walk in that direction. Right-click the minimap for zoom options.

How do I use the Varrock teleport tablet?

Buy Varrock Teleport tabs from the GE (~5,000 GP each) or another player. Right-click the tablet in your inventory and select "Break". You'll be instantly teleported to Varrock's center square. One-time use per tablet. Keep 5–10 in your inventory for emergency escapes.

What's the Wilderness and why should I avoid it?

The Wilderness (north of Varrock, level 1–53) is a PVP zone where other players can attack you. You lose most of your items on death (except your 3 most valuable). Always check the minimap for a skull icon before entering. If you see it, turn back. The Wilderness has some good money-making spots (Green Dragons, Chaos Altar), but they carry high risk.

Can I teleport while in combat?

No. You cannot teleport (or use most items) while in combat. You must wait 5 seconds after your last attack/being attacked before you can teleport. Exception: The Amulet of Glory and some special teleport items can be used during the 5-second delay (but not during active combat).

What's the difference between teleport tabs and spellbook teleports?

Spellbook teleports (from your Magic spellbook) require runes each time. They're free if you have runes, but they take a spot in your inventory for runes. Teleport tabs are single-use items that don't require runes — just break the tablet. They're more expensive per use but more convenient (no need to carry runes). Buy tabs from the GE for convenience, use spellbook teleports when you're training Magic anyway.

How do I unlock the Fairy Ring network?

You need to complete two quests: "Lost City" (to enter Zanaris and meet the fairies) and "Fairytale Part I" (to gain access to the fairy ring network). After both quests, you can use any fairy ring. You'll also need a Dramen Staff (obtained during Lost City) to activate the rings.

What's the fastest way to get from Lumbridge to Varrock?

F2P: Walk/run north from Lumbridge through the forest (2-3 minutes). P2P: Use Lumbridge Lodestone → teleport to Varrock Lodestone (instant). Alternatively, cast Varrock Teleport (Level 25 Magic, 3 Air + 1 Fire + 1 Law rune).

Can I use teleport spells in the Wilderness?

No. You cannot use teleport spells or teleport tabs in the Wilderness above level 20. Below level 20, you can use Wilderness Obelisks to teleport between obelisk locations, but standard teleports are blocked. This is why PvP players lure you into the Wilderness — you can't escape via teleport.

How do I find my way back to a location I discovered randomly?

Method 1: Check your Quest Journal (in the game interface) — many quest locations are marked there. Method 2: Ask in the game chat ("Where is the Draynor Manor?"). Method 3: Use RuneLite's World Map plugin — it shows points of interest. Method 4: Google "OSRS [location name] map" for a screenshot.
