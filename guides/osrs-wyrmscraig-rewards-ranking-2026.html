Quick Verdict

Must-grind (S-Tier): Hallowfell — the Mad Angel boss unique. A 2H sword with a cleave mechanic that hits up to 2 additional targets at 50% max hit. Projected 80M-150M GP. Every PvMer needs this.

High priority (A-Tier): Ornate Glassblowing Pipe (Crafting QoL, 5M-15M) and the Basalt Amulet with both upgrade attachments (teleport infrastructure, 3M-10M combined).

Situational (B-Tier): Granite Dust, Goat Horns / Combat Potions, Tiny Angel Pet, Mr McGroot. Good passive collection or collector items.

Skip (C-Tier): Goat Fur, Boss Jar. Collect passively if you must, but these are not worth targeting.

S-Tier — Must-Grind

Hallowfell — The Multi-Target Melee Dream

Source: Mad Angel boss — unique drop table

Requirements: 75 Attack

Estimated Value: 80M-150M GP (projected)

Hallowfell is the headline unique from Wyrmscraig, and for good reason. This two-handed sword features a Cleave mechanic that, when attacking a primary target, sweeps through nearby enemies. It hits up to two additional targets — one in the 3 tiles in front of you and another in the 2 tiles adjacent to you. Each secondary target takes 50% of your max hit.

What makes Hallowfell particularly interesting is how its damage scaling works. Unlike the Scythe of Vitur (which reduces damage by 50% then another 50% across three targets — 100% / 50% / 25%), Hallowfell's damage reduction happens only once: the primary target takes full damage, and both secondary targets take 50%. This means when hitting 3 targets, your total damage output per swing is 200% of your max hit, compared to the Scythe's 175%.

Why it's S-Tier:

  • Multi-target dominance: In content like the Nylocas room in Theatre of Blood, baboon waves in Tombs of Amascut, or multi-combat Slayer tasks, Hallowfell's cleave dramatically increases clear speed.
  • No per-swing cost: Unlike the Scythe which consumes blood runes on every hit, Hallowfell has no consumable cost beyond standard weapon charges. This makes it far more economical for extended bossing sessions.
  • Mid-game accessible: Requiring only 75 Attack, it's usable by a much wider range of players than the Scythe (85 Attack) or Soulreaper Axe (85 Attack).
  • Unique mechanic: The cleave specifically targets "unique targets" — individual creatures rather than different parts of a large boss. This makes it excellent for rooms with many small enemies but less effective against 3x3 bosses where the Scythe shines.

We project Hallowfell to settle in the 80M-150M GP range — competitive with the Soulreaper Axe. If you're an ironman, this should be your single highest priority from Wyrmscraig.

Jagex's own words: "If a player is consistently hitting three targets, Hallowfell should significantly outperform every other melee weapon in the game." — June 26 News Post

A-Tier — High Priority

Ornate Glassblowing Pipe — The Crafting QoL Revolution

Source: Sunstone Golem Crafting — collected as two separate pieces with duplicate protection

Estimated Value: 5M-15M GP

This is one of those deceptively simple updates that changes everything for Crafting trainers. The Ornate Glassblowing Pipe is an equippable glassblowing pipe. When you have it equipped, your inventory can hold unlimited molten glass — no more banking every 27 pieces. As part of the same update, molten glass gets a left-click "Use" option to streamline the entire glassblowing workflow.

Why it's A-Tier:

  • Massive time savings: During the 61-99 Crafting grind via glassblowing, you bank hundreds of times. This eliminates almost all of those trips. The time saved across the entire 1-99 journey could be 5-10 hours.
  • Ironman essential: For ironmen who depend on glassblowing as their primary Crafting method, the Ornate Glassblowing Pipe is arguably mandatory. It directly addresses one of the most cited frustrations of ironman crafting.
  • Durable demand: Because new players are always training Crafting, this item will maintain steady demand indefinitely. It's not a one-time novelty.
  • Duplicate-protected grind: The two pieces are obtained with a duplicate protection system, meaning you will eventually get both. The grind is predictable, not RNG-dependent.

The only reason this isn't S-Tier is that it doesn't directly improve combat performance. But for skillers and ironmen, it's the most impactful non-combat unique in the entire Wyrmscraig update.

Basalt Amulet + Cathedral & Sunstone Upgrades — The Transportation Backbone

Source: Basalt Amulet from the Fallen From Grace quest; Cathedral Teleport upgrade from Mad Angel boss; Sunstone Resonator upgrade from Sunstone Golem Crafting

Estimated Value: 3M-10M GP (combined for both upgrades)

The Basalt Amulet is your first Wyrmscraig reward — it's the quest reward for completing Fallen From Grace, providing a teleport to the island. Each teleport consumes 1 charge, and you can bank up to 1,000 charges. Recharging costs 1 law rune, 5 body runes, and 5 earth runes per 5 charges — extremely cheap.

The two upgrades transform the amulet from a basic quest reward into a genuine utility item:

  • Cathedral Teleport (Boss drop): Adds a direct teleport to the Cathedral, which is the Mad Angel boss arena. For anyone farming Hallowfell, this is a massive time saver.
  • Sunstone Resonator (Golem Crafting): Adds a direct teleport to the Sunstone Mine, streamlining the golem crafting loop.

Why they're A-Tier:

  • Direct time savings for boss runs and skilling loops — the hallmark of valuable utility items.
  • Cheap to charge with widely available runes.
  • The Cathedral teleport will be in high demand from Mad Angel farmers.
  • Creates a self-contained loop where you can teleport between activities without leaving the island.

B-Tier — Situational but Worth Having

Granite Dust — Passive Income from Bossing

Source: Mad Angel boss — occasional drop

Estimated Value: 500K-2M GP (per stack)

Granite Dust is already in the game with limited sources (notably from the Grotesque Guardians). The Mad Angel boss adds a supplementary source that makes accumulation more convenient without significantly disrupting the existing market. Jagex described it as an "occasional" drop that won't "significantly change how it's currently obtained."

Why B-Tier:

  • Supplementary source — the boss isn't worth farming purely for Granite Dust.
  • As a passive drop while grinding Hallowfell, it's welcome bonus income.
  • Used in various crafting/construction projects including Granite Crab pets.
  • Expect a moderate price drop (20-40%) in the first month as supply increases.

Goat Horns → Combat Potions — Hunter-Herblore Bridge

Source: Goat Hunting — common drop

Estimated Value: 200K-800K GP (per stack of Goat Horn Dust)

Goat Horns can be ground into Goat Horn Dust, which is used to make Combat Potions. This creates a direct connection between the Hunter and Herblore skills — a welcome addition to the game's skill ecology. For ironmen, this provides an alternative pathway to combat potions without relying on the Grand Exchange.

Why B-Tier:

  • Combat Potions aren't meta-defining — Super Combat Potions already exist and are well-established for high-level PvM.
  • However, as a byproduct of Goat Hunting (which is itself decent Hunter XP at levels 60-80), the passive income is solid.
  • Ironmen will appreciate another source of combat potion ingredients that ties into skilling progression on the island.
  • Price will likely be stable at 200-800 GP per horn dust, adding 50K-100K GP/hr to hunting sessions.

Tiny Angel Pet — The "Slightly Mad" Boss Pet

Source: Mad Angel boss — very rare drop

Estimated Value: Untradeable (pet) — status value only

Described by Jagex as a "slightly mad, tiny angel," this pet drops from the Mad Angel boss. It joins the ranks of OSRS's extensive pet collection. Like all boss pets, it's purely cosmetic but highly sought after by pet hunters and completionists.

Why B-Tier:

  • Pets have zero gameplay impact — purely cosmetic.
  • Boss pet drop rates are typically 1/3000-1/5000, making it a long-term grind for collectors.
  • As a Mad Angel pet, you'll naturally obtain it while grinding for Hallowfell if you're persistent enough.
  • Dedicated pet hunters will spend hundreds of hours here.

Mr McGroot — The Goat Pet (Poll Pending)

Source: Rare drop from Goat Hunting

Estimated Value: Untradeable (pet) — status value OR transmog value

Mr McGroot is a goat pet obtained from hunting goats on Wyrmscraig. Its exact implementation is currently subject to a community poll with three options:

  1. Standalone pet: Mr McGroot is an independent pet you can get from Goat Hunting.
  2. Transmog: Mr McGroot is a transmog for an existing pet. Players must first obtain the original pet before using this appearance.
  3. Scrapped: Mr McGroot is not added to the game.

Why B-Tier:

  • If it passes as a standalone pet, it will be a status symbol for Hunter enthusiasts.
  • If it's a transmog, its value depends entirely on the base pet's popularity.
  • Pet hunters will grind for it regardless, but the general playerbase can treat it as a fun bonus.

C-Tier — Collector or Niche Only

Goat Fur — The Connector Material

Source: Goat Hunting — common drop

Estimated Value: 50K-200K GP

Goat Fur is used in the Sunstone Golem Crafting activity to provide "clothing" for your golems. It's designed as a connector between the Hunting and Crafting activities on Wyrmscraig, encouraging self-sufficiency. However, higher-quality fur purchased from other players offers better XP rates, so low-tier goat fur has limited economic value.

Why C-Tier:

  • Extremely common drop from Goat Hunting — supply will massively outstrip demand.
  • Jagex has explicitly mentioned monitoring "ShopScape" to prevent store-bought fur from undermining the activity. This suggests the fur economy is fragile and likely to be low-value.
  • Primarily useful for ironmen who want to stay self-sufficient on the island. For mains, buying higher-quality fur is more efficient.
  • Best collected passively while hunting for Mr McGroot or training Hunter.

Boss Jar (Slightly Mad, Tiny Angel) — POH Decoration

Source: Mad Angel boss — rare drop

Estimated Value: 1M-5M GP

The boss jar is a Player-Owned House decoration, continuing the tradition of boss jars for the aquarium and display cases. It features the "slightly mad, tiny angel" theme consistent with the Mad Angel boss fight.

Why C-Tier:

  • Pure cosmetic — zero stat or gameplay benefits.
  • Very rare for a purely cosmetic item. You'll likely get it passively while grinding for Hallowfell.
  • Limited demand from the POH decoration community — jars are among the least sought-after boss uniques.
  • Worth 1-5M mostly because of rarity, not demand.

Optimal Grinding Strategy

If You're a Main Account (GP-Focused)

  1. Complete Fallen From Grace first — The Basalt Amulet is your ticket to everything on Wyrmscraig. Without it, you're relying on Sailing to reach the island every time.
  2. Farm Mad Angel for Hallowfell — This is your primary GP target. The boss also drops Granite Dust, the Cathedral Teleport upgrade, the Boss Jar, and the Tiny Angel Pet as bonus income or collection items.
  3. Alternate with Golem Crafting for the Pipe — Break up bossing sessions with the Sunstone Golem activity. The Ornate Glassblowing Pipe has steady demand from the Crafting community.
  4. Skip Goat Hunting for GP — Goat Horns and Goat Fur are too common to be genuinely profitable. Only hunt if you enjoy the activity or need the Hunter XP.

If You're an Ironman

  1. Hallowfell is your top priority — It's a massive upgrade over the Abyssal Whip for multi-combat scenarios and has no per-swing consumption cost. For ironmen who can't buy a Scythe, this is your best multi-target melee option.
  2. Ornate Glassblowing Pipe is essential — Without the Grand Exchange, ironmen rely heavily on glassblowing for Crafting training. This saves dozens of hours of banking.
  3. Basalt Amulet upgrades are required — Both upgrades significantly reduce travel time on the island. Gather them as early as possible.
  4. Goat Hunting for self-sufficiency — The connection between Goat Fur and Golem Crafting lets you stay on the island longer without leaving. This is particularly valuable for ultimate ironmen.

What to Skip or Delay

  • Boss Jar — Nice if you get one while grinding Hallowfell, but not worth extending your boss grind for.
  • Goat Fur — Only collect it passively while Hunter training. Don't grind specifically for it.
  • Tiny Angel Pet / Mr McGroot — Only go for these if you're a dedicated pet hunter or completionist. They have zero gameplay impact.

Time Investment Estimates for Full Completion

If your goal is to collect every unique reward from Wyrmscraig, here are realistic time estimates:

  • Mad Angel Boss (Hallowfell + Jar + Pet + Cathedral Upgrade + Granite Dust): 50-200 hours depending on Hallowfell and pet RNG. The Jar and Granite Dust will accumulate naturally. The Cathedral Teleport upgrade is expected to be a reasonable rare drop.
  • Sunstone Golem Crafting (Ornate Glassblowing Pipe + Sunstone Resonator): 20-40 hours. The duplicate protection on the Pipe pieces means you'll have both by the time you finish the Crafting grind.
  • Goat Hunting (Mr McGroot + Goat Horns + Goat Fur): 30-80 hours for Mr McGroot (assuming pet rates of 1/5000-1/10000). The other drops accumulate naturally.

FAQ — Wyrmscraig Rewards

What is Wyrmscraig and how do I get there?

Wyrmscraig is a player-designed island in the Unquiet Ocean, accessible via the Sailing skill. You'll need to complete the Fallen From Grace quest to unlock the Basalt Amulet, which provides the primary teleport access to the island. The amulet can hold up to 1,000 charges and is cheap to refill.

Is Hallowfell better than the Scythe of Vitur?

It depends on the content. Against 3+ small targets (Nylocas, baboons, multi-combat Slayer), Hallowfell outperforms the Scythe because its cleave damage only halves once (100% / 50% / 50% = 200% total) versus the Scythe's double reduction (100% / 50% / 25% = 175%). Against 2x2 or 3x3 bosses, the Scythe is superior because it hits multiple sizes. They're complementary weapons for different scenarios.

How rare is the Ornate Glassblowing Pipe?

The Pipe is assembled from two pieces obtained through Sunstone Golem Crafting. Critically, the activity has duplicate protection, meaning you won't get duplicate pieces. This makes the Pipe a predictable grind-based reward rather than an RNG-dependent rare drop — you'll have it after enough Golem Crafting sessions.

Will the Basalt Amulet upgrades be tradeable?

The upgrade components (Cathedral Teleport upgrade and Sunstone Resonator) are expected to be tradeable, similar to how other equipment upgrade components work in OSRS. The Basalt Amulet itself is untradeable as a quest reward. Once you apply an upgrade, the amulet becomes permanently upgraded on your account.

Can I make money from Goat Hunting?

Goat Hunting is primarily a Hunter training method (recommended for levels 60-80). Goat Horns and Goat Fur have modest value, but they're common drops so supply will be high. Expect to make 50K-100K GP/hour from Goat Hunting — decent supplementary income but not a primary money-maker.

Is Mr McGroot a standalone pet or a transmog?

This is being decided by a community poll. Three options are on the table: (1) standalone pet, (2) transmog for an existing pet, or (3) scrapped entirely. Check the poll results — if it passes as a standalone pet, it will be a dedicated Hunted pet that pet collectors will chase.

What's the difference between the Tiny Angel Pet and Mr McGroot?

The Tiny Angel Pet drops from the Mad Angel boss and is a "slightly mad, tiny angel" — a boss pet in the traditional sense. Mr McGroot comes from Goat Hunting and is a goat-themed pet. They are completely separate rewards from different activities.

Do I need a Fire Cape to fight the Mad Angel?

Jagex has designed the Mad Angel for mid-game PvM players who have obtained a Fire Cape. While there's no hard requirement, the boss mechanics and expected DPS thresholds assume you have a Fire Cape or better. If you haven't got one yet, check our Fire Cape Guide first.

Will Granite Dust crash in price when Wyrmscraig launches?

Yes, expect a moderate price drop. Granite Dust currently has limited sources (primarily Grotesque Guardians) at around 1K-2K GP each. Adding the Mad Angel as a supplementary source will increase supply. We project a 20-40% price drop in the first month, followed by stabilisation as the market absorbs the new supply.

When does Wyrmscraig release?

Based on Jagex's June 26 news post, Wyrmscraig is expected to launch in late July 2026 as part of the Sailing content rollout. The unique rewards are subject to community polls, so some items may change before the final release.

Final Tips

  • Start with Fallen From Grace — The Basalt Amulet is your gateway. Don't go to Wyrmscraig without completing this quest first.
  • Prioritise Hallowfell above all else — It's the most impactful unique and will hold its value well. Everything else is secondary.
  • Grind the Ornate Glassblowing Pipe early — If you train Crafting, every day without this item is wasted time on banking trips.
  • Don't go out of your way for Goat Hunting uniques — The hunting rewards are best collected passively while training Hunter.
  • Use both Basalt Amulet upgrades — The Cathedral + Sunstone Mine teleports create an efficient loop between bossing and skilling.
