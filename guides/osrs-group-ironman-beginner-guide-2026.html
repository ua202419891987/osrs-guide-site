👥 OSRS Group Ironman Beginner Guide 2026

Group Ironman (GIM) is the ultimate co-op OSRS experience — you and your friends start from scratch, share resources, and conquer Gielinor together. This guide covers everything a new GIM team needs: how to form your group, divide early-game tasks, share loot, and progress faster than any solo Ironman ever could. Updated for all 2026 game changes.

📅 Updated: June 2026 — Complete GIM team strategy · 18 min read
💡 Quick Verdict — GIM Beginner Strategy 2026

Start with 3-5 trusted players. Assign each member a "profession" (Miner/Smith, Fletcher/Fisher, Mage/Runecrafter, Herblore Secondary, Crafter). Rush Waterfall Quest → Fight Arena → Tree Gnome Village for fast early combat levels. Share all resources through a group bank held by your most active player. The biggest advantage GIM has over solo Ironman is the ability to specialise — one person grinds 99 Fishing while another does Agility and Herblore. By dividing labour 5 ways, your team progresses 3-5x faster than a solo Ironman.

① 👥 What Is Group Ironman Mode?

Group Ironman (GIM) is an official OSRS game mode released in October 2021, then reworked with the Group Storage update in 2023 and further refined through 2025-2026. It lets 2-5 players form a "group" and play together under Ironman restrictions — no trading with outsiders, no Grand Exchange, no player-to-player item transfers outside the group.

What makes GIM unique is group-wide resource sharing. Within your group, you can freely trade items, share loot, and contribute to a shared Group Storage. This transforms the Ironman experience from a solo grind into a co-operative MMO within OSRS itself.

① Key Features

  • Group Size: 2-5 players per group. You can upgrade from 2 to 5 later by inviting new members.
  • Group Storage: A shared 96-slot bank accessible to all group members at any bank booth.
  • Shared Progression: All group members see the same "Group Hiscores" — total level, total XP, boss KC.
  • No Restrictions Within Group: Trade items freely, share drops, split GP — you're a single economy.
  • Prestige System: Groups can earn "Prestige" by completing all F2P quests then becoming members together. Prestige adds a permanent rank suffix to your group name.
  • Hardcore GIM: If your group is Hardcore, each member has 1 life. When one dies, they become a "regular" GIM but can stay in the group. When ALL die, the group loses Hardcore status.
💡 GIM vs. Ultimate Ironman Group

There is no "Ultimate Group Ironman" mode. All GIM groups use regular shared storage. Individual members can choose to play as "Ultimate" style (no banking) within their group, but the group storage feature requires standard banking. If you want a true "hardcore" GIM experience, start as Hardcore Group Ironman — but remember, one mistake costs your whole group the Hardcore status.

② 🤝 How to Form Your GIM Team

Team composition is the single most important decision in GIM. A badly balanced team (e.g., 5 players who all want to be "the fighter") will hit resource bottlenecks. A well-balanced team breezes through the early game.

① Ideal Team Size: 3-5 Players

2-player teams work but are inefficient — you're basically 2 solo Ironmen who can trade. 3-5 players is the sweet spot. With 5, you can assign 5 different professions and cover all early-game needs simultaneously.

③ Choosing Your Teammates Wisely

GIM is a long-term commitment — your group will exist for months or years. Here's what to look for in teammates:

  • Time Zone Match: If you play at 8 PM EST and your teammate plays at 8 AM GMT, you'll rarely play together. Match time zones for best co-op efficiency.
  • Reliability: One player who quits after week 1 leaves a gaping hole in your team's skill coverage. Choose friends who've shown commitment in previous games.
  • Role Acceptance: Everyone must agree on their role before starting. "I only want to PvM" doesn't work when the team needs a Herblore specialist.
  • Communication Tool: Use Discord, in-game chat, or a WhatsApp group. Voice chat during bossing is almost mandatory for GIM co-op content.
💡 The "Alt" Strategy

Each GIM member can have ONE alt account in the group. This is useful for: an extra AFK skiller (fishing while you boss), a pure for Wilderness content, or a mule for holding resources. But be careful — alt accounts split your attention and can burn you out faster. We recommend no alt until week 2-3 when you have a rhythm established.

① The "Shared GP" Economy

In regular Ironman, you use GP for: gear upgrades, runes, construction materials, and bonds. In GIM, GP is a shared resource. One player can grind 1M GP from Agility Pyramid while another does Barrows, and both contribute to the group GP pool. This means your group can afford big-ticket items (like a Dragon axe for the Woodcutter, or 70 Construction for the group house) much faster than a solo Ironman.

② The "No Trader" Constraint Still Applies

GIM groups cannot trade with players outside the group. This means: no buying from the Grand Exchange, no player-to-player trades, no trading items between groups. You are still an Ironman — just not a solo one.

💡 Can You "Solo" in a GIM Group?

Yes, absolutely. Many GIM players play 80% of the time solo and 20% in co-op. The group storage lets you dump resources for teammates to pick up later. If your schedule doesn't align with teammates, you can still make progress — just drop ore in the group bank and your Miner/Smith teammate can process it when they log in.

④ 📦 Resource Sharing Strategy — How to Share Like a Pro

Resource sharing is the core advantage of GIM. But unstructured sharing leads to disputes and burnout. Here's how to set up a sharing system that works.

① The Group Bank — Who Manages It?

Designate one player as the Group Bank Manager. This should be your most active, most organised member. Their responsibilities:

  • Maintain the shared Group Storage (~96 slots). Keep it organised with tags.
  • Track "group projects" — what resources are needed for the next big milestone (e.g., 70 Construction, 55 Crafting for Battlestaves).
  • Assign surplus resources to the right player (e.g., excess coal goes to the Smith, excess logs go to the Fletcher).
  • Keep a mental (or spreadsheet) note of who owes what to the group — don't let one player freeload.

② The "Tithe" System — Fair Contribution

To prevent freeloading, use a simple tithe system:

Crafted items (potions, food, gear)50% to group bank50% for personal useHerblore player makes 100 prayer potions → 50 in group, 50 personal
Boss loot (rares, high-value items)100% to group0% personal (split GP value or share)Bandos chestplate drops → group decides who needs it most

③ The "Gear Rotation" — Sharing Expensive Items

Your group will get rare drops (Dragon scimitar, Amulet of Fury, Bandos chestplate). Instead of one player hoarding it, use a gear rotation:

  • Shared Bossing Gear: Items used during group bossing (e.g., Trident of the Seas) stay in the group bank for anyone to withdraw.
  • Personal Progression Gear: Items for individual progression (e.g., Dragon scimitar for Slayer) rotate weekly or by task completion. "You use it for 1 week, then pass it to the next player."
  • BiS Gear: Give to the player who benefits most. For example: a Bandos chestplate goes to your tank (highest Defence) first, then rotates when they upgrade.
  • Dupes: Duplicate rares are personal property or sold-for-group-funds. Decide this BEFORE you start bossing.
💡 The "Group Goal" Whiteboard

Use a Discord channel or Google Doc as your "Group Goal Whiteboard." List current goals, who's working on what, and what resources are needed. Example: "Goal: 70 Construction for group house portal nexus. Need: ~5,000 mahogany planks. Player 1: cut mahogany logs. Player 2: make planks at Sawmill. Player 3: grind GP for plank make spell. ETA: 3 days."

💡 The "One Big Grind" Strategy

In GIM, you can do something impossible in solo Ironman: focus 100% of your playtime on one skill, knowing someone else covers your other needs. If you're the Miner/Smith, you can mine+smith for 20 hours straight without touching Fishing — because your Fisher/Cook teammate is covering food. This concentrated focus means each player hits 99 in their primary skill 2-3x faster than a solo Ironman hitting 99 in the same skill.

  • Horror from the Deep: Unlocks God Books (Arma book = +10 Prayer, useful for bossing). One person does this; the group shares pages.
  • Recruitment Drive: Unlocks Initiate armour set (good Prayer bonus gear). One person does this; the set can be traded within the group.
💡 The "Quest Splits" Method

Don't make every group member do every quest. Instead, split quests by reward type. For example: the Miner/Smith does The Giant Dwarf (Smithing XP), the Herblore player does Druidic Ritual (unlocks Herblore), and the Fisher/Cook does Swan Song (unlocks monkfish). Each player gets XP in their primary skill, and the group benefits from unlocked content faster.

⑦ 🚀 Early Game Progression — Your First Week

Here's a day-by-day roadmap for your group's first week of GIM. Adjust based on your playtime and team size.

① Day 1-2: Account Creation + Core Quests

  • Create your GIM group on the OSRS website. Choose a group name that represents your team's identity. Pick "Hardcore" if everyone commits — but prepare for the risk.
  • Tutorial Island → Lumbridge. All members complete Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree immediately.
  • Train Attack to 40+ via quest rewards. Start using rune weapons (your Miner/Smith should be mining iron ASAP).
  • Complete Druidic Ritual (Herblore player only) to unlock Herblore for the group.
  • Set up Group Storage at a bank. Designate the Group Bank Manager.
  • Goal by end of Day 2: All members at 40+ Attack, 25+ Defence, 20+ HP. Miner/Smith has 35+ Mining. Fisher has 30+ Fishing.

② Day 3-4: Skill Specialisation Begins

  • Miner/Smith: Power-mine iron ore at Al Kharid or Trahaearn mine. Smelt steel bars → Smith steel platebodies for the group. Aim for full steel gear by end of Day 4.
  • Fisher/Cook: Fish trout/salmon at Shilo Village (unlock Shilo Village quest first). Cook everything. Deposit cooked fish in Group Storage.
  • Woodcutter/Fletcher: Cut oak trees → make oak planks for Construction. Fletch willow shortbows for early alch GP.
  • Herblore/Farming: Complete Fairytale I for fairy ring access. Start farming runs (potato seeds → onions → cabbages). Buy Vial of Water + Guam leaves from Drogo's shop.
  • Crafter/Mage: Complete Family Crest for Chaos Gauntlets. Craft gold rings → high-alch for group GP.

③ Day 5-7: First Milestones

  • Group Goal: All members at 500+ total level. This unlocks GIM group prestige rank (if you're all F2P quest champions).
  • Miner/Smith: Smithing 45 (mithril gear for the group). Start Construction — build a house with portal room + mounted glory.
  • Fisher/Cook: Fishing 50+ (lobsters). Cooking 50+ (cook all lobsters without burning). Deposit 500+ cooked food in Group Storage.
  • Woodcutter/Fletcher: Woodcutting 50+ (yew logs). Fletching 45+ (yew longbows). High-alch the bows for group GP (~500 GP profit each).
  • Herblore/Farming: Farming 32+ (ranarrs unlocked). Herblore 15+ (attack potions). Start making prayer potions as soon as possible (level 38).
  • Crafter/Mage: Crafting 50+ (emerald jewellery). Magic 55+ (high alch, superheat). Deposit 100+ games necklaces + duelling rings in Group Storage.
💡 Wintertodt — The GIM Starter

Wintertodt is a fantastic group activity in early GIM. All members with 10+ Firemaking can participate. The rewards include: magic logs, yew logs, and the Tome of Fire — which is amazing for the group's mage. Plus, Wintertodt gives early Construction, Farming, and Herblore XP. Do 50-100 Wintertodt KC as a group in your first week. The supplies you get will fuel your skill division for weeks.

⑧ 🏹 Mid-Game Goals — Your First Month

After your first week, your team should have base 40s in core skills and a functioning group economy. Here's your roadmap for weeks 2-4.

① Week 2: Base 60s + Group House

  • Group Goal: Build the group house. Have your Miner/Smith (or whoever has highest Construction) rush 50+ Construction. Build: Portal Room (with Varrock/Lumbridge/Falador teleports), Mounted Glory (need Crafting 50 + dragonstone), Altar for prayer training. The group house is the single biggest quality-of-life upgrade in GIM.
  • Herblore Rush: Get Herblore to 38 (prayer potions). This is non-negotiable — prayer potions enable all group bossing.
  • Agility: Have your Crafter/Mage rush 50+ Agility for Graceful outfit and key shortcuts. Graceful helps everyone's herb runs and questing.
  • Slayer Start: All members start Slayer at Turael (Lumbridge). Assign the group's "Slayer master rush" — one person does Slayer to 85 for whips, while others focus on production skills.

② Week 3: Barrows + First Bossing

  • Barrows: When your group has 60+ Magic, 60+ Ranged, and 43 Prayer, start Barrows as a group. Bring one tank, one DPS, and one healer. Group Barrows is 2-3x faster than solo per player.
  • Managing Miscellania: Complete Royal Trouble as a group. Assign one player to manage Miscellania (requires Completion of Throne of Miscellania + Royal Trouble). Set it to: 50% Coal, 25% Mahogany, 25% Herbs. This generates 50K-100K GP/day in passive resources.
  • Farming Guild: Get Farming to 45 (medium tier contracts). Farming contracts give seeds that fuel your Herblore player's potion-making.

③ Week 4: God Wars Dungeon Entry

  • Requirement: 70+ Defence, 70+ HP, 43+ Prayer, 70+ Magic (for trident). All members should have these stats before GWD.
  • Group Gear Check: Your Miner/Smith should provide rune+ armour for everyone. Your Herblore player should have 100+ prayer potions banked. Your Fisher/Cook should have 500+ sharks inventoried.
  • First GWD Boss: General Graardor (Bandos). Tank + 2 DPS + Healer is the standard 4-man setup. 5-man is Tank + 3 DPS + Healer. Expect 5-10 kills per trip with basic gear.
  • Group Fund: By week 4, your group should have 5M+ GP in the group bank. Use this for: 70 Construction (portal nexus), 55 Crafting (battlestaves), and your first "big" group purchase (Dragon pickaxe, Dragon axe, Trident of the Seas).
💡 The "Group Donation" System

At this stage, set up a voluntary Group Donation System. Each player donates 20% of their personal alch/Thieving/Slayer GP to the group fund. This builds a 2-5M GP war chest for group projects. The group fund pays for: house upgrades, potion supplies, and group gear (like a shared Trident of the Seas). Track donations in a simple spreadsheet — it prevents disputes and keeps everyone accountable.

💡 GIM Bossing Tip: The "Bulk Drop" Strategy

When a rare drop occurs in group GIM bossing, don't split it 5 ways with GP. Instead, keep the item in the group bank and give it to the player who benefits most. For example: when a Bandos chestplate drops, your group tank gets it first. When a Dragon axe drops, your Woodcutter gets it first. This keeps gear in the group (not sold for GP) and accelerates progression. Only sell dupes. This strategy works because GIM groups share boss loot — selling a unique and splitting GP 5 ways is usually a net loss for the group's overall power.

⑩ ⚠️ Common GIM Mistakes to Avoid

Even experienced OSRS players make these GIM-specific mistakes. Avoid them to keep your group healthy and progressing.

① Mistake 1: Not Assigning Roles at the Start

The #1 reason GIM groups fail is "role clash" — two players both want to be the Mage, and nobody wants to be the Miner. Assign roles BEFORE you create the group. Write them down. If someone won't commit to a production skill role, don't invite them.

② Mistake 2: Uneven Contribution

One player grinds 8 hours/day while another plays 1 hour/day. The active player feels resentful that their resources are "feeding" the casual player. Solution: set a minimum contribution expectation. For example: "Each member must deposit at least 50K GP worth of resources per week, or 2 hours of bossing together." Adjust based on your group's playtime. Use a simple Google Sheet to track contributions transparently.

③ Mistake 3: Not Using Group Storage Properly

Some groups treat Group Storage as a "junk dump" — full of random low-value items that nobody uses. Solution: organise Group Storage with tags. Create categories: "Food," "Potions," "Ore/Bars," "Logs/Planks," "Gear," "Runes." Only deposit items that another group member can use. If nobody in the group trains Runecrafting, don't deposit 10K air runes — just alch them for GP.

④ Mistake 4: Ignoring Herblore Until It's Too Late

Herblore is the bottleneck for all GIM progression. Without prayer potions, you can't boss. Without super restores, you can't raid. Your Herblore player should start farming herbs on day 1. The rest of the group should feed all herb seeds to the Herblore player. Don't let anyone "stockpile" herb seeds for personal use — they belong to the group.

⑤ Mistake 5: Buying Bonds with GP Instead of Using Real Money

In GIM, bonds are purchased with group GP. If one player buys a bond with 10M group GP, that's 10M GP that could have been used for 70 Construction or a Trident of the Seas. Recommendation: if you can afford it, buy membership with real money (£5-£8/month). Save group GP for group progression items. If you must use GP, treat it as a group decision with a vote.

⑥ Mistake 6: Not Setting Expectations for "Rare Drop Ownership"

This is the #1 cause of GIM group breakups. "I got the Bandos chestplate drop, it's MINE" vs. "We were bossing as a group, it's GROUP property." Solution: BEFORE your first group bossing session, agree on a drop policy. We recommend: "All boss drops (rares and unique) belong to the group. The group decides who gets it first based on need. Dupes are sold and split equally among active members." Write it down. Make everyone agree.

⑦ Mistake 7: Burning Out from "Always Grouping"

Playing 100% co-op is exhausting. Schedule solo time. Many GIM players do their daily herb runs, birdhouses, and farm contracts solo, then group up for bossing 2-3 evenings per week. This prevents burnout and lets each player progress at their own pace.

❓ FAQ — Group Ironman Beginner Questions

Q: Can I leave my GIM group and join another?

A: Yes, but with restrictions. If you leave a GIM group, your account becomes a regular Ironman — you lose GIM status and cannot rejoin a GIM group with that account. The only exception is the Group Splitting feature (added 2025): if your group votes to split, all members keep GIM status and form new groups. But individual departure = permanent Ironman status for that account.

Q: What happens if a GIM teammate quits the game?

A: Their items remain in the Group Storage and their personal bank. You can withdraw their items from Group Storage, but items in their personal bank are locked. You can remove them from the group via the Group Management interface (requires 30 days inactivity or group leader vote). If removed, their account becomes a regular Ironman. Recommendation: before removing a quitter, have them log in one last time to deposit all valuable items into Group Storage.

Q: Can I play GIM on mobile?

A: Yes! OSRS mobile supports GIM fully. You can access Group Storage, trade with group members, and see group hiscores on mobile. The only limitation is that group creation must be done on the OSRS website (not mobile). Once your group is created, all gameplay works on mobile.

Q: How does the GIM prestige system work?

A: Prestige ranks are awarded when all group members complete all F2P quests and become members together. The ranks are: Unranked → Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum → Diamond. Each rank requires more cumulative total level. Prestige unlocks cosmetic rewards and a prestige suffix on your group name (e.g., "OSRS Gurus [BRONZE]"). Prestige is optional — it doesn't affect gameplay, but it's a fun goal for completionist groups.

Q: Can a GIM group have different game modes (e.g., 2 HCIM + 3 regular)?

A: No. All members of a GIM group must be the same game mode: either all Regular GIM or all Hardcore GIM. You cannot mix modes. If you start as Hardcore GIM and one player dies, that player becomes a "Regular GIM" but stays in the group — the group itself loses Hardcore status only if ALL members have died.

Q: What's the fastest way to get early GP in GIM?

A: The fastest early GP methods for GIM groups are: (1) Agility Pyramid — 200K-300K GP/hour with 40+ Agility, safe and consistent. (2) Wintertodt — 100-200K GP/hour + supplies, good for all members. (3) High-alching — your Crafter/Mage makes gold rings or battlestaves and alchs them for 300-500K GP/hour. (4) Group bossing (Crazy Archaeologist) — 400-800K GP/hour split among 2-3 players. Have your Crafter/Mage do Agility Pyramid solo while the group does Wintertodt together.

Q: Can we trade items between GIM groups?

A: No. GIM trading is restricted to group members only. You cannot trade items between two GIM groups, even if they're "allied." This prevents boosting and RWT. If you want to transfer items between groups, you'd need to leave your group (becoming a regular Ironman) and trade with the other group — but then you can never return to GIM status.

Q: How do we get a Trident of the Seas as a GIM group?

A: The Trident of the Seas is a rare drop from the Kraken boss (87 Slayer required) or from Cave Kraken (87 Slayer). Since it's untradeable in GIM, you must farm it. The fastest method is: have your highest-Slayer player (85+) do Kraken tasks until a Trident drops. Once obtained, deposit it in Group Storage for everyone to use. With 4-5 members, you'll need 1-2 tridents for efficient group bossing. Alternative: the Trident of the Swamp (from Raids 3) is also untradeable but slightly better.

Q: Is it worth getting 99 Fishing in GIM?

A: Not for the entire group — only for your designated Fisher/Cook. Having one player with 99 Fishing + 99 Cooking means your group has unlimited high-tier food (sharks, manta rays, anglerfish). The other 4 members should focus on their primary skills. Having all 5 members at 99 Fishing would be a massive waste of time — the whole point of GIM is specialisation.

Q: What's the best way to train Herblore in GIM?

A: Herblore in GIM is a group-effort skill. Here's the optimal pipeline: (1) Your Farming player plants herbs (ranarr → snapdragon → torstol) daily using seeds from Slayer/Birdhouse runs. (2) Your Group feeds all herb seeds to the Farming player — never sell or alch herb seeds. (3) Your Herblore player uses the Herblore globe to clean herbs and make potions. (4) Secondaries (limpwurt roots, chocolate dust, snape grass) are gathered by whoever has time. The bottleneck is always seeds, so prioritise birdhouse runs + farming contracts + Slayer for seed drops.

Q: Can we do Raids as a GIM group?

A: Yes! Raids (Chambers of Xeric, Theatre of Blood, Tombs of Amascut) are fully supported in GIM. Your group can enter as a team of 3-5. All loot from raids is tradeable among group members. Raids are the ultimate GIM endgame content — the Twisted Bow, Scythe of Vitur, and Osmumten's Fang are the best weapons in the game, and getting them as a group is incredibly rewarding. Start with Chambers of Xeric (easiest) at ~80+ combat stats, then graduate to Theatre of Blood (hardest) at ~90+ stats.

Q: How do I find a GIM group if I don't have friends who play OSRS?

A: Use these resources to find GIM teammates: (1) Reddit r/GroupIronman — the dedicated subreddit for GIM recruitment. (2) OSRS Forums — Group Ironman Recruitment Thread — official Jagex forum with hundreds of LFG posts. (3) Discord servers — "OSRS GIM" and "Ironman CC" have recruitment channels. (4) In-game "GIM" friends chat — join the chat and ask. When recruiting random players, do a "trial week" before committing long-term. Check their previous game experience and playtime availability.
