1. Why Train Farming? — The Ultimate Passive Skill

Farming is unique in OSRS. It's the only skill that trains itself while you do other activities. You plant crops, go train other skills or PvM for hours, then come back to harvest massive XP and profit. It's the definition of passive income in Gielinor.

Why Every Account Needs Farming

  • Massive money maker at ALL levels: Herb runs alone can generate 500K–2M GP per day depending on your level and seed choice. Tree runs add another 200K–500K GP profit daily.
  • Compost/Supercompost/Ultracompost: Reduces crop death chance dramatically. Always use ultracompost on valuable crops — it pays for itself easily with extra yields.
  • Quest requirements: Many quests require Farming levels (25 for Mourning's End Part I, 43 for My Arm's Big Adventure, etc.).
  • Diary requirements: Multiple Achievement Diaries require Farming levels across all tiers (Easy, Medium, Hard, Elite).
  • Access to useful teleports: Spirit trees (83 Farming) provide a transportation network across Gielinor.
  • Hespori boss: The Hespori patch (65 Farming) lets you fight a boss weekly for a chance at the Anatole drop worth millions.

💡 Key Insight: Farming Trains Itself

Unlike most skills where you actively grind XP, Farming lets you plant → log off/do other content → come back hours later to massive XP and GP. A single tree run takes 10-15 minutes but gives XP equivalent to hours of other training methods. This makes Farming one of the most time-efficient skills in the game.

Starting Farming is simple, but there are a few optimizations that will save you time and GP in the long run. Let's get your first handful of Farming levels efficiently.

Step 2: Allotment Farming (Levels 1–31+)

Once you've done the quests (or if you're impatient), here's the fastest way to get your first Farming levels:

🥔 Allotment Crop Progression

  • Level 1–5: Potatoes — Plant in any allotment patch. Buy potato seeds from farming shops (~3 GP each) or Grand Exchange. Always use supercompost (reduces death rate from ~30% to ~10%). Harvest after 35 minutes.
  • Level 5–7: Onions — Similar to potatoes, slightly more GP from onion sales. Still use supercompost.
  • Level 7–12: Cabbages — Minimal profit, but fast growth (35 minutes). Good for getting past the early levels quickly.
  • Level 12–31: Tomatoes — First "real" allotment crop with decent profit (~500-800 GP per harvest). Growth time: 70 minutes. Use supercompost.
  • Level 31–47: Strawberries — Very useful for making supercompost! Each strawberry can be used as supercompost bucket fodder. Growth time: 70 minutes. Plant these in bulk to fuel your compost needs.
  • Level 47–61: Watermelons — Good for supercompost making. Growth time: 140 minutes. Switch to these once you have enough strawberries for compost.
  • Level 61–99: Snape Grass — Actually profitable! Snape grass sells for ~1,500 GP each. Growth time: 140 minutes. If you're going to do allotments past 61, do snape grass.

Important: Always Use Compost!

This is the #1 mistake beginners make. Never plant without compost in the patch first.

Compost TypeDeath Chance ReductionAvg Extra YieldsCostWhen to Use
Regular Compost~50% reduction+1-2 items~200 GP (make from weeds)Early game, low-value crops
Supercompost~80% reduction+2-3 items~1,500 GPAlways use this (mid-game+)
Ultracompost~90% reduction+3-4 items~3,000 GPAlways use this (late-game, expensive crops)

Bottom line: Ultracompost on herbs and expensive trees. Supercompost on everything else. Never skip compost — crop death will eat your profits.

Get Your Magic Secateurs ASAP!

The Magic Secateurs (reward from Fairy Tale Part I quest) give a +10% yield bonus on herbs and flowers. This means 10% more herbs per harvest = 10% more profit. Equip them before EVERY herb harvest. They're free and the single best Farming item in the game.

Best Herbs for Each Budget

  • Early game (Levels 9–31): Stick to Guam/Marrentill/Harralander. Seeds are cheap, profit is reliable. Don't waste GP on expensive seeds yet.
  • Mid game (Levels 32–61): Ranarr weeds are the sweet spot. ~15K GP/seed but ~8K GP profit per herb. With 8 patches × 6-10 herbs = ~300K-500K GP per run.
  • Late game (Levels 62–84): Snapdragon is the money printer. ~50K GP/seed but ~10K GP profit per herb. High risk (expensive seeds) but highest reward.
  • End game (Level 85+): Torstol. ~100K+ GP/seed. Extremely high risk but can generate 600K-1M GP per run. Only for rich accounts.

Critical Herb Running Tips

🌿 MANDATORY: Ultracompost + Magic Secateurs

Two things you MUST do for every herb run:

  1. Use ultracompost on every herb patch. Ultracompost reduces death chance to ~10% and gives +3-4 extra herbs per patch. With Ranarr/Snapdragon seeds, the extra herbs pay for the ultracompost easily.
  2. Equip Magic Secateurs before harvesting. +10% yield = +10% profit. If you forget to equip them, you're leaving money on the table. (Get them from Fairy Tale Part I quest if you haven't already.)

Herb Run Strategy: 8 Patches in 5-10 Minutes

There are 8 herb patches in OSRS (9 if you count Trollheim, which requires Eadgar's Ruse quest). Here's the optimal order:

  1. Falador herb patch (near Teleport to House/Falador Teleport) — use Explorer's Ring 2+ for free compost
  2. Port Sarim herb patch (near Port Sarim Teleport) — easy access
  3. Catherby herb patch (near Catherby Teleport) — also has allotments
  4. Ardougne herb patch (near Ardougne Teleport) — also has a bush patch nearby
  5. Canifis herb patch (near Canifis Teleport or use Spirittree) — unlocked after Priest in Peril
  6. Harmony Island herb patch (requires 50 Construction for teleport) — dangerous (monsters), but no farmers needed
  7. Trollheim herb patch (requires Eadgar's Ruse) — mountain path, but no farmers needed
  8. Weiss herb patch (requires 72 Agility + 70 Mining) — snowy area, but no farmers needed

Pro tip: Use the Moonclan Teleport (Lunar spellbook) or Ardougne Cloak 2+ for faster teleports between patches.

Herb-Only Worlds

World 330 is the traditional "herb run world" — less crowded, easier to find empty patches. Some players also use World 509 (USA) or World 317 (UK).

5. Tithe Farm (Level 34+) — Fast AFK XP Alternative

Tithe Farm is a minigame in Zeah (Great Kourend) that gives fast Farming XP without needing seeds or compost. It's fully AFK-friendly and perfect for players who want pure XP without managing inventory or risking crop death.

Requirements & Setup

  • Location: Hosidius House, Zeah (Great Kourend)
  • Farming Requirement: 34 Farming (to enter), but 54+ recommended for efficient runs
  • Hosidius Favour: 100% favour required (do kitchen tasks + woodland tasks, takes 3-5 hours)
  • No seed/compost cost: All supplies are free within Tithe Farm

How Tithe Farm Works

Tithe Farm is a mini-game style farming activity:

  1. You're given a plot of 10 "patches" (actually just interactive spots in the minigame)
  2. You plant seeds (provided free), then must keep them watered and healthy
  3. Each plant goes through growth stages. You need to water them at the right times to prevent withering
  4. Successfully harvested plants earn you points
  5. Points can be exchanged for Farming XP or Farming supplies (seeds, compost, etc.)

XP Rates & Strategy

RoundPlants per RoundXP Rate (approx)Notes
Round 1–410 plants~20K–30K XP/hrLearning phase, slower
Round 5–910 plants~40K–50K XP/hrDecent XP, still learning timing
Round 10+10 plants~55K–65K XP/hrMaximum efficiency — aim for this

Strategy for maximum XP:

  • Use 3 watering cans at a time. Don't wait for plants to wither — preemptively water them.
  • Round 10+ gives best XP/hour. Try to hit round 10 consistently. Each round takes ~8-10 minutes.
  • Fully AFK friendly. You can watch Netflix while doing Tithe Farm. Just check every 30-60 seconds to water plants.
  • No banking needed. Everything is provided. Just plant → water → harvest → repeat.

Tithe Farm Rewards

You can spend points on:

  • Farming XP (recommended): 1 point = 100 Farming XP. This is the fastest way to spend points.
  • Seeds: Various herb/tree seeds. Usually not worth it (slower than just buying seeds from GE).
  • Compost/Supercompost: Not worth it (slower than making your own).
  • Farmer's Outfit pieces: Each piece gives +0.5% Farming XP (2.5% total with full set). Buy this first!

Recommended point spending order:

  1. Farmer's Outfit (all 5 pieces) — permanent 2.5% XP boost
  2. Farming XP (bulk spend remaining points)

⏱️ Tithe Farm Time Investment

To get 99 Farming from 34 using ONLY Tithe Farm:

  • XP needed (34 to 99): ~12.5M XP
  • XP rate: ~55K XP/hr (optimized)
  • Time required: ~227 hours of active Tithe Farm
  • Compare to tree/herb runs: 227 hours of tree/herb runs would get you 99 Farming in ~2-3 months of casual play (2 runs/day). Tithe Farm is faster XP/hour but requires active play.

Verdict: Tithe Farm is great for active XP grinding when you don't feel like doing bossing/PvM. But tree/herb runs are better for passive XP + profit.

7. Optimal Farming Route & Run Optimization

To maximize XP and profit, you need an optimal farming route that minimizes running time between patches. Here's the most efficient circuit for tree + fruit tree + herb runs.

Optimal Full Farming Circuit (12-16 minutes)

This route covers all major patches in the most time-efficient order:

  1. Start at Gnome Stronghold (fruit tree patch) — Use Spirit Tree teleport or Gnome Glider. Plant fruit tree here.
  2. Teleport to Taverley (tree patch) — Use Taverley Teleport (tablet) or run from Gnome Stronghold. Plant tree here.
  3. Run to Gnome Tree Village (fruit tree + calquat patch) — From Taverley, run north to the Gnome Tree Village. Plant fruit tree + calquat here.
  4. Teleport to Falador (tree patch + herb + flower + allotment) — Use Falador Teleport or Explorer's Ring. Plant tree, herb, flower, and allotment here. Use Explorer's Ring 2+ for free compost!
  5. Run to Port Sarim (allotment + flower + herb) — From Falador, run south to Port Sarim. Plant allotment, flower, and herb here.
  6. Teleport to Ardougne (tree patch + herb + bush) — Use Ardougne Teleport or Ardougne Cloak. Plant tree, herb, and bush here.
  7. Run to Catherby (fruit tree + allotment + herb) — From Ardougne, use Camelot Teleport or run east. Plant fruit tree, allotment, and herb here.
  8. Teleport to Varrock (tree patch) — Use Varrock Teleport. Plant tree here.
  9. Teleport to Lumbridge (tree patch) — Use Lumbridge Teleport. Plant tree here.
  10. Teleport to Troll Stronghold (cactus + bush patch) — Use Trollheim Teleport (after Eadgar's Ruse) or Stony Basin teleport. Plant cactus and bush here.

Total time: ~12-16 minutes for full circuit. Do this 2x per day minimum.

8. Farming Equipment Checklist

Having the right tools makes farming runs faster and more profitable. Here's what you need at each stage of the game.

Essential (Get ASAP)

  • Magic Secateurs (from Fairy Tale Part I quest): +10% herb/flower yield. EQUIP THESE BEFORE EVERY HERB HARVEST. This is the single most important Farming item. If you lose them, talk to Malignius Mortifer in Draynor Village to get a replacement.
  • Secateurs (basic, from farming shops): Required to harvest herbs/flowers. Buy from any farming shop (~5 GP).
  • Gardening Trowel (spade): Required to dig up dead crops and clear patches. Buy from farming shops (~5 GP).
  • Seed Dibber: Required to plant seeds. Buy from farming shops (~5 GP).
  • Watering Can: Needed for allotments and Tithe Farm. Buy from farming shops (~25 GP). Get 2-3 watering cans for Tithe Farm.
  • Supercompost/Ultracompost bucket: Bring 8-10 per run (one per patch). Never skip compost!
  • Rake: Required to clear weeds from patches. Buy from farming shops (~5 GP).

Recommended (Mid-Game)

  • Farmer's Outfit (pieces from Tithe Farm): Each piece gives +0.5% Farming XP (2.5% total with full set). Get this ASAP from Tithe Farm.
  • Bone Meal (early game alternative to supercompost): Worse than supercompost but free if you have spare bones. Crush bones with a pestle and mortar, then use on patch.
  • Plant Cure: If a crop gets diseased, you can cure it with plant cure (bought from farming shops). Only use this on expensive crops (Magic trees, Snapdragon, etc.). Not worth it for cheap crops — just dig them up and replant.
  • Graceful Outfit: For faster run routes. Reduces weight, making you run longer before draining run energy. Get this ASAP from Rogues' Den.
  • Explorer's Ring 2+ (Morytania Medium Diary): Gives 30 free compost per day (organized by the ring). Huge compost saver!

Quality of Life (Late-Game)

  • Bottomless Compost Bucket (reward from Nightmare Zone): Never run out of compost space. Holds up to 10,000 compost. Game changer for bulk farming.
  • Compost Mound (tameable pet): Gives invisible Farming boost (chance to save a harvest). Very rare drop from giant mole — not necessary but nice.
  • Scroll of Farming (Fossil Island): Gives 5% chance to save harvest (stacks with Magic Secateurs). Buy from Fossil Island botanist for 200 numulites.
  • Farming Cape (99 Farming): Gives +5% yield boost on all crops. The ultimate endgame goal.
  • Ancient Totem (Kourend & Kebos Diary Elite): Gives 5% chance to not deplete trees when chopping. Useful if you're also training Woodcutting.

10. Pro Tips for Maximum Profit & Efficiency

💰 Tip #1: Ultracompost is NON-NEGOTIABLE on Expensive Crops

If you're planting Ranarr, Snapdragon, Torstol, Magic trees, Palm trees, or Dragonfruit trees — ALWAYS use ultracompost. The cost (~3K GP) is negligible compared to the seed cost (15K-100K+ GP). Crop death will eat your profits if you skip compost.

⏰ Tip #2: Do Farm Runs TWICE Per Day Minimum

Farming is passive, but you need to actually DO the runs. Set a timer on your phone:

  • Morning: Do herb + tree + fruit tree runs
  • Evening: Do herb + tree + fruit tree runs again
  • That's it. 20-30 minutes of gameplay per day for massive XP and profit.

Even 1 run per day is better than nothing. But 2 runs/day is the sweet spot for optimal growth.

🎒 Tip #3: Use the Bottomless Compost Bucket

The Bottomless Compost Bucket (reward from Nightmare Zone) holds up to 10,000 compost. This eliminates the need to bank between patches. Buy one from the Nightmare Zone reward shop for 1,000 points (easy to get).

🏃 Tip #4: Graceful Outfit + Explorer's Ring = Fast Runs

Graceful Outfit (from Rogues' Den) reduces your weight, letting you run longer. Explorer's Ring 2+ (Morytania Medium Diary) gives 30 free compost per day. Combine these for maximum efficiency.

📊 Tip #5: Track Your Profit with a Spreadsheet

Keep a simple spreadsheet tracking:

  • Seed costs (per run)
  • Compost costs (per run)
  • Harvest value (per run)
  • Net profit (per run)

This helps you optimize which herbs/trees to plant based on GE prices. Prices fluctuate — check GE tracker before buying seeds!

🌿 Tip #6: Join "Herb Run World" (World 330)

World 330 is the traditional "herb run world." Patches are less crowded, and you're less likely to have someone else plant on "your" patch before you. Some players also use World 509 (USA) or World 317 (UK).

🎯 Tip #7: Hespori is Weekly — DON'T FORGET!

The Hespori patch (unlocked after Totem of Intimidation) can be planted weekly. The Hespori boss drops the Anatole (worth 10M+ GP!). Set a weekly reminder to plant and fight Hespori. It's free money!

11. FAQ — Common Farming Questions

Q: Is farming worth it without ultracompost?

A: No. Always use supercompost or ultracompost. Crop death will eat your profits otherwise. The only exception is ultra-low-level stuff (potatoes, onions) where seeds are ~3 GP each. But once you're planting herbs (9+), always use compost.

Q: Should I pay farmers to protect my trees?

A: For expensive trees (Magic, Palm, Dragonfruit) — YES, absolutely. It's cheap insurance. A Magic tree seed costs ~120K GP. Paying a farmer 25 coconuts (~25K GP) is 21% of the seed cost to guarantee the tree survives. Always pay for expensive trees.

Q: How often should I do farm runs?

A: Minimum 2x/day for herbs/allotments. Trees every 4-6 hours ideally. Even 1 run per day is better than nothing. Farming is passive — the crops grow while you do other content. You just need to spend 10-15 minutes planting and harvesting.

Q: Best way to get 100% Hosidius favour for Tithe Farm?

A: Do the favour tasks in order:

  1. Help in the kitchen (cook stews) — gives Hosidius favour
  2. Do woodland tasks (chop trees, give logs to forester) — gives Hosidius favour
  3. Do messroom tasks (clean up messhall) — gives Hosidius favour

Takes about 3-5 hours total for 100% favour. Alternatively, you can pay 1.2M GP to Percy for 100% favour (not recommended for main accounts).

Q: What's the most profitable herb to farm?

A: It depends on GE prices (which fluctuate daily). Generally:

  • Snapdragon (62 Farming): Highest consistent profit (~500K-800K GP/run)
  • Ranarr (32 Farming): Good profit, cheaper seeds (~300K-500K GP/run)
  • Torstol (85 Farming): Highest potential profit but extremely expensive seeds (~600K-1M GP/run)

Check GE Tracker before buying seeds! Prices change based on meta (e.g., if a new boss is released that uses a specific potion, that herb's price will spike).

Q: Can I train Farming on an Ironman account?

A: Yes! Farming is actually EASIER on Ironman. You don't need to buy seeds — you get them from:

  • Herb seeds: Monster drops (hill giants, moss giants, etc.)
  • Tree seeds: Birdhouse runs (28 Hunter) give tons of tree seeds
  • Fruit tree seeds: Monster drops + farming contracts
  • Compost: Use the Compost Mound pet (drops from giant mole) or make your own from weeds

Ironman farming is slower early on (can't just buy seeds), but by mid-game you'll have more seeds than you know what to do with.
