👹 Boss Guide — Updated June 2026

Vorkath is the most consistent money-making boss in OSRS, generating 2M to 5M GP per hour with even a budget setup. This guide covers low-cost gear, the Woox Walk technique, every attack pattern, and exactly how to turn this blue dragon into your personal ATM — all without spending 50M+ on a Dragon Hunter Crossbow.

🏹 Bolt Strategy: Use Ruby Bolts (e) for the first 50-60% of Vorkath's HP — their special procs hit 100s on high-HP targets. Switch to Diamond Bolts (e) when Vorkath drops below ~350 HP, as Ruby specs become weaker on low-HP targets. Bring about 50% Ruby and 50% Diamond in your quiver.

5. The Woox Walk — Acid Phase Advanced Technique

The Woox Walk is what separates casual Vorkath farmers from efficient ones. During the acid phase, you can continue attacking Vorkath while dodging acid pools. Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Identify a clear row: As soon as the acid phase begins, find a straight line of 5+ tiles with no acid pools. This is your walking lane.
  2. Starting position: Stand at one end of your clear row. Click one tile forward — your character starts walking.
  3. Attack click: The instant your character begins moving, click Vorkath to attack. Your character will take one step then turn to fire.
  4. Repeat: As soon as you fire, click the next tile (back the other direction), then immediately click Vorkath again. The rhythm is: Move → Attack → Move → Attack.
  5. Timing: You must complete each move-attack cycle before the acid pool spawns under you. This is roughly a 2-tick window. Practice with a crossbow first (slower attack speed = easier timing) before trying with a Blowpipe.
  6. Beginner variation: If you can't get the attack timing, just walk back and forth without attacking. Surviving the acid phase is better than getting hit by 30+ acid damage because you tried to squeeze in an attack.
🎯 Practice Mode: On your first 10 kills, don't try to Woox Walk. Just walk the clear row without attacking. Once you're comfortable dodging acid, add one attack per walk cycle. Build up to the full rhythm gradually — most players need 50+ kills before the Woox Walk feels natural.

7. Common Mistakes That Get You Killed

  1. Not having Crumble Undead ready. Set it as your autocast spell BEFORE entering the instance. Fumbling with the spellbook during the freeze is how most people eat a 60.
  2. Panic-eating during acid phase. Don't eat while dodging acid — it stalls your movement and you'll stand in a pool. Wait until you reach the end of your walking row, eat, then continue.
  3. Using Ruby Bolts (e) below 350 HP. Ruby bolt specs deal damage based on the target's current HP percentage. Below 350 HP, Diamond Bolts (e) outperform them. Switch when Vorkath is around half health.
  4. Forgetting to re-pot Extended Super Antifire. Each dose lasts 12 minutes. If it runs out mid-kill, Vorkath's dragonfire will shred you. Set a 12-minute timer.
  5. Standing too far from Vorkath. If you're at max range, you won't have enough space to walk during the acid phase. Stay 3-5 tiles away — close enough to attack, far enough to dodge.
  6. Not using F-keys. Bind Inventory to F1, Prayer to F2, Spellbook to F3, and Equipment to F4. You'll need all four tabs during the fight. F-key muscle memory saves lives.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I do Vorkath with a melee setup?

A: Yes, but it's harder. Melee requires a Dragon Hunter Lance (best), Zamorakian Hasta (budget), or Fang (mid). You'll use Protect from Magic + Piety and need to walk back 2 tiles to dodge the dragonfire bomb special. Melee is slightly less efficient than ranged for learners but viable once you know the mechanics.

Q: Is Salve Amulet (ei) or Void better for Vorkath?

A: Salve Amulet (ei) is better. Vorkath is an undead creature, so the Salve Amulet (ei) provides a +20% damage and accuracy bonus that stacks with Void. The combination of Elite Void + Salve (ei) is the meta setup for ranged. If you had to choose only one, Salve (ei) with Black D'hide outperforms Elite Void with a Fury.

Q: How many kills per trip can I expect with budget gear?

A: 2-4 kills per trip. With the ~8M budget setup, expect 2-3 kills consistently. As you improve at dodging acid and handling the undead spawn, you'll stretch to 3-4 kills. Max gear players can get 5-8 kills per trip. The limiting factor is always food — Vorkath's ranged auto-attacks chip away at your HP regardless of prayer.

Q: Is Vorkath better money than Zulrah in 2026?

A: Vorkath is more consistent; Zulrah has higher potential. Vorkath's guaranteed drops (bones + hides) mean you never have a "bad" kill. Zulrah's unique drops (Tanzanite Fang, Magic Fang) are more valuable individually, but you can go dry for hundreds of kills. Vorkath is recommended for steady income; Zulrah for gambling on big ticket drops.

👹 Vorkath Is Your First Big Money Printer

Vorkath is the boss that turns OSRS from a game into a career. Once you can consistently kill this blue dragon, money stops being an obstacle. Your first 100 kills will be messy — you'll die to the spawn, misclick during acid phase, and forget to re-pot antifire. That's fine. By kill 50, the Woox Walk starts clicking. By kill 100, you're doing 25+ kills per hour. By kill 500, you've made 100M+ GP.

Stick with it. Every death teaches you something. Soon Vorkath will feel like muscle memory. 💰
