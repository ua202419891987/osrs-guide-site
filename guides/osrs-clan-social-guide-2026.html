① Why Join a Clan? Benefits Explained

A Clan (also spelled "clan") is a group of players who share a chat channel, a clan chat (CC), and often a Discord server. Joining one transforms OSRS from a solo grind to a social experience.

① 5 Big Benefits of Joining a Clan

👥 Why Clans Matter

  • Knowledge sharing: Ask "what's the best gear for X?" and get instant answers
  • Group activities: Boss together, do Chambers of Xeric, run Barrows as a team
  • Help with difficult content: Stuck on a quest? Someone will walk you through it
  • Social motivation: Seeing others grind motivates YOU to log in and play
  • Clan events: Many clans host giveaways, competitions, and group skilling events

Even if you're a solo player, having a clan chat open makes the game feel alive. You'll see people chatting, ask questions, and learn tricks you'd never figure out alone.

💡 You Can Join MULTIPLE Clans!

OSRS allows you to be in unlimited clans as a "Guest". You can be in 1 main clan (with full privileges) + join 10+ others as a guest. This is how many players access multiple clan chats for different activities (ba, pc, cw, etc.).

② Finding the Right Clan for You

Not all clans are the same. Picking the wrong one = leaving after 3 days. Here's how to find YOUR perfect match.

③ 3 Ways to Find a Clan

🔍 Method 1: Official Forums (Recommended)

Go to the OSRS Official Forums → "Clans" section. Browse posts with titles like "New Player Friendly Clan — Social & Help". Look for clans that mention "Learner-friendly" or "No minimum stats".

🔍 Method 2: Reddit r/ironscape & r/OSRS

Search "clan recruitment" on Reddit. Many clans post there. Look for recent posts (within 2 weeks) — old recruitment posts = clan might be dead.

🔍 Method 3: In-Game "Clan Hub" World

Travel to the Clan Hub (use Minigame teleport → Clan Hub). Talk to players there — many are recruiting. Ask: "Are you recruiting new players?"

💡 Red Flags — AVOID These Clans

❌ Reqs > 1,000 total level (you're not ready)
❌ No Discord link (sign of inactive clan)
❌ "Donate to rank up" (obvious scam — real clans don't sell ranks)
❌ Toxic chat (join their CC for 5 minutes — if people are rude, leave immediately)

③ Applying & Getting Accepted

Found a clan you like? Here's how to apply and actually get accepted.

④ How to Apply (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Join Their Clan Chat (CC)

Right-click the "Clan Chat" tab → "Join Clan Chat" → type the clan name. You're now in their guest chat.

Step 2: Be Polite & Ask

Type: "Hi! I'm a new player (level XX). Do you have space for a new member?" Don't just demand an invite — be friendly!

Step 3: Fill Out an Application (If They Ask)

Many clans have an application form (Google Forms link in their Discord or CC message). Fill it out honestly. Mentioning you're a beginner is FINE — many clans love helping new players!

⑤ What Clans Look For in New Members

✅ Green Flags (You're Likely to Get Accepted)

  • Polite in chat: No spamming, no begging, no rude comments
  • Active: Say you can play 5-10 hours/week (even if it's less)
  • Willing to learn: "I'm new but eager to improve" is a great answer
  • Discord access: Most clans use Discord for coordination — having it = big plus

④ Friend Chat vs Clan Chat — When to Use Which

OSRS has 2 types of chat channels. Knowing when to use each saves you confusion.

⑥ Friend Chat (FC) — Public Channels

📢 What Is a Friend Chat?

  • Open to everyone: Anyone can join (no application needed)
  • Temporary: You leave when you log out (or manually leave)
  • Use for: Finding teams for minigames, price checking, general help

⑧ Clan Chat (CC) — Private Clan Channel

🔒 What Is a Clan Chat?

  • Clan members only: Need to be accepted into the clan first
  • Permanent: You stay in the clan until you leave or get kicked
  • Use for: Clan events, private help, social chat with clanmates

⑤ Discord Communities — Essential Servers to Join

Most OSRS activity happens on Discord, not in-game. Joining the right servers will 10x your OSRS experience.

⑨ Must-Join Discord Servers (Beginner-Friendly)

🥇 #1: OSRS Wiki Discord

The official OSRS Wiki Discord (linked from osrs.wiki). Has channels for help, gear checks, bossing teams, and LFG (Looking for Group). Very beginner-friendly — no question is too basic.

🥈 #2: Your Clan's Discord

Once you join a clan, join their Discord immediately. This is where raid teams form, help happens fastest, and clan events are announced.

🥉 #3: RuneLite Discord (Plugin Help)

Join the RuneLite Discord if you use RuneLite (you should!). Get help with plugin setup, report bugs, and learn pro tips from power users.

⑩ How to Use Discord for OSRS (Beginner Guide)

🎮 Discord 101 for OSRS Players

  1. Download Discord app (PC/ Mobile) or use browser version
  2. Create an account (use an email you check — important!)
  3. Join servers via invite links (find them on Reddit, clan websites, or osrs.wiki)
  4. Use text channels for questions, voice channels for group content (raids, BA, etc.)
  5. Read the #rules channel FIRST — every server has different rules
💡 Discord = Faster Help Than In-Game

Stuck on a quest at 2 AM? Someone on Discord will answer in 2 minutes. In-game help chat might take 30+ minutes. Join Discord — it's a game-changer.

⑥ Social Features You Should Use

OSRS has built-in social features beyond clans and Discord. Here's what you should know:

⑪ Adding Friends & Ignore List

👫 How to Add Friends

Right-click a player → "Add Friend". You can have up to 200 friends. When they log in, you'll see a notification. Great for remembering helpful players you met!

🚫 Ignore List (Use It!)

Right-click a player → "Add Ignore". Their chat messages won't show. Use this for scammers, toxic players, and spammers. You can ignore up to 100 players.

⑫ Using the "Follow" Feature

Right-click a player → "Follow". Your character automatically walks behind them. Useful for:

  • Following a high-level player to learn their route
  • Following a friend to a quest location
  • Following someone who knows a shortcut you don't

⑬ Private Chat (PM) — How to Use It

💬 Private Messaging Basics

Right-click player → "Message". Opens a private chat window. Use this to:
✅ Ask for help privately
✅ Thank someone for helping you
✅ Coordinate trades/meetups
❌ DON'T spam PMs (annoying — you might get ignored/blocked)

⑦ Building Your Own Network (No Clan Yet?)

Don't want to join a clan? You can still build a solid OSRS social network. Here's how:

⑭ 5 Ways to Make Friends in OSRS

① Be Helpful in "help" FC

Answer questions you DO know. Helping others = people remember you = they add YOU as a friend.

② Do Minigames (BA, PC, CW)

You'll naturally meet players. After a few games, ask: "Mind if I add you? Want to run more BA later?"

③ Trade with Others (Safely!)

Buying/selling at GE? Chat with the other person. Be polite. Some become regular trading partners.

④ Join a Discord LFG Channel

"LFG" = Looking for Group. Post: "New player, looking for BA team (defender role). Can learn!" Someone will invite you.

⑤ Be Polite & Memorable

Simple: Say "thank you" after help. Congratulate others on milestones. Be the player people WANT to talk to.

💡 Your Network = Your OSRS Experience

Players with 200+ friends online have a radically better time. They never struggle to find a team, always have someone to ask for help, and the game feels alive. Invest in friendships — it pays off more than any GP grind.

⑧ Social Features FAQ — Common Questions

Can I be in multiple clans at once?

Yes! You can be in 1 main clan (with full privileges like clan bank access) + unlimited guest clans. Join as many guest clans as you want — it's free and has no downsides.

What if I join a clan and it turns out toxic?

Leave immediately. Right-click your clan rank → "Leave Clan". No drama needed — just leave. There are 1,000+ active clans in OSRS. You'll find a better one.

Do I need Discord to enjoy OSRS?

Technically no — but practically yes. Almost all group content ( Chambers of Xeric, Theatre of Blood, high-level BA) is coordinated via Discord voice chat. Without it, you'll miss out on 70% of the social experience.

Someone in my clan is bullying me. What do I do?

Tell a clan admin/leader FIRST. If they don't act, leave and report the player to Jagex (right-click → Report). OSRS has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment.

Can Ironmen join clans?

YES! Many clans have Ironman-friendly policies (no sharing GP but can help with skilling/quests). Look for clans that mention "Ironman welcome" in their recruitment post.
