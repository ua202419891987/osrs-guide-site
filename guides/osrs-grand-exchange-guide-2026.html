OSRS Grand Exchange Guide 2026

The Grand Exchange (GE) is OSRS's marketplace — where every item in the game is bought and sold. This guide covers how it works, buy limits, flipping strategies, reading price graphs, and avoiding GG taxes eating your profits.

① How the GE Actually Works

The Grand Exchange (GE) is a centralized marketplace where players buy and sell items. Think of it like eBay — but for OSRS items, with automatic price-matching.

① 3 Core Mechanics

📊 How Prices Are Determined

  • Insta-buy price: Current lowest sell offer in the system
  • Insta-sell price: Current highest buy offer in the system
  • Your offer price: You can set it above/below current price to prioritize speed vs profit
  • GE Tax: 1% tax on selling (minimum 1 GP, maximum 10M GP)

② GE Slot Limits

P2P (Members)8 buy + 8 sellPlenty for casual trading

💡 Where Is the GE?

Northwest Varrock. Use the Varrock Teleport (Spellbook) or walk through the west gate. The GE has 4 floors — all lead to the same central trading area.

② Buying Smart — Instant Buy vs Offer Strategy

There are 2 ways to buy items at the GE. Knowing when to use each can save you thousands of GP per day.

③ Method 1: Instant Buy (Fast, Slightly Pricier)

When to Use Instant Buy

Click "Buy" → select item → choose "Instand-buy" (or shift+click). You pay ~5% above market price but get the item RIGHT NOW.

Best for: Items you need now (gear for a quest, food for a boss). The convenience is worth the small premium.

④ Method 2: Place an Offer (Cheaper, Slower)

How to Place a Smart Offer

Click "Buy" → select item → set your price → confirm. Your offer waits in the system until a seller matches it.

Pro tip: Check the current price (middle number in GE interface) and offer +1 GP above it. Your offer will be first in line to fill!

⑤ Margin Check (The #1 Skill for Smart Buying)

📈 How to Check Margins

  1. Check the item's current price (GE interface or RuneLite plugin)
  2. Insta-buy 1 item (see what you paid)
  3. Insta-sell 1 item (see what you get after tax)
  4. Margin = Buy price - Sell price
  5. If margin > 0 → you can flip for profit!

⑦ The GE Tax Math (IMPORTANT!)

💡 1% Tax Applies to SELERS Only

When you sell an item for 10M GP, you get 9.9M GP (100k tax).

Tax cap: Maximum 10M GP tax per item (on sales > 1B GP).

Workaround: For very expensive items (> 100M), sell to other players directly (use "Price Check" FC to find buyers).

⑧ When to Avoid the GE Entirely

Situations Where GE Is a Bad Idea

  • Selling rae items (>500M): 10M tax is huge — find a private buyer
  • Buying rae items: Sellers may not list at GE — check FC "Price Check"
  • High-volume flipping: GE tax eats margins — use insta-buy/sell quickly

④ Buy Limits Explained — Why Can't I Buy More?

Every item at the GE has a Buy Limit — the maximum quantity you can buy in a 4-hour window. This prevents price manipulation and botting.

⑩ How to Work Around Buy Limits

🔄 3 Strategies

  1. Wait 4 hours: Limits reset every 4 hours (real-time, not game-time)
  2. Buy on multiple accounts: Not recommended (against rules if trading with yourself)
  3. Buy from shops: Some items (like pure essence) have no limit at NPC shops
💡 Pro Tip: Stack Your Buys

If you need 5,000 Dragon Bones (limit 10,000/4h), you can buy them all in one 4-hour window. But if you need 15,000 — you MUST wait 8 hours (two windows).

⑤ Reading GE Graphs — Predict Price Movements

The GE interface has a price graph button. Click it to see historical prices. This helps you predict whether to buy now or wait!

⑪ 3 Graph Patterns Every Beginner Should Know

📈 Patern 1: Steady Upward Trend

What it means: Item is becoming more valuable. BUY NOW before it goes higher. Example: Dragon Med Helmet during Twitch drops events.

📉 Patern 2: Steady Downward Trend

What it means: Item is losing value. WAIT to buy, or SELL now if you own some. Example: OPthery scrolls after a major update.

〰️ Patern 3: Flatline (Stable)

What it means:Price is stable. Safe to buy/sell anytime. Example: Food items (Sharks, Lobsters) — always in demand.

⑥ Flipping Basics — Make Money from Price Differences

Flipping = buying items below market price and reselling above market price. It's one of the fastest legal GP-making methods in OSRS.

⑬ Is Flipping for You?

✅ You SHOULD Flip If...

  • You have 5M+ GP to invest
  • You understand buy limits (learned in Section 4)
  • You're willing to cancel and re-list offers (takes 5-10 min/hour)
  • You want risk-free profit (no combat, no supplies needed)

❌ You SHOULDN'T Flip If...

  • You have <5M GP (profits are too small to matter)
  • You don't understand GE mechanics yet (read Sections 1-5 first!)
  • You're an Ironman (can't trade — flipping is impossible)

⑦ Common GE Mistakes Costing You Millions

Even experienced players make these mistakes. Don't be one of them!

⑮ Mistake #1: Insta-Buying Everything

Cost: ~5% premium on every purchase. Over a month, that's millions of GP wasted.

Fix: Always check the price and place an offer +1 GP above market. Wait 1-30 minutes for it to fill.

⑯ Mistake #2: Forgetting About the 1% Tax

Cost: 10K GP lost on a 1M sale (seems small, but adds up).

Fix: When pricing your flips, always subtract 1% from your expected sell price.

⑰ Mistake #3: Ignoring Buy Limits (Over-investing)

Cost: Your GP is tied up in items you can't sell for 4+ hours.

Fix: Never buy more than 50% of the buy limit in one 4-hour window. Keep GP liquid!

⑱ Mistake #4: Panic Selling During Price Dips

Cost: Selling at a loss when prices temparily drop.

Fix: Check the 180-day graph. If the dip is temporary, HOLD. If it's a permanent meta shift — sell quickly!

⑧ GE FAQ — Common Beginner Questions

My offer has been "waiting" for 2 hours. What do I do?

Cancel and re-list at a slightly higher price. Your original price might be too low. Check the GE graph to see the current market price.

Can I sell items for "Noted" versions separately?

Yes! Noted items are treated identically to unnoted. You can sell 1 Noted item or 28 Noted items — the GE sees them as the same item type.

Why did my buy limit reset, but I still can't buy more?

The reset timer is 4 hours of REAL TIME, not game time. Also, buy limits are per item, per account. If you bought 10,000 Dragon Bones, you must wait exactly 4 hours from the moment you completed the purchase.

Is there a way to check an item's buy limit without testing it?

Yes! Use the RuneLite plugin "Item Prices" — it shows buy limits directly in the GE interface. Or check the OSRS Wiki page for any item — buy limit is always listed.

Can I get banned for flipping/merchanting?

NO! Flipping and merchanting are 100% allowed. The only bannable offense is Real-World Trading (RWT) — buying/selling GP for real money. As long as GP stays in-game, you're safe.
