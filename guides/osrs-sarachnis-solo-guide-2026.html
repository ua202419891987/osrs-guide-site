Sarachnis is widely considered the best first "real" boss for OSRS beginners. Unlike Giant Mole (which is essentially a high-HP cow), Sarachnis teaches you actual boss mechanics — prayer switching, movement during attacks, and dealing with minions — all in a safe death environment where you keep your items if you die. This guide covers everything you need to start soloing Sarachnis confidently in 2026, from requirements and gear to advanced kill strategies and loot analysis.

Chapter 2: Getting to Sarachnis — Routes & Fast Travel

The Forthos Dungeon is located on Great Kourend. There are several ways to get there, and learning the fastest route will save you hours over the course of your Sarachnis farming.

Method 1: Potion Castle (Recommended for Regular Trips)

  1. Teleport to Potion Castle (better known as Castle Wars) using a ring of dueling or the minigame teleport.
  2. From Castle Wars, run south-west toward the Great Kourend border.
  3. Cross the bridge into Great Kourend and continue south until you see the Forthos Dungeon entrance — it is a trapdoor icon on the map, south of the Hosidius mine.
  4. Pay the 20 GP entrance fee to the doorman and enter.
  5. Run through the dungeon (it is a short, linear path) until you reach Sarachnis's lair.

Total travel time: ~45 seconds from Castle Wars teleport to Sarachnis lair.

Method 2: Fairy Ring (Code: BJQ)

  1. Use a Fairy Ring to teleport to code BJQ (requires partial completion of Fairytale II).
  2. This drops you near the South of Hosidius area.
  3. Run north briefly to find the Forthos Dungeon entrance.
  4. Enter and run to Sarachnis.

Total travel time: ~30 seconds from Fairy Ring to Sarachnis lair. This is the fastest method if you have Fairy Rings unlocked.

Method 3: Sense Route (After "A Porcine of Interest")

  1. After completing the quest, you unlock the ability to sense the entrance more easily.
  2. Use the Xeric's Talisman ("Xeric's Heart" teleport) — this drops you at the Kourend Woodland.
  3. Run south to the Forthos Dungeon entrance.

Note: Xeric's Talisman is obtained during the Client of Kourend quest, which is highly recommended for any player planning to spend time in Great Kourend.

Fastest Banking Method

When you need to bank between trips, use one of these methods:

  • House Teleport (if you have a house in Hosidius): Build a jewellery box and teleport directly to your house, then use a mounted Xeric's talisman or pool of refreshment to restore stats and bank via your butler.
  • Ring of Dueling to Castle Wars: Bank at Castle Wars, then run back. This takes ~1 minute per trip.
  • Fairy Ring BJQ + Skills Necklace: Teleport to Woodcutting Guild (Skills Necklace), bank there, then Fairy Ring back to BJQ.

Chapter 3: Sarachnis Mechanics — Attacks, Minions & Web Traps

Understanding Sarachnis's attack patterns is essential for efficient kills. Fortunately, her mechanics are straightforward and predictable once you learn the rhythm.

Attack Styles

AttackProjectile ColorProtection PrayerMax Hit
RangedGreenProtect from Missiles~16 (with protection: ~0-4)
MagicBlueProtect from Magic~20 (with protection: ~0-5)

Key fact: Sarachnis alternates between Ranged and Magic attacks. She always starts with Magic when you enter her room. This means you can predict her next attack after the first one — if she just used Magic, the next attack will be Ranged, and vice versa.

Web Trap Mechanic

Periodically, Sarachnis will spawn web traps on the ground around her. These appear as web-like patterns on the floor.

  • If you stand in a web trap, you become rooted in place for several seconds and cannot move.
  • While rooted, Sarachnis can hit you freely with both attacks.
  • Counter: Simply step away 2+ tiles when you see the web animation. The webs are easy to see — they appear as white web patterns on the ground.
  • Webs disappear after a few seconds. You can re-engage immediately after moving away.

Spiderling Minions

At 75% HP, 50% HP, and 25% HP, Sarachnis spawns spiderlings — small, weak minions that attack with melee.

  • Spiderlings have very low HP (~5-10 each) and deal minimal damage (~2-4 per hit).
  • You have two options: (1) Ignore them and tank the chip damage, or (2) Kill them quickly between Sarachnis attacks.
  • Recommended: If you have 70+ Defence and good food, you can usually ignore spiderlings entirely. If you are lower levelled, kill 2-3 between boss attacks to reduce chip damage.
  • Spiderlings do not drop loot, so there is no GP reason to kill them. Only kill them if they are affecting your ability to survive.

Safe Spot (Solo Method)

There is a safe spot in Sarachnis's lair that allows you to attack her without taking damage from her ranged or magic attacks. This is invaluable for learning the fight or for low-level accounts.

  • The safe spot is located behind the northern pillar in her room. Stand so that the pillar is between you and Sarachnis.
  • From this position, Sarachnis cannot hit you with ranged or magic projectiles because the pillar blocks line of sight.
  • However, she can still attack you with melee if she walks around the pillar. Be ready to move if she approaches.
  • Note: Using the safe spot makes kills slower because Sarachnis may wander. For faster kills, learn to prayer switch instead.
💡 Pro Tip — Predict the Next Attack: Since Sarachnis alternates attacks, you can switch your protection prayer BEFORE she fires the next projectile. Watch the color of the LAST attack: if it was blue (Magic), switch to Protect from Missiles immediately because the next attack will be green (Ranged). Mastering this timing reduces your damage taken to near zero.

Chapter 4: Solo Strategy — Prayer Switching & Inventory Setup

Prayer Management Strategy

Efficient prayer management is the difference between 10 kills per trip and 30 kills per trip. Here is the optimal approach:

  1. Activate Piety (or Chivalry): If you have 70+ Prayer, use Piety the entire fight. The +15% Strength and +20% Attack bonus makes kills ~25% faster.
  2. Switch protection prayers: Alternate between Protect from Magic and Protect from Missiles based on Sarachnis's last attack color.
  3. Do not over-pray: You only need one protection prayer active at a time. Do not activate both simultaneously — that wastes prayer points rapidly.
  4. Bring a prayer potion (4): One (4)-dose prayer potion gives 28 prayer points. With Piety (22 PP/min) and protection switching (~12 PP/min), one potion lasts ~8-10 kills.

Step-by-Step Kill Rotation

  1. Enter the lair. Sarachnis starts with a Magic attack (blue projectile). Activate Protect from Magic before she attacks.
  2. Attack immediately. Use your crush weapon. Apply your special attack weapon at the start of the fight for free burst damage.
  3. Watch the projectile: After the Magic attack, the next one will be Ranged (green). Switch to Protect from Missiles.
  4. Repeat: Keep attacking and switching prayers. Sarachnis alternates perfectly — blue, green, blue, green.
  5. At 75% HP: Spiderlings spawn. Ignore them if you can tank the damage. If not, kill 1-2 quickly with a few melee hits.
  6. Avoid web traps: When you see webs appearing on the ground, step 2 tiles away. Resume attacking after the webs disappear.
  7. Finish the kill: Continue praying and attacking. At 0 HP, Sarachnis dies and drops loot on the ground.
  8. Loot quickly: Grab the drops and move to the next spawn. Sarachnis has a ~10-second respawn timer.

What to Do If You Are About to Die

Remember: Sarachnis is in a safe death area. If you run out of food and your HP drops to 0, you will respawn at the Forthos Dungeon entrance with ALL your items. However, you lose any uncollected loot. If you are about to die:

  • Eat your last food immediately.
  • If you have a teleport tab, use it to escape and re-enter.
  • If you die, do not panic — walk back in and try again. You lose nothing.

Chapter 5: Loot Table — Blighted Longsword, Cudgel & Pet

Sarachnis has one of the most beginner-friendly drop tables in the game. The loot is consistently valuable, and there are several high-value rare drops that make every kill exciting.

Blighted Longsword is the jackpot drop. At ~7M GP, a single Blighted Longsword drop pays for hundreds of hours of gameplay. The drop rate is approximately 1/800, meaning you have a ~6% chance per 50-kill trip. Many players camp Sarachnis specifically hunting this drop.

Sarachnis Cudgel is a Best-in-Slot crush weapon until the Inquisitor's Mace from the Nightmare boss. For beginners, getting the Cudgel is a genuine account upgrade that makes all crush-weak bosses significantly easier.

Pet Drop — S Charizard

The Sarachnis pet, S Charizard, is a small spider pet that follows you around. It has a drop rate of 1/1,697. On average, players need 1,697 KC to get the pet. In terms of hours, at 30 kills per hour, that is approximately 56 hours of solo farming on average.

However, OSRS uses a drop rate formula that improves your odds the more kills you do without getting the pet. This means your effective rate gets better as you approach 1,697 KC. Some players get the pet in under 500 KC; others may need 3,000+ KC. That is the nature of RNG.

Including Rare Drops (Expected Value)

When you factor in the expected value of rare drops, the GP per hour increases significantly:

  • Blighted Longsword (1/800): Adds ~8,750 GP per kill in expected value. Over 30 kills/hr, that is ~262K GP/hr in expected value.
  • Cudgel (1/500): Adds ~1,600 GP per kill in expected value. Over 30 kills/hr, that is ~48K GP/hr.
  • Combined expected value: ~310K GP/hr from rares alone. This brings the total expected GP/hr for an intermediate player to ~1.5-2M GP/hr.

Of course, rares are not guaranteed. You might go 2,000 kills without a Blighted Longsword, or you might get one in your first 100 kills. That is the nature of OSRS drop rates.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I do Sarachnis at combat level 50?

A: Combat level 50 is too low for efficient Sarachnis kills. You need at least 60 Attack, 60 Strength, 60 Defence, and 43 Prayer — which typically requires a combat level of 80+. At combat 50, your stats are likely in the 50s, making kills very slow and dangerous. Train to 70+ melee stats first.

Q: How many KC for the Sarachnis pet?

A: The drop rate is 1/1,697. On average, players need 1,697 kills. At 30 kills per hour, that is about 56 hours of farming. But RNG varies — some get it in 200 KC, others need 4,000+ KC. The drop rate formula improves your odds as you approach the average.

Q: Is Sarachnis better than Giant Mole?

A: For learning boss mechanics, yes. Giant Mole is essentially a high-HP monster with almost no mechanics. Sarachnis teaches prayer switching and movement — skills you need for every harder boss. For pure GP/hr with zero risk, Giant Mole is slightly easier but pays less.

Q: What if I keep running out of prayer points?

A: Bring more prayer potions. A (4)-dose prayer potion gives 28 points. With Piety (22 PP/min), one potion lasts about 8 kills. Bring 6 prayer potions per trip for a comfortable buffer. You can also flick Piety (turn it on only during your attack tick) to save points.

Q: Do I need to complete "A Porcine of Interest"?

A: Yes, this short quest unlocks the Forthos Dungeon. It takes 10-15 minutes and has no skill requirements. Talk to the natural historian in the Hosidius museum to start it.

Final Tips

  • Bring a friend if you are nervous. Sarachnis is perfectly soloable, but having a friend with you makes the experience less intimidating. You can learn together and split the loot (or each take your own drops — Sarachnis has separate loot per player).
  • You do not need expensive gear. A Dragon Mace (20K GP) and Rune armor (50K GP) are enough to start. Do not let a lack of Barrows gear stop you from trying.
  • Practice prayer switching on a low-stakes account. If you have an Ironman or a low-level alt, practice the fight there first. Dying on a main account is safe, but dying on an Ironman still costs time.
  • Use the safe spot for your first few kills. Stand behind the northern pillar and learn the attack patterns without taking damage. Once comfortable, transition to prayer switching for faster kills.
  • Bring nature runes to alch drops. Many Sarachnis drops (rune items, dragonhide) are alchables. Alching them on the spot saves inventory space and increases your effective GP per hour.
  • Be patient with the pet grind. 1,697 KC sounds like a lot, but at 30 kills per hour, you will get 1,000 KC in just 33 hours. Many players enjoy the relaxing nature of Sarachnis farming — put on a podcast or YouTube video and enjoy the grind.
  • Check the wiki for live prices. GP values in this guide are based on June 2026 Grand Exchange prices. Always check the current price before deciding whether to farm a specific boss.
