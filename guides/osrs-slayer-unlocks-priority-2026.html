OSRS Slayer Unlocks Priority Guide 2026 | What to Unlock First

Slayer points are your most precious resource in OSRS. Spend them wrong and you waste hours. Spend them right and you unlock massive XP boosts, profit multipliers, and the best content in the game. This guide ranks every Slayer unlock by priority so you never waste a single point.

1. Introduction to Slayer Unlocks

Slayer points are earned by completing assigned tasks from any Slayer Master. You get points at task completions, with larger bonuses for streaks of 10, 50, 100, 250, and 1,000 tasks. The points are spent at any Slayer Master's reward shop on permanent upgrades.

💡 Smart Point Farming Strategy

Use the Turael boosting (9+1) method to accumulate points fast. Do 9 quick tasks at Turael (1-3 minutes each), then a 10th task at your highest available master for the streak bonus. This yields 300-500 points per hour. See our Slayer Points Guide for the full breakdown.

Total Point Requirements

To unlock every meaningful Slayer reward, you need approximately 5,700 Slayer points. However, you don't need to unlock everything — many unlocks are skippable. This guide helps you prioritize what actually matters.

For beginners just starting out, you need ~500 points for the first essential unlocks, then another ~1,500 points for mid-game upgrades. Plan your point spending carefully. If you're new to Slayer entirely, check our Beginner Slayer Guide first.

2. Must-Unlock Rewards (Priority 1)

These unlocks are non-negotiable. Every Slayer player, from level 1 to 99, needs these immediately. Do not spend points on anything else until these are unlocked.

Priority 1: Bigger and Badder — 75 Points

Effect: Enables Superior Slayer monsters to spawn while on task. Superior monsters are tougher versions with dramatically better drops including Imbued Hearts (~30M GP), Dust Battlestaves (~450K GP), and Mist Battlestaves (~1.2M GP).

Why it's #1: This is the single most profitable unlock in the game. A single Imbued Heart drop pays for your entire Slayer journey. The spawn rate is 1/200 on task, and superiors appear roughly every 5-10 minutes of active killing. The 75-point investment yields millions in profit.

When to unlock: As soon as you have 75 Slayer points — ideally before level 20 Slayer.

Priority 2: Block List — 500 Points Total

Effect: Expands your block list from 2 slots to 5 slots (and eventually 6 with 500 more Quest Point unlocks). Blocked tasks never appear from your current Slayer Master.

Block slot costs: First additional slot: 100 pts, Second: 125 pts, Third: 150 pts, Fourth: 125 pts (total: 500 pts for 4 extra slots). These are unlocked individually from any Slayer Master reward shop.

Why it's essential: Without block slots, you waste time on terrible tasks like Metal Dragons, Hellhounds, and Black Demons. Each blocked bad task saves you 30+ skip points or hours of miserable grinding. See our Block and Skip List for which tasks to block at each level.

Priority 3: Task Storage — 250 Points

Effect: Allows you to store one Slayer task with Krystilia (the Wilderness Slayer Master) and retrieve it later. This is essential for Wilderness Slayer — you can bank dangerous tasks and do safe ones first.

Why it matters: Wilderness Slayer offers 2x Slayer points and excellent money. Task storage lets you skip the riskiest assignments while keeping your streak alive. Combined with the Wilderness Slayer cave update, this is a must for efficient point farming.

Priority 4: Slayer Helmet (All Components) — ~490 Points

Effect: The Slayer helmet provides a 15% damage and accuracy boost against Slayer tasks. It combines the effects of Nose Peg (dust), Facemask (smoke), Ear Muffs (screaming), Spiny Helmet (wall beasts), and Slayer Gloves (rock slugs).

Component costs: Nose Peg unlock: 10 pts, Facemask: 20 pts, Ear Muffs: 30 pts, Spiny Helmet: 30 pts, Slayer Gloves: 100 pts, Helmet crafting recipe: 300 pts. Alternatively, buy the physical items from Masters and skip the unlock costs — but you need the 300-pt recipe to craft the helmet.

Why so high priority: 15% more damage means 15% more kills per hour. This single item is the difference between 25K XP/h and 30K XP/h on every task. Unlock it as soon as you have the points and the necessary Slayer levels for its components.

⚠️ Order Matters: First 500 Points

Here is the exact order for your first 500 Slayer points: (1) Bigger and Badder — 75 pts, (2) Task Storage — 250 pts (if doing Wilderness), or Block List slot 1 — 100 pts, (3) Block List slot 1-2 — 225 pts, (4) Nose Peg/Facemask/Ear Muffs unlocks — 60 pts. Then save for the Slayer Helmet recipe at 300 pts.

💡 The Charming Imp Decision

The Charming Imp (1,000 pts) is the most expensive non-helmet unlock. Consider this: by the time you reach 99 Slayer, you will accumulate roughly 30K-50K crimson charms. The imp saves you hundreds of hours of manually picking up charms. Most endgame players consider it essential — but save it for after all your combat extensions are unlocked.

Auto-Smash (Gargoyles) — 50 Pts

A small but very useful unlock at 50 points. It automatically smashes Gargoyles when they reach low HP instead of requiring manual Rock Hammer interaction. This saves ~1 tick per kill, adding up to significant time savings over the course of thousands of Gargoyle kills.

Boss Task Unlocks — Worth It?

Boss task unlocks let you kill the boss version of a Slayer monster instead of the regular version. These are valuable if you have the stats and gear for bossing:

  • Cerberus (91 Slayer): Unlock Hellhound boss tasks. 1.5M-2.5M GP/h. Requires 300 pts to unlock.
  • Kraken (87 Slayer): Unlock Cave Kraken boss tasks. 800K-1.5M GP/h. Requires 100 pts.
  • Thermonuclear Smoke Devil (93 Slayer): Unlock Smoke Devil boss tasks. 600K-1M GP/h. Requires 200 pts.
  • Grotesque Guardians (75 Slayer): Unlock Gargoyle boss tasks. 500K-1M GP/h. Requires 100 pts.

Only unlock boss tasks when you are 85+ Slayer and have the gear to kill them efficiently. For low-level money making, see our Low-Level Slayer Money Makers guide instead.

6. Master-Specific Slayer Unlocks

Different Slayer Masters offer different rewards. Here is what each master unlocks and when to use them:

Duradel (Shilo Village)

The standard endgame master. Duradel offers all standard Slayer rewards plus the best regular task list for XP. Assign him at 85 Slayer or 100 Combat. His task weight favors high-XP tasks like Abyssal Demons, Black Demons, and Greater Demons.

Nieve/Steve (Tree Gnome Stronghold)

The best mid-game master. Nieve offers excellent weight toward burstable tasks (Dust Devils, Nechryaels) and cannon tasks (Bloodvelds, Dagannoth). Use her from level 60-85 Slayer.

Konar (Brimhaven/Mount Karuulm)

Konar assigns tasks in specific locations, with a bonus for completing them in the assigned area. Her streak bonus is the highest (6 base pts, 75 at 10-streak). Use Konar for Slayer point farming and Hydra tasks (95 Slayer). Her area-locked nature means some tasks take longer, but the bonus points compensate.

Master Selection Strategy

  • Level 1-50: Turael (1-15) then Vannaka (15-50) — focus on keeping points low cost
  • Level 50-85: Nieve/Steve — best task weights for mid-game burst/cannon tasks
  • Level 85-99: Duradel — best for XP with occasional Konar swaps for Hydra tasks
  • Point farming: Turael boosting (9+1) with Konar for the 10th task (highest streak bonus)

Krystilia (Wilderness Slayer)

Krystilia in Edgeville offers Wilderness-exclusive tasks. She gives 2x Slayer points compared to other masters, making her the fastest way to farm points. Tasks include: Ents, Skeletons, Zombies, Ankou, Lesser Demons, Greater Demons, Green Dragons, and boss tasks (Vet'ion, Callisto, Venenatis).

⚠️ Wilderness Slayer Warning

Wilderness Slayer is high risk. PKers patrol all popular task locations. Never bring more than 50K GP worth of gear. Use Protect Item prayer. Bring a one-click teleport at all times. The 2x points are tempting but losing multiple inventories of gear negates the benefit.

8. Slayer Unlocks FAQs

What Slayer unlocks should I buy first?

In order: Bigger and Badder (75 pts) for superior monsters, Block List extension to 6 slots (500 pts), Task Storage (250 pts), and Slayer Helmet unlock (490 pts total with components). These form the foundation of efficient Slayer training.

How many Slayer points do I need for all unlocks?

Completing every Slayer unlock requires approximately 5,700 total Slayer points. This includes all task extensions (900 pts), all helmet upgrades (490 pts), block slots (500 pts), superior unlock (75 pts), and miscellaneous unlocks like Task Storage (250 pts), Slayer Rings (225 pts), and Charming Imp (1,000 pts).

Are superior slayer monsters worth unlocking?

Yes, absolutely. Superior Slayer monsters are unlocked with Bigger and Badder (75 points) and are essential for mid- and late-game Slayer. They drop Imbued Hearts (1/400 roll on any superior, worth ~30M GP), Dust Battlestaves, Mist Battlestaves, and the highest-tier Slayer loot in the game. The 75 points pay for themselves rapidly.

Should I unlock all task extensions?

No — only extend high-XP tasks. Priority extends: Smoke Devils (100 pts), Nechryaels (100 pts), Dust Devils (100 pts), Abyssal Demons (100 pts), Gargoyles (100 pts), and Kraken (100 pts). Skip extends for low-value tasks like Black Demons, Greater Demons, and Hellhounds.

Is the Charming Imp worth 1,000 Slayer points?

For serious Summoning training, yes. The Charming Imp automatically picks up all charm types, saving you hundreds of hours of manually looting. By 99 Slayer you will accumulate 30K-50K crimson charms. However, as a low-level player with limited points, prioritize combat extensions and Slayer helmet upgrades first. Buy the Charming Imp only when you have 1,000 spare points and plan to train Summoning to 99.
