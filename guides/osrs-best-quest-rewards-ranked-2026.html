1. How We Rank Quest Rewards

Quest rewards in OSRS fall into four categories. We rank them based on a combination of: GP equivalency (how much gold you would need to pay for the same benefit), time saved (training hours skipped), content access (bosses, areas, and activities unlocked), and longevity (how long the reward remains relevant in your account progression).

TierDefinitionExample RewardImpact Score
S-TierBest-in-slot forever or unlocks 3M+ GP/hr contentBarrows Gloves, Vorkath access10/10
A-TierBest-in-slot for years or unlocks 1-3M GP/hr contentDragon Scimitar, Ancient Magicks8/10
B-TierGood permanent unlock or huge XP burst for early gameFairy Rings, Piety, Dragon Defenders6/10
C-TierDecent XP or niche unlocksWaterfall XP, Morytania access4/10
D-TierSmall rewards, easily replaceable5,000 GP, 2,000 Cooking XP2/10

This ranking focuses on permanent, non-tradeable rewards. While some quests give tradeable items (like the Dragon Scimitar), those items are valuable because quest-locked content gates access to them. We do not factor in GP rewards (which are always replaceable).

🏆 The #1 Reward: Barrows Gloves

Barrows Gloves from Recipe for Disaster are the single best quest reward in OSRS — full stop. They provide +12 to Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, and Magic bonuses, making them best-in-slot gloves for virtually every combat situation. No other glove slot item comes close until Torva gloves or Ferocious gloves. They never become obsolete. The 176 hours of questing required to unlock Recipe for Disaster's final reward is the single best time investment you can make in OSRS.

💡 XP Lamp Optimization

Many quests give XP lamps that can be used on any skill. The single best use for any XP lamp is Runecrafting — it is the slowest skill to train in OSRS at roughly 30-60K XP/hr at high levels. The second best is Agility (50-70K XP/hr), and third is Herblore (expensive to train). Avoid using lamps on fast skills like Cooking (300K+ XP/hr) or Fletching (500K+ XP/hr).

🗡 The Unsung Hero: Magic Secateurs

Magic Secateurs from Fairy Tale I are permanently best-in-slot for farming. They provide a +10% invisible farming yield boost when equipped during herb runs. Over hundreds of herb runs across your OSRS career, this single item gives you millions of GP in free profit. Never do a herb run without them.

💰 GP Value of Quest Items

If you had to buy equivalent power on the Grand Exchange, the total GP value of quest-locked items across all quests exceeds 500 million GP. The Dragon Scimitar alone saves you from needing a 20M+ GP weapon at mid levels. Barrows Gloves would cost 50M+ if they were tradeable.

🌎 The Most Underrated Unlock: Fairy Rings

Fairy Rings are arguably the most underrated unlock in OSRS. With over 40 teleport destinations across the game, they connect you to nearly every major content hub. The Fairy Tale II quest can be started with moderate stats (57 Farming, 59 Magic), and you gain full Fairy Ring access without completing the quest. Make this your transportation priority after the basic teleport spells.

7. Early Game Priority — Best Rewards First

For new members (0-50 QP), the goal is maximum power gain for minimum time. Focus on quests that require no skill training and give combat XP. Here is the optimal early game quest order based on reward value.

Your First 10 Members Quests (In Order)

1. Waterfall Quest (10 minutes, zero requirements)

27,500 total combat XP. This is the single best time-to-reward ratio in all of OSRS. Do this immediately upon becoming a member.

2. Tree Gnome Village (15 minutes, zero requirements)

11,450 Attack XP + Spirit Tree access. Combined with Waterfall Quest, you now have 25,200 Attack XP — enough for level 32 Attack.

3. Fight Arena (20 minutes, zero requirements)

12,150 Attack XP. Your total is now 37,350 Attack XP — enough for level 37 Attack.

4. The Grand Tree (30 minutes, 25 Agility)

18,400 Attack XP + Glider network. After 1 hour of members questing, you have 55,750 Attack XP — enough for level 43 Attack.

5. Vampyre Slayer (8 minutes, zero requirements)

4,825 Attack XP. This takes you to level 44+ Attack.

6. Holy Grail (30 minutes, zero requirements)

15,300 Prayer XP and 11,000 Defence XP. This can take your Prayer from level 1 to level 24 instantly.

7. Priest in Peril (20 minutes, zero requirements)

Morytania access + 1,406 Prayer XP. Opens Barrows, Slayer Tower, and more.

8. Lost City (30 minutes, 31 Crafting, 36 Woodcutting)

Dragon dagger + Zanaris access. Required for Fairy Tale series.

9. Fairy Tale I (2 hours, 17 Farming)

17,350 Crafting + 17,350 Farming XP. Magic Secateurs (+10% farming yield forever).

10. Fairy Tale II — Start Only (57 Farming, 59 Magic)

Fairy Ring network unlocked. The single best transportation unlock in OSRS.

💡 Early Game Reward Strategy

After completing these 10 quests, you will have: 44+ Attack, 25+ Strength, 10+ Defence, 24+ Prayer, Fairy Ring access, Spirit Tree access, Glider network, and the ability to wield Rune weaponry. All achieved in approximately 4-5 hours of gameplay. From here, push for Monkey Madness I (Dragon Scimitar at 40 Attack) and Recipe for Disaster (Barrows Gloves). 📈 The Grandmaster Roadmap

  1. Start with King's Ransom — Piety is a passive DPS boost that benefits every hour of melee combat you ever do. It requires 70 Prayer and the Knight Waves Training Grounds.
  2. Monkey Madness II next — The requirements are lower than DS2 and SoTE. Zenyte jewelry (Tormented, Anguish, Torture, Suffering) is best-in-slot until the absolute endgame.
  3. Dragon Slayer II third — Vorkath is your ticket to consistent high GP/hr. Rigour and Augury prayers also come from this quest and are essential for Ranged/Magic builds.
  4. Song of the Elves last — The highest skill requirements of any quest (70 in 8 skills). The Gauntlet is excellent but requires learning. Save this for when you have strong stats.
  5. A Night at the Theatre — Do this whenever you have 80+ combat stats and 2-3 friends. The 50,000 XP reward is excellent, and ToB entry mode is great practice.

9. Quest Rewards FAQs

What is the single best quest reward in OSRS?

Barrows Gloves from Recipe for Disaster. They provide +12 to all offensive stats and +12 Strength bonus, making them best-in-slot gloves for all combat styles. No other glove slot item comes close until endgame raid drops (Torva gloves, Ferocious gloves for melee specifically). They are the definition of a reward that never becomes obsolete.

Which quest gives the most XP in a single reward?

Desert Treasure I gives the single largest XP reward: 50,000 Magic XP. For total XP across multiple skills, Dragon Slayer II gives approximately 45,000 XP spread across Defence, Magic, Smithing, and an XP lamp. For a single non-combat skill, Mourning's End Part II gives 30,000 Crafting XP. When you factor in time spent per XP earned, Waterfall Quest (27,500 XP in 10 minutes) has the best ratio in the game.

What is the best quest reward for making money?

Dragon Slayer II unlocks Vorkath, which consistently earns 3-4M GP per hour with moderate gear and stats. This is the single best quest-locked money maker. Second is Song of the Elves (unlocks Gauntlet at 3-5M GP/hr and Zalcano at 1-2M GP/hr). Third is Monkey Madness II (demonic gorillas at 2-3M GP/hr with zenyte shard drops).

Which quest should I do first for best rewards as a new member?

Waterfall Quest is the definitive first members quest. It has zero requirements, takes 10 minutes, and gives 27,500 total combat XP (13,750 Attack and 13,750 Strength). Follow it immediately with Tree Gnome Village, Fight Arena, and The Grand Tree. These four quests take about 1.5 hours and push your Attack from level 1 to 43+. After that, pursue Monkey Madness I for the Dragon Scimitar.

Are quest reward XP lamps worth saving for later?

XP lamps give a fixed amount of XP regardless of your level. A 10,000 XP lamp gives 10,000 XP whether you are level 1 or level 90. However, the percentage of a level it fills drops dramatically at higher levels. The optimal strategy is: use XP lamps immediately on the slowest skill you want to train. Runecrafting, Agility, and Herblore are the top three choices. Never save lamps for endgame — the benefit of having those extra levels now far outweighs the hypothetical benefit of saving them.
