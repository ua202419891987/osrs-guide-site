OSRS Regional Worlds Guide 2026 — Japan, Singapore & South Africa Servers

Jagex just launched 9 new regional worlds on June 3, 2026! If you've been playing OSRS with high ping from Asia or Africa, this is the biggest quality-of-life change of the year. Here's everything you need to know to switch worlds and finally enjoy smooth gameplay.

1. What Are Regional Worlds?

OSRS has always had most of its game servers based in Europe and North America. For players in Asia, Africa, and South America, this meant one thing: high ping, laggy clicks, and rubber-banding during tick-sensitive content like Zulrah, Raids, or even just chopping trees with perfect timing.

Regional worlds are game servers physically located closer to specific regions. By connecting to a regional world instead of a UK/US server, your click is processed faster — which means:

  • ✅ More accurate tick manipulation (fishing, combat flicking)
  • ✅ Smoother boss fights with less delay
  • ✅ Better experience in PvP and PvM content
  • ✅ Reduced rubber-banding when moving around

Jagex already tested this with Brazilian regional worlds in 2024, and it was a massive success. Now they've expanded to three more regions: Japan, Singapore, and South Africa.

💡 No Requirements! Any player — F2P or Members — can switch to regional worlds. There's no quest, level requirement, or application needed. Just pick your world and play.

3. How to Switch to a Regional World

Switching worlds takes less than 30 seconds. Here's exactly how to do it:

On PC (RuneLite or Official Client)

  1. Click the World Switcher icon in your minimap (or press F10 in RuneLite)
  2. In the search bar, type your world number (e.g., 661 for Singapore Members)
  3. Click the world to select it — you'll be prompted to log out and log back in
  4. Log back in — you're now on the regional server ✅
💡 RuneLite Tip: You can also filter worlds by typing "Singapore", "Japan", or "South Africa" if the world list has region labels. Alternatively, look for the flag emoji next to world numbers.

On Mobile

  1. From the login screen, tap "Change World"
  2. Scroll down or search for the world number
  3. Tap to select and login

Staying on Your Regional World

Once you switch worlds, OSRS remembers your last world. You'll automatically reconnect to your regional world when you log back in — unless you manually switch again.

4. Who Benefits Most?

Not everyone will notice a huge difference — if you're already on a low-ping server (UK/US), regional worlds may not help. But if you fall into any of these categories, you should absolutely switch:

🟢 High Priority: Switch Now

  • Players in Japan, South Korea, China, Southeast Asia → Use Singapore (660-664) or Japan (698-700) worlds
  • Players in South Africa, East Africa, Middle East → Use South Africa (667-668) worlds
  • Anyone doing tick-sensitive content (Zulrah, Vorkath, Raids, Prayer flicking, AFK skilling) with noticeable lag

🟡 Medium Priority: Test It

  • Players in Australia/New Zealand (Singapore may help, but existing worlds might be closer)
  • Players in Western Europe doing PvP (current worlds should be fine, but test Singapore)

⚪ Low Priority: No Change Needed

  • Players in UK, Europe, and North America (current servers are already close)
📊 How to Check Your Ping: In RuneLite, install the "World Hopper" plugin — it shows ping (ms) for each world in real time. Compare your current world ping vs. a regional world to see if you benefit.

5. Bonus: Farming Quality-of-Life Changes (June 3 Update)

The regional worlds weren't the only thing in the June 3 update. Several long-requested Farming QoL changes also went live:

Tool Leprechaun "Deposit-All" Option

You can now right-click the Tool Leprechaun to get a "Deposit-All" option, quickly storing all your farming tools and buckets without opening the storage interface. A small change, but huge time-saver for active farmers.

Remove Healthy Plants Mid-Growth

Planted the wrong seed? Now you can use a spade to dig up a healthy plant before it reaches the check-health stage. This lets you fix planting mistakes mid-cycle.

⚠️ Warning: Digging up early means you lose the planting XP. You won't get it back. This doesn't work for single-crop patches (Mushroom, Belladonna, Grapevine, Redwood, Crystal Tree, Spirit Tree).

Seed Box & Vault Improvements

  • When you have an active Farming Contract, the required seed/sapling now appears at the top of the Seed Vault automatically
  • Using a Seed Box on a patch now auto-attempts to plant the first compatible seed inside — no need to manually select
  • Seed Box now supports drag-and-drop reordering (right-click "Check" to access)

Herb Patch Right-Click Update

Herb patches now show the name of the herb you planted in the right-click menu. Finally! No more clicking to check which herb is growing.

Cactus Patch "Clear" Option

You can now clear a Cactus patch directly even when the cactus still has remaining harvest. Perfect for cleaning up patches you've neglected.

Sulphurous Fertiliser Returns

A blast from the past — Sulphurous Fertiliser (Saltpetre + Compost) is back, usable at the Hosidius Vinery for grapevine patches. Effect: +20% grape yield, including Zamorak's grapes. Great for Wintertodt prep or Zamorak Wine runs.

6. Bonus: Sailing Improvements

If you're training Sailing, several buffs came with the June 3 patch:

Courier Tasks XP Buff

Courier (delivery) tasks now give 10% more Sailing XP. They're not the fastest method (top-tier Trial activities are better), but they're much more viable as a secondary training method.

Bounty Tasks Overhaul

Bounty tasks (hunting specific sea creatures) got a major rework:

  • Each creature type now has at least one guaranteed spawn port (e.g., Deepfin Point → guaranteed Veiled Kraken)
  • Task quantity requirements increased ~5x (Pygmy Krakens: 6 → 30, Vampyre Krakens: 6 → 30) — fewer trips back to port

Better Drop Rates

Dragon Cannon Barrel, Dragon Hook, Bottled Storm, and Dragon Metal Sheet all have improved drop rates from Bounty tasks. If you were grinding for these, now's a better time.

"Teleport to Last Ship" Option

New teleport option on Teleportation Tablets, Portal Nexus, Player-Owned House portals, and Scrying Pools — one-click return to your last used ship. No more running to the docks every time.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a membership to use regional worlds?

No — each region has at least one F2P world (Singapore: 660, South Africa: 667, Japan: 698). Members players get additional worlds with more population.

Will my progress carry over if I switch worlds?

Yes, 100%. Your character, items, bank, stats, and quest progress are all stored on Jagex's central servers — not tied to a specific world. Switching worlds is just changing which game server processes your actions.

I'm in China — which world should I use?

Try Singapore worlds (660-664) first — Singapore typically has the lowest ping from mainland China. Japan worlds (698-700) are a close second. Use the RuneLite World Hopper plugin to compare actual ping in real time.

Will Jagex add more regional worlds in the future?

Yes — Jagex explicitly stated they're exploring more regions. They cited the success of the Brazil servers as inspiration. India and the Middle East are likely candidates for future expansion.

Does playing on a regional world affect game content or drops?

No — all worlds share the same game content, drop rates, Grand Exchange prices, and mechanics. The only difference is server location (and therefore your ping/responsiveness).

When is the next OSRS update?

No update on June 10 (Game Jam VIII week). Next regular update: June 17, 2026. However, the Leagues VI: Demonic Pacts rewards shop opens on June 10 without a restart — you can still claim your rewards.
