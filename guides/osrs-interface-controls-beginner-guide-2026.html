1. Your First Look at the Game Screen

When you finish Tutorial Island and see the full game screen for the first time, it's normal to feel overwhelmed. There are numbers, icons, a chat box, a minimap, and menus everywhere.

Don't panic. You only need to understand about 8 core UI elements to play the game comfortably. Everything else is secondary and you'll learn it naturally over time.

🎮 The Good News

If you install RuneLite (the free community client), the interface becomes MUCH cleaner and more customizable. We cover RuneLite setup in detail in our RuneLite guide.

2. Main Game Interface Elements — Explained

③ 🗨️ The Chat Box (Bottom-Left)

The chat box is where the game talks to you. It has 5 tabs:

  • All — Every message (game, public, private, clan)
  • Game — Only game messages (loot, XP, warnings)
  • Public — Messages from players near you
  • Private — PMs from other players
  • Clan — Your clan chat messages
💡 Chat Tip: Turn Off Public Chat Early

Public chat can be spammy and sometimes toxic. Right-click the chat box → "Filter" → "Friends" to only see messages from friends. You can always turn it back on later.

④ 📦 The Inventory (28 Slots)

Your inventory holds everything you're carrying. 28 slots total. When it's full, you'll need to bank or drop items.

  • Stackable items (coins, runes, bolts): multiple stack into 1 slot
  • Non-stackable items (food, weapons, armor): each takes 1 slot
  • Wearing items: click an item → "Wield" or "Wear" → it moves to Equipment slot

⑤ ⚔️ Combat Style Tab (Bottom-Right)

This is CRITICAL and many beginners miss it. When you have a weapon equipped, a "Combat Options" tab appears. Here you choose your Attack style:

  • Accurate → trains Attack (hit more often)
  • Aggressive → trains Strength (hit harder)
  • Defensive → trains Defence (get hit less)
  • Controlled → trains all three evenly (slowest path)
⚠️ Common Beginner Mistake

Many new players never change their combat style and wonder why their Attack stays at level 1 while they have 20 Strength. Check this tab every time you train combat!

You need to control the camera to see where you're going, target NPCs, and admire the scenery. Here's how:

Go to Settings (wrench icon) → Display and enable:

  • "Camera zoom adjustment" — lets you zoom much further out (game-changer for bossing)
  • "Rotate minimap" — minimap stays oriented to your camera (or disable for north-always-up)

4. Mouse & Click Controls

OSRS is a point-and-click game. Understanding what different clicks do will save you hundreds of misclicks.

⑥ Left Click vs Right Click

ActionLeft ClickRight Click (Options Menu)
NPC (e.g., Cow)Attack (if combat ready)Examine, Talk-to, Follow, Attack
Item on groundTake itExamine, Take, (if shift-click: Drop)
Object (tree, rock)Interact (chop, mine)Examine, different interaction options
PlayerFollow (if allowed)Follow, Trade, Examine, Report
Inventory itemUse item (eat food, equip)Drop, Examine, Use (for combining items)

⑦ Important Mouse Settings

⚙️ "Shift-Click Drop" Is a Must-Enable Setting

Go to Settings → Gameplay → Controls and enable "Shift-click to drop items". This lets you quickly drop items by Shift+clicking them instead of right-click → Drop. Saves hours of inventory management time.

How to Check Your Stats (Gear Bonuses)

Click the Equipment tab → "Show Equipment Stats" button. This shows your:

  • Attack bonuses (stab, slash, crush, magic, ranged)
  • Defence bonuses (all styles)
  • Other bonuses (strength bonus, prayer bonus, melee accuracy)

As a beginner, the most important number is your Max Hit in the Melee section — it tells you the highest damage you can deal in one hit.

7. Minimap & World Map — Don't Get Lost

⑨ The Minimap (Top-Right Corner)

The minimap shows what's immediately around you. Here's how to read it:

  • Yellow dot = You (always in center)
  • Red dots = NPCs / Monsters (potential enemies)
  • Blue dots = Other players
  • Green dots = Fifeplaces, anvils, etc. (interactive objects)
  • Door icon = Building entrance
  • Minimap icon (top-right of minimap) = Click to open full World Map

⑩ The World Map (Hotkey: Accented character / click minimap icon)

The full world map is VAST. In F2P it shows Misthalin (the starting continent). In Members it shows the entire world of Gielinor.

🗺️ How to Use the World Map Efficiently

  1. Open the map (click the minimap icon or press the accented char key)
  2. Click on a location name on the left panel to center the map there
  3. Right-click a location → "Add marker" to create a custom waypoint
  4. Close the map — a small arrow appears on your screen pointing toward the marked location
  5. Follow the arrow until you arrive!
💡 Pro Tip: Mark Your Bank

As soon as you visit a bank for the first time (Lumbridge castle has a bank chest on the second floor), mark it on your world map. You'll be going back there constantly in your first weeks.

8. Chat Box & Communication — Stay Safe

How to Talk to Other Players

Press Enter to open the chat box, type your message, press Enter again to send. Your message appears in the Public tab (if enabled) and other nearby players can see it.

⚠️ CRITICAL: Chat Safety Rules

OSRS has scammers, just like anywhere online. NEVER click a link sent in chat. NEVER share your password. NEVER give your items to someone promising to "double them" or "bless them." These are ALL scams. Real players will never ask for your password.

Useful Chat Commands

CommandWhat It Does
::bank(In some worlds) Teleports you to a bank (not in standard worlds)
/trade [name]Send a trade request to another player
/msg [name] [message]Send a private message
//report [name] [reason]Report a player for rule violations

Note: Most chat commands aren't needed as a beginner. Focus on playing the game. You'll learn these naturally if you join a clan later.

FAQ — Game Interface & Controls

How do I rotate the camera?

Click and hold the middle mouse button (or press and hold the arrow keys) to rotate the camera around your character. You can also hold Right-click + Left-click simultaneously on mobile or laptop touchpads. The camera angle affects what you can see in the game world.

What's the difference between Fixed and Resizable mode?

Fixed mode is the classic OSRS layout (small game window, fixed interface panels). Resizable mode lets you resize the game window to fill your screen — highly recommended for modern monitors. Resizable (modern) mode also has more keybind options and smoother camera control.

How do I set up keybinds for quick access?

Open Settings → Controls → Keybinds. The most important keybinds to set: F1 (inventory), F2 (equipment), F3 (prayer), F4 (magic), F5 (combat options), Escape (log out). This lets you switch panels instantly without clicking tabs.

What are chat commands and how do I use them?

Type :: followed by a command in the chat box. Useful commands: ::stats (open skill stats), ::bank (open bank if near a bank chest), ::toggle (toggle items on/off), ::level (check your level). These are client commands, not cheats — they're built into the official client.

How do I use the Quest Journal efficiently?

Click the shield icon in the bottom-right tab (or press Alt+Q). The journal shows all quests with colored dots: Red = not started, Yellow = in progress, Green = completed. Right-click a quest name for a quick summary. Completed quests show a completion time and date.

What does the "Wilderness Level" mean on the minimap?

When you enter the Wilderness (north of Varrock), your minimap shows a Wilderness Level (1–53). This indicates how deep you are. At Wilderness Level 1, players within 3 combat levels of you can attack you. At Level 50, ANYONE can attack you. The deeper you go, the more dangerous. Always check before entering.

How do I use the Grouping System?

The Grouping icon (two people icon, bottom-right) lets you form groups with other players. Groups are needed for some activities (Raids, Chambers of Xeric, Theater of Blood). For solo play, you don't need it. But joining a clan (via the Clan Chat tab) is highly recommended for getting help and finding groups.

What is RuneLite and should I use it?

RuneLite is a third-party client with hundreds of plugins that enhance the game. Popular plugins: GE Price Checker (shows item values), Inventory Setups (switch gear sets with one click), XP Tracker (track your skill XP rates), Nightmare Zone (auto-absorption tracking). Recommended for all players. Download from runelite.net.

How do I move the minimap or resize panels?

In Resizable mode, you can click and drag the edges of interface panels to resize them. Right-click a panel tab and select "Detatch" to move it freely. To reset everything to default: Settings → Reset → Interface. Note: Some panel positions save automatically between sessions.
