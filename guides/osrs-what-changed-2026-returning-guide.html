What Changed in OSRS 2026 — Complete Guide for Returning Players

Novice

If you took a break from OSRS in 2025 or early 2026, you've come back to a very different game. Sailing is here, the meta has shifted, and massive content updates have reshaped how you train, fight, and profit. This guide covers every major change — backed by Jagex official data, zero speculation.

1. Sailing — OSRS's 24th Skill

The biggest change in OSRS history: Sailing launched on November 19, 2025. It's OSRS's first brand-new skill in over a decade and introduces a complete naval system.

If you haven't touched Sailing yet, start with The Pandemonium miniquest at Port Sarim. It takes 15 minutes and starts you at level 6 with your first ship (Raft). Four training methods are available: Port Tasks, Shipwreck Salvaging (15+), Sea Charting, and Barracuda Trials (30+). Estimated 1-99 time is 120-150 hours with estimated costs of 20-60M GP for upgrades.

See our complete Sailing Training Guide for full details.

2. Summer Sweep-Up 2026 — Gear Overhaul

Released June 2026, the Summer Sweep-Up update brought one of the biggest equipment balancing passes in OSRS history:

  • Gauntlet overhaul — corrupted gauntlet mechanics refined, new strategies
  • 15+ gear buffs — underused weapons and armor adjusted to be competitive
  • 7 new Agility shortcuts — improving travel efficiency across Gielinor
  • Hunter quality-of-life improvements — birdhouse and trap mechanics smoothed

This update significantly changed the PvM meta, particularly for mid-game players. Check our Summer Sweep-Up guide for the full gear change log.

3. Frost Dragons & Dragon Sheets

Two new high-value monsters were added with Sailing:

Dragon Sheets are the hot new commodity — used for Dragon-tier ship keels and cannonballs. High demand on the GE makes these monsters profitable farming targets.

4. Leagues VI — New Mechanics

Leagues VI introduced new mechanics that changed how temporary game modes work. Key changes include:

  • New relics and tier structure
  • Updated task system with better progression
  • Integration with Sailing content for the first time

While Leagues are temporary, the mechanics introduced in Leagues VI have influenced permanent game design decisions.

5. Bank Tags, Trouver & PvP Changes

Bank Tags — A highly-requested quality-of-life feature that lets you organize your bank with custom tags. Search by tag, filter by activity, and save loadouts. Game-changing for gear management.

Trouver (Item Retrieval) Rework — The Trouver system for retrieving items on death was overhauled, making item recovery simpler and more consistent across different death scenarios.

PvP Changes — Multiple PvP balance tweaks throughout 2025-2026, including weapon adjustments and wilderness mechanic improvements. The wilderness remains an active area of ongoing balance work by Jagex.

6. FAQs

Is Sailing members-only?

Yes. Sailing is a members-only skill. Free-to-play accounts cannot train Sailing or access any Sailing content.

How long does 1-99 Sailing take?

Approximately 120-150 hours using optimal methods (Barracuda Trials). Costs roughly 20-60M GP for ship upgrades and materials.

Did the equipment meta change?

Yes. The Summer Sweep-Up update buffed 15+ items, and the Gauntlet overhaul changed PvM strategies. Some previously "off-meta" items are now viable.

What are Dragon Sheets worth?

Dragon Sheets are in high demand for endgame ship upgrades. They are tradeable on the GE and dropped by Lava Strykewyrms and Frost Dragons.

Is Bank Tags a permanent feature?

Yes. Bank Tags were released as a permanent feature in late 2025 and are now an essential quality-of-life tool for all members.
