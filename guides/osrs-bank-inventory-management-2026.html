Novice

1. What Is the Bank? (And Why You Need It)

Your Inventory holds 28 items. That's it. After 20 minutes of gameplay, your inventory will be full. You need a place to store everything else — that's what the Bank is for.

🏦 What the Bank Does

  • Stores UNLIMITED items (well, 2,304 slots — effectively unlimited for most players)
  • Accessible from any bank booth / banker NPC / bank chest in the game
  • Lets you withdraw exactly the items you need for a specific activity
  • Lets you deposit loot from a long skilling or bossing session

How to Find a Bank

Every major town has a bank. The closest one to Tutorial Island is:

  • Lumbridge: Bank chest on 2nd floor of Lumbridge Castle (easiest for new players!)
  • Varrock: West and East banks (very popular)
  • Falador: South of town square
  • Edgeville: Just north of the Wilderness border (convenient for high-level content)
💡 Mark Your First Bank on the World Map

As soon as you visit Lumbridge Castle, open your world map, find the bank chest on the 2nd floor, right-click → "Add marker." Now you'll always have a green arrow pointing you home.

2. How to Bank — Step by Step

Here's exactly how to use a bank:

📥 Depositing Items (Putting Stuff IN the Bank)

  1. Walk to a bank booth / banker NPC / bank chest
  2. Right-click the banker → "Bank" (or left-click if it says "Bank")
  3. The bank interface opens (large window with your stored items)
  4. To deposit: Click an item in your inventory → it goes into the bank. OR click "Deposit inventory" (bottom-right) to deposit EVERYTHING at once.
  5. Close the bank when done (Esc key or click X)

📤 Withdrawing Items (Taking Stuff OUT of the Bank)

  1. Open the bank (same as above)
  2. Find the item you want in the bank interface
  3. Left-click: Withdraws 1 item
  4. Right-click: Shows options: Withdraw-1, Withdraw-5, Withdraw-10, Withdraw-All, Withdraw-All-but-1
  5. Click the amount you want → item appears in your inventory
  6. Close bank

Withdraw Mode: Note vs Item

This is a setting in the bank interface that confuses many beginners:

ModeWhat HappensWhen to Use
Item (default)Withdraws actual items (each takes 1 inventory slot)Withdrawing gear, food, potions
NoteWithdraws items as "bank notes" (stackable, 1 slot for up to 2,147,483,647 items)Selling large quantities to the GE
⚠️ Beginner Mistake: Withdrawing in Note Mode by Accident

You click "Withdraw-All" on your gear and suddenly your armor is a "note" that you can't wear. To fix: right-click the note in your inventory → "Withdraw as item." Always check the "Note/Item" toggle at the bottom of the bank interface before withdrawing!

3. Bank Tabs — Organize Your Life

The bank has 8 tabs (or more with the "Bank Organiser" feature unlocked later). You can rename tabs and assign items to them. This is CRITICAL for staying organized.

How to Set Up Bank Tabs

  1. Open the bank
  2. Click the tab name (e.g., "Tab 1") at the top of the bank interface
  3. Type a new name (e.g., "Combat Gear")
  4. Drag items into that tab to assign them
  5. Repeat for all 8 tabs

Recommended Tab Setup for Beginners (F2P)

Tab #NameWhat to Put Inside

2🍳 FoodCooked fish, bread, any food items

4🎣 Skilling GearFishing rod, bait, axe, pickaxe
5📦 MaterialsOre, logs, raw fish, uncooked food
6📜 Drops / LootUnsorted drops from monsters (sort later)
7🎒 Spare GearLower-tier armor (keep as backup)
8🔮 Quest ItemsQuest-related items you might need later

4. Inventory Management Tips

Your inventory is only 28 slots. Good inventory management means less running back to the bank. Here's how to maximize it:

Stackable vs Non-Stackable Items

TypeExamplesSlots Used
StackableCoins, runes, arrows, bolts, feathers1 slot (holds up to 2.1B of that item)
Non-stackableFood, weapons, armor, raw materials1 slot per item

📦 Inventory Management Rules (Memorize These)

  1. Always fill your inventory before banking. Don't run to the bank with 10 empty slots. Kill 10 more monsters or gather 10 more resources first.
  2. Drop low-value items. If an item is worth less than 100 GP and you have full inventory, drop it. Your time is worth more than the GP you'd get from selling it.
  3. Use the "Shift-click drop" setting. Enabled in Settings → Gameplay → Controls. Makes dropping items 10x faster.
  4. Carry teleport runes. Keep 3-5 teleport runes (Law + Air + Earth) in your inventory for quick banking trips.

5. What Items to Keep vs Drop/Sell

As a beginner, you'll constantly ask: "Is this item worth keeping or can I drop it?" Here's a decision framework:

Item Value (GE Price)ActionExamples
<100 GPDrop it (not worth inventory space)Bronze items, bones (unless training Prayer), low-level herbs
100-1,000 GPSell to GE or drop if inventory is fullIron items, cowhides, low-level fish
1,000-10,000 GPAlways keep (bank it or sell at GE)Steel/Mithril items, uncut gems, herbs
>10,000 GPALWAYS bank or sell (these add up fast!)Rune items, clue scrolls, dragon items, herbs (Grimy Ranarr, etc.)
💰 The "Cowhide Trick" — Your First Income Source

Kill cows near Lumbridge. Collect cowhides (each worth ~100-200 GP). Fill inventory (28 hides). Bank them. Once you have 50-100 hides, sell them at the Grand Exchange. This is the classic F2P beginner money method. Easy, no stats required, and you train combat while doing it!

6. Bank Space — When You Run Out of Room

The bank has 2,304 item slots (8 tabs × 288 items per tab). Most players never fill it, but if you're a hoarder, here's what to do:

How to Free Up Bank Space

  • Convert items to bank notes: Withdraw items as notes (they stack in your bank too if noted). Wait — actually, items ALREADY stack in the bank. Notes are only for withdrawal.
  • Drop/sell low-value items: Be honest — are you really going to use those 500 Bronze arrows? No? Drop them.
  • Use the "Placeholders" feature: In the bank settings, enable "Placeholders" — this keeps an empty slot with the item name visible, so you know you own it but it's not taking space.
  • Get a member's "Bank Organiser": Members can unlock additional bank tabs (up to 8+ with certain unlocks). More tabs = more organization options.

7. Useful Bank Features You Should Know

Search Bar (Top of Bank Interface)

Type an item name → the bank shows only matching items. Saves you from scrolling through 500 items looking for your Rune scimitar.

Deposit Inventory / Deposit Worn Items Buttons

At the bottom of the bank interface:

  • "Deposit inventory": Deposits EVERYTHING in your inventory. One click.
  • "Deposit worn items": Takes off all your equipped gear and deposits it. Perfect after a combat session.

Bank PIN (Security)

Set a Bank PIN so that even if someone hacks your account, they can't steal your bank items immediately. Set this up in: Settings → Display & Sound → Bank PIN.

⚠️ Don't Forget Your Bank PIN

If you set a Bank PIN and forget it, you'll need to go through Jagex account recovery. It's a huge hassle. Write it down somewhere safe (but NOT in a file on your computer).

8. Beginner Bank Tab Setup (Step-by-Step)

Let's set up your bank tabs properly. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to any bank and open it
  2. Click "Tab 1" → rename to "⚔️ Gear"
  3. Click "Tab 2" → rename to "🍳 Food"
  4. Click "Tab 3" → rename to "💰 Sell/GP"
  5. Click "Tab 4" → rename to "🎣 Tools"
  6. Click "Tab 5" → rename to "📦 Raw Materials"
  7. Click "Tab 6" → rename to "🔮 Quest Items"
  8. Click "Tab 7" → rename to "📚 Spare Gear"
  9. Click "Tab 8" → rename to "❓ Unsorted"
  10. Now drag each item from your bank into the appropriate tab
  11. Done! Your bank is now organized.

9. Common Bank Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️ Top 5 Bank Mistakes
  1. Not using bank tabs: You have 100+ items in Tab 1 with no organization. You spend 5 minutes looking for your teleport runes. Set up tabs!
  2. Accidentally withdrawing notes: You withdraw your armor as notes and can't equip it. Check the Note/Item toggle!
  3. Not banking often enough: You fill your inventory, keep playing, then die and lose everything. Bank every 15-20 minutes when training.
  4. Hoarding worthless items: You have 500 Bronze arrows from 2024. They're worth 5 GP each. Just drop them.
  5. Forgetting to set a Bank PIN: Someone hacks your account and cleans out your bank in 30 seconds. Don't let this be you — set a PIN today.
