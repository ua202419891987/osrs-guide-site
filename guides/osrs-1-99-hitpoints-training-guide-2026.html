OSRS 1-99 Hitpoints Training Guide 2026

Skill Training Hitpoints Combat Skill Updated 2026

Hitpoints (also called Constitution) is your character's health pool in OSRS. Unlike other skills, it levels up automatically as you deal damage in combat — you cannot directly "train" it like Woodcutting or Fishing. However, understanding exactly how Hitpoints XP works allows you to maximize its gain while training other combat stats, ensuring you reach maximum HP as fast as possible.

How Hitpoints XP Works — The Complete System

In OSRS, Hitpoints XP is earned whenever your character deals damage to an NPC (or another player in PvP). The key formula is simple:

The Hitpoints XP Formula

  • For every point of damage you deal: You receive 1.33 Hitpoints XP
  • This means: If you hit a monster for 20 damage, you get ~26.6 Hitpoints XP
  • Important caveat: Damage from familiars, thralls, and poison does NOT give Hitpoints XP
  • Cannon damage: Does NOT give Hitpoints XP (or any combat XP)
  • Dwarven Multicannon: Great for killing speed but gives zero XP of any kind

What DOES Give Hitpoints XP

  • Melee damage (Attack/Strength hits)
  • Ranged damage (Ranged hits)
  • Magic damage (Magic hits)
  • Barrage/burst damage on multiple targets (each target gives full XP)
  • Special attack damage
  • Vesta's Blighted Longbow/Spear special attacks (in PvP)

What Does NOT Give Hitpoints XP

  • Multicannon cannonballs
  • Familiar/thrall attacks
  • Poison/venom damage over time
  • Recoil ring damage
  • Damage reflected via Elysian Spirit Shield / DFS effect
  • Any indirect or passive damage source

#1 Method: Nightmare Zone (NMZ) with Dharok's

The Nightmare Zone is hands down the most efficient way to train Hitpoints while also leveling melee stats. Using Dharok's set at low HP maximizes your damage output, which in turn maximizes Hitpoints XP per hour.

NMZ Dharok's Setup

  • Gear: Full Dharok's set (hit more at lower HP), Dragon defender/toktz-ket-xil, Primordial boots, Infernal cape
  • Overload potions: Required (boost all combat stats by +5 to +16 depending on level)
  • Super Absorption potions: Prevents Defence XP (keeps HP low = more Dharok's damage)
  • Absorption points: Maintain 300–900 absorption while keeping HP at 1–10
  • Customizable set: Only include easy bosses that don't hit hard (e.g., Khazard Warlord, Tree Spirit, etc.)
  • HP XP/hr: 450,000 – 600,000+ depending on setup
  • AFK window: Up to 20 minutes (with absorption management)

Why NMZ is Best for Hitpoints

NMZ lets you maintain near-zero HP safely thanks to absorption mechanics. This means Dharok's set bonus stays at maximum (+30% damage at 1 HP) for the entire session. Every hit you land deals massive damage, which converts into massive Hitpoints XP. A single 5-hour NMZ session can net you 2–3 million Hitpoints XP — equivalent to weeks of regular combat training.

#2 Method: Chinning (Monkey Madness I Tunnels)

"Chinning" refers to using Chinchompas (Red or Black) in multi-combat areas where one ranged attack can hit multiple targets simultaneously. This is the fastest Ranged training method in the game and also provides incredible Hitpoints XP.

Chinning Setup & Strategy

  • Location: Monkey Madness I underground tunnels (multi-combat, dense monkey spawns)
  • Weapon: Red Chinchompas (level 65 Ranged) or Black Chinchompas (level 73 Ranged)
  • Style: Rapid (faster attack speed = more hits = more HP XP)
  • Potions: Range potion, Super restore prayer, Anti-venom+
  • Prayer: Protect from Melee (monkeys are aggressive melee)
  • HP XP/hr (Red chins): ~350,000 – 420,000
  • HP XP/hr (Black chins): ~400,000 – 500,000
  • Cost per hour: ~1.5M – 3M GP (chins are expensive)

Chinning is significantly faster than normal combat training but costs millions of GP per hour in ammunition. Reserve this method when you have a large budget and want to push Hitpoints (and Ranged) to 99 quickly.

#3 Method: Ice Barrage / Burst (Multi-Splashing)

Magic barraging in multi-combat areas is another excellent way to gain Hitpoints XP alongside Magic experience. The key advantage is that barrage spells hit ALL targets in a 3x3 area, meaning each cast can deal massive cumulative damage.

Barrage Setup Options

  • Option A: Maniacal Monkeys (MM2) — Dense spawns, safe spot available. Best post-MM2 option.
  • Option B: Skeletons in Catacombs of Kourend — Multi-combat, good density, slayer task compatible.
  • Option C: Dust Devils / Nechryael — Slayer tasks with multi-spawn areas.
  • Spell: Ice Barrage (level 94) or Ice Burst (level 70) for freeze effect
  • HP XP/hr: ~250,000 – 400,000 depending on spawn density
  • ~500k–1M GP (runes cost)

Early Game Hitpoints (Levels 1–50)

New players don't need any special method. Just follow standard combat training and your Hitpoints will keep pace:

  1. Levels 1–20: Kill chickens/cows/goblins in Lumbridge area. Your HP will naturally reach ~20 by the time combat stats hit similar levels.
  2. Levels 20–40: Rock Crabs (Hosidius beach or Rellekka) or Sand Crabs (Hosidius). Very low-risk, decent HP XP (~60k–80k/hr), extremely AFK.
  3. Levels 40–60: Ammonite Crabs (Fossil Island). Slightly better XP than crabs, still very AFK-friendly. Use this range until you're ready for real combat training.
  4. Levels 60–70: Start Slayer (complete first Slayer Master quests), fight ogres/dwarves, or begin early NMZ (if you've completed required quests).
  5. Levels 70–85: NMZ with basic gear, mid-tier Slayer tasks, or begin Barrows runs.
  6. Levels 85–99: NMZ Dharok's, chinning, or high-level Slayer bosses (Hydra, Cerberus, etc.)

Tips to Maximize Hitpoints Gain

  • Avoid multicannon for XP training — cannons kill faster but give zero Hitpoints XP. Only use them when you don't care about HP (slayer tasks for speed).
  • Use weapons with high max hit — Higher damage per hit = more HP XP per click. Dharok's, Vesta's blighted longbow, and Dragon knife are excellent choices.
  • Train on high-HP monsters — Monsters with more HP let you deal more total damage before they die, which means more XP per monster.
  • Piety/Rigour/Augury increase your accuracy and damage — which indirectly increases HP XP by letting you hit higher/more consistently.
  • Don't "safe-spot" if you care about HP XP — Safe spots reduce damage taken but also slow down kills. For pure XP efficiency, tank and DPS aggressively.
  • Use the Bonecrusher (Slayer reward) — While not direct HP XP, it saves you inventory space and keeps your prayer up, letting you use offensive prayers longer.
  • Consider HP as a "free" stat — You never need to specifically grind Hitpoints. Just focus on efficient combat training, and 99 will come naturally.
