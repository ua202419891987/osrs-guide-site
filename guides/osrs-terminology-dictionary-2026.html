OSRS Terminology & Slang Dictionary 2026

New players often feel lost in OSRS chat — everyone seems to speak a different language. This complete dictionary decodes every abbreviation, acronym, and community term you'll encounter in Old School RuneScape.

📅 Beginner Series · 20 min read

⚡ Quick Verdict

Bookmark this page. OSRS has hundreds of abbreviations that new players can't reasonably memorize at once. The most important terms to know immediately: XP (experience), GP (gold pieces), GE (Grand Exchange), PvM (Player vs Monster), PvP (Player vs Player), DPS (damage per second), BIS (best in slot), AFK (away from keyboard), RNG (random number generator), and GG (good game).

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What does "btw" mean in OSRS?

"By The Way" — but it's also used as a self-identifier. "HC BTW" means "I'm a Hardcore Ironman." It's a meme at this point but also a genuine user tag.

Q: What does "smh" mean in OSRS chat?

Shaking My Head — expression of disbelief or frustration. Not unique to OSRS but very common in OSRS communities.

Q: Why do people say "GF" after killing me?

"GF" = Good Fight. It's a sportsmanlike gesture in PvP. Most Wilderness deaths end with "GF" from the winner. Sometimes sarcastic, usually genuine.

Q: What does "F" mean in OSRS chat?

"F" = Pay Respects. Originating from Call of Duty, it's typed when someone dies or gets a bad drop. Example: "Got 99 RC" → "F" (since Runecraft is tedious).

Q: What does "x" after a number mean?

Multiplication or "times." "2x XP" = double experience. "3x loot" = triple loot. Not the same as "XX" (kisses).

Q: What is "Pmod" and how do I become one?

Player Moderator — a volunteer role appointed by Jagex. You cannot apply. Jagex picks players who are helpful, active, and follow the rules. Pmods help moderate chat and report rulebreakers. They have no special powers other than a silver crown icon.

Q: What does "maxed" mean?

A maxed account has level 99 in all skills (2277 total level). Maxing is considered the "end of the beginning" — after maxing, players focus on bossing, collection logs, pets, and other completionist content.

Q: What's a "pure" account?

An account built with restrictions to optimize for specific content. Common types: 1 Defense Pure (PvP), Void Pure (Pest Control), Zerker Pure (PvP with 45 Defense), Obby Mauler (Strength-only melee).

Q: What is "de-iron" or "de-ironing"?

Converting an Ironman account to a regular account. This is permanent and irreversible. Players de-iron when they want to trade or group with friends after reaching their Ironman goals.

Q: What does "botted" mean?

An account that uses automation software (bots) to play. Jagex bans botted accounts. "Botted" is also used mockingly: "Nice botted 99 fishing" is a joke about someone training too quickly.
