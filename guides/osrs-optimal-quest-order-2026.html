1. Why Quest Order Matters More Than You Think

Old School RuneScape has over 150 quests totaling more than 4 million XP in rewards. The difference between a random quest order and an optimized one is 50-100 hours of saved training time. A well-planned order ensures you:

  • Skip early combat training entirely — Quest XP rewards take you from level 1 to 40+ in Attack, Strength, and Defence without killing a single monster
  • Unlock transportation early — Fairy Rings, Spirit Trees, and teleport spells drastically reduce travel time between activities
  • Access money makers sooner — High-reward content like Vorkath, Zulrah, and Gauntlet are locked behind quests
  • Meet requirements efficiently — Many quests have skill requirements that other quests help fulfill (e.g., Waterfall Quest gives 13,750 Attack/Strength XP for nearly zero requirements)

This guide organizes quests by Quest Point milestones rather than combat level. This is the most effective way to track progression because Quest Points directly correlate with account advancement in OSRS. Every bracket unlocks game-changing content that makes the next bracket faster and easier.

💡 The Golden Rule of Quest Order

Always complete quests in the order that gives you the lowest-requirement quests first. Many quests have skill level requirements that can be met by XP rewards from earlier quests. For example, if Quest A requires 20 Mining and Quest B gives 8,000 Mining XP, do Quest B first.

💡 Fairy Ring Priority

As soon as you have the required stats (57 Farming, 59 Magic, 49 Thieving, 40 Crafting), start Fairy Tale II - Cure a Queen. You do NOT need to finish it. Simply starting the quest gives you access to every Fairy Ring in Gielinor. This single unlock saves hundreds of hours of travel over your OSRS career.

📊 XP Lamp Strategy

Many quests offer XP lamps that can be applied to any skill. Always use these on your slowest-to-train skills: Runecrafting (no quest directly rewards RC XP), Agility (if you hate rooftops), or Herblore (expensive to train traditionally). Never waste lamps on skills you can train quickly like Cooking or Fletching.

10. Optimal Quest Order FAQs

What is the best quest order for a brand new OSRS account in 2026?

Start with all 18 F2P quests (51 QP), then immediately complete Waterfall Quest, Tree Gnome Village, Fight Arena, and The Grand Tree for 40+ Attack/Strength. Then unlock Fairy Rings by starting Fairy Tale II, get the Dragon Scimitar from Monkey Madness I, unlock Ancient Magicks from Desert Treasure I, and work toward 150 QP for Barrows Gloves via Recipe for Disaster. Finally tackle Grandmaster quests in this order: Monkey Madness II, Dragon Slayer II, Song of the Elves, then cleanup to 284 QP for the Quest Cape.

How many quest points do I need for the Quest Cape?

You need 284 Quest Points as of July 2026. This requires completing every single quest in the game, including recently added content like the Sailing quest chain, Curse of the Empty Lord, and Blood Moon Rises. The Quest Cape is the ultimate account progression milestone and a requirement for the Max Cape.

What quests give the best XP rewards for early game skilling?

The top 5 quests for early combat XP are: Waterfall Quest (13,750 Attack + 13,750 Strength), The Grand Tree (18,400 Attack), Fight Arena (12,150 Attack), Tree Gnome Village (11,450 Attack), and Vampyre Slayer (4,825 Attack). These five quests take you from level 1 to 40+ Attack in under 2 hours. For non-combat, Fairy Tale I gives 17,350 Crafting and Farming XP, and Mourning's End II gives 30,000 Crafting XP.

Can I unlock Fairy Rings without high skills?

Yes. Fairy Tale II - Cure a Queen requires 57 Farming, 59 Magic, 49 Thieving, and 40 Crafting to start. However, you do NOT need to complete the quest. Simply speaking to the Fairy Godfather in Zanaris after starting the quest unlocks all Fairy Rings. This is one of the most valuable early-game unlocks and should be prioritized as soon as you meet the skill requirements.

How long does it take to complete the optimal quest order to 150 QP?

Following the optimized order, expect 15-25 hours to reach 150 Quest Points. Breakdown: F2P section takes 3 hours, 1-50 QP early members takes 4-6 hours, and 50-150 QP mid-game takes 8-15 hours depending on your skill levels. The biggest variable is Recipe for Disaster subquests which require various skill levels that may need some manual training.
