1. Khopesh Overview – The New Contender

The Khopesh is one of the most exciting melee weapon additions to OSRS in recent years. As a one-handed sword that requires 75 Attack to wield, it has quickly established itself as a versatile option for players who want high DPS without the extreme cost of the Scythe of Vitur or the complexity of the Blade of Saeldor.

Key Stats at a Glance:

  • Attack Requirement: 75
  • Damage: 52 (effective against most high-defence bosses)
  • Speed: 3.6 seconds per hit (fast attack speed)
  • Special Attack: "Fanatical Flourish" – a powerful strike that deals up to 125% damage and lowers the target's Defence by 15% for 30 seconds (stackable)
  • Two-Handed Option: Can be paired with an off-hand Khopesh for dual-wielding, significantly boosting DPS

The Khopesh truly shines in sustained DPS scenarios. Its special attack not only deals impressive burst damage but also provides a defence-lowering debuff that benefits your entire team in co-op content. For players who enjoy Slayer tasks, the Khopesh is exceptional against high-defence monsters thanks to its accuracy bonus and the defence-lowering special.

Pro Tip: The Khopesh's special attack is often underrated. Against bosses with Defence level 200+, the 15% defence reduction effectively gives you a free accuracy and damage boost equivalent to +10 Attack levels for subsequent hits.

Obtaining the Khopesh requires completing a challenging boss encounter and some rare drop RNG. Our complete Khopesh acquisition guide walks you through the entire process step by step, including the most efficient kill strategies and expected drop rates.

Khopesh Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Excellent DPS for its cost bracket
  • Defence-lowering special attack
  • Works with any shield or off-hand
  • Dual-wield option for max DPS
  • Strong against high-defence targets

Cons

  • 75 Attack requirement locks out early-game
  • Special attack has a cooldown
  • Not as effective in mass AoE as Scythe
  • Requires investment in multiple pieces for max efficiency

2. Khopesh vs Scythe of Vitur – End-Game Showdown

The Scythe of Vitur remains the undisputed king of melee DPS in OSRS when facing multiple targets or large monsters. However, against single targets, the gap between the Scythe and the Khopesh is narrower than many players realize.

When the Scythe Wins:

  • Mass AoE situations (3+ targets in a line)
  • Large boss hitboxes (Nex, some ToB bosses)
  • Maximum theoretical DPS is unmatched
  • Prestige and resale value are excellent

When the Khopesh Wins:

  • Single-target sustained DPS (dual-wield setup)
  • Significantly lower cost (no blood rune consumption)
  • More versatile – can use a shield for defensive duties
  • Special attack provides utility beyond raw damage
  • Better for players who cannot consistently maintain Scythe AoE angles

The Scythe of Vitur consumes blood runes with each attack, which adds up to millions of GP per hour in sustained bossing. The Khopesh has zero ongoing consumable costs, making it far more economical for extended mid-game PvM sessions. For players working toward ToA solo runs or other content where every GP counts, the Khopesh's cost efficiency is a major advantage.

In terms of raw DPS against a single target with max melee stats (99 Attack, 99 Strength, full Inquisitor's), the dual-wield Khopesh setup achieves approximately 85-90% of the Scythe's DPS, at roughly 30-40% of the total cost when you factor in consumables over time. This makes the Khopesh arguably the best value-for-money weapon in the game for single-target content.

3. Khopesh vs Blade of Saeldor – Mid-to-High Game DPS

The Blade of Saeldor is the signature reward from the Gauntlet and Corrupted Gauntlet. It scales in power as the player's Hitpoints decrease, making it a high-risk, high-reward weapon that rewards aggressive play and precise HP management.

Damage Scaling: The Blade of Saeldor's max hit scales from approximately 38 at full HP to 53 at very low HP. This means that to get the most out of the Blade, you need to maintain low Hitpoints, which requires either very confident play or specific setups like using Guthan's or bunyip pouches for controlled healing.

Khopesh Advantage – Consistency: Unlike the Blade of Saeldor, the Khopesh deals consistent damage regardless of your current HP. This makes it far more newbie-friendly and less punishing in high-pressure situations. If you are learning a new boss, the last thing you need is your weapon's damage output fluctuating based on your health bar.

Cost Comparison: The Blade of Saeldor is effectively "free" if you can consistently complete the Corrupted Gauntlet. However, the time investment to acquire and repair (with crystal shards) the Blade is significant. The Khopesh, once obtained, has no maintenance costs beyond normal equipment degradation if applicable, making it more convenient for players who prefer consistent performance over time investment.

For players who enjoy Nex and other end-game PvM, the Blade of Saeldor can outperform the Khopesh in specific scenarios where you can safely maintain low HP. However, for general all-around use including Slayer and mid-tier bosses, the Khopesh is the more practical choice for most players.

4. Khopesh vs Abyssal Whip – The Mid-Game Benchmark

The Abyssal Whip has been the gold standard of mid-game melee weapons since its introduction. It is affordable, requires only 70 Attack, and has a fast attack speed. However, in 2026, the Khopesh makes a compelling case for being the whip's true successor.

DPS Difference: Against an enemy with 80 Defence (typical mid-game Slayer monster), the Khopesh with 75 Attack + 99 Strength out-DPSs the Abyssal Whip by approximately 15-20%. Against high-defence bosses (150+ Defence), this gap widens to 25-30% due to the Khopesh's superior accuracy and higher max hit.

Cost Consideration: The Abyssal Whip typically costs 1.5-2M GP on the Grand Exchange, making it accessible to most mid-game players. The Khopesh, being a rare drop from a high-level boss, is significantly more expensive on the secondary market. However, for players who obtain it themselves, the Khopesh represents a massive DPS upgrade that can shorten Slayer tasks by 20-30%, effectively paying for itself through increased efficiency.

Special Attack Comparison: The Abyssal Whip has no special attack, which is a significant downside in both PvM and PvP. The Khopesh's defence-lowering special attack provides both burst damage and team utility, making it the clear winner in any scenario where special attacks matter.

If you are currently using an Abyssal Whip and working toward your first major melee upgrade, the Khopesh should be high on your priority list. The DPS increase is immediately noticeable, and the weapon remains relevant well into the end-game. Many players who have obtained the Khopesh report that it revitalized their interest in melee training and Slayer tasks due to the faster kill speeds.

For ironman accounts, the Abyssal Whip remains easier to obtain (obtained from Abyssal Demons, which are accessible much earlier than the Khopesh's source boss). However, once you have the stats and gear to attempt the Khopesh boss, it should be a top priority. Check out our Slayer 1-99 guide for tips on efficiently reaching the combat levels needed for this upgrade.

5. Khopesh vs Dragon Scimitar – Budget Options

The Dragon Scimitar is often a player's first "real" melee weapon, requiring 60 Attack and completion of the Monkey Madness I quest. While it served generations of OSRS players well, the gap between the Dragon Scimitar and the Khopesh is substantial.

Raw DPS Comparison: The Dragon Scimitar has a max hit of approximately 28 with 99 Strength, while the Khopesh (even single-handed) achieves a max hit of 44-48 depending on strength bonus. This represents a 60-70% damage increase per hit, compounded by the Khopesh's better accuracy against high-defence targets.

Who Should Skip the Dragon Scimitar? In 2026, the Dragon Scimitar is largely a stepping-stone weapon. Players with the stats and resources to obtain an Abyssal Whip or Khopesh should generally bypass the Dragon Scimitar entirely, or use it only until they can afford/obtain a better option. The only exception is for pure accounts or accounts with low Attack levels where the Dragon Scimitar's 60 Attack requirement is more accessible than the Whip's 70 or Khopesh's 75.

Upgrade Path: Dragon Scimitar → Abyssal Whip → Khopesh → Blade of Saeldor / Scythe of Vitur. This is the standard melee weapon progression for most main accounts in 2026. Each step represents a significant DPS increase and expands the content you can efficiently tackle.

The Dragon Scimitar still has niche uses—it is the best weapon for training Attack from 60 to 70 if you cannot afford a Whip, and it is occasionally used in specific PvP setups where its fast attack speed and low strength bonus help control combat experience ratios. However, for PvM and general gameplay, the Khopesh operates in an entirely different league.

7. Cost vs Performance Analysis

When evaluating weapons in OSRS, it is essential to consider not just the upfront purchase price but also the opportunity cost, ongoing expenses, and resale value. This section breaks down the true economic impact of each weapon choice.

Khopesh Economic Profile: The Khopesh holds its value remarkably well on the Grand Exchange. Unlike weapons with high usage costs (Scythe's blood runes) or degradation (some Sage weapons), the Khopesh has minimal hidden costs. Over a 100-hour bossing session, a Scythe of Vitur will cost you approximately 30M GP in blood runes alone. The Khopesh costs 0 GP in consumables. This means the Khopesh "pays for itself" relative to the Scythe in approximately 150-200 hours of sustained use, purely in consumable savings.

Resale Value: All the weapons discussed here (except the Dragon Scimitar) maintain strong resale value. The Khopesh, being a relatively rare drop from a popular boss, tends to appreciate over time as the player base grows. This makes it a reasonable "investment" for players who may eventually upgrade to a Scythe and want to recover most of their GP.

Budget Alternative – The Abyssal Whip: For players with less than 10M GP to spend on weapons, the Abyssal Whip remains the undisputed king of value. Its DPS-per-GP ratio is the best in the game for main accounts. However, once you have the funds for a Khopesh, the upgrade is more than justified by the dramatic reduction in task completion times.

Ironman Considerations: Ironmen cannot simply buy the Khopesh; they must obtain it as a drop. This makes the "cost" of the Khopesh primarily a time investment. For irons, the weapon upgrade priority is typically: Dragon Scimitar → Abyssal Whip (farm from Abyssal Demons) → Khopesh (farm from boss) → Blade of Saeldor (learn Corrupted Gauntlet) → Scythe of Vitur (ToB teams). Each step requires progressively more time and skill, but the Khopesh is often the first "gear wall" that separates mid-game from late-game ironmen.

8. Recommended Upgrade Paths by Budget

Not every player has billions of GP to spend on melee weapons. This section outlines the optimal upgrade path based on your current budget and account progression stage.

Budget: Under 5M GP

Recommended Weapon: Abyssal Whip (1.8M GP) + Dragon Defender (free from Warriors' Guild). This combo will carry you through most mid-game content including Slayer, God Wars Dungeon, and entry-level bossing. Supplement with the best strength gear you can afford (Obsidian legs, Barrows gloves, etc.).

Budget: 5M – 50M GP

Recommended Weapon: Khopesh (single-handed). At this budget, you can either buy the Khopesh directly or farm it yourself if you are an ironman. Pair it with a Dragon Defender or, if you have the stats, an off-hand weapon. This setup provides excellent DPS for all mid-to-high-end Slayer tasks and most boss content including ToA and Nex (with proper gear).

Budget: 50M – 200M GP

Recommended Weapon: Khopesh (dual-wield set). At this stage, you should aim to acquire both the main-hand and off-hand Khopesh for maximum DPS. The second Khopesh is a significant investment but pushes your DPS into the high-end bracket. You should also consider upgrading your armour to Bandos or Justiciar depending on the content you are doing.

Budget: 200M+ GP

Recommended Weapon: Scythe of Vitur. At this budget level, the Scythe becomes accessible. However, many players at this stage maintain both a Scythe (for mass AoE and maximum DPS) and a Khopesh set (for single-target efficiency and content where the Scythe's AoE is wasted). Having both weapons allows you to adapt to any PvM scenario.

Ironman Upgrade Path (No GP Constraint)

  1. Dragon Scimitar: Complete Monkey Madness I (early game)
  2. Abyssal Whip: Farm Abyssal Demons (70 Attack required)
  3. Dragon Warhammer: Obtain for spec weapon (Olm, Nex)
  4. Khopesh: Farm from its source boss (75 Attack, strong gear recommended)
  5. Blade of Saeldor: Learn Corrupted Gauntlet (flexible weapon option)
  6. Scythe of Vitur: Team ToB for the drop (end-game goal)

This progression ensures you always have a progression-appropriate weapon for the content you are tackling. Rushing to the Scythe without mastering earlier weapons is a common mistake that leads to deaths and wasted supplies in high-end content.

9. FAQ – Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: Is the Khopesh worth it if I already have a Blade of Saeldor?

A: It depends on your playstyle. If you are comfortable maintaining low HP and enjoy the Gauntlet, the Blade of Saeldor is excellent. However, the Khopesh offers more consistent damage, has a useful special attack, and does not require HP management. For general Slayer and mid-tier bossing, many players prefer the Khopesh. For end-game bosses where you can safely stay at low HP (e.g., some ToB encounters), the Blade of Saeldor can outperform. Having both is ideal.

Q2: Can I use the Khopesh at Commander Zilyana or other Saradomin boss?

A: The Khopesh is a slash weapon, which is effective against most monsters. However, Commander Zilyana is particularly vulnerable to crush attacks, so a crushing weapon (like a mace or Scythe) would be more effective. Always check the monster's specific weakness before committing to a weapon choice. The Khopesh's defence-lowering special attack can still be valuable even if slash is not the primary weakness.

Q3: How does the Khopesh compare to the Scythe for Vorkath?

A: Vorkath is a ranged-only boss for melee attackers (meaning you cannot melee him effectively in normal mode), so this comparison is largely irrelevant for main gameplay. However, in any theoretical single-target scenario where both are usable, the Scythe's AoE is wasted on a single target, making the Khopesh (dual-wield) surprisingly competitive. For actual Vorkath kills, stick to ranged or magic.

Q4: What is the best off-hand to use with the Khopesh?

A: The best off-hand is another Khopesh for maximum DPS. However, this is expensive. Budget alternatives include the Dragon Defender (excellent strength bonus for its accessibility) or a Blessed Spirit Shield for defensive setups. For end-game content where you need prayer bonus, an Arcane or Spectral spirit shield may be preferable despite the DPS loss.

Q5: How long does it take to obtain the Khopesh as an ironman?

A: The drop rate for the Khopesh varies depending on the specific boss, but estimates suggest approximately 1/200 to 1/300 kill chance for the drop. With an average kill time of 2-3 minutes (once you are proficient), expect 10-15 hours of farming for a 50% chance at the drop. Using Slayer points to skip unwanted tasks can help you focus on tasks that align with your bossing goals.

Q6: Does the Khopesh work with the Inquisitor's armour set effect?

A: Yes! The Khopesh is a crush/slash hybrid (depending on the attack style selected), and the Inquisitor's set effect (+2.5% damage and accuracy per piece when using crush attacks) applies when you use the Khopesh's crush option. This makes the Khopesh even more powerful in an Inquisitor's setup, particularly for bosses weak to crush attacks.

Q7: Should I buy the Khopesh or save for the Scythe of Vitur?

A: If you have the GP for a Scythe, buy the Scythe. However, most players do not have 1.2B GP lying around. The Khopesh is accessible to a much broader range of players and will serve you well for hundreds of hours of gameplay. Think of the Khopesh as the "Scythe for the rest of us" – it delivers excellent DPS without requiring you to liquidate your entire bank.

10. Final Tips & Verdict

The Bottom Line: The Khopesh is the best all-around melee weapon for players who are serious about PvM but not yet at the billions-of-GP level required for a Scythe of Vitur. It offers excellent DPS, has no annoying consumable costs, provides useful utility with its special attack, and holds its value well on the Grand Exchange.

Key Takeaways:

  • The Khopesh (dual-wield) achieves ~85% of the Scythe's single-target DPS at a fraction of the cost
  • Its defence-lowering special attack provides both personal DPS and team utility in co-op content
  • Against high-defence bosses, the Khopesh significantly outperforms the Abyssal Whip and Dragon Scimitar
  • The weapon has zero ongoing costs, making it ideal for sustained Slayer and bossing sessions
  • For ironmen, the Khopesh is a major milestone that signals the transition from mid-game to late-game

Verdict by Player Type:

  • New Main Account (0-50M bank): Abyssal Whip → save for Khopesh
  • Established Main (50M-500M bank): Khopesh is essential; consider dual-wield setup
  • Wealthy Main (500M+ bank): Get both Khopesh and Scythe for different scenarios
  • Mid-Game Ironman: Farm Khopesh as a top priority after Abyssal Whip
  • End-Game Ironman: Khopesh + Blade of Saeldor cover most melee scenarios until Scythe

The Khopesh vs Alternative Weapons debate ultimately comes down to your budget, playstyle, and goals. There is no single "best" weapon for all situations – the Scythe reigns supreme for AoE, the Blade of Saeldor rewards skillful HP management, and the Khopesh delivers the best balance of performance, cost, and convenience for the majority of OSRS players in 2026.

Regardless of which weapon you choose, remember that your combat stats, gear, and game knowledge matter just as much as the weapon itself. A player with 99 Attack and Strength using an Abyssal Whip will out-DPS a player with 75/75 using a Khopesh. Focus on well-rounded account progression, and the weapon upgrades will follow naturally.

Good luck with your PvM journey, and may RNGesus bless your Khopesh grind!
