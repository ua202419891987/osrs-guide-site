OSRS Melee Weapon Comparison 2026 — Whip, Tentacle, Blade of Saeldor & Scythe

Published: June 28, 2026 | By OSRS Guru Team

Choosing the right melee weapon in Old School RuneScape is one of the most important decisions you'll make for your account's PvM progression. With prices ranging from the humble Dragon Scimitar (~60K) to the eye-watering Scythe of Vitur (~1.2B), understanding exactly what you're getting for your GP is critical. This guide provides real, verified data on every major OSRS melee weapon — their stats, costs, DPS performance, and best use cases. We'll help you make the right choice at every budget level.

2. Abyssal Whip — The Mid-Game Standard

The Abyssal Whip has been the gold standard of mid-game melee since 2005. Obtained from Abyssal Demons (85 Slayer), it requires 70 Attack and offers excellent stats for its price.

Key Stats

  • Slash Bonus: +82
  • Strength Bonus: +82
  • Attack Speed: 4 ticks (same as scimitar)
  • Special Attack: None

Pros & Cons

Pros

  • Best DPS at its price point (~1.8M)
  • Fast attack speed (4 ticks)
  • Requires only 70 Attack
  • Can be upgraded to Tentacle
  • Pairs with Avernic/Avernic defender

Cons

  • No special attack (unlike Scimitar's Sever)
  • Slash-only — ineffective against crush-weak monsters
  • Outclassed by Tentacle for only ~2M more
  • Can't be used with shield (2-handed)

Best for: Mid-game players with 70 Attack who want the best DPS for their GP. The Whip is the default melee weapon for Slayer from 70-85 and handles most mid-tier bosses comfortably.

3. Abyssal Tentacle — Whip Upgrade

The Abyssal Tentacle is created by combining an Abyssal Whip with a Kraken Tentacle (from the Kraken boss, 87 Slayer). It's a direct upgrade to the whip with higher stats and a useful special attack.

Key Stats

  • Slash Bonus: +90 (vs whip's +82)
  • Strength Bonus: +86 (vs whip's +82)
  • Special Attack: "Constrict" — 50% spec, binds target and deals poison damage

Tentacle vs Whip — Is It Worth It?

The Tentacle costs ~2.2M more than a regular whip but degrades over 10,000 attacks (~10 hours of combat). The +8 slash and +4 strength bonus translate to approximately 5-8% more DPS over the whip. The special attack is useful for:

  • Binding targets in PvP or slayer tasks
  • Extra poison damage over time
  • Added accuracy against high-defense targets

Verdict: Worth it if you have the GP and do extended melee combat. The degradation cost (~220K/hr) is negligible for most content. However, if you're saving for a Blade of Saeldor or Fang, skip the Tentacle and go straight to the next tier.

4. Blade of Saeldor (c) — The Gauntlet Reward

The Blade of Saeldor (corrupted) is obtained from the Corrupted Gauntlet and requires 75 Attack. It's charged with Crystal Shards and does not degrade when corrupted (one-time charge of 2,000 shards). It's the best one-handed slash weapon in the game.

Key Stats

  • Slash Bonus: +94 (best 1H slash)
  • Strength Bonus: +89
  • Attack Speed: 4 ticks
  • Special Attack: None

Pros

  • Best 1H slash DPS in the game
  • No degradation cost (corrupted version)
  • "Free" if obtained from Gauntlet
  • Works with Crystal Armor set effect (+range def)

Cons

  • Requires Song of the Elves quest
  • 2,000 Crystal Shards to corrupt (~65M)
  • No special attack
  • 75 Attack requirement

Best for: Mid-to-high level players who can complete Corrupted Gauntlet. The Blade is the best weapon for Slayer (after Whip) and performs excellently at most bosses weak to slash. For players who can't afford a Scythe, the Blade of Saeldor is the next best thing.

5. Scythe of Vitur — Endgame King

The Scythe of Vitur is dropped by Verzik Vitur in the Theatre of Blood. At ~1.2B GP, it's the most expensive weapon in OSRS — and for good reason. Its unique multi-target mechanic makes it unmatched for DPS on large monsters or stacked targets.

Key Stats

  • Slash Bonus: +89
  • Strength Bonus: +73
  • Attack Speed: 5 ticks
  • Special Attack: None (but hits up to 3 targets in a line)
  • Consumes: 3 Blood Runes per swing (~6K per attack)

How DPS Works

The Scythe's power comes from multi-target hits. Against a single target (1x1 size), it deals normal damage. Against a 2x2 monster, it hits twice. Against 3x3+ (e.g., Verzik, Tekton, Great Olm), it hits 3 times. This effectively triples its DPS against large targets.

Who Should Buy It: Endgame players with 500M+ banks who do ToB, CoX, and high-level bossing. The Scythe pays for itself through faster kill times and team splits.

Who Should Skip It: Anyone with less than 500M bank. The Scythe's upkeep cost (~6K per attack) and massive upfront cost make it a poor investment for mid-game accounts. Blade of Saeldor or Fang will serve you better.

6. Other Notable Weapons

Osmumten's Fang (ToA, ~50M)

The Fang is a stab weapon with a unique passive: it rerolls its accuracy check, making it exceptionally accurate against high-defense targets. It's the best melee weapon for ToA and performs well at Corporeal Beast, Vorkath (melee), and other stab-weak bosses. At 5 ticks, it's slower than the whip but more consistent.

Inquisitor's Mace (Nightmare, ~400M)

The Mace is the best crush weapon in the game. With full Inquisitor's armor, it gains +2.5% damage and accuracy per piece (+10% total). Essential for Nightmare, Tekton (CoX), and any crush-weak boss.

Dragon Hunter Lance (Vorkath/Dragon tasks, ~95M)

The Lance is effectively a spear that deals +25% damage against dragons. It's best-in-slot for Vorkath (melee), KBD, and draconic slayer tasks. Requires 75 Attack.

Saradomin Sword (GWD, ~1.5M)

A budget crush weapon with a decent special attack. Good for training Strength and for crush-weak slayer tasks. The Blessed version (from Sarachnis) is a good mid-game crush option.

8. Recommended Upgrade Paths

Budget (Under 5M GP)

Dragon Scimitar → Abyssal Whip — Dragon Scimitar is free from Monkey Madness I. Save for an Abyssal Whip. Skip the Tentacle unless you have spare GP.

Mid-Game (5M-100M GP)

Abyssal Whip → Osmumten's Fang or Blade of Saeldor — If you do ToA, get the Fang (~50M). If you do Corrupted Gauntlet, get the Blade (~65M in shards). Both are significant upgrades over the whip. The Fang is better for bossing, the Blade is better for Slayer.

High-End (100M-500M GP)

Both Fang and Blade — Own both for different content. Consider Dragon Hunter Lance (~95M) for Vorkath/dragon tasks. Save for Scythe.

Endgame (500M+ GP)

Scythe of Vitur — The Scythe transforms your PvM capability. It's essential for ToB, S-tier at CoX, and excellent at any large-boss content. Complement with full Inquisitor's for crush tasks.

9. FAQ

Q1: Is the Thunder khopesh worth considering?

A: The Thunder khopesh (60 Attack, Deadman Mode reward, ~1M) has a unique AoE special attack but lower base stats than the Whip. It's a niche item — fun for multi-combat slayer but not a primary weapon. The Abyssal Whip outperforms it in almost every scenario.

Q2: Should I buy a Scythe or max my other gear first?

A: Max your other gear first. A Scythe with budget gear is worse than a Fang/Blade with max strength gear. Get Rigour, Anguish, Torture, Tormented bracelet, and all zenyte jewelry before considering a Scythe.

Q3: How important is the Scythe for ToB?

A: Very important. The Scythe is mandatory for efficient ToB clears. The multi-target hits at Verzik, Sotetseg, and Xarpus make it irreplaceable. ToB teams will expect you to have one.

Q4: Blade of Saeldor vs Fang — which first?

A: Fang first if you do ToA. It's the best weapon in the raid and makes learning significantly easier. Blade first if you do general Slayer and bossing. The Blade's 4-tick speed makes it better for all-around use.

Q5: Is the Abyssal Tentacle worth the cost?

A: Yes, if you have the GP and don't plan to upgrade to Blade/Fang soon. The Tentacle's special attack (bind + poison) is useful at various bosses. It's a ~2M upgrade that gives ~5-8% more DPS than the regular Whip.

Final Verdict

The Bottom Line: The best melee weapon for you depends entirely on your budget and content. Dragon Scimitar for budget, Abyssal Whip for mid-game, Fang/Blade for high-end, Scythe for endgame. There is no single "best" weapon — the best weapon is the one you can afford and that matches the content you do most. Focus on your account progression, and upgrade when the next tier unlocks content you can't currently do efficiently.
