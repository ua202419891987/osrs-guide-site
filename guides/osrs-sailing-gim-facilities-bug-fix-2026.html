OSRS Sailing + GIM Facilities Bug Fix 2026: Group Ironman Ship Facilities Not Progressing

Novice

If you run a Group Ironman (GIM) and your ship's facilities — Trawling Net, Furnace, Supplies Locker, Navigator's Bench, Mob Akua, and the rest — have stopped gaining progress or simply refuse to work, you are not imagining it. This is a documented interaction between group-shared instances and the Sailing skill. Here is exactly what is happening, the workarounds that actually help, what Jagex has acknowledged, what to avoid, and how to report it so it gets fixed faster.

🏅 Level: Advanced · 11 min read · Updated July 2026 · Status verified against community bug reports

  • 📌 Report it properly through the in-game "Report a Bug" button with your group name and ship screenshot to speed up the fix.

🐛 1. The Bug: What's Actually Happening

Sailing is the first new skill added to Old School RuneScape since 2005, and its core loop is built around a player-owned ship. Your ship has facility slots — modular stations such as the Trawling Net, Furnace, Supplies Locker, Navigator's Bench, Mob Akua, and Loom — that you install, upgrade, and use during voyages. Each facility contributes to training, money making, or voyage efficiency.

The problem shows up almost exclusively on Group Ironman (GIM) accounts. Because a GIM group shares a single collective bank and (in the Sailing model) can share or co-edit a group ship, the facility state has to stay synchronized across every group member's client. When that synchronization desyncs, the facility appears installed but its progress loop never starts, or it silently resets between sessions.

💡 Why GIM and Not Regular Ironman?

On a regular Ironman or main account, your ship is a single-player instance — there is only one client that needs to agree with the server. On a GIM, the server must reconcile the ship's facility state with multiple players who may be editing the ship, depositing resources into the shared bank, or sailing simultaneously. That extra reconciliation step is where the progress check stalls. This is why the same facility works flawlessly for a solo player but "freezes" for a group.

In plain terms: the facility is there, the server thinks it is there, but the client-side progress tick that awards XP and consumes inputs is not running. You see the station, you interact with it, but nothing happens — no XP, no resource consumption, no voyage progress. It is a desync, not a missing unlock.

This guide is based on community-reported behavior and public Jagex bug-tracker entries current to the July 2026 update cycle. Bug states change fast after a patch. Always confirm the live status on the official OSRS bug tracker (linked in Section 6) before assuming a specific workaround still applies.

"Acknowledged" means Jagex has confirmed the report is valid and added it to the engineering queue. It does not mean a hotfix shipped today. If you want to confirm the current status yourself, the fastest route is the in-game bug reporter (Section 6) — if your exact report is marked "Duplicate" of an existing tracked issue, that is actually good news: it means Jagex already has it filed and is consolidating reports.

🔧 4. Workarounds That Actually Work

These are ordered from "try this first" to "last resort." They are all safe, in-game actions — no external tools, no rule-breaking.

① Full Re-Log (Resolves ~Most Cases)

  1. Completely log out to the character select screen (do not just hop worlds).
  2. Wait ~10–15 seconds so the server releases your group ship session.
  3. Log back in and re-open the Ship Management panel before interacting with any facility.
  4. Re-attempt the facility action. In the majority of community reports this restarts the progress tick.

② Have a Different Group Member Edit the Ship

Because the desync is often per-client, having a different GIM member open Ship Management, toggle the facility off and back on, then save, can re-sync the state for the whole group. This is the single most-reported successful workaround after a re-log fails.

③ Avoid Editing the Ship Mid-Voyage

If you install, remove, or upgrade a facility while a voyage is active, the progress loop can fail to attach. Finish the current voyage (or abandon it cleanly at port), then make ship edits, then start a fresh voyage.

④ Deposit/Withdraw via the Group Bank at Port Only

Several reports tie the freeze to a shared-bank deposit that happened mid-sail. Do your group-bank logistics while docked at a port (Port Sarim Docks, Sailing hub, etc.) rather than while the ship is in transit.

⑤ Last Resort: Re-Install the Facility

If the facility still shows but never progresses after steps 1–4, remove it and re-install it from the Ship Management menu. You may lose partial in-progress upgrade progress, but you restore a working station. Treat any lost progress as temporary until the official fix lands.

⚠️ 5. What NOT to Do

The bug is annoying, but the "fixes" some players suggest in Discord and on Reddit can get your entire GIM group banned. Avoid all of the following:

🚫 Never Use a "Bug-Fix" or "Auto-Sync" Client

Any third-party client or plugin that claims to "force-sync your GIM ship" or "unstick Sailing facilities" is running unauthorized code against the game. The July 2026 bot-client shutdown (RuneLite third-party forks, injected clients, and macro tools) shows Jagex is actively detecting and banning this category. One member using such a tool can flag the whole group's shared bank. See our OSRS Bot Clients Shutdown July 2026 breakdown for the current risk level.

  • Don't trust "pay to fix" DMs. Scammers target frustrated GIM players with offers to "repair your ship for GP." The only fix path is in-game. Read our Account Security Guide before handing anyone anything.
  • Don't spam world hops. Hopping 20+ times in a row can look like automated behavior and may trigger anti-bot checks. One clean re-log (Section 4, step 1) is enough.
  • Don't "test" by trading duplicated resources. If your group bank shows weird numbers after the desync, report it — do not try to exploit it. Duplicate-item exploits carry swift bans.
  • Don't rage-quit and delete the ship. Removing the whole ship can compound the state issue. Re-install a single facility first (Section 4, step 5).
  • Don't file fake "I lost items" tickets. Support cannot restore Sailing progress lost to a desync, and false item-loss claims waste investigator time and can be actioned.

📝 6. How to Report the Bug Properly

A good bug report is what gets a fix prioritized. A vague one ("sailing broken pls fix") gets ignored. Follow this template:

Step 1 — Use the In-Game Reporter

Press the Report a Bug button (the red flag in your sidebar, or type ::bug on supported worlds). This attaches your client session data automatically — far more useful than a forum post.

Step 2 — Title It Clearly

Use: "[GIM] Ship facility <name> does not progress / desyncs after group bank edit." Include "GIM" — it routes the ticket to the correct team faster.

Step 3 — Include Repro Steps

List exactly what you did: ship tier, facility name, Sailing level, what the group mate did right before (e.g., "member B deposited Supplies into shared bank mid-voyage"), and the result.

Step 4 — Attach Proof

A screenshot of the installed-but-inert facility plus your Sailing level and group name. If the report is marked "Duplicate," that confirms Jagex already tracks it — no need to re-submit.

💡 Bonus: Upvote Existing Reports

If your exact issue already exists on the official bug tracker, adding a "still happening" comment with your repro steps helps engineers reproduce it. Volume of confirmed reports directly influences patch priority — without inflating with duplicates.

💡 7. GIM-Specific Sailing Tips to Avoid Triggering It

Once your facilities are working again, these habits keep them working and protect your group's shared progress:

  • One ship editor at a time. Designate a single group member as the "ship captain" who makes all facility changes. Parallel edits from two members are the most common trigger.
  • Sail as a group when possible. Group voyages keep all clients on the same ship instance, which reduces desync versus members sailing separately and rejoining.
  • Bank at port, always. Make shared-bank deposits and withdrawals while docked, never in transit.
  • Batch your facility upgrades. Do all installs/upgrades in one session while docked, then go sail — instead of tweaking the ship between every voyage.
  • Keep a screenshot log. If progress ever vanishes, dated screenshots make your bug report (Section 6) ironclad.

When the official fix ships, Jagex usually notes it in the weekly update post. ❓ 8. Sailing + GIM Bug FAQs

Will this bug get my GIM group banned?

No. Jagex has treated the GIM/Sailing facility desync as a content bug, not a rules violation. Your account and group are safe as long as you use only official clients and don't exploit any resulting oddities (like duplicated resources). The only ban risk comes from the third-party "fix" tools discussed in Section 5.

Does this affect regular Ironman or main accounts too?

Almost never. The desync relies on the multi-client shared-state reconciliation that only exists for Group Ironman. Solo Ironmen and main accounts run a single-player ship instance and rarely hit it. If a solo player's facility is stuck, it is far more likely a level, supply, or ship-tier requirement (see the symptoms table in Section 2).

Will Jagex restore the Sailing progress I lost?

Generally no. Support does not roll back or restore skill XP lost to a desync, and filing a false item-loss claim to force a restore is itself bannable. The realistic remedy is the official code fix plus the workarounds in Section 4 to get your facilities running again. Document lost progress with screenshots so your report shows impact.

How long until Jagex fixes it?

There is no public ETA. Content/desync bugs are typically batched into regular game updates rather than hotfixed same-day. The best lever you have is a clean, well-documented bug report (Section 6) and upvoting the existing tracker entry — engineering prioritizes issues with clear, reproducible reports and confirmed volume.

Can I just use a plugin to auto-fix the sync?

No — and you shouldn't. Any plugin or client that injects code to "force-sync" your ship is unauthorized and breaches the Rules of RuneScape. With the July 2026 bot-client crackdown, detection and bans in this category are active and aggressive. Stick to the in-game workarounds; they are enough.
