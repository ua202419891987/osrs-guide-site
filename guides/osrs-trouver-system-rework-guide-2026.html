Trouver Parchment System Rework 2026 — Wildy Item Protection Guide

The Trouver Parchment system was completely overhauled on June 17, 2026. The old system — where your parchment disappeared after every death — is gone. Now, applying a Trouver Parchment to an item is permanent. This changes EVERYTHING for wilderness PvM, deep wildy bossing, and PK risk management. Here's exactly what changed and what it means for your items.

🛡️ Update Guide · Featured in our June 2026 Update Series · 14 min read

Published: June 28, 2026 · Updated: June 28, 2026

⚡ Quick Verdict

This is the single biggest wilderness PvM buff in years. The new three-tier system means: (1) cosmetic untradeables like Graceful never get lost, (2) mid-tier items like Fire Cape and Defenders get damaged (repairable at Perdu), and (3) high-tier items like Infernal Cape and Void require a one-time Trouver Parchment application — then they're permanently protected. If you PK in the wilderness, apply Trouver Parchments to all your tier-3 items immediately. If you die above level 20 wildy with a protected item, you pay 500k GP to repair it instead of losing it forever.

1. What Changed? — Old vs New System

Before June 17, 2026, the Trouver Parchment system was simple but punishing: you applied a parchment to an untradeable item, and the next time you died in the wilderness, the parchment was consumed. If you forgot to re-apply it before your next trip, the item was lost permanently.

Three-Tier System Overview

The core of the rework is a three-tier classification system that categorizes untradeable items based on their combat value:

  • Tier 1 — Non-Combat Items: No combat stats → never lost in wildy
  • Tier 2 — Partial Combat Items: Some combat value → damaged on death, repaired at Perdu
  • Tier 3 — High Combat Items: Major combat value → requires Trouver Parchment, but once applied, protection is permanent
📖 Source: Official News Post

This information is verified from the official Jagex news post "Bank Tags, Trouver System Rework & More!" published June 17, 2026 on the Old School RuneScape website. Read the full post here.

2. Tier 1: No Combat Benefit Items (Never Lost)

Items in this category provide no combat bonuses. They are now permanently safe — you will never lose them to a wilderness death, even if skulled above level 20 wildy.

🛡️ Tier 1 Item List (Never Lost)

  • Full Graceful outfit (all pieces — hood, top, legs, boots, gloves, cape)
  • All untradeable items not listed in Tier 2 or Tier 3 below
  • Quest items and other non-combat untradeables
  • Cosmetic overrides and cosmetic untradeables
💡 Why This Matters

Before this update, taking your Graceful set into deep wildy for agility courses or clue scrolls was risky. Now it's completely safe. The same applies to any cosmetic or non-combat item you own — they're effectively permanent.

3. Tier 2: Partial Combat Items (Damaged, Repairable)

These items have some combat value but are no longer permanently lost on death. Instead, they become damaged and can be repaired at Perdu for a small fee. No Trouver Parchment required.

🔥 Tier 2 Item List (Partial Combat Benefit)

Capes:

  • Fire cape
  • Imbued god capes (Guthix, Saradomin, Zamorak)
  • Ava's assembler
  • Masori assembler
  • Max capes incorporating any of the above

Defenders:

  • All defenders: Bronze, Iron, Steel, Black, Mithril, Adamant, Rune, Dragon, Avernic defender

Other:

  • Barronite mace
  • Void knight mace
  • All halos (all colors)
  • Penance hats (Fighter, Ranger, Healer, Runner)
  • Penance skirt
  • Golden decorative armour
  • Magic and Ranged decorative armour

⚙️ How It Works

  1. You die in the wilderness (any level) with a Tier 2 item
  2. The item becomes damaged (not lost!)
  3. Visit Perdu (found in most major banks, or at the Wilderness entry points)
  4. Pay a small repair fee — exact amount depends on the item
  5. Your item is fully restored
💡 No Trouver Parchment Needed!

Unlike the old system where Fire capes and Ava's Assemblers could be permanently lost, Tier 2 items are now automatically protected. You do NOT need to apply Trouver Parchments to any items in this list.

4. Tier 3: High Combat Items (Trouver Required, Permanent)

These are the most powerful untradeable items in the game. They still require a Trouver Parchment to protect — but here's the key change: once applied, the protection is permanent. No more re-applying after every death.

👑 Tier 3 Item List (High Combat Benefit)

Void Equipment:

  • All Void helmets, tops, bottoms, and gloves (including ornamented versions)

Top Tier Capes:

  • Infernal cape
  • Infernal max cape

Ancient Sceptres:

  • Blood ancient sceptre
  • Ice ancient sceptre
  • Smoke ancient sceptre
  • Shadow ancient sceptre

Other:

  • Fighter torso (including ornamented versions)
  • Rune pouch
  • Divine rune pouch
  • Dizana's quiver (including blessed version)
  • Dizana's max cape

💀 Death Mechanics for Tier 3 Items

When you die with a Trouver-protected Tier 3 item, what happens depends on where you die:

PvP Worlds (e.g., Grand Exchange)Damaged (standard)Standard repair fee
⚠️ CRITICAL: Still Must Apply Trouver Parchment

If you take a Tier 3 item above level 20 Wilderness without a Trouver Parchment applied, the item will still be permanently lost to your killer. The rework only helps if you've actually applied the parchment. Apply yours today at Perdu.

How to Apply a Trouver Parchment

  1. Purchase a Trouver Parchment from the Grand Exchange or Perdu
  2. Take your Tier 3 untradeable item to Perdu
  3. Use the parchment on Perdu, or use Perdu's menu to apply it to your item
  4. The parchment is consumed — your item now has permanent protection
  5. You can also remove a Trouver Parchment at Perdu if you want to trade/sell the item (e.g., for ornament kit versions)
💡 Which Items to Parchment First (Priority Order)
  1. Infernal Cape — most expensive to lose, highest PvM value
  2. Dizana's Quiver — irreplaceable without hours of Colosseum
  3. Rune Pouch / Divine Rune Pouch — used in every activity
  4. Void set — essential for many PvM encounters in wildy
  5. Ancient Sceptres — if you use them for wildy bursting tasks

Is 500k GP Too Expensive?

500,000 GP sounds like a lot — and it is, for a single death. But consider the alternative: losing an Infernal Cape requires spending 10-20 hours re-completing the Inferno. At current money-making rates, 500k is roughly 10-15 minutes of PvM. The value proposition is overwhelmingly in favor of the new system.

💡 Trouver Parchment Market Impact

Since Trouver Parchments are now a one-time purchase per item rather than a consumable for every death, demand has shifted. Players only need to buy parchments for their core Tier 3 items once, rather than stocking up weekly. This has stabilized the Trouver Parchment GE price significantly.

6. How to Choose Your Protection Strategy

Your protection strategy depends on what you're doing in the wilderness. Here's a decision framework for different activities.

🎯 By Activity Type

Wildy Agility Courses:

  • No protection needed — Graceful and other non-combat items are Tier 1 (never lost)

Wildy Slayer (low level, under lvl 20):

  • Tier 2 items (Fire Cape, Defenders) are automatically safe — they'll just get damaged
  • No Trouver needed for these items

Wildy Bosses above lvl 20 (Vet'ion, Callisto, Venenatis, etc.):

  • Essential: Apply Trouver Parchment to all Tier 3 items you take
  • Recommended: Budget 500k GP per death for repairs
  • Consider: Using cheaper substitutes to minimize repair costs

PvP World Skilling:

  • Same rules apply — Tier 3 items need Trouver, Tier 2 auto-protected
  • Death below lvl 20 means standard repair fees (much cheaper than 500k)

Deep Wildy Clue Scrolls:

  • Equip Graceful (Tier 1 — safe) or cheap gear
  • If taking a Rune Pouch (Tier 3), make sure it's Trouver'd

🛡️ Recommended Protection Checklist

  1. Visit Perdu and apply Trouver Parchments to ALL your Tier 3 items
  2. Verify which items you own are Tier 3 (Infernal Cape, Void, Rune Pouch, etc.)
  3. Keep a 500k GP emergency fund in your bank for potential repairs
  4. Before entering deep wildy, double-check you haven't unequipped items that need Trouver protection
  5. For low-risk activities (under lvl 20 wildy), don't worry — Tier 1 and 2 items are fully protected

7. Ornament Kits & Special Cases

Some items have ornament kit versions — tradeable cosmetics applied to untradeable items. The new system handles these differently.

🎨 How Ornament Kits Work Now

For items with tradeable ornament kits (e.g., Dragon defender (t)):

  • The base item (e.g., Dragon defender) is Tier 2 — it gets damaged but NOT lost
  • The ornament kit is tradeable — it drops to your killer on death
  • You keep the base defender, but lose the cosmetic upgrade
⚠️ Don't Forget: Ornament Kits Are Tradeable

If you care about keeping your ornament kit, consider NOT taking ornamented versions of items into the wilderness. The base version works just as well and you won't lose the cosmetic.

Removing Trouver Protection

You can remove a Trouver Parchment from an item at Perdu. This is useful if:

  • You want to apply an ornament kit (some require the item to be un-parchmented)
  • You're trading/selling the item (though untradeables can't be traded anyway)
  • You accidentally applied a parchment to an item you don't use in wildy

Note: Removing a Trouver does NOT refund the parchment. Only do this if necessary.

8. Upcoming Features & Warnings

Jagex has confirmed that additional features are in development to complement the Trouver rework.

🚧 Coming Soon: Wilderness Warning System

Jagex is developing a warning prompt that will appear when you attempt to enter the Wilderness while carrying high-tier untradeable items that do NOT have Trouver Parchments applied.

This feature was not included in the June 17 update but is planned for a future patch. Until then, it's your responsibility to check your gear before entering deep wildy.

⚠️ Your Responsibility: Check Your Gear

Without the warning system active, there is NO automatic check when you cross the Wilderness ditch. You must manually verify that all your Tier 3 items have Trouver Parchments applied. Do not rely on memory — check your equipment screen before entering level 20+ wildy.

Known Issues & Limitations

  • Potion Storage: Bank Tags and Potion Storage are currently separate systems — potions must be moved to main bank before adding to tags
  • Warning system: Not yet implemented (coming in a future update)
  • Slayer Helm ornament kits: Jagex noted they are actively discussing player feedback about the Leagues VI Slayer Helmet unlock requiring 1000 extra slayer points

9. Trouver System FAQs

Do I still need to buy Trouver Parchments?

Yes, but only once per item. You need to apply a Trouver Parchment to each Tier 3 item you want to protect. After that, the protection lasts forever — even if you die, the parchment stays on the item.

What happens if I die above level 20 wildy with a Trouver'd Tier 3 item?

The item becomes mangled (a special damaged state). You take it to Perdu and pay 500,000 GP to repair it. The Trouver Parchment stays on the item — you do NOT need to re-apply.

What if I die with a Tier 3 item that DOESN'T have Trouver protection?

It is permanently lost. The rework only helps if you've applied the Trouver Parchment. The item drops to your killer. This has NOT changed from the old system.

Is Graceful still loseable in wilderness?

No. Graceful and all non-combat untradeable items are now Tier 1 — they are never lost under any circumstances in the wilderness. This is a major QoL improvement for wildy agility and clue scrolls.

Can I remove Trouver protection from an item?

Yes. Talk to Perdu and select the option to remove Trouver Parchment. Note: you do NOT get the parchment back. Only do this if you need to apply an ornament kit or similar.

Do I need Trouver Parchments for Fire Cape or Ava's Assembler now?

No. These items are Tier 2 — they no longer require any Trouver Parchment. If you die with them, they simply get damaged and can be repaired at Perdu for a small fee.

Does the 500k repair cost apply to PvP world deaths?

No. The 500k mangled repair cost only applies to deaths above level 20 Wilderness. Deaths in PvP worlds (like the Grand Exchange) or below level 20 wildy result in standard (cheaper) repair fees.

What happens to ornament kits on death in the wildy?

The base item (e.g., Dragon defender) is protected as a Tier 2 item, but the tradeable ornament kit drops to your killer. If you want to keep your cosmetic upgrades, don't take ornamented items into the wilderness.

Where is Perdu located?

Perdu can be found in the following locations: Edgeville (near the bank), Lumbridge (ground floor of the castle), Varrock (west bank area), and near the Wilderness ditch entrance. Use the Perdu helper icon on the world map if you're having trouble finding him.

When will the Wilderness warning system be added?

Jagex has confirmed the warning system is in development but was not included in the June 17 update. No specific release date has been announced. Check the official OSRS news page for future patch notes.
