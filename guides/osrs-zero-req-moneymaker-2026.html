① Mort Myre Fungi: The Undisputed Champion

At 300K-400K GP/hr with zero skill requirements (just Priest in Peril quest + nature runes), this is the clear winner for P2P players. The downside: it's active clicking for the full hour. You walk through the swamp, cast Bloom, and pick fungi repeatedly. It's not AFKable but the GP/hr is unmatched.

② Swamp Toads: The Runner-Up

200K-300K GP/hr with even fewer requirements (no quest needed, 0 GP start). Run around the Gnome Stronghold picking up toads. Each toad sells for ~2,000 GP on the GE. The trade-off: more competition, and it's less consistent if the GE price fluctuates.

Bird house trapping (requires only 5 Hunter, takes 20 minutes to unlock) is the best "effortless" method. One bird house run takes 5 minutes and earns ~20K-40K GP. ⚠️ Wine of Zamorak Risk Assessment

Wine of Zamorak is in level ~16 Wilderness. PKers are common. Your risk: losing inventory of wines (worth ~20K GP per trip). Mitigation: bring 3 items max (Protect Item protects 1 more = 4), use the "telegrab only" technique from the safe spot, and hop worlds if you see white dots. Many players do wines for hours without dying, but it only takes one PKer to wipe your profits for the day.

📊 Head-to-Head: The Efficiency Showdown

Mort Myre Fungi (350K/hr avg) vs Swamp Toads (250K/hr avg): Fungi wins on pure GP, but toads require 0 quests and 0 GP to start. If you've already done Priest in Peril for quest cape progression, pick fungi. If you're brand new to members, pick toads for instant profit.

Wines (175K/hr) vs Monk Robes (50K/hr): Wines are 3.5x faster but 10x more stressful (Wilderness). If you have 30 min play sessions, do monk robes (zero stress). If you can focus for 2-3 hours, do wines.

Bird Houses (150K/hr passive) vs Active Methods (300K/hr): Bird houses win on time efficiency — 15 min/day for 150K GP. Active methods need 30 minutes for the same GP. If your time is limited, prioritize passive methods.

8. Zero Requirement Moneymaker FAQs

Which method is fastest overall?

Mort Myre Fungi at 300K-400K GP/hr. It requires membership and Priest in Peril (30 minute quest), but zero skill levels. For F2P, Wine of Zamorak at 150K-200K GP/hr (requires 33 Magic, 2-3 hours from level 1).

Which method is most stable?

Snape Grass (60K-90K GP/hr) has the most predictable GP/hr. The price and spawn rate haven't fluctuated significantly in years. Monk robes (40K-60K GP/hr) is equally stable for F2P players.

Which method requires the least effort?

Bird House Runs — 5 minutes of active time every 50 minutes. You earn 100K-200K GP per day with almost no effort. Bandit Shop Flip (check once per 30 minutes) is also very low effort.

Can I make bond money with zero requirements?

It takes ~36 hours of Mort Myre Fungi (P2P) to earn ~12.6M GP for a bond. For F2P, monk robes would take ~250+ hours. The most realistic path: F2P monk robes (5-10 hours, 250K-500K GP) → Buy members with real money → Use fungi to sustain membership. Alternatively, consider paying for membership with real money (cheaper per hour than grinding for it).

Can I combine methods for better GP/hr?

Yes. Do bird house runs as passive income (5 min per 50 min), then fill the gaps with active methods. Example schedule: Set bird houses (2 min) → Active method for 45 min → Collect bird houses (2 min) → Repeat. This combined approach gives you passive 100K-200K/day + active 300K-400K/hr.

Are zero-requirement methods being nerfed?

Jagex occasionally adjusts spawn rates and item prices, but collection-based methods are generally stable. Mort Myre fungi has been unchanged since 2020. Swamp toads have been stable for years. The biggest risk is market competition, not Jagex intervention. Wine of Zamorak is the most likely to be adjusted (it's been discussed in the community).

What if the GE price drops while I'm collecting?

Check the OSRS Wiki money making guide or osrs.exchange for price trends before starting a method. If prices are at a 3-month low, consider a different method temporarily. Most zero-requirement items (mort myre fungi, snape grass, monk robes) have stable long-term demand because they're used in Herblore, clue scrolls, and crafting.

Which method is best for low-end computers/mobile?

All collection methods work well on mobile and low-end PCs since they don't require precise clicks or fast reactions. Bird house runs and monk robes are particularly good on mobile (simple tap-to-collect mechanics). Wine of Zamorak is more challenging on mobile due to the Wilderness risk.
