1. Getting Your First 10K GP (15 Minutes)

Before you can do anything with the Grand Exchange, you need a tiny amount of starter capital. Here's how to get your first 10,000 GP in under 15 minutes with zero requirements:

📍 Stronghold of Security — 10,000 GP Free

Located beneath Barbarian Village (north of Falador, west of Edgeville). Enter the ladder at the center of the village.

  • Complete four levels of simple maze/combat obstacles
  • Each level gives you a reward — total 10,000 GP upon completion
  • Also gives you Fancy Boots (cosmetic) and Creaky Floorboard (emote unlock)
  • Takes approximately 10-15 minutes
  • Zero requirements: No skills, no quests, no combat needed
💡 Pro Tip

The Stronghold of Security is also a great place for noobs to learn basic game mechanics — it's designed to be completable by any account. The final reward room gives you a choice of boots; pick whichever you like, they're all cosmetic. The real prize is the 10K GP.

📍 Alternative: Pickpocket Men in Lumbridge

Men in Lumbridge (near the castle) can be pickpocketed at level 1 Thieving. Each success gives 1-6 GP and occasionally a coin stack. While slow (~2K GP/h), it requires zero effort and zero travel. Not recommended as a primary method, but useful if you're already in Lumbridge.

🎯 Best Starter Path

Create account → Run to Barbarian Village (2 min walk from Lumbridge) → Complete Stronghold of Security (15 min) → You now have 10K GP → Use this to start shop arbitrage or buy a Fire Staff for more efficient starter methods.

2. Cowhide Collecting (Lumbridge) — 40K-60K GP/h

The classic starting money maker. Cows in Lumbridge (east of the castle) drop Cowhides worth 150-200 GP each on the Grand Exchange, plus Bones (80-120 GP each) and Raw Beef.

🐄 How It Works

  • Walk to the cow field east of Lumbridge Castle
  • Attack cows (level 2 — they're harmless, no combat level needed)
  • Collect Cowhides from your kills
  • Also pick up Cowhides left on the ground by other players (many train here and don't pick up leather)
  • Bank at Lumbridge Castle basement
  • Sell hides on the Grand Exchange

💡 Efficiency Tip

Many players in the cow field kill cows and don't pick up the hides (they're training combat and inventory space is limited). Walk around the field picking up ground hides — you can fill an inventory in 2-3 minutes without fighting anything. This is faster than killing cows yourself.

3. Wilderness Ditch Looting (Edgeville) — 50K-200K+ GP/h

This method uses the Wilderness Ditch just north of Edgeville. PvP players fight in the Wilderness and drop items on death. You stand at the ditch (level 1 Wilderness) and collect what falls out.

⚠️ Risk Level: Low to Medium

  • You are in level 1 Wilderness — PKers can attack you
  • However, you're right next to the ditch — one click and you're safe
  • Never bring items you can't afford to lose
  • Watch your minimap for white dots (players approaching)

  • DO NOT cross the ditch into deeper Wilderness — stay on the Edgeville side
  • DO NOT bring anything valuable — you're in the Wilderness, you can be attacked
  • DO watch the minimap constantly — run south (toward ditch) if you see a white dot approaching
  • DO bank frequently — there's a bank in Edgeville just south of the ditch

4. Banana Picking (Karamja) — 20K-30K GP/h

The most peaceful zero-requirement money maker. Ideal for second-monitor play or when you want something completely zero-risk.

🍌 How It Works

  • Go to Port Sarim and buy a one-way ticket to Karamja for 30 GP
  • Walk south to Musa Point — the banana plantation
  • Pick bananas from the trees (they respawn quickly)
  • Use the banana crate storage at the plantation dock to bank them
  • Once you have a full inventory or crate, sell on the Grand Exchange (~100-150 GP each)
  • Alternative: Sell directly to the bandos store on Karamja for ~30 GP each (less profit but instant)
💡 When to Use This Method

Banana picking is the slowest of all zero-req methods. Use it only if: (a) you want to watch Netflix and make some GP passively, (b) you need a zero-risk break from other methods, or (c) you're a hardcore Ironman and need cooking XP too (bananas can be cooked).

5. Wilderness Bone Yard — 80K-120K GP/h

The Bone Yard is located in level 20-25 Wilderness (north of the Graveyard of Shadows). It contains scattered bones on the ground that you can pick up and sell on the Grand Exchange.

⚠️ High Risk, High Reward

  • This is level 20-25 Wilderness — PKers patrol here regularly
  • Never bring anything you can't afford to lose
  • Wear light clothing (no armor) so you can run fast
  • Bring a Teleport to House tablet if you have one (instantly leaves Wildy)
  • Watch the minimap constantly — if you see a white dot, run south immediately

🎯 Best Strategy for the Bone Yard

The "Empty Inventory" Method: Bring absolutely nothing except the clothes you're wearing. Run from Edgeville to the Bone Yard. Pick up bones until inventory is full. Teleport out or run south to ditch. Bank at Edgeville. Repeat. If a PKer kills you, you lose nothing (you're not wearing anything valuable). This makes the Bone Yard zero-risk from a gear perspective — the only loss is the bones you collected that trip.

6. Shop-to-GE Arbitrage (Best With Starter Capital)

Once you have 10K-50K GP from the Stronghold of Security and cowhides, shop arbitrage becomes your most scalable zero-requirement method. The idea is simple: buy items from NPC shops at fixed low prices, then sell them on the Grand Exchange for profit.

🔄 The Cycle

  1. Teleport/bank to the shop location (Port Sarim is a great starting hub)
  2. Buy the shop's entire stock of the item
  3. Walk to the nearest bank and deposit
  4. Repeat until your bank has a full inventory stack
  5. Sell the entire stack on the Grand Exchange
  6. Repeat the cycle — shop stock respawns in about 1-2 game minutes
💡 Scaling Up

With 10K GP, you can do about 10-15 trips before needing to sell. With 100K GP, you can stockpile efficiently. The profit margin on Ball of Wool is particularly good — 150 GP profit per item at a shop price of 2 GP is a 75x return. The only limiting factor is shop stock (stores typically sell 10-50 of each item at a time).

7. Grand Exchange Flipping (Zero Stats Required)

Once you have at least 50K-100K GP in capital, you can start flipping items on the Grand Exchange. No skills, no quests — just market knowledge.

📈 Beginner Flipping Strategy

  1. Find high-volume items: Runes, arrows, food, and raw materials have high trade volume and predictable margins
  2. Buy at low: Place a buy offer at 1-5% below current market price
  3. Sell at high: Place a sell offer at 1-5% above current market price
  4. Repeat: Each completed cycle gives you 2-10% profit on your capital

💡 Flipping Tip for Zero-Stat Accounts

Use the OSRS Wiki Prices tool (prices.runescape.wiki/osrs) to check real-time GE data. The margin between buy and sell price is visible for most items. Focus on items with at least 1,000+ traded per day — that ensures your offers fill quickly. Start with Feathers and Runes to learn the basics.

9. Zero-to-Bond Roadmap

A Bond costs ~12.8M GP (June 2026). Here's the fastest path from zero to bond using only zero-requirement methods:

Step 1: 0 → 10K GP (15 minutes)
Complete Stronghold of Security in Barbarian Village. This is your starter capital.
Step 2: 10K → 500K GP (8-12 hours)
Cowhide collecting in Lumbridge. At 50K GP/h, this takes about 10 hours. Bank the hides and sell in bulk on GE.
Step 3: 500K → 5M GP (40-60 hours)
Option A (Safer): Continue cowhides + shop arbitrage. Combine both for ~70K GP/h.
Option B (Faster but risky): Wilderness Bone Yard at 100K GP/h. This cuts the time in half but requires Wilderness awareness.
Step 4: 5M → 12.8M GP (Needed for Bond)
At this point you have enough capital to start GE flipping effectively. Combine flipping with your best active method. With 5M GP, you can make 100K-300K GP/h flipping mid-volume items.

⏱️ Total Time Estimate

Fastest path (Bone Yard + flipping): ~60-80 hours
Safest path (Cowhide + arbitrage): ~100-130 hours
Hybrid path (recommended): Cowhide to 500K, then shop arbitrage + flipping. ~80-100 hours.

10. Zero Requirement Money Making FAQs

Can I really make enough for a bond with zero stats?

Yes, but it takes time. A bond costs ~12.8M GP. At the fastest zero-req method (Wilderness Bone Yard, ~100K GP/h), that's about 128 hours of grinding. Most players find it more efficient to earn the bond through cowhides + shop arbitrage (slower but safer) or to buy a single month of membership with real money and then sustain bonds through members' money makers (which earn 500K-3M GP/h easily).

What's the fastest zero-req method?

Wilderness Ditch Looting during peak hours can reach 200K+ GP/h, but it's inconsistent. The most consistent high-GP method is the Wilderness Bone Yard at ~80K-120K GP/h. If you want consistency without Wilderness risk, Cowhide collecting (~50K GP/h) is the most reliable starter method.

Do I need to worry about bots?

Bots do affect cowhide prices and some other markets, but the methods in this guide are still profitable. Cowhides fluctuate between 150-200 GP — even at 150 GP, the method is worth doing. Shop arbitrage is immune to bots because shops have fixed prices and limited stock.

Can I do these methods as an Ironman?

Mostly no. Ironmen cannot trade on the Grand Exchange, so cowhide selling, shop arbitrage, and GE flipping don't work. However, Stronghold of Security works (gives 10K GP), and Bone Yard collecting can be used for Prayer training. For Ironman-specific money, you'll need actual combat or skill methods.

What should I spend my first GP on?

Priority order: (1) A Fire Staff (~1K GP) for free fire runes when casting spells. (2) Amulet of Power (~2K GP) for combat. (3) Starting GE flipping capital (keep at least 50K GP liquid). (4) Bond for membership (12.8M GP) — membership unlocks 10x better money makers.

Is there a one-time money maker I missed?

The Stronghold of Security (10K GP) is the only notable one-time reward with zero requirements. Other one-time rewards (like Prince Ali Rescue's 10K GP, or Vampyre Slayer's 4.5K GP) require quest completions. All methods in this guide are repeatable indefinitely.

How much GP can I make flipping with zero stats?

With 50K GP capital: ~500-2,000 GP per flip cycle (about 5-15 minutes per item type). With 500K GP capital: ~5K-25K GP per cycle. With 5M GP capital: ~50K-250K GP per cycle. Flipping scales linearly with your available capital.

What's the best zero-req method for a brand new F2P account?

Do the Stronghold of Security first (10K GP in 15 min). Then go to Lumbridge cow field and collect/pick up cowhides until you have ~100K GP. Then switch to shop arbitrage (Eye of Newt at Port Sarim, Ball of Wool at general stores). This three-phase approach is the most efficient start for any new account.
