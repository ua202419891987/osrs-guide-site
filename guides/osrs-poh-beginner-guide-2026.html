OSRS POH Guide for Beginners 2026

Player-Owned House (POH) is one of the most powerful quality-of-life upgrades in OSRS. This guide covers everything a beginner needs: how to get your first house, which rooms to build first, Portal Chamber teleports, Gilded Altar prayer training, and the most cost-effective way to train Construction.

① What Is POH & Why Every Beginner Needs One

Your Player-Owned House (POH) is a customizable home you can build, decorate, and use for powerful in-game benefits. Think of it as your personal base of operations — once you have one, you'll wonder how you ever played without it.

① Why POH Is a Game-Changer

🏠 Top 5 Reasons to Build a House Early

  • Portal Chamber: Instant teleport to 8+ locations (saves millions on travel costs)
  • Gilded Altar: 350% prayer XP per bone — fastest prayer training in the game
  • Spellbook Altar: Change between Normal/Ancient/Lunar spellbooks instantly
  • Mounted Glory: Unlimited teleports to Edgeville (no charges needed)
  • Repair NPCs: Fix Barrows equipment for free instead of using GP

Even at Construction level 1, you can enter a house and use other players' portals and altars. You don't need to build anything yourself to benefit — many high-level players host "Gilded Altar worlds" (World 330) where anyone can use their altar for free.

💡 New Player Priority

You don't need to train Construction early. Instead, use other players' houses via World 330 (Rimmington portal). Get your prayer to 43+ using their Gilded Altar. Then start building your own house once you have spare GP.

② Unlocking Your First House

Getting your own house is simple and costs only 1,000 GP. Here's the exact step-by-step process:

② Step 1: Talk to an Estate Agent

There are 3 Estate Agents in Gielinor. Visit any one of them:

LocationHow to Get There
Varrock (South)West of Varrock South Gate, near the clothes shop
Falador (East)East Falador, north of the Mining Guild entrance
Seers' VillageWest side of Seers' Village, near the bank

Right-click the Estate Agent → choose "Buy House". Pay 1,000 GP. That's it — you now own a house!

③ Step 2: Enter Your House

There are two ways to enter your house:

Option A: Use a House Portal (Free)

Each major city has a House Portal (purple swirl icon on map). Walk into the portal, select "Enter My House". Your house starts in Rimmington by default.

Option B: Teleport to House (After Unlocking)

Cast the Teleport to House spell (requires 25 Magic, 1 Astral rune + 1 Law rune + 5 Fire runes). Or right-click your House Teleport tablet if you have one.

④ Step 3: Building Mode

While inside your house, right-click the House Options icon (bottom-right of game screen) → select "Building Mode: ON". This lets you place/remove rooms and furniture.

③ Room Building Costs for Beginners

Building rooms requires Planks (not logs!) and Nails (for some rooms). Here's what a beginner needs to know about costs:

⑥ How to Get Planks (3 Methods)

🪚 Method 1: Sawmill (Recommended)

Take logs to the Sawmill operator northeast of Varrock (or in your house if you build a Sawmill room). Cost: 100 GP per regular plank conversion.

🪚 Method 2: Own House Sawmill Room

Build a Sawmill room (Construction 25). Costs 200 GP per plank but saves running back and forth. Requires water in the Operate Water Conveyor.

🪚 Method 3: Plank Make Spell (Magic 30)

Cast Plank Make (2 Nature runes + 1 Astral rune per cast). Converts logs to planks instantly. Expensive but fastest method if you have high Magic.

💡 Budget Starter House

A basic functional house with Portal Chamber + Chapel costs approximately 500,000 — 800,000 GP for materials. This gives you teleport access and prayer training. You can expand later as you earn more GP.

④ Portal Chamber — The #1 Quality-of-Life Upgrade

The Portal Chamber is the single most useful room in any POH. It allows you to teleport instantly to 8 different locations by clicking portals inside your house.

⑦ Building Your First Portal Chamber

Requirements: Construction level 50 (or use someone else's house if below 50).

Cost: ~150,000 GP (Teak planks + marble blocks for portal frames).

Step 1: Buy Portal Runes

Each portal needs specific runes to activate. For example, the Varrock portal needs 3 Law runes + 3 Air runes + 3 Fire runes. Buy these from the Grand Exchange.

Step 2: Activate Portals

Enter Building Mode → click an empty portal frame → select a destination city → use the required runes from your inventory on the portal. Once activated, the portal stays forever (no recurring cost).

⑤ Chapel & Gilded Altar — Free Prayer XP

The Chapel room with a Gilded Altar is the fastest way to train Prayer in OSRS. It gives 350% XP per bone — that's 3.5x faster than burying!

⑨ Building a Basic Chapel (No Gold Leaves)

If you can't afford a Gilded Altar yet, build a Basic Altar (any altar from the Chapel):

🙏 Basic Altar XP Rates

  • Oak Altar: 150% XP per bone (50% bonus vs burying)
  • Teak Altar: 200% XP per bone (2x bonus)
  • Cloth Altar: 250% XP per bone (2.5x bonus)
  • Gilded Altar: 350% XP per bone (3.5x bonus) ← GOAL

⑩ Building a Gilded Altar (The Goal)

Requirements: Construction level 75 (or use World 330 hosted altars).

Cost: ~1,200,000 GP (5 Gold leaves x ~200k each + 2 Molten glass + 2 Marble blocks).

Step 1: Get Gold Leaves

Buy Gold leaves from the Grand Exchange (~200k GP each). You need 5 total. Alternatively, craft them yourself using Gold leaf (Smithing 70 + furnace) — but this is slower and requires more GP investment.

Step 2: Build the Altar

Enter Building Mode → click the Altar space in your Chapel → select "Gilded Altar" → confirm. The altar is now active!

⑪ Using Incense Burners (+50% More XP!)

Build 2 Incense Burners (Construction 60) on either side of your altar. Light them with Clean marigold (Farming 8+) or Marigold incenses (Herblore 33).

With both burners lit, your altar gives 525% XP per bone (350% × 1.5). That's the maximum possible prayer training speed in the game!

💡 Don't Have 75 Construction?

Go to World 330 (Rimmington house portal). Walk into any house with "[GA]" or "Gilded Altar" in the name. Right-click the altar → choose "Offer Bones". Most hosts accept tips (50k-100k GP) but many let you use it for free. This is how 90% of players get 99 Prayer!

⑥ Workshop — Craft Your Own Gear

The Workshop room lets you repair Barrows equipment, craft ammo, and make battlestaves. It's not essential for beginners, but very useful once you start doing Barrows runs or ranged training.

⑫ 3 Useful Workshop Stations

🔨 Repair Bench (Construction 15+)

Repair Barrows equipment (Ahrim's, Dharok's, etc.) for 15,000 GP per item instead of paying NPCs 75,000+ GP. Huge long-term savings!

🏹 Ammo Mould (Construction 20+)

Craft your own bronze/siron/steel arrows (Smithing required). Not super useful for money, but good for Ironman mode self-sufficiency.

🪄 Battlestaff Crafting (Construction 40+)

Make battlestaves from scratch (requires 66 Crafting). This is a popular money-making method for mid-level players — you can profit ~200k GP/hour making Air/Steam/Volatile battlestaves.

⑬ Building a Workshop

Cost: ~80,000 GP (Oak planks + steel nails).

Requirement: Construction level 15 (for basic Workshop).

You don't need a fancy workshop early on. Focus on Portal Chamber + Chapel first. Add a Workshop later when you start using Barrows gear regularly.

⑦ House Layout Tips for Beginners

Your house layout determines how fast you can move between rooms. A good layout saves time every single visit.

⑭ Layout Rule #1: Keep Portals Close

Place your Portal Chamber directly adjacent to your entrance point (the room you spawn in when teleporting home). This way you can step out of your spawn room and immediately click a portal.

⑮ Layout Rule #2: Chapel Should Be 1 Room Away

You'll visit your Gilded Altar a lot (every time you train Prayer). Make sure the Chapel is only 1 room away from your entrance. Don't make yourself run through 3 rooms just to offer bones.

⑯ Layout Rule #3: Use a Cost-Effective Design

🏆 Recommended Beginner Layout (Construction 50-74)

  • Entrance (spawn): Ground floor, center of house
  • Portal Chamber: Connected to Entrance (1 step)
  • Chapel: Connected to Entrance (1 step, opposite side from Portal)
  • Workshop: Connected to Chapel (for repair trips)
  • Storage Room: Optional, for extra bank space (Construction 23+)

⑰ Moving Rooms Around

In Building Mode, you can remove and rebuild rooms for free (you get all planks back). Experiment with layouts! There's no penalty for rearranging. As you level Construction, you'll unlock more rooms — just keep the important ones (Portal, Chapel) close to your entrance.

💡 Servants (Construction 30+)

Once you have a Servant's Quarter (Construction 30+), you can hire a servant to help with tasks. The Gardener (5k GP) can treat farming patches for you. The Demon butler (10k GP) can bring items from the bank and take items to the bank (very useful for construction training).

⑧ POH FAQ — Common Beginner Questions

What's the minimum GP I need to start a useful house?

About 500,000 GP. This gets you a Portal Chamber (50k-150k) + Chapel with Oak Altar (200k) + basic decorations. You can add a Gilded Altar later when you have more GP saved up.

Can I use other players' houses instead of building my own?

Yes! Absolutely. In fact, most beginners should use other players' houses. Go to World 330 (Rimmington portal). Find a house with "Gilded Altar" or "[GA]" in the name. Walk in, use the altar, and tip the host if they ask. This saves you millions of GP while you're still learning the game.

How do I train Construction efficiently?

The most cost-effective method is Oak larders (Construction 33-50) → Teak larders (50-74) → Mahogany larders (74-99). Using the Demon butler to unnote logs saves massive time. Total cost to 99: ~170M GP using Mahogany planks.

My portals aren't working — what's wrong?

You need to activate each portal by using the required runes on it while in Building Mode. Just building the portal frame isn't enough! Also, make sure you're using the correct rune combinations (different portals need different runes).

Can my house get destroyed or deleted?

No. Your house is permanent. Even if you never log in for years, it stays exactly as you left it. The only thing that expires is your servant contract (you need to re-hire them after 24 hours of gameplay).
