Chapter1: Google Play Store Method (Android) — Step by Step

The Google Play Store method is the most common way Android players purchase OSRS membership. This method charges your Google Play balance or a linked payment method (credit card, PayPal, etc.).

Prerequisites

  • You must have the OSRS Mobile app installed from the Google Play Store (not a side-loaded APK).
  • You must be logged into the same Google account on your device that you want to use for payment.
  • Your Google Play account must be in a supported region (most regions are supported; restricted regions include some countries with Google Play limitations).
  • You must have a valid payment method linked to your Google Play account (credit/debit card, PayPal, Google Play gift card balance, etc.).

Step-by-Step: Buying Membership on Android

  1. Open the OSRS Mobile app and log into your account.
  2. Tap the side panel button (three horizontal lines in the top-left corner of the screen). This opens the main navigation menu.
  3. Look for the "Membership" or "Upgrade to Member" button. In the 2026 version of the app, this is typically near the top of the side panel, below your username. It may also appear as a green button that says "Get Membership."
  4. If you do not see the button in the side panel, tap "Settings" in the side panel, then look for a "Manage Membership" or "Purchase Membership" option within the Settings menu.
  5. Tap the membership button. A window will appear showing the available membership options (3-day, 1-month, 3-month, 12-month).
  6. Select your desired membership duration. The price in your local currency will be displayed next to each option.
  7. Confirm the purchase. Google Play will handle the payment using your default payment method. You may be asked to confirm your Google account password or use biometric authentication (fingerprint/face unlock).
  8. Wait for the confirmation. Once the payment is processed, you will see a confirmation message. Your membership is now active! You can verify by logging into the game — your account will have full member access.

Using Google Play Gift Cards

If you prefer not to link a credit card, you can use Google Play gift cards:

  1. Buy a Google Play gift card from a retail store or online.
  2. Open the Google Play Store app (not OSRS — the actual Play Store app).
  3. Tap your profile picture in the top-right corner.
  4. Select "Redeem gift code" and enter the code from your gift card.
  5. The balance will be added to your Google Play account. When you purchase OSRS membership, Google Play will use this balance automatically.
💡 Android Tip: If the membership button is missing, check if your Google Play Store country is set correctly. Open the Play Store app, tap your profile, go to Settings > General > Account and device preferences. Make sure your device's country matches your actual location. An incorrect country setting can hide in-app purchases.

Chapter2: App Store Method (iOS) — Step by Step

iOS players use the Apple App Store to purchase OSRS membership. The process is similar to Android but uses Apple's payment system (Apple ID balance, credit card, or iTunes gift card).

Prerequisites

  • You must have the OSRS Mobile app installed from the Apple App Store.
  • You must be signed into an Apple ID with a valid payment method.
  • Your Apple ID region must support in-app purchases for OSRS.
  • Family Sharing note: If you use Family Sharing, the purchase will be charged to the organizer's payment method unless you have your own Apple ID balance.

Step-by-Step: Buying Membership on iOS

  1. Open the OSRS Mobile app on your iPhone or iPad and log into your account.
  2. Tap the side panel button (three horizontal lines in the top-left corner).
  3. Look for "Membership" or "Upgrade to Member." On iOS, this is usually a green button in the side panel.
  4. If the button is not visible, go to "Settings" in the side panel and look for a membership option there.
  5. Tap the membership button. A sheet will slide up from the bottom showing membership durations and prices.
  6. Select your membership duration. Apple will display the price in your local currency.
  7. Authenticate the purchase. You will be asked to confirm using Face ID, Touch ID, or your Apple ID password.
  8. Wait for confirmation. Once processed, you will see a success message. Your membership is active immediately.

Using an iTunes Gift Card (Popular iOS Method)

Many iOS players use iTunes gift cards to pay for OSRS membership. Here is how:

  1. Buy an iTunes & App Store gift card from a retail store, supermarket, or Apple's website.
  2. Open the App Store on your iOS device.
  3. Tap your profile picture (or the circle with your initials) in the top-right corner.
  4. Tap "Redeem Gift Card or Code."
  5. Use your camera to scan the gift card code, or type it manually.
  6. The balance is added to your Apple ID. When you buy OSRS membership, Apple will deduct from this balance first.

Chapter3: Bond Purchase via Grand Exchange (Alternative Method)

If you cannot or do not want to use Google Play or the App Store, you can purchase a Bond using in-game GP and redeem it for membership. This method is popular among players who have accumulated GP and want to avoid spending real money.

What Is a Bond?

A Bond is an in-game item that can be redeemed for 14 days of membership, or exchanged for 280 RuneCoins (for cosmetics), or used to transfer GP between accounts (within certain limits). Bonds are tradeable and can be bought from or sold to other players via the Grand Exchange.

Step-by-Step: Buying a Bond with GP

  1. Accumulate GP. As of June 2026, a Bond costs approximately 14M-16M GP on the Grand Exchange. The price fluctuates daily based on supply and demand.
  2. Go to the Grand Exchange. Travel to Edgeville (use a teleport tab or walk from Varrock) and enter the Grand Exchange area.
  3. Talk to a Grand Exchange clerk. Right-click a clerk and select "Exchange."
  4. Search for "Bond." Type "Bond" in the search bar. The Bond item will appear.
  5. Set your buy offer. Enter the amount you are willing to pay (check the current GE price first by hovering over the Bond in the list). Set the quantity to 1.
  6. Collect your Bond. Once your offer is fulfilled (this can take seconds or hours depending on the price), collect the Bond from the clerk.
  7. Redeem the Bond for membership. Right-click the Bond in your inventory and select "Redeem." Choose "Redeem for Membership" from the options. This will add 14 days of membership to your account.
  8. Verify activation. Log into the game — you now have member access. The 14 days begin immediately upon redemption.

Bond Price History & GP Farming Tips

Bonds cost real money when bought from Jagex (~ 14 USD), and their GE price reflects that. Here are some efficient ways to farm the GP needed for a Bond:

  • Slayer tasks (70+ combat): 200K-400K GP per hour.
  • Vorkath (higher level): 1.5M-2M GP per hour.
  • Zulrah (snake boss): 1M-1.5M GP per hour.
  • Herb runs (Farming): ~100K-200K GP per run, multiple runs per day.
  • Birdhouse runs (Hunter): ~50K-100K GP per run, passive while doing other things.

At 1M GP per hour, you need about 14-16 hours of gameplay to afford one Bond. For many players, this is an enjoyable way to "earn" their membership through gameplay rather than spending real money.

Redeeming a Bond on Mobile

If you bought a Bond on PC but want to redeem it on mobile (or vice versa), do not worry — Bonds are in your account's inventory regardless of where you log in. Simply log into OSRS Mobile, open your inventory, right-click the Bond, and redeem it. The membership will be added to your account instantly and will work on all platforms.

💡 Bond Tip: If the GE price of Bonds is too high for your budget, consider waiting for a price dip. Bond prices tend to drop slightly after major GP-inflation events (like new money-making methods being discovered) and rise when many players are buying membership for upcoming content releases. Check the GE price trend on the OSRS wiki before buying.

Chapter4: Troubleshooting — Common Issues & Fixes

Even with clear instructions, things can go wrong. This section covers the most common problems mobile players face when trying to purchase OSRS membership, along with step-by-step fixes.

Problem1: "The Marketplace/Get Membership Button Is Missing"

Possible CauseHow to Fix
App needs updatingGo to Google Play Store / App Store, search for OSRS, and tap "Update" if available.
Wrong region settingCheck your device's region in Settings. In-app purchases may be blocked in your current region.
Already a memberCheck your account status at oldschool.runescape.com. If you are already a member, the button may be hidden.
Temporary app bugForce-close the app and restart it. On Android: swipe up from bottom and close the app. On iOS: swipe up from bottom (or double-tap home button) and close the app.
Using a modified APKIf you sideloaded the app, download the official version from the Play Store / App Store. Modified apps often break in-app purchase functionality.

Problem2: "Payment Failed" or "Payment Was Declined"

  • Check your card details: Make sure the card number, expiry date, and CVV are correct. Also verify the billing address matches what your bank has on file.
  • Check your card balance: Ensure you have sufficient funds for the membership purchase.
  • Contact your bank: Some banks block international or digital purchases automatically. Call your bank and ask them to authorize the payment to "Jagex Ltd" or "Google/Apple" (depending on the platform).
  • Try a different payment method: If your card keeps failing, try PayPal, a different card, or a gift card balance.
  • Wait and retry: Sometimes Google Play or App Store has temporary server issues. Wait 30 minutes and try again.

Problem3: "I Paid but Membership Is Not Showing"

  1. Restart the app completely. Force-close OSRS Mobile and reopen it. Membership status syncs when you log in.
  2. Check your email. Look for a receipt from Google Play or Apple. If you have a receipt, your payment went through — the membership just has not synced yet.
  3. Log into the OSRS website. Go to oldschool.runescape.com and log in. Check your account status there. If the website shows active membership but the app does not, it is an app sync issue — try reinstalling the app.
  4. Wait up to 30 minutes. Rarely, there is a delay between payment processing and membership activation. If 30 minutes pass and you still have no membership, contact Jagex Support with your payment receipt.

Problem4: "I Was Charged Twice"

If you see two charges for the same membership purchase:

  • Check if you accidentally bought membership for two different accounts (it happens more often than you think).
  • If it is a genuine double charge, contact Jagex Support at support.runescape.com. Provide both transaction IDs and your account name. Jagex typically refunds duplicate charges within 3-5 business days.

Problem5: "My Subscription Won't Cancel"

OSRS membership purchased through Google Play or App Store is a recurring subscription. To cancel:

  • Android: Open Google Play Store > tap profile > Payments & subscriptions > Subscriptions > find OSRS > Cancel.
  • iOS: Open Settings > tap your Apple ID (your name) > Subscriptions > find OSRS > Cancel.
  • Important: Cancelling stops future charges but does NOT refund the current period. You keep membership until the period you already paid for expires.

Chapter6: Cross-Platform Membership — Buy Anywhere, Play Everywhere

A common concern among mobile players is whether membership purchased on one platform works on another. The answer is a clear YES — with one important caveat about recurring subscriptions.

How Cross-Platform Membership Works

OSRS membership is tied to your Jagex account, not the device or platform you used to purchase it. This means:

  • If you buy membership on Android (Google Play), it works immediately on PC, iOS, and any other device where you log into the same account.
  • If you buy membership on iOS (App Store), it also works on PC, Android, and all other devices.
  • If you buy membership on the OSRS website (using a credit card directly with Jagex), it works on all platforms, including mobile.
  • If you redeem a Bond on any platform, the membership works on all platforms.

The Recurring Subscription Caveat

While membership itself is cross-platform, recurring subscriptions are managed by the platform you used to purchase them:

  • A Google Play subscription can only be cancelled or modified through Google Play.
  • An App Store subscription can only be cancelled or modified through Apple's subscription settings.
  • A website-purchased subscription can be managed at oldschool.runescape.com/store.

This means if you buy a 3-month recurring subscription on Android, and then switch to iPhone, you still need to use Google Play (on any device) or the web to cancel that subscription. You cannot manage a Google Play subscription through Apple, or vice versa.

Best Practice for Multi-Device Players

If you play on both mobile and PC regularly, the recommended approach is:

  1. Buy membership through the OSRS website (oldschool.runescape.com/store) using a credit card or PayPal directly with Jagex.
  2. This avoids the 30% platform fee (Google/Apple), meaning Jagex gets more of your money — and sometimes you get better deals.
  3. You can manage the subscription from any device with a web browser.
  4. Your membership works seamlessly on mobile and PC without any extra steps.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I use an iTunes gift card to buy OSRS membership on iOS?

A: Yes! Redeem the iTunes gift card to your Apple ID balance, then use that balance to purchase membership through the App Store. Open the App Store, tap your profile, select "Redeem Gift Card or Code," enter the code, and the balance will be available for your next OSRS membership purchase.

Q: Why did the Marketplace button disappear from my OSRS Mobile app?

A: Several possible reasons: (1) You updated the app and the UI changed — check the side panel or Settings menu. (2) Your device region does not support in-app purchases. (3) You already have active membership. (4) A temporary bug — restart the app. If the button is truly gone, use Google Play Store / App Store directly, or buy a Bond from the Grand Exchange.

Q: How do I cancel my OSRS Mobile membership?

A: Android — Google Play Store > profile > Payments & subscriptions > Subscriptions > OSRS > Cancel. iOS — Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions > OSRS > Cancel. Cancelling stops future charges but does not refund the current period. You keep membership until the paid period ends.

Q: Can I buy membership on mobile and use it on PC?

A: Absolutely! Membership is tied to your Jagex account, not the device. Buy on mobile, play on PC — and vice versa. Your membership status syncs across all platforms automatically. This is one of the best features of OSRS's cross-platform support.

Q: Is buying a Bond with GP cheaper than buying membership with real money?

A: It depends on your ability to earn GP vs. real money. A Bond costs ~14.5M GP and gives 14 days of membership. If you can farm 14.5M GP faster than you can earn $11 USD at your job, Bonds are effectively free. For most players with a job, real-money membership is simpler and cheaper.

Final Tips

  • Use Bonds if you have excess GP. If you have 30M+ GP sitting in the bank and are not sure what to spend it on, convert some of it to Bonds. Playing OSRS "for free" by farming GP is a rewarding achievement and lets you enjoy the game without spending real money.
  • Check for seasonal discounts before buying. Jagex typically offers membership discounts during Black Friday, summer sales, and end-of-year holidays. If your membership expires in November, wait for Black Friday — you might save 20-30%.
  • Buy through the website for better value. Google and Apple take a 30% cut of all in-app purchases. When you buy through oldschool.runescape.com/store, more of your money goes to Jagex (supporting the game's development) and you sometimes get promotional bonuses.
  • Set a calendar reminder to cancel if you do not want to auto-renew. It is easy to forget about recurring subscriptions. If you only want one month, cancel immediately after purchase. You will still get the full month you paid for, but will not be charged again.
  • Consider the 12-month plan if you play regularly. At $0.33 per day, the annual plan is an excellent value. Even if you take a break from OSRS for a month or two, the annual plan still works out cheaper than month-to-month over the course of a year.
  • Keep your payment method up to date. Nothing is more frustrating than your membership expiring mid-session because your credit card expired. Check your payment method expiry dates and update them before they expire.
  • Contact Jagex Support if anything goes wrong. Jagex customer support is generally helpful and responsive. If you have a billing issue, go to support.runescape.com, log in, and submit a ticket. Include your transaction ID and any receipts you have.
