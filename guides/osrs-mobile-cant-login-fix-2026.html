OSRS Mobile Can't Log In? Fix It After the July 2026 Jagex PSA

After the July 7, 2026 Jagex PSA and server maintenance, thousands of players hit "Failed to log in", an endless loading spinner, or "Invalid credentials" on the OSRS Mobile app — even with the right password. This is one unified fix flow that covers every cause, in the right order, so you can get back in fast.

📅 Stage 0 · Beginner Troubleshooting — Updated July 2026 · 11 min read
🎓 Level: Beginner

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Don't panic & don't spam login — most July 2026 failures were Jagex-side maintenance, now fixed. Confirm status first.
  • 📌 #1 cause: wrong account type — Steam, Google (Android), and Apple (iOS) logins each need their own button. Email+password won't work for them.
  • 📌 2FA/Authenticator: the maintenance force-signed many players out; you need your 6-digit code or backup codes, not your password.
  • 📌 Stuck token? Force-stop + clear cache (Android) or Offload (iOS) to wipe cached credentials without losing data.
  • 📌 Still stuck? Clean reinstall, then log in through the Jagex Launcher flow with your verified Jagex account.

1. 🔍 Is It You or Jagex? Check Server Status First

On July 7, 2026, Jagex published a PSA about planned server maintenance that briefly disrupted login across both the desktop client and the OSRS Mobile app. During that window, players saw "Failed to log in", a spinner that never finished, or a sudden "You have been disconnected" the moment they tapped Play. None of those were account problems — the login servers were simply not accepting sessions.

Before you change a single setting, confirm whether the problem is on Jagex's side. This takes 30 seconds and saves you from "fixing" something that isn't broken:

Step 1 — Check the official status channels

  • Open @JagexSupport on X (Twitter) — outages and maintenance windows are posted here first.
  • Visit the official Old School RuneScape status / news page for any "Login issues" banner.
  • Ask in a Discord or look at r/2007scape — if hundreds of players can't log in at the same time, it's Jagex, not you.

Step 2 — Rule out your own connection

  • Switch from mobile data to Wi-Fi (or vice-versa) and retry once.
  • Open any website in your phone's browser. No internet at all? Fix the connection first.
  • Try logging into the desktop client or the official website with the same account. If desktop also fails, it's account/server side, not the app.
💡 The 10-Minute Rule

If status channels confirm maintenance, just wait. Hammering the login button does not speed things up and can temporarily rate-limit your account. Step away for 10–15 minutes, then come back and continue this flow from Step 2 (account type).

2. 🔑 Wrong Account Type — Steam vs Google vs Apple

This is the single most common reason mobile players who have the right password still can't get in. Old School RuneScape accounts are not all "email + password" accounts. Depending on how you first created or linked your account, you must use a specific login button — and the wrong one always returns "Invalid credentials" or "Failed to log in".

🎯 The Golden Rule of Mobile Login

You must log in on mobile the same way your account was created. If you made it through Steam, you sign in with Steam. Through an Android phone with Google, you use Google. On an iPhone with "Sign in with Apple", you use Apple. There is no universal password fallback for these linked accounts on mobile.

③ How to tell which one you are

  • On PC right now? However you log into the desktop client or Jagex Launcher is exactly how you log into mobile. Mirror it.
  • Not sure if you have a Jagex account? Go to the official Jagex account site and try "Sign in". If it recognizes your email and asks for a password + (possibly) 2FA, you're a Jagex-account user.
  • Bought membership through Steam/iOS/Google Play? That usually means your character is linked to that platform — use that button.
⚠️ Common Mix-Up

Many players who normally play on PC via the Jagex Launcher assume "my account is email + password" — and they're right. But if you originally migrated from a Steam or Apple login, the mobile app may still present those SSO buttons as the only working route. When in doubt, try the SSO button that matches where you bought or created the account.

3. 🛡️ Authenticator / 2FA Issues (Lost Codes, New Phone)

The July 2026 maintenance force-signed a large number of sessions out at once. When you next opened OSRS Mobile, the app asked for your 6-digit Authenticator code — not your password. If you couldn't produce it, you were locked out even though your credentials were correct.

④ You have your Authenticator app

  • Open your authenticator app (Google Authenticator, Authy, FreeOTP, or similar) and find the entry for your Jagex account.
  • Enter the current 6-digit code. Codes rotate every 30 seconds — type the one on screen now, not the previous one.
  • If the code is "always wrong", your phone's clock may be off. Enable automatic date & time; an unsynced clock breaks TOTP codes.

⑤ You lost your phone / can't generate codes

Option A — Use backup codes

When you first enabled 2FA, Jagex gave you one-time backup codes. Find that saved list (notes app, password manager, printed copy). Each code works once and bypasses the authenticator app.

Option B — Account recovery

No backup codes? Start the official Jagex account recovery process from a browser. You'll verify ownership (creation details, past transactions, linked emails). This can take time but is the only route if both the app and backup codes are gone.

🚨 Never Share Your Code

Jagex support will never ask for your authenticator code or password. Anyone who DMs you "to verify your account" during an outage is a scammer. Report and block them. See our Account Security Guide for the full anti-phishing checklist.

4. 🧹 Cached / Stuck Credentials — Force Sign-Out

Even after Jagex fixed the servers, the app can hold a stale login token from before the maintenance. That dead token keeps the app stuck on "Loading" or loops you back to the login screen. You don't need to delete the app — just clear the cached session.

⑥ Android — Force stop + clear cache

  1. Open Settings → Apps → Old School RuneScape.
  2. Tap Force Stop (this kills the stuck process).
  3. Tap Storage → Clear Cache. (Use Clear Data only if cache alone doesn't work — it removes locally saved settings but not your account.)
  4. Reopen the app and log in fresh.

⑦ iPhone / iPad — Offload, don't delete your data

  1. Open Settings → General → iPhone Storage → Old School RuneScape.
  2. Tap Offload App. This removes the app binary but keeps your documents & data (your cached login is wiped).
  3. Reinstall from the App Store and open it.
  4. If offload isn't available, delete and reinstall (your character lives on Jagex's servers, not your phone, so nothing is lost).
💡 Why this fixes the "spinner of death"

The cached token is like an expired ticket. The app keeps presenting it, the server rejects it, and you loop forever. Clearing cache throws the bad ticket away so the app is forced to request a brand-new session — which is exactly what the post-maintenance login needs.

5. ♻️ Clean Reinstall (Android & iOS)

If clearing cache didn't help, do a full clean reinstall. This removes any corrupted local files from the interrupted July maintenance update.

🤖 Android

  • Long-press the OSRS icon → Uninstall (or Settings → Apps → Uninstall).
  • Restart your phone once.
  • Open the Google Play Store, search "Old School RuneScape", and reinstall. Avoid third-party APK mirrors.
  • Open the app and log in with the correct account-type button from Section 2.

🍎 iOS (iPhone / iPad)

  • Long-press the OSRS icon on the home screen → Remove App → Delete App.
  • Restart the device.
  • Reinstall from the App Store (not a backup restore, which can re-copy the bad cache).
  • Log in with the matching SSO or email method.
🔋 Don't Forget the Basics

After reinstall, make sure the app has background data / cellular permission enabled and that your OS is reasonably up to date. An outdated OS version can silently break the secure login handshake even with a fresh install.

6. 💻 Jagex Launcher Login on Mobile (2026 Change)

In 2026 Jagex expanded how accounts are managed, and a growing number of players now use a Jagex account governed by the Jagex Launcher ecosystem. If your character was migrated or created under a Jagex account, the most reliable mobile login is to first confirm the credentials on the web, then reuse them in the app.

Step 1 — Verify on the web

On a browser, go to the official Jagex account sign-in page and log in with your email + password + 2FA. If this works, your credentials are correct and the mobile failure is almost certainly the account-type button (Section 2) or a cached token (Section 4).

Step 2 — Re-link if prompted

Some July 2026 accounts were asked to re-link their platform (Steam/Google/Apple) to the Jagex account during the maintenance cleanup. If mobile says "account not linked", complete the link once on the website, then retry on mobile.

Step 3 — Use the same email on mobile

Once verified, open OSRS Mobile and choose the email + password route (not an SSO button) if your account is a full Jagex account. The July maintenance reset many sessions, so expect to re-enter your 2FA code one time — that's normal, not a hack.

💡 Still "Invalid credentials" after all this?

Triple-check for typos: mobile keyboards sometimes auto-capitalize the first letter or add a trailing space. Type your email in all lowercase and paste or carefully type the password. If it still fails on both web and mobile, your account may be locked or recovered — contact official Jagex Support with your character name and creation details.

8. ❓ FAQ — OSRS Mobile Login

Why did OSRS Mobile suddenly log me out after July 7, 2026?

Jagex's July 2026 PSA covered server maintenance that force-expired a large batch of active login sessions for security and stability. Being signed out was expected behavior, not a ban or a hack. Just sign back in using the correct account-type button and, if prompted, your 2FA code.

I play on Steam on PC — how do I log into mobile?

Tap the "Steam" button on the mobile login screen, not the email/password fields. Your Steam-linked character has no usable email+password login on mobile. If you'd rather use email+password, first convert/link the account to a Jagex account on the website, then use that email on mobile.

My authenticator codes are "always wrong" — what's happening?

Nine times out of ten your phone clock is out of sync. Authenticator codes are time-based; a wrong clock makes every code invalid. Turn on automatic date & time in your phone settings, wait 30 seconds, and retry. If that fails, use a one-time backup code or start account recovery.

Will a clean reinstall delete my character or my bank?

No. Your character, levels, and bank are stored on Jagex's servers, never on your phone. Uninstalling and reinstalling OSRS Mobile only removes the app and its local cache. You'll log back into the exact same account afterward.

I've tried everything and still can't log in — what now?

Confirm you can log in on the official website with the same method. If web works but mobile doesn't, it's app-side — try the clean reinstall (§5). If neither works and you have no 2FA access, use the official Jagex account recovery form with your character name and original creation details, then contact Jagex Support.
