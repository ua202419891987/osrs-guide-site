1. Combat Basics — Understanding the Combat Triangle

OSRS uses a combat triangle system: Melee beats Ranged, Ranged beats Magic, Magic beats Melee. Each style has strengths and weaknesses against the others. As a beginner, understanding this early saves you hours of frustration.

StyleStrong AgainstWeak AgainstBest For
⚔️ MeleeRangedMagicHigh DPS up close, cheapest training
🏹 RangedMagicMeleeSafe spotting, multi-combat, range-based bosses
✨ MagicMeleeRangedMagic-based bosses, utility (teleports, alchemy)

② How Combat Level Is Calculated

Your Combat Level is calculated from: Attack + Strength + Defence + (Hitpoints ÷ 2) + (Prayer ÷ 2) + (Ranged ÷ 2) + (Magic ÷ 2), all divided by 4. This means Ranged and Magic contribute half as much per level compared to melee stats. Plan your build accordingly.

🎯 Recommended Beginner Combat Path (Priority Order)

  1. Melee first (Levels 1–60/70) — Cheapest, simplest, prepares you for all quests
  2. Ranged (Levels 1–60) — Safe, powerful for Slayer and bosses
  3. Magic (Levels 1–55+) — High Alchemy for GP, teleports for mobility, required for quests
  4. Slayer (Combat 60+) — Combines all combat styles, best long-term training method
⚔️ The Combat Style Tab — This Changes Everything

When you have a weapon equipped, open your Combat Options tab (sword icon in the game interface). You can choose your attack style:

  • Aggressive — Trains Strength (hit harder, higher max hit)
  • Accurate — Trains Attack (more accurate, unlocks better weapons)
  • Defensive — Trains Defence (take less damage, wear better armor)
  • Controlled — Trains Attack + Strength + Defence slowly (NOT recommended for beginners)

Rule of thumb: Set to Aggressive until Strength is 1 level above Attack, then switch to Accurate to catch up. Never use Controlled early on.

③ Understanding Attack Speed (Ticks)

Every weapon in OSRS has an attack speed measured in game ticks (0.6 seconds per tick). Faster weapons = more XP per hour but lower damage per hit. Slower weapons = higher damage but slower XP.

2. Core Training Principles Every Beginner Must Know

④ Principle 1: Attack > Strength > Defence Priority

For melee training, the most efficient order is: Attack to 60 → Strength to 70+ → Defence to 40–70. Why? Attack 60 unlocks the Dragon Scimitar (best melee weapon until 70 Attack). Strength gives you higher max hit (more damage per swing). Defence is least impactful for DPS — only raise it when you need to wear specific armor.

⑤ Principle 2: Safe-Spotting = Free Training

Many monsters can be killed without taking any damage by standing behind a tree, rock, or fence that the monster can't path around. This is called "safe-spotting" and it means you don't need food. Read our Safe Spots Guide for exact locations.

⑥ Principle 3: Aggressive Monsters Save you Time

Aggressive monsters (marked with a yellow skull icon in the combat interface) will automatically attack you when you're nearby. This means you can AFK (away from keyboard) while they come to you. Non-aggressive monsters require you to click on them every few seconds.

⑦ Principle 4: Quests Give Free Combat XP

Before grinding combat, complete these quests for free XP:

Some activities give bonus XP: Fighting in Multi-combat areas (marked by a blue skull icon) lets you hit multiple monsters at once with Area-of-Effect attacks (Magic Burst/Barrage, or Chinchompas for Ranged). This can triple your XP rates.

3. Melee Training: Complete 1–70 Step-by-Step Route

This is the most detailed section. Follow this exact route and you'll go from Level 1 to 70 Melee in the fastest, cheapest way possible.

⑧ Step 1: Levels 1–10 (Chickens & Cows)

Where: Chicken coop north of Lumbridge (north exit, cross the bridge, go north).

What to do: Equip your Bronze sword (from Tutorial Island). Set combat mode to Aggressive. Kill chickens. They die in 1–2 hits. Pick up feathers — they sell for ~5 GP each at the Grand Exchange.

When you reach Combat Level 5: Cows east of Lumbridge become available. Cows have more HP and drop cowhides (200 GP each at GE). Switch to cows at Level 10.

Time: ~15 minutes to reach Level 10 in all melee stats.

Loot to pick up: Feathers, cowhides (bank them or sell at GE), bones (bury for Prayer XP or save for later).

⑨ Step 2: Levels 10–20 (Al-Kharid Warriors — F2P Option)

Where: Al-Kharid Palace (south of Al-Kharid bank, go through the palace gates).

What to do: Equip Iron/Steel armor (buy from Grand Exchange for ~5k GP total). Bring 10–15 Salmon (buy from GE ~100 GP each). Attack Al-Kharid Warriors. They have low Defence and decent HP.

Members Option: If you're a member, skip Al-Kharid and go to Barbarians (Barbarian Village, west of Varrock) — they're aggressive, you don't need food, and they're right next to a respawn point.

Time: ~30 minutes from Level 10 to 20.

⑩ Step 3: Levels 20–40 (Barbarians or Stronghold of Security)

Where: Barbarian Village (use Varrock teleport, walk west).

What to do: Kill Barbarians. They're aggressive (will come to you), have low Defence, and drop herbs (valuable). Stronghold of Security is in the same area — complete all 4 floors for free Armor, GP, and Emote rewards. The Stronghold alone can get you from Level 20 to 30.

Gear at Level 20: Mithril armor (buy from GE, ~10k GP set). Weapon: Steel scimitar or better.

Time: ~1 hour from Level 20 to 40.

Step 4: Levels 40–60 (Flesh Crawlers or Hill Giants)

Option A — Flesh Crawlers (F2P/P2P): Stronghold of Security Floor 2. They are aggressive, cluster in groups, and have low Defence. Great AFK spot. Bring 5–10 Lobsters just in case.

Option B — Hill Giants (Members): Edgeville Dungeon (requires 22 GP to enter, or use a Brass Key). They drop Big Bones (burry for Prayer XP) and have decent HP. Safe-spot them behind the dungeon fence.

Key Upgrade at Level 40 Attack: Buy a Rune Scimitar (~20k GP at GE). This is a massive DPS upgrade. Don't skip this.

Time: ~2–3 hours from Level 40 to 60.

Step 5: Levels 60–70 (Experiments or Rock Crabs)

Option A — Experiments (Members, HIGHLY RECOMMENDED): Near Canifis (after Creature of Fenkenstrain quest, which has no requirements). Experiments have very high HP, never move, and deal 0 damage if you stand in the right corner. Perfect for AFK training. XP rate: 40,000–60,000 XP/hr.

How to get there: Teleport to Canifis (Fairy Ring code: CKS, or walk from Port Phasmatys after Ghosts Ahoy quest). Talk to Dr. Fenkenstrain to start the quest (can be completed in 5 minutes). Then go to the Experiment cave south of the castle.

Option B — Rock Crabs (Members): Rellekka (Fairy Ring code: AJR). Crabs have high HP, are aggressive, and regenerate HP when you move away. Perfect for AFK. Reset aggression by moving 5+ tiles away. XP rate: 35,000–50,000 XP/hr.

Time: ~3–5 hours from Level 60 to 70.

4. Melee Training: 70–99 Efficient Routes

Once you reach Level 70, you have two choices: continue manual training, or start Slayer (recommended). Slayer gives combat XP while also training the Slayer skill, which unlocks boss access and unique drops.

Method A: Slayer (Best Overall, 70–99)

Assign a Slayer master (Turael for easy tasks, Mazchna for medium, Konar for harder). Each task gives a specific monster to kill. This is the most engaging way to reach 99 because you're always killing different monsters and making GP from drops.

Best Slayer monsters for Melee XP (70–99): Gargoyles (75 Slayer), Nechryaels (80 Slayer), Abyssal Demons (85 Slayer), Armarx (95 Slayer).

Method B: Nightmare Zone (NMZ) — AFK 99

NMZ is a P2P mini-game where you fight bosses from quests in a dream world. With Dragon Scimitar + Absorbtion potions, you can AFK for 20-minute intervals. XP rate: 60,000–80,000 XP/hr. Requires completion of at least 5 quests that have bosses (Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village, etc.).

Method C: Manual Grinding (Fastest XP)

If you just want 99 fast and don't care about GP or variety:

  • Levels 70–80: Ammonite Crabs (Fossil Island) — 50,000–70,000 XP/hr, fully AFK
  • Levels 80–90: Sand Crabs (Hosidius) or Monkey Guards (Ape Atoll, 65+ Defence) — 60,000–80,000 XP/hr
  • Levels 90–99: NMZ or Slayer — Slayer is more profitable

Total time to 99 Melee from 70: ~150–200 hours of active play (or ~300 hours AFK at crabs).

5. Ranged Training: Complete 1–99 Route

Levels 1–30: Chickens & Cows with Shortbow

Weapon: Shortbow (buy from Lumbridge General Store for 40 GP) + Bronze arrows (5 GP per 10 arrows).

Method: Stand outside the chicken coop fence (safe-spot). Shoot chickens. Pick up your arrows — they can be reused 4–5 times before breaking. At Level 20 Ranged, switch to Oak Shortbow + Iron arrows.

Time: ~30 minutes to Level 30 Ranged.

Levels 30–60: Rock Crabs (Members) or Ogress Warriors (F2P)

Members Method: Rock Crabs (Rellekka, Fairy Ring: AJR). Use Willow Shortbow + Steel arrows (or Maple Shortbow + Iron arrows at Level 30 Fletching). XP rate: 20,000–40,000 XP/hr.

F2P Method: Ogress Warriors (Corsair Cove, unlocked after Dragon Slayer I). They have high Ranged Defence but drop valuable loot. Use Crossbow + Iron bolts. XP rate: 15,000–25,000 XP/hr.

Key Upgrade at Level 40 Ranged: Rune Crossbow (~50k GP at GE) + Broad Bolts (~40k GP per 1000 bolts). Huge DPS upgrade.

Levels 60–70: Chinning or Slayer

Red Chinchompas (Level 45+ Ranged, 55+ Huning): Chinchompas are throwing explosives that hit multiple monsters in a 3x3 area. XP rate: 200,000–300,000 XP/hr (at Maniacal Monkeys or Chaos Druids). Cost: ~3–5M GP for 99 Ranged. Fastest but most expensive method.

Blowpipe (Level 60+ Ranged, 65+ Attack): Toxic Blowpipe is the best Ranged weapon in the game. Requires Zulrah scales + ammo. If you can't afford it yet, use Rune Crossbow + Dragon bolts.

Levels 70–99: Slayer with Ranged

Most Slayer tasks are best completed with Ranged because you can safe-spot and use Prayer protection. Tasks like Black Dragons, Demons, and Bosses are all Ranged-friendly.

Most efficient Ranged training post-70: Do Slayer tasks with Rune Crossbow (Dragon bolts) until you can afford Blowpipe or Armadyl Crossbow.

🏹 Essential: Get Ava's Device (Saves Thousands in Ammo)

Complete Animal Magnetism quest (requires 35 Woodcutting, 30 Crafting, 18 Slayer, 19 Ranged). This unlocks Ava's Attractor (Ranged 30+) or Ava's Accumulator (Ranged 50+). These devices automatically collect 80% of your spent arrows/bolts. Without Ava's, you'll spend 500k+ GP on ammo per hour of training. With Ava's, that drops to ~50k GP per hour.

How to start Animal Magnetism: Talk to Ava in Draynor Manor (south of Draynor Village). The quest takes ~30 minutes and has low requirements.

6. Magic Training: Complete 1–99 Route

Magic is unique because it's useful for combat, utility (teleports), and money-making (High Alchemy). Train it alongside your other combat styles.

Levels 1–7: Imp Catcher Quest (Instant XP)

Complete Imp Catcher quest (talk to Wizard Mizgog on the top floor of the Wizards' Tower, south of Draynor). Rewards: 875 Magic XP (instant Level 7) + a sapphire amulet. Takes 5 minutes.

Levels 7–25: Splashing (AFK Method)

What is Splashing? Equip cursed gear (cursed gorilla armor or just wear no armor) to reduce your Magic accuracy to -64 or lower. Then cast the lowest level spell (Wind Strike) on a monster with 0% magic defence (chickens, crabs). Because your accuracy is negative, you'll never hit, but you'll still get full Magic XP per cast. This is 100% AFK.

Cost: ~100k GP for runes to reach Level 25. XP rate: 5,000–8,000 XP/hr (fully AFK, you can watch a video while doing this).

Where: Rock Crabs (they have 0 Magic defence). Stand next to a crab and cast continuously.

Levels 25–55: Superheat Item (Smithing XP + Money)

Superheat Item (Level 25 Magic) lets you smelt bars from ore without a furnace. Requirements: 1 Nature rune + 1 Fire rune per cast. Use on Iron ore → Iron bars (profit ~100 GP per bar after rune costs).

Why this is amazing: You get Magic XP + Smithing XP + small profit simultaneously. 53 Magic lets you superheat Gold bars (requires 40 Smithing). Gold bars give 56.2 XP per cast and can be profitable with the Goldsmith's Gauntlets (unlocked from Family Crest quest).

XP rate: 20,000–30,000 XP/hr.

Level 55+: High Level Alchemy (Passive Income)

High Alchemy (Level 55 Magic) converts items into GP at 60% of their GE value. This is the single most important money-making spell in the game.

What to alch: Yew Longbows (buy uncut yew logs, fletch into longbows, alch them), Maple Longbows, or any item you craft/buy below 60% GE value.

Pro tip: Buy a Magic Shortbow (unstrung) from the GE for ~1,500 GP, string it (10 GP), alch it for ~3,900 GP. Profit + Magic XP.

XP rate: 60,000–80,000 XP/hr (if you have the GP to buy items to alch). In practice, most players alch while doing other activities (Slayer, questing) for passive XP.

Levels 55–99: Combat Magic (Bursts/Barrages)

Once you have 70+ Magic, the fastest way to 99 is bursting/barraging Slayer tasks in multi-combat areas:

  • Levels 55–70: Ice Burst (Slayer 65) at Dust Devils or Nechryaels — 50,000–80,000 XP/hr
  • Levels 70–94: Ice Barrage (Slayer 85) at Maniacal Monkeys or NMZ — 100,000–200,000 XP/hr
  • Levels 94–99: Ice Barrage at Zulrah (boss) or raids — 150,000–300,000 XP/hr

Cost warning: Bursting/Barraging costs 3–10M GP per 99. Most players combine this with Slayer so the drops offset the rune costs.

✨ Magic 55 Is the Biggest Magic Milestone

High Alchemy unlocks at Level 55. From this point on, you can pay for all your other skills just by alching items you get from Slayer or skilling. Rush to Magic 55 as fast as possible — it's a total game-changer for your economy.

10. When & How to Start Slayer — The Best Combat Training Method

Slayer is a skill where you kill specific monsters assigned by an NPC called a Slayer Master. It's the #1 best way to train combat past Level 60 because:

  • You get combat XP + Slayer XP simultaneously (two skills at once)
  • Unique monsters drop valuable loot (Dragon items, herbs, seeds, resources)
  • It's never boring — every task is a different monster in a different location
  • Unlocks boss access (KBD, Kalphite Queen, Zulrah, etc.) through higher-level tasks

How to Start Slayer (Step-by-Step)

  1. Become a member (Slayer is P2P only, except Turael who gives very easy F2P tasks)
  2. Teleport to Burthorpe (use Games Necklace or walk from Falador)
  3. Talk to Turael (south of Burthorpe, in a small house). He'll give you your first task.
  4. Kill the assigned monsters (Turael gives tasks like "10 Goblins" or "15 Cows" — very easy)
  5. Return to Turael after completing the task. He'll give you Slayer XP and a new task.
  6. After 10+ tasks from Turael, switch to Mazchna (Canifis, after Restless Ghost quest) for better XP.

📋 Slayer Unlocks You Need (Priority Order)

  1. Slayer Helmet (Level 50 Slayer, 400 GP): Combine all Slayer helmets. Gives +15% accuracy against assigned monsters. This is the single best Slayer item. Buy from any Slayer master after 50 Slayer points.
  2. Bigger and Badder (500 GP): Unlocks stronger versions of normal monsters as task alternatives. More XP and better drops.
  3. Ring of Wealth (Slayer 60+, 2,500 GP): Gives a chance to double loot from Slayer monsters.
  4. Stop the Wyverns (1,000 GP): lets you skip Fossil Island Wyverns (annoying task).

11. Advanced Combat Training Methods (70+ Combat)

Once you have 70+ in your chosen combat style, these methods become available and offer much higher XP rates.

Nightmare Zone (NMZ) — AFK Melee 60k–80k XP/hr

NMZ is a P2P mini-game. You fight quest bosses in a dream world. Requirements: Complete 5+ quests with bosses (Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village, Vampyre Slayer, etc.). Setup: Dragon Scimitar + Absorbtion potions (4) + Overload potions (optional). Method: Drink Absorbtion, stand in a corner, let monsters hit you (Absorbtion absorbs all damage), and AFK for 20 minutes. XP rate: 60,000–80,000 XP/hr.

Bursting/Barraging — Magic 100k–300k XP/hr

Use Ice Burst (Level 70 Magic, 65 Slayer) or Ice Barrage (Level 94 Magic, 85 Slayer) on monsters in multi-combat areas. Each cast hits all monsters in a 3x3 area. Best spots: Dust Devils (Slayer 65), Nechryaels (Slayer 80), Maniacal Monkeys (quest requirement). Cost: 3–10M GP per 99. Offset: Drops from tasks usually cover 50–80% of rune costs.

Chinning — Ranged 200k–300k XP/hr

Red Chinchompas (Level 45+ Ranged, 55+ Hunting) are throwing explosives. Stand in a multi-combat area (Chaos Druids, Maniacal Monkeys), throw a Chinchompa, and it explodes — hitting all monsters in range. Cost: ~5M GP for 99 Ranged. XP rate: Up to 300,000 XP/hr at Maniacal Monkeys. This is the fastest Ranged training in the game, but also the most expensive.

Cannon — +30% XP Boost (Costs GP)

Dwarf Multicannon (750k GP from Nulodion, near the Gnome Stronghold) automatically fires at monsters within range. It gives +30% XP boost to your normal training. However, cannonballs cost ~7 GP each, and the cannon uses 1 cannonball every 2 seconds. That's ~12,600 cannonballs per hour = ~90k GP/hour in ammo costs. Only use a cannon if you have GP to burn.

12. Combat Training FAQs — 15 Questions Every Beginner Asks

Should I train Attack, Strength, or Defence first?

Train Attack to 60 first (unlocks Dragon Scimitar, the best melee weapon until 70 Attack). Then Strength to 70+ (higher max hit = more damage per swing). Then Defence to 40–70 as needed for armor unlocks. Defence is the least impactful for DPS — prioritize Attack and Strength. A pure Strength build (1 Attack, 99 Strength, 1 Defence) is viable for PVP, but for PVE (PvM), you want balanced stats.

How much food should I bring to training sessions?

At Levels 1–40: Bring 5–10 pieces of food (Salmon/Trout, ~50 GP each). You can bank frequently. At Levels 40–70: Bring 10–20 pieces (Lobster, ~150 GP each). At Level 70+: With good armor and Defence, you may not need food at all (especially at crabs/Experiments with proper safe-spotting). Pro tip: Always carry a Ring of Recoil (475 GP) — it reflects 10% of damage dealt back to the monster. This effectively gives you free damage without using food.

Is it worth buying a Dwarf Multicannon for early training?

Not until you have 500k+ GP to spare. The cannon costs 750k GP to buy, and cannonballs cost ~90k GP per hour of use. For early training (Levels 1–60), manual grinding is faster and cheaper. Save your GP for Rune Scimitar (20k), Dragon Scimitar (60k), and Ava's Device (free with quest) first. Only buy a cannon if you're doing Slayer tasks where cannons are effective (e.g., Dagannoths, Kalphite).

What's the fastest way to 99 combat?

For Melee: Slayer (70–99) is the most efficient because you get Slayer XP too. For pure speed (ignoring cost): NMZ (Nightmare Zone) for AFK, or Sand/Ammonite Crabs for semi-AFK. For Ranged: Chinning (Red Chinchompas) is fastest but costs 5M+ GP. For Magic: Ice Barrage bursting Slayer tasks. But as a beginner, just enjoy the content — the levels come naturally if you do quests and Slayer.

Do I need to train all three combat styles (Melee, Ranged, Magic)?

Yes, eventually. Most endgame content requires all three styles. Melee for general bossing, Ranged for safe DPS and specific bosses (Zulrah, Vorkath), Magic for teleports, High Alchemy, and Magic-based bosses ( Chambers of Xeric). However, as a beginner, focus on one style first (usually Melee) to get to 60+ combat, then branch out. Trying to train all three at once spreads your XP too thin.

What's the best free-to-play (F2P) combat training route?

F2P route: Chickens (1–10) → Cows (10–20) → Al-Kharid Warriors (20–40) → Moss Giants in Varrock Sewers (40–60) → Ogress Warriors in Corsair Cove (60+, after Dragon Slayer I). F2P has fewer options, but Ogress Warriors are surprisingly good (high HP, good drops). Consider becoming a member at Level 40+ for access to Rock Crabs and Slayer — it's 10x more efficient.

How does the "Attack Speed" of weapons affect my XP rates?

Faster weapons (4 ticks = 2.4 seconds per hit) give more XP per hour because you hit more often. Slower weapons (6 ticks = 3.6 seconds) hit harder but give less XP per hour. For training, always use the fastest weapon available for your level: Dragon Scimitar (4 ticks) > Dragon Longsword (5 ticks) > Dragon 2h Sword (6 ticks). The only exception is if you're bursting/barraging with Magic — then tick timing matters for multi-hitting.

Why do some players say " train Defence last"?

Defence only affects how often you get hit and how much damage you take. It doesn't increase your DPS (damage per second). Attack increases accuracy (you hit more often), Strength increases max hit (you hit harder). For pure DPS efficiency, train Defence last. However, some Defence is necessary to wear better armor (Rune needs 40 Defence, Dragon needs 60, Barrows needs 65+). Plan your Defence levels around gear unlocks, not for DPS.

What quests should I prioritize before combat training?

These 5 quests give the most combat XP for the least effort:

  1. Waterfall Quest: Attack 30 + Strength 30 instantly (no requirements!)
  2. Tree Gnome Village: 11,450 Attack XP (Level 45 Attack instantly if you already have some XP)
  3. Fight Arena: 43,740 combined combat XP (one of the highest XP quests)
  4. Vampyre Slayer: 2,400 Attack + 4,800 HP XP (5-minute quest)
  5. Dragon Slayer I: Unlocks Ogress Warriors (best F2P Ranged training) + gives various XP lamps

Do these before you grind. They'll save you 10+ hours of manual training.

How do I safe-spot monsters? (Step-by-step)

Safe-spotting means attacking a monster without it being able to hit you back. How: Stand behind an obstacle (tree, rock, fence, or pillar) that the monster can't walk through. Shoot Ranged/Magic, or use a long-range melee weapon (Halberd) from behind the obstacle. Best safe-spot locations: Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon, behind the fence), Ogress Warriors (Corsair Cove, behind the fence), most dragons (behind a rock/pillar). Read our Safe Spots Guide for exact map locations with screenshots.

What's the best way to train Hitpoints (HP) to 99?

Hitpoints XP is gained automatically when you take damage or deal damage. You don't need to "train" HP separately. It will level up naturally as you train Attack/Strength/Defence. However, if you want to boost HP faster: Eat food during combat (each bite heals you and gives small HP XP), or get hit more often (train with lower Defence gear). Most players reach 99 HP naturally by the time they have 90+ in other combat stats.

Should I use a special attack weapon for training?

Special attacks consume Special Attack energy (shown as a bar under your weapon). Most special attacks are not worth using for training because they drain energy you could use on bosses later. Exceptions: Dragon Scimitar's special (accuracy boost, useful for landing hits on high-Defence monsters), Dragon Dagger's special (high damage, good for finishing off low-HP monsters). For general training, just use normal attacks.

How much GP should I have before starting serious combat training?

You need very little GP for early combat training:

  • Levels 1–40: ~10k GP (food + basic armor)
  • Levels 40–60: ~50k GP (Rune Scimitar + Rune armor + food)
  • Levels 60–70: ~200k GP (Dragon Scimitar + Dragon armor + food)
  • Levels 70–99 (Slayer): ~1–2M GP (Barrows gear + supplies for tasks)

If you're low on GP, read our Beginner Money Making Guide to get your first 100k–1M GP quickly.

What's the difference between "Controlled" and "Aggressive" combat styles?

Controlled distributes XP equally across Attack, Strength, and Defence (33% each). It's slow and inefficient. Aggressive gives 100% XP to Strength (hit harder). Accurate gives 100% XP to Attack (more accurate). Defensive gives 100% XP to Defence (take less damage). Rule: Never use Controlled. Switch between Aggressive and Accurate depending on which stat you want to train. Use Defensive only if you need Defence levels for a specific armor unlock.

Final Tips — Combat Training for Beginners

  • Complete Waterfall Quest before you grind. It gives you Attack 30 + Strength 30 for free. That's 2–3 hours of saved training time.
  • Buy a Rune Scimitar at Level 40 Attack. It costs ~20k GP and doubles your DPS. Don't train with a Steel scimitar — it's a massive waste of time.
  • Get Ava's Device as soon as you reach 35 Ranged. The Animal Magnetism quest takes 30 minutes and saves you thousands in ammo costs. It pays for itself in one training session.
  • Always carry a Ring of Recoil (475 GP). It reflects 10% of dealt damage back to your opponent. Against high-HP monsters, this adds up to hundreds of free damage per hour.
  • Don't cannon until you have 500k+ GP to spare. Cannonballs cost ~90k GP per hour. Train manually first, then use a cannon only for specific Slayer tasks where it's efficient.
  • Start Slayer at Combat Level 60+. It's the most engaging and efficient combat training. You'll never run out of things to kill, and the drops pay for your gear upgrades.
  • Use the OSRS Wiki combat calculator. Before you start a training session, check the Wiki's combat simulator to see exactly how much XP per hour you'll get at your current level. This prevents wasting time at inefficient spots.
  • Join a Clan Chat for help. OSRS has an amazing community. If you're stuck on a quest, can't find a training spot, or need help with a boss — just ask in your clan chat. Someone will help you within minutes.
