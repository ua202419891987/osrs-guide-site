1. Upgrade to Jagex Account (Do This First)

In 2024, Jagex launched Jagex Accounts — a complete rework of OSRS account security. If you're still using an old "RSN + password" login, you are vulnerable. Upgrading is free, takes 5 minutes, and adds bank-level security to your account.

② What Is a Jagex Account?

A Jagex Account is a single login that manages all your OSRS/RS3 characters. It includes:

  • Mandatory 2FA: You need a code from your phone every time you log in (even from a new device)
  • Device Trust: Your usual computer/phone is "trusted" — no 2FA needed unless you log in from a new device
  • Email Verification: Any password change or sensitive action requires email confirmation
  • Recoverable: If you lose access, Jagex can verify your identity via email + security questions

③ How to Upgrade (Step by Step)

Step 1: Go to the Jagex Account Upgrade Page

Visit secure.runescape.com (type it manually — don't click links). Log in with your current OSRS credentials.

Step 2: Click "Upgrade to Jagex Account"

Follow the prompts. You'll need to provide an email address (use a secure, unique email — not your OSRS-associated email if possible).

Step 3: Set Up the Authenticator App

Download Google Authenticator or Authy on your phone. Scan the QR code shown on screen. Save the backup codes somewhere safe (not on your computer — write them down or use a password manager).

Step 4: Confirm and Finish

Enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app to confirm. Your account is now upgraded. Next time you log in, you'll need your email + authenticator code.

⚠️ Important: Once you upgrade to a Jagex Account, you CANNOT go back to the old login system. This is permanent. But it's also 1000x more secure, so just do it.

⑤ What If I Lose My Phone?

This is the #1 fear. Here's the backup plan:

  1. Backup codes: During setup, Jagex gives you 10 backup codes. Save them somewhere safe (password manager, written on paper in a safe). Each code can be used ONCE to log in without your phone.
  2. Authy cloud backup: If you use Authy and enabled cloud backup, you can restore your codes on a new phone by verifying your phone number.
  3. Contact Jagex Support: If all else fails, contact Jagex Support with your original ID documents. They can remove 2FA after identity verification (takes 3-7 days).

🔐 Password Manager Recommendation

Use a password manager (1Password, Bitwarden, LastPass) to store your OSRS password, backup codes, and email password. This way, even if your computer is compromised, your passwords are safe in an encrypted vault. Bitwarden is free and open-source — highly recommended.

3. 10 Most Common OSRS Scams in 2026 (With Examples)

Scammers evolve, but the core tricks stay the same. Here are the 10 scams you'll encounter in 2026, with exactly how to avoid them:

⑥ Scam #1: The "Double Your GP" Trust Trade

How it works: A player DMs you: "I'm filming a YouTube video. Drop 1M GP to me, I'll double it and send it back in 10 seconds for the video!"
Reality: They take your GP and log out. You never see it again.
Protection: Never drop trade with strangers. No legitimate player needs you to "prove trust" with real GP.

⑦ Scam #2: Fake Giveaway Links

How it works: You see a chat message: "Jagex is giving away 10 bond packs! Click here: [link]" The link looks like "jagex-giveaway.com" or similar.
Reality: The site is a phishing page that steals your login credentials.
Protection: Jagex NEVER gives away items via DM links. Always type "runescape.com" manually. If a link isn't "runescape.com" or "jagex.com", it's a scam.

⑧ Scam #3: The "I Accidentally Reported You" Panic

How it works: A player DMs you: "Sorry, I accidentally reported you for RWT. Contact Jagex Support at [discord link] to clear it up or you'll be banned in 24 hours."
Reality: The Discord server is run by scammers. They'll ask for your login to "verify your account" and then steal it.
Protection: Jagex NEVER contacts players via Discord DMs. All official communication is through email (from "@jagex.com" addresses only).

⑨ Scam #4: Fake Clan Recruitment DMs

How it works: You get a DM: "Hey, want to join our clan? We have weekly drops and giveaways. Join our Discord: [link]"
Reality: The Discord server is a scam. They'll eventually ask for your OSRS login "for verification."
Protection: Never join a clan that recruits via DM. Legitimate clans recruit in the official OSRS Clan Recruitment world (W380).

⑩ Scam #5: The "Your Account Will Be Deleted" Email

How it works: You get an email: "Your OSRS account has been reported for botting. Click here to appeal or your account will be deleted in 48 hours."
Reality: The link goes to a phishing site.
Protection: Hover over the sender's email address. If it's not "@jagex.com", it's fake. Also, Jagex doesn't delete accounts without multiple warnings via the in-game message system.

🚩 Red Flag Checklist: If ANY of these are true, it's 99% a scam: (1) They contacted you first via DM, (2) They ask for your password or email verification, (3) The link isn't "runescape.com" or "jagex.com", (4) They promise "free GP" or "double your money", (5) They create urgency ("do it now or you'll be banned").

4. How to Spot Phishing Links & Fake Websites

Phishing is the #1 cause of account hijacking. Here's how to spot a fake site in 10 seconds:

oldschool.runescape.comoldschool-runescape.com, osrs-gp.com

⑫ Check for HTTPS (But Don't Trust It Blindly)

Legitimate sites use HTTPS (padlock icon in browser). BUT: phishing sites can also have HTTPS. The padlock only means the connection is encrypted — it doesn't mean the site is legitimate. Always check the actual domain name.

⑬ What to Do If You Clicked a Phishing Link

Step 1: Change Your Password Immediately

Go to runescape.com (type it manually) → Account Settings → Change Password. Use a strong, unique password (not used on any other site).

Step 2: Enable 2FA (If Not Already On)

Even if you think the phishers didn't get your password (maybe you didn't finish entering it), enable 2FA immediately. It's the ultimate backup protection.

Step 3: Check Your Email for Suspicious Activity

If the phishers got your password, they might try to change your registered email. Check your email (the one associated with your OSRS account) for "Password Change Request" or "Email Change Request" messages from Jagex. If you see one you didn't trigger, contact Jagex Support IMMEDIATELY.

5. How to Avoid Real-World Trading (RWT) Bans

RWT (buying/selling GP for real money) is the fastest way to get your account banned. Jagex's detection is extremely good in 2026. Here's what to avoid:

⑭ What Counts as RWT (You Might Be Surprised)

ActivityIs It RWT? (Ban Risk)Notes
Buying GP with real money (any site)✅ YES (High risk)Immediate ban if caught
Selling GP for real money✅ YES (High risk)Same as buying
Buying OSRS accounts✅ YES (High risk)Account gets banned, you lose everything
"GF" (Giving free GP to friends)❌ NO (Safe)As long as no real money changes hands

Buying bonds with real money (from Jagex)❌ NO (Safe)This is the ONLY legit way to convert real money to GP

⑮ How Jagex Detects RWT

Jagex uses multiple detection methods:

  • Trade pattern analysis: If you receive 100M GP from a new account with no history, that's flagged.
  • IP analysis: If multiple accounts log in from the same IP and transfer GP, all accounts get flagged.
  • Payment tracking: If Jagex receives a report from PayPal/a bank about OSRS-related transactions, they investigate.
  • Chat keywords: Saying "PM me for GP" or "selling gold" in game chat triggers auto-flags.

💡 The Only Safe Way to Buy GP: Buy Bonds from the official Jagex store (or in-game via the GE). Redeem bonds for GP. This is the ONLY method that won't get you banned. Everything else risks your account.

6. My Account Was Hijacked — Recovery Steps

If the worst happens and your account is hijacked, here's the exact recovery process:

⑯ Step 1: Submit an Account Recovery Request

Go to runescape.com/changepassword and click "Forgotten login" at the bottom. You'll need to provide:

  • Your original RSN (display name)
  • Original email address associated with the account
  • Approximate date of account creation
  • Any old transaction IDs (if you bought membership via credit card/PayPal)

⑰ Step 2: Contact Jagex Support (If Recovery Fails)

If the automated recovery doesn't work (wrong email, lost access), contact Jagex Support directly: jagex.com/support. Provide as much proof as possible:

  • Screenshots of your account (character screen, skill stats)
  • Original purchase receipts (membership, bonds)
  • Original email address + any old passwords you remember
  • Details about your character (combat level, total level, expensive items you owned)

⑱ Step 3: Secure Your Email Account

If your OSRS account was hijacked, your email might also be compromised. Change your email password immediately and enable 2FA on your email account (Gmail, Outlook, etc. all support 2FA).

⏱️ Recovery Time Expectations

Automated recovery: Instant (if you have access to the original email). Manual support ticket: 3-7 business days. If you don't have the original email and no transaction proof, recovery is very difficult. This is why upgrading to a Jagex Account (with 2FA) is so important — it makes hijacking virtually impossible.

8. Security FAQs

Q: I got a DM from "Jagex Mod" saying I won a contest. Is it real?

A: 100% fake. Jagex Mods (JMods) have a gold crown next to their name in-game, but they NEVER DM individual players. Any DM claiming to be from Jagex is a scam. Report the player and block them.

Q: Is it safe to use OSBuddy or RuneLite?

A: RuneLite is officially supported by Jagex and is 100% safe. OSBuddy is also safe (but less popular now). Third-party clients that are NOT on the official Jagex-approved list might contain keyloggers. Only use RuneLite or the official game client.

Q: Can I share my account with my brother/sister/friend?

A: Technically against Jagex's rules (one account per person), but Jagex rarely bans for casual family sharing. However, if your friend does something stupid (like RWT or botting) on your account, YOU are responsible. Never share your account password with anyone you wouldn't trust with your bank password.

Q: What should I do if I already clicked a phishing link and entered my password?

A: (1) Go to runescape.com immediately and change your password. (2) Enable 2FA if not already on. (3) Check your email for suspicious "password change" messages. (4) If you can't log in (hijacker already changed your password), submit an account recovery request immediately. Don't wait.

Q: Are "account checks" on Discord safe? (Sites that show your account value)

A: No. These sites ask for your RSN, then show a fake "account value" estimate. But behind the scenes, they're logging your RSN and tracking your wealth. Some even ask you to "verify ownership" by entering your password. Never use these sites. If you want to check your account value, use the OSRS Wiki's gear setup page or calculate it manually.

Q: My friend's account got hijacked and he got it back. How?

A: If he had the original email and some transaction proof (membership purchase receipts), Jagex's recovery process is pretty good. The key is having proof that you originally owned the account. This is why it's important to keep old email confirmations and screenshots of your account from when you first created it.

Q: Is it worth buying "unban services" on Skype/Discord?

A: Absolutely not. These are 100% scams. Nobody can "unban" your account except Jagex. If someone claims they have a "contact at Jagex" or can "hack the ban system," they're lying. They'll take your money and do nothing. Only use the official Jagex Support system.

Q: How do I report a scammer or hacker?

A: In-game: Right-click the player → "Report". Select the appropriate reason (Scamming, Phishing, etc.). Jagex reviews reports and bans scammers. For serious cases (someone hacked your account), submit a support ticket at jagex.com/support with full details. Include screenshots if you have them.
