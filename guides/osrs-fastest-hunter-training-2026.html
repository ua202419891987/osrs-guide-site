② ⚙️ Tick Manipulation Methods (3-Tick Hunting)

Tick manipulation is the technique that separates speedrunners from casual players. In OSRS, a tick is 0.6 seconds. Most Hunter actions take 3–5 ticks. Tick manipulation reduces the delay between actions, letting you catch chinchompas faster than the game normally allows.

2.1 ⏱️ How 3-Tick Box Trapping Works

Normally, when you reset a box trap after a successful catch, there's a 3-tick delay before the trap becomes active again. The 3-tick hunting method cancels this delay by using a secondary action (like a knife on a log, or a herb on a tar) between traps. Here's the step-by-step:

  1. Set up your box traps in a diamond pattern (4–6 traps)
  2. When a chinchompa is caught and the trap collapses: do not immediately click the trap
  3. Instead, use a knife on a teak/mahogany log (or clean herb on swamp tar). This action takes exactly 3 ticks.
  4. Immediately after the secondary action completes, click the fallen trap to reset it
  5. The trap resets instantly — no animation delay
  6. Repeat for every fallen trap in your rotation

This technique increases your catch rate by roughly 15–25%, pushing Black Chinchompa XP rates from 150K/hour to 190K–220K/hour at peak performance.

⚠️ Tick Manipulation Is Exhausting

Maintaining 3-tick hunting for more than 1–2 hours is mentally draining. Most speedrunners use tick manipulation in 30–45 minute bursts, then switch to normal box trapping for recovery. Don't burn yourself out trying to 3-tick your way from 63 to 99 — your brain will hate you.

2.3 📐 Perfect Trap Layout for Tick Manipulation

For 3-tick hunting, trap placement matters more than with normal methods. You need every trap within 1–2 tiles of your character's path so you can reach them within a single tick. The optimal layout:

  • 4-trap setup (levels 60–80): Diamond pattern — stand in the center, traps in each cardinal direction (N, S, E, W) exactly 1 tile away. Rotate clockwise.
  • 5-trap setup (80+): Diamond + center offset. Stand between two traps, with three more in a triangle behind you.
  • 6-trap setup (heirloom): Full hexagon — you stand in the center, traps forming a ring around you. This is the ideal setup for Black Chinchompas.

Each chinchompa location has slightly different terrain. Spend 5 minutes adjusting your trap layout before starting a long session — a 1-tile difference in trap position can mean 5K+ XP/hour lost or gained.

③ 🌍 Best Worlds & Competition Management

Competition is the #1 XP killer in Hunter. A single other player at your chinchompa spot can cut your XP by 30–50%. Here's how to manage it.

3.2 ⏰ Peak vs. Off-Peak Hours for Black Chinchompas

Black Chinchompas are in level 32–35 Wilderness. PKer activity varies dramatically by time of day:

  • Peak PK hours (avoid): 5PM–11PM EST (US evenings), Friday–Sunday afternoons (weekend warriors). Expect 3–5 PK encounters per hour. XP drops to 80K–100K/hour due to constant logging and retreating.
  • Off-peak hours (best): 2AM–6AM EST (US sleeping, EU early morning). Expect 0–1 PK encounters per hour. Full XP rates achievable.
  • Golden window: Tuesday mornings (weekly update day) — servers reset, PKers haven't logged in yet, and competition is minimal.

3.3 🔄 World Hopping Efficiency

When your current world becomes crowded, hop immediately. Don't "share" a spot — you'll both get 50% XP. Use these world hop shortcuts:

  • RuneLite World Hopper plugin: Right-click the world switcher → "Hop to" → sort by ping and population. Takes 5 seconds.
  • Pre-hop preparation: Before logging in, open the world list and note 3–4 low-pop worlds. If your first world is crowded, you have backups ready.
  • Don't hop during peak XP: If you're in a rhythm at 180K XP/hour, a 5-second hop that saves you from 30% competition is worth it. But hopping 3+ times in 10 minutes costs more time than it saves. If you can't find a good world in 3 hops, take a 15-minute break and try again.

④ 🧪 XP Boosts Checklist (Potions, Gear, Pets)

Hunter has fewer XP boosts than combat skills, but the ones that exist are powerful. Stack everything you can.

4.1 🧪 Hunter Potions (+3 Boost)

Hunter Potions boost your Hunter level by 3 (scales with level). They require 53 Herblore to make (avantoe + kebbit teeth dust), but you can buy them on the GE for ~3K GP each. At level 60, a +3 boost lets you catch Red Chinchompas 3 levels early. At level 70, it lets you catch Black Chinchompas 3 levels early. Each dose lasts 5 minutes, and the cost is trivial compared to the XP gain from using a better chinchompa type earlier.

Using Hunter potions to access Black Chinchompas at level 70 (instead of 73) saves you roughly 4 hours of Red Chin hunting — 4 hours where you'd be getting 140K XP instead of 180K XP. The potion cost (~100K GP for 4 hours' worth) is paid back in 10 minutes of Black Chin profits.

4.2 👕 Weight-Reducing Gear (The Silent XP Boost)

Your weight determines how fast your run energy drains. Since Hunter requires constant running between traps, lower weight = more running = more traps checked per minute. At 0 kg (or negative), you can sprint almost indefinitely with stamina potions. Above 20 kg, you'll be walking half the time.

  • Target weight: 0 kg or below
  • Best setup: Full Graceful (-25kg) + Spottier Cape (-4.5kg) + Boots of Lightness (-4.5kg) = -34kg total
  • Budget setup: Boots of Lightness (-4.5kg) + Spotted Cape (-2.2kg) + any light gear

4.3 🏆 Other Boosts

  • RuneLite XP Tracker: Set it to show XP/hour and "time to level." When you see the number ticking up, it's a psychological boost that keeps you grinding.
  • Hunter's Heirloom: From completing enough Hunter Rumours (Varlamore 2026 update). Gives +1 trap slot. This is a 20–25% XP boost and the single most impactful Hunter item in the game.
  • Smoke Bombs: Throw at trap spots to improve catch rate by ~10%. At Black Chin prices, the extra chins pay for the bombs in under 5 minutes.
  • No pet boost for Hunter XP. Pets don't directly increase Hunter XP, but Herbiboar's Herbi pet (1/6,500) and the Chinchompa pet (1/94,000) give you something to look forward to during the grind.

⑤ 🗺️ Levels 1-99 Speedrun Route

Here's the detailed, step-by-step speedrun route. Every level range includes the exact location, supplies needed, expected XP rates at that level, and tips for squeezing out maximum efficiency.

5.1 ⚡ Levels 1→9: Varrock Museum (5 Minutes)

Location: Varrock Museum, ground floor. Talk to Orlando Smith. Answer all exhibit questions. Reward: 1,000 XP lamp in Hunter and Slayer. Levels 1→9 instantly.
XP/hour: N/A (instant)
Speed tip: Use the OSRS Wiki quick-guide page on a second monitor. You can finish the quiz in under 4 minutes if you know the answers.

5.2 ⚡ Levels 9→15: Tropical Wagtails (25 Minutes)

Location: Feldip Hills Hunter area (Fairy Ring AKS). South side, near the Crimson Swift spawns.
Supplies: 1 bird snare
XP/hour: 22K
Speed tip: When a Wagtail is caught, click to reset the snare while the animation is still playing — you can queue the reset action and save 1–2 ticks per catch. Every tick counts.

5.3 ⚡ Levels 15→29: Ruby Harvests (1 Hour)

Location: Piscatoris Hunter Area, north of the falconer. Ruby Harvest spawns in the flower patches.
Supplies: Butterfly net, butterfly jars (buy 50 from Yanille Hunter shop)
XP/hour: 38K–42K with 3-tick jarring
Speed tip: Position yourself between two flower patches. Catch from patch A, then immediately click patch B while the catch animation plays. This cancels the end-lag of the catch animation and lets you catch two Ruby Harvests in the time it normally takes for one.

5.4 ⚡ Levels 29→43: Swamp Lizards (1.5 Hours)

Location: Canifis Hunter Area, south of the Slayer Tower. Use the Canifis teleport (Kharyrll portal in POH, or Ectophial and run west).
Supplies: Noose wand, ropes + small nets (for resetting lizard traps)
XP/hour: 50K
Speed tip: Swamp Lizards spawn in a triangle pattern of three. Stand in the center and rotate between them clockwise. As with Wagtails, queue your next wand click before the catch animation finishes. At 50K+ XP/hour, this is the fastest pre-Falconry method and beats Orange Salamanders by 15K+ XP/hour.

5.5 ⚡ Levels 43→53: Falconry (2.5 Hours)

Location: Piscatoris Hunter Area, talk to Matthias the Falconer.
Supplies: 500 GP for falcon rental. Stamina potions. Weight-reducing gear.
XP/hour: 85K (Spotted Kebbit, 43–47) → 100K (Dark Kebbit, 47–53)
Speed tip: At 47 Hunter, switch to Dark Kebbits. They give 27% more XP per catch and the spawn density is high enough that you never wait for respawns. Don't waste inventory on kebbit fur/spikes — drop them. Your inventory should be empty except for stamina potions.

💡 Falconry Click Rhythm

The key to max Falconry XP is clicking the next kebbit while your falcon is still mid-animation on the current catch. The game queues your next command, and the falcon moves to the next target as soon as the current animation ends. Players who wait for the falcon to return lose 1–2 ticks per catch — that's 10–15% XP lost.

5.6 ⚡ Levels 53→63: Grey Chinchompas (5 Hours)

Location: Piscatoris Hunter Area, north of the falconer. Grey Chinchompas are in a fenced enclosure.
Supplies: 5 box traps, stamina potions
XP/hour: 110K–115K
Speed tip: This is your first box trap method. Practice the diamond pattern loop now — it's the same rhythm you'll use for Red and Black Chins. Set 4 traps in a diamond, stand in the center, and rotate clockwise. When traps are all set, don't just stand there — continue the rotation: check trap 1 (whether it has a chin or not), reset if needed, check trap 2, etc. Constant cycling beats reactive checking. You can start practising 3-tick resets here (knife + teak log) to build muscle memory for Black Chins later.

5.7 ⚡ Levels 63→73: Red Chinchompas (8 Hours)

Location: Feldip Hills Hunter Area, south of the Hunter shop. Fairy Ring AKS, run south-west.
Supplies: 5 box traps, stamina potions, Hunter potions (boost from 60 to 63 for early access)
XP/hour: 140K–155K (170K+ with 3-tick manipulation)
Speed tip: Red Chinchompas are the most competitive Hunter spot. Use total level 1250+ worlds to filter out bots and low-level players. If your world has even one other person at Red Chins, hop. The drop from 150K to 90K XP/hour isn't worth "sharing." At level 70, start using Hunter potions to boost to 73 and switch to Black Chinchompas for the remaining 63→73 grind.

5.8 ⚡ Levels 73→99: Black Chinchompas (25 Hours)

Location: Wilderness, level 32–35, north of the Chaos Temple. Use the Ardougne Wilderness lever, then run north.
Supplies: 6 box traps (with heirloom), stamina potions, knife + teak logs (for 3-tick), minimal risk gear
XP/hour: 180K–220K with 3-tick manipulation, 150K–160K without
Speed tip: This is the finale. Every tick you save here compounds over 25 hours of grinding. Use 3-tick hunting with knife + teak logs. Play during off-peak hours (2AM–6AM EST). Use total level 1750+ worlds. Bank your chinchompas every 100–150 catches via the Ferox Enclave (teleport with Ring of Dueling). Never carry more than 200 chins — the risk of losing 400K+ to a PKer isn't worth the 30 seconds saved per bank run.

At level 80, you unlock your 5th trap. At level 80+ with the Hunter's heirloom, you have 6 traps. At this point, your XP/hour jumps to 200K+ consistently. The final stretch from 92–99 (6.5M XP) takes roughly 35 hours — a single weekend if you grind hard.

⚠️ PK Survival at Black Chins

Speed PK survival checklist:
• Player Attack Options → Hidden (never skull)
• Protect from Magic active at all times (most PKers use ice barrage)
• Escape route memorised: run south → level 30 Wilderness → Ring of Dueling to Ferox Enclave
• Minimap zoomed in sufficiently to see white dots at the edge of your screen
• When you see a white dot: don't finish your trap loop. Log out or run immediately. A 10-second escape costs less XP than a death.
• Expected deaths: 8–15 over the full 73–99 Black Chin grind. Budget 150K GP per death (lost traps + small chin stack). Total cost: ~1.5M–2.5M GP — trivial compared to the 100M+ you'll earn.

⑥ ❓ FAQ — Fastest Hunter Methods

Q: What's the absolute maximum XP/hour possible in Hunter?

A: The theoretical maximum is ~220K–240K XP/hour at level 99 with 6 traps (Hunter's heirloom), 3-tick knife+teak manipulation, perfect trap layout at Black Chinchompas, 0 kg weight, stamina potions active, and an empty world. Sustaining this for more than an hour is extremely difficult. For most speedrunners, 180K–200K XP/hour is the realistic peak for sustained sessions.

Q: Is 3-tick hunting worth it for Red Chinchompas?

A: Yes, but the margin is smaller than at Black Chins. Without 3-tick: 140K–150K XP/hour. With 3-tick: 165K–175K XP/hour. That's a ~15% boost. Over the 63–73 grind (roughly 3M XP), 3-tick saves you about 2 hours. Whether that's worth the extra clicking is up to you. At Black Chins (73–99), 3-tick is absolutely worth it — the 13M XP grind is long enough that every % matters.

Q: Should I use Hunter potions to boost to Black Chinchompas at level 70?

A: Yes. Black Chinchompas at level 70 give ~150K XP/hour (lower catch rate at low level). Red Chinchompas at level 70 give ~140K XP/hour. The gap is small at first, but Black Chins also earn 2× the GP. More importantly, every hour at Black Chins builds muscle memory for the trap layout and PK escape routes, making your 73+ grind smoother. Start at 70 with potions.

Q: How important is the Hunter's heirloom for speed?

A: Very. The heirloom gives +1 trap slot (6 instead of 5 at level 80). A 6th trap is a ~20% increase in traps set, which translates to roughly 15–18% more XP/hour at high levels. If you're serious about speedrunning Hunter, grind the Hunter Rumours until you get the heirloom. The 6–8 hours spent getting it are paid back in 20+ hours of faster chinchompa XP on the 73–99 stretch. For full details on unlocking it, see our main Hunter guide.

Q: Can I do this speed route on an ironman?

A: Partially. Ironmen benefit from the same methods (Museum skip, Falconry, box traps) but can't buy stamina potions, teak logs for 3-tick, or Hunter potions on the GE. Ironmen should follow the Ironman route in our main guide: Museum → Bird Houses → Falconry → Red Chins → Herbiboar. Ironmen can do Black Chinchompas but the risk of losing your hard-earned chin stack to a PKer is devastating. For ironman-specific strategies, check our ironman beginner guide.

Q: How do I avoid carpal tunnel during a Hunter speedrun?

A: Real talk: Hunter is one of the most click-intensive skills in OSRS. Take these precautions: stretch your wrists and fingers for 2–3 minutes every hour; use a wrist rest; set your mouse sensitivity higher so you move less; if you feel pain, stop immediately — a 99 isn't worth RSI. Alternate between intense 3-tick sessions and relaxed bird house runs. For the sake of your hands, don't grind more than 4–5 hours of tick manipulation per day.

⑦ 🏆 Final Tips for Speedrunning Hunter

⚡ The Speedrunner's Mindset

Speedrunning Hunter isn't about "playing well" — it's about eliminating downtime. Every second your character is standing still, waiting for a trap animation, running out of energy, or world-hopping is XP you've lost. The difference between 150K XP/hour and 200K XP/hour isn't a special method — it's the cumulative effect of saving 0.6 seconds (one tick) on every trap reset, every bank run, every log-in. If you reset 500 traps per hour and save one tick each, that's 300 ticks saved — 3 minutes of extra training per hour. Over 50 hours, that's 2.5 hours of free XP. Every tick matters.

  • Pre-pot stamina before you start. Sip a stamina potion before your first trap set, not when you're already out of energy. A 1-tick delay mid-run compounds across your session.
  • Empty your inventory. Every inventory slot should be dedicated to traps, potions, and tick-manipulation items. Drop all chinchompas (you can pick them up later) or bank every 100 catches. Don't let a full inventory interrupt your rhythm.
  • Disable public chat and private messages. Distractions cost ticks. Go into "Do Not Disturb" mode during speedrun sessions.
  • Set hourly XP goals. Track your XP/hour in RuneLite. If it drops below your target, diagnose the issue: competition? bad trap layout? low run energy? Fix it immediately.
  • Use the Osborne effect. Named after a speedrunner who discovered that switching activities every 45–60 minutes maintains peak focus. Do 45 min of intense 3-tick Black Chins → 15 min of casual Red Chins → repeat. You'll sustain higher average XP than grinding one method non-stop.
  • Log your deaths at Black Chins. Track when PKers kill you — time of day, world, what you lost. After 5–10 deaths, you'll spot patterns and learn which hours to avoid.
  • Record your session and review it. Use RuneLite's session recording or OBS. Watch back 5 minutes of your trap rotation. You'll spot inefficiencies (traps you're slow to reset, paths you take that are 1 tile too long) that you never noticed in the moment.
