1. Prayer Overview & Training Mechanics

Prayer is one of OSRS's most impactful combat skills. Every level increases your prayer points (1 point per level), and certain thresholds unlock game-changing prayers. At level 43, you unlock all three Protect Prayers (Melee, Missiles, Magic) that reduce incoming damage by 100% — this single milestone changes the entire game. For an overview of all skills and how Prayer fits in your progression, check the Skills Overview for Beginners 2026.

How Prayer Training Works

You gain Prayer XP by burying bones or ashes, offering them at altars, or processing ensouled heads. The key multipliers:

4. Gilded Altar — Safest Method (3.5x XP)

The Gilded Altar in your Player-Owned House (or someone else's on World 330) is the safest and most popular Prayer training method. With 2 burners lit using Marrentill herbs, you get 3.5x the bone's base XP.

How to Use World 330 (Free, No Construction Needed)

  1. Go to World 330 (House Party world)
  2. Go to Rimmington house portal
  3. Look for a host advertising their Gilded Altar (check for "Altar" or "G Altar" in public chat)
  4. Enter the house, light burners if needed (bring noted Marrentill + coins to unnote at Phials in Rimmington)
  5. Use bones on the altar — repeat until inventory is empty

Build Your Own Gilded Altar

Requires 75 Construction (~500K GP materials for the room + altar) and two incense burners (light with Marrentill). The convenience of having your own altar with a bank nearby (using Portal Nexus + Spirit Tree + Jewellery Box) saves significant time if you plan to train Prayer beyond level 70.

5. Chaos Altar — Cheapest Method (3.5x + 50% Bone Save)

The Chaos Altar in level 12 Wilderness offers the same 3.5x XP as Gilded Altar but with a 50% chance to save each bone when offered. This effectively doubles your bones, making it ~7x effective XP per bone purchased. The catch: you're in the Wilderness and can be PK'd.

Setup & Strategy

  • Location: Level 12 Wilderness, west of the Graveyard of Shadows
  • Fastest route: Use a Burning Amulet (Lava Maze teleport) → run south
  • Inventory: 1 inventory of bones + nothing else of value
  • Safe strategy: Bring 1 inventory at a time. Offer bones → if PKer appears, teleport or die → repeat

6. Ectofuntus — 4x XP, Zero Risk (Ironman Friendly)

The Ectofuntus gives 4x XP — the highest multiplier in the game — but it's slower than Gilded Altar because you need to grind bones into bonemeal first. This is the preferred method for ironman accounts who want to maximize XP per bone.

How It Works

  1. Take bones to the Ectofuntus grinder (north-west of Port Phasmatys, requires Priest in Peril quest)
  2. Grind bones into bonemeal (use bones on hopper → pull lever → collect bonemeal in pots)
  3. Fill buckets with slime from the Ectofuntus pool (bottom floor)
  4. Worship at the Ectofuntus altar with bonemeal + bucket of slime → 4x XP per bone

When to Use Ectofuntus

  • Ironman accounts — maximize XP from limited bones
  • Players who absolutely want zero PK risk
  • Completing the Morytania Diary (requires Ectofuntus worship)

XP/hr: ~30K–50K with dragon bones. XP per bone: Best in game at 4x, but the slow processing makes this impractical for main accounts past diary requirements.

11. Prayer Training FAQs

How long does 1–99 Prayer take?

With dragon bones at Gilded Altar: ~43–45 hours (300K XP/hr). With superior dragon bones: ~25–30 hours (500K XP/hr). With Chaos Altar: same rates but 50% fewer bones needed — the time is the same but the cost is halved.

What's the cheapest GP/XP for Prayer in 2026?

Chaos Altar with dragon bones = ~6 GP/XP (due to 50% bone save). Ensouled heads can be 1.5–3 GP/XP but are much slower (80K vs 300K XP/hr). Infernal Ashes at Gilded Altar = ~6.5 GP/XP but hard to gather in bulk.

Is the Chaos Altar safe after the Summer Sweep-Up 2026?

Yes, the Chaos Altar was not changed in the Sweep-Up. It remains in level 12 Wilderness with the same 3.5x XP + 50% bone save rate. The Wyrmscraig Altar (new Sailing content) offers a middle ground with 3.5x XP and 25% bone save with less PK risk.

What Prayer level should I aim for?

43 minimum — Protect from Melee changes the entire combat experience. 70 for Piety — best-in-slot melee prayer, essential for mid-game PvM. 74/77 for Rigour/Augury — endgame bossing requirements. 85+ for diary requirements and more prayer points.

Best Prayer training for Ironman accounts?

Green Dragons (Wilderness) for dragon bones → Ectofuntus for 4x XP (safest) or Chaos Altar for 7x effective XP. Late-game: Vorkath for superior dragon bones + Hydra/Slayer for passive bones. Complete Morytania Diary for the Bone Crusher to earn passive XP during Slayer.

Can I train Prayer on F2P?

Yes, but it's very slow. Big Bones are the best F2P option (bury for 15 XP each). Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon) or Giant Frogs (Lumbridge Swamp) drop them. Expect 5K–10K XP/hr max — it would take hundreds of hours to reach 43 Prayer. F2P Prayer is not recommended beyond burying bones you naturally collect.

Are ensouled heads worth it in 2026?

For GP/XP, yes — some heads are under 2 GP/XP. But they're significantly slower than altar methods (~80K vs 300K XP/hr). Use them when you want to combine combat + Prayer training (e.g., reanimating heads during Slayer), not for rushing 99. The Summer Sweep-Up did not change ensouled head mechanics.

What's the best Prayer training method for under 10M GP budget?

Chaos Altar with Dragon Bones. With a 10M GP budget, you can buy ~3,300 dragon bones. At Chaos Altar (50% save), that's effectively 6,600 bones worth = 1.66M XP. That takes you from 43 to ~68 Prayer, just 2 levels short of Piety.

Is it worth doing Prayer quests first?

Absolutely. Quest XP rewards can take you from 1 to ~44 Prayer entirely for free. Complete Restless Ghost (1,125 XP), Priest in Peril (1,406 XP), Holy Grail (11,000 XP), and Ghosts Ahoy (2,400 XP) first — that's ~55K free XP worth ~2M GP in dragon bones saved.

What changed for Prayer in the 2026 Summer Sweep-Up?

The Sweep-Up buffed Arceuus Offering spells (Demonic Offering at 84 Magic and Sinister Offering at 92 Magic) from 3x to 3.5x XP, making them more competitive. The new Wyrmscraig Altar (via Sailing) offers 3.5x XP with 25% bone save — a middle-ground between Gilded and Chaos Altars. Gilded and Chaos Altars were unchanged.

12. Final Tips — Train Prayer Efficiently

  • Do all Prayer quests first. Restless Ghost, Priest in Peril, Holy Grail, and Ghosts Ahoy give ~55K free XP. That's level 44 Prayer without spending a single GP.
  • Use Chaos Altar if you can handle the risk. Saving 50% on bones means saving ~77M GP from 43 to 99 vs Gilded Altar. Even losing 1 in 20 trips to PKers (5% loss rate) still saves you ~70M GP.
  • Prayer bonus gear matters. While using altars, wear prayer bonus gear (Proselyte, God Books, Monk's Robe) to reduce prayer drain between offerings, allowing longer trips before needing to restore.
  • Lit burners are critical at Gilded Altar. An unlit burner gives only 2x XP instead of 3.5x. Always bring Marrentill herbs + a tinderbox when using someone else's house on W330.
  • Combine with Slayer for passive XP. The Bone Crusher (Morytania Hard Diary) gives passive Prayer XP during Slayer in the Catacombs of Kourend. At 99 Slayer, this adds up to 200K+ bonus Prayer XP.
  • Inventory optimization matters. With the Bonecrusher Necklace (Hydra Tail + Dragonbone Necklace), burying bones during ensouled head reanimation restores prayer points, saving inventory space on potions.
  • Consider the time value of GP. Spending 154M GP on Gilded Altar vs 77M GP on Chaos Altar — that 77M GP difference could buy you gear upgrades that earn more GP/hr. Chaos Altar is almost always the better choice if you're not afraid of a little Wilderness risk.
