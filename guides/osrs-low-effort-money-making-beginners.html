Whether you are F2P or members, there is a method here that works for you.

1. Picking & Spinning Flax (Members)

DetailValue
Profit (Flax Only)~60,000 GP/hr (flax sells for ~100-130 GP each, ~600-700 picked per hour)
Profit (Flax + Spin)~100,000-120,000 GP/hr (bowstrings sell for ~180-220 GP each)
RequirementsNone for picking; 10 Crafting for spinning (takes ~15 minutes to get)
LocationFlax field south of Seers' Village bank; spinning wheel in Seers' Village

How to do it: Travel to Seers' Village (Camelot teleport or walk from Falador). Pick a full inventory of flax (28) from the flax field just south of the bank. Bank at Seers' Village bank (20 seconds away). Optionally, withdraw the flax and spin it into bowstrings at the spinning wheel on the 2nd floor of the house just north of the bank. Each bowstring adds ~80-100 GP profit per flax.

2. Tanning Hides at Al Kharid (Members)

DetailValue
Profit~80,000-150,000 GP/hr
RequirementsNone (members); some starting GP to buy cowhides (~1.8M investment for 1,000 hides)
LocationEllis the Tanner in Al Kharid (just north of the bank)

How to do it: Buy cowhides from the Grand Exchange (~150 GP each). Take them to Ellis in Al Kharid. Tan into hard leather (costs 45 GP per hide at Ellis). Sell hard leather on the GE for ~240-280 GP each. Net profit: ~40-80 GP per hide. With 2,000-3,000 hides tanned per hour, this yields a solid consistent profit. Always check current GE prices before bulk-buying.

💵 Scaling Up: With a Ring of Dueling to Castle Wars and a Glory Amulet to Al Kharid, you can speed up the banking and tanning cycle significantly. At higher volumes (5,000+ hides), the profit per hour approaches ~1.8M GP/hr. Dragonhide tanning (green, blue, red, black) has higher margins but requires higher investment.

3. Grand Exchange Flipping (No Stats Needed)

DetailValue
Profit100K-1.8M+ GP/hr (scales with capital)
RequirementsStarting gold (50K+ recommended); patience; GE access
LocationGrand Exchange (Varrock)

How to do it: Search for items with a buy/sell margin of 5% or more. Place a buy offer at the low end, wait for it to fill, then place a sell offer at the high end. Good starter items for 2026: cannonballs (~5 GP margin), nature runes (~3-5 GP margin), ranarr weed (~400-400 GP margin), pure essence (~2-3 GP margin), raw sharks (~20-30 GP margin). Start with 50K-100K capital and reinvest all profits -- never spend flipping capital on gear.

4. Collecting Snape Grass (Members)

DetailValue
Profit~100,000 GP/hr
RequirementsNone (members only)
LocationHobgoblin Peninsula (west of Void Knights' Outpost, near Port Sarim)

Snape grass sells for ~1,000-1,400 GP each in 2026 and is used extensively in Herblore (prayer potions). The Hobgoblin Peninsula spawns snape grass on the ground. Take the Pest Control boat from Port Sarim, walk west to the peninsula, collect grass, and bank at the nearby chest or teleport out. You can collect approximately 100+ snape grass per hour, making this one of the best zero-requirement methods in the game.

5. Killing Cows -- Classic Early Game (F2P + Members)

DetailValue
Profit~40,000-70,000 GP/hr
RequirementsLow combat stats (even level 3 can use Wind Strike)
LocationLumbridge East Farm (near Al Kharid gate) or Crafting Guild (40 Crafting)

The timeless beginner method. Cows drop cowhides worth ~150-400 GP each. You can also collect raw beef (sell to GE or cook for Cooking XP). The Lumbridge East Farm is the best spot -- cows respawn quickly and the bank at the top of Lumbridge Castle is a short run. This method also trains combat stats simultaneously, making it doubly efficient for new accounts.

6. Wines of Zamorak via Telegrab (Members)

DetailValue
Profit~120,000 GP/hr
Requirements33 Magic (Telegrab spell)
LocationWilderness Chaos Temple (level 38 Wildy -- PK risk) OR Asgarnia Ice Dungeon (safe)

Wines of Zamorak sell for 3,000-4,000 GP each. Using Telegrab, you can collect them without triggering the monks who guard the altar. Critical: The Wilderness Chaos Temple has PK risk. The safer alternative is the Asgarnia Ice Dungeon (south of Port Sarim, F2P-accessible), where wines also spawn. You can collect approximately 100+ per hour with consistent telegrabbing.

7. Looting in the Wilderness (F2P + Members)

DetailValue
Profit50K-1.8M+ GP/hr (unpredictable, can spike higher)
RequirementsZero stats. Walk into low-level Wilderness and pick up items.
LocationMulti-combat zones in level 1-10 Wilderness, near Edgeville

In multi-combat Wilderness zones (near Edgeville, levels 1-10), player-vs-player fights frequently happen. After fights, rune items, arrows, and food are often left on the ground. You can loot these items without any combat involvement. Bring nothing (empty inventory) and pick up anything valuable. Teleport out when your inventory is full. This method is highly unpredictable but can spike to 1.8M+ per hour during active PvP times.

8. Mort Myre Fungi (Members)

DetailValue
Profit~400,000-400,000 GP/hr
RequirementsNature Spirit quest, silver sickle (b)
LocationMort Myre Swamp (near Canifis)

Mort Myre fungi sell for ~500-600 GP each and are used in Super Energy potions. Use a silver sickle (b) to cast Bloom on dead logs in the swamp, collecting the fungi that spawn. You can collect 400-500 fungi per hour. Requires the Nature Spirit quest (intermediate, ~30 minutes), but otherwise has very low requirements and excellent profit.

9. Cannonball Smithing (F2P Option)

DetailValue
Profit~80,000-120,000 GP/hr (F2P-accessible)
Requirements35 Smithing, Dwarf Cannon quest (members only to unlock, then F2P-usable)
LocationEdgeville furnace (closest to bank)

Buy steel bars (~600 GP each) from the GE, smith into cannonballs (4 cannonballs per bar, worth ~400 GP each). Each steel bar yields ~800 GP in cannonballs, netting ~400 GP profit per bar. This method is very AFK -- each inventory of steel bars takes about 2.5 minutes to smith. The Dwarf Cannon quest must be completed on a members world, but cannonball smithing can be done in F2P afterward.

10. Collecting Red Spider's Eggs (Members)

DetailValue
Profit~150,000-350,000 GP/hr
RequirementsNone (but dangerous -- level 34-35 Wilderness or Edgeville Dungeon)
LocationEdgeville Dungeon (level 1-5 Wilderness area, near the Earth Warriors)

Red spider's eggs sell for ~800-1,000 GP each and are used in Saradomin Brews. The Edgeville Dungeon spawn (near the Earth Warriors, accessible from the Edgeville yew tree entrance) has 2-3 egg spawns. Collect them and bank at Edgeville. Bring a stamina potion for faster banking. This is risk-free if you stay in the level 1-5 Wilderness portion of the dungeon.

12. Pro Tips for Beginner Money Makers

  • Always check GE prices first. Prices shift daily. A margin of 400 GP today might be -50 GP tomorrow. Use the OSRS Wiki or GE Tracker to verify before committing an hour to a method.
  • Combine training with profit. Killing cows trains combat AND makes gold. Cannonball smithing trains Smithing AND makes gold. Maximize your time by choosing methods that advance your account.
  • Don't hold onto resources. Sell weekly. Hoarding items hoping prices rise is a gamble. Consistent selling and reinvesting compounds faster than speculation.
  • Stack your GP -- don't spend it. Once you hit 1.8M GP, better money makers unlock. At 1M, GE flipping becomes significantly more profitable. At 5M, you can access mid-tier PvM money makers.
  • GE flipping is the ultimate passive income. Even while doing other methods, have GE offers active. The key is finding items with reliable daily margins -- cannonballs, nature runes, and raw materials are the most consistent.

13. Common Mistakes

  1. Doing a method without checking prices. The single biggest mistake. What a guide says is profitable might not be profitable today. Spend 30 seconds checking GE prices before starting.
  2. Forgetting to bring a teleport. Always have an escape. An emergency teleport saves you minutes of walking back to a bank -- and in the Wilderness, it saves your loot.
  3. Carrying your cash stack while doing risky methods. Bank your GP. Only bring the supplies needed for the method. Especially critical for Wilderness methods.
  4. Expecting to get rich overnight. Beginner money making is about consistency, not bursts. 50K/hr for 10 hours = 1.8M. Focus on accumulating hours rather than chasing a magic 1M/hr method you can't do yet.
  5. Buying gear before building capital. Your first 1.8M should stay in your money-making setup. Better gear comes later. The Rune Scimitar vs Adamant Scimitar earns you zero extra GP when you're flipping or collecting resources.

14. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I make 1M GP on a fresh level 3 account in F2P?

A: Yes, but it takes patience. The best F2P methods are killing cows (40K-70K/hr), collecting wines of Zamorak at Asgarnia Ice Dungeon (120K/hr at 33 Magic), and GE flipping (requires starting capital). Expect 15-25 hours of playtime to hit 1M GP from a fresh level 3 F2P account. Once you hit ~1.8M, use that capital for GE flipping to accelerate the process.

Q: Which method requires the least attention?

A: Cannonball smithing and GE flipping are the most AFK. Cannonball smithing takes ~2.5 minutes per inventory with zero interaction. GE flipping requires you to set offers and wait -- you can log out and come back. For resource collection, flax picking is moderately AFK (click-flax, click-bank, repeat). Red spider's eggs require periodic clicking but no complex actions.

Q: Are these methods viable for Ironman accounts?

A: Partially. Ironmen cannot use the GE, so flipping and tanning hides are out. Flax spinning, snape grass collection, killing cows, and Mort Myre fungi all work for Ironmen since they produce sellable items. However, Ironmen should focus on methods that directly progress their account (killing cows for Crafting, spinning flax for Fletching, collecting fungi for Herblore) rather than pure GP generation.

💰 From Zero to Your First Million

Every OSRS player starts with almost nothing. The methods in this guide have been used by thousands of players to build their first cash stack. The key is consistency: pick one or two methods you don't mind repeating, stack your GP, and don't spend your capital on gear until you've built a solid foundation. Once you hit 1M GP, a whole new tier of money makers opens up.

Start small, stay consistent, and watch your coin pouch grow. Good luck!
