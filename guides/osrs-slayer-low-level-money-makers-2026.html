1. Why Slayer = Best Early Money Making

Most money-making methods in OSRS force you to choose between making GP and gaining XP. Flipping items on the Grand Exchange makes GP but zero XP. Cutting Yew logs makes some GP and Woodcutting XP, but the GP/hour is terrible. Slayer is different — every minute you spend on a Slayer task generates both Slayer XP and GP. This dual-income nature makes Slayer the single most efficient use of your time as a low-level player.

To understand why Slayer dominates early-game money making, let's compare it to the alternatives:

Over a 10-hour gameplay session, a Slayer player gains 10 hours of combat progression AND 60K–150K GP/hour. A pure money maker gains zero XP and similar GP. The choice is obvious.

🎯 The Slayer Advantage Breakdown

  • Dual rewards: Every task gives GP + Slayer XP + Melee/Ranged/Magic XP (depending on your combat style). No other skill does this.
  • Unique drops: Slayer monsters have exclusive drop tables. A single rare drop (Dragon weapon, etc.) can be worth 100K–1M GP, dramatically skewing your hourly profit upward.
  • Low barrier to entry: You can start making GP from Slayer at Combat 30 with budget gear worth less than 50K GP total.
  • Scales with your account: As your Combat stats improve, your Slayer tasks become more profitable. You never "outgrow" Slayer as a money maker.
  • Variety prevents burnout: Unlike cutting 5,000 Yew logs in a row, every Slayer task is different. This keeps money making fresh and engaging.

💡 The "Lucky Hour" Factor

Slayer GP/hour is heavily influenced by rng. You might have an "unlucky" hour where you get zero valuable drops (60K GP). You might have a "lucky" hour where you get a Dragon item drop (1.8M+ GP in one task). Over the long term, the averages above hold true. But in any given 1-hour session, your results can vary by 400% or more. Don't judge Slayer profitability by a single task — look at your total GP over 10+ tasks.

3. Task-by-Task Strategy Guide

Knowing which tasks are profitable is only half the battle. You also need to know HOW to complete each task efficiently. Here are detailed strategies for the top 5 money-making tasks:

🎯 Strategy Deep-Dive: Hill Giants (The Beginner's Goldmine)

Step 1: Gear Up

Bring: Steel/Adamant scimitar, full armor set (Steel minimum), 10–15 Lobsters, and an Amulet of Strength. Teleport to Edgeville (Amulet of Glory or walk from Varrock). Enter Edgeville Dungeon and run past the low-level monsters until you reach the Hill Giant area (north side, past the spiders and skeletons).

Step 2: Safe-Spot Setup

Hill Giants can be safe-spotted behind the column in the north-east corner of their room. Stand on the east side of the column, attack a Giant, and retreat behind the column. The Giant will get stuck and you can kill it without taking damage. This method requires Ranged or Magic (melee can't reach around corners). If you're using Melee, just tank the hits — Hill Giants only hit up to 3 damage, so 10 HP is enough to survive a full task.

Step 3: Loot Everything

After each kill, loot: Big Bones (always), Limpwurt Root (if dropped), Steel plateskirts (if dropped), Nature/Law runes (if dropped), and GP. Ignore low-value drops like Iron arrows. When your inventory is full, bank at Edgeville (run north, use the bank, run back). With practice, a full Hill Giant task (30–40 kills) takes 20–30 minutes.

Step 4: Repeat and Profit

After banking, return to the Hill Giant area and continue. A full task typically yields: 30–40 Big Bones (5,400–7,400 GP) + 0–3 Limpwurt Roots (0–3,600 GP) + various runes and steel items (10K–30K GP) = ~30K–50K GP per task. At 2–3 tasks per hour, that's 60K–150K GP/hour.

🎯 Strategy Deep-Dive: Banshees (Efficient Herb Farming)

Step 1: Essential Item Check

BEFORE getting the task, buy Earmuffs from the Slayer Equipment shop (6,000 GP). Without them, Banshees will constantly disable your prayers, making the task miserable. Also bring: Steel/Adamant scimitar, 10+ food, and a teleport to Canifis (Ectophial or Fairy Ring).

Step 2: Navigate to Fremennik Slayer Dungeon

Enter the dungeon (requires The Fremennik Trials quest partially completed or 81 Agility to bypass the gate). Run to the Banshee area (north-west section). The dungeon is large, so use the in-game map or follow other players.

Step 3: Kill and Loot

Banshees have 15 HP and hit up to 3. They're very easy with Melee. Loot: Grimy herbs (marrentill, tarromin — ~400–400 GP each), Mithril bolts (400 GP/set), and any GP drops. Over a full task (40–60 Banshees), you'll get 10–20 herbs and 5–10 Mithril bolt sets = ~15K–30K GP per task just from herbs/bolts, plus miscellaneous drops.

🎯 Strategy Deep-Dive: Cave Bugs (Caviar Farming)

Step 1: Bring Ranged Gear

Cave Bugs are most efficiently killed with Ranged from a safe distance. Bring: Oak/Willow/Maple shortbow, 100+ Steel/Mithril arrows, and 10+ food (they can hit accurately). Enter Fremennik Slayer Dungeon and go to the south-west section.

Step 2: Safe-Spot and Kill

Use the terrain to safe-spot Cave Bugs (stand around a corner where they can't reach you). Shoot arrows until they die. Loot Caviar immediately — it's the most valuable drop. A single task with 10–15 Caviar drops is worth 10K–15K GP from Caviar alone.

🎯 Strategy Deep-Dive: Lesser Demons (Rune Item Farming)

Step 1: Choose Your Location

Wizard's Tower (south of Draynor Village) is the best location for low-level players. It has multiple demons spawn close together, and the nearby Draynor bank is convenient. Alternative: Karamja Volcano (further from bank but less competition).

Step 2: Efficient Killing

Lesser Demons have 82 HP and hit up to 8. With Adamant scimitar and 60+ Combat, you should be able to kill them while only eating 2–3 Lobsters per demon. Use Protect from Melee prayer to take zero damage. Loot: Ashes (bank them for Prayer XP later), Rune items (15K–18K GP each), Death/Law/Nature runes, and the occasional Dragon bones.

Step 3: The Rune Drop Lottery

Lesser Demons have a ~1/128 chance to drop a Rune item. Over a full task (60–80 demons), you should get 0–2 Rune drops on average. One Rune med helm = 15,000 GP. Two Rune scimitars = 36,000 GP. When you get lucky, your GP/hour spikes dramatically. This is why Slayer money making has such high variance.

🎯 Strategy Deep-Dive: Kalphites (Risk vs. Reward)

Step 1: Prepare for Poison

Kalphites apply poison damage. Bring 1–2 doses of Antipoison potion (or lots of food). Enter the Kalphite Lair with a rope (first time only). The deposit box at Shantay Pass is your banking solution.

Step 2: Kill and Watch for Rares

Kalphites have a small chance to drop Dragon bolts (6,000 GP each). Over a full task, you might get 0–5 Dragon bolt drops. This is where the real profit comes from. Even 2 Dragon bolts = 12,000 GP (significant for a low-level player). Also loot all Rune items and Nature/Law runes.

💡 Pro Tip: Use NPC Contact Spell

If you have 67 Magic and have completed the Lost City quest, you can use the NPC Contact spell to contact your Slayer Master and get a new task without traveling to them. This saves massive amounts of time over the course of 100+ tasks. Each skipped trip saves 2–5 minutes, which adds up to hours saved per week. The runes cost about 500 GP per cast — well worth it for the time saved.

5. Maximizing Profit Per Task

Getting a profitable task is only half the battle. You also need to maximize how much GP you actually keep from each task. Here are the advanced strategies that separate 80K GP/hour players from 150K GP/hour players.

📦 Loot Management Strategy

① Note Valuable Drops with Magic Notepaper

Magic Notepaper (purchased from the GE or obtained from various sources) allows you to note items directly in your inventory. This means you can continue killing without banking every 15 minutes. For tasks that drop many similar items (Big Bones, herbs, ashes), using Notepaper saves 5–10 minutes per task. At 2–3 tasks per hour, that's 10–30 minutes saved per hour = more tasks completed = more GP.

Cost-benefit: A sheet of Magic Notepaper costs ~5,000 GP and notes 50 items. If it saves you one banking trip per task (worth ~10 minutes = ~15K GP in missed kills), it pays for itself instantly.

② Use the NPC Contact Spell (67 Magic)

As mentioned earlier, NPC Contact lets you get a new Slayer task without traveling to your Slayer Master. This saves 2–5 minutes per task. Over 100 tasks, that's 400–500 minutes saved = 3–8 hours of extra killing time. The spell requires 67 Magic and 1 Astral rune + 1 Cosmic rune + 2 Air runes per cast (about 500 GP). The time saved is easily worth 100x the cost.

③ Optimize Your Banking Route

Different tasks have different optimal banking methods. Learn them:

  • Hill Giants: Edgeville bank (closest)
  • Banshees: Canifis bank (via Ectophial teleport)
  • Cave Bugs: Fremennik diary reward (bank deposit box in dungeon) or bank at Rellekka
  • Lesser Demons: Draynor bank (closest to Wizard's Tower)
  • Kalphites: Shantay Pass deposit box (free, no GP cost)
  • Fire Giants: Seers' Village bank (via Camelot teleport)

Using the optimal bank for each task saves 30–60 seconds per trip. Over a full task, that's 5–10 minutes saved.

⚔️ Combat Efficiency Tips

🎯 Attack Style Matters

Most low-level players use "Aggressive" attack style (boosts Strength XP) for everything. But for maximum GP/hour, you should use the style that kills monsters fastest:

  • Against low-defence monsters (Hill Giants): Use "Accurate" or "Aggressive" — whichever gives higher max hit. The faster the kill, the more GP/hour.
  • Against high-defence monsters (Fire Giants): Use "Controlled" to evenly train all Combat stats while maintaining decent kill speed.
  • With Ranged: Always use the highest Ranged level attack style ("Accurate" for bows, "Rapid" for crossbows). The +1 Ranged level significantly increases hit chance.

② Use Combat Potions (If You Can Afford Them)

Super Attack and Super Strength potions (or even regular Attack/Strength potions) increase your max hit and hit chance. This translates directly to faster kills and higher GP/hour. A 3-dose Super Strength potion costs ~8,000 GP and lasts 6 minutes. If it increases your kill speed by 20%, that's an extra 3–5 tasks per hour = 30K–75K GP extra profit. The math strongly favors using potions for money-making tasks.

③ Protect Prayers = Zero Food Cost

If you have 43 Prayer, using Protect from Melee makes most Slayer tasks completely free (no food cost). Over a 10-task session, not buying food saves 50K–100K GP. That's pure profit. Even if you're below 43 Prayer, using occasional prayer potions to extend your Protect prayer is cheaper than constantly buying food.

💎 Slayer Points Spending Priority for Money Makers

If your goal is to maximize GP from Slayer, here's the optimal order for spending your Slayer points:

  1. Slayer Helmet (400 points): Saves inventory space (no need to bring Earmuffs/Nose Peg/etc.) = more loot per trip = higher GP/hour.
  2. Broad Bolts (400 points): Required to kill certain monsters with Ranged (Kurasks, etc.) that are very profitable.
  3. Ring of Wealth (350 points): Teleport to Grand Exchange for quick loot selling. Saves 2–3 minutes per banking trip.
  4. Herb Sack (350 points): If you're also training Herblore, this saves trips. If not, skip it.
  5. Extra Block Slots (100 points each): Block unprofitable tasks to increase the % of tasks that are money makers.

6. Tasks to Avoid (Money Losers)

Not all Slayer tasks are created equal. Some tasks look okay on paper but actually lose you GP when you factor in time cost, supply costs, and opportunity cost. Here are the tasks you should consider blocking or skipping as a low-level money maker.

① Crocodiles & Alligators

Why they lose money: These tasks are assigned frequently by mid-level masters, but the drops are terrible. You'll spend 30–45 minutes killing 40–60 crocodiles and walk away with 10K–20K GP of loot. That's 15K–30K GP/hour — worse than cutting Willow logs. Verdict: Skip or block once you have the points.

② Bats & Rats

Why they lose money: Extremely low-value drops. You'll spend 20 minutes killing 50+ bats and get maybe 5K GP of loot. Verdict: Only do these if you're desperately trying to accumulate Slayer points. Otherwise, skip.

③ Ice Warriors / Ice Giants (F2P area)

Why they lose money: They hit hard (especially Ice Giants, which can hit 14+) and the drops are mediocre. You'll spend more on food than you make in loot. Verdict: Skip unless you have 43 Prayer and can Protect from Melee for free kills.

④ Any Task in the Wilderness (If You're Not Prepared)

Why they can lose money: Wilderness tasks (Green Dragons, some Skeletons, Revs) expose you to PKers. If you die, you lose everything you're carrying (including untradeables if you don't have Protect Item prayer on). One death can wipe out 10 hours of Slayer profit. Verdict: Only do Wilderness tasks if you're confident in PvP or you're willing to risk the Gear.

⑤ Zombies (Most Locations)

Why they lose money: Zombies have very low-value drops (mostly bones and low-level herbs). An hour of killing zombies yields maybe 30K–50K GP — far below the 80K–150K GP/hour you could be making on Hill Giants or Banshees. Verdict: Skip unless you need the Slayer points urgently.

7. Slayer Money Making FAQs

What's the single most profitable Slayer task for Combat 40-60 players?

Fire Giants (if you have Combat 60+) are the most profitable, yielding 160K–350K GP/hour. For Combat 40–50 players who can't do Fire Giants yet, Hill Giants and Lesser Demons are the best options (80K–150K GP/hour). The exact number depends heavily on drop RNG — a single Rune item drop can double your hourly profit.

Do I need membership to make GP from Slayer?

Technically yes and no. F2P Slayer exists (Turael assigns tasks), but the task pool is tiny and the drops are worth very little. With membership, you unlock Vannaka, Chaeldar, and Duradel (better tasks), plus access to the Grand Exchange (easier selling loot). If you're serious about Slayer money making, membership is effectively required.

How many tasks does it take to afford the Dragon Scimitar?

The Dragon Scimitar costs ~400,000 GP on the GE (or is free if you get it as a quest reward from Monkey Madness I). At 80K–120K GP per task (Hill Giants, Banshees), you need about 2–3 profitable tasks to afford it. If you're skipping bad tasks, budget 5–8 tasks to accumulate 1.8M GP. The Dragon Scimitar is one of the best investments you can make as a low-level player — it pays for itself in 2–3 tasks through faster kills.

Should I use Magic for Slayer tasks to save money?

No. Magic is the most expensive combat style because you consume runes with every cast. Melee is the cheapest (no ammo cost, and food is reusable via banking). Ranged is in the middle (arrows/bolts cost GP, but you take less food damage because you're safe-spotting). For money-making tasks, always use Melee unless the task specifically requires Ranged (safe-spotting Dragons, etc.).

Is it worth buying Slayer points to skip bad tasks?

At 2–5 points per skip, skipping a bad task costs about 2–5 tasks worth of points. If a bad task costs you 50K–100K GP in opportunity cost, and a good task makes 100K–150K GP, then skipping saves you 150K–350K GP in lost time. At 2–3 tasks per hour, that's 50K–125K GP saved per skip. Since each Slayer point is "worth" about 1,000–2,000 GP in time saved, spending 2–5 points to skip is almost always worth it once you have 30+ points banked.

What should I do with all the herbs I get from Slayer?

You have three options: (1) Sell them immediately on the GE for quick GP (easiest, recommended for pure money makers), (2) Save them for Herblore training later (if you plan to train Herblore), or (3) Clean them immediately for Herblore XP + slightly higher GP (cleaning herbs gives 2.5 XP per herb and increases their GE value by ~10–20 GP). For pure money makers, option 1 is usually best — get the GP now and worry about Herblore later.

How long until I can afford the Abyssal Whip from Slayer?

The Abyssal Whip costs ~1.8M GP on the GE. At 100K GP/hour (average for Combat 50–60), you need about 18 hours of efficient Slayer to afford it. That's roughly 40–60 tasks (depending on task luck). For most players, this is a 1–2 week goal if playing 1–2 hours per day. The Whip is a massive upgrade that increases your GP/hour to 150K–1.8M+, so it pays for itself in about 15–20 hours of additional play.
