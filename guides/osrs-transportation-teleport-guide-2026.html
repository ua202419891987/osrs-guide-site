🚶 Beginner Travel Options — Walk, Home Teleport & Basic Movement

Every OSRS journey starts in Lumbridge. When you first step off Tutorial Island, you have zero teleports, zero run energy management skills, and a whole lot of walking ahead of you. This section covers the absolute basics every new player needs to know about getting around Gielinor without spending a single coin.

① Set Your Home at Lumbridge

The Lumbridge Home Teleport is your most important tool in the early game. Right after Tutorial Island, your respawn point and default home teleport location is set to Lumbridge. You can change this later by speaking to Father Aereck in the Lumbridge Church or by using a bed in a player-owned house, but for now, keep it at Lumbridge. The Lumbridge Castle courtyard is centrally located near the Grand Exchange (via the Varrock road), Draynor Village, and the Al Kharid toll gate. This makes it the ideal hub for new players.

To confirm your home location, open your quest tab (the green scroll icon) and look for the "Home Teleport" option. It should say Lumbridge. If it doesn't, speak to Father Aereck and ask him to reset it.

② Activate Your Home Teleport

Using the Home Teleport is simple. Open your spellbook (keyboard shortcut: F4 or click the magic book icon). You will see the Home Teleport spell at the very top of the standard spellbook. It costs no runes and no magic levels to cast. Click it, and after a 10-second cast time, you will be transported back to Lumbridge. You can cast it once every 30 minutes, though this cooldown can be reduced by completing certain quests like The Lumbridge & Draynor Diary tasks.

Pro tip: You can right-click the Home Teleport icon in your spellbook and select "Configure" to change your left-click behavior. Set it to "Home Teleport" for faster one-click casts.

③ Immediate Free Teleports — Lodestones & Minigame Teleports

Beyond the Home Teleport, there are several free movement options available immediately. The most notable is the minigame teleport system. Open the quest tab, click the minigame icon (brown star), and you can teleport to over a dozen minigames absolutely free — once every 20 minutes. Early game picks include:

  • Barbarian Assault: Teleports you to the Barbarian Outpost, near the Baxtorian Falls and Tree Gnome Stronghold.
  • Castle Wars: Puts you in the heart of Kandarin, close to Catherby, the Fishing Guild, and the Yanille agility dungeon.
  • Fishing Trawler: Teleports you to the Fishing Platform, near Port Khazard and the Gnome Maze.
  • Duel Arena: Teleports you to Al Kharid, close to the desert entrance and Shantay Pass.

Using minigame teleports strategically early on can save you hours of walking. Just remember the 20-minute cooldown applies per teleport group, not per minigame.

🔮 Magic Teleports — Standard, Ancient & Lunar Spellbooks

Once you level up your Magic skill, the real transportation options open up. Magic teleports are the backbone of mid-to-late game travel in OSRS. There are three spellbooks, each offering unique teleport locations. Let's break them down by spellbook and level requirement.

① Standard Spellbook Teleports

The standard spellbook is what you start with. It offers the widest variety of teleports, and the most important ones unlock at relatively low Magic levels:

  • Varrock Teleport (Level 25): One of the most used teleports in the game. Gets you to the Grand Exchange in seconds. Requires 1 Law rune, 3 Air runes, 1 Fire rune. With the Varrock diary, you can unlock a Varrock Teleport that lands right outside the GE.
  • Lumbridge Teleport (Level 31): Useful early on. Requires 1 Law, 3 Air, 1 Earth.
  • Falador Teleport (Level 37): Great for mining, farming, and the Falador Park. Requires 1 Law, 3 Air, 1 Water.
  • Camelot Teleport (Level 45): The king of farming runs. Lands you near the Seers' Village bank and the Catherby farming patches. Requires 1 Law, 5 Air.
  • Ardougne Teleport (Level 51): Essential for Plague City quest series and the Ardougne cloak teleport. Requires 2 Law, 2 Water.
  • Watchtower Teleport (Level 58): Useful for the Yanille area. Requires 2 Law, 2 Earth.
  • Trollheim Teleport (Level 61): Key for God Wars Dungeon access. Requires 2 Law, 2 Fire.

Always carry a stack of Law runes plus the elemental runes for your most-used teleports. Air staves can replace the elemental rune requirements, saving inventory space.

② Teleport to House (Player-Owned Homes)

Teleport to House (Level 40 Construction, or using a house tablet) is arguably the most versatile teleport in OSRS. Why? Because inside your house you can have a portal nexus, mounted amulets, and even a fairy ring. By building teleport portals in your house, you can access dozens of destinations from a single teleport.

  • Portal Nexus (Level 76 Construction): The ultimate teleport hub. Can hold up to 30 teleport portal connections. Once built, you can teleport to almost any location in the game from your house.
  • Mounted Amulets: Mount glory, skills, dueling, and other amulets on your wall for unlimited teleports.
  • Fairy Ring in POH (Level 75 Construction): Requires the Fairy Tale II quest but gives you access to the entire fairy ring network from your house.

Even a basic house with a mounted glory amulet and a few portal rooms is a massive quality of life upgrade. You can build a portal room for under 100K if you use limestone bricks and basic oak furniture.

③ Ancient Magicks Teleports

Unlocked by completing the Desert Treasure quest, Ancient Magicks offers four teleports that cover specific endgame areas:

  • Padewwa Teleport (Level 54): Ancient Prison in the God Wars dungeon.
  • Senntisten Teleport (Level 60): Near the Digsite and the Temple of Ikov.
  • Kharyrll Teleport (Level 66): Draynor Village and the Canifis area.
  • Lassar Teleport (Level 72): Ice Mountain and the Wilderness.
  • Dareeyak Teleport (Level 78): Western Wilderness. Be careful in the Wilderness!
  • Carrallangar Teleport (Level 84): Graveyard of Shadows in the Wilderness.
  • Annakarl Teleport (Level 90): Demonic Ruins in the Wilderness.
  • Ghorrock Teleport (Level 96): Frozen Waste Plateau in the Wilderness.

Ancient teleports require blood runes and soul runes, making them expensive to cast. They are mostly used by high-level PvMers and PKers who need rapid access to Wilderness bosses.

④ Fairy Rings — The Secret Network

Fairy rings deserve their own dedicated section below, but it is worth noting here that the fairy ring network is unlocked through the Fairy Tale I quest. Once you have a Dramen or Lunar staff, you can use nearly 50 fairy ring codes to teleport across the entire map. The system is complex but incredibly rewarding. See Section 6 for the full breakdown.

🧭 Teleport Tablets — Grand Exchange, House & Boss Teleports

Teleport tablets are one-handed, stackable items that let you teleport without needing runes, magic levels, or even the correct spellbook. They are created using a player-owned house with a lectern, but you can also buy them directly from the Grand Exchange. Tablets are the preferred travel method for ironmen and mains alike because they are so convenient.

① Grand Exchange Tablets — What to Buy

Here are the most commonly used teleport tablets available on the GE, along with approximate prices (which fluctuate, but these are ballpark 2026 averages):

  • Varrock Teleport Tablet (~500 gp): Grand Exchange access in one click. Stock up on 100+ at a time.
  • Camelot Teleport Tablet (~600 gp): Farming and clue scroll runs. Cheaper than buying runes separately.
  • Ardougne Teleport Tablet (~700 gp): Essential for fairy ring access and Ardougne cloak tasks.
  • Falador Teleport Tablet (~500 gp): Mining Guild and Falador Park.
  • Lumbridge Teleport Tablet (~400 gp): Cheap emergency teleport back to civilization.
  • House Teleport Tablet (~700 gp): If you have a portal nexus at home, this tablet gives you access to dozens of destinations.
  • Boss Teleport Tablets: Various tablet types for God Wars (Saradomin, Zamorak, Bandos, Armadyl), Corporeal Beast, and Kraken. Prices range from 2K to 15K per tablet.

② How to Use Teleport Tablets Efficiently

Tablets work by breaking on use — they are single-use items. To use one, simply have it in your inventory and click it, or right-click and select "Break." The teleport will activate immediately with no cast time (unlike spellbook casting which has a 2-3 second animation). This makes tablets much faster for emergency teleports. Pro tip: You can use tablets while running, while under attack (assuming you are not teleblocked), and even while you are in combat. Just make sure you are not in the Wilderness with a teleblock active.

For clue scrolls, keep a stash of teleport tablets for every major city. It saves inventory space since you can buy exactly the tablets you need for a specific clue step instead of carrying runes for every spell.

③ Cheapest Teleport Options — Budget Travel

If you are on a tight budget, here are the cheapest ways to teleport around Gielinor:

  • Necklace of Passage (Level 1 Magic): Buy one for ~2K gp. It gives 5 charges and teleports you to the Outpost, Wizards' Tower, and the Champion's Guild. Cost per teleport: ~400 gp.
  • Games Necklace (Level 1 Magic): ~1.5K gp for 8 charges. Teleports to Burthorpe, Barbarian Outpost, Corporeal Beast, Tears of Guthix, and Wintertodt. Cost per teleport: ~190 gp.
  • Dueling Ring (Level 1 Magic): ~1.8K gp for 8 charges. Teleports to the Duel Arena (Al Kharid) and Castle Wars. Cost per teleport: ~225 gp.
  • Ring of Dueling (same as dueling ring): Also useful for Clan Wars (ferox enclave) for quick HP and prayer restoration.
  • Necklace of Bonds (Level 1 Magic): ~2K gp for 5 charges. Teleports to the Grand Exchange, Lumbridge Market, and other bond-related areas.
  • Skull Sceptre (Level 1 Magic): A free teleport to the Varrock sewers. Requires killing minotaurs in the Stronghold of Security. Unlimited charges once assembled.

These charged jewelry items cost less than 500 gp per teleport and do not require any magic level. Every new player should buy a Ring of Dueling and a Games Necklace as soon as they have 5K gp to spare.

🏃 Agility Shortcuts — Faster Routes at Every Level

Agility is not just about run energy regeneration. It unlocks physical shortcuts scattered across Gielinor that let you skip long detours. Some shortcuts save 30 seconds or more, and when you are doing hundreds of trips for a skill or boss, those seconds add up to hours.

① Level 20–40 Agility Shortcuts

  • Level 21 — Falador West Wall Crumbling Wall: Shortcut to the Falador farming patch and Cabbage Patch. Saves walking around the wall.
  • Level 22 — Edgeville Dungeon Pipe: Allows you to squeeze through the pipe in Edgeville Dungeon, bypassing the long path around. Key for Hill Giant farming.
  • Level 24 — Yanille Agility Shortcut: Jump from the Yanille wall to the Watchtower. Useful for the Watchtower quest area.
  • Level 26 — Burthorpe Rope Swing: Access to the Burthorpe agility course and the Trollheim area.
  • Level 28 — Varrock Sewers Pipe: Shortcut through the sewers near the Digsite.
  • Level 30 — Ardougne Wall Jump: Jump over the Ardougne wall near the market. Very useful for Ardougne cloak teleport users.
  • Level 34 — Taverley Pipe: Shortcut to the Taverley dungeon entrance.
  • Level 36 — Draynor Manor Loophole: Jump over the Draynor Manor wall near the entrance.
  • Level 40 — Catherby Cliffside: Shortcut near the Catherby bank to the farming patches.

② Level 40–60 Agility Shortcuts

  • Level 42 — Shilo Village Cart: Push the cart to get to the Shilo Village gem mine faster. Requires completion of the Shilo Village quest.
  • Level 45 — Fremennik Slayer Dungeon: Jump over the rocks in the Fremennik Slayer Dungeon for Kurask and Gargoyle tasks.
  • Level 48 — Morytania Swamp Pipe: Shortcut through the swamp to reach Barrows faster. Essential for barrows runs.
  • Level 50 — Ourania Cave Entrance: Shortcut to the ZMI altar. Requires 50 Agility and the Ourania Runecrafting mini-game access.
  • Level 52 — God Wars Dungeon: The infamous "GWD obstacle course." Shortcuts inside the dungeon save massive time when fighting Aviansies, Skeleton Wyverns, and the god generals.
  • Level 56 — Lava Maze Wilderness: Shortcut to the Lava Maze and the Wilderness Agility course.
  • Level 60 — Taverley Dungeon Rocks: Jump over rocks to reach the Black Demon and other high-level monsters faster.

③ Level 60–99 Agility Shortcuts

  • Level 62 — Relekka Lighthouse: Shortcut to the lighthouse and the Dagannoth Kings area.
  • Level 65 — Smoke Devil Dungeon: Jump over obstacles to reach the Smoke Devil boss.
  • Level 66 — Ardougne Sewers: Shortcut through the Ardougne sewers for Plague City quest follow-ups.
  • Level 70 — Zul-Andra: Jump over the bridge to Zulrah. Requires 70 Agility for the most efficient Zulrah trips.
  • Level 72 — Lumbridge Swamp Caves: Shortcut to the Cave Horrors and the Moss Giant Island.
  • Level 75 — Divine Rune Pouch: Not an agility shortcut per se, but the Agility requirement for certain RuneCrafting shortcuts in the Abyss.
  • Level 80+: Most endgame shortcuts are in the Wilderness and Morytania. The Ghormak Ice Cave shortcut (level 82) and the Wintertodt shortcut (level 85) are notable mentions.
  • Level 90+: Elite shortcuts like the one to the Relekka hunting area and the Cerberus shortcut in the Smoke Devil Dungeon.

If you plan to do any bossing, Slayer, or clue scrolls, aim for at least 60 Agility. The shortcuts unlocked at levels 52–60 transform the God Wars Dungeon and Morytania boss trips from frustrating to smooth.

💰 Minigame Teleports — Free Travel Every 20 Minutes

The minigame teleport system is one of the most underrated travel methods for new players. You unlock them immediately with no requirements, and they provide completely free teleports to locations all over the map. Here is every minigame teleport and where it takes you.

① Complete List of Minigame Teleports

  • Barbarian Assault: Teleports to the Barbarian Outpost (near Tree Gnome Stronghold, Baxtorian Falls).
  • Blast Furnace: Teleports to Keldagrim (via the Dwarven mines). Useful for Smithing training.
  • Brimhaven Agility Arena: Teleports to Brimhaven (Karamja).
  • Castle Wars: Teleports to central Kandarin (Catherby, Fishing Guild, Yanille).
  • Celtic Knot (Gnome Restaurant): Teleports to the Tree Gnome Stronghold.
  • Champions' Challenge: Teleports to the Champions' Guild (Varrock).
  • Clan Wars: Teleports to Ferox Enclave (Wilderness edge, free HP/Prayer restore).
  • Duel Arena: Teleports to Al Kharid (desert entrance, Shantay Pass, mining spots).
  • Farming Minigame (Tithe Farm): Teleports to the Hosidius farming area.
  • Fishing Trawler: Teleports to the Fishing Platform (Port Khazard, Gnome Maze).
  • Gnomeball: Teleports to the Tree Gnome Stronghold.
  • League (if applicable): Varies by league.
  • Nightmare Zone: Teleports to Yanille (south of the Watchtower).
  • Pest Control: Teleports to Void Knights' Outpost (Port Sarim island).
  • Pyramid Plunder: Teleports to the Jalsavrah Pyramid (Sophanem desert).
  • Rogues' Den: Teleports to the Rogues' Den in Burthorpe.
  • Sorceress' Garden: Teleports to the Sorceress' Garden (Draynor Manor area).
  • Temple Trekking: Teleports to the Mort'ton area (Morytania).
  • Theatre of Blood: Teleports to Ver Sinhaza (Morytania).
  • Trouble Brewing: Teleports to Mos Le'Harmless.
  • Twisted League (if applicable): Varies.
  • Volcanic Mine: Teleports to the Volcano on Fossil Island.
  • Wintertodt: Teleports to the Wintertodt camp (Great Kourend). Must have started the quest.

② Best Minigame Teleports for Travel

For general travel, the three most useful minigame teleports are:

  • Clan Wars: Free HP and Prayer restore at Ferox Enclave. Use this between slayer tasks to reset without using food or prayer potions.
  • Castle Wars: Drops you right next to Catherby bank. Perfect for fishing, farming runs, and the Farming Guild.
  • Pest Control: Quick access to Port Sarim and the Void Knights' Outpost. Useful for the explorers' ring teleport.

③ How to Reset Minigame Teleport Cooldown

The 20-minute cooldown applies to the entire minigame teleport group. You cannot bypass it except by waiting. However, you can use the minigame teleport to travel to a location, then use a ring of dueling or games necklace to leave, effectively getting two teleports in quick succession. A common strategy is: Minigame teleport to Castle Wars → use a Ring of Dueling to go to the Duel Arena → do your business → Home Teleport back to Lumbridge. This gives you three teleports within a couple of minutes.

🌲 Fairy Rings & Spirit Trees — The Full Network

Fairy rings and spirit trees form a complementary fast-travel network that spans all of Gielinor. Fairy rings are unlocked through the Fairy Tale I quest, and spirit trees require completion of the Tree Gnome Village quest and its follow-ups. Together, they give you access to over 50 teleport destinations.

① How to Unlock Fairy Rings

To unlock fairy rings, you must start or complete the "Fairy Tale I — Growing Pains" quest. Even if you do not finish the quest, starting it gives you access to the fairy rings as long as you have a Dramen staff or a Lunar staff in your inventory or equipped. The Dramen staff is obtained by killing a Dramen tree on Entrana (requires Lost City quest). The Lunar staff is obtained from the Lunar Diplomacy quest. Once you have either staff, you can use any fairy ring in the game. The closest fairy ring to a new player is in the enchanted valley near the Sorceress's Garden (just south of Draynor Manor).

For POH owners with level 75 Construction, you can build a fairy ring in your house. This is the single best travel upgrade in OSRS — you teleport home, step onto the fairy ring, and reach any fairy ring destination instantly.

② Complete Fairy Ring Code List

Here are the most important fairy ring codes organized by region. Each code is a three-letter combination that you input at any fairy ring.

Misthalin / Asgarnia:

  • AIQ: Mudskipper Point (near Piscatoris fishing colony)
  • AJR: Slayer Tower roof (Morytania)
  • AKS: Feldip Hills (near Rantz)
  • ALP: Lighthouse (Dagannoth Kings area)
  • ALS: McGrubor's Wood (near Seers' Village)
  • BIP: Isle of Souls (south of Feldip Hills)
  • BJQ: Ice Mountain (near the Dwarven Mine entrance)
  • BKP: Piscatoris hunting area
  • BJS: Ardougne Zoo (south of Ardougne market)
  • CKP: Cosmic entity plane (near the Cosmic altar)
  • CKS: Canifis (north of the Slayer Tower)

Kandarin / Fremennik:

  • CIQ: Waterbirth Island (Dagannoth Kings)
  • CIR: Mount Karuulm (Wyrm & Hydra dungeon)
  • DIP: Wizards' Tower (south of Draynor)
  • DIR: Gorak plane (near the Cosmic altar)
  • DIS: Lumbridge Castle (behind the castle on the west side)
  • DIU: Ice Queen's lair (north of the Lighthouse)
  • DKR: Morytania mushroom forest (near Barrows)
  • DLQ: Zanaris (the fairy homeworld)
  • DLS: Myreque hideout (Mort'ton)

Wilderness & Misc:

  • BJR: Wilderness (level 50, near the Lava Maze)
  • AKQ: Wilderness (level 45, near the Agility course)
  • DLR: Poison Waste (south of the Tyras Camp)

③ Fast Routes Using Fairy Rings

Some of the fastest routes in OSRS rely on fairy ring combinations:

  • To Barrows: Use fairy ring code DKR (Mort'on mushroom forest) then run south-west to the Barrows entrance.
  • To Zulrah: Use fairy ring code BIP (Isle of Souls) then run south to the Zul-Andra docks.
  • To God Wars Dungeon: Use Trollheim Teleport or fairy ring AJR → ALP combo.
  • To Slayer Tower: Fairy ring CKS or AJR puts you right outside Canifis.
  • To Tree Gnome Stronghold: Fairy ring ALP → AIQ or simply use the spirit tree at the Grand Exchange.

Spirit Trees: Spirit trees are another teleport network unlocked through Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree, and the Hard Farming Diary. Spirit trees connect: Grand Exchange, Tree Gnome Stronghold, Tree Gnome Village, Port Sarim (via the Farming Guild), the Farming Guild (Hosidius), and the POH spirit tree (level 83 Construction). Spirit tree + fairy ring in your POH is the ultimate travel hub.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the fastest teleport in OSRS?

A: The fastest teleport is the House Teleport tablet (instant cast, one click). Combined with a portal nexus or mounted glory in your house, it gives you access to over 30 destinations in seconds.

Q: Do I need Magic levels for teleport tablets?

A: No. Teleport tablets can be used by any player at any Magic level. You just need to have the tablet in your inventory and click it to break it.

Q: What is the cheapest way to travel in OSRS?

A: Walking is free, but the cheapest teleport method is the Home Teleport (free, 30-minute cooldown) or minigame teleports (free, 20-minute cooldown per group). For item-based teleports, the Games Necklace (~190 gp per charge) and Ring of Dueling (~225 gp per charge) are extremely cheap.

Q: How do I unlock fairy rings?

A: Start the Fairy Tale I — Growing Pains quest. Once you have a Dramen staff (from Lost City quest) or a Lunar staff (from Lunar Diplomacy), you can use any fairy ring in the game.

Q: Can I teleport in the Wilderness?

A: Yes, but only if you are below level 20 Wilderness and are not teleblocked. Above level 20 Wilderness, no teleports work except the Mage Arena teleport. If you are teleblocked, wait 30 seconds or drink an imbued heart potion to reduce the duration.

Q: What are the best teleports for clue scrolls?

A: For beginner and easy clues, use the Ardougne cloak, Varrock teleport, and Games Necklace. For hard and elite clues, you will need Camelot Teleport, Watchtower Teleport, and fairy rings for the more remote steps.

Q: How many fairy ring codes are there?

A: There are 47 unique fairy ring codes that lead to different locations around Gielinor. Some codes lead to the same location, so the actual unique destinations number is around 35.

Q: Is it worth building a portal nexus in my POH?

A: Absolutely. The portal nexus (level 76 Construction) is the single best teleport upgrade in the game. It can hold up to 30 teleport connections and can be combined with a mounted glory, mounted amulet, and fairy ring for a complete teleport hub.

Q: What Agility level do I need for God Wars Dungeon shortcuts?

A: You need at least 52 Agility for the main obstacle course inside the God Wars Dungeon. However, 62 Agility is recommended for the additional shortcuts that save even more time.

Q: Can I recharge my run energy faster with Agility?

A: Yes! Each Agility level increases your run energy restoration rate. At level 1 Agility, you recover 1 energy per 2 seconds standing still. At level 99, you recover 1 energy per ~1.33 seconds. You also get a 5% run energy consumption reduction per 10 Agility levels.
