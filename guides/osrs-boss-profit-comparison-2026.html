1. How Boss Profits Are Calculated

Before we dive into the rankings, it's important to understand how boss profit numbers are calculated. Too many guides just quote the wiki's raw drop value without accounting for real-world factors. Here's what actually goes into our 2026 profit numbers:

FactorHow It's CalculatedExample
Unique DropsDrop value divided by drop rate, spread across all kills. A 150M drop at 1/400 = 375K per kill from that unique alone.Enhanced Weapon Seed (140M, 1/400 from CG) = 350K per kill on average
Consistent LootAverage value of non-unique drops per kill (bones, hides, alchables, runes, supplies).Vorkath: 130K-170K per kill in consistent drops
Supply CostsPrayer potions, food, divine potions, runes, bolts, scales, and teleport costs per kill.Zulrah: ~15K supplies per kill. Nex: ~80K supplies per kill (team split).
Death CostsGravestone reclaim cost or death storage fee, amortized across kills assuming 1 death per 50-100 kills depending on boss difficulty.ToB Hard Mode death: ~500K reclaim. Split across 50 kills = 10K per kill.
GE Tax (1%)All sold items incur a 1% GE tax capped at 5M per item. This is subtracted from total profit.Selling a 1.5B Tumeken's Shadow = 5M tax. Spread across expected kills.
Kills Per HourNumber of kill completions per hour at average player skill level — not world record speed. Includes banking time.Vorkath: 24-28 kills/hr with DHCB at average skill, not the theoretical max of 33.

Understanding Average Profit: Boss profit is a long-term average. You might kill Zulrah 500 times without seeing a Tanzanite Fang (1/128), but over 1,000+ kills your profit will converge toward the expected average. Never judge a boss by one dry streak or one lucky hour — play the long game.

Theatre of Blood (Hard Mode) — The Pinnacle

ToB Hard Mode is the most profitable content in OSRS — but also the most punishing. One mistake during Verzik's final phase and the entire team wipes, resetting 30 minutes of progress. The Scythe of Vitur (~800M, 1/19.1 from HM) is the single most valuable boss drop in the game, and the Sanguinesti Staff (~80M) and Avernic Defender hilt (~55M) provide consistent value between scythes.

Reality check: ToB HM requires a dedicated team that knows every room perfectly. You cannot "learn" Hard Mode through random teams. Join a PvM clan, master Normal Mode first, and only attempt HM when you can complete Normal Mode deathless at least 8 out of 10 runs.

Corrupted Gauntlet — The Best Mid-Game Money Maker (Zero Cost!)

Corrupted Gauntlet is unique among S-tier bosses because it costs zero GP to attempt. No supplies, no gear repairs, no death fees. You walk in with nothing and everything you earn is 100% pure profit. The Enhanced Weapon Seed (~140M, 1/400) and Armor Seeds (~9M, 1/50) drive the profit, but the real magic is the Crystal Crown drop table buff in June 2026 that increased average loot per chest by roughly 15-20%.

Vorkath — The People's Money Printer

Vorkath is the most reliable solo boss in OSRS. After completing Dragon Slayer II, you unlock a boss that averages 130K-170K in guaranteed drops per kill — no unique required. The consistent loot (superior dragon bones ~20K, 2 blue d'hides ~5K, and alchables) means you make solid profit even on your worst hour. With a Dragon Hunter Crossbow and Void ranged, you'll average 26 kills/hour for roughly 3.5M GP/hr.

Vorkath Profit Per Kill (2026 GE Prices): Superior Dragon Bones (19K) + 2 Blue Dragonhides (4.5K) + Alchable drops average (80K-130K) + Rune/Dragon items (20K) = ~130K-170K per kill. Multiplied by 24-28 kills/hr = 3.1M-4.8M raw GP. Subtract ~200K/hr in supplies (prayer pots, ranging pots, sharks, house tabs, ruby/diamond bolts) = 2.9M-4.6M net GP/hr. Vorkath's head (1/50) is worth 10K each from Ava's accumulator upgrades or the Assembler — a minor bonus that adds up over time.

Zulrah — The Rotation Boss with Consistent Profit

Zulrah's profit comes primarily from scales (150-250 per kill, ~250 GP each = 40K-60K per kill in scales alone) plus the two Fang drops at 1/128 each. The Sanguinesti Staff buff in 2026 made the magic phase roughly 15% faster, bumping kills/hour from ~24 to ~27-28 for experienced players. The rotation learning curve takes 2-4 hours to memorize, but once mastered, Zulrah becomes a near-automatic 3M/hr money printer.

Phantom Muspah — The Most Consistent A-Tier Boss

Muspah's Ancient Essence drops (100+ per kill, ~1.5K each) provide a guaranteed 150K+ per kill in value — no unique items needed. The Venator Bow shard (1/100, ~6M) adds another 60K per kill on average. Combined with alchable drops and supply drops (prayer potions, ranging potions, and noted supplies), Muspah delivers the most consistent profit of any A-tier boss. The Frozen Cache shield phase is the only mechanical complexity, and it's forgiving enough that one mistake doesn't end your trip.

God Wars Dungeon Bosses — The B-Tier Backbone

The four God Wars bosses have been OSRS staples for over a decade. General Graardor is the most popular — simple mechanics (tank and spank with a prayer switch for ranged minion), the Bandos Chestplate (28M) and Tassets (22M) hold value well. K'ril Tsutsaroth (Zamorak) offers the most consistent unique drop rate with the Zamorakian Spear at 1/128. Kree'arra (Armadyl) has the highest-value uniques but is the most punishing solo — the ranged chip damage from minions adds up fast without high Defense and good gear.

GWD Solo Strategies: Graardor: Melee + Protect from Melee, kill melee minion during boss death animation to save prayer. K'ril: Protect from Melee, freeze method with Blood Barrage optional for extended trips. Zilyana: Ranged kiting around the room, avoid her melee hit which hits 31. Kree'arra: Ranged only, chinchompas for minions, expect shorter trips without high Defense.

Dagannoth Kings — Three Bosses, Three Profits

Dagannoth Kings become profitable once you can kill all three in a rotation before the first one respawns. The Berserker Ring (2.5M, 1/128 from Rex) and Archer Ring (2.8M, 1/128 from Supreme) are your main profit drivers, with the Seers Ring (1.2M, 1/128 from Prime) as a bonus. Each DK also drops noted dagannoth bones (125 base Prayer XP each) and a variety of alchables. With a tribrid setup (Melee for Rex, Ranged for Supreme, Magic for Prime), you can sustain 40-60 kills/hour total for 800K-1.5M GP/hr.

Barrows — The Gateway to Bossing

Barrows is the quintessential first boss. Six brothers, six crypts, one chest at the center. With Iban's Blast (50+ Magic) and Protect from Melee (43+ Prayer), you can farm Barrows at combat level 60+. The Morytania Hard Diary doubles your rune drops, making each chest give 30K-60K in runes alone. With 86.2% potential (kill all 6 brothers + 2 skeletons + 1 bloodworm), you maximize your chance at Barrows equipment while still getting good rune rewards.

The Boss Progression Path for New Players

Recommended Boss Learning Order: Start with Barrows (learn prayer switching) → Sarachnis (learn prayer flicking under pressure) → Vorkath (learn movement + special attacks) → Zulrah (learn rotation memorization + gear switching) → Corrupted Gauntlet (learn prep phase + survival) → Tombs of Amascut (learn raid mechanics + solo optimization). Each boss teaches skills that make the next one easier.

Expected time to profitability: Barrows = 30 min to profit. Sarachnis = 2 hours. Vorkath = 4 hours (including DS2). Zulrah = 8 hours (including 50+ deaths). CG = 15-25 hours (including 30-80 deaths). ToA Expert = 60+ hours from zero raid experience.

Don't rush to the highest GP/hr boss. Rush to the boss that's slightly above your current skill level. Profits will follow naturally as your PvM skills improve.

8. FAQ + Final Boss Recommendation

Q: Which boss should I farm with 70 combat stats and 5M gear?

A: Barrows is your starting point. With Iban's Blast (upgrade from Underground Pass) and 43 Prayer, you can farm Barrows at combat level 60-70. Invest your first 5M in: Iban's Staff (free from quest), Mystic robes (~300K), Occult Necklace (~500K), Book of Darkness (~200K), and prayer potions. Barrows will fund your progression to Vorkath and beyond.

Q: What boss has the best profit-to-effort ratio?

A: Vorkath is the undisputed king of profit-to-effort. Once you learn the 4 mechanics (acid phase, fireball phase, spawn phase, auto-attack prayer switching), Vorkath requires zero mental energy. You can watch YouTube on a second monitor while making 3M-4M GP/hr. No other boss offers this combination of profit and brainlessness.

Q: Which boss has the lowest requirements for 1M+ GP/hr?

A: Zulrah technically has the lowest requirements (Regicide + 75 Magic/Ranged), but the learning curve is significant. In practice, Vorkath (Dragon Slayer II + 80 Ranged) is easier to learn and more consistent. For truly low requirements, Barrows with Morytania Hard Diary reaches ~800K-1M GP/hr with just 60 Magic and 43 Prayer.

Q: Are Wilderness bosses worth doing for profit?

A: Generally no for pure profit. While bosses like Callisto, Venenatis, and Vet'ion can reach 1-2M GP/hr, PKer interruptions reduce effective profit by 30-60% depending on time of day. The Voidwaker pieces from multi-boss variants (Artio, Spindel, Calvar'ion) can be profitable if you're willing to risk cheap gear and teleport at the first sign of PKers. For most players, stick to safe-zone bosses.

Q: What gear do I need before attempting bosses?

A: Here's the minimum viable gear per boss tier:

  • Barrows/Sarachnis: Iban's Blast or Trident of the Seas, Mystic robes, 43 Prayer, prayer potions. Budget: ~2M.
  • Vorkath: Blowpipe (or Dragon Crossbow) + Void ranged (or blessed d'hide). Budget: ~15M for Blowpipe + supplies.
  • Zulrah: Trident of the Swamp + Blowpipe + Ahrim's + God d'hide. Budget: ~20M.
  • God Wars Solo: 85+ combat stats, Barrows/GWD gear, 70+ Prayer (Piety). Budget: ~30M+.
  • Corrupted Gauntlet: Nothing. Zero. It's free. Just your stats and skill.

Q: Final Boss Recommendation Flowchart

If you have <5M and 60 combat: Barrows → save GP → buy better gear

If you have 5M-20M and 70-80 combat: Sarachnis or Rex safe-spot → get rings → sell for upgrades

If you have 20M-50M and 80+ Ranged with DS2 done: Vorkath → consistent 3M/hr → fund everything else

If you have 50M+ and want to learn harder bosses: Zulrah → learn rotations → 2.5M-3.5M/hr

If you have 80+ combat and Song of the Elves done (and patience): Corrupted Gauntlet → zero cost, 4M-7M/hr potential, best mid-game boss in 2026

If you have 100M+ gear and 90+ combat: Tombs of Amascut Normal → learn raid mechanics → progress to Expert for 8M+

If you have a 1B+ bank and a dedicated team: Theatre of Blood or Nex → the true endgame, 10M-15M/hr

Final Pro Tip — Diversify Your Bosses: Don't farm one boss exclusively. The GE market fluctuates. Zulrah scales rose from 180 GP to 320 GP in 2025 when a new piece of content consumed scales. Bandos crashed 20% when Torva was released but recovered 6 months later. Farming 2-3 bosses means you're hedged against market shifts. Vorkath + Zulrah + Muspah covers 3 different drop tables and virtually guarantees one of them is always in a favorable market position.
