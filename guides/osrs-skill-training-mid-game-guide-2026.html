1. What "Mid-Game" Means for Skills

The transition from early game to mid-game is one of the most exciting — and confusing — phases in OSRS. You are no longer a complete beginner, but you are not yet tackling endgame content like Raids, Chambers of Xeric, or maxing your account. Understanding exactly where you stand helps you make smarter training decisions and avoid wasting GP on methods that don't match your current level.

📊 The Mid-Game Skill Definition

In the OSRS community, "mid-game" for skills is generally defined as having your core combat stats in the 50-70 range, with most other skills sitting between 40 and 60. This is the phase where you have unlocked enough content to train efficiently, but you have not yet reached the point where the absolute fastest methods (like Invention training or extreme bossing) become available.

🎯 Typical Mid-Game Account Profile

  • Combat Level: 70-90 total combat level
  • Attack/Strength/Defence: 50-65 each
  • Ranged: 50-60 (Chinchompa training starting)
  • Magic: 55-65 (High Alchemy unlocked, advanced spells accessible)
  • Prayer: 43-52 (Protect prayers unlocked, starting Dragon bones)
  • Hitpoints: 60-70 (gained naturally through combat)
  • Most other skills: 40-60 range
  • Quest Points: 100-150 (enough for most quest requirements)

⚠️ When to Switch From Beginner Methods

One of the biggest mistakes mid-game players make is continuing to use beginner training methods long after they have become inefficient. Here are the clear signals that it is time to upgrade your training method:

Signal 1: Your XP Per Hour Drops Below 30K/Hr for Combat

If you are still fighting Rock Crabs or Sand Crabs at level 60+ combat, you are leaving massive XP on the table. At this stage, you should be transitioning to >strong>Slayer or controlled dungeon grinding (Catacombs of Kourend, Forthos Dungeon, etc.) that gives 40-60K combat XP per hour plus valuable drops.

Signal 2: You Have 500K+ GP and Still Burying Bones

Once you have half a million GP, Dragon bones at a Gilded Altar become affordable. Burying bones gives 72 XP each; offering at a Gilded Altar gives 300 XP each. That is more than a 4x efficiency increase for the same bone. Stop burying and start using altars.

Signal 3: Your Gathering Skills Are Above 45 But Still Using Level 1 Methods

At level 45 Fishing, you should be catching Lobsters (yielding 70K+ GP per hour) instead of Trout/Salmon. At level 41 Mining, you should be at Motherlode Mine (AFK, decent XP) instead of Copper/Tin. Recognize when a method has become obsolete and look up the next unlock.

💡 The Mid-Game Mindset Shift

Early game is about unlocking content. Mid-game is about optimizing efficiency and profit. Every training session should ideally make you GP while gaining XP, or at minimum not cost you GP. If you are spending more than 100K GP per hour on training at mid-game, you are doing something wrong — there are almost always profitable or break-even methods available at this stage.

2. Combat Skills at Mid-Level (50-70+)

Combat is the heart of OSRS, and mid-game is when combat training truly opens up. You are no longer limited to crablets and cows — you now have access to meaningful PvM content that rewards you with valuable loot while training your stats.

⚔️ Attack, Strength, Defence (50-70)

At level 50, you have outgrown Sand Crabs as an efficient training method. Here is the optimal progression path for your melee stats:

📍 Level 50-60 Melee Training

Best Method: Slayer (get a Slayer task from Vannaka at level 50+ Combat). Slayer gives comparable XP to crab training while also dropping valuable loot (Rune items, herbs, seeds). You also unlock the ability to use the Dragon Scimitar at 60 Attack (requires Monkey Madness I completion, which should be a priority mid-game quest).

Alternative (Faster XP, No Loot): Catacombs of Kourend — dense monster spawns, aggressive monsters, and the area effect (need 75% KQ head for totem) boost XP rates to 50-60K per hour. Bring food and a weapon with a special attack for quick kills.

AFK Option: 一级Woodcutting Hill Giants (or Brutal Green Dragons if you have 55 Slayer) — decent XP, decent loot, relatively AFK with Protect from Melee active.

📍 Level 60-70 Melee Training

Best Method: Continue Slayer with Chaeldar (requires 60+ Combat and completion of Lost City). Chaeldar assigns higher-level monsters that drop better loot. At this stage, Slayer becomes genuinely profitable (2-3M GP per hour on good tasks like Gargoyles, Nechryael, or Dust Devils).

Gear Upgrade Priority: Dragon Scimitar (60 Attack) → Abyssal Whip (70 Attack, very expensive but best training weapon) → Dragon Defender (requires 60 Attack + 60 Defence + Warrior's Guild access). The Dragon Defender alone increases your DPS by ~20% compared to Dragon Scimitar.

🏹 Ranged (50-70)

Mid-game Ranged training is dominated by one method: Chinchompas. At level 55 Ranged, you unlock the ability to use Red Chinchompas, which are the single best Ranged training method from 55 to 99.

💡 Chinchompa Training Tips

Always use short fuse chinchompas for maximum XP per hour (they hit faster). Set up at multi-combat areas like the Waterfall Dungeon or Catacombs of Kourend (be careful of PKers in multi-Wilderness areas). Bring ranging potions to boost your accuracy. At level 60 Ranged, consider completing Monkey Madness II — it unlocks the Dragon Crossbow and Rune Crossbow, massive upgrades.

✨ Magic (55-70)

Magic training at mid-game splits into two paths: combat Magic (training by fighting) and utility Magic (training via spell casting on NPCs orAlchemy).

Combat Magic Training (55-70)

Best Method: Casting offensive spells on Slayer tasks. At level 55 Magic, you unlock High Alchemy (a utility spell that turns items into GP — critical for your economy). For combat, use Fire Wave (level 65) or Claws of Guthix (after Mage Arena) for high damage. The Trident of the Seas (requires 75 Magic to use, but achievable at mid-game with quest unlocks) is the ultimate mid-game Magic weapon.

AFK Magic Training: Casting offensive spells on Sand Crabs with Shallow Water run setups. Very AFK, ~35K Magic XP per hour, costs ~200K GP per hour in runes.

Utility Magic Training (55-70)

High Alchemy: After completing Merlin's Crystal (unlocks High Alch), you can train Magic for free (or even profit) by alchemizing items you obtain from skilling or PvM. At level 55 Magic, High Alch gives 65 XP per cast and turns any tradeable item into GP at 60% of its GE value. This is the single best GP-generation method for mid-game players with excess inventory space.

Enchanting: Enchanting jewelry (like Sapphire or Emerald amulets) gives decent XP and can be profitable if you buy uncut gems, cut them, string the amulets, and then enchant. Requires some setup but can be done while watching a video.

🙏 Prayer (43-70)

At mid-game, Prayer training shifts from burying bones to using Gilded Altars or the Chaos Altar. The goal is to reach level 70 Prayer for Piety (the best melee prayer until 95 Prayer's Augury/Rigour alternatives).

🏠 Optimal Prayer Training at Mid-Game

  • Level 43-52: Dragon bones at Gilded Altar (300 XP per bone). Need ~1,850 bones for 52 Prayer. Cost: ~4.5M GP. Time: ~6 hours.
  • Level 52-70: Continue Dragon bones OR switch to Lava Dragon bones (354 XP per bone at altar) for faster rates. Need ~6,500 total bones from 43 to 70. Cost: ~18M GP total.
  • Budget Option: Chaos Altar in level 38 Wilderness. Bring 14 unnoted bones per trip (use a friend or alternate account to note them). Risky but saves 100% on altar costs (no GP spent on bones if you collect them yourself).
  • Free Option: Ensouled heads (Reanimate spells). At 82 Magic, you can reanimate Ensouled Wolf heads for 520 XP each. Very slow but completely free if you collect heads yourself.

❤️ Hitpoints (60-70+)

Hitpoints train passively as you take damage during combat. At mid-game, you should be gaining HP XP naturally through Slayer and bossing. If you need to boost HP specifically:

3. Gathering Skills — Scaling Up

Gathering skills (Mining, Fishing, Woodcutting, Farming, Hunter, Firemaking) are the foundation of your GP economy. At mid-game, these skills transition from "slow and barely profitable" to "decent XP with real GP per hour." This is when skilling starts to pay for your combat training.

⛏️ Mining (41-60+)

Mining at mid-game is defined by the choice between Motherlode Mine (AFK, decent XP) and active mining (higher XP, higher GP).

📍 Level 41-60 Mining

AFK Method: Motherlode Mine (requires 30 Mining to enter, but 41+ for better pay-dirt). XP rate: ~25-30K per hour. GP rate: ~100-150K per hour (if you process pay-dirt through the washer and then bank the ores). Extremely AFK — you can watch a video while clicking every 2-3 minutes.

Active Method: Granite mining (3-tick with granite hammer from the Corsair Curse quest). At level 45 Mining, mine 5kg and 2kg granite blocks. XP rate: ~45-50K per hour. GP rate: ~200K per hour. Requires active gameplay but much faster than MLM.

Best Balance: Blast Mine (requires 72 Mining) — not accessible until late mid-game, but once unlocked, it gives 72K+ XP per hour and 400K+ GP per hour. This is the ultimate mid-game Mining method.

🐟 Fishing (50-70+)

Fishing is one of the most relaxed mid-game skills. At level 50, you unlock Lobsters, which are the first truly profitable fish.

After completing Tai Bwo Wannai Trio (a mid-game quest), you can fish Karambwans at level 65 Fishing. They are used for combating potions (combine with Super combat potions for boosted stats) and are always in demand. Even at level 50 Fishing (with the quest completed), you can catch them at a slower rate. This is one of the most consistent money-makers in the game.

🪓 Woodcutting (50-70+)

Woodcutting at mid-game is about choosing between AFK methods (Teak/Mahogany trees) and active methods (Magic/Redwood trees).

Level 50-60: Teak Trees (AFK)

Plant Teak trees in farming patches (Prifddinas). They grow in ~4 hours and give 7150 XP each. This is technically Farming, but the logs can be sold for ~400 GP each (decent GP). Alternatively, cut Teak trees on Ape Atoll (after Monkey Madness I) for ~35K XP per hour AFK.

Level 60-70: Mahogany Trees / Maple Trees

Mahogany trees on Ape Atoll give ~30K XP per hour and ~300K GP per hour (logs sell for ~450 GP each). Requires respawn timers management. Maple trees (level 45) are easier to access and give ~25K XP per hour with ~150K GP per hour. Not amazing, but very AFK.

Best Method (Late Mid-Game): Redwood trees (level 90, not accessible yet) — skip for now. Focus on buying teak/mahogany logs from the Grand Exchange and using them for Firemaking training instead (see below).

🌱 Farming (45-65+)

Farming is unique — it gives XP in bursts (plant, wait, harvest) rather than continuously. At mid-game, Farming becomes genuinely profitable through herb runs and tree runs.

🌿 Optimal Mid-Game Farming Routine

  • Herb Runs (Every 80 minutes): Plant Ranarr weeds (level 32) or Snapdragon (level 62) in all available herb patches. Harvest after 80 minutes. Profit: ~200-500K GP per run (depending on herb prices). XP: ~1,500-3,000 per run.
  • Tree Runs (Every 4 hours): Plant Teak (level 35) or Mahogany (level 55) in hardwood patches. Also plant fruit trees (level 33+) in all fruit tree patches. Profit: ~100-300K GP per run. XP: ~5,000-10,000 per run.
  • Flower Patches: Use these for Tithe Farm (mini-game unlocked after 34 Farming) — gives Farming XP and a chance at Farmer's outfit pieces (which boost Farming XP by 2.5% per piece).
  • Specialty: Giant Seaweed (requires 23 Farming) — plant in the underwater patches near the Whirlpool. Grows in 40 minutes with compost. Great for Construction training later.

🦊 Hunter (50-70+)

Hunter is one of the most profitable skills at mid-game. At level 50, you unlock Red Chinchompas — the single best Ranged training tool in the game.

📍 Level 50-70 Hunter

Red Chinchompas (level 50-63): Hunt in the Feldip Hills (south of Yanille). Each chin takes ~2 minutes to catch. XP rate: ~60-80K per hour. GP rate: ~250-350K per hour (Red Chins sell for ~500 GP each). Absolute best mid-game Hunter method.

Black Chinchompas (level 67+): Hunt in the Wilderness (level 32-36 Wildy). Much more dangerous (PKers) but Chins sell for ~1,200 GP each. XP rate: ~50-70K per hour. GP rate: ~400-600K per hour. Only attempt with Protect Item prayer and escape teleports ready.

Herbiboar (level 80+): Not accessible at mid-game, but worth noting for late mid-game. Gives Herblore XP and Hunter XP simultaneously.

🔥 Firemaking (50-70+)

Firemaking is boring but essential — and Wintertodt makes it both profitable and fast. At level 50, you can start Wintertod, a boss-like activity that gives Firemaking XP, HP XP, and valuable loot (seed packs, herb boxes, ore packs).

Plus, the reward points can be spent on herb boxes (used for Herblore training) and seed packs (used for Farming). Do Wintertodt until 99 Firemaking — it is free and profitable.

4. Artisan Skills — Profitability Turns Positive

Artisan skills (Smithing, Cooking, Crafting, Herblore, Fletching, Runecrafting, Construction) are often expensive to train. However, at mid-game levels, many of these skills become profitable or at least break-even. This is when skilling starts to fund your PvM gear instead of draining your bank.

🔨 Smithing (50-70+)

Smithing at mid-game is transformed by two methods: Blast Furnace (requires 60 Smithing for best results) and Giant's Foundry (unlocked after Forsaken Tower quest, requires 60 Smithing).

📍 Blast Furnace (Level 30-60)

How it works: Blast Furnace is a mini-game where you smelt bars using half the coal of regular furnaces. At level 30 Smithing, you can smelt Steel bars (profit: ~100K GP per hour). At level 50, you can smelt Mithril bars (profit: ~200K GP per hour). At level 60, you can smelt Adamant bars (profit: ~400K GP per hour).

XP Rates: ~50-60K Smithing XP per hour at level 60+ (making Adamant bars). This is the fastest Smithing method until Giant's Foundry.

Requirements: 30 Smithing to use; 60 for best XP. Also need 3,000-6,000 GP per hour for the foreman's fee (unless you have completed the Hard Kandarin Diary for free entry).

📍 Giant's Foundry (Level 60-80+)

How it works: After completing The Forsaken Tower quest, you unlock Giant's Foundry — a mini-game where you create giant swords from 2-handed sword pieces. You collect sword pieces by smithing them (using bars) or buying them from the Grand Exchange.

XP Rates: ~70-90K Smithing XP per hour at level 70+. GP rates: ~200-400K per hour (depending on sword quality). This is the best mid-to-late game Smithing method.

Strategy: Collect preserved sword pieces from the reward shop and use them to make high-quality swords. Higher quality = more XP and more GP.

🍳 Cooking (50-70+)

Cooking is one of the easiest mid-game skills. At level 50, you unlock Lobsters; at level 62, Swordfish; at level 80, Sharks (technically late mid-game).

At level 32 Cooking (and 100K GP), you can enter the Cooking Guild in west Ardougne. It has multiple ranges and never burns high-level food (once you have the requisite level). This is the best place to cook in the game. Also, consider completing Gheradic's quest for the Cooking Gauntlets — they reduce the burn chance for specific foods.

💎 Crafting (50-70+)

Crafting is usually expensive, but at mid-game, there are ways to train it profitably.

Level 50-63: Battlestaves (Profit!)

Buy unpowered orbs and battlestaff halves from the Grand Exchange. Attach orbs to staves (requires 54 Crafting). Then buy earth orbs or water orbs and attach them to the staves. Each complete battlestaff sells for more than the cost of materials. Profit: ~100-200K GP per hour. XP rate: ~60-80K per hour.

Level 63-70: Dragonstones / Gem Cutting

Cut uncut dragonstones into cut dragonstones (requires 55 Crafting). Each dragonstone gives 137.5 XP. Cost: ~5,000 GP per dragonstone. Total cost to 70 Crafting: ~8M GP. This is expensive but very fast (~200K XP per hour). Alternatively, make dragonstone bolts (requires 71 Fletching + 55 Crafting) for slightly better XP rates.

🌿 Herblore (45-70+)

Herblore is infamous for being expensive, but at mid-game, you can start making profit on certain potions. The key is to identify which potions are in high demand and which herbs are cheap.

💊 Mid-Game Herblore — From Loss to Profit

  • Level 45-55: Make Strength potions (harralander + limpwurt root). Cost: ~200K to 55. XP: ~50K per hour.
  • Level 55-65: Make Prayer potions (ranarr weed + snape grass). Cost: ~2M to 65. XP: ~70K per hour. Prayer potions are always in demand.
  • Level 65-70: Make Super Attack/Strength/Defence potions (avantoe + frog spawn / dwarf weed + mort myre fungus). Cost: ~3M to 70. XP: ~80K per hour.
  • Profit Method: Wintertodt herb boxes — each box contains 5-14 herbs (random). Save these for Herblore training. Also, Farming herb runs give free herbs for Herblore.
  • Best GP Source: Super combat potions (after 75 Herblore) — very profitable to make if you have the herbs.

🏹 Fletching (50-70+)

Fletching is one of the fastest skills to train in OSRS. At mid-game, you can reach 70 Fletching in a single day of focused training.

Many players get 99 Fletching in a single day because it is so fast and AFK-able. At mid-game (50-70), you can easily gain 20 levels in 3-4 hours of focused fletching. If you have 5-10M GP to spare, consider pushing to 70+ Fletching immediately — it unlocks better arrows and bolts for Ranged training.

✨ Runecrafting (44-70+)

Runecrafting is widely considered the most tedious skill in OSRS. At mid-game, your options are limited, but the Abyss method (unlocked after Enter the Abyss mini-quest) makes it significantly more bearable.

Level 44-66: Abyss Method (Nature Runes)

At level 44 Runecrafting, you can craft Nature Runes (2 per essence at level 44, 3 per essence at level 91). Each Nature rune sells for ~200 GP. The Abyss method involves entering the Abyss, repairing the broken rifts, and teleporting to the Nature altar. XP rate: ~25-35K per hour. GP rate: ~150-250K per hour.

Level 66-77: Abyss Method (Law Runes + Cosmic Runes)

At level 66, you can craft Death Runes (2 per essence). Death runes sell for ~180 GP each. XP rate: ~35-45K per hour. GP rate: ~200-300K per hour. This is the best mid-game Runecrafting method for both XP and profit.

Alternative: Ourania Altar (unlocked after completion of Tale of the Left Hand quest). You can craft any rune here (random) using a single altar. XP rate: ~30-40K per hour. More AFK than Abyss but slightly slower XP.

🏠 Construction (50-70+)

Construction is expensive but essential for mid-game players. Your Player-Owned House (POH) is a hub for teleportation, prayer training (Gilded Altar), and utility.

🏗️ Essential Mid-Game Construction Upgrades

Level 47-50: Build a Gilded Altar in your Chapel (requires 2,000 GP for materials + 1,000 GP for burners). This is the single best Construction upgrade — it enables 350% Prayer XP per bone.

Level 58: Build a Portal Nexus in your POH (requires 58 Construction + 1,500 GP materials). Add teleport tablets to it (buy from GE or make yourself). This gives you one-click teleport to 8+ locations. Saves hours of run time over your OSRS career.

Level 65: Build a Jewelry Box (requires 65 Construction + 3,000 GP materials). Store all your teleport jewelry in one compact box. Essential for efficient training.

Level 70+: Build Spirit Trees (requires 83 Farming + 70 Construction). Connects your POH to the Spirit Tree network for even more teleport options.

Training Method: Oak dungeon doors (3 XP per nail) or Mahogany tables (costs GP but gives 560 XP per table). Most players buy GP bonds to fund Construction training.

5. Support Skills Unlock Real Power

Support skills (Agility, Thieving, Slayer) don't produce tangible items, but they dramatically improve your efficiency and unlock access to the best content in the game. At mid-game, these skills transition from "annoying chores" to "genuinely useful utilities."

🏃 Agility (50-70+)

Agility is hated by many players, but it is secretly one of the most impactful skills. Higher Agility reduces run energy drain, gives access to shortcuts, and unlocks the best XP rates in the game through Hallowed Sepulchre (level 72+) and Arceuus rooftop course (level 60+).

📍 Level 50-60: Draynor Village Rooftop

Location: Draynor Village (south of Varrock). XP Rate: ~25-30K per hour. Full Completion: ~1.5-2 hours to 60 Agility. This is the best pre-60 course. The Mark of Grace (collect 10) unlocks the Graceful outfit (reduces run energy drain by 30%). Getting full Graceful should be a top priority at mid-game.

📍 Level 60-70: Arceuus Rooftop Course

Location: Arceuus (Kourend). XP Rate: ~35-40K per hour. Full Completion: ~2-3 hours to 70 Agility. This course is longer but gives more XP per lap. Also, you can collect Ancestor spirit if you have 72 Agility for Hallowed Sepulchre (the best Agility training in the game).

Alternative (Late Mid-Game): Hallowed Sepulchre (level 72 Agility, unlocked after Sins of the Father quest). XP rate: 60-80K per hour. GP rate: ~200-400K per hour (from loot chests). This is the absolute best Agility training method in the game and should be your goal for late mid-game.

💡 Graceful Outfit — Your First Priority

The Graceful outfit is the single most important item set for mid-game players. It reduces run energy drain by 30% and increases run energy restoration by 15%. This means you can run longer and restore energy faster. It is essential for efficient skilling and Slayer. Collect 10 Marks of Grace from any rooftop course (Draynor gives them fastest at low levels) and exchange them with Uriel Razor in the Rogues' Den.

🗡️ Thieving (50-70+)

Thieving is a fantastic mid-game skill — it gives decent XP, gives GP, and unlocks very useful utilities (like the Ardougne Chest for teleportation).

Pyramid Plunder — The Best Mid-Game Thieving Method

After completing Icthlarin's Little Helper (a short quest), you can access Pyramid Plunder in Sophanem. You have 5 minutes per room to loot as many urns and sarcophagi as possible. Each room gives Thieving XP and valuable loot (gold artifacts, stone artifacts, etc.). At level 65, you can loot rooms 1-5 efficiently. This is the best balance of XP and GP at mid-game Thieving.

⚔️ Slayer (50-70+)

Slayer is not a separate skill but a skill modifier — it makes your combat training meaningful by assigning tasks that give XP and loot. At mid-game, Slayer becomes the primary way you train combat.

🎯 Mid-Game Slayer Progression

  • Level 50-60 Combat: Get tasks from Vannaka (Edgeville Dungeon). He assigns low-to-mid level monsters (Hill Giants, Moss Giants, Goblins, etc.). XP rate: ~30-40K combat XP per hour. GP rate: ~100-200K per hour.
  • Level 60-70 Combat: Get tasks from Chaeldar (Zanaris). She assigns higher-level monsters (Gargoyles, Nechryael, Dust Devils, etc.). XP rate: ~40-50K combat XP per hour. GP rate: ~200-400K per hour.
  • Level 70-85 Combat: Get tasks from Duradel (Shilo Village, requires Shilo Village quest). He assigns the best mid-to-high level monsters (Dagannoth, Trolls,Suqahs, etc.). XP rate: ~50-60K combat XP per hour. GP rate: ~300-600K per hour.
  • Slayer Helmets: At level 55 Slayer (and completion of Priest in Peril + Jungle Potion), you can combine a Black mask with earmuffs, nose peg, etc. to make a Slayer Helmet. This gives +15% accuracy and damage on Slayer tasks. Essential for efficient Slayer.

6. Balancing Skill Training With Other Goals

One of the hardest parts of mid-game is deciding what to focus on. Unlike early game (where the path is linear) or endgame (where you have specific goals like Raids), mid-game offers so many options that it's easy to get paralyzed or waste time on inefficient activities.

📅 The 40-30-20-10 Rule

Here is a simple framework for dividing your play time at mid-game. Adjust based on your personal goals, but this is a solid baseline:

⏱️ Weekly Time Allocation (for active players)

  • 40% Combat/PvM: Slayer tasks, bossing, Dungeoneering. This is where you gain combat levels and get the best loot. Prioritize Slayer above all else — it gives combat XP, GP, and unlock supplies simultaneously.
  • 30% Money Making: Farming runs, Hunter (Chins), Fishing (Sharks), Wintertodt (herb boxes), Crafting (battlestaves). Always be generating GP so you can afford better gear and training methods.
  • 20% Skill Gaps: Identify skills that are holding you back (usually Prayer, Herblore, Construction) and push them to the next breakpoint. Don't try to max every skill — just push the ones that unlock meaningful content.
  • 10% Quests/Diaries: Complete quests that unlock content (Monkey Madness I/II, Lost City, Shilo Village, etc.). Also start working on Achievement Diaries (easy/medium) for the rewards (GP, teleports, XP lamps).

🚀 Skills to Prioritize

These are the skills that give the highest "return on investment" at mid-game. Push them as high as you can afford:

Priority 1: Prayer (to 70 for Piety)

Piety (+23% Melee stats) is the single biggest combat DPS increase available at mid-game. Save up 15-20M GP and push Prayer to 70 as soon as possible. This pays for itself through faster Slayer kills and better boss kills.

Priority 2: Slayer (to 75+ for Duradel)

Duradel assigns the best tasks. Every Slayer level unlocks new monsters. Push Slayer by doing tasks — don't skip them. The unlocks (like the Slayer Helmet, Broad bolts/arrows, and Superior Slayer equipment) are game-changing.

Priority 3: Herblore (to 70 for Super Combat)

Super Combat potions (+118% melee stats) make a massive difference in mid-game PvM. They require 75 Herblore to make (or 70 to use if bought from GE). Save your Wintertodt herb boxes and Farming herbs for this push.

💡 The "Bottleneck" Concept

Identify which skill is your current bottleneck — the one skill that, if raised, would unlock significantly better training methods or gear. For most mid-game players, the bottleneck is Prayer (can't use Piety) or Slayer (can't kill high-level monsters). Focus your GP and time on removing that bottleneck before spreading your effort across multiple skills.

8. Mid-Game Training FAQs

What is the single most efficient thing I can do at mid-game?

Get your Slayer level to 75 and start getting tasks from Duradel. Slayer is the most efficient use of your time because it gives combat XP, GP, and supplies simultaneously. A single Slayer task at mid-game can give you 50K combat XP, 200K GP in loot, and herbs/seeds for Herblore/Farming. No other activity comes close in terms of account value per hour.

How much GP should I have at mid-game, and how do I make more?

A healthy mid-game bank has 10-30M GP. This allows you to buy supplies for training, upgrade gear, and push key skills (like Prayer to 70). To make GP at mid-game: (1) Farming herb runs (200-500K per run), (2) Hunter (Red Chins) (250-350K per hour), (3) Fishing (Sharks or Karambwans) (200-300K per hour), (4) Wintertodt (herb boxes + loot worth 200-400K per hour), (5) Slayer tasks (100-400K profit per task depending on assignment).

Should I finish all quests before training skills, or train skills while doing quests?

Do both simultaneously. Quests unlock critical content (like Monkey Madness I/II, which unlock the best weapons in the game). Skipping quests means you are training with inferior methods. However, you don't need to finish every quest immediately — focus on quests that unlock gear, teleports, or training methods. Use the quest helper plugin in RuneLite to track requirements and plan your quest order efficiently.

My combat stats are 60/60/60 but I'm still using Bronze armor. What gear should I buy?

At 60/60/60, you should immediately buy: Dragon Scimitar (60 Attack, ~100K GP), Dragon Boots (~400K GP), Dragon Med Helm (~50K GP), Dragon Platelegs (~200K GP), Obsidian Platebody (requires 60 Defence + Tai Bwo Wannai Trio quest, ~150K GP), and an Amulet of Glory (~20K GP). Total cost: ~1M GP. This gear set will last you until 70+ Defence when you can upgrade to Barrows equipment.

How long does mid-game take? When do I become "endgame"?

For most players, mid-game lasts 2-4 months of consistent play (2-3 hours per day). You "become" endgame when you have base 70+ combat stats, 70+ Prayer, 75+ Slayer, and the ability to complete Chambers of Xeric (Raid 1) or Theatre of Blood (Raid 2) consistently. Some players skip mid-game entirely by buying GP bonds and rushing stats, but this is not recommended — mid-game is where you learn the mechanics that will serve you in endgame PvM.

Is it worth it to get 99 in any skill at mid-game?

A few skills are worth rushing to 99 even at mid-game because the cape unlock is so valuable: (1) Farming (99) — Farming cape gives 5 daily teleports to any farming patch. (2) Construction (99) — gives access to the best POH setup. (3) Agility (99) — permanent 30% run energy reduction (stacks with Graceful). Other 99s are mostly for bragging rights and can be postponed until you have more GP and better gear.
