😴 Passive Income — AFK & Offline — Updated June 2026

Passive income is the OSRS equivalent of compound interest. These methods generate GP while you sleep, work, or play on another account. Set them up once, maintain them in minutes per day, and watch your bank value grow. This guide covers the four best passive money makers in 2026.

Setup Steps:

  1. Complete Throne of Miscellania quest (moderate length, easy boss fight).
  2. Complete Royal Trouble quest (doubles your worker cap from 10 to 15 — this is mandatory for maximum profit).
  3. Deposit 750,000-1,500,000 GP into the Kingdom coffers (workers deduct 75K/day at max efficiency).
  4. Set workers to your preferred resource allocation (recommended: 9 Herbs + 6 Coal for best GP).
  5. Maintain 100% approval rating — chop maple trees or rake weeds for 2 minutes daily to top it up.
👑 Kingdom Math: With Royal Trouble complete, workers cost 75K GP/day (50K for salary + 25K for resources). With 15 workers on herbs/coal, daily output averages 250K GP. Net profit = 175K GP/day, or ~5.2M GP/month for 2 minutes of daily maintenance. Check and top up your coffers weekly.

4. GE Flipping While Offline

The Grand Exchange processes buy and sell orders while you're logged out. This is true passive income — place orders, log off, and return to completed trades.

Offline Flipping Routine:

  1. Before logging off, place buy offers 5-10% below market price on high-volume items (nature runes, cannonballs, raw shark).
  2. Use all 8 GE slots across 3-4 different item types to diversify.
  3. When you log back in, check which offers filled.
  4. Relist filled items at market price (or 1 GP below the current lowest offer).
  5. Repeat — your profit cycle operates whether you're online or not.

6. Common Mistakes With Passive Income

  1. Letting Kingdom approval drop below 100%. Approval affects resource yield proportionally. At 50% approval, you get 50% of the resources but still pay full maintenance. Check daily.
  2. Underfunding the Kingdom coffers. Workers deduct 75K/day. If the coffers drop below 750K, resource yield decreases. Keep at least 750K-1M in reserve.
  3. Planting herbs without ultracompost. Regular compost has higher death rates. Ultracompost pays for itself many times over in saved herbs.
  4. Not using all 8 GE slots for overnight flipping. Empty GE slots are wasted passive income. Even a 5K profit per slot adds up to 40K per night — that's 1.2M/month from zero effort.
  5. Skipping birdhouses because "the profit is small." Birdhouses also give passive Hunter XP. Over months, you'll reach 70+ Hunter without ever actively training it.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Do I really need Royal Trouble for Kingdom management?

A: Yes, it's worth the 1-hour quest. Royal Trouble increases your workers from 10 to 15 — a 50% increase in daily resources. Over a month, the difference is ~2.5M GP. The quest pays for itself in under a week.

Q: Can I do these methods on an ironman?

A: All except GE flipping. Kingdom (herbs and logs), herb runs, and birdhouses are essential ironman activities. Kingdom is the primary source of herbs for Herblore training. Birdhouses provide tree seeds for Farming contracts. Only GE flipping is members-only for main accounts.

Q: How long does it take to set up all these methods?

A: About 4-6 hours of questing and setup. Throne of Miscellania (1.5 hrs) + Royal Trouble (1 hr) + Bone Voyage (30 min) + Farming tutorial (15 min) + teleport unlocks (1-2 hrs). After the initial investment, daily maintenance is under 10 minutes.

💰 Compound Your Wealth Passively

The beauty of passive income is that it stacks with everything else you do. While you're bossing for active GP, your Kingdom is collecting resources. While you're training Agility, your herbs are growing. Over weeks and months, this passive income funds your gear upgrades, buyable skills, and supplies — without you ever grinding for cash.

Set it up once, maintain it daily, and let your bank value climb while you sleep.
