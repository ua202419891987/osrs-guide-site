🎯 Why Ironman Is Different: GP Economy

On a regular account, money making is straightforward: do an activity, sell the output on the Grand Exchange, receive GP. On an ironman, there's no secondary market to absorb your output. This creates three fundamental differences in how ironmen generate and manage GP:

💡 Key Ironman Money Making Principles

  1. Prioritize Methods That Generate Both GP and Resources: Slayer tasks that drop alchables + herbs + seeds are better than pure GP methods because you need all three. Agility Pyramid gives GP + Agility XP. Thieving gives GP + seeds + herbs.
  2. Nature Runes Are Your Currency: Every Nature Rune is worth its alch profit margin. Don't waste Nature Runes on low-value alchs. The break-even: if Nature Rune + item alch value is less than what you could buy with the GP equivalent, don't alch — sell the item to a general store instead.
  3. Shop Stock Matters: Ironman GP is primarily used at shops. Buy battlestaves from Zaff daily even if you can't craft them yet. Buy broad bolts from Slayer masters. Buy runes from the Mage Arena and Baba Yaga's Magic Shop on Lunar Isle. These daily purchases are some of the best uses of GP on an ironman.
  4. Stack GP Before Buyable Skills: Ironmen eventually need massive GP for Construction (plank making), Smithing (gold ore at Blast Furnace), and Fletching (broad arrows). Don't spend GP on marginal upgrades — save it for skill unlocks that gatekeep quest requirements.

🏃 Agility Pyramid: 100K-200K/hr (Level 30+)

The Agility Pyramid, located between Sophanem and Nardah in the Kharidian Desert, is the single best early-game ironman GP method. With only 30 Agility required, it generates 10K GP per completed lap and is completely self-sufficient — no resources, no gear, no supplies. Just run, collect GP, repeat.

🗺️ Agility Pyramid Route Guide

  1. Start at the Pyramid Base (East Side): Talk to Simon Templeton if this is your first trip. He'll offer 1K GP for the golden pyramid if you return it — ignore this, you're running laps for 10K each.
  2. Climb the Obstacles (Ground to Top): The pyramid has a series of obstacles: climb low walls, cross balancing ledges, climb rocks, and walk across planks. Each obstacle can be failed, dealing 8-10 damage and sending you back to the previous safe tile. Just click the next obstacle — failing is normal.
  3. Click the Golden Pyramid (Top): At the top, there's a golden pyramid artifact. Click it to pick it up — you now have the "Pyramid Top" in your inventory.
  4. Climb Down the Back Side: Slide down the agility shortcut on the back (west) side of the pyramid. This deposits you at the pyramid base near Simon Templeton.
  5. Talk to Simon Templeton (East Side): He will buy the Pyramid Top from you for 10K GP. He then places it back at the top of the pyramid for the next lap.
  6. Repeat.

🎯 Ironman-Specific Considerations

  • Desert Heat Management: You'll need 4-6 waterskins for an hour of pyramid running. Waterskins can be refilled at the Nardah fountain or at water sources near the pyramid (the small pond south-east of the base). If you have the Desert Amulet 2+ (from the Desert Hard Diary), the Nardah teleport gives you instant access to the altar (restores HP + prayer) and the fountain (refills waterskins). This dramatically increases runs per hour.
  • Food Source (Ironman): Bring cheap food — cakes from the Ardougne bakery (thieve stall, level 5 Thieving, 3 bites healing 4 HP each) are the ironman staple for Agility Pyramid. Alternatively, wines from Draynor Village (level 28 Magic for Telekinetic Grab and level 33 Magic for Telegrab on the wine of Zamorak) are easy to mass-produce.
  • Graceful Outfit: Prioritize obtaining the full Graceful set (Agility rooftop courses, ~13 hours at Canifis). The weight reduction significantly reduces run energy drain at the pyramid, translating to faster laps.
  • When to Stop: The Agility Pyramid is great for early GP (your first 500K-1M), but it becomes outclassed by other methods once you hit 60+ combat stats and 55+ Thieving. Use it to fund your initial rune purchases and daily battlestaff buying, then transition to Thieving and Slayer for sustained income.

🏠 HAM Store Rooms — The Early Game Ironman GP Strategy

For ironmen with 15-40 Thieving, HAM Store Rooms are the best source of early GP. The process:

  1. Pickpocket HAM Members (west of Lumbridge, HAM Hideout) for HAM keys (always keep keys — they have 10 charges).
  2. Enter the Store Rooms (south of the main room, use key). Open chests for jewelry (Sapphire through Diamond amulets, rings, bracelets, necklaces).
  3. Disenchant or Alch the Jewelry: Unenchanted jewelry alchs for reasonable GP (Sapphire Ring = 100-200 GP alch, Diamond Amulet = 1.5K-2K GP). Alternatively, enchant the jewelry and use it for teleports — an ironman needs teleport jewelry heavily.
  4. Sell to Grum's Gold Exchange: Port Sarim jewelry shop buys jewelry at ~60-70% of High Alchemy value. This is faster than alching if you have limited Nature Runes. Sell unenchanted rings/amulets here for quick GP.

🌾 Master Farmers — The Ironman Meta

Master Farmers are not primarily a GP method — they're a seed acquisition method. But because the seeds they provide (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Torstol) produce herbs that can be made into potions (which ironmen desperately need), the indirect value is enormous. Consider:

  • 1 hour of Master Farmers at 71 Thieving (with Rogue's Outfit + Ardougne Hard Diary): ~200 Ranarr seeds, 80 Snapdragon seeds, 30 Torstol seeds, plus limps, Snape Grass, watermelon, and herb seeds.
  • Farming these seeds: The Ranarr seeds alone produce ~1,400 Ranarr Weeds (at 7 herbs per seed average). Those Ranarrs become ~1,400 Prayer Potions — which would require 1,400 Snape Grass (farmed or gathered).
  • GP Value Equivalent: If you could buy these seeds on the GE (ironmen can't), the seeds would be worth 5M-8M GP. As an ironman, the value is in the potion supplies — Prayer Potions are the lifeblood of PvM, and Master Farmers are how you get them.

✨ High Alchemy: Profit List (Battlestaves + Bows)

The key to efficient alching is only alching items where the alch value significantly exceeds the supply cost, and where the item can be produced reliably without GE purchases.

🌿 Nature Rune Supply for Ironmen

The limiting factor for alching is always Nature Runes. Ironmen have several sources:

  • Runecrafting (44 RC): Crafting Nature Runes at the Nature Altar (Karamja, accessed via fairy ring CKR or Amulet of Glory — Karamja). Each pure essence gives 1 Nature Rune (2x at 91+ RC). At 44-50 RC, expect 2K-2.5K Nature Runes per hour.
  • Buying from Baba Yaga's Magic Shop (Lunar Isle): Sells 300 Nature Runes in stock (base, growing with restocks). Cost: 180-250 GP each depending on stock level. At 200 GP average, that's 60K GP per 300 Nature Runes. Worth it if you're alching high-profit items (2K+ profit per alch). Lunar Diplomacy required.
  • Mage Arena (Wilderness): Sells 300 Nature Runes. Similar pricing to Baba Yaga. Riskier due to wilderness location but accessible earlier (no quest requirement).
  • Monster Drops: Slayer monsters drop Nature Runes frequently. Dust Devils, Nechryaels, Abyssal Demons, and Gargoyles all drop 30-75 Nature Runes at a time. Extended Slayer sessions naturally stockpile Nature Runes.
  • Guardians of the Rift (27 Runecrafting): The GOTR minigame gives catalytic talismans + Nature Runes in the reward pool. Excellent for early-game ironmen — trains Runecrafting while generating Nature Runes.

💡 Slayer Money Management Tips for Ironmen

  • Always Bring Alch Runes: Pack 200-300 Nature Runes and a Fire Staff (or Tome of Fire) on every Slayer task. Alch every Rune and Adamant item as it drops. This frees inventory space and converts drops to GP immediately. Explorer's Ring (Lumbridge/Draynor Diary) gives 30+ free High Alchs per day — use it on your longest tasks.
  • Extend Profitable Tasks: Use Slayer Points to extend tasks that give the best GP/hr for your account stage. Gargoyles, Nechryaels, Dust Devils, and Wyverns are all worth extending. Block tasks that give poor drops (Spiritual Creatures without God Wars access, Iron Dragons before Dragonfire protection, etc.).
  • Count GP/hr Correctly: An ironman's Slayer GP/hr should include: alchable items + raw coin drops + the alch value of any items you won't use. Don't discount items because "you might need them later" — if you already have 10 Rune Full Helms, the 11th is alch material.
  • Kourend Catacombs Priority: The Catacombs of Kourend are always better for ironmen than the Slayer Tower when bursting/barraging is possible. The Catacombs drop Ancient Shards and Dark Totem pieces (for Skotizo, which drops 50K-150K in alchable items per kill) in addition to normal loot. Plus the Catacombs prayer-restore mechanic (burying bones restores prayer) saves prayer potions.

🔨 Crafting/Fletching: GP + XP (Battlestaves + Bows)

Crafting and Fletching are two of the easiest skills for ironmen to convert into GP. 🏹 Fletching for GP — Yew Longbows and Magic Longbows

Fletching is inherently a "production" skill that creates alchable items. Every bow you fletch during training becomes alch GP:

  • Yew Longbow (strung): Alchs for 768 GP. Requires 70 Fletching. Yew logs from Woodcutting (60+ WC) or boss drops (Zulrah, Vorkath, Wyverns). Bow strings from Temple Trekking (50-100 strings per trek at high difficulty = ~500-1,000 per hour) or from Zulrah (100 noted strings per drop).
  • Magic Longbow (strung): Alchs for 1,536 GP. Requires 85 Fletching. Magic logs from bossing (Zulrah, Vorkath, Wyverns, skilling bosses) — ironmen don't typically cut their own Magic Logs in bulk.
  • Broad Arrows (Shaft + Head + Feather): Ironmen buy Broad Arrowheads from Slayer Masters and attach them to headless arrows. Each broad arrow gives 10 Fletching XP. This is an XP method, not a GP method — the arrows cannot be alched or sold. However, the XP is extremely fast (300K-500K XP/hr) and the GP cost is low (~15K GP per 1K arrowheads). This is how ironmen train Fletching quickly for diary requirements.

🔨 Giant's Foundry — Smithing Items into GP

Giant's Foundry, added with the Giants' Foundry update, is a Smithing minigame in the Kharidian Desert that allows ironmen to convert metal items (bars, weapons, armour) into coins, Smithing XP, and the Colossal Blade / Smithing Outfit. While the GP/hr isn't the highest, it converts otherwise useless metal items (drops from Slayer, loot from clue scrolls) into a triple resource: GP + Smithing XP + minigame rewards.

🎯 Ironman Integration with Giant's Foundry

Giant's Foundry is not a primary GP method for ironmen — it's a complementary method that converts excess metal items into value:

  • Recycle Slayer Drops: Every Rune Full Helm, Adamant Platebody, and Mithril item that drops during Slayer can be recycled at Giant's Foundry. Instead of alching these items (which uses Nature Runes), you can convert them directly into Smithing XP + GP + reward points. This saves Nature Runes for higher-value alchs (battlestaves, jewelry).
  • Metal Item Value Comparison: A Rune Full Helm alchs for 21,120 GP and costs 1 Nature Rune. Recycling it at Giant's Foundry gives Smithing XP + GP. For ironmen conserving Nature Runes, Foundry recycling is often the better choice.
  • Colossal Blade: The reward shop sells the Colossal Blade (a 2H sword with max hit scaling based on enemy size). For ironmen, this is a niche but useful PvM item (good at Sarachnis, Grotesque Guardians, and training on large enemies).
  • Smithing Outfit: +2.5% Smithing XP per piece (helmet, platebody, legs, boots, gloves). Worth obtaining before grinding Smithing to 70+ for quest requirements.
Pro Tip — Shop Item Recycling

Ironmen can buy platebodies (Mithril, Adamant) from shops (Horvik's Armour Shop in Varrock) at near-alch price, recycle them at Giant's Foundry, and receive Smithing XP + GP + reward points. The GP cost is recovered through the GP reward. This is an alternative Smithing training method for ironmen who don't enjoy Blast Furnace or want the minigame rewards.

📋 The Ironman Daily GP Blueprint

Here's a sample daily routine that generates consistent GP and resources for a mid-game ironman (combat 70-90, total 1500+):

  1. Start of Play Session (5 min): Buy battlestaves from Zaff, collect Miscellania resources (rake a herb patch for 100% approval, withdraw from coffer if needed).
  2. First Herb Run (5 min): Full 7-8 patch run. Harvest Toadflax/Ranarr/Snapdragon, replant. Use Fertile Soil spell (Lunar spellbook) or Ultracompost.
  3. Birdhouse Run (2 min): Reset birdhouses on Fossil Island. Use digsite pendant for fast travel.
  4. Active Session (1-3 hours): Slayer tasks, Barrows runs, or skilling (Agility, Thieving, Crafting). Focus on methods that generate GP + resources.
  5. Second Herb Run + Birdhouse Run (7 min): Repeat herb and birdhouse runs. At this point, your Ranarr/Snapdragon seeds from Master Farmer sessions are producing potions.
  6. End of Session (10 min): Alch any accumulated battlestaves and Slayer drops. Deposit GP. Check Miscellania coffer balance. Plant herbs before logging out (they grow while offline).

Daily GP Outcome: 63K-126K from battlestaves + 200K-500K herb GP equivalent + 100K-300K seed GP equivalent + 200K-400K Miscellania = 560K-1.3M GP/day in direct GP + saved costs. Over a month, this is 17M-39M GP equivalent. The actual cash stack will grow more slowly (you're "spending" the herb and seed value on potions and Farming XP), but the effective wealth accumulation is real.

❓ FAQ

What's the best very-early-game ironman GP method (combat 3-40)?

HAM Store Rooms (15 Thieving) and Agility Pyramid (30 Agility) are the two best early-game methods. HAM Store Rooms give jewelry (for teleports + GP) while Agility Pyramid gives raw GP + Agility XP. Spend 1-2 hours at HAM Store Rooms to get teleport jewelry and an initial 50K-100K GP, then use the Agility Pyramid for another 200K-500K GP. This funds rune purchases and initial gear.

How do ironmen get Nature Runes for alching?

Nature Rune sources, in order of accessibility: (1) Buy from Aubury's Rune Shop in Varrock (300 stock, 180-250 GP each). (2) Buy from Baba Yaga's Magic Shop on Lunar Isle (300 stock, Lunar Diplomacy required). (3) Craft at the Nature Altar (44 Runecrafting, use fairy ring CKR or Glory — Karamja). (4) Slayer drops (Dust Devils, Nechryaels, Gargoyles, Abyssal Demons all drop 30-75 at a time). (5) Guardians of the Rift minigame (27 Runecrafting, gives catalytic talismans + runes in reward pool). The most efficient approach is to mix buying from shops (for immediate alching needs) with Slayer drops (which accumulate naturally) and Runecrafting (for bulk supply when needed).

Should I prioritize GP or resources as an ironman?

Resources over GP, but GP is required for shops. An ironman needs herbs, seeds, secondaries, and runes far more than raw GP. GP is only useful for shops (runes, battlestaves, broad bolts, construction materials). If you have 10M GP but no Prayer Potions, you can't do PvM. If you have 500 Prayer Potions but only 500K GP, you can still do all PvM but can't buy runes. The balance: accumulate enough GP for daily shop purchases (2M-3M cash stack), then prioritize resource-generating activities (Slayer, herb runs, birdhouses) over pure GP methods.

Is wintertodt good GP for ironmen?

Wintertodt (50 Firemaking) is excellent for early-game ironmen because it gives: raw GP (10K-25K GP per crate), seeds (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Torstol at higher levels), herbs, uncut gems, logs, and the Pyromancer outfit. At 50-70 Firemaking, wintertodt is a good supplementary GP + supply source. At 70+ Firemaking, the rewards scale with your other skill levels, making it even better. However, wintertodt should not be your primary GP method — it averages 100K-200K GP/hr. Use it for Firemaking XP + early supplies, not sustained GP.

How do ironmen afford Construction (5M-15M GP)?

Construction is the biggest GP sink for ironmen. Funding strategies: (1) Daily battlestaves from Zaff (Varrock Hard Diary = 126K GP/day, Elite = 252K GP/day). (2) Alch all Slayer drops — Gargoyles, Wyverns, and Abyssal Demons add up quickly. (3) Thieving Ardougne Knights at 95+ (300K-400K GP/hr, fully AFK). (4) Save GP from the early game — don't spend on marginal upgrades. (5) Hallowed Sepulchre at 82+ Agility (1.5M-2.5M GP/hr). The Construction grind is the ironman's first real test of GP accumulation. A month of disciplined daily battlestaves + Slayer + Thieving = 15M+ GP.

What Slayer tasks should I block/prioritize for GP?

Prioritize: Gargoyles, Nechryaels (burst in Catacombs), Dust Devils (burst in Catacombs), Wyverns, Abyssal Demons, Bloodvelds. These give the best alchable drops + runes + seeds. Block: Spiritual Creatures (unless farming Ecumenical Keys at GWD), Iron/Steel Dragons (slow kills, bad drops, high supply cost), Hellhounds (no drops except hard clues — only unlock if you want Cerberus). Skip: Suqahs, Black Demons (unless doing Demonic Gorillas after Monkey Madness II for Zenytes).

Can ironmen make GP from Farming?

Not directly — you can't sell herbs or crops to shops for meaningful GP. However, Farming reduces your GP needs by providing: herbs for potions (saving the need to buy from shops), Limpwurt Roots for Strength Potions (herblore XP + potion supply), and coconuts/papayas/cactus spines (secondaries for potions). The best "GP" from Farming is the savings on potion costs and the self-sufficiency it provides. A single Ranarr Weed, turned into a Prayer Potion (3), saves 8K-10K GP compared to buying Prayer Potions from shops. Multiply by hundreds of herbs per week, and the savings are enormous.
