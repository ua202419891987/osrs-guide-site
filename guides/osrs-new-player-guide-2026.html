1. What is OSRS? (The 30-Second Version)

Old School RuneScape (OSRS) is a massively multiplayer online role-playing game (MMORPG) released by Jagex in 2013. It's a faithful recreation of the 2007 version of RuneScape — the game millions of players grew up with.

The Quick Pitch

Imagine a medieval fantasy world where you can do almost anything: kill dragons, build wealth, master skills, complete epic quests, fight terrifying bosses, and build your character exactly the way you want. There are no classes — you can be a warrior who cooks gourmet food, a mage who chops trees, or a merchant who never picks up a sword. Your character, your rules.

3. Account Creation & Safety Basics

Before you even enter the game, set up your account correctly. These 5 minutes of preparation will save you from headaches later.

Step 1: Creating Your Account

  1. Go to runescape.com/create
  2. Choose your email address (use a real one you check — it's needed for account recovery)
  3. Create a unique password you don't use anywhere else
  4. Set your display name (can be changed later for free once per month)
  5. Critical: Set up Jagex Account Guardian (JAG) immediately after login

Step 2: Security — Do NOT Skip This

⚠️ Account Hacking Is REAL in OSRS

Thousands of accounts get hacked every month. Hackers don't care if you're level 3 or level 1000 — they'll strip your bank and leave you with nothing. Protect yourself NOW before you have anything worth stealing.

6. Your First 30 Minutes After Leaving Tutorial Island

Congratulations! You've arrived in Lumbridge — the starting town for all new adventurers. For the first time, you're free. No more instructors telling you what to do. This is where most new players get overwhelmed and quit.

Not you. Here's your exact first 30-minute action plan:

Minutes 1-5: Orient Yourself

  • You're standing near Lumbridge Castle. The guy who greeted you is Duke Horacio.
  • Open your world map (click the globe icon near the minimap). Zoom out. See how tiny Lumbridge is compared to the whole world.
  • Check your inventory (the backpack icon). You should have some basic items: bronze dagger, sword, shield, Tinderbox, and some coins.
  • Important: Open Settings → Turn ON "Accept Aid", set your house to Lumbridge, and check your attack style (bottom-left combat tab).

Minutes 5-15: Your First Money (Kill Chickens)

ActionWhyReward
Walk north-east from castle to the chicken penFirst easy combat practice + money source~5-15 raw chickens per trip
Kill chickens (bring your sword/dagger)Combat XP + feathers drop5-15 feathers each kill
Pick up FEATHERS (not meat)Feathers stack = no banking trips neededFeathers sell for ~6-8 GP each on GE

Minutes 15-25: Visit the Grand Exchange (GE)

This is the MOST IMPORTANT location you need to know:

What is the Grand Exchange?

The GE is a centralized marketplace where every player can buy and sell items instantly. Think of it as the Amazon of Gielinor. 90% of your economic activity will happen here. It's located north-west of Varrock (walk west from Varrock Palace).

  1. Follow the road west from Lumbridge → Draynor Village → Varrock
  2. Navigate to the GE (north-west side of Varrock, look for the collection of bankers and clerks on stools)
  3. Sell your feathers: Click the + button (sell), put in your feathers, set price to lowest (instant sell). Click Confirm.
  4. Congratulations — you just made your first gold in OSRS!

Minutes 25-30: Set Your Long-Term Goals

Open the Skills tab (the blue icon). Look at all those level 1s. Every single one of them can reach level 99. Your journey starts now.

7. Core Concepts Every New Player Must Know

New players quit because they don't understand HOW the game works under the hood. Let me explain the key systems in plain English — no jargon, no assumptions about prior gaming knowledge.

7.1 The Combat Triangle

OSRS combat revolves around three styles that counter each other:

⚔️MELEE
Swords, axes
Beats Ranged
→ counters →
🏹RANGED
Bows, crossbows
Beats Magic
→ counters →
🔮MAGIC
Spells, staves
Beats Melee

Practical meaning: If you're fighting a monster with a bow and it switches to melee, switch to magic spells. If it uses magic, pull out your sword. Adapting mid-fight separates good players from great ones.

7.2 The Skill System — 23 Skills, Infinite Progression

Every skill goes from Level 1 to Level 99. XP (Experience Points) levels you up. Higher levels unlock:

Skill TypeExamplesWhy Level It
Combat Skills (7) Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Prayer, Ranged, Magic Higher damage, better armor, access to better weapons/equipment. Your "level" shown to others = combat level based on these.
Gathering Skills (6) Mining, Fishing, Woodcutting, Farming, Hunter, Thieving Collect raw materials to sell or craft into valuable items. Primary money makers.
Artisan Skills (5) Smithing, Crafting, Cooking, Herblore, Fletching, Construction Turn gathered materials into useful items (potions, food, gear). Often required for quests.
Support Skills (5) Runecrafting, Agility, Firemaking, Slayer, Magic Unlock teleportation, shortcuts, new monster types, and utility features.

7.3 Death Mechanics — What Happens When You Die?

💀 Death in OSRS Is Forgivable (Mostly)
ScenarioWhat You LoseRecovery Method
Died to NPC (monster)All items except 3 most valuable (kept on death)Return to death spot within timer to retrieve
Died to another player (PvP)Most items (except skulled = lose ALL)Killer gets your stuff — gone forever
Died in dangerous instanceVaries (some instances keep items safe)Pay fee or complete challenge to reclaim
Died with nothing valuableNothing meaningful lostRespawn with basic equipment

Golden rule: Never carry more than you're willing to lose. Bring cheap gear for risky fights until you know what you're doing.

7.4 Quest System — Not Optional, Actually Essential

Quests in OSRS aren't side content — they're the FASTEST way to gain XP and unlock game features:

  • XP Rewards: One quest can give 10K+ XP across multiple skills (saves hours of grinding)
  • Unlocks: Quests unlock new areas, spells, prayers, equipment, and game mechanics
  • Recipe for Disaster: The ultimate quest chain that unlocks BiS (Best-in-Slot) gloves
  • No "failure" state: Unlike skills, you can't "fail" a quest. Just follow the steps.

7.5 Economy & The Grand Exchange

The OSRS economy is entirely player-driven:

  • Supply & Demand: When a new boss releases, its drops flood the market → prices crash → smart players buy low and wait
  • Flipping: Buy low, sell high. Some players never kill a monster — they just trade on the GE all day
  • Merchanting Clans: Groups that manipulate item prices (risky but profitable)
  • Skill supplies: High-level players buy materials from low-level players → everyone benefits

FAQ — Questions Every New Player Asks

Is OSRS free to play?

Yes! OSRS has a robust F2P mode with 15 skills, 22 quests, and plenty of content for 50+ hours of gameplay. Membership ($7.99/month or via in-game Bond) unlocks the remaining 8 skills, 130+ quests, the full world map, bosses, and much more. Many players start F2P and eventually earn enough in-game currency to buy Bonds — effectively playing for free indefinitely.

How long does it take to reach "endgame"?

It depends entirely on your goals and playtime. Casual players (1-2 hours/day) typically reach mid-game competence in 2-3 months. Hardcore players (4-6 hours/day) can reach bossing-ready status in 4-6 weeks. True "maxed" accounts take years — but that's the beauty of OSRS, there's no rush and no subscription fatigue.

Should I play on PC or mobile?

Both! PC offers superior performance, multiple monitors, and precise clicking. Mobile lets you train AFK skills (woodcutting, fishing, etc.) during downtime. The same account works on both platforms seamlessly. Many successful players use PC for serious gameplay and mobile for casual skilling sessions.

What's the difference between OSRS and RS3?

They're completely different games sharing similar roots. OSRS (this guide) is based on the 2007 version — classic combat, simpler graphics, nostalgic feel, 200K+ active players. RS3 evolved into a modern MMO with microtransactions, Evolution of Combat (EoC), very different gameplay. OSRS is generally considered the more popular and community-driven version. Once you pick one, your account doesn't transfer between them.

Can I make real money playing OSRS?

Technically yes through legitimate means (streaming, content creation, services), but Jagex strictly prohibits Real World Trading (RWT) — selling in-game GP for real money. Stick to legitimate methods. Many players earn enough in-game GP to sustain their membership (Bonds) without ever spending real cash, which is the closest to "profitable" most players get.

What's an Ironman? Should I start as one?

Ironman Mode is a challenging playstyle where you cannot trade with other players or use the Grand Exchange. Everything you use must be gathered or crafted yourself. It's incredibly rewarding but significantly harder. Recommendation: Start as a regular account for your first character. Experience the full economy and social aspects of OSRS. You can always create an Ironman later once you understand the game deeply.

I keep dying and losing stuff. Is this normal?

Absolutely! Death is part of OSRS. Veterans die regularly — sometimes intentionally for strategic reasons (teleporting to spawn faster, respawning with full health). The key is to never risk what you can't afford to lose. Carry cheap gear for exploration, save your best equipment for situations you understand well.

How do I find people to play with?

OSRS has several ways to connect: Clan Chat (join any public clan), Discord communities (hundreds of OSRS servers), Reddit (r/2007scape has 500K+ members), and in-game Friends List. The community is famously welcoming to new players — don't be afraid to ask questions!

What's the best skill to train first as a brand new player?

Melee combat (Attack + Strength) is the best starting choice because it's cheapest, simplest, and prepares you for all quests and content. After reaching 40+ Attack/Strength, train Magic to 55 for High Alchemy (passive income). Then branch into Ranged for bossing. Read our Combat Training Guide for the exact route.

How do I set up my bank efficiently from day one?

Open your bank at Lumbridge Castle (2nd floor). Click the "tab" icons at the top of your bank to create tabs. Recommended tab setup: Tab 1 = Food/Potions, Tab 2 = Gear, Tab 3 = Skilling Supplies, Tab 4 = Quest Items, Tab 5 = Valuable Items. Always right-click → "Withdraw-All" instead of clicking items individually.

Should I use a guide for every quest, or try blind first?

Use guides for your first 10 quests to learn how quests work in OSRS. After that, try some quests blind — they're designed to be solvable with the in-game clues. If you get stuck for 10+ minutes, look up the wiki. Never feel shame about using guides — even veteran players use them for complex quests like Desert Treasure or Monkey Madness.
