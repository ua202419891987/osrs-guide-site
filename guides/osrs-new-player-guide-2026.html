1. What is OSRS? (30-Second Version)

Old School RuneScape (OSRS) is an MMORPG released by Jagex in 2013, based on the 2007 version of RuneScape. It's a medieval fantasy world where you can do almost anything: kill dragons, master 23 skills, complete 150+ quests, fight bosses, and build your character exactly how you want. There are no classes — be a warrior who cooks, a mage who chops trees, or a merchant who never fights. If you need help with any specific skill, check our Complete Skill Training Guide 2026.

2. Account Creation & Safety Basics

Before you even enter the game, set up your account correctly. These 5 minutes of preparation save you from losing everything later. Thousands of accounts get hacked every month — don't let yours be one of them.

Step 1: Create Your Account

Go to runescape.com and create an account. Use a real email you check regularly — it's needed for recovery. Create a unique password you don't use anywhere else. Set your display name (can be changed monthly for free).

Step 2: Security — Do NOT Skip This

Security MeasureHow to EnablePriority
Jagex Account Guardian (JAG)Settings → Account → Set up trusted devices🔴 CRITICAL
Authenticator (2FA)Settings → Security → Authenticator (phone app)🔴 CRITICAL
Bank PINIn-game: Settings → Display → Bank PIN🟠 HIGHLY Recommended
Unique EmailUse a separate email for RuneScape only🟠 Recommended

For a deep dive on phishing scams and recovery prevention, read our Account Security & Anti-Scam Guide 2026.

Step 3: Download the Game Client

  • Official Client: runescape.com/download (safe, official)
  • RuneLite (Recommended): runelite.net (free, open-source, essential plugins) — we cover setup in Section 5
  • Mobile: Search "Old School RuneScape" on App Store / Google Play

💡 3 Things to Know Before Starting
  1. You can change your appearance later — don't spend 20 minutes on hair color. The Makeover Mage south of Falador changes it for free.
  2. Pick a clean display name — avoid numbers if possible. Clean names are more memorable for clan/friend interactions.
  3. Read the dialogue — instructors explain core mechanics you'll use for years. Skipping = confusion later.

4. Your First 30 Minutes After Tutorial Island

You've arrived in Lumbridge. You're free for the first time. Most new players get overwhelmed here and quit. Not you. Follow this exact 30-minute plan to start strong. If your first task is earning GP, our Money Making Guide for Beginners 2026 has all the details.

Minutes 1–5: Orient Yourself

  • You're near Lumbridge Castle. Duke Horacio greeted you.
  • Open your world map (globe icon near minimap). See how tiny Lumbridge is.
  • Check your inventory: bronze dagger, sword, shield, tinderbox, coins.
  • Open Settings → Turn ON "Accept Aid" → Set house to Lumbridge → Check attack style.

Minutes 5–15: Your First Money

ActionWhyReward
Walk north-east from castle to the chicken penFirst combat practice + money source5–15 feathers per chicken
Kill chickens with your swordCombat XP + feathers dropFeathers sell for ~6–8 GP each
Collect 100–200 feathersYour first stack of wealth~600–1,600 GP total

Minutes 15–25: Visit the Grand Exchange

The GE is the central marketplace in Varrock (north-west from Lumbridge). Walk there, sell your feathers by clicking the + button, setting price to lowest (instant sell). Congratulations — you just made your first gold! The Grand Exchange Flipping Guide 2026 will teach you how to use the GE effectively.

Minutes 25–30: Set Your Goals

Open the Skills tab. Look at all those level 1s. Every single one can reach level 99. Your journey starts now. The most important first goal: complete Waterfall Quest (speak to Duke Horacio to start, it gives level 30 Attack and Strength for free).

6. Core Concepts Every New Player Must Know

New players quit because they don't understand how the game works under the hood. Here are the key systems explained simply. For more on combat mechanics, read the Combat Training Guide for Beginners 2026.

🔺 The Combat Triangle

Melee beats Ranged → Ranged beats Magic → Magic beats Melee. If a monster uses Magic, attack with Melee. If it uses Melee, use Magic. Adapting mid-fight separates good players from great ones.

📊 The Skill System — 23 Skills

TypeSkillsWhy Level Them
Combat (7)Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Prayer, Ranged, MagicHigher damage, better armor, boss access
Gathering (6)Mining, Fishing, Woodcutting, Farming, Hunter, ThievingCollect materials to sell or craft
Artisan (6)Smithing, Crafting, Cooking, Herblore, Fletching, ConstructionTurn materials into useful items
Support (4)Runecrafting, Agility, Firemaking, SlayerUnlock teleports, shortcuts, and profit

💀 Death Mechanics

If you die to a monster, you can return to your gravestone within a timer to retrieve your items. If you die to a player in PvP (Wilderness), you lose most unprotected items. Never carry more than you're willing to lose. The Wilderness Survival Guide 2026 covers this in detail.

📖 Quest System — Not Optional

Quests in OSRS are the FASTEST way to gain XP and unlock game features. One quest can give 10K+ XP (saves hours of grinding). Recipe for Disaster is the ultimate quest chain — it unlocks BiS gloves. Start with Waterfall Quest (free level 30 Attack + Strength, takes 20 minutes).

8. FAQ — Questions Every New Player Asks

Is OSRS free to play?

Yes! OSRS has a robust F2P mode with 15 skills, 22 quests, and ~50 hours of content. Membership ($7.99/month or via in-game Bonds) unlocks the full game. Many players start F2P and eventually earn enough GP to buy Bonds indefinitely. For the transition plan, see F2P to P2P Membership Guide 2026.

How long does it take to reach endgame?

Casual players (1–2 hrs/day) reach mid-game in 2–3 months. Hardcore players (4–6 hrs/day) reach bossing-ready in 4–6 weeks. True max accounts take years — but the journey is the fun part. There is no subscription fatigue in OSRS.

Should I play on PC or mobile?

Both. PC offers better performance for bossing and quests. Mobile is perfect for AFK skilling (woodcutting, fishing) during commutes or breaks. The same account works on both platforms seamlessly. Many players use PC for serious gameplay and mobile for casual skilling.

What's the difference between OSRS and RS3?

They're completely different games. OSRS is based on the 2007 version — classic combat, simpler graphics, nostalgic feel, 200K+ active players. RS3 has microtransactions, modern combat (EoC), and fewer active players. Once you pick one, accounts don't transfer between them.

What's an Ironman? Should I start as one?

Ironman Mode means you cannot trade or use the Grand Exchange. Everything must be gathered yourself. It's rewarding but much harder. Recommendation: start as a regular account for your first character. Create an Ironman later once you understand the game deeply. See Ironman Beginner Guide 2026 for details.

What's the best skill to train first?

Melee combat (Attack + Strength) is the best starting choice. It's cheapest, simplest, and prepares you for all quests and content. After reaching 40+ Attack/Strength, train Magic to 55 for High Alchemy (passive income). Then branch into Ranged for bossing.

How do I find people to play with?

OSRS has Clan Chat (join any public clan), Discord communities (hundreds of OSRS servers), Reddit (r/2007scape with 500K+ members), and in-game Friends List. The community is famously welcoming to new players — don't be afraid to ask questions!

I keep dying. Is this normal?

Absolutely. Death is part of OSRS — even veterans die regularly. The key is to never risk what you can't afford to lose. Carry cheap gear for exploration, save your best equipment for situations you understand well. Gravestones give you time to recover your items.

Should I buy membership or earn a bond?

If you can afford $7.99, buy membership directly. One month of membership easily earns 50M+ GP — enough for 4+ future bonds with GP to spare. If you must earn it in F2P, expect 55–75 hours of grinding for your first bond.

What's the biggest mistake new players make?

Not securing their account (no JAG, no 2FA, no Bank PIN). Thousands get hacked monthly. Second biggest: ignoring quests. Quests like Waterfall give free levels that skip hours of grinding. Third: not installing RuneLite. It's free and makes the game 10x easier.

9. Final Tips — Your Path Forward

  • Bookmark this guide. You'll reference it for your first month. The 7-day checklist alone is worth saving.
  • Install RuneLite immediately. It's approved by Jagex, free, and used by 90% of players. The GPU plugin, Ground Items, and Quest Helper transform the game.
  • Complete key quests first. Waterfall Quest, Dragon Slayer I, The Grand Tree, and Tree Gnome Village give massive XP rewards that skip hours of grinding.
  • Don't hoard GP — invest it. Buy gear upgrades that increase your kill speed. A Rune Scimitar (20K GP) doubles your money making rate.
  • Join a clan. OSRS is better with others. Find a beginner-friendly clan for advice, group content, and motivation.
  • Take breaks. OSRS is a marathon, not a sprint. Burnout is real. Set small goals (level 40 in a skill, first 100K GP) and celebrate each achievement.
  • Never fall for scams. "Doubling money," trust trades, and phishing links will steal your bank. If it sounds too good to be true, it is.
