1. How to Start Sailing in OSRS

Starting Sailing is incredibly easy — there are zero skill requirements. Follow these steps:

  1. Go to Port Sarim docks (south of Falador)
  2. Talk to Will or Anne at the dock
  3. Accept and complete the Pandemonium quest
  4. Receive your Raft (first boat) + Captain's Log
  5. You're now ready to train Sailing!
💡 Pro Tip: Complete Pandemonium immediately on any account — it takes under 15 minutes and unlocks everything. Even level 3 skillers can do it with zero combat.

2. How Sailing Works — 5 Core Training Activities

Sailing has five distinct training methods, each with different XP rates, intensity levels, and profit potential. You'll mix and match these as you level up.

🐟 Deep Sea Trawling 50 Semi-AFK ~40K Sailing + Fishing ✅ Good

📋 Port Tasks (Level 1+)

Port Tasks are your starting activity. Visit the noticeboard at any major port, pick up a task, and complete it for XP. Two types exist:

  • Courier deliveries: Sail cargo between ports. Easy, consistent XP.
  • Bounty hunts: Hunt down a specific target at sea. More XP, slightly more risk.

Port Tasks are best from level 1-30 before you unlock Barracuda Trials. After level 30, only do them when you want a break from trials.

🗺️ Sea Charting (Level 1+)

Sea Charting is a one-time exploration activity. Sail to uncharted map zones, and you're rewarded with a lump sum of XP for each new area discovered. In total, charting every zone grants 691,000 XP — enough to take you from level 1 to approximately level 55 on its own.

Use the OSRS Wiki Sailing Map with WikiSync to track which charting spots you've completed. The RuneLite Sailing plugin highlights undiscovered zones on your in-game map.

⚓ Shipwreck Salvaging (Level 15+) — The AFK King

This is the most popular training method for a reason. You sail to shipwreck locations, cast your salvaging hook, and wait 2–4 minutes while your character automatically gathers loot and XP. It's incredibly low-effort — perfect for second-screen gaming or working from home.

🏎️ Barracuda Trials (Level 30+) — Fastest XP

Barracuda Trials are time-trial obstacle courses on the water. Think of them as Sailing's equivalent of Rooftop Agility courses. Each trial has a fixed layout, and your goal is to navigate through checkpoints as fast as possible.

The faster you complete a trial, the more XP you earn. Mastering each course's layout is key to maximizing your XP rates.

🐟 Deep Sea Trawling (Level 50+)

Deep Sea Trawling is the hybrid method — it trains both Fishing and Sailing simultaneously. Deploy your trawling net, sail through rich fishing waters, and haul in fish while gaining Sailing XP. The raw marlin caught here can be cut for massive profit (see Section 8).

11. Pro Tips & RuneLite Plugins for Sailing

🔌 Essential RuneLite Plugins

  • Barracuda Trials Pathfinder — Shows optimal routes through every trial course. Absolutely mandatory for Gwenith Glide.
  • Sailing Plugin — Highlights uncharted zones on your world map, tracks charting progress, and overlays shipwreck locations.
  • WikiSync — Syncs your charting progress with the OSRS Wiki Sailing Map so you always know which zones you've completed.

🧠 Expert Tips

  1. Chart everything early. The 691K one-time Sea Charting XP is worth roughly 5–8 levels of free XP. Do it before you grind trials — it unlocks higher-level trials faster.
  2. Always upgrade your Salvaging Hook first. A Dragon Hook at level 87 pays for itself in under 2 hours of salvaging.
  3. Complete Horizon's Lure. Chart all zones for the Horizon's Lure item — a permanent +2.5% global Sailing XP boost. That's 325K free XP toward 99.
  4. Use smelling salts during Gwenith Glide. If you have them from ToA, the boosted stats improve trial times slightly.
  5. Do your daily Port Task. Some tasks have boosted XP rewards — check the noticeboard daily.
  6. Import tile markers. The OSRS Wiki has ready-made tile marker packs for all three Barracuda Trials. Import them and your XP rates jump immediately.

12. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Sailing worth training for a main account?

A: Absolutely. Sailing unlocks some of the best money makers in the game (Doom of Mokhaiotl at 15M-23M/hr), provides excellent AFK training, and the cost to 99 is only ~500K GP. It's one of the most cost-effective skills in OSRS.

Q: Is Sailing good for Ironmen?

A: Yes! Shipwreck Salvaging provides resources (planks, ores, gems) that are extremely useful for Ironmen. The Crystal Extractor generates passive crystal shards. Frost Dragons drop noted bones. Sailing is ironman-friendly from day one. See our Ironman Money Making Guide for more.

Q: Can I do Sailing with low combat stats?

A: Yes. Port Tasks, Sea Charting, and Shipwreck Salvaging require zero combat. Only ship combat (Cannons facility) and certain high-end content (Frost Dragons) need combat stats. Level 3 skillers can comfortably reach 99 Sailing.

Q: What's better — AFK Salvaging or sweating Trials?

A: Trials are ~2x faster XP (180K vs 90K/hr). But Salvaging earns you 20M+ GP profit and lets you work/watch shows at the same time. Our recommendation: do Trials when you're focused, Salvaging when you're busy. The hybrid approach is the most sustainable.

Q: When should I upgrade from Skiff to Sloop?

A: Right at level 50. The Sloop's 5 crew slots and 3 facility slots are game-changing — you can run a full Salvager + Lookout crew, install Wind Catcher, Salvaging Hook, and Trawling Net simultaneously. Don't delay this upgrade.
