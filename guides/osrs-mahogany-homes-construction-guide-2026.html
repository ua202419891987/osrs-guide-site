  • Cost: Effectively free — the client pays for all materials

The XP per hour is lower than traditional POH methods, but the GP cost is dramatically cheaper — making it the go-to method for ironmen, mid-game players, and anyone who values their GP.

① 📋 Requirements & Getting Started

Before you can start earning contract XP through Mahogany Homes, you need to meet a few prerequisites. The good news: the barrier to entry is very low compared to other Construction training methods.

① Skill Requirements

  • Construction Level 28 — This is the minimum level to accept Beginner contracts from the Mahogany Homes board. You can achieve this by doing the standard Oak Larder or Crude Wooden Chair method in your POH (costs about 100-200k GP).
  • No quest requirements — Unlike many OSRS training methods, Mahogany Homes is completely quest-free.

② The Architect Minigame — Unlocking Mahogany Homes

Mahogany Homes is accessed through The Architect minigame. Here's how to start:

  1. Head to Carpenter's Square in East Ardougne (south-east of the market, near the zoo entrance).
  2. Speak to Charles, the NPC standing next to the contract board. He'll explain the basics of the contracting system.
  3. Interact with the Mahogany Homes Contract Board right next to Charles. This is where you'll pick up all your assignments.
  4. Read the contract scroll to see which NPC client needs help, what furniture they need, and what tier the job is.

③ Recommended Pre-Training (Level 28-34)

While you can start Beginner contracts at level 28, you'll have very limited furniture options. Consider pre-training to level 34 using Oak Chairs in your POH (costs ~50k GP total) to unlock access to Adept contracts immediately. This saves you from wasting time on the lowest-tier Beginner jobs.

💡 Pro Tip — What Is The Architect?

The Architect is the minigame framework that Mahogany Homes falls under. Some players confuse it with a standalone minigame — it's not. It's simply the system that manages all Construction contracts. Think of it as the "quest-like" wrapper around the contract board. Charles and the board are the only NPCs you need.

② 🎯 How Mahogany Homes Contracts Work

Understanding the contract flow is the key to efficient Mahogany Homes training. Each contract follows the same basic loop, but the specifics change based on your tier and client.

① The Contract Loop — Step by Step

  1. Pick Up a Contract: Use the contract board in Carpenter's Square. You'll receive a scroll detailing a client, their location, and the furniture they need built.
  2. Check Your Supplies: Look at the contract to see what planks, nails, and optional materials you need. Beginner contracts typically need regular planks; Adept and Expert need oak, teak, and mahogany.
  3. Travel to the Client: Each client is an NPC located in a house across Gielinor. You'll need to teleport or run to their location. Common teleports include Ardougne Cloak, Varrock Teleport, Falador Teleport, and Kharyrll Teleport.
  4. Build the Furniture: Use your planks and nails on the marked hotspot in the client's house. You'll automatically build the required piece. Each build action gives Construction XP and counts toward contract completion.
  5. Return to Charles: Once the contract is complete, go back to Charles in Carpenter's Square. He'll reward you with bonus XP and Mahogany Homes points.
  6. Repeat: Grab a new contract and go again. Each contract takes 2-6 minutes depending on tier and distance.

② Mahogany Homes Reward Points

Completing contracts earns you Mahogany Homes points in addition to Construction XP. These points can be spent at the Carpenter's Square reward shop on:

  • Plank Sack (500 points) — Holds up to 28 planks in a single inventory slot. Absolutely essential for efficient training.
  • Teleport Scrolls — Various teleport items that can speed up travel between contracts.
  • Construction XP Lamps — Bonus XP on top of your contract XP.

Your first priority with points should always be the Plank Sack. It effectively doubles your inventory capacity for planks and makes Expert contracts much more practical.

③ Contract Completion Requirements

Each contract requires you to build a specific number of furniture items (usually 2-4 per contract) from the client's list. You cannot do partial contracts — you must complete all required items in a single visit. If you run out of materials, you'll have to bank, restock, and return (or cancel the contract and lose the progress).

💡 Key Insight — Cancelling Contracts

You can cancel a contract at any time by speaking to Charles, but you lose all progress on that contract. Only cancel if a client is unreachable (e.g., you can't solve the furniture puzzle) or you've realized you brought the wrong materials. Cancelling too often wastes time — always double-check your supplies before leaving Carpenter's Square.

③ 📊 Beginner, Adept & Expert Contract Tiers

Mahogany Homes has three contract tiers. Each tier unlocks at a specific Construction level and offers progressively better XP rates at the cost of more expensive materials.

Tier Min Level Plank Type XP Per Contract XP Per Hour
Beginner 28 Regular Planks ~2,500-3,000 ~100-130k
Novice 28 Regular / Oak ~3,000-3,800 ~130-170k
Adept 34 Oak / Teak ~6,000-8,000 ~60-80k
Expert 48 Teak / Mahogany ~12,000-18,000 ~110-150k

* XP rates assume efficient travel, Plank Sack equipped, and optimal route planning. Rates vary based on client location RNG and player focus.

① Beginner Contracts (Level 28+)

Beginner contracts are your introduction to the Mahogany Homes system. They require only regular planks and steel nails, making them extremely cheap to complete. Clients are located in easy-to-reach areas like Lumbridge, Draynor Village, and Rimmington. The XP is low, but the cost is nearly negligible — you'll spend maybe 1-2 GP per XP at this tier.

② Adept Contracts (Level 34+)

Adept contracts are the sweet spot for most players training from 34-99. They require oak or teak planks and offer significantly better XP than Beginner contracts. Clients are spread across Varrock, Falador, and Ardougne. At 60-80k XP per hour, Adept contracts are slower than POH methods but cost a fraction of the price. Most players stay at Adept from 34 to 99 unless they're in a rush.

③ Expert Contracts (Level 48+)

Expert contracts are the highest tier. They require teak or mahogany planks and involve clients in harder-to-reach locations like Hosidius, Prifddinas, and the Piscarilius House. The XP per hour (110-150k) is slower than POH methods, but the supply cost is still cheaper. Expert contracts also reward the most Mahogany Homes points per hour, making them ideal for farming the Plank Sack and teleport scrolls.

④ 🪑 Furniture Needed Per Tier

Each contract tier has a specific pool of furniture items you'll be asked to build. Knowing what each item requires lets you pre-stock your inventory and avoid wasted trips.

① Beginner Furniture (Regular Planks + Steel Nails)

  • Crude Wooden Chair — 2 planks, 2 nails
  • Wooden Bookcase — 4 planks, 3 nails
  • Wooden Table — 3 planks, 3 nails
  • Wooden Bed — 3 planks, 2 nails
  • Wooden Shelf — 2 planks, 2 nails
  • Wooden Drawer — 3 planks, 2 nails

Typical contract: 2-3 items, each consuming 2-4 planks. Total: ~6-12 regular planks per contract.

② Adept Furniture (Oak Planks + Steel/Mithril Nails)

  • Oak Chair — 2 oak planks, 2 nails
  • Oak Bookcase — 4 oak planks, 3 nails
  • Oak Table — 3 oak planks, 3 nails
  • Oak Bed — 3 oak planks, 2 nails
  • Oak Dresser — 3 oak planks, 2 nails
  • Oak Drawer — 3 oak planks, 2 nails
  • Oak Wardrobe — 4 oak planks, 3 nails
  • Teak Armchair — 2 teak planks, 2 nails (less common at this tier)

Typical contract: 2-3 items. Total: ~8-14 oak planks per contract.

③ Expert Furniture (Teak/Mahogany Planks + Mithril/Adamant Nails)

  • Teak Chair — 2 teak planks, 2 nails
  • Teak Bookcase — 4 teak planks, 3 nails
  • Teak Table — 3 teak planks, 3 nails
  • Teak Bed — 3 teak planks, 2 nails
  • Teak Drawer — 3 teak planks, 2 nails
  • Teak Wardrobe — 4 teak planks, 3 nails
  • Mahogany Chair — 2 mahogany planks, 2 nails
  • Mahogany Table — 3 mahogany planks, 3 nails
  • Mahogany Bookcase — 4 mahogany planks, 3 nails
  • Mahogany Bed — 3 mahogany planks, 2 nails
  • Mahogany Wardrobe — 4 mahogany planks, 3 nails

Typical contract: 2-4 items. Total: ~8-16 teak/mahogany planks per contract.

💡 Pro Tip — Nail Selection

Always use the lowest tier nails that work with your plank type. Steel nails work for regular and oak planks. Mithril nails work for teak. Adamant nails for mahogany. Using higher-tier nails than necessary is a waste of GP — Mahogany Homes is supposed to be the cheap option!

⑥ ⚔️ Mahogany Homes vs Traditional POH Training

This is the million-gold question every Construction trainer asks: should you do Mahogany Homes or the classic POH methods? Here is the direct comparison.

① Traditional POH Methods — Fast but Expensive

The standard POH training methods are all about raw speed at maximum cost:

  • Mythical Cape Racks (Level 76+): ~680k XP/hr at ~13 gp/XP. Requires a quest cape and a maxed POH costume room. Extremely fast but extremely expensive at ~130M GP to 99.
  • Oak Larders (Level 33-74): ~450k XP/hr at ~10 gp/XP. The classic mid-level method. Requires constant banking at a POH with a butler.
  • Mahogany Tables (Level 52+): ~700k XP/hr at ~12-14 gp/XP. The fastest method in the game, but burns through cash at an alarming rate.
  • Teak Tables (Level 52+): ~450k XP/hr at ~7 gp/XP. A decent middle-ground that is still faster than Mahogany Homes.

② When Mahogany Homes Wins

  • Budget training: You save 60-70% of the cost compared to POH methods. For Adept teak contracts (~3.6 gp/XP) vs Oak Larders (~10 gp/XP), the savings are enormous.
  • Ironman accounts: Gathering planks is tedious enough — Mahogany Homes stretches your plank supply much further because you get bonus XP from the contract completion reward.
  • Level 83 or 90 goals: If you only need Construction for a max house (83) or elite diaries (90+), Mahogany Homes is the most cost-effective path.
  • Active playstyle preference: Some players prefer the variety of locations and furniture types over clicking the same larder 10,000 times.

③ When POH Training Wins

  • Maximizing XP per hour: If you have the GP and want level 99 as fast as possible, POH methods are 2-3x faster.
  • GP-rich endgame players: If you can make 5M+ GP/hr through PvM, spending extra on faster Construction XP is an efficient use of your time.
  • AFK preference: POH with a butler is more AFK than Mahogany Homes, which requires constant movement and interaction.

⑦ 🧠 Best Strategies for Fast Contracts

Speed is everything in Mahogany Homes. The actual building is fast — what slows you down is travel. Here are the strategies that maximize your contracts per hour.

① Prioritize Close-By Clients

Not all contracts are created equal. When you pick up a contract, look at the client location. If you get a client in a far-away location (e.g., Great Kourend without a teleport), it might be worth cancelling and getting a new one. The best locations in order of speed:

  1. Ardougne (Ardougne Cloak teleport + short run)
  2. Varrock (Varrock Teleport + short run)
  3. Falador (Falador Teleport + short run)
  4. East Ardougne (Ardougne Cloak teleport)
  5. Rimmington (Falador Teleport + run south)
  6. Draynor Village (Ardougne or Lumbridge Teleport)

If you get a contract in Hosidius, Piscarilius, or Prifddinas without a teleport option, consider cancelling unless you have the relevant teleport items ready.

② Use the Plank Sack Immediately

The Plank Sack (500 Mahogany Homes points) should be your first purchase. It holds 28 planks in one inventory slot. With it, you can carry a full stack of planks + nails + a hammer + teleport runes without needing to bank between contracts. This alone improves your XP/hr by 20-30%.

③ Optimize Your Teleport Rotation

Keep these teleport items in your inventory or equipped:

  • Ardougne Cloak 2+ — Teleports to Ardougne, covering the most common client locations.
  • Varrock Teleport Runes — Law rune + Varrock teletab or standard spell.
  • Falador Teleport Runes — Law rune + Falador teletab or standard spell.
  • Kharyrll Teleport (if you have 66 Magic) — Quick access to Canifis area contracts.
  • Construction Cape / POH Teleport — For quick bank trips using your POH portal.

④ Cancel the Slowest Contracts

Develop a mental "kill list" of clients you refuse to do. Common skips include:

  • Stefan (Prifddinas) — Long run from the teleport point.
  • Hosidius clients — Unless you have the Xeric's Talisman teleport.
  • Piscarilius clients — Tedious layout and long run from bank.

Each cancel wastes about 30 seconds (talk to Charles, grab new contract). If a contract would take 4+ minutes due to travel, cancelling is faster.

⑤ Batch Your Contracts

Pick up 5+ contracts at once by asking Charles for multiple assignments. This is especially useful if you have high Construction and want to do a long session without returning to the board after every single contract. Note that you can only hold one active contract at a time though — the "batch" feature lets you see the next few contracts to plan your routing.

⑧ 🎒 Recommended Inventory & Gear Setups

Your inventory setup makes or breaks your Mahogany Homes efficiency. Here are the optimized layouts for each tier.

① Beginner / Adept Setup (Oak Planks)

Inventory (28 Slots):

  • Plank Sack (1 slot) — Holds 28 oak planks.
  • Extra oak planks (10-14 slots) — Enough for 2-3 contracts without banking.
  • Steel nails (1 slot) — 100+ nails should last many contracts.
  • Hammer (1 slot) — Required for all building actions.
  • Saw (1 slot) — Also required for building.
  • Ardougne Cloak 2+ (1 slot or worn) — Main teleport.
  • Law runes + Fire runes + Air runes (1-3 slots) — For Varrock/Falador teleports.
  • Dramen/Lunar staff (1 slot or equipped) — For fairy ring access if needed.

Equipment:

  • Graceful outfit (weight reduction helps with running between clients)
  • Ardougne Cloak (worn)
  • Boots of Lightness (optional)
  • Spotted/Spottier cape (if no Construction cape)

② Expert Setup (Teak/Mahogany Planks)

Inventory (28 Slots):

  • Plank Sack (1 slot) — Filled with teak or mahogany planks.
  • Extra teak planks (10-16 slots) — Expert contracts use more planks per job.
  • Mithril or Adamant nails (1 slot) — Match to your plank type.
  • Hammer (1 slot)
  • Saw (1 slot)
  • Xeric's Talisman (1 slot) — For Kourend-area contracts.
  • Ardougne Cloak 4 (1 slot or worn) — Best teleport in the game.
  • Construction Cape (1 slot or worn) — For banking via POH.
  • Teleport runes / tablets (2-3 slots) — Varrock, Falador, Camelot.

Equipment:

  • Graceful or weight-reducing gear
  • Ardougne Cloak 4 (worn)
  • Ring of Elements (free fire/air/water runes for teleports)
  • Stamina potions (1-2 doses for long sessions)

③ Alternative: Bank Teleport Setup

If you have a Construction Cape or Max Cape with the Construction perk, you can use this strategy:

  1. Teleport to your POH using the cape.
  2. Use the portal to your house's teleport room to bank quickly.
  3. Re-stock planks from the bank chest in your POH (if you have one).
  4. Teleport back to your client using your next teleport item.

This setup reduces banking time to nearly zero but requires a high Construction level to build the right POH rooms.

💡 Quick Tip — Ring of Elements

The Ring of Elements (from the Guardians of the Rift minigame) provides infinite fire, air, and water runes when worn. This means you can cast Varrock Teleport (law + fire + air) and Falador Teleport (law + water + air) without carrying any runes. It saves 2-3 inventory slots and is highly recommended for Mahogany Homes.

⑨ 💡 Efficiency Tips & Pro Tricks

These advanced tips will shave seconds off every contract, adding up to hours saved on the road to 99.

① Use Hotkeys for Menu Options

Set up your F-keys (function keys) so you can quickly switch between tabs. Recommended layout:

  • F1 — Combat / Inventory toggle
  • F2 — Inventory (for checking planks)
  • F3 — Spellbook (for casting teleports)
  • F4 — Prayer (not needed for Mahogany Homes, but keep consistent)

Being able to tab between inventory and spellbook without clicking the tiny UI icons saves significant time over thousands of contracts.

② Never Run Out of Stamina

Mahogany Homes involves a lot of running. Keep Stamina Potions in your inventory and sip them whenever your run energy drops below 60%. If you're wearing full Graceful, you can stretch a single stamina dose for 2-3 contracts. At higher levels, Energy Potions work as a cheaper alternative.

③ Use the RuneLite Mahogany Homes Plugin

The RuneLite Mahogany Homes plugin (built into the base RuneLite client) provides:

  • Contract tracking — shows your current contract details in the side panel.
  • Highlighted hotspots — glows the furniture hotspot in the client's house so you never miss it.
  • Progress tracking — shows how many furniture items you've built vs required.
  • Point tracking — shows your total Mahogany Homes points earned.

Enable this plugin in RuneLite's plugin hub before starting your training session. It's officially maintained and works flawlessly.

④ Pre-Make Planks Via the Sawmill

Buying planks directly from the Grand Exchange is convenient but expensive. If you have access to the Woodcutting Guild or a high Woodcutting level, consider cutting your own logs and converting them at the Sawmill in Varrock or the Sawmill operator in Prifddinas. This can save 20-30% on plank costs at the expense of your time.

⑤ Use Butlers Strategically

If you have a Demon Butler (level 50 Construction) or Maid (level 40 Construction), you can use them to bank planks while you continue building. This is particularly useful if you're doing multiple contracts in the same area and need to restock quickly. Note: Butlers require payment per trip, so only use this when it genuinely saves time.

⑥ Know When to Bank vs Keep Going

A common time-waster is returning to the bank when you could complete one more contract with what's in your inventory. As a rule of thumb:

  • If you have 6+ planks remaining and the next contract needs 2 items, push for one more contract.
  • If you have fewer than 6 planks, bank and re-stock to avoid getting stranded mid-contract.

🎯 Target Hours to 99 Construction (via Mahogany Homes)

  • Level 34-70 (Adept Oak): ~25-35 hours, ~4M GP
  • Level 70-80 (Adept Teak): ~18-22 hours, ~6M GP
  • Level 80-90 (Expert Teak): ~40-50 hours, ~13M GP
  • Level 90-99 (Expert Teak): ~55-65 hours, ~13M GP
  • Total 34-99: ~140-170 hours, ~36M GP

Compare this to POH Oak Larders: ~30 hours and ~100M GP. You save ~64M GP by spending an extra ~110-140 hours.

⑩ ❓ FAQ — Mahogany Homes Construction

Q: Is Mahogany Homes worth doing in 2026?

A: Yes. Mahogany Homes remains the most cost-effective Construction training method in OSRS. The 2026 meta has not changed this — if anything, with plank prices being stable, the GP savings are just as relevant as ever.

Q: Mahogany Homes vs POH — which is faster?

A: POH is faster. Myth capes (~680k XP/hr) and mahogany tables (~700k XP/hr) are roughly 4-12x faster than Mahogany Homes (~50-150k XP/hr at Expert). However, POH methods cost 3-4x more per XP point.

Q: What level should I start Mahogany Homes?

A: At least level 34 Construction to access Adept contracts. You can start at 28 with Beginner contracts, but the XP is too slow to be worth it for long. Spend 30 minutes doing Oak Chairs in your POH to reach level 34 first.

Q: How many Mahogany Homes points do I need for the Plank Sack?

A: 500 points. You'll earn about 20-40 points per contract depending on the tier. At Adept tier, you'll have 500 points in about 15-20 contracts (roughly 1-1.5 hours). Buy the Plank Sack as your first reward — it's the single biggest efficiency upgrade.

Q: What planks should I use for Mahogany Homes?

A: Teak planks offer the best balance of cost and XP. Oak planks are slightly cheaper but much slower. Mahogany planks are too expensive for the minimal XP gain over teak. For Adept contracts, oak is fine. For Expert contracts, use teak exclusively.

Q: Can ironmen do Mahogany Homes?

A: Absolutely — Mahogany Homes is arguably the best Construction method for ironmen. The bonus XP from contract completions stretches your hard-earned planks further. Ironmen should plank their own logs at the Sawmill (using the Firemaking Cape teleport or Ardy Cloak for fast banking).

Q: What is the best Mahogany Homes tier for XP?

A: Expert contracts with teak planks give the best XP rates at ~110-130k XP/hr. However, Adept contracts with oak/teak are more cost-efficient. Choose based on your budget.

Q: How long does 99 Construction take with Mahogany Homes?

A: Approximately 160-190 hours from level 34 at Expert teak tier. This is about 130-160 hours slower than POH methods, but you'll save 60M+ GP.

Q: Do I need The Architect minigame completed to start?

A: No. "The Architect" is just the name of the minigame feature, not a quest or achievement. You can walk straight to Charles in Carpenter's Square and start at level 28 Construction without any prerequisites.

Q: What's the best way to get planks for Mahogany Homes?

A: For main accounts, buy them from the Grand Exchange. For ironmen, cut teak logs at the Woodcutting Guild (or Ape Atoll on Fossil Island) and convert them at the Varrock Sawmill. The Butlers in your POH can also bring planks for a fee.

Q: Does Mahogany Homes give bonus XP?

A: Yes. Each build action gives base Construction XP, and completing the contract gives a bonus XP reward from Charles. This bonus is what makes Mahogany Homes more XP-efficient per plank than regular POH building.
