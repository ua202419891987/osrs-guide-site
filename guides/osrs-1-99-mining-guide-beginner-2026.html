OSRS 1-99 Mining Guide for Beginners (2026)

Skill Training Mining Updated July 2026

Mining is one of the most accessible skills in Old School RuneScape, perfect for beginners who want a relaxed, AFK-friendly training experience. This comprehensive guide walks you through every level from 1 to 99, covering the most efficient methods, money-making opportunities, and essential tips that will save you countless hours. Whether you're a brand-new player just leaving Tutorial Island or an experienced account looking to finally get that Mining pet, you'll find everything you need right here.

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 6+ methods ranked by XP/hr and profit — from 10K/hr (tin/copper) to 70K/hr (3-tick granite) and up to 3M GP/hr (amethyst)
  • 📌 Motherlode Mine at level 30 — fully AFK training from 30 to 99, ~150 hours total, perfect for watching Netflix
  • 📌 Best budget pickaxe path: Iron (100 GP) → Mithril (1.5K) → Rune (25K) — skip Dragon pickaxe (5-6M) as a beginner
  • 📌 Total profit to 99 at MLM: 15-30 million GP from pay dirt ores over ~150 hours
  • 📌 Complete Digsite Quest first — unlocks Motherlode Mine, the game's most AFK-friendly training area

Why Train Mining? (Benefits & Uses)

Mining might seem like a simple click-and-wait skill, but it offers several compelling reasons to train it seriously. Understanding these benefits will help you stay motivated through the grind to 99.

① 🏗️ Essential for Smithing

Mining and Smithing go hand-in-hand like no other skills in OSRS. If you plan to train Smithing (which lets you craft armor, weapons, and useful items like cannonballs), you'll need a steady supply of ores. Mining your own ores saves millions of GP compared to buying them from the Grand Exchange. Even if you don't plan to train Smithing immediately, having high Mining makes it trivial to gather materials for future Smithing training or for making combat gear on an Ironman account.

② 📜 Quest Requirements

Several important quests have Mining level requirements that can block your progression if you neglect this skill. Here are the most significant ones:

  • Heroes' Quest: Requires 60 Mining (along with other stats). This quest is essential for accessing the Heroes' Guild and starting the Legend's Quest.
  • Lunar Diplomacy: Requires 70 Mining. This unlocks the powerful Lunar spellbook, which provides invaluable utility spells like Vengeance, NPC Contact, and Hunter kits.
  • Underground Pass: Requires 25 Mining for one of the paths through the quest.
  • Haunted Mine: Requires 45 Mining and is necessary for access to the Salve Amulet (huge boost for melee combat against undead).
  • The Giant Dwarf: Requires 12 Mining and unlocks the Blast Mine (a unique Mining training method).

Training Mining early prevents these quest roadblocks and keeps your account progression smooth.

③ 💰 Money Making Potential

While Mining isn't the most profitable skill at low levels, it becomes increasingly lucrative as you progress. Here's what you can expect:

  • Mid levels (40-60): Gem rocks and gold ore mining can yield 200k-400k GP per hour.
  • High levels (70+): Runite ore sells for over 11,000 GP each. Even mining a few ores per hour adds up.
  • Endgame (95+): Amethyst mining generates around 3 million GP per hour, making it one of the best afk money makers in the game.
  • Motherlode Mine: Even the "afk route" pays out with coal, gold, mithril, adamantite, and runite ores from your pay dirt.

④ 😌 AFK-Friendly Training

Mining is arguably the most AFK-friendly skill in OSRS. The Motherlode Mine (available at level 30) allows you to train completely afk for hours at a time. You can watch Netflix, do homework, or work on other tasks while your character mines away. This makes Mining perfect for players with busy schedules who want to make progress without staring at the screen constantly.

The ability to AFK train from level 30 all the way to 99 is what makes Mining so popular among casual players. You can literally go from 30 to 99 Mining by playing just 1-2 hours a day while doing other things.

⑤ 🎒 Access to Motherlode Mine at 30

Reaching level 30 Mining is a major milestone because it unlocks the Motherlode Mine (MLM). This special mining area revolutionizes the skill by providing:

  • Consistent, predictable XP rates without competition from other players
  • AFK gameplay with minimal attention required
  • Passive income from the ores you receive in pay dirt
  • Prospector outfit pieces that give a 2.5% XP bonus when worn as a set
  • A social, relaxed environment where you can chat with other players

Once you experience MLM, you'll never want to go back to traditional ore mining.

⑥ 🐾 The Mining Pet (Rocky)

Like all skills, Mining has a chance to reward you with a pet at level 99 (or earlier if you're lucky). Rocky the rock creature is one of the cutest pets in the game, and getting him at level 99 is a proud achievement. The pet chance is based on your mining actions, so the more you mine, the better your odds.

Levels 1–15: Tin and Copper Rocks (The Very Beginning)

The first 15 levels of Mining are quick and straightforward. Your goal here is to reach level 15 as fast as possible so you can start mining iron ore, which is much faster XP.

⑦ 📍 Best Locations for Levels 1-15

You have two excellent options for mining tin and copper:

  • Southeast Lumbridge Swamp: This is the closest mine to a bank for free-to-play players. It has 3 tin rocks and 5 copper rocks. The nearby Lumbridge swamp is safe and rarely crowded.
  • Rimmington Mine: Located south of Rimmington (use Rimmington teleport on a Portal Nexus or come by boat from Port Sarim). This mine has 6 tin rocks and 5 copper rocks, making it slightly better for efficiency. However, it's a bit further from a bank.

Recommendation: Start at Lumbridge Swamp mine since it's right after Tutorial Island. Only switch to Rimmington if Lumbridge is crowded.

⚡ Power-Mining (Drop Mining) for Speed

Here's the most important tip for early Mining: DO NOT BANK YOUR ORE. Instead, use the "power-mining" method:

  1. Mine a full inventory of tin/copper ore
  2. Drop all the ore (don't walk to a bank)
  3. Repeat immediately

This is much faster than banking because you eliminate travel time. Yes, you get zero profit, but you'll reach level 15 in about 15 minutes instead of an hour. The time saved is worth far more than the ~2,000 GP worth of ores you'd get from banking.

How to drop quickly: With a full inventory, right-click each ore and select "Drop." Even faster: enable "Shift-click to drop" in your game settings (right-click any inventory item → "Toggle shift-click drop"). Now you can just shift-click each ore to drop it instantly.

⑧ 📊 XP Rates and Time Estimates

Method XP/Hour Time to Level 15 Profit
Power-mine tin/copper 10,000–13,000 ~15 minutes 0 GP
Bank tin/copper 3,000–5,000 ~45-60 minutes ~2,000 GP

Clearly, power-mining wins by a huge margin. Don't waste time banking low-level ores.

⑨ ⚒️ Pickaxe Progression (Levels 1-15)

You start Tutorial Island with a bronze pickaxe, which works fine for levels 1-15. However, as soon as you can afford better pickaxes, upgrade immediately. Here's the progression:

  • Bronze Pickaxe: Automatically given at Tutorial Island. Works until level 1.
  • Iron Pickaxe: Buy from the Grand Exchange for ~100 GP. Drastically faster than bronze. Get this ASAP.
  • Steel Pickaxe: Available at level 6 Mining, costs ~250 GP. Small upgrade over iron.
  • Black Pickaxe: Same speed as steel but looks cooler. Costs ~600 GP. Optional.

Critical tip: Always use the best pickaxe you can afford and equip it. A better pickaxe significantly increases your mining speed, which means more XP per hour. The difference between a bronze and steel pickaxe is noticeable even at low levels.

⑩ 🗺️ Step-by-Step: Getting from Level 1 to 15

  1. Finish Tutorial Island and teleport to Lumbridge
  2. Walk to the Lumbridge Swamp mine (southeast of Lumbridge)
  3. Mine 3 tin ores and 3 copper ores (you need both for the next step if you want to smith, but for pure Mining XP, just mine whichever respawns first)
  4. Drop all ores. Don't bank them.
  5. Continue until you reach level 15 Mining
  6. Congratulations! You can now mine iron ore.

Total time: Approximately 15 minutes of active playing.

Levels 15–30: Iron Ore and Preparing for Motherlode Mine

At level 15, iron ore becomes available. Iron mining is significantly faster XP than tin/copper, and it's your bridge to the Motherlode Mine at level 30.

🪨 Option A: Power-Mine Iron Ore in Al Kharid (Levels 15-30)

This is the fastest way to reach level 30 Mining if you don't yet have access to Motherlode Mine.

Location: Al Kharid mining site (northwest of Al Kharid, just past the scorpion pit).

Why Al Kharid?

  • 7 iron rocks in a small area
  • Close to a bank (Al Kharid East Bank is nearby)
  • Usually not too crowded on low-population worlds
  • Free-to-play accessible

The Method:

  1. Mine iron rocks until your inventory is full
  2. Drop all iron ore (power-mine) — don't bank yet
  3. Repeat until level 30

XP Rate: ~20,000 XP per hour (power-mining)

Time to level 30: Approximately 1.5 to 2 hours from level 15.

Alternative: If Al Kharid is crowded, try the Crafting Guild mine (requires 40 Crafting to enter, but has 6 iron rocks) or the Legends' Guild mine (requires Legends' Quest).

🪨 Option B: Motherlode Mine at Level 30 (Requires Digsite Quest)

Once you reach level 30, you can start using the Motherlode Mine. However, there's a catch: you must complete the Digsite Quest first.

The Digsite Quest is a low-requirement quest that unlocks the Digsite area and, more importantly, gives you access to the Motherlode Mine.

📜 Digsite Quest Quick Guide

Requirements:

  • Level 25 Thieving (boost with garden pie if needed)
  • Level 10 Agility
  • Level 10 Herblore

Why it's easy for beginners: If you just finished Tutorial Island, you might not meet these stats yet. But they're very easy to get:

  • Thieving 25: Pickpocket men in Lumbridge until level 5, then pickpocket warriors in Edgeville. Takes about 1-2 hours.
  • Agility 10: Complete the Gnome Stronghold Agility Course once or twice. Takes 5 minutes.
  • Herblore 10: Make attack potions from Guam leaves + Eye of Newt. Buy ingredients from the Grand Exchange.

Quest rewards: 2 quest points, 200 XP in Thieving/Herblore/Agility, and access to the Digsite (and Motherlode Mine).

Time to complete: About 30-45 minutes if you have the stats.

🎯 Recommendation: Get to 30 ASAP, Then Switch to MLM

Here's my recommended path for levels 15-30:

  1. If you don't have 25 Thieving / 10 Agility / 10 Herblore, go get those stats first (they're useful for other quests too)
  2. Complete the Digsite Quest
  3. Power-mine iron ore in Al Kharid from level 15 to 30 (about 1.5-2 hours)
  4. At level 30, immediately go to the Motherlode Mine and start your AFK journey to 99

This path ensures you have access to MLM as soon as possible. Don't waste time banking iron ore unless you desperately need GP for bond money.

⚒️ Pickaxe Upgrades (Levels 15-30)

Between levels 15-30, upgrade your pickaxe as you progress:

  • Mithril Pickaxe (level 21 Mining): Buy from Grand Exchange for ~1,500 GP. Noticeable speed increase over steel.
  • Adamant Pickaxe (level 31 Mining): Buy for ~3,000 GP. You'll reach level 31 quickly, so consider buying this right before you hit 30.

Pro tip: If you have the GP, buy the next-tier pickaxe before you actually reach the level requirement. There's nothing worse than missing out on faster mining speeds because you forgot to upgrade.

Levels 30–99: Motherlode Mine — The Core Method for Beginners

Congratulations! You've reached level 30 Mining and completed the Digsite Quest. Now begins the most AFK, relaxed training method in OSRS: the Motherlode Mine (MLM).

This section will explain everything you need to know about MLM, from how it works to maximizing your XP rates and profits.

📍 Where is the Motherlode Mine?

The Motherlode Mine is located in the Digsite area, north of the Digsite (east of Varrock). Here's how to get there:

  • Fastest method: Use a Digsite teleport (unlocked after Digsite Quest) or a teleport to Varrock, then run east.
  • Alternative: Use the Mine cart system from Keldagrim (requires started Dwarf Cannon Quest) or from the Grand Exchange (after completing the Digsite Quest).
  • F2P note: Motherlode Mine is members-only. Free-to-play players cannot access it.

⛏️ How Motherlode Mine Works (Step-by-Step)

The Motherlode Mine is not like regular mining. Instead of mining ore rocks and getting ores directly, you mine "ore veins" and collect "pay dirt," which you then process for random ores. Here's the full process:

  1. Mine Ore Veins: In the MLM cavern, you'll see special ore veins (they look different from normal rocks). Click on them to mine. You'll get pay dirt in your inventory instead of actual ores.
  2. Fill the Hopper: When your inventory is full of pay dirt, go to the hopper (a large machine in the center of the cavern) and deposit your pay dirt into it.
  3. Operate the Hopper Controls: Pull the lever next to the hopper to send your pay dirt through the washing machine.
  4. Collect from the Sack: Walk to the sack (large cloth bag) and withdraw your cleaned ores. The pay dirt "washes" into various ores depending on your Mining level.

What ores do you get from pay dirt?

The ores you receive depend on your Mining level. At higher levels, your pay dirt yields better ores:

Mining Level Ores from Pay Dirt
30-39 Clay, Copper, Tin, Iron
40-54 + Silver, Gold
55-69 + Mithril
70-84 + Adamantite
85+ + Runite (very rare but valuable!)

This means MLM gets more profitable as you level up, since higher-level ores are worth more GP.

📊 XP Rates: Upper Level vs. Lower Level

MLM has two mining areas: the Upper Level (available at level 30) and the Lower Level (unlocked at level 72). Here's the difference:

Upper Level (Levels 30-71)

  • XP Rate: ~35,000–45,000 XP per hour
  • AFK Level: Very high. You can easily go 3-4 minutes between clicks.
  • Pros: Extremely relaxed, almost fully AFK, perfect for watching videos or doing other tasks.
  • Cons: Slower XP than lower level; you'll be here for a long time (levels 30-72).

Lower Level (Levels 72-99)

  • XP Rate: ~50,000–55,000 XP per hour
  • AFK Level: Moderate. You need to pay more attention because lower-level veins deplete faster.
  • Pros: Faster XP, better profit per hour, more engaging gameplay.
  • Cons: Less AFK than upper level; requires more active playing.

My recommendation: Stay on the upper level until level 72. The difference in XP (10,000-15,000 per hour) isn't worth the loss of AFK comfort for most players. Once you unlock the lower level at 72, switch down there for the rest of your journey to 99.

🎽 Essential Gear for MLM

While MLM is low-requirement, having the right gear makes a significant difference:

Pickaxe

Always use the best pickaxe you can afford. Here's the progression:

  • Rune Pickaxe (level 41): Huge upgrade over adamant. Buy for ~25,000 GP or get one as a drop from the Heroes' Guild minigame.
  • Dragon Pickaxe (level 61): 5% faster than rune. Very expensive (~5-6 million GP), but the time saved might be worth it for you. Alternative: Use a Dragon pickaxe special attack from the Ork statue in God Wars Dungeon (requires 70 Strength).
  • Crystal Pickaxe (level 71): Best pickaxe in the game. Requires 71 Mining and completion of Song of the Elves quest. Very expensive (multiple millions), but the ultimate goal for efficiency.
  • Infernal Pickaxe (level 61): Obtained as a very rare drop from the Motherlode Mine (1/1,000,000 chance). It has the same speed as Dragon but also smelts ores automatically. Mostly a trophy item.

Full Graceful Outfit

Wearing the full Graceful outfit reduces your run energy depletion by 30% and increases run energy restoration by 30%. This matters because MLM requires a lot of running between ore veins and the hopper.

How to get Graceful: Complete agility courses (Gnome, Draynor, Al Kharid, etc.) to earn marks of grace, then trade them for the Graceful outfit. Takes about 3-5 hours of agility training.

Alternative: If you don't have Graceful yet, bring plenty of stamina potions (4-dose) to restore run energy.

Prospector Outfit (XP Bonus)

The Prospector outfit is a reward from MLM that gives a 2.5% XP bonus when worn as a full set. Here's how to get each piece:

Piece Cost (nuggets) XP Bonus
Prospector Helmet 40 nuggets +0.5%
Prospector Jacket 60 nuggets +0.5%
Prospector Legs 50 nuggets +0.5%
Prospector Boots 30 nuggets +0.5%
Full Set Bonus 180 nuggets total +2.5%

How to get golden nuggets: You automatically receive golden nuggets when mining pay dirt. The amount depends on your Mining level (higher level = more nuggets per pay dirt).

Time to full outfit: Approximately 15-20 hours of MLM training.

Priority: Buy the boots first (cheapest), then helmet, legs, jacket. The full set bonus is worth the grind!

💰 Sack Upgrades: Bigger Capacity = Less Clicking

Your sack (where you collect ores from pay dirt) has a limited capacity. At level 30, it holds 81 pay dirt. But at level 81, you can upgrade it to hold 162 pay dirt!

Why this matters: A bigger sack means you can AFK longer without emptying it. Instead of clicking the sack every 5-10 minutes, you can go 15-20 minutes between trips.

How to upgrade: Talk to the NPC "Prospector Percy" in the MLM cavern. The upgrade costs 100 golden nuggets (which you're already earning from mining pay dirt).

Recommendation: Prioritize the sack upgrade over Prospector outfit pieces if you value AFK time over XP bonus. The quality-of-life improvement is massive.

💵 Profit from MLM: It Adds Up!

While MLM isn't the most profitable Mining method, it does generate passive income. Here's what you can expect:

  • Upper Level (30-71): ~50,000–100,000 GP per hour (mostly coal, some gold/mithril)
  • Lower Level (72-99): ~100,000–200,000 GP per hour (more mithril, adamantite, occasional runite)

Over the course of 150 hours to 99, you'll earn approximately 15-30 million GP from your pay dirt ores. That's enough to buy multiple 99 capes or a nice cash stack for endgame content!

What to do with the ores:

  • Coal: Sell on GE (demand is always high for Smithing training).
  • Gold: Sell or use for Crafting training (gold bars for jewelry).
  • Mithril/Adamantite: Sell or smelt into bars for Smithing.
  • Runite: SELL IMMEDIATELY. Each ore is worth 11,000+ GP.

Alternative Mining Methods (Faster XP or More Money)

While Motherlode Mine is the best method for AFK training, you might want to try other methods if you prefer faster XP or higher profits. This section covers the best alternatives.

⚡ 3-Tick Granite Mining (Levels 50-99) — Fastest XP

If you want to get 99 Mining as fast as possible and don't mind intense clicking, 3-tick granite mining is the best method in the game.

What is 3-Ticking?

"3-ticking" is a game mechanic where you perform an action every 3 game ticks (1.8 seconds) instead of the normal 4 ticks (2.4 seconds). This makes your character mine faster because you're starting the next mining action before the current one finishes (if the rock depletes).

How to 3-Tick Granite Mine

  1. Location: Desert Quarry (south of the Shanty Pass in the Kharid Desert). Requires level 50 Mining.
  2. What you mine: Granite rocks (they give 500g, 2kg, or 5kg granite blocks).
  3. The 3-tick cycle:
    • Click the granite rock
    • Immediately drop the granite (or fill your inventory and drop everything)
    • Click the rock again
    • Repeat in a rhythm
  4. Food/Water: Bring waterskins (4-dose) and fill them at the desert well. The desert heat will damage you if you don't stay hydrated. Alternatively, wear the Desert amulet 4 (after completion of Desert Treasure II) for infinite desert heat protection.

XP Rates and Requirements

  • XP Rate: ~65,000–75,000 XP per hour (much faster than MLM!)
  • Profit: Negative (you spend money on waterskins, though they're cheap)
  • Intensity: High. This is NOT AFK. You must maintain the 3-tick rhythm constantly.
  • Time to 99 from 50: Approximately 70-80 hours (vs. 150+ hours at MLM).

Is 3-Tick Granite Worth It?

Yes, if: You want 99 Mining quickly and don't mind intense gameplay. It's also great if you're a streamer or content creator who needs to show progress fast.

No, if: You prefer relaxed, AFK training. 3-tick granite is exhausting and not enjoyable for long sessions.

3-Tick Timing Guide

Mastering the 3-tick rhythm takes practice. Here's a simple way to learn:

  1. Turn on "F-key behaves as hotkey" in settings (this lets you use F1-F5 to open tabs quickly)
  2. Mine a rock, then immediately click an empty inventory slot (drop the ore)
  3. Click the rock again
  4. The rhythm is: Click rock → drop → click rock → drop
  5. After some practice, it becomes muscle memory

Pro tip: Watch a YouTube video of 3-tick granite mining to see the rhythm in action. It's much easier to understand visually.

💎 Gem Rocks (Level 40+) — Great Money, Decent XP

Gem rocks are special mining spots that give you uncut gems instead of ores. They're an excellent money-making method, especially for mid-level players.

Where to Find Gem Rocks

  • Shilo Village (Karamja): Requires completion of Shilo Village quest. Has 4 gem rocks. This is the best location.
  • Al Kharid Mine: Has 1 gem rock (very slow since only one rock).
  • Northwest of Shilo Village: 3 gem rocks (requires Shilo Village quest).
  • Fossil Island: 3 gem rocks (requires Bone Voyage quest).

XP Rates and Profit

  • XP Rate: ~25,000 XP per hour (not amazing, but not terrible)
  • Profit: ~200,000–400,000 GP per hour (depending on luck with diamonds and dragonstones)
  • AFK Level: Moderate to high. Gem rocks take a long time to deplete.

Which Gems Are Valuable?

Gem GE Price (approx.) Notes
Uncut Sapphire 500 GP Common, low value
Uncut Emerald 700 GP Common, low value
Uncut Ruby 1,200 GP Uncommon, decent value
Uncut Diamond 2,500 GP Rare, good value
Uncut Dragonstone 15,000 GP Very rare, excellent profit

Recommendation: Gem rocks are a great side method. You can mine them when you want a break from MLM but still want some Mining XP and profit. Just note that the Shilo Village quest has a combat requirement (level 32 Agility and some fighting), so it's not pure beginner content.

🌋 Volcanic Sulphur (Level 43+) — Fast XP, Good Money

Volcanic Sulphur is a unique Mining method found on Fossil Island. It offers fast XP and decent profit, but it damages your character over time.

How It Works

  1. Go to Fossil Island (requires Bone Voyage quest, which needs 21 Mining, 20 Smithing, 10 Herblore, and 17 Agility).
  2. Enter the Volcanic Mine area (southwest part of Fossil Island).
  3. Mine sulphur veins. Each mine gives you volcanic sulphur (used for making sulphur for Herblore).
  4. The sulphur damages you periodically (2-5 damage every few seconds). Bring food!

XP Rates and Profit

  • XP Rate: ~50,000 XP per hour
  • Profit: ~300,000–500,000 GP per hour (volcanic sulphur sells for about 150 GP each, and you get a lot of them)
  • AFK Level: Moderate. You need to watch your health and eat food.

Requirements and Recommendations

  • Quests: Bone Voyage (for Fossil Island access)
  • Combat: Ability to survive with ~15-20 damage per minute (bring lobsters or better food)
  • Gear: Full Graceful (to run between sulphur veins), food, and a pickaxe

Who should use this method? Players who want faster XP than MLM and don't mind managing their health bar. It's a nice middle ground between AFK and intensive training.

💜 Amethyst Crystal Mining (Level 95+) — Best Money, Great XP

Amethyst mining is the ultimate endgame Mining method. It requires level 95 Mining but offers incredible profit and good XP.

Where to Mine Amethyst

Amethyst rocks are found in two locations:

  • Arceuus Mine (Great Kourend): Requires 82 Mining to enter the city (Thessalia's quest line). Has 4 amethyst rocks.
  • Iorwerth Mine (Prifddinas): Requires completion of Song of the Elves quest and 82 Mining. Has 4 amethyst rocks. Better location because Prifddinas has a bank nearby.

XP Rates and Profit

  • XP Rate: ~55,000 XP per hour
  • Profit: ~3,000,000 GP per hour (amethyst sells for about 1,100 GP each, and you get 2,500+ per hour)
  • AFK Level: High. Amethyst rocks take a long time to deplete.

What to Do with Amethyst?

Amethyst has several uses:

  • Sell on GE: Easiest option. Currently about 1,100 GP each.
  • Craft into arrows/bolts: With level 85 Crafting, you can make amethyst arrows (good profit) or amethyst bolts (Reqs 80 Fletching).
  • Make amethyst javelin heads: Requires 84 Fletching. Very profitable.

Recommendation: If you're level 95+ Mining and need GP, amethyst mining is one of the best afk money makers in the entire game. You can easily make 10-20 million GP per session while barely paying attention.

Note on pickaxe speed: "Ticks/rock" means how many game ticks (0.6 seconds each) it takes to mine one ore. Lower is better. The difference between rune and dragon is about 5% faster mining speed.

Budget tip: If you can't afford a Dragon pickaxe (5-6M GP is a lot for a beginner), don't worry. A Rune pickaxe is perfectly fine for all content except maximum efficiency grinding. You can use the GP for other priorities like combat gear or quest requirements.

🎽 Prospector Outfit (2.5% XP Bonus)

I mentioned this in the MLM section, but it's so important that it deserves its own detailed explanation.

The Prospector outfit is obtained from Prospector Percy in the MLM cavern in exchange for golden nuggets. Each piece gives a 0.5% XP bonus, for a total of 2.5% with the full set.

Why the 2.5% matters:

  • At MLM upper level (40,000 XP/hr), 2.5% = +1,000 XP/hr
  • Over 150 hours to 99, that's +150,000 XP total — enough to save about 1 hour of training time
  • It's not HUGE, but every bit helps, and the outfit looks cool

Purchase order recommendation:

  1. Prospector Boots (30 nuggets): Cheapest, get these first
  2. Prospector Helmet (40 nuggets): Next cheapest
  3. Prospector Legs (50 nuggets): Most expensive pants
  4. Prospector Jacket (60 nuggets): Most expensive, get last

Time to full set: About 15-20 hours of MLM. You'll naturally earn nuggets while training, so you'll have the full set before you reach 99 (unless you get 99 extremely quickly).

🏅 Mining Cape (99) and Varrock Armour

Mining Cape (99 Cape)

Upon reaching level 99 Mining, you can buy the Mining Cape from the Mining Guild for 99,000 GP. Benefits:

  • Teleport to Mining Guild: Very useful for various mining methods
  • +5% XP bonus: When worn, gives a 5% XP boost to Mining (stacks with Prospector outfit)
  • Status symbol: Shows off your achievement

Varrock Armour (from Achievement Diaries)

Varrock Armour is a reward from the Varrock Achievement Diaries. Each tier gives a chance to mine an extra ore per rock (doubling your ore yield).

💊 Useful Consumables and Items

Stamina Potions

Stamina potions restore your run energy and prevent it from draining for 2 minutes per dose. Essential for MLM (all that running between rocks and hopper) and for running to remote mining sites.

How to get: Mix Harralander + Spirit weed (level 77 Herblore) or buy from GE (~5,000 GP per 4-dose potion).

Budget alternative: Super energy potions (cheaper but less effective) or just walk and wait for run energy to restore.

Food (for Volcanic Sulphur)

If you're mining volcanic sulphur, you'll take damage over time. Bring lobsters (heals 12) or better food. Swordfish (heals 14) or monkfish (heals 16) are ideal.

Waterskins (for Desert Quarry)

If you're 3-tick granite mining, you need waterskins to avoid desert heat damage. Buy empty waterskins (4) from the Grand Exchange (~1,000 GP each) and fill them at the desert well (near the Shanty Pass).

Alternative: Complete Desert Treasure II quest and wear Desert amulet 4 for infinite desert heat protection.

Mining Guild Access (Quick Teleport)

At level 60 Mining, you can enter the Mining Guild (located in Falador). The guild has:

  • 6 coal rocks and 6 mithril rocks (deposit box nearby for banking)
  • A +7 invisible Mining boost (you mine as if your level is 7 higher)
  • A teleport option on the Mining Cape (if you have 99)

Not essential for MLM training, but useful if you need to bank ores for Smithing or profit.

⚡ Pro Tips for Faster Mining

  1. Always drop, don't bank (below level 30): Power-mining is 3x faster than banking low-level ores.
  2. Upgrade your pickaxe immediately: Don't wait. Even a small upgrade helps.
  3. Do quests that give Mining XP: Digsite (2,000 XP), The Giant Dwarf (1,500 XP), Between a Rock... (5,000 XP), and others. These can save you hours of grinding.
  4. Use the "Shift-click to drop" setting: enable it in game settings for faster inventory management.
  5. Join a clan: Clan chat can answer questions and provide a community for long AFK sessions.
  6. Watch YouTube guides: Visual learners benefit from seeing 3-tick timing and MLM strategies in action.
  7. Set realistic goals: "I'll get 99 Mining in 3 months" is better than "I need 99 NOW" (which leads to burnout).
  8. Take breaks: Mining can be monotonous. It's okay to take a day off and come back fresh.
  9. Consider alternative training: If MLM feels too slow, try gem rocks or volcanic sulphur for variety.
  10. Get the Mining pet (Rocky): Okay, you can't control this, but the pet rolls are based on mining actions. The more you mine, the better your chances!

🎯 3-Tick Granite Mining: Detailed Timing Guide

Since 3-tick granite is the fastest Mining method but also the most confusing for beginners, here's a more detailed explanation:

The Theory Behind 3-Ticking

OSRS runs on a "tick system" where everything happens in 0.6-second intervals. Normally, mining a rock takes 4 ticks (2.4 seconds). But if you perform an action on the 3rd tick (like dropping an ore), you can start mining the next rock 25% faster.

Step-by-Step 3-Tick Granite Method

  1. Go to the Desert Quarry with a pickup and a full inventory of waterskins (4-dose).
  2. Find a granite rock that's not being mined by someone else.
  3. Click the granite rock.
  4. The moment your character starts swinging the pickaxe, RIGHT-CLICK an inventory slot with an ore and select "Drop." (Or shift-click if you enabled shift-click drop.)
  5. As soon as the ore is dropped, click the granite rock again.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 in a continuous rhythm.

Common Mistakes

  • Clicking too early: If you click the rock before your character finishes the current mining action, it cancels the action. Wait for the "ore obtained" message.
  • Not dropping fast enough: If you don't drop by the 3rd tick, you lose the speed bonus. Practice until it's muscle memory.
  • Running out of water: Desert heat will damage you. Check your waterskins regularly and refill at the desert well.
  • Mining the wrong size granite: Granite comes in 500g, 2kg, and 5kg sizes. It doesn't matter which you mine; they all give the same XP. Just mine whichever respawns first.

YouTube is your friend: Search "OSRS 3-tick granite mining guide" and watch a video. It's much easier to understand the timing when you see it in action.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Here are the most common questions beginners have about Mining, answered in detail.

❓ Q: Should I bank ores or power-mine (drop them)?

A: Below level 30, always power-mine (drop ores). The time saved is worth far more than the ~2,000 GP you'd get from banking tin/copper/iron ores. At level 30+, if you're at MLM, you're not banking ores anyway — you're collecting pay dirt and withdrawing the cleaned ores later.

If you need GP desperately (e.g., for a bond), you can bank iron ore from level 15-30 and sell it on the GE. But for pure XP, power-mining is always faster.

❓ Q: Is Motherlode Mine better than 3-tick granite?

A: It depends on your playstyle and goals:

  • Choose MLM if: You want to relax, watch Netflix, work on other tasks, or play casually. It's much less intense and almost fully AFK.
  • Choose 3-tick granite if: You want 99 Mining as fast as possible and don't mind (or enjoy) high-intensity clicking. It's 70-80 hours to 99 vs. 150+ hours at MLM.

My recommendation: Start with MLM. If you find it too slow, you can always switch to granite later. But most beginners prefer the relaxed pace of MLM.

❓ Q: What quests help with Mining? Which should I do first?

A: Several quests give Mining XP or unlock important areas. Here are the most useful, in priority order:

  1. Digsite (2,000 XP): Unlocks Motherlode Mine. Do this FIRST. Requires 25 Thieving, 10 Agility, 10 Herblore.
  2. The Giant Dwarf (1,500 XP): Unlocks the Blast Mine (a unique Mining method). Requires 12 Crafting, 16 Firemaking, 33 Magic.
  3. Plague City (no XP but unlocks Haunted Mine): Haunted Mine (22,000 XP) requires 45 Mining and gives access to the Salve Amulet (huge combat boost).
  4. Between a Rock... (5,000 XP): Requires 40 Mining, 50 Smithing, and completion of Dwarf Cannon and Fishing Contest quests. Unlocks the Blast Mine upgrade.
  5. Shilo Village (no Mining XP but unlocks gem rocks): Requires 32 Agility and some combat. Unlocks the best gem rock mining spot.
  6. Bone Voyage (no XP but unlocks Fossil Island): Unlocks Volcanic Sulphur mining. Requires 21 Mining, 20 Smithing, 10 Herblore, 17 Agility.

Strategy: Do Digsite first (for MLM access), then work on the others as you progress toward higher Mining levels.

❓ Q: Can F2P (Free-to-Play) players train Mining effectively?

A: Yes! F2P Mining is completely viable, though slower and less profitable than members' methods.

F2P Mining progression:

  • Levels 1-15: Power-mine tin/copper in Lumbridge Swamp or Rimmington.
  • Levels 15-30: Power-mine iron ore in Al Kharid.
  • Levels 30-40: Mine iron ore and BANK it (sell on GE for profit). F2P doesn't have MLM, so you can't AFK train effectively.
  • Levels 40-99: This is where F2P struggles. Your options are:
    • Mine coal in the Mining Guild (level 60) or Barbarian Village
    • Mine mithril/gold in the Mining Guild (very slow XP)
    • Mine runite (level 85) — extremely slow because rocks take 20+ minutes to respawn

Realistic F2P 99 Mining time: 300-500+ hours (compared to 150 hours at MLM for members). F2P Mining is slow and tedious. Consider becoming a member (even temporarily) to access MLM.

F2P money making: Mine iron ore (level 15+) and coal (level 30+), then sell on GE. It's slow but generates some income.

❓ Q: How long does it take to get 99 Mining at MLM?

A: Approximately 120-150 hours of actual play time. Here's the breakdown:

  • Levels 30-72 at upper MLM: ~105 hours (at 40,000 XP/hr)
  • Levels 72-99 at lower MLM: ~48 hours (at 53,000 XP/hr)
  • Total: ~153 hours

If you play 2 hours per day, that's about 2.5-3 months to 99. If you can AFK while working or studying, you might finish in 1-2 months.

Comparison to other methods:

  • 3-Tick Granite: ~70-80 hours (much faster but intense)
  • Amethyst (95+): ~40-50 hours from 95 to 99 (if you already have 95)
  • MLM: ~150 hours (slow but almost fully AFK)

❓ Q: What's the best pickaxe for a beginner with a low budget?

A: Here's a budget-friendly pickaxe progression:

  • Levels 1-20: Iron pickaxe (100 GP) → Steel pickaxe (250 GP)
  • Levels 21-30: Mithril pickaxe (1,500 GP)
  • Levels 31-40: Adamant pickaxe (3,000 GP)
  • Levels 41-60: Rune pickaxe (25,000 GP) — big upgrade, worth saving for
  • Levels 61-70: Rune pickaxe is fine. Save for Dragon or Crystal later.
  • Levels 71+: Crystal pickaxe (if you have the GP and quest requirements)

Don't buy a Dragon pickaxe (5-6M GP) as a beginner. It's a luxury item. A Rune pickaxe is 95% as good and costs 200x less.

❓ Q: Should I use Swiftness potions or other boosts for Mining?

A: Unlike some skills (like Smithing with dwarven stouts or Cooking with cooking gauntlets), Mining has very few XP boosts:

  • Prospector outfit: +2.5% XP (buy with nuggets at MLM)
  • Mining Cape: +5% XP (only at level 99)
  • Gauntlet of the Ruin (from Barbarian Assault): No effect on Mining
  • Swiftness potions: Don't exist in OSRS
  • Dwarven stout: Lowers Mining level by 1 (useless)

The bottom line: There are no "mining potions" or temporary boosts that increase your Mining speed. Your only boosts are the Prospector outfit, Mining Cape, and using the best pickaxe available. Focus on those instead of looking for potions that don't exist.

❓ Q: I'm an Ironman. Does this guide still apply?

A: Yes! This guide works perfectly for Ironman accounts. In fact, Mining is even MORE important for Ironmen because you need ores for Smithing training (which you can't buy from the GE on an Ironman).

Ironman-specific tips:

  • Bank your ores from MLM pay dirt! You'll need coal, mithril, adamantite, and runite for Smithing training.
  • Consider doing Blast Mine (after completion of The Giant Dwarf) for faster Smithing training. Blast Mine gives half-baked ores that you smelt into bars with 100% success rate (no smithing level required).
  • Get 70 Smithing before 99 Mining (so you can smelt your ores into bars and craft them into useful items).
  • Consider mining gem rocks on your Ironman for crafting training (gems are used in Crafting).

Ironman 1-99 Mining is slower because you'll bank ores instead of power-mining. But it's still completely doable with patience.

❓ Q: What's the best way to make money with Mining at low levels?

A: Here are the best money-making methods by level:

  • Levels 1-15: No money making. Power-mine for speed.
  • Levels 15-30: Mine iron ore and BANK it. Sell on GE for ~100 GP per ore. ~20,000 GP per hour.
  • Levels 30-40: MLM pay dirt gives some profit (coal, some gold). ~50,000 GP per hour.
  • Levels 40-60: Gem rocks (Shilo Village) — ~200,000-400,000 GP per hour.
  • Levels 60-95: Volcanic Sulphur — ~300,000-500,000 GP per hour.
  • Levels 95-99: Amethyst — ~3,000,000 GP per hour.

Most profitable route: Get to level 40 ASAP (do some quests for XP), then mine gem rocks in Shilo Village. It's not amazing profit, but it's the best low-level money making in Mining.
