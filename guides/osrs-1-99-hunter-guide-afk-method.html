OSRS 1-99 Hunter Guide — AFK Methods 2026

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 13 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
🐾 Skill Training — Updated June 2026

Hunter is one of OSRS's fastest skills to 99, but traditional methods require constant clicking. This guide focuses on AFK-friendly and passive training methods — birdhouse runs, Maniacal monkeys, and Herbiboar — so you can max Hunter with minimal effort while watching shows, working, or playing other games.

Start with the Natural History Quiz in the Varrock Museum basement — it takes 5 minutes and gets you to level 9 Hunter instantly. Then do Eagles' Peak and Bone Voyage. By the time these are done, you'll be ~level 15 and have access to birdhouses.

Levels 1–47: Getting to Salamanders

After the quest XP rush, do Crimson Swifts (1-9), Golden Warblers (9-19), Copper Longtails (19-29), and Prickly Kebbits (29-37). At level 37, switch to Swamp Lizards until level 47 for Orange Salamanders. This phase takes about 3-5 hours of active play.

3. Levels 47–80: Salamanders to Maniacal Monkeys

Orange Salamanders (47–59)

Head to the desert south of Uzer with 3-4 small fishing nets and 3-4 rope. Set up traps at the 3 young trees. Orange salamanders are fast XP at 30K-45K/hr. This takes 2-3 hours to reach level 59.

Red Salamanders (59–67) — The XP Jumps

Move to the Ourania Altar area (ZMI) west of the Observatory. Red salamanders provide 60K-80K XP/hr with 4 traps. Bring waterskins if not wearing desert gear. This is 4-5 hours to level 67.

Black Salamanders (67–80) — Optional Fast Track

In the Wilderness (level 20-24), black salamanders offer 80K-100K XP/hr. Only worth it if you're comfortable with low-risk Wilderness; otherwise stick with red salamanders until 80.

Maniacal Monkeys (60+) — True AFK Training

After completing Monkey Madness II, head to Kruk's Dungeon on Ape Atoll. Bring 4-5 box traps and a light source. Place traps in the monkey pit and the monkeys constantly destroy them, forcing re-trap attempts. This is the best AFK Hunter method in OSRS at 60K-110K XP/hr with only 1-2 clicks per minute.

🐒 Maniacal Monkey Setup: Bring a light source, 4-5 box traps, and a bonecrusher (optional — for passive Prayer XP from monkey bones). Wear weight-reducing gear (Graceful). Stand in the southwest corner of the monkey pit for optimal trap placement. The monkeys are aggressive — Protect from Melee if you're a lower level.

4. Levels 80–99: Herbiboar — Best XP + Profit

Herbiboar is the best Hunter training method in the game for players who want both XP and profit. You track a giant boar through Fossil Island's mushroom forest, inspecting objects along its path. Each catch takes 30-60 seconds and grants herbs based on your Herblore level.

At 80-90 Hunter with maximum efficiency, Herbiboar yields 140K-180K XP/hr and 500K-1M GP/hr in herbs (at high Herblore levels). It's moderately AFK — each tracking sequence requires 5-8 clicks but has built-in pauses while the boar moves between objects.

🧪 Herbiboar Efficiency Tip: Use the RuneLite "Herbiboar" plugin — it highlights the next tracking object on your minimap, reducing each sequence to 2-3 seconds. Wear full Graceful outfit (you'll run constantly). Bring a Herb Sack from the Slayer reward shop to store herbs without banking — this doubles your trips between banks.

5. Advanced Training Strategy — Hybrid Approach

The most efficient path to 99 Hunter combines all methods:

  1. Daily Birdhouse Runs: Do 4-8 birdhouse runs per day (roughly every 50-100 minutes while you're online). This alone provides 3M-5M passive XP on the road to 99.
  2. Active Training Sessions: When you want to actively train, use salamanders (47-80) then Herbiboar (80-99). Each active session of 1-2 hours builds birdhouse XP in the background.
  3. AFK Downtime: If you have a work meeting or are watching a show, do Maniacal monkeys. Set up traps, switch windows, and click once every minute. You'll passively gain 60K-80K XP/hr while barely paying attention.
  4. Combine with Farming: Birdhouse runs pair perfectly with herb runs — both use the 50-minute growth cycle. Do a birdhouse run right after your herb run for maximum efficiency.

7. Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  1. Skipping birdhouse runs entirely. Birdhouses are free passive XP. Not doing them means you need 3M-5M more active XP — that's 20+ extra hours of active training. Do them.
  2. Using wrong tier birdhouses. Always use the highest tier birdhouse your level allows. The XP difference between regular and yew birdhouses is roughly 4x. Pay the extra 200 GP per log — it's worth it.
  3. Not bringing enough traps. Salamander spots have 3 trees, so bring at least 3 traps. For Maniacal monkeys, bring 4-5. One less trap = 25% less XP/hr.
  4. Forgetting Digsite Pendants. The pendant is your only fast teleport to Fossil Island (besides the Spirit Tree at level 85 Farming + Hosidius favour). Always keep charged pendants in your bank.
  5. Training Hunter without Graceful. Almost every Hunter method involves running between trap spots or tracking objects. Graceful saves 30% run energy drain, essentially doubling your effective uptime.
  6. Waiting too long to collect birdhouses. Birdhouses are ready after 50 minutes, but they don't degrade. Even after 6 hours, you still get the same XP. Don't stress about exact timing — just do a run when convenient.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get 99 Hunter with birdhouses alone?

A: Yes, technically. Using redwood birdhouses (90+), you'd gain ~20K XP per run. At 4 runs per day, that's ~80K XP/day. From level 9 to 99 (~13M XP), it would take roughly 160 days. In practice, combine birdhouses with active training — you'll reach 99 in 4-6 weeks playing casually.

Q: Is Monkey Madness II worth it just for Maniacal monkeys?

A: Yes, if you value AFK training. MM2 is a difficult Grandmaster quest with high requirements (69 Slayer, 70 Crafting, 60 Thieving), but Maniacal monkeys are the only true AFK Hunter method. If you plan to max Hunter and want to minimize attention required, MM2 is absolutely worth the effort. The quest also unlocks other valuable content (Demonic Gorillas, Heavy Ballista).

Q: When should I switch from salamanders to Herbiboar?

A: At level 80 Hunter exactly. Herbiboar at level 80 provides comparable XP to black salamanders (~100K-120K XP/hr initially) but also makes 300K-600K GP/hr from herbs. As your Hunter level increases, Herbiboar XP scales up to 180K/hr at 95+. Switch at 80 and never look back.

Q: How profitable is 1-99 Hunter?

A: 5M-20M GP, depending on methods. Birdhouse runs generate 5M-10M GP in bird nests and seeds. Herbiboar from 80-99 generates 8M-15M GP in herbs (higher Herblore = higher profit). Active salamander training generates zero profit. Maniacal monkeys cost supplies (prayer potions if using Protect from Melee). Overall, expect a net gain of 5M-20M GP on the road to 99.

🐾 You Can AFK Your Way to 99 Hunter

Hunter has a reputation as a click-intensive skill — and it can be. But with birdhouse runs providing passive XP and Maniacal monkeys letting you train while barely paying attention, you can reach 99 Hunter with minimal effort. The key is consistency: do your birdhouse runs daily, spend AFK time at monkeys, and when you're actively playing, crush Herbiboar for that 150K+ XP/hr.

By the time you have that Hunter cape, you'll have made a tidy profit, maxed one of the fastest skills in the game, and barely felt the grind. Now go build some birdhouses. 🐦
