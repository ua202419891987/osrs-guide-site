OSRS How to Increase Slayer Points Fast 2026 -- 9+1 Method & Best Masters

This guide covers the fastest point farming methods, best masters for each account stage, and how to earn 500-800 points per hour in 2026.

1. How Slayer Points Work

Slayer points are awarded every time you complete a Slayer task. The amount varies by master and whether you are on a task streak milestone.

Key mechanics: Every 10th task gives a point multiplier (5x for 10th, 15x for 50th, 25x for 100th, etc.). Tasks can be skipped for 30 points or blocked permanently for 100 points per slot. Points are only awarded for completing tasks from the master who assigned them.

2. The 9+1 Turael Boosting Method -- Fastest Points Per Hour

The 9+1 method is the fastest way to accumulate Slayer points. Complete 9 trivial tasks from Turael (0 points each) and then do your 10th task at Konar or Duradel for the streak bonus.

How the 9+1 Method Works

  1. Get a task from Konar (best) or Duradel.
  2. If the task is good (fast, high XP) -- complete it. If it's bad -- go to Turael to cancel it (this resets your current task but NOT your streak count).
  3. Complete 9 tasks from Turael in Burthorpe. Turael assigns trivial tasks: chickens, bats, goblins, cows, cave crawlers, etc. Each takes 1-3 minutes.
  4. After the 9th Turael task, go back to Konar or Duradel for your 10th task.
  5. You receive the 10th-streak bonus (180 points from Konar, 150 from Duradel).
  6. Complete that 10th task for the bonus points.
  7. Repeat: 9 Turael + 1 Konar/Duradel, over and over.

⚠️ Important -- Don't Reset Your Streak: When you use Turael to cancel a task from another master, he will WARN you that this resets your streak. This ONLY resets the CURRENT task, not your total task count. Your 10-task milestone counter continues from where it was. However, getting a task from Turael while you have an active task from another master DOES count as a reset of your current task -- which is exactly what you want for the 9+1 method.

Konar's location-specific tasks can be frustrating (e.g., Bloodvelds only in the God Wars Dungeon), but her point rewards are unmatched. She also drops Brimstone keys on task kills, which open Konar's chest for additional GP (~70K per key on average). Konar is universally the best master for pure point farming.

Duradel -- Best Balance of Points and XP

Duradel (Shilo Village, 100 combat + Shilo Village quest) gives 15 points per task and assigns the best high-level Slayer creatures: Abyssal Demons, Hellhounds, Dark Beasts, Dagannoths, etc. He is the best master for players who want to earn points while also training combat and Slayer efficiently.

Nieve / Steve -- Accessible Mid-Game Option

Nieve (or Steve, her replacement) is in the Gnome Stronghold and requires 85 combat. She gives 12 points per task and assigns a similar task list to Duradel. Best for players who don't have 100 combat yet but want reasonable points while training.

6. Advanced Tips for Maximum Point Efficiency

  • Save 50th and 100th milestones for Konar ONLY. Track your task count (RuneLite plugin or Slayer gem). When you are about to hit the 50th or 100th task, go to Konar for the massive 900-point (50th) or 1,800-point (100th) reward. This single decision can earn you hundreds of extra points per milestone.
  • Pre-set your block list before heavy point farming. Block tasks from Konar's or Duradel's rotation before you start farming. A clean block list means fewer skips, which means more points accumulating over time.
  • Use the Slayer ring to teleport directly to Turael, Konar, Nieve, and Duradel. Crafting a Slayer Ring (75 Crafting) or buying an Eternal Slayer Ring saves massive travel time during point farming.
  • Abyssal Demons at 85 Slayer are the best task in the game. Extend them immediately. Each Abyssal Demon drops ~2.5M GP (Abyssal Whip at 1/512), and you should always extend for the maximum kill count.
  • Use Expeditious Bracelets on Turael tasks. Expeditious Bracelets have a 25% chance to count a kill as 2 kills toward your task. This cuts Turael's already-fast tasks in half -- getting through the 9 tasks even faster.
  • 99 Slayer cape perk: At 99 Slayer, the cape lets you receive two tasks at once from any master. This effectively doubles your streak-building speed. If you are close to 99, push for the cape before heavy point farming.

7. Common Mistakes

  1. Wasting 50th/100th milestones on a low-tier master. A 50th task gives 0 points at Turael, 500 at Chaeldar, and 900 at Konar. Always do milestone tasks at the highest-tier master available.
  2. Blocking tasks before buying the Slayer Helmet. The Slayer Helmet's 15% boost saves more time across ALL tasks than blocking a few bad ones. Buy the helmet first, then block.
  3. Using Turael without understanding the streak reset. Turael's tasks count toward your task number, but getting a task from him while holding a task from another master resets that specific task. This is fine for the 9+1 method, but if you accidentally talk to Turael right before your 50th task, you can mess up your streak timing.
  4. Not using an Expeditious Bracelet on bad 10th tasks. If your 10th task from Konar is something slow (e.g., 150 Fire Giants), use an Expeditious Bracelet to speed through it. The goal is the bonus points, not the task itself.
  5. Hoarding points without spending them. Slayer points sitting in your account do nothing. Spend them on block slots, task extensions, and unlocks that immediately improve your Slayer experience. The earlier you unlock these, the more value they generate.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How many Slayer points do I need to unlock all the essentials?

A: Approximately 2,000-2,500 points. Slayer Helmet (400) + 6 block slots (600) + Broader Fletching (300) + key task extensions (~300) + Ring of Slaughter (75) + Ring of Wealth for Slayer (75) = ~1,750. Add 750 for the Rune Pouch if buying with points. With the 9+1 Konar method at 500-800 pts/hr, you can unlock all essentials in 3-5 hours of dedicated point farming.

Q: Is Konar always better than Duradel for points?

A: For pure points per hour, yes. Konar gives 18 points/task vs Duradel's 15. However, Konar's location-specific tasks can sometimes assign terrible locations (e.g., Fire Giants in the Stronghold Slayer Cave instead of the Catacombs). Duradel's tasks are generally faster to complete even though they give fewer points. If you don't mind location restrictions, Konar is better. If you want faster tasks, Duradel may feel better.

Q: Can I use the 9+1 method at low Slayer levels (before Konar)?

A: Yes! Use Duradel, Nieve, or Chaeldar as your "10th task" master. The principle is the same: 9 fast Turael tasks + 1 task from your highest-available master. At 70 combat, use Chaeldar (100 pts on 10th). At 85 combat, use Nieve (120 pts on 10th). At 100 combat, use Duradel (150 pts). At 75 Slayer, use Konar (180 pts). The method scales with your account progression.

The 9+1 Turael method is the fastest way to accumulate them -- and once you have the Slayer Helmet, 6 block slots, and your best tasks extended, the skill transforms. Every task becomes faster, more profitable, and more enjoyable.

Track your streak, talk to Turael, and stack those Konar milestones. Your Slayer helmet awaits.
