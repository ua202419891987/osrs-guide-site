1. Why Quest = Fastest Skill Training

In OSRS, you can train skills by grinding (killing monsters, chopping trees, etc.) or by questing (completing quests that reward XP). Questing is almost always faster, cheaper, and more fun than grinding.

📊 Quest XP vs Grinding XP: The Numbers

MethodXP per HourCost per HourEnjoyment
Grinding (e.g., power-mining Iron)~35,000Free (drops ore)😴 Boring
Questing (e.g., completing Dig Site for 15K XP)~200,000+ (if you do 5 quests in 1 hour)Free or cheap🎮 Fun (story+puzzles)
ConclusionQuesting = 5-10x fasterSimilar or cheaperMuch more fun

🎯 The Core Advantage of Quest Training

  • Speed: A single quest can give 10,000-50,000 XP in 30-90 minutes. Grinding that same XP could take 2-6 hours.
  • Multi-Skill Rewards: Many quests give XP in multiple skills at once. For example, Recipe for Disaster (RFD) gives combat XP, Cooking XP, and unlocks the Cooks' Guild.
  • Unlocks: Quests unlock new areas, bosses, teleports, and gear — which make future training faster.
  • Quest Points (QP): Many late-game quests require a certain number of QP. Doing quests early builds up QP for these requirements.

🧮 The Math: When Quests Beat Grinding

Let's say you want to get 50,000 XP in a skill. Here's how long it takes by method:

MethodTime to 50K XPGP Cost
Grinding (average 30K XP/h)~1 hour 40 minutesVaries
1 Quest giving 20K XP + 1 quest giving 30K XP~60-90 minutes (playtime) + cutscenes~10-50K GP (quest items)
Winner:Quest (similar time, way more fun)Quest (similar cost)
💡 The Golden Rule of Efficient Training

Always check if a quest gives XP in your target skill BEFORE you start grinding it. You might save hours of grinding by doing 1-2 quests instead. This guide exists to help you find those quests quickly.

💰 Quests That Unlock GP/h Methods (Indirect)

🎯 The Big Money Unlocks

  • Song of the Elves: Unlocks Prifddinas. In Prifddinas, you can do Zalcano (500K-1M GP/h) and Gauntlet (1-3M GP/h). This single quest unlocks the highest GP/h methods in the game.
  • Dragon Slayer II: Unlocks Vorkath (800K-1.5M GP/h). One of the best money-makers in the game.
  • Monkey Madness II: Unlocks Ape Atoll and better Ranged training (Red Chins = 300K+ GP/h if you sell them).
  • Beneath Cursed Sands: Unlocks the Tombs of Amascut (raid) — high-level PvM with 1-5M GP/h potential.
💡 Money-Making Priority

If your goal is to make GP fast, prioritize quests in this order:

  1. Song of the Elves (unlocks Prifddinas — highest GP/h in game)
  2. Dragon Slayer II (unlocks Vorkath — 800K-1.5M GP/h)
  3. Desert Treasure (unlocks Ancient Magicks — better bossing)
  4. Monkey Madness II (unlocks Red Chins — sell for GP)
  5. The Feud (unlocks Pollnivneach Thieving — 200K GP/h)

9. FAQ — Quest Training Questions

Which quest should I do FIRST on a new account?

Waterfall Quest. It gives 13,750 XP in Attack, Strength, Defence, and Hitpoints — enough to go from Level 1 to ~Level 30 in all those skills. It takes ~30 minutes and has almost no requirements. Do it on day one of any new account.

Are there any quests that give 99 in a skill immediately?

No. The maximum XP from any single quest is ~80,000 (Plague's End for some skills), which is enough to go from Level 1 to ~Level 38. You'll still need to grind or do multiple quests to reach 99. However, doing ALL quest rewards can get you to ~Level 50-60 in many skills for free.

I'm an Ironman. Should I prioritize different quests?

Yes. As an Ironman, you can't buy supplies from the GE, so quests that give useful items (like the Amulet of Glory from Horror from the Deep) are more valuable. Also prioritize quests that unlock farming patches (for herbs) and fishing spots (for food).

How many quest points (QP) do I need for late-game content?

Most grandmaster quests require 150-200 QP. As of June 2026, there are ~300 QP available in the game (from ~150 quests). You don't need to complete every quest, but you should aim for ~200 QP to have access to all late-game content.

Can I skip low-XP quests and just grind the skills instead?

You can, but it's usually less efficient. Even low-XP quests (like Witch's Potion which gives only 325 Magic XP) are worth doing because they take almost no time and the XP adds up. The only quests worth skipping are ones that are very hard and give very little XP (like One Small Favour — 3-5 hours for only ~20K XP).

Where can I find walkthroughs for these quests?

We have step-by-step walkthroughs for every quest on OSRSGuru.com. Alternatively, the official OSRS wiki has detailed guides. For very complex quests (like Plague's End), we recommend using a video guide (YouTube) alongside the text guide.

Do quest rewards scale with my level? (Like, if I do Waterfall Quest at Level 50 instead of Level 1, do I get more XP?)

No. Quest XP rewards are fixed amounts. It doesn't matter if you do the quest at Level 1 or Level 50 — you'll get the same XP. However, it's more efficient to do high-XP quests early (because the XP will level you up faster when your XP requirements are low).
