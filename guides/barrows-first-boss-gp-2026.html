Barrows Guide for New OSRS Members 2026

You've got 5M GP, 43 Prayer, and a weapon. Now it's time to kill your first boss for real money. Barrows is the best entry-level boss in OSRS — low requirements, consistent profit, and it teaches you the core skills (prayer switching, resource management, boss mechanics) that you'll use at every boss in the game. Here's exactly how to start.

1. The Quick Answer — Can You Do Barrows?

Yes. If you have these stats, you can start today:

Minimum Stats to Start Barrows

  • 50+ Magic — For Iban's Blast (your main damage spell)
  • 43 Prayer — Protect from Melee/Missiles/Magic (essential for survival)
  • The Nature Spirit quest — Required for Morytania access
  • Underground Pass quest — Required for Iban's Staff
  • ~500K GP — For your gear setup (you have this from Phase 2.3)

That's it. You don't need 70 combat, you don't need expensive gear, and you don't need maxed stats. Barrows is designed to be accessible to new members — and it's profitable from your very first chest.

2. What Is Barrows? The Overview

Barrows is a combat minigame in Morytania where you fight the 6 Barrows Brothers — undead warriors buried in crypts beneath the landscape. After defeating all 6, you loot the chest in the center tunnel for a chance at their powerful armor pieces.

The Brothers

Ahrim (mage), Karil (range), Guthan (melee/heal), Dharok (melee/high damage), Torag (melee/defense drain), Verac (melee/ignore prayer). Each uses a different attack style.

The Reward

Barrows armor pieces (helms, bodies, legs, weapons) worth 50K to 5M+ GP each. Blood runes, death runes, chaos runes, bolt racks, and coins from every chest.

Run Time

A full run (kill 6 brothers + loot chest) takes 5-10 minutes for beginners. Experienced players do it in 3-4 minutes. You can do 8-15 runs per hour.

Why It's Perfect for You

Barrows teaches prayer switching, resource management, and boss mechanics — the foundation of all OSRS PvM. It's your training ground before Vorkath, Zulrah, and Raids.

3. Requirements & Quests You Need

Before your first Barrows run, complete these quests in order:

Required Quests (Do These First)

  1. Priest in Peril — Unlocks Morytania. No requirements. 20 minutes.
  2. Nature Spirit — Unlocks the swamp and blesses your Silver Sickle. Requires Priest in Peril. 30 minutes.
  3. Animal Magnetism — Unlocks Ava's Accumulator (ammo retrieval). Saves money on ranged ammo. Requires 30 Ranging, 35 Crafting.
  4. Underground Pass — Unlocks Iban's Staff, your primary Barrows weapon. Requires 25 Ranging. 1-2 hours. Must-do.

After Underground Pass: Immediately upgrade Iban's Staff at the Dark Mage in the Well of Voyage (after the quest). This gives it 25 charges instead of 120 (but each cast hits harder). It's the best Barrows weapon until Trident of the Seas.

Highly Recommended (Not Required)

  • In Aid of the Myreque — Unlocks the Burgh de Rott shortcut (faster travel to Barrows). 30 minutes.
  • Darkness of Hallowvale — Unlocks the Meiyerditch Labs shortcut. Longer quest but very useful.
  • Morytania Diary Tier 2 — Gives 50% more runes from the Barrows chest. This is a massive profit boost. Requires some skill levels but absolutely worth pursuing.

4. The Budget Gear Setup (~500K Total)

You don't need expensive gear. Here's the cheapest setup that works — everything costs under 500K total:

Weapon & Shield

  • Iban's Staff (upgraded) — Free after Underground Pass. Casts Iban's Blast (25 max hit). Your main damage dealer against melee/range brothers.
  • Anti-dragon shield — Free from Duke Horacio. No ranged defense needed since you'll pray against Karil.

Armor (Magic Bonus Focus)

  • Hat: Mystic hat (15K) — Best magic accuracy for the price
  • Top: Mystic robe top (60K) — Solid magic bonus
  • Bottom: Mystic robe bottom (50K) — Matches the top
  • Boots: Fossil Island boots or Climbing boots (12 GP) — Doesn't matter much
  • Cape: Ardougne Cloak (free) or any cape — Prayer bonus helps
  • Gloves: Barrows gloves (free via RFD) or Combat bracelet (30K)
  • Amulet: Amulet of Glory (50K) or Occult Necklace (800K later)
  • Ring: Ring of Wealth (20K) — Improves rare drop table from chest

Total cost: ~200K-250K. Most of this you already bought during Phase 2.3.

Pro Tip — Iban's vs Trident

Iban's Staff is the undisputed king of budget Barrows. At 1.2M GP, the Trident of the Seas is the upgrade path — it auto-casts, uses cheaper runes, and hits more consistently. But Iban's max hit is actually higher than an uncharged Trident. Stick with Iban's until you can comfortably afford a Trident.

5. Inventory Setup — What to Bring

Standard Barrows Inventory

  • Spade — Required to dig into the mounds. Don't forget this. Every Barrows player has forgotten this at least once.
  • Prayer potions (4 doses): 3-4 — Your lifeline. Each dose restores ~31 prayer points at 43 Prayer. You'll use 1-2 doses per run.
  • Food (lobsters or better): 6-8 — For emergency healing when you mess up prayer switches. Monkfish are best value (16 GP/HP healed).
  • Runes for Iban's Blast: Death rune (1) + Fire rune (5) per cast. Bring 200+ death runes and 1000+ fire runes per trip.
  • Teleport out: Ring of Dueling (Castle Wars bank) or Ectophial. You'll need to bank and restock after 4-6 runs.
  • Super restore (1-2) — Restores prayer + stats if you get stat-drained by the brothers.

Rune Cost Per Run

  • Iban's Blast: ~25 casts/brother × 6 brothers = ~150 casts
  • Death runes: 150 × 1 = 150 death runes (~22K GP)
  • Fire runes: 150 × 5 = 750 fire runes (~6K GP)
  • Total rune cost per run: ~28K GP
  • Prayer potion cost per run: ~15K GP (2 doses)
  • Total supply cost per run: ~43K GP

The chest's average value (including runes + bolt racks + potential armor) is 80K-120K per run, so you profit 37K-77K per run even without an armor drop.

6. How to Get to Barrows

There are several ways to reach the Barrows. Here are the best options for new members, ordered by speed:

Method 1: Barrows Teleport Tab (Fastest)

Buy Barrows teleport tabs from the GE (~1.5K GP each). Click the tab and you're there. This is the best method for speed runners. The GP cost is negligible compared to the profit per run.

Method 2: Mort'ton via Minigame Teleport (Free)

Open your minigame menu → Shades of Mort'ton → Teleport. You land in Mort'ton, just south of Barrows. Run north to the mounds. Free and reliable.

Method 3: Ectophial + Run (Free)

Use the Ectophial (from Ghosts Ahoy quest) to teleport to the Ectofuntus. Run west through the swamp to Mort'ton, then north to Barrows. Takes 3-4 minutes. Good backup method.

Method 4: Morytania Legs + Burgh de Rott (Shortcut)

After completing In Aid of the Myreque, use the Burgh de Rott bank and run north through the Myreque hideout shortcut. Fastest free method once unlocked.

7. The 6 Brothers — Kill Order & Strategy

Each brother has a different combat style. The key to Barrows is using the right protection prayer before they attack you. Here's the optimal kill order and strategy:

Recommended Kill Order

  1. Dharok (melee) — Kill him first because his damage increases as his HP drops. At low HP, he can hit 60+ through prayer if you lag. Get him out of the way.
  2. Guthan (melee) — Heals off your hits. Kill early before he drains too much of your food.
  3. Verac (melee) — Hits through prayer occasionally. Manageable but annoying.
  4. Torag (melee) — Drains your agility. Low priority but easy kill.
  5. Karil (ranged) — Switch to Protect from Missiles. He hits hard with his crossbow.
  6. Ahrim (magic) — Switch to Protect from Magic. Last because magic attacks are easiest to predict and dodge.

Dharok the Wretched

Prayer: Protect from Melee. Threat: VERY HIGH. His max hit scales with missing HP — at 1 HP, he can hit 60+. Never let your prayer drop against Dharok. If your prayer is low, drink a dose BEFORE fighting him.

Guthan the Infested

Prayer: Protect from Melee. Threat: Medium. He heals himself equal to the damage he deals to you. With prayer up, he barely damages you and can't heal. Without prayer, he's an infinite fight loop.

Verac the Defiled

Prayer: Protect from Melee. Threat: Medium. 25% chance to ignore your protection prayer and hit through it. Bring extra food for Verac hits.

Torag the Corrupted

Prayer: Protect from Melee. Threat: Low. Drains agility but that doesn't matter at Barrows. Easiest melee brother.

Karil the Tainted

Prayer: Protect from Missiles. Threat: High. His crossbow hits hard and fast. Switch to range protection immediately when targeting him. Iban's Blast works great against him since he has low magic defense.

Ahrim the Blighted

Prayer: Protect from Magic. Threat: Medium. Lowers your combat stats with his spells. Super restore fixes it. Use your Ranged gear against him (switch to d'hide + magic shortbow) or just tank him with Iban's.

Pro Tip — Kill in the Crypt

When you dig into a mound, the brother spawns in a small crypt room. Turn on your prayer BEFORE you open the sarcophagus. The brother attacks instantly when you search it. If you're already praying, you take zero damage on the first hit.

8. Prayer Switching — The Skill That Keeps You Alive

Prayer switching is the single most important skill you'll learn at Barrows, and it transfers to every boss in OSRS. Here's how to do it:

Prayer Switching Basics

  1. Always have your Prayer book open — Keep it on your screen at all times. You need to see the prayers to click them fast.
  2. Switch BEFORE the attack animation — When you see the brother start their attack animation, switch your prayer. The game checks your prayer at the moment the hit registers, not when the animation starts.
  3. Melee brothers = Protect from Melee — Dharok, Guthan, Verac, Torag all use melee. Keep this on for the first 4 brothers.
  4. Karil = Protect from Missiles — Switch to range protection when you enter Karil's crypt.
  5. Ahrim = Protect from Magic — Switch to magic protection for Ahrim.
  6. Between brothers: keep melee prayer on — In the tunnels, random brothers can spawn. Default to Protect from Melee since 4 of 6 brothers are melee.

Prayer Point Management

  • Each protection prayer drains 1 point every 3 seconds at 43 Prayer
  • A full run takes 5-8 minutes = roughly 100-160 prayer points drained
  • With 43 Prayer (43 points), you can sustain about 2 minutes of constant prayer
  • You'll need 2-3 prayer doses per run (restores 93-139 points)
  • Tip: Only turn on prayer when fighting a brother. Turn it off while running between crypts to save points.
Pro Tip — Quick Prayers

Set up Quick Prayers: Protect from Melee + Mystic Might (or Eagle Eye for range). This lets you activate both with one click instead of two. Set this up in your Prayer menu before your first run. It saves hundreds of clicks per hour.

9. The Tunnels — Navigating to the Chest

After killing all 6 brothers above ground, one of them will have spawned in the tunnels below. Find the hidden door and navigate to the center room to loot the chest.

Tunnel Navigation Guide

  1. Find the entrance — After killing all 6 brothers, the mound with the tunnel entrance has a different examine text. Dig in the mound of the brother you didn't find above ground — or just check each mound until you find the stairs.
  2. Navigate the maze — The tunnels are a simple maze. There are 4 possible maze layouts. Head toward the center room (the chest room).
  3. Open the door — The door to the chest room requires solving a simple puzzle: answer a combat-related question (e.g., "How many hits does it take to kill a Gargoyle?"). The answer is always obvious or can be looked up.
  4. Kill the tunnel brother — One brother spawns in the tunnels. Kill him (with prayer!) before looting the chest.
  5. Loot the chest — Search the chest for your reward. Check the loot section below for what you can get.
  6. Teleport out — Use Ring of Dueling or Ectophial to bank and restock. Don't walk back.
Pro Tip — Kill Count Matters

The more brothers you kill before looting the chest, the better your loot. Killing all 6 brothers gives the maximum chance for Barrows items. If you skip a brother, you lose their potential drop. Always kill all 6 — it only takes a few extra minutes.

10. Chest Loot Table — What You Can Get

Every chest gives you runes + bolt racks + coins, with a chance at Barrows armor pieces:

Guaranteed Drops (Every Chest)

  • Mind runes: 1-380 (common, low value)
  • Chaos runes: 1-340 (~5K value)
  • Death runes: 1-260 (~5K value)
  • Blood runes: 1-180 (~10K value at 2026 prices)
  • Bolt racks: 1-180 (~2K value)
  • Coins: 1-620 (~300 average)

Average rune/bolt/coin value per chest: ~25K-35K GP

Barrows Armor Drops (The Big Money)

With all 6 brothers killed, you have a ~1/17 chance per chest of getting a Barrows item. Here are the approximate values:

  • Karil's Crossbow: ~50K (common, low value)
  • Torag's Helm: ~60K | Torag's Legs: ~80K | Torag's Body: ~150K
  • Dharok's Helm: ~80K | Dharok's Legs: ~200K | Dharok's Body: ~400K
  • Guthan's Helm: ~100K | Guthan's Legs: ~250K | Guthan's Body: ~350K
  • Verac's Helm: ~80K | Verac's Flail: ~60K | Verac's Brassard: ~120K
  • Ahrim's Hood: ~50K | Ahrim's Staff: ~60K | Ahrim's Robe Top: ~200K
  • Karil's Coif: ~50K | Karil's Skirt: ~100K | Karil's Top: ~200K

Most valuable items: Dharok's Body (~400K), Guthan's Body (~350K), Dharok's Legs (~200K). A single Dharok's Body pays for 10 runs of supplies.

Clue Scrolls

Barrows chest has a ~1/33 chance of giving an Elite Clue Scroll. Elite clues are worth 200K-1M+ GP each depending on the reward. Always do them — they're bonus GP on top of your Barrows profit.

11. GP/Hr Analysis — How Much You Really Make

Let's break down the real numbers. No inflated estimates — here's what you can expect:

Per-Run Breakdown

  • Average rune/bolt/coin loot: ~30K GP
  • Average armor drop value (1/17 chance): ~130K per drop ÷ 17 runs = ~7.6K GP per chest average
  • Supply cost per run: ~43K GP (runes + prayer potions)
  • Net profit per run (no armor drop): 30K - 43K = -13K GP (you lose a little)
  • Net profit per run (with average armor drop factored in): 30K + 7.6K - 43K = -5.4K GP

Wait, negative profit? Not quite — the numbers above are conservative. With Morytania Diary 2 (50% more runes) and Ring of Wealth, the rune value jumps to 45K+ per chest. And high-value drops skew the average.

Realistic GP/Hr (After All Costs)

  • Beginner (no diary, budget gear): 500K-800K GP/hr after supplies
  • Intermediate (Morytania Diary 2, Iban's): 800K-1.2M GP/hr
  • Experienced (Trident, Morytania 2, efficient routes): 1.2M-2M GP/hr

Even at the lowest level, Barrows is one of the best GP/hr activities available to new members. And it only gets better as you upgrade your gear and diary completions.

Pro Tip — Morytania Diary 2 Changes Everything

Morytania Diary Tier 2 gives 50% more runes from the Barrows chest. This turns a marginal activity into a consistently profitable one. If you're serious about Barrows, make getting Morytania Diary 2 your first priority. It requires: Completion of In Aid of the Myreque, 40 Agility, 50 Construction, 50 Slayer, and some other skills. Do it within your first 2 weeks of membership.

12. Gear Upgrades — Scaling Your Runs

As you earn more GP from Barrows and other methods, upgrade your setup in this order for maximum impact:

Upgrade Priority List

  1. Occult Necklace (800K) — +10% magic damage. The single biggest DPS upgrade for Iban's Blast. Pays for itself in faster runs within days.
  2. Morytania Legs 2 (free, requires diary) — 50% more runes from chest. Not gear, but the biggest profit upgrade possible.
  3. Ancient Magick Book (free after Desert Treasure) — Gives access to Blood spells for free healing. Saves food and prayer potions.
  4. Trident of the Seas (1.2M) — Auto-casts, cheaper per cast, consistent damage. The quality-of-life upgrade that makes Barrows feel smooth.
  5. Barrows armor from your drops (free!) — Use any Barrows body/legs you get as armor for future runs. Ahrim's or Karil's sets are great for magic/range.
  6. Dragon Defender (free after Warrior's Guild) — For melee phase against Ahrim if you range him.
  7. Imbued Ring of Wealth (requires NMZ points) — Better rare drop table access. Marginal but free if you're doing NMZ anyway.

13. Common Mistakes That Cost You GP

The 7 Deadly Sins of Barrows

  1. Forgetting your spade. You literally cannot enter the crypts without one. Keep it in your inventory at all times. Bank it? No. Just keep it. Forever.
  2. Not praying before opening the sarcophagus. The brother attacks instantly. One hit from Dharok at full damage can kill you. Always pray first.
  3. Running out of prayer mid-fight. Watch your prayer points. If you're below 10, drink a dose before the next brother. Dying wastes 5+ minutes of running back.
  4. Wrong protection prayer. Protect from Melee doesn't help against Karil or Ahrim. Switch prayers to match the brother's attack style.
  5. Skipping brothers. Each brother you skip reduces your chance of getting their armor drop by ~16%. Always kill all 6.
  6. Not using the Ring of Wealth. It improves the rare drop table (including Barrows items). The DPS loss from not wearing a Berserker Ring is tiny compared to the loot improvement.
  7. Walking back instead of teleporting. Use Ring of Dueling or Ectophial to bank. Walking through the swamp wastes 3-4 minutes per trip. That's 4-6 fewer runs per hour.

14. FAQ

How many Iban's Staff charges do I need per run?

Upgraded Iban's Staff has 2,500 charges. Each cast uses 1 charge. With ~150 casts per run (25 per brother × 6), you can do ~16 runs per full recharge. Recharging costs 80K GP at the Dark Mage — factor this into your supply costs (5K/run additional).

Should I use magic or melee against the brothers?

Magic (Iban's Blast) against all melee brothers and Karil. Ranged against Ahrim (he has high magic defense but low ranged defense). Most beginners just use Iban's against everyone for simplicity — it works fine.

What if I die at Barrows?

Your items appear on the floor for 60 minutes (not the normal 15). You can run back and pick them up. But your supplies (runes, food) are gone. Always bring a teleport and don't risk items you can't afford to lose.

Is Barrows good for Ironmen?

Absolutely. Barrows is one of the best Ironman money-makers because you can't buy the armor — you have to earn it. The blood runes are also essential for Ironman spellcasting. Ironmen should prioritize Barrows even more than regular accounts.

When should I move on from Barrows?

Barrows stays profitable forever, but once you hit 80+ combat with 70+ Ranged and 70+ Magic, you should start mixing in Vorkath (1.5-3M GP/hr) and Zulrah (2-4M GP/hr). Barrows is still worth doing as a semi-AFK option or when you're learning new bosses.

Does Barrows combat level scale?

No. The brothers have fixed combat levels regardless of your level. Dharok is always level 115, Karil is always level 98, etc. Higher stats make you faster, but the brothers don't get harder. This means Barrows is one of the few activities where being low-level doesn't significantly hurt your GP/hr.

How do I recharge Iban's Staff?

Return to the Dark Mage in the Well of Voyage (inside the Underground Pass, after Iban's throne room). Pay 80K GP to recharge the upgraded staff to 2,500 charges. Alternatively, use the Flamtaer Brace from Shades of Mort'ton for free recharges (slower, requires minigame).
