OSRS F2P Combat Training Guide — Level 3 to 30+ (2026)

Skill Training F2P Combat Beginner Updated 2026

Starting out in Old School RuneScape as a free-to-play player can feel overwhelming. You've just finished Tutorial Island with level 3 in all combat stats, and suddenly you're thrust into Lumbridge with nothing but a bronze sword and big dreams. Don't worry — this comprehensive guide will take you from a level 3 noob to a combat level 30+ warrior entirely in F2P, setting you up perfectly for when you decide to become a member. We'll cover every aspect of F2P combat training: melee progression, ranged training, prayer grinding, quest rewards that boost your stats for free, and a day-by-day training schedule that actually works.

Section 1: Understanding F2P Combat Limits

Before we dive into the training methods, it's crucial to understand exactly what free-to-play players can and cannot do. Many new players assume F2P is hopelessly limited — it's not. You can actually achieve quite respectable levels without ever paying a cent.

What's Possible in F2P

Free-to-play mode gives you access to a surprising amount of content. You can train Attack up to level 60, Strength up to 60, Defence up to 60, Ranged up to 50+, Magic up to 59 (with high alchemy), and Prayer up to 43 (Protect from Melee). The absolute maximum combat level for a pure F2P account is 126, though reaching that without membership would take an absurd amount of time.

For the purposes of this guide, our goal is more modest and practical: get you to combat level 30-40+ entirely in F2P. Why? Because once you buy membership (either with real money or by buying a bond with in-game GP), you'll be ready to jump straight into member content without feeling underleveled or undergeared.

Combat Level Formula (Important!)

Your combat level is calculated using this formula:

Combat Level = 0.25 × (Attack + Strength) + 0.25 × Defence + 0.25 × Constitution + 0.125 × Prayer + 0.125 × (max of Ranged/Magic)

This means your base combat stats (Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints) contribute the most to your combat level. Prayer and your highest non-melee attack style (Ranged or Magic) contribute half as much. For a new F2P player, focusing on Attack and Strength first gives you the fastest combat level gains.

Pro tip: Don't neglect Defence entirely. While some players make "pures" (keeping Defence at 1), as a F2P player you'll want at least 20-30 Defence to wear decent armor. It makes training much less painful because you'll take less damage and use less food.

Section 2: F2P Gear Progression (Free Player Gear Tiers)

One of the most satisfying parts of OSRS is watching your character transform from a bronze-geared noob to a rune-wearing warrior. In F2P, gear progression is straightforward but important — using the best gear you can afford and wield makes a massive difference to your XP rates and survivability.

Melee Gear Progression (Attack/Strength/Defence)

The golden rule of F2P melee training: always use a scimitar. Scimitars have the fastest attack speed in the game for slash weapons (same speed as a whip, which is the best member weapon), making them far superior to swords, longswords, or 2-handed swords for training. The only exception is if you're doing controlled training (to level Attack, Strength, and Defence simultaneously), where a 2H sword might be useful — but even then, scimitar wins for pure DPS.

Ranged Gear Progression

Ranged is excellent in F2P because you can safespot monsters (attack them from a distance where they can't reach you). The key to good ranged training is using the shortbow (faster attack speed than longbow) and the best arrows you can afford.

Important F2P Gear Notes

  • Scimitars (slash) are BEST for training — fastest attack speed, maximizing DPS
  • Always wear the best armour you CAN wield for your Defence level — more armour = less damage taken = less food needed
  • Amulets: Amulet of Power (best all-round F2P amulet, +6 to all melee attack bonuses) or Amulet of Accuracy (cheaper alternative, +10 slash accuracy)
  • Cape: Strength cape (+2 to +4 Strength bonus) from the Warrior Guild — requires combined Attack + Strength combat levels totaling 130 (you can do this in F2P!)
  • Shield: Always use a shield when training Defence (set attack style to "Defensive" or "Controlled") — the Defence bonus reduces damage taken
  • Ring: Ring of life (drops summoning stone when you die, F2P accessible) or undoubtedly the best F2P ring is the Ring of Life

Section 3: Levels 1–10 Attack/Strength (Chickens & Cows)

Every OSRS journey starts the same way: in Lumbridge, surrounded by chickens. Don't skip this phase! The first 10 levels go by incredibly fast, and you can make some decent starting cash by collecting drops.

Best Monster: Chickens (Lumbridge & Falador)

Chicken Stats: Hitpoints: 3 | Defence: Very low | Max Hit: 1

Chickens are the classic starting monster for a reason. They have only 3 HP, which means they die in 1-2 hits even with bronze weapons. More importantly, they drop feathers — and feathers are stackable items that sell for about 5-6 GP each at the Grand Exchange.

XP Rates: ~200-300 combat XP per kill at level 3 (it adds up fast when you're one-shotting everything)

Strategy:

  1. Equip your best scimitar (iron or steel if you can afford it)
  2. Set your attack style to "Attack" (trains Attack) or "Strength" (trains Strength) — your choice
  3. Find the chicken coop northwest of Lumbridge Castle (or the one near Falador farm)
  4. Kill chickens, collect feathers, ignore the raw chicken (unless you want to train Cooking)
  5. Bank feathers at the Lumbridge bank deposit box (top floor of Lumbridge Castle)... actually, there's NO deposit box in Lumbridge. You'll need to run to the Draynor Village bank or just drop the feathers if your inventory fills up. Alternatively, keep them and make your first 10-20k GP!

Money Maker: 1,000 feathers ≈ 5,000-6,000 GP. That's enough to buy your steel scimitar and armor!

Time: Takes about 30-45 minutes to reach Attack level 10 from level 1.

Alternative: Cows (Lumbridge)

Cow Stats: Hitpoints: 8 | Defence: Low | Max Hit: 2

Cows are slightly tougher than chickens but drop cowhides, which sell for about 100-120 GP each! This is actually one of the best early money-making methods in F2P. A full inventory of 28 cowhides sells for over 3,000 GP.

Drops: Cowhide (bank them for GP!), Raw beef (can cook for Cooking XP), Bones (bury for Prayer XP)

Cowhides are excellent F2P money! Here's the strategy: kill cows north of Lumbridge, collect the hides, and bank them at the Draynor Village bank (west of Lumbridge). Once you have a full inventory, run to Draynor and deposit them. Repeat. With 50-100 cowhides, you'll have enough GP to buy your first set of steel or even mithril gear.

Good for: Training Strength alongside Attack (cows have more HP so you get more combat XP per kill)

Time: Takes about 1 hour to get Attack and Strength to level 10.

Gear for this stage: Iron or Steel scimitar, basic armor (iron or steel full helm/body/legs). If you're really broke, stick with bronze — it'll do fine for another few levels.

Section 4: Levels 10–20 Attack/Strength (Minotaurs & Goblins)

At combat level 10-15, you're ready to leave the farm animals behind and face some real monsters. The Stronghold of Security is your best friend here — it's a free mini-dungeon that gives massive rewards.

Best Monster: Level 12 Minotaurs (Stronghold of Security Floor 1)

Minotaur Stats: Hitpoints: 10-12 | Defence: Low | Max Hit: 3

The Stronghold of Security is located underground in Barbarian Village (west of Varrock, north of Lumbridge). It has 4 floors, each with progressively harder monsters and a reward at the end of each floor.

WHY Minotaurs are amazing:

  • Located in a safe dungeon (if you die, you keep your items)
  • They have low defence and decent HP for your level
  • They drop iron arrows (sometimes noted!)
  • Completing Floor 1 of the Stronghold gives you 20,000 GP!
  • The Stronghold also gives XP rewards and access to the fancy boots (not F2P, but still cool)

XP Rate: ~15,000–20,000 combat XP per hour at levels 10-20

Strategy:

  1. Travel to Barbarian Village (use the lodestone/teleport if you have one, or walk from Varrock)
  2. Enter the Stronghold of Security (big entrance in the ground, look for the ladder)
  3. Go to Floor 1 (the first floor you reach)
  4. Kill Minotaurs until you've explored the whole floor and found the reward room
  5. Answer the security questions (it's a fun anti-phishing measure) and claim your 20k GP!

Time: Takes about 1-1.5 hours to push from 10→20 in a combat stat.

Money: The iron arrows they drop add up, and the 20k GP reward is enough to buy your mithril scimitar!

Also Good: Goblins (Various Locations)

Goblin Stats: Hitpoints: 5-9 | Defence: Very low | Max Hit: 1-2

Goblins are everywhere in F2P — behind Lumbridge, in the Goblin Village north of Falador, and near the Stronghold. They're not as efficient as Minotaurs for XP, but they're easier to find and good for mixing things up if the Stronghold is crowded.

Drops: Bronze arrows, bones, low-level herbs (rare)

Gear now: Black or Mithril scimitar (save up for mithril at level 20 — it's a big DPS upgrade!), iron/steel/black armor set. If you can afford it, upgrade to mithril armor as soon as you hit 20 Defence.

Section 5: Levels 20–30 Attack/Strength (Hill Giants & Flesh Crawlers)

Now we're getting into the "real" combat training. At level 20+, you can access some of the best F2P training spots in the game. This is where you'll start seeing noticeable XP rates and actually making some profit while training.

Best Monster: Hill Giants (Edgeville Dungeon)

Hill Giant Stats: Hitpoints: 28 | Defence: Low attack/defence | Max Hit: 9

Hill Giants are arguably the best F2P combat training monster. They have 28 HP (meaning lots of combat XP per kill), decent drops, and they're in a convenient location. Most importantly, they drop Big Bones — which are excellent for Prayer training!

Location: Edgeville Dungeon (enter the dungeon entrance west of Edgeville, bring a brass key or walk around the wilderness entrance). You can buy a brass key from the Grand Exchange for about 500 GP if you don't want to run through the wilderness.

XP Rate: ~25,000–30,000 combat XP per hour

Profit Source:

  • Big Bones: Bank them for Prayer training! They sell for ~300-400 GP each if you don't want to bury them.
  • Limpwurt Roots: Rare drop (~1/40 kill chance), sells for 1,500-2,000 GP each!
  • Nature Runes: Occasionally dropped, worth ~200-300 GP each

Perfect for training both melee AND collecting prayer bones simultaneously. If you're planning to train Prayer (and you should — Protect from Melee at level 43 Prayer is a game-changer), kill Hill Giants and save every Big Bone they drop.

Time: Takes about 2-3 hours to push from 20→30 in a combat stat.

Safespotting: Hill Giants can be safespotted in Edgeville Dungeon. There's a specific rock formation where you can stand so they can't reach you but you can still attack them with melee. Look up "Edgeville Dungeon Hill Giant safespot" on YouTube for a visual guide.

Alternative: Flesh Crawlers (Stronghold of Security Floor 2)

Flesh Crawler Stats: Hitpoints: 29 | Defence: Low | Max Hit: 1 (almost always misses!)

Flesh Crawlers are absolutely broken for AFK training. Here's why they're special:

  • They have a max hit of 1, and they almost always miss at levels 20+
  • They are aggressive for 10-20 minutes — meaning they will attack YOU automatically. You can stand still and just click "Attack" once, and they'll keep coming to you!
  • This makes them fully AFK-able — you don't waste time running around clicking the next monster

XP Rate: ~30,000+ XP per hour (because you don't waste time clicking, not because they give more XP per kill)

Downside: They have no valuable drops. You'll get body runes and the occasional herb (ranarr, avantoe, etc. — but very rare). The real value here is the AFK XP.

BEST AFK option for levels 20-30. If you want to train while watching a video or doing something else, Flesh Crawlers are perfect.

Gear now: Mithril scimitar (at level 20) or Adamant scimitar (at level 30 — another big DPS upgrade!), mithril/adamant armor. If you've been saving your cowhides and Hill Giant drops, you should be able to afford the adamant set by level 30.

Section 6: Levels 30–40 Attack/Strength (Hobgoblins & Skeletons)

The final stretch to "respectable" F2P combat levels. At this point, you should be using adamant weapons (or saving up for rune). Your XP rates will be solid, and the money you can make from monster drops will easily pay for your gear upgrades.

Best Monster: Hobgoblins (Near Crafting Guild)

Hobgoblin Stats: Hitpoints: 28-42 (depending on type) | Defence: Medium | Max Hit: 6-8

There are several types of Hobgoblins in F2P. The ones near the Crafting Guild (west of Falador, south of the Guild) are level 42 and drop some of the best loot available to F2P players.

Location: Near the Crafting Guild (requires 40 Crafting to enter, but you can kill Hobgoblins outside), or in Edgeville Dungeon (deeper section), or in the wilderness (not recommended for low levels).

Why they're great:

  • Limpwurt Root drops: Hobgoblins have a decent drop rate for Limpwurt Roots (~1/30-40 chance), which sell for 1,500-2,000 GP each!
  • They also drop herbs (guam, marrentill, tarromin — all sell for GP), nature runes, and law runes
  • Decent HP for good combat XP per kill

XP Rate: ~30,000–35,000 combat XP per hour

Profit: With consistent Limpwurt Root drops, you can make 50,000-100,000 GP per hour just from the roots and herbs!

Also Good: Moss Giants (Varrock Sewers)

Moss Giant Stats: Hitpoints: 42 | Defence: Decent | Max Hit: 8

Moss Giants are tougher but more rewarding. They're located in the Varrock Sewers (access via the manhole in Varrock's southeast corner, or via the Champions' Guild).

Drops: Big Bones (more Prayer training!), herbs (better chance than Hobgoblins), runes (nature, law, cosmic), and Mossy Keys (rare — opens Moss Giant boss chest in members, but still valuable).

More challenging but rewarding. If you have 30+ Defence and an adamant scimitar, Moss Giants are a good challenge.

For the Level 40 Attack Goal: You NEED the Rune Scimitar

The Rune Scimitar is the single biggest DPS upgrade available to F2P players. It requires 40 Attack to wield and has a +43 slash bonus (compared to +35 for adamant scimitar). That's not just a small upgrade — it's a massive increase in DPS.

Cost: Buy from the Grand Exchange for about 15,000-20,000 GP. That might sound like a lot when you're used to cowhides being 100 GP each, but trust me — it's worth every GP. With a few hours of Hobgoblin killing or cowhide collecting, you'll have enough.

Pro tip: If you can't afford a Rune Scimitar yet, keep using the Adamant Scimitar. It's still good. But make getting that Rune Scimi your top priority — it dramatically speeds up all remaining combat training.

Section 7: F2P Defence Training

Defence is often neglected by new players, but it's actually one of the most important stats in F2P. Higher Defence means you can wear better armor (Rune armor requires 40 Defence!), take less damage, and train for longer without needing food.

Why Raise Defence?

  • Wear better armor: Rune armor (the best F2P armor) requires 40 Defence. Without Defence training, you're stuck with adamant or lower.
  • Take less damage: Every Defence level reduces the max hit monsters can land on you. At 40 Defence, most low-level monsters will hit 0-2 instead of 4-6.
  • Less food needed: If you're taking less damage, you don't need to bring as much food. That means longer training sessions.
  • Higher combat level: Defence contributes to your combat level, making you eligible for more content.

Defence Training Method

Defence training follows the same monster progression as Attack/Strength training, but with one key difference: set your combat style to "Defensive" or "Controlled".

When you have a scimitar or any melee weapon equipped, you'll see three attack style options: "Accurate" (trains Attack), "Aggressive" (trains Strength), and "Defensive" (trains Defence). There's also "Controlled" (trains Attack, Strength, and Defence evenly — available on some weapons like the scimitar).

Recommended approach: Don't train Defence in isolation. Instead, use the "Controlled" attack style (available on scimitars) to train all three melee stats simultaneously. This is much more efficient than grinding Defence separately.

Goal for F2P: Reach 30-40 Defence so you can wear Mithril, Adamant, and ultimately Rune armor sets. Rune armor (requires 40 Defence) gives a massive armour bonus and makes you much tankier.

Training tip: Alternate between Attack/Strength training and Defence training rather than "pure" levelling. Balanced accounts progress through content much smoother, especially in F2P where you don't have access to prayer potions or high-level food.

Section 8: F2P Ranged Training (Levels 1-40)

Ranged is incredibly useful in F2P because it allows you to safespot monsters. A safespot is a location where you can attack a monster but they can't reach you, meaning you take zero damage. This makes Ranged excellent for low-level players who want to train safely.

Ranged Training Progression

Levels 1-10: Chickens & Cows

Same as melee, but with a shortbow and bronze/iron arrows. Stand at a distance and click the monster. Ranged XP is based on how many HP the monster had when you hit it (so you get more XP for hitting higher-HP monsters).

Levels 10-20: Minotaurs & Goblins (safespotted)

In the Stronghold of Security, there are safespots where you can stand behind a barrier and range monsters while they can't reach you. This is safe and AFK-able.

Levels 20-30: Hill Giants (SAFE SPOT in Edgeville Dungeon!)

There's a safespot in Edgeville Dungeon where Hill Giants can't reach you. Look for a rock formation or a corner where you can stand and attack with arrows. This is excellent ranged training because Hill Giants have 28 HP (more HP = more Ranged XP per kill) and they drop Big Bones.

Levels 30-40: Moss Giants (safespotted in Varrock Sewers) or Hobgoblins

Moss Giants in Varrock Sewers can be safespotted. They have 42 HP, making them excellent Ranged training. Hobgoblins are also good if you can find a safespot.

Ranged Training Tips

  • Always use the best bow you can equip: Maple shortbow at level 30 is a huge upgrade over willow. The attack speed and accuracy increase noticeably.
  • Don't waste money on Rune arrows for training: Adamant arrows are fine (much cheaper). Save your Rune arrows for bossing or PvP.
  • Shortbow > Longbow: Shortbows have faster attack speed. In OSRS, DPS (damage per second) matters more than max hit for training.
  • XP rates: ~15,000 XP/hr at low levels to ~35,000 XP/hr at levels 30-40 with a maple bow and adamant arrows.
  • Range gear: Wear leather armor (hard leather body at 22 Defence is best F2P ranged body). Coif (20 Ranged required) is the best F2P ranged helm.

Section 9: F2P Prayer Training (Levels 1-43)

Prayer is one of the most underrated skills in F2P. At level 43 Prayer, you unlock Protect from Melee — a prayer that makes you immune to melee damage from monsters. This is absolutely game-changing, especially when you start facing tougher monsters or doing boss fights.

Prayer in F2P: Is It Worth It?

YES. Even though F2P has limited bone types (no Dragon Bones, no Superior Dragon Bones, etc.), you can still reach level 43 Prayer entirely in F2P. It takes time, but the payoff is huge.

Methods:

  1. Burying Bones: The simplest method. Kill monsters, collect bones, right-click and bury them. Each bone gives XP based on its type.
  2. Chaos Altar (Wilderness): The Chaos Altar in level 38 Wilderness offers 3.5x normal XP (350% instead of 100% per bone). This is the best F2P Prayer training method by far. However, it's in the Wilderness, which means you can be attacked by other players (PvP). At low combat levels, you're unlikely to be targeted, but it's still a risk.
Bone Type XP per Bury XP per Chaos Altar Offering Source in F2P
Normal Bones 4.5 15.75 Chickens, Goblins, Minotaurs, etc.
Big Bones 15 52.5 Hill Giants, Moss Giants
Baby Dragon Bones 30 105 Baby Green Dragons (members only, but bones can be transferred)
Dragon Bones 72 252 Very rare in F2P (occasionally from bosses)

Prayer Training Strategy for F2P

  • Collect Big Bones from Hill Giants: While training melee on Hill Giants (Section 5), save every Big Bone they drop. Each one gives 15 XP when buried, or 52.5 XP at the Chaos Altar!
  • Target: Level 43 Prayer for Protect from Melee. This requires about 23,000 XP from level 1. That's about 1,500 Big Bones (buried) or 440 Big Bones (offered at Chaos Altar).
  • Chaos Altar method: Bring bones to the Chaos Altar in level 38 Wilderness. Be careful of PKers (player killers). Bring minimal gear, don't bring anything you're not willing to lose. At combat level 30-40, you're unlikely to be hunted, but always be cautious.
  • Alternative: If you're scared of the Wilderness, just bury bones the old-fashioned way. It's slower but 100% safe.

Pro tip: If you're planning to buy membership soon, consider waiting to train Prayer until you have access to Dragon Bones and the Chaos Altar in a safer way (using a house or guilded altar). But if you're committed to F2P for a while, get that 43 Prayer — it's worth it.

Section 10: F2P Quest Recommendations (Boost Your Stats FOR FREE)

Quests in OSRS don't just give Quest Points — many of them give massive stat boosts that would take hours of grinding to achieve otherwise. As a F2P player, you should complete every F2P quest as early as possible. They're free XP!

All F2P Quests (In Recommended Order)

Here are the F2P quests that give combat-related XP rewards, listed in the order a new player should complete them:

Day 1 Quests (No requirements, do these IMMEDIATELY)

  1. The Blood Pact (no requirements) — 1 Attack, 1 Strength, 300 XP in your chosen combat stat. Takes 5 minutes. DO THIS FIRST. It also unlocks the ability to kill the creatures in the {@link https://oldschool.runescape.wiki/w/Dungeoneering Dungeoneering} skill (though that's members only).
  2. Imp Catcher (no requirements) — Magic XP reward, unlocks imp spawning (minor). Quick and easy.
  3. Restless Ghost (no requirements) — 1,125 Prayer XP! This gets you to about Prayer level 9 instantly. Also a prerequisite for Priest in Peril (members).
  4. Ernest the Chicken (no requirements) — Gives XP in Attack, Strength, Defence, Prayer, and Hitpoints. A fun little quest.
  5. Cook's Assistant (no requirements) — Cooking XP reward. Quick.
  6. Sheep Shearer (no requirements) — Crafting XP + Farming XP. One of the easiest quests.

Day 2 Quests (Slightly more involved)

  1. Romeo & Juliet — Quick Quest Point. No combat reward but easy Quest Points.
  2. Pirate's Treasure (no requirements) — Thieving XP, ~10k GP reward. Funds your gear upgrades!
  3. Witch's Potion (no requirements) — Small Magic XP reward.
  4. Goblin Diplomacy (no requirements) — Funny quest, small rewards.

Day 3 Quests (Big rewards!)

  1. Rune Mysteries (no requirements) — Unlocks Runecrafting. No combat reward but essential.
  2. Doric's Quest (no requirements) — Mining XP reward, 2 Quest Points, 180 GP.
  3. Vampyre Slayer (no requirements) — 4,825 Attack XP + Slayer XP! This instantly gets you to Attack level 26-28 depending on your starting level. HUGE free boost.
  4. Dragon Slayer (requires 20 Smithing, 20 Crafting recommended) — THE big one. 18,650 Strength XP + 18,650 Defence XP + the ability to wear Rune platebody! This quest alone can get you to combat level 30+ instantly. Do this AS SOON AS POSSIBLE (around combat level 30-35).

Recommended Quest Order for Brand New F2P Player

Day 1: Blood Pact → Imp Catcher → Restless Ghost → Ernest the Chicken → Cook's Assistant → Sheep Shearer

Result: You'll be at about combat level 8-10 just from quest rewards!

Day 2: Romeo & Juliet → Pirate's Treasure → Witch's Potion → Goblin Diplomacy

Result: A few more Quest Points and some GP for gear.

Day 3: Rune Mysteries → Doric's Quest → Vampyre Slayer → Dragon Slayer (big milestone!)

Result: After Dragon Slayer, you'll be at minimum combat level 33 (if you did it at level 30). With the Strength and Defence XP, you might be closer to 35-40!

Pro tip: Dragon Slayer is considered one of the hardest F2P quests. You'll need to be able to kill a level 83 dragon (the quest boss). At combat level 30-35 with Rune gear, bring plenty of food (lobsters or better) and be prepared to pray Protect from Magic. It's doable — thousands of players have done it at low levels.

Section 11: Sample Training Schedule (F2P Week 1)

Theory is great, but let's put it all together into a practical day-by-day training schedule. This schedule assumes you're playing about 2-3 hours per day and follows the methods outlined in the previous sections.

Day-by-Day F2P Combat Training Plan

Day 1: The Setup

  • Morning: Complete quick quests (Blood Pact, Restless Ghost, Ernest the Chicken, Imp Catcher, Cook's Assistant, Sheep Shearer). Takes about 1-2 hours. Result: You're now at about combat level 8-10 with some GP and Quest Points.
  • Afternoon: Kill chickens and cows to get Attack and Strength to level 10. Collect feathers and cowhides. Sell them at the Grand Exchange. Result: You now have enough GP to buy steel or mithril gear.

Day 2: The Stronghold

  • Complete Floor 1 of the Stronghold of Security (kill Minotaurs, get 20k GP reward).
  • Continue killing Minotaurs until Attack and Strength are level 15-18.
  • Result: Combat level ~15-18, 20k GP richer, maybe some iron arrows to sell.

Day 3: Hill Giants & Bones

  • Travel to Edgeville Dungeon (buy a brass key from GE if needed).
  • Kill Hill Giants until Attack and Strength are level 20-25.
  • Collect and bank every Big Bone they drop (for Prayer training later).
  • Result: Combat level ~20-25, a stash of Big Bones, maybe a Limpwurt Root or two.

Day 4: Defence & Ranged

  • Train Defence to 20-25 (use "Controlled" attack style on Hill Giants or Flesh Crawlers).
  • Start Ranged training (shortbow + iron/steel arrows, safespot Hill Giants).
  • Result: More balanced combat stats, some Ranged XP under your belt.

Day 5: Quests & Dragon Slayer Prep

  • Complete remaining F2P quests (Romeo & Juliet, Pirate's Treasure, Rune Mysteries, Doric's Quest, Vampyre Slayer).
  • Vampyre Slayer gives 4,825 Attack XP — that might get you to Attack level 30 instantly!
  • Prepare for Dragon Slayer: buy Rune armor (needs 40 Defence) or Adamant armor, stock up on lobsters (200+ food).
  • Result: Higher combat stats, ready for the big quest.

Day 6: The Push to 40

  • Complete Dragon Slayer quest (the 18,650 Strength + Defence XP is massive).
  • After quest: push to 40 Attack (Rune scimitar!), 30-35 Strength, 25-30 Defence.
  • Kill Hobgoblins for profit (Limpwurt Roots!) and XP.
  • Result: Combat level ~35-40, Rune scimitar equipped, decent GP from loots.

Day 7: Prayer & Gear Upgrades

  • Train Prayer (bury Big Bones or risk the Chaos Altar in Wilderness).
  • Final gear upgrades: full Rune set if you have 40 Defence, Amulet of Power, best Ranged gear.
  • Consider starting Ranged training to 40 (Maple shortbow!).
  • Result: Combat level ~38-45, 43 Prayer (if you grinded it), ready for membership content!

End result after 7 days: Combat level ~38-45, with decent melee stats (40 Attack, 30-35 Strength, 30-40 Defence), some Ranged levels, possibly 43 Prayer, and a stash of GP and gear. You're now ready to buy membership and jump into member content without feeling like a total noob!

Section 12: Tips & FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I train Attack or Strength first?

A: Train Attack first (to 40) for accuracy. Higher Attack means you hit more often. Once you have 40 Attack and a Rune scimitar, train Strength for damage (higher max hits). Keep Defence slightly lower initially if you want to be a "semi-pure", but for general F2P play, balanced stats are fine.

Q: Is F2P combat training boring?

A: Early levels, yes, a bit. Killing chickens isn't exactly thrilling. But it goes FAST. The first 20 combat levels take only a few hours. Once you're killing Hill Giants and Hobgoblins, it gets more interesting (and more profitable).

Q: How do I afford Rune equipment?

A: Cowhides! Seriously. Kill cows near Lumbridge, collect 100-200 cowhides, sell them at GE. That's 10,000-20,000 GP right there — enough for a full Rune set. Alternatively, collect Limpwurt Roots from Hobgoblins (1,500-2,000 GP each) or sell the nature runes and herbs that monsters drop.

Q: Should I get membership as soon as possible?

A: YES. Membership opens up 10x more content: better training methods, more quests, bosses, mini-games, the entire game basically. Use this guide to get to combat level 30+ quickly, THEN buy a bond (with GP) or pay for membership. Don't rush to membership at combat level 5 — you'll struggle to do member content.

Q: What about Magic in F2P?

A: Train Magic alongside combat! Use Curse (level 1 Magic) on the monsters you're meleeing. It lowers their defence and gives you Magic XP at the same time. Once you have 13 Magic, you can use the "Confuse" spell for even better defence reduction. At 19 Magic, "Weaken" reduces their strength. Magic in F2P is mostly for supporting melee, not for primary combat (though you CAN train Magic as a combat style — it's just less efficient than melee for training).

Q: How long does it take to get 99 Attack/Strength in F2P?

A: Don't. Just don't. 99 Attack in F2P would take hundreds of hours and massive amounts of GP for food and gear repairs. Get membership, then train to 99. The member methods are 5-10x faster.

Q: What's the best F2P money-making method to fund my combat training?

A: Cowhides (as mentioned), collecting Limpwurt Roots from Hobgoblins, mining Iron ore (level 15 Mining) and selling it, or doing the "Fly Fishing" money-making method (catch trout/salmon at the Shilo Village fishing spot, cook them, sell the cooked fish). With 50,000-100,000 GP, you can buy all the gear you need for F2P combat.

Final Tips for F2P Combat Training Success

  • Join a Clan: OSRS clans are friendly and often help new players with advice, gear, or GP. Look for F2P-friendly clans in the clan chat "Join Clan" interface.
  • Use the Wiki: The OSRS Wiki is your best friend. Every monster, item, quest, and skill has a detailed page.
  • Don't be afraid to die: In F2P, if you die in a safe area (like the Stronghold or most dungeons), you keep your items. Die = respawn at Lumbridge with full inventory. It's not the end of the world.
  • Take breaks: Combat training can be grindy. Do a quest, train a different skill (Cooking, Mining, Fishing), then come back to combat. Variety keeps the game fun.
  • Have fun: OSRS is a game, not a job. If you're not enjoying F2P combat training, switch to something else. The beauty of OSRS is there's always something else to do.
