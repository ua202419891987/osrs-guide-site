OSRS Chambers of Xeric Loot & Profit Guide 2026 — Twisted Bow, Drops & GP/hr

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 11 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
🏰 Raids — Chambers of Xeric — Updated June 2026

The Chambers of Xeric (Raids 1) is one of the most profitable activities in OSRS. The Twisted Bow alone is worth over 1.1 billion GP, and even the common loot provides steady income. This guide breaks down the complete CoX loot table, the points system that determines your rewards, and exactly how much GP/hr solo and team CoX generates in 2026.

2. How the CoX Points & Loot System Works

CoX uses a personal points system that directly determines your rewards. Points are earned by dealing damage in each puzzle room and to the Great Olm (final boss). At the end of the raid, your personal points are used to calculate:

  1. Common Loot Value: Higher points = higher-value common drops (runes, herbs, ore, gems).
  2. Unique Drop Chance: Your personal points divided by the team's total points determines your share of the unique roll.

6. CoX Profit Tips

  1. Maximize personal points. Deal damage in every room. Olm is the biggest point source — learn efficient Olm cycles (4:1 melee hand, 3:0 mage hand) to maximize your contribution.
  2. Don't die. One death reduces your personal points by 30-40%, essentially wiping out a third of your potential rewards. Prioritize survival over speed until you're experienced.
  3. Scout for fast layouts. Not all room layouts are equal. Scout for raids without Vespula, Vanguards, or Tekton if you're pushing for speed. Faster raids = more unique rolls per hour.
  4. Make overloads. If your team has 78+ Herblore, gather the supplies inside the raid to make overloads. They boost your combat stats by 5-19 levels for 5 minutes each and dramatically increase DPS.
  5. Avoid masses. Large teams (8+) complete raids quickly, but your individual unique chance drops to nearly zero since uniques are assigned randomly among all players. Small teams or solos are far more profitable per hour.
  6. Use the Raids helper plugin on RuneLite. It tracks your personal points in real-time, shows Olm attack cycles, and helps you optimize your damage output.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is solo CoX worth it for GP?

A: Yes, but it requires significant practice. Solo CoX with deathless runs and 30K+ points averages 2M-3M GP/hr including uniques. However, learning to solo efficiently takes 50-100 raids. Start in teams to learn the mechanics, then transition to solos once you're comfortable with all rooms and Olm.

Q: How long does it take to see a Twisted Bow drop?

A: On average, 750 solo raids at 30K+ points. However, this is an average — some players go 2,000+ raids dry, while others see a bow in their first 100. The expected value is what matters: even without the bow, the other uniques (Kodai, Ancestral, Claws) provide significant income that sustains you between big drops.

Q: What's better for GP — CoX or ToB?

A: CoX is more accessible; ToB has higher potential GP/hr. Theatre of Blood (Raids 2) averages 4M-6M+ GP/hr with uniques, but requires higher stats, better gear, and a reliable team. CoX is more beginner-friendly, has lower entry requirements, and solo CoX is viable while solo ToB is extremely difficult. Start with CoX, then graduate to ToB.

Even without hitting the bow, the consistent common loot and other uniques make CoX one of the best long-term money makers in the game. Learn the mechanics, find a team, and start raiding.
