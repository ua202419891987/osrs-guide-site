① Training on Wrong Monsters (Losing GP/hour)

The #1 mistake beginners make is training combat on low-value monsters. You're not just losing XP/hour — you're losing GP/hour too.

❌ Bad Monster Choices (Avoid These)

  • Cows: Drops are worth ~50 GP each. You're making 20k GP/hour worst-case.
  • Al-Kharid Warriors: Decent XP but drops are terrible. ~30k GP/hour.
  • Goblins: Extremely slow XP and low drops. ~10k GP/hour.
  • Dwarves (F2P): Decent XP but low drops. ~15k GP/hour.

✅ Good Monster Choices (Do These Instead)

  • Hill Giants (Level 36): Big Bones (always in demand) + Limpwurt Roots. ~100k GP/hour + good XP.
  • Moss Giants (Level 42): Higher XP + better drops. ~150k GP/hour.
  • Slayer Tasks: Assign monsters with good drops. ~200k+ GP/hour depending on task.
  • Cows (only for 100% Completionist): Cowhide for Crafting XP only. Don't expect profit.

The Difference: Training on Hill Giants vs Cows = +50k GP/hour. Over 100 hours of combat training, that's 5 million GP extra in your pocket.

💡 The Golden Rule of Monster Selection

Always check the drop table before grinding a monster. Use the OSRS Wiki "Monster Drops" page. If the average drop value is less than 500 GP per kill, find a better monster.

③ Not Using Protection Prayers (Dying = Losing Gear)

Nothing wastes more GP than dying in OSRS. When you die, you lose all your equipped gear and inventory (except 3 items if you've completed the Hard Clan Wars minigame).

① 🛡️ Protection Prayers Save Your Life (and Gear)

At Level 43 Prayer, you unlock Protect From Melee. This makes melee attacks deal 0 damage. Combine this with Level 40 Protect From Missiles and Level 37 Protect From Magic, and you become nearly invincible against most monsters.

💀 Real Example: Dying at Barrows Without Prayer

  • Cost of Death: Full Barrows gear (~10M GP) + Inventory (~1M GP) = 11M GP lost.
  • Cost of Using Prayer: A few Prayer Potions (~5k GP) + time to restore Prayer = 5k GP spent.
  • Difference: 10.995 million GP saved by using Protection Prayers.

② ⚔️ Overhead Prayers for PvP (Wilderness Danger)

In the Wilderness (PvP zone), always keep Protect Item prayer active. This ensures that if you die, you keep 4 items instead of 3 (one extra item saved).

For PvP combat, learn to switch your overhead prayer based on your opponent's attack style:

Opponent's Attack StyleYour Overhead PrayerDamage Reduction
Melee (Dragon Scimitar, etc.)Protect From Melee100%
Ranged (Bow/Crossbow)Protect From Missiles100%
Magic (Spell Book)Protect From Magic100%
💡 Prayer Training Priority

Get to Level 43 Prayer ASAP. It's the single best investment in the game. Train Prayer by burying Big Bones (cheap) or using a Gilded Altar in a friend's POH (fast, but costs GP for bones).

④ Wasting Money on Unnecessary Items (Shop Prices vs GE)

Beginners often buy items from in-game shops at 2-10x the Grand Exchange price. This is the fastest way to waste GP.

① 🏪 Shop Prices are a Ripoff (Use GE Instead)

❌ Buying from Shops (Don't Do This)

  • Iron Chainbody (Shop): 198 GP → GE Price: ~50 GP (Save 148 GP per item).
  • Mind Runes (Shop): 6 GP each → GE Price: ~3 GP each (Save 50% per rune).
  • Air Runes (Shop): 5 GP each → GE Price: ~2 GP each (Save 60% per rune).
  • Food (Shops): Tuna 135 GP → GE Price: ~100 GP (Save 35 GP per tuna).

✅ Buying from Grand Exchange (Do This)

  • Always check the Grand Exchange (GE) before buying from any shop.
  • Use Buy Orders (set your price) instead of Insta-buying (paying max price).
  • For common items (runes, food, arrows), you can usually save 10-50% by using Buy Orders.

② 💰 When Shops ARE Worth It (Limited Cases)

There are a few exceptions where shop prices are okay:

  • Broad Bolts/Tips (Slayer Master): These are the same price as GE (no markup).
  • Spirit Trees (Farming): You can only buy from certain NPC shops.
  • Untradeable Items: Some quest items can only be bought from specific NPCs.
💡 GE Buying Strategy

When buying from the GE, always place a Buy Order at 1-5% below the current mid-price. You'll usually get filled within a few hours, and you'll save GP on every single item. For items you need immediately, Insta-buy is fine, but you'll pay ~5% more.

⑤ Not Joining a Clan (Missing Free Resources)

Clans in OSRS are free to join and give you massive benefits. Beginners who play solo are missing out on millions of GP worth of free resources and help.

① 🏰 Clan Benefits (Why You Need One)

✅ Free Benefits from Clans

  • Clan Wars (PvP Practice): Safe PvP practice without risking gear. Learn PvP for free.
  • Clan Citadel (Resources): Gather free resources (wood, ore, herbs) weekly. ~100k+ GP worth per week.
  • Clan Chat (Help): Ask questions, get help with bosses, find teams for group content.
  • Clan Events (Rewards): Weekly events give XP lamps, GP, and unique rewards.
  • Clan Wars (Hard Mode): Carry extra items when dying (keep 4 instead of 3 items).

② 🔍 How to Find a Good Clan

Not all clans are equal. Look for:

  • Active Chat: 50+ members online during your playtime.
  • Helpful Members: Willing to teach bosses, answer questions.
  • Weekly Events: Regular meetings, competitions, group activities.
  • No Drama: Avoid clans with constant arguments in chat.

How to Join: Go to the Clan Chat tab (bottom-right of interface), type "Help Clan" in the search, and apply to active clans. Or ask in the OSRS Discord for clan recommendations.

💡 Clan Citadel Priority

Visit your Clan Citadel every week. You can gather free resources (wood, ore, herbs) that would cost millions if bought from the GE. One hour per week = ~100k GP worth of free resources.

⑥ Ignoring Daily Activities (Free GP & XP)

OSRS has daily and weekly activities that give free GP, XP, and items. Beginners who ignore these are wasting millions per month.

① 📅 Daily Activities (Do These Every Day)

🌅 Daily Activities (Reset at 00:00 UTC)

  • Daily Tasks (Varrock Museum): Answer 5 quiz questions → 5,000 XP lamp (any skill). Takes 2 minutes.
  • Spirit Trees (Farming): Check health of your trees. Free Farming XP daily.
  • Herb Runs (Farming): Plant herbs, harvest every ~80 minutes. ~100k+ GP per run.
  • Ardougne Monastery (Prayer): Collect 7 bone fragments daily for Prayer XP.
  • Fossil Island (Mushrooms): Collect 10 mushrooms daily. ~20k GP.

② 📆 Weekly Activities (Do These Every Week)

🗓️ Weekly Resets (Reset on Monday 00:00 UTC)

  • Clan Citadel (Resources): Gather free wood, ore, herbs. ~100k+ GP/week.
  • Achievement Diaries (Some Rewards): Certain diaries reset weekly (e.g., Moss Giant Boss).
  • Bounty Hunter (PvP): Weekly points reset. Earn free GP by playing.
  • EDGEVILLE RESTOCK (Shops): Some shops restock weekly. Check for cheap items.

Total Value of Daily + Weekly Activities: ~500k-1M GP per week in free resources and XP. Over a year, that's 50-100 million GP — all for free.

💡 Set a Daily Routine

Create a daily checklist in RuneLite (use the Activity Tracker plugin). Spend 15 minutes every day doing dailies, and you'll accumulate massive wealth passively.

⑦ Not Understanding Grand Exchange (Buy Orders vs Insta-Sell)

The Grand Exchange (GE) is the most important money tool in OSRS. Beginners who don't understand how it works lose GP on every transaction.

① 📊 Buy Orders (Save Money) vs Insta-Buy (Convenient)

💸 Insta-Buy (Don't Do This Unless Urgent)

  • How it works: You pay the current middle price and get the item immediately.
  • Cost: You pay ~5-10% more than necessary.
  • Example: Dragon Scimitar (Insta-buy: 180k GP) vs (Buy Order: 165k GP) = 15k GP wasted.

💰 Buy Orders (Always Do This)

  • How it works: You set your price, and wait for someone to sell at that price.
  • Benefit: You save 5-50% on every purchase.
  • Strategy: Set Buy Order at 1-5% below mid-price. You'll usually get filled within hours.
  • Example: Buying 100 Dragon Scimitars via Buy Orders = 1.5M GP saved vs Insta-buying.

② 📈 Insta-Sell (Lose Money) vs Sell Orders (Get Full Value)

The same logic applies to selling items:

MethodHow it WorksGP LostWhen to Use
Insta-SellSell at lowest price immediately5-10% below mid-priceEmergency GP needed
Sell OrderSet your price, wait for buyer0% (get full value)Always (unless urgent)
💡 GE Merchanting Tips

Learn to "flip" items on the GE. Buy at low price, sell at high price. Even flipping 10 items per day can make you 100k-500k GP profit per day. Use the GE Tracker plugin in RuneLite to see price trends.

⑧ Inefficient Combat Training (XP/hour Matters)

Not all combat training methods are equal. Beginners who train inefficiently waste hundreds of hours and millions of GP on food and gear repairs.

① ⚔️ Efficient Melee Training (Attack/Strength/Defense)

❌ Inefficient Methods (Avoid)

  • Rock Crabs (Low Level): Decent XP but no profit. ~20k XP/hour.
  • Sand Crabs (Mid Level): AFK but low XP. ~30k XP/hour.
  • Hill Giants (Mid Level): Good profit but slow XP. ~25k XP/hour.

✅ Efficient Methods (Do These)

  • Slayer Tasks (All Levels): XP + GP combined. ~30-50k XP/hour + ~100-300k GP/hour profit.
  • NMZ (Nightmare Zone) (Level 70+): AFK XP. ~60-80k XP/hour. Costs ~10k GP/hour for absorption pots.
  • Barrows (Level 70+): XP + massive profit. ~40k XP/hour + ~500k-2M GP/hour profit.

② 🏹 Efficient Ranged/Magic Training

For Ranged and Magic, the most efficient methods are:

  • Ranged: Chinning (Red Chinchompas) at Ape Atoll or Cannon + Slayer. ~200k XP/hour (expensive but fast).
  • Magic: Bursting/Barraging Slayer tasks (Nieve). ~150k XP/hour (expensive but fast).
  • Low Level Magic: Curse spells on monsters (free). ~20k XP/hour.
💡 Combat Training Priority

Always prioritize Slayer for combat training. You get XP, GP, and unlock new monsters/bosses. Once you have ~70 combat stats, move to NMZ (Melee) or Chinning (Ranged) for fast XP.

⑨ Not Using Teleports (Wasting Time Walking)

Time is money in OSRS. Beginners who walk everywhere instead of using teleports waste hours per day that could be spent training or making GP.

① 🗺️ Essential Teleports for Beginners

✅ Must-Have Teleports

  • Home Teleport (Spell): Free, unlimited uses. Sets to Lumbridge by default.
  • Lumbridge Teleport (Tablet): Buy from GE for ~1k GP each. Faster than spell.
  • Varrock Teleport (Level 25 Magic): Free, teleports to Varrock square.
  • Falador Teleport (Level 37 Magic): Free, teleports to Falador park.
  • Camelot Teleport (Level 45 Magic): Free, teleports to Seers' Village (close to many areas).

Time Saved: Using teleports instead of walking saves ~30 seconds per trip. Over 100 trips per day, that's ~50 minutes saved daily.

⑩ Ignoring Achievement Diaries (Huge Unlocks)

Achievement Diaries (ADs) are optional challenges that give massive rewards. Beginners who ignore them miss out on game-changing unlocks.

① 🏆 Key Achievement Diary Rewards

💎 Best AD Rewards (Worth Doing)

  • Ardougne Cloak 2: Free teleport to Ardougne (useful for farming runs).
  • Falador Shield 3: Free teleport to Falador (useful for mining).
  • Karamja Gloves 3: Free teleport to Karamja (useful for fishing).
  • Desert Cloak 2: Free teleport to Nardah (useful for questing).
  • Varrock Armor 2: +10% XP when smithing (huge for Ironmen).

How to Start: Talk to the Squire in each major city's castle (Varrock, Falador, Ardougne, etc.) to start the Achievement Diary for that region.

Skills in OSRS require planning. Beginners who train skills randomly waste millions of GP on inefficient methods.

① 📊 Efficient Skill Training (Do Research First)

Before training any skill, always check the OSRS Wiki "Training" page for that skill. It lists the most efficient methods at each level.

✅ Efficient Methods (Examples)

Mini-games in OSRS offer some of the best XP and GP rates in the game. Beginners who skip them are missing out on efficient training methods.

① 🎮 Best Mini-Games for Beginners

🌟 Top Mini-Games (XP/GP)

⑭ Not Understanding Item Stats (Equipment Matters)

Not all gear is created equal. Beginners who equip items based on looks or price instead of stats are weakening their character.

① 📊 Understanding Gear Stats (How to Compare)

Every item in OSRS has hidden stats (Accuracy, Damage, Defense). Always check the Equipment Stats tab (right-click item → Examine → Equipment Stats).

✅ How to Choose Gear

  • Melee: Prioritize Strength bonus (increases max hit) and Attack bonus (increases accuracy).
  • Ranged: Prioritize Ranged Strength (damage) and Ranged Accuracy.
  • Magic: Prioritize Magic Damage% and Magic Accuracy.
  • Defense: Prioritize Defense bonus against the monster's attack style.

⑮ Wasting Money on Cosmetic Items (Prioritize Gear)

OSRS has many cosmetic items (holo-cloaks, fancy boots, etc.) that provide zero combat bonuses. Beginners who buy these early are wasting GP that should go to useful gear.

① 💰 When to Buy Cosmetics (After Gear is Set)

Only buy cosmetics after you have:

  • ✅ Full Dragon armor set (or better for your level).
  • ✅ A weapon that matches your level (Dragon Scimitar, etc.).
  • ✅ Enough GP for Prayer training (Big Bones or Gilded Altar usage).
  • ✅ Enough GP for Slayer unlocks (Broad bolts, etc.).

Rule of Thumb: If the item doesn't improve your combat stats or GP/hour rate, don't buy it until you're rich.

⑯ Not Learning Boss Mechanics (Dying = Repair Costs)

Bossing in OSRS requires learning mechanics. Beginners who rush into bosses without researching are guaranteed to die repeatedly, wasting GP on gear repairs and supplies.

① 🎯 How to Learn Boss Mechanics (Before Fighting)

✅ Preparation Steps (Do These First)

  • Watch Guides: YouTube has excellent OSRS boss guides (search "OSRS [Boss Name] Guide").
  • Read Wiki: OSRS Wiki has detailed boss mechanics pages.
  • Practice on Easy Mode: Some bosses have easier versions (e.g., Barrows tunnels).
  • Bring Cheap Gear First: Learn the mechanics with cheap gear before using expensive gear.

⑰ Ignoring Farming (Passive GP & XP)

Farming is the most profitable skill in OSRS. Beginners who ignore it are missing out on passive GP generation that requires minimal effort.

① 🌱 Farming for Profit (Herb Runs)

Planting herbs (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Torstol) and harvesting them ~80 minutes later gives massive profits.

💰 Herb Run Profit (Per Run)

  • Ranarr Weeds: ~150k GP profit per run (requires 70 Farming).
  • Snapdragon: ~200k GP profit per run (requires 75 Farming).
  • Torstol: ~250k GP profit per run (requires 99 Farming).
  • Time Investment: ~5 minutes per run, every ~80 minutes.

Total Passive GP: ~1-2M GP per day from herb runs alone. That's 30-60M GP per month for minimal effort.

⑱ Not Using Bank Organization (Wasting Time)

A disorganized bank wastes minutes per hour of gameplay. Beginners who don't organize their bank spend more time searching for items than playing the game.

① 📁 Bank Organization Tips (Save Time)

✅ How to Organize Your Bank

  • Use Tabs: Create tabs for Combat Gear, Skilling Gear, Supplies, Valuables, etc.
  • Stack Similar Items: Keep all food in one tab, all potions in another, etc.
  • Use Placeholders: Keep important items (Dragon Scimitar, etc.) in a fixed slot so they don't move.
  • Regularly Clean: Every week, sell items you don't need (check GE prices first).

⑲ Wasting Time on Inefficient Methods (Do Research First)

The biggest mistake in OSRS is not researching before playing. Beginners who jump into activities without checking the Wiki or guides waste hundreds of hours on inefficient methods.

① 🔍 How to Research Efficiently (Save Time)

✅ Research Checklist (Do This Before Starting Anything)

  • Check OSRS Wiki: Search "[Activity Name] OSRS Wiki". Read the guide.
  • Watch YouTube Guides: Search "OSRS [Activity Name] Guide 2026".
  • Ask in Clan Chat: Veteran players are usually happy to help.
  • Use RuneLite Plugins: GE Tracker, NPCHighlight, etc. help you play efficiently.

⑳ Not Asking for Help (Community is Friendly)

OSRS has one of the friendliest gaming communities. Beginners who don't ask for help are missing out on free advice, free items, and free help with bosses.

① 💬 How to Get Help (Don't Be Shy)

✅ Where to Ask for Help

  • Clan Chat: Join an active clan and ask questions in chat.
  • OSRS Discord: Official Discord has channels for help and advice.
  • Reddit r/OSRS: Post questions and get answers from veteran players.
  • YouTube Comments: Ask guide creators questions (they often reply).
  • In-Game Help Channel: Type "::help" to join the help channel.
💡 The Community is Generous

Veteran players often give away millions of GP worth of free items to new players. Don't be afraid to ask for help — most players are happy to assist. Just be polite and say "thank you".

❓ FAQ — Common New Player Questions

Q: I've already made some of these mistakes. Am I doomed?

A: No! Everyone makes mistakes in OSRS. The important thing is to learn from them and play more efficiently going forward. You can always make up for lost GP by following the efficient methods in this guide.

Q: Which mistake costs the most GP?

A: Dying in expensive gear (Mistake #3) and not using the GE properly (Mistakes #4 & #7) are the biggest GP sinks. A single death at Barrows can cost you 10M+ GP if you're not careful with Protection Prayers.

Q: How do I know if I'm training efficiently?

A: Check the OSRS Wiki "Training" pages for your skill. They list the most efficient methods at each level. Also, join the OSRS Discord and ask veteran players for advice.

Q: Can I fix my mistakes if I've already wasted millions?

A: Yes! Start by doing daily activities (Mistake #6) to accumulate free GP. Then, use efficient training methods (Mistake #8) to gain XP faster. Within a few weeks, you'll have made up for your losses.
