OSRS 1-99 Crafting Guide (2026) – All Methods Compared

⏱️ 30-Second Quick Summary

Get 99 Crafting in OSRS by picking the right method for your budget and playstyle:

  • Fastest XP: Dragonhide bodies at 250K–350K XP/hr (expensive but efficient).
  • Best Ironman: Giant seaweed glassblowing at ~120K XP/hr, almost self-sufficient.
  • Most profitable: Battlestaves at 100K–180K XP/hr while earning GP.
  • AFK friendly: Jewelry crafting at 30K–80K XP/hr with minimal effort.
  • Beginner option: Gem cutting at 50K–100K XP/hr with low upfront cost.

1. Crafting Overview & Core Mechanics

Crafting is one of OSRS's most versatile skills, producing everything from jewelry and armor to glass items and battlestaves. It's required for many quests, diaries, and late-game gear creation (Zenyte jewelry, Amulet of Fury, etc.).

Why Train Crafting?

Amulet of Glory80Unlimited teleports, essential for any account
Amulet of Fury90Best-in-slot amulet before zenyte/occult
Zenyte Jewelry87-98Anguish (93), Tormented (95), Torture (98), Suffering (87)
Black D'hide Body84Best-in-slot ranged body for budget/PvP
Slayer Rings75Fast Slayer teleports (unlocked via quest)
Fury>Blood Fury80+High-value crafting for profit
Diary Requirements40-85Multiple Achievement Diaries require Crafting
Quest RequirementsVariousLunar Diplomacy (61), RFD (40), Fremennik Exiles (65)

Core Training Categories

  • Glassblowing: Molten glass → items. Best Ironman method. Up to 120K XP/hr. Low cost or profitable.
  • Battlestaves: Unpowered orb + battlestaff. Profitable at mid-high levels. 100K-180K XP/hr.
  • Jewelry: Gold bar + gem → jewelry. Semi-AFK. 30K-80K XP/hr. Often profitable.
  • Dragonhide: Dragon leather → armor. Fastest XP in game. 250K-350K XP/hr. Expensive but worth it at high levels.
  • Gem Cutting: Uncut → cut gems. Low-effort. 50K-100K XP/hr. Variable profit.
  • Pottery/Leather/Spinning: Low-level training. 20K-40K XP/hr.

3. Glassblowing — Best Ironman Method (120K XP/hr)

Glassblowing is the single most important Crafting method for Ironman accounts. It uses giant seaweed + buckets of sand → molten glass → glass items. With Superglass Make, you create more molten glass than the raw materials, making it extremely efficient.

How to Get Raw Materials

MaterialHow to ObtainPer Hour
Giant SeaweedFossil Island seaweed patches (23 Farming)~60-80 per run (40 min grow time)
Buckets of SandSandstorm (Yanille) or Bert (after Hand in the Sand)Sandstorm: 3K-5K/hr. Bert: 84/day
Soda AshCook seaweed on range/fire (or farm giant seaweed)Giant seaweed = 6 soda ash each when cooked

Superglass Make Method (77 Magic Required)

Setup

  • Required: 77 Magic for Superglass Make (Lunar spellbook)
  • Recommended: Steam battlestaff (infinite water + fire runes), Earth runes, Astral runes
  • Inventory: 3 giant seaweed + 18 buckets of sand per cast

Process

  1. Stand at a bank (Farming Guild bank or Fossil Island bank recommended)
  2. Withdraw: 18 sand + 3 giant seaweed (or 13 sand:13 seaweed for beginners)
  3. Cast Superglass Make → creates 1.3x to 1.6x molten glass per material
  4. Bank all molten glass, repeat

XP Rates: ~70K Crafting XP/hr (making glass) + ~40K Magic XP/hr (casting). With blowpipe glassblowing, total can reach 100K-120K Crafting XP/hr.

4. Battlestaves — Profitable Training (100K-180K XP/hr)

Making battlestaves is one of the most profitable Crafting methods while still giving solid XP rates. The process involves three steps: making unpowered orbs → charging orbs → attaching to battlestaves. Many players buy charged orbs and skip step 2.

Battlestaff Profit Calculator

Battlestaff TypeLevelCrafting XPGE Profit (varies)
Water Battlestaff54100~1,000-2,000 GP each
Earth Battlestaff58112.5~1,000-2,000 GP each
Fire Battlestaff62125~1,500-2,500 GP each
Air Battlestaff66137.5~1,000-2,000 GP each

Daily Battlestaves: After completing Varrock Medium Diary, Zaff sells 30 battlestaves/day at 7K each. After Elite Diary, it's 120/day. Always buy these for guaranteed profit.

Optimal Battlestaff Training Route

  1. Buy daily battlestaves from Zaff (Varrock)
  2. Buy charged orbs from GE (or charge your own for Magic XP)
  3. Attach orbs to battlestaves at any bank
  4. Sell completed battlestaves on GE for profit

XP/hr: 100K-180K depending on how fast you click. Profit: 500K-2M GP/hr depending on GE margins.

5. Jewelry Crafting — Semi-AFK Method (30K-80K XP/hr)

Jewelry crafting is the semi-AFK option — you can craft an inventory in ~60 seconds, then relax while banking. It uses gold bars + gems, and some jewelry (especially ruby/diamond) can be profitable.

AFK Jewelry Crafting Setup

  • Equip a Mould (necklace mould / ring mould / amulet mould from crafting shop in Al Kharid)
  • Have gold bars + cut gems in bank
  • Use Edgeville furnace (closest to bank) or Port Phasmatys (if done Ghosts Ahoy)
  • Click furnace → make inventory → bank → repeat

XP/hr: 30K-80K depending on jewelry type and focus level. Very chill method.

6. Dragonhide Crafting — Fastest XP (250K-350K XP/hr)

This is the fastest Crafting XP in the game, but also the most expensive. Dragon leather + needle + thread → bodies/vambraces. At high levels with black dragonhide bodies, you can hit 350K+ XP/hr.

Optimal Strategy

Use bodies, not vambraces — bodies are ~3x more XP per leather. Each body uses 3 leathers. After 84, black d'hide bodies all the way to 99.

Where to Craft

  • Al Kharid bank + tanner — shortest run
  • Crafting Guild (40 Crafting, brown apron) — tanner inside guild
  • Buy leather from GE, tan it, craft bodies, sell back to GE

8. Other Methods — Pottery, Leather, Spinning

Pottery (1-20)

  • Use soft clay on a potter's wheel → make unfired items → fire in kiln
  • Bowls (level 8): 33 XP each. ~15K XP/hr
  • Can be profitable (soft clay → bowls sell for ~100-200 GP)

Leather Crafting (1-46)

  • Needle + thread + leather → items
  • Leather Coifs (level 38): 37 XP each. ~30K-50K XP/hr
  • Hardleather Bodies (level 28): 35 XP each from hard leather
  • Very cheap method, but slow

Spinning (1-10)

  • Wool → balls of wool at spinning wheel
  • 2.5 XP per wool. ~5K XP/hr
  • Bowstrings (level 10): 15 XP each. ~20K-30K XP/hr. Profitable!
  • Flax → bowstrings is a classic profitable low-level method

10. Ironman 1-99 Crafting Strategy

Crafting is essential for Ironman progression. It gates your jewelry upgrades (Glory, Fury, Zenyte), your armor (black d'hide for ranged), and many quests/diaries.

Early Game (1-46)

  • Do all Crafting quests → ~level 25 from quests alone
  • Pick up every seaweed and bucket of sand you see
  • Unlock Fossil Island (Bone Voyage quest) ASAP for giant seaweed
  • Do Hand in the Sand quest for daily sand from Bert (84/day delivered to bank!)

Mid Game (46-87) — Giant Seaweed + Superglass Make

  • This is the core Ironman Crafting method.
  • Giant seaweed runs every 40 minutes (2 patches at Fossil Island, 23 Farming required)
  • Mine sandstone in the Desert Quarry (Yanille) for buckets of sand — 3K-5K/hr
  • Alternatively, use Bert's daily sand (84/day) for passive sand income
  • Cast Superglass Make (77 Magic) → blow unpowered orbs from 46-87
  • Weekly: Tears of Guthix for free Crafting XP

Late Game (87-99)

  • Switch to Light Orbs at 87 (70 XP each, 120K XP/hr)
  • Continue giant seaweed + sand mining
  • Consider charging orbs → battlestaves for Magic XP + alch GP
  • Zenyte crafting at 87-98 (use Onyx/Zenyte from Zulrah/Demonics)

Ironman Material Requirements to 99

Using Superglass Make + glassblowing (46-99): ~70,000 buckets of sand + ~11,500 giant seaweed → ~95,000 molten glass. This sounds like a lot, but with daily sand + regular seaweed runs, it's very manageable over a few months.

12. Cost & Profit Analysis

Budget Route to 99 (~5M GP)

  • 1-8: Quest XP → Free
  • 8-23: Gold amulets → ~50K total
  • 23-46: Sapphire/emerald jewelry → ~500K total (near breakeven)
  • 46-87: Unpowered orbs (glassblowing) → +5M GP profit
  • 87-99: Light orbs → +2M GP profit

Budget total: ~7M GP profit earned while training! Takes 80-100 hours.

Fast Route to 99 (~50M GP)

  • 1-46: Quest + sapphire/emerald jewelry → ~1M total
  • 46-63: Air battlestaves → ~3M total
  • 63-84: Green D'hide Bodies → ~25M total
  • 84-99: Black D'hide Bodies → ~50M total

Fast total: ~80M GP, ~35-40 hours of active training.

Profit-Focused Route (~20M GP)

  • 1-46: Quest + jewelry → breakeven
  • 46-87: Unpowered orbs + Lantern lenses → +8M GP
  • 87-99: Light orbs (glassblowing) → +5M GP

Profit route: ~13M GP earned, but takes 90-120 hours.

13. Pro Tips for Maximum Efficiency

  1. Superglass Make Is King for Ironmen: At 77 Magic, Superglass Make creates 1.3x more molten glass per input. Always use 3 giant seaweed + 18 sand per cast for max efficiency.
  2. Daily Battlestaves Are Free Money: After Varrock Medium Diary, buy 30/day from Zaff at 7K each. Attach charged air orbs and high-alch or sell. Minimum 60K-100K profit/day for 2 minutes of work.
  3. Giant Seaweed Is Passive: Plant once, harvest 40 minutes later. Each run yields 40-80 giant seaweed. With two patches, you can stockpile thousands with minimal effort.
  4. Sandstorm > Bert's Sand: Bert gives 84 sand/day. Sandstorm mining gives 3K-5K/hr. For active training, sandstorm is far superior.
  5. Edgeville Furnace: Closest bank-to-furnace run for jewelry. If you've done Ghosts Ahoy, Port Phasmatys is a good alternative.
  6. Always Use a Mould: Buy all moulds (ring, necklace, amulet, bracelet, tiara, holy symbol) from the Al Kharid crafting shop for a few hundred GP total.
  7. Tears of Guthix Weekly: If Crafting is your lowest skill, Tears of Guthix gives free Crafting XP every week. Don't skip it.

14. FAQ

Q: How long does 1-99 Crafting take?

Fast (dragonhide): 35-40 hours. Medium (battlestaves): 60-70 hours. Slow (glassblowing): 80-100 hours.

Q: What's the best method for Ironman?

Giant seaweed + Superglass Make → unpowered orbs (46-87) → light orbs (87-99). This is THE Ironman Crafting meta. Farm giant seaweed on Fossil Island, mine sandstone for sand, cast Superglass Make, blow the glass.

Q: Is Crafting expensive to train?

It depends on your method. Glassblowing and battlestaves are profitable. Dragonhide crafting is expensive but fast. Jewelry is roughly breakeven. You can train 1-99 Crafting for free or even profit if you choose the right methods.

Q: When should I stop glassblowing and switch to something faster?

For main accounts: switch to battlestaves at 54 (water), or dragonhide at 63 (green). For Ironman: glassblowing all the way to 99 is standard because you can't buy supplies.

Q: What's the best Crafting item for profit?

Air battlestaves (66 Crafting) are typically the most consistent profit. Diamond/dragonstone jewelry can be better but requires more attention to GE margins.

Q: Can I train Crafting on F2P?

Yes, but options are limited. Gold amulets (8 Crafting) are the best F2P method. You can also make hardleather bodies (28 Crafting). F2P Crafting maxes out around 30K XP/hr.

Q: Do I need Crafting for any important quests?

Yes! Lunar Diplomacy requires 61 Crafting. Recipe for Disaster (finale) requires 40. Fremennik Exiles requires 65. Sins of the Father requires multiple skills. Don't sleep on Crafting quest requirements.
