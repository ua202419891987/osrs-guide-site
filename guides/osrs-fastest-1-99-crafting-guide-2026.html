OSRS Fastest 1-99 Crafting Guide 2026

Skill Training Crafting Fastest Method Updated 2026

Crafting is a versatile skill in OSRS that lets you create everything from leather armor and jewelry to dragonhide bodies and crystal items. While it is traditionally known as an expensive buyable skill, there are now more methods than ever — including the new Varlamore crafting updates that offer competitive alternatives to the classic dragonhide route. This guide covers every stage from level 1 to 99 with exact costs, XP rates, and the most efficient path for both ironmen and mains.

Crafting Training Overview

Crafting is trained primarily by creating items at workbenches, furnaces, spinning wheels, and looms. Most methods are "buyable" — meaning you purchase raw materials from the Grand Exchange or gather them yourself (for ironmen). The skill unlocks useful items like dragonhide armor for ranged combat, amulets for combat stats, and teleport tablets for convenience.

Key Milestones for Combat Players

  • Level 10: Leather gloves/chaps for early F2P range gear
  • Level 20: Silver items, sapphire jewellery (basic gold-making)
  • Level 38: Green dragonhide body (best free-to-play range defense)
  • Level 57: Blue dragonhide body (excellent mid-game range armor)
  • 66: Craft cosmic runes (Lunar Diplomacy quest)
  • 71: Red dragonhide body (best standard range armor)
  • 77: Black dragonhide body (elite range defense)
  • 82–84: Crystal shields/tools (Song of the Elves)
  • 99: Crafting cape + Master Crafting outfit bonus

The early levels of Crafting are quick and inexpensive. Focus on fast, low-cost methods that get you past the beginner stages quickly.

Fastest Route: Silver Bars at Furnace

  • Location: Any furnace — Edgeville, Falador, or Al Kharid recommended (closest bank)
  • Requirements: Level 7 Crafting, silver bars (~300 bars needed)
  • XP/hr: ~35,000
  • Cost: ~100 GP per bar (silver ore cost)
  • Total time to level 20: ~25 minutes

Buy silver bars on the GE and craft unholy symbols or tiaras at any furnace. This is significantly faster than spinning wool or cutting gems. If you are on a budget, cut sapphires (level 1) or emeralds (level 20) instead — they give good XP and can even be profitable if you buy uncut gems cheaply.

Gem Cutting — Best Balance of Speed & Cost

  • Requirements: Chisel, uncut gems
  • Best gem: Emerald (level 20) or Ruby (level 34) for speed
  • XP/hr (Emerald): ~55,000
  • Cost: ~200–400 GP per gem (varies by market)
  • Why it works: Click-intensive but very fast, no furnace required

Glassblowing (Unpowered Orbs)

  • Location: Any furnace with molten glass (buy seaweed/soda ash on GE)
  • Requirements: Level 33 Crafting, Glassblowing pipe
  • XP/hr: ~50,000
  • Cost: Moderate (molten glass cost fluctuates)
  • Note: Good alternative if gem prices spike

Levels 38–57: Dragonhide Armor Begins

At level 38 you unlock green dragonhide bodies — this marks the transition into the iconic dragonhide crafting route that will carry you to level 99. Dragonhide bodies give excellent XP per item and are always in demand for ranged players.

Dragonhide Body Crafting Setup

  • Location: Lumbridge (bank south of castle, crafting building north) or Crafting Guild
  • Requirements: Needle, thread, dragonhide leather
  • Inventory setup: Needle + thread in first two slots, rest filled with hides
  • Method: Use needle on hide → select body/vambrace → repeat
  • AFK window: Low-medium (click-intensive but fast)

Levels 57–71: Blue & Red Dragonhide

This range is where Crafting becomes expensive but also very efficient. Blue dragonhide vambraces and bodies are your best bet for pure XP throughput.

Optimal Path: Blue Vambraces (57–68) → Red Bodies (68–71+)

  1. Levels 57–68: Craft blue dragonhide vambraces. They give 95 XP each, require only 1 hide, and are cheaper than blue bodies. Expect ~85,000–90,000 XP/hr.
  2. Levels 68–71: Switch to red dragonhide vambraces (level 68, 132 XP) then red dragonhide bodies (level 71, 370 XP). Red bodies are excellent value — high XP per item means fewer clicks.
  3. Levels 71–84: Continue red dragonhide bodies until black dragonhide unlocks. This is the sweet spot for cost-effective XP.

Alternative: Battlestaffs

If you want variety, battlestaff crafting (attach orb to staff) is competitive with dragonhide. It gives 137 XP per staff (air, water, earth, fire) at level 54 and scales up to 225 XP for elemental battlestaves at level 62.

Levels 71–99: The Endgame Grind

From level 71 onward, you have several strong options. Choose based on whether you prioritize speed, cost efficiency, or AFK comfort.

Option A: Red Dragonhide Bodies (Most Popular)

Red Dragonhide Bodies

  • Level Required: 71
  • XP per body: 370
  • XP/hr: ~105,000 – 120,000
  • Cost: ~11,000 GP per body (loss of ~6–8k per body)
  • Time to 99 from 71: ~25–30 hours
  • Total cost to 99: ~25M – 30M GP
  • Best location: Lumbridge (bank nearby), Crafting Guild, or Rimmington

Option B: Black Dragonhide Bodies (Fastest)

Black Dragonhide Bodies

  • Level Required: 84
  • XP per body: 468
  • XP/hr: ~115,000 – 130,000
  • Cost: ~14,000 GP per body (higher loss than red)
  • Time to 99 from 84: ~15–18 hours
  • Best for: Speed over economy — fastest conventional method

Crafting Tips & Efficiency Hacks

  • Crafter's Outfit: Obtained from Treasure Trails. Grants up to 2.5% bonus XP when worn. Worth getting before serious grinding.
  • Premium Lumbridge Bank Chest: Use the chest near the crafting area for faster banking than the regular bank booth.
  • Lumbridge Teleport: Use the home tele button or Construction cape to return to Lumbridge quickly between trips.
  • Portable Crafter: If available from TH, place one near a bank for instant crafting without moving to the workshop.
  • Ironman Tip: Kill blue/red/black dragons for their hides. Tanning costs money, so consider using the Wilderness tanner (free but risky) or the Crafting Guild tanner.
  • Check GE prices before buying bulk: Dragonhide prices can shift dramatically. Always verify current margins before committing to a large purchase.
  • Varlamore Update: The Children of the Sun quest unlocks new Varlamore crafting stations. Check these after completing the quest line for potentially better methods.
  • Quest XP Rewards: Ghosts Ahoy (2,250 XP), The Giant Dwarf (2,500 XP), and Horror from the Deep (5,000 XP) provide significant early Crafting XP boosts.
