① Winners and Losers

Biggest Winners: Soulreaper Axe (from meme to meta), Inquisitor set (from dead to viable), Zaryte Crossbow (spec buff makes it relevant).

Biggest Losers: Dinh's Bulwark (nerfed hard), Dragon Claws (outclassed), Ghrazi Rapier (facing crush competition).

💡 Practical Takeaway

If you own a Soulreaper Axe, hold it — prices are rising 15-25% post-buff. If you're choosing between Rapier and Inquisitor Mace for a melee upgrade, the Mace is now the better choice (especially for Vault of Ralos raid, which has crush-weak enemies). Sell Dinh's if you were holding for inferno — it's no longer optimal.

2. Vault of Ralos Raid: Meta & Requirements

The Vault of Ralos raid, released in early 2026, has established its own meta. Here's what you need to know:

① Recommended Gear

StyleEntry-Level GearOptimal Gear
MeleeWhip + Dragon defender, Bandos, NeitiznotSoulreaper Axe, Inquisitor set, Ultor ring
RangedBlowpipe, God d'hide, AnguishBow of Faerdhinen, Masori (F), Zaryte crossbow
MagicTrident of the Swamp, Ahrims, OccultTumeken's Shadow, Ancestral, Saturated heart

② Meta Breakdown

  • Crush melee is king — The raid's toughest enemies are crush-weak. Inquisitor Mace or Soulreaper Axe outperforms slash/stab weapons
  • Ranged is secondary — Needed for some puzzle rooms, but not the primary DPS style
  • Magic is support — Freeze spells and utility, not primary DPS (unless you have Shadow)
  • Sailing level 50+ required — Raid entrance area requires ship travel. Level 40 bare minimum, 50 recommended for shortcuts
  • Team size: 3-5 players — Scales well. Trio and 4-man teams are most efficient
💡 Vault of Ralos: What to Farm

The raid's unique drops include new weapons (currently being priced by the market), cosmetic rewards, and Sailing-related consumables. Early data suggests ~3-5M GP/hr for efficient teams, making it competitive with ToA Expert mode. If you're learning, expect 1-2M GP/hr — still excellent.

🎯 Blood Moon Boss: Accessible Mid-Game Content

The Blood Moon boss requires 70+ combat stats and basic Barrows-level gear. It's designed as a "bridge boss" — harder than Vorkath, easier than CG. With consistent kills, you earn 1.5-2.5M GP/hr while having a chance at the new equipment drops. For mid-game players, this is now a must-do boss.

9. 2026 Mid-Year Meta FAQs

Is Sailing mandatory for endgame in 2026?

It's highly recommended but not strictly mandatory. You need level 50 Sailing for the Vault of Ralos raid entrance. Level 73 is needed for Crystal Extractor (best passive GP method). Level 87 unlocks Frost Dragons (2-3M GP/hr boss). Most serious endgame players have at least level 70 Sailing.

Should I sell my Dragon Claws after the Sweep-Up?

If you're not using them regularly for ToB, yes. Dragon Claws received no buff and have fallen behind newer spec weapons (Zaryte Crossbow, Soulreaper Axe). Prices are trending down. Sell and invest in Soulreaper Axe or Inquisitor Mace instead.

Is the Vault of Ralos raid harder than ToA?

It depends on invocation level. At base difficulty, Vault of Ralos is similar to ToA at 150-200 invocation. The mechanics are different (more movement-focused, Sailing puzzles), but the DPS requirements are comparable. Most players find it easier to learn than ToB but harder than CoX.

What's the best new money maker from the meta shift?

For mid-game players: the Blood Moon boss (1.5-2.5M GP/hr, accessible gear). For endgame players: the Vault of Ralos raid (3-5M GP/hr, efficient teams). For passive income: Crystal Extractor + Gale Catcher (200-290K GP/hr while Sailing).

Should I prioritize Sailing or Slayer for account progression?

If you're mid-game (base 60s), prioritize getting Sailing to level 50 for Vault of Ralos access, then continue Slayer. Sailing provides unique unlocks but doesn't replace Slayer's combat benefits. The optimal path: Sailing 50 → Slayer 85+ → Sailing 73+ for passive income.

Are any skills obsolete now that Sailing exists?

No. Sailing complements existing skills but doesn't replace any. It adds new training methods for Woodcutting (island trees), Mining (seafloor ores), Fishing (marlin), and Hunter (crab hunting), but the existing methods remain viable.

What's the biggest change most players haven't noticed yet?

The passive income from Crystal Extractor + Gale Catcher is still underutilized. Most Sailing players focus on active training methods and ignore wind motes. At 200-290K GP/hr completely passively, it's one of the best "do nothing" money makers in the game. Set it up, go do something else, collect GP later.

Is the Soulreaper Axe worth buying now?

Yes. Post-buff, it's competitive with Scythe of Vitur at many bosses and costs 10x less (90M vs 1.1B). It's now best-in-slot at crush-weak bosses and strong at several slash-weak ones. The price has already risen 12% since the buff announcement and is expected to continue climbing through summer.

What should I focus on for the second half of 2026?

Top 3 priorities: (1) Get Sailing to 50+ for Vault of Ralos access, (2) Complete Blood Moon Rises quest for the new boss and gear, (3) Upgrade your melee setup with crush options (Inquisitor or Soulreaper Axe) for the new meta. The second half of 2026 is about adapting to the crush meta and Sailing integration.
