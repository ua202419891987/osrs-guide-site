OSRS Maxing Guide 2026 —
Which 99 First?

The Optimal 99 Order — Fastest Skills First, Slowest Last, Zero Burnout

📅 June 11, 2026📖 11 min read🎯 Maxing / Skilling

Maxing in OSRS takes roughly 1,700-2,000 hours from a fresh account. If you do skills in the wrong order, that number balloons to 2,500+. The order matters — not just for speed, but for burnout prevention. You don't want to leave Agility, Runecrafting, and Mining for last. You also don't want to max combat stats first and have nothing to do but click rocks for 300 hours. This guide gives you the psychologically optimal maxing order: fastest dopamine hits first, slowest grinds spaced out, and maximum benefit from every cape's perk along the way.

1. The Maxing Philosophy — "Cape Perks First"

Every skill cape in OSRS has a perk. Some are useless (Firemaking). Some redefine how you play the game (Construction, Crafting, Agility). The golden rule of efficient maxing:

🏆 GOLDEN RULE

Get capes that help you get more capes — as early as possible.

Example: 99 Construction cape = unlimited house teleports. This saves hundreds of hours of running. Get it early. 99 Agility cape = faster run regen + grace shortcuts. Get it before you walk thousands of tiles for other skills.

2. Phase 1: The Fast Dops (99s 1-7)

Your first seven 99s should be fast wins that build momentum. Each one takes under 40 hours and gives you a cape perk that helps with later skills:

1. Fletching (First 99)

Time to 9915-20 hours (darts) or 30 hours (longbows, profitable)
Cost0-5M (depends on method)
Cape PerkSearch for mithril grapple + crossbow (saves inventory space for clues)

Why first: It's the fastest 99. Seeing a cape in under 20 hours makes the remaining 22 feel possible. Psychological momentum is real.

2. Cooking (Second 99)

Agility must be your final 99. When you max, the Agility cape's graceful effect + unlimited run + shorter shortcuts means you'll never walk anywhere again on your maxed account. It's the most impactful perk for a maxed account. Getting it last means you've earned the right to never run out of energy.

FAQ

Q: How long does maxing actually take?

1,700-2,000 hours for an efficient player who follows this guide. At 4 hours/day, that's 14-17 months. At 8 hours/day, 7-8 months. The key is never doing two slow skills back to back. Alternate: fast skill → slow skill → fast skill → slow skill.

Q: Should I max combat at NMZ or through Slayer?

Slayer every time. NMZ is pure AFK but gives zero GP and zero Slayer XP. Training combat through Slayer gets you 99 Slayer + 200M+ GP by the time you max melees. NMZ gives you nothing but XP.

Q: What's the hardest skill mentally?

Agility. 200 hours of clicking the same rooftop course. But Hallowed Sepulchre (72+) breaks the monotony — it's engaging, profitable, and gives better XP at 92+. Do rooftops to 72, then alternate Sepulchre/rooftops.

Q: Is Runecrafting still 250 hours?

No longer. Guardians of the Rift (GotR) makes Runecrafting significantly more tolerable and gives 40-50K XP/hr at 77+. Blood runes at 77+ are semi-AFK (45-50 seconds per inventory). Combined with GotR, RC is 150-200 hours now, not 250.

Are You Ready to Max?

Start with Fletching. Get that first dopamine hit. Then follow this path one 99 at a time. 1,700 hours later, you'll be wearing a Max Cape.
