OSRS 1-99 Magic Training Guide — Cheap Methods (2026)

Skill Training Magic Budget Friendly Updated 2026

Magic is notoriously expensive in OSRS — everyone tells you to High Alch your way from 55 to 99 and burn 150M+ GP in the process. But what if you don't have 200M lying around? This guide covers every budget-friendly method to get 99 Magic without breaking the bank, including profitable enchanting strategies, AFK splashing techniques, and Lunar spell alternatives that can actually make you money while training.

Why Train Magic Cheaply?

Magic is one of the most punishing skills in OSRS if you train it the "standard" way. High Alchemy from level 55 to 99 costs approximately 150–180M GP depending on nature rune prices. That's roughly the cost of a Twisted Bow for a skill that's primarily about utility — teleportation, combat accuracy, and enchanting.

But here's the thing: you don't need to spend 150M to get 99 Magic. There are multiple methods that cost a fraction of that, and some that actually make you profit while gaining XP. This guide is built for players who want 99 Magic but aren't sitting on a maxed main's bank.

What Magic Unlocks for You

  • Teleportation: Home teleport, Varrock, Lumbridge, Falador, Camelot, Ardougne — these save hours over time
  • Combat Spells: God spells for Slayer, Ancients for bossing (Barrage/Burst)
  • Enchanting: Jewelry enchanting can be profitable (yes, really)
  • Utility Spells: Superglass Make, Tan Leather, Plank Make, Bones to Peaches
  • Quest Requirements: Many high-level quests require 60, 70, or even 90+ Magic

💡 Key Insight

If you're an Ironman, this guide is even more critical. You can't just buy your way to 99. You need efficient, resource-light methods. The enchanting strategies in Section 4 are especially valuable for Ironmen who mine their own gems and smelt their own jewelry.

Levels 1–13: Wind/Water/Earth/Fire Strike Spells

The very first levels of Magic are essentially free. You'll be using the basic Strike spells on low-level monsters, and the rune costs are negligible — we're talking single-digit GP per cast.

Where to Train (1–13)

The classic spot is chickens outside Lumbridge. They have low HP, don't fight back, and you can collect feathers while training. Alternatively, if you want to avoid combat entirely, you can cast spells at the dummies in the Combat Academy (after Tutorial Island) or at the experimental anvil in the Varrock Museum's basement (after The Dig Site quest).

Estimated time to 13 Magic: 30–45 minutes
Total rune cost: ~5,000–10,000 GP (or free if you buy from magic shops)
XP rate: ~10,000–15,000 XP/hr

Buying Runes Efficiently

For these early levels, buy your runes from:

  • Magic Guild (after 60 Magic): Best prices in the game
  • Varrock Magic Shop: Decent prices, right next to the Grand Exchange
  • Lumbridge Magic Shop: Convenient for early game
  • Grand Exchange: Usually the cheapest for bulk runes

🎯 Pro Tip

If you have a tiny amount of GP, buy an Elemental Staff (available at Zaff's shop in Varrock for 1,500 GP after completing the "What Lies Below" quest). It provides unlimited Air, Water, Earth, and Fire runes for combat spells. You'll still need Mind runes, but that's only 1 GP per cast.

Levels 13–27: Low-Level Enchant & Curse Spells

At level 13, you unlock Enchant Level 1 Jewelry and Confuse (a Curse spell). This opens up two budget-friendly paths: profitable enchanting and low-cost combat alternatives.

Path A: Enchant Opal/Jade/Topaz Jewelry (PROFITABLE!)

Many players don't realize this, but low-level enchanting can actually make you money. The margin fluctuates based on Grand Exchange prices, but Opal and Jade jewelry often sell for less than the enchantment value.

Enchant Spell Magic Level Jewelry Type XP per Cast Typical Profit/Cast
Enchant Lvl-1 Jewelry 7 (can use at 13+) Opal/Sapphire 17.5 ~50–200 GP
Enchant Lvl-2 Jewelry 27 Jade/Emerald 27 ~100–400 GP
Enchant Lvl-3 Jewelry 49 Topaz/Ruby 37 ~200–600 GP

How to do it: Buy unenchanted Opal jewelry (rings, necklaces, or amulets) from the Grand Exchange, cast Enchant Lvl-1 Jewelry, and sell the enchanted version. Check margins before committing — some days the profit is 300+ GP per cast, other days it's only 20 GP.

Path B: Curse Spells (Splashing Option)

Confuse (level 3 Magic) and Weaken (level 11 Magic) are Curse spells that can be splashed. Splashing means casting a spell that always "splashes" (deals 0 damage) on a target in a safe area. You gain full XP while consuming minimal runes.

Why consider this at low levels? If you're planning a long-term AFK training session (overnight, during work), starting with Curse spells is cheaper than Strike spells because you only need 1 Body rune + 1 Water/Air/Earth rune per cast.

XP rate (13–27 with enchanting): ~20,000–35,000 XP/hr depending on method and clicking speed
Profit potential: Some enchanting methods actually MAKE money (check GE margins daily)

📊 Real Example (as of 2026 GE prices)

Opal rings (unenchanted) often cost ~400 GP. Enchanted Opal rings sell for ~550–650 GP. That's 150–250 GP profit per cast, and you get 17.5 Magic XP per cast. At ~1,200 casts per hour, that's ~180K XP and ~250K profit per hour. Not bad for a "budget" method!

Levels 27–49: The Sweet Spot — Enchant Jewelry for PROFIT

This is where cheap Magic training truly shines. At level 27, you unlock Enchant Sapphire Jewelry. At level 49, you unlock Enchant Emerald Jewelry and Enchant Ruby Jewelry. These are the most consistent profit-making Magic training methods in the game.

Best Method for Ironmen: Mine + Craft Your Own

If you're an Ironman, this method is even better. Here's the workflow:

  1. Mine gems: Shilo Village gem rocks, Lunar Isle mine, or Motherlode Mine (rare gems)
  2. Craft gems into jewelry: Use a furnace with a gold bar + cut gem → unenchanted jewelry
  3. Enchant: Cast the appropriate enchant spell
  4. Equip or sell: Rings of dueling (Enchant Ruby) are always useful for teleportation

This turns a "cost" into essentially free XP since you're using self-gathered resources.

Best Method for Mains: Unpowered Orbs + Enchant

At level 56 Magic, you can enchant unpowered orbs (air, water, earth, fire orbs). These are used in God Wars Dungeon preparations and battlestaves. The margins fluctuate, but this can be surprisingly profitable.

Step-by-step:

  1. Buy unpowered orbs from the Grand Exchange (typically ~2,000–4,000 GP each)
  2. Enchant them with the appropriate elemental spell (requires 56 Magic for Air Orb, 58 for Water Orb, etc.)
  3. Sell the enchanted orbs (typically ~3,500–6,000 GP each depending on market)
  4. Profit + XP!

Superglass Make (77 Cooking + 33 Magic)

If you have 77 Cooking, you can use Superglass Make (Lunar spell, requires Lunar Diplomacy). This converts buckets of sand and soda ash/seaweed into molten glass instantly. The molten glass can be sold or used for Crafting training.

XP per cast: 30 (Cooking XP) + 3 (Magic XP per glass made)
Profit: Varies with GE prices of sand, soda ash, and molten glass. Often profitable by ~100–300 GP per glass.

🎯 Pro Tip

Always check GE margins before starting any enchanting method. Use the OSRS Wiki's "Item" pages to see historical price trends. Some weeks Sapphire necklaces are 200+ GP profit per cast; other weeks they're barely breaking even. Patience pays off.

Levels 49–70: Multiple Budget Paths

Once you hit level 49, you have three distinct budget paths. Choose based on your playstyle, GP situation, and patience.

Path A: Continue Enchanting (Cheapest, Can Profit)

If you enjoyed the profit from Sections 3–4, keep going! At 49+, you can enchant:

  • Enchant Diamond Jewelry (57 Magic): ~52 XP/cast, decent profit potential
  • Enchant Dragonstone Jewelry (68 Magic): ~67 XP/cast, higher cost but better profit margins
  • Enchant Onyx Jewelry (87 Magic): ~97 XP/cast, expensive but very profitable when margins are good

Enchanting Bolts: From level 51+, you can enchant crossbow bolts (Pearl bolts, Jade bolts, Opal bolts, etc.). The Slayer community always needs bolts, so demand is consistent. Margins are usually thinner than jewelry, but it's an option if jewelry margins are bad.

Path B: Splashing (AFK but Slow)

Splashing deserves its own section (see Section 7), but the summary is: you can cast spells that always "splash" (deal 0 damage) on a target in a safe area. You gain full Magic XP while consuming runes, and you can AFK for up to 20 minutes at a time (the logout timer).

Why consider splashing at 49–70?

  • You can do it overnight while sleeping
  • Zero effort required (once set up)
  • Costs only ~20,000 XP/hr but it's "free" time (you're sleeping anyway)
  • Total cost to splash 1–99: ~10–15M GP (vs 150M+ for High Alch)

Path C: Teleport Spells (Useful + Moderate Cost)

If you're actively playing and want useful spells, train by teleporting:

  • Camelot Teleport (45 Magic): ~55.5 XP/cast, ~35,000–40,000 XP/hr, moderate cost (~200 GP/cast with staff)
  • Ardougne Teleport (51 Magic): ~61 XP/cast, useful for run energy recharge (Camelot is often better XP/hr)
  • Watchtower Teleport (58 Magic): ~68 XP/cast, useful for Yanille access
  • Trollheim Teleport (61 Magic): ~68 XP/cast, useful for God Wars approach

Strategy: Need to teleport somewhere anyway? Use the teleport spell instead of a tablet. You get XP and transportation. It's like getting paid to travel.

Levels 70–99: High-Level Cheap Options

At level 70+, you have access to the Lunar spellbook (after completing Lunar Diplomacy) and high-level enchanting. These open up the best budget methods in the game.

Lunar Spells: The Budget Player's Best Friend

The Lunar spellbook has several spells that are AFK-friendly and low-cost (some even profitable):

String Jewelry (80 Magic, Lunar)

Converts unstrung symbols into amulets. Requires a ball of wool per cast.

  • XP per cast: 83
  • XP/hr: ~50,000
  • Cost/hr: Small profit to small loss (depends on GE margins of unstrung symbols vs. strung amulets)
  • AFK? Yes — can click every ~4 seconds comfortably

  • XP/hr: ~65,000
  • Cost/hr: Small loss to small profit (depends on cowhide and leather prices)
  • AFK? Yes — ~3 second cast time, very manageable

Plank Make (86 Magic, Lunar)

Converts logs into planks instantly. Used for Construction training.

  • XP per cast: 90
  • XP/hr: ~55,000
  • Cost/hr: Varies wildly with GE margins. Can be profitable or cost up to -1M/hr.

Humidify (75 Magic, Lunar)

Fills vials, jugs, and bowls with water. Extremely cheap, can be done anywhere near a water source.

The Splashing Guide: Complete AFK Method

Splashing is the ultimate budget Magic training method. It's slow, but it's completely AFK and costs a fraction of other methods. If you have time but not GP, this is your best option.

How Splashing Works

In OSRS, if you cast a combat spell on a target that has 100% magic defense (or is in a safe area where damage is impossible), the spell will always "splash" — dealing 0 damage. You still gain the full Magic XP for the cast, but you don't need to worry about the monster fighting back or dying.

The 20-minute logout timer: OSRS logs you out after 20 minutes of inactivity. When splashing, "inactivity" means not interacting with the game. Simply having a spell auto-cast at a target does not reset the timer. You need to perform an action (move camera, click a tile, open inventory) at least once every ~18–19 minutes to stay logged in.

What You Need for Splashing

  • Elemental Staff: Provides unlimited runes of that element (Air, Water, Earth, or Fire Staff). Buy from staff shops or Grand Exchange.
  • Other runes for the spell: For example, Fire Strike needs 1 Fire rune (covered by Fire Staff) + 1 Air rune (covered by any Air-providing staff) + 1 Mind rune. So you only need Mind runes!
  • Full magic defense armor (optional but recommended): If you're splashing on something that can attack you, wear Dragonhide or Barrows armor to take 0 damage. For 100% magic defense NPCs, you take 0 damage anyway.

Anti-Ban Measures (Staying Undetected)

Jagex's bot detection looks for perfectly repetitive behavior. While splashing is not against the rules (it's a legitimate game mechanic), extremely obvious AFK behavior can trigger a temporary ban if done for days on end without any variation.

How to stay safe:

  • Move your camera every 10–15 minutes (can be done with an auto-hotkey script or manually)
  • Click a different tile or open/close your inventory every ~18 minutes to reset the logout timer
  • Don't splash 24/7 for weeks. Take breaks. Play other content.
  • Some players use a simple mouse-jiggler or script to move the camera periodically. Be careful — third-party clients are against the rules. The built-in "camera follow" option is safe.

Total Cost to Splash 1–99

If you splash from 1 to 99 Magic:

  • Total casts needed: ~37.5 million XP total / ~10 XP average per cast = ~3.75 million casts
  • Rune cost: At 2 GP per cast (Mind runes only with staff), that's ~7.5M GP total
  • Realistic cost (higher-level spells): ~10–15M GP total
  • Time required: ~350–400 hours of active splashing time (or ~3–4 months of overnight AFK)

Compare this to High Alch: 150–180M GP for the same 99. Splashing is 10–15x cheaper.

🌙 Overnight Splashing Setup

Want to splash overnight? Here's a safe setup:

  1. Go to Varrock West Bank, stand behind the bank
  2. Right-click the Knight of Ardougne NPC, select "Spell" → choose your splash spell (e.g., Fire Strike)
  3. Your character will now auto-cast this spell at the NPC every 2–3 seconds
  4. Set an alarm for 18 minutes. When it goes off, wiggle your mouse to reset the logout timer
  5. Go back to sleep. Repeat.
  6. In the morning, you'll have ~8–10 hours of splashing done (with timer resets).

Warning: Don't leave your PC unattended for more than ~6 hours. Power outages, Windows updates, and internet drops happen. 4–5 hours of overnight splashing is a safe target.

My Personal Recommendation (Budget Player)

If I were training Magic on a budget in 2026, here's exactly what I'd do:

  1. Levels 1–27: Enchant Opal/Jade jewelry for profit. If margins are bad, splash Curse spells.
  2. Levels 27–57: Enchant Sapphire/Emerald/Ruby/Diamond jewelry. Check GE margins daily. Profit or tiny loss.
  3. Levels 57–70: Continue enchanting, or start splashing overnight while keeping enchanting as daytime activity.
  4. Levels 70–99: Complete Lunar Diplomacy → use Lunar spellbook → Tan Leather or String Jewelry (AFK, low cost). Supplement with splashing overnight.
  5. Total estimated cost: 5–20M GP (or profit!) instead of 150M+.
  6. Total estimated time: ~250–300 hours (vs. ~180 hours for High Alch).

Is it slower? Yes. Is it 130M GP cheaper? Also yes. That 130M difference is enough to buy a truncated Twisted Bow, or max your bank in other skills. The "cost" of fast Magic training is simply too high for most players — and it doesn't have to be that way.

Tips & FAQs

Q: Which spellbook should I use?

A: Use the Standard spellbook until level 70+, then complete Lunar Diplomacy and switch to the Lunar spellbook. Lunar spells are far better for budget training (Tan Leather, String Jewelry, Plank Make, Humidify). The only reason to stay on Standard is if you need High Alch for quick GP or God spells for combat.

If you want Ancient Magicks (Barrage/Burst), you'll need to complete Desert Treasure. But Ancient spells are expensive — only switch if you have the GP for it or plan to use Barrage for Slayer/bossing (the XP is fast, but the cost is 200M+).

Q: Should I buy runes or craft them?

A: For almost all players, buy from the Grand Exchange. Runecrafting is its own skill with its own grind. The only exception is if you're already training Runecrafting and have a surplus of runes — then by all means use them!

If you do want to craft your own runes, the break-even point is roughly:

  • Air runes: Craft if you have 77 Runecrafting (87 with Runecrafting cape for 2x多发性)
  • Mind runes: Craft if you have 66 Runecrafting (77 with cape)
  • Body runes: Craft if you have 44 Runecrafting (never really worth it)
  • Nature runes (for High Alch): NEVER worth crafting for Magic training. Just buy them.

Q: Is enchanting jewelry really profitable?

A: Yes — but it depends on Grand Exchange margins, which change daily. Here's the strategy:

  1. Check the Wiki or GE for the price of unenchanted jewelry vs. enchanted jewelry
  2. If the difference is >200 GP, it's worth doing
  3. If the difference is <50 GP, skip it and do something else
  4. Some weeks, Sapphire necklaces are 300+ GP profit per cast. Other weeks, they're 10 GP. Patience is key.

Real example: In January 2026, Enchant Ruby Necklace was ~500 GP profit per cast. At ~1,200 casts/hr, that's ~600K profit per hour while getting ~55,000 Magic XP. That's literally the best training method in the game — profit AND XP.

Q: When should I switch from cheap methods to fast methods?

A: The turning point is roughly when you have 50M+ GP in the bank. At that point, the opportunity cost of your time might outweigh the GP savings. If you value your time at more than ~1M GP per hour, switch to High Alch or Barrage NMZ.

But if you're enjoying the grind, there's no shame in sticking with Lunar spells or enchanting all the way to 99. The total cost difference between "cheapest" and "fastest" is 150M+ GP — that's a lot of bonds or endgame gear.

Q: What gear helps with Magic training?

A: For budget training, you don't need expensive gear. Here's what helps:

  • Elemental Staves: Unlimited runes of that element. Buy once, use forever. (Air Staff, Water Staff, Earth Staff, Fire Staff)
  • Staff of the Dead / Staff of Light: Expensive but provides unlimited Fire runes and +10% magic damage. Only for rich players.
  • Mystic/Robes: +20–30 Magic accuracy. Cheap on the GE. Wear these for combat spell training.
  • God Robes / Spellcaster Gear: +10% magic damage. Expensive but noticeable for High Alch or combat.
  • Tome of Fire: +10% Fire spell damage. Useful if using Fire spells for combat training.
  • Amulet of Magic: Cheap amulet that boosts Magic by +10. Wear while training.

For splashing specifically: You don't need any gear. Wear whatever you want. Some players wear full Dragonhide just for style points. It doesn't affect splashing XP.

Q: Can I use a cannon to train Magic?

A: No. The Dwarf Multicannon gives Ranged XP only. It does not give Magic XP. Don't waste your time.

Q: What about NMZ (Nightmare Zone) for Magic?

A: NMZ is great for combat XP (Attack/Strength/Defence) while using Magic attack style. But it does NOT give Magic XP — it gives Combat XP for the style you're using. If you want to train Magic specifically, NMZ is not the answer (unless you're using Ancients like Ice Barrage, which is expensive).

For Magic XP at NMZ, you'd need to use Ancient Magicks (Barrage/Burst) on the NPCs. This costs 200M+ GP from 70–99. It's fast (~130K XP/hr) but devastatingly expensive. Only do this if you have the GP and want PvP/bossing readiness.

Q: I'm an Ironman. What's different for me?

A: As an Ironman, you can't buy your way to 99. But you have advantages:

  1. Mine your own gems: Shilo Village gem rocks, Lunar Isle mine. Craft them into jewelry yourself.
  2. Smith your own gold bars: Mine gold ore, smelt into bars, craft into jewelry, enchant. 100% free.
  3. Use Lunar spells: After Lunar Diplomacy, Tan Leather and String Jewelry are nearly free if you gather the materials yourself.
  4. Quest for early levels: Many quests give Magic XP. Fairytale Part II (Wishing Well) alone gives ~40,000 XP. Do all Magic-giving quests before grinding.

📊 Quest Magic XP (Up to 99)

Before you start grinding, here are some quests that give Magic XP:

  • Fairytale Part II: ~40,000 XP (requires 40 Farming, 49 Magic)
  • The Eyes of Glouphrie: ~10,000 XP (requires 45 Magic)
  • Lunar Diplomacy: ~10,000 XP (requires 65 Magic)
  • Dream Mentor: ~15,000 XP (requires 85 Combat, 61 Magic)
  • Dragon Slayer II: ~15,000 XP (requires 75 Combat, 200 Quest Points)

That's ~90,000 free XP from quests alone. Do these before you start splashing!
