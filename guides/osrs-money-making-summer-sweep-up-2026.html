1. Summer Sweep-Up 2026 — What Actually Changed

The Summer Sweep-Up 2026 was one of the most significant economy updates in OSRS history. Jagex didn't just tweak a few numbers — they fundamentally altered how players earn GP across every game stage. If you're still using pre-Sweep-Up money making guides, you're likely following strategies that are 20-40% less efficient than they used to be.

📊 Major Balance Changes Overview

Here's what Jagex actually changed and why it matters:

🎯 Key Philosophy Behind the Changes

Jagex's stated goal was to "reduce GP inflation while making money making more engaging and less click-intensive." In practice, this meant:

  • Nerfing passive/farming methods: Activities that generated GP without active gameplay were hit hardest
  • Buffing active PvM: Bossing and combat-focused methods received significant boosts
  • Rebalancing skilling: Some gathering skills became more profitable, while others less so
  • New content integration: Several new money making methods were added that didn't exist before

Method Spotlight: Cowhide Collection (Still #1 for F2P)

Despite the changes, cowhide collection remains the most reliable F2P money maker. Here's the optimized strategy:

📍 Location: Al Kharid Cows (North of Al Kharid)

Why here: Cows respawn quickly, area is safe, bank is close (Al Kharid east bank)

What to do:

  1. Kill cows, collect cowhides (every cow drops 1 hide)
  2. Bank hides at Al Kharid east bank
  3. Once you have 28 hides, travel to Grand Exchange
  4. Sell hides for ~550-600 GP each (current price as of June 2026)
  5. Repeat — each loop takes ~8-10 minutes for 28 hides = ~15K-17K GP per loop

Optimization tip: Use the Al Kharid Lodestone (unlocked after "Prince Ali Rescue" quest) for fastest banking. If you haven't done that quest, use the Lumbridge cow field instead (slower banking but zero quest requirements).

F2P Method: Iron Ore Mining (East Varrock)

This method dropped from ~130K to ~110K GP/hour post-Sweep-Up, but it's still solid for low-level accounts:

📍 Location: East Varrock Mine (3 iron rocks close together)

Strategy:

  1. Mine iron ore at East Varrock (3 rocks in a triangle formation)
  2. When rocks deplete, world hop (ctrl + F1-F10) to find fresh rocks
  3. Bank at Varrock east bank (close to mine)
  4. Sell iron ore at GE for ~100-120 GP each

Requirement: 15 Mining (very easy to reach — mine copper/tin from level 1-15)

Expected yield: ~900-1000 iron ore per hour = ~100K-120K GP

🆕 Newly Viable: Chaos Druids (Taverley Dungeon)

This method jumped from ~140K to ~200K GP/hour thanks to improved herb drop rates post-Sweep-Up. Here's how to do it efficiently:

📍 Setup

Requirements: 35 Combat (recommended: 40 Attack, 40 Strength, 40 Defense for armor)

Gear: Steel armor set (~5K GP from GE), Steel scimitar (~100 GP), Amulet of Strength (~2K GP)

Inventory: 28 slots for loot (no food needed if using Protect from Melee at 43 Prayer, or safespotting)

📍 Location & Strategy

Location: Taverley Dungeon (enter from Taverley, go south past the black knights)

Why Chaos Druids: They have low defense (can hit consistently with Steel scimitar), drop valuable herbs (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Cadantine), and are clustered in groups of 3-4.

Combat strategy:

  1. Attack a Chaos Druid, let it attack you (they hit 0-2 damage, negligible)
  2. Kill it, pick up herbs (priority: Ranarr > Snapdragon > Cadantine > Kwuarm)
  3. Move to next druid, repeat
  4. When inventory full, bank at Falador west bank (closest)

Expected drops per hour: ~80-100 herbs total, including 2-5 Ranarr (4500 GP each), 5-10 Snapdragon (2000 GP each), 10-15 Cadantine (1000 GP each) = ~200K GP/hour

💡 Pro Tip for Low-Level Players

If you have 43 Prayer, use Protect from Melee while fighting Chaos Druids — you'll take zero damage and can stay there indefinitely without food. This turns a medium-intensity method into a low-intensity one. If you don't have 43 Prayer yet, safespot the druids using Ranged (20+ Ranged with Bronze arrows works fine).

Method Spotlight: Wine of Zamorak (Non-Combat Option)

If you want to make GP without fighting, Wine of Zamorak is still one of the best low-level methods. Post-Sweep-Up, the Wine of Zamorak sell price stabilized at ~3,500-4,000 GP each.

📍 Location: Chaos Temple (Level 38 Wilderness)

Warning: This is in the Wilderness. You can be attacked by PKers. Only bring what you can afford to lose.

Strategy (safespot method — no Wilderness risk):

  1. Go to the Chaos Temple in the safe area (just outside Wilderness, south of the actual temple)
  2. Wait for a Wine of Zamorak to spawn (spawns every 28 seconds)
  3. Click on it instantly when it appears (right-click → Take)
  4. Teleport to bank (using Amulet of Glory or similar) before going to Wilderness
  5. Sell wines at GE for ~3,500 GP each

Optimized route (members): Use the Ardougne cloak 1 teleport to Monastery, run north to Chaos Temple (takes ~45 seconds per trip). With perfect timing, you can get 1 wine per minute = 60 wines per hour = ~210K GP/hour.

Realistic expectation: ~35-45 wines per hour (accounting for competition and travel time) = ~150K-180K GP/hour

🆕 Overlooked Method: Astral Runecrafting (Post-Sweep-Up Buff)

Astral runes got a significant price buff post-Sweep-Up (from ~120 GP to ~180 GP per rune). This makes Astral Runecrafting viable at lower Runecrafting levels than before.

Requirements & Setup

Requirements: 40 Runecrafting, completion of "The Eyes of Glouphrie" quest (unlocks Astral altar access)

Gear: Graceful outfit (recommended for run energy), Ring of dueling (optional, for banking)

Inventory: 28 Pure essence, Ring of dueling (for teleport to Castle Wars for banking)

Strategy

  1. Bank at Castle Wars (withdraw 28 Pure essence)
  2. Use fairy ring code BLQ to reach Astral altar (requires starting "Fairy Tale II" quest)
  3. Craft essence into Astral runes (2 essence per rune at 40 Runecrafting = 14 runes per trip)
  4. Teleport back to Castle Wars via Ring of dueling
  5. Repeat — each trip takes ~90 seconds = ~56 runes per hour = ~10K GP per hour... wait, that's terrible.

Correction: At 40 Runecrafting, you only make 14 runes per trip, which is indeed slow. The real money starts at 82 Runecrafting when you can craft 2 Astral runes per essence (56 runes per trip = ~100K GP/hour). Consider this a long-term goal method rather than an immediate low-level option.

Slayer jumped from ~300K to ~480K GP/hour post-Sweep-Up. The changes include:

  4. Use Slayer helmet (i) for +15% accuracy and damage on Slayer tasks

Method Spotlight: Barrows (Biggest Nerf Survivor → Now Buffed!)

Barrows actually got BUFFED post-Sweep-Up, going from ~400K to ~650K GP/hour. This makes it one of the best mid-level PvM money makers.

📍 Requirements & Gear

Requirements: 70 Combat, 43 Prayer (essential for Protect prayers), completion of "In Search of the Myreque" (access to Morytania)

Recommended gear:

  • Weapon: Dragon scimitar (60 Attack) or Abyssal whip (70 Attack)
  • Armor: Dragon armor set or Barrows armor (if you have it)
  • Inventory: 18 Food (Sharks or better), 3 Prayer potions, 6 slots for loot

📍 Strategy (Post-Sweep-Up Optimized)

  1. Enter Barrows (use Barrows teleport tablet or Minigame teleport)
  2. Kill all 6 brothers (use Protect prayer matching their attack style):
    • Ahrim (Magic) → Protect from Magic
    • Dharok (Melee) → Protect from Melee
    • Guthan (Melee) → Protect from Melee
    • Karil (Ranged) → Protect from Missiles
    • Torag (Melee) → Protect from Melee
    • Verac (Melee) → Protect from Melee (his prayer ignore is rare)
  3. Loot the chest (requires killing all 6 brothers + opening the chest in the tunnel)
  4. Repeat — each full run takes ~8-12 minutes = 5-7 runs per hour

Expected loot per hour: 5-7 Barrows runs = 5-7 chances at Barrows armor pieces (150K-2M GP each) + consistent GP from coins, herbs, runes, bolt racks = ~650K GP/hour on average

Note: Barrows rewards are RNG-based. Some hours you'll get 2M+ GP from multiple armor pieces. Other hours you'll get 200K GP from junk. The 650K/hour is a long-term average.

🆕 New Method: Fortis Colosseum (Medium Difficulty)

Fortis Colosseum is a brand new minigame added in post-Sweep-Up. It's a wave-based combat challenge where you fight increasingly difficult waves of monsters. At medium difficulty, it's accessible to mid-level players and offers excellent GP/hour.

📍 Access & Requirements

Location: Fortis Colosseum (teleport via Minigame teleport or walk from Shayzien)

Requirements: 70 Combat, decent gear (Dragon or better), 43 Prayer recommended

Difficulty levels: Easy (50 Combat), Medium (70 Combat), Hard (85 Combat), Elite (110 Combat)

📍 Medium Difficulty Strategy

  1. Start the minigame (talk to the Colosseum master, select "Medium")
  2. Fight waves 1-10 (increasingly difficult monsters, primarily melee-based)
  3. Use Protect from Melee for most waves (switch to Protect from Missiles for ranged waves)
  4. Collect rewards after completing all 10 waves (or dying, whichever comes first)
  5. Rewards include: Colosseum tokens (trade for GP or items), GP coins, rare drops (Dragon weapons, herbs, runes)

Expected GP/hour: ~600K-800K (depending on how far you get and RNG rewards)

Why it's good: Engaging combat (not mindless clicking), scalable difficulty, excellent rewards, fun!

Method Spotlight: Vorkath (Now #1 GP/Hour)

Vorkath is widely considered the best solo money maker in OSRS post-Sweep-Up. Here's the optimized strategy:

📍 Requirements & Gear

Requirements: 80 Combat, 80 Ranged, completion of "Dragon Slayer II" (access to Vorkath), 43 Prayer

Recommended gear:

  • Weapon: Rune crossbow (60 Ranged) or Dragon hunter crossbow (75 Ranged, BiS)
  • Ammo: Diamond bolts (e) or Ruby bolts (e)
  • Armor: Armadyl armor (Ranged bonus) or Blessed d'hide
  • Inventory: 16 Food (Sharks or better), 4 Prayer potions, 3 Antifire potions, 5 slots for loot

📍 Strategy (Post-Sweep-Up Meta)

  1. Enter Vorkath's lair (use Vorkath teleport tablet or walk from Rellekka)
  2. Fight Vorkath (use Ranged, pray Protect from Magic for most of the fight):
    • Phase 1 (100-66% HP): Vorkath uses Magic attacks → Protect from Magic
    • Phase 2 (66-33% HP): Vorkath uses Fire breath → Antifire potion + Protect from Magic
    • Phase 3 (33-0% HP): Vorkath uses mixed attacks → Adapt prayer based on attack style
  3. Loot Vorkath (100% drop rate for valuable loot: Dragon bones, Blue dragonhide, Vorkath's scales, Rune items, GP)
  4. Repeat — each kill takes ~2-3 minutes = 20-30 kills per hour

Expected loot per hour: 20-30 kills = ~1.7M GP (including Vorkath's scales, Dragon bones, Rune items, and rare drops like Vorkath's head)

Skill requirement: This is a high-skill boss. If you're consistently dying, practice the mechanics on a lower-level boss first (like Zulrah or Chaos Elemental).

The Chambers of Xeric (CoX) raid got several changes that make it more profitable and accessible:

📍 What Changed

  • New raid rooms added (more variety, less repetitive)

Note: CoX requires a team. If you don't have friends to raid with, join the "OSRS Raiding" Discord or use the in-game clan system to find a team.

8. FAQ — Summer Sweep-Up Money Making

Are the old money making guides completely useless now?

Not completely — but they're significantly less efficient. Methods that got nerfed by 20-40% are still viable if you enjoy them, but you could be making 20-40% more GP per hour by switching to post-Sweep-Up optimized methods. Think of it this way: if a guide was written before June 2026, the numbers are wrong. Use this guide instead.

What's the absolute fastest way to make GP as a new member?

If you have 70 Combat and 43 Prayer: Barrows (~650K GP/hour). If you don't have those stats yet: Chaos Druids in Taverley Dungeon (~200K GP/hour) while training Combat. Once you reach 75 Combat: Slayer with Duradel (~480K GP/hour) — this has the added benefit of training Combat and Slayer at the same time.

Should I buy membership just for the money making methods?

Yes — almost certainly. F2P methods cap at ~120K GP/hour. Member methods start at ~200K GP/hour and go up to 1.7M GP/hour. Even if you only play 10 hours a week, membership pays for itself in GP within 2-3 weeks. Plus, you get access to way more content (quests, skills, bosses, etc.).
