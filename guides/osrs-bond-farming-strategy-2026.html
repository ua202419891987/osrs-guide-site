② Buy the Bond... But Strategically

Current bond price: ~12.5-12.6M GP (June 2026, down 10% from May 2026). Key strategic considerations:

  • Option A — Pay for first month (~$11 USD): By far the most time-efficient. Use those 30 days to build passive income systems. One $11 payment saves you ~25 hours of F2P grinding for your first bond.
  • Option B — F2P grind to 12.6M: At 500K/hr (soft clay), you need ~25 hours of grinding. This is doable but burns many players out before they even start membership.

📊 Bond Farming vs Real Money: The 2026 Math

$11 for 14 days (real money): Zero GP pressure. Full freedom to do quests, train skills, and build your account.

12.6M GP for 14 days (bond): You need to earn ~900K GP/day. At 500K/hr (soft clay, F2P), that's 1.8 hours/day for the bond alone. At 400K-600K/hr (dragonhide tanning, P2P), that's 1.5-2.3 hours/day. At 2.5M-3M/hr (Vorkath, mid-game), that's just 18-20 minutes/day.

Verdict: If your GP/hr is below 300K, bonds are a time sink. If you enjoy money making gameplay itself, bonds are fine. The average player switches to real money within 3-6 months of bonding to avoid burnout.

8. Bond Price Strategy: Buy Low, Bond Smart

Bond prices fluctuate significantly. At 12.6M GP per bond, strategic buying can save you millions over the course of a year:

① Bond Price Patterns (2024-2026 Data, Source: Jagex Official GE)

  • Current price (June 2026): ~12.6M GP guide price. Down 10% over 1 month and 15% over 6 months — bonds are in a rare downtrend.
  • Cheapest time to buy: Late January (post-holiday lull) and September (back-to-school). Prices can be 10-15% below peak. If this downtrend continues, bonds could hit 11M-12M by late summer.
  • Most expensive time: Summer (June-August, peak player count) and December (holiday season). Prices 5-15% above average.
  • Weekly pattern: Buy on Tuesday-Wednesday (lowest demand), avoid buying Friday-Sunday (highest demand).

② The Bond Stockpile Strategy (12.6M Edition)

When you have surplus GP (after essential gear upgrades), buy 2-3 bonds in advance during low-price periods. Store them in your bank. Example: buy 3 bonds at 12M each (36M total) during a September lull. When winter hits and bonds spike to 14M+, you've saved 6M GP. If you bond every 14 days, that's 6 bonds over 12 weeks — saving roughly 12M GP total.

⚠️ Never Buy Bonds at Peak Price if You Can Wait

If your current bond has 3+ days remaining and the price is 13M+ (peak), WAIT. Check prices daily. In the current market (June 2026), bond prices are in a rare downtrend, down from ~14M+ earlier in the year. If you can delay buying by 2-3 days, you might save 500K-1M GP. Patience pays in the bond market.

9. Bond Farming FAQs

How much do bonds actually cost in June 2026?

As of June 2026, the official Jagex Grand Exchange guide price for an Old School bond is 12.6M GP (source: secure.runescape.com item database). Real-time tracker osrs.exchange shows ~12.5M GP live price. The price has decreased roughly 10-15% over the last 6 months from ~14M+ levels, making this a relatively favorable time for bond buyers.

Can I sustain bonds forever without high combat stats?

Yes. With tanning dragonhide (400K-600K/hr), bird house runs (100K-200K/day passive), and herb runs (100K-250K/day), you can sustain the 12.6M bond with ~2 hours/day of active play plus 20 minutes of passive checks. You don't need bossing — but bossing makes it MUCH easier (20 min/day vs 2 hours/day). The gap between zero-skill methods and bossing has grown with bond prices.

Is bond farming worth the time at 12.6M GP?

Financially? No. $11/month for membership is less than one fast food meal. But if you genuinely enjoy the GP-making gameplay, bonds are fine. The key question: are you spending 2+ hours per day on bond farming because you enjoy it, or because you feel forced to? If forced, pay real money. Many long-time bond farmers eventually switch to real money after realizing the time:cost ratio doesn't make sense at minimum wage equivalency.

What's the single best bond farming method for a new member?

Mort Myre Fungi. 300K-400K/hr, no skills required, just Priest in Peril quest (30 min). In 2.5 hours of fungi, you earn ~875K GP — almost exactly your daily 900K bond target. Combine with bird houses (5 min, 50 min cycle) and you're ahead of target. Once you have 100K+ capital, switch to tanning dragonhide for 400K-600K/hr.

How long does the bond pay for itself at 12.6M?

If you're doing Mort Myre Fungi (350K/hr average): ~36 hours to earn 12.6M GP for the next bond. With passive income from bird houses and herb runs during those 14 days, the effective time is much lower. With the full routine (Section 3), you break even on your bond investment in about 8-10 days, leaving 4-6 days of pure profit. Once you reach mid-game bossing, the payback drops to 2-3 days.

Can I sustain multiple accounts with bonds at 12.6M each?

Technically yes, but it's very time-consuming. Each account needs its own 12.6M every 14 days. For 2 accounts: 25.2M GP/14 days = 1.8M GP/day. For 3 accounts: 37.8M GP/14 days = 2.7M GP/day. This is achievable at endgame (a few hours of raiding) but nearly impossible for mid-game. Most multi-account players pay for alts with real money.

What if bond prices keep rising faster than my GP/hr?

This is a real concern. Bonds rose from ~10M (2024) to ~14M+ (early 2026) before the current correction to ~12.6M. If your GP/hr isn't increasing at the same rate, you'll need to play more each year. The solution: progress your account. Early game: tanning (400K/hr) → Mid game: Vorkath (2.5M/hr) → End game: raids (5M+/hr). As your account improves, the bond becomes an increasingly smaller percentage of your hourly income.

Should I prioritize account progression or bond farming?

Balance them. Spend 60% of play time on progression (quests, skills, bosses) and 40% on money making. The passive income routine (Section 5) covers 33-61% of the 12.6M bond automatically. Your active money making should be short and efficient. Don't spend all 14 days grinding fungi or tanning — that's how you burn out. Remember: every quest you complete and every combat level you gain unlocks faster money making methods.

What if I only play 5 hours per week?

At 5 hours/week with passive setup earning 350K GP/day (from bird houses + herbs + Miscellania), that's 4.9M GP over 14 days. You'd need to actively earn the remaining 7.7M GP — at 500K/hr (tanning dragonhide), that's ~15.4 hours over 14 days, or about 1.1 hours/day. Total: ~1.5 hours/day to sustain a bond at 5 hours/week gameplay. If that's too much, pay real money. With 5 hours/week and a $11/month membership, you can progress your account much faster than grinding bonds.

I have 100M bank — how should I manage bonds?

At 100M bank value: buy 3 bonds in advance (~37.8M at current prices). That's 6 weeks of membership covered immediately. Use the remaining 62.2M for gear upgrades that increase your GP/hr (Blowpipe, Trident of the Seas, Berserker Ring, etc.). Each gear upgrade pays for itself by making bond farming faster. Never let your bank drop below 50M just for bonds — the opportunity cost of missed gear progression is higher than the bond cost.
