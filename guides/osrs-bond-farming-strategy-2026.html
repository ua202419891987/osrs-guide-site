The danger is burnout: if bond farming feels like a second job, you'll quit. This guide focuses on SUSTAINABLE methods that don't feel like grinding.

📊 Bond Farming vs Real Money: The Math

$11 for 14 days of membership (real money): You spend $11 and have 14 days to play without any GP pressure. You can focus on account progression.

11M GP for 14 days (bond): You need to earn ~785K GP/day. At 150K/hr (F2P wines), that's 5.2 hours/day just for the bond. At 350K/hr (P2P fungi), that's 2.2 hours/day.

Verdict: If you value your time at more than $1/hour, pay for membership. If you enjoy the money-making gameplay itself, bonds are fine. The average player switches to real money after 3-6 months of bonding.

8. Bond Price Strategy: Buy Low, Bond Smart

Bond prices fluctuate. Strategic buying can save you millions:

① Bond Price Patterns (Historical Data)

Based on GE tracker data from 2024-2026:

  • Cheapest time to buy bonds: Late January (post-holiday lull) and September (back-to-school). Prices can be 10-15% below peak
  • Most expensive time: Summer (June-August, peak player count) and December (holiday season). Prices 5-15% above average
  • Weekly pattern: Buy on Tuesday-Wednesday (lowest), avoid buying Friday-Sunday (highest demand)

② The Bond Stockpile Strategy

When you have surplus GP (after equipment upgrades), buy 2-3 bonds in advance during low-price periods. Store them in your bank. This protects you against price spikes and removes the "I need GP for my bond" pressure.

Example: Buy 3 bonds at 10M each (30M total) in January. When summer hits and bonds spike to 12M+, you've saved 6M GP. If you bond every 14 days, that's 6 bonds over 12 weeks — saving ~12M GP.

⚠️ Never Buy Bonds at Peak Price if You Can Wait

If your current bond has 3+ days remaining and the price is 12M (peak), WAIT. Check prices daily. Bonds typically drop 500K-1M within 2-3 days of a peak. Patience saves GP.

9. Bond Farming FAQs

Can I sustain bonds forever without high combat stats?

Yes. With Mort Myre Fungi (300K-400K/hr), bird house runs (80K-200K/day passive), and herb runs (100K-250K/day), you can sustain bonds at 2-3 hours of active play per day. You don't need bossing — but bossing makes it MUCH easier (20 min/day vs 2-3 hours/day).

How much GP do bonds actually cost in 2026?

As of June 2026, bonds trade at ~10.5-11.5M GP. The price has increased roughly 15-20% per year over the last 3 years due to OSRS GP inflation. Budget for 12-13M by the end of 2026 to be safe.

Is bond farming worth the time?

Financially? No. $11/month for membership is less than a coffee per week. But if you genuinely enjoy the GP-making gameplay, bonds are fine. The key question: are you spending 2+ hours per day on bond farming because you enjoy it, or because you feel forced to? If forced, pay real money.

What's the single best bond farming method for a new member?

Mort Myre Fungi. 300K-400K/hr, zero skills required, just Priest in Peril quest (30 min). In 2 hours of fungi, you earn 600K-800K GP — your daily bond target. Combine with bird houses (5 min, 50 min cycle) and you're ahead of curve.

How long does it take to break even on a bond?

If you're doing Mort Myre Fungi (350K/hr): ~31-33 hours to earn 11M GP. But if you include passive income from bird houses and herb runs during those 14 days, the effective time is much lower. With the full routine described in Section 3, you break even on your bond investment in about 8-10 days, leaving 4-6 days of pure profit.

Can I sustain multiple accounts with bonds?

Technically yes, but it's extremely time-consuming. Each account needs its own bond (11M every 14 days). For 2 accounts: 22M GP every 14 days = 1.57M GP/day. For 3 accounts: 2.36M GP/day. This is doable at endgame but nearly impossible for mid-game players. Most multi-account players pay for the alts with real money.

What if bond prices increase faster than my GP/hr?

This is a real risk. Bond prices have risen ~15-20% per year. If your GP/hr isn't increasing at the same rate, you'll need to play more each year to sustain bonds. The solution: progress your account. Early game fungi (350K/hr) → Mid game Vorkath (2.5M/hr) → End game raids (5M/hr). As your account improves, your bond cost becomes a smaller percentage of your income.

Should I prioritize account progression or bond farming?

Balance them. Spend 60% of your play time on progression (quests, skills, bosses), 40% on money making. Follow the routine in Section 3 — passive income covers most of the bond, active money making is short. Don't spend all 14 days doing fungi — that's how you burn out and quit.

What if I only play 5 hours per week?

At 5 hours/week with 350K GP/hr, you earn 1.75M GP/week = 3.5M GP/2 weeks. That's not enough for a bond. Options: (1) Pay for membership with real money ($11), (2) Increase efficiency (use passive income to supplement active earnings), (3) Play more during weekends. With 5 hours/week, bonds are not sustainable without passive income.

I have 100M bank — how should I manage bonds?

At 100M bank value: buy 3 bonds in advance (33M). That's 6 weeks of membership covered immediately. Use the remaining 67M for gear upgrades that increase your GP/hr (Blowpipe, Trident, Torture, etc.). Each gear upgrade pays for itself by making bond farming faster. Never let your bank drop below 50M just for bonds.
