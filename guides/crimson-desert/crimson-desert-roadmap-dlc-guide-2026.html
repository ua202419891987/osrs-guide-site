🔮 1. June 2026 Roadmap Overview — What Just Launched

Pearl Abyss published an official development roadmap on June 2, 2026, outlining their content plans for June through September 2026. This roadmap is the most transparent communication the studio has provided since launch, and it signals a major shift toward community-driven development. The roadmap covers four major content drops, each bringing significant new features, quality-of-life improvements, and story content to Crimson Desert.

The June update (version 1.10) has already launched and is covered in detail in our 1.10 Update Complete Guide. This article focuses on what is coming next — the July, August, and September updates — as well as long-term DLC expectations and community wishlist items.

① Roadmap Summary at a Glance

June (1.10): Flying dragon mount, cuckoo pet, mini-games, building rework. July (1.11): Cross-Save functionality, new combat content (weapon skill tree expansion), story chapter 8. August (1.12): New region "The Shattered Isles," naval combat expansion, co-op dungeons. September (1.13): First DLC "Echoes of the Abyss," new endgame raid, legendary equipment tier. Each update is free except the September DLC, which will be a paid expansion.

🔑 2. July Update (1.11) — Cross-Save & Combat Expansion

The July update, version 1.11, is focused on two major features: Cross-Save functionality and a significant combat content expansion. Cross-Save has been the most requested feature since launch, allowing players to share their save file between PC and console platforms. The combat expansion adds new weapon skill tree branches, giving players more build diversity and playstyle options.

② Cross-Save — How It Will Work

Cross-Save will allow you to link your Crimson Desert account across PC (Steam), PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S. Once linked, your save file syncs to the cloud and can be accessed from any platform. This means you can play on PC at home and continue your progress on a console while traveling. Cross-Save does not include cross-play — multiplayer is still platform-specific. However, the developers have confirmed that cross-play is being investigated for a future update. Cross-Save will be free for all players and will require a one-time account linking process through the Pearl Abyss website.

③ Weapon Skill Tree Expansion

Version 1.11 adds a third skill tree branch to each weapon type. Currently, each weapon has two branches: an offensive branch and a defensive branch. The third branch will be a "utility" branch that focuses on crowd control, mobility, and support abilities. For example, the sword's utility branch includes a taunt ability (draws enemy aggro), a dash strike (quick repositioning), and a guard break (bypasses enemy blocks). These utility skills are designed to expand tactical options without power-creeping existing builds — they are side-grades, not upgrades.

⛵ 3. August Update (1.12) — New Region & Naval Combat

The August update is the largest content drop since launch, introducing a brand-new region (The Shattered Isles), a naval combat expansion, and the first co-op dungeons. This update significantly expands the playable world and adds entirely new gameplay systems. If you have been waiting for more endgame content and multiplayer features, August is the update for you.

④ The Shattered Isles — New Region

The Shattered Isles is an archipelago region located off the eastern coast of Pywel. It consists of 7 major islands and numerous smaller ones, each with unique biomes, enemies, and resources. The islands are connected by water and can be explored by ship or flying mount. The region is designed for levels 40-60 and includes a new main story chapter, 15 side quests, 3 new world bosses, and 2 new dungeons. The visual design of the Shattered Isles is inspired by volcanic and tropical environments, a stark contrast to the desert and forest biomes of the base game.

⑤ Naval Combat Expansion

Naval combat is being expanded from the basic ship-to-ship cannon combat of the base game into a full naval warfare system. Players will be able to customize their ships with different cannon types, armor plating, and sail configurations. Ship-to-ship combat will include boarding mechanics, where you can grapple an enemy ship and fight its crew in melee combat. The Shattered Isles region will feature naval world events, including pirate attacks, sea monster encounters, and fleet battles. A new "Captain" skill tree will be added, with abilities that improve ship performance and crew effectiveness.

🎮 4. September DLC — Echoes of the Abyss

The September update brings the first paid DLC expansion for Crimson Desert: "Echoes of the Abyss." This expansion adds a new endgame raid, a legendary equipment tier, and a substantial story expansion that takes players into the Abyss itself. The DLC is priced at $24.99 and includes all content from the 1.13 update — players who do not purchase the DLC will still receive the 1.13 quality-of-life updates and balance changes, but will not have access to the new raid, equipment, or story content.

⑥ New Endgame Raid — The Abyssal Citadel

The Abyssal Citadel is a 6-player co-op raid designed for max-level characters with endgame equipment. The raid consists of 5 boss encounters, each with unique mechanics that require coordination and communication. The bosses are themed around the five elements (fire, water, earth, air, void), and each has a multi-phase encounter with enrage timers. The raid has a weekly lockout — you can attempt it as many times as you want, but loot is limited to once per week per boss. Completing the raid awards Legendary equipment materials, which are used to craft the new Legendary tier gear.

⑦ Legendary Equipment Tier

Legendary equipment is a new tier above Epic (purple). Legendary items have unique set bonuses that provide powerful gameplay-altering effects. For example, the "Abyss Walker" set (5 pieces) grants a 20% chance to ignore incoming damage and reflects 50% of blocked damage back to the attacker. Legendary items cannot be traded or sold — they are soulbound to the character who crafts them. The crafting materials drop exclusively from the Abyssal Citadel raid and the new Abyss story quests.

🤝 5. Co-op Features — What We Know So Far

Co-op has been promised since before launch, and the August update (1.12) will finally deliver the first co-op content. The developers have been careful to emphasize that Crimson Desert remains primarily a single-player experience, but co-op content will provide optional multiplayer activities for players who want to team up with friends.

⑧ Co-op Dungeons

The August update will introduce 3 co-op dungeons, each designed for 2-4 players. These dungeons are separate from the main story and offer unique rewards including exclusive cosmetic items, high-tier crafting materials, and titles. Co-op dungeons use matchmaking — you can queue solo and be matched with other players, or form a pre-made group with friends. The dungeons are designed to be completed in 20-30 minutes and have adjustable difficulty scaling based on party size.

⑨ Voice Chat and Social Features

Version 1.12 will also add in-game voice chat for co-op groups, a friends list system, and a guild system. Guilds can have up to 50 members and offer shared storage, guild quests, and guild-exclusive cosmetic rewards. The social features are designed to support the co-op content without turning Crimson Desert into an MMO — the core experience remains single-player, with co-op as an optional layer.

💬 6. Community Wishlist & Predictions

Beyond the official roadmap, the Crimson Desert community has been vocal about what they want to see in future updates. This section covers the most popular community requests and our predictions for which features are most likely to be added.

⑩ Most Requested Features

Based on Reddit, Discord, and official forum polls, the top community requests are: (1) Cross-play between PC and consoles (most requested, confirmed as "under investigation"), (2) New game+ mode with carry-over progress and increased difficulty, (3) Housing system with full interior customization, (4) More mount types including a water mount for ocean exploration, (5) Photo mode with filters and free camera, (6) Mod support for PC players, and (7) More story content featuring fan-favorite NPCs.

⑪ Our Predictions for 2026-2027

We predict that cross-play will be announced by the end of 2026, likely as part of a Q4 update. New game+ is a strong candidate for the October or November update, as it requires relatively little new content development. Housing is likely to come in early 2027 as part of a major content update. Photo mode is a low-effort, high-impact feature that could be added in any update — we expect it by September. Mod support is unlikely given Pearl Abyss's history with Black Desert Online, but the community can hope.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Is the September DLC required to keep playing?

No, the September DLC is optional. Players who do not purchase the DLC will still receive the 1.13 update with balance changes, quality-of-life improvements, and bug fixes. The DLC only gates the new raid, legendary equipment, and story content.

Will cross-save work between PC and console?

Yes, cross-save will work between PC (Steam), PlayStation 5, and Xbox Series X/S. You will need to link your accounts through the Pearl Abyss website. Your save file syncs to the cloud and can be accessed from any linked platform.

How much will the DLC cost?

The Echoes of the Abyss DLC is priced at $24.99. It includes the new endgame raid, legendary equipment tier, and a substantial story expansion. Players who own the base game can purchase the DLC separately.

Will co-op be available on all platforms?

Yes, co-op dungeons will be available on all platforms (PC, PS5, Xbox). However, cross-play is not included in the August update — you can only play with people on the same platform.

When will we see more story content?

July (1.11) adds story chapter 8, August (1.12) adds new story content in The Shattered Isles, and September (DLC) adds a substantial story expansion. Pearl Abyss has confirmed that the main story will continue to be expanded through 2027.
