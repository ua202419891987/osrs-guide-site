Crimson Desert FSR4 Rain Rendering Bug Fix (Flickering & Artifacts)

After the 1.1x update, many players on AMD RDNA4 and supported GPUs saw the screen flicker and shimmer with rainbow artifacts whenever it rained. This guide explains the FSR4 rain bug and gives you a step-by-step fix you can apply today on PC and console.

🌧️ Rendering Fix · Intermediate · 9 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 The bug: FSR4 upscaling corrupts the rain shader after the 1.1x update — flickering, rainbow shimmer, and artifacting in wet weather.
  • 📌 Fastest fix: temporarily disable FSR4 (or drop to Quality mode) until the rain shader is patched.
  • 📌 Best alternative: switch to FSR3 or XeSS — both render rain cleanly on most setups.
  • 📌 Also do: update your GPU driver and verify game files to rule out corrupt shaders.
  • 📌 Help the fix: report the issue to Pearl Abyss with your GPU, driver, and a screenshot/clip.

🌧️ What Is the FSR4 Rain Rendering Bug

Starting with the 1.1x update, Crimson Desert's upgrade to FSR4 (FidelityFX Super Resolution 4, AMD's RDNA4 machine-learning upscaler) exposed a conflict between the new upscaler and the game's rain particle and screen-space wetness shader. On affected GPUs, the moment the weather turns to rain the image starts to flicker, shimmer with colored artifacts, and tear at the edges of moving objects.

The bug is reproducible and tied to FSR4 specifically — not to your hardware failing. Players on Radeon RX 9000-series cards (and some 7000-series using the FSR4 fallback path) report it most, but a smaller number of console players on the unified renderer have seen similar shimmer during storms since 1.1x.

⚠️ How to Confirm It's This Bug

If the flickering only appears during rain or heavy wet weather and disappears the instant you disable FSR4, you are hitting the FSR4 rain shader bug — not a driver crash or a hardware fault.

🛑 Step 1 — Disable FSR4 Temporarily

The quickest and most reliable workaround is to turn FSR4 off until Pearl Abyss ships a shader fix. You won't lose the game — you simply render at native resolution (or a different upscaler).

① Open Graphics Settings

From the main menu or pause menu, go to Settings → Graphics → Upscaling.

② Set Upscaler to "Off" or "Native"

Select Off / Native to render at your display resolution. If FPS drops too far, jump to Step 2 and use FSR3 or XeSS instead.

③ If You Must Keep FSR4, Use Quality Mode

Some players find the bug is far milder at FSR4 Quality (less aggressive upscaling) than at Balanced/Performance. This is a partial mitigation, not a cure.

💡 Console Note

On PS5 / PS5 Pro the FSR4 path is fixed by the publisher. If you see rain shimmer on console, the only lever is lowering the upscaling/sharpness setting and waiting for the 1.1x follow-up patch.

🔄 Step 2 — Switch to FSR3 or XeSS

If native resolution tanks your frame rate, FSR3 and XeSS are the best drop-in replacements. Both render the rain shader correctly on the current build for the large majority of players, and both still give you a solid upscaling boost.

🖥️ How to Switch Upscalers (PC)

  1. In Settings → Graphics → Upscaling, change the engine from FSR4 to FSR3 or XeSS.
  2. Pick Balanced or Quality to keep image clarity high.
  3. Apply, then reload the area (fast-travel or re-enter) so the rain shader re-initializes.
  4. Test in a rainy zone (e.g. the coastal or highland regions during a storm) before long sessions.

Which Should You Pick?

FSR3 is ideal on AMD GPUs and keeps the familiar look. XeSS (Intel's AI upscaler) often looks sharper on NVIDIA and Intel Arc hardware and is a great fallback if FSR3 also shimmers on your machine.

💡 Frame Generation

If you use FSR3's frame generation, keep it on — it is unaffected by the rain bug. Only the FSR4 reconstruction path is the culprit.

📟 Step 3 — GPU Driver Fixes

Outdated or buggy GPU drivers can amplify shader artifacts. A clean driver update often reduces (and in some cases eliminates) the residual shimmer even on FSR3/XeSS.

① Update to the Latest Game-Ready Driver

Grab the newest driver from your vendor: AMD Adrenalin, NVIDIA Game Ready, or Intel Arc. Crimson Desert's 1.1x era saw several hot driver revisions that specifically improved FSR compatibility.

② Use a Clean Install

Pick the "Clean Install" / "Factory Reset" option in your driver installer. Leftover shader caches from the old driver can keep feeding corrupted rain data into FSR4.

③ Clear the Shader Cache

After the driver install, delete the game's shader cache folder (or use the "Clear Shader Cache" toggle in the launcher if present) so Crimson Desert rebuilds clean rain shaders on next launch.

For a full, vendor-by-vendor walkthrough of stable driver versions and clean-install steps, see our GPU Driver Guide 2026.

🗂️ Step 4 — Verify Game Files

A partially downloaded or corrupt 1.1x patch can leave the rain shader half-installed, which looks identical to the FSR4 bug. Rule this out before assuming it's purely the upscaler.

✅ Verify on Steam

  1. Open Steam → Library → Crimson Desert.
  2. Right-click → Properties → Installed Files.
  3. Click "Verify integrity of game files".
  4. Let it finish; if it re-downloads anything, restart and re-test in rain.

Platform Alternatives

On Epic / console, use the "Check for Updates" / "Repair" option in the client or system storage menu. A repair pass fixes a surprising number of "rendering glitch" reports that turn out to be corrupt assets.

💡 Still Flickering After Verify?

If file verification passes and the flicker remains only with FSR4, you've confirmed it's the upscaler-shader conflict — proceed to the report step so Pearl Abyss has your data point.

📨 Step 5 — Report to the Developers

Pearl Abyss prioritizes bugs with the most reproducible reports. A good report dramatically speeds up the shader fix.

① Gather the Essentials

Note your GPU model, driver version, in-game upscaler setting, and the exact 1.1x build number (shown on the main menu).

② Capture Proof

Take a screenshot or short clip of the rain flicker, ideally toggling FSR4 off and on to show the difference. Attach it to your report.

③ Submit Through Official Channels

Use the in-game Support → Report a Bug tool and the official Crimson Desert community/discord bug board. Mention "FSR4 rain rendering artifact — 1.1x" in the title so it groups with other reports.

🔔 Don't Use "Fix" Mods

Third-party shader mods that claim to patch FSR4 can corrupt your save or trip anti-tamper protection. Stick to official settings and wait for the patch.

❓ FAQs

Is my GPU dying if I see rainbow artifacts in rain?

Almost certainly not. The FSR4 rain bug is a software shader conflict introduced in 1.1x, reproducible across many GPUs. If the artifacts vanish the moment you disable FSR4, your hardware is fine.

Will switching to FSR3 lower my image quality a lot?

Only slightly. FSR3 is a spatial upscaler versus FSR4's ML path, so fine detail is a touch softer, but rain renders cleanly and you keep most of the FPS gain. Most players can't tell the difference in motion.

Does this bug affect console players too?

A small number of console players on the unified 1.1x renderer reported similar rain shimmer. Console has no upscaler toggle, so the only fixes are lowering sharpness/upscaling settings and waiting for Pearl Abyss's patch.

Will verifying game files alone fix the flicker?

Only if the rain shader was corrupt from a botched download. If files verify clean and the flicker persists with FSR4 on, it's the upscaler conflict — disable FSR4 or switch to FSR3/XeSS.

When will Pearl Abyss fix FSR4 rain?

No fixed date yet, but the bug is widely reported and tied to the 1.1x upscaler upgrade. Expect a shader correction in a follow-up 1.1x hotfix. Track the official news page and report your case to raise its priority.
