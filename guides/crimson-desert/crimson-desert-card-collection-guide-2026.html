ð 1. Card Collection Overview

Crimson Desert features a robust Card Collection system that goes hand-in-hand with the Duo and Five-Card mini-games. While the standard game uses a 20-card Seotda deck (numbers 1-10 in red and yellow), the collection system introduces special variant cards â?gilded, weathered, blood-stained, and illuminated versions â?each with unique visual designs and lore descriptions. Collecting all cards unlocks permanent account-wide bonuses and cosmetic rewards.

The collection is divided into 5 sets of 20 cards each (100 total cards), with cards obtained through three primary methods: winning games at card tables, trading with NPC collectors, and finding hidden cards in chests and quest rewards. As of Patch 1.12.02 (June 24, 2026), the collection system is fully functional with no known bugs preventing 100% completion.

Tip: Start your card collection early. Several cards in the Hernand set become permanently missable if you progress past certain story chapters without completing specific NPC requests. We'll flag these in each section.

ð The Save-Scum Method for Rare Cards

Just like with silver farming, the save-reload method works for rare card drops. Before playing a high-stakes match against a rare card NPC:

  1. Manual save outside the gambling location.
  2. Enter and play â?if the NPC doesn't offer the rare card as a reward, reload.
  3. Win the match â?if the card doesn't drop, reload and try again.
  4. Rare cards are rolled at match start â?if you reload and re-enter, the reward pool resets.

This method is most effective for the Black Market Dealer (Illuminated Red 1) and Huntmaster Voss (Blood-Stained Red 4), where card reward pools are randomised each match.

Once you have duplicates, the Card Exchange system lets you trade unwanted cards for missing ones. This is one of the most efficient ways to complete your collection without relying entirely on RNG.

How Card Exchange Works

The exchange system is accessible from any card table NPC by selecting "Exchange Cards" from their dialogue menu. The exchange operates on a simple tier system:

  2. Travel to the Stormtalon Ridge summit at midnight (in-game time).

Tip: The Crimson Jester fight is designed to be nearly impossible without the Cheat ability. If you haven't unlocked cheating yet (observe cheaters 3 times at any card table), do that before attempting. Use Red 1 as your hidden card and pray for a good draw.

â?FAQ â?Frequently Asked Questions

How many cards are there to collect in Crimson Desert?

100 cards across 5 sets (Standard, Gilded, Weathered, Blood-Stained, Illuminated), plus 1 hidden secret card (The Crimson Jester) for a total of 101 collectible cards.

Can I complete the card collection after finishing the main story?

Yes, with one major exception: Silas the Collector in Demeniss leaves permanently after Chapter 9. All other cards remain available in the post-game. Visit Silas before starting "The Shadow of the Throne" quest.

What's the fastest way to get Illuminated cards?

The fastest method is farming Blood-Stained duplicates at the Beighen Five-Card table, then using Premium Exchange (3 Blood-Stained â?1 random Illuminated). This yields roughly 1 Illuminated card per 15-20 minutes of focused farming.

Do card collection bonuses carry over to New Game+?

Yes. All set rewards (Lucky Gambler's Charm, Gilded Card Back, Veteran Gambler's Hat, Blood Debt Token, Seotda Master's Crown) are account-wide unlocks that carry into New Game+. Your collection progress also carries over.

Is there any way to get missable cards after the story?

If you missed Silas's Illuminated cards (IL4-10), the Card Exchange system still allows you to obtain them via Exalted Trade (3 Illuminated duplicates â?1 specific Illuminated card), but you'll need to farm Illuminated cards from other sources first.

Which card set should I prioritise for gameplay benefits?

Prioritise the Standard Deck first (+5% named combo chance is a massive advantage at card tables), then Blood-Stained Set (the Blood Debt Token is a free death save). The Illuminated Set's Seotda Master's Crown is the best reward but requires the most work.

Can I trade cards with other players?

No. Card collection is single-player only. There is no trading or gifting system between player accounts. All cards must be obtained through your own gameplay.

Does the Cheat ability help with card collection?

Absolutely. The Cheat ability is essential for the Crimson Jester fight and extremely useful for securing consecutive wins against high-stakes NPCs like Lord Rennard and Merchant Lord Kalev. Unlock cheating early (observe 3 cheater animations at any table) to speed up collection.
