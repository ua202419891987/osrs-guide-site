⛏️ 1. Resource Types & Why Gathering Matters in Crimson Desert

Crimson Desert has six primary resource categories that feed into its crafting and economy systems. Understanding which resources you need — and when — prevents the common beginner mistake of hoarding useless materials while lacking critical ones for crafting upgrades.

① The Economy Rule: Gather What You Can Sell, Not Just What You Need

New players often only gather materials they immediately need for a specific recipe. This is inefficient because it means multiple trips to the same zone for different materials. Instead, follow this principle: whenever you enter a zone with valuable resources, gather everything you pass regardless of whether you need it right now. Stockpile it. Sell surplus at city merchants. The cumulative silver from "gathering along the way" adds up to thousands of silver over time that you would otherwise leave on the ground.

🗺️ 2. Best Mining Routes by Zone (Ranked)

Mining nodes respawn approximately 10–12 minutes after being harvested. All mining requires a Pickaxe (basic version is free from any blacksmith). Here are the best routes ranked by silver-per-hour yield:

#1 — Valenport Iron Loop (Beginner-Friendly, ~450 silver/hr)

Route: Start at Valenport east gate → follow river north → mine 3 iron veins near waterfall → continue north to old quarry → mine 5 iron + 2 copper → circle back through forest path → 2 more iron veins near west gate → return to start.
Total nodes per loop: 12 (10 iron, 2 copper)
Loop time: ~8 minutes walking pace
Why #1 for beginners: Zero combat encounters on the loop path, nodes are close together (no wasted travel), and all ore sells reliably to Valenport merchant.

#2 — Kordat Highlands Silver Run (~720 silver/hr)

Route: Fast-travel to Kordat Shrine → head southeast toward Silver Ridge → mine 4 silver veins (high value!) → continue up ridge → 3 iron veins → descend into canyon → 2 rare gem nodes (random drop) → return via canyon floor (3 more iron) → fast-travel back.
Total nodes: 12 (5 iron, 4 silver, 3 gem)
Loop time:~11 minutes
Warning: Level 8–10 enemies patrol the canyon floor. Bring healing potions or be confident in combat.

#3 — Frostpeak Abyss Shards (~900+ silver/hr, Endgame)

Route: Frostpeak Village shrine → climb north slope → 2 Abyss Shard nodes (very rare, sell for 80 silver each) → ice cave entrance → 3 iron inside cave → exit south side → 2 more Abyss Shards on glacier ledge → return.
Total nodes: 7 (3 iron, 4 Abyss Shard)
Loop time:~13 minutes
Note: Requires cold resistance gear or Frost Elixir. Level 12–15 enemies in area. Not recommended before hour 15 of gameplay.

🌿 3. Best Foraging & Herb-Gathering Paths (Ranked)

Herbs and plants are gathered by approaching and pressing interact (△/Y/F). No tool required. They are faster to gather than ores (1 second vs 3 seconds for mining) but generally sell for less per unit. The real value of herbs is in potion crafting — a single Healroot Potion sells for 25 silver but costs only 5 silver of materials to craft if you gathered the herbs yourself.

⚡ 4. Gathering Efficiency Pro Tips

  • Tip 1 — Gather while en route: Never make a dedicated gathering trip unless you specifically need bulk materials. Instead, gather every node you pass between quest objectives. This "passive gathering" approach yields 60–70% of what a dedicated run produces with zero extra time investment.
  • Tip 2 — Upgrade your pickaxe first: The Iron Pickaxe (250 silver at any blacksmith) reduces mining time from 3 seconds to 2 seconds per node. That is a 33% speed improvement across all future mining. Buy it as soon as you can afford it — it pays for itself in under 3 mining loops.
  • Tip 3 — Know respawn timers: Mining nodes respawn in exactly 10 minutes (tested). If you do a full loop of a route in 8 minutes, wait 2 minutes at the start point, then repeat. This maximizes node availability on every loop.
  • Tip 4 — Sell at cities, not villages: City merchants (Valenport, Kordat) pay 20–30% more for raw materials than village merchants. Hoard your gathered materials until you reach a city, then sell everything at once for maximum profit.

❓ FAQ — Gathering Questions Answered

Q: Should I focus on gathering or combat for making money?

In the early game (hours 1–10), combat (killing enemies and looting bodies) generates silver faster than gathering. But gathering has two advantages: (1) it is zero-risk (no death, no potion consumption) and (2) the materials you gather save you money on crafting later. Our recommendation: combine both. Fight enemies on your main path, gather resources you pass along the way.

Q: Do resources ever run out permanently?

No. Every resource node in Crimson Desert respawns indefinitely. The game does not have finite resource pools (unlike some survival games). You can mine the same iron vein hundreds of times over hundreds of hours and it will always come back after 10 minutes.

Q: Is there a bag space issue with gathering too much?

Yes, this is the main limiting factor. Your base backpack holds 40 slots. A full mining loop can easily fill 15–20 slots. Solution: (1) Upgrade backpack capacity at city vendors (first upgrade = +10 slots for 200 silver — buy it ASAP). (2) Process raw materials into crafted items (which stack) rather than carrying raw ore (which doesn't stack beyond 99). (3) Make regular selling trips to clear inventory space.

Q: Which resource type should I prioritize for crafting my first armor upgrade?

The Iron Armor Set (your first meaningful armor upgrade) requires 15 Iron Ingots + 5 Leather Straps + 2 Silver Wire. You need iron ore (mine it), leather (hunt wolves/boars), and silver wire (buy from merchant or find as rare loot). Priority order: iron ore > animal parts > everything else. Focus on the iron mining loops in Section 2 above.
