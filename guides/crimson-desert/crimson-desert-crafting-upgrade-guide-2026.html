🔨 1. Crafting System Overview — How It Works

Crimson Desert's crafting system is one of the deepest and most rewarding in any action RPG. From basic weapon repairs to legendary equipment forging, the crafting system touches every aspect of your progression. Understanding how crafting works — the stations, the recipes, the material requirements, and the upgrade paths — will save you thousands of coins and give you access to equipment that cannot be purchased from any merchant.

Crafting in Crimson Desert is divided into four disciplines: Weaponsmithing (swords, axes, hammers), Armorsmithing (chest, legs, helm, gauntlets), Alchemy (potions, buffs, consumables), and Cooking (food, drinks, feasts). Each discipline has its own crafting station, recipe list, and material requirements. You can learn all four disciplines on a single character — there is no need to specialize.

① Crafting Stations and Where to Find Them

Crafting stations are available at two locations: your camp and the city blacksmith. At your camp, you can build a forge (for weaponsmithing and armorsmithing), an alchemy table (for alchemy), and a kitchen (for cooking). The city blacksmith in Uzkigrad and Pywel City has all stations available for public use, but crafting at a city station costs a 10% material surcharge. For regular crafting, build stations at your camp to avoid the surcharge. City stations are useful for one-off crafting when you are away from camp.

🗡️ 2. Weapon Forging — From Iron to Abyss

Weapon forging is the process of creating new weapons from raw materials. Each weapon type (sword, greatsword, axe, hammer, bow, etc.) has its own recipe tree, starting from basic iron weapons and progressing through steel, mithril, and Abyss tiers. Higher-tier weapons require rarer materials and higher crafting skill, but the stat improvements are significant — an Abyss-tier sword deals approximately 3x the damage of a basic iron sword.

② Weapon Tier Progression

Iron weapons (level 1-10): Require iron ore and wood. Base damage weapons, easily crafted. Steel weapons (level 10-25): Require steel bars (smelted from iron ore + coal) and leather. 50% more damage than iron. Mithril weapons (level 25-40): Require mithril ore (Highland Ridge) and gemstones. 100% more damage than iron. Abyss weapons (level 40-50+): Require Abyss Iron (Abyssal Depths mining) and Abyss Shards (Abyssal boss drops). 200% more damage than iron, with unique enchantments. Each tier also has variant weapons with different stat distributions — for example, a "heavy" variant trades attack speed for damage, while a "light" variant does the opposite.

③ Special Weapon Effects

Abyss-tier weapons have unique enchantments that cannot be found on lower-tier weapons. These enchantments include: Life Steal (heals you for 5% of damage dealt), Critical Surge (+15% critical hit chance after a successful parry), Void Damage (deals additional damage that ignores enemy armor), and Soul Reaver (10% chance to instantly execute enemies below 20% HP). Each Abyss weapon has one enchantment, chosen randomly when the weapon is crafted. You can re-roll the enchantment by reforging the weapon at the cost of additional Abyss Shards.

🛡️ 3. Armor Crafting — Defense & Resistance

Armor crafting follows a similar tier progression to weapon forging, but with additional complexity in resistance types. Each armor piece provides physical defense, elemental resistances (fire, water, earth, air, void), and sometimes utility stats like stamina regeneration or carry weight. Building a balanced armor set that covers your defensive needs is critical for surviving high-level content.

④ Armor Set Bonuses

Wearing multiple pieces from the same armor set activates set bonuses. A 2-piece set bonus provides a minor stat boost, a 4-piece set bonus provides a moderate effect, and a full 5-piece set bonus provides a powerful gameplay-altering effect. For example, the "Highland Warden" set (craftable at armorsmithing level 30) provides: 2-piece: +10% physical defense, 4-piece: +20% stamina regeneration, 5-piece: Reduces all incoming damage by 10% and increases movement speed by 5%. Set bonuses are a major factor in armor selection — a complete set of lower-tier armor with a good set bonus can outperform mismatched higher-tier pieces.

⑤ Resistance Strategy for Bosses

Different bosses deal different types of elemental damage, and having the right resistance can make a huge difference. The Void Colossus deals void damage — wear Void-resistant armor (crafted from Void Crystals). The Highland Drake deals fire damage — wear fire-resistant armor (crafted from Drake Scales). Keep two or three armor sets in your camp storage: one for physical defense (general use), one for fire resistance (drake fights), and one for void resistance (Abyss content). Swapping armor sets before a boss fight is one of the most effective ways to reduce damage taken.

⬆️ 4. Equipment Enhancement — Upgrade Paths

Enhancement is the process of upgrading existing equipment to higher levels, increasing its stats without replacing it. This is separate from crafting — you enhance weapons and armor you already own using enhancement materials and coins. Enhancement is essential for keeping your favorite gear viable as you progress through the game.

⑥ Enhancement Levels and Materials

Equipment can be enhanced from +0 to +15. Each enhancement level increases the item's base stats by approximately 5%. Enhancement requires enhancement stones (common drop from all enemies) and coins. The cost increases exponentially: +1 to +5 costs 100-500 coins per level, +6 to +10 costs 1,000-5,000 coins per level, +11 to +15 costs 10,000-50,000 coins per level. Enhancement has a 100% success rate up to +10. From +11 onward, there is a chance of failure — a failed enhancement reduces the item's level by 1 but does not destroy the item.

⑦ Enhancement Protection

To prevent the frustration of failed enhancements, you can use "Protection Scrolls" (rare drops from world bosses) which guarantee a successful enhancement without the risk of level loss. Protection Scrolls are consumed on use — you need one per enhancement attempt. Save your Protection Scrolls for +12 to +15 enhancements, where the failure rate is highest. Using a Protection Scroll on a +1 enhancement is a waste — the success rate is already 100% at low levels.

Pro Tip: Do not enhance weapons you plan to replace soon. Enhancement materials and coins are not refunded when you discard or sell an item. Only enhance weapons and armor that you intend to use for 10+ levels. A good rule of thumb: only enhance to +10 on your current tier, and save +11 to +15 enhancements for endgame gear.

🧪 5. Abyss Equipment — The Endgame Gear

Abyss equipment is the highest tier of gear in Crimson Desert, surpassing even enhanced Mithril gear in raw stats. Abyss equipment is crafted from materials found exclusively in the Abyssal Depths zone and from Abyssal boss drops. It is the ultimate goal for endgame players and is required for tackling the hardest content in the game, including the upcoming Abyssal Citadel raid.

⑧ Crafting Abyss Weapons

Abyss weapons require Abyss Iron (mined in the Abyssal Depths), Abyss Shards (dropped by Abyssal bosses), and Soul Gem fragments (found in Abyssal Depths treasure chests). The recipe for an Abyss sword is: 20 Abyss Iron, 10 Abyss Shards, 5 Soul Gem fragments, and 50,000 coins. The crafting must be done at the Abyssal Forge, a special crafting station located deep in the Abyssal Depths zone. Crafting an Abyss weapon also requires Weaponsmithing level 50 (max), which takes significant time to achieve through regular crafting.

⑨ Abyss Armor Set

The "Abyss Walker" armor set is the defensive counterpart to Abyss weapons. The full set (5 pieces) requires: 50 Abyss Iron, 25 Abyss Shards, 10 Soul Gem fragments, and 150,000 coins. The set bonuses are: 2-piece: +15% physical defense, 4-piece: +25% void resistance, 5-piece: 20% chance to ignore incoming damage and reflect 50% of blocked damage. This set is the best all-around endgame armor and is highly recommended for any player tackling Abyssal content. The reflect effect is particularly powerful against fast-attacking enemies, as it effectively turns their own damage against them.

📋 6. Crafting Priority & Tips

With so many crafting options and limited materials, it can be difficult to know where to invest your resources. This section provides a clear crafting priority guide to help you make efficient use of your materials and coins at every stage of the game.

⑩ Early Game (Level 1-25) Priority

Focus on crafting steel weapons and armor as soon as you can. Do not waste materials on iron gear — you will outgrow it within hours. Prioritize a steel sword (or your preferred weapon type) and a full set of steel armor. Cook hearty stews and stamina soups for combat buffs. Do not enhance anything above +5 at this stage — you will replace this gear soon. Save your enhancement stones and coins for later.

⑪ Mid Game (Level 25-40) Priority

Upgrade to mithril weapons and armor. Craft a full mithril armor set to activate the set bonus. Begin enhancing your mithril weapon to +7 or +8. Start stockpiling Abyss Iron and Abyss Shards from any Abyssal Depths mining trips and boss fights. Do not craft Abyss gear yet — you need to be level 50 to equip it. Instead, store the materials and level your crafting skills to 50.

⑫ Endgame (Level 40+) Priority

Craft your Abyss weapon first — the damage increase is the most impactful single upgrade you can make. Then craft Abyss armor pieces one at a time, prioritizing the chest piece (highest defense) and the helm (often has the best enchantments). Enhance your Abyss weapon to +12 as soon as you can afford it. Save Protection Scrolls for +13 to +15 enhancements. Once your weapon is at +12, shift focus to enhancing your armor to +10 for survivability. The goal is a full Abyss set with a +12 weapon and +10 armor — this is the standard loadout for endgame content.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best weapon type to craft?

There is no universally best weapon type — it depends on your playstyle. Swords offer balanced offense and defense. Greatswords deal the highest burst damage but are slow. Axes have good damage and stagger potential. Bows are ideal for ranged players. The best approach is to try each weapon type early in the game and craft steel versions of the two you like best.

Can I craft legendary equipment?

Legendary equipment will be introduced in the September DLC Echoes of the Abyss. It requires materials from the Abyssal Citadel raid and cannot be crafted without the DLC. Legendary items have unique set bonuses that are significantly more powerful than Abyss set bonuses.

What happens if an enhancement fails?

From +11 onward, a failed enhancement reduces the item's level by 1 (e.g., +11 becomes +10). The item is not destroyed. You can prevent level loss by using a Protection Scroll, which guarantees success but is consumed on use.

Is it better to craft or buy equipment?

Crafting is almost always better than buying. Merchant-sold equipment is 2-3 tiers behind crafted equipment at the same level, and it costs significantly more coins. The only exception is early game (levels 1-10), where buying basic iron gear from merchants is faster than gathering materials and crafting.

How do I level up my crafting skill quickly?

The fastest way to level crafting is to craft items in bulk using cheap materials. For weaponsmithing, craft iron swords repeatedly (they only require iron ore and wood). For armorsmithing, craft iron chest pieces. Each crafted item grants crafting XP, and the XP requirement per level scales linearly, so consistent bulk crafting is the most efficient approach.
