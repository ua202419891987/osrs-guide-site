🎣 1. Fishing Overview — Why It Is Different in Crimson Desert

Fishing in Crimson Desert is one of the most overlooked life skills, and the game does a poor job of explaining how it works. Unlike most RPGs where fishing is a simple "press button when prompt appears" affair, Crimson Desert's fishing system has a unique quirk: the fishing rod occupies your bow/ranged weapon equipment slot. This means you cannot fish while your bow is equipped, and you cannot use ranged weapons while your rod is equipped. This single design choice confuses almost every new player who tries to fish for the first time.

The community FAQ at AskPywel confirms this is one of the most frequently asked questions — players report being unable to fish because they do not realize they need to unequip their bow first. Once you understand this, fishing becomes a relaxing and profitable side activity that provides ingredients for some of the best buff foods in the game.

📍 2. Where to Get the Fishing Rod

The fishing rod is not a craftable item or a quest reward — it must be purchased from a Provisioner vendor. There are Provisioners in every major settlement, but the earliest one you will encounter is in Uzkigrad, the first major city you visit in Chapter 2.

1

Buy the Basic Fishing Rod

Visit the Provisioner shop in Uzkigrad (look for the fish icon on your minimap, located in the merchant district near the market square). The Basic Fishing Rod costs 500 silver. This is the only rod you need for the first 10+ hours of fishing — upgraded rods improve catch speed but do not unlock new fish types.

2

Equip the Rod

Open your inventory, go to the Equipment tab, and equip the fishing rod in your ranged weapon slot. A prompt will appear warning you that your bow will be unequipped — confirm this. You can swap back to your bow at any time by re-equipping it from the inventory, but you cannot have both equipped simultaneously.

Warning: If you enter combat while your fishing rod is equipped, you will not be able to use ranged attacks. Always swap back to your bow before exploring dangerous areas. The game does not auto-swap weapons when combat begins.

🎯 3. Fishing Mechanics — The Mini-Game Explained

Once you are standing near water with your rod equipped, press the interact button (Triangle/Y/E) to cast. The fishing mini-game has three phases: Cast, Wait, and Reel. Each phase requires different actions.

Phase 1: Cast

A power meter appears when you press the cast button. Hold to increase cast distance, release at the optimal zone (highlighted in green). Casting farther does not increase fish quality — it only changes which fish pool you are accessing. For most spots, a medium cast (50-60% power) is ideal.

Phase 2: Wait

After the bobber lands, wait. This takes 5-20 seconds depending on the spot. When a fish bites, the bobber dips and your controller vibrates. You have a 1.5-second window to press the interact button — miss it and the fish escapes.

Phase 3: Reel

This is where most players struggle. The reel mini-game displays a moving marker on a tension bar. You must hold the interact button to reel in, but release when the tension enters the red zone. The fish will pull in random directions — when it pulls left, tap left on your stick to reduce tension. When it pulls right, tap right. The key rhythm is: hold to reel → release when red → tap direction → hold to reel → repeat.

Pro Tip: The tension bar's red zone shrinks based on fish rarity. Common fish have a wide green zone (easy). Rare fish have a razor-thin green zone. If you are consistently losing rare fish, upgrade to the Reinforced Rod — its -10% escape rate effectively widens the green zone.

Best Money-Making Route: The Uzkigrad Loop

Start at the Uzkigrad River East Bank and fish for 20 minutes. You will catch primarily Glowing Catfish (used in Spirit-Infused Stew — a high-demand buff food). Run to the Uzkigrad market and sell surplus fish to the fishmonger NPC. Run back to the river and repeat. This loop earns approximately 1,000 silver per hour and provides cooking ingredients worth another 500 silver in saved recipe costs. It is the best low-risk, low-level money-making method in the game.

❓ FAQ — Fishing Questions Answered

Q: Why can I not equip the fishing rod?

The fishing rod goes in your bow/ranged weapon slot. If you currently have a bow equipped, the game will not let you equip the rod until you manually unequip the bow first. Open your equipment menu, remove the bow, then equip the rod.

Q: Can I fight while my fishing rod is equipped?

You can fight with melee weapons, but you lose access to all ranged/bow attacks. The rod itself cannot be used as a weapon. Always swap back to your bow before entering dangerous areas.

Q: Do I need to unlock fishing through a quest?

No. Fishing is available as soon as you reach Chapter 2 and can access a Provisioner shop. There is no tutorial quest — you simply buy the rod and start fishing. The game does not explain the mechanics, which is why many players do not discover fishing until much later.

Q: My fish are stuck and I cannot pick them up — is this a bug?

Yes. This is a known bug affecting certain fishing zones (particularly the Ruined Garrison Moat during rainy weather). Save your game and reload — the caught fish will transfer to your inventory after the reload. Pearl Abyss has confirmed a fix is in development.

Q: Is the Abyss-Touched Rod worth 8,000 silver?

Only if you plan to fish at the Spire of Frost Abyssal Pool for Abyssal Leviathan. The Abyssal Sashimi buff (+30% Abyss Artifact drop rate) is extremely powerful for endgame farming. If you are not at endgame yet, the Reinforced Rod (2,500 silver) is the better investment — the catch speed improvement applies everywhere.
