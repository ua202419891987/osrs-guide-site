📋 1. 1.11 Update Overview — What Changed in June 2026

Pearl Abyss released Crimson Desert version 1.11.00 on June 12, 2026, following the massive 1.10 content drop. While 1.11 is a smaller patch by comparison, it delivers meaningful quality-of-life improvements, important combat adjustments, and over 40 bug fixes that address community pain points. This guide breaks down every change in the patch, from the expanded pet registration system to the revamped pinball mini-game physics.

Version 1.11.00 went live across all platforms simultaneously — Steam (PC/Mac), PlayStation, Xbox, Epic Games Store, and Mac App Store. The download is approximately 3.2 GB on PC. No new paid content was introduced; this patch focuses entirely on refinement and fixes. For a look at what came next, see our 1.12 Update Guide, and if you are experiencing stability issues, visit our Frame Gen Crash Fix Guide.

Patch Highlights at a Glance

  • Pet Registration Expansion — Register up to 100 pets, summon up to 50 at camp
  • Shopkeeper Equipment Resale — Lost rare equipment can now be repurchased within 7 days
  • Pinball Mini-Game Rework — Physics overhaul, wall-clipping fixes, and repositioned bumpers
  • Damiane & Oongka Skill Fixes — Focus skill spirit recovery now scales correctly
  • Controller Key Customization — Bind inventory, map, skills, journal, and photo mode buttons
  • New Items & Equipment — Hernand shops stock new bows and flower baskets
Pro Tip: If you completed pet-related challenges before 1.11, check your mail — Pearl Abyss retroactively granted reward items for all previously completed pet challenges.

⚔️ 2. Combat Changes — Skill Adjustments & Balance

The 1.11 update includes targeted combat adjustments affecting the companion characters Damiane and Oongka, alongside weapon-specific fixes that improve combat reliability across all characters.

Damiane & Oongka — Focus Skill Spirit Fix

A significant bug affecting Damiane and Oongka has been corrected: the "Focus" skill upgrade was not properly increasing spirit recovery amounts. Players who invested skill points into this upgrade were receiving no benefit. After the 1.11 fix, spirit recovery now scales correctly with each upgrade level, making sustained combat with these characters far more viable.

Special Arrow Effects Fixed

Two key skills — Explosive Evasion Shot and Multi-Shot — were failing to trigger their additional effects when special arrows were equipped. The 1.11 update corrects this, meaning status effects, elemental damage, and utility effects from special arrows now apply correctly during these skills. This is a meaningful DPS increase for bow-focused builds.

Dragon Mount Combat Tuning

The flying dragon mount's land-based attacks were consuming excessive stamina, making ground combat with the dragon impractical. The 1.11 patch reduces the stamina cost of dragon land attacks to reasonable levels. Additionally, the dragon can now be mounted using "Axiom Force" without requiring the "Back Hang" skill, making aerial combat more accessible for newer players.

Combat Tip: With the special arrow fix, consider crafting Explosive Arrows for Multi-Shot — the AoE damage now applies correctly and significantly improves crowd clearing efficiency.

🎯 3. Boss Adjustments — Puppet Monster & Enemy AI

Version 1.11 adjusts the attack patterns of Puppet-type monsters, a category that includes several mid-game and late-game enemies. While specific numerical changes were not detailed in the official patch notes, players have reported (and data mining confirms) the following adjustments:

  • Attack Telegraph Timing — Puppet monsters now have slightly longer wind-up animations before their heavy attacks, giving players more reaction time
  • Pattern Variety — Some Puppet monsters have had new attack sequences added to their rotation, preventing repetitive cheese strategies
  • Hitbox Refinement — Several oversized hitboxes on Puppet monster sweeping attacks have been tightened, reducing frustration from attacks that visually miss but still connect

These changes bring Puppet monster encounters more in line with the design philosophy seen in 1.10's boss adjustments — harder to exploit, but fairer when fought properly.

Heads Up: While Puppet monster telegraphs are longer, their new attack patterns can catch you off guard. Do not assume you know the full moveset even if you have fought these enemies before.

✨ 4. New Features — Pet Expansion, Shop Resale & Pinball Rework

Pet Registration Expansion — 100 Pets Max

The single biggest feature in 1.11 is the pet registration expansion. Previously capped at a much lower limit, players can now register up to 100 pets total, though only 50 can be summoned at camp simultaneously. This is excellent news for pet collectors and players who use different pets for different activities (combat buffs, resource gathering, cosmetic preference).

Pet challenge rewards are now retroactively granted. If you achieved the requirements for any pet-related challenge before 1.11, check your in-game mail — Pearl Abyss distributed all outstanding rewards with the patch. A new map icon was also added specifically for the baby dragon pet, making it easier to locate.

Shopkeeper Equipment Resale — Lost Gear Recovery

One of the most requested features: rare equipment obtained from chests, quests, and other sources that is subsequently lost can now be repurchased from shopkeepers at a higher price. This item resale inventory persists for 7 days after the item is lost, giving you a generous window to recover your gear. The price is elevated compared to the original acquisition cost, but it is still far cheaper than crafting or farming a replacement from scratch.

Pinball Mini-Game Rework

The pinball mini-game introduced in 1.10 received a substantial physics overhaul. Community feedback highlighted several issues: balls clipping through walls, getting stuck in corners, and inconsistent launch behavior. The 1.11 rework addresses all of these:

  • Ball physics are now heavier and more stable — less floaty behavior
  • Reduced instances of balls passing through wall geometry
  • Bumpers repositioned, some fixed (non-movable) bumpers removed for cleaner flow
  • Fixed the ball getting stuck in the top-left corner area
  • Adjusted launcher strength and angle for more consistent launches
  • Fixed balls falling off the launcher tray before being launched
Pinball Tip: With the physics rework, the high-score strategies from 1.10 may no longer work. Experiment with aiming for the center bumpers first — the new heavier ball physics make chain combos more predictable.

⚠️ 6. Known Issues — What Pearl Abyss Is Still Working On

While 1.11 resolved dozens of issues, several known problems remain on Pearl Abyss's radar. These are tracked on the official Known Issues page:

  • AMD Driver Crashes — Game crashes on AMD GPU driver version 26.6.2 or higher. Downgrading to 26.6.1 or earlier is recommended. AMD is actively investigating
  • GTX 1060 White Screen — Enabling FSR Upscaling + Frame Generation simultaneously on GTX 1060 causes a white screen. Avoid this combination on this GPU
  • FSR4 Rain Visual Bug — Rain disappears or appears distorted when FSR4 upscaling is active
  • Damiane Shield Throw Bug — If Damiane dies immediately after using Shield Throw, she loses access to her shield and related skills (workaround: save and reload)
  • Mac Cursor Offset — Cursor misalignment at certain resolutions on Mac
  • Greymane Faction Quest Block — Damiane and Oongka cannot progress Greymane faction quests (workaround: switch characters)
Important: The AMD driver crash (versions 26.6.2+) affects all RX 5000, 6000, and 7000 series cards. If you experience crashes, roll back to Adrenalin 26.6.1 until AMD or Pearl Abyss releases a permanent fix.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

How do I access the new pet registration expansion?

The pet registration cap increase is automatic after updating to 1.11. No quest or NPC interaction required. You can register up to 100 pets from the pet menu immediately.

What happens if I miss the 7-day window for shopkeeper resale?

If an item's resale window expires, it rotates out of the shopkeeper's inventory permanently for that instance of loss. You will need to obtain the item through its original method again. Set a reminder if you lose something valuable.

Are the pinball high score rewards still the same after the rework?

Yes, the reward thresholds and items remain unchanged. The 50,000-point golden weapon skin is still available. However, the physics changes mean high-score strategies have shifted — expect a period of adjustment.

Does 1.11 fix the AMD driver crash issue completely?

No. The AMD driver crash (versions 26.6.2+) is a separate driver-level issue that Pearl Abyss and AMD are investigating. Patch 1.11 does not resolve it. Roll back to driver 26.6.1 as a temporary workaround.

Can Damiane and Oongka now wear the Sigil of Valor?

No. The Sigil of Valor equipment restriction for Iron Eagle and Phoenix was mentioned in the patch notes but was subsequently removed from the game on June 12, 2026. It remains unavailable.

Is there a performance difference between platforms in 1.11?

PC and PlayStation 5 Pro see the most benefit from the optimization changes. Xbox Series S performance remains largely unchanged due to its hardware limitations. Mac players should see improved loading times.

Will there be a 1.11.01 hotfix?

Pearl Abyss has not announced a 1.11.01 hotfix. Development focus has shifted to the 1.12 update cycle. Critical issues like the AMD crash are being tracked separately.

How do I claim retroactive pet challenge rewards?

Check your in-game mail (the envelope icon in the main menu). All retroactive rewards were distributed during the 1.11 patch deployment. If you cannot find them, contact Pearl Abyss support.
