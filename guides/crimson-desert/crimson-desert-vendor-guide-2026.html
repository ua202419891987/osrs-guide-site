Crimson Desert What to Keep vs Sell Guide — Inventory Management 2026

Master your inventory in Crimson Desert — a complete reference on what to keep, what to sell, which crafting materials matter, gear to salvage, and how to maximize vendor profits in Pywel.

Crimson Desert uses a slot-based inventory system — every item, from a tiny herb to a massive greatsword, takes up exactly one slot. With only 24 inventory slots at the start (expandable to 40+ through bags and quests), every slot counts. Knowing what to keep, what to sell, and what to salvage is not just about making money — it is about being able to actually pick up valuable loot without having to drop things. This guide gives you a complete reference for inventory decision-making throughout your journey in Pywel. For early-game earning strategies, check our Beginner Money Guide.

Golden Rule of CD Inventory: If you are unsure whether to keep or sell something, keep it — but only until you return to a town. Store anything questionable in your camp chest. You can always come back and sell it later. You can never unsell something you regret selling (without the recovery methods in our Lost Items Guide).

🔨 2. Crafting Materials to Keep

Crafting materials are the backbone of Crimson Desert's upgrade and crafting systems. Selling them early for quick cash is tempting, but it will cost you 3-5x more to buy them back later when you actually need them for upgrades. Here is the priority list for crafting materials.

Tier 1: Absolutely Keep (Never Sell)

⛏️ Gold Ore

Gold Ore is the most valuable crafting material in the game because it is the primary ingredient for Gold Bars (worth 500 silver each at the bank). With the Gold Bar recipe (3 Gold Ore + 1 Golden Apple = 1 Gold Bar), each Gold Ore is effectively worth ~165 silver when crafted. Selling raw Gold Ore for ~3 silver each is throwing away 98% of its value. Always keep Gold Ore and craft it into bars.

💎 Upgrade Stones

Weapon and armor upgrade stones are the rarest and most essential crafting materials in the game. They are required for every equipment upgrade beyond +1. Vendor availability is limited, and the price increases with each tier. Never sell upgrade stones. If you have excess lower-tier stones, save them for future upgrades rather than selling them for a few silver.

🌀 Abyss Cores & Abyss Artifacts

Abyss Cores attach to equipment to provide stat bonuses and special effects. They are the primary way to customize your build. Faded Abyss Artifacts (available from Elowen the witch in Hernand) allow you to reset your skill tree. Both of these item types are scarce and essential for endgame character development. Do not sell them under any circumstances.

Tier 2: Keep Until You Have Surplus

🪵 Wood, Leather, Cloth

These basic materials are used in many early and mid-game crafts. Wood is used for housing (Patch 1.12 introduced 58 new outdoor decoration recipes). Leather can be crafted into armor that sells for 3-4x the raw material value. Cloth is used in multiple recipes. Keep the first 30-50 of each, and only sell surpluses beyond that.

💊 Herbs (Glowroot, Sunpetal, Red Bacca)

Herbs are used for cooking, alchemy, and some quests. Red Bacca Flowers in particular are worth 5-15 silver each and grow densely in specific areas (check the fields southwest of Hernand). However, some herbs are also used in powerful cooking recipes. Keep a stack of 20 for cooking, sell the rest to an alchemist for the best prices.

Tier 3: Sell Freely

🦴 Bones, Common Gems, Excess Meat

Bones have very low demand and minimal crafting use. Common gems (diamonds, sapphires) sell well to collector NPCs but are not used in essential crafts. Extra meat (beyond what you need for cooking) is worth selling — raw meat spoils (if that mechanic exists) or just takes up space. These items are inventory filler and should be sold first when you need space.

📜 3. Quest Items to Hold

Quest items fall into three categories: temporary, learn-and-release, and permanent. Understanding which is which will save you from both inventory clutter and permanent loss.

Temporary Quest Items (Sell After Completion)

These items are only useful during the quest and become vendor trash afterward. Examples include: delivery packages, specific quest keys (not story keys), investigation notes, and witness statements. After the quest is turned in, you can safely sell these items. The game will warn you before selling some of them, but not all.

Learn-and-Release Items (Sell After Reading)

Books, recipes, blueprints, and bounty posters all work the same way: you read/learn them, the content is permanently added to your character, and the physical item becomes useless. Sell these immediately after learning. A learned recipe takes zero inventory space, while the physical book takes one slot. Some rare recipes sell for 5-15 silver to the right NPC.

Pro Tip: Before selling a book or recipe, check whether the NPC vendor has a "Books" or "Knowledge" tab. Some vendors pay premium prices for learned materials. For example, the alchemist in Kalet pays 20-30% more for recipe books than general goods merchants.

Permanent Quest Items (Keep Forever)

These items are yours permanently and have ongoing uses. Examples include: the Thieves' Mask (used for stealing throughout the entire game), special tools (Rhett's Pickaxe, fishing rod), story keys that open lore-relevant areas, and unique weapons obtained from quest completion. Never sell these items. If you are tight on inventory space, store them in your camp chest rather than selling them.

⚔️ 4. Gear to Salvage vs Sell

When you find duplicate or inferior gear, you have three options: equip it (if it is an upgrade), sell it to a vendor, or salvage it for materials. Each option has different benefits depending on your current needs.

Common (White) and Uncommon (Green) Gear

Sell these to any vendor. Common gear sells for 5-15 silver, and uncommon gear sells for 15-35 silver. The salvage value is minimal — you get 1-2 basic materials (cloth, leather, or iron scrap) that are worth less than the sale price. Always sell common and uncommon gear unless you are desperately short on low-tier crafting materials.

Rare (Blue) Gear

Rare gear is where the sell vs salvage decision becomes interesting. Rare gear sells for 50-150 silver to vendors, but salvaging it yields mid-tier materials (Refined Leather, Steel Ingot, Fine Cloth) that are used in +3 to +5 upgrades. If you are actively upgrading gear, salvage rare gear for the materials. If your upgrades are on hold, sell the gear and bank the silver.

Epic (Purple) and Legendary (Gold) Gear

This is the trickiest category. Epic gear sells for 200-800+ silver, and Legendary gear can sell for 1000+ silver. However, the salvage materials from high-tier gear are extremely valuable and may be difficult to obtain otherwise. The general rule: salvage Epic gear if you are actively upgrading towards endgame, sell it if you are saving up for a specific item from a vendor. For Legendary gear: never sell or salvage unique Legendary items. For duplicate Legendary items, check the player marketplace — players will often pay 2-3x vendor price for high-end gear.

📍 Where to Sell Gear for Best Prices

Weapons & Armor: Blacksmith in Kalet pays 15-20% more than Uzkigrad vendors. If Kalet is not yet unlocked, use the Uzkigrad blacksmith — but avoid selling weapons to general goods merchants, who pay 20-30% less.

Accessories: The collector NPC (gem icon on the map, available in Kalet and Uzkigrad) pays 2-3x the standard vendor price for rare jewelry and accessories.

Magic Items: The witch/wizard NPCs (Elowen in Hernand, other magic vendors) pay premium for magical gear and staves.

🍖 5. Consumables Priority

Consumables take up inventory space but are essential for survival and efficiency. Here is your priority system for managing consumables in your limited bag space.

Essential Carry (Always Keep 5-10 Slots)

Healing Potions: Keep at least 5 healing potions in your inventory at all times. In combat, you cannot afford to be searching through menus for heals. Use the "Consumable Hotbar" feature to bind your most-used heals for quick access.

Cooked Meals: Cooked food is the most efficient healing source in Crimson Desert. Keep 3-5 cooked meals in your inventory. Unlike potions, meals provide buffs in addition to healing.

Anti-Venom: If you are farming areas with poisonous creatures (marsh vipers, certain spiders), keep 2-3 anti-venom on hand. Dying to poison is embarrassing and costly.

Nice to Have (Keep 2-3 Slots)

Buff Potions: Strength, Defense, and Speed potions are situational but powerful. Carry 1-2 of each if you have room. Save them for boss fights and difficult encounters.

Throwing Knives / Arrows: If you use a ranged weapon, keep your ammo stocked. Ammo stacks are efficient (stacks of 50+ take 1 slot).

Bandages / Field Dressings: Used to heal bleed damage. Not essential for every area, but vital in zones with enemies that cause bleed.

Safe to Keep in Storage (Not Inventory)

Excess raw ingredients: Store in camp chest. Only carry what you need for the current expedition.

Rare event consumables: If you have limited-time consumables from events, store them. They may become valuable later or be useful in a specific context.

Crafting materials: Store everything except what you need for immediate upgrades. Your inventory is for active use — your storage chest is for long-term holding.

Pro Tip: Develop a consistent inventory layout. For example: Slots 1-3 = healing potions, Slots 4-5 = cooked meals, Slot 6 = buff potions, Slots 7-10 = current mission items, Slots 11-24 = loot. Muscle memory for your inventory layout saves precious seconds during combat and makes selling/restocking much faster.

🏪 6. Vendor Prices Guide

Not all vendors pay the same prices for your items. Crimson Desert has a surprisingly complex vendor economy where prices vary by region, NPC type, and even the time of day (some vendors offer better prices in the morning). Here is your guide to maximizing silver from every sale.

Vendor Type Price Multipliers

Item Type Best Vendor Premium Avoid Selling To
Weapons & Armor Blacksmith (Kalet) +15-20% General Goods merchants (-20-30%)
Magic Items / Staves Witch NPCs (Elowen) +15-25% General Goods
Herbs & Alchemy Mats Alchemist (any major city) +10-15% General Goods, Tavern
Food & Ingredients Tavern Keeper (Uzkigrad) +5-10% Blacksmith
Gems & Jewelry Collector NPC (Kalet/Uzkigrad) +100-200% Any other vendor
Gold Bars Banker Grover (Hernand) Fixed 500 silver Normal vendors pay nothing
Books & Recipes Alchemist / Scholar NPCs +10-20% General Goods
Trading Goods Goldleaf Trading Post / Black Market Variable Normal vendors (low prices)

Regional Price Differences

Beyond vendor type, the city or region affects prices. Kalet is generally the best city for selling most item types. Hernand is good for early-game selling. Uzkigrad is convenient but pays slightly less. The southern trade cities (accessible mid-game onwards) offer premium prices for rare goods. As a rule of thumb: the further you travel from your starting region, the better prices you will find for your high-value items.

Vendor Gold Limits and Refresh Timers

Each vendor has a limited amount of gold they can spend on buying items from you. If you sell too many high-value items to the same vendor, they will run out of gold and stop buying (or offer reduced prices). Vendor gold refreshes every in-game day. If you have lots of items to sell, rotate between vendors in different cities rather than dumping everything on one merchant.

Warning: Vendors also have a "supply" mechanic — if you sell dozens of identical items to the same vendor, their buy price for that item decreases. This is the game's way of simulating supply and demand. Rotate your sales across multiple vendors to maintain high selling prices.

The Player Marketplace

For rare and high-end items, the player marketplace (accessible in major cities) offers much better prices than any vendor. There is a 10% listing fee and a delay before items sell, but items that vendor for 100 silver can sell for 300-1000+ silver to other players. Use the marketplace for: rare crafting materials, Epic+ gear, cosmetic items, and unique items that vendors undervalue. Use NPC vendors for common/uncommon items and anything worth less than 100 silver (where marketplace fees eat too much profit).

❓ FAQ

Should I sell Gold Ore or save it for crafting?

Always save Gold Ore for crafting Gold Bars. Raw Gold Ore sells for ~3 silver each, but 3 Gold Ore + 1 Golden Apple crafts into a Gold Bar worth 500 silver at Banker Grover in Hernand. That is ~165 silver per ore — a 55x value increase. Never sell raw Gold Ore.

What is the first item I should buy with my silver?

Your first purchase should be a Small Bag from any general goods merchant. It costs only a few copper but adds 2-4 inventory slots. More inventory space means more loot per farming run, which means more silver per hour. The bag pays for itself within minutes.

Can I sell quest items after completing the quest?

Yes, most quest items are safe to sell after you complete the associated quest. However, unique weapon and armor rewards from quests should never be sold — they cannot be re-obtained. A good rule: if the quest item has a unique name and gives stats, keep it. If it is a document, key (non-story), or delivery item, sell it after completion.

Are there any items I should buy from vendors and resell for profit?

Yes. At low levels, check for recipes and blueprints that sell for more in other regions. At mid-game, the trading system allows you to buy goods from one Goldleaf Trading Post and sell them at another for profit. Some rare items (like the Box of Fortune from Patch 1.12's Mysterious Shop for 100 silver) can contain items worth much more, though the contents are random.

How do I expand my inventory beyond the starting 24 slots?

You can expand inventory by: (1) purchasing Small and Medium Bags from vendors, (2) completing specific Hernand side quests that reward bag slots, (3) finding rare bag items in chests and dungeons, (4) progressing through the main story (some chapters reward inventory expansions). Aim for 35-40 slots by mid-game.

Is it worth repairing damaged equipment or should I just sell it?

If the equipment is common or uncommon, sell it damaged — the repair cost exceeds the value increase. If the equipment is rare or better, check the repair cost at a blacksmith. If the repair costs less than 50% of the item's vendor value, repair then sell. If repair costs more, sell as-is.

What is the best vendor for selling collected herbs?

The alchemist in any major city pays 10-15% above base price. The alchemist in Kalet offers the best rates. Avoid selling herbs to general goods merchants or the tavern keeper — they pay significantly less. For rare herbs, check if the alchemist has a "Daily Special" for certain herb types — they sometimes offer double price for specific ingredients.

Should I keep duplicate unique items?

Duplicate unique items are extremely rare, but if you find one, keep it. You can use duplicates for: (1) upgrading one copy while keeping the other as a backup, (2) equipping different characters with the same item type, (3) selling on the player marketplace (players pay top silver for uniques). Never vendor a duplicate unique item — the marketplace will get you 3-5x the vendor price.
