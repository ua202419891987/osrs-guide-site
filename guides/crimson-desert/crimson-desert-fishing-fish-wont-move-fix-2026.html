Crimson Desert Fish Won't Move or Bite? Fishing Fix (2026)

🔧 Technical & Fixes

You cast, the bobber drops, and then… nothing. The fish just sit there frozen and never bite — and waiting longer does nothing. It's a known fishing mini-game glitch, not a broken save. Here are three workarounds you can try right now, plus how to report it so Pearl Abyss fixes it for everyone.

“I've been staring at a still pond for five minutes. The fish are literally not moving.” — you, right before finding this guide.

🐟 1. What Is the "Fish Won't Move / Won't Bite" Bug

The fishing mini-game in Crimson Desert is a small timing-based activity: you cast near a body of water, wait for a fish to approach the bobber, and then press the prompt when it bites. Under normal conditions the fish swim toward your line, the bobber dips, and you get a short window to reel in.

In this bug, that loop breaks. After you cast, the water goes still. No fish swim in, the bobber never twitches, and the "bite" prompt never appears. You can stand there for minutes and nothing happens. It is not a controller issue — the input is fine — the mini-game's spawn and bite logic has simply failed to start.

⚠️ Don't Keep Waiting

Holding your line indefinitely will not "catch up." Once the fishing state has desynced, the only fix is to reset it. The three workarounds below do exactly that.

🔍 2. Why It Happens in the Fishing System

This glitch is specific to the fishing system, not the broader game. The fishing mini-game relies on a localized "active spot" state that tracks which fish are spawned, whether the bobber is armed, and whether the bite timer is running. The bug appears when that state gets out of sync — usually from one of these triggers:

📋 Common Triggers

  • Fast travel or quest cutscene fired while a line was still cast.
  • Leaving the fishing spot mid-bite and returning without reloading.
  • Network / co-op sync hiccup if you are playing with others.
  • Auto-save landing on the exact cast frame, locking the state.

The good news: because the problem lives in the session's mini-game state rather than your save file, a simple reset restores normal fishing. Your caught-fish progress and inventory are untouched.

💡 Is It Just a Slow Spot?

Some fishing locations genuinely have long waits. Before assuming it is the bug, count to 60 seconds. If zero fish approach and the bobber never moves at all, it is the glitch — not slow RNG.

🔄 3. Workaround 1 — Reload Your Save

The fastest, most reliable fix is to reload your save. This wipes the broken session state and rebuilds the fishing system cleanly.

① Reload from the Pause Menu

  1. Open the pause menu (Options / ESC).
  2. Select System → Load Game (or "Reload Save").
  3. Pick your most recent autosave — it is usually only seconds behind.
  4. Once loaded, walk back to the water and cast again.

This is the option we recommend trying first. It costs you almost no progress and resolves the frozen-fish state in the large majority of cases.

🚶 4. Workaround 2 — Re-Enter the Fishing Spot

If you would rather not reload, you can often clear the bug just by cycling the fishing spot's active state. Think of it as "rebooting" the mini-game without touching your save.

① Leave and Return

  1. Reel in (or cancel) your current cast if the prompt is available.
  2. Walk far enough away that the fishing spot unloads — roughly two to three screen-widths, or until the spot marker disappears from your compass.
  3. Open and close the map once to force a state refresh (optional but helps on console).
  4. Return to the exact water edge and cast again.
💡 Co-op Tip

If you are in a shared session, the host should be the one to leave and re-enter. Client-side spot reloads sometimes do not propagate to the host's fishing state.

📂 5. Workaround 3 — Verify Game Files

If the fish won't bite everywhere — not just one spot — the issue may be a corrupted fishing asset rather than a session desync. In that case, reload and re-entry will not help, and you should verify your game files.

🖥️ PC (Steam / Launcher)

  1. Steam → Library → Crimson Desert → right-click → Properties → Installed Files → Verify integrity of game files.
  2. Wait for the scan. Any corrupted fishing data file is redownloaded automatically.
  3. Launch and test the nearest fishing spot.

Console (PS5 / Xbox)

Consoles cannot verify files directly. Instead, rebuild the database (PS5: Settings → Storage → Console Storage → "Rebuild Database" via Safe Mode) or simply reinstall if the bug is persistent across all spots. A full reinstall is the console equivalent of a file check.

⚠️ Back Up First

Before any reinstall, make sure your cloud save is synced. Your fishing progress is tied to your character save, so a backup keeps everything safe.

📨 7. How to Report to the Devs

Workarounds fix it for you, but they do not fix it for everyone. If you hit this bug, a clean bug report to Pearl Abyss helps the team prioritize a permanent patch. A good report includes:

📋 What to Include

  • Platform (PS5, PS5 Pro, Xbox, or PC with spec summary).
  • Game version — check the main menu build number.
  • Exact fishing spot (region / lake / river name).
  • What you were doing right before — fast travel, cutscene, co-op, etc.
  • Steps to reproduce in 2–3 lines.
  • Screenshot or clip of the frozen bobber (very helpful).

Submit through the official in-game support / "Report a Bug" menu, or the Pearl Abyss customer support portal. Reference "fishing mini-game — fish won't move / won't bite" in the subject so it is triaged correctly.

💡 One Report, Many Helped

Duplicate reports still help — they show the team how widespread the fishing bug is. Don't assume someone else already filed it.

🛡️ 8. Preventing It Next Time

While you wait for a permanent fix, a few habits make the bug far less likely:

① Reel in Before You Leave

Always cancel or reel in your line before fast traveling, starting a quest, or walking away. Casting-then-leaving is the #1 trigger.

② Let the Bite Finish

If a fish is mid-bite, complete the reel before opening the map or menu. Interrupting the bite window is the second-most-common trigger.

③ Save Before a Long Fishing Run

Manual-save before settling in for a fishing session. If the state desyncs, your reload is only seconds old.

❓ 9. FAQs

Will reloading my save delete the fish I already caught?

No. Caught fish and inventory are part of your character save, which is written continuously. Reloading only rolls back a few seconds of session state — your catches stay.

Is this a sign my save file is corrupted?

Almost never. The bug lives in the live mini-game state, not your save data. A corrupted save would show far broader problems (broken quests, missing items), not just frozen fish.

Does verifying files fix the one-spot version too?

It can, but it is overkill for a single frozen spot. Try reload or re-enter first — they are faster. Use file verification only when every fishing spot is broken.

My co-op friend can fish fine but I can't — why?

The fishing state can desync on one client without affecting others. Have the host leave and re-enter the spot; if that fails, the affected player reloads their own save.

Is there a mod or cheat that forces fish to bite?

We do not recommend any third-party tool. It risks your account, your save, and potentially your hardware. The official workarounds above are safe and sufficient.
