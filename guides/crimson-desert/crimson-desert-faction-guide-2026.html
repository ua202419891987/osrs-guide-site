🌍 1. All Factions Overview — How the Reputation System Works

Crimson Desert features over 110 distinct factions spread across the continent of Pywel. While that number sounds overwhelming, only a handful of major factions directly impact your gameplay experience. The rest are local guilds, trade houses, and regional groups that offer smaller-scale rewards. Understanding how the reputation system works is the first step to making informed faction choices.

Every faction uses a five-tier reputation scale that determines how NPCs treat you, what services are available, and what rewards you can unlock. For efficient strategies to raise your standing, see our Reputation Farming Guide, and for the quests that grant the best rewards, check the Side Quests That Matter Guide.

TierNameEffect on Gameplay
1WarMaximum hostility — faction members attack on sight. Most services blocked.
2HostileLow standing — NPCs are aggressive. Limited access to areas.
3NeutralDefault starting state. Basic services available, no special perks.
4FriendlyUnlocks additional quests, better shop prices, and access to faction services.
5AllianceHighest rank — unlocks exclusive merchants, allied troops, restricted resource nodes, and secret dungeons.

Your reputation with a faction is earned primarily by completing their quests and advancing the main storyline. However, there is a catch: some quests that raise reputation with one faction will lower it with another. You cannot be best friends with everyone. The key exceptions are the Greymanes, who never conflict with other factions — making them the safest investment for any player.

Key Insight: Faction reputation does NOT affect the main story ending. You can complete the campaign with any reputation configuration. What it does affect is the "world state" — which NPCs are friendly, what areas are safe, and what exclusive loot you can access.

Beyond the named major factions, there is also the Contribution System specific to each region (Hernand, Demeniss, Pailune, etc.). Contribution is a separate, region-locked score earned by liberating strongholds, completing local commissions, and turning in bounties. Spending contribution at regional shops is how you acquire some of the best mid-game armor and weapons in the game — including the Bolton Plate Set, Dragon Flame set, and Loyal Friendship set.

🛡️ 2. Greymane Faction — The Mercenary Alliance

The Greymanes are Kliff's own mercenary company and the faction closest to the main storyline. They start at Friendly reputation — the highest starting rank of any faction — and can reach Alliance purely through main story progression. This makes them the most accessible and least punishing faction to build reputation with.

The Greymane questline is divided into three major branches: Scattered Embers (34 quests), Grounds of the Sunrise (38 quests), and Greymane Commissions (27 quests). These quests cover everything from recruiting lost mercenaries to rebuilding your camp infrastructure. Completing this questline is essential for anyone who wants to expand their inventory — many of these quests reward Medium Backpacks, which are the primary way to increase your carry capacity.

Key Rewards at Alliance Rank

  • Allied Troops: Greymane soldiers can be summoned during combat encounters — they are the strongest allied NPCs in the game.
  • Exclusive Merchant: Access to a camp vendor who sells unique weapons and crafting materials not found anywhere else.
  • Restricted Resource Nodes: Special mining and alchemy nodes around Howling Hills that are locked until you reach Alliance.
  • Camp Upgrades: Unlock private storage, expanded workforce, and better passive income at your Greymane camp.
Pro Tip: Focus on the "Scattered Embers" questline first. It rewards multiple Medium Backpacks and +500 camp funds — both critical in the early game. The "Grounds of the Sunrise" branch unlocks private storage, which is transformative for inventory management.

The Greymanes are also unique in that they never conflict with other factions. You can max out Greymane reputation without worrying about negative impacts elsewhere. For this reason alone, they are the recommended first faction for every player to focus on.

🏜️ 3. The Deserters — Ex-Soldiers of the Wasteland

The Deserters are a loose coalition of former soldiers who abandoned their posts during the great wars of Pywel's history. They operate primarily in the Crimson Desert and Tommaso regions, where they have established hidden camps and supply caches across the arid wasteland. Unlike the Greymanes, the Deserters start at Neutral reputation and require deliberate effort to raise.

The Deserters' questline focuses on survival, resource acquisition, and uncovering the truth behind their former commanders. Key hubs include the Redwind Merchant Guild and the Muiquun Outlaws faction nodes. Their quests often involve reclaiming lost supply depots, hunting desert beasts, and navigating the dangerous politics of the Crimson Desert's independent settlements.

Key Rewards at Alliance Rank

  • Sahazhad Plate Set: One of the best heavy armor sets in the game, available through the Tommaso Contribution Shop after reaching Deserter Alliance.
  • Desert Trading Routes: Unlock secret merchant routes that offer rare materials like Bloodstone and Malachite at 40% discount.
  • Sandwurm Lair Access: A restricted dungeon that drops unique Abyss Artifacts and the crafting recipe for the Dragon Flame set.
  • Wind-Runner Mount Variant: A unique camel-like mount with superior stamina for desert travel (hidden 8000 reputation milestone).
Warning: Raising reputation with the Deserters will lower your standing with the Desert Cult and Muiquun Outlaws. If you plan to explore the Crimson Desert thoroughly, consider completing Desert Cult content before committing to the Deserters.

The Deserters are ideal for players who enjoy heavy armor, two-handed weapons, and desert exploration. Their rewards lean heavily toward defensive gear and resource efficiency, making them a strong choice for tank-oriented builds.

🌿 4. The Free Folk — The Independent Survivors

The Free Folk is not a single unified faction but rather a collective term for several independent groups across Pywel who resist alignment with any major power. This includes the Pororin Forest Guardians, the Vellua Fishermen's Guild, the Beggars' Alliance, and various independent settlements like the Scholastone Institute. Each operates independently but shares the common trait of being neutral-to-friendly toward outsiders who prove their worth.

The Free Folk quests are heavily concentrated in the Hernand region and surrounding areas. These quests tend to reward Contribution Points rather than direct faction reputation, which makes them invaluable for unlocking regional contribution shops. The Scholastone Institute questline, for example, culminates in the "Where Starlight Converges" quest, which rewards the Sword of Starlight — one of the best weapon rewards available through side content.

This makes them an excellent secondary or tertiary reputation target after you have secured your primary faction choice.

⚔️ 5. Best Faction by Build Type — Sword, Bow, Greatsword & More

Not all factions are created equal for every build. While you can reach Alliance with any faction using any weapon, certain factions offer rewards that dramatically complement specific playstyles. Here is our build-by-build recommendation based on the exclusive merchant items, armor sets, and allied troop types each faction provides.

Pro Tip: Your first priority should always be reaching Friendly rank with the Greymanes (automatic through the main story). Then pick a secondary faction based on your build type. If you are still unsure, the safest universal recommendation is the Greymanes + House Celeste combination. Greymanes handle your defensive and camp needs, while House Celeste's bounty network provides a steady silver income and access to Icewing Plate Armor — a fantastic mid-game medium armor that works for almost any build.

🏆 6. Faction Rewards — Alliance Tier and Hidden Milestones

Reaching Alliance rank is the primary goal for most players, but there are hidden milestone rewards beyond the Alliance tier that the game does not openly advertise. These hidden milestones appear in the faction reputation UI but are not called out explicitly, causing many players to miss them entirely.

Standard Alliance Rewards (Tier 5)

  • Allied Troops: Faction members appear as reinforcements during combat encounters in their territory. Each faction's troops have different strengths — Greymane troops are generalist fighters, Deserter troops are heavily armored, Free Folk troops are mixed specialists.
  • Exclusive Merchants: Each major faction unlocks a unique vendor selling items not available through any other source. These include faction-specific weapons, armor schematics, rare materials, and cosmetic items.
  • Resource Node Access: Restricted mining, herbalism, and alchemy nodes become usable. These nodes have higher yields and can drop rare materials needed for top-tier crafting.
  • Secret Dungeon Entry: Certain dungeons require Alliance status with a specific faction to enter. These dungeons contain unique bosses, Abyss Artifacts, and crafting recipes.

Hidden Milestone Rewards (Not Shown in UI)

Important: The hidden milestone rewards are NOT retroactive. If you reach 5,000 reputation and then lose reputation below the threshold, you will lose access to the secret merchant until you climb back above 5,000. Plan your reputation-grinding sessions carefully.

For comparison, reaching Alliance rank typically requires between 2,500 and 4,000 reputation depending on the faction. The hidden milestones at 5,000 and 8,000 represent a serious time investment — expect 20-40 hours of focused reputation grinding per faction if you want the exclusive mount.

💡 7. Reputation Tips — Gaining and Protecting Your Standing

Managing reputation across multiple factions requires strategy. One wrong move — attacking a faction member, completing a quest for their enemy — can undo hours of progress. Here is everything you need to know to build and protect your faction reputation efficiently.

How to Gain Reputation Fast

  • Main Story Quests: These award the largest reputation gains per hour. The Greymanes benefit most from this, as their reputation is tied directly to campaign progression.
  • Liberate Strongholds: Capturing enemy-held territories in a region raises reputation with the dominant faction of that region. This is especially effective for factions like House Celeste in Hernand.
  • Faction Commissions: Repeatable daily quests that offer small but steady reputation gains. Best used as filler between major quest objectives.
  • Bounty Turn-Ins: Bounties are issued by House Celeste and provide reputation + contribution simultaneously. This is the most efficient activity for reputations in the Hernand region.
  • Defeat Named Bosses: Certain bosses, like Kailok the Hornsplitter at Unicorn Cliffs, grant reputation to specific factions (Goldleaf Guild in this case) upon defeat.

How to Avoid Losing Reputation

  • Check quest rewards before accepting: Some quests display "+X [Faction A] reputation / -Y [Faction B] reputation" in the tooltip. Read before you click.
  • Never attack faction NPCs: Each attack reduces reputation with that faction. Accidental hits during combat happen — be extra careful in crowded areas.
  • Avoid public crimes: Theft (-5 contribution), assault (-30 contribution), and murder (heavy penalty) in Hernand and other civilized regions will drop your contribution score, which is tied to faction access.
  • Use masks and disguises: Certain faction armors or the mask from the Jeffrey bounty quest allow you to enter hostile faction territory without triggering combat. This does not raise reputation, but it prevents further loss.

⚡ Recommended Reputation Route for New Players

Step 1: Play the main story through Chapter 4 — this will push Greymanes to Friendly or higher automatically.
Step 2: Unlock House Celeste bounties in Hernand (Chapter 2). Complete all available bounties for silver + contribution.
Step 3: Defeat Kailok the Hornsplitter to unlock Goldleaf Guild reputation. Do their merchant questline to reach Friendly.
Step 4: Choose your secondary faction based on build (see Section 5). Focus their quests exclusively until you reach Alliance.
Step 5: Once Alliance is secured, grind to 5,000 reputation for the secret merchant reward before moving to another faction.

❓ FAQ — Faction Questions Answered

Can I max out reputation with every faction?

Technically yes, but not in a single playthrough. Some factions are locked into permanent hostility by the main story (Black Bears, Wolf Trackers, Desert Cult). There is no known way to reach Alliance with these factions in the current patch (1.12.02). Other factions have mutual reputation penalties — raising one lowers the other. Plan for 2-3 maxed factions per playthrough.

Does faction reputation affect the ending?

No. The main story ending is determined by narrative choices, not faction standing. However, faction reputation affects which side quests and resources are available during the endgame, which can influence your post-game power level.

What is the fastest faction to reach Alliance with?

The Greymanes are the fastest by far — you can reach Alliance purely through main story progression by Chapter 6. House Celeste is the second fastest if you focus on their bounty questline. The Deserters require the most grinding due to their longer quest chains and remote location.

Are the hidden reputation milestones worth it?

Yes, but only for your primary faction. The secret merchant at 5,000 reputation is valuable because it provides rare crafting materials at a discount. The exclusive mount at 8,000 is a nice cosmetic bonus but not game-changing. We recommend pushing to 5,000 for your main faction and stopping there unless you are a completionist.

Can I recover reputation after losing it?

Yes. Reputation loss from quest choices can be recovered by completing more quests for that faction. Reputation loss from committing crimes is permanent — the contribution penalty cannot be reversed, only offset by future gains. The contribution system has its own recovery mechanism: completing Hernand government quests restores contribution over time.

Which faction has the best armor rewards?

The Deserters have the best heavy armor (Sahazhad Plate Set, Dragon Flame Set). The Greymanes offer the best medium armor path (Bolton Plate Set via Hernand Contribution). For light armor users, the Vellua Fishermen's Guild and Pororin Forest Guardians offer the best options.

Do faction choices affect multiplayer or co-op?

Co-op features are planned for a future free update (no release date confirmed as of June 2026). It is unknown whether faction reputation will carry over to co-op sessions. For now, treat faction choices as single-player progression.

What is the Contribution System and how is it different from reputation?

Contribution is a region-locked score system separate from faction reputation. It tracks your good deeds (quests, bounties, stronghold liberations) and crimes (theft, assault) within a specific region. Contribution Points are spent at regional shops for equipment, unlike reputation which unlocks services and NPCs. See our Reputation Farming Guide for detailed strategies.
