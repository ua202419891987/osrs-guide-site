Crimson Desert First 2 Hours Checklist — What to Do Step by Step

Don't waste your opening hours wandering aimlessly. This is your exact minute-by-minute roadmap from character creation to combat-ready mercenary — optimized for efficiency and zero wasted time.

⏱️ 1. Minutes 0–15: Tutorial & Character Creation

The opening sequence of Crimson Desert is a guided tutorial that teaches you basic movement, interaction, and a single combat encounter. Here is how to get through it as quickly as possible while absorbing everything you need.

1

The Opening Sequence Checklist

  1. Skip cutscenes on second playthrough — press Options/Menu twice to skip
  2. During character customization, focus on function over looks: pick a build that matches your preferred playstyle (balanced/strong/fast)
  3. Complete the movement tutorial — pay attention to sprint, jump, and climb mechanics
  4. Complete the first combat encounter against the training dummy — try parrying at least once even if you miss
  5. Loot the chest after the tutorial fight — it contains your first healing potion (save it!)

① Character Build Selection — Pick the Right Starting Stats

🗡️ 2. Minutes 15–45: Leave the Starting Zone Ready for Real Combat

After the tutorial, you are released into the open world near Uzkigrad village. The next 30 minutes are critical because they establish whether you will be struggling or thriving when you hit your first real enemies outside the safe zone.

2

The "Get Out of Town" Priority List

  1. Talk to the blacksmith in Uzkigrad center — he gives you a free Iron Longsword (better than your starter weapon)
  2. Visit the merchant stall next to the well — buy 3 Small Healing Potions (15 silver total) and 1 Stamina Potion (10 silver)
  3. Open your inventory (D-Pad Up or I key) and equip the Iron Longsword from your bag to primary weapon slot
  4. Walk north out of Uzkigrad toward Bandit's Rise — this is your first real combat zone
  5. Fight 3–5 bandits at Bandit's Rise — focus on practicing light attack combos (L-L-L), not trying to parry yet
  6. Loot every bandit body — you should collect roughly 80–120 silver and some crafting materials
Economy Tip: Do not buy armor or weapons from the Uzkigrad merchant. His prices are 40% above market rate compared to the main city vendors. Use his shop only for consumables (potions, antidotes). Save your silver for when you reach Valenport (the first major city) where prices are fair and selection is 3x larger.

② The Fast Travel System Explained

Shrines are scattered across the world map — usually one per major zone plus one near each boss arena. When you discover a shrine for the first time (walk close to it and interact), it becomes permanently available on your fast travel network. Using a shrine costs no resources but has a 5-minute cooldown between uses, preventing abuse during combat sequences. You cannot fast travel while in combat or inside dungeons.

⚔️ 4. Minutes 90–120: Combat Training & First Boss Preparation

In your final 30 minutes before the 2-hour mark, shift focus from exploration to deliberate combat practice. This is the single highest-ROI activity you can do because the skills you practice here will save you dozens of deaths throughout the rest of the game.

The 20-Minute Combat Drill (Do This Before Any Boss Fight)

Step 1 (5 min): Go back to Bandit's Rise. Find one basic bandit. Practice parrying only — do not attack, just block/parry their swings. Watch for the white glint on their weapon. Your goal: land 10 successful parries.
Step 2 (5 min): Same bandit, but now practice dodge timing. Try to perfect-dodge (press ×/A/Space at exact impact moment) 5 times. Each success gives you Adrenaline buff — use it to deal bonus damage on the follow-up attack.
Step 3 (5 min): Execute full combo chains: L-L-H (Wolf's Bite), L-H-L (Serpent's Coil), H-H-L (Falcon's Descent). Complete each combo 3 times without getting interrupted.
Step 4 (5 min): Fight 2–3 bandits at once using everything combined: parry when attacked, dodge grabs, chain combos, manage stamina (never drop below 20%). If you survive without using a healing potion, you are ready for Staglord.

⚠️ 5. Common Early Mistakes That Waste Hours

Every mistake below costs new players between 30 minutes and 3 hours of wasted time. Avoid them all by reading this section carefully before continuing.

  • Mistake 1 — Spending all silver immediately: You need at least 500 silver reserve for emergency potions and fast travel preparation. Never drop below 200 silver until you have established a reliable income source (around hour 5–6).
  • Mistake 2 — Ignoring the skill tree: Many players go 3–4 hours without spending any Abyss Artifacts on skills. Check the Keen Senses tree after every major quest completion and spend available points. Unspent points give you zero benefit.
  • Mistake 3 — Running past shrine activations: Every shrine you pass without activating is a missed fast travel node. Always detour 10 seconds to activate shrines — the cumulative time saved later is enormous.
  • Mistake 4 — Over-leveling one area before moving on: Killing every enemy in the starting zone sounds thorough but actually hurts you. Enemies scale slightly with your level, and staying too long in low-level zones means you enter mid-game areas under-leveled relative to the story progression. Move forward when quests tell you to move forward.
  • Mistake 5 — Not saving consumables: Healing potions are rare drops in the first 2 hours. If you use them for minor scrapes during routine exploration, you will have nothing left when a boss fight starts. Follow the rule: never heal above 70% HP with a potion during normal exploration.

❓ FAQ — First Hours Questions

Q: Should I follow the main quest or explore freely?

Main quest priority for the first 2 hours. The story introduces core mechanics (companions, spirit arts, camps) in a structured order that the game assumes you know. Free exploration becomes much more rewarding once you have completed Act 1 (approximately hour 3–4).

Q: How much silver should I have by hour 2?

A healthy target is 400–600 silver. Below 300 means you probably missed loot opportunities or spent too early. Above 800 means you were very efficient with looting and selling. Either range is perfectly fine.

Q: When should I unlock my first Spirit Art?

Your first Spirit Art unlocks automatically during the main quest in Act 1 (around hour 1.5–2 if following the story). Do not worry about finding it manually — the tutorial sequence handles this. After unlocking it, practice using it against weak enemies before saving it for bosses.

Q: Is co-op available in the first 2 hours?

No. Co-op multiplayer unlocks after completing the "Allies of Pywel" mission in Act 2 (approximately hour 4–5). The first 2 hours are single-player only, which is good because you want to learn the fundamentals solo before coordinating with other players.

Q: What level should I be at the end of 2 hours?

Level 4–6 is normal depending on how much optional combat you engaged in. Level is less important than skill mastery — a level 4 player who can consistently parry will beat a level 7 player who mashes buttons every time.
