Г°ВџВѓВЏ 1. Card Game Basics ГўВЂ?Duo & Five-Card Overview

Crimson Desert features two gambling mini-games called Duo and Five-Card, both based on the traditional Korean card game Seotda (also known as "A Bloom of High Stakes"). These games use numbered chips (1 through 10 in red and yellow) instead of traditional playing cards. You can find card tables in taverns and gambling dens scattered across Pywel, and mastering them is one of the fastest ways to earn silver in the game.

As of Patch 1.12.02 (June 24, 2026), both games are fully functional with no restrictions on how many times you can play ГўВЂ?no daily caps, no cooldown timers, and no diminishing returns. This makes card games one of the most reliable silver-farming methods in Crimson Desert.

Two Game Types

Duo is the simpler game: each player receives 2 cards from a 20-card deck. It's easier to learn, has lower buy-in costs, and is available in two locations. Five-Card is a more complex variant where colour (red vs. yellow) matters for certain hand rankings. It's only available in one location but offers higher potential payouts per session.

Game Locations Overview

There are three card game locations in Crimson Desert, each with different buy-in costs and difficulty levels:

Г°ВџВ“ВЌ Hernand ГўВЂ?Duo (15 Silver)

The cheapest table, located on the second floor of the Hernand inn. Look for a blue-jacketed man near the corridor past the closed door on your right. This is the best place to learn the rules and build starting capital. Unlocked by completing the "A Move on the Table" quest.

Г°ВџВ“ВЌ Beighen ГўВЂ?Five-Card (150 Silver)

The only Five-Card table in the game. Located in a standalone hut on the outskirts of Beighen village in the Pailune region (north of Hernand). This is the best mid-game silver farming spot, with payouts of 400-600 silver per winning round.

Г°ВџВ“ВЌ Tommaso ГўВЂ?Duo (300 Silver)

The high-stakes Duo table in the northeastern settlement of Tommaso. Located in the central gambling hall. Single wins can net you 800-1,500+ silver. Recommended only after you've mastered the game mechanics.

Г°ВџВЋВЇ 2. How to Play ГўВЂ?Rules & Game Flow

Both Duo and Five-Card follow similar core mechanics but differ in deck composition and hand evaluation. Here's a detailed breakdown of how each game works.

Duo Rules

In Duo, each player is dealt 5 cards from a 20-card deck (numbers 1-10, one red and one yellow of each number). The game system automatically selects 3 cards that sum to 10, 20, or 30. The remaining 2 cards become your playing hand. If no valid three-card combination exists, you Bust (Bust) and automatically lose the round.

Once hands are dealt, each player has 10 seconds to choose an action:

  • Check ГўВЂ?Pass without betting. Used when you have a medium hand and want to observe.
  • Call ГўВЂ?Match the current bet. Good when your hand is average but you want to stay in.
  • Half Raise ГўВЂ?Raise by half the current pot. Good for medium-strong hands.
  • Double Raise ГўВЂ?Raise by double the previous bet amount. Use when you have a pair or named combination.
  • All-In ГўВЂ?Bet all your silver. The core strategy for winning big with strong hands.
  • Fold ГўВЂ?Surrender the round. Do this when your hand scores 0-3 points.

Five-Card Rules

Five-Card is built on the same foundation as Duo but introduces colour (red vs. yellow) as a tie-breaking and ranking factor. Some high-tier hands require specific colour combinations. The buy-in at Beighen is 150 silver, and single-round payouts range from 400-600 silver. Hand rankings are broadly similar to Duo but with colour-sensitive tiers.

Pro Tip: You can see one of each opponent's cards during play ГўВЂ?it's shown in a small window on the left side of the screen. Use this information to calculate whether your hand beats theirs before betting big. If an opponent is showing a red 4, be extremely cautious ГўВЂ?red 4 forms many powerful combinations including the Executor (red 4 + red 7).

Save-Scumming (The Reload Method)

Card hands are randomly generated when you sit at a table. Crucially, hands re-roll when you reload a save. This is not a bug ГўВЂ?it's an intentional design feature that lets you leverage save management for consistent profits.

  1. Manual save outside the gambling den ГўВЂ?creates a safety checkpoint.
  2. Enter the table and pay the buy-in.
  3. Evaluate your hand. Have a pair, a named combination, or Perfect Nine (9+ points)? Proceed. Weak hand (under 4 points)? Fold or go All-In to force a loss, then reload.
  4. Go All-In immediately on round one ГўВЂ?AI opponents are extremely reckless in the first round and almost always call max bets.
  5. Win ГўВ†?exit the table and manual save. Lose ГўВ†?reload and try again.
  6. Reset the table by resting at a campfire or fast-travelling away and back. This refreshes opponents and the card pool.

Г°ВџВ§В  4. Best Strategies ГўВЂ?How to Win Every Hand

Winning at Duo and Five-Card isn't about luck ГўВЂ?it's about knowing when to be aggressive, when to fold, and how to exploit the AI's predictable behaviour patterns.

Strategy 1: First-Round All-In Aggression

AI opponents are extremely reckless in the first round. When you hold a hand ranked Perfect Nine (9 points) or higher, go All-In immediately. The AI almost always calls max bets in the first round, and if three opponents all call, you can win several hundred silver in a single hand.

Strategy 2: Selective Folding & Reloading

Any hand worth 4 points or less is statistically unlikely to win. Fold immediately, or better yet, go All-In (the AI will call, you lose, and you reload to try again). Never "chase losses" ГўВЂ?the save-reload method is more efficient than trying to bluff your way back.

Strategy 3: Playing the Opponent's Visible Card

The left-side window shows one card from each opponent. Use this information mercilessly:

  • Opponent shows a red 1? They could have Superior Pair (1+3 or 1+8) or any named combination. Be very cautious.
  • Opponent shows a red 4? Extremely dangerous ГўВЂ?red 4 forms Executor (4+7), Warden (4+9), High Warden (red 4+9), Dok-Sa (1+4), and Jang-Sa (4+10). Consider folding.
  • Opponent shows a 9 or 10? They may have a pair or a named combination. Raise cautiously.

Strategy 4: The Cheat System

Cheating is one of the most powerful tools in your arsenal ГўВЂ?but it must be unlocked first.

1

How to Unlock Cheating

During card games, watch for opponents with a blue outline. This indicates they may be cheating. Observe the cheating opponent's card-dealing animation three times ГўВЂ?once you've witnessed it three times, you permanently unlock the Cheat ability. Pay close attention to hand movements: cheaters keep their elbows low and deal from the side of the deck instead of the top.

2

How to Execute a Cheat

When it's your turn to deal, hold the Hide Hand button. Time will freeze, allowing you to select a specific card (number + colour) to secretly add to your hand. Your best cheat card is Red 1, since it forms the most powerful combinations (Superior Pairs and nearly all named combos). In Five-Card, consider Red 3 or Red 8 for a shot at Prime Pair. You can only hold one hidden card at a time, and it persists until you leave the table or get caught.

Warning: Do not cheat every round. You can be caught. Only use your hidden card when the pot is large and the hand is close. If you are caught, you are banned from the gambling den for roughly 2 in-game days.

Accusing Opponents of Cheating

If you witness an opponent cheating (they'll have a blue aura and use suspicious dealing animations), you can accuse them:

  • Correct accusation: The cheater is removed from the table, and their silver is split among remaining players.
  • False accusation: You are removed from the table and lose all silver currently in play. You'll also be banned for ~2 in-game days.

Only accuse when you are 100% sure. The telltale sign: when dealing to others, cheaters keep their elbows at shoulder height but take cards from the side of the deck rather than the top. When dealing to themselves, they turn their palm downward instead of fully outward.

ГўВќ?FAQ ГўВЂ?Frequently Asked Questions

Can I play Duo and Five-Card as many times as I want?

Yes. There are no daily limits, cooldown timers, or diminishing returns on card games in Patch 1.12.02. You can play endlessly.

What's the fastest way to earn silver through card games?

Beighen's Five-Card table is the fastest. With the save-reload method, you can earn 3,000-4,000+ silver per hour. The 150 silver buy-in yields 400-600 silver per winning round.

Does the save-scum method still work in the latest patch?

Yes, as of Patch 1.12.02 (June 24, 2026), card hands re-roll when you reload a save. Pearl Abyss has not patched this mechanic. Future updates may change this behaviour (verify with in-game testing after major patches).

How do I unlock the Cheat ability?

Observe an opponent cheating (blue outline + suspicious dealing animation) three times. After the third observation, the Cheat ability is permanently unlocked for your entire playthrough.

What's the best hidden card to cheat with?

Red 1 is the best choice for Duo because it forms Superior Pairs and all named combinations except Se-Lyuk and Jang-Sa. For Five-Card, Red 3 or Red 8 also work well as they can form Prime Pair.

Can I lose my cheat ability?

No. Once unlocked, it is permanent. However, being caught cheating will get you banned from that gambling den for approximately 2 in-game days.

Is there a way to guarantee a Prime Pair hand?

Not without cheating. The only reliable way is to use the Cheat ability to add Red 3 or Red 8 to your hand and hope the other card is the matching piece. Even then, Prime Pair remains rare.

Are card games available from the start of Crimson Desert?

No. The Hernand table is unlocked by completing the "A Move on the Table" quest, which becomes available after progressing through the main story to Hernand. This typically happens in Chapter 3 or 4.
