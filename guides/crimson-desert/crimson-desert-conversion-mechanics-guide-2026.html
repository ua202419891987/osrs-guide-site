The Conversion Rate is Crimson Desert's underlying economic multiplier that determines how efficiently your donated items, currency, and resources are transformed into camp provisions and Kliff rewards. It is not a single number displayed in your UI â?rather, it is a hidden system composed of three independent multiplication layers:

Personnel BonusFull party of companions assigned1.0x+40% (1.4x)
Conversion BonusChurch donation box offering1.0x+40% (1.4x)

These three layers are multiplicative, not additive. This means the total maximum conversion multiplier is:

In practical terms: if a dispatch mission would normally yield 20,000 silver coins, running it with all three bonuses active yields approximately 54,880 silver coins â?nearly 2.75x the base reward. This is why understanding and optimizing your conversion rate is the single most impactful thing you can do for your camp economy.

Common Misconception: Many players believe Conversion Rate is a single slider or percentage shown in the camp menu. It is not. The "Conversion" name comes from the church donation system (the Conversion Bonus layer), but players have adopted "Conversion Rate" as shorthand for the combined multiplier across all three layers. We use the same convention in this guide.

Camp Provisions are the five resource pools managed by Carl at Greymane Camp: Equipment, Ore, Lumber, Food, and Coin. Every item you donate is converted into one of these five categories. The conversion rate determines how many provision points you get per item.

Five Provision Categories & Donation Values

OreLazurite, refined oresLazurite = 63 ptsConstruction, Engineering

Conversion Rate Impact by Category

The conversion rate does NOT apply equally to all five categories. Based on community testing, the skill/personnel/conversion bonuses apply primarily to mission output (what dispatch missions produce), not the initial donation. This creates an important distinction:

  • Donation Conversion: Fixed 1:1. The value of an item when donated is static. A piece of cloth is always 2 equipment points. The conversion rate does not multiply donation values.
  • Mission Conversion: Multiplied. The output of dispatch missions is multiplied by the conversion rate. A mission that says "rewards 500 food" with all three bonuses active actually gives ~1,372 food.

What this means: You should never worry about maximizing the per-item donation value. Instead, focus on maximizing the missions that produce resources. Donations are the fuel; missions with high conversion are the engine.

Conversion Bonus Testing (Church Donation Box)

The church donation box is the most misunderstood mechanic. Key findings:

  • Location: Each major city has a church with a small donation box in a corner (Hernand, Howling Hill, Delphine's Rest)
  • Effect: Grants +40% to all dispatch missions within the church's regional range
  • Range: Very limited. Hernand church only affects missions in the Hernand city vicinity â?not missions at Howling Hill camp
  • Duration: Persists until you leave the region. Does not persist through fast travel
  • Cost: Variable. Minimum donation of ~500 silver triggers the bonus, but larger donations do NOT increase the bonus beyond +40%
  • Refreshing: Must re-donate each time you enter the region after leaving

â?5. Optimization Strategies

Optimizing your conversion rate is about maximizing all three multiplier layers simultaneously while maintaining a self-sustaining resource loop.

Strategy 1: The Self-Sustaining Triangle

This is the foundation of any efficient camp economy. You run three dispatch missions simultaneously that feed into each other:

Triangle Setup

Security Mission (consumes Food â?produces Coin) â?with full party + church bonus
Food Harvest Mission (consumes Coin â?produces Food) â?with full party + church bonus
Mining Mission (consumes Food + Coin â?produces Ore) â?with full party + church bonus

Result: All three run indefinitely, producing Coin, Food, and Ore at 2.744x speed. The surplus fuels Construction missions for camp XP.

Strategy 2: Camp Level Rush

Focus all resources on Construction missions until you reach the next camp level. Each camp level unlocks:

  • New dispatch mission types (higher rewards)
  • Additional companion slots (more missions simultaneously)
  • Merchant upgrades (better item quality in camp shop)
  • Physical camp upgrades (workbenches, kitchen, storage)

The 4th camp expansion requires approximately: 1,500 Equipment, 2,500 Stone, 2,500 Lumber, 4,000 Food, 100,000 Silver. Plan your donations around these targets. Equipment is consistently the bottleneck â?prioritize gathering weapons and armor for donation.

Strategy 3: Shield-Swipe Farming

Equip a shield, hold block, and parry enemy attacks with Shield-Swipe (parry â?heavy attack). This knocks weapons and shields off humanoid enemies, making them lootable. This is the fastest way to generate Equipment provision points. A 10-minute farming session at the Greymane bandit camp can yield 15-25 weapons worth 200-400 total equipment points.

Strategy 4: Church Rotation

Since the church conversion bonus is region-locked, your strategy changes based on what missions you are running:

  • Hernand vicinity: Activate Hernand church bonus â?run Hernand dispatch missions
  • Howling Hill (Greymane Camp): The nearest church is in the Howling Hill village â?activate before running camp dispatch missions
  • Delphine's Rest: Late-game zone. Activate church here for high-tier expedition missions

Q: What exactly is Conversion Rate in Crimson Desert?

Conversion Rate is the community term for the combined three-layer multiplier (Skill Bonus Ã Personnel Bonus Ã Church Conversion Bonus) that increases the output of dispatch missions. It is not a single UI stat. The maximum conversion rate is 2.744x (174.4% increased output) with all three layers active.

Q: How does Conversion Rate affect Kliff's personal rewards?

Kliff benefits indirectly from higher conversion rates through increased silver income from dispatch missions, faster camp leveling (unlocking better merchants and gear), and more efficient food production. The silver from optimized security missions alone can fund weapon refinement and Contribution Insignia purchases.

Q: Does the church donation box bonus stack across different churches?

No. Each church bonus is region-locked. Activating Hernand church does not affect Howling Hill missions. They are independent buffs for their respective regions. You can activate multiple churches by traveling between regions, but the effect does not persist across fast travel â?you must re-donate upon arrival.

Q: What is the best item to donate for Equipment points?

Unwanted blue-rarity or higher weapons and armor. The single highest-value common donation is Lazurite (63 Ore points), but for Equipment, you should farm humanoid enemies with Shield-Swipe â?you can collect 15-25 weapons in a 10-minute farming session at bandit camps. There is no single "god item" for Equipment donations.

Q: Should I donate silver directly to the camp?

Only after you have a comfortable personal reserve (50,000+ silver). Direct silver donation converts at 1:1 to Coin provision points. However, silver is also the universal fuel for all dispatch missions. A better strategy is to use your silver to fund dispatch missions (which then produce more resources) rather than donating it directly.

Q: How do I unlock the church donation system?

The church donation box becomes available after reaching Chapter 3 and completing the "A Fresh Start" quest at Howling Hill. Look for small wooden or metal donation boxes in the corner of churches in major settlements. The Hernand church box is the earliest and most convenient â?it is located behind the pews on the left side of the main hall.

Q: Can I undo a donation?

No. Donations are permanent and irreversible. The only way to "undo" a donation is to load a save from before the donation. Always double-check what you are donating, especially unique items and crafting materials. When in doubt, save before a large donation session.

Q: Does the conversion rate affect Trade Goods?

Partially. Trade Goods (items with a box/package icon) enter a separate trade system managed by the camp's trade post. The packaging fee is deducted from camp Coin reserves. The conversion rate bonuses apply to the dispatch missions that produce trade goods, not directly to the trade good donation values themselves. Trade Goods are a late-game system â?do not worry about them until camp level 3+.
