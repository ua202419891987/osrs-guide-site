🐎 1. Mount System Overview — Everything You Can Ride

Mounts are an essential part of navigating the vast open world of Crimson Desert. From the humble horse you start with to the majestic Pywellan Wyvern introduced in the 1.10 update, each mount offers unique advantages in speed, stamina, terrain handling, and carrying capacity. Understanding the full mount ecosystem will help you choose the right companion for every situation.

As of version 1.10, there are five mount categories in Crimson Desert: horses, camels, the Highland Warg (wolf), the Pywellan Wyvern (flying dragon), and special event mounts that rotate seasonally. Each category has multiple visual variants with slightly different stats.

① Mount Categories and Base Stats

Horses are the default mount, available from the start of the game. They offer balanced speed (60 km/h sprint), moderate stamina (150 base), and can carry up to 50 additional inventory slots. Camels are slower (45 km/h) but have higher stamina (250 base) and can carry 80 additional slots, making them ideal for long-distance trade runs. The Highland Warg is the fastest ground mount (75 km/h sprint) but has low stamina (100 base) and limited carrying capacity (30 slots). The Pywellan Wyvern is the only flying mount, with a sprint speed of 90 km/h in the air and 200 base flight stamina.

🐲 2. The Pywellan Wyvern — Flying Dragon Mount Guide

The Pywellan Wyvern is the most coveted mount in Crimson Desert, and for good reason. Being able to fly completely changes how you explore the world, bypassing dangerous enemy territories, reaching hidden areas, and dramatically reducing travel time. However, the dragon is not simply a fast travel tool — it has its own mechanics, limitations, and growth system.

② Quest Walkthrough: Wings of the Desert

The quest "Wings of the Desert" is a three-part chain that begins at the Uzkigrad stable. First, speak to Renna, who tells you about ancient dragon eggs hidden in the Highland Caverns. Travel to the caverns (northeast of Uzkigrad) and navigate to the deepest chamber, which is behind a waterfall at coordinates (X:340, Y:180). The chamber is pitch black — bring a torch or cast a light spell. The egg is sitting on a pedestal in the center of the chamber. Pick it up (it weighs 50 units) and return to Renna.

The second step involves incubating the egg at your camp. You need to build a "Dragon Incubator" structure, which requires 20 stone, 15 iron ingots, and 5 fire crystals. Place the incubator near your camp fire and interact with it to insert the egg. The incubation takes 3 in-game days (approximately 2 hours of real time). You can speed up the process by feeding fire crystals to the incubator — each crystal reduces incubation time by 4 in-game hours.

③ Raising the Hatchling

Once the egg hatches, you get a baby wyvern that cannot yet be ridden. You must raise it by feeding it raw meat (5 pieces per day) and interacting with it daily. The hatchling goes through three growth stages: hatchling (days 1-5), juvenile (days 6-12), and adult (day 13+). Once it reaches adulthood, you can ride it. The entire raising process takes about 5-6 hours of real time if you interact with the hatchling consistently.

Pro Tip: Feed your hatchling Highland Drake Meat instead of regular raw meat. This premium food increases growth speed by 30% and gives the adult wyvern a permanent 10% stamina bonus. Highland Drake Meat drops from drake enemies in the Highland region.

🐔 3. The Cuckoo Pet — Your Feathered Companion

The cuckoo pet is Crimson Desert's first non-mount companion creature. Unlike mounts, which serve a transportation function, the cuckoo is a passive companion that provides gameplay bonuses through its bond system. The cuckoo flies alongside Kliff during exploration, perches on his shoulder during dialogue, and occasionally assists in combat by distracting enemies.

④ Obtaining the Cuckoo — A Bird in the Hand

To get the cuckoo, travel to the Pywel market district and look for an NPC named Maren near the fruit stalls. She will tell you about a runaway cuckoo bird that has been terrorizing the market. The quest involves tracking the bird through the market district using audio cues — the cuckoo chirps louder as you get closer. Once you find it, you must corner it by blocking its escape routes without getting too close. If you approach too fast, the bird flies away and you must start the tracking phase again.

⑤ Bond Levels and Passive Buffs

The cuckoo has 10 bond levels, each unlocking or improving a passive buff. Bond XP is earned by feeding the cuckoo (berries, nuts, small fish) and by simply having it active during gameplay — it earns passive XP at a rate of approximately 1 level per 3 hours of active play. Key milestones include: Level 3 unlocks the item drop rate buff (+3%), Level 5 unlocks the scavenging ability (occasionally brings you items during combat), Level 7 improves the drop rate buff to +7%, and Level 10 (max) grants +10% item drop rate, +5% out-of-combat movement speed, and the combat distraction ability.

🏆 4. Mount Tier List — Which Mount Is Best?

Not all mounts are created equal. Depending on what you are doing — exploring, trading, combat, or just getting from point A to point B — different mounts excel in different scenarios. This tier list ranks all current mounts based on their overall utility, with specific use-case recommendations.

⑥ S-Tier: Pywellan Wyvern

The flying dragon is unambiguously the best mount in the game. Flight alone makes it S-tier — the ability to bypass terrain, avoid ground enemies, and access previously unreachable areas is invaluable. Its 90 km/h flight speed, 200 base stamina, and moderate carrying capacity (40 slots) make it the best all-around mount once you have invested in its bond system. The only downside is the significant time investment required to unlock and raise it.

⑦ A-Tier: Highland Warg

The Highland Warg is the fastest ground mount and excels in short-to-medium distance travel where flight is unnecessary. Its 75 km/h sprint speed makes it ideal for combat-oriented travel — you can quickly close distance to enemy camps and dismount to fight. The low stamina (100 base) is a limitation, but the Warg's aggressive nature means it can even attack enemies while mounted, dealing moderate damage with its bite.

⑧ B-Tier: Camel

The camel is the trade-runner's best friend. While slow, its massive stamina pool (250 base) and high carrying capacity (80 additional slots) make it the optimal choice for long-distance trade runs and material gathering expeditions. If you spend significant time farming resources or running trade routes, the camel will save you more time than any other mount through reduced rest stops.

✨ 5. Mount Growth & Bond System

Every mount in Crimson Desert has a bond system that improves its stats as you use it. The bond system is passive — you earn bond XP by simply riding your mount, feeding it, and caring for it. Each mount type has 20 bond levels, with each level providing incremental stat improvements.

⑨ How Bond XP Works

Bond XP is earned through three activities: riding (1 XP per minute of active riding), feeding (5 XP per feeding, once per in-game day), and grooming (3 XP per grooming session, once per in-game day). At this rate, it takes approximately 15-20 hours of active play to max out a mount's bond level. The stat improvements per level are modest individually but significant in aggregate — a max-bond horse has 30% more stamina, 15% more speed, and 20% more carrying capacity than a base-level horse.

⑩ Mount-Specific Bond Rewards

Each mount type has unique bond rewards. The wyvern unlocks aerial combat maneuvers at bond level 15, allowing you to perform a dive-bomb attack that deals damage to ground enemies. The Warg unlocks a mounted charge attack at bond level 10. The horse unlocks a sprint-boost ability at bond level 12 that temporarily increases speed by 50% for 5 seconds. These unique abilities add another layer of depth to the mount system and reward long-term investment in a single mount.

📍 6. Mount Care Tips & Common Mistakes

Mounts in Crimson Desert require regular care to maintain peak performance. Neglecting your mount — not feeding it, not grooming it, letting it take too much damage — will reduce its stats temporarily and slow bond XP gain.

⑪ Daily Care Routine

Each in-game day, you should feed and groom your mount. Feeding requires one unit of appropriate food (meat for carnivores, grain for horses, fruit for the cuckoo). Grooming requires a grooming brush, which can be purchased from any stable for 50 coins. Both activities take about 10 seconds each and provide bond XP. If you skip a day, your mount's mood decreases, reducing its stamina regeneration by 10% per missed day (up to a maximum of 50% penalty). Mood recovers fully after one day of proper care.

⑫ What Not to Do

Do not ride your mount into deep water — most mounts cannot swim and will panic, throwing you off. The wyvern cannot fly in storms — attempting to fly during a thunderstorm will result in the dragon refusing to take off. Do not leave your mount in a hostile area — enemies can attack and kill unattended mounts, requiring you to resurrect them at a stable for a fee. Finally, do not forget to stable your mount before fast-traveling — the game will automatically transport your active mount to the nearest stable, but you must retrieve it manually.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Can I have multiple mounts at the same time?

Yes, you can own multiple mounts. They are stored at stables, and you can switch between them at any stable location. There is no limit to how many mounts you can own, but only one can be active at a time.

Do mounts die permanently?

No, mounts do not die permanently. If a mount is defeated in combat, it can be resurrected at any stable for a fee that scales with the mount's bond level. The fee ranges from 100 coins for a base horse to 1000 coins for a max-bond wyvern.

Can I use the flying dragon in all areas?

The flying dragon can be used in most open-world areas, but it is restricted in certain story dungeons, caves, and indoor areas. The game will automatically dismount you when entering a restricted area and your dragon will wait at the entrance.

Is the cuckoo pet worth the effort to max out?

Yes, the cuckoo pet's max-bond buffs are genuinely useful. The 10% item drop rate increase is particularly valuable for farming rare materials, and the scavenging ability provides a steady stream of free items during combat.

Can mounts be customized visually?

Yes, all mounts can be customized with cosmetic items. Horses can wear armor barding, the Warg can wear spiked collars, and the wyvern can wear decorative saddle gear. These cosmetics are obtained through quests, mini-game rewards, and special events.
