üèõÔ∏?1. What Is Abyss Sanctum? ‚Ä?Unlocking the Witch Questline

Abyss Sanctums are endgame challenge dungeons scattered across Pywel. They combine puzzle-solving with intense boss encounters, rewarding unique weapons, Sanctum Cores, and Abyss Artifacts. There are 16 corrupted sanctums on the world map, though the core progression path focuses on a chain of sanctums tied to the Witch Sisterhood questline.

The Sanctum system unlocks during Chapter 5 when you meet Elowen, the Wise Witch, at Greymane Camp. She gives you the quest "Wise Witch" which tasks you with purifying corrupted sanctums throughout Pywel. Before Chapter 5, sanctum entrances display the message "Your connection is not strong enough" ‚Ä?do not waste time trying to enter early.

Each sanctum follows a three-phase structure: (1) Clear all enemies in the area, (2) Solve a physics-based puzzle using Axiom abilities (Force Palm, Force Grab, Flash), and (3) Defeat an Antumbra boss variant. After purification, a secondary "cleansing" phase may be available to extract the Sanctum Core ‚Ä?a valuable crafting resource.

Pro Tip: Mark all sanctum locations on your map from the quest journal before heading out. The game does not reveal them automatically ‚Ä?you must open the quest tracker and manually place waypoints for each sanctum. Do this at the start of Chapter 5 to save hours of backtracking.

Sanctum Categories

Sanctums fall into two categories. The Main Sanctums (4 total) are tied to individual witches and must be completed in any order to progress the sisterhood questline. The Deep Sanctums (Absolution, Penitence, Renunciation, Transcendence, and others) are harder challenges that reward the strongest weapons in the game.

üèõÔ∏?2. Sanctum 1-6 Walkthrough ‚Ä?Room Layouts, Puzzles & Solutions

Below is the complete walkthrough for the primary sanctum progression path. All sanctums are available simultaneously once unlocked ‚Ä?you can tackle them in any order. However, we recommend the sequence below for a natural difficulty curve.

‚ë?Sanctum of Temperance ‚Ä?Central Pywel

Puzzle Type: Energy block placement + missing pillar top

  • Step 1: Clear all enemies in the sanctum courtyard.
  • Step 2: Drop through the ground opening in the sanctum floor to find an underground chamber containing an energy block.
  • Step 3: Use Force Grab to retrieve the block and bring it back to the main room.
  • Step 4: Insert the block into the single empty square slot on the floor mechanism.
  • Step 5: Activate the block with Force Palm (ground version).
  • Step 6: Locate the ivy-covered building on the right side. Use Blinding Flash or Fire Arrow to burn the vines.
  • Step 7: Inside, find the missing pillar top. Carry it back using Force Grab.
  • Step 8: Place the pillar top on the broken column. Lock it with aerial Force Palm (jump first, then activate).

Boss: Antumbra (Temperance variant) ‚Ä?appears after puzzle completion.

Hidden Chest: Behind a destructible wall in the underground chamber.

Pro Tip ‚Ä?Aerial Force Palm: Many players fail the pillar installation because they use Force Palm while standing on the ground. You MUST jump and use Force Palm in mid-air to lock the pillar top into place. This applies to ALL sanctums with column-restoration puzzles.

‚ë?Sanctum of Benediction ‚Ä?West Hernand

Puzzle Type: Dual pillar top installation

  • Step 1: Clear all enemies in the surrounding area.
  • Step 2: Find the first pillar top nearby. Transport it to the broken column using Force Grab.
  • Step 3: Jump and use aerial Force Palm to lock the first pillar top.
  • Step 4: The second pillar top is locked inside the tall central building. Light the lantern to the right of the building's door to open it ‚Ä?this step is the most commonly missed mechanic in the entire game.
  • Step 5: Enter the building, retrieve the second pillar top with Force Grab.
  • Step 6: Place it on the remaining broken column and lock with aerial Force Palm.

Boss: Antumbra (Benediction variant).

Hidden Chest: On a ledge behind the central building ‚Ä?accessible by climbing the broken pillars.

‚ë?Sanctum of Penitence ‚Ä?Delesyia Region

Puzzle Type: Standard pillar restoration

  • Step 1: Clear hostiles in the sanctum area.
  • Step 2: Search the surrounding environment for hidden pillar components. They blend into the terrain ‚Ä?look for rectangular stone pieces with decorative edges.
  • Step 3: Transport each component with Force Grab and lock with aerial Force Palm.

Boss: Antumbra's Spear ‚Ä?considered one of the harder Antumbra variants. Use Force Palm on the boss during its charge attack to stagger it, creating a reliable damage window.

Hidden Chest: Near the water edge behind the sanctum structure.

Warning ‚Ä?Penitence Boss: Antumbra's Spear has a long-range thrust attack that covers half the arena and deals massive damage. The tell is a gold flash on the spear tip followed by a 0.5-second wind-up. Dodge sideways ‚Ä?NOT backward ‚Ä?as the thrust has forward tracking. Time your dodge to the flash, not the spear movement.

‚ë?Sanctum of Insight ‚Ä?Abyss Realm

Zone: Inside the Abyss (accessed via Abyss Portal)

Puzzle Type: Vertical climbing + Axiom leap + platforming

  • Step 1: Enter through the Abyss portal marked on your map.
  • Step 2: Climb upward through a vertical shaft with multiple platforms. Use Force Leap between platforms ‚Ä?you need Axiom rank 2 for some jumps.
  • Step 3: At the top, interact with the central mechanism to purify the sanctum.

Boss: None (pure puzzle challenge). This sanctum counts toward both Sanctum completion and Abyss restoration progress.

Reward: Progress in Abyss restoration chain + Abyss Artifacts.

‚ë?Sanctum of Absolution ‚Ä?Post-Chapter 7

Difficulty: Very Hard. This is considered one of the hardest encounters in the early endgame.

Boss: Antumbra's Sword. The boss summons phantoms that mirror its attacks and fires wide sword waves that cover 60% of the arena. Requires precise dodging ‚Ä?panic-rolling will get you killed.

Key Mechanic: Each phantom killed reduces the boss's attack speed by 10%. Prioritise phantoms over boss damage.

Core Reward: Vessel of Dark Pursuit ‚Ä?one of the strongest swords in the game. Extracted ability "Dark Wound" replaces Spin Slash with a vertical dark energy slash.

‚ë?Sanctum of Transcendence ‚Ä?Dry Valley

Difficulty: Hard

Puzzle Type: One of the easiest Abyss island puzzles. Involves destroying three pixelated barriers using Axiom abilities, then manipulating a central mechanism.

  • Step 1: Clear enemies in the area.
  • Step 2: Locate three pixelated force barriers around the sanctum. Destroy each with charged Force Palm or weapon attacks.
  • Step 3: After all barriers are down, manipulate the central mechanism to purify the sanctum.

Boss: Antumbra (Transcendence variant). Similar to base Antumbra but with an additional AOE ground pound attack ‚Ä?watch for the red circle indicator.

Hidden Chests: Multiple secret treasure chests requiring platforming detours. Spend extra time exploring.

Universal Antumbra Strategy

Phase 1 (100-60% HP): Play reactively. Parry every white-glint attack to build spirit. Spend spirit on skills when the boss recovers. Do not overcommit ‚Ä?Antumbra recovers faster than it looks.
Phase 2 (60-30% HP): Boss gains one additional attack in its combo chain. Previously safe openings become dangerous. Delay your punishment by 0.5 seconds to account for the extended combo.
Phase 3 (Below 30% HP): Enrage ‚Ä?red eyes, all attacks unblockable. Switch to perfect dodge + counterattack. If you have Adrenaline buffed critical hits, this is the time to unleash your full combo chain. One Spirit Surge (L1+R1 after parry) can deal 300% damage and end the fight.

Sanctum Cores ‚Ä?The Second Puzzle Phase

Most players miss this. After purifying a sanctum, check the central generator for a small floating antenna above the refracting gem. If present, the sanctum has an active cleansing phase:

  1. Equip Blinding Flash. Aim at the refracting gem and press "Focus Light" (LB on controller, Ctrl on PC).
  2. Surrounding nodes will flash in a Morse-code-like rhythm.
  3. You must replicate the rhythm by tapping (quick press) and holding (release on audio cue) the corresponding buttons at each node.
  4. Any mistake resets the puzzle. The sequence gets longer with each correct cycle (3, 5, 7 nodes typically).
  5. On completion, the antenna lowers and a glowing shard pops out. Use Axiom Hook to grab and seal it ‚Ä?you now have the Sanctum Core.
Pro Tip: Sanctum Cores unlock exclusive Kuku Pot recipes and crafting blueprints that cannot be obtained any other way. Core of Temperance unlocks fire-elemental armour blueprints. Core of Benediction unlocks the Kuku Watcher Backpack. Prioritise core extraction before leaving each sanctum.

Abyss Artifact Farming Through Sanctums

Sanctum challenges are one of the most reliable sources of Abyss Artifacts in the endgame. A full clear of all available sanctums yields approximately 40-60 Abyss Artifacts, which is enough to unlock major skill tree branches. The 16 total sanctums across Pywel provide a significant portion of the 141 sealed Abyss Artifacts needed for maximum progression.

Dimensional Bonds ‚Ä?The True Final Sanctum (Post-Chapter 12)

The deepest Abyss challenge, Dimensional Bonds, is located at the Centre of the Abyss. This is not a traditional sanctum but a culmination of all 40 Abyss challenges available in the game. Unlocking it requires:

  1. Complete the main story through Chapter 12.
  2. Clear all preceding challenge chains: Path of Providence, Wanderer's Way, Dry Valley, Triangle Ring, and Sleet Isles chains must be fully completed.
  3. Complete the final chain: Roots of Truth ‚Ü?Chaos Forest ‚Ü?Dimensional Bonds.

Reward for completing Dimensional Bonds: The "Conqueror of the Abyss" achievement/trophy, 10 Abyss Artifacts (the largest single payout), access to the game's hidden ending, and 6 secret books in the Axiom Archive that fill in missing story fragments.

Important: Dimensional Bonds requires Focus level 3+ and gear refined to at least +6. Do not attempt this challenge with unoptimised gear ‚Ä?the difficulty spike is significant. Prepare at least 10 healing consumables and a full repair kit before entering.

‚ù?FAQ ‚Ä?Abyss Sanctum Questions Answered

Q: How many sanctums are in Crimson Desert?

There are 16 corrupted sanctums on the world map, plus additional challenges counted under the Abyss challenge system (40 total challenges). The core witch questline involves 4 main sanctums, and the endgame path includes 12 deep sanctums plus the Abyss sanctum (Insight).

Q: When can I start doing sanctum challenges?

Sanctums become available in Chapter 5 when you meet Elowen the Wise Witch at Greymane Camp. Before Chapter 5, sanctum entrances will reject you with the message "Your connection is not strong enough." Do not attempt them early.

Q: What is the hardest sanctum boss?

Most players consider Antumbra's Sword in the Sanctum of Absolution the hardest encounter. It summons phantoms, fires arena-wide sword waves, and has the most punishing enrage phase. Sanctum of Penitence (Antumbra's Spear) is a close second due to the long-range thrust attack that can one-shot undergeared players.

Q: Can I skip the cleansing puzzle and still get the core?

No. The Sanctum Core is only obtainable by completing the optional cleansing phase (the antenna + light-refracting puzzle) after the main purification. If the antenna is not present the sanctum may not have a core ‚Ä?not all sanctums do. Check every purified sanctum at least once.

Q: How do I unlock the Power Witch?

Purify the sanctums assigned to the Wise Witch, Vitality Witch, and Fate Witch first. Once all three are done, the Power Witch's sanctum unlocks. She offers the strongest enhancement services in the game. You can check your progress in the quest journal under "Wise Witch" quest.

Q: What gear do I need for Dimensional Bonds?

Minimum +6 weapon refinement, +5 armour, Focus level 3+, and a full legendary Abyss core loadout. Bring at least 10 healing consumables. This is the hardest challenge in the game ‚Ä?do not attempt undergeared. The recommended setup is +8 weapon with Destruction III, Spirit Siphon II, Critical Rate II, and Malicebane cores.

Q: Do sanctum rewards respawn?

No. Each sanctum can only be purified once per playthrough. The Sanctum Cores, boss weapons, hidden chests, and Abyss Artifacts are one-time rewards. Make sure you fully explore each sanctum and complete the cleansing phase before leaving ‚Ä?you cannot come back later for missed loot.

Q: Can I do sanctums in co-op?

Crimson Desert does not have traditional co-op. Sanctums are single-player encounters. However, you can summon A.T.A.G. (the mech companion) in some outdoor sanctum areas for additional firepower. At.A.G. is not available in indoor sanctum chambers, so plan accordingly.
