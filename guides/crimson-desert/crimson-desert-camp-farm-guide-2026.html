🏕️ 1. Camp System Overview — Your Home in Pywel

The camp system in Crimson Desert is more than just a place to rest — it is a fully functional base of operations that serves as your crafting hub, storage center, farm, and fast-travel waypoint. A well-developed camp can generate passive income, produce food and materials, and provide buffs that enhance your combat performance. Understanding the camp system is essential for any player who wants to maximize their efficiency and enjoy everything Crimson Desert has to offer.

Your first camp is established early in the story as part of the "Mercenary Camp" quest. After that, you can establish additional camps at designated camp sites throughout the world. There are 15 camp sites in total, scattered across all major regions. You can only have one camp active at a time, but you can switch between sites for a small fee.

① Camp Site Selection

Choosing the right camp site depends on your current goals. For general crafting and storage, the Uzkigrad camp site is ideal — it is close to merchants and the blacksmith. For farming and cooking, the Pywel Forest camp site offers the best access to fertile soil and water. For mining, the Highland Ridge camp site puts you near rich ore deposits. You can change camp sites at any time by interacting with the camp move signpost at your current site.

🌾 2. Farming Basics — Plant, Grow, Harvest

The farm system allows you to plant seeds, grow crops, and harvest them for food, crafting materials, and trade goods. Farming is a passive activity — once planted, crops grow over real time (not in-game time) and can be harvested when ready. A well-managed farm can produce a steady stream of valuable goods with minimal daily effort.

② How to Start Farming

To start farming, you need to build a "Farm Plot" structure at your camp. Each plot costs 10 wood planks and 5 rope, and can hold up to 9 plants. You can build up to 4 farm plots per camp, for a total of 36 plants. Seeds are obtained from foraging (wild plants drop seeds when harvested), from merchants (common seeds cost 10-20 coins each), and as quest rewards. To plant, simply interact with an empty plot slot and select a seed from your inventory.

③ Crop Growth Times and Yields

Different crops have different growth times and yields. Wheat grows in 6 hours and yields 5-8 wheat per plant. Herbs grow in 4 hours and yield 3-5 herbs. Vegetables grow in 8 hours and yield 4-6 vegetables. Rare crops like Sunpetal and Moonberry grow in 12 hours and yield 2-3 units. Crops do not die if left unharvested — they remain at full maturity until you collect them, so you do not need to log in at specific times. However, crops do not regrow after harvesting — you must replant each time.

Pro Tip: Use fertilizer (crafted from compost) to reduce growth time by 30%. Compost is made from spoiled food and plant scraps, so never throw away food you do not need — turn it into compost instead.

🍳 3. Cooking System — From Farm to Feast

Cooking is the natural extension of farming — it transforms raw ingredients into powerful consumables that provide combat buffs, healing, and utility effects. A well-stocked kitchen at your camp can give you a significant edge in combat, making cooking one of the most rewarding activities in the game.

④ Building a Kitchen

To cook, you need a "Camp Kitchen" structure, which costs 15 stone, 10 iron ingots, and 5 cloth. The kitchen unlocks all basic recipes. Upgrading to a "Reinforced Kitchen" (costs 30 stone, 20 iron ingots, 10 leather) unlocks advanced recipes that provide stronger buffs. The kitchen also includes a storage chest for food ingredients, keeping your cooking supplies organized.

⑤ Best Recipes and Buffs

Hearty Stew (2 meat, 2 vegetables, 1 herb) restores 200 HP over 10 seconds and provides +10% max HP for 30 minutes. Stamina Soup (2 fish, 1 herb, 1 spice) restores 100 stamina and provides +20% stamina regeneration for 30 minutes. Combat Chili (3 meat, 2 spices) provides +15% damage for 30 minutes. Dragon's Breath Brew (1 fire crystal, 2 spices, 1 rare herb) provides +25% fire damage and +10% fire resistance for 1 hour. These buffs stack with equipment bonuses and skill tree bonuses, making food an essential part of your combat preparation.

💹 4. Trade Economy — Turning Crops into Coins

The farm-to-market economy is one of the most reliable income sources in Crimson Desert. By growing high-value crops and cooking them into premium dishes, you can generate significant passive income. Understanding which crops and dishes offer the best return on investment will help you optimize your farm for maximum profit.

⑥ Profit-Per-Hour Analysis

Raw wheat sells for 5 coins per unit, yielding 25-40 coins per plot per 6-hour cycle. Cooked into bread (1 wheat = 1 bread, sells for 15 coins), the profit increases to 75-120 coins per plot. Herbs sell for 10 coins raw but cooked into potions they are worth 30-50 coins. The most profitable crop-to-product chain is Sunpetal: raw Sunpetal sells for 50 coins, but brewed into Sunpetal Tea (1 Sunpetal + 1 water) it sells for 150 coins. A full farm of 36 Sunpetal plants, harvested every 12 hours and brewed into tea, generates 5,400 coins per day — a substantial passive income.

⑦ Trade Routes and Merchants

Different merchants pay different prices for goods. The Uzkigrad merchant pays base price, the Pywel City merchant pays 110% base price, and the Highland Outpost merchant pays 120% base price but has a limited inventory (only buys 20 items per day). The most profitable approach is to sell your highest-value goods at the Highland Outpost and your bulk goods at Pywel City. For maximum efficiency, establish a trade route: cook at your camp, ride to Pywel City to sell bulk goods, then ride to Highland Outpost to sell premium goods.

🐄 5. Animal Husbandry — Livestock at Your Camp

In addition to crop farming, you can raise livestock at your camp. Animals provide a steady supply of meat, leather, and other animal products that are essential for cooking and crafting. The animal husbandry system is simple but rewarding — feed your animals daily and they will produce materials continuously.

⑧ Available Livestock Types

You can raise three types of livestock: chickens (produce eggs daily, cost 50 coins each), sheep (produce wool every 2 days, cost 200 coins each), and cows (produce milk daily and can be slaughtered for meat, cost 500 coins each). Each animal requires a specific pen structure: a chicken coop (holds 6 chickens), a sheep pen (holds 4 sheep), or a cow pasture (holds 2 cows). You can build one of each pen type per camp, for a total of 12 animals.

⑨ Animal Care and Production

Animals must be fed once per in-game day. Chickens eat grain (1 grain per chicken per day), sheep eat vegetables (1 vegetable per sheep per day), and cows eat grain and vegetables (2 of each per cow per day). If you skip feeding, animals stop producing but do not die. A fully stocked farm with 6 chickens, 4 sheep, and 2 cows produces 6 eggs, 2 wool, and 2 milk per day — enough materials to sustain a steady cooking and crafting operation.

🔨 6. Advanced Camp Tips & Optimization

A truly optimized camp is a well-oiled machine that generates income, produces buffs, and serves as a convenient hub for all your adventuring needs. This section covers advanced tips for taking your camp from functional to exceptional.

⑩ Layout Optimization

Camp layout matters more than you might think. Place your kitchen next to your farm plots and food storage chest — this minimizes walking distance when harvesting and cooking. Place your forge next to your ore storage chest and near the camp entrance for quick access when returning from mining trips. Place your bed (rest point) in a central location for easy access. The 1.10 building update's template system makes it easy to experiment with layouts — design your ideal layout once, save it as a template, and deploy it at every camp site.

⑪ Passive Income Calculation

A fully optimized camp can generate 8,000-12,000 coins per day in passive income. This comes from: Sunpetal tea sales (5,400 coins), livestock product sales (1,500 coins), and excess crop sales (1,500-5,000 coins depending on crop choices). This passive income covers all your consumable expenses (potions, food, repair costs) and leaves a healthy profit for equipment upgrades. The initial investment to build a fully optimized camp is approximately 5,000 coins in structures and seeds, which pays for itself within the first day.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

How many camps can I have at once?

You can only have one active camp at a time, but you can switch between any of the 15 camp sites in the game for a 100-coin fee. Your structures and furnishings are preserved when you switch sites.

Do crops grow while I am offline?

Yes, crop growth is based on real time, not in-game time. If you plant a crop with a 6-hour growth time and log off, it will be ready when you return 6 hours later. This makes farming perfect for players who can only play in shorter sessions.

Can my camp be attacked by enemies?

No, camps are safe zones. Enemies cannot enter your camp or damage your structures. However, wild animals may occasionally wander near the perimeter — they are harmless but can be hunted for extra materials.

What is the best crop for beginners?

Wheat is the best beginner crop. It has a short growth time (6 hours), is cheap to plant (5 coins per seed), and is versatile (can be sold raw, cooked into bread, or used as animal feed).

Can I automate farming?

Partially. The Trade Post structure allows you to hire an NPC farmer who will automatically plant and harvest crops for you. The NPC farmer costs 500 coins per day and maintains your farm plots while you are adventuring.
