Crimson Desert's graphics settings menu is deep — over 20 individual toggles and quality sliders, plus three upscaling technologies (DLSS 4.5, FSR 3.0/Redstone, XeSS) and per-platform optimizations. Console players get between two and three preset modes, but not all console modes are created equal. PC players face the opposite problem: too many options, with some settings costing 20 FPS for negligible visual gain.

This guide provides the optimal configuration for every platform based on extensive testing across PS5, PS5 Pro, Xbox Series X, Xbox Series S, and PC at multiple hardware tiers. All recommendations are verified against the latest patches (1.02.00+ for consoles, 1.04.00+ for PC) and community-reported results from r/CrimsonDesert.

🎮 1. PS5 Best Settings — Performance vs Quality Mode

The PS5 offers three graphics modes, but our testing confirms that one mode is the clear winner for most players. Here is exactly what each mode delivers and who should use which.

PS5 Recommendation: Use Performance Mode with VRR enabled on your display. This gives you a near-locked 60 FPS with High textures and acceptable ray tracing. The visual difference between Performance and Quality mode is noticeable in static screenshots but barely perceptible during combat, where the 60 FPS fluidity makes a much bigger difference. Only use Quality Mode if you have a non-VRR display and strongly prefer visual fidelity over smoothness.

PS5 System-Level Optimizations

⚡ PS5 Display Settings (Recommended)

Enable VRR: Settings → Screen and Video → Video Output → VRR → Set to "Automatic." This eliminates the screen tearing that occurs in Performance mode on non-VRR displays.

Enable 120 Hz Output: Even though the game is capped at 60 FPS, enabling 120 Hz output allows the Balanced mode's 40 FPS target to display with even frame pacing (40 divides evenly into 120). This results in smoother motion than 40 FPS on a 60 Hz display.

HDR Calibration: Go to the in-game Display settings and calibrate HDR using the three-step slider. Set Peak Brightness to match your TV's actual peak brightness (typically 600-1000 nits for most OLED/LED TVs). Turn "Paper White" to around 200 nits for comfortable daytime visibility.

ALLM (Auto Low Latency Mode): Ensure your TV has ALLM enabled. Crimson Desert automatically triggers game mode on compatible displays, reducing input lag by 10-30ms compared to standard picture modes.

The PS5 Pro is the most powerful console option for Crimson Desert, but its graphics modes have important nuances — especially after the Patch 1.02.00 update that added PSSR Native AA to Quality mode. Here is the optimal configuration for the Pro.

PS5 Pro Quality Mode Warning: After the 1.02.00 patch, Quality mode on PS5 Pro uses PSSR as an anti-aliasing solution (PSSR Native AA). While this produces an extremely sharp 4K image, the GPU overhead causes frame rate dips to 25 FPS in demanding scenes. Digital Foundry confirmed that Quality mode is "not recommended for actual gameplay" on PS5 Pro. Stick to Performance or Balanced mode for regular play.
PS5 Pro Recommendation: Use Performance Mode with PSSR 2 upscaling. You get High-quality ray tracing at a near-locked 60 FPS with 4K output. This is the best overall console experience for Crimson Desert. If you have a 120 Hz VRR display, Balanced mode at 40 FPS is also excellent — the added resolution makes static scenes look noticeably crisper.

PS5 Pro Specific Tips

  • PSSR 2 is better than FSR: The PS5 Pro's PSSR 2 upscaling produces a significantly cleaner image than FSR at equivalent resolutions. Do not manually enable FSR in game settings — the console handles upscaling automatically in each mode.
  • Turn off 120 Hz if using Performance mode: Performance mode targets 60 FPS, and 120 Hz output can cause occasional frame pacing issues. If you notice micro-stutters in Performance mode, disable 120 Hz output in the PS5 system settings.
  • HDR difference is real: The PS5 Pro supports higher peak brightness HDR output than the base PS5. Re-calibrate HDR after switching to a Pro, as the base PS5 settings will underutilize the Pro's HDR capabilities.

Xbox Series X Recommendation (with VRR): Use Performance Mode. The 45-60 FPS range is smoothed effectively by VRR, giving you a fluid experience with 4K output. The upscaling from 1280p is slightly softer than PS5's Performance mode, but the higher base resolution (1280p vs 1080p) in the Xbox implementation means less aggressive upscaling artifacts.
Xbox Series X Recommendation (without VRR): Use Balanced Mode. The locked 40 FPS on a 120 Hz display (4:1 frame pacing) provides smoother motion than 30 FPS without the variable frame rate of Performance mode. Screen tearing is also less noticeable in Balanced mode.

Xbox System-Level Optimizations

⚡ Xbox Display Settings (Recommended)

Enable VRR: Settings → General → TV & Display Options → Video Modes → Check "Variable Refresh Rate." On HDMI 2.1 displays, also enable "Allow VRR" for games.

Enable 120 Hz: Settings → General → TV & Display Options → Refresh Rate → 120 Hz. This is especially important for Balanced mode's 40 FPS target.

Disable Auto HDR: If using an Xbox Series X, go to Settings → General → TV & Display Options → Video Modes → Uncheck "Auto HDR" for Crimson Desert. The game's native HDR implementation is superior, and Auto HDR can cause washed-out colors.

Calibrate HDR: Use the Xbox HDR Calibration app (available in the Accessories section of Settings) before launching the game. Set the minimum luminance to 0, maximum to your display's peak brightness, and follow the in-game calibration tool as well.

Wired Controller: As noted in our platform comparison, input lag on Xbox Series X was a significant issue at launch. Ensure your controller is connected via USB cable and that the console firmware is up to date for the lowest input latency.

🔄 4. Xbox Series S Optimization — Surviving on a Budget

The Xbox Series S is the weakest platform for Crimson Desert, but with the right settings and expectations, the game is playable. Here is how to get the most out of the little white box.

Series S Recommendation: Use Quality Mode. While 30 FPS is not ideal for an action RPG, Quality mode at 1080p is dramatically sharper than Performance mode at 720p. On a 4K TV, the 720p Performance mode image is so soft that details like character faces, weapon textures, and distant scenery become muddy and indistinct. Quality mode at least preserves some degree of visual clarity. The 30 FPS lock is stable in most areas, and the trade-off is worth it.

Series S Specific Optimizations

  • Storage matters: The Series S's 512GB SSD (on launch models) is slower than the PS5 and Series X SSDs. If you are experiencing excessive texture pop-in, try freeing up at least 50GB of free space on the internal drive — the SSD controller performs better with free space overhead.
  • External SSD warning: Do not install Crimson Desert on an external USB hard drive or even an external SSD connected via USB. The game's streaming system requires the full bandwidth of the internal NVMe interface. External storage will cause severe texture streaming issues.
  • Consider playing at 1080p display: If you have a 1080p monitor or TV, the Xbox Series S actually looks reasonable — Quality mode outputs native 1080p without upscaling artifacts. On a 4K display, the 1080p signal gets upscaled by the TV, which introduces additional softness.

PC Preset Recommendations by GPU Tier

🟢 Budget GPUs (GTX 1060 - RTX 3050 / RX 580 - RX 6600)

Target: 1080p / 30-60 FPS. Preset: Low-Medium with FSR 3.0 Performance upscaling. Key settings: Volumetric Fog Low, Shadows Medium, Textures High (if VRAM permits), FSR Performance mode. Turn off Motion Blur and Depth of Field. Expect 30-45 FPS in open world and 45-60 in interiors.

🟡 Mid-Range GPUs (RTX 3060 Ti - RTX 4060 / RX 6700 XT - RX 7700 XT)

Target: 1080p-1440p / 60 FPS. Preset: High with DLSS/FSR Quality. Key settings: Volumetric Fog Medium, Shadows High, Textures Ultra, DLSS Quality or FSR Quality. Expect a locked 60 FPS at 1080p and 50-60 FPS at 1440p. Use DLSS 4 (not 4.5) for less flickering.

🔴 High-End GPUs (RTX 4070 - RTX 4070 Ti Super / RX 7800 XT - RX 7900 XT)

Target: 1440p / 60+ FPS or 4K / 60 FPS with upscaling. Preset: High-Ultra with DLSS/FSR Quality. Enable Ray Reconstruction (NVIDIA) or Ray Regeneration (AMD). Lighting set to Cinematic (not Max — Max introduces noise). Expect 70-90 FPS at 1440p DLSS Quality.

💎 Enthusiast GPUs (RTX 4080 Super - RTX 5090 / RX 7900 XTX - RX 9070 XT)

Target: 4K / 60-120 FPS. Preset: Ultra with DLSS/FSR Quality or Balanced. Lighting set to Cinematic. All RT effects enabled at highest quality. Expect 60-80 FPS at 4K DLSS Quality on RTX 4080 Super, and 90-120 FPS at 4K DLSS Balanced on RTX 5090.

⚡ 6. DLSS vs FSR vs XeSS Configuration — Exact Presets for Every GPU

Crimson Desert supports three upscaling technologies, each with multiple presets. The right choice depends on your GPU brand and generation. Here is the definitive guide.

NVIDIA DLSS 4.5 vs DLSS 4 — Critical Note

Crimson Desert ships with both DLSS 4 and DLSS 4.5 support. DLSS 4.5 has confirmed flickering issues that do not exist in DLSS 4. DSOGaming and multiple community sources have verified this. The flickering appears as bright pixel flashes on object edges, particularly in foliage and character hair.

🔧 How to Force DLSS 4 (Not 4.5)

Method 1 — NVIDIA App: Open NVIDIA App → Graphics → Crimson Desert → DLSS Model Preset → Set to "Custom" → Set Super Resolution to "Preset K." This forces the game to use the DLSS 4 preset instead of DLSS 4.5.

Method 2 — DLSS Swapper: Download DLSS Swapper (free tool) and replace the game's DLSS DLL with version 3.10.5.3, then set "Preset L" in the NVIDIA App. This provides the best image quality with minimal artifacts.

Note for RTX 50-series: If you have an RTX 5080 or 5090, DLSS 4.5's flickering is less pronounced due to the newer Tensor Core generation, but we still recommend using DLSS 4 for the cleanest image.

Ghosting & Shimmering Alert: Grass ghosting (trailing artifacts behind foliage when moving the camera) is a known issue in Crimson Desert. Frame Generation — When to Use It

Frame Generation (DLSS 3 FG, FSR 3 FG, XeSS FG) can effectively double your frame rate, but it comes with trade-offs:

  • Use FG when: Your base frame rate is above 60 FPS. Frame generation works best when it has high-quality input frames. Below 60 FPS base, generated frames introduce noticeable artifacts.
  • Avoid FG when: Playing competitively or in PvP modes. FG adds 8-12ms of input lag, which matters in timing-sensitive combat. For single-player exploration, FG is excellent.
  • NVIDIA DLSS FG note: Works on RTX 40-series and 50-series only. Produces the cleanest results with minimal artifacts.
  • AMD FSR FG note: Works on any GPU (including NVIDIA). More prone to artifacts during fast camera movement. Disable FG during boss fights if you notice ghosting.

🔧 7. Troubleshooting Common Settings Issues

Issue 1: Screen Tearing on PS5 and Xbox

Fix: Enable VRR

Screen tearing is most pronounced on PS5 (especially PS5 Pro) and Xbox Series X in Performance mode. The fix is to enable VRR on your display. If your TV does not support HDMI 2.1 VRR, try Balanced mode instead — the 40 FPS cap with 120 Hz output reduces tearing significantly. On Xbox Series X, tearing is less severe than on PS5, but VRR is still recommended.

Issue 2: Texture Pop-In on All Platforms

Fix: Storage Speed & VRAM

Texture pop-in is a known engine characteristic of BlackSpace Engine. To minimize it: ensure the game is installed on the fastest available storage (internal NVMe on consoles, PCIe 4.0 NVMe on PC). On PC, increase the texture streaming pool in Engine.ini by adding r.Streaming.PoolSize=4096 (adjust based on your VRAM). On console, there is no user setting for this — accept that some pop-in is unavoidable.

Issue 3: DLSS Flickering on PC

Fix: Switch to DLSS 4 (Preset K)

As detailed in Section 6, DLSS 4.5 has confirmed flickering issues. Use the NVIDIA App to force DLSS Model Preset to "Preset K" (DLSS 4). If the flickering persists, use DLSS Swapper to replace the DLSS DLL with version 3.10.5.3. AMD users experiencing similar flickering should ensure they are using FSR 3.0 (not FSR 1.0, which is sometimes selected by default on older drivers).

Issue 4: Input Lag on Xbox Series X

Fix: Update + Wired Controller

The launch version of Crimson Desert on Xbox Series X had significant input lag — described by Game Informer as feeling like "issuing commands to a Pokémon." Patch 1.02.00 (April 4, 2026) largely resolved this. If you still experience input lag: use a wired controller connection (disable Bluetooth), enable Game Mode on your TV, and check that your Xbox firmware is up to date. If the issue persists, try restarting the console completely (not just sleep mode).

Issue 5: Low FPS on PC Despite Good Hardware

Fix: Disable Full-Screen Optimizations

Windows 10 and 11 full-screen optimizations can interfere with Crimson Desert's render loop. Right-click CrimsonDesert.exe → Properties → Compatibility → Check "Disable fullscreen optimizations" → Apply. This fixes stuttering for approximately 40% of affected players. Also ensure Windows Game Mode is enabled (Settings → Gaming → Game Mode → On).

Issue 6: HDR Looks Washed Out

Fix: Turn Off Auto HDR

Windows Auto HDR can conflict with Crimson Desert's native HDR implementation. Disable Auto HDR for this specific game: Windows Settings → System → Display → Windows HD Color → Advanced Color → Disable Auto HDR for Crimson Desert. Then calibrate in-game HDR: set Peak Brightness to match your display (check your monitor/TV specs), and set Paper White to 150-200 nits. On Xbox, disable Auto HDR in the system settings and use the game's native HDR calibration.

❓ FAQ — Settings Questions Answered

Should I use DLSS Quality or DLSS Balanced for Crimson Desert?

DLSS Quality is strongly recommended for all RTX cards. It renders at 67% of native resolution and produces an image that is virtually indistinguishable from native in motion. DLSS Balanced (58% of native) is acceptable if you need a few more FPS, but it introduces slightly more softness and occasional ghosting. Avoid DLSS Performance unless you are GPU-limited at 4K.

Does Crimson Desert support FSR 3 Frame Generation on NVIDIA cards?

Yes. FSR 3 Frame Generation works on any GPU, including NVIDIA cards that do not support DLSS 3 Frame Generation (RTX 20/30-series). However, the image quality is not as clean as DLSS 3 FG on RTX 40-series cards. Use FSR 3 FG only if you need the FPS boost and do not have an RTX 40/50-series card.

Is Ray Reconstruction worth enabling on NVIDIA RTX cards?

Yes, with one significant caveat. Ray Reconstruction fixes the grass ghosting issue that plagues the game, making foliage look clean and sharp during movement. However, it currently causes rain effects to disappear — a known bug that Pearl Abyss is working on. If you are in a rainy region, you must choose between "non-ghosted grass" and "visible rain." We recommend enabling RR and accepting the rain bug, as grass ghosting is more distracting during general gameplay.

What is the best settings preset for a mid-range PC?

For a PC with an RTX 3060 Ti / RX 6700 XT, 16GB RAM, and an NVMe SSD: set the preset to "High," enable DLSS Quality or FSR Quality, set Volumetric Fog to Medium, Motion Blur OFF, Depth of Field OFF, and Lighting to High. This should deliver 50-60 FPS at 1440p or a locked 60 FPS at 1080p. If you have an AMD GPU, enable Radeon Image Sharpening at 80% strength in the driver to counteract FSR softness.

Why does my game look blurry on Xbox Series S?

Blurriness on Xbox Series S is caused by the low native resolution — 720p in Performance mode and 1080p in Quality mode. On a 4K TV, the image is upscaled twice (once by the game and once by the TV), resulting in significant softness. The best fix is to use Quality mode (1080p) and ensure your TV's sharpness setting is between 10-20% (too much sharpness creates artifacts, too little looks soft). Alternatively, play on a native 1080p display.

How do I enable 40 FPS Balanced mode on PS5?

To enable Balanced mode (40 FPS), you must have a display that supports 120 Hz output. Go to PS5 Settings → Screen and Video → Video Output → Enable 120 Hz Output. Then in Crimson Desert's in-game settings, select "Balanced" mode. The game will run at 40 FPS with proper frame pacing. If you do not enable 120 Hz output, the Balanced mode option may not appear in the menu.

Does Crimson Desert support NVIDIA Reflex?

Yes. Crimson Desert supports NVIDIA Reflex, which reduces system latency by optimizing the CPU/GPU render queue. If you have an NVIDIA GPU, ensure Reflex is enabled in the in-game settings (Options → Graphics → NVIDIA Reflex → On). On AMD GPUs, the equivalent is Radeon Anti-Lag, which should be enabled in the AMD Adrenalin software. Both technologies reduce input lag by 20-40% in GPU-bound scenarios.
