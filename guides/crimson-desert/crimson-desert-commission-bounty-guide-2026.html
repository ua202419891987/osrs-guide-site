📋 1. Commission Basics — What They Are and How They Work

Commissions are the lifeblood of Crimson Desert's side content economy. Unlike main story quests, commissions are repeatable, renewable, and region-specific tasks that provide reputation, contribution, silver, and unique item rewards. Understanding how commissions work is essential because they are the primary way you expand your inventory, unlock faction reputation, and earn steady income between story missions. For details on other valuable side content, see our Side Quests Guide, and to make the most of your profits, check the Early Game Money Making Guide.

There are two types of commissions in Crimson Desert:

Faction Commissions

These are quests given by NPCs within a specific faction's hub. Each major faction has its own set of commission-givers — for example, the Greymanes have 27 commission-giving NPCs at their camp (Carl, Ross, Ronald, Tranan, Ronnie, and 22 others). Faction commissions always reward faction reputation (usually +50 per commission) and often reward a Medium Backpack, which is the primary way to increase your carry capacity.

Hernand Commissions (Regional)

There are 63 Hernand Commissions in the Hernand region alone. These are issued by various merchants and citizens. Unlike faction commissions, regional commissions reward Contribution Points instead of faction reputation. Contribution Points are spent at regional shops to buy equipment like the Bolton Plate Set (best early tank armor) and other exclusive items.

Key Insight: Faction commissions and regional commissions serve different purposes. Faction commissions = reputation + backpacks. Regional commissions = contribution + shop currency. Do not confuse the two — if you need contribution for gear, do regional commissions. If you need reputation for faction unlocks, do faction commissions.

Commissions refresh after each in-game day (approximately 2 real-time hours). Each commission-giver typically has 1 active commission at a time. When you complete it, that NPC will have a new commission available after the refresh cycle. The maximum number of active commissions you can hold at once is limited by your quest log, but since most commissions auto-complete on delivery, this is rarely an issue.

There is one critical rule: commission availability is tied to reputation tier. At Neutral rank, you will only see 2-3 commission-givers per faction. Reaching Friendly unlocks 3-4 more. Alliance unlocks the full set. If you are struggling to find commissions for a faction, raise your reputation first — the quests will appear.

💰 2. Bounty System Explained — Capture vs Kill, Rewards, and Routes

The bounty system is introduced in Chapter 2: Golden Greed and is managed by House Celeste. Bounties are wanted posters found on contract boards, walls, and lamp posts throughout Hernand city. Unlike commissions, bounties are one-time completions — once you turn in a bounty target, that specific bounty is permanently cleared and does not respawn.

Capture vs Kill — The Most Important Decision

Every bounty target can be either captured alive or killed. This choice dramatically affects your rewards:

OutcomeSilver RewardContribution XPSpecial Drop
Captured AliveFull amountFull amountSuspicious Symbol Note (100% drop)
KilledHalf amountFull amount (no loss)Suspicious Symbol Note (still drops)

The silver difference is significant. For high-value bounties like Warren (25 silver alive), killing him means losing 12.5 silver — which is a substantial amount in the early game. However, capturing alive requires careful execution: you must chase, tackle, and subdue the target without killing them, then transport the bound prisoner to a guard station on horseback. You cannot fast-travel while carrying a bounty, so plan your route carefully.

How to Capture a Bounty Alive

  1. Locate the target on the map (they are marked with a red skull icon once you pick up the bounty).
  2. Approach — the target will try to flee on sight. Sprint and press the tackle button to initiate capture.
  3. Punch the target 2-4 times to fill the stun meter. Do not use weapons or heavy attacks — these can kill the target.
  4. When the "Bind" prompt appears, press the interact button to tie them up.
  5. Pick up the bound prisoner, call your horse, and load them onto the horse (stand behind the horse and follow the on-screen prompt).
  6. Ride to the designated guard station (marked on your map) and deliver the prisoner.
Critical Tip: You can carry two bound prisoners on your horse at once — one on the back and one in your arms. This doubles your silver per trip. Plan routes that collect two bounties before returning to the guard station. For example, pick up Billy (Hernand city) and Jeffrey (Hernand city) together, deliver both to the Hernand Guard Station in one trip.

All bounties are issued by House Celeste, which means completing them raises House Celeste reputation alongside Hernand Contribution. This dual progression makes bounties the single most time-efficient activity in the early game — you gain faction reputation, contribution, silver, and story-relevant items (Suspicious Symbol Notes) all in one activity.

⏱️ 5. Time vs Reward Analysis — What Is Actually Worth Doing

Not all commissions are created equal. Some take 15 minutes for +50 reputation, while others take 5 minutes for +200 contribution and a unique item. Here is the hard truth: many commissions in Crimson Desert are not worth your time if your goal is efficient progression. This section separates the wheat from the chaff.

Tier 1: Always Do (Best time-to-reward ratio)

  • Bounties: 10-25 minutes for 1-25 silver + 100-300 contribution + House Celeste reputation. The best all-around activity in the game.
  • Greymane Backpack Commissions: Any commission that rewards a Medium Backpack is priority #1. Inventory space is the single biggest quality-of-life factor in Crimson Desert. Complete all 27 Greymane Commissions.
  • Liberation Quests: "Liberate Roothold," "Liberate Hellwood," etc. These are one-time events that give massive reputation chunks (200-500) in a single action.
  • Boss-Slayer Commissions: "King of the Fallen Kingdom" (Staglord's Shield), "The Eldertree" (Icewing Plate Armor). Unique equipment rewards that you cannot get elsewhere.

Tier 2: Do When Convenient (Good rewards, longer time investment)

  • Regional Commissions (Hernand, Demeniss, etc.): These are filler content. Do them when you are already in the area delivering a bounty or completing a story quest. Do not make a special trip just for regional commissions.
  • Delivery Commissions: Commissions that involve moving items between two NPCs in the same region. Use these to make passive progress while traveling between main objectives.
  • Material Gathering Commissions: "Gather 5 Iron Ore," "Collect 3 Herbs." These are quick if you already have the materials. Keep a stockpile of common crafting materials in your storage for instant turn-ins.

Tier 3: Skip Unless You Want Completion (Low time-to-reward)

  • Cross-Region Delivery Commissions: Any commission that requires traveling between regions (e.g., Hernand to Scholastone) for a single item. The travel time kills the efficiency.
  • Low-Reputation Commissions: Commissions that reward only +50 reputation with no additional item. These are fine if you are grinding for the 5,000 milestone, but skip them if you are still working toward Alliance.
  • "The Tales of the Hernandian Residents": This is a random-progression quest that appears one at a time with no clear trigger condition. It can take dozens of trips to complete. Not worth the effort for the reward.

⏳ Efficiency Rule of Thumb

If a commission takes more than 10 minutes to complete, it should reward at least +200 reputation or a unique item to be worth your time. Bounties (15 min for 12-25 silver + reputation) pass this test. Cross-region material deliveries (20 min for +50 reputation) fail it. Use this rule when deciding whether to accept a commission.

⚠️ 6. Missable Content — Quests That Disappear

Crimson Desert has several quests that are permanently missable if you progress the main story past certain points. Unlike many open-world games where side content remains available post-game, Crimson Desert's world state changes as the story advances, and some quest-givers can become unavailable, hostile, or dead.

Critical Warning: The Blix bounty is the most commonly missed content in the game. Blix only spawns before 1:00 PM in-game time and disappears permanently once you defeat Kailok the Hornsplitter in the main story. If you beat the Hornsplitter without collecting the Blix bounty, you lose 15 silver + a Suspicious Symbol Note forever. Make Blix a priority in your first visit to Unicorn Cliffs.

How to Avoid Missing Content

  • Complete all bounties before advancing the main story. Bounties are tied to world state. If you see a bounty poster, grab it immediately — even if you do not plan to complete it right away.
  • Talk to every NPC with a quest marker. Some quests are only available during specific story chapters. If you see a quest giver with a blue or yellow marker, complete their quest before progressing the main story.
  • Check the Hernand Request board regularly. Some requests (like "The Price of Coin") appear only for a limited window. Clear the request board after every major story beat.
  • Do not rush Chapter 4-5. These chapters trigger significant world state changes. Slow down and clear outstanding side content before pushing the main story forward.
  • Back up your save before major story decisions. If you are unsure whether a choice will lock content, create a manual save. Crimson Desert allows multiple save slots.

The general rule is: if you see a quest that involves a named NPC in a specific location, complete it before advancing the story. Named NPCs are more likely to be affected by world state changes than generic commission-givers. The Blix bounty, the Jeffrey bounty chain, and "The Price of Coin" are the most high-profile examples of this.

❓ FAQ — Commission and Bounty Questions

How often do commissions refresh?

Commissions refresh every in-game day (approximately 2 real-time hours). Each commission-giver has one active commission at a time. After completing it, the NPC will have a new commission available after the next refresh cycle.

Can I fail a bounty by killing the target?

Yes and no. You can still turn in the bounty for half silver, so you do not "fail" entirely. However, you lose half the silver reward. As long as you recover the Suspicious Symbol Note from the body, you still complete the story component.

Do commissions contribute to the 100% completion achievement?

Yes. There is an achievement for completing all quests in a region, and commissions count toward that total. If you are going for 100%, you must complete every available commission before world state changes lock some of them permanently.

What is the best commission for silver?

Bounties are the best silver source among commission-like activities. The Warren bounty (25 silver) is the highest single payout. For non-bounty commissions, delivery commissions that involve transporting goods between merchants can net 5-10 silver per run, but they require more travel time.

How many Medium Backpacks can I get from commissions?

There are 27 Greymane Commissions that each reward a Medium Backpack. Combined with backpack rewards from other Greymane quests (Luke's Request, Ben's Request, Kamu's Request), you can collect approximately 35-40 Medium Backpacks total. This is enough to expand your inventory from 80 to over 200 capacity.

Are commissions shared between regions?

No. Each region has its own commission pool. Hernand Commissions reset based on Hernand's in-game clock, Pailune Commissions reset based on Pailune's clock, etc. You cannot complete a Hernand Commission and have it count toward Pailune progression.

Can I stack multiple bounties before turning in?

Yes, and you should. Your horse can carry two bound prisoners simultaneously. This is the most efficient way to clear bounties: collect two targets, deliver both, collect double rewards per trip.

What happens if I abandon a commission?

Abandoned commissions disappear from your quest log but do not count as completed. The commission-giver will offer the same commission again after the next refresh cycle. There is no penalty for abandoning commissions.
