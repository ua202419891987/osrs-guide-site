Crimson Desert Early Game Money Making — 10 Beginner Profit Tips

Your first 10 hours in Crimson Desert can set you up for life. These 10 beginner-friendly profit tips will take you from broke mercenary to silver-rich adventurer, all without high-level gear or endgame knowledge.

Your first few hours in Crimson Desert are simultaneously the most exciting and the most financially fragile. You start with barely enough silver for a healing potion, your inventory is tiny, and every vendor seems to sell items you cannot afford. This guide is specifically designed for players in their first 10 hours — the tips here require minimal gear, no high-level skills, and zero prior game knowledge. Follow these 10 tips, and you will enter the mid-game with a healthy bank balance and the knowledge to keep growing it. For advanced farming after the early game, see our Gold Farming After Patch 1.12 Guide, and learn what to keep versus sell in the Vendor Guide.

Quick Start: If you only have 10 minutes and want the fastest possible start, do Tip #4 first (Hernand Bank heist for 30-55 silver), then Tip #5 (Lioncrest Gold Bar for 500 silver). Those two alone give you enough capital to buy bags, potions, and start farming properly.

1 Sell Unnecessary Gear

Your very first source of income is sitting in your inventory. The game hands you a starting weapon and armor, but you will quickly find replacement gear as you explore. Here is exactly what to sell and what to keep.

Sell Immediately

Common (white) weapons and armor that you are not using. If you find a green (uncommon) sword, equip it and sell the white one. Common gear sells for 5-15 silver each. Over your first few hours, you will accumulate 5-10 pieces of replaced gear — that is 50-150 silver from gear alone.

Damaged equipment — any item with a "Damaged" label cannot be repaired. It is vendor trash. Sell it immediately (2-10 silver each).

Duplicate loot — if you find a second copy of a weapon you already own, sell the duplicate. You only need one of each non-unique item.

Keep at All Costs

Your Thieves' Mask — the one you get from Grimrak or from bounty rewards. Never sell this. It is required for every stealing activity in the game.

Any tool — pickaxe, axe, fishing rod. These are essential for gathering and cannot be replaced cheaply at low level.

Unique named weapons — if an item has a unique name (not "Iron Sword" but "The Fang of Greymane"), keep it. It cannot be re-obtained.

Pro Tip: When selling gear, always check which vendor pays the most. The blacksmith in Hernand pays 15-20% more for weapons and armor than the general goods merchant. It is worth the extra walk.

2 Gather Everything You See

Crimson Desert's world is filled with harvestable resources, and many of them sell for decent silver. In your first 10 hours, gathering is the safest, easiest, and most consistent way to earn money. You do not need to fight a single enemy to make good silver from gathering.

What to Gather

Red Bacca Flowers — the most valuable early-game herb. Found in large clusters in the fields southwest of Hernand (follow the road from Hernand's south gate, look for red-flowered plants). Each flower sells for 5-15 silver to an alchemist. A full gathering run yields 40-60 silver worth of flowers. Respawn: approximately 1 in-game week.

Herbs (Glowroot, Sunpetal) — found along riverbanks and in shaded areas. Glowroot sells for 12-18 silver each, Sunpetal for 20-30 silver each. Less dense than Red Bacca but higher per-item value.

Common bugs and insects — found in the same areas as flowers. They sell for 1-3 silver each. Not a fortune, but picking them up while gathering herbs adds 10-20% to your route income.

Meat from hunted animals — you do not need to be a hunter to gather meat. Kill wolves, deer, or boar that attack you during travel, and skin them for meat (sells for 2-3 silver each).

Pro Tip: Always gather while traveling between quest objectives. Your travel time is "free" time — by gathering along the way, you effectively double your hourly income without spending extra time. Set a goal: "Every time I travel to a new area, I will fill 5 inventory slots with gathered resources before arriving."

3 Complete Easy Bounties

The Hernand notice board (located in the central square) offers bounty contracts that reward silver, items, and reputation. For beginners, not all bounties are worthwhile — some are too time-consuming or require travel to dangerous areas. Here is how to pick the right bounties.

Best Beginner Bounties

"Jeffrey's Bandit Problem" — a low-level bounty that sends you to clear a small bandit camp near Hernand. The bandits are level 5-8, the camp is a 5-minute walk from Hernand, and the reward is 10-15 silver plus a chance at a Thieves' Mask drop from Jeffrey himself. This is arguably the best first bounty in the game.

"Wolf Infestation" — kill 5-8 wolves in the Northern Woods. The wolves drop pelts (8-12 silver each) in addition to the bounty reward, making this a double-income bounty. The wolves are very easy to kill with starting gear.

"Delivery to Greymane Camp" — a simple courier quest that pays 5-10 silver for walking to the nearby camp. No combat required. Good for filling time between other activities.

Bounty Warning: Read the bounty description carefully before accepting. Some bounties require you to travel to areas you have not unlocked yet, or to kill enemies 10+ levels above you. If a bounty seems too hard, abandon it and pick an easier one. The penalty for abandoning is minimal.

4 Rob the Hernand Bank Storeroom

This is the single best "first thing to do" for money in Crimson Desert. It requires minimal setup, takes only a few minutes, and yields 30-55 silver plus items. Here is the exact step-by-step.

🏦 Bank Heist: Step by Step

Step 1 — Get a Mask: You need a Thieves' Mask. Buy one from Grimrak, the back-alley merchant in Hernand (southeast of the church, down an alley) for 10 copper. Alternatively, kill Jeffrey (from the bounty in Tip #3) for a guaranteed mask drop.

Step 2 — Get a Key: Also buy from Grimrak for 30 copper, or loot from enemies. Do not attempt the heist without the key — you cannot pick the lock.

Step 3 — Manual Save: Save your game before entering the bank. If you get caught, reload.

Step 4 — Enter the Bank: The bank is the large building on the north side of Hernand's central square. Equip your mask.

Step 5 — Open the Locked Door: On the left side of the bank interior, there is a locked door. Use your key to open it. Close the door behind you.

Step 6 — Steal Everything: Inside are chests and safes. Open all of them. Each contains 1-5 silver plus random items. Watch the theft meter in the top-right corner — stay hidden until it disappears.

Step 7 — Leave and Collect: Exit the room, leave the bank, and open the coin pouches and silver bags from your inventory. Use "Use All" to open them all at once.

Pro Tip: The bank chests refill every 2-3 in-game days, but the safes do not refresh. After the initial heist, you can return every few days for 10-20 silver from the chests. Mark the bank on your map and make it a regular stop.

5 Steal the Lioncrest Manor Gold Bar

Lioncrest Manor (northwest of Hernand) contains a Gold Bar worth 500 silver — a life-changing amount for a beginner. This is the single highest-value item you can obtain in your first few hours.

👑 Gold Bar Heist: Step by Step

Step 1 — Prerequisites: You need a Thieves' Mask (from Tip #4). No key required for this one.

Step 2 — Location: Lioncrest Manor is northwest of Hernand. Follow the road north out of town, then west at the fork. The manor is a large building with a fenced courtyard.

Step 3 — Enter Through the Rear: Instead of the front gate, circle around to the back of the manor. Look for a wooden window on the second floor. Climb up, open the window, and enter.

Step 4 — Navigate to the Fireplace: Go downstairs to the main hall. The gold bar is on the fireplace mantle. A guard patrols this room.

Step 5 — Manual Save: Save before trying to steal. The guard's patrol route is predictable — he walks from the main hall toward the right corridor, pauses, then returns.

Step 6 — Steal: When the guard turns toward the right corridor, quickly grab the gold bar from the fireplace. Then immediately hide in the corner to the left of the fireplace. Wait for the theft meter to disappear.

Step 7 — Escape: Exit the way you came (upstairs, out the window). Do not run — walking is less suspicious.

Step 8 — Sell to Banker: Take the gold bar to Banker Grover in Hernand (north side of town). He will exchange it for 500 silver.

Critical: If you get caught, the guards will attack you and you will accumulate a bounty debt. Escape if possible (run out of the zone and wait for the wanted meter to expire). If you cannot escape, reload your manual save. Do NOT try to fight the guards at low level — you will die.

6 Mine Diamonds at Hernand Highlands Cavern

Diamond mining is the safest consistent income method for beginners. No combat is required if you are careful, and the diamonds sell for good silver.

💎 Diamond Mining Guide

Prerequisite: Complete the "Rhett's Request" mining tutorial quest (available in Hernand) to obtain a pickaxe. If you skipped it, buy a pickaxe from a general goods merchant for a few copper.

Location: Hernand Highlands Cavern — located west of Unicorn Cliff, south of Hernand. The entrance is a cave opening in the cliff face. Look for glowing crystal formations near the entrance.

What to Mine: Diamond deposits (sparkling white crystals) yield 2-3 diamonds each. The cavern contains approximately 10 diamond deposits. Each diamond sells for ~2.28 silver. Total haul: ~23 silver from a single run.

Also Mine: Stalactite deposits (brown formations) yield common ore worth ~57 copper each. There are approximately 24 stalactite deposits in the cavern. Total haul: ~14 silver extra.

Estimated Total: ~37-40 silver for a 5-10 minute mining run. That is ~220-240 silver per hour, making this one of the best beginner activities.

Respawn: Diamond deposits respawn slowly. Visit every 2-3 in-game days for the best results.

Pro Tip: The Hernand Highlands Cavern has no aggressive enemies near the diamond deposits. However, deeper in the cave, there are bats and spiders. Stick to the entrance area — the diamonds near the entrance are more than enough for a full run. If you do venture deeper, bring a weapon and healing potions.

7 Play Archery & Duo for Quick Cash

Two mini-games in Hernand offer reliable, risk-free income for beginners. Neither requires good gear or combat skill — just a bit of patience and (for Duo) some luck.

Archery Competition (North Hernand)

The archery range is located just north of Hernand's main gate. The entry fee is 80 copper, and each round takes about 2-3 minutes. If you are decent at aiming, you will earn approximately 1.2 silver profit per round. Key tip: after each shot, move your crosshair back to center screen — this makes the next target acquisition faster. The archery game can be repeated infinitely and is completely safe. Use this as a "filler" activity when you have 5 minutes of downtime.

Duo Card Game (Hernand Inn, Second Floor)

The Duo card game is located in the Hernand Inn (the building where you had the wrestling match earlier). Go upstairs and find the card table. Entry fee: 15 silver. The game is similar to poker — you are dealt two sticks (cards) of different colors and numbers, and you compare your hand to the opponent's hand ranking.

♠️ Duo Strategy for Maximum Profit

1. Manual save outside the inn. This is critical — you will reload when you get a bad hand.

2. Enter the Duo table. Check your hand ranking. If your hand is 5 points or higher, go all-in. The AI opponent frequently calls with weak hands, meaning you win 50-200+ silver per good hand.

3. If your hand is below 5 points, reload your save. Do not waste silver on bad hands.

4. If you lose a good hand (the AI occasionally gets lucky), reload. Consistent play with this strategy yields 3-4x your starting capital within 10-15 minutes.

5. Watch for cheating: If the opponent glows blue, they are cheating. Accuse them after observing 3 times — you will permanently unlock the "cheating" ability and win bonus silver.

Pro Tip: Use the Duo game as a "money multiplier" after you have accumulated 30-50 silver from other methods. Starting with 15 silver, one good Duo session can turn that into 100-200+ silver, which then funds bag purchases, better gear, and more farming. Do not do Duo as your first money method — build a small bank first.

8 Farm Fundamentalist Goblin Masks

Once you have basic combat gear (any weapon will do), the Fundamentalist Goblin camp is the best combat-based money maker for beginners. It is accessible, safe, and profitable.

👺 Goblin Mask Farm

Location: Sunrise Plains, south of Greymane Camp (accessible from Hernand via the southern road, just past Greymane Camp). Look for the goblin encampment with crude wooden structures and bonfires.

Enemies: Fundamentalist Goblins — level 3-6, very weak, easy to kill with starting gear. They attack in groups of 3-5 but have low health and predictable patterns.

Loot: Crude Devil Masks — approximately 25% drop rate, each sells for ~13.71 silver. Also drops: occasional copper/silver coins, low-tier weapons.

Strategy: Clear the camp, loot all masks, then leave the area (walk 100+ meters away) and return. The goblins respawn when the area unloads and reloads. Do not kill the camp boss (if there is one) — that may trigger a different respawn behavior.

Expected Income: 10 minutes of farming yields 60-100 silver. Per hour: 300-400+ silver. This is the best combat money maker available to level 1-10 players.

Risk: Near zero. The goblins are very weak. The only danger is pulling too many at once, but even then, you can retreat and heal.

9 Pickpocket Nobles for Gold Bars

Once you have a Thieves' Mask and some confidence, Noble Pickpocketing is the highest-potential money maker for early-game players. The skill ceiling is higher than other methods, but the rewards are proportionally better.

🎭 Noble Pickpocketing Guide

Best Location: Oakenshield Manor, central Hernand. Between 11 AM and 2 PM in-game time, 3-10 nobles gather in the front courtyard. This is the highest density of noble NPCs in the early game.

Equipment: Thieves' Mask (required for all stealing).

Method: Equip your mask, then use your lantern (press R1/RB/Ctrl depending on platform) to scan nobles. Look for the gold bar icon above their head — this indicates they are carrying a Gold Bar worth 500 silver. If you see only a coin pouch icon, that noble has only pocket change — skip them.

Technique: Approach the noble from behind, knock them down (non-lethal), and pickpocket the gold bar. Immediately run away from the area and hide until the wanted meter expires. The gold bar is now yours.

Save Scumming: Manual save at Oakenshield Manor at 11 AM. Scan all nobles. If nobody has a gold bar, reload the save. Repeat until you find a gold-carrying noble. This reduces the "finding" time from 40-60 minutes to 5-15 minutes.

Expected Income: Variable, but with save scumming, you can reliably get 1-2 gold bars per 15 minutes = 500-1000 silver per 15 minutes = 2000-4000 silver per hour. This is the highest possible silver per hour for any beginner method.

Warning: Pickpocketing reduces your reputation with the faction the noble belongs to. If you overdo it in one area, you may temporarily lock yourself out of faction shops and quests. Rotate between Oakenshield Manor, Lioncrest Manor, and the Hernand church to spread the reputation loss. Do not pickpocket more than 3-4 nobles in the same day.

10 Expand Your Inventory Immediately

Your starting inventory has only 24 slots. Every slot you add increases your farming efficiency by 4-5% (more slots = more loot per run = fewer trips to town). Inventory expansion is the single best investment you can make in your first 10 hours.

How to Expand

Buy Small Bags: Available from almost every general goods merchant for a few copper. A Small Bag adds 2-4 inventory slots. Buy 2-3 small bags immediately after your first bank heist. Cost: negligible. Benefit: immediate.

Complete Hernand Side Quests: Several Hernand quests reward inventory expansions. Look for quests involving delivery, gathering, and helping townsfolk. Each quest-based expansion is "free" (costs only your time) and permanent.

Find Medium Bags: Medium Bags (4-6 slots) can be found in chests, dropped by mini-bosses, or purchased from specialty vendors. Keep an eye out — they are not common, but they are a massive upgrade.

Check Camp Storage: Your camp's personal storage chest (unlocked during story progression) is not inventory expansion per se, but it serves the same function — you can dump materials and items there, freeing your inventory for loot.

Pro Tip: Your target inventory size by hour 10 should be 35+ slots. This allows you to do full farming runs without needing to return to town mid-run. Every inventory slot below 35 is lost profit. Make bag hunting a priority equal to money making.

🚫 Bonus: Avoid These Money Traps

Knowing what NOT to do is just as important as knowing what to do. Here are the most common money traps that beginners fall into.

❌ Trap 1: Buying Premium Gear Too Early

Weapons and armor from vendors are overpriced compared to what you find from enemies and quests. A "Fine Steel Sword" from a blacksmith costs 200-300 silver, but you can find an equally good weapon from the Fort Perwin or Hernand Highlands cave for free. Do not buy gear from vendors unless it is a unique item you cannot find elsewhere. Spend your silver on bags, tools, and consumables instead.

❌ Trap 2: Over-Upgrading Low-Tier Gear

Upgrading a common (white) weapon from +0 to +3 costs upgrade stones and silver. Those upgrade stones are scarce and would be much better spent on a rare (blue) or unique weapon later. Rule of thumb: only upgrade gear that you will still be using 10+ hours from now. Common and uncommon gear is temporary — use it, replace it, sell it. Do not invest upgrade stones in it.

❌ Trap 3: Dying With Full Inventory

When you die, you drop your death cache at the death location. If you die again before recovering it, the original cache is lost forever. Beginners frequently die in dangerous areas while carrying hours of farming loot. The fix: return to town and deposit your loot in storage before exploring dangerous areas. Never carry more than you are willing to lose.

❌ Trap 4: Selling Gold Ore to Vendors

Raw Gold Ore sells for ~3 silver each. Three Gold Ore + one Golden Apple crafts into a Gold Bar worth 500 silver. Selling Gold Ore raw means you lose 98% of its potential value. Never sell Gold Ore to vendors — store it until you can craft it into bars.

❌ Trap 5: Ignoring the Bank Investment System

Many beginners never discover the bank investment system at Hernand Bank. Once you have 10-15 Gold Bars (5,000-7,500 silver), you can invest in the "Medium Risk" option for 15-20% returns every 3 in-game days. That is 75-275 silver per cycle with zero effort. This is passive income that accrues while you are doing other activities. Do not ignore it — set it up as soon as you have enough capital.

❌ Trap 6: Fast Travel Everywhere

Fast travel costs 10-50 silver per use depending on distance. While convenient, these costs add up quickly. In your first 10 hours, walk between nearby locations and save fast travel for emergencies or long-distance journeys. You can easily save 50-100 silver per play session by walking instead of fast-traveling.

❌ Trap 7: Not Checking Coin Pouches

Coin pouches and silver bags drop from enemies and are found in chests. You must open them from your inventory — they cannot be sold directly to vendors. Many beginners sell items to vendors without realizing their coin pouches contain 5-20+ silver each. Always open coin pouches immediately after looting them using the "Use All" feature.

❓ FAQ

What is the very first thing I should do for money when I reach Hernand?

Buy a Thieves' Mask (10 copper) and a key (30 copper) from Grimrak in the back alley, then rob the Hernand Bank storeroom. This takes 5 minutes and gives you 30-55 silver plus items. Then use that silver to buy a Small Bag and some healing potions. Then proceed to Lioncrest Manor for the 500-silver gold bar.

How much silver should I have by hour 5?

A well-played beginner should have 200-400 silver by hour 5. If you did the bank heist, Lioncrest gold bar, some goblin mask farming, and a few bounties, you should be at 600-800+ silver. A great start (with Noble Pickpocketing) can reach 1,500-2,000 silver by hour 5. Below 200 silver means you are spending too much and farming too little.

Is the Duo card game worth the 15 silver entry fee for a beginner?

Yes, but only after you have a 30-50 silver bankroll. If you only have 15 silver total, losing the Duo game puts you at zero. Build a small reserve first, then use Duo as a "multiplier" to turn 30-50 silver into 100-200+ silver. With the save scumming strategy described in Tip #7, Duo is very profitable.

How do I avoid getting caught when stealing?

Three rules: (1) Always equip your Thieves' Mask before stealing. (2) Make sure no NPC is looking directly at you — pay attention to their head direction and the yellow "eye" icon above their head. (3) After stealing, hide immediately and wait for the theft meter to fully disappear before moving. If the meter fills completely, you are caught and must escape or reload.

Should I buy a weapon from a vendor or find one in the world?

Find one in the world. The first "free" upgrade weapon comes from the Fort Perwin area (Chapter 3) and from the Hernand Highlands cave. Vendor weapons are 2-3x overpriced. If you must buy, purchase the cheapest uncommon weapon available and save your silver for bags and tools instead.

What skills should I invest in first for better money making?

Priority 1: "Appraisal" in the Spirit skill tree (increases vendor sell prices by up to 25%). Priority 2: "Gathering Speed" in the Stamina tree (30% faster gathering). Priority 3: "Movement Speed" nodes (faster travel between farming spots). These three skills directly improve your silver per hour more than any combat skill in the early game.

Can I play Crimson Desert without ever stealing?

Yes, absolutely. Stealing is the fastest money method, but it is not required. You can make good silver through gathering (Tip #2), bounty hunting (Tip #3), goblin mask farming (Tip #8), and diamond mining (Tip #6). Stealing simply accelerates your early game economy. If you prefer to play a "lawful" character, skip Tips #4, #5, and #9 and focus on the other seven tips.

What is the best item to sell for consistent early-game income?

Crude Devil Masks from goblins (Tip #8) are the best consistent income item. They have a high drop rate (~25%), sell for ~13.71 silver each, and the goblins are very easy to kill. A 10-minute run yields 60-100 silver. If you want non-combat income, Red Bacca Flowers (Tip #2) are the best option — safe to gather, good sell price, and abundant near Hernand.
