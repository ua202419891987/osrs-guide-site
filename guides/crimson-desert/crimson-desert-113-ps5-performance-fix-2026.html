Crimson Desert 1.13 Patch PS5 Performance Regression Fix

After the 1.13.00 update (July 3–4, 2026), many PS5 and PS5 Pro players reported worse frame rates than before. This guide explains the official known issue and gives you concrete fixes you can apply today — on console and PC.

🎮 Performance Fix · 8 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 1.13.00 (July 3–4, 2026) caused frame rate regression on PS5 / PS5 Pro.
  • 📌 Official known issue: "frame rate drop or game crash under certain conditions" is under investigation.
  • 📌 1.13.01 hotfix only partially fixed it — not fully resolved.
  • 📌 PS5 fix now: use Performance Mode, disable FSR/frame gen, enable VRR, lower shadows.
  • 📌 PC fix now: rollback to 1.12 via Steam beta branch; update GPU driver.

1. What Happened in 1.13

Pearl Abyss shipped 1.13.00 on July 3–4, 2026. Almost immediately, PS5 and PS5 Pro players reported that the game ran worse than on 1.12 — noticeable frame drops and stutter in towns, combat, and cutscenes. Independent performance analyses (Ali Mehdi, Reforge Gaming) measured combat FPS dropping into the low 20s on base PS5.

⚠️ Not Just You

This is a widespread regression, not a hardware fault on your end. The official known-issues board lists it explicitly.

2. Official Response

Pearl Abyss added this to the official notice (boardNo=68):

📋 Known Issue (verbatim summary)

"Under certain conditions, frame rate may drop or the game may crash. We are investigating and will address it in a future update."

The 1.13.01 hotfix (July) patch notes stated it "optimized frame rate drop in some environments" — but multiple players confirmed the regression was not fully fixed. A proper resolution is expected in 1.13.02.

3. How to Fix on PS5 / PS5 Pro

Consoles cannot roll back, so your only lever is in-game settings. Try these in order:

① Use Performance Mode, Not Resolution Mode

In Settings → Graphics, pick Performance Mode. Resolution Mode prioritizes 4K and tanks FPS on PS5.

② Disable FSR / Frame Generation

Temporarily turn off FSR upscaling and frame generation. On some PS5 builds these introduce stutter rather than help.

③ Enable VRR (if your TV supports it)

VRR smooths inconsistent frame delivery. Turn it on in both PS5 system settings and the in-game display options.

④ Lower Shadow Quality

Shadows are a major FPS cost in 1.13. Drop shadow quality to Medium or Low.

💡 Reality Check

These settings reduce the regression but may not restore 1.12-level performance until 1.13.02 ships. Hang tight — it is being investigated.

4. How to Fix on PC

PC players have one big advantage: you can roll back.

🖥️ Roll Back to 1.12 (Steam)

  1. Open Steam → Library → Crimson Desert.
  2. Right-click → Properties → Betas.
  3. Select the 1.12 beta branch (if offered) → let it download.
  4. Verify the build date shows pre-July-3 in the main menu.

Update GPU Driver

Separately from the patch, some AMD drivers (26.6.2+) crash the game. Update to the latest stable driver from your vendor.

6. When to Expect a Real Fix

Pearl Abyss has acknowledged the regression and listed it as under investigation. Based on the 1.13.01 partial fix, a fuller resolution is expected in 1.13.02. Track the official news page for the exact date.

🔔 Stay Updated

Check the in-game notice board and the official Crimson Desert news page weekly. Do not trust "fix" mods — they risk your account and save data.

7. FAQs

Will a factory reset on my PS5 fix it?

No. This is a software regression in 1.13, not a console problem. A reset will not change FPS.

Can I roll back on PS5?

No. Console versions are fixed by the publisher. You must wait for 1.13.02.

Is my PS5 broken?

Almost certainly not. The regression is reproducible across many consoles and is on Pearl Abyss's known-issue list.

Does the PC rollback lose my save?

Generally no — saves are separate from the game build. But back up your save folder before switching branches, just in case.
