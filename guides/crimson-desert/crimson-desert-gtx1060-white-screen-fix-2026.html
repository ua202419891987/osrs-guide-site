Crimson Desert GTX 1060 White Screen on Launch — Fixed

You click Play, the launcher disappears, and Crimson Desert opens to a solid white (or blank) screen that never loads. If you are on an NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1060, this is a known, fixable combination of driver and rendering issues. Follow the five steps below, built specifically for the 6GB GTX 1060.

🎮 White Screen Fix · Novice Level · 7 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 The GTX 1060 white screen is usually a driver + DX12 conflict, not a broken GPU.
  • 📌 Roll back to a stable Game Ready driver (or a clean-install the 560/550 branch).
  • 📌 Force DX11 instead of DX12 — the 1060 is far more stable on DX11.
  • 📌 Verify game files to rule out a corrupted shader cache.
  • 📌 Drop texture quality and use borderless windowed to stop the blank launch.

🔍 1. Why the GTX 1060 Shows a White Screen

A white or blank screen at launch means the renderer initialized the window but never drew the first frame. On a GeForce GTX 1060 (6GB, Pascal architecture from 2016) this almost always comes down to three things working against each other:

🧩 The Usual Suspects

  1. A too-new or buggy GPU driver. Crimson Desert leans hard on Unreal Engine 5 and modern DX12 features. The latest "Game Ready" driver is not always the most stable for a 9-year-old card like the 1060.
  2. DX12 initialization failure. The 1060 supports DX12, but UE5's DX12 path can stall on first shader compile and present as a white screen.
  3. Corrupted shader cache or game files. A botched update leaves broken shaders that the 1060 cannot recover from without a verify.

The good news: none of these mean your card is dead. The GTX 1060 still runs Crimson Desert at 1080p — it just needs the right driver and rendering path. If your screen goes white right after the splash, start with Step 1.

💡 Quick Test

Press Alt + Enter a few times right after the white screen appears. If the game flickers or resizes, it is a display-mode issue — jump straight to Step 5 (windowed mode).

📉 2. Step 1 — Roll Back Your GPU Driver

This is the single most effective fix for the GTX 1060 white screen. Many players fixed it simply by moving off the newest driver to a known-stable one.

① Open Device Manager → Roll Back

  1. Press Win + X → Device Manager.
  2. Expand Display adapters → right-click NVIDIA GeForce GTX 1060.
  3. Properties → Driver tab → Roll Back Driver (greyed out? then use the clean-install method below).
  4. Reboot and try launching Crimson Desert.

② Clean Install a Stable Game Ready Driver

If rollback is unavailable, download the previous stable Game Ready driver from NVIDIA's site (the 560 or 550 branch is a safe bet for the 1060 in 2026). Run the installer and choose Custom → Clean install. This wipes the corrupted shader cache and resets profiles that conflict with Crimson Desert.

⚠️ Avoid Studio Drivers for Gaming

Studio drivers are tuned for creative apps, not UE5 games. Stick to Game Ready branches for Crimson Desert on the GTX 1060.

After the clean install, launch the game once more. If the white screen is gone but you still see stutter, continue to Step 2 — the DX12 path is likely the remaining culprit.

🎮 3. Step 2 — Switch Between DX11 and DX12

Crimson Desert lets you pick the rendering API. On the GTX 1060, DX11 is dramatically more stable than DX12, even though DX12 promises better efficiency. A failed DX12 init is the most common cause of the white launch screen on this card.

🖥️ How to Force DX11

  1. Open the Steam library → right-click Crimson Desert → Properties.
  2. Under General → Launch Options, type: -dx11
  3. Close properties and launch the game.
  4. No launch-option support? Open the in-game settings (once it loads) → Graphics → set API / Rendering to DirectX 11.
💡 DX11 vs DX12 on the 1060

DX11 gives you reliable launches and roughly the same 1080p frame rate on a GTX 1060. DX12 can edge out a few FPS later in towns, but the risk of a white-screen launch is not worth it on this card. Stay on DX11 until you upgrade.

If you must test DX12, do it only after the game launches cleanly on DX11. Most 1060 users report the white screen returns under DX12 after a driver update — so keep -dx11 pinned in launch options.

🛠️ 4. Step 3 — Verify Game Files

Even with the right driver and API, a corrupted shader or missing file can leave the 1060 stuck on a white frame. Verifying the install replaces broken assets without touching your save.

① Steam File Verification

  1. Steam → Library → Crimson Desert → right-click → Properties.
  2. Installed Files (or Local Files) → Verify integrity of game files.
  3. Wait for the check. Steam redownloads anything mismatched.
  4. Relaunch with -dx11 still set.

② Clear the Shader Cache Manually

If verification does not help, delete the local shader cache so UE5 rebuilds it on a clean 1060 profile:

  • Close the game and Steam.
  • Open %LOCALAPPDATA% and look for the Crimson Desert / Unreal folder; delete any ShaderCache or DerivedDataCache subfolders.
  • Reboot and launch — the first load will be slower while it recompiles, but the white screen should be gone.
🔄 When to Re-Verify

Re-run verification after every Crimson Desert patch. The 1060 is sensitive to partially-downloaded updates, which is a frequent white-screen trigger right after a new version drops.

🎨 5. Step 4 — Lower Texture Settings

The GTX 1060 has only 6GB of VRAM. Crimson Desert's high/ultra texture packs can exceed what the 1060 can map at launch, and when VRAM is exhausted the renderer can stall to a white frame before the first scene composites.

① Set Textures to Medium

In Graphics → Texture Quality, choose Medium. This keeps the game looking good at 1080p while staying safely under the 6GB ceiling. Reserve High only if you also drop resolution scale and shadows.

② Trim VRAM-Hungry Extras

  • Virtual Textures: keep on, but pair with Medium textures.
  • Ray-traced reflections / GI: OFF on the 1060 — these alone can starve VRAM and white-screen the launch.
  • Resolution Scale: 100% (do not push above; supersampling eats VRAM).
  • Shadow Quality: Medium or Low.
⚠️ Do Not Max Textures on 6GB

If you set textures to Ultra on a GTX 1060, expect either a white launch screen or a crash within minutes of loading a dense area. Medium is the sweet spot for this card.

🪟 6. Step 5 — Fullscreen vs Windowed Mode

The final common trigger is the display mode itself. On the GTX 1060, exclusive fullscreen sometimes fails to acquire the display correctly and presents a white canvas. Switching to a windowed mode forces the desktop compositor to draw the frame, bypassing the stall.

🖼️ Recommended: Borderless Windowed

  1. If the game loads at all, open Settings → Display → set Window Mode = Borderless.
  2. If it only white-screens, use the -windowed launch option (add it next to -dx11): -dx11 -windowed.
  3. Once it launches reliably in borderless, you can try exclusive fullscreen again — but keep borderless as your fallback.
💡 Why Borderless Helps the 1060

Borderless windowed lets Windows handle the frame presentation. The 1060's older display engine is more reliable through the compositor than through exclusive fullscreen hand-off, which is exactly where many white screens happen.

Combine -dx11 -windowed with a clean driver and Medium textures and the GTX 1060 white screen is, in the vast majority of cases, fully resolved.

❓ 8. FAQs

Is my GTX 1060 broken if I get a white screen?

Almost certainly not. A white launch screen on the 1060 is overwhelmingly a driver/DX12/VRAM issue, not hardware failure. Try the driver rollback and DX11 switch first — they fix the large majority of cases.

Which driver version should I roll back to?

Use the previous stable Game Ready branch (the 560 or 550 series is a reliable choice for the 1060 in 2026). Always pick Clean install so the old shader cache and profiles are wiped.

Will DX11 look worse than DX12 on my 1060?

Visually, the difference at 1080p is minimal. DX11 loses a little efficiency but delivers the same image quality and, crucially, a reliable launch on the GTX 1060. The stability win is worth far more than a couple of FPS.

Does lowering texture settings actually prevent the white screen?

Yes — indirectly. Ultra textures can push the 6GB 1060 past its VRAM ceiling at launch, which stalls the renderer before the first frame. Medium textures keep headroom and let the game present normally.

I did all five steps and it still white-screens. Now what?

Check Windows for pending updates and confirm your display cable is seated. Then visit our Crash Fix Guide 2026 for deeper D3D12 and UE5 fatal-error troubleshooting, and the GPU Driver Guide 2026 for card-specific driver advice.
