⚔️ 1. Primary Weapon & Secondary Weapon Loadout

Your weapon choice is the single biggest factor in boss fight performance. A poorly matched weapon can make a fight twice as hard as it needs to be. Here is the optimal setup for your first boss encounters:

① For Your First Boss (Staglord) — Recommended Loadout

SlotRecommendedAlternativeWhy
PRIMARYIron Longsword +2 (upgraded)Steel LongswordBalanced speed/power; good parry window; all combos viable
SECONDARYHunter's AxePolearm (for reach)Axe deals highest damage per hit during stagger windows when Staglord can't retaliate
Before any boss fight: Repair ALL weapons at a workbench or blacksmith. Weapons at below 70% durability deal reduced damage (approximately -1% damage per 1% durability lost). Fighting a boss with a damaged weapon is fighting with one hand tied behind your back.

② When to Swap Weapons Based on Boss Type

  • Fast/Aggressive bosses (Matthias): Primary = Dual Daggers or Longsword (fast attacks). You need speed to keep up with their attack rhythm.
  • Slow/Hard-Hitting bosses (Staglord Phase 2+): Primary = Greatsword or Axe. Their slow attacks give you time for heavy combo finishers.
  • Flying/Ranged bosses (Tenebrum): Primary = Polearm (reach) or Longsword (reliable). You need gap-closing ability for when they fly away.
  • Magic/AOE bosses (Umbra): Primary = Any weapon with high stability shield. Greatsword has the best block stability for absorbing magic projectiles while closing distance.

🎒 3. Consumable Inventory Checklist

Before ANY boss fight, verify you have these items in your inventory. If any are missing, go buy/craft them first. Entering a boss arena under-stocked is the #1 preventable cause of deaths among new players.

1

Minimum Consumable Requirements Per Boss Attempt

  1. Small Healing Potions × 5 minimum (10 recommended for first-timers)
  2. Stamina Potions × 2 (only needed for long fights like Tenebrum; optional for Staglord)
  3. Antidote × 1 (many bosses apply poison status in phase 2+; don't get caught without it)
  4. Buff Food × 1 cooked item (Hunter's Ration for ATK+ or Ironbread for DEF+) — see our Camp Cooking guide
  5. Empty backpack slots × 5 minimum — boss fights drop loot that fills your bag; if full, auto-loot fails silently

③ How Many Healing Potions Do You Actually Need?

This depends entirely on your parry accuracy and how well you know the boss pattern:

  • First attempt ever (pattern unknown): 10–12 potions. Expect to take hits as you learn. Better to over-prepare than under-prepare.
  • Second attempt (you know the pattern now): 6–8 potions. You will take significantly fewer hits because you know what to expect.
  • Speedrun attempt (pattern mastered): 2–3 potions (for emergency only). Elite players beat Staglord with 0 potions used by perfect-parrying every attack.

🌲 4. Skill Tree Setup Before Boss Fight

Your Keen Senses skill tree configuration matters enormously for boss fights. Here is the recommended skill priority allocation for players approaching their first major boss encounter (around level 6–8):

"Boss Fighter" Skill Allocation (First 20 Abyss Artifacts)

Row 1 (must-have, spend first 8 points here):
• Parry Extension (+3 frames to parry window) — 3 points
• Quick Recovery (stamina regenerates 25% faster when standing still) — 3 points
• First Strike (first hit after parry deals +20% damage) — 2 points

Row 2 (next priority, 8 points):
• Spirit Surge Unlock — 4 points (REQUIRED for parry→surge combo)
• Armor Breaker (heavy attacks ignore 15% enemy defense) — 2 points
• Evasion Instinct (perfect dodge Adrenaline buff lasts 20s instead of 15s) — 2 points

Row 3 (remaining 4 points):
• Spirit Efficiency (spirit skills cost 15% less) — 4 points

Total: 20 Abyss Artifacts spent. This build maximizes defensive options (parry/dodge) while unlocking the core offensive tool (Spirit Surge).

❓ FAQ — Gear Prep Questions Answered

Q: What if I can't afford the full iron armor set?

Buy pieces in this order: Chest (most DEF per silver) → Legs → Helmet → Gloves (least impact on survival). Even having just the Chest and Legs piece gives you 60% of the full set's benefit for 55% of the cost. Fill the remaining slots later when you have more silver from quest rewards and looting.

Q: Should I upgrade my weapon or my armor first?

Weapon upgrade first, always. Here is why: better weapon means enemies die faster (fewer opportunities for them to hit you), which effectively reduces the damage you take indirectly. Upgrading armor only reduces damage AFTER it lands. Preventing damage through faster kills beats reducing damage after the fact. Upgrade weapon to +2, then upgrade armor.

Q: Is there an ideal HP threshold before attempting Staglord?

For a comfortable first attempt, aim for 350+ HP (check character screen). Below 250 HP and some of Staglord's Phase 2 combos can one-shot-combo you from full health. Between 250–350 HP, those same combos leave you critically low but survivable with a potion. Above 400 HP and you have significant room for error even on sloppy attempts.

Q: Can I change gear mid-boss fight?

No. Once you enter a boss arena (the door locks behind you), your entire loadout is locked until the fight ends (victory or defeat). This is why checking your gear BEFORE entering is so important. The game does allow you to consume potions and use equipped items during the fight, but no weapon/armor/accessory swaps.
