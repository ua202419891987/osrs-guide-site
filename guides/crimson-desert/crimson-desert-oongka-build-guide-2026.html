Oongka is the third playable character in Crimson Desert â?a massive orc warrior who fights with pure, uncompromising power. Unlike Kliff's technical finesse or Damiane's lightning speed, Oongka's combat philosophy is simple: stand your ground, absorb the hit, and hit back twice as hard. With the highest base HP in the game (1,125 HP â?significantly more than Kliff or Damiane), Oongka is designed to be the team's unstoppable frontline juggernaut.

Oongka exclusively uses two-handed weapons â?Greataxes, Greathammers, and Arm Cannons. His signature mechanic is Dual-Wielding Mastery (unlocked at skill level 4), which allows him to wield two greataxes or greathammers simultaneously, creating devastating sweeping attacks. His unique Rage ability grants super armor, letting him tank through boss attacks and continue his combos uninterrupted. For mobility, Oongka uses a Jetpack instead of climbing or double-jumping, which fits his heavy-hitting, ground-bound playstyle.

Playstyle Summary: Slow, deliberate, and devastating. Oongka does not dodge â?he tanks. He does not parry â?he powers through. His combat loop is: activate Rage for super armor, unleash heavy-hitting combos, use Arm Cannon for ranged pressure, and reset with Quaking Fury when the enemy is staggered. His weakness is mobility â?fast, teleporting bosses can give Oongka trouble, but his damage output and survivability more than compensate.

Dual-Wielding: How It Works

At Dual Wielding Mastery Lv4, Oongka can equip two two-handed weapons simultaneously. This changes his light attack chain to wide, sweeping arcs that hit everything in a 180-degree arc in front of him. Heavy attacks become double strikes (both weapons hit at once). The damage output increase is roughly 60-80% compared to single-wielding, at the cost of 25% higher stamina consumption per attack. This is why stamina management is critical on dual-wielding Oongka â?you are trading endurance for speed of kills.

â?FAQ

Q: Is Oongka better than Kliff for endgame?

It depends on the content. Kliff has higher theoretical single-target DPS and is more mobile, making him better against highly mobile bosses. Oongka has higher effective DPS in practice because his Rage super armor lets him maintain 100% uptime â?Kliff must stop to dodge or parry, losing DPS windows. For Abyss Sanctum and world bosses, Oongka is generally considered easier and more consistent. For speedruns and crit-fishing, Kliff (Vow of the Dead King build) edges ahead.

Q: What is the best weapon for Oongka?

The Hellhound's Chopping Axe is best-in-slot for both Siege Titan and Raging Spin builds. It has innate Crit Rate Lv2 and 5 Abyss Core slots. Pair it with the Orc Blaster (or upgraded Finest Rhinard Cannon) for ranged utility. If you prefer the Mountain Breaker build, the Bekker Greathammer (ATK 45) is your best option.

Q: How do I manage stamina on Oongka?

Stamina management is Oongka's biggest challenge. Dual-wielding consumes 25% more stamina per attack. Follow these rules: (1) Never drop below 20% stamina. (2) Use unarmed strikes between combos to recover stamina (they cost zero stamina). (3) Equip accessories with stamina recovery (Hernandian Signet, Delesyian Signet). (4) Take the Stamina recovery nodes in the Stamina skill tree early. (5) Use Raging Lightning in short bursts (3-4 seconds) rather than holding it until empty.

Q: Can I skip Rage and rely on HP instead?

No. Rage is not optional â?it is Oongka's core defensive mechanic. Without Rage, Oongka can be interrupted mid-swing by any enemy attack. His slow attack animations make him vulnerable to trading. Rage's super armor ensures your attacks always land. Even if you invest maximum points in Health, an interrupted attack deals zero damage. Always unlock Rage and keep it active during combat. The skill cooldown is manageable with proper core investment.

Q: What is Oongka's weakness?

High-mobility bosses are Oongka's counter. Bosses that teleport, fly, or have long-range phases (some Abyss Sanctum encounters) force Oongka to chase, wasting his Rage uptime. His Jetpack mobility is inferior to Kliff's dashes and Damiane's double-jump. For these encounters, consider using the Arm Cannon more liberally or switching to Kliff. Additionally, enemies that apply movement-impairing effects (slows, roots) are disproportionately dangerous for Oongka.

Q: Should I dual-wield or single-wield?

Dual-wield at all times once you unlock Dual Wielding Mastery Lv4. The damage increase (60-80%) far outweighs the stamina penalty (25%). The only exception is when you need the Arm Cannon for ranged pressure â?in that case, keep the Cannon in your off-hand slot and only switch to dual-wield when engaging in melee. The stamina cost of dual-wielding is manageable with proper accessory choices (prioritize stamina recovery signets).

Q: How do I unlock Oongka permanently?

Complete the side quest "Gentle Sound of Flowing River" immediately after the Chapter 7 Ashclaw Keep event. This quest is easily missable â?the main story advances rapidly after Chapter 7, and failing to complete this side quest first will lock Oongka permanently for that playthrough. If you have already passed this point without completing the quest, you must wait for New Game+ to unlock him. Save manually before the Ashclaw Keep event to be safe.

Q: What is the best Abyss Core setup for a new Oongka player?

Start with Destruction III + Insight III as your first two cores on any 5-slot weapon. Destruction III provides the flat attack power that Oongka's slow weapons scale best with, and Insight III gives you crit chance on your big hits. Add Swift III as your third core â?it helps with Oongka's slow attack speed. Fill remaining slots with Ancient Retribution (bonus damage on hit) or Malicebane (boss damage). Avoid elemental cores â?Oongka's strength is raw physical damage, not elemental procs.
