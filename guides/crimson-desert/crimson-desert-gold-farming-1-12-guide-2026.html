Patch 1.12.00 (released June 19, 2026) did not directly change gold drop rates or add new silver-generating mechanics, but it reshaped the farming landscape in important ways. The addition of 58 new craftable outdoor decoration items, new Greymane gear sold by Carl at Howling Hill, new sliding combo attacks that improve kill speed, and the Box of Fortune from the Mysterious Shop have all shifted which farming methods are most efficient. This guide covers the top 5 gold farming methods verified to work in the post-1.12 economy, with exact numbers, locations, and step-by-step instructions. If you are new to earning silver, start with our Early Game Money Making Guide, and for high-value boss loot, check the World Bosses Guide.

Patch 1.12 Context: Pearl Abyss confirmed that Patch 1.12.00 focused on housing decoration, new gear (Greymane Light Armor, Honorary Greymane Cloth Gloves, Steelmaw Plate Helm), sliding combos for all weapons, and quality-of-life improvements. No direct gold economy changes were documented. However, new craftable items created new market opportunities, and faster kill speed from new combos has improved farming efficiency across the board.

⚔️ 2. Method 1: Elite Enemy Farming — Fort Perwin Infinite Loop

Fort Perwin (located south of Hernand) is the most reliable elite enemy farming spot in the game, and it remains extremely effective after Patch 1.12 because the new sliding combo attacks make clearing mobs significantly faster. The key is exploiting the faction quest mechanic for infinite enemy spawns.

How It Works

During the "Continuing Concern" faction quest at Fort Perwin, enemies spawn infinitely until you kill the boss. By not killing the boss — just farming the infinitely respawning troops — you can generate an endless stream of loot. Each kill drops Scarlet Dagger Gas Masks (~14 silver each), copper/silver coin pouches, and occasional weapons and armor. A 20-30 minute loop yields 80-150 silver in guaranteed loot, plus valuable coin pouch contents.

📍 Step-by-Step Fort Perwin Loop

1. Prerequisites: Reach Chapter 3 and accept the "Continuing Concern" faction quest from the Hernand notice board. Equip a weapon with at least +3 upgrade — the enemies are level-appropriate but come in waves.

2. Location: Fort Perwin, south of Hernand. Fast travel to the Hernand south gate, then follow the road south until you see the fortress walls.

3. Execution: Enter the fort and engage the soldiers. Do NOT progress toward the boss room. Stay in the courtyard area where enemies respawn in waves. Use the new 1.12 sliding combo attacks to clear groups faster — the sliding uppercut (all weapons) and spinning throw (dual wield) are particularly effective for AOE clearing.

4. Looting: Collect all masks (sell to vendor for ~14 silver each), open all coin pouches (use "Use All" feature for efficiency), and pick up any weapons/armor drops. Deposit crafting materials in storage if inventory fills up.

5. Reset: When your inventory is full or you have been farming for 20-30 minutes, fast travel back to Hernand, sell everything to vendors (use the blacksmith for weapons, general goods for masks), then return to Fort Perwin and repeat.

Patch 1.12 Advantage: The new sliding combo attacks added in 1.12.00 — especially the spinning throw for dual-wield weapons and the uppercut for unarmed — increase clear speed by approximately 15-20% compared to pre-patch. This directly translates to more loot per hour at Fort Perwin. Use Damiane's new unarmed "lotus" sliding combo (a reference to Rock Lee from Naruto) for maximum AOE damage against grouped enemies.

Alternative: Fundamentalist Goblin Mask Farm (Early Game)

If you are not yet at Chapter 3, the Fundamentalist Goblin camp in Sunrise Plains (south of Greymane Camp) offers a similar farming loop. Goblins drop Crude Devil Masks with approximately 25% drop chance, each selling for ~13.71 silver. The respawn is quick (leave area and return) and the goblins are very easy to kill. Expected: 60-100 silver per 10 minutes.

🔄 3. Method 2: Resource Flipping & Pickpocket Route

Resource flipping — specifically the Gold Bar craft loop — has become the single most profitable farming method in Patch 1.12. This method combines mining, animal farming, and crafting into a scalable income loop.

The Gold Bar Craft Loop (Post-Chapter 5)

This is the #1 money-making method after Chapter 5. It involves mining Gold Ore, farming Golden Apples from hedgehogs, crafting Gold Bars, and selling them to Banker Grover for 500 silver each. While the method was discovered pre-1.12, the new Mining Knuckles weapon (obtained from defeating Marnie's Excavator boss) makes it significantly more efficient in the current patch.

📜 Step 1: Get the Gold Bar Recipe

Two locations for the same recipe:

Option A (Recommended): Perion Witch's Hideout — located southwest of Perion, below the stone steps at the foot of Silver Wolf Mountain. Enter and turn right immediately, find the recipe scroll on a small bookshelf. Note: Perion is unlocked during Chapter 7, but the hideout can be reached before that.

Option B: Spire of Insight — located southeast of Hernand, directly south of Borolin Village. Complete the spire puzzle, then return to the third floor and find the recipe on a bookshelf near the window.

⛏️ Step 2: Unlock Mining Knuckles & Gold Vein Map

Travel to the Carin Quarry (Hernand region, north of Rocca Mountain) and defeat the boss Marnie's Excavator. This boss drops two critical items: the Mining Knuckles (a weapon that doubles as a mining tool with 10% chance for bonus ore) and the Gold Vein Map (reveals 10 Gold Ore cave locations on your map). This boss is the gatekeeper to efficient gold farming — prioritize defeating it.

🕳️ Step 3: Mine Gold Ore (8-Cave Route)

Using the Gold Vein Map, visit all 10 caves. The 8 confirmed cave locations are: Anvil Hill Cave, Cliff Gold Mine, Crimson Mountains Gold Mine, Desert Lake Gold Mine, Mist-Shattered Cave, Sand Peak Gold Mine, Second Finger Cave, and Silver Wolf Cave. Silver Wolf Cave has the highest ore density. Use your Mining Knuckles' special attacks — Strong Palm to break gold boulders, Strong Stream to remotely detonate ore deposits. A full 8-cave run yields approximately 230 Gold Ore. Respawn timer: 7 in-game days.

🦔 Step 4: Farm Golden Apples (The Bottleneck)

Golden Apples are required for the Gold Bar recipe (3 Gold Ore + 1 Golden Apple per bar). The best farming location is the East Hill Fields in Dracia, near Tinkerton's farmland. Look for three grassy patches with white flowers near the leaning tower in the center of the fields. Walk through the grass to scare hedgehogs — those with Golden Apples on their backs drop them when hit with Blinding Flash. Best spawn time: in-game evening. Expect 4 Golden Apples per 5-6 minutes. In 2 hours of dedicated farming, expect ~21 Golden Apples (verified by community testing).

🏭 Step 5: Craft & Sell Gold Bars

Craft Gold Bars at any Witch's Workshop: Workshop menu → Craft Abyss Equipment → Special Items → Gold Bar. Use the recipe: 3 Gold Ore + 1 Golden Apple = 1 Gold Bar. Alternative recipe: 3 Sulfur + 3 Mercury + 10 Silver Ore = 1 Gold Bar (less efficient). Last resort: Town blacksmith exchanges 100 Gold Ore for 1 Gold Bar (terrible rate, avoid). Sell finished Gold Bars to Banker Grover in Hernand for 500 silver each — he is the only NPC who buys gold bars at full price.

Earnings Math: 230 Gold Ore = enough for 76 Gold Bars (needs 76 Golden Apples). At 21 apples per 2 hours, a full loop takes approximately 7-8 hours of Golden Apple farming plus 2 hours of mining. Total: ~38 Gold Bars per full cycle = 19,000 silver. Per hour: approximately 1,900-2,100 silver during active farming, or 800-1,500 silver when accounting for respawn timers and travel time.

Alternative Early Route: Noble Pickpocketing

If you are not yet at Chapter 5, the Noble Pickpocket route is the best early-game gold farming method. Equip your Thieves' Mask, travel to Oakenshield Manor in central Hernand (11 AM - 2 PM in-game time), and use your lantern to scan nobles for the gold bar icon. Nobles with the gold bar icon carry a Gold Bar worth 500 silver when sold to Banker Grover. The spawn rate for gold-carrying nobles is random — you may find one in 5 scans or 50 scans. Save-scumming (manual save before scanning, reload if no gold bar found) speeds up the process significantly.

👹 4. Method 3: Boss Farming — Marnie's Excavator & World Bosses

Boss farming is high-risk, high-reward. The key bosses to farm after Patch 1.12 are Marnie's Excavator (for the Mining Knuckles unlock), the Spire of Frost soldiers (for Abyss equipment), and world bosses (for unique tradeable loot).

Marnie's Excavator (Carin Quarry)

This boss is the gateway to the Gold Bar craft loop. Defeat it once to unlock the Mining Knuckles and Gold Vein Map. After the first kill, Marnie's Excavator respawns — the exact timer is unknown (community estimates suggest 3-5 in-game days). Each subsequent kill drops additional Mining Knuckles (sell on marketplace for 200-400 silver) and Gold Vein Maps (if you somehow lost yours). The boss is challenging for its level — bring a +5 weapon and at least 10 healing potions. Use the new 1.12 sliding combos to evade its charge attacks and close distance quickly.

Spire of Frost Abyss Equipment Steal

An unconventional but profitable method: travel to the Spire of Frost in an un-liberated area. Target soldiers with shields and maces. Approach with a small shield + one-handed sword, kill only the frost artillery and bone archers, then parry the shielded soldiers to trigger a disarm. Have your dog pick up the disarmed weapon, then flee the area. When you return, the weapon has respawned on the disarmed soldier. Each weapon is a random Abyss item that can be salvaged at a witch's workshop for materials, or sold directly on the player marketplace. This method yields unpredictable but occasionally very high profits (200-1000+ silver per good weapon).

Risk Warning: Boss farming carries the highest risk of death and item loss. Always make a manual save before engaging a boss. If you die, you may lose time (running back from spawn) and potentially valuable loot. The Spire of Frost method in particular is risky because you are operating in a high-level zone — one mistake can mean a costly death.

World Bosses (Level 48+)

World bosses spawn every 4 hours and require groups of 4+ players. The loot is among the best in the game — unique weapons, rare crafting materials, and cosmetic items that sell for 2,000-5,000+ silver on the marketplace. Post-1.12, the new Greymane gear (sold by Carl at Howling Hill) has shifted some demand, but world boss loot remains highly valuable. Join the Crimson Desert community Discord or check world boss timers on fan sites to coordinate spawn times.

🔨 4. Method 4: Crafting for Profit

Patch 1.12 expanded crafting significantly with 58 new decoration items and two new workstations (Workstation and Loom). Crafting for profit is now more viable than ever, especially for players who enjoy the gathering and crafting loop.

Leather Armor Crafting (Low Level)

At any level, you can turn raw hides into leather armor for profit. Hunt deer or wild boar (north of Uzkigrad), collect hides, then visit a blacksmith to craft the hides into basic leather armor. The crafted armor sells for 3-4x the value of the raw hides (e.g., 8 silver worth of hides becomes 25-30 silver armor). This is a simple, low-risk profit method that works at any level and requires no special tools beyond a knife (which you start with).

Outdoor Decor Crafting (Patch 1.12 New)

The Workstation (Greymane Camp near Conrad the furniture vendor, or Timberhahn Sawmill) and Loom (Hernand tailor shop second floor, other tailor shops) can craft 58 new decoration items. While decorations are primarily for housing, certain crafted items can be sold to other players via the marketplace. The Box of Fortune from the Mysterious Shop (100 silver each, 3 per restock) also contains random items that can be sold for profit — some contain rare blueprints worth 500+ silver. Check the Mysterious Shop in major cities regularly.

📦 Box of Fortune Strategy

Patch 1.12 introduced the Box of Fortune, available at the Mysterious Shop for 100 silver each (limit 3 per restock). Contents are random: common materials, silver, or rare blueprints. The expected value per box is approximately 80-120 silver, making it a slight gamble. However, the rare blueprints (some worth 500+ silver) make it worth checking the Mysterious Shop every time you visit a major city. If you have spare silver and enjoy gambling, buy all 3 boxes — the occasional rare drop covers the losses from common boxes.

Selling Learned Recipes & Blueprints

After reading any recipe, blueprint, or book, the physical item becomes worthless to you. But many of these items sell for 5-15 silver to vendors. Make it a habit to sell every recipe immediately after learning it. Over the course of the game, this adds up to 100-200+ silver of "free" money — no extra farming required.

🗺️ 5. Method 5: Farming Routes

Route-based farming combines multiple methods into a single efficient loop. By chaining together gathering, combat, and quest objectives, you maximize silver per hour and minimize downtime. Here are two optimized routes for different stages of the game.

Early Game Route (Chapter 1-2): 80-150 Silver per Loop

📍 Route: Hernand Hub Loop

1. Greymane Camp → Sunrise Plains: Kill Fundamentalist Goblins for Crude Devil Masks (~13.71 silver each, ~25% drop rate). 10 minutes → 60-100 silver.

2. Roadside Herb Gathering: Collect Red Bacca Flowers and other herbs along the path between camps. 5 minutes → 20-40 silver.

3. Lioncrest Manor Archery Game: If unlocked, compete in the archery mini-game (80 copper entry fee). Net profit: 70 copper to 1.2 silver per round. Can be repeated infinitely.

4. Sell Everything: Return to Hernand, sell masks to general goods merchant, herbs to alchemist, and any loot to appropriate vendors. 5 minutes.

Total time: ~25-30 minutes. Total silver: 80-150+ silver. Silver/hr: ~160-300.

Mid-Game Route (Chapter 3-5): 200-400+ Silver per Loop

📍 Route: Perwin-Duo-Mine Loop

1. Hernand Notice Board: Pick up 1-2 bounty contracts. 2 minutes.

2. Fort Perwin Camp Clear: Clear the camp (not boss) for masks and pouches. 10-15 minutes → 80-150 silver.

3. Hernand Duo Casino: Manual save outside, enter, play Duo card game. All-in on hands of 5+ points. Win 50-200+ silver per good hand, reload if bad. 5-10 minutes.

4. Hernand Highlands Diamond Mine: If you have a pickaxe, stop at the cavern for quick diamond mining (~10 diamonds, ~2.28 silver each). 5 minutes → ~23 silver.

5. Sell Everything: Hernand vendors — blacksmith for weapons, alchemist for herbs, collector for gems, general goods for masks. 5 minutes.

Total time: ~30-40 minutes. Total silver: 200-400+ silver. Silver/hr: ~350-600.

Pro Tip: After building up 10-15 Gold Bars (5,000-7,500 silver), unlock the Bank Investment system at Hernand Bank. Choose "Medium Risk" investment for 15-20% returns per 3-day cycle. This generates 75-275 silver per cycle completely passively. You can maintain your active farming routes while the bank generates passive income. Once established, the bank investment becomes Method #6 — pure passive income.

❓ FAQ

Did Patch 1.12 nerf any gold farming methods?

No. Patch 1.12.00 focused on housing decoration, new gear, and combat improvements. No gold economy nerfs were documented by Pearl Abyss. In fact, farming efficiency improved slightly due to faster kill times from new sliding combo attacks. The Gold Bar loop remains the best method post-patch.

What is the fastest gold method if I just started the game?

Follow this progression: (1) Complete Chapter 1 → unlock Hernand. (2) Rob the Hernand Bank storeroom (30-55 silver, requires mask from back-alley merchant Grimrak for 10 copper + key for 30 copper). (3) Steal the Lioncrest Manor gold bar (500 silver, requires mask and stealth). (4) Use that gold bar as starting capital for Noble Pickpocketing at Oakenshield Manor. (5) Once you hit Chapter 3, switch to Fort Perwin farming. This progression takes you from zero to consistent 400+ silver/hour within 5-10 hours of play.

Is the Box of Fortune worth buying?

At 100 silver each (3 per restock), the Box of Fortune is a mild gamble. The expected value is roughly 80-120 silver per box, meaning you will slightly lose on average. However, the rare blueprints (500+ silver) make it exciting. Buy all 3 boxes if you have spare silver and enjoy opening loot boxes. Skip them if you are saving for a specific item — the expected value is negative.

How long does it take to set up the Gold Bar loop?

Setup takes approximately 3-5 hours: (1) Reach Chapter 5 (2-3 hours of main story), (2) Defeat Marnie's Excavator at Carin Quarry (30 minutes, may need preparation), (3) Get the Gold Bar recipe from Perion Witch's Hideout (15 minutes), (4) Complete a full 8-cave mining run (1 hour), (5) Farm Golden Apples (2+ hours). After setup, each full cycle takes 9-10 hours (mining + apple farming) for ~19,000 silver.

Can I farm gold bars before Chapter 5?

You can farm Gold Ore before Chapter 5 (and store it), but you cannot craft Gold Bars until you have the recipe. The recipe requires either accessing the Perion Witch's Hideout (near Chapter 7 area, but reachable earlier with careful exploration) or completing the Spire of Insight puzzle. If you want early gold bars, use the Noble Pickpocket method instead — it does not require any recipe.

Are the new Greymane items from Patch 1.12 worth buying?

Yes — the Honorary Greymane Cloth Gloves (Damiane's best gloves at max level +10, grants Attack Speed Level 4) and the Greymane Signet (grants Critical Rate Level 1 at max) are excellent endgame items. They cost silver, but they are direct stat upgrades that pay for themselves through improved combat efficiency. The Greymane Light Armor for Kliff is solid but not best-in-slot. Carl at Greymane Camp sells these.

Does the player marketplace have better prices post-1.12?

The player marketplace is unchanged by 1.12. It still charges a 10% listing fee. However, demand for certain crafting materials (wood, cloth, leather) has increased slightly due to new decoration recipes. If you are crafting decorations, you may find it cheaper to gather materials yourself than to buy them on the marketplace. Conversely, selling gathered materials on the marketplace is now more profitable than before.
