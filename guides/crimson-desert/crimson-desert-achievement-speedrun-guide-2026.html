🏃 1. Speedrun Strategy Overview — What Optimisation Looks Like

A standard 100% playthrough of Crimson Desert takes 200–300 hours. A speedrun-optimised route can cut that to 150–180 hours by eliminating backtracking, batching missable objectives, and front-loading time-gated activities. This guide assumes you are playing on Patch 1.12.02+ and have a basic grasp of combat mechanics. If you need a full achievement list first, read our Complete Achievement Guide.

150-180h
Optimised Route vs. 200-300h Standard

Core Principles of the Speedrun Route

  • Batch by region, not by category. Do not separate "exploration" from "combat" from "collection" — complete everything in one region before moving to the next.
  • Missable-first ordering. The 5 missable trophies dictate your route. Chapters 4–8 are the critical window. One wrong step costs 80+ hours of New Game+.
  • Passive grinding during downtime. Fish, cook, hunt, and send dispatch missions while waiting for story triggers or travelling between zones.
  • Save rotation discipline. Use all 9 manual save slots. Keep one save at each chapter milestone and one "clean" save before any major decision point.
  • Fast travel efficiency. Activate every bell tower and fast travel point immediately upon entering a new zone. This single habit saves 15–20 hours over a full playthrough.
Speedrunner's Note: This route prioritises real-world time to platinum, not speedrun.com leaderboard time. You will play at a steady pace with zero wasted movement. If you are targeting a pure speedrun record, adapt this route by skipping all non-essential fights and using movement tech (dodge-cancelling, mount swapping).

The route is divided into 4 phases that align with the main story progression. Each phase has a checklist of objectives that must be completed before advancing to the next chapter. Follow them in order and you will never need to backtrack more than 15 minutes for any missed objective.

Mountaineer — The "Extra" Missable

Technically not one of the 5 officially missable trophies, but Mountaineer requires climbing Stormcrest Peak before Chapter 4's avalanche destroys the path. This is the earliest missable in the game. Climb it the moment you reach the Stormcrest region — do not wait.

⚔️ 4. Combat Achievements Fastest Path

Combat achievements require the most raw skill. Here is the speedrun-optimised approach to each one — minimising repeat attempts through preparation.

Weapon Mastery (8 Achievements) — 2 Hours Total

All 8 weapon mastery achievements can be completed at any training ground in a single session. The challenges are isolated sequences that test input timing, not gear or stats. Here is the fastest order:

  1. Sword & Shield — easiest, warm up here (10 min)
  2. Dual Blades — fast inputs, build rhythm (15 min)
  3. Spear — reach and timing (15 min)
  4. Axe — heavy timing windows (15 min)
  5. Blunt — slowest weapons, patience required (20 min)
  6. Greatsword — mix of slow and precise combos (20 min)
  7. Bow — aim and distance management (10 min)
  8. Crossbow — familiar if you did bow (10 min)
Speedrunner Tip: If you get stuck on a challenge, skip it and come back. The game tracks progress per weapon independently. You can complete 7/8 weapons, save, and tackle the last one fresh. Muscle memory resets matter — do not burn out on Greatsword for 2 hours when 15 minutes of sleep would fix it.

Dragonslayer — The Run-Killer

Kaelx in the Sealed Sanctum is the single hardest combat encounter. To minimise attempts in a speedrun:

  • Gear check: Level 40+, +8 refined weapon, Fortress III Abyss Core + Barrier III Abyss Core
  • Consumables: 20+ top-tier healing potions, 5+ stamina recovery items
  • Strategy: Phase 1 is pattern recognition. Phase 2 adds environmental hazards. Phase 3 is a DPS race. Watch for the "Wings of Annihilation" telegraph — dodge left, not right.
  • Save scum: Make a manual save outside the Sanctum. Each attempt costs 15 minutes. Use the save to retry without re-travelling.

Colosseum Champion — All 10 Tiers

The Colosseum unlocks after Chapter 4. Complete it in one sitting during Phase 2. The difficulty spikes at Tier 7 (dual mage fighters). Recommended approach:

  • Tiers 1–4: Any weapon, no special strategy
  • Tiers 5–6: Spear or Greatsword for reach and stagger
  • Tiers 7–8: Focus the mages first. Use Bow/Crossbow to interrupt their casting.
  • Tiers 9–10: Full consumable loadout. Kite the boss and punish openings.

Abyssal Conqueror — All 4 Abyss Dungeons

These dungeons are designed for co-op but are soloable with good gear. Complete them post-story (Phase 4). Each dungeon has a distinct boss mechanic:

🗺️ 5. Exploration Achievements — Efficient Sweeps

Exploration achievements require covering the entire map. The speedrun approach is to batch them during Phase 4 using a region-by-region sweep pattern.

Cartographer (100% Map Reveal) — 6 Hours

Revealing 100% of the map is deceptively time-consuming. The most commonly missed areas:

  • West Coast islands — 3 small islands off the Phidel coast. Need a boat from the Phidel docks.
  • Howling Plateau cave network — an underground zone that does not appear on the overworld map. Entrance is behind the waterfall at coordinates (347, 211) (verify in-game).
  • Gheist Bell Tower rooftop — accessible by grappling hook from the northeast balcony.
  • Stormcrest Peak summit — only accessible before Chapter 4. See Mountaineer warning above.

Efficient Sweep Route

Start at Phidel (southwest), move clockwise: Phidel coast → Stormcrest → Abyss Wastes → Gheist → Howling Plateau → Pailune → Thornwood → Lake Serenia. Use your mount for overland travel and fast travel between bell towers for gaps. Mark any fog-of-war patches on your map with a custom pin — return to them after the initial circuit.

Lore Keeper (80 Knowledge Tablets) — 4 Hours

Tablets glow blue when you use Abyss Sight (L1+R1). The speedrun trick: do not hunt tablets randomly. They are concentrated in specific zones:

  • Ancient Ruins (18 tablets) — always check ruins interiors
  • Abyss Wastes tunnels (14 tablets) — linear underground paths
  • Gheist cathedral district (11 tablets) — rooftops and bell towers
  • Thornwood treetop village (9 tablets) — platforming required
  • Remaining 28 tablets scattered across all other zones — pick up during Cartographer sweep

Treasure Hunter (40 Chests) + Ruin Raider (8 Ruins)

Buy treasure maps from Cartographer NPCs in each major settlement. There are 5 Cartographers total — each sells maps for their region. Buy all maps immediately, then open chests as you pass through zones during your Cartographer sweep. The 8 Ancient Ruins are all marked on the map with a distinct icon — complete them in any order.

❓ FAQ — Speedrun Achievement Questions

Q: What is the fastest time to 100% Crimson Desert?

The optimised speedrun route in this guide targets 150–180 hours. Pure speedrun.com records (which skip all side content) are around 40–50 hours for the main story only. For all achievements, 176 hours is the realistic estimate for a focused player.

Q: Can I get all achievements in one playthrough?

Yes — but only with careful planning around the 5 missable trophies. Follow the Phase 2 checklist strictly. Make manual saves at every chapter milestone. If you miss a missable, you will need a New Game+ run adding 80+ hours.

Q: What is the most common mistake in achievement speedruns?

Missing Battlefield Conqueror (the cannon challenge in Chapter 8). Players forget to use the siege cannon during the Rebel Suppression mission, then cannot find 5 densely-packed enemies afterward. The second most common is not starting Greymane Camp dispatches early enough.

Q: Should I play on Easy mode for the speedrun?

Crimson Desert does not have a traditional difficulty selector — enemy scaling is dynamic. However, enabling "Story Focus" in the gameplay options reduces enemy aggression and damage. This is recommended for the speedrun route, especially for the Colosseum and Abyss Dungeons. It does not disable achievements.

Q: How do I get the Abyss Eel fast?

Fish at Lake Serenia's deepest point (northwest corner of the lake) at night (in-game time 21:00–05:00). Use Glowing Worm bait, which is sold by the fishing vendor at the Lake Serenia dock. The Eel has approximately a 5% catch rate under these conditions (verify with in-game testing).

Q: Are any achievements bugged in Patch 1.12.02?

No confirmed bugged achievements. The Shadowlord challenges have been reported as slightly inconsistent — if a special weapon challenge does not register, try unequipping and re-equipping the weapon. If it still fails, reload from your manual save.

Q: What gear do I need before attempting Kaelx?

Minimum: Level 40, +8 refined weapon and armour, Fortress III and Barrier III Abyss Cores. Recommended: Level 45+, two Abyss Cores at rank IV, 20+ supreme healing potions, 5+ stamina tonics. The fight has three phases — watch for the "Wings of Annihilation" telegraph and dodge left.

Q: How many manual saves should I keep?

Use all 9 slots. Recommended rotation: save 1 = start of Phase 2 (Ch 4 start), save 2 = before Chapter 7, save 3 = before Chapter 8 cannon, saves 4–6 = rotating chapter milestones, saves 7–9 = before any major boss or decision. Rotate the last 3 slots for general backup.
