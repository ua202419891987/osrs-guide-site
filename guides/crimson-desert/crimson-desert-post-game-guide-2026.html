√∞¬ü¬é¬Ø 1. First Steps After Beating the Game

Once the credits roll on Crimson Desert's main story (approximately 40-50 hours), the game does not end. An Epilogue questline titled "Journey's End" (8 quests) unlocks automatically when you load your cleared save file. This questline serves as the bridge between the main story and the true endgame, introducing you to the systems and challenges that define post-game Crimson Desert. Complete these 8 quests first √¢¬Ä?they are your tutorial for everything that follows.

Immediate Post-Game Priority List

1. Complete "Journey's End" Epilogue (8 quests): Unlocks boss rematches, Abyss Sanctum access, and the re-blockading system. Do not skip these √¢¬Ä?they unlock features you need for everything below.
2. Collect All Sealed Abyss Artifacts (141 total): The game does not retroactively track your combat progress. If you start farming weapon mastery kills before collecting a weapon's Sealed Artifact, those kills will not count. This is the single most important post-game task.
3. Unlock All Abyss Spires: Complete the Lightning, Fire, Frost, and Wind Spire puzzles to unlock Imbue Element skills. These are among the most powerful abilities in the game and many endgame builds require them.
4. Max Your Greymane Camp: Reach Stage 3 expansion and begin Pailune reconstruction. This unlocks the trading system, the dye shop, the barber, and passive income through dispatch missions.
5. Liberate All Re-Blockaded Areas: After the story, enemy factions re-occupy previously cleared zones with tougher enemies. Clearing these is the best source of post-game resources and faction reputation.

The post-game world of Pywel remains fully open after the credits. All NPCs, merchants, and quest-givers persist. The day/night cycle and weather continue. Your Greymane Camp dispatch missions keep running. Nothing is permanently missable after the main story √¢¬Ä?you can tackle content in any order. A full 100% completion run typically takes 250-300 hours total.

√∞¬ü¬î¬Æ 2. Abyss Sanctum Challenges

The Abyss Sanctums are 16 corrupted sanctuaries scattered across Pywel, each tied to one of the game's Witch NPCs. These are the primary endgame puzzle-combat hybrid challenges. Each Sanctum requires solving a physical puzzle to reach the boss arena, then defeating an Antumbra variant √¢¬Ä?corrupted echoes of the game's final enemy type. The rewards include unique legendary weapons, Abyss Artifacts, and skill points.

Boss Mechanics and Strategy

Each Antumbra variant has unique attack patterns, but all share a few common mechanics: they summon phantom copies of themselves, they use wide-reaching elemental wave attacks, and they have Stagger Windows √¢¬Ä?brief moments after certain attacks where they become vulnerable. Learning these windows is the key to efficient sanctum clears.

Antumbra of the Sword: Summons 3-4 phantoms that mimic its sword wave attack. The real boss glows slightly darker. Use Kliff's Force Palm to stagger the real one after it finishes its 3-hit combo. Recommended Abyss Gear: Groundsurge + Momentum for spin damage, plus Greysoul Howling for AoE phantom clear.

Antumbra of the Spear: Fewer phantoms (1-2) but each hit deals massive damage. Its charge attack can be parried for a guaranteed Stagger Window. Bring a shield if you are not confident with parry timing. This boss is vulnerable to Frost damage √¢¬Ä?socket Frostbite Abyss Gear if available.

Pro Tip: You must have encountered the associated Witch NPC before you can purify a Sanctum. If a Sanctum is locked, progress the main story to meet the corresponding Witch. Elowen (Witch of Wisdom, unlocked in Chapter 5) is required for several Sanctum puzzles √¢¬Ä?if you missed her, backtrack to Hernand and initiate her questline.

These are not optional in the traditional sense √¢¬Ä?they are the ultimate test of your build and skill, and they gate some of the best gear in the game. Each requires level 50+, Refinement +7 gear, and a deep understanding of your character's kit.

Boss Rematch System (Patch 1.05.00+)

Patch 1.05.00 introduced the ability to rematch story bosses from a dedicated menu in your Greymane Camp. This is the most reliable way to farm specific boss materials. Each boss has a unique drop table, and repeated kills yield diminishing returns √¢¬Ä?but the first 3-5 kills per boss are highly rewarding. Priority rematch targets:

  • Myurdin (Chapter 6) √¢¬Ä?Drops Melted Ambition weapon (Groundsurge Abyss Gear pre-attached). Farm until you have Groundsurge at Tier III.
  • Saigord, the Staglord (Chapter 4) √¢¬Ä?Drops Fallen Kingdom Leather Helmet with Momentum (+35% spin damage). Farm for Momentum Tier III upgrades.
  • Crowcaller (Chapter 5) √¢¬Ä?Drops Tauria Curved Sword (Crow's Pursuit Abyss Gear). Essential for interrupt-heavy builds.
  • Final Boss (Chapter 12) √¢¬Ä?Drops endgame crafting materials for Greater Abyss Gear synthesis.
Important: Super-bosses Beloth, Forgotten General, and Ator do not respawn. Each can only be defeated once per save file. Make sure you are fully prepared before engaging √¢¬Ä?bring max Refinement gear, the best food buffs, and all three characters leveled to at least 45+ so you can switch if one build is not working against a specific boss.

Greater Abyss Gears √¢¬Ä?The Endgame Grind

Greater Abyss Gears are upgraded versions of standard Abyss Gears with significantly stronger effects. For example, a Greater Insight III might push Critical Rate past level 10 instead of the standard level 3 cap. These are crafted at Witch NPCs through a special synthesis recipe that has only a 4% success rate. The strategy is quantity √¢¬Ä?accumulate stacks of duplicate Tier III Abyss Gears through boss rematches and zone farming, then batch-craft them at a Witch. Expect to burn through 50-100+ standard Abyss Gears per successful Greater craft.

Target Greater Abyss Gears for your build: Greater Momentum (+spin damage, priority #1), Greater Groundsurge (AoE damage), Greater Insight III (crit rate), and Greater Composure III (stamina recovery). These four cover the core stats for virtually every endgame build.

√∞¬ü¬î¬Ñ 6. New Game+ Discussion

As of June 2026 (Patch 1.12.02), Crimson Desert does not feature an official New Game+ mode. Despite high community demand √¢¬Ä?the topic is consistently among the top-voted suggestions on the Pearl Abyss official forums √¢¬Ä?the developers have not confirmed whether NG+ is in development. Here is what we know and what to expect.

What the current post-game offers instead: The re-blockading system (enemies re-occupy cleared zones at higher difficulty), boss rematches (added in Patch 1.05.00), and the super-boss trio serve as the de facto NG+ experience. Enemies in re-blockaded zones are scaled to approximately level 50-55, which is roughly equivalent to a NG+ difficulty curve. If you are craving harder content, the super-bosses √¢¬Ä?especially Ator √¢¬Ä?will test everything you have learned.

Community speculation on future NG+ features: Based on datamined strings in Patch 1.12.01 and developer interviews referencing "replayability features coming in a future update," the community expects an official NG+ mode to arrive sometime in late 2026 or early 2027. Expected features include: retained character levels and Abyss Artifacts, increased enemy scaling (levels 55-70), new NG+ exclusive Abyss Gear variants, and potentially a new super-boss tier. However, nothing is confirmed. We will update this guide as soon as Pearl Abyss makes an official announcement.

Important: Do not wait for New Game+ to start your endgame grind. The current post-game content √¢¬Ä?Sanctums, super-bosses, re-blockading, 100% completion √¢¬Ä?provides 150-250 hours of meaningful gameplay. If NG+ does arrive, your geared character will transfer over. Everything you grind now carries forward.

√¢¬ù?FAQ

Q: Can I keep playing after the credits?

Yes. After the credits roll and the Epilogue begins, you can continue exploring Pywel freely. All quests, NPCs, and activities remain available. Nothing is locked after the main story ends √¢¬Ä?the world is yours to complete at your own pace.

Q: What is the first thing I should do after beating the game?

Complete the "Journey's End" Epilogue questline (8 quests) immediately. This unlocks boss rematches, the re-blockading system, and full access to all 16 Abyss Sanctums. Then start collecting all 141 Sealed Abyss Artifacts before farming any combat challenges √¢¬Ä?the game does not retroactively track progress.

Q: How many super-bosses are there?

Three: Beloth (easiest, recommended first), The Forgotten General (harder, requires positional awareness), and Ator (hardest fight in the game). Each can only be defeated once per save file, so prepare thoroughly before engaging.

Q: Is there a New Game+ mode?

Not as of June 2026 (Patch 1.12.02). Pearl Abyss has not confirmed NG+ development, though community datamining suggests it may arrive in a future update. Current post-game content (super-bosses, Sanctums, re-blockading) provides 150-250 hours of additional gameplay.

Q: What is the best endgame armor set?

The Frostcursed Armor Set is widely considered the best-in-slot for all-around endgame content due to its exceptional defense, frost resistance, and universal stat bonuses. For crit/speed builds, the Blackwing Set offers better offensive stats. Choose based on your playstyle.

Q: How long does 100% completion take?

Approximately 250-300 hours total, assuming efficient play and multi-tasking. The most time-consuming components are weapon mastery (40-60 hours), pet collecting (20-30 hours), and liberating all re-blockaded areas (25-35 hours).

Q: Do I need to level all three characters for endgame?

Highly recommended but not strictly required. Each super-boss favors different playstyles √¢¬Ä?Beloth is manageable with Kliff alone, but Ator is significantly easier with Oongka (high stagger damage) or Damiane (speed to avoid phase transitions). Level all three to at least 45 for flexibility.

Q: What is the rarest community achievement?

The "No-Hit Super-Bosses" challenge (defeating all three super-bosses without taking damage on the same save) has zero verified completions as of June 2026. The "Petless 100%" run has only 12 completions worldwide.
