🗺️ 1. How Treasure Maps Work

Treasure maps in Crimson Desert are collectible items that lead to buried chests containing silver, gear, and rare crafting materials. There are 15 treasure maps hidden across Pywel — some are purchased from vendors, others are found in hidden locations or awarded from side quests. Each map shows a rough sketch of a landmark where the treasure is buried. You must find the landmark, then use the "Dig" interaction (appears automatically when standing on the correct spot) to unearth the chest.

Pro Tip: Treasure map items are stored in your Key Items inventory (they do not take up regular slots). You can view any map at any time by opening your inventory and selecting it. The map sketch is intentionally vague — look for distinctive landmarks like unique rock formations, bridge shapes, or building ruins.

Map 10 — Flight Required

Treasure Map 10 is the first map that requires flight to reach. The map itself is on a ledge approximately 30 meters up the canyon wall — you must fly to reach it. If you have not unlocked flight yet (Chapter 4+), come back later. The treasure itself is at ground level and does not require flight to dig up — only the map acquisition requires flight. See our Tenebrum Guide for flight control basics.

❓ FAQ — Treasure Questions

Q: Can I sell treasure maps?

Treasure maps are Key Items and cannot be sold or discarded. They remain in your Key Items inventory permanently. You do not need to worry about them taking up regular inventory space.

Q: What happens if I dig in the wrong spot?

Nothing — if you are not on the correct burial spot, the "Dig" prompt will not appear. There is no penalty for digging in the wrong place because you simply cannot dig there. Move around the area until the prompt appears.

Q: Do treasures scale with my level?

No. Treasure rewards are fixed regardless of when you open them. The silver and items inside are the same whether you dig at level 10 or level 50. However, some treasures contain gear that may be outdated if you wait too long to collect them.

Q: Is the Crown of Pywel worth collecting all 15 maps?

Yes. The Crown of Pywel is a legendary headpiece with the best all-around stats in the game — +15% damage, +10% defense, +20% stamina. It is viable for the entire endgame and NG+. The 10,000 silver and 5 Abyss Artifacts are also significant rewards.

Q: Can I find treasure maps in co-op?

Yes, but only the host can pick up map items. If you are playing co-op and your friend finds a map, they must pick it up — you cannot share map items. However, treasure chests can be opened by any player in co-op.
