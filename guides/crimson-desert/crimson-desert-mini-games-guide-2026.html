🎱 1. Mini-Games Overview — What's Available

The 1.10 update introduced Arcade Pavilions to Crimson Desert's major cities, each housing two new mini-games: Pinball and Ball Roll. These are not throwaway distractions — they are fully realized games with their own reward systems, leaderboards, and hidden secrets. Whether you are taking a break from combat or hunting for exclusive cosmetic rewards, the Arcade Pavilion has something to offer every player.

Arcade Pavilions can be found in Uzkigrad, Pywel City, and the Highland Outpost. Each pavilion has both mini-games available, and your scores are shared across all locations. There is no entry fee — the mini-games are completely free to play.

① Arcade Pavilion Locations

In Uzkigrad, the pavilion is located in the eastern market district, next to the blacksmith. In Pywel City, it is in the central plaza, near the fountain. In the Highland Outpost, it is a small tent near the trade post. Each location has the same two machines, so you can play wherever is most convenient. The Pywel City pavilion is the largest and has the most NPC spectators, creating a lively atmosphere.

🎱 2. Pinball Machine — Complete Strategy Guide

The pinball machine is a surprisingly deep mini-game that rewards both reflexes and strategy. The table is modeled after a classic pinball layout with Crimson Desert-themed decorations. You control two flippers using the left and right trigger buttons (LT/RT on controller, A/D on keyboard). The ball physics are realistic — the ball accelerates as it falls, bounces off surfaces with energy loss, and behaves differently on the two main ramps versus the bumpers.

② Table Layout and Target Guide

The table features five main scoring zones. The three bumpers at the top of the table are worth 100 points each and are the primary scoring method for beginners. The left ramp awards 500 points and lights up one of five targets when hit. The right ramp awards 500 points and activates the spinning wheel mini-bonus. The center hidden chamber (accessible only by hitting all five targets) awards 2,000 points and triggers a multiball mode. The spinner at the top-right awards 50 points per revolution and can rack up points quickly if you can keep the ball in the spinner lane.

③ Scoring Strategy — How to Reach 50,000 Points

The golden weapon skin reward requires a score of 50,000 points, which seems daunting but is achievable with the right strategy. The key is to focus on lighting all five targets to open the center chamber, which triggers multiball mode. During multiball, every target is worth 3x points, and the bumpers alone can generate 900 points per second. The optimal strategy is: (1) hit the left ramp repeatedly to light targets, (2) enter the center chamber for multiball, (3) during multiball, focus on keeping at least one ball in the bumper area while hitting ramps with the other ball. A skilled player can score 15,000-20,000 points during a single multiball session.

Pro Tip: The most common mistake is flipping both flippers simultaneously. Learn to flip independently — use the left flipper for left-side shots and the right flipper for right-side shots. This gives you far more control over ball direction and prevents the ball from draining down the center.

⚽ 3. Ball Roll — Complete Course Guide

Ball Roll is a physics-based puzzle game with 20 courses of increasing difficulty. You guide a ball through courses by tilting the environment with the left stick (or arrow keys). The ball responds to gravity realistically — it accelerates on slopes, decelerates on flat surfaces, and can fall off edges if you tilt too aggressively. Each course has a par time (for a time bonus reward) and a hidden collectible (for a completion reward).

④ Beginner Courses (1-7)

The first seven courses are designed to teach you the basic mechanics. They feature simple ramps, flat platforms, and gentle curves. The par times are generous (30-45 seconds), and the collectibles are usually on the main path. Use these courses to develop a feel for the tilt sensitivity — small movements are better than large ones. A common beginner mistake is over-tilting, which sends the ball flying off the edge. Use micro-adjustments and let momentum do the work.

⑤ Intermediate Courses (8-14)

Courses 8-14 introduce moving platforms, narrow walkways, and obstacles. The par times tighten to 20-30 seconds, and the collectibles are often on alternate paths that require detours. The key technique here is "edge balancing" — when the ball is on a narrow walkway, make tiny left-right corrections to keep it centered. If the ball starts to drift toward an edge, tilt gently in the opposite direction rather than panicking and overcorrecting. Course 12 is particularly tricky because it features a rotating platform that requires you to time your movements to the rotation cycle.

⑥ Advanced Courses (15-20)

The final six courses are genuinely challenging. They feature traps (holes that reset the ball to the last checkpoint), speed boosts, and sections that require near-frame-perfect timing. Course 18 is considered the hardest in the game — it features a series of speed boost ramps that launch the ball across gaps, and the timing must be precise. Completing all 20 courses unlocks the armored horse barding cosmetic, which is one of the rarest visual items in the game.

🎁 4. Reward Tables — Everything You Can Earn

Both mini-games offer tiered rewards based on your performance. The rewards are exclusively cosmetic — they do not provide any combat advantage, which keeps the mini-games optional and prevents them from feeling like a requirement. However, some of the cosmetic items are visually stunning and serve as status symbols for dedicated players.

⑦ Pinball Reward Tiers

Score 5,000 points: Bronze pinball badge (cosmetic for your camp flag). Score 15,000 points: Silver pinball trophy (camp decoration). Score 30,000 points: Pinball champion title (displayed in multiplayer). Score 50,000 points: Golden weapon skin (applies to all weapon types). Score 75,000 points: Platinum pinball trophy + exclusive camp music track. The golden weapon skin is the most sought-after reward — it gives all your weapons a shimmering gold appearance that is visible in both single-player and multiplayer.

⑧ Ball Roll Reward Tiers

Complete 5 courses: Bronze ball badge (cosmetic). Complete 10 courses: Silver ball trophy (camp decoration). Complete 15 courses: Ball Roll champion title. Complete all 20 courses: Armored horse barding (mount cosmetic). Collect all 20 hidden collectibles: Golden ball trophy + exclusive saddle design. The armored horse barding is the standout reward — it gives your horse a suit of plate armor that looks incredible and is visible from a distance.

🎯 5. Pro Tips & Advanced Techniques

Both mini-games have advanced techniques that separate casual players from high scorers. These techniques are not explained in the game — they must be discovered through practice and experimentation.

⑨ Pinball: Dead Bouncing

"Dead bouncing" is a technique where you let the ball bounce off a raised flipper without flipping, causing it to transfer to the other flipper. This is useful for controlling the ball's position and setting up precise shots. To dead bounce, simply hold the flipper up (do not press the flip button) when the ball is about to hit it. The ball will bounce off the raised flipper and roll to the other side, where you can then flip it toward your intended target. This technique is essential for high-level play and is the foundation of most advanced strategies.

⑩ Ball Roll: Momentum Conservation

In advanced courses, maintaining momentum is critical. When going uphill, tilt aggressively at the start of the slope and ease off as you near the top — the momentum will carry you over. When going downhill, tilt gently to prevent the ball from accelerating too fast and flying off the edge. On moving platforms, wait for the platform to be at its closest point before making your move. The most important advanced technique is "rail riding" — on narrow edges, tilt very gently in the direction of the curve.

⚠️ 6. Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Both mini-games have common pitfalls that frustrate new players. Recognizing these mistakes early will save you hours of frustration and help you achieve high scores faster.

⑪ Pinball: Flipping Both Flippers at Once

This is the single most common mistake in pinball. Flipping both flippers simultaneously reduces your control and leaves the center of the table unprotected. When both flippers are up, any ball that comes down the center will drain without you being able to save it. Train yourself to flip independently — this single change will dramatically improve your scores.

⑫ Ball Roll: Over-tilting on Narrow Platforms

The most common cause of failure in Ball Roll is over-tilting on narrow platforms, causing the ball to roll off the edge. The fix is simple but requires discipline: use the smallest possible stick movements. On narrow walkways, the tilt should be barely perceptible — if you can see the ball visibly accelerate, you are tilting too much. Practice on courses 1-5 with the goal of using minimal tilt, and you will develop the fine motor control needed for advanced courses.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Are the mini-game rewards pay-to-win?

No, all mini-game rewards are purely cosmetic. The golden weapon skin and armored horse barding do not provide any stat bonuses — they are visual only. The mini-games are designed as optional entertainment, not as a requirement for progression.

Can I play mini-games with friends?

Currently, the mini-games are single-player only. However, local leaderboards allow you to compare scores with other players on the same server. Pearl Abyss has hinted at adding multiplayer mini-game tournaments in a future update.

Do mini-game scores reset?

No, your high scores are saved permanently. Once you achieve a score threshold and earn the reward, it is yours forever. You can replay the mini-games as many times as you want to improve your scores.

What happens if I close the game mid-course in Ball Roll?

Your progress in the current course is lost, but your overall completion progress (courses completed, collectibles found) is saved. You will need to restart the course you were on, but you do not need to replay completed courses.

Is there a difficulty setting for the mini-games?

No, the mini-games have a fixed difficulty. However, the courses in Ball Roll naturally scale in difficulty from 1 to 20, so you can practice on easier courses before attempting the harder ones.
