💎 1. What Are Mysterious Stones?

Mysterious Stones are one of the most intriguing collectible categories in Crimson Desert. These ancient, glowing relics are scattered across Pywel's six regions and are tied directly to the game's Abyss system and lore progression. Unlike regular collectibles that you sell for Bells or gift to NPCs, Mysterious Stones serve a unique dual purpose: they permanently enhance your character's Abyss stats and unlock hidden lore entries in the Knowledge system. For the locations of key NPCs who interact with these collectibles, see our All NPC Locations Guide.

There are 27 Mysterious Stones in total as of Patch 1.12 (June 2026). Each stone is tied to a specific environmental puzzle, hidden boss arena, or platforming challenge. The game provides almost no direct guidance — you must rely on visual cues, sound design, and environmental storytelling to locate them. The first stone you encounter is almost always by accident, but dedicated collectors will want all 27 for the permanent stat bonuses they provide.

Key Insight: Mysterious Stones are NOT the same as Sealed Abyss Artifacts (which unlock Abyss abilities) or Ancient Tablets (which provide lore fragments). Stones give permanent passive stat boosts that stack with gear. This makes them invaluable for min-maxing any build. If you see a faint purple glow emanating from a cracked wall, a tree hollow, or underwater — investigate. That is almost certainly a Mysterious Stone waiting to be claimed.

How Mysterious Stones Work

When you approach a Mysterious Stone, you will hear a low-frequency hum that increases in pitch as you get closer. Interacting with the stone triggers a brief activation animation where the stone's energy transfers into Kliff. The stone itself then crumbles to dust, and you receive:

  • A permanent passive stat bonus (varies per stone — see Section 3)
  • A Knowledge entry added to your Codex (required for the "Scholar of Pywel" achievement threshold)
  • 50-200 XP depending on the stone's rarity

Stones do NOT take up inventory space — they are consumed immediately upon activation. You cannot sell, trade, or store them. This is a one-and-done mechanic.

🗺️ 2. All Stone Locations Map

Below is the complete list of all 27 Mysterious Stone locations organized by region. We recommend tackling them in the order listed below, starting from Hernand and working outward as your level allows. Stones in higher-level regions (Crimson Desert, Delesyia) are guarded by aggressive enemies or require advanced traversal abilities.

Hernand Region (6 Stones — Recommended Level 1-15)

🔹 Stone #1 — Whisperwind Grotto Chamber

Location: Inside Whisperwind Grotto (the same cave as the Alchemist's Forgotten Laboratory from the Secrets guide). After entering the cave, take the left tunnel instead of the right. The stone rests on a stone pedestal surrounded by glowing mushrooms.

Requirement: None. Accessible immediately after the prologue.

Stat Bonus: +2 Abyss Resistance

🔹 Stone #2 — Uzkigrad Fountain Base

Location: At the base of the large stone fountain in Uzkigrad city center. Crouch and look under the fountain's lower rim. The stone is wedged between two decorative stones. Easy to miss because the fountain's water spray obscures it.

Requirement: None. Available from the moment you enter Uzkigrad.

Stat Bonus: +1 Maximum Stamina

🔹 Stone #3 — Hidden Waterfall Cave (Lake Voryn)

Location: On the eastern shore of Lake Voryn, there is a small waterfall that is not marked on the map. Walk directly through the waterfall to find a small cave. The stone is at the back of the cave behind a breakable wooden crate.

Requirement: None. Bring a weapon to break the crate.

Stat Bonus: +1 Abyss Energy Recovery

🔹 Stone #4 — Greymane Camp Underground

Location: After unlocking Greymane Camp (Chapter 2), go to the back of Kliff's personal quarters. There is a loose floorboard near the bed. Interact with it to reveal a small crawlspace. The stone is inside.

Requirement: Must have Greymane Camp unlocked (Chapter 2+).

Stat Bonus: +1 Maximum HP

🔹 Stone #5 — Hernand Church Bell Tower

Location: Inside the Hernand Church (same building where Alfred sells Writs of Absolution). Climb the ladder behind the altar to reach the bell tower. The stone is on the windowsill, partially hidden by a bird's nest.

Requirement: None, but the church is locked at night. Visit during daytime hours.

Stat Bonus: +1% Critical Hit Chance

🔹 Stone #6 — Old Mill Basement

Location: The abandoned windmill northwest of Hernand (visible from the main road). Enter the windmill and descend the ladder into the basement. The stone is embedded in the far wall. You need to hit it with a melee attack to dislodge it.

Requirement: None.

Stat Bonus: +2 Poison Resistance

Demeniss Region (5 Stones — Recommended Level 15-25)

🔹 Stone #7 — Serpent Marsh Island

Location: At the center of Serpent Marsh, on a small island accessible by jumping across partially submerged stones. The stone is inside a hollowed-out tree stump. Be careful — the water surrounding the island has poison status effect.

Requirement: Anti-poison consumables recommended.

Stat Bonus: +2 Poison Damage

🔹 Stone #8 — Beighen Dead Volcano Crater

Location: Climb to the rim of the dead volcano northwest of Beighen. The stone is inside the crater itself, requiring you to descend into the caldera. There is a faint purple glow visible from the rim.

Requirement: Requires climbing gear (purchasable from Hernand general store). Level 18+ recommended due to lava-dwelling enemies.

Stat Bonus: +3 Fire Resistance

🔹 Stone #9 — Demeniss Castle Dungeon

Location: In the dungeons beneath Demeniss Castle. After completing the main story quest that takes you through the dungeons, backtrack to the cell block on the lowest level. The stone is hidden behind a loose stone in the wall of the last cell.

Requirement: Must have completed the Demeniss Castle main story segment. If you miss this, you can return later — the dungeon remains accessible.

Stat Bonus: +1% Block Efficiency

🔹 Stone #10 — Crossroads Shrine

Location: At the crossroads between Hernand and Demeniss, there is a small, unmarked shrine with a broken statue. Offer 500 gold at the shrine's donation box (interact with the box). The stone appears on the altar after the donation.

Requirement: 500 Gold.

Stat Bonus: +1 Luck (increases rare drop chance by a small amount)

🔹 Stone #11 — Serpent's Maw Cave

Location: A hidden cave on the western edge of Serpent Marsh. The entrance is camouflaged by hanging vines. Inside, solve a simple pressure-plate puzzle (step on plates in the correct order: left, right, middle) to reveal the stone.

Requirement: Level 20+ recommended. Enemies inside are level 22-24.

Stat Bonus: +2 Abyss Damage

Pailune Region (5 Stones — Recommended Level 25-35)

🔹 Stone #12 — Silver Wolf Mountain Summit

Location: At the very peak of Silver Wolf Mountain. Requires a 5-minute climb using stamina-consuming handholds. The stone is embedded in the snow at the summit, near a frozen flag.

Requirement: Full stamina bar recommended. Cold resistance gear required (or you take damage-over-time from the cold).

Stat Bonus: +2 Maximum Stamina

🔹 Stone #13 — Wayward Woods Hollow Tree

Location: In the Wayward Woods (southern Pailune). Find the largest tree in the forest — it has a face carved into its trunk. Enter the hollow at the tree's base. The stone is inside, guarded by a level 28 Ent-like enemy.

Requirement: Level 28+ or stealth approach.

Stat Bonus: +3% Stealth

🔹 Stone #14 — Crimson Lake Depths

Location: At the bottom of Crimson Lake (central Pailune, near Bari the Witch's island). Requires swimming to the deepest point. The stone rests on a rock formation shaped like a hand.

Requirement: Water Breathing Potion or Amphibious perk. Level 25+.

Stat Bonus: +2 Abyss Resistance

🔹 Stone #15 — Forgotten Temple Ruins

Location: Northwest Pailune, the Forgotten Temple ruins (marked by collapsed pillars). The stone is on the altar at the temple's center. Activating it triggers a wave of 3 elite ghost enemies.

Requirement: Level 30+ recommended. Bring anti-ghost consumables (Holy Water or equivalent).

Stat Bonus: +1% Magic Damage

🔹 Stone #16 — Ice Cave Labyrinth

Location: In the Ice Cave system beneath the Pailune Glacier. Navigate the labyrinth (follow the RIGHT wall at every junction — this is the known solution). The stone is in the final chamber with a frozen waterfall.

Requirement: Cold resistance gear. The labyrinth is dark — bring a torch or lantern.

Stat Bonus: +3 Ice Resistance

Delesyia Region (4 Stones — Recommended Level 30-40)

🔹 Stone #17 — Delesyia Observatory Telescope

Location: At the Delesyia Astronomical Observatory. Climb to the top of the main telescope structure. The stone is wedged inside the telescope's focusing mechanism.

Requirement: Must have completed the "Starfall" side quest to access the observatory interior.

Stat Bonus: +1% Ranged Damage

🔹 Stone #18 — Gearworks Factory Core

Location: Inside the Gearworks Factory (Delesyia's industrial district). Navigate to the central reactor chamber. The stone is glowing inside the reactor's viewing window. You must deactivate the reactor shield (solve the 3-lever puzzle nearby) to retrieve it.

Requirement: Level 32+. The factory has level 35 mechanized enemies.

Stat Bonus: +2 Abyss Energy Recovery

🔹 Stone #19 — Alchemist's District Rooftop

Location: In Delesyia's alchemist quarter. Parkour across the rooftops (start from the inn with the green awning, jump to the chimney, then to the next building). The stone is on the highest rooftop, inside a bird's nest.

Requirement: Precise platforming. No combat required.

Stat Bonus: +1% Movement Speed

🔹 Stone #20 — Underground Reservoir

Location: Beneath Delesyia's main square, there is an underground reservoir accessible via a manhole cover (interact with it to enter). Swim to the far end of the reservoir. The stone is on a small ledge above the water line.

Requirement: Water Breathing or quick stamina management.

Stat Bonus: +2 Maximum HP

Crimson Desert Region (5 Stones — Recommended Level 35-50)

🔹 Stone #21 — World's Navel Crater

Location: At the World's Navel (the massive crater in the Crimson Desert). Descend to the bottom — the stone is embedded in the central obelisk. Interacting with it triggers a cutscene showing the history of the Abyss.

Requirement: Level 35+. High heat resistance gear required.

Stat Bonus: +3 Abyss Damage

🔹 Stone #22 — Tashkalp Ruins Hidden Chamber

Location: In the Tashkalp ruins (north of Tommaso Fork Posthouse). Find the collapsed pillar with a crack in it. Use a explosive barrel or heavy weapon to break the wall. The stone is inside the hidden chamber.

Requirement: Explosive barrel (found nearby) or a heavy weapon (greatsword/hammer).

Stat Bonus: +1% Attack Speed

🔹 Stone #23 — Sandstorm Shelter Oasis

Location: During a sandstorm (random weather event in the Crimson Desert), a hidden oasis appears at the map coordinates roughly center-east. The stone is at the base of the palm tree in the center of the oasis. If it is not sandstorm weather, the oasis is invisible.

Requirement: Must wait for a sandstorm. Alternatively, use the "Weather Manipulation" consumable (rare, sold by Delesyia alchemist).

Stat Bonus: +3 Heat Resistance

🔹 Stone #24 — Nomad's Camp Firepit

Location: At the Nomad's Camp (wandering camp that shifts position weekly in the Crimson Desert). In the current patch (June 2026), the camp is southwest of the World's Navel. Sit at the campfire and wait until nighttime. The stone appears in the firepit after the fire dies down.

Requirement: Must find the Nomad's Camp (check the Crimson Desert interactive map for its current position).

Stat Bonus: +1 Luck

🔹 Stone #25 — Abyssal Rift Entrance

Location: At the entrance to the Abyssal Rift (endgame secret dungeon). Before entering the rift, examine the left pillar. The stone is hidden in a small alcove that requires you to jump onto a narrow ledge behind the pillar.

Requirement: Must have unlocked the Abyssal Rift (post-game, after completing "The Abyss Stares Back" questline).

Stat Bonus: +5 Abyss Resistance (largest single-stat bonus from any stone)

Post-Game / Hidden (2 Stones — Recommended Level 45+)

🔹 Stone #26 — The Forgotten King's Throne

Location: Inside the Forgotten King's boss arena. After defeating the Forgotten King (see our Secrets & Easter Eggs guide for unlock conditions), examine the throne at the back of the arena. The stone is embedded in the armrest.

Requirement: Must have defeated the Forgotten King (endgame secret boss).

Stat Bonus: +3% All Stats (unique — no other stone provides this)

🔹 Stone #27 — Developer Room Reward

Location: Inside the Debug Chamber (Developer Room). After accessing the chamber (fountain at 3:33 AM, Sit emote, sewer key, walk into painting), the stone is on the desk next to the "Oops" chest.

Requirement: Must access the Developer Room (see our Hidden Secrets guide for the walkthrough).

Stat Bonus: +1 to All Stats (smaller version of #26, but much easier to obtain)

Important: Stone #25 (Abyssal Rift Entrance) and Stone #26 (Forgotten King's Throne) are the two most commonly missed stones because they require endgame content that many players never complete. Stone #23 (Sandstorm Shelter Oasis) is also frequently missed because the sandstorm weather condition is random and unpredictable. If you are going for all 27 stones, we recommend tackling these three first when you reach the appropriate level.

Special Collectibles Worth Noting

🎨 Dark Red Dye

Value: 57 Bells (highest-value single collectible in the game)

Crafting Recipe: Pink Herb x10 + Rhino Beetle x3 + Longhorn Beetle x1 + Stag Beetle x2

Location: Crafted at any Cooking Station or Alchemy Table. The recipe is unlocked automatically when you have all ingredients in your inventory for the first time.

Tip: Farm beetles in the forest regions (Hernand and Pailune). Pink Herbs are abundant in the fields around Uzkigrad. This is the best "craft-to-sell" collectible for making Bells quickly.

⚖️ Scales (Special Collectible)

Value: 19 Bells

Location: Found in the Delesyia Gearworks Factory, on a desk in the accounting office. Only spawns after completing the "Factory Audit" side quest.

Note: This is a one-time spawn — if you sell it, you cannot get it back. Consider gifting it to Yann at Greymane Camp for a significant trust boost.

🦋 Blue Dragon Scale Decorative Stone

Value: Variable (not sold in shops)

Location: Dropped by the world boss "Ancient Wyrm" in the Crimson Desert region. Drop rate is approximately 15% per kill.

Description: "Art carved by wind, water, and time." This stone has a subtle iridescent sheen with patterns that sometimes look like scales and sometimes like flames. A prized decoration item for Greymane Camp.

📜 Paintings (Glenbritt Manor Heist)

Items: "Prelude to War — Ode", "Land of Gold — Study"

Location: Both paintings are in Glenbritt Manor (Hernand region). You must steal them during the "Manor Heist" side quest. They are in the eastern wing gallery on the second floor.

Warning: These are the only collectibles that are truly missable (see Section 6). Stealing them does not fail any quests, but you must physically pick them up during the heist.

Ancient Tablets & Sealed Abyss Artifacts

Two additional collectible-like systems deserve special mention because they function similarly to Mysterious Stones but serve different purposes:

📜 Ancient Tablets

Total: 23 tablets across all regions

Function: Each tablet adds a lore entry to the Knowledge system. Collecting all 23 unlocks the "Ancient Scholar" achievement and reveals a hidden cutscene about Pywel's prehistory.

Key Difference from Stones: Tablets do NOT provide stat bonuses. They are purely lore-focused, though they contribute to the Knowledge completion percentage required for the "Scholar of Pywel" achievement.

🔮 Sealed Abyss Artifacts

Total: 12 artifacts

Function: Each artifact unlocks a new Abyss ability or passive skill. These are essentially ability-gated — you can find them before you have the corresponding Abyss level, but they remain in your inventory until you meet the requirement.

Key Difference from Stones: Artifacts take up inventory space until used. They can also be sold (though we strongly advise against it — the abilities they unlock are too valuable). See our Abyss Artifacts Farming Guide for full details.

Explorer Passive Skill — The Ultimate Collection Tool

In the skill tree's "Academic" branch, there is a passive skill called Explorer. Unlocking this skill removes ALL fog of war from your map and displays every point of interest, hunting ground, merchant, and fast travel point. This is arguably the most useful skill for completionists. It costs 3 skill points and requires 5 points invested in the Academic branch to unlock. We recommend saving skill points for this as early as possible if you plan to 100% the game.

❓ FAQ — Collectibles Questions Answered

How many Mysterious Stones are in Crimson Desert as of Patch 1.12?

There are 27 Mysterious Stones total as of the June 2026 Patch 1.12. Two stones were added in Patch 1.11 (Stones #26 and #27 — the Forgotten King's Throne and Developer Room stones), so if you are using an older guide, you might see references to only 25 stones. All 27 are required for the "Master of Stones" achievement.

Can I collect all 27 stones in a single playthrough?

Yes — absolutely. None of the stones are permanently missable with the exception of Stone #9 (Demeniss Castle) and Stone #4 (Greymane Camp), which become inaccessible after Chapters 7 and 9 respectively. As long as you grab those two before their deadlines, you can collect all 27 on one save file. Stones #26 and #27 require endgame content (Forgotten King boss and Developer Room access), but those are also available post-game.

What is the best collectible to sell for Bells?

The Dark Red Dye is the highest-value single collectible at 57 Bells per unit. It is craftable using renewable materials (Pink Herb x10, Rhino Beetle x3, Longhorn Beetle x1, Stag Beetle x2), making it the best renewable income source among collectibles. For comparison, most basic collectibles sell for 1-5 Bells, and even the Scales (19 Bells) and Weapons Box (38 Bells) do not match the Dark Red Dye's value-to-effort ratio.

Do Mysterious Stones respawn or can I only get them once?

Mysterious Stones are one-time-only pickups. Once you activate a stone, it crumbles to dust and never respawns. This is permanent character progression, not a farming mechanic. If you have already activated a stone on your current save file, you cannot get its stat bonus again — but you also do not need to, as the bonus is permanent.

What happens if I sell a collectible by accident?

Most basic collectibles (ceramic, wood, metal items, candles) can be reacquired — either by repurchasing from the supply shop or by finding them again in the open world. However, UNIQUE collectibles (paintings, Scales, Blue Dragon Scale Decorative Stone) are one-time finds. If you sell a unique item, it is gone permanently. We strongly recommend storing all unique collectibles in your Greymane Camp chest (unlocked after Chapter 3) until you decide what to do with them.

Are there any collectibles that require co-op to obtain?

No collectibles (including Mysterious Stones) require co-op. However, the Sealed Abyss Artifacts system has two artifacts that are significantly easier to obtain with a partner because they are located in areas with co-op puzzles (the Twin Pedestals and Bridge of Light areas). You can still solo these areas with precise timing and specific gear, but having a co-op partner reduces the difficulty substantially. None of the 27 stones require co-op.

How does the NPC gifting system work with collectibles?

Each NPC has a hidden "preference" value for different collectible types. While no NPC will refuse a gift, they gain more trust from items they like. Gifting a Metal Ware item to a blacksmith gives more trust than gifting a Candle. The exact preference tables have not been fully datamined as of June 2026, but community testing suggests that NPCs prefer collectibles related to their profession. Our general advice: gift low-value duplicates first to establish baseline trust, then use higher-value items for the last trust tier. See our Vendor Guide for NPC-specific gift recommendations.

Will more stones be added in future updates?

Pearl Abyss has confirmed that the 27 stones constitute the complete set for the base game. However, the upcoming expansion "Echoes of the Abyss" (expected Winter 2026) will introduce a new region with at least 5 additional Mysterious Stones. The achievement "Master of Stones" will likely be updated to require 32 stones after the expansion launches. We will update this guide accordingly when the expansion drops.
