🔑 1. Side Quests That Unlock Endgame — Don't Miss These

Some Crimson Desert side quests are not optional if you want to access the full endgame. These quests unlock systems and content that remain relevant for the entire playthrough — and some are permanently missable if you progress the main story too quickly. Here are the essential quest chains that gate endgame content. Many of these quests tie directly into the Commission & Bounty system and provide reputation boosts across major factions — see our Faction Guide for which groups to prioritize.

🔥 1. The Greymane Camp Reconstruction (Grounds of the Sunrise)

Quest chain length: 38 quests
Unlocks: Private Storage, Expanded Camp, Multiple Medium Backpacks
Why it matters: The "Reconstructing Pailune" → "The Return Home" chain is the only way to unlock private storage in Crimson Desert. Without this, your inventory management will be a nightmare throughout the entire game. The chain also rewards 5+ Medium Backpacks, taking your carry capacity from 80 to 140+. Complete this before Chapter 6.

⚔️ 2. The Abyssal Dragon Armor (Witches Questline — "Wings of Iron")

Quest chain length: 22 quests
Unlocks: Abyssal Dragon Armor (top-tier endgame armor), Abyssal Fusion crafting
Why it matters: The Witches questline is the most important endgame side chain. It culminates in "Wings of Iron," which rewards the Abyssal Dragon Armor — widely considered the best all-around armor set in the game. However, this quest is only available after Chapter 11 and requires visiting the Kilnden Workshop. You also need 5+ Abyss Artifacts from other side quests to craft the armor. Start collecting artifacts early.

🌟 3. The Sword of Starlight (Scholastone Institute — "Where Starlight Converges")

Quest chain length: 10 quests + 13 constellation puzzles
Unlocks: Sword of Starlight (top hybrid weapon), Starlight crafting set
Why it matters: The Sword of Starlight is one of the best hybrid weapons in the game — it scales with both physical and magical stats. The quest requires finding all 13 constellations across Pywel, which also reveals hidden lore and world secrets. This is a long-term investment quest that pays off massively in the endgame.

🛡️ 4. House Serkis Alliance ("King of the Fallen Kingdom")

Quest chain length: 13 quests
Unlocks: Staglord's Shield, House Serkis allied knights, Serkis Charger mount
Why it matters: House Serkis provides the best shield in the game (Staglord's Shield) and a permanent allied knight summon that makes overworld combat trivial. The chain also rewards the Great Thief's Gloves and Golden Piggy Bank — both valuable mid-game items. Prioritize this chain if you use a sword-and-shield build.

Critical Path: If you only have time for three side quest chains, do: (1) Greymane Camp Reconstruction for storage, (2) Witches for Abyssal Dragon Armor, (3) Scholastone Institute for Sword of Starlight. Everything else is secondary.

#1 Missable Content Trap: The Blix goblin bounty is the most commonly missed item in the game. Blix only spawns at Unicorn Cliffs before 1:00 PM in-game time. Once you defeat Kailok the Hornsplitter as part of the main story, Blix despawns permanently. Before fighting the Hornsplitter, check if you have the Blix bounty poster and complete it first. The poster is on the contract board in Hernand or on a wall near the Unicorn Cliffs entrance.

The safest strategy to avoid missing content: complete all visible side quests and bounties before advancing the main story. Every time you finish a story chapter, spend 30 minutes clearing outstanding side content before starting the next chapter. This habit alone will save you from 90% of missable content.

🤝 3. Companion Quests — Recruiting Your Mercenary Company

One of Crimson Desert's hidden systems is the companion recruitment mechanic. Certain side quests allow you to recruit named NPCs to your Greymane camp, where they provide passive bonuses, combat support, and unique services. Companion quests are spread across multiple regions and are easy to miss if you are not actively looking for them.

Pro Tip: Oliver is the most valuable companion for reputation farming because he adds an extra daily commission slot at the Greymane Camp. Complete Oliver's Request as soon as you see it — it doubles your daily reputation gain from Greymane Commissions. Oliver's Request is one of the 27 Greymane Commission quests, so cycle through all commission-givers at camp to find him.

Companions do not follow you into combat like traditional RPG companions. Instead, they stay at your Greymane Camp and provide passive bonuses. However, the Hound (from the Hellwood quest) is an exception — it actively assists during fights and can be summoned using a whistle item. The Hound is particularly useful for staggering small enemies, giving you an opening for critical hits.

Final Tip: This checklist is designed for efficiency, but do not let it ruin your enjoyment. Crimson Desert is a beautiful game meant to be explored. If you want to chase a random side quest because it looks interesting, do it. The most important missable items are the Blix bounty, The Price of Coin, and the Jeffrey chain — everything else can be completed at your own pace.

❓ FAQ — Side Quest Questions Answered

How many side quests are in Crimson Desert?

Counting all faction quests, commissions, requests, and bounties, there are over 300 individual quests across all regions. The exact number varies by patch — Patch 1.12.02 (June 24, 2026) added several new commissions to the Demeniss region. This guide covers the ~60 quests that actually matter for progression.

Can I complete side quests after the main story?

Most side quests remain available post-game, but some are permanently locked by story progression. The "missable" quests listed in Section 2 of this guide are the ones that disappear. Post-game, you can freely explore, complete remaining side content, and grind reputation without any time pressure.

What happens if I miss a Suspicious Symbol Note?

The Suspicious Symbol Notes are collectible items dropped by bounty targets. If you miss one (e.g., the Blix note), you permanently lose access to that specific note. There are 8 notes total, and collecting all 8 unlocks a hidden House Celeste quest that rewards an Abyss Artifact. Missing one note means you cannot complete this quest chain in that playthrough.

Is the Abyssal Dragon Armor worth the grind?

Yes. The Abyssal Dragon Armor is the best all-around armor in the game — it offers the highest defense-to-weight ratio, excellent stamina regeneration bonuses, and a unique damage reflection effect. The 22-quest Witches chain takes about 8-12 hours to complete, but the armor serves you for the entire endgame.

Do I need to complete all side quests for 100% achievement?

Yes, there is a "Completionist" achievement that requires all quests completed in a single playthrough. However, due to the missable nature of some quests, this may require multiple playthroughs or a dedicated guide. Focus on the missable quests first, then clean up the rest post-game.

Can I recruit Diederick from the Redfox Trading Post?

Yes, Diederick is recruited through the Greymanes questline at Redfox Trading Post (Delesyia). The quest is called "A Familiar Voice at the Redfox Trading Post." He provides +15% camp material production speed. However, this quest auto-completes if you progress too far in Diederick's recruitment chain, so complete it when you first arrive at the trading post.

What is the best side quest for early-game gear?

"King of the Fallen Kingdom" (House Serkis) rewards the Staglord's Shield — the best shield in the game — and is available as early as Chapter 4. "Savior of the Battlefield" (Hernand Requests) rewards the Rising Arrow Shield, which is easier to obtain but less powerful. For weapons, "The Eldertree" (House Celeste, Chapter 5+) rewards Icewing Plate Armor.

Are there any New Game+ benefits for side quests?

As of Patch 1.12.02 (June 2026), there is no New Game+ mode. Side quest progress, reputation, and collected items do not carry over to new save files. Pearl Abyss has not announced plans for a New Game+ feature, though community speculation suggests it may arrive in a future update.
