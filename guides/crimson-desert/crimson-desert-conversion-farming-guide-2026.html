Conversion rate farming is the backbone of Crimson Desert's endgame economy. The system allows you to transform raw materials into higher-tier items at camp workshops, but the conversion rate ŌĆ?the percentage chance of receiving bonus output from a conversion ŌĆ?depends on multiple factors. How Conversion Rate Works

Every time you convert materials at a camp workshop, the game rolls a conversion check. Your conversion rate determines the chance of triggering bonus output. For example, if you are converting 10 Iron Ore into 5 Steel Ingots with a 20% conversion rate, you have a 20% chance of producing 6-7 ingots instead of 5. The conversion rate applies per batch, not per item, making high-volume conversions more predictable.

Camp Facility Priority Order

  3. Alchemy Station (Lv.3 priority) ŌĆ?Potion and herb conversions. Level 3 unlocks Advanced Potions, which sell for 2.5x the value of basic potions even before conversion bonuses.

Provision Spoilage System

Food provisions have a spoilage timer (real-time, not game-time). Raw ingredients spoil after 48 hours, cooked provisions after 72 hours, and preserved provisions (with Chef Mira assigned) after 120 hours. Spoiled provisions cannot be converted and must be discarded. Always convert food first before other materials, and never stockpile more than 24 hours' worth of raw ingredients unless you have Chef Mira's spoilage reduction.

Not all conversions are created equal. Some items offer significantly better return on investment (ROI) based on market value, conversion rate, and material availability. Below is the ranked list of everything you should be converting, from most efficient to least efficient.

S-Tier: Iron Ore Ōå?Steel Ingot

Iron ore is abundant from Chapter 1 onward, and steel ingots are used in virtually every crafting recipe from weapons to camp upgrades. With Garrett assigned and Workshop Lv.5, your conversion rate reaches 35%. The return on investment: 100 iron ore (easily farmed in 15 minutes) converts into roughly 45-50 steel ingots with bonus procs. Steel ingots sell for 45 gold each at vendors, making this the single most profitable conversion loop in the game.

S-Tier: Common Herbs Ōå?Advanced Potions

With Professor Lennox assigned and Alchemy Station Lv.3, your potion conversion hits 40%. The key here is that Advanced Potions are used in all endgame boss fights. Even if you do not need potions yourself, they trade for Abyss Core fragments at the Veliant exchange NPC (5 potions = 1 Insight III core fragment). This indirect conversion is the real value ŌĆ?potions become crit rate cores.

A-Tier: Raw Food Ōå?Preserved Provisions

With Chef Mira assigned and Kitchen Lv.4, food conversion reaches 50%. Preserved provisions are essential for dispatch missions. Each dispatch consumes 1-3 provisions and returns materials worth 3-5x the provision's cost. This is a force multiplier ŌĆ?provisions are not the end goal but the fuel for your dispatch economy. Prioritize provision conversion over all other food uses.

A-Tier: Timber Ōå?Refined Planks

Wood is less profitable than ore but has one unique advantage: it is used for camp upgrades. Every camp level requires large quantities of refined planks. Since camp upgrades directly increase all conversion rates, timber conversion has a compound return. Invest in this early to accelerate your camp's growth.

B-Tier: Raw Cloth Ōå?Durable Fabric

Cloth conversion is only worth doing when you specifically need armor materials. The conversion rate caps at 28%, and cloth is harder to farm than ore. However, Durable Fabric is required for Black Wing Leather Armor upgrades, so it becomes essential in endgame. Farm cloth only when preparing for specific armor crafts.

B-Tier: Uncut Gems Ōå?Polished Gems

Gem conversion has the lowest base rate (5%) and a low cap (25%). The value is in the special effects ŌĆ?polished gems are used for weapon socket enhancements. This is a late-game activity when you have maxed out other conversion lines. Do not bother with gems until your Workshop is Lv.5 and your camp reputation is maxed.

Storage Expansion Priority

Your camp storage starts with 200 slots and can be expanded to 1000. The expansion costs escalate: 500 gold (to 300), 2000 gold (to 500), 5000 gold (to 750), 10000 gold (to 1000). Prioritize expansion to 500 slots before upgrading any facility past Lv.4. Without enough storage, you cannot accumulate the 10-batch minimum for efficient conversion sessions, and your NPCs cannot queue overnight conversions.

Resource Loss Events

Occasionally, random events at your camp can destroy stored resources. These include bandit raids, fire, and NPC accidents. The chance of a resource loss event scales inversely with your camp defense level. Keep your camp defense upgraded to at least Lv.3 to reduce loss events to less than 5% chance per week. Fortification Abyss Cores socketed in camp facilities also reduce loss severity ŌĆ?each core reduces loss by 15%.

Never Store Everything in One Place: Resource loss events can destroy up to 30% of a single material type. If you have 500 steel ingots in storage, a fire event could destroy 150 of them. Split high-value materials across your camp storage, your personal inventory (50-100 slots), and the Greymane Camp storage (separate 200 slots unlocked via reputation). This way, a single event cannot wipe out your entire stockpile.

Q: What is the fastest way to increase my conversion rate?

Upgrade your Workshop to Lv.5 ŌĆ?this is the single biggest conversion rate boost (flat +10% to all workshop conversions). Second, recruit Garrett (Blacksmith) for +5% ore conversion. Third, assign NPCs correctly ŌĆ?the wrong NPC in a slot provides no bonus. Check the NPC table in Section 2 for the best assignments.

Q: Should I sell converted materials or use them for crafting?

Use converted materials for crafting first, sell the surplus. Steel ingots used in weapon upgrades provide 10x the value of sold ingots. Advanced Potions used in boss fights prevent death costs (repair bills of 500+ gold). The exception is Provisions ŌĆ?their primary value is dispatch missions, not personal use. Convert food Ōå?provisions Ōå?dispatch, then sell or use dispatch returns.

Q: How do I prevent provision spoilage?

Chef Mira (recruited in Chapter 3) reduces spoilage rate by 50%, extending provision shelf life from 72 hours to 120 hours. Additionally, Preserved Provisions (converted from Raw Food) have a 50% longer shelf life than raw ingredients. Never convert more than 48 hours' worth of food at once, and always process provisions first in your daily routine before any other conversion.

Q: Is it worth converting timber if I am not upgrading camp?

No. Timber Ōå?Refined Planks has a 30% conversion rate, which is decent, but refined planks have no use outside camp upgrades and a few specific quests. If you are not actively upgrading your camp, sell raw timber at 10 gold each or skip farming it entirely. Revisit timber conversion only when preparing for the next camp level upgrade.

Q: What should I do with duplicate NPC conversion items like Ancient Relics?

Ancient Relics are used in specific high-level conversions: 3 Relics + 5 Steel Ingots = 1 Refined Ancient Ingot (used for endgame weapon +11 refinement attempts). Keep at least 15 Relics in storage. Sell any surplus at 25 gold each. Do not convert Relics through normal workshop queues ŌĆ?they require a separate NPC interaction (the Scholar NPC at Greymane Camp, recruited via "Ancient Knowledge" quest in Chapter 2).

Q: Can I automate conversion with dispatches?

Partially. Dispatches from Greymane Camp automatically gather and convert materials while you are offline. However, the dispatch conversion rate is only 60% of your camp's active rate (e.g., 35% becomes 21%). Use dispatches for low-priority materials (timber, cloth) and save high-priority conversions (ore, herbs) for your active conversion sessions. Dispatch slots unlock with camp reputation ŌĆ?aim for Reputation Lv.3 for 3 dispatch slots.

Q: What is the best character for farming materials?

Damiane with Greatsword (Absolute Justice) is the fastest material farmer due to her wide sweeping attacks and high mobility. For ore nodes specifically, Kliff with a pickaxe weapon (Chapter 3 reward) has a passive +15% ore yield bonus. For herb gathering, Oongka has no gathering bonuses but his high HP lets him ignore enemies while looting. Our Damiane Build Guide covers the Greatsword farming setup in detail.

Q: How do I unlock the Veliant exchange for potion-to-core trading?

The exchange NPC is located at Veliant Outpost (Chapter 3), behind the main building. Unlocking the exchange requires completing the "Veliant Connection" side quest (rep requirement: Greymane Reputation Lv.3). Once unlocked, you can trade Advanced Potions for Insight III core fragments, Steel Ingots for Destruction III fragments, and Preserved Provisions for Fortification III fragments. This exchange is the primary reason to pursue conversion farming at endgame.
