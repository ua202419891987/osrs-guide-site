ð¯ 1. What Is Mid-Game in Crimson Desert?

Mid-game in Crimson Desert roughly covers Acts 4 through 8, typically between levels 25 and 40 on your first playthrough, or roughly 25-40 hours in. You have left Uzkigrad, explored Hernand and the surrounding regions, and started making real progress toward the end of the main story. This is the phase where the training wheels come off â?enemies hit harder, boss mechanics get more complex, and your starter gear will no longer cut it. You also gain access to the Abyss Gear system around this time, which fundamentally changes how you build your character.

The defining characteristic of mid-game gearing is the introduction of Abyss Artifacts as a core upgrade material. From Refinement Level +5 onward, you cannot progress without them. This is also when you should start thinking seriously about which build you want to commit to for endgame, because resources become scarce and spreading yourself thin across multiple weapon types will slow your progression significantly.

Key Milestone: You have officially entered mid-game when you unlock the Abyss Gear system through the main story, encounter your first Abyss Artifact drops, and start seeing Refinement materials that require more than just ore and silver. If you are still buying Ironclad weapons from Hernand's blacksmith, you are early-game â?keep pushing the story.

The fastest way to get from early mid-game to endgame-ready is not to grind, but to make targeted progress through specific content. Here is the recommended mid-game progression path based on our testing.

The Mid-Game Gear Progression Roadmap

Step 1 â?Start Act 4: Push the main story until you unlock the Abyss Gear system and start seeing Abyss Artifact drops. Do not side-track yet â?the system tutorial is gated behind story progress.
Step 2 â?Secure Your Core Accessories: Travel to Demeniss and loot the four strongboxes: Southern Court Stronghold (Witch's Earring), Lioncrest Manor (Engraved Gold Earring), Windmere Manor (Mark of Darkness), Eastern Court (Necklace of Lightning). This takes about 30 minutes and gives you endgame-viable accessories.
Step 3 â?Craft or Acquire Your Primary Weapon: Pick your weapon from Section 2 above and upgrade it to +5. For Kliff, the Timberham Axe is the easiest to craft. For Oongka, Honed Dual Axes. For Damiane, the Silverwind Rapier.

Step 5 â?Obtain Your First Abyss Gears: Slot your primary weapon with Groundsurge (Chapter 6 boss Myurdin) and Momentum (+35% spin attack damage, from the Staglord boss). These two Abyss Gears form the core of most early mid-game builds.
Step 6 â?Push Your Primary Weapon to +6: Farm Abyss Artifacts from Ashen Rift and Scholastone Institute. +6 is the mid-game "sweet spot" that makes Chapter 7-9 bosses manageable.
Step 7 â?Start Your Endgame Armor Set: Once your weapon and accessories are solid, begin collecting materials for your chosen endgame armor set (Frostcursed or Blackwing). These require high-tier materials from mid-game zones.

â?FAQ

Q: What level should I start worrying about mid-game gear?

Start thinking about dedicated mid-game gear around level 25-30, or when you reach Act 4. Before that, whatever weapon and armor you picked up in the first three acts is sufficient. The gear check comes at the Chapter 5 boss (Crowcaller) â?if you are struggling there, it is time to revisit your loadout.

Q: Should I upgrade early-game weapons or skip straight to mid-game weapons?

Skip straight to mid-game weapons. Early-game weapons (Ironclad, basic Bolton) are not worth investing Abyss Artifacts into. Push your early weapon to +3 using only ore and silver, then save all Abyss Artifacts for the mid-game weapon you choose from Section 2 above.

Q: Which Abyss Gears should I prioritize in mid-game?

Groundsurge (spin attack AoE) and Momentum (+35% spin damage) are the two most impactful early Abyss Gears. Get these first on your weapon. For your helmet, socket Momentum. For chest, prioritize Composure III (stamina recovery) and Vigor III (HP recovery). These three Abyss Gears form the foundation of virtually every mid-game build.

Q: Can I respec my character if I choose wrong weapons?

Yes, but respec costs increase each time you use it. The first respec is cheap (a few thousand silver). By the fifth respec, it costs Abyss Artifacts. Plan your build carefully to avoid wasting resources. Check the CD Guru build guides before committing to a weapon setup.

Q: Is there a best-in-slot weapon for mid-game Kliff?

For Kliff, the Timberham Axe (dual-wield) is generally considered the best mid-game weapon because of its easy crafting recipe, high crit rate, and compatibility with the Groundsurge + Momentum build. If you prefer two-handed swords, the Aeserion Longsword is an excellent alternative with better reach and AoE potential.

Q: When should I stop upgrading mid-game gear and save for endgame?

Stop at Refinement +6 for your primary weapon and +5 for accessories. Beyond these thresholds, the cost in Abyss Artifacts increases dramatically while the returns diminish. Once you reach Chapter 10+, start saving your Abyss Artifacts for endgame gear like the Frostcursed Armor Set and Greater Abyss Gears (4% chance crafts).

Q: Are faction-specific weapons worth using in mid-game?

Yes, but only if you have committed to that faction's path. The Warmaster's Greatsword (Free Companies), Greymane Commander Armor (Loyalists), and Shadowfang Daggers (Shadow Accord) are all viable mid-game options. However, the general-purpose weapons listed above are stronger if you have not yet chosen a faction or want to keep your options open.

Q: What food buffs should I use for mid-game content?

Prioritize foods that boost Stamina Recovery and Critical Rate. Hearty Stew (+stamina recovery) and Spiced Meat (+crit rate) are craftable from mid-game ingredients and last 30 minutes each. For boss fights, add a Fire Capsule (+ATK) for the full buff suite. Check our Cooking & Alchemy Guide for full recipes.
