📜 1. What Unlocks After the Main Story — Full Post-Game Overview

Completing the main story of Crimson Desert is a major milestone — the credits roll, the narrative arc of Kliff and his mercenary company reaches its climax, and you are treated to a bittersweet sense of accomplishment. But the journey does not end there. In fact, for many players, the true game begins after the story concludes. Post-game Crimson Desert is expansive, challenging, and packed with content that the developers deliberately gated behind story completion to preserve the intended narrative pacing. This section explains everything that unlocks, how to access it, and what order to tackle it in.

When you first regain control after the ending cutscene, you will notice that the world state has changed. Certain NPCs have moved to new locations, some regions have become accessible for the first time, and a new quest marker will appear in Uzkigrad directing you to speak with the "Mercenary Chronicler" — an NPC who serves as your gateway to post-game content. Do not skip this conversation. The Chronicler provides your first post-game contract and explains the new world state in digestible terms.

① New Regions Unlocked Post-Story

Three major regions become fully accessible only after completing the main story. These are not just "more of the same" — they feature new enemy types, higher-level gear, and some of the most challenging content in the game.

🌿 The Whispering Marshes (Recommended Level: 65-75)

This toxic swamp region was partially accessible during the story, but the deeper areas were blocked by a magical barrier tied to the main antagonist. Post-story, the barrier dissipates, revealing the Marshes' true scope. The region is home to poison-based enemies, new Spirit-type bosses, and the hidden settlement of the Marsh Dwellers, who sell unique light armor and Spirit-infused weapons. The Marshes also contain one of the five legendary weapon locations (the Whisperwind Bow, detailed in Section 2).

⛰️ The Peak of Uzkigrad (Recommended Level: 75-85)

The mountain region north of Uzkigrad fortress was visible throughout the entire game but unreachable due to a collapsed bridge. Post-story, a new questline ("The Broken Path") tasks you with repairing the bridge and ascending the mountain. The Peak is a high-altitude region with ice physics, wind hazards that affect movement, and the game's hardest mandatory boss (the Frostbound Colossus). This region also contains the endgame crafting stations where you can upgrade legendary gear to its maximum potential.

🏚️ The Sunken Catacombs (Recommended Level: 70-80, Dungeon)

An underground dungeon complex that was sealed at the start of the game. Access is granted by finding the Catacomb Key during a post-story side quest chain ("Echoes of the Fallen Kingdom"). The Catacombs are a traditional dungeon crawl — narrow corridors, trap mechanics, and waves of undead enemies. The reward at the end is substantial: a guaranteed legendary armor piece (random based on your current equipped armor type) and the recipe for endgame consumables.

② New Systems and Features

Beyond new regions, several game systems unlock or expand after the story:

  • Mercenary Company Management: Pre-story, your mercenary company is largely cosmetic. Post-story, you can recruit new company members, assign them to roles (quartermaster, scout, blacksmith), and send them on automated contracts that generate passive income. This is the game's "base building" light system.
  • Difficulty-Scaled Bounties: The contract board in Uzkigrad now offers "Elite" and "Mythic" tier contracts. These are significantly harder than story contracts and offer the best gold and gear rewards in the game. Elite contracts are recommended for level 65+, Mythic for level 80+.
  • New Game+ Activation: After completing all post-story side quests (approximately 15-20 hours of content), you gain the option to start NG+ from the title screen. NG+ is covered in detail in Section 3.
  • Secret Ending Requirement: There is a secret "true" ending that can only be unlocked by completing specific post-story content before clicking "Yes" on the NG+ prompt. The requirements are: complete all 12 mercenary company side quests, find all 5 legendary weapons, and defeat the secret boss "The Abyssal Knight." This is the ultimate completionist goal.
Pro Tip: Before diving into post-story content, make sure you have a fully upgraded weapon and at least one piece of epic-quality armor. The difficulty spike in the Whispering Marshes is real. If you struggle, go back to Uzkigrad and upgrade your gear by one more level before proceeding.

🗡️ 2. Legendary Weapon & Armor Locations — Complete Map Guide

Legendary gear in Crimson Desert represents the pinnacle of equipment — each piece has unique stats, set bonuses, and often a special passive ability that defines an entire build. There are exactly 5 legendary weapons and 4 legendary armor sets in the base game. All of them require solving multi-step quests, defeating challenging bosses, or exploring hidden areas. This section provides a complete location guide for every legendary item, including the exact steps to acquire them and tips for making the fights easier.

⚔️ Inverted Edge Greatsword (Weapon — Two-Handed Sword)

Stats: 285 base damage, +15% heavy attack damage, +10% armor penetration, special passive "Executioner's Judgment" (deals 50% max health as bonus damage to enemies below 25% health).

Location: Dropped by the optional boss "The Fallen Champion" in the Whispering Marshes. To spawn this boss, you must first light the three Marsh Beacon fires (marked on your map after accepting the "Champion's Remains" side quest from the Mercenary Chronicler). Once all three beacons are lit, return to the central marsh at night (in-game time 22:00–04:00) and the boss arena will open.

Tips for the fight: The Fallen Champion uses the same greatsword moveset as the player, so his attacks are highly telegraphed. However, he has one mechanic you do not — a "champion's charge" where he dashes across the arena. Dodge toward him when he charges to avoid the AOE slam that follows. Bring fire-resistance potions; his weapon has a hidden fire enchantment that applies burn DOT.

🏹 Whisperwind Longbow (Weapon — Bow)

Stats: 195 base damage, +20% arrow velocity, +15% Spirit damage on arrows, special passive "Wind's Whisper" (arrows have a 15% chance to silence enemies, preventing Spirit casts for 5 seconds).

Location: Found in a hidden chest in the Whispering Marshes. The chest is not marked on the map. From the Marshes' fast-travel point, head southeast to the three withered trees. Behind the middle tree is a narrow path that leads to a cave. The chest is at the back of the cave, behind a breakable wall (use a heavy attack to break it). No boss fight required, but the cave is guarded by 8 high-level archer enemies.

Tips: The cave archers have exceptional accuracy. Use Spirit magic to create a distraction (summon a spirit ally) before entering, or snipe them from outside the cave entrance with a long-range bow. The breakable wall does not respawn, so you only need to clear the cave once.

🛡️ Tower Shield of Uzkigrad (Weapon — Shield, Off-Hand)

Stats: 450 physical defense, 300 Spirit defense, +25% block efficiency, special passive "Uzkigrad's Last Stand" (when health drops below 20%, automatically triggers a defensive aura that reduces all incoming damage by 50% for 10 seconds; cooldown 5 minutes).

Location: Reward for completing the "Echoes of the Fallen Kingdom" side quest chain (5 quests). This chain starts by speaking to the Historian NPC in Uzkigrad's library (unlocked post-story). The final quest requires defeating the "Sentinel Spirit" boss in the Sunken Catacombs. The shield is awarded automatically upon completion.

Tips: The Sentinel Spirit boss is weak to lightning damage. If you have access to Lightning Spirit magic or a weapon with lightning enchantment, the fight becomes significantly easier. The Historian NPC gives you a hint about this if you exhaust all his dialogue options before starting the quest chain.

🗡️ Twin Fang Daggers (Weapon — Dual-Wield)

Stats: 165 base damage (per dagger), +30% bleed stack application speed, +12% critical hit chance, special passive "Twin Fang Technique" (dual-wielding attacks have a 10% chance to trigger an additional strike that deals 100% weapon damage).

Location: Crafted, not found. The recipe is sold by the "Exiled Blacksmith" NPC, who appears at a random location in the open world each in-game day. To find him, check the blacksmith's note on the Uzkigrad contract board — it updates daily with his current location. Once found, the recipe costs 50,000 gold and requires: 10 Inverted Steel Ingots, 5 Shadow Essence (dropped by Shadow-type enemies in the Marshes), and 1 Eternal Fang (dropped by the "Alpha Fenris" world boss).

Tips: The Alpha Fenris world boss spawns in the open world every 3 in-game days at a random location (marked by a wolf head icon on the map). He is extremely difficult solo — bring the best gear you have and consider summoning co-op allies if you have access to multiplayer. The fight has a enrage timer at 5 minutes; if you have not killed him by then, he gains 300% damage and will wipe you.

📖 Archmage's Tome (Weapon — Spirit Tome, Off-Hand)

Stats: +45 Spirit damage, +30% Spirit regen rate, +2 Spirit summon slots, special passive "Archmage's Insight" (reduces Spirit cast time by 20% and makes Spirit casts unable to be interrupted by enemy attacks).

Location: Found in the secret library within the Sunken Catacombs. After completing the Catacombs for the first time, a hidden lever becomes interactable in the final room. Pulling it opens a passage to the secret library. The tome is on the central altar. However, taking it triggers a boss fight with the "Bound Librarian" spirit — a challenging enemy that uses all Spirit magic types against you.

Tips: The Bound Librarian teleports frequently. Use Spirit magic that leaves a damage-over-time effect on his last known position, then dodge away. The fight is essentially a 1v1 mage duel. If you are not a Spirit build, consider respeccing temporarily or asking a Spirit-focused friend to help.

③ Legendary Armor Sets

In addition to the five legendary weapons, there are four legendary armor sets. Each set has a 4-piece bonus that defines a specific playstyle. The sets are: Executioner's Set (heavy, greatsword synergy), Windwalker Set (light, bow synergy), Bastion Set (heavy, tank synergy), and Spirit Weaver Set (light, mage synergy).

🎯 3. New Game+ Preparation — What Changes and How to Prepare

New Game+ (NG+) in Crimson Desert is not just a simple "restart with your gear." It is a carefully designed endgame mode that preserves most of your progression while significantly increasing the challenge. Understanding exactly what carries over, what resets, and what changes in enemy behavior is essential for a successful NG+ run. This section is your complete preparation guide — follow it, and your NG+ experience will be challenging but fair rather than a frustrating stat check.

④ What Carries Over to NG+

  • Character Level and Stats: Your level, stat point allocation, and skill tree upgrades all carry over. This is the primary power fantasy of NG+ — you are the same powerful Kliff, but the world has become harder to match you.
  • Equipment: All weapons, armor, and accessories in your inventory carry over. This includes legendary gear. However, enemy scaling means that even with endgame gear, early-game enemies will still pose a challenge (see "Enemy Scaling" below).
  • Mercenary Company: Your company members, their assigned roles, and your company reputation level all carry over. This means you start NG+ with passive income already flowing.
  • Map Exploration: Areas you have discovered remain discovered, but fog of war returns for unexplored portions of previously visited regions. Fast-travel points remain unlocked.
  • Achievements and Collectibles: Achievement progress carries over (they are account-wide). Collectible items (lore pages, weapon schematics) do not reset — if you collected them in your first playthrough, they remain collected.

⑤ What Resets in NG+

  • Story Progress: Obviously, the story resets. You start back at the prologue. However, story events that granted permanent unlocks (like the skill tree or the contract board) are skipped — you get immediate access to these systems without redoing the tutorials.
  • Side Quest Progress: All side quests reset. This is actually a good thing — it gives you reasons to engage with the world again and provides a fresh source of XP and rewards.
  • NPC Relationships: NPC attitude toward Kliff resets to neutral. Your story choices from the first playthrough do not carry over. This means you can experience different narrative paths and endings in NG+.
  • World State: Barriers, locked doors, and region access restrictions all reset. You will need to progress through the story again to reach post-game regions, though you can do it much faster with your endgame gear.

⑥ Enemy Scaling in NG+

Enemies in NG+ have their stats scaled based on your character level. The exact formula is not published, but community testing suggests that enemy health is multiplied by approximately 2.5x, enemy damage by 2.0x, and enemy aggression/AI intelligence is noticeably improved. Early-game enemies that you one-shot in your first playthrough will now take 3-4 hits and can actually kill you if you are not paying attention. This scaling makes NG+ feel like a genuine challenge rather than a victory lap.

✅ NG+ Preparation Checklist — Do This Before Starting

  • Reach level 80 (max) and allocate all stat points optimally for your chosen build
  • Upgrade your main weapon to +10 (maximum) and your armor set to +8 or higher
  • Complete the "Echoes of the Fallen Kingdom" side quest chain to get the Tower Shield (if tank build)
  • Farm at least 100,000 gold — you will need it for upgrading new gear in NG+
  • Complete all 12 mercenary company side quests (required for secret ending in NG+)
  • Find all 5 legendary weapons (optional, but makes NG+ significantly easier)
  • Defeat the Abyssal Knight secret boss (optional, unlocks secret ending path)
  • Stock up on high-tier consumables (healing draughts, stamina potions, resistance elixirs) — craft or buy at least 50 of each
  • Consider a "NG+ specific build" respec — some builds are stronger in NG+ than others due to enemy scaling

⑦ Recommended Builds for NG+

Not all builds that are strong in the first playthrough remain strong in NG+. Due to enemy scaling and improved AI, certain builds become more or less effective. Based on community feedback from NG+ runners:

  • Best NG+ Build: Spirit Archer (S-tier in base game, S+ in NG+). The safety of ranged combat becomes even more valuable when enemies hit harder. Spirit buffs also scale exceptionally well with NG+ enemy health pools.
  • Viable but Harder: Berserker Greatsword. Still deals massive damage, but enemies in NG+ have faster attack patterns and will punish your recovery frames more often. Only recommended if you are confident in your dodge timing.
  • Surprisingly Strong: Sword & Board Tank. The improved enemy AI means enemies are better at flanking and surrounding you, which makes the defensive utility of a shield more valuable than in the first playthrough. Not the fastest clear speed, but the safest NG+ experience.
Pro Tip: NG+ allows you to skip cutscenes. If you are doing NG+ primarily for the challenge and the secret ending, do not feel bad about skipping dialogue you have already read. The game even adds a "lore summary" option that recaps the story so far, letting you refresh your memory without sitting through cutscenes.

👹 4. Endgame Boss Roster — All Optional & Post-Story Bosses

The endgame boss roster in Crimson Desert is where the game's combat system truly shines. These are not the scripted, telegraphed story bosses — they are intelligent, aggressive, and designed to test everything you have learned. There are 9 endgame bosses in total: 6 optional bosses scattered across post-story regions, 2 post-story mandatory bosses, and 1 secret boss that requires solving an environmental puzzle to access. This section provides a complete roster with strategies for each.

Frostbound Colossus EXTREME

Location: Peak of Uzkigrad, summit area (post-story only)
Recommended Level: 80+
Mechanics: This is the hardest mandatory boss in the game. The Colossus is a massive ice golem that fills half the arena. Its attacks have enormous AOE but slow wind-up times. The key mechanic is "Frost Armor" — every time you break his posture, he regenerates it with increased defense. You must break his posture three times to expose his core, then unleash your strongest attacks during the 15-second vulnerability window. If you fail to kill him in three vulnerability windows, he enrages and gains one-shot potential on all attacks.
Strategy: Use fire damage (weapon enchantment or Spirit magic) to negate his ice resistance. Stay close to his legs to avoid his AOE slam, and never attack his head — it has 80% damage reduction. The fight takes 8-12 minutes for most builds. Bring at least 10 healing draughts.

The Abyssal Knight EXTREME (Secret)

Location: Hidden arena beneath Uzkigrad (secret boss)
Recommended Level: 80+
Mechanics: The Abyssal Knight is widely considered the hardest boss in Crimson Desert. He uses a moveset that combines every weapon type — he switches between sword, greatsword, bow, and Spirit magic mid-fight. He also has a "shadow clone" phase at 50% health where he creates two copies of himself that must be distinguished from the real one (the real one has a faint purple aura around his weapon). Getting hit by his ultimate attack ("Abyssal Execution") deals 80% max health as damage in a single hit.
Strategy: There is no "cheese" strategy for this boss. You must learn his full moveset and respond appropriately. The community consensus is that this boss is easier at range — his melee combos are devastating, but his ranged attacks are more telegraphed. Spirit Archer build is recommended. The fight drops the "Knight's Resolve" accessory, which is required for the secret ending.

Alpha Fenris HARD (World Boss)

Location: Random spawn in open world (marked by wolf head icon)
Recommended Level: 75+
Mechanics: Alpha Fenris is a world boss that spawns every 3 in-game days. He is a massive wolf with lightning-fast bite attacks and a howl that summons 4 smaller wolves. The smaller wolves must be killed quickly, or they buff Alpha Fenris with "Pack Leader" (+30% damage). At 30% health, he enters "Blood Frenzy" — attack speed doubled, damage doubled, but defense halved. This is the ideal time to use your strongest skills.
Strategy: Kill the smaller wolves ASAP. They have low health but high damage. Use AOE attacks or Spirit summons to draw their aggro. The "Blood Frenzy" phase is when you should use your ultimate abilities and consumables. Solo viable but much easier with co-op allies.

The Fallen Champion HARD

Location: Whispering Marshes (night only)
Recommended Level: 70+
Mechanics: Covered in Section 2 (drops the Inverted Edge Greatsword). The key additional mechanic to know: at 40% health, he gains a "champion's shield" that blocks all damage for 10 seconds. The shield can be broken by attacking his weapon (the glowing part of his greatsword) during his recovery animation. Missing this window extends the fight significantly.
Strategy: Watch for the shield activation animation (he raises his sword and glows gold). Immediately switch targets to his sword and attack it. Three heavy attacks will break the shield. If you miss the window, you must dodge for 10 seconds until the shield expires naturally.

Sentinel Spirit MEDIUM

Location: Sunken Catacombs, final chamber
Recommended Level: 70+
Mechanics: A Spirit-based boss that primarily uses magic attacks. He teleports between four fixed positions in the arena, casting Spirit orbs from each. Every time he teleports, he leaves a Spirit mine on the ground that explodes after 3 seconds. The fight is essentially a dodge-fest with occasional windows to attack.
Strategy: Use the arena pillars for cover against his Spirit orbs. Attack him only when he teleports to a pillar and has a cast-time animation. Lightning damage is super effective. The fight is on the easier side for endgame bosses but can be frustrating due to the constant movement required.

Bound Librarian MEDIUM

Location: Secret library, Sunken Catacombs
Recommended Level: 70+
Mechanics: A Spirit mage boss that uses the player's own magic against them. He can cast Spirit tome skills, including summoning Spirit clones. The fight is a duel of attrition — he does not have high health, but he heals 10% every time he successfully hits you with a Spirit cast.
Strategy: Interrupt his casts with fast attacks (light attack chains). If you are a melee build, close the gap immediately and stay on him — he has no melee moves and will try to teleport away. If you are a mage build, mirror his casting with faster spells. The fight drops the Archmage's Tome.

🎯 5. Best Endgame Activities — What to Do at Max Level

Reaching max level and acquiring legendary gear is not the end of Crimson Desert — it is the beginning of the "real" endgame where you can engage with the game's systems at their fullest. But with so many activities available, it can be unclear where to spend your time. This section ranks the best endgame activities by value — where "value" means a combination of fun, rewards, and long-term progression. Whether you are a completionist, a challenge seeker, or a social player, there is meaningful content here for you.

⑨ Activity #1: Mythic Contract Hunting (Best Gold and XP)

Mythic-tier contracts are the highest difficulty of side quests, recommended for level 80+ players with fully upgraded gear. They are essentially miniature boss runs — each contract sends you to a specific location to defeat an elite enemy or clear a high-level dungeon. The rewards are substantial: 15,000-25,000 gold per contract, legendary-tier crafting materials, and enough XP to reach level caps that were previously inaccessible. There are 12 Mythic contracts in total, and completing all 12 is required for the "Mythic Mercenary" achievement.

Why it is the best activity: The gold-per-hour ratio of Mythic contracts is unmatched. If your goal is to farm gold for crafting or upgrading, this is where you do it. The contracts also drop rare materials that cannot be obtained任何其他方式, making them mandatory for players who want to max-out their legendary gear.

⑩ Activity #2: Endless Tower (Challenge Mode)

The Endless Tower is a post-story unlock — a roguelite-style challenge mode where you fight through waves of enemies in an ascending tower. Each floor has a random modifier (e.g., "All enemies have +50% health but deal -20% damage," or "Healing items are 50% less effective"). You can attempt the tower solo or with up to 2 co-op partners. The tower has 50 floors in total, and each 10th floor has a mini-boss. Reaching floor 50 awards the "Tower Master" title and a unique cosmetic armor set.

Why it is great: The Endless Tower is the best test of build flexibility. The random modifiers force you to adapt your playstyle, and the co-op mode is genuinely fun with friends. The unique cosmetic reward is also a status symbol — only a small percentage of players have reached floor 50.

⑪ Activity #3: Mercenary Company Management (Passive Progression)

Post-story, your mercenary company becomes a meaningful system. You can recruit up to 8 additional company members (beyond the story characters) and assign them to roles that generate passive income or provide bonuses. For example, assigning a member to "Quartermaster" reduces the cost of all shop items by 10%. Assigning a member to "Scout" reveals hidden chests on your map. The company members also have their own mini-quests that deepen their character arcs and provide unique rewards.

Why it matters: This is the closest thing Crimson Desert has to "base building." It is not for everyone, but if you enjoy the mercenary fantasy of managing a company and sending subordinates on missions, it adds dozens of hours of content. The passive income also funds your other endgame activities, creating a virtuous cycle.

⑫ Activity #4: Achievement Hunting and Completionism

Crimson Desert has 87 achievements, ranging from simple ("Reach level 10") to excruciatingly difficult ("Defeat the Abyssal Knight without taking damage"). Many achievements reward unique cosmetics, titles, or rare crafting recipes. The "completionist" ending (unlocked by earning all achievements in a single save file) is widely considered the true ending of the game, even more so than the secret story ending.

Which achievements to prioritize: Start with the missable ones. Several achievements are tied to specific story choices or NPCs that can be permanently killed. The community maintains a "missable achievements guide" on the official forums — consult it before making any major story decision in NG+. The "Defeat all bounty targets" achievement is also worth prioritizing, as bounty targets only appear under specific weather and time conditions and can take hours to spawn.

⑬ Activity #5: Co-Op Endgame Content (Multiplayer)

Crimson Desert's co-op mode allows you to summon up to 2 other players into your world (or join theirs) for collaborative gameplay. Post-story, co-op becomes significantly more active because high-level players are running Mythic contracts and Endless Tower attempts. Co-op is also the only way to access "Raid" content — 3-player coordinated boss fights with mechanics that require role distribution (tank, DPS, support).

How to find co-op partners: The game has an in-game LFG (looking for group) system accessible from the main menu. You can set your preferred role, desired activity, and language. The community is active and helpful — don't be afraid to put yourself out there even if you are not a pro player. Most co-op veterans are happy to carry newer players through tough content in exchange for the social experience.

Pro Tip: If you are struggling with a specific endgame activity, check if it can be co-op'd before grinding solo. Many activities that feel impossible alone become manageable (and more fun) with two teammates. The co-op summoning system uses "Mercenary Sigils" — consumable items that drop from any boss. Stock up on them during easier content so you always have a way to call for help.

⑭ Endgame Activity Priority List (Recommended Order)

If you are unsure where to start, follow this priority order based on reward value and progression impact:

  1. First: Complete all 12 Mythic contracts (best rewards, unlocks crafting recipes)
  2. Second: Farm and upgrade one legendary weapon to +10 (massivers your DPS)
  3. Third: Attempt the Endless Tower (tests your build, fun co-op)
  4. Fourth: Develop your mercenary company (passive benefits compound over time)
  5. Fifth: Achievement hunt (long-term goal, unique rewards)
  6. Sixth: Start NG+ when you feel ready (recommended after achieving at least 70% completion)

❓ FAQ — Endgame Questions Answered

How long does it take to reach the endgame (max level, legendary gear)?

For a focused player, reaching level 80 and acquiring one legendary weapon takes approximately 60-80 hours. This assumes you are doing side content along the way and not just rushing the main story. If you are a completionist who wants all legendary gear and full completion, budget 120-150 hours for your first playthrough. NG+ can be completed in 30-40 hours due to your retained power level and knowledge of the game systems.

Can I access all post-story content in a single playthrough, or do I need NG+?

All post-story content (new regions, legendary gear, endgame bosses) is accessible in a single playthrough after completing the main story. NG+ is not required to experience any of the content described in this guide. However, NG+ does add increased difficulty and the potential for a secret ending, which provides motivation for a second playthrough. Think of post-story content as "endgame" and NG+ as "endgame+" — both are valuable.

What is the most efficient way to farm gold at max level?

Mythic contracts are the most efficient gold source (15,000-25,000 per contract, ~20 minutes per contract = ~45,000-75,000 gold per hour). The second-best method is selling excess crafting materials to the Exiled Blacksmith — he pays 2x the rate of regular merchants for rare materials. If you have already completed all Mythic contracts, the Endless Tower floors 30-40 drop valuable materials consistently.

Is the secret ending worth the effort required to unlock it?

Yes, if you care about narrative closure. The secret ending provides additional story context that recontextualizes the entire game and gives Kliff's character arc a more satisfying conclusion. It also awards a unique accessory (Knight's Resolve) that provides +10 to all stats, which is valuable for NG+. That said, if you primarily play for gameplay rather than story, the secret ending is skippable — the gameplay rewards, while nice, are not game-breaking.

Can I change the difficulty of endgame content if it is too hard?

Crimson Desert does not have traditional difficulty settings, but you can effectively "lower" the difficulty of endgame content by upgrading your gear further, respeccing into a stronger build, or summoning co-op allies. The game's adaptive difficulty means that enemy scaling is partly based on your gear level. Wearing weaker gear does not actually make enemies weaker — it just makes you weaker. So the only way to make content easier is to improve your character, not adjust a menu setting.

What happens if I start NG+ before completing all post-story content?

You can start NG+ at any time after the main story ends — you are not forced to complete post-story content first. However, once you start NG+, your current save file is archived and you begin a new playthrough. Your post-story progress on the old save is preserved — you can return to it at any time by loading the old save from the title screen. So there is no risk in starting NG+; you can always go back. That said, many players prefer to complete all post-story content first to experience it with their strongest character.
