How to Beat Matthias in Crimson Desert

Matthias fights aggressively and will try to close the distance. Learn to interrupt his combos with kicks and Force Palm, exploit his recovery windows, and counter his relentless pressure with calculated aggression.

⚔️ 1. Matthias Overview — The Aggressive Duelist

Matthias is Crimson Desert's first real skill-check boss. Unlike the Staglord, which tests your patience and positioning, Matthias tests your reaction speed and combo knowledge. He is a humanoid duelist who fights with a longsword and uses many of the same combat mechanics you do — parries, combos, spirit skills, and stamina management. This makes him both familiar and dangerous, because he punishes the exact mistakes that the game has been letting you get away with until now.

Warning: Matthias will parry your heavy attacks if you use them predictably. Unlike normal enemies, he reads your input and will block heavy attacks (R2) if you use them more than twice in a row. Mix up light and heavy attacks, or he will parry you and counterattack for massive damage.

🗡️ 2. Phase 1 — Reading His Combo Strings

Matthias has four primary combo strings in Phase 1. Each starts with a distinct opening animation that tells you which combo is coming. Learning to read these openings is the key to the entire fight — if you can identify the combo in its first 0.3 seconds, you can choose the correct response every time.

ComboOpening AnimationFollow-UpBest Response
Slash Combo (L-L-H)Right foot steps back, sword raised highTwo horizontal slashes into overheadDodge backward after first slash, punish the overhead recovery
Lunge ThrustSword drops to hip level, body coilsSingle fast forward thrustDodge sideways — do not block (pierces guard)
Spin AttackBody turns slightly right, sword extends360-degree spinning slashDodge backward, then close in for 2-3 hits
Parry BaitMatthias raises shield stance, waitsIf you attack, he parries and countersDo NOT attack — wait for him to drop the stance (2s), then punish
Pro Tip: The Parry Bait is Matthias's most deceptive move. He will enter a defensive stance and wait for you to attack. If you do, he parries and deals 40% of your health. The correct response is to do nothing — stand still and wait. After 2 seconds, he exits the stance with a slow overhead slash that is easy to parry or dodge, giving you a free punish window.

🔥 3. Phase 2 — Spirit Weapon & Range Pressure

At 50% health (approximately 9,000), Matthias sheathes his longsword and draws a spirit weapon — a glowing energy blade that extends his reach by 30% and adds spirit damage to all attacks. His combo strings change, and he gains two new abilities.

Phase 2 New Attacks

AttackTelegraphCounter
Spirit WaveSword glows bright, then horizontal slash releases energy waveDodge sideways or jump — wave travels along ground
Phantom StepMatthias briefly disappears, reappears behind youDodge forward the moment he vanishes — he always appears behind

The Phantom Step Counter

Phantom Step is Matthias's most dangerous Phase 2 ability. When he vanishes, you have 0.5 seconds to react. The key insight: he ALWAYS reappears directly behind your current position. Instead of turning around, dodge forward immediately when he vanishes. This puts you behind HIM when he reappears, giving you a 1.5-second window to land 3-4 hits on his back (which takes 30% bonus damage).

🦵 4. Interrupt Strategy — Kicks & Force Palm

The community has discovered that Matthias is uniquely vulnerable to interrupts. While most enemies have hyper-armor that prevents interruption during combo startup, Matthias does not — his combo startup frames can be interrupted by specific attacks. This is the secret to making the fight significantly easier.

1

Kick Interrupt

Press the kick button (Triangle/Y/Right Click) during Matthias's combo startup animation. The kick has a fast startup (0.2 seconds) and will stagger him out of his combo, leaving him open for 1 second. This works on all Phase 1 combos except Parry Bait. Best used against the Slash Combo — kick during the right-foot step-back animation.

2

Force Palm Interrupt

Force Palm (unlocked in Keen Senses tree, row 2 left — costs 2 Abyss Artifacts) is the stronger interrupt. It deals spirit damage and stagger simultaneously. Use Force Palm against the Spin Attack startup — the extended range of Force Palm catches Matthias before his spin begins. Force Palm costs 20% spirit per use, so you can interrupt 3-4 times per spirit bar.

3

The Pump Kick Strategy

The community calls this the "Pump Kick" strategy: alternate kicks and Force Palms to keep Matthias permanently staggered. Kick → he staggers → hit twice → he starts a combo → Force Palm → he staggers → hit twice → he starts a combo → kick. This loop can carry you through the entire fight without ever letting Matthias complete a single combo. It requires rhythm and spirit management, but it makes the fight trivially easy once mastered.

Pro Tip: The Pump Kick strategy is especially effective in Phase 2, where Matthias's spirit weapon makes his combos more dangerous. By interrupting every combo before it starts, you never have to deal with Spirit Wave or Phantom Step at all. The fight becomes a rhythm game rather than a reaction test.

❓ FAQ — Matthias Questions Answered

Q: Can I grapple Matthias?

No. Matthias has hyper-armor against grapples. He will counter-grapple you if you attempt it. Use kicks and Force Palm for interrupts instead of grapples.

Q: Is Matthias's Longsword worth using?

Yes — it is one of the best longswords in the early-to-mid game, with balanced stats and a unique passive that increases parry window by 5%. Keep it until you can craft the Staglord Antler Greatsword or find a Chapter 4 weapon.

Q: How do I unlock Force Palm if I do not have enough Abyss Artifacts?

Visit a Memory Keeper to respec, or farm more Artifacts using the methods in our Abyss Artifacts Farming Guide. Force Palm costs 2 Artifacts and is worth every point.

Q: What happens if Matthias parries me?

You take approximately 40% of your health as damage and are staggered for 1 second. Immediately dodge backward after recovering to avoid his follow-up. Do not attack again until he has finished his counter combo.

Q: Can I use the Pump Kick strategy in Phase 1 and Phase 2?

Yes. The strategy works in both phases. In Phase 2, Matthias's combos are more dangerous, making interrupts even more valuable. The rhythm is the same — kick, hit, Force Palm, hit, repeat.
