📋 1. 1.10 Update Overview — What Changed and Why It Matters

The June 2026 1.10 update for Crimson Desert is the most significant content drop since launch, introducing flying mounts, a pet companion system, two new mini-games, and a complete overhaul of the building and camp system. Pearl Abyss has packed an enormous amount of new content into this patch, and if you have not logged in for a while, now is the perfect time to return. This guide covers every major addition and change in exhaustive detail.

Version 1.10 went live on June 6, 2026, after a brief server maintenance window. The update is free for all players and requires no additional purchase. The download size is approximately 8.4 GB on PC and 6.2 GB on consoles. If you are playing on Steam Deck, the update is fully compatible and runs at a stable 40 FPS with the recommended settings.

① Patch Highlights at a Glance

The headline features of 1.10 are the flying dragon mount, the cuckoo bird pet, two new mini-games (pinball and ball roll), and the complete rework of the building system. Beyond these marquee additions, the patch also includes over 60 bug fixes, balance adjustments to combat, improved AI behavior for enemies, and quality-of-life improvements such as faster loading times and a redesigned inventory sorting algorithm.

② How to Access the New Content

Most of the new content is available immediately after updating, but some features require progression unlocks. The flying dragon mount becomes available after completing the new side quest "Wings of the Desert," which is triggered by speaking to the NPC Renna at the Uzkigrad stable. The cuckoo pet is obtained through a separate quest chain starting at the Pywel market district. The mini-games can be found in major cities at the new "Arcade Pavilion" buildings.

⛵ 2. New Mounts — Flying Dragon & Riding Changes

The flying dragon mount is the crown jewel of the 1.10 update. For the first time in Crimson Desert, players can take to the skies and explore Pywel from above. The dragon, officially named the Pywellan Wyvern, is a mid-sized winged mount that can glide, dive, and perform aerial sprints. It fundamentally changes how you navigate the open world.

③ How to Unlock the Flying Dragon

To unlock the Pywellan Wyvern, you must complete the side quest "Wings of the Desert," which becomes available after reaching level 25 and completing the main story chapter "The Mercenary's Code." The quest chain involves three steps: finding the dragon egg in the Highland Caverns, incubating it at your camp, and raising the hatchling to maturity by feeding it specific meats and minerals. The entire process takes approximately 3-4 hours of gameplay.

Pro Tip: The dragon egg is located in the deepest chamber of the Highland Caverns, behind a waterfall. Bring a torch — the chamber is pitch black. The egg weighs 50 units, so make sure your inventory has space before picking it up.

④ Flying Controls and Stamina System

The flying dragon uses a stamina-based flight system. Holding the sprint button causes the dragon to flap its wings and gain altitude, consuming stamina at a rate of approximately 15 points per second. Releasing the button allows the dragon to glide, slowly descending while regenerating stamina at 5 points per second. Diving builds speed and can extend your flight range significantly. A skilled rider can cross the entire map of Pywel in a single flight by alternating between climbing, gliding, and diving.

The dragon has a base stamina pool of 200 points, which can be increased to 350 through the new "Mount Bond" growth system. Each level of Mount Bond adds 15 stamina points and is earned by flying, feeding, and caring for your dragon. The bond system is passive — you do not need to grind specific activities. Simply using the mount regularly will naturally increase the bond level over time.

🐔 3. New Pet System — The Cuckoo Bird Companion

The cuckoo bird is the first pet companion introduced in Crimson Desert, and it is far more than a cosmetic follower. This colorful bird perches on Kliff's shoulder during dialogue scenes and flies alongside you during exploration. In combat, the cuckoo provides passive buffs that scale with its bond level. At max bond, the cuckoo grants a 10% increase to item drop rates, a 5% boost to movement speed out of combat, and occasionally distracts enemies by swooping at them during fight encounters.

⑤ How to Obtain the Cuckoo Pet

The cuckoo pet is obtained through the quest "A Bird in the Hand," which starts at the Pywel market district. An NPC named Maren will ask you to help catch a runaway cuckoo bird that has been causing chaos in the market stalls. The quest involves a short tracking sequence, a mini-game where you must corner the bird without scaring it away, and finally a bonding scene where the cuckoo decides to stay with Kliff. The entire quest takes about 20 minutes to complete.

⑥ Pet Growth and Bond Mechanics

The cuckoo pet has its own growth system separate from Kliff's level. Pet experience is earned by feeding the cuckoo specific foods — berries, nuts, and small fish. Each food type provides different amounts of XP and affects the cuckoo's mood. A happy cuckoo (fed its favorite foods) earns 50% more XP per feeding. The cuckoo has 10 bond levels, with each level unlocking new passive abilities or improving existing ones. At bond level 5, the cuckoo learns to scavenge for small items during combat, occasionally bringing you healing herbs or crafting materials.

🎱 4. New Mini-Games — Pinball & Ball Roll

Version 1.10 introduces two new mini-games that can be found at Arcade Pavilions in major cities. These are not just distractions — they offer unique rewards including cosmetic items, crafting materials, and exclusive equipment skins that cannot be obtained anywhere else in the game. Both mini-games support local leaderboards, allowing you to compete with friends and other players in your region.

⑦ Pinball Machine — How It Works

The pinball machine is a surprisingly deep mini-game that faithfully recreates the physics of a real pinball table. You control two flippers using the left and right trigger buttons (or the A and D keys on PC). The objective is to keep the ball in play as long as possible while hitting targets to accumulate points. The table features three bumpers, two ramps, a spinning wheel, and a hidden bonus area that opens when you hit all five targets in sequence. High scores unlock cosmetic rewards, with the top tier (50,000 points) awarding an exclusive golden weapon skin.

⑧ Ball Roll — Precision and Patience

Ball Roll is a physics-based puzzle game where you guide a ball through increasingly complex courses by tilting the environment. You control the tilt using the left stick (or arrow keys), and the ball responds to gravity in real time. There are 20 courses in total, each with a par time and a hidden collectible. Completing all 20 courses unlocks a unique mount cosmetic — a set of armored barding for your horse. The courses range from simple ramps to fiendish labyrinths with moving platforms and traps.

🔨 5. Building System Rework — What Changed

The building system has been completely overhauled in 1.10, addressing community feedback about the clunky placement mechanics and limited customization options. The new system introduces snap-to-grid placement, a modular wall system, and the ability to save and load building templates. These changes make base construction faster, more intuitive, and far more flexible than before.

⑨ New Placement Mechanics

Buildings now snap to an invisible grid, eliminating the frustrating misalignment issues that plagued the old system. You can toggle between free-place and grid-snap modes with a single button press. The grid size is adjustable in three increments: fine (0.5m), medium (1m), and coarse (2m). Walls, floors, and roofs now use a modular system where you can mix and match materials — stone foundations with wooden walls and thatched roofs, for example. This opens up vastly more architectural possibilities without requiring additional resources.

⑩ Template Save & Load

You can now save up to 10 building templates and load them at any camp site. This means you can design your ideal base layout once and instantly deploy it wherever you set up camp. Templates preserve the exact placement of every wall, door, furnishing, and crafting station. Loading a template requires the same total materials as building from scratch, but it eliminates the placement time entirely — the structure assembles itself in a satisfying animation over about 10 seconds.

⚡ 6. Combat Balance Changes & Bug Fixes

Version 1.10 includes a substantial round of combat balance adjustments and over 60 bug fixes. The most impactful changes are to the parry system, enemy AI, and weapon scaling. These changes are designed to make combat feel more responsive and fair without reducing the overall difficulty.

⑪ Parry Window Adjustments

The parry window has been increased by approximately 3 frames (from 8 to 11 frames at 60 FPS). This change was made in response to community feedback that parrying felt inconsistent, particularly against fast-attacking enemies. The golden shimmer visual tell has also been made more visible, appearing 2 frames earlier than before. These changes make parrying more reliable without making it trivial — you still need to time your press correctly, but the margin of error is now more forgiving.

⑫ Enemy AI Improvements

Enemy AI has been significantly improved. Enemies now use more varied attack patterns, coordinate better in groups, and are less likely to stand passively while you attack their allies. Group enemies will now attempt flanking maneuvers, and ranged enemies will reposition to maintain optimal distance. These changes make combat encounters more dynamic and less predictable, requiring you to stay alert throughout every fight.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

Is the 1.10 update free?

Yes, the 1.10 update is completely free for all Crimson Desert owners. There is no additional purchase required, and all new content — mounts, pets, mini-games, and building improvements — is included in the base game update.

Do I need to start a new save to access the new content?

No, you can access all new content on your existing save file. The flying dragon quest becomes available at level 25, the cuckoo pet quest is available immediately, and the mini-games can be played at any time by visiting an Arcade Pavilion.

How big is the update download?

The update is approximately 8.4 GB on PC (Steam) and 6.2 GB on PlayStation and Xbox. If you are on a metered connection, plan accordingly. The update includes new assets for mounts, pets, mini-game environments, and building materials.

Will my existing buildings be affected by the building rework?

No, your existing buildings will remain intact. The new building system applies to new construction only. However, you can dismantle old structures for a full material refund and rebuild them using the new snap-to-grid system if you prefer the updated mechanics.

Are the mini-games required to progress in the story?

No, the mini-games are entirely optional. They offer cosmetic rewards and exclusive skins, but none of the rewards are required for story progression or combat effectiveness. They are purely for fun and collection.
