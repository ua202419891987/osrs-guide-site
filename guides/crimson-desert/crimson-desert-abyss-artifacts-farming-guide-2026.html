💎 1. What Are Abyss Artifacts & Why They Matter

Abyss Artifacts are the single most important progression currency in Crimson Desert. Every skill in the Keen Senses tree — from basic combat passives to game-changing abilities like Spirit Surge and Aerial Force Palm — costs Abyss Artifacts to unlock. Unlike silver, which flows freely from combat and selling loot, Abyss Artifacts are deliberately scarce. The game gives you just enough through normal story progression to unlock roughly half the skill tree by endgame, which means every Artifact you spend matters.

The core problem most players face is that they spend Artifacts impulsively on early skills, then arrive at critical boss fights like Tenebrum without the specific abilities needed to win. This guide will help you farm efficiently AND spend wisely, so you never find yourself stuck behind a skill-check boss without the tools to succeed.

🎭 3. Method 1 Deep Dive — Pickpocketing at Scholar Stone Institute

Pickpocketing is the surprise winner of the farming efficiency race. The Scholar Stone Institute, located in the academic district of Uzkigrad, is filled with NPCs who carry Abyss Artifacts in their pockets. The pickpocketing mechanic is unlocked through the Keen Senses skill tree (row 1, far left node — costs 1 Artifact to unlock, which pays for itself within 10 minutes).

1

Unlock Pickpocketing

Spend 1 Abyss Artifact on the "Light Fingers" skill in the Keen Senses tree (row 1, far left). This is the only prerequisite skill you need. Without it, the pickpocket prompt will not appear when you approach NPCs.

2

Go to the Scholar Stone Institute

Travel to the Scholar Stone Institute in Uzkigrad. The institute has 12-15 NPCs wandering the halls, most of them scholars and students. Approach an NPC from behind (you must be behind them — pickpocketing from the front always fails) and press the interact button when the pickpocket prompt appears.

3

The Pickpocket Mini-Game

A timing bar appears with a moving marker. Press the button when the marker is in the green zone. Success rate depends on your timing accuracy — perfect timing gives 100% success, while missing the green zone results in failure and NPC aggro. Failed pickpockets cause the NPC to become hostile and alert nearby guards, so precision matters.

Warning: If you are caught pickpocketing, the NPC and nearby guards will become hostile. You can run away and leave the area for 5 minutes to reset their aggro — they will not remember you when you return. Do not fight the guards; they scale to your level and are not worth the consumables.

With practice, a full clear of the Scholar Stone Institute takes approximately 8-10 minutes and yields 4-7 Abyss Artifacts. Reset and repeat for a steady 6-10 Artifacts per hour — the best rate available before endgame.

🔮 4. Method 2 Deep Dive — Sealed Challenges

Sealed Challenges are special combat encounters scattered across Pywel. They are marked on your map with a purple seal icon. Each challenge locks you into an arena with waves of enemies and a time limit. Completing a Sealed Challenge rewards 2-3 Abyss Artifacts, and each challenge can be repeated once per in-game day.

There are 15 Sealed Challenges in total, but only 8 are available before endgame. The most efficient farming route is to clear all 8 available challenges in a circuit — this takes approximately 45-60 minutes and yields 16-24 Abyss Artifacts. Combined with pickpocketing, you can earn 25-35 Artifacts per hour using both methods together.

⚙️ 5. Abyss Gears — The Conversion Currency

Abyss Gears are a secondary currency that can be converted into Abyss Artifacts at a 1:1 ratio at any Abyss Altar (found in major cities). Abyss Gears drop from Abyss-tier enemies, endgame bosses, and can be obtained by dismantling Abyss-tier equipment. If you have outgrown Abyss gear and replaced it with better equipment, dismantle the old gear for Abyss Gears, then convert them to Artifacts at an altar.

Pro Tip: Never sell Abyss-tier equipment to vendors — they pay only 500-800 silver per piece. Dismantling gives you 1-3 Abyss Gears, which convert to 1-3 Abyss Artifacts. Since each Artifact is worth far more than 800 silver in terms of skill power, dismantling is always the better choice. See our Inventory Guide for more keep-vs-sell decisions.

❓ FAQ — Abyss Artifact Questions Answered

Q: Can I farm Abyss Artifacts infinitely?

Technically yes, but with diminishing returns. Pickpocketing can be repeated infinitely by resetting the area, and Sealed Challenges reset daily. Abyss Gears from dismantling Abyss equipment is finite (limited by how much Abyss gear exists in the game), but farming Abyss-tier enemies provides a slow but infinite source.

Q: What happens if I respec — do I lose Artifacts?

No. Respeccing at a Memory Keeper refunds all spent Artifacts. You can then re-spend them however you like. The first respec is free; subsequent respecs cost silver starting at 5,000 and doubling each time up to 80,000.

Q: Is pickpocketing worth the risk of getting caught?

Absolutely. Getting caught only results in temporary NPC aggro that resets after 5 minutes. The reward — 6-10 Artifacts per hour — far outweighs the minor inconvenience of running away. Just do not fight the guards; run and wait.

Q: How many Abyss Artifacts do I need for the full skill tree?

The complete Keen Senses tree costs approximately 80 Artifacts. Through normal story progression, you will earn about 40-50. The remaining 30-40 must be farmed. Using the methods in this guide, expect to spend 4-6 hours of dedicated farming to unlock the entire tree.

Q: Should I buy Abyss Artifacts from vendors?

Yes, but vendor stock is limited. Each major city's provisioner sells 1-2 Artifacts for 3,000-5,000 silver each. Buy every one you can afford — they are the cheapest skill points you will ever purchase. Check back after each in-game day, as stock refreshes.
