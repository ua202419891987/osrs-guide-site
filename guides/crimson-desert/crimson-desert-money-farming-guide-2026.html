1. Crimson Coin Economy — How Money Works in Pywel

Before diving into specific farming routes and methods, it is essential to understand how the in-game economy of Crimson Desert functions. The developers at Pearl Abyss have created a surprisingly deep economic system that goes beyond simple "kill monsters, sell loot" gameplay. Understanding the economy — including currency types, vendor price mechanics, the player-driven market, and hidden money sinks — will help you make informed decisions about where to invest your time and how to manage your hard-earned wealth. This foundational knowledge will make every subsequent farming method more profitable and your overall progression smoother.

The primary currency in Crimson Desert is the "Crimson Coin" (CC), a gold-like currency that is used for virtually all transactions: buying equipment, upgrading weapons, purchasing consumables, paying for fast travel, and bribing NPCs. Crimson Coins are obtained through almost every activity in the game: defeating enemies, completing contracts, selling loot, finding hidden treasures, and receiving quest rewards. However, the amount of CC you earn from each activity varies dramatically, and understanding these variances is the key to efficient farming. A well-informed player can earn 3-4x more CC per hour than a player who simply plays without economic awareness.

① Vendor Pricing and Item Value Tiers

Not all loot is created equal, and understanding vendor pricing tiers will help you decide what to keep, what to sell, and what to dismantle for crafting materials. Items in Crimson Desert are divided into five rarity tiers: Common (white), Uncommon (green), Rare (blue), Epic (purple), and Legendary (gold). Common items have very low vendor value — typically 1-10 CC — and are usually not worth keeping unless you need them for crafting. Uncommon items are worth 10-50 CC, Rare items 50-200 CC, Epic items 200-1000 CC, and Legendary items 1000+ CC. However, these are base vendor prices — the actual selling price is influenced by several factors discussed below.

Vendor prices are not fixed across all merchants. Different merchants in different regions offer different prices for the same item, reflecting the regional economy. For example, a "Fine Steel Sword" might sell for 80 CC to the blacksmith in Uzkigrad but 95 CC to the weapons dealer in the southern trade city of Kalet. The difference is not huge, but when you are selling dozens of items per session, these small differences add up. More importantly, some merchants have limited gold reserves — if you sell too many items to a single merchant in a short period, their buy price will decrease temporarily. Rotating between multiple merchants in different cities is a simple but effective strategy to maintain high selling prices.

Pro Tip: The "Appraisal" skill (unlocked in the Spirit skill tree) increases the vendor value of your loot by up to 25% at max level. If you are serious about money farming, invest at least a few points into this skill early. The return on investment is dramatic — the skill pays for itself within 2-3 hours of farming.

② Money Sinks — Where Your Gold Goes

Understanding where your gold will be spent is just as important as knowing how to earn it. Crimson Desert has several significant money sinks that will drain your coffers if you are not careful. The largest money sink is equipment upgrades — upgrading a weapon from +5 to +6 can cost 500-1000 CC depending on the weapon rarity, and the cost increases exponentially at higher upgrade levels. The second largest sink is consumables — healing potions, buff potions, and ammunition for ranged weapons add up quickly, especially if you die frequently and need to restock. Fast travel is a smaller but noticeable sink, costing 10-50 CC per use depending on distance.

Knowing these money sinks helps you prioritize your farming methods. If you are currently focused on upgrading your weapon, you need a steady income of 500-1000 CC per gameplay session. If you are also stocking up on consumables for a difficult boss, you might need 1500-2000 CC per session. Having a clear financial goal for each play session — "I need to earn 800 CC tonight to afford my next weapon upgrade" — makes farming feel purposeful rather than aimless, and purpose-driven farming is always more efficient than wandering around hoping to stumble into money.

③ The Player Market — Trading with Other Players

While vendor NPCs are the primary way to convert loot into gold, Crimson Desert also features a player-to-player trading system. You can sell items directly to other players, either through the in-game marketplace (accessible in major cities) or through direct trades. The marketplace is particularly valuable for selling rare crafting materials, high-end equipment, and cosmetic items that vendors will not pay high prices for. Some items that vendor for 50 CC can sell to other players for 500-1000 CC if there is demand, making the marketplace an essential tool for serious money farmers.

However, the player market has risks. Marketplace fees (a 10% tax on all sales) reduce your profits, and there is a delay between listing an item and it selling — your gold is tied up in listed items until they sell. Direct trades with other players avoid the tax but require finding a buyer and agreeing on a price, which takes time and social effort. For most players, a hybrid approach works best: sell common and uncommon loot to vendors for immediate gold, and use the marketplace only for rare and Epic+ items that are worth the effort of listing and waiting.

💰 2. Beginner Farming Routes (Level 1-20)

The early levels of Crimson Desert are when money is tightest and when efficient farming matters most. You need gold for your first weapon upgrade, your first set of decent armor, and a stock of healing potions to survive the challenges ahead. Fortunately, the areas around Uzkigrad (your starting hub city) are rich with accessible farming opportunities that require minimal gear and low combat skill. This section details the best beginner farming routes, the exact locations to farm, the respawn timers you need to know, and the expected gold per hour for each method. Even as a new player, you can earn a steady 200-400 CC per hour with these routes.

All of the routes in this section are designed to be safe for low-level players. They involve enemies that are at or below your level, have predictable attack patterns, and are located in areas with quick access to a merchant for selling your loot. None of these routes require a group — they can all be done solo. However, if you do have a friend to play with, the routes become even more efficient because you can split the farming duties (one player focuses on killing, the other on looting) and cover more ground per hour.

🌾 Route 1: Wolf Pelt Run — Uzkigrad Northern Woods (Level 3-10)

Location: The forested area immediately north of Uzkigrad, marked as "Northern Woods" on the map. Look for the pack of grey wolves that spawn near the river bend.

Enemies: Grey Wolf (Level 3-5), Alpha Wolf (Level 6-8, rare spawn).

Loot: Wolf Pelt (sells for 8-12 CC each), Wolf Meat (sells for 2-3 CC each, used in cooking), occasionally Wolf Fang (sells for 15-20 CC, used in crafting).

Respawn Timer: Wolves respawn in packs of 4-6 every 90 seconds. The Alpha Wolf is a rare spawn with approximately a 10% chance to appear in place of a regular wolf.

Gear Required: None — your starting longsword is sufficient. Bring 2-3 healing potions just in case.

Expected GP/hr: 200-300 CC per hour. At higher levels (8+), you can increase this to 350-400 CC by also killing the occasional bandit patrol that passes through the area.

Risk Assessment: Low — Wolves are easy to kill and rarely pose a threat. The only risk is accidentally aggroing multiple wolves at once, which can be avoided by pulling them one at a time.

⚔️ Route 2: Bandit Camp Clear — Southern Crossroads (Level 8-15)

Location: The bandit camp south of Uzkigrad, marked on the map as "Crossroads Bandit Camp." It is a small encampment with 5-7 bandits, a lookout tower, and a chest in the leader's tent.

Enemies: Bandit Thug (Level 8-10), Bandit Archer (Level 9-11), Bandit Leader (Level 12-14, mini-boss that spawns only when you attack the camp).

Loot: Bandit's Dagger (sells for 25-35 CC), Leather Scraps (sells for 5-8 CC each, used in crafting), occasionally Bandit's Map (leads to a small treasure cache worth 100-200 CC). The chest in the leader's tent always contains 50-100 CC plus a random uncommon item.

Respawn Timer: The entire camp resets every 8 minutes. This is the optimal loop time — clear the camp, loot everything, then go do something else for 8 minutes (or go to another nearby farming spot) before returning.

Gear Required: A weapon with at least +1 upgrade and a few healing potions. The bandit archers can be annoying at low level, so consider bringing a shield to block their arrows.

Expected GP/hr: 350-500 CC per hour. The Bandit Leader mini-boss drops significantly better loot, so if you can defeat him consistently, your hourly rate can reach 600 CC.

Risk Assessment: Medium — Bandits attack in groups and the archers can deal significant damage if you do not deal with them first. However, with basic combat skills, this route is very manageable and offers the best early-game gold per hour.

🍄 Route 3: Herb Gathering + River Spot — Eastern Riverbanks (Level 5-15)

Location: The riverbanks east of Uzkigrad, stretching from the city's eastern gate to the waterfall. This route combines combat farming with resource gathering.

Enemies: River Crab (Level 5-7), Marsh Viper (Level 8-10, venomous — bring anti-venom if you have it).

Loot: River Crab Shell (sells for 10-15 CC each, used in crafting), Snake Venom Gland (sells for 30-40 CC each, used in crafting poisons), plus various herbs that grow along the riverbanks (Glowroot, sells for 12-18 CC each; Sunpetal, sells for 20-30 CC each).

Respawn Timer: Crabs and snakes respawn every 2 minutes. Herbs have a longer respawn timer of 5 minutes, so you need to memorize multiple herb spawn locations to maintain efficiency.

Gear Required: None for the gathering portion. For the combat portion, any weapon will work, but a spear or polearm is ideal for keeping distance from the snakes.

Expected GP/hr: 250-400 CC per hour. The variation depends on how many herbs you find — some sessions you will get lucky with rare herb spawns, other sessions less so. This is the safest route for low-level players.

Risk Assessment: Low — The enemies are weak, and the herb gathering requires no combat at all. This is the ideal route for players who want to farm while also exploring and enjoying the scenery.

Pro Tip: When farming the Bandit Camp (Route 2), do not just kill the bandits — loot everything, including the arrows from the archers (you can use them if you have a bow) and the food from their campfire (free healing!). Every item has some value, and the habit of thorough looting will serve you well throughout the entire game.

💰 3. Mid-Game Farming Methods (Level 20-45)

Once you reach level 20 and have upgraded your gear to at least +3, a whole new world of farming opportunities opens up. The methods in this section require better gear, more combat skill, and sometimes a small amount of travel to reach the farming locations. However, the rewards are significantly higher — you can expect to earn 600-1200 CC per hour with these methods, which is more than enough to fund your weapon upgrades, armor purchases, and consumable needs. This is also the level range where you should start paying attention to the player marketplace, as some of the loot you obtain will be worth significantly more to other players than to vendors.

At this stage of the game, you should also start thinking about "farming efficiency" — not just how much gold you earn per hour, but how much play time you are investing versus how much progression you are making. The best mid-game farming methods are those that combine gold farming with other progression goals: gaining experience points, collecting crafting materials for your own use, and discovering new areas of the map. The routes below are all designed to provide multiple progression benefits simultaneously, ensuring that your farming time is never "wasted" time.

🏰 Method 1: Castle Ruins Mob Run — Forsaken Castle (Level 20-30)

The Forsaken Castle is a ruined fortress located northeast of Uzkigrad. It is inhabited by ghostly soldiers and undead knights, which drop "Spirit Shards" (sells for 40-60 CC each) and "Rusted Armor Scraps" (sells for 15-25 CC each). The castle also has a 15% chance to drop "Ghostly Essence," a crafting material that sells for 150-250 CC on the player marketplace. The enemies here are tougher than bandits but have predictable attack patterns. A full clear of the castle interior and courtyard takes about 12 minutes, and the respawn timer is 10 minutes — meaning you can loop this method efficiently. Expected GP/hr: 700-900 CC. Risk: Medium — the undead knights have a charging attack that can one-shot low-health players.

🐉 Method 2: Wyvern Scale Farm — Scaleridge Mountains (Level 28-38)

The Scaleridge Mountains, located in the northern part of the map, are home to juvenile wyverns that drop "Wyvern Scales" (sells for 80-120 CC each) and "Wyvern Hearts" (sells for 200-350 CC each, rare drop). This is one of the most profitable mid-game farming methods, but it requires a weapon with at least +4 upgrade and a good understanding of dodge timing, as wyverns have a breath attack that deals damage over time. The wyverns spawn in groups of 2-3 and respawn every 4 minutes. Expected GP/hr: 900-1300 CC (higher if you get lucky with Wyvern Heart drops). Risk: High — Wyverns hit hard and their breath attack can be deadly if you do not have fire resistance gear.

🚢 Method 3: Smuggler's Cove Contract Loop — Coastal Caves (Level 25-35)

Smuggler's Cove is a series of coastal caves that can be accessed by accepting the "Smuggler's Cove" contract from the Uzkigrad contract board. This contract is repeatable and resets every in-game day. The caves are filled with smugglers (human enemies with good loot) and trapped chests (disarm the traps for bonus loot). The smugglers drop "Smuggled Goods" (sells for 60-100 CC each) and occasionally "Black Market Ledger" (sells for 500-800 CC to the informant NPC in Uzkigrad). The cave also has mining nodes for iron and silver ore, adding gathering income to your gold income. Expected GP/hr: 800-1100 CC (higher if you get a Black Market Ledger drop). Risk: Medium — The smugglers use ranged pistols and traps, which can deal significant damage if you are not careful.

④ Optimizing Mid-Game Farming Efficiency

The key to efficient mid-game farming is "route chaining" — linking multiple farming spots into a single loop so that by the time you return to the first spot, the enemies have respawned. For example, you can combine the Forsaken Castle (Method 1) with the nearby herb gathering spots in the adjacent forest, and then swing by a bandit camp on your way back. This approach ensures that you are always engaged and always earning, with no downtime waiting for respawns. Plan your farming routes carefully, and consider using the in-game map markers to mark respawn locations for future reference.

Another efficiency tip specific to mid-game farming is to invest in "Gathering Speed" skills in the skill tree. The "Gathering Speed" node (located in the Stamina skill tree) reduces the time it takes to harvest herbs, mine ore, and loot containers by 30%. This may seem like a minor bonus, but over the course of a farming session, those saved seconds add up to minutes of additional farming time. Similarly, the "Movement Speed" nodes (also in the Stamina tree) make traveling between farming spots faster, which directly increases your gold per hour by reducing downtime.

Warning: At around level 30-35, you may be tempted to attempt high-level farming spots that are technically accessible but challenging. Avoid this temptation unless you are confident in your gear and skills. Dying at a farming spot means losing progress and potentially losing loot (if you die with a full inventory and cannot retrieve your death cache). It is better to farm a slightly lower-level spot efficiently than to repeatedly die at a higher-level spot and make no progress.

💰 4. High-Level Gold Farming (Level 45+)

At level 45 and above, you are considered an endgame player in Crimson Desert. Your gear is likely upgraded to +7 or higher, you have a well-developed skill tree, and you have mastered the combat system. At this stage, money farming becomes less about "surviving" and more about "optimizing" — you have the capacity to earn massive amounts of gold, but you need to choose your methods wisely to maximize your hourly rate. The methods in this section are the most profitable in the game, with potential earnings of 1500-3000+ CC per hour. However, they also require significant time investment to set up (unlocking the locations, obtaining the required gear) and carry higher risks.

High-level farming is also where group play becomes a major advantage. Many of the best high-level farming spots are challenging or inefficient to farm solo, but become highly profitable and manageable with a group of 2-3 friends. If you have a regular co-op group, prioritize farming spots that benefit from coordinated play — the gold per hour is significantly higher, and the content is more enjoyable when shared. That said, all of the methods below can be done solo if you prefer single-player farming.

💎 Method 1: Diamond Coast Treasure Hunting (Level 45-55)

The Diamond Coast is a late-game region on the southeastern edge of the map. It is famous for its "buried treasure" mechanic — using treasure maps (obtained from various sources) to dig up chests that contain gold, gems, and rare items. A single buried treasure chest can contain 500-2000 CC worth of loot, and some chests contain "Raw Diamonds" (sells for 800-1200 CC each). Treasure maps can be obtained from enemies, purchased from the collector NPC in Kalet, or found in the world. The respawn timer for treasure dig spots is 20 minutes, but there are 15+ dig spots along the Diamond Coast, so you can chain them into a long loop. Expected GP/hr: 1500-2500 CC (highly variable based on map luck). Risk: Low — The Diamond Coast has few hostile enemies, and treasure hunting requires no combat.

⚔️ Method 2: Arena of the Fallen — Wave Defense (Level 50+)

The Arena of the Fallen is an endgame arena located in the central region of Pywel. It offers a wave-defense mode where you fight increasingly difficult waves of enemies for rewards. Each wave takes 2-4 minutes to complete, and the rewards scale with the wave number — wave 10+ rewards are significantly better than wave 1-5. The arena can be attempted solo or in a group (up to 4 players). In a group, the waves are more difficult, but the rewards are multiplied, making group arena runs the most gold-efficient endgame activity. Expected GP/hr: 2000-3500 CC (group runs). Risk: High — Higher waves feature elite enemies and bosses that can easily defeat an unprepared player.

🌋 Method 3: Volcanic Forge Mining — Ignis Peak (Level 52+)

Ignis Peak is a late-game zone accessible only after completing the "Ignis Peak" story mission. It features unique mining nodes that yield "Ignis Ore" (sells for 200-350 CC each) and "Volcanic Crystal" (sells for 500-800 CC each, used in high-end crafting). The mining nodes respawn every 6 minutes, and there are 12 nodes in the zone, making for an efficient farming loop. However, the zone is inhabited by fire elementals that hit extremely hard and have AOE fire attacks. You need fire resistance gear and a weapon with at least +8 upgrade to farm here safely. Expected GP/hr: 1800-2800 CC. Risk: High — The fire elementals can one-shot unprotected players, and the zone has environmental heat damage that drains your health over time.

🏆 Method 4: World Boss Farming (Level 48+)

As mentioned in the Co-op Guide, World Bosses spawn every 4 hours and drop unique loot that can be sold for high prices. At endgame, World Boss farming becomes one of the most profitable activities — not because the bosses drop gold directly, but because they drop rare items that sell for 2000-5000+ CC on the player marketplace. World Bosses require a group (ideally 4+ players) and significant combat skill, but the rewards are among the best in the game. Expected GP/hr: Highly variable — if you get a good drop, you can earn 5000+ CC in a single boss kill. However, bad luck can mean several boss kills with minimal profit. Risk: High — World Bosses are challenging and require coordination.

Pro Tip: At endgame, consider "flipping" items on the player marketplace — buy low, sell high. This requires knowledge of item prices and market demand, but can be more profitable than farming enemies. Spend some time studying the marketplace to understand price trends before investing your gold.

6. Selling Tips — Maximize Your Profits

Earning loot is only half the battle — you also need to sell it effectively to convert your farming efforts into usable gold. Many players leave significant amounts of gold on the table by selling items suboptimally: selling to the first vendor they see, selling at low marketplace prices, or holding onto items that they should sell immediately. This section covers the art of selling in Crimson Desert, including vendor selection, marketplace strategy, price checking, and when to hold onto items for future use rather than selling them immediately. Master these selling tips, and you can increase your effective gold income by 20-30% without farming a single additional minute.

⑤ Vendor Selection — Who Pays the Most?

As mentioned in Section 1, different vendors offer different prices for the same item. However, the differences go beyond simple regional pricing. Some vendors have "specialties" — the blacksmith in Kalet pays 15-20% more for weapons and armor than the blacksmith in Uzkigrad, while the alchemist in the mage quarter pays more for herbs and crafting materials. Learning which vendors pay the most for which item types is a significant efficiency booster. Here is a quick reference:

🗡️ Weapons & Armor

Sell to: Blacksmith in Kalet (pays 15-20% premium). If Kalet is not yet accessible, the blacksmith in Uzkigrad is the second-best option. Avoid selling weapons to general goods merchants — they pay 20-30% less.

🌿 Herbs & Crafting Materials

Sell to: Alchemist in any major city (pays 10-15% premium). The alchemist in Kalet has the highest prices, but the difference between cities is small for crafting materials. For common herbs, selling to any alchemist is fine.

🍖 Food & Consumables

Sell to: General goods merchant or the tavern keeper. The tavern keeper in Uzkigrad pays slightly more for food items (5-10% premium), reflecting the tavern's need for ingredients. However, if you have the cooking skill, consider keeping food items to cook into more valuable meals.

💎 Gems & Rare Items

Sell to: The collector NPC (available in Kalet and Uzkigrad, marked with a gem icon on the map). The collector pays significantly more for rare items, gems, and cosmetics — often 2-3x the price of a regular vendor. If you have a rare item, always check the collector's buy price before selling it elsewhere.

⑥ Marketplace Strategy — Selling to Players

The in-game marketplace is where you can sell items to other players, often for much higher prices than vendors will pay. However, the marketplace has a 10% tax on all sales, a listing fee (5-10 CC per item), and a delay before your item sells. To use the marketplace effectively, you need to understand what items are in demand, how to price competitively, and when to accept vendor prices instead of listing on the marketplace.

The most important rule of marketplace selling is: only list items that vendor for at least 100 CC. Below that threshold, the marketplace fees and delay make vendor selling more profitable. For items that vendor for 100-500 CC, the decision depends on how quickly you need the gold — if you need gold now, sell to a vendor; if you can wait, list on the marketplace and hope for a higher price. For items that vendor for 500+ CC, the marketplace is almost always the better choice, as players will often pay 2-3x the vendor price for rare crafting materials, high-end equipment, and cosmetics.

Pro Tip: Use the marketplace's "Price History" feature (accessible when listing an item) to see the average selling price of that item over the past 7 days. Price your item slightly below the average to ensure a quick sale, or slightly above the average if you are willing to wait for a buyer who does not check price history. Never list an item at the maximum price in the hope of getting lucky — overpriced items sit on the marketplace for weeks without selling.

⑦ When NOT to Sell — Item有用性判断

Not every item should be sold. Some items are more valuable to keep than to convert into gold, either because they are useful for your own progression or because their value will increase in the future. Here are some guidelines for when to hold onto items rather than selling them:

Crafting Materials: If you are working toward a specific crafting recipe, keep the materials even if they are worth good gold. The cost of buying them back later (if they are even available) will be higher than the gold you got from selling them. Upgrade Materials: Items used for weapon/armor upgrades (like "Upgrade Stones") should never be sold — they are scarce and essential. Quest Items: Some quest items can be sold after the quest is complete, but others are needed for later quests or achievements — when in doubt, keep them. Cosmetic Items: If you think you might want to use a cosmetic item for transmogrification later, keep it. Cosmetic items are account-bound and cannot be repurchased if you sell them.

💰 7. AFK and Low-Attention Farming Options

Not every farming session needs to be an intense, high-focus experience. Sometimes you want to make progress while watching a video, chatting with friends, or just relaxing. Crimson Desert offers several "AFK-friendly" or "low-attention" farming methods that allow you to earn gold with minimal active input. These methods are not the most gold-efficient — you will earn less per hour than with active farming — but they are valuable for players who want to make progress while multitasking, or for players who find active farming stressful or boring. This section details the best AFK and low-attention farming options, their expected gold per hour, and how to set them up safely.

Important Note: True "AFK" farming (leaving your character unattended) is not possible in Crimson Desert — you will be disconnected after 15 minutes of inactivity, and even before disconnection, enemies will kill your unattended character. The methods below are "low-attention," meaning they require occasional input but can be done while doing something else. Do not attempt to leave your game completely unattended — it will not work and may result in death and lost loot.

🌊 Method 1: AFK Fishing — Any Water Source (Level 5+)

Fishing is the closest thing Crimson Desert has to true AFK farming. Equip a fishing rod (purchased from any general goods merchant), find a body of water, and press the fish key. Your character will automatically cast and reel in fish. You need to click to reel in when a fish bites (indicated by a splash animation and audio cue), but this only requires occasional input every 20-40 seconds. Different fish sell for different amounts — the "Glowfish" (caught in glowing water at night) sells for 40-60 CC each and is the most profitable catch. Expected GP/hr: 150-300 CC. Low attention required: Very low — you can easily do something else while watching for the fishing cue.

🪓 Method 2: Passive Resource Gathering — Safe Zones (Level 10+)

Resource nodes (herbs, ore veins, wood logs) in safe zones (areas without hostile enemies) can be gathered with minimal attention. The key is to find a safe zone with multiple resource nodes close together, mine/harvest them, and then wait for them to respawn while doing something else. The respawn timer for resource nodes is 3-5 minutes, giving you plenty of time to alt-tab or look away. Expected GP/hr: 200-400 CC (depending on resource type and market prices). Low attention required: Low — check back every few minutes to gather respawned nodes.

🎯 Method 3: Low-Level Mob Grinding — Safe Enemy Groups (Level 15+)

Some enemy groups in the game are weak enough that you can defeat them with minimal effort — for example, using AOE attacks or abilities that automatically target nearby enemies. Find a group of low-level enemies in a safe area (no higher-level enemies nearby), and rotate between AOE attacks. You still need to pay attention to your health and occasionally use healing items, but the combat is so easy that it becomes almost automatic. Expected GP/hr: 300-500 CC. Low attention required: Medium — requires occasional input but is very repetitive and low-stress.

While AFK and low-attention farming methods are less gold-efficient than active farming, they serve an important purpose in a long-term farming strategy. Not every gaming session needs to be optimally efficient — sometimes you just want to relax and still feel like you are making progress. Use these methods when you are tired, when you want to multitask, or when you just want to enjoy the game world without the pressure of optimization. Gaming should be fun, and if active farming stops being fun, switch to a low-attention method and give yourself a break.

Pro Tip: If you are going to do low-attention farming for an extended period, consider doing it in a co-op session with friends who are also doing low-attention farming. You can keep each other company, revive each other if something goes wrong, and the social aspect makes the repetitive farming much more enjoyable. Low-attention co-op farming is a great way to spend time with friends who also play Crimson Desert but may not be available for intense boss fights.

FAQ — Money Farming Questions Answered

What is the single best farming method for a new player?

The Bandit Camp Clear (Route 2 in Section 2) is the best all-around farming method for new players. It is accessible at level 8, requires minimal gear, has a good gold per hour (350-500 CC), and teaches you Combat skills that are useful throughout the game. Plus, the bandit camp is close to Uzkigrad, making it easy to sell your loot and restock on supplies.

How much gold do I need to enjoy the game comfortably?

For a comfortable experience, aim to have 500-1000 CC on hand at all times for consumables and repairs, plus enough saved for your next weapon/armor upgrade (which can cost 500-2000 CC depending on the item). You do not need to be rich to enjoy Crimson Desert — the game is balanced so that casual farming covers your expenses. Only if you want the absolute best gear will you need to do serious gold farming.

Are there any money-making methods that do not involve combat?

Yes! Fishing, herb gathering, and treasure hunting (using treasure maps) are all non-combat farming methods. They are less gold-efficient than combat farming, but they are perfectly viable for players who dislike combat or want a break from fighting. The Diamond Coast treasure hunting (Method 1 in Section 4) is particularly profitable and requires no combat at all.

Should I buy gold from third-party websites?

Absolutely not. Buying gold from third-party websites is against the game's terms of service and carries a high risk of your account being banned. It also hurts the game's economy and funds unethical practices. All the gold you need can be earned through legitimate farming methods — it may take time, but it is safe, legal, and more satisfying to earn your wealth through gameplay.

What should I do with my gold once I have "enough"?

Once you have enough gold to cover your expenses and upgrades, consider investing in cosmetic items, supporting other players (trade them good gear), or saving for future content updates that may introduce new expensive items. There is no "gold cap" or endgame sink that requires infinite gold — once you are comfortable, feel free to spend your gold on fun rather than necessity.
