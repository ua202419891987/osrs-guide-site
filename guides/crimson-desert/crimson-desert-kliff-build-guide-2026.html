√∞¬ü¬é¬Ø 1. Kliff Overview & Playstyle

Kliff Macduff is the protagonist of Crimson Desert and the most versatile character in the entire game. He is the only character capable of using all 13 weapon types (including the Patch 1.08.00 firearms), making him adaptable to any combat situation. Kliff's unique ability, Watch & Learn, allows him to permanently learn enemy skills √¢¬Ä?even surviving respecs √¢¬Ä?giving him access to boss abilities that no other character can wield.

Kliff's combat identity revolves around weapon fluidity. Unlike Oongka who relies on raw stats and super armor, Kliff thrives on perfect timing √¢¬Ä?parrying enemy attacks, chaining weapon swaps mid-combo, and exploiting stagger windows with precision. His skill tree branches into three paths: Stamina (blue) for combat skills and DPS, Health (red) for survival and elemental damage, and Spirit (green) for counters and utility.

Expert Tip from √®¬Ä¬Å√•¬º¬Ý: Kliff is not a one-trick character. The best players maintain three weapon slots at all times √¢¬Ä?a primary DPS weapon, a secondary for utility, and always keep unarmed as your third slot for combo extension. Unarmed strikes extend your combo timer without consuming stamina, and Patch 1.07.00 added Blinding Flash Finisher to the unarmed chain, letting you reset into a full weapon combo.

Playstyle Summary: Aggressive, adaptive, and technical. Kliff rewards players who learn enemy patterns and can execute parries and weapon swaps under pressure. He offers the highest skill ceiling in the game, but even beginners can succeed with the right build √¢¬Ä?starting with the Sword and Shield Tank and graduating to more aggressive setups as you learn boss patterns.

This weapon carries Kliff from Chapter 3 all the way to Chapter 7 when you replace it with Ignir.

Mastering Kliff's combos is the difference between a good player and a great one. Each build has an optimal rotation that maximizes damage during stagger windows while conserving stamina for defensive options.

Lariat Cycle (Universal Core Combo)

This is Kliff's fundamental combo, effective on every build and every enemy type:

Lariat Cycle

Step 1: Lariat (grapple) √¢¬Ä?high stagger accumulation, 1-2 uses break most enemies

Step 2: Enemy enters stagger state

Step 3: Turning Slash Lv.2 √¢¬Ä?with Momentum Abyss Gear, this deals 25-35% of a boss's HP per cycle

Step 4: Blinding Flash Finisher √¢¬Ä?unarmed finisher resets combo timer

Step 5: Repeat from Step 1

Elemental Blade Combo Chain

Open: Activate elemental infusion (Fist of Flame / Mantle of Frost / Surge of Sparks)

4. Unarmed strikes √¢¬Ü?Blinding Flash Finisher √¢¬Ü?Reset

Each hit procs elemental damage. The damage compounds with attack speed √¢¬Ä?faster hits = more elemental triggers = exponentially higher DPS.

Crit Boss Killer Rotation

Two-Button Boss Delete

1. Turning Slash (R1+R2) √¢¬Ä?high stagger + physical damage

2. Fist of Flame infusion (L2+R2) √¢¬Ä?fire element + additional stagger

3. Repeat. That's it.

This two-button rotation is widely considered overpowered by the community. The stagger loop prevents bosses from retaliating. Crits from Vow of the Dead King + 2x Crit signets double your damage on every other hit. Combine with 5 projectile cores for screen-wide destruction.

Advanced Tech √¢¬Ä?Falling Palm: All three skill branches converge on Falling Palm √¢¬Ä?a finisher that consumes all remaining stamina for massive damage. Once you have completed any full branch, unlock Falling Palm. Use it as a combo ender when you know the boss is about to phase-change or recover from stagger. The damage scales with stamina consumed, so save it for when your stamina bar is full.

Unarmed Extender Tech

Every Kliff player should master unarmed extension. Switch to your unarmed slot mid-combo and land 2-3 light punches. This refreshes your combo timer, costs zero stamina, and keeps you in close range. Finish with Blinding Flash Finisher (Patch 1.07.00) to stun the enemy and restart your weapon combo. This technique alone increases your effective combo length by 50-80%.

√¢¬ù?FAQ

Q: What is the best overall Kliff build for a first playthrough?

The Sword & Shield Tank is the safest choice for a first playthrough. It prioritizes survival (Iron Will, Perfect Dodge Mastery) and lets you learn boss patterns without dying constantly. Once you reach Chapter 7 and obtain the Ignir Sword, respec to the Elemental Blade build for higher DPS. The respec cost at that point is only 5,000 silver √¢¬Ä?trivial by mid-game standards.

Q: Which weapon should I rush in the early game?

Rush the Soul Spear from Antumbra Ritual Grounds (west of Hernand) as soon as you reach Chapter 3. At ATK 27, it is the strongest weapon available at that point and will carry you through Chapters 3-7 until you replace it with the Ignir Sword. The Soul Spear's spectral projectile also provides excellent clearing for mob-heavy encounters.

Q: How many Abyss Core slots do I need to be effective?

For mid-game, 3 slots on your weapon is the minimum. For endgame (Abyss Sanctum, world bosses), you need 5 slots. A 5-slot weapon with Destruction III + Velocity III cores will deal approximately 60% more DPS than a 3-slot weapon with max cores. The Ignir Sword (5 slots) and Vow of the Dead King (5 slots) are the best endgame options. Never invest heavily in a weapon with fewer than 3 slots.

Q: Is the Watch & Learn ability worth using?

Absolutely. Watch & Learn is Kliff's unique passive that permanently learns enemy skills √¢¬Ä?even surviving respecs. Prioritize learning boss counter skills (for free parries), ranged projectile attacks (for extra options), and ground slam attacks (for AoE control). Skip normal enemy melee skills √¢¬Ä?they are weaker than Kliff's base attacks. Grind for 30-60 minutes at each major boss arena to learn their full movesets.

Q: What's the best Abyss Core farm location?

Root's End Ruins in Hernand is the most efficient farm. Each full clear takes approximately 60 seconds and drops roughly 1 core every 1-2 runs. Focus on Destruction III and Velocity III as your first targets √¢¬Ä?they benefit every Kliff build. Upgrade at the Witch NPC: 2√É¬ó Lv1 √¢¬Ü?Lv2, 2√É¬ó Lv2 √¢¬Ü?Lv3. You need 16 Lv1 cores to craft a single Lv5 core, so expect to spend 2-3 hours farming for a full endgame setup.

Q: Should I use the new Patch 1.08.00 firearms for Kliff?

Firearms (Flintlock and Shotgun) are a strong secondary option but do not replace your primary melee weapon. Slot Infinite Arrows II/III into the firearm for unlimited ammunition, then use it as a ranged opener before closing to melee range. The shotgun is particularly effective against mob packs. However, Kliff's melee DPS with a properly built Ignir Sword or Vow of the Dead King still significantly outdamages firearms against single targets.

Q: How important is stamina management on Kliff?

Stamina management is the single most important skill for Kliff. Every attack, dodge, block, and parry consumes stamina. Running out of stamina in a boss fight means death. Follow these rules: (1) Never drop below 20% stamina √¢¬Ä?always leave enough for one dodge and one parry. (2) Use unarmed strikes to extend combos (costs zero stamina). (3) Use the Perfect Dodge Mastery ultimate (Stamina branch row 5) √¢¬Ä?perfect dodges refill 30% stamina. This is mandatory for aggressive playstyles.

Q: Can I switch builds in the middle of the game?

Yes. Respec is available at any Memory Keeper NPC (blue brain icon on the map). First respec is free, second costs 5,000 silver, third 10,000, doubling up to 80,000 cap. You can respec attribute points and skill points independently. Recommended respec points: after Chapter 7 (when you get Ignir Sword, switch from Tank to Elemental Blade), and at level 50+ (when you have enough points for a full endgame build). Save your game manually before any respec to test for free.
