💰 1. Material Types Overview — What You Need to Know

Crimson Desert's crafting and upgrade systems require a vast array of materials, from common ores like iron and copper to rare drops like Abyss Shards and Highland Drake Scales. Understanding the material hierarchy and knowing where to find each type is the foundation of efficient farming. This guide covers every major material category, the best farming routes for each, and tips for maximizing your gathering efficiency.

Materials in Crimson Desert are divided into five tiers: Common (gray), Uncommon (green), Rare (blue), Epic (purple), and Legendary (gold). Common materials are found everywhere and form the backbone of basic crafting. Uncommon materials are region-specific and required for mid-tier equipment. Rare materials drop from elite enemies and are needed for high-tier gear. Epic materials come from boss encounters and world events. Legendary materials are endgame drops required for the best equipment in the game.

① Common Materials and Where to Find Them

Common materials include iron ore, copper ore, wood (various types), leather, cloth, and basic food ingredients. Iron ore is found in rocky areas throughout Pywel — look for dark-colored rock formations with visible metallic veins. Copper ore is found near water sources and has a distinctive greenish tint. Wood is harvested from trees using a hatchet — hardwood trees yield stronger wood for crafting. Leather is obtained by hunting animals and skinning their hides. Cloth is crafted from plant fibers gathered from bushes.

⛏️ 2. Best Mining Routes — Ore Farming Guide

Mining is the primary method for obtaining metal ores, gems, and crystals. Ore deposits are scattered throughout the world, but some routes are far more efficient than others. The key to efficient mining is finding routes with high deposit density and minimal enemy interference.

② Uzkigrad Mining Loop (Beginner-Friendly)

This route is ideal for new players and covers a loop just outside Uzkigrad. Start at the north gate and follow the cliff face east. You will find 8 iron deposits and 4 copper deposits along this route. The loop takes about 10 minutes and yields approximately 40 iron ore and 20 copper ore per run. Enemy encounters are minimal — occasionally a single wolf or bandit. This route can be repeated indefinitely and is the best way to stockpile common metals for early-game crafting.

③ Highland Ridge Route (Mid-Game)

The Highland Ridge area is rich in mid-tier ores including silver, gold, and the rare fire crystal. Start at the Highland Outpost and follow the ridge north. The route passes through a series of mountain ledges with 12 ore deposits (6 silver, 4 gold, 2 fire crystal). The loop takes 15 minutes and requires a mount for efficient travel between deposits. Watch out for drake enemies in this area — they are aggressive and can knock you off ledges. Bring a weapon and be prepared to fight.

④ Abyssal Depths Route (Endgame)

The Abyssal Depths is an endgame mining zone accessible after completing the main story chapter "The Descent." This area contains the rarest ores in the game: Abyss Iron, Void Crystal, and Soul Gem fragments. The route is dangerous — it is populated by level 50+ enemies and requires a full combat loadout. However, a single run yields materials worth 5,000-10,000 coins at market. The route takes 25-30 minutes and should only be attempted with upgraded armor and weapons.

🌿 3. Foraging Routes — Plants, Herbs & Rare Flora

Foraging is the gathering of plant-based materials: herbs, berries, mushrooms, and rare flora used in alchemy and cooking. Unlike mining, foraging does not require any tools — you simply walk up to a plant and press the interact button. However, knowing where to find specific plants is crucial for efficient alchemy and cooking.

⑤ Pywel Forest Foraging Loop

The Pywel Forest, located south of Pywel City, is the best foraging area in the game. A circular route through the forest yields 15-20 plant nodes per loop, including healing herbs, mana berries, and poison mushrooms. The loop takes about 8 minutes and can be repeated indefinitely. The forest is safe — no aggressive enemies spawn here — making it perfect for relaxed gathering sessions. Key plants to look for: Sunpetal (healing), Moonberry (mana restoration), and Shadowcap mushrooms (poison resistance).

⑥ Desert Oasis Rare Flora

The desert regions of Pywel contain oasis areas with unique flora not found anywhere else. These plants are used in high-tier alchemy recipes and fetch premium prices at market. The main oasis is located at coordinates (X:200, Y:450) and contains 5-8 rare plant nodes. Look for Desert Lotus (fire resistance), Sandroot (stamina regeneration), and Mirage Bloom (rare, used in legendary potions). The oasis is patrolled by desert bandits, so stay alert.

💎 4. Rare Material Farming — Boss Drops & World Events

Rare and Epic materials cannot be farmed from static nodes — they drop from boss enemies and world events. This section covers the most important rare materials, which bosses drop them, and strategies for farming them efficiently.

⑦ Abyss Shards — The Most Important Endgame Material

Abyss Shards are required for crafting and upgrading Abyss-tier equipment, which is the strongest gear tier in the game. They drop exclusively from Abyssal bosses — powerful world bosses that spawn in the Abyssal Depths zone. There are three Abyssal bosses: the Void Colossus (drops 2-4 shards), the Soul Harvester (drops 3-5 shards), and the Abyss Warden (drops 5-8 shards). Each boss has a 4-hour respawn timer after being defeated. A full set of Abyss equipment requires approximately 50 shards, meaning you will need to farm these bosses 10-15 times.

⑧ Highland Drake Scales

Drake Scales are used in crafting fire-resistant armor and are a key ingredient in the wyvern mount's premium food (Highland Drake Meat). Drakes are found throughout the Highland region and respawn every 30 minutes. Each drake has a 20% chance to drop a scale, meaning you can expect 4-5 scales per hour of farming. The best drake farming spot is the Drake Nest at coordinates (X:380, Y:200), where 3 drakes spawn in close proximity.

📦 5. Trade Goods & Market Strategy

Not all materials are meant for crafting — some are trade goods that can be sold for profit. Understanding the market system and knowing which materials to sell versus keep is essential for maintaining a healthy economy in Crimson Desert.

⑨ What to Sell vs. What to Keep

As a general rule, keep all Rare (blue) and above materials — you will need them for crafting. Sell excess Common and Uncommon materials. Iron ore, copper ore, and basic wood are so abundant that you will always have more than you need — sell the excess for steady income. Silver and gold ore should be kept for crafting unless you have a large surplus. Gems and crystals should always be kept — they are used in high-tier crafting and are worth more as materials than as sold goods.

⑩ Market Price Fluctuations

Market prices for materials fluctuate based on supply and demand. When many players are online and farming, prices drop. During off-peak hours, prices rise. The best time to sell is during weekday mornings when fewer players are online. The best time to buy is during weekends when prices are lowest due to high supply. Pay attention to world events — when a new boss is introduced, the materials it drops will be expensive for the first week and then drop in price as supply increases.

📋 6. Farming Efficiency Tips & Storage Management

Efficient farming is about more than just knowing where to go — it is about optimizing your route, your inventory, and your time. This section shares advanced tips for maximizing your gathering output and managing your material storage effectively.

⑪ Mount Choice for Farming

Use a camel for farming expeditions — its 80-slot carrying capacity means fewer trips back to town to unload. If you do not have a camel, use a horse with saddlebags (adds 30 slots). The flying dragon is not ideal for farming because its 40-slot capacity is relatively low, though its ability to bypass terrain can save time on routes that cross difficult terrain.

⑫ Storage Organization

Your camp storage is the best place to store materials. Build storage chests (each holds 100 items) and organize them by material type: one chest for metals, one for plants, one for monster drops, and one for trade goods. This organization saves significant time when crafting — you will always know exactly where to find the material you need. Upgrade your storage chests to reinforced variants (holds 200 items) as soon as you can afford them. The reinforced variant requires 30 iron planks and 10 locks per chest.

❓ FAQ — Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best material to farm for money?

Gold ore and gems are the most profitable common materials to farm. A single gold ore sells for 50 coins, and a full mining loop in the Highland Ridge can yield 15-20 gold ore per run. For rare materials, Abyss Shards sell for 500-1000 coins each on the market.

Do gathering nodes respawn?

Yes, all gathering nodes respawn. Mining deposits respawn in 30 minutes, plant nodes respawn in 15 minutes, and rare deposits (fire crystals, Abyss Iron) respawn in 2 hours. The respawn timer is real-time, not in-game time.

Is there a gathering skill or tool upgrade?

Yes, you can upgrade your gathering tools at a blacksmith. A steel pickaxe mines 50% faster than the basic iron pickaxe, and a mithril pickaxe adds a 10% chance to get double ore per deposit. Tool upgrades are worth the investment if you farm regularly.

Can I hire NPCs to gather materials for me?

Yes, after building a Trade Post at your camp, you can hire NPC gatherers who will automatically collect common materials (iron, wood, basic herbs) over time. They generate 10-20 materials per in-game day.

What is the maximum stack size for materials?

Most materials stack to 99 in your inventory and 999 in camp storage. Rare materials (Epic and Legendary) stack to 10 in inventory and 100 in storage. Plan your farming trips accordingly.
