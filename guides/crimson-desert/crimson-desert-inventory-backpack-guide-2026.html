🎒 1. Inventory System Overview — How Slots Work

Inventory management is one of the most frequently asked-about topics in the Crimson Desert community. The AskPywel FAQ page receives repeated questions about backpack space, whether to keep or sell cooking recipes, and how to expand storage capacity. This is because Crimson Desert's inventory system is deliberately restrictive — you start with only 30 slots, and the game floods you with crafting materials, cooking ingredients, quest items, and equipment that all compete for the same space.

The inventory is divided into four categories, but they all draw from the same slot pool:

CategoryWhat Goes HereStacks?Notes
EquipmentWeapons, armor, accessoriesNo — each piece takes 1 slotBiggest space drain
ConsumablesPotions, food, buff itemsYes — up to 99 per slotEfficient if you stack
MaterialsCrafting mats, monster drops, oreYes — up to 99 per slotMost cluttered category
Key ItemsQuest items, keys, mapsNo — but do not count toward limitSafe — never discard
Warning: Key items (quest items, treasure maps, keys) do NOT count toward your 30-slot limit. However, some items that look like key items — such as cooking recipes — DO count toward your limit. Many players hoard recipes not realizing each one takes a precious slot. Once you have learned a recipe by using it, you can safely sell the physical recipe item.

📈 2. How to Expand Backpack Slots

There are exactly three ways to increase your backpack capacity in Crimson Desert. None of them are explained well by the game, which is why the FAQ receives so many questions about this topic.

1

Method 1: Complete Faction Quests (+5 slots each)

Each major faction in Crimson Desert offers a questline that rewards a "Backpack Expansion" item upon completion. There are three factions, each giving +5 slots:

  • Greymane Faction (Chapter 2-3): Complete "The Supply Run" quest → +5 slots
  • Scholar Stone Institute (Chapter 4): Complete "Knowledge is Weight" quest → +5 slots
  • Merchant's Consortium (Chapter 5): Complete "The Pack Mule" quest → +5 slots

Total from faction quests: +15 slots (bringing your total from 30 to 45).

2

Method 2: Purchase from Vendors (+3 slots per purchase)

Certain vendors sell "Satchel Upgrades" that permanently add +3 slots each. These are expensive but available early:

  • Uzkigrad General Store: 3,000 silver → +3 slots (available Chapter 2)
  • Merchant's Rest General Store: 6,000 silver → +3 slots (available Chapter 3)
  • Spire of Frost Outpost: 12,000 silver → +3 slots (available Chapter 5)

Total from vendors: +9 slots (bringing your total from 45 to 54).

3

Method 3: Camp Upgrade — Storage Tent

Build a Storage Tent at your camp (requires 20 Wood, 10 Iron, 5 Leather). This does not increase your carried slots, but it gives you access to a 100-slot storage chest at your camp, effectively doubling your total storage capacity. See our Camp & Farm Guide for the full building tutorial.

🗄️ 4. Storage Chests — Every Location in Pywel

Storage chests are static containers where you can deposit items for safekeeping. They are shared across all chests — items deposited in one chest can be retrieved from any other chest in the game. This makes them extremely useful for offloading materials you do not need immediately.

🔧 5. Inventory Optimization — Pro Strategies

The "3-Stack Rule"

Never carry more than 3 stacks (3 slots worth) of any single material type. If you have more than 99 of any material, deposit the excess in a storage chest. This simple rule prevents 80% of inventory overflow problems. The only exceptions are Abyss Remnants and Abyss Gears — carry as many of those as you can.

The "Pre-Dungeon Purge"

Before entering any dungeon or boss arena, visit a storage chest and deposit all non-essential items. Carry only: 1 weapon set, 1 backup weapon, 15 health potions, 5 spirit tonics, 5 stamina tonics, and 3 buff foods. This gives you room to pick up dungeon loot without having to discard items mid-run.

The "Recipe Learning Sweep"

Every time you visit a town, open your inventory and check for recipe items. Use any you have not learned yet, then immediately sell the physical recipe. Do this as a habit — it takes 30 seconds and prevents recipe clutter from accumulating. Most players have 10+ unlearned recipes clogging their inventory at any given time.

❓ FAQ — Inventory Questions Answered

Q: Can I sell cooking recipes after I have learned them?

Yes. Once you use a recipe item, the recipe is permanently added to your cooking menu. The physical recipe item serves no further purpose and can be safely sold. This is confirmed in the official community FAQ and is one of the easiest ways to free up inventory space.

Q: What is the maximum backpack size?

54 carried slots (30 base + 15 from faction quests + 9 from vendor purchases). Additionally, you can store 100 items in storage chests, plus 100 more in your camp Storage Tent. Total maximum storage: 54 carried + 200 stored = 254 items.

Q: Are storage chests shared across locations?

Yes. All storage chests in the game share the same inventory. An item deposited in Uzkigrad can be retrieved from the Spire of Frost chest. This means you only need to visit the nearest chest — you do not need to travel back to where you deposited an item.

Q: Should I dismantle or sell old equipment?

Sell it. Dismantling gives you basic materials (iron, leather) that are easy to gather anyway. Selling gives silver that can be used for satchel upgrades and consumables. The only exception is Abyss-tier equipment — dismantle those for Abyss Gears, which are extremely rare.

Q: How do I stop auto-gathering useless plants?

Open Settings → Gameplay → Auto-Gather, and set it to "Off" or "Rare Only." This prevents Kliff from automatically picking up common herbs and flowers as you walk past them, saving inventory space and reducing interruptions.
