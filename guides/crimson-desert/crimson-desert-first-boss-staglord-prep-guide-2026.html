✅ 1. Pre-Fight Checklist — Do NOT Enter Until These Are Done

Staglord is not a pushover boss — he is designed to teach you that button-mashing will not work anymore in this game. Many players die 5–10 times before adjusting their approach. Use this checklist to ensure your first attempt is also a winning attempt.

1

The "Staglord Ready" Pre-Fight Protocol

  1. Weapon: Iron Longsword +2 or better equipped in primary slot. Secondary = Hunter's Axe.
  2. Armor: Full Iron set (or at minimum Chest + Legs). Shield equipped in off-hand slot.
  3. HP: 300+ (if below 300, go level up by doing side quests first).
  4. Potions: Minimum 8 Small Healing Potions + 1 Antidote in inventory.
  5. Buff Food: 1 Ironbread (DEF+15%) consumed before entering arena. Lasts 15 minutes.
  6. Skills: Parry Extension unlocked. Spirit Surge unlocked. At least 12 Abyss Artifacts spent.
  7. Combat Practice: Completed at least Phase 1 and Phase 2 of the Combat Training Drill (from our Combat Mastery Guide) — you should be landing 50%+ parries against basic bandits.
  8. Mental State: Calm, patient, ready to learn from mistakes rather than rage-quit. This matters more than any gear check.
If you fail items 1–7: Go prepare them first. Staglord waits for you — there is no time limit. Entering under-prepared wastes 10–15 minutes per failed attempt plus the frustration of repeated deaths. Invest 30 minutes in preparation and save yourself hours of retrying.

🦌 2. Phase 1 (100%–70% HP): Learning His Rhythm

Staglord's Phase 1 is intentionally forgiving. He uses only 4 attack patterns in a predictable cycle. Your goal here is NOT to deal maximum damage — it is to learn his timing without taking unnecessary risks. Think of this phase as an extended tutorial within the fight itself.

The Phase 1 Winning Loop

Stand at medium range (about 3 body lengths away from Staglord). Wait.

When you see white/red glint → Tap parry. If successful (clang sound), immediately input L-L-H (Wolf's Bite combo). All 3 hits will land during his 1.5s stagger window. Deal ~140 damage total.

If you see purple circle → Dodge backward immediately. Do NOT try to parry this. After he stomps, run in and do a quick L-L combo (2 hits, ~90 damage).

If stamina drops below 30% → Back off. Stand still for 3 seconds. Let it regenerate. Re-engage when above 60%. Never fight with low stamina against Staglord — his Phase 2 punishes exhausted players brutally.

Repeat this loop. Phase 1 should take approximately 4–6 minutes depending on your DPS. There is no rush.

⚔️ 3. Phase 2 (70%–35% HP): The Real Fight Begins

At exactly 70% HP, Staglord enters Phase 2. Visual cue: his eyes glow red, his antlers crackle with electricity, and he lets out a loud roar that stuns you briefly (unavoidable, deals 0 damage but breaks any action). Here is what changes:

  • All attacks become ~20% faster (shorter windup animations)
  • Charge Rush now chains into a second charge if the first misses
  • New attack: "Antler Spin" — rapid 360° AOE spin that hits 3 times in quick succession
  • He gains hyper-armor during Antler Sweep (your light attacks bounce off; use heavy attacks or parry instead)

② Dealing With the New Antler Spin Attack

This is the attack that kills most players in Phase 2. It has almost no windup (the indicator appears only ~0.15s before impact), it hits 3 times rapidly, and each hit deals ~35 HP (105 total if all 3 connect). Here is how to survive it:

  1. Watch for Staglord to lower his head and spin his entire body — this is the only telegraph before Antler Spin
  2. As soon as you see the spin start, press DODGE backward TWICE in quick succession (×/A/Space × 2)
  3. The first dodge avoids hit 1 and 2; the second dodge creates i-frame coverage for hit 3
  4. After his spin ends, he has a 2-second recovery where he stands panting — free combo window!
Phase 2 Pro Move: When you see him start charging (red glint), do NOT try to parry it. Instead, wait until he commits to the charge direction, then sidestep LEFT or RIGHT (not backward). As he passes beside you, unleash a charged heavy attack (hold R2/RT/RClick) into his flank. Charged heavies break his armor by 10% per hit. Three well-timed flanking charged attacks will break all his armor pieces, which triggers a long stagger where you can deal massive damage.

🔥 4. Phase 3 (35%–0% HP): Enrage Mode

At 35% HP, Staglord enters enrage. His eyes glow bright red continuously. ALL his attacks become unblockable (red glint on everything). Ground Stomp now has a wider AOE radius. He moves faster. And he gains a new desperation move:

③ The "Desperation Charge" — Survive This and You Win

When Staglord drops below 15% HP, he has a chance (approximately 40% per cycle) to use Desperation Charge: a continuous running attack that chases you across the arena for up to 6 seconds. If he catches you, he deals ~25 HP per tick (total 150+ HP if you get caught for the full duration). This is the attack that turns winning attempts into deaths at the last second.

How to survive Desperation Charge: The moment you see Staglord enter the low-HP red-eyed state, keep your stamina above 50% at all times. When he starts the charge animation (he roars and lowers his entire body toward you), SPRINT diagonally AWAY from him while making sharp direction changes every 1.5 seconds. The charge tracks your position with slight lag — sharp turns break his tracking. Keep sprinting for 6 seconds. He stops. He is exhausted for 3 seconds. This is your kill window. Unload EVERYTHING: Spirit Surge → Wolf's Howl combo → weapon swap → charged heavy. If you execute this perfectly, Desperation Charge becomes his death sentence, not yours.

❓ FAQ — Staglord Questions Answered

Q: What level should I be before fighting Staglord?

The game suggests level 6–8. In practice, players have beaten Staglord as low as level 4 (with perfect parry skills and good gear) and some level 10 players still struggle because they never learned proper combat mechanics. Level matters less than skill here. If your parry accuracy is above 50%, you can probably beat Staglord at level 5–6. If it is below 30%, go practice more regardless of your level.

Q: How long should my first attempt take?

A clean first-time kill takes approximately 12–18 minutes across all three phases. Most first attempts take 20–30 minutes because players spend Phase 1 learning patterns cautiously (which is smart!). If your attempt exceeds 35 minutes, you are probably being too defensive and not dealing enough damage during stagger windows. Take slightly more risk on your combos after successful parries.

Q: Is Damiane useful in this fight?

Honestly, not particularly for Staglord. Staglord's main threats are melee AOE and charges — things that a shield companion does not help much with. Damiane shines more against ranged/magic bosses like Umbra where her shield can absorb projectiles. For Staglord, you are better served focusing purely on your own combat skills rather than relying on companion AI.

Q: I keep dying in Phase 2. Any specific advice?

Yes — the most common Phase 2 death cause is the Antler Spin triple-hit catching players mid-combo. Solution: after EVERY combo you land (even a 2-hit quick one), immediately release all inputs and watch Staglord for 0.5 seconds before starting your next action. That 0.5-second observation window prevents you from being animation-locked when he spins. Also: never let your stamina drop below 25% in Phase 2. If it does, create distance and stand still. He cannot reach you across the full arena width.

Q: What rewards do I get for defeating Staglord?

Staglord drops: (1) Staglord's Horn Fragment (quest item, used for unique crafting recipe), (2) 500 silver, (3) Abyss Artifacts ×15 (huge skill point boost!), (4) Random rare equipment piece (often Iron-tier or Steel-tier), and (5) Unlocks the "Boss Slayer" achievement/trophy. The Abyss Artifacts reward alone makes this fight worth attempting multiple times until you win.
