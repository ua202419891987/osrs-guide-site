There are two ways to level in OSRS: the slow way and the fast way. The slow way means grinding tree after tree, mining rock after rock, and clicking the same monster for hours. The fast way means using the highest XP/hour method available at your level, skipping low-level content entirely through quest XP, and spending your XP lamps on the slowest skills. This guide is about the fast way. Every skill has a "best method" — this guide tells you exactly which one to use and when to switch. For the broader picture on skill training, check our complete OSRS training guide.

① 🧠 Fastest Leveling Principles

Before we dive into individual skills, understand these core principles that apply to every skill in the game:

1.1 ⏱️ XP/Hour Is Everything

The metric that matters is XP per hour, not XP per click or XP per item. A method that gives 5 XP per click but you can click 100 times a minute (50K XP/hr) beats a method that gives 50 XP per click but only allows 10 clicks a minute (30K XP/hr). Always compare methods by XP/hour, not by per-action XP. This guide lists XP/hour for every method so you can make direct comparisons.

1.2 💰 GP/XP Tradeoff

The fastest method is almost never the cheapest. In many skills, you can double your XP/hour by spending 5–10× more GP per XP. The question is: how much is your time worth? If you can make 2M GP/hour at your current money-maker, spending 500K GP to save 1 hour of training is a net gain. This guide includes cost estimates for every method so you can decide.

1.3 🔄 AFK vs. Click-Intensive

Some methods are nearly AFK (click once every 5–10 minutes) while others require constant clicking. AFK methods typically give 30–60% of the XP/hour of click-intensive methods. Choose AFK methods for skills you hate or for multitasking (work, studying), and click-intensive methods for skills you enjoy or when you're focused purely on efficiency. This guide labels every method as AFK, Semi-AFK, or Click-Intensive.

1.4 📐 The Quest Skip Strategy

Quests that award XP can skip dozens of hours of low-level training. The Waterfall Quest alone gives 13,750 Attack and Strength XP — that's level 1 to 30 in both skills in 10 minutes instead of 5+ hours of combat. Always do XP-awarding quests before training a skill manually. See Section ② for the full quest skip list.

💡 The Ironman Exception

Ironman accounts can't buy materials on the GE, so the "fastest" method (buying materials) isn't available. Ironmen should use our Ironman leveling guide instead, which accounts for self-sourcing requirements.

2.3 🧮 Calculating Quest XP to Levels

To figure out what level a quest's XP award gets you, use the formula: Total XP = sum of all XP from level 1 to target level. You can look this up on any OSRS XP table. As a quick reference: going from level 1 to level 30 in any skill requires ~13,360 XP. Going from 1 to 40 requires ~37,220 XP. Going from 1 to 50 requires ~86,830 XP. So a quest that awards 13,750 XP takes you from 1 to ~30 in that skill.

③ 💡 XP Lamps & Optimization

OSRS gives you XP lamps and books from quests, random events, achievement diaries, and minigames. Where you spend these lamps matters enormously — a lamp on a fast skill wastes it, while a lamp on a slow skill saves hours.

3.1 🏆 Lamp Priority Order (Use Lamps Here First)

Always spend lamps on the slowest, most expensive, or most tedious skills first. Here's the priority order:

At 70+ combat, the Nightmare Zone (NMZ) absorption method gives the highest AFK combat XP in the game. Set up a "hard" dream with bosses that use melee only, drink absorption potions, and let your character auto-fight for up to 6 hours without clicking. Slayer is unique — it's both a combat skill and a gateway to the best money-makers in OSRS. The fastest Slayer XP comes from barraging/bursting tasks, but that requires high Magic levels and significant GP.

5.2 🔒 Task Blocking & Skipping

To maximize XP/hour, you should block tasks that are slow, low-XP, or annoying, and skip tasks that don't support bursting. Use your block list on: Metal Dragons, Spiritual Creatures, Black Demons (if not bursting), and Waterfiends. Always skip tasks that require excessive travel time or single-target combat when you're aiming for maximum XP.

⑩ ❓ FAQs — OSRS Fastest Leveling

Q: What's the fastest 99 to get in OSRS?

A: Fletching — with dragon dart tipping at 150K–200K XP/hour, you can hit 99 in under 15 hours for ~80M GP. Construction is also fast at ~15–20 hours but costs ~200M. Prayer at a Gilded Altar can hit 99 in ~10–15 hours but costs ~200M+ in bones. If you have the GP, these three are the quickest 99s in the game.

Q: What's the slowest skill to 99 in OSRS?

A: Runecraft — even at the fastest method (lava runes at 25K–40K XP/hour), 99 Runecraft takes 150–200+ hours. Farming is also slow in total elapsed time (3–4 months) but only requires ~10 minutes of active play per run. Agility takes 80–100+ hours of active clicking. These three are the skills you should prioritize lamps on.

Q: Should I train combat with Slayer or without?

A: It depends on your goals. Pure combat training (Sand Crabs → NMZ) gives 2–3× faster combat XP per hour but leaves you with low Slayer. Training combat through Slayer is slower per hour but builds Slayer XP simultaneously, which unlocks high-level money-makers (Gargoyles, Zulrah, Cerberus). The meta approach: train combat to 70 via Sand Crabs/NMZ for speed, then switch to Slayer for 70–99 to build your money-maker foundation.

Q: Can I train skills AFK while doing other things?

A: Yes — several skills have strong AFK methods: NMZ combat (6-hour AFK), Sand/Ammonite Crabs, Motherlode Mine, Fishing (monkfish/sharks), Woodcutting (yews/magics/redwoods), and Wintertodt. These give 30–60% of maximum XP/hour but require minimal clicking. Perfect for studying, working, or watching content. See our AFK training guide for details.

Q: How much GP does it cost to max all skills?

A: Roughly 500M–800M GP total if you use the fastest methods for every skill. The biggest expenses are Construction (~200M), Prayer (~200M), Herblore (~60M–80M), and Ranged/Magic (if barraging/chinning, ~100M–150M each). If you use cheaper/slower methods, the total drops to ~200M–300M but takes 2–3× longer. For a budget approach, see our affordable leveling guide.

Q: Are 3-tick and 2-tick methods worth learning?

A: Only if you're pushing for maximum efficiency and enjoy precise clicking. 3-tick Mining (granite) and 2-tick Woodcutting (teaks) give 50–100% more XP/hour than normal methods, but they require exact 0.6-second timing and constant attention. For most players, Volcanic Mine and Sulliusceps are better choices — they give 70–80% of the tick-manipulation XP rate without requiring perfect timing. If you're going for a speed record or hate a particular skill, tick methods are worth it. Otherwise, stick to standard methods.

Q: What order should I max skills in?

A: The optimal maxing order prioritizes skills that unlock content for other skills: Agility first (shortcuts help everywhere), Quests next (unlock areas and methods), Slayer third (unlocks money-makers), then combat skills, then expensive fast skills (Construction, Herblore, Prayer) when you have GP. Slow skills (Runecraft, Farming) should be done through lamps and passive methods throughout. For the full order, read our maxing order guide.

🎯 Final Thoughts

The fastest leveling path in OSRS is simple: quest skips first → fastest method per level → lamps on slow skills → AFK methods for multitasking. Don't waste hours on outdated methods when the game gives you tools to skip them. Do Waterfall Quest before touching a monster. Buy materials instead of gathering them if you can afford it. Save your lamps for Runecraft and Construction. Use AFK methods when you're busy and click-intensive methods when you're focused. Follow this guide and you'll save hundreds of hours compared to the average player. For more leveling resources, check our leveling milestones guide and our F2P leveling guide.
