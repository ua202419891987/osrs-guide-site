Sailing Skill Deep Dive

Sailing is the first new skill added to OSRS since its 2013 release. Developed through extensive community polls and beta testing, Sailing introduces a whole new dimension to Gielinor: the seas.

How Sailing Works

Sailing is trained by building and upgrading ships, navigating waters, exploring islands, engaging in naval combat, and establishing trade routes. It's a gathering, PvM, and profit skill all rolled into one.

💡 Money-Making Tip: High-level Sailing trade routes can be extremely profitable. At level 80+, you can earn 1-3M GP/hour with minimal effort, making Sailing one of the best money-making skills in the game.

Vault of Ralos Raid — What We Know

Vault of Ralos is the first new raid in OSRS since Tombs of Amascut (2022). Set in an ancient underground archive, this 1-8 player raid offers challenging mechanics, epic boss fights, and lucrative rewards.

Raid Overview

  • Location: Southern Islands (requires Sailing to access, or teleport after completion)
  • Team Size: 1-8 players (solo-able in Entry Mode with high stats)

  • Modes: Entry Mode (easier, lower drops) and Normal Mode (harder, better drops)

Blood Moon Rises Quest Finale

The Blood Moon Rises is the thrilling conclusion to the Myreque questline. After years of battling Vyrewatches and uncovering the secrets of the Myreque, you'll face the ultimate threat: the Blood Moon avatar itself.

Quest Requirements

Requirement Type Details
Quests Sins of the Father, A Taste of Hope, Darkness of Hallowvale, In Search of the Myreque, The Lost Tribe, Nature Spirit
Skills 70 Attack, 70 Strength, 70 Defence, 70 Hitpoints, 65 Prayer, 60 Magic, 60 Ranged, 50 Agility
Items Ivandis Flail (or upgraded version), Blisterwood weapon, 2M+ GP recommended
Recommended 75 Combat, 70 Prayer, high-healing food (sharks+), prayer potions

Quest Walkthrough ( Spoiler-Light)

  1. Speak to Veliaf Hurtz in the Myreque Hideout (Canifis) to begin the quest.
  2. Investigate the Blood Moon Temple — travel to the new area (unlocked during quest).
  3. Solve the Blood Moon Puzzle — align the lunar altars to weaken the Blood Moon avatar.
  4. Defeat the Blood Moon Avatar (Phase 1) — use Blisterwood weapons for extra damage.
  5. Navigate the Collapsing Temple — agility and timing puzzle to escape.
  6. Final Showdown (Phase 2) — party with Drezel and Veliaf to defeat the Avatar permanently.
  7. Claim Your Rewards — XP lamps, access to Blood Moon Island, new gear.

Quest Rewards

  • XP Rewards: 50K XP in Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Prayer (choice)
  • New Area: Blood Moon Island (new Slayer tasks, resources)
  • New Gear: Blood Moon Amulet (+10% damage vs. Vampyres)
  • Quest Points: 2 Quest Points
  • Unlocks: New prayers, new teleport, Myreque reputation rewards

League 6 — What to Expect

Leagues are limited-time, accelerated game modes that offer unique relics, region locks, and exclusive rewards. League 6 is expected to launch in Q3 2026.

Predicted League 6 Theme

While nothing is confirmed, community speculation suggests League 6 may feature an Islands or Oceanic theme, tying into the Sailing skill release. Imagine a League where you can only Island-hop, with Sailing-based relics!

League Mechanics (Based on Previous Leagues)

  • Region Lock: Start in a limited region, unlock more as you progress
  • Relics: Choose from 15+ relics that dramatically alter gameplay (e.g., "Rapid Leveling", "God Mode", "Double Drops")
  • League Points: Earn points by completing tasks, spend in the League Store
  • Leaderboards: Compete with other players for total XP/points

How to Prepare for League 6

  1. Research previous Leagues (Trailblazer, Twisted, Shattered Relics) to understand mechanics
  2. Plan your relic selection ahead of time (community will publish ideal paths)
  3. Consider what account type to play (Main, Ironman, Ultimate Ironman)
  4. Clear your schedule — Leagues are time-limited and addictive!
⚠️ Account Separation: League accounts are separate from your main account. You cannot transfer items or XP between them. League rewards (cosmetics, teleports) are unlocked for your main account based on League points earned.

Should You Risk the Wilderness in 2026?

With the updates, the Wilderness may become more appealing for PvM. However, always be cautious — PKers will still be around. Consider:

  • Using a cheap gear setup for Wilderness PvM
  • Team up with friends for protection
  • Avoiding peak hours if you're worried about PKers
  • Wearing protect item prayer to keep your most valuable item on death

Don't get caught off guard! Here's a comprehensive checklist of what to prepare before the major 2026 updates drop.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Sailing really coming in 2026?

A: Yes! Sailing was polled and passed by the community in 2024. It's been in development since, with a targeted 2026 release (Q1 for Phase 1, Q3 for Phase 2).

Q: Will Sailing be members-only?

A: Yes, Sailing is a members-only skill. F2P players will not have access to Sailing content. Consider buying a bond to experience the new skill!

Q: What's the best way to make money with Sailing?

A: High-level trade routes (80+ Sailing) are extremely profitable, earning 1-3M GP/hour. Additionally, island exploration can yield rare resources that sell for high prices on the GE.

Q: Can I solo the Vault of Ralos raid?

A: Entry Mode is solo-able with high stats (90+ combat, BIS gear). Normal Mode is designed for teams of 3-5 players. Soloing Normal Mode is possible but extremely difficult.

Q: When exactly does League 6 start?

A: No official date yet, but based on previous League patterns, expect League 6 to launch in September 2026 and run through December 2026/January 2027.

Q: Are the Wilderness updates safe for PvM?

A: The Wilderness will always have some risk, but the 2026 updates aim to make PvM in the Wilderness more appealing and slightly safer. Always be cautious and consider bringing a cheap gear setup.

Q: Will there be a new skill after Sailing?

A: Nothing confirmed, but the OSRS community regularly discusses potential new skills (e.g., Thieving rework, Summoning, Warding). Don't expect a new skill until at least 2028.
