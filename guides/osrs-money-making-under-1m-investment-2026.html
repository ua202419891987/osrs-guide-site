1. Flipping Myth: "You Need Millions to Start"

If you've ever watched a YouTube video about GE flipping, you've probably seen someone flipping 10M+ GP worth of items and making 500K-1M GP per hour. It's easy to think: "I need millions to start flipping. I only have 100K GP. This isn't for me."

That's completely wrong. You can start flipping with 100K GP and make consistent profits. In fact, small-capital flipping has several advantages over large-capital flipping, especially for beginners.

Small Capital vs Large Capital Flipping

FactorSmall Capital (100K-1M)Large Capital (10M+)
Risk per flipLow (can't buy too many items)High (can lose millions on one bad flip)
Learning curveGentle (small mistakes = small losses)Steep (big mistakes = big losses)
Time investmentLow (10-30 mins/day enough)High (need to monitor large portfolios)
GP/hour potentialModerate (50K-200K/hour)High (500K-2M/hour)
CompetitionLow (whales ignore small items)High (everyone wants Dragon claws)

Why Small-Capital Flipping Is Perfect for Beginners

  • You can't overextend: With 100K GP, you can only buy ~10-20 items at a time. This forces you to be selective.
  • Low stress: If a flip goes bad and you lose 5K GP, it's not a big deal. You'll still have 95K GP to try again.
  • Fast learning: Small flips complete quickly (often within hours), so you get rapid feedback.
  • Less competition: Most experienced flippers ignore low-value items (under 5K GP each).
  • Compound growth: 100K → 200K → 400K → 800K → 1M. Each doubling takes less time than the last.

2. Getting Started — The 100K Setup

If you don't have 100K GP yet, don't worry — it's easy to get. This section covers how to get your first 100K GP and the absolute basics of how flipping works.

How to Get Your First 100K GP

Method 1: Cowhide Collection (F2P) — ~120K GP/hour

Time needed: ~50 minutes. Kill cows in Al Kharid, collect cowhides, sell at GE for ~550-600 GP each. 6-7 trips = 100K GP.

Method 2: Chaos Druids (Members) — ~200K GP/hour

Time needed: ~30 minutes. Kill Chaos Druids in Taverley Dungeon, collect herbs, sell at GE. Requires 35 Combat.

Flipping Basics: How It Works

Flipping is simple. You:

  1. Buy an item at a low price (the "buy price")
  2. Sell the item at a higher price (the "sell price")
  3. Profit = Sell price - Buy price - GE tax (1%)
Example: A Simple Flip

Let's say you're flipping Iron ore:

  • Buy price: 100 GP each (you buy 100 Iron ore for 10,000 GP total)
  • Instant sell price: 115 GP each (you sell 100 Iron ore for 11,500 GP total)
  • Gross profit: 11,500 - 10,000 = 1,500 GP
  • GE tax: 1% of sell price = 115 GP
  • Net profit: 1,500 - 115 = 1,385 GP (13.85% return)

4. Margin Checking — The Most Important Skill

Margin checking is the skill that separates profitable flippers from losing flippers. If you don't know the margin, you're gambling, not flipping.

What Is Margin Checking?

Margin = Sell price - Buy price - GE tax (1%)

For example: Iron ore buy at 100 GP, sell at 115 GP. Margin = 115 - 100 - 1 = 14 GP per item (14% margin).

How to Check Margins Accurately

Method 1: Manual Margin Checking (Free)

Go to GE, search for your item, look at instant buy and sell prices. Calculate: Margin = Instant sell - Instant buy - (1% of instant sell).

Method 2: Use Margin Checking Tools

  • OSRS Wiki: Free, has historical price data
  • GE Tracker: Real-time margin checking (~$5/month for full features)
  • OSRS Flip: Free flip suggestions for small capital
Avoiding Margin Traps
  • Low-volume items: Huge margin but low volume = you can't sell
  • Manipulated prices: Check price history graph
  • Price crashes: Buy at 100 GP, can't sell above 80 GP tomorrow

5. Risk Management for Small Accounts

When you only have 100K-1M GP, losing 10K-50K GP on a bad flip hurts. Good risk management ensures that one mistake doesn't wipe out your progress.

Rule 1: Never Invest More Than 20% of Your Capital in One Flip

If you have 100K GP, don't put 80K GP into one flip. Invest 20K GP per flip. If it goes bad, you lose 20% (not 80%).

Rule 2: Set a Time Limit (Cut Losses Early)

Set a time limit (e.g., 24 hours). If your sell offer hasn't completed within 24 hours, cancel it and lower your sell price or accept a small loss to get your GP back.

Rule 3: Diversify (Don't Put All GP in One Item)

Even if an item looks like a sure thing, don't bet everything on it. Prices can change for unexpected reasons.

Rule 4: Track Your Flips

Keep a simple spreadsheet tracking: Date, Item, Buy price, Sell price, Profit/loss, Time held. After 10-20 flips, you'll see patterns.

Combining Flipping with Other OSRS Activities

While your GE offers are working, you can do other things in OSRS:

  • Skilling: Set up buy offers, then go power-Mine. Check GE every 30-60 minutes.
  • Slayer: Set up buy offers before starting a task. Check GE after each task.
  • Questing: Perfect time to check GE between quest steps.

The "Overnight Flip" Strategy

  1. Before sleep: Collect completed sells, set up 2-3 buy offers
  2. Sleep (your buy offers might complete in 8 hours)
  3. Wake up: Collect bought items, set up sell offers
  4. Go to work/school (your sell offers might complete)
  5. Return: Collect profit and repeat

Result: You made 50K-200K GP while sleeping and working!

8. Common Beginner Mistakes (And How to Avoid)

Mistake #1: Flipping Too Many Different Items at Once

The fix: Start with ONE item. Master it. Then add a second item. Slowly scale up to 3-5 items at a time (max).

Mistake #2: Not Checking Prices Before Buying/Selling

The fix: Always check current GE prices before setting buy/sell offers. Prices change daily.

Mistake #3: Impatience (Cancelling Offers Too Early)

The fix: Be patient. Most flips take 30 minutes to 6 hours to complete. Give your offers time to work.

Mistake #4: Ignoring the GE Tax

The fix: Always include the GE tax in your margin calculations: Margin = Sell price - Buy price - (Sell price × 0.01)

Mistake #5: Emotional Trading (Chasing Losses)

The fix: Take a break. Walk away from the GE. Come back later with a calm mind.

How much GP can I make per hour flipping?

It depends on your capital:

  • 100K-300K GP: ~20K-50K GP/hour (learning phase)
  • 300K-1M GP: ~50K-150K GP/hour (getting efficient)
  • 1M-5M GP: ~100K-300K GP/hour (experienced)

Is flipping bannable?

No. Flipping is 100% legal and allowed by Jagex. It's just using the Grand Exchange as intended (buy low, sell high).

What's the best item to start flipping with 100K GP?

Iron ore. It has high volume, decent margin (10-15%), and low price (100 GP each). Perfect for learning the mechanics of flipping.

How do I know if an item is worth flipping?

Check three things:

  1. Margin: At least 5-10% after GE tax
  2. Volume: At least 1,000 units traded per day
  3. Price stability: Check the price history graph. Avoid items with wild price swings.

My sell offer isn't completing. What should I do?

First, check if you set the sell price too high. Look at the current instant buy price. If your sell price is above that, lower it. If it still doesn't sell after 24 hours, consider cancelling and either lowering further or holding the item and waiting for the price to rise.
