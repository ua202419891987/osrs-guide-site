🎯 What Are Combat Achievements?

Combat Achievements (often abbreviated as CAs) are OSRS's formal PvM progression system, introduced in 2021 and expanded with every major boss update. Think of them as a slayer master for bossing — a structured checklist of combat-related challenges that reward you with permanent account upgrades as you complete them.

① 📖 How the System Works

CAs are divided into six difficulty tiers: Easy, Medium, Hard, Elite, Master, and Grandmaster. Each tier contains a set of tasks tied to specific bosses, Slayer creatures, and combat scenarios. Every task you complete grants points toward that tier. Hit a certain number of points, and you unlock that tier's rewards permanently on your account.

🏆 Key Facts About the CA System

  • Total tasks: 1,501 across all six tiers (as of mid-2026)
  • Account-wide: Progress is shared across your entire account, not per character
  • Permanent rewards: Once unlocked, you never lose them — even if new tasks are added
  • PvM-focused: Tasks involve killing bosses, speed kills, no-damage kills, and special mechanics
  • Integrates with the Collection Log: Many CA rewards add unique cosmetic items to your log

② 🔓 How to Access the CA Interface

Open your Quest tab (the scroll icon) and click the Combat Achievements button at the bottom. This opens the CA interface showing all six tiers, your current points, completed tasks, and reward unlocks. You can also filter by boss or task type to find specific challenges.

💡 Pro Tip

Use the Runelite plugin "Combat Achievement Checklist" to track your exact progress, highlight uncompleted tasks, and see the fastest route to your next reward threshold. This plugin is a game-changer for efficient CA grinding.

③ 📜 A Brief History of Combat Achievements

Combat Achievements launched in March 2021 with the initial five tiers (Easy through Master), containing roughly 800 tasks. Grandmaster was added in a major expansion later that year, nearly doubling the task count. Since then, every new boss release has added tasks to the relevant tier. Major additions include tasks for The Nightmare (2021), Tombs of Amascut (2022), the Four Bosses awakened variants (2023), the Colosseum (2024), and the Valamore-themed boss lineup in 2025–2026. The system now encompasses virtually every combat encounter in OSRS, making it a literal encyclopedia of PvM mastery.

④ 🎯 Task Types Explained

CAs are not all the same. Understanding the different task categories helps you plan efficiently:

  • Kill Count tasks — Simply kill a boss X number of times. These are the easiest and most common, especially in lower tiers. Examples: "Kill Obor 5 times," "Kill Vorkath 25 times."
  • Mechanic tasks — Perform a specific action during a fight, like avoiding a special attack or using a specific item. Examples: "Kill Bryophyta without taking damage," "Kill Alchemical Hydra without hitting the fire walls."
  • Speed tasks — Kill a boss within a strict time limit. These require high DPS and perfect execution. Examples: "Kill Kalphite Queen in under 3 minutes," "Complete Corrupted Gauntlet in under 5:30."
  • Variant tasks — Kill a boss using a specific gear setup, combat style, or restriction. Examples: "Kill Zulrah using only Ranged," "Kill the Kraken with a Trident."
  • Perfection tasks — Complete an entire encounter flawlessly, usually without taking any damage. These are the hardest tasks and are concentrated in Master and Grandmaster tiers.

① 🥉 Easy Tier (115 Tasks)

Easy tasks are designed for early-game players. They involve killing low-level bosses like Obor, Bryophyta, Hespori, Scurrius, and King Black Dragon. Most tasks are simple: "Kill X boss once" or "Kill X boss without taking damage from its special attack." You can complete the entire Easy tier with sub-60 combat stats and basic gear like Rune armor and an Amulet of Glory.

② 🥈 Medium Tier (155 Tasks)

Medium tasks introduce mid-game bosses: Barrows, Crazy Archaeologist, Deranged Archaeologist, Sarachnis, Kalphite Queen, and Mythic Scurrius. You will need overhead prayers and basic boss mechanics. Expect to use Dragon-tier weapons, Barrows armor, and some potions. 43 Prayer is essential for most Medium tasks.

③ 🥇 Hard Tier (195 Tasks)

Hard tier is where things get serious. Bosses include Vorkath, Zulrah, Grotesque Guardians, Alchemical Hydra, Demonic Gorillas, and Skotizo. You need 70+ combat stats, 70+ Prayer for Piety, and gear worth 10–50M GP. Speed kill tasks and no-damage challenges become common here.

④ 💎 Elite Tier (235 Tasks)

Elite is the first truly end-game tier. You face Cerberus, Thermonuclear Smoke Devil, Kraken, Abyssal Sire, God Wars Dungeon generals (Kree'arra, Graardor, Zilyana, K'ril), Nightmare, and Phantom Muspah. Expect 90+ combat stats, 74+ Prayer for Rigour/Augury, and gear worth 100M+. The standout reward here is the Combat Achievement Cape.

⑤ 🔥 Master Tier (280 Tasks)

Master tasks require you to dominate the toughest mid-game and some early-endgame content: Chambers of Xeric (CoX), Theatre of Blood (ToB) entry modes, The Gauntlet and Corrupted Gauntlet, Vardorvis, Duke Sucellus, Leviathan, Whisperer, and more. You need max or near-max combat, 95+ in relevant skills, and 500M+ gear. Speed challenges and perfection runs (zero damage, restricted gear) are standard.

⑥ 👑 Grandmaster Tier (521 Tasks)

Grandmaster is the pinnacle — the hardest challenges OSRS has to offer. Tasks cover The Inferno (including Zuk speed kills), Theatre of Blood (hard mode), Chambers of Xeric (Challenge Mode), Tombs of Amascut (Expert Mode, high invocation), Nex, the Colosseum, and awakened variants of the Four Bosses (Awakened Vardorvis, Duke, Leviathan, Whisperer). Expect to spend hundreds of hours and multiple billions of GP in gear.

⑦ 📊 Task Distribution Visualization

The distribution of tasks across tiers is intentionally weighted toward the higher end. Easy and Medium together account for only 270 tasks (18% of the total), while Grandmaster alone contains 521 tasks (35%). This reflects the system's design philosophy: low-tier content is quick to complete, while Grandmaster is an endurance marathon. Master and Grandmaster combined account for over 53% of all tasks, meaning the majority of CA content is aimed at maxed or near-maxed players.

🎁 Rewards Per Tier — What You Actually Get

The rewards scale dramatically with each tier. Here is every unlock, organized by tier:

① 🥉 Easy Rewards

  • 5% Slayer point boost — All Slayer tasks grant 5% more points. Minor but helpful early on.
  • Slayer helm unlock (basic recolor) — Unlocks the ability to recolor your Slayer Helm with basic colors at the Slayer master.
  • Boss scoreboard access — See your kill counts on the hiscores.

② 🥈 Medium Rewards

  • 10% Slayer point boost — Stacks with Easy for 15% total.
  • 2 additional Slayer block slots — Block up to 4 tasks instead of 2. This alone is worth rushing Medium CAs.
  • 1 additional Slayer skip/block extend — Extends your task count when skipping or blocking.
  • Ghommal's Hilt 1 — Teleports you to the Warriors' Guild, useful for defenders.

③ 🥇 Hard Rewards

  • 12.5% Slayer point boost — Combined total reaches 27.5%.
  • 3 additional Slayer block slots — 7 total block slots, enabling a fully optimized block list.
  • 2 additional Slayer skip/block extends — More flexibility with your task management.
  • Ghommal's Hilt 2 — Teleports to Warriors' Guild + the top of the guild for convenient bank access.
  • Slayer helm recolor (advanced) — Unlock unique boss-themed recolors for your Slayer helmet.

④ 💎 Elite Rewards

  • 15% Slayer point boost — Total boost now 42.5% with all previous tiers.
  • 4 additional Slayer block slots — 11 total block slots — enough to block almost every bad task.
  • 3 additional Slayer skip/block extends — Maximum flexibility.
  • Combat Achievement Cape — A cosmetic cape showing your PvM prowess. This is a major flex item.
  • Ghommal's Hilt 3 — Same teleports as hilt 2 but with unlimited charges and a 15% run energy restore.
  • Ornament kit: Slayer helm (Elite) — Adds a sleek purple-and-gold trim to your Slayer helm.

⑤ 🔥 Master Rewards

  • 17.5% Slayer point boost — Hits 60% total with all lower tiers.
  • 6 additional Slayer block slots — 17 total block slots — you can basically design your ideal task list.
  • 4 additional Slayer skip/block extends — Complete control over your Slayer experience.
  • Ghommal's Hilt 4 — Best hilt: teleports to Warriors' Guild, top of guild, and now also to the Myths' Guild with full run restore.
  • Ornament kit: Slayer helm (Master) — Fiery orange-and-black recolor with animated particle effects.
  • Ornament kit: Infernal cape / Fire cape — Gives your capes a sleek dark recolor with animated particles.

⑥ 👑 Grandmaster Rewards

  • 20% Slayer point boost — 80% total boost from all tiers.
  • 8 additional Slayer block slots — Unlock every possible block slot (25 total).
  • 5 additional Slayer skip/block extends — Maximum possible Slayer management.
  • Ghommal's Hilt 5 (Grandmaster) — Teleports to Warriors' Guild, Myths' Guild, top of guild, and now also the Farming Guild. Full run restore + prayer restore on use.
  • Bloodhound pet — The ultimate flex pet. Animated dog that follows you, showing everyone you've completed Grandmaster CAs.
  • Ornament kit: Slayer helm (Grandmaster) — The most prestigious Slayer helm recolor: jet black with gold accents and unique particle trail.
  • Ornament kit: Infernal cape (Grandmaster) — Gives your Infernal Cape a black-and-gold Grandmaster recolor.

📈 Point Thresholds & How Unlocking Works

① 🧮 How Points Work

Every task you complete gives you 1 point. Multi-point tasks (usually 2 or rarely 3) are reserved for exceptionally difficult challenges like "Kill Zuk without taking damage" or speed runs with strict time limits. These are most common in Master and Grandmaster tiers.

② 🔄 Cross-Tier Progress

Your points accumulate separately per tier. Completing Easy tasks only counts toward the Easy reward threshold. However, you do need to unlock each lower tier to access the next one. In practice this means: you must unlock Easy rewards before you can earn points toward Medium, and so on.

💡 Efficient Unlocking Strategy

Focus on completing the easiest tasks first within each tier. Many tiers have "free" tasks like "Kill boss X" (simple kill count) or "Kill boss X in the Wilderness" (single kill + escape). Prioritize these before touching speed runs or no-damage challenges. You only need ~90% completion per tier, so you can skip the truly painful tasks.

💀 The Hardest Task in Each Tier

Here is a breakdown of the single hardest task in each tier as of the 2026 game meta:

① 🥉 Easy — "Bryophyta: No Damage"

Kill Bryophyta without taking any damage. The challenge is that she has a ranged attack that can hit through prayer, and her special attack summons spawns that can damage you. Solution: use Protect From Magic, stand under her after each attack to avoid the ranged auto, and kill the spawns instantly with a halberd or burst spell. Requires precise positioning and fast reaction.

② 🥈 Medium — "Kalphite Queen: Speed Runner"

Kill the Kalphite Queen within 3 minutes of entering the lair. The problem is the 30-second phase transition cutscene and the fact that both phases have high Defence. You need near-max DPS gear for your combat level (Void Knight equipment, Trident of the Swamp, Blowpipe) and an excellent rotation. Most players fail 5–10 times before getting this one.

③ 🥇 Hard — "Alchemical Hydra: Walk the Path"

Kill Alchemical Hydra without taking damage from its fire walls. The floor fire pools move in a chessboard pattern, and you need to predict where they appear while maintaining DPS. One tick of fire wall contact = instant fail. This task demands perfect movement and awareness of the vent colors and fire wall timings simultaneously.

④ 💎 Elite — "Phantom Muspah: Prayer Smasher"

Kill Phantom Muspah without letting it restore its prayer. The boss periodically drains your prayer and heals itself. You must maintain Smite prayer, land every attack, and use the Lightbearer + Dragon Dagger combo to drain its prayer faster than it restores. Miss one spec cycle and the boss heals back to full, wasting 5+ minutes.

⑤ 🔥 Master — "Corrupted Gauntlet: Speed Runner"

Complete the Corrupted Gauntlet in under 5:30 from entering the lobby. This requires a near-perfect prep phase (gathering resources in under 2 minutes), perfect Hunllef mechanics, and 6:0 or 5:1 method with flawless pathing. Any mistake in the Hunllef fight costs 10+ seconds, making the time limit extremely tight. Most maxed players spend 10–20 hours grinding this single task.

⑥ 👑 Grandmaster — "Zuk: No Damage (The Inferno)"

Kill TzKal-Zuk without taking any damage from any source in the entire Inferno run. This means you must complete all 67 waves and the Zuk fight with zero damage. A single hit from a bat on wave 2, a stray ranger hit, or Zuk's shield explosion = instant fail. This is widely considered the single hardest achievement in all of OSRS. Fewer than 500 players have completed this task as of mid-2026. You need perfect prayer flicking, tick-perfect shield management, and nerves of steel.

🗺️ Recommended Progression Order

Do not attempt to brute-force CAs in tier order. Instead, follow this smart progression designed to maximize your efficiency per hour of playtime:

① 🟢 Step 1: Easy Tier (40–70 Combat)

Goal: Get 100 points. Time: 3–5 hours. Start with Obor and Bryophyta (both safe-spottable with Ranged). Kill the King Black Dragon with a friend or in a group. Kill Hespori (requires 45 Farming). Skip any Wilderness-related tasks if you're a HCIM. The Easy tier is essentially free — you can complete 90% of it in one afternoon with minimal gear investment.

② 🟡 Step 2: Medium Tier (70–90 Combat)

Goal: Get 140 points. Time: 8–12 hours. Tackle Barrows first — the tasks are straightforward (kill each brother, loot the chest). Move on to Sarachnis (safe-spottable with Ranged), then Deranged Archaeologist. Save the Kalphite Queen speed run for when you have higher stats or a partner. Avoid the Wilderness boss tasks unless you're comfortable with PvP.

③ 🟠 Step 3: Hard Tier (90–105 Combat)

Goal: Get 180 points. Time: 15–25 hours. Focus on Vorkath and Zulrah tasks first — they have the easiest sub-tasks. Grotesque Guardians are also manageable with mid-level gear. Alchemical Hydra requires 95 Slayer but the tasks themselves are straightforward. Demonic Gorilla tasks require MM2 completion but are relatively easy. Skip "Walk the Path" (Alchemical Hydra) and the speed-run tasks until you have max gear.

④ 🔵 Step 4: Elite Tier (100–115 Combat)

Goal: Get 220 points. Time: 30–50 hours. Start with Kraken and Thermonuclear Smoke Devil — these are easy with high Slayer. Then Cerberus (requires 91 Slayer) and the God Wars Dungeon generals. Phantom Muspah is the gatekeeper boss here; learn its mechanics thoroughly. Nightmare tasks can be done in masses. The Combat Achievement Cape is a realistic goal for any player with 100+ combat and 500M+ net worth.

⑤ 🔴 Step 5: Master Tier (110–126 Combat)

Goal: Get 265 points. Time: 60–100 hours. You need max combat and endgame gear (Twisted Bow, Scythe of Vitur, Tumeken's Shadow). Start with CoX and ToB entry mode tasks. Then move to the Four Bosses (Vardorvis, Duke, Leviathan, Whisperer). The Corrupted Gauntlet speed run will be your biggest obstacle. Consider joining a CA-focused Discord community for tips and gear sharing.

⑥ ⚫ Step 6: Grandmaster Tier (115–126 Combat)

Goal: Get 500 points. Time: 200–500 hours. This is the long haul. Start with the "easier" Grandmaster tasks: Nex mass kills, CoX CM completions, ToB normal mode speed runs. Then work up to Inferno completion, ToB Hard Mode, and finally the awakened bosses. The Grandmaster tier is not something you casually grind — it is a dedicated multi-month project that requires max gear (2B+ GP), max stats, and deep mechanical knowledge of every piece of endgame content.

⚡ Efficient Strategy by Combat Bracket

Time is your most valuable resource in OSRS. Here is how to maximize CA point gain per hour based on your combat level:

① 🔰 Bracket 1: Level 40–70 (Early Game)

Best tasks per hour: Obor, Bryophyta, Scurrius, King Black Dragon. At this stage, you can complete 8–12 tasks per hour. Focus exclusively on Easy tier. Do not attempt Medium yet — the time-to-reward ratio is terrible at low stats. Use Ranged (Magic Shortbow or Rune Crossbow) since most early bosses are weak to it. Your goal is 100 points for the combined 15% Slayer boost.

📊 Recommended Gear Budget: 500K GP

  • Rune Crossbow + Broad Bolts
  • Green D'Hide body + chaps
  • Amulet of Glory
  • Ring of Wealth (durability ring)
  • 1–2 Prayer Potions per trip

② ⚔️ Bracket 2: Level 70–90 (Mid Game)

Best tasks per hour: Barrows, Sarachnis, Deranged Archaeologist, Crazy Archaeologist. At this bracket you can complete 5–8 tasks per hour in Medium. Clear Easy fully if you haven't already — the remaining Easy tasks are fast. Prioritize 140 Medium points for the 4 block slots. A Slayer block list with 7+ slots dramatically speeds up your Slayer training.

③ 🛡️ Bracket 3: Level 90–105 (Late Mid Game)

Best tasks per hour: Vorkath, Zulrah, Demonic Gorillas, Skotizo. This is where you transition from Medium to Hard tasks. Clear all remaining Medium tasks first (you should be able to finish the Kalphite Queen speed run now). Then dive into Hard. Prioritize Vorkath and Zulrah tasks — they are mechanically simple and have many sub-tasks. Shoot for 180 Hard points to unlock the 11 total block slots and Ghommal's Hilt 2.

④ 💀 Bracket 4: Level 100–115 (Early Endgame)

Best tasks per hour: Kraken, Cerberus, GWD generals, Phantom Muspah. At this stage you should have 90+ in all combat stats and at least 74 Prayer. Focus on clearing Elite tasks. The Kraken and Smoke Devil tasks are essentially free at this level. Cerberus requires learning the ghost prayer switching but is straightforward. The Combat Achievement Cape at 640 total points should be your primary goal.

⑤ 🌟 Bracket 5: Level 110–126 (Endgame)

Best tasks per hour: CoX and ToB entry mode, The Gauntlet, Four Bosses. With max combat and endgame gear, you can clear Master tier at roughly 3–5 tasks per hour. Prioritize raid entry mode tasks first — they have very lenient requirements and yield 2–3 points each. Then work on the Four Bosses. Skip the Corrupted Gauntlet speed run until you are willing to put 10+ hours into a single task. The ornament kit for your infernal cape at Master tier is a huge motivator — that black-and-dark recolor is one of the best cosmetic rewards in the entire game.

⑥ 🏆 Bracket 6: Maxed (Grandmaster Progress)

Best tasks per hour: CoX CM completions, ToB normal mode, Nex masses. At this stage you are likely approaching max total level and have 1B+ in gear. Grandmaster tasks yield roughly 1–2 points per hour on average — some tasks require 20+ hours of attempts. The most efficient order is: Nex tasks → CoX CM completion tasks → ToB Speed Tasks → Inferno tasks → Awakened bosses. Do not rush the awakened bosses; they are the hardest content in the game and require specific gear setups that cost 500M+ each.

⑦ 📋 Quick Reference: Task Completion Checklist

Use this simple flowchart when deciding which CA tasks to prioritize during any play session:

  • Step 1: Open the CA interface and sort by "Incomplete" → filter to your current tier.
  • Step 2: Identify all "Kill X times" tasks — these are free points. Complete them first.
  • Step 3: Scan for tasks you can complete in the next 15 minutes (e.g., "Kill boss X in the Wilderness" if you are already there).
  • Step 4: Check if any mechanic tasks overlap with bosses you are already grinding for drops.
  • Step 5: Save speed tasks and perfection tasks for dedicated practice sessions with no distractions.
  • Step 6: Every time you unlock a new tier's rewards, take a moment to appreciate the progress — then get back to grinding.

Pro tip: Set a weekly CA goal. For example: "This week I will finish all remaining Easy tasks" or "This week I will learn Phantom Muspah well enough to complete its mechanic tasks." Small, consistent progress adds up faster than sporadic grinding sessions. Most players who reach Grandmaster completion did so by dedicating just 2–3 hours per week specifically to CA tasks.

❓ Combat Achievements FAQs

Do I need to complete every single task to unlock a tier?

No. You only need approximately 87–96% of tasks per tier (see the point thresholds table above). This means you can skip the hardest tasks in each tier and still unlock the rewards. For example, in Grandmaster you need 500 out of 521 tasks, so you can skip the 21 hardest tasks.

What is the single best reward from Combat Achievements?

The extra Slayer block slots from Medium (2 blocks) and Hard (3 blocks) are the highest-impact rewards in the entire system. Going from 2 block slots to 7 means you can block every bad task, dramatically improving Slayer XP and GP per hour. The Combat Achievement Cape at Elite is a close second for prestige, and the Bloodhound pet at Grandmaster is the ultimate flex.

How long does it take to complete all Combat Achievements?

For a brand-new account starting from scratch: expect 300–800 hours of focused CA grinding spread across 6–18 months of gameplay. Most players take 2–3 years to reach Grandmaster completion because certain tasks require max gear (2B+ GP) and deep mechanical skill. The Inferno alone can take 50–200 hours to learn for the average player.

Can I do Combat Achievements on an Ironman account?

Yes! CA progression works identically for Ironmen. However, gear restrictions make some tasks harder — you cannot simply buy a Twisted Bow or Scythe from the GE. Ironmen often need to grind specific drops (BowFa, DWH, BGS) before attempting higher tiers. The good news: CA rewards (especially Slayer block slots) are arguably more valuable for Ironmen due to their limited task selection.

What combat stats should I have before attempting each tier?

Easy: 40+ Attack/Strength/Ranged. Medium: 60+ combat stats + 43 Prayer. Hard: 75+ combat stats + 70 Prayer. Elite: 85+ combat stats + 74 Prayer. Master: 95+ combat stats + 77 Prayer + Piety/Rigour/Augury. Grandmaster: 99 all combat, 77+ Prayer, 90+ Herblore for overloads in raids. These are minimums — higher stats make everything smoother.

What is the Bloodhound pet drop rate from Master CAs?

The Bloodhound pet is the reward for completing the Grandmaster tier (not Master). It is a guaranteed unlock — you receive it automatically when you earn 500 Grandmaster points. There is no RNG involved. Once obtained, it is a permanent pet on your account that follows you around, just like any other pet.

Are there any Combat Achievement tasks in the Wilderness?

Yes, several tiers have Wilderness-specific tasks: killing the Chaos Elemental, Chaos Fanatic, Crazy Archaeologist, Scorpia, Vet'ion, Callisto, and Venenatis. These are mandatory if you want full completion, but you can skip them for tier unlocks (they typically amount to 5–10 tasks per tier). If you dislike the Wilderness, save these for last or skip them entirely.

Does dying reset my CA progress on a specific task?

It depends. Speed run tasks reset if you die (you must restart from outside the boss room). Kill count tasks persist — if you have 8/10 kills and die, you keep those 8. Perfection tasks (no damage, no prayer loss, etc.) reset on death. Always read the task description carefully. Tasks with "without dying" in the name mean one death = restart from scratch.

What is Ghommal's Hilt and why do I want it?

Ghommal's Hilt is a stackable teleport item unlocked through CA progression. Hilt 1 (Medium) teleports to the Warriors' Guild. Each upgrade adds more destination options and utility features. Hilt 5 (Grandmaster) teleports to the Warriors' Guild, Myths' Guild, Farming Guild, and top of the Warriors' Guild, and restores run energy + prayer points on use. It is one of the most convenient teleport items in the game.

Can I use other players' services to complete CA tasks?

No. CA tasks are designed to be completed personally on your account. Account sharing (paying someone to log into your account to complete Inferno or awakened bosses) is against Jagex's rules and can result in a permanent ban. Additionally, CA completions are a measure of your own skill — buying them defeats the purpose. Learn the content properly; the satisfaction of earning your CA Cape yourself is unmatched.

How often are new CA tasks added?

Jagex adds new CA tasks whenever a new boss is released or an existing boss receives a significant rework. In 2025 alone, over 80 new tasks were added across multiple game updates. The 2026 roadmap includes tasks for the upcoming sailing-expansion bosses and the rumored third raid. This means the CA system is always growing — Grandmaster completion is a moving target that requires staying current with every new piece of content.
