1. Why Quests Are the Best Investment for Money Making

In OSRS, there are two ways to increase your income: grind harder or unlock better content. Questing is the single most efficient way to unlock better content. Every hour you spend completing a quest that gatekeeps a 3M GP/hr boss pays for itself many times over. The math is straightforward: if Dragon Slayer II takes you 3 hours to complete and unlocks Vorkath at 3M GP/hr, that time investment is earning you back at an effective rate of 3M GP/hr for every future hour you spend at Vorkath.

Quest Rewards vs. Quest Unlocks

When evaluating quests for money making, distinguish between two categories:

Key Point: Focus on the "Quest Unlocks" category. A quest that gives you 100K GP as a reward is nice. A quest that unlocks a 3M GP/hr money maker is account-changing. The GP/hour return on questing dwarfs nearly every other activity in the game.

The Opportunity Cost of Not Questing

Consider a player camping Green Dragons for 500K GP/hr. If they spent 10 hours completing Dragon Slayer II instead, they'd sacrifice 5M GP in the short term — but every future hour at Vorkath earns them 3M GP/hr instead of 500K. After just 2 hours at Vorkath, the quest has paid for itself. After 10 hours, they've earned 30M GP — compared to 5M from Green Dragons. The difference is 25M GP earned from the same amount of play time.

Quick Wins vs. Long-Term Investments

Not all of these quests require the same account progression. If you're a new member or a mid-game account, prioritize the "Quick Wins": Bone Voyage (birdhouses for passive GP), Fairytale II (fairy rings for farm runs), Cabin Fever (Cave Horrors for Black Masks), and Ghosts Ahoy (Ectophial). These can be completed within your first week of membership and immediately boost your GP/hr.

The "Long-Term Investments" — Dragon Slayer II, Song of the Elves, and Sins of the Father — require significant stat and quest requirements, but they unlock the best GP/hr methods in the game. Every account should work toward these three quests as their primary progression goal.

3. Dragon Slayer II — Vorkath Guide (~3M GP/hr)

Dragon Slayer II is widely considered the most important quest in OSRS for money making. It unlocks Vorkath, a dragon boss on Ungael that consistently generates 2.5M-3.5M GP/hr with modest gear requirements. Vorkath is popular because it's predictable, has no complex mechanics, and drops a guaranteed 20K+ GP per kill plus valuable unique items.

Why Vorkath Is the Best Quest-Unlocked Money Maker

  • Guaranteed Profit Per Kill: Vorkath always drops 2 Superior Dragon Bones (worth ~20K each) and 2 Blue Dragonhides (~6K combined). That's ~46K per kill before any unique drops.
  • Unique Drops: Vorkath's head (24K), Dragonbone Necklace (~100K), Jar of Decay (~200K), Vorki pet, Draconic Visage (~4M), and the Skeletal Visage (~11M).
  • Consistent Kill Times: 1:30-2:30 per kill with mid-game gear. Players average 25-30 kills per hour.
  • Low Supply Cost: ~15K per kill in bolts and potions at the low end, or ~30K per kill using Dragon Hunter Crossbow.

Critical Requirement: Salve Amulet (ei) from the Haunted Mine and Tarn's Lair miniquest. This amulet provides a 20% damage and accuracy boost against undead monsters, and Vorkath counts as undead. Without it, your GP/hr drops by roughly 25-30%. Complete Haunted Mine, Tarn's Lair, and then use the Salve Shard on the amulet for the (ei) enchantment.

Vorkath Inventory Setup

  • Ranged Potion + Extended Super Antifire: Essential. Without Super Antifire (85 Herblore), you must use a regular Antifire + Anti-Dragon Shield + Protect from Magic — lower DPS but functional.
  • Prayer Potions (2): For Protect from Magic/Ranged prayer flicking. Vorkath's attacks alternate between Magic and Ranged.
  • Food: 8-12 Sharks/Manta Rays. With experience, you'll need less food as you dodge mechanics better.
  • House Tab or Rune Pouch (Dust/Law/Chaos): Teleport to house for pool restoration between kills. Repeat teleport + pool + portal to Lunar Isle (bank) for fastest resupply.
  • Crush Weapon (optional): Vorkath's spawn (white spider) is immune to Ranged. Use a Dragon Warhammer or Blowpipe on the spawn.
  • Slayer Staff (optional): Auto-casts Crumble Undead on the spawn for instant kills. More inventory-efficient than bringing a crush weapon.

Vorkath Kill Mechanics (Quick Reference)

  1. Standard Attacks: Magic (blue fireball) and Ranged (spike). Pray Protect from Magic and tank the Ranged hits with good gear.
  2. Fireball Barrage: Vorkath launches a large fireball. Move 2 tiles away from the impact zone — it deals 40+ damage if you stand in it.
  3. Poison Pool Phase: Vorkath spits acid pools across the arena. Walk continuously along the edge without stopping — stopping for 1 tick deals 10+ rapid damage. Count 7-8 attacks during this phase, then resume attacking.
  4. Ice Dragonfire Freeze: Vorkath freezes you and launches a one-shot dragonfire attack. Click the ground to move, then quickly click on the Crumble Undead spell to cast on the white spider spawn. If you're too slow, the spawn explodes for 60 damage. With a Slayer Staff, you auto-cast Crumble — just click the spawn immediately.
  5. Dragonfire Breath: Vorkath fires dragonfire that hits through Protect from Magic. The Extended Super Antifire + Anti-Dragon Shield reduces damage to 0-10. Without Super Antifire, this attack can hit 20-30.
Pro Tip — Woox Walking

Advanced players use "Woox Walking" during the acid phase — a precise movement pattern that lets you attack Vorkath while dodging pools. This increases kills/hr by 10-15%. Practice at a POH combat dummy first. Watch a guide on YouTube and practice the click rhythm before attempting in a real kill.

4. Song of the Elves — Gauntlet & Zalcano (~4M+ GP/hr)

Song of the Elves unlocks access to Prifddinas, the Elven city, which contains two of the most profitable money makers in OSRS: The Gauntlet (and its harder variant, Corrupted Gauntlet) and Zalcano. While the quest itself has steep requirements (70 in 8 skills), the payoff is enormous.

Corrupted Gauntlet (3M-6M GP/hr)

The Corrupted Gauntlet is a minigame/raid where you enter with no items, collect resources, craft gear and supplies, and fight the Corrupted Hunllef — all within a strict 7.5-minute prep timer. It's one of the most skill-intensive money makers, but also among the most profitable. Unlike most PvM methods, CG requires zero gear investment — you go in naked every time.

The Enhanced Seed Factor: The Enhanced Crystal Weapon Seed is a 1/400 drop from Corrupted Gauntlet. At ~120M GP in 2026, a single drop adds 300K GP/hr to your long-term rate (120M ÷ 400 completions). Even at dry streaks of 800+, the standard loot alone maintains 3M GP/hr.

Corrupted Gauntlet Strategy

  1. Prep Phase (7.5 minutes): Collect 7 of each resource (wood, ore, fiber), 2 herbs (Grym leaf), 2 weapon frames, and as many fish as you can. Craft T1 or T2 armor (T1 saves prep time, T2 gives more survivability). Craft two T3 weapons (Staff + Bow is most consistent). Cook 16-24 fish.
  2. Hunllef Fight: Protect from Magic/Ranged (prayer switches every 6 attacks). Dodge tornadoes — they deal rapid 15+ damage. Stand on safe tiles when floor patterns change. Count Hunllef attacks to predict prayer switches. Stomp attack at 1/3 HP — pick up the weapon and keep attacking.
  3. Learning Curve: Expect 20-30 deaths before your first Corrupted Gauntlet completion. Practice on Regular Gauntlet until you can consistently complete it with T1 armor and 12 fish. The skills transfer directly to CG.

Zalcano (1.5M-2.5M GP/hr)

Zalcano is a skilling boss in Prifddinas that rewards mining, smithing, and runecrafting supplies. It's significantly easier than Corrupted Gauntlet and can be done in mass worlds (3+ players) or small teams (2-4 players for better loot). Ideal for players who want a lower-effort, consistent alternative to CG.

5. Sins of the Father — Hallowed Sepulchre & Vyrewatch Guide

Sins of the Father completes the Myreque quest line and unlocks access to Darkmeyer, the Vyrewatch city. This quest opens two distinct money makers: the Hallowed Sepulchre (an Agility minigame with enormous profit potential) and Vyrewatch Sentinels (a reliable combat money maker for mid-game accounts).

Hallowed Sepulchre (1M-2.5M GP/hr)

The Hallowed Sepulchre is arguably the best non-PvM money maker in OSRS. It's a floor-based Agility course where you navigate traps, loot coffins, and reach the final Grand Hallowed Coffin for valuable loot. The GP/hr scales massively with your Agility level because higher floors unlock better loot.

The Ring of Endurance: The Floor 5 Grand Hallowed Coffin has a 1/200 chance of containing a Ring of Endurance, valued at approximately 30M GP in 2026. This single drop adds ~150K GP/hr to your long-term rate. Even without it, the standard loot from Floor 5 runs is highly profitable.

Hallowed Sepulchre Strategy

  • Learn Each Floor Separately: Don't rush to Floor 5. Master Floors 1-3 first, then add Floor 4, then Floor 5. Each floor introduces new trap mechanics (flame statues, crossbow traps, teleport pads, spinning swords).
  • Time Efficiency: Loot all coffins on Floors 3-5. Skip coffins on Floors 1-2 unless they're directly on your path — the loot is minimal compared to higher floors.
  • Hallowed Tools: Prioritize buying the Hallowed Grapple, Hallowed Focus, and Hallowed Symbol from the reward shop. These reduce trap failure rates and speed up runs.
  • XP + GP Combined: At 82+ Agility, the Sepulchre gives 75K+ Agility XP/hr alongside 1.5M-2.5M GP/hr. That's the best combined XP+GP rate in the game for any non-combat skill.

Vyrewatch Sentinels (500K-800K GP/hr)

Vyrewatch Sentinels are located in Darkmeyer and require the Ivandis Flail (or Blisterwood Flail, upgraded during Sins of the Father) to damage. They're an AFK-friendly combat method with consistent profit from alchable drops, blood shards, and noted resources.

6. Other Quest Money Methods

Beyond the big three (DS2, SotE, SotF), several other quests unlock money making methods that are especially valuable for early and mid-game accounts. These methods have lower requirements and provide steady, reliable income.

Bone Voyage — Birdhouse Runs (300K-600K GP/day, Passive)

Bone Voyage is arguably the single best "GP per minute of active effort" quest in OSRS. It unlocks Fossil Island and birdhouse runs — a passive money maker that takes 90 seconds per run and generates 100K-150K GP per run. Runs can be repeated every 50 minutes, making this a 300K-600K GP/day passive income stream.

How It Works: Place 4 birdhouses on Fossil Island, add 10 seeds (any type — cheap hop seeds work), wait 50 minutes, then harvest for bird nests. Nests contain tree seeds (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Yew, Magic), rings (Gold through Onyx), and bird eggs. 4 runs per day at Yew+ tier easily generates 400K-600K+ GP daily for roughly 6 minutes of total active time.

Fairytale II — Cure a Queen (Fairy Rings for Farm Run Efficiency)

Fairytale II doesn't directly generate GP, but it unlocks the Fairy Ring teleport network, which is the single biggest efficiency upgrade for herb runs. Without fairy rings, a full herb run takes 8-10 minutes. With fairy rings (specifically ALQ for Morytania, CKR for Ardougne, and CIQ for Canifis), farm run times drop to 5-7 minutes — a 30-40% speed increase.

GP Impact: If your herb runs generate 500K per run, a 30% speed increase means you can fit 40% more runs into the same play time. Over a month, that's millions of extra GP. Fairy rings also enable faster travel to Slayer tasks, boss lairs, and clue scroll locations — all of which add indirect GP/hr.

Cabin Fever — Cave Horrors for Black Masks (400K-800K GP/hr)

Cabin Fever grants access to Mos Le'Harmless and the Cave Horror dungeon. Cave Horrors drop the Black Mask at a 1/512 rate, and Black Masks are consistently worth 800K-1.5M GP each in 2026 due to demand from Slayer (required to create the Slayer Helmet).

7. Quest Completion Order for Maximum GP

Questing for GP isn't about completing quests in alphabetical order. The optimal approach layers quest rewards so each quest makes the next one easier, and passive income methods fund the gear needed for bossing. Here's the optimal quest path for building a money-making account:

Phase 1: Foundation (First Week — ~15 QP)

These quests have minimal requirements and immediately unlock passive or low-effort GP methods:

  1. Waterfall Quest — 13,750 Attack/Strength XP. Levels 1-30 in both skills instantly. Saves hours of early combat training.
  2. The Restless Ghost + Priest in Peril — Unlocks Prayer training and Morytania access. Required for many later quests.
  3. Ghosts Ahoy — Ectophial teleport. Adds Morytania herb patch to farm runs. 50K-120K extra per farm run.
  4. The Dig Site + Bone Voyage — Unlocks Fossil Island birdhouse runs. Start accumulating passive GP immediately (300K-600K/day at higher Hunter levels).
  5. Fairytale I — Start Fairytale II (you only need the first few steps for fairy ring access — no stat requirements). Unlocks fairy rings, which will accelerate everything else.

Phase 1 GP Outlook: 400K-800K/day from birdhouses + herb runs combined. Use this GP to fund Dragon Scimitar, Glory Amulet, and basic gear.

Phase 2: Mid-Game Unlocks (Weeks 2-4 — ~50-70 combat stats)

  1. Monkey Madness I — Dragon Scimitar (best 60 Attack weapon). Massive upgrade for all Slayer and melee content.
  2. Underground Pass + Regicide — Unlocks Zulrah and the Elven lands poison line. Continue to Roving Elves for Crystal equipment.
  3. Cabin Fever — Black Masks from Cave Horrors. Grind Cave Horrors until you get 1-2 masks to fund gear upgrades (or sell for 800K-1.5M each).
  4. Lunar Diplomacy — NPC Contact spell, Tan Leather, Superglass Make. These spells add efficiency and profit to crafting and slayer.
  5. Haunted Mine + Tarn's Lair — Salve Amulet (ei). +20% damage to undead monsters. Essential for Vorkath later.

Phase 2 GP Outlook: 1M-2M/day from birdhouses, herbs, Cave Horrors, and early Slayer. Start accumulating a 10M+ gear fund.

Phase 3: Major Unlocks (Month 2+ — ~80+ combat stats, 70+ skills)

  1. Fremennik Exiles — Neitiznot Faceguard upgrade, Basilisk Knight access with V's Shield.
  2. Sins of the Father — Hallowed Sepulchre (stack Agility GP while working toward 72+ Agility for Floor 4-5), Vyrewatch Sentinels for AFK profit.
  3. Dragon Slayer II — The crown jewel. Vorkath at 2.5M-3.5M GP/hr. With 10M+ in gear and 80+ Ranged, you can sustain 25+ kills/hr immediately.
  4. Song of the Elves — The ultimate endgame unlock. Corrupted Gauntlet at 3M-6M GP/hr, Zalcano at 1.5M-2.5M GP/hr, and Prifddinas utilities (anvil, sandpit, soft clay mine).

Phase 3 GP Outlook: 5M-10M+/day at Corrupted Gauntlet or Vorkath for 2-3 hours. Passive income from birdhouses and herb runs continues in the background. At this stage, you're making enough GP to sustain bonds without paying real money.

8. FAQ

What's the absolute minimum quest list for money making?

Bone Voyage (birdhouses), Fairytale II (fairy rings), Ghosts Ahoy (Ectophial), and Cabin Fever (Black Masks). These four quests unlock 500K-1.5M GP/day of passive and low-effort income with minimal requirements. Everything beyond these scales up, but these four form the foundation.

Should I do Dragon Slayer II or Song of the Elves first?

Dragon Slayer II first for most accounts. DS2 has lower skill requirements (no 70s in 8 skills), costs less GP in supplies to complete, and Vorkath is easier to learn than Corrupted Gauntlet. The predictable profit from Vorkath funds the gear and supplies you'll need for Song of the Elves and CG learning. However, if you already have 85+ in all combat stats and want the highest potential GP/hr, SotE → CG is the better long-term investment.

How much GP should I have before starting Dragon Slayer II?

Budget setup: ~3M GP for Blessed d'hide, Dragon Crossbow, Ruby/Diamond Bolts (e), Anti-Dragon Shield, and basic supplies. Comfortable setup: ~8-10M GP for better food, stamina potions, and teleport tabs. The quest itself costs about 500K-1M in supplies (food, potions, runes for the boss fights). Note that the quest bosses (Galvek specifically) hit hard — bring Ruby Bolts (e) for the high-HP phases and Diamond Bolts (e) for the final phase.

Is Corrupted Gauntlet worth learning?

Yes, but only if you're willing to invest the time to learn it. Expect 20-50 deaths before consistent completions. The zero-gear-requirement model means you lose nothing on death (you go in naked and lose nothing when you die). CG is the best GP/hr method in OSRS for players who can't afford a Twisted Bow or Scythe — and it covers the gap until you can afford them. It's also excellent for Ironmen because it drops massive quantities of resources (runes, gems, ores, herbs).

What if I don't have the stats for the big quests yet?

Focus on Phase 1 quests and methods. Birdhouses + herb runs alone can generate 300K-600K/day while you train. That GP funds buyable skills (Construction, Prayer, Herblore) for quest requirements. Run the quest checklist in the order listed in Section 7 — each quest makes the next one easier by providing XP, items, or GP.

How do herb runs relate to quests for money making?

Herb runs aren't quest-unlocked (you can do them at 32 Farming without any quests), but quests massively improve their GP/hr. Ghosts Ahoy → Morytania patch, Fairytale II → Fairy rings (faster runs), My Arm's Big Adventure → Trollheim patch (disease-free), Making Friends with My Arm → Weiss patch (also disease-free), and the Kourend & Kebos diary → Hosidius patch (disease-free with 50% favour). Each patch adds 80K-150K per run. A full 9-patch herb run with all unlocks generates 400K-600K per run — roughly 2M-3M GP/day at 4-5 runs.

Are there any quests that unlock profitable skilling methods?

Yes. Bone Voyage → birdhouses (already covered). Song of the Elves → Zalcano (skilling boss). Sins of the Father → Hallowed Sepulchre (Agility GP). Lunar Diplomacy → Tan Leather spell (1M+ GP/hr at certain margins), Superglass Make (crafting profit), and Humidify (Clay softening for 300K-500K/hr). The Eyes of Glouphrie → Crystal Saw (invisible +3 Construction boost, saves millions on training). Dream Mentor → Plank Make spell (400K-600K GP/hr + Magic XP).
