1. What Are Combat Achievements?

The Combat Achievements (CA) system was introduced to OSRS in late 2022 as a way to give PvM players structured progression goals outside of the regular quest and leveling systems. Think of it as a "achievement diary" specifically for combat — but with far better rewards.

📊 The Six Tiers of Combat Achievements

There are six difficulty tiers in total. Each tier has its own set of tasks and its own unique reward item (Ghommal's Hilt). Here's the full breakdown:

🎁 Why Combat Achievements Matter

The rewards are genuinely game-changing. The Ghommal's Hilt series provides a damage boost that stacks with every single DPS boost in the game — prayers, potions, gear, slash bonuses, everything. At just the Easy tier, you get +5% damage across all three combat styles. That is equivalent to permanently having a low-tier Strength potion active, except it never runs out and works for Ranged and Magic too.

🏆 Ghommal's Hilt 1 Effect (Easy Tier Reward)

  • +5% damage boost to all melee, ranged, and magic attacks
  • Works in PvM, PvP, Slayer — everywhere
  • Stacks with Piety, Rigour, Augury, potions, gear
  • Can be upgraded to Gilded Spear (cosmetic + higher hilt tiers)
  • No skill requirements to equip (except completion of Easy CA)

📱 How to Access Your Combat Achievements

Open your Achievement Interface by clicking the trophy icon in the game's main interface (near the mini-map), or right-click your character and select "Combat Achievements." The interface shows:

  • All tasks organized by tier (Easy → Grandmaster)
  • Your completion progress for each tier
  • Exact requirements for each task (hover over task name)
  • One-click teleport to task locations (for supported tasks)
  • Reward claim button once a tier is complete
💡 Pro Tip: Task Tracking

You can "track" up to 6 Combat Achievements at once by clicking the green plus icon next to any task. Tracked tasks show progress on your game screen in real time. Always track your current target tasks so you don't lose progress accidentally.

2. Easy Combat Achievements — Complete List (All 35 Tasks)

This is the core of the guide. Every one of the 35 Easy-tier tasks is explained in detail below, organized by category. For each task you'll find: the exact requirement, the best method to complete it, recommended gear, and approximate time investment.

📌 Reading This Section

Tasks are grouped into 5 categories: General Combat (10), Style-Specific (8), Boss-Related (7), Slayer Tasks (5), and PvM Milestones (5). You do NOT need to complete them in this order — see Section 3 for the optimal completion route. This section is for reference as you work through each task.

🗡️ Category 1: General Combat Tasks (10 Tasks)

These tasks focus on basic combat fundamentals. Most will complete naturally as you play the game normally.

Task 1: Deal 100 Damage to a Single Monster

Requirement: Deal a cumulative total of 100 damage to one monster in a single encounter (damage dealt after the monster dies does not count).

Method: This completes naturally during normal training. Any monster with 100+ HP works. Hill Giants (HP: 37) need about 3 kills; Cows (HP: 8) need about 13. The easiest approach: find a monster with high HP like a Giant (Hill/Moss) and attack it continuously. The task tracks damage dealt, not kills.

Recommended Gear: Any weapon you're currently using. No special gear needed.

Estimated Time: 30 seconds (natural completion).

Task 2: Deal 500 Damage to a Single Monster

Requirement: Deal 500 cumulative damage to one monster before it dies.

Method: You need a monster with at least 500 HP. Options: Moss Giants (HP: 120), Ice Giants (HP: 140), or any boss. The simplest method: attack a Moss Giant in the Moss Giant Island (Brimhaven) or the Moss Giants in the Catacombs of Kourend. With a decent weapon (Dragon Scimitar or better), this takes about 2–3 minutes per giant. You can also do this at Cows in Lumbridge if you're very low level, but it will take many kills.

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar or better; full Rune armor or better.

Estimated Time: 2–5 minutes.

Task 3: Deal 1,000 Damage to a Single Monster

Requirement: Deal 1,000 cumulative damage to one monster before it dies.

Method: This requires a high-HP monster. Best options: Quest Bosses with high HP, or Slayer monsters in the Catacombs. The absolute easiest: fight Hill Giants in a multi-combat area... no, their HP is too low. You need a boss or high-level Slayer creature. Recommended: Sarachnis (HP: 325) — attack her and let her heal between phases, or Barrows brothers (each has 200+ HP). Actually, the simplest method for most players: fight any boss with 200+ HP and use a slow weapon (like a 2h sword) to maximize damage per hit. The game tracks total damage including overkill, so you can exceed the monster's HP.

Recommended Gear: Any mid-tier setup. Dragon weapons or better.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

Task 4: Kill 10 Monsters

Requirement: Kill any 10 monsters (any combat level, any location).

Method: Natural completion. Kill 10 Cows, 10 Chickens, 10 Goblins — anything counts. If you're doing this deliberately, the fastest spot is Cows in Lumbridge (north of the river).

Estimated Time: 1–2 minutes (natural completion).

Task 5: Kill 50 Monsters

Requirement: Kill any 50 monsters.

Method: Also natural completion. Doing a Slayer task of 50+ kills completes this automatically. If rushing: Al-Kharid Warriors (near the duel arena) are aggressive and fast to kill.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

Task 6: Kill 100 Monsters

Requirement: Kill any 100 monsters.

Method: Natural completion during Slayer or combat training. One Slayer task often covers this. Deliberate method: Rock Crabs (Rellekka area) have 10 HP and respawn almost instantly, making them the fastest 100-kill grind in the game.

Estimated Time: 10–20 minutes.

Task 7: Deal 50 Damage with a Single Hit

Requirement: Land a single hit that deals 50+ damage.

Method: Requires a decent strength level (50+) and a good weapon. Dragon Scimitar at 60 Attack/Strength can hit 50+ reliably. Dragon 2h Sword special attack (60+ Strength) can hit 50+ in one special. Rune 2h Sword max hit at 50 Strength is about 38 — not enough. Upgrade to Dragon weapons. At 70 Attack with Abyssal Whip, max hit is ~58, so this completes naturally.

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar (60 Attack) or Abyssal Whip (70 Attack). Strength potion helps.

Estimated Time: Natural completion at 60+ Strength.

Task 8: Deal 100 Damage with a Single Hit

Requirement: Land a single hit that deals 100+ damage.

Method: Much harder than Task 7. Requires high Strength (80+) and a powerful weapon. Options:

  • Abyssal Whip (70 Attack, 82 Strength): max hit ~64 — not enough
  • Dragon Warhammer special: can hit 100+ at 80+ Strength
  • Granite Maul special attack (2 hits in one tick): combined damage can exceed 100
  • Vertical slash with Dragon 2h spec: possible at 85+ Strength
  • Blowpipe with Dragon darts (Ranged): max hit ~38 per dart — not enough
  • Easiest method: Use a Dragon Warhammer special attack on a low-defense monster. The spec rolls against the monster's defense separately and can hit very high.

Recommended Gear: Dragon Warhammer (special attack), or 85+ Strength with any high-tier weapon.

Estimated Time: May require several attempts. 10–30 minutes.

Task 9: Cast 50 Spells

Requirement: Cast any combat spell 50 times (on monsters or players).

Method: Equip a Magic weapon (staff or wand), select a combat spell (Wind Strike is fine), and cast it 50 times on any monster. Lowest level monsters (Cows, Chickens) work best since they die in 1 hit. Each cast counts as 1, even if it deals 0 damage.

Recommended Gear: Staff of Air (or any elemental staff), Wind Strike / Wind Bolt spell.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

Task 10: Eat 50 Pieces of Food

Requirement: Eat any food 50 times.

Method: Completes naturally during any extended PvM session. If rushing: buy 50 Cooked Meat or Bread from the Grand Exchange (cheapest food), go to a safe area, and eat them one by one. Each click = 1 food eaten.

Estimated Time: 2–3 minutes (if rushing).

🏹 Category 2: Style-Specific Tasks (8 Tasks)

These tasks require you to use specific combat styles. They encourage you to try all three styles (Melee, Ranged, Magic).

Task 11: Kill a Monster with Melee Only

Requirement: Kill any monster using only melee attacks (no range/magic/prayers that deal damage).

Method: Wield a melee weapon, attack any monster, and kill it without using any other combat style. Cows, Goblins, or Rats are the easiest targets. Make sure auto-retaliate is set to "melee" only.

Estimated Time: 30 seconds.

Task 12: Kill a Monster with Ranged Only

Requirement: Kill any monster using only ranged attacks.

Method: Equip a bow/crossbow and arrows/bolts, attack a monster from a distance. Cows in Lumbridge are ideal (stand north of the fence). You can also use Magic Shortbow or Maple Longbow for this.

Recommended Gear: Maple Shortbow / Maple Longbow + Steel arrows (or better).

Estimated Time: 1 minute.

Task 13: Kill a Monster with Magic Only

Requirement: Kill any monster using only magic attacks.

Method: Equip a staff, select a combat spell, and kill a monster using only spells. Al-Kharid Warriors have low magic defense and work well. Cows also work (Wind Strike kills in 2–3 casts).

Recommended Gear: Staff of Air + Wind Bolt (or higher).

Estimated Time: 1–2 minutes.

Task 14: Kill 10 Monsters with Melee

Requirement: Kill 10 monsters using only melee attacks.

Method: Same as Task 11, but kill 10 instead of 1. Rock Crabs (10 HP each) are the fastest target. They also never deal damage to you, so this is completely safe.

Estimated Time: 2–3 minutes.

Task 15: Kill 10 Monsters with Ranged

Requirement: Kill 10 monsters using only ranged attacks.

Method: Ranged train at Cows (Lumbridge) or Al-Kharid Scorpions. With Maple Longbow + Steel arrows, each scorpion takes 2–3 shots. 10 kills takes about 3–5 minutes.

Estimated Time: 3–5 minutes.

Task 16: Kill 10 Monsters with Magic

Requirement: Kill 10 monsters using only magic attacks.

Method: Chaos Druids (Taverley Dungeon) have low magic defense and are a popular magic training target. With Wind Blast or better, each druid dies in 2–3 casts. 10 kills = about 5 minutes.

Recommended Gear: Staff of Fire + Fire Blast (or Air staff + Wind Blast).

Estimated Time: 5–8 minutes.

Task 17: Attack a Monster Using All Three Combat Styles in One Fight

Requirement: In a single fight against one monster, deal damage using melee, ranged, and magic (in any order).

Method: Find a monster with enough HP to survive all three attacks. Hill Giant (HP: 37) is perfect. Steps:

  1. Attack with melee weapon → deal damage
  2. Switch to bow → shoot one arrow (ranged damage)
  3. Switch to staff → cast one spell (magic damage)
  4. Kill the monster (any style)

You must deal actual damage with each style (not just attempt the attack). Make sure your accuracy is high enough.

Estimated Time: 1 minute per attempt. Usually completes in 1–2 tries.

Task 18: Complete a Slayer Task Using Only One Combat Style

Requirement: Complete an entire Slayer task (all kills) using only one combat style.

Method: Get a Slayer task from any Slayer Master (Turael is easiest for low levels). Choose a task with monsters weak to one style. Example: Turael assigns Rats (weak to melee) → kill all Rats using only melee. Or Cows (if assigned) → use only ranged.

Note: Using protection prayers during the task is allowed. Using non-combat styles (like using a special attack that deals a different style) may invalidate the task — check the exact requirement in-game.

Estimated Time: Depends on task size. 5–30 minutes.

👑 Category 3: Boss-Related Tasks (7 Tasks)

These tasks introduce you to bossing. All Easy-tier boss tasks use entry-level bosses with low requirements.

Task 19: Kill the Giant Mole (1 Kill)

Requirement: Kill the Giant Mole once.

Method: The Giant Mole is located in the Falador Mole Lair (access via the tunnel south of Falador Park, requires a rope first time). Requirements: 60 Combat recommended, light source (bullseye lantern or shedder), and a Spade (to dig to the mole hill entrance).

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar, Rune armor, Strength potion, food (10–15 Lobsters). The Mole has 180 HP and deals low damage. Safe to kill at 60+ Combat.

Strategy: The Mole digs underground and appears at a random mole hill on the surface. Follow the fresh dirt particles to find where it resurfaced. Attack when it appears. Bring a Watchtower teleport or walk back after the kill.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes per kill.

Task 20: Kill Barrows Brother (Any Brother, 1 Kill)

Requirement: Kill any one Barrows brother.

Method: Enter the Barrows tunnels (requires starting the Temple of Ikov quest or having access to the Barrows area). Kill any brother (Ahhren, Dharok, Guthan, Karil, Torag, Verac). Each brother has about 200 HP and uses their signature attack style.

Recommended Gear: 70 Combat, Dragon Scimitar, Adamant/Rune armor, food (15–20 Lobsters), Prayer potions (4+).

Strategy: Use the Protect prayers corresponding to each brother's attack style. Ahhren = Melee (Protect from Melee), Karil = Ranged (Protect from Missiles), etc. Kill one brother and leave — you don't need to complete the full Barrows run for this task.

Estimated Time: 3–7 minutes.

Task 21: Kill the King Black Dragon (1 Kill)

Requirement: Kill the King Black Dragon (KBD) once.

Method: KBD lair is accessed via the Wilderness Lever in Edgeville (pull lever → appears in level 52 Wilderness → run north to the KBD lair entrance). Requirements: Anti-dragon shield (absolute must — without it, KBD's dragonfire kills you in 1 hit), 70+ Combat recommended.

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar, Rune armor, Anti-dragon shield, Super Antifire potion (or regular Antifire + shield), food (15–20 Lobsters/Swordfish), Prayer potions.

Strategy: KBD has 255 HP and uses dragonfire + melee. With Anti-dragon shield equipped, dragonfire deals 0 damage. Use Protect from Melee. Safe to kill at 70+ Combat with good food.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

Task 22: Kill the Kraken (1 Kill)

Requirement: Kill the Kraken boss once.

Method: Kraken Cove is accessed after completing the Zulrah quest (Regicide partial completion) and having 87 Slayer (to kill Cave Kraken for the whirlpool spawn). Actually, for the Kraken boss specifically, you need 87 Slayer to kill Cave Kraken and spawn the boss version. This is a higher requirement than other Easy boss tasks.

Alternative for low-level players: Get a higher-level friend to help, or skip this task temporarily and come back after reaching 87 Slayer. The Kraken boss has 210 HP and uses magic attacks exclusively.

Recommended Gear: Ranged setup (Magic Shortbow / Rune Crossbow + Mithril bolts or better), Adamant armor, Prayer potions, food.

Estimated Time: 3–6 minutes (once 87 Slayer is reached).

Task 23: Kill Skotizo (1 Kill)

Requirement: Kill Skotizo once.

Method: Skotizo is a boss in the Catacombs of Kourend. To spawn him, you need to use 3 Dark totems on the altar in the Catacombs. Dark totems are made from Key pieces dropped by monsters in the Catacombs. Requirements: 60+ Combat, completion of Client of Kourend quest (to access Catacombs).

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar or better, Adamant/Rune armor, food (15–20), Prayer potions (Skotizo uses prayer-draining attacks).

Strategy: Skotizo has 300 HP and attacks with Melee + Magic. Use Protect from Melee most of the time, switch to Protect from Magic when he uses magic attacks. Bring a Teleport tablet in case things go wrong.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes per kill (plus time to gather Dark totems).

Task 24: Complete a Barrows Brother Encounter (Kill All 6 Brothers)

Requirement: Kill all 6 Barrows brothers in a single trip.

Method: Enter Barrows, kill all 6 brothers, then loot the chest. This is the "full" Barrows run. You need 70+ Combat, decent gear, and prayer potions. Each brother must be killed in their crypt.

Recommended Gear: Dragon Scimitar, Rune/Dragon armor, food (20+), Prayer potions (6+), Super Combat potion (optional).

Strategy: Kill brothers in order of difficulty: Ahhren (easiest) → Guthan → Torag → Verac → Dharok → Karil (ranged, trickiest). Use Protect prayers for each. After all 6 are dead, enter the tunnel and kill the spawn (usually an NPC using the Brothers' gear sets). Loot the chest.

Estimated Time: 15–25 minutes per full run.

Task 25: Kill a Boss While Wearing a Full Set of Rune Armor

Requirement: Kill any boss while equipped with a full set of Rune armor (helmet, body, legs, gloves, boots — shield optional).

Method: Equip full Rune armor, then kill any boss. The Giant Mole is the easiest boss for this — low defense requirement, low damage, accessible to mid-level players. Alternatively, Barrows brothers (one brother only) also count.

Recommended Gear: Full Rune armor set (bought from Grand Exchange for ~100K GP total), Dragon Scimitar, Strength potion, food.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

⚔️ Category 4: Slayer Tasks (5 Tasks)

These tasks introduce you to the Slayer skill, which is essential for efficient PvM in OSRS.

Task 26: Complete a Slayer Task Assigned by Turael

Requirement: Complete any Slayer task given by Turael (the lowest-level Slayer Master, located south of Burthorpe).

Method: Talk to Turael, get a task, complete all kills, then return to Turael to claim completion. Turael assigns very easy tasks (Rats, Cows, Sheep, Goblins) with no combat or Slayer level requirement.

Note: You must have started the Wolfbane quest or have access to Turael (available from the start of the game). No requirements at all.

Estimated Time: 2–10 minutes depending on task.

Task 27: Complete a Slayer Task Assigned by Mazchna

Requirement: Complete a Slayer task from Mazchna (Slayer Master north of Canifis, requires 20 Combat).

Method: Same as Task 26 but with Mazchna. Mazchna assigns slightly harder tasks (Cave Bugs, Wall Beasts, Banshees) but still very manageable. Requires 20 Combat to use.

Recommended: 30+ Combat, decent armor, food. Some tasks require Slayer helmet upgrades (like earmuffs for Banshees) — buy these from the Slayer equipment shop.

Estimated Time: 10–30 minutes.

Task 28: Kill 10 Slayer Creatures While on a Slayer Task

Requirement: Kill 10 monsters that are assigned to you as part of an active Slayer task.

Method: Get a Slayer task (any master), kill 10 of the assigned monsters. This completes naturally as you work on Tasks 26 and 27. If rushing: get a task of 10+ kills from Turael (e.g., "Kill 15 Rats"), kill 10, and the task completes.

Estimated Time: Natural completion.

Task 29: Use a Slayer Item on a Slayer Creature

Requirement: Use a Slayer-specific item on a matching Slayer creature (e.g., Earmuffs on a Banshee, Nose peg on a Ghost, etc.).

Method: Get a Slayer task that requires a Slayer item (Banshees → Earmuffs, Wall Beasts → Spiny helmet, etc.). Equip the item BEFORE engaging the creature. If you forget, you take massive damage or get special debuffs.

Easiest Method: Get a Banshee task from Mazchna (20 Combat required). Buy Earmuffs from the Slayer equipment shop (Burthorpe). Equip earmuffs, then kill Banshees in the Slayer Tower (Canifis).

Estimated Time: 5–15 minutes.

Task 30: Complete 5 Slayer Tasks in a Row (Consecutive)

Requirement: Complete 5 Slayer tasks without skipping or canceling any (consecutive streak).

Method: Choose a low-level Slayer Master (Turael) and complete 5 tasks in a row. Do NOT use Turael's "skip" option — always complete the task. After 5 consecutive completions, the achievement unlocks.

Strategy: Turael's tasks are very short (5–20 kills). 5 tasks in a row = about 30–60 minutes total. This is one of the longer Easy tasks but very achievable.

Estimated Time: 30–60 minutes.

🏆 Category 5: PvM Milestones (5 Tasks)

These tasks test your ability to handle slightly more challenging PvM content.

Task 31: Kill 5 Bosses (Any Bosses, Cumulative)

Requirement: Kill any 5 bosses (total, not necessarily unique).

Method: Killing the Giant Mole 5 times, or 1 Giant Mole + 1 KBD + 3 Barrows brothers, etc. Any combination works. The easiest boss to farm for this is the Giant Mole (quick respawn, safe, low requirements).

Recommended Route: Kill Giant Mole 5 times. With ~70 Combat and Dragon Scimitar, each kill takes 5–8 minutes. 5 kills = 25–40 minutes.

Estimated Time: 25–40 minutes.

Task 32: Deal 50 Damage to a Boss Using Ranged

Requirement: Deal 50 ranged damage to a boss in a single encounter.

Method: Attack any boss with ranged weapons and deal 50 cumulative damage. Giant Mole is the easiest target (low ranged defense). With a Maple Longbow + Steel arrows, you can deal 50 damage in 5–10 shots.

Recommended Gear: Maple Shortbow / Maple Longbow + Iron/Steel arrows, full Rune armor (for defense).

Estimated Time: 2–5 minutes.

Task 33: Deal 50 Damage to a Boss Using Magic

Requirement: Deal 50 magic damage to a boss in a single encounter.

Method: Same as Task 32 but with magic. Giant Mole has low magic defense, making it the best target. Cast Wind Blast / Fire Blast (or higher) 5–10 times.

Recommended Gear: Staff of Air/Fire + Wind Blast / Fire Blast, magic armor (Wizard/Splitbark).

Estimated Time: 2–5 minutes.

Task 34: Kill a Boss Without Taking Any Damage

Requirement: Kill any boss without taking any damage during the fight.

Method: This sounds hard but is actually easy with the right approach. The trick: use Protect prayers + safe spotting. The easiest boss for this is the Giant Mole:

  1. Equip Ranged or Magic (so you can attack from a distance)
  2. Use the fences/terrain in the Mole Lair to safe-spot the Mole
  3. Or use Protect from Melee + stand behind an obstacle
  4. Another easy method: Kill the Giant Mole using Only Ranged from a safe spot

Alternative (even easier): If you have high enough Combat, you can simply out-damage the boss while keeping Protect active and using food proactively. But the safe-spot method is 100% reliable.

Estimated Time: 5–10 minutes.

Task 35: Open a Barrows Chest (Loot the Chest After Killing All Brothers)

Requirement: Loot the Barrows chest after killing all 6 brothers.

Method: This is essentially the same as Task 24 (Complete a Barrows Brother Encounter). After killing all 6 brothers and the tunnel spawn, loot the chest. The achievement triggers when you open the chest, not when you kill the brothers.

Note: You get Barrows loot (potentially worth millions) from the chest. Even if the loot is bad, the task still completes.

Estimated Time: 15–25 minutes.

🎉 Congratulations — You've Completed All 35 Easy Tasks!

Return to the Combat Achievements interface, click the "Claim Rewards" button for the Easy tier, and collect your Ghommal's Hilt 1. The next section explains exactly what you've unlocked and how to use it.

3. Optimal Completion Order

You do NOT need to complete the 35 Easy tasks in the order listed above. In fact, completing them in the right order can save you hours of unnecessary grinding. This section presents the optimal route — the order that completes the most tasks in the least amount of time.

📋 Phase 1: Natural Completion (0 Extra Time)

These tasks will complete automatically as you play the game normally. Do NOT go out of your way to complete them — just let them happen:

TaskHow It Naturally Completes
Kill 10 / 50 / 100 MonstersNormal combat training or Slayer
Cast 50 SpellsMagic training or combat
Eat 50 Pieces of FoodAny PvM session longer than 10 minutes
Deal 100 / 500 Damage to Single MonsterFighting higher-level monsters
Kill with Melee / Ranged / Magic (10 each)Using multiple styles during training

📋 Phase 2: Quick Wins (1–2 Hours Total)

These tasks are fast and easy to complete deliberately. Do them all in one session:

⚡ Quick Win Checklist

  1. Kill a monster with all 3 styles (Task 17): 1 minute at Hill Giants
  2. Kill 10 monsters with melee (Task 14): Rock Crabs, 2 minutes
  3. Kill 10 with ranged (Task 15): Cows, 3 minutes
  4. Kill 10 with magic (Task 16): Chaos Druids, 5 minutes
  5. Complete Slayer task (Turael, Task 26): 2–10 minutes
  6. Complete Slayer task (Mazchna, Task 27): 10–30 minutes (requires 20 Combat)
  7. Kill 10 Slayer creatures (Task 28): Natural during Tasks 26–27
  8. Use Slayer item (Task 29): Banshee + Earmuffs, 5–15 minutes

📋 Phase 3: Boss Tasks (2–5 Hours Total)

These require preparation and are the "meat" of the Easy CA tier. Recommended order:

  1. Giant Mole (Task 19): Lowest requirements, safe, quick. Do 5 kills (completes Task 31 too).
  2. One Barrows brother (Task 20): Quick intro to Barrows mechanics.
  3. Full Barrows run (Tasks 24 + 35): Now that you know the mechanics, do a full run.
  4. KBD (Task 21): Requires Anti-dragon shield. Moderate difficulty.
  5. Skotizo (Task 23): Requires Catacombs access + Dark totems. Higher difficulty.
  6. Kraken (Task 22): Requires 87 Slayer. Skip temporarily if below 87.

📋 Phase 4: The Long Grind (Tasks 30 + 8 + 100 Damage)

These are the last tasks you should tackle:

  • Task 30 (5 consecutive Slayer tasks): 30–60 minutes using Turael. Do this last so you're comfortable with Slayer.
  • Task 8 (Deal 100 damage in 1 hit): May require gear upgrades. Borrow a Dragon Warhammer from a friend or use a Granite Maul spec.
  • Task 18 (Complete Slayer task with 1 style): Plan this during your normal Slayer progression.

💡 Should You Rush or Let It Happen Naturally?

For most players, the best approach is hybrid: let the easy tasks complete naturally during your first 50–100 hours of gameplay, then deliberately knock out the remaining 5–10 tasks in a focused 2-hour session. This gives you the Ghommal's Hilt 1 reward right when it becomes most useful (around level 70 Combat).

4. Ghommal's Hilt Guide — Rewards & Usage

Completing all 35 Easy Combat Achievements unlocks the Ghommal's Hilt 1 — one of the most impactful single items in the game for its accessibility. This section covers everything you need to know about this reward.

🏆 Ghommal's Hilt 1: Full Stats & Effect

PropertyValue
Damage Boost+5% to all melee, ranged, and magic attacks
RequirementsComplete all 35 Easy CA tasks (no skill/quest requirements)
SlotShield slot (can be wielded with 2h weapons using the off-hand slot trick)
Weight0 kg
Special EffectGilded Spear cosmetic upgrade available
TradeableNo (bound to account)

⚔️ How the +5% Damage Boost Works (In Detail)

The damage boost is applied as a multiplier to your max hit and your average DPS. Here's a concrete example:

📊 Damage Calculation Example

Without Ghommal's Hilt 1:
Max hit = 30 (with Dragon Scimitar + 80 Strength + Strength potion)
Average DPS = 12.5

With Ghommal's Hilt 1 (+5%):
Max hit = 30 × 1.05 = 31.5 (rounded to 32 in-game)
Average DPS = 12.5 × 1.05 = 13.1

Result: Over a 10-minute fight, you deal 5% more total damage, which means the fight ends 5% faster. At higher levels (with better gear), the difference is even more noticeable because your base DPS is higher.

The boost applies to every single attack — melee, ranged, and magic. It even works with special attacks, poison, and venom. The only thing it doesn't affect is overhead prayers (Protect prayers) and passive effects from other items.

🎨 Gilded Spear Upgrade

After obtaining Ghommal's Hilt 1, you can upgrade it to the Gilded Spear cosmetic version. This requires:

  • Completing certain Medium-tier CA tasks (unlocks the Gilded upgrade)
  • Talking to Ghommal (located in the Warrior's Guild, Burthorpe)
  • Paying a fee (usually a few hundred thousand GP)

The Gilded Spear has the same +5% damage boost but with a golden cosmetic appearance. It's purely aesthetic — no stat difference.

6. FAQ & Quick Reference Table

Can I complete Easy Combat Achievements on an Ironman account?

Yes! All 35 Easy tasks are Ironman-friendly. The only potentially tricky task is Task 22 (Kraken, requires 87 Slayer) — but you can skip it temporarily and come back later. Ironman players may need more time to gather gear, but the tasks themselves don't require any GP-only items.

Do Combat Achievement tasks need to be completed in a specific world or region?

No._tasks complete in any world, any region, any game mode (Normal, Ironman, Hardcore Ironman, Ultimate Ironman). The only exception is tasks that involve specific locations (like "Kill the Giant Mole" — you must actually go to the Giant Mole Lair).

What happens if I die while working on a Combat Achievement?

Nothing — Combat Achievements track cumulative progress. If you die, you don't lose any progress toward task completion. The only exception is tasks with specific timing requirements (like "Kill a boss without taking damage") — if you take damage, you need to start that specific attempt over, but your overall progress toward other tasks is safe.

Can I see my progress toward each task in real time?

Yes. Open the Combat Achievements interface and click the green "+" icon next to any task to "track" it. Tracked tasks display a progress bar on your game screen. You can track up to 6 tasks simultaneously.

Is the +5% damage from Ghommal's Hilt 1 noticeable?

Yes, especially over long PvM sessions. A 5% damage boost means 5% faster kills, which translates to 5% more XP per hour and 5% more loot per hour. Over a 10-hour Slayer session, that's equivalent to getting 30 free minutes of extra kills. It's not game-breaking, but it's absolutely worth getting.

Do I need to complete the tasks in order?

No. You can complete the 35 Easy tasks in any order. The game tracks which ones you've completed and automatically updates your progress toward the tier completion reward.
