💡 Quick Verdict — F2P Slayer 2026

F2P Slayer is niche but has a dedicated following. You can reach Slayer level 99 in F2P (it caps at 99, same as members), but the task list is tiny (only ~15 tasks) and the GP/X rates are far lower than members Slayer. The main reasons to do F2P Slayer: (1) you're a pure/zerker building combat stats without members, (2) you enjoy the slower pace and want a long-term F2P goal, (3) you're an Ironman who wants Slayer XP before going members. Verdict: worth it for goal-oriented F2P players; skip it if you plan to go members within 30 days.

Slayer is one of the most popular skills in OSRS because it combines combat training with variety — you never know what monster you'll be sent to fight next. In Free-to-Play, Slayer is a stripped-down version of the members skill, but it's still a complete skill that you can train from level 1 to 99. This guide covers everything F2P Slayer: which masters to use, all available tasks, the best gear for each level bracket, GP/X rates, and whether F2P Slayer is worth your time in 2026.

① 🗡️ What Is F2P Slayer?

Slayer is a members-only skill in RuneScape 3, but in Old School RuneScape, Slayer is available in Free-to-Play as well. The F2P version has a smaller task list and fewer Slayer masters, but the core mechanics are identical:

  • Visit a Slayer Master to get a task
  • Fight the assigned monsters until the task is complete
  • Return to the master for a new task and Slayer XP
  • Higher Slayer level unlocks tougher masters and better tasks

Key difference from members: F2P Slayer has roughly 20-25 available tasks (vs. 80+ for members). There are no boss tasks, no Konar, no Duradel, and no Slayer points (you earn 0 Slayer points in F2P). This means no access to unlocks like Slayer Helm, Ring of Wealth (iRotS), or Block/Skip list.

💡 Do You Earn Slayer Points in F2P?

No. Slayer points were introduced with members Slayer content. F2P Slayer masters give 0 points per task. If your goal is to unlock the Slayer Helmet or build a Block/Skip list, you must go members. This is the single biggest disadvantage of F2P Slayer.

2.2 🎯 Which Master Should You Use?

  • Brand-new accounts (total level < 200): Start with Turael. His tasks are very easy (cows, chickens, goblins) and let you learn the basics.
  • Mid-level F2P (combat 40-60): Switch to Mazchna. He assigns higher-XP tasks (Hill Giants, Moss Giants, Skeletons) that are worth your time.
  • Wilderness-capable F2P: Try Krystilia for variety, but be ready to skip tasks that send you into deep Wilderness (level 30+).

3.3 🚫 Tasks to Skip (If You Could — You Can't in F2P)

Since F2P Slayer earns 0 points, you cannot skip or block any tasks. This means you're stuck with whatever the master assigns. If you get a task you hate:

  • Talk to Turael to reset your task (he'll give you an easy replacement, but you lose the current task's XP)
  • Cancel and re-get from the same master (sometimes RNG gives you a better task)

Pro tip: If you're a member, always do your Block/Skip unlocks first, then come back to F2P Slayer. In F2P, you're at the mercy of RNG.

5.2 🎯 Who Should (and Shouldn't) Do F2P Slayer

✅ Do F2P Slayer If...
  • You're a F2P Pure (e.g., 1 Defence, 60 Attack, 85 Strength) and want Slayer XP without members
  • You're an Ironman building early combat stats and want Slayer rewards (rune drops, gems) before going members
  • You enjoy the slow-paced, old-school OSRS experience and don't care about optimal XP/hr
  • You want a long-term F2P goal (Slayer 99 takes ~1,500 hours in F2P)
❌ Skip F2P Slayer If...
  • You plan to go members within 30 days — just wait and do members Slayer instead
  • You care about Slayer points (Helmet, Block/Skip list) — you earn 0 in F2P
  • You want fast combat XP — Slayer in F2P is slower than NPC farming (Hill Giants, etc.)
  • You hate RNG task assignment — without points, you can't skip bad tasks

⑥ 💡 F2P Slayer Pro Tips

  • Use Turael to reset bad tasks: If Mazchna gives you a task you can't do (e.g., Ice Giants at combat 45), talk to Turael in Burthorpe. He'll give you a new (easy) task, letting you skip the undesirable one.
  • Bank Big Bones: Hill Giants drop Big Bones (always). In F2P, you can't offer bones at a guild altar (members feature), but you can sell them on the GE for ~200-300 GP each. That's an extra ~20K-30K GP per task.
  • Wilderness tasks = high risk, high reward: Moss Giants and Ice Giants drop rare herbs (ranarr, snapdragon). If you do these tasks, bring only 3-valuable items (rune scimitar, rune armor, amulet of power) and be ready to log out if a PKer appears.
  • Magic level matters: If you plan to safespot with Magic, get to 31 Magic (Fire Strike) or 52 Magic (Fire Blast) for better DPS. F2P has no Magic training shortcuts — it's slow.
  • Quest for early Slayer XP: Witch's Potion (give 325 Slayer XP) and Demon Slayer (give 1,000 Slayer XP) are free and fast. Do them before visiting a Slayer master.

⑦ ❓ FAQ — F2P Slayer Guide 2026

Q: Can I get a Slayer Helmet in F2P?

A: No. The Slayer Helmet requires 400 Slayer points to unlock (learned from a Slayer master). You earn 0 points per task in F2P. To get the helmet, you must go members and complete enough tasks to earn 400 points (roughly 160-200 tasks with the best point-per-task rate).

Q: Which F2P Slayer master gives the best XP per hour?

A: Mazchna (Canifis) gives the highest-XP tasks in F2P. His assignments (Hill Giants, Moss Giants, Ice Giants) can yield 22K-42K combat XP per hour, plus Slayer XP. Turael is easier but gives much lower XP (~5K-15K/hr).

Q: Can I use Protected Combat (Prayer) in F2P Slayer?

A: Yes, but F2P has very limited Prayer training. You can bury Big Bones (drains 1 Prayer point per bone) or offer bones at the Altars in Lumbridge Church (free, but slow). Without members bones (Dragon Bones, Frost Dragons), Prayer leveling in F2P is extremely slow. Most F2P Slayer players fight with 0 Prayer bonus.

Q: Do F2P Slayer tasks count toward the Combat Achievement system?

A: Yes! Combat Achievements (CA) were released in 2023 and apply to both F2P and members content. Several CA tasks can be completed in F2P (e.g., "Kill X monster without taking damage", "Kill X monster in Y seconds"). If you're an F2P CA hunter, Slayer tasks are a great way to progress.

Q: Is F2P Slayer good for Ironmen?

A: Moderately. F2P Slayer gives you rune drops (scimitars, platebodies) and gems (ruby, diamond) that Ironmen need. However, the lack of Slayer points means you can't unlock the Block/Skip list, which is critical for Ironmen who want to optimize their task list. Verdict: do some F2P Slayer for early resources, but go members ASAP for the full Slayer experience.

Q: Can I farm GP with F2P Slayer?

A: Kind of. The best GP tasks are Moss Giants (ranarr weeds, ~5K GP each) and Ice Giants (blood runes, ~200 GP each). But you need to be in the Wilderness, which carries PvP risk. If you want safe GP farming, Non-Slayer Hill Giant farming (Edgeville Dungeon, safe) is better for consistent income.

Q: What's the fastest way to reach Slayer level 99 in F2P?

A: There is no "fast" way — F2P Slayer is inherently slow. Rough estimates: Slayer 1-99 takes ~1,500-2,000 hours in F2P (vs. ~800-1,000 hours in members). The bottleneck is task variety and lack of high-XP assignments. If your goal is 99 Slayer, go members and use Konar (best XP rates).

🎯 Final Tips — F2P Slayer in 2026

  • Don't expect members Slayer rewards: No helmet, no points, no Warriors' Guild access. F2P Slayer is its own thing.
  • Join the F2P Slayer Discord: There's a small but active community of F2P Slayer players who share tips and task RNG stories.
  • Consider going members after Slayer 50: At Slayer 50, you've seen most F2P tasks. The XP rates don't improve much beyond this point. If you're enjoying Slayer, go members and never look back.
  • Have fun with the journey: F2P Slayer is a marathon, not a sprint. Enjoy the slower pace, the simpler gear, and the lack of complex mechanics. It's OSRS in its purest form.
