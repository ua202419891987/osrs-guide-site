🤖 OSRS Mobile Crashing & Black Screen on Android — Fix Guide 2026

Is the Old School RuneScape app freezing, force-closing, or stuck on a black screen every time you log in? This guide walks through the real, working fixes for OSRS Mobile on Android in 2026 — from the 30-second cache clear that solves most cases to 32-bit device limits and GPU driver bugs.

📅 Updated: July 2026 — Troubleshooting Guide · 12 min read · Level: Novice

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Clear the in-app cache first — it fixes ~90% of black-screen/force-close cases in 30 seconds and never touches your account.
  • 📌 Keep 1–2 GB free — a full phone is the #2 cause of mid-game freezes and crashes.
  • 📌 32-bit Android phones are no longer supported — the app is 64-bit only and will refuse to run.
  • 📌 GPU driver bugs cause black screens on some devices; updating the driver or lowering graphics settings helps.
  • 📌 A clean reinstall (your account is server-side, so nothing is lost) repairs corrupted installs.

🔍 1. Why OSRS Mobile Crashes or Black-Screens on Android

Before diving into fixes, it helps to know what is actually happening. The OSRS Mobile client is Jagex's official Android app (downloadable from Google Play). Unlike the desktop client, it runs inside a constrained mobile environment where storage, memory, and GPU drivers behave very differently from a PC.

Most "crashes" fall into one of four buckets:

🧩 The Four Root Causes

  • Corrupted cache — the locally downloaded game assets get out of sync and the app hangs on a black screen at launch.
  • Resource starvation — low free storage or low RAM makes the OS kill the app for memory.
  • Device incompatibility — a 32-bit processor or an unsupported Android version.
  • Driver / OS conflict — a buggy GPU driver or an aggressive battery saver terminating the process.

The good news: every one of these has a fix, and you almost never lose progress — your character, bank, and membership all live on Jagex's servers, not on your phone.

💡 Rule of Thumb

Always try Fix 1 (clear cache) before anything else. It is reversible, takes under a minute, and resolves the majority of black-screen reports on the official OSRS Mobile support threads.

🧹 2. Fix 1 — Clear the App Cache (Fastest Fix, 90% of Cases)

A corrupted or partially-downloaded cache is the single most common reason OSRS Mobile shows a black screen and immediately force-closes. Clearing it forces the app to re-download clean assets on next launch.

Option A — Clear Cache Inside the App (Best)

If you can get past the black screen even briefly, or you previously enabled it:

  1. Open OSRS Mobile.
  2. Tap the menu (☰) button in the top-left corner.
  3. Go to Settings → scroll to the bottom.
  4. Tap Clear cache (sometimes labeled "Storage" → "Clear cache").
  5. Confirm, then fully close and reopen the app.

Option B — Clear Cache From Android Settings

  1. Open your phone's Settings → Apps (or "Apps & notifications").
  2. Find OSRS Mobile in the list.
  3. Tap Storage & cache → Clear cache.
  4. Do not tap "Clear storage/data" unless you intend a full reset — cache only is enough.
⚠️ Cache vs. Data

Only clear cache. Clearing storage/data logs you out and removes local settings, though your account itself is safe. Cache-only is the safe, targeted fix.

💾 3. Fix 2 — Free Up Storage Space

OSRS Mobile downloads a large cache of game assets (maps, models, textures) that can grow past several gigabytes over time. When your phone's storage is nearly full, the app cannot write temporary files, and it freezes or crashes — often right after you enter a busy area like the Grand Exchange or a bank.

Jagex recommends keeping at least 1–2 GB of free space. To check and free space:

🚀 Quick Space-Clearing Steps

  • Open Settings → Storage and see how much is free.
  • Use Files by Google or your manufacturer's cleaner to remove junk and duplicate files.
  • Offload or uninstall apps you no longer use.
  • Move photos/videos to cloud storage or an SD card (note: the OSRS cache itself must stay on internal storage).
  • After freeing space, clear the cache (Fix 1) and relaunch.

If you are constantly low on space, consider lowering the in-game Graphics settings (Fix 3) so the client requests smaller, lighter assets.

🎮 4. Fix 3 — GPU & Graphics Driver Issues

A black screen that appears after a game update — especially right after you tap "Play" — is frequently a GPU driver bug. Certain Android chipsets (notably some older Adreno, Mali, and PowerVR GPUs) have rendering bugs that the OSRS renderer trips over.

Step 1 — Update the GPU Driver

On many modern Android phones (Android 13+) GPU drivers can be updated independently of the OS:

  • Open the Google Play Store → tap your profile → Settings → About → Update Play Store.
  • Some devices (e.g., select Pixel and Snapdragon-powered phones) expose a "Update GPU driver" option under Settings → About phone → GPU driver or via a dedicated "Graphics Driver" app from the Play Store.
  • Install any available driver update and reboot.

Step 2 — Lower In-Game Graphics

If you cannot update the driver, reduce the rendering load:

  • Open OSRS Mobile → menu (☰) → Settings → Graphics.
  • Set Overall quality to Low or Medium.
  • Turn off "GPU" rendering if a toggle is present, or disable advanced lighting/shadow options.
  • Reduce screen resolution / render scale if available.
💡 Still Black After a Driver Update?

Some devices simply have an incompatible GPU stack. As a temporary workaround, keep graphics on Low and avoid crowded worlds (use a quieter world like 301–310 for training). A clean reinstall (Fix 5) also re-downloads a known-good asset set.

📱 5. Fix 4 — 32-Bit Devices Are No Longer Supported

Jagex ended support for 32-bit Android devices. The current OSRS Mobile app is built as a 64-bit-only application. If your phone has a 32-bit processor, the Play Store will show "Your device isn't compatible with this version" and the app will not install or run.

Common 32-bit-only devices include many budget phones sold before ~2015, certain MediaTek-powered models, and some older tablets. You can check your architecture:

🔧 How to Check If Your Phone Is 64-Bit

  • Install a free app like "Device Info HW" or "Droid Hardware Info" from the Play Store.
  • Look for the CPU architecture / ABI field.
  • If it says arm64-v8a or x86_64 → you are 64-bit and supported. ✅
  • If it says armeabi-v7a or x86 (32-bit) → you are 32-bit and the app will not run. ❌
🪫 Your Options on a 32-Bit Phone

There is no setting to force 32-bit support — it is a hard requirement of the current client. Your account is safe; you can keep playing on a 64-bit phone, a tablet, or the desktop client (including the free RuneLite client on PC). Consider this the time to upgrade the device if mobile play matters to you.

♻️ 6. Fix 5 — Reinstall the App Cleanly

If the install itself is corrupted (often after an interrupted update or a phone restore), a clean reinstall is the most reliable repair. Because your account lives on Jagex's servers, reinstalling does not delete your character, bank, or membership.

Clean Reinstall Steps

  1. Make sure you know your Jagex account email and password (you will need to log in again).
  2. On your phone, long-press OSRS Mobile → Uninstall (or Settings → Apps → OSRS Mobile → Uninstall).
  3. Open the Google Play Store, search "Old School RuneScape" by Jagex, and reinstall.
  4. Open the app and log in with your Jagex account.
  5. Let the cache fully download on a stable Wi-Fi connection before playing.
🔐 Before You Uninstall

If you previously linked the app through a third-party login, confirm your Jagex account is set up with a working email + password and (strongly recommended) two-factor authentication. See our account security guide in the Related Guides below.

🛡️ 7. Fix 6 — Boot Into Safe Mode (Rule Out App Conflicts)

Some third-party apps — overlay tools, screen recorders, battery savers, "game boosters," or malware cleaners — interfere with OSRS Mobile and cause crashes. Safe Mode temporarily disables all downloaded apps so you can test the client in isolation.

Enter Safe Mode (varies slightly by phone)

  • Press and hold the power button until the power menu appears.
  • Long-press "Power off" until "Reboot to safe mode" appears, then tap it.
  • Once in Safe Mode (text shows in the corner), open OSRS Mobile.

If the app runs fine in Safe Mode, a third-party app is the culprit. Reboot back to normal mode, then uninstall recently added or suspicious apps one by one until the crashes stop. Screen-recording and overlay apps are the most common offenders.

🔋 8. Fix 7 — Disable Battery Optimization

Android's battery optimization (and "Adaptive Battery") can silently kill background apps to save power. If OSRS Mobile closes a few minutes after you switch away, or stutters then crashes, battery management is likely the cause.

🚫 Exclude OSRS Mobile From Battery Optimization

  • Open Settings → Battery → Battery optimization (or "Apps → OSRS Mobile → Battery").
  • Find OSRS Mobile and set it to "Don't optimize" / "Unrestricted".
  • Disable "Adaptive Battery" impact for the app if the option exists.
  • Turn off any manufacturer "game booster" or "memory cleaner" that auto-closes games.
💡 Manufacturer Quirks

Samsung (Device Care), Xiaomi (MIUI), Huawei (AppGallery battery), and OnePlus all have aggressive extra battery managers. Search "your phone model + don't kill background apps" if the standard toggle does not help — each maker buries the setting in a different menu.

❓ 10. Android Crash FAQs

Will clearing the cache delete my account, bank, or membership?

No. Cache is only the locally downloaded game assets on your phone. Your character, bank, and membership are stored on Jagex's servers and tied to your Jagex account. After clearing the cache the app simply re-downloads the assets and you log back in normally.

My phone is 32-bit — is there any way to run OSRS Mobile?

No setting or workaround enables 32-bit support; the current client is 64-bit only. Your account is unaffected — play on a 64-bit phone/tablet or the desktop client (including the free RuneLite client on PC). Treat this as a device-upgrade situation rather than a fixable app bug.

The Play Store says "your device isn't compatible" — why?

This usually means your device is 32-bit, runs an Android version below the minimum, or has an unsupported GPU. Check your architecture (Fix 4) and Android version (Fix 2 table). If both meet requirements, make sure Google Play Services is up to date and try again.

OSRS Mobile only crashes in crowded areas (Grand Exchange, banks, world events) — why?

Crowded areas render many players and overhead texts at once, spiking GPU and memory use. This points to either low free storage, a GPU driver bug (Fix 3), or an underpowered device. Free storage, lower graphics to Low, and play on a quieter world to reduce the load.

I reinstalled and now it asks me to log in — did I lose my account?

No. Reinstalling logs you out of the local app, but the account is server-side. Log in with your Jagex account email and password. If you cannot remember the login, recover it through the Jagex account recovery flow on a browser before reinstalling again.
