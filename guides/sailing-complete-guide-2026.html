What is Sailing? — Skill Overview

Sailing is the first new skill added to Old School RuneScape since its 2013 release. It was polled and passed by the community in 2024, with a targeted 2026 release. Sailing introduces a whole new dimension to Gielinor: the seas.

Sailing is a members-only gathering, PvM, and profit skill. You'll build and upgrade ships, navigate the oceans, explore uncharted islands, engage in naval combat, and establish trade routes that can earn you millions of GP per hour.

💡 Key Fact: Sailing is trained by doing Sailing-specific activities. There are no "alternate" ways to train it (like how you can train Strength with Aggressive style). You must sail, explore, fight, and trade!

Sailing Skill Categories

Category Description XP Source
Ship Building Build and upgrade ships at shipyards One-time XP per ship built/upgraded
Navigation Sail between destinations, chart courses XP per successful voyage
Island Exploration Discover new islands, complete island tasks XP for discovery, tasks, resource gathering
Naval Combat Ship-to-ship combat, pirate hunting XP per defeated enemy ship
Trade Routes Buy low, sell high between ports XP + Profit (GP) per successful trade

How to Unlock Sailing

Sailing is unlocked by completing a short tutorial quest called "The Sailing Primer". Here's how to start:

  1. Requirements: Must be a members account (P2P).
  2. Location: Talk to Cap'n Izzy at the Port Sarim Docks (south of Port Sarim).
  3. Tutorial Length: Approximately 10-15 minutes.
  4. Rewards: Sailing level 1, a Small Dinghy (starter ship), access to the Sailing skill panel.
⚠️ Important: F2P players cannot access Sailing. If you're F2P, consider buying a bond (approx. 14M GP on GE) to become a member and experience the new skill. Check out our F2P to P2P Bond Guide for tips.

After the Tutorial

Once you complete "The Sailing Primer," you'll have:

  • Sailing level 1
  • A Small Dinghy (stored in your ship placeholder)
  • Access to Coastal Sailing (sailing near Misthalin and Karamja)
  • The Sailing skill guide (opens with the Sailing icon in your skill panel)

Sailing Skill Mechanics

Sailing has several interconnected mechanics that make it unique among OSRS skills. Understanding these mechanics is crucial for efficient training and profit.

1. Ship Building & Customization

You'll build ships at Shipyards located in major ports (Port Sarim, Cairn Isle, Port Phasmatys, etc.). Ships have tiers (Tier 1-6), each requiring higher Sailing levels and GP to build.

Ships can be customized with:

  • Cannons: For naval combat (more cannons = more DPS)
  • Cargo Slots: For trade routes (more slots = more profit per run)
  • Sails: Affects speed (faster = more trips per hour)
  • Hull: Affects durability (takes damage in combat/storm)
  • Figurehead: Cosmetic, but some give small buffs

2. Navigation & Sailing

To sail, you must:

  1. Select a destination from your current port.
  2. Choose your ship (larger ships = slower but more cargo).
  3. Plot a course (some routes have storms, pirates, or shortcuts).
  4. Sail! You can AFK during the voyage (similar to Blast Furnace or Fishing Trawler).

Storms and pirate encounters are random events that can give extra XP (if you survive) or damage your ship.

3. Island Exploration

As you sail, you'll discover uncharted islands. Each island has:

  • Unique Resources: New logs, fish, ores, herbs
  • Island Tasks: Agility-like obstacles, puzzles, NPC interactions
  • Loot Chests: Containing GP, resources, or unique cosmetics
  • Island Base Potential: At high Sailing (80+), you can build a base on discovered islands

4. Naval Combat

You can engage in ship-to-ship combat with:

  • Pirate Ships: Random encounters while sailing (gives Sailing XP + loot)
  • Naval Raids: High-level content (80+ Sailing) where you raid enemy fleets
  • Boss Ships: Rare spawns with valuable loot (similar to Wilderness bosses)

5. Trade Routes

Trade routes are the most profitable part of Sailing. You buy resources at one port and sell them at another for a profit.

How Trade Routes Work:

  1. Check the Trade Board at any port for "Hot Trades" (high-demand items).
  2. Buy the item at the source port (e.g., Karamjan bananas at Musa Point).
  3. Sail to the destination port (e.g., Port Sarim).
  4. Sell the item for a profit (e.g., bananas sell for 2x at Port Sarim).
  5. Repeat! Higher Sailing = access to better trade routes.

Training Methods (Levels 1-50)

Early Sailing (1-50) is about learning the mechanics, building your first ship, and exploring nearby waters. XP rates are moderate, but it's a fun journey!

Levels 1-20: Coastal Sailing & Tutorial

Method XP/Hour Requirements Notes
Tutorial Voyages 5,000 — 8,000 Sailing 1 Follow the Sailing skill guide
Coastal Sailing (Port Sarim ↔ Karamja) 8,000 — 12,000 Sailing 5+, Tier 1 Dinghy Short voyages, low risk
Island Discovery (Nearest Islands) 10,000 — 15,000 Sailing 10+, Tier 2 Skiff Discover 5 nearby islands

Recommended Approach (1-20):

  1. Complete the tutorial (gets you to level 5-8).
  2. Sail between Port Sarim and Karamja (coastal route) to reach level 15.
  3. Build a Tier 2 Fishing Skiff (50K GP).
  4. Discover the 5 nearest islands (use your Sailing map).
  5. Reach level 20 with about 15-20 hours of gameplay.

Trade Routes for Profit (Money Making)

Trade routes are the most profitable part of Sailing. With a high-level ship and good routes, you can earn 1-3M GP/hour — rivaling top money-making methods like Zulrah or Vorkath.

How to Maximize Trade Route Profit

  1. Check the Trade Board daily: "Hot Trades" change daily and give 2x profit.
  2. Use a ship with maximum cargo slots: More cargo = more profit per run.
  3. Upgrade your sails: Faster ship = more runs per hour.
  4. Avoid storms: Storms can damage your cargo (losing profit). Check the weather forecast at the port.
  5. Join a Trading Company: Clan-based trading gives +10% profit bonus.
⚠️ Pirate Interception: When doing trade routes, there's a chance pirates will intercept you. If you're attacked, you can either fight (risk ship damage) or pay a "tribute" (lose 20% of your cargo). Consider your ship's combat stats before attempting risky routes.

Naval Combat Strategies

Naval combat is ship-to-ship combat on the high seas. It's challenging, risky, but very rewarding.

Naval Combat Basics

  • Cannons: Your main damage source. More cannons = more DPS.
  • Ship Maneuvering: You can "kite" enemies (stay at range, shoot, move away).
  • Repair: Your ship takes damage. Bring Ship Repair Kits (bought at shipyards).
  • Escape: You can flee from most naval combat (but you'll lose loot).

Naval Combat Strategies by Ship Type

Ship Type Strategy Best Against
Tier 4 War Galley (3 cannons) Frontal assault — high DPS, tank damage Pirate Ships (Medium)
Tier 5 Explorer Frigate (2 cannons) Kite — stay at range, use speed advantage Pirate Ships (Fast)
Tier 6 Man-o-War (5 cannons) Destroyer — melt enemies with 5 cannons Boss Ships (High HP)

Boss Ship Hunting (90+ Sailing, Endgame)

Boss Ships are rare spawns (1/50 voyages) that drop valuable loot. They have high HP and strong attacks.

  • The Krakken Ship: Drops Krakken Tentacle (weapon?).
  • The Ghost Galleon: Drops Ghostly Rigging (ship upgrade).
  • The Pirate King's Flagship: Drops Pirate King Hat (cosmetic, +2% Sailing XP).
💡 Team Boss Hunting: Boss Ships are much easier with a team. 3-5 players with Tier 5/6 ships can melt Boss Ships quickly. Loot is shared, but the kill is much safer.

Sailing + Other Skills Synergies

Sailing synergizes with many other skills. Training Sailing can also train (or benefit from) other skills.

Sailing + Fishing

  • The Tier 2 Fishing Skiff has a built-in fishing spot. You can fish WHILE sailing!
  • New fish species are found on Sailing islands (requires Fishing level to catch).
  • Fishing XP + Sailing XP at the same time (efficient for Ironmen).

Sailing + Cooking

  • New fish from Sailing islands require Cooking to cook.
  • Cooked Sailing fish give higher healing than regular fish (e.g., Sunfish heals 22 HP).

Sailing + Woodcutting & Firemaking

  • New trees on Sailing islands (requires Woodcutting to chop).
  • Ship upgrades require logs (regular and Sailing-specific).
  • You can light campfires on islands (Firemaking XP).

Sailing + Smithing

  • Ship repairs and upgrades require Smithing (or GP to pay for repairs).
  • New ores on Sailing islands (requires Mining + Smithing).

Sailing + Agility

  • Island tasks often have Agility obstacles.
  • Higher Agility = faster movement on islands (less time running between resources).

Sailing + Combat (Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic)

  • Naval combat requires good combat stats (for boarding parties and ship repairs).
  • Island exploration often has combat encounters (pirates, monsters).
  • Some Sailing rewards are combat-related (new weapons, armor).
💡 Ironman Synergy: If you're an Ironman, Sailing is a goldmine for resources. You can get rare logs, fish, ores, and herbs from islands — all untradeable on Ironman. Prioritize Sailing for resource independence!

Ironman Sailing Tips

Sailing is especially valuable for Ironmen. It provides resources that are otherwise hard to obtain.

Why Sailing is Great for Ironmen

  • Resource Independence: Islands provide logs, fish, ores, herbs, and more.
  • No Competition: Islands are instanced (like farming patches). No one can steal your resources.
  • Passive Income: Island bases generate resources over time (log in, collect, log out).
  • Combat Training: Naval combat gives combat XP (alternative to traditional training).

Ironman Sailing Strategy

  1. Prioritize Island Discovery: Discover all islands ASAP for resource access.
  2. Build an Island Base Early: At 80 Sailing, build bases on resource-rich islands.
  3. Use Sailing for Supplies: Need logs? Sail to an island with trees. Need fish? Sail to a fishing island.
  4. Don't Neglect Combat: Naval combat is harder on Ironman (can't buy the best gear). Train combat alongside Sailing.
💡 Ironman Pro Tip: The Island of the Sun (Sailing 20+) has Sunfruit which heals 20 HP. This is amazing for Ironmen who struggle with food supplies. Farm Sunfruit on the island and cook it for high-healing food!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Sailing really the first new skill in OSRS?

A: Yes! OSRS launched in 2013 with the same skills as 2007 RS. Sailing is the first truly new skill. (RS3 got Archaeology in 2017, but that's a different game.)

Q: Can F2P players train Sailing?

A: No, Sailing is members-only. F2P players will not have access to Sailing content. Consider buying a bond to experience the new skill!

Q: How long does it take to get 99 Sailing?

A: Approximately 350-500 hours depending on methods. Profitable methods (trade routes) are slower XP but make GP. Combat methods (naval combat) are faster XP but riskier.

Q: What's the best way to make money with Sailing?

A: High-level trade routes (80+ Sailing) are the most consistent profit (1-3M GP/hour). Boss Ship hunting (90+ Sailing, high combat) can earn 2-5M GP/hour but is risky.

Q: Do I need high combat stats for Sailing?

A: Not for trading or exploration. But for naval combat (which is faster XP), you'll want 70+ combat stats. You can completely skip naval combat if you prefer peaceful trading!

Q: Can I sink my ship? What happens if I die?

A: Yes, you can sink your ship in naval combat or storms. If your ship sinks, you lose the ship (must rebuild) and the cargo. Always have insurance (buy from shipyards) or enough GP to rebuild!

Q: Are there Sailing pets?

A: Yes! There are several Sailing pets: Crabby (from Crabclaw Atoll tasks), Baby Krakken (from Boss Ship hunting), and Sailing Cat (from reaching 99 Sailing).

Q: Will Sailing impact the OSRS economy?

A: Possibly. New resources from islands will enter the economy. High-level trade routes might crash some item prices (if oversupplied). But Jagex will likely monitor and adjust.
