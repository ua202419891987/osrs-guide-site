🏃 Why Speedrun Quests in OSRS?

If you're starting a new account in OSRS in 2026, the single most efficient thing you can do is follow a structured OSRS quest order. Quests in Old School RuneScape are not just lore — they are the fastest way to skip early-game grinds and unlock game-changing content. While many new players spend their first 50 hours chopping trees and killing cows, smart players complete a OSRS quest speedrun route that launches them straight into mid-game content.

The philosophy is simple: quests give you experience lamps, item unlocks, transportation networks, and access to bosses. By completing the right OSRS best quests to do first, you can achieve in 10 hours what takes most players 100+ hours of traditional skilling.

⚡ Quick Verdict

The fastest OSRS quest route to members: Complete Cook's Assistant → Sheep Shearer → Romeo & Juliet → Prince Ali Rescue → Dragon Slayer in F2P, then immediately rush Waterfall Quest → Tree Gnome Village → Fight Arena → The Grand Tree → Lost City as a member. This route gets you 40+ Attack, Dragon Scimitar access, and teleport unlocks in under 8 hours.

① Why Quest XP Beats Skilling

Early-game skilling is painfully slow. Cutting regular trees or mining tin and copper earns you maybe 10–15 XP per action. A single quest like Waterfall Quest gives you 13,750 Attack XP and 13,750 Strength XP — that's essentially level 30 Attack and 30 Strength from one 15-minute quest. There is no faster way to start your account. Quests also unlock content that stays relevant forever. Fairy rings, spirit trees, teleport spells, and barrows gloves are permanent unlocks that make every future hour of gameplay faster and more profitable.

💡 Pro Tip: Always complete quests in batches. Group quests by location and requirements. For example, do Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree, and Monkey Madness I back-to-back since they share quest lines and locations. This saves hours of running around Gielinor.

② What This Guide Covers

This guide provides a complete OSRS quest speedrun route broken into four phases: F2P preparation, Day 1 member power spike, Day 2-3 unlocking everything, and money-making quests. Each section includes the exact quest order, requirements, rewards, and priority level so you know exactly what to do and when. By the end of this route, you'll have barrows gloves, fairy ring transportation, a dragon scimitar, and access to OSRS's best money-making methods.

③ What You Need Before Starting

Before following this quest order, make sure you have completed Tutorial Island. If you're a new player, Tutorial Island takes about 15 minutes and teaches you the basic controls. For F2P quests, you need no prior skills. For the member portion, you should ideally have 10 Gold for basic transportation and some food for the early combat quests. That's it — this route is designed for fresh accounts with nothing.

🗺️ F2P Quest Order — The Quick Path to Members

Before you can access members content, you need to complete Dragon Slayer as a free-to-play player. Dragon Slayer is a requirement for several important members quests and is the gatekeeper quest that proves you're ready for membership. This F2P quest order gets you to Dragon Slayer completion as fast as humanly possible.

① Cook's Assistant

Requirements: None | Length: 3 minutes | Reward: 300 Cooking XP, access to Cooking skill | Priority: ★★★★★

Cook's Assistant is the first quest every account should complete. Talk to the Cook in Lumbridge Castle kitchen. He needs flour, milk, and an egg. Milk is in the dairy churn north of Lumbridge castle. Eggs are in the chicken coop east of Lumbridge. Flour requires you to pick wheat in the field east of Lumbridge, use a hopper in the mill, then collect the flour. This takes maybe 3 minutes and gives you 300 Cooking XP for free. It also unlocks the Cooking skill, which is useful throughout your account's life.

💡 Pro Tip: While gathering items for Cook's Assistant, also pick up the 10 Gold coins from the upstairs room in Lumbridge Castle. You'll need gold for transportation later in the quest order.

② Sheep Shearer

Requirements: None | Length: 2 minutes | Reward: 150 Crafting XP | Priority: ★★★★★

Talk to Fred the Farmer in Falador (north of the cow fields). He needs 20 balls of wool. Shears are available in Fred's house. Shear 20 sheep in the field outside his house, then use a spinning wheel (located in Fred's house or Lumbridge castle basement) to turn the wool into balls of wool. This quest takes about 2 minutes and gives free Crafting XP. It's also a requirement for a future member quest.

③ Romeo & Juliet

Requirements: None | Length: 8 minutes | Reward: 1 Quest Point, 5 Gold | Priority: ★★★★☆

Start this quest in Varrock Square. Talk to Romeo, then Juliet (in her house northwest of Varrock Square), then run back and forth delivering messages. The quest involves some backtracking, but it only takes about 8 minutes. The main reason to do this quest is the 1 Quest Point, which helps you meet Dragon Slayer's Quest Point requirement. It's tedious but quick.

④ Prince Ali Rescue

Requirements: None | Length: 10 minutes | Reward: 1 Quest Point, 3 Gold | Priority: ★★★★★

Start at the Al Kharid palace. You need to free Prince Ali from the jail cell. The quest involves getting a blonde wig (kill a guard east of the prison), a rope (available in Lumbridge general store), a pink skirt (Varrock clothing shop), a beer, and a key. The key is the tricky part — you need to buy it from Osman for some Gold. This quest provides a free Quest Point and is required for several members quests down the line.

⑤ Dragon Slayer

Requirements: 32 Quest Points, 23 Defense (can be skipped with lamps), ability to defeat Elvarg | Length: 45 minutes | Reward: 18,650 Strength XP, 8,400 Defense XP, ability to wear rune platebody, access to Crandor | Priority: ★★★★★

Dragon Slayer is the culminating quest of free-to-play. It's the hardest F2P quest and the one that requires the most preparation. You need 32 Quest Points, which you'll have from completing the earlier quests plus a few F2P miniquests. You also need 23 Defense or you can use the XP lamps from earlier quests to reach it. The quest involves navigating the Melzar's Maze beneath Varrock, sailing to Crandor, and ultimately facing Elvarg — a level 83 dragon.

For Elvarg, bring the best gear you can afford. Use a strength potion if available. The key strategy is to use an anti-dragon shield (from the Duke of Lumbridge), which blocks most of her dragonfire. Without it, she can hit 50+ through your prayer. Bring at least 3 pieces of food and a weapon. If you die, you can try again — just reclaim your items from Death.

🎯 Dragon Slayer Strategy

Gear: Full rune or adamant armor, rune scimitar, anti-dragon shield, strength amulet.
Inventory: 3 pieces of swordfish or better, 1 strength potion (4).
Fight: Protect from Magic prayer if available. Otherwise, rely on the anti-dragon shield. Attack with slash style. Eat when below 15 HP.
After: Congratulations — you can now wear rune platebodies and access Crandor. You're ready for membership!

🎯 Day 1 Member Quests — Power Spike

Once you activate your membership, the first day is all about maximizing your combat stats through quest rewards. These are the best OSRS quests to do first as a member. They require minimal effort but give massive XP rewards that skip hours of early combat training.

① Waterfall Quest

Requirements: None | Length: 15 minutes | Reward: 13,750 Attack XP, 13,750 Strength XP | Priority: ★★★★★

Waterfall Quest is the single most important quest for a new members account. It requires zero skills and takes only 15 minutes, yet it gives enough XP to take your Attack and Strength from level 1 to level 30 each. To start, talk to Almer (west of Baxtorian Falls) and read the book on the table. The quest involves navigating the Waterfall Dungeon, which has level 50+ enemies, so use the safe spots shown in guides. The final reward is two XP lamps applied to Attack and Strength.

Route: Start at the Baxtorian Falls, which is just east of the Tree Gnome Stronghold. If you haven't unlocked teleports yet, use the Grand Exchange teleport and run northeast. The entire quest can be completed in under 15 minutes with a guide open.

② Tree Gnome Village

Requirements: None | Length: 20 minutes | Reward: 11,450 Attack XP, access to Spirit Trees | Priority: ★★★★★

After Waterfall, head straight to Tree Gnome Village. Start by talking to the King in the Tree Gnome Stronghold's grand tree. The quest involves navigating a maze (the Tree Gnome Village maze), which contains level 50+ gnomes. The maze is straightforward if you follow a guide — just bring food and watch for the level 55 Khazard soldiers. The reward gives you 11,450 Attack XP, pushing you toward level 40 Attack for the Dragon Scimitar. More importantly, it unlocks Spirit Trees, which provide free teleportation between major cities.

③ Fight Arena

Requirements: None | Length: 15 minutes | Reward: 12,175 Attack XP, 2,000 Gold | Priority: ★★★★★

Fight Arena takes place near the Tree Gnome Stronghold, so knock it out while you're in the area. Talk to General Khazard at the Fight Arena entrance. The quest involves fighting in several arena battles against progressively harder opponents. You can safespot most fights by using ranged or magic from behind obstacles. The reward gives another 12,175 Attack XP, which combined with Waterfall and Tree Gnome Village brings you close to 40 Attack.

④ The Grand Tree

Requirements: 25 Agility | Length: 25 minutes | Reward: 18,400 Attack XP, 2,150 Agility XP, access to Gliders | Priority: ★★★★★

The Grand Tree is the final quest in the Gnome quest line and requires 25 Agility. If you don't have 25 Agility, spend 30 minutes at the Gnome Stronghold agility course — it's the best early Agility training and gets you to 25 quickly. The quest involves climbing through the Grand Tree, fighting demons, and solving puzzles. The reward includes 18,400 Attack XP (enough to push you past 40 Attack), 2,150 Agility XP, and most importantly, access to Gnome Gliders. Gliders give you free teleportation to several key locations across the map.

💡 Pro Tip: After completing all four gnome quests, you should have 40+ Attack. Now is the perfect time to buy and equip a Dragon Scimitar — the best weapon you can wield at this stage. Head to the Grand Tree and buy one from the Scimitar shop for 100K Gold (or save up by selling cowhides).

⑤ Lost City

Requirements: 31 Crafting, 36 Woodcutting | Length: 20 minutes | Reward: Access to Zanaris, ability to wield Dragon Dagger (p++) | Priority: ★★★★☆

Lost City requires 31 Crafting and 36 Woodcutting. You should get these from quest rewards and a bit of training. The quest starts in Lumbridge Swamp and involves finding the secret entrance to the Lost City of Zanaris. The quest rewards access to the cosmic plane, which is essential for many activities. More importantly, it unlocks the ability to use a Dragon Dagger (p++) with poison, which is excellent for special attacks in PvM and PvP.

⚡ Day 2-3 Member Quests — Unlock Everything

With your combat stats established, days 2 and 3 are about unlocking the game's best transportation, gear, and content. These OSRS best quests to do first after the power spike will set you up permanently with the account upgrades that last forever.

① Monkey Madness I

Requirements: Completion of Tree Gnome Village and The Grand Tree | Length: 1.5 hours | Reward: 20,000 Attack XP, 20,000 Strength XP, ability to wield Dragon Scimitar | Priority: ★★★★★

Monkey Madness I is the most important member quest for melee builds. It requires the completion of Tree Gnome Village and The Grand Tree. The quest takes you through Ape Atoll, where you must complete a stealth section, solve puzzles, and defeat the level 86 Jungle Demon at the end. The demon can be safespotted with ranged or magic. The main reward is the ability to wield the Dragon Scimitar, which is the best one-handed melee weapon until level 75 Attack. The 20K Attack and 20K Strength XP are also substantial.

Strategy for Jungle Demon: Bring 100+ bolts and a magic shortbow or crossbow. Stand behind the rocks near the demon and attack with longrange. The demon can't path to you, making it a safe kill. If you're pure melee, bring prayer potions and use Protect from Melee.

② Recipe for Disaster — Freeing the Goblin Generals

Requirements: Cook's Assistant, 10 Cooking, 40 Attack (recommended) | Length: 30 minutes (first subquest) | Reward: Barrows gloves (after all subquests), 1,000 Cooking XP | Priority: ★★★★★

Recipe for Disaster (RFD) is a series of subquests that ultimately reward Barrows Gloves — the best-in-slot gloves until late-game raids gear. Barrows Gloves provide +12 to all attack styles and +12 Defense. Completing the full RFD quest requires 10 subquests and roughly 6-8 hours total. However, you should start working on RFD requirements early. The first subquest (freeing the Goblin Generals) is quick and requires only Cook's Assistant completion and 10 Cooking.

RFD Subquests Quick Overview:
1. Goblin Generals — Easy, start immediately
2. Evil Dave — Requires 40 Cooking and 50 Firemaking
3. Pirate Pete — Requires 31 Fishing
4. Lumbridge Guide — Requires 20 Farming
5. Skrach Uglogwee — Requires 30 Woodcutting
6. Sir Amik Varze — Requires 100 Combat (or 110 for the final fight)
7. King Awowogei — Requires 60 Cooking
8. Culinaromancer — The final boss fight, requires level 100+ combat stats

🎯 Barrows Gloves Priority

Why: Barrows Gloves are +12 to all stats — no other gloves come close until end-game content.
Time: 8-10 hours spread across your first week as a member.
Strategy: Complete one RFD subquest per day. Don't try to finish it all at once — the skill requirements mean you need to train in between subquests anyway.
Reward: Barrows gloves are the single biggest power upgrade in the game. Every PvM guide assumes you have them.

③ Dwarf Cannon

Requirements: None | Length: 20 minutes | Reward: Ability to use Dwarf Cannon, 3,000 Crafting XP | Priority: ★★★★☆

Dwarf Cannon unlocks the ability to place a multi-cannon, which is essential for fast Ranged training. The quest is straightforward — talk to the Dwarf in the Ice Mountain and help him with his cannon troubles. The cannon is a deployable device that auto-attacks nearby enemies, giving massive Ranged XP per hour at places like Slayer Tower, Dagannoths, and Ice Trolls. Even if you don't plan to train Ranged immediately, completing this quest early saves you from doing it later when you inevitably want the cannon.

④ Fairy Tale II — Cure a Queen

Requirements: 49 Farming, 49 Herblore, 40 Crafting | Length: 45 minutes (partial completion) | Reward: Access to Fairy Rings (with partial completion) | Priority: ★★★★★

Fairy rings are arguably the most important transportation unlock in OSRS. They connect over 40 locations across Gielinor, including GWD, Slayer Tower, DKS, and Morytania. You don't need to fully complete the quest to unlock fairy rings. Simply start the quest (requires Fairytale I — which is quick) and speak to the Fairy Godfather. After partial completion, you can use fairy rings even without the required skill levels. The full quest requires 49 Farming, 49 Herblore, and 40 Crafting, which are useful skills to train anyway.

💡 Pro Tip: Start Fairy Tale II as soon as you can reach the required skill levels. You don't need to finish it — just progressing to the point where you can use fairy rings is enough. The rings save you hours of running around and are essential for efficient Slayer, bossing, and clue scrolls.

💰 Quests That Make You Money

While the early quests focus on combat XP, several quests unlock passive money-making methods that fund your entire account. These OSRS quests that make you money should be prioritized after your combat stats are established.

① Recipe for Disaster — Barrows Gloves

We covered this above, but it bears repeating: Barrows Gloves are not just a combat upgrade — they're an economic enabler. With better stats, you kill monsters faster, complete Slayer tasks quicker, and earn more GP per hour. The gloves pay for themselves literally hundreds of times over during your account's lifetime.

② Fairy Rings for Transportation

Fairy rings save enormous amounts of time. Time saved on running between locations means more time spent on money-making activities. For example, reaching the Mole boss without fairy rings takes 5 minutes of running. With fairy rings, it takes 30 seconds. Over a year of playing, fairy rings save hundreds of hours — all of which you can spend making money. Unlocking fairy rings via partial Fairy Tale II completion is completely free and takes minimal time.

③ Royal Trouble — Kingdom Management

Requirements: 40 Construction, 43 Magic, completion of Throne of Miscellania | Reward: Access to Kingdom management (passive GP and resources) | Priority: ★★★★☆

Throne of Miscellania and its sequel Royal Trouble unlock the Kingdom Management system. After completing both quests, you can assign workers in Miscellania to gather resources for you. By investing 750K GP from your coffer, you can earn 100K+ profit per day in resources like maple logs, coal, herbs, and flax. The kingdom generates resources while you're offline, making it truly passive income. The quests themselves require 40 Construction and 43 Magic, which are useful to train anyway.

Setting Up Your Kingdom: After completing Royal Trouble, invest 750K GP into the kingdom coffer and set your subjects to gather hardwood and herbs. Hardwood logs are consistently profitable for construction, and herbs are always in demand for Herblore. Check your kingdom daily to keep the approval rating above 95%.

④ Bone Voyage — Fossil Island

Requirements: 35 Agility, 30 Thieving, 40 Farming | Length: 30 minutes | Reward: Access to Fossil Island | Priority: ★★★☆☆

Fossil Island unlocks passive prayer experience through the Camulet teleport and the fossil cleaning activity. More importantly, it gives you access to birdhouses (passive Hunter XP), the Giant Mole boss, and fossil exhibits that give Prayer XP lamps. The Fossil Island seaweed patches are also the best way to train Crafting for end-game gear. While not directly profitable, Fossil Island unlocks training methods that save you millions of GP in the long run.

❓ FAQ — Best Quest Order for Beginners

What is the best quest order for OSRS beginners in 2026?

The best quest order for beginners is: Cook's Assistant → Sheep Shearer → Romeo & Juliet → Prince Ali Rescue → Dragon Slayer (F2P), then Waterfall Quest → Tree Gnome Village → Fight Arena → The Grand Tree → Monkey Madness I → Recipe for Disaster → Fairy Tale II (members). This order prioritizes combat XP first, then unlocks the best gear and transportation.

What quests should I do first as a new member in OSRS?

As a new member, do Waterfall Quest first. It takes 15 minutes, requires no skills, and gives 27,500 total combat XP. Then do Tree Gnome Village, Fight Arena, and The Grand Tree to reach 40 Attack for Dragon Scimitar. Then complete Monkey Madness I to wield it. These five quests give over 75,000 combat XP and take about 3 hours total.

How many quests do I need to complete to unlock Dragon Scimitar?

You need to complete three quests to unlock Dragon Scimitar: Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree, and Monkey Madness I. You also need 40 Attack and 53 Hitpoints (to complete Monkey Madness I). These three quests give enough Attack XP to take you from level 1 to level 35+, so you'll only need minimal training to reach 40 Attack.

Is Waterfall Quest worth doing in 2026?

Absolutely. Waterfall Quest remains the single best early-game quest in OSRS. It gives 13,750 Attack and 13,750 Strength XP with absolutely no requirements. That's enough to go from level 1 to level 30 in both skills. No other method comes close to this XP rate for a fresh account. Waterfall Quest is always worth doing, regardless of the year.

What are barrows gloves and why are they important?

Barrows Gloves are the reward from completing all Recipe for Disaster subquests. They provide +12 to all attack styles and all defense styles, making them the best-in-slot gloves for almost all content until you reach end-game raids. They are critically important because every PvM setup assumes you have them. Getting barrows gloves should be a top priority for any account.

How long does it take to complete all F2P quests?

All free-to-play quests take about 3-4 hours to complete if you follow an efficient guide. The main time sink is Dragon Slayer, which takes about 45 minutes by itself. Most F2P quests are short (under 10 minutes). You can complete the essential F2P quests needed for membership in about 2 hours.

Can I skip F2P quests and go straight to members?

Yes, you can become a member immediately without completing any F2P quests. However, you will eventually need to complete Dragon Slayer for several members quest chains, and you'll need Quest Points for Recipe for Disaster. It's more efficient to complete the F2P quests before becoming a member, as they provide free XP and prepare you for the members quests.

What quests unlock transportation in OSRS?

The key transportation-unlocking quests are: Tree Gnome Village (Spirit Trees), The Grand Tree (Gnome Gliders), Fairy Tale II (Fairy Rings), and Bone Voyage (Fossil Island access). For boats, you need various quests like Pirate's Treasure, The Feud, and Horror from the Deep. Fairy Rings are the most important unlock as they connect 40+ locations and are used by every high-level player.

Should I use a quest order guide or just quest freely?

Using a structured quest order guide significantly improves your efficiency. By following the OSRS best quest order, you ensure that you always have the right requirements, you group quests by location, and you get combat XP upgrades before training. Questing freely can result in wasted time traveling between locations and missing important unlocks that would have made earlier quests easier.

What's the fastest route to unlock all members content?

The fastest route is: Dragon Slayer (F2P) → Waterfall Quest → Tree Gnome Village → Fight Arena → The Grand Tree → Monkey Madness I → Lost City → Fairy Tale I and II → Recipe for Disaster (full). This route gives you 40+ Attack, Dragon Scimitar, Barrows Gloves, Fairy Rings, and Spirit Trees — the core unlocks you need for nearly all OSRS content. Expect this to take about 20-25 hours of focused play.
