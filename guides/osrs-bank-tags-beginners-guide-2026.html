① What Are Bank Tags & Why You Need Them

Bank Tags are an official in-game feature added on June 17, 2026, as part of the Bank Tags, Trouver System Rework & More update. They let you create custom-labeled views of your bank that show only the items you choose — perfect for organizing gear setups, skilling runs, and clue scroll requirements.

Before Bank Tags, players relied entirely on RuneLite's plugin or manually memorized which tab their items were in. Now, everyone — including mobile players and official client users — gets the same organizational power.

🏆 What Bank Tags Do For You

  • One-click access to specific item sets (boss gear, farm runs, clue items)
  • Up to 20 custom tags — enough for every major activity
  • Works on Mobile and Official Client — not just RuneLite
  • No impact on main bank — tags are just views, your items stay where they are
  • Icon system — use any item as a visual label for your tag
💡 Why This Matters

A well-organized bank saves you 10-20 seconds per trip to the bank. If you bank 50 times per day, that's 8-17 minutes saved daily — over 50 hours per year. Bank Tags make this easy.

② Creating Your First Tag — Step by Step

Let's create your first Bank Tag. The entire process takes under 2 minutes. We'll use a "Slayer Gear" tag as our example.

① Find the + Button

Open your bank. Look in the top-left corner of the bank interface. You'll see a small "+" button. This is your gateway to creating tags. Click it.

② Name Your Tag

A chat box opens. Type a name for your tag — up to 25 alphanumeric characters. For our example, type "Slayer Gear" and press Enter. Tag names follow the in-game chat filter rules, so no special characters or offensive language.

③ Choose an Icon

After naming, a search box appears. You can search for any item in the game to use as your tag icon. Type "Abyssal whip" and select it from the results. This icon will appear on the tag button in your bank. Almost any item works, including untradeables.

④ Add Items to Your Tag

Now the fun part. From your main bank, drag items onto the tag icon at the top-left. Each dragged item gets added to the tag. For our Slayer Gear example, add: your melee weapon, armor pieces, Slayer helmet, prayer potions, and food. You can also hold Shift and click items to add them quickly.

⑤ Switch Between Tag View & Main Bank

Click the tag icon once → you see only the tagged items. Click it again → you return to your full bank. This toggle lets you jump between focused views and your complete inventory instantly.

② Choosing the Best Icons

Your tag icon is the visual cue that helps you spot tags at a glance. Some pro tips:

  • Use the most iconic item for that activity — Zulrah for a Zulrah tag, Dragon pickaxe for a Mining tag
  • Stackable items make great icons (runes, coins) because they're always in your bank
  • Avoid seasonal or quest items that might leave your bank — if you lose the icon item, the tag still works but shows a default icon

③ Reordering & Removing Tags

Tags appear in the order you create them, but you can drag and drop them to reorder. Put your most-used tags first. To delete a tag, right-click and select "Delete tag" — a confirmation prompt prevents accidents. Note: Deleting a tag does NOT delete your items, just the tag view.

💡 Pro Tip: Duplicate Tags

Right-click a tag and select "Duplicate" to make a copy. This is perfect for similar setups — for example, duplicate your "Slayer" tag and rename it to "Wildy Slayer" with a few item swaps. You can also hold Shift + drag an item onto a tag icon to add it.

④ Layout Plugin & Preset Functionality

While Bank Tags organize what items are shown, the Layout plugin organizes where they appear. Combined, they create a preset-like experience that many players call "game-changing."

① What the Layout Plugin Does

The Layout plugin lets you pin items to specific positions in your tag view. Even if the item is in your inventory or not in your bank at all, its position stays fixed. Empty slots show a gray placeholder so you know exactly what's missing.

② How to Set Up a Layout

📋 Layout Setup — Step by Step

  1. Open a Bank Tag — click on a tag you've already created
  2. Enable Edit Layout — right-click the tag icon and select "Edit Layout"
  3. Arrange Items — drag items to exactly where you want them. You can leave empty rows for visual breathing room
  4. Save Layout — click the save button. Your layout is now locked in
  5. Profit — every time you open this tag, items appear in the exact same positions

③ Using Layouts as "Presets"

Here's where it gets powerful. Set up your Layout to mirror your inventory setup for a specific activity. For bossing:

  • Row 1: Weapons (main + special attack)
  • Row 2: Armor pieces (helmet, body, legs)
  • Row 3: Accessories (amulet, ring, cape, boots)
  • Row 4: Supplies (food, potions, teleports)
  • Row 5: Rune pouch, bolts, and ammo

When you gear up, you can grab items in the exact order they appear, building your inventory from top to bottom. With practice, this cuts gear-up time from 20 seconds to under 5.

💡 Think of Layouts as Gear Templates

Each Layout is essentially a gear template. Create one for Melee Slayer, Ranged Slayer, Zulrah, Farm Runs, Clue Scrolls — whatever you do regularly. Switch between them with a single click.

④ Preset Behavior on Mobile

On the Mobile client, Bank Tags work slightly differently. The layout functionality is more limited — you can create tags and add items, but the advanced position-locking features are available primarily on the desktop C++ client. Mobile tags still save you time though — one tap to filter to the items you need.

③ Emoji in Tag Names (If Supported)

💡 The "Less is More" Rule

Don't create 20 tags immediately. Start with 3-5 tags for your most frequent activities. Add more as you get comfortable. Too many tags can be just as overwhelming as a messy bank.

⑦ Bank Tags FAQs

How many Bank Tags can I create?

You can create up to 20 tags. This limit may vary slightly if you use features like the Incinerator, Potion Storage, or Group Ironman Storage, which take up a slot in the tag bar.

Will Bank Tags mess up my main bank organization?

No. Tags are views of your items, not separate storage. Your main bank tab organization stays exactly as it is. The only thing that affects your actual bank is withdrawing and depositing items (which you'd do anyway).

Do Bank Tags work on Mobile?

Yes! This is one of the biggest advantages of the official Bank Tags feature. Mobile players get the same tag creation and viewing functionality as desktop players, though the advanced Layout position-locking is more polished on the desktop C++ client.

Can I use Bank Tags with RuneLite?

Yes, but note that the official in-game Bank Tags (added June 17, 2026) are separate from RuneLite's Bank Tags plugin. The official tags work on the C++ client and Mobile, while RuneLite's plugin continues to work as before. They do not interfere with each other.

How do I remove an item from a tag?

Right-click the item in the tag view and select "Remove from tag". This does NOT delete the item from your bank — it just removes it from that tag's view. To remove all items, you can delete the entire tag and create a new one.

Can I use the same item in multiple tags?

Yes. An item can belong to as many tags as you want. For example, your Abyssal whip could appear in both a "Slayer" tag and a "Bossing" tag. Tags are just filters, not storage containers.

What happens if I delete an item from my bank that's in a tag?

The item simply disappears from the tag view. If you're using the Layout plugin with pinned positions, the slot will show as a gray placeholder.

Can I share my Bank Tags with other players?

Currently, there is no in-game sharing feature for Bank Tags. However, third-party websites like RuneTags.com and BankLayouts.com let you create and share layouts. You'll need to rebuild them in-game manually using screenshots as references.

Do Bank Tags work with Potion Storage?

Currently, Bank Tags and Potion Storage are independent. To add potions from Potion Storage to a tag, you need to first move them to your main bank, then add them to the tag. This is a known limitation that may be addressed in future updates.

What's the difference between Bank Tags and the Layout plugin?

Bank Tags control what items are visible (they filter your bank). The Layout plugin controls where items appear (they pin positions). Used together, they create a preset-like experience where one click shows exactly the items you need in exactly the right positions.
