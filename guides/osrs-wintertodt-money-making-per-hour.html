OSRS Wintertodt Money Making Per Hour 2026 -- GP, Crates & Rewards

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 11 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
🔥 Money Making -- Updated June 2026

Wintertodt is one of the best early-to-mid game money makers in OSRS, offering consistent GP alongside Firemaking XP. The supply crates contain seeds, herbs, logs, ores, and occasionally rare drops worth hundreds of thousands of GP. This guide explains how to optimise your Wintertodt runs for maximum profit per hour.

1. What is Wintertodt?

Wintertodt is a Firemaking minigame boss located north of Arceuus in Great Kourend. Players work cooperatively to subdue the Wintertodt by lighting and maintaining fires around the arena. When fully subdued, all players who contributed enough points receive a supply crate proportional to their points earned.

  • Requires 50 Firemaking to participate
  • Higher levels in Firemaking, Woodcutting, Herblore, and Construction increase points earned per action
  • Supply crate quality scales with your skilling levels -- more Herblore = better herb drops
  • No combat required -- entirely a skilling activity
  • Passive Construction XP from fixing braziers (~5K-15K Construction XP/hr)
  • Can be done on Ironman accounts for passive herb and supply sourcing

3. How to Earn Points Efficiently

You need a minimum of 500 points to receive a crate. Most players aim for 750-1,000 points per game to maximise crates per hour. Points come from multiple actions:

ActionPointsNotes
Chopping Bruma Roots7-12 per rootScales with Woodcutting level
Fletching Kindling4-8 per rootExtra points on top of chopping -- always worth it if 50+ Fletching
Feeding Brazier25-75 per fuelThe primary point source; feed kindling not raw roots
Healing Pyromancers30 per healHelp the group and earn points
Fixing Braziers25 per repairAlso gives Construction XP (passive)
Lighting Brazier25-50Quick points when braziers go out

Optimal Gameplay Loop

  1. Position yourself next to a brazier at the start of each game
  2. Chop Bruma Roots until your inventory is full
  3. Fletch all roots into kindling (if 50+ Fletching -- this is critical for point stacking)
  4. Feed kindling to a lit brazier -- do not feed an unlit brazier (wastes fuel)
  5. If your brazier goes out, immediately relight it with your tinderbox
  6. Heal pyromancers when they fall below 30% health
  7. Fix broken braziers between root-chopping runs
  8. Eat food when cold damage brings you below 20 HP

6. Advanced Tips for Maximum Profit

  • Bank crates and open in bulk. Opening all crates at once at the bank is significantly faster than opening between games. Stockpile 50-100 crates, then open them all together and sell immediately.
  • Train Herblore first. Herblore level determines the herb tier in crates. Getting 75 Herblore before grinding Wintertodt can double your crate value compared to 50 Herblore. The difference between guam drops and snapdragon/torstol drops is massive.
  • Fletch every root. At 50+ Fletching, fletching roots into kindling adds ~20% more points per inventory. This directly translates to ~20% more crates per hour. The 2-game-tick fletching animation is worth the time investment.
  • 4 warm items minimum. With Pyromancer hood + garb + robe + boots, you take almost zero cold damage. This eliminates food costs and keeps you at the brazier longer. Use the Fire Cape as a warm item if missing a Pyromancer piece.
  • Low HP accounts get a unique advantage. Damage from Wintertodt scales with your Hitpoints level. At 10 HP, you take 1 damage per cold tick -- requiring only cakes or wines for healing. At 99 HP, you take up to 15 damage per tick. Ironmen often do Wintertodt at 10 HP exclusively for this reason.
  • Stay at the same brazier. Don't hop between braziers. Pick one, stick to one, and only move to heal pyromancers or fix a broken brazier. Moving wastes time that could be spent chopping.

7. Common Mistakes

  1. Feeding unlit braziers. Feeding kindling to a brazier that is not lit wastes your fuel completely. Always check that the brazier is lit before feeding.
  2. Not bringing a knife. Without a knife, you cannot fletch roots into kindling, losing ~20% of your potential points per hour. Always have a knife in your inventory.
  3. Ignoring pyromancers. Healing pyromancers gives 30 points and helps the group. Ignoring them slows down the entire game since fewer braziers stay lit.
  4. Using high-healing food at low HP. If you're running at low HP (10-25), eat cheap food like cakes or jugs of wine. A shark is overkill when you're taking 1-2 damage per cold tick.
  5. Neglecting Herblore. Doing Wintertodt at 1 Herblore vs 75 Herblore can mean the difference between 100K GP/hr and 500K+ GP/hr. Train Herblore first if profit is your goal.
  6. Banking too slowly between games. The bank chest is right outside the Wintertodt doors. Be fast -- every 30 seconds between games is a missed crate over an hour of play.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I solo Wintertodt or do the mass world?

A: Mass worlds (official Wintertodt world 309) are better for GP/hr. Games end faster (3-4 minutes), you get more crates per hour. Solo Wintertodt yields more Firemaking XP per game (because you stay longer and accumulate more points per game), but fewer crates per hour overall. For pure money making, join the Wintertodt mass world.

Q: At what Firemaking level should I stop Wintertodt for profit?

A: Wintertodt remains profitable all the way to 99 (and beyond). The profitable window doesn't close at any level. Many players get 99 Firemaking entirely from Wintertodt and earn 30-50M+ GP along the way. After 99, Wintertodt remains a decent mid-game money maker because the supply crates are always in demand.

Q: How do I get the Pyromancer outfit?

A: Pyromancer pieces come from supply crates. The first time you subdue Wintertodt (earning your first crate), you are guaranteed one piece. Every subsequent 50, 100, 150, 200, and 250 subjugations also guarantee a piece (in sequence: garb, hood, robe, boots, gloves). After all guaranteed pieces, any future Pyromancer drops are random from crates at a rate of 1/150 and are worth ~30K GP per piece.

🔥 Warm Yourself by the Fire (and the Profits)

Wintertodt is one of the most well-rounded activities in OSRS. You train Firemaking, earn passive Construction XP, collect valuable supply crates, and have a shot at the Phoenix pet -- all with zero combat risk. For early-game accounts, it is a phenomenal way to build a gold stack while progressing a skill. For mid-game players with 75+ Herblore, it's a consistent 400K-500K+ GP/hr with very low effort.

Grab your warm clothes, a tinderbox, and head north of Arceuus. The Wintertodt is waiting.
