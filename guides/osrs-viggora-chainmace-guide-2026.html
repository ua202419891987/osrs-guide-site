⚔️ Viggora's Chainmace OSRS — Complete Weapon Guide 2026

Viggora's chainmace is one of the most unique weapons in OSRS — a crush weapon with a built-in attack speed bonus in the Wilderness. This guide covers its stats, how to obtain it, PvP and PvM use cases, GE price history, and whether it's worth your hard-earned GP.

Viggora's chainmace is a weapon that most OSRS players have heard of but relatively few actually use. Named after the cursed general Viggora — one of Zaros's most loyal human commanders — this chainmace carries his legacy as a fearsome melee weapon. But is it actually good? Should you buy one? And what makes it special compared to the dozens of other crush weapons in the game?

In this guide, we'll break down everything you need to know about Viggora's chainmace: its exact stats, how to obtain it, where it shines (and where it doesn't), current Grand Exchange price, and a head-to-head comparison with alternative weapons. Whether you're a PvP enthusiast looking for a Wilderness edge or a completionist who wants to know if this weapon deserves a spot in your bank, you'll find the answer here.

💡 Quick Verdict

Viggora's chainmace is a niche PvP weapon that becomes incredibly powerful inside the Wilderness (levels 1-56), where its attack speed increases from 6 to 4 ticks. Outside the Wilderness, it's outclassed by nearly every alternative. If you PK in the Wilderness, it's worth owning. If you're purely a PvM player, skip it.

① 📋 What Is Viggora's Chainmace?

Viggora's chainmace is a one-handed crush weapon that requires 60 Attack to wield. It was introduced to OSRS as part of the Wilderness content updates and is directly tied to the lore of Viggora, the cursed general who served Zaros during the Second Age.

1.1 🧬 Lore Connection

In OSRS lore, Viggora was renowned for his skill with a chainmace — a weapon type that combines the reach of a whip with the crushing force of a mace. After betraying Zaros to join Zamorak's rebellion, Viggora was cursed to become an invisible ghost, wandering Gielinor for eternity. The chainmace that bears his name is said to be a replica of his signature weapon, imbued with a fragment of his restless spirit. You can learn more about Viggora's full story in our Viggora Complete Guide or the Curse of the Empty Lord quest guide.

1.2 ⚡ The Wilderness Bonus — What Makes It Special

The defining feature of Viggora's chainmace is its Wilderness bonus. When the player is inside the Wilderness (any depth), the chainmace's attack speed increases from 6 ticks to 4 ticks — effectively making it attack 50% faster. This transforms it from a mediocre crush weapon into one of the highest damage-per-second (DPS) weapons in the game, but only within the Wilderness.

This mechanic mirrors the real lore: Viggora's power was greatest in the chaos of battle, and the weapon channels that energy when surrounded by the danger of the Wilderness.

1.3 🎨 Appearance & Animations

The chainmace features a unique attack animation where the wielder swings the chain in a wide arc, dealing crush damage. The weapon's examine text reads: "A weapon of the cursed general Viggora." The chainmace has a distinctive spiked chain design with a dark metal finish, making it visually distinct from other one-handed weapons.

2.3 🧮 What Does 4-Tick Attack Speed Mean in Practice?

OSRS runs on a 600ms tick system. A standard one-handed weapon attacks every 6 ticks (3.6 seconds). Viggora's chainmace in the Wilderness attacks every 4 ticks (2.4 seconds). This means:

  • Outside Wilderness: ~16.7 attacks per minute
  • Inside Wilderness: ~25 attacks per minute
  • That's 8 extra attacks per minute — a massive DPS increase

At 99 Strength with standard gear, this translates to roughly 50-60% more DPS inside the Wilderness compared to outside. This makes Viggora's chainmace one of the highest-DPS weapons in the entire game when used in its intended environment.

③ 🔍 How to Obtain Viggora's Chainmace

Unlike many Wilderness weapons, Viggora's chainmace is tradeable and can be purchased directly from the Grand Exchange. You don't need to complete a quest or defeat a specific boss to obtain it. However, understanding where it comes from helps contextualise its value.

3.1 🏪 Method 1: Grand Exchange (Easiest)

Simply search for "Viggora's chainmace" on the Grand Exchange and buy it with GP. The price fluctuates based on supply and demand, typically ranging from 200K to 500K GP depending on market conditions. This is by far the easiest method — no quest requirements, no boss fights, just GP.

3.2 🎁 Method 2: Player Drops & PvP Worlds

If a player is killed while carrying Viggora's chainmace in a PvP scenario (Wilderness, PvP worlds, Bounty Hunter), the chainmace drops to the killer as loot. This means you could potentially obtain one by killing another player who's carrying it — though this is far less reliable than simply buying from the GE.

3.3 📦 Method 3: Mystery Boxes & Clue Scrolls (Rare)

Viggora's chainmace can very rarely appear as a reward from certain Wilderness-related content, including:

  • Larran's small/big chest (requires Slayer task key)
  • Wilderness Slayer rewards (extremely rare)
  • Emir's Arena reward shop (intermittent availability)

These methods are not recommended as a primary way to obtain the chainmace — the drop rates are extremely low and you'd spend far more time than the GP you'd save.

💡 Best Strategy

Just buy it from the Grand Exchange. At ~200-500K GP, it's affordable for any mid-level player. If you decide you don't want it later, you can re-sell it on the GE for roughly the same price (minus the 2% GE tax). Think of it as a rental with a small deposit.

④ ⚔️ PvP & Wilderness Use

This is where Viggora's chainmace truly shines. The 4-tick attack speed inside the Wilderness makes it one of the most dangerous weapons you can bring into a PK scenario — if you know how to use it.

4.1 🎯 Best PvP Builds for the Chainmace

Viggora's chainmace is a crush weapon, which means it pairs best with builds that maximise crush accuracy and strength. The most effective builds include:

4.2 🏃 PK Tactics — How to Use the Chainmace Effectively

The chainmace's 4-tick attack speed gives you a unique advantage in PvP: you can stack damage faster than opponents expect. Here's how to exploit this:

  • Combo with Vengeance: Cast Vengeance before engaging. The rapid chainmace hits combined with Vengeance recoil can instantly KO opponents who aren't expecting the speed.
  • Switch from ranged/magic: Start with a ranged or magic attack, then instantly switch to the chainmace for a rapid melee combo. The 4-tick speed means 2-3 hits land before the opponent can react.
  • Use in multi-combat zones: In multi-combat Wilderness areas (like the Chaos Temple or Rev Caves), the chainmace's speed shines even more — you can hit multiple targets rapidly.
  • Pair with Dragonfire Shield: The DFS special adds burst damage that complements the chainmace's rapid hits.

4.3 ⚠️ Risks of Using the Chainmace in PvP

Remember: if you die in the Wilderness with the chainmace, you lose it. At 200-500K GP, this isn't catastrophic, but it adds up if you die frequently. Some tips to mitigate risk:

  • Only bring the chainmace when you're confident in your PKing ability
  • Use Protect Item prayer (requires 25 Prayer) to keep one item on death — but note that this only protects your most valuable item, so if you're wearing more expensive gear, the chainmace might still drop
  • Consider using it in low-risk Wilderness (levels 1-20) where you can quickly escape if outmatched

⑤ 🐉 PvM Viability — Can You Boss With It?

Outside the Wilderness, Viggora's chainmace reverts to its 6-tick attack speed, making it significantly less impressive. But can you still use it for PvM content? The short answer: not really, but there are a few exceptions.

5.1 🚫 Why the Chainmace Falls Short in PvM

For nearly all PvM content (bossing, Slayer, minigames), the chainmace is outclassed by:

  • Abyssal Whip / Abyssal Tentacle: Higher slash accuracy and strength bonus, same attack speed
  • Dragon Scimitar / Crystal Halberd: Better slash stats for most Slayer tasks
  • Zamorakian Hasta: Higher strength bonus, can stab and slash
  • Dragon Mace / Bandos Godsword: Better crush options for specific weak-to-crush bosses

5.2 ✅ Exceptions: Where the Chainmace Is Decent in PvM

There are a few PvM scenarios where Viggora's chainmace is a reasonable choice:

5.3 🌟 The Real PvM Sweet Spot: Wilderness Bosses

The one place where Viggora's chainmace is genuinely excellent for PvM is Wilderness bossing. Bosses like Callisto, Venenatis, Vet'ion, and Scorpia are all located in the Wilderness, which means the chainmace gets its 4-tick speed bonus. Against these bosses, the chainmace can compete with — and sometimes outperform — much more expensive weapons.

However, you still need to weigh the risk: dying to a PKer while bossing means losing your chainmace. Most Wilderness bossers use cheaper gear for this reason.

6.2 📊 Is It a Good Investment?

As an investment (buying low, selling high): not really. The chainmace's price is relatively stable and unlikely to spike dramatically unless Jagex releases a major Wilderness update that increases demand. It's not a flip-worthy item.

As a utility purchase: yes, if you PK. At 200-350K GP, it's cheaper than most competitive PvP weapons and provides a genuine advantage in the Wilderness. If you PK regularly, the chainmace pays for itself in kills.

6.3 💡 Cost-Per-Death Analysis

One way to think about the chainmace's cost: how much GP do you lose per death? If you die 5 times per PK session, losing the chainmace each time at 300K GP = 1.5M GP lost. Compare this to bringing a Dragon Scimitar (~30K) — the chainmace costs 10x more per death. For casual PKers, the chainmace may be too expensive to risk. For experienced PKers who die rarely, it's affordable.

⑧ ❓ FAQ — Viggora's Chainmace

8.1 ❓ Does Viggora's chainmace work in PvP worlds (not Wilderness)?

No. The 4-tick attack speed bonus only activates in the Wilderness (the area north of Edgeville, levels 1-56). PvP worlds and the Duel Arena do not trigger the bonus. The chainmace remains at 6-tick speed in these areas.

8.2 ❓ Can I use the chainmace with a shield?

Yes. Viggora's chainmace is a one-handed weapon, so you can equip a shield or off-hand item in your left hand. Popular combinations include the Dragon Defender, Dragonfire Shield, and Book of Balance.

8.3 ❓ Is Viggora's chainmace better than the Abyssal Whip?

In the Wilderness: yes, for crush-weak targets. The 4-tick speed gives it significantly higher DPS than the Whip's 6-tick speed. Outside the Wilderness: no. The Whip's superior slash accuracy and similar strength make it the better general weapon.

8.4 ❓ Does the chainmace degrade or need repair?

No. Viggora's chainmace does not degrade. Once you buy it, it's yours forever (or until you lose it in the Wilderness). There are no repair costs, which makes it more economical than degrading weapons like the Tentacle or Abyssal Bludgeon over time.

8.5 ❓ Can Ironman accounts obtain Viggora's chainmace?

Yes, but only through non-tradeable methods. Ironmen cannot buy it from the Grand Exchange. They must obtain it from Wilderness content like Larran's chest or as a rare drop from Wilderness Slayer. This makes it quite rare among Ironman accounts.

8.6 ❓ What's the best gear setup for Wilderness PKing with the chainmace?

A typical mid-level setup: Viggora's chainmace + Dragon Defender, Berserker Helm, Amulet of Glory, Fighter Torso, Rune Platelegs/Kiteshield, Combat Bracelet, Climbing Boots, and an Inventory of Sharks, Super Combat Potion, and Teleport runes. Keep your total risk under 500K to make deaths affordable.
