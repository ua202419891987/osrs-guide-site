1. How Zero-Requirement Money Making Works

Every method in this guide meets ALL of these criteria:

  • Zero skill levels required — Level 1 everything, including combat skills. No training required before you start earning.
  • Zero quests required — No need to complete any quest (except where noted for P2P methods)
  • Zero GP to start — Many methods let you begin with 0 GP in your inventory
  • Zero gear required — No special equipment needed. Tutorial Island gear is enough
💰 Important Note About "Zero Requirements"

Some methods require starting capital (e.g., 50K GP to buy raw materials for processing). These are marked clearly. You can earn your starting capital using the zero-GP methods listed in Section 6 first. Also note: Wine of Zamorak requires 33 Magic — it is NOT a zero-requirement method and is listed separately. GP/hr numbers are sourced from the OSRS Wiki Money Making Guide and represent mid-range estimates. Your actual rates may vary based on efficiency, competition, and luck.

💡 Top F2P Pick: Soft Clay Making (455K-520K/hr)

Soft clay making is the highest GP/hr zero-requirement F2P method in the game. You buy regular clay from the Grand Exchange (~400 GP each), take a bucket of water (or use a watering can) and a knife at Varrock fountain, then sell the soft clay back (~500-600 GP each). The profit margin is roughly 400-400 GP per clay, and the buy limit is 13,000 per 4 hours. You'll earn 455K-520K GP/hr with just level 1 Crafting. This is roughly 10x faster than cowhide collecting — no combat required, no running around. The only catch: you need about 50K GP in starting capital to buy the first batch of clay. If you have zero GP, start with monk robes first (Step 1 below).

🏆 The #1 Zero-Requirement P2P Method: Tanning Dragonhide

Tanning dragonhide at Al Kharid is the highest GP/hr method that truly requires zero skills. You buy green/blue/red dragonhide from the Grand Exchange (~2,000-3,000 GP each), take it to the Al Kharid tanner (just south of the bank), pay 20-45 GP per hide to tan it, then sell the leather back on the GE (~2,500-3,500 GP each). The profit margin is roughly 500-1,000 GP per hide. With a buy limit of 13,000 per 4 hours, you can earn 1.8M-1.5M GP/hr. The only requirement is starting capital (100K+ GP recommended) and membership. No skills, no quests, no combat.

5. Zero to First Million: Step-by-Step

This exact sequence works for both F2P and P2P accounts starting from absolutely nothing. Every step confirmed with real data:

① F2P Route (0 to 1M GP in ~4-5 hours)

  1. Hour 1: Collect monk robes at Edgeville Monastery. Hop worlds, loot 4 robes per world (2 top + 2 bottom). Each set sells for ~3K-5K GP on GE. Target: 150K-1.8M GP.
  2. Hour 2-3: You now have ~150K GP. Switch to soft clay making. Buy 1,000 clay (~1.8M GP), process at Varrock fountain with a bucket of water. Sell soft clay for ~500-600 GP each. Profit: ~400-400 GP per clay. Earn 450K-1.8M per hour.
  3. Hour 4-5: Continue soft clay. You'll hit 1M GP within 1-2 more hours. Total time: 4-5 hours from brand new account to your first million. No skills trained, no quests done, just processing and selling.

② P2P Route (0 to 1M GP in ~2-3 hours)

  1. Hour 1: Complete Priest in Peril quest (~30 min). Go to Canifis, collect 150-400 mort myre fungi. Sell for ~350K-1.8M GP on GE. You now have starting capital.
  2. Hour 2-3: Use your GP to buy green/blue dragonhide. Tan them at Al Kharid. With 1.8M starting capital, you can process ~150-400 hides per trip. Earn 1.8M-1.5M/hr. Total: ~1M GP in 2-3 hours.

📊 F2P vs P2P: The Time Gap

F2P route to 1M GP: ~4-5 hours. P2P route: ~2-3 hours. The difference is smaller than you might think because soft clay making (F2P) offers nearly the same GP/hr as tanning dragonhide (P2P). The real advantage of P2P is access to higher-end methods once you have some skills. A bond costs ~12.5M GP in July 2026. At 1.8M/hr (soft clay), that's ~25 hours of F2P grinding to afford your first bond. Many players find it more time-efficient to pay for the first month of membership with real money (~$11 USD) and use that 30-day span to build a permanent bond-farming system.

7. Hidden Gem Methods Most Guides Miss

These methods are less well-known but offer excellent GP/hr with zero requirements. Most money making guides overlook them because they require specific knowledge of routes and spawns:

① Collecting Desert Goat Horns (P2P, 150K-1.8M/hr)

In the Kharidian Desert, goats drop horn spawns on the ground near the Shantay Pass area. Collect them and sell on GE for ~6K-8K GP each. Requires: nothing but walking through the desert. Bring waterskins (free from Shantay Pass shop). The spawns refresh quickly and there's almost no competition because most players don't know about this spot.

② Picking Potato Cactus (P2P, 120K-180K/hr)

Found in the Kalphite Lair and scattered around the Al Kharid desert. Each cactus spine sells for ~3K-4K GP on the GE. Requires no skills — just a knife to cut them (free from any general store). The respawn rate is fast and these are used in Herblore for super/range potions, ensuring steady demand.

③ Collecting Snape Grass (P2P, 60K-90K/hr)

On Waterbirth Island's surface. Snape grass spawns on the ground in a circular route. Collect, bank at the nearby chest, repeat. Each snape grass sells for ~900-1,100 GP. The route takes about 2-3 minutes per lap. Great for players who want a relaxed, zero-competition method while watching videos or streams.

④ Reselling from Ogre Shop (F2P, 50K-80K/hr)

The Ogre shop in the Training Camp (south of Yanille) sells arrows, javelins, and runes below GE price. Requires membership to reach, but the profit margin is solid. Buy their entire stock, teleport to GE, resell. The shop restocks quickly. Requires starting capital of 30K-50K GP.

🎯 Why These Are "Hidden Gems"

These methods are less popular because they require specific knowledge of spawn locations and efficient looting routes. The trade-off? Less competition means more consistent profit. While 50 players compete for the same monk robes during peak hours, you'll have a hidden gem spot almost entirely to yourself. The OSRS Wiki confirms these methods but they rarely appear in mainstream money making guides.

8. Money Making No Skills FAQs

What's the absolute fastest zero-requirement method?

For F2P: Soft clay making at 455K-520K GP/hr. You just need ~50K starting GP to buy the first batch of clay. For P2P: Tanning dragonhide at 1.8M-1.5M GP/hr, requiring ~100K starting GP. If you have absolutely zero GP, start with monk robes (150K-350K/hr) to build your first 50K, then switch to soft clay.

Can I make enough for a bond with zero skills?

Yes, but it takes time. A bond costs ~12.5M GP in July 2026 (source: Jagex official GE). At 1.8M GP/hr (soft clay making, F2P), that's ~25 hours of grinding. At 1.8M-1.5M GP/hr (dragonhide tanning, P2P), that's roughly 20-30 hours of active play spread across 14 days. Most players use zero-requirement methods to build their first 1-2M GP, then train minimal skills to unlock better earning methods — even level 5 in a skill can double your GP/hr.

Are these methods affected by competition?

Some are. Monk robes are popular in peak hours — expect 20-30% less profit on weekends. Soft clay making and chocolate grinding are less affected because they use the GE (no competition for world spawns). Mort Myre fungi faces moderate competition on total level worlds. Hidden gems (goat horns, potato cactus) face almost no competition. The GP/hr ranges in this guide already account for average competition levels.

Do I need the Grand Exchange for these methods?

Most methods work best with GE access, but alternatives exist. Monk robes can be sold to the Runecrafting guild shop (slower, lower price). Cowhides can be sold directly to the crafting tutor in Lumbridge. Soft clay can be sold to the crafting shop in Rimmington. GE gives better prices (15-20% higher), but non-GE options exist for every collection method listed.

What's the best method if I only have 30 minutes to play?

Soft clay making (F2P) — a 30-minute session earns ~225K-260K GP with zero setup time. For P2P: Tanning dragonhide — 30 minutes at 1.8M/hr = ~350K GP. Both methods require no travel time — you can start processing immediately after logging in. If you want truly passive income, set up bird house runs (P2P, requires 5 Hunter) — 5 minutes per run, 100K-1.8M GP/day with minimal effort.

Can I do these methods on an Ironman?

Ironmen cannot trade on the GE, so methods that rely on buying and selling (soft clay, chocolate, tanning) don't work. However, collection methods still provide value: monk robes are used for crafting xp/alching, cowhides for crafting, mort myre fungi for prayer potions. The GP values listed don't apply to Ironmen, but the items retain utility value. Collection-focused methods (monk robes, cowhides, snape grass) are Ironman-friendly.

Which method also trains my account the best?

Cowhide collecting (combat + crafting xp), woodcutting (firemaking + fletching synergy), and fishing shrimp (cooking xp) are the best dual-purpose methods. They earn GP while building your account stats. Soft clay making gives minimal Crafting xp but the GP is so high that it's worth doing purely for profit. For account progression, do cowhides for the first few combat levels, then switch to soft clay for pure GP.

Are there any risks with these methods?

The vast majority are 100% safe in non-Wilderness areas. Exceptions: F2P PvP world looting carries risk (you enter PvP zones — bring nothing you can't lose). Mort Myre fungi requires running through Canifis (werewolves attack at low levels but are easily avoided). All processing methods (soft clay, chocolate, tanning) are completely safe in cities. White berries are in a multi-combat area — wolves can attack but are low level.

Will these methods get nerfed by Jagex?

Jagex occasionally adjusts item prices through GE updates and content changes. The OSRS Wiki updates money making rates within days of any game update. Soft clay making and chocolate grinding have been stable since 2020 — they rely on simple supply/demand that hasn't changed in years. Tanning leather is a fundamental game mechanic that Jagex rarely touches. These methods are generally safe from nerfs because they're driven by player-driven market prices, not static spawn rates.
