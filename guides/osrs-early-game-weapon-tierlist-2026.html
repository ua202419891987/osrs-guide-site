⚔️ Melee Weapon Tier List — Level 1 to 60

Melee is the most common combat style for early game OSRS players. The right weapon choice doubles your kill speed, which means faster XP and more GP per hour. This tier list ranks weapons by DPS, accuracy, and cost efficiency. S-Tier is best-in-slot, D-Tier means skip entirely.

① Bronze to Rune — The Full Progression Path

Here is your weapon upgrade path from level 1 Attack to level 50 Attack (Rune tier). Each tier represents approximately 10 Attack levels.

S-Tier Weapons (Best in Slot)

  • Rune Scimitar (Level 40, ~15K gp): The undisputed king of F2P melee. +45 slash attack, +44 strength bonus. Fast attack speed (4 ticks). Best DPS for levels 40–60. Buy this the moment you hit 40 Attack.
  • Rune 2H Sword (Level 40, ~25K gp): Highest max hit in F2P (12–14 at 40 Strength). Slow but devastating for aggressive training. Pair with a rune scimitar for slashing tasks.
  • Dragon Scimitar (Level 60, Members, ~100K gp): The ultimate early-mid game melee weapon. +66 slash, +66 strength. Special attack (Sever) reduces opponent Defence by 20%. The goal weapon for every melee build.

A-Tier Weapons (Strong Contenders)

  • Adamant Scimitar (Level 30, ~1K gp): Best weapon for levels 30–39. Cheap, fast, effective.
  • Mithril Scimitar (Level 20, ~400 gp): The bridge between iron and adamant. Very affordable.
  • Dragon Longsword (Level 60, Members, ~60K gp): Good alternative to the dragon scimitar. Has a special attack. Cheaper than the scimitar.
  • Ancient Staff (Level 50, Members, ~40K gp): Surprisingly good melee weapon with +15 crush and +10 strength bonus. Also auto-casts Ancient Magicks.

B-Tier Weapons (Decent Budget Picks)

  • Steel Scimitar (Level 5, ~100 gp): Your first real weapon upgrade from bronze. Cheap and fast.
  • Black Scimitar (Level 10, ~500 gp): Not accessible in F2P (black weapons are members only). Good for members at low levels.
  • Iron Scimitar (Level 1, ~50 gp): The starter scimitar. Fine until steel.
  • Rune Longsword (Level 40, ~10K gp): Slower than the scimitar but higher max hit. Use for strength training.

C/D-Tier Weapons (Avoid These)

  • All battleaxes and warhammers: Too slow for training. The scimitar out-DPSes them despite lower max hits.
  • Bronze/iron maces: Lowest DPS in their tier. Maces offer prayer bonus but the DPS loss is not worth it.
  • Rune dagger: Fast but low max hit. Only useful for special attacks (poison).
  • All claws before level 60: No good claw options in early game. Skip until dragon/barrows.

② Special Attacks Worth Using

Some early game weapons have special attacks that can give you an edge in combat:

  • Rune Longsword Special (Slice and Dice): Hits twice in one tick. 65% special energy. Good for finishing off enemies.
  • Dragon Dagger (Level 60, Members): Poke/Poke special hits twice with high accuracy. Use with poison for maximum effect.
  • Dragon Scimitar (Level 60, Members): Sever reduces opponent Defence by 20%. Use at the start of boss fights.
  • Rune 2H Special (No special in F2P): The rune 2H has no special attack in F2P. In members, the dragon 2H has a cleave attack.

③ Best-in-Slot Per Attack Level

  • Level 1: Iron Scimitar
  • Level 5: Steel Scimitar
  • Level 10: Black Scimitar (members) or Steel Scimitar (F2P)
  • Level 20: Mithril Scimitar
  • Level 30: Adamant Scimitar
  • Level 40: Rune Scimitar — the king of F2P
  • Level 50: Rune Scimitar (stay with it, or upgrade to Ancient Staff for members)
  • Level 60: Dragon Scimitar (members) — the ultimate early-mid game weapon

Always prioritize the scimitar over longsword, battleaxe, or 2H sword. The scimitar attack speed (4 ticks vs 5–7 ticks for other weapons) provides significantly higher DPS.

🏹 Ranged Weapon Tier List — Level 1 to 60

Ranged training is slower than melee in the early game, but having a good ranged weapon is essential for safespotting monsters, completing quests, and eventually fighting dragons. Here is the progression from beginner crossbows to the magic shortbow.

① Shortbow vs Longbow — Which Is Better?

The short answer: shortbows are better for training, longbows are better for safespotting. Here is the breakdown:

  • Shortbows: Faster attack speed (3 ticks vs 5 ticks). Lower accuracy and range. Better DPS for active training. Best for most situations.
  • Longbows: Slower but longer range. Better for safespotting monsters that cannot reach you. Also better for tagging monsters in multi-combat areas.
  • Composite bows: Members only. Same speed as shortbows but with higher accuracy and range. The magic composite bow is excellent for clue scrolls (stringing).

Ranged Weapon Tier List (Levels 1–60)

S-Tier:

  • Magic Shortbow (Level 50, Members, ~10K gp): +69 ranged attack, 3-tick speed. Special attack (Snapshot) hits 2-3 times in rapid succession. Best ranged weapon until level 75 (Rune Crossbow or Dark Bow).
  • Magic Shortbow (i) — Imbued (Level 50, Members): Upgraded version from the Nightmare Zone. +75 ranged attack. Even better special attack.

A-Tier:

  • Maple Shortbow (Level 30, F2P + Members, ~500 gp): Best F2P shortbow until you reach members. Fast, cheap, and effective with adamant arrows.
  • Willow Shortbow (Level 20, ~200 gp): Good for levels 20–30. Use with iron or steel arrows.
  • Oak Shortbow (Level 10, ~100 gp): Gets you through the early levels.

B-Tier:

  • Yew Shortbow (Level 40, ~5K gp): Upgrade from maple at level 40. Good accuracy. Use with mithril or adamant arrows.
  • Rune Crossbow (Level 50, Members, ~30K gp): Slower than the MSB (5 tick speed) but higher accuracy and damage. Better for PvM where you need range and accuracy over DPS.
  • Crossbow (all tiers): Bronze through adamant crossbows all fill the same role — slow but accurate. Use them for Dragon Slayer and monster safespotting.

C-Tier (Avoid):

  • All longbows: Too slow for DPS training. Only use for quest requirements or safespotting.
  • Dorgeshuun Crossbow (Level 28, Members): Low accuracy, bone bolts are weak. Not worth the quest requirements.
  • Phoenix Crossbow (Level 1, Members): Very low stats. Only useful as a meme weapon.

② Best Arrows by Level — Cost vs Performance

Arrow choice matters more than bow choice for your DPS. Here is the optimal arrow progression:

  • Level 1–10: Bronze arrows (~1 gp each). Use them for chickens, cows, and goblins.
  • Level 10–20: Iron arrows (~2 gp each). Good balance of cost and damage.
  • Level 20–30: Steel arrows (~4 gp each). Use with willow shortbow.
  • Level 30–40: Mithril arrows (~6 gp each). Use with maple shortbow.
  • Level 40–50: Adamant arrows (~8 gp each). Use with yew shortbow. Best F2P arrow.
  • Level 50–60: Rune arrows (~15 gp each). Use with magic shortbow. Best DPS for early members.
  • Level 60+: Amethyst arrows (~25 gp each) or dragon arrows (~200 gp each). Dragon arrows are expensive but BiS for bossing.

For F2P, stick with adamant arrows and a maple shortbow until you hit 40+ Ranged. The cost per shot is very low and the DPS is competitive for the price.

③ Magic Shortbow Special Attack — Why It Matters

The Magic Shortbow's special attack (Snapshot) costs 55% special energy and fires two arrows at once with increased accuracy. In practice, this means your DPS doubles for 3 ticks. The imbued version (MSB(i)) deals even more damage. The MSB special is so good that many players use it as a primary spec weapon well into the mid-game (paired with a dragon dagger and dark bow). For early game Ranged training, the MSB is the clear winner. Buy one as soon as you hit level 50 Ranged. It costs only 10K GP and will serve you until you unlock the Rune Crossbow or blowpipe.

🔮 Magic Weapon Tier List — Level 1 to 60

Magic training requires both a weapon and runes. The right staff can save you thousands of GP by providing unlimited free cast of certain spells. This section covers the best staves, wands, and spellbooks for early game magic.

Magic Weapon Tier List (Levels 1–60)

S-Tier:

  • Battlestaves (Elemental): Fire battlestaff (~5K gp), Water battlestaff (~3K gp), Air battlestaff (~2K gp), Earth battlestaff (~2K gp). Each provides unlimited free casts of its elemental spell. This saves you thousands of runes. A fire battlestaff with nature runes lets you cast Fire Bolt for free (just need nature runes). Best for mid-level combat training.
  • Mystic Staves (Level 40, Members): Mystic fire staff (~20K gp), mystic water staff (~20K gp). Same unlimited elemental rune benefit as battlestaves but with +10 magic attack and defence. Significant accuracy boost.
  • Ancient Staff (Level 50, Members, ~40K gp): Also good for melee (+15 crush, +10 strength). Auto-casts Ancient Magicks.

A-Tier:

  • RunePouch (Members): Not a weapon, but essential for magic training. Holds 16K runes in your ammo slot. Combined with a battlestaff, you only need to carry runes for non-elemental spells.
  • Smoke Battlestaff (Level 30, Members, ~100K gp): Provides unlimited Air and Fire runes. Best in slot for training Standard Magic and making fire runes in the RuneCrafting guild.
  • Mud Battlestaff (Level 30, Members, ~80K gp): Unlimited Water and Earth runes. Excellent for barraging and alching.

B-Tier:

  • Elemental Staves (All): The basic battlestaves. Fine for training but lack the magic attack bonus of mystic staves.
  • Beginner Wand (Level 1, F2P): You start with air strike. No need to buy a wand.
  • Staff of Air/Fire/Water/Earth (Level 1, ~1–5 gp): The basic staves. Each provides infinite casts of one element. Budget friendly.

C-Tier (Avoid):

  • All wands before level 60: Wands are primarily for high-level PvM (volcanic mine, inferno). Early game wands are expensive and offer minimal benefit over staves.
  • Mage Arena staves (Level 50, Members): Good for the god spells (Saradomin Strike, Claws of Guthix, Flames of Zamorak) but the staves themselves are weak magic weapons. Only use them for the god spells.

① Elemental Staves — Unlimited Free Casts

The biggest magic training cost is runes. Elemental staves eliminate the cost of one rune type per staff. The best early game strategy: Buy an air battlestaff (~2K gp). This gives you unlimited Air runes. Then buy fire runes and nature runes. You can now cast Fire Strike (1 Air, 3 Fire, 1 Mind → free Air, pay for Fire and Mind) at a reduced cost. As you level up, upgrade to the fire battlestaff which gives unlimited Fire runes, letting you cast fire spells for just the Mind/Chaos/Death rune cost. This cuts your magic training cost by 30–50%.

② Best Spells for Training (Level 1–60)

  • Level 1–13: Air Strike. Use with an air staff. Free to cast.
  • Level 13–23: Fire Strike. Use with fire battlestaff (free fire runes) + mind runes. Very cheap.
  • Level 23–37: Fire Bolt. Fire battlestaff + chaos runes. Good damage, moderate cost.
  • Level 37–50: Fire Blast. Fire battlestaff + death runes. Best DPS for levels 37–50.
  • Level 50–55: Fire Wave. Fire battlestaff + blood runes. Push for 55 for high alch.
  • Level 55: High Level Alchemy. The real money maker. Alch items for profit and magic XP simultaneously.

Curse spells (Level 14–50) are also good for cheap XP. Cast Curse on a monster for 29 XP per cast with zero cost (no runes needed for the first hit, then you can use a staff to supply the elemental runes). This is the most cost-efficient way to train magic from 14–50.

③ Splash Gear for AFK Training

"Splash" training is the act of casting a spell on a monster with negative magic attack bonus so you never hit (splash) and get full XP without dealing damage. This is useful for AFK training while watching movies or working. You need:

  • Gear: Full metal armor (bronze or iron — negative magic attack).
  • Weapon: A staff (any staff — does not affect splash rate).
  • Spell: Fire Strike with fire battlestaff and mind runes.
  • Target: A monster with good Defence. Rats in the Lumbridge castle basement work well. Chickens also work.
  • Mechanic: Cast Fire Strike on the monster. You will splash 100% of the time, earning 14 XP per cast. Each cast costs ~6 GP (mind rune). At 1,000 casts per hour, that is 14K magic XP per hour for only 6K gp.

Splash training is the cheapest and most AFK way to train magic from level 13 to 55. It takes 30–40 hours to reach 55 Magic, but you can do it entirely AFK. The cost is approximately 150K–200K GP for the entire journey.

🛡️ Armor Priority List — What to Buy First

Armor in OSRS is less important than weapons for DPS, but having the right armour keeps you alive longer, reduces food consumption, and lets you fight tougher monsters. This priority list tells you exactly what to buy at each defence level, and in what order.

① Melee Armor — The Upgrade Path

  • Defence Level 1: Bronze armor (free from Tutorial Island). No need to buy.
  • Defence Level 5: Iron armor (~2K for full set). Upgrade body first, then legs, then helm.
  • Defence Level 10: Steel armor (~5K for full set). The first notable defence increase.
  • Defence Level 20: Mithril armor (~12K for full set). Solid defence bonus.
  • Defence Level 25: Black armor (Members only, ~20K for full set). Better than mithril, not as good as adamant.
  • Defence Level 30: Adamant armor (~25K for full set). The first "real" armor set. Prioritize platebody first.
  • Defence Level 40: Rune armor (~65K for full set). Best in slot for F2P. Full helm (~7K), platebody (~35K), platelegs (~15K), kiteshield (~10K). Buy in that order.
  • Defence Level 45: Granite body (Members, ~80K gp). High defence, high weight, DPS penalty. Situational.
  • Defence Level 50: Dragon armor (Members). Dragon med helm (~100K), Dragon platelegs (~150K), Dragon kiteshield (~200K). The ultimate melee armor before Barrows.

Priority order within each tier: Weapon first, then platebody, then platelegs, then full helm, then kiteshield (unless you already have a good shield from a quest). The platebody provides the most defence. The weapon provides the most DPS.

② Ranged Armor — Accuracy over Defence

Ranged armor focuses on ranged attack bonus, not defence. You want to maximize accuracy while keeping weight low.

  • Level 1: Leather armor (free). Goblin mail if you want a laugh.
  • Level 10: Studded leather (Members, ~5K for set). +10 ranged attack. Worth investing in.
  • Level 20: Snake hide (Members, ~8K for set). +15 ranged attack. Good for mid-level range training.
  • Level 30: Green dragonhide (Members, ~10K for set). +20 ranged attack. The first "real" ranged armor.
  • Level 40: Blue dragonhide (Members, ~15K for full set). +25 ranged attack. BiS until black d'hide.
  • Level 50: Red dragonhide (Members, ~25K for full set). +30 ranged attack. Good stepping stone.
  • Level 60: Black dragonhide (Members, ~40K for full set). +35 ranged attack. BiS until Armadyl/Karils.

For F2P, your ranged armor options are limited. The best you can use is leather (level 1) followed by hard leather (level 10, F2P). For members, dragonhide is the standard. Always buy body first, then chaps, then vambraces.

③ Magic Armor — Attack Bonus for Higher DPS

Magic armor trades defence for magic attack bonus. Every +1 magic attack matters for your accuracy.

  • Level 1: Wizard robes (free). The starting robes. +5 magic attack for the full set.
  • Level 20: Splitbark (Members, ~30K for full set). +10 magic attack, decent defence. Heavy but worth it for the accuracy.
  • Level 25: Mystic robes (Members, ~40K for full set). +15 magic attack. The standard magic armor for early-mid game. Lightweight, good accuracy.
  • Level 40: Mystic (elite) robes (Members, ~60K for full set). +18 magic attack. Slightly better than standard mystic.
  • Level 50: Infinity robes (Members, ~1.2M for full set). Heavy cost for low stats. Go straight from mystic to Ahrim's if possible.
  • Level 60: Ahrim's robes (Barrows, Members, ~1.5M for full set). +10 magic attack per piece. The standard for PvM magic setups.

For F2P, wizard robes are your only option. They are free from the Wizards' Tower. For members, invest in mystic robes as soon as you can. They are cheap and provide the best magic attack bonus for the cost.

💀 Best Budget Gear Under 500K — Complete Setups

You do not need millions of GP to have an effective combat setup. Here are the best gear sets for under 500K GP total, broken down by combat style. These setups are optimized for DPS, defence, and cost efficiency.

① Top 5 Melee Budget Setups (Under 500K)

  • 1. F2P Melee (~100K): Rune scimitar, rune full helm, rune platebody, rune platelegs, rune kiteshield, amulet of power. Total: ~90K gp. Best F2P setup.
  • 2. Members Starter (~150K): Rune scimitar, dragonhide chaps, berserker helm, fighter torso (free from Barbarian Assault), dragon platelegs (or rune). Total: ~120K.
  • 3. Obsidian Setup for Nightmare Zone (~300K): Obsidian sword (toktz-xil-ak), obsidian helmet, obby shield. Total: ~280K. The sword has +38 slash and +52 strength. Great for NMZ training.
  • 4. Granite Mauler (~250K): Granite maul (50 Attack, ~100K gp), dragonhide or rune armor. The maul special attack hits 2 ticks after the first. Devastating for PvP.
  • 5. Leaf-Bladed Sword (Level 50 Slayer, ~350K): +20 stab, +23 strength. Free to use after the Slayer requirement. Excellent for Kurask tasks and early-mid game PvM.

② Top 3 Ranged Budget Setups (Under 500K)

  • 1. Magic Shortbow Setup (~100K): Magic shortbow (10K), 5,000 rune arrows (75K), green d'hide body/chaps/vambraces (10K), amulet of accuracy or power (5K). Total: ~100K. Best budget ranged DPS.
  • 2. Rune Crossbow Setup (~200K): Rune crossbow (30K), 2,000 broad bolts (50K), green d'hide armor (10K). Total: ~90K. Better for bossing than MSB.
  • 3. Bone Crossbow Setup (~30K): Bone crossbow (8K from Canifis quest), 10,000 bone bolts (20K). Extremely cheap ranged training. Low DPS but great for AFK.

③ Top 3 Magic Budget Setups (Under 500K)

  • 1. Splash Training Setup (~20K): Fire battlestaff (5K), mind runes (bulk buy ~5K for 1,000), full bronze armor (2K). Total: ~12K. Used for AFK splash training to level 55 Magic.
  • 2. Alching Setup (~50K): Fire battlestaff (5K), nature runes (bulk buy ~10K for 1,000), high alch items (~35K stack). Total: ~50K. Used for High Alchemy profit.
  • 3. Mystic Combat Setup (~200K): Mystic fire staff (20K), mystic robes (40K for full set), runes for combat spells (50K). Total: ~110K. Effective combat magic for levels 40–60.

All of these setups cost under 250K GP total, leaving you with another 250K for food, potions, and other supplies. You do not need to spend millions on bis gear at these levels.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the best weapon at level 40 Attack?

A: The Rune Scimitar is the best weapon at level 40 Attack. It has +45 slash attack and +44 strength bonus with 4-tick attack speed. It is available in F2P and costs ~15K GP on the GE.

Q: Is the rune 2H sword better than the rune scimitar?

A: No. The rune 2H has a higher max hit, but the scimitar attacks 50% faster, resulting in higher DPS. The 2H is only better for Strength training in controlled scenarios.

Q: What bow should I use at level 30 Ranged?

A: Use a Maple Shortbow with mithril arrows. The maple shortbow costs ~500 gp and the arrows cost ~6 gp each. This is the best combination of cost and DPS for level 30 Ranged.

Q: How do I train Magic from level 1 to 55 cheaply?

A: Use the splash training method. Cast Fire Strike (with fire battlestaff and mind runes) on a rat while wearing negative magic attack gear. Each splash gives 14 XP and costs ~6 GP. This is the cheapest AFK magic training method.

Q: What armor should I buy at level 40 Defence?

A: Full Rune armor. Buy in this order: platebody (~35K GP) first for the most defence, then platelegs (~15K GP), then full helm (~7K GP), then kiteshield (~10K GP). Total cost: ~65K GP.

Q: Is the dragon scimitar worth the 100K GP?

A: Yes. The Dragon Scimitar (+66 slash, +66 strength, special attack Sever) is the best melee weapon for levels 60–75. It is a massive upgrade from the rune scimitar and is essential for Slayer, PvM, and questing.

Q: What is the best budget ranged gear for bossing?

A: Rune crossbow with broad bolts and full green or blue dragonhide armor. Total cost: ~100K GP. This setup is effective for Barrows, Dagannoth Kings, and most Slayer bosses.

Q: Should I buy mystic robes or splash train in wizard robes?

A: If you are splash training, use wizard robes (negative magic attack not needed for splashing — you want 0 accuracy). If you are doing active combat magic, buy mystic robes at level 25 Magic for the accuracy boost.

Q: What weapon should I use for Strength training?

A: For Strength training, use a scimitar on Aggressive style (gives 3x Strength XP). If you want pure Strength XP per hit, use a longsword on Aggressive for higher max hits. A Rune Longsword with Controlled style gives balanced Attack/Strength/Defence XP.

Q: Are Granite weapons worth using in the early game?

A: The Granite Maul (level 50 Attack) is good for PvP due to its special attack. The Granite Hammer and Granite Longsword are not recommended for training — they are too slow for effective DPS. Stick with scimitars for training and use the Granite Maul for PvP spec.
