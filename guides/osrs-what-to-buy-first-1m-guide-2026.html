⚡ Quick Verdict

Best first purchase with 1M GP: A Rune Crossbow + Broad Bolts (~100K) and Full Graceful Outfit (~30 hours of agility, free). Don't blow it on expensive armor you'll outgrow in 10 combat levels. Invest in items that unlock content, not just stats. See the full priority table below.

1. The #1 Mistake Beginners Make With Their First 1M

You got your first million GP. Maybe you flipped that lucky clue scroll reward. Maybe a generous player gave you a cash stack. Maybe you finally saved up from hours of grinding. Your first instinct is probably the wrong one.

"I'll buy the best armor I can afford!"

That's the trap. Buying full Bandos or Armadyl at combat level 60 is a waste of GP — you don't have the defense level to wear the best pieces, and the marginal upgrade over budget gear (Rune armor, Green D'hide) is tiny compared to what else that 1M could do for you.

🎯 The Right Mindset

  • Prioritize unlocks over stat bumps: A Dragon Scimitar (100K) unlocks more content than a Bandos Chestplate (20M)
  • Invest in money makers: Spend GP on things that earn you more GP
  • Skip intermediate gear: Jump from budget directly to endgame — don't buy the mid-tier stuff you'll replace in a week
  • Keep a cash reserve: Always keep 200-300K liquid for supplies, runes, and teleports

Your goal at this stage isn't to look cool. It's to build a foundation that generates more GP. Every purchase should pass this test: "Will this help me earn my next million faster?"

3. Skill Investments That Pay for Themselves

Some of the best uses of your first 1M aren't items — they're skill levels that unlock money-making methods far more valuable than any armor upgrade.

① 55 Magic: High Alchemy (20K in runes)

Training to 55 Magic costs about 20K in runes (curse/bolt spells). At 55 Magic, High Alchemy turns junk loot into pure GP. Every slayer task you do after 55 Magic becomes 20-30% more profitable because you can alch the drops instead of selling them at GE minus margins.

Payoff: ~2-5K GP per minute while doing other activities. Passive money machine.

② 50 Construction: Portal Nexus (1M build cost)

Getting Construction to 50 costs roughly 800K-1M in Oak Planks (316 Oak Planks at ~3K each). At 50 Construction, you can build a Portal Nexus in your house that teleports you everywhere. This saves thousands of GP in runes and tablet costs over your OSRS career.

Payoff: At 10 teleports saved per hour, the house pays for itself in ~20 hours.

③ 55 Slayer: Broad Bolts (Supplies ~100K)

Getting Slayer to 55 unlocks Broad Bolts — the cheapest effective ranged ammunition in the game. Before 55 Slayer, you're spending 50-100 GP per shot on Rune Arrows or Addy Bolts. After 55 Slayer, Broad Bolts cost 2 GP each (from Slayer Masters) and do nearly identical damage.

Payoff: Saves 48-98 GP per shot. If you fire 5,000 bolts in a slayer task, that's 250K-500K saved.

④ 44 Runecraft: Nature Runes (~500K investment)

Getting Runecraft to 44 unlocks Nature Runes through the Abyss. At current prices, crafting Nature Runes earns 300-400K GP/hour. Compare that to early-game money makers (50-100K/hour).

4. Quest & Quality of Life Upgrades

Some of the most impactful purchases with your first 1M unlock content through quests and QoL improvements rather than raw stats.

① Graceful Outfit (Free — 30 hours of Agility)

Costs no GP, only time. Full Graceful is the single most useful item in OSRS. It restores run energy 30% faster and reduces weight by 20kg. Every single activity — questing, slayer, skilling, bossing — is easier with Graceful.

But you have 1M — why not buy Stamina Potions? Stamina potions cost 5-8K each. At 20 potions per hour of questing, that's 100-160K/hour in potion cost. Graceful saves you that money forever.

② Grace of the Elves (7.5M — Save for it)

Too expensive for your first 1M, but worth saving for. GotE gives you a permanent teleport to Prifddinas, +10% chance of double loot from seren spirits, and serves as the best skilling necklace in the game.

③ Xeric's Talisman (Free — 100% Zeah favor)

Complete 100% Hosidius favor (takes ~2 hours) to get Xeric's Talisman. Unlimited teleports to multiple locations around Zeah, including Catacombs of Kourend and Xeric's Shrine. Save the GP and spend the 2 hours.

④ Dragon Pickaxe (1.5M — Save for mining)

If you plan to train mining past 60 (and you should — it's needed for quests and diaries), the Dragon Pickaxe is the best investment. Speeds up mining by ~15% over Rune Pickaxe. The special attack gives +3 invisible mining levels.

5. What NOT to Buy (Common Noob Traps)

Here are the purchases that will waste your first 1M. Avoid these at all costs.

🚫 Noob Trap #1: Full Rune (G) or Trimmed Armor

Cost: 500K-1M | Actual value: ~50K

Gold-trimmed Rune armor looks cool but has identical stats to regular Rune. You're paying 10x the price for pixels that make you look like you don't know what you're doing. Don't do it.

🚫 Noob Trap #2: God Runes (800K)

Air, Fire, Water, Earth, Mind, Chaos — there is a "God" variant of every rune that costs 5-10x more and does the exact same thing. If a friend or GE scammer tries to sell you "Guthix Air Runes," laugh and walk away.

🚫 Noob Trap #3: Obsidian Equipment (1.5M+)

Obsidian armor costs 1.5M+ and requires completion of TzHaar Fight Cave. The stats are worse than Dragon in most cases. The only niche use is a specific Tokkul-zone training method. Skip it.

🚫 Noob Trap #4: Spamming GE with "Buying [Item]" offers

New players often pay 5-10% above market price because they don't check the GE price. Always use the GE tracker (RuneLite plugin or OSRS Wiki prices) to see the actual buy/sell spread. Set your offer at mid-price and wait 10 minutes.

🚫 Noob Trap #5: Lamps and XP items

Players in the GE will try to sell you "Lamps" or "Quest Items" for inflated prices. Every lamp in OSRS is untradeable. If someone offers to sell you a lamp, they're trying to scam you with a random junk item.

6. How to Turn 1M Into 10M

Your first 1M is the hardest. The next 10M is easier — if you invest it right. Here's the step-by-step path from 1M to 10M.

① Level 55 Slayer (1-2 weeks)

With melee gear from the Tier 1 list above, train Slayer from 1 to 55. At 55 Slayer, you unlock Broad Bolts (2 GP each) and start making consistent 200-300K/hour from slayer drops. This is your first reliable income stream.

② 100 Quest Points + Barrows Gloves (2-3 weeks)

Use your 1M to buy quest supplies (runes, food, teleport tablets). Complete Recipe for Disaster for Barrows Gloves. The quest rewards + Barrows Gloves upgrade your account by 15-20% in raw combat stats. This unlocks higher-level PvM content.

③ Barrows Runs (Consistent 500K-1M/hour)

With 43 Prayer and 50 Magic (Trident/Drakans), Barrows is the best entry-level boss for profit. Average 500K-1M/hour depending on RNG. Do 50 runs (about 10 hours), and you'll have 5-10M in your bank.

👑 The 1M to 10M Pipeline: Buy Slayer gear (100K) → Train Slayer to 55 → Unlock Broad Bolts → Save for quest supplies → Complete RFD → Unlock Barrows Gloves → Farm Barrows → 5-10M bank. Rinse and repeat with upgraded gear.

④ Flip Your First 10M (Optional, Advanced)

Grand Exchange flipping with 10M can earn 300-500K/hour with zero combat. Focus on high-volume items: Rune items, dragon bones, cannonballs, prayer potions. Buy at 2-3% below mid, sell at 2-3% above mid. With 10M capital, that's 400-600K profit per full flip cycle.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I have 1M GP and combat level 50. What should I buy first?

Dragon Scimitar (~95K) if you have 60 Attack. If below 60 Attack, train to 60 Attack then buy the D Scim. Then buy an Amulet of Glory (~15K) and Rune Crossbow (~15K). Save the rest for Slayer supplies.

Q: Should I spend my first 1M on armor?

No. Wear the best armor you can afford under 100K per piece (Rune for melee, Green/Blue D'hide for ranged). Armor has diminishing returns — the difference between Rune and Dragon platebody is only +9 Strength bonus for 10x the price.

Q: Is it worth buying a bond with my first 1M?

Bonds cost ~7M GP (June 2026). No — 1M is not enough. You need roughly 10M total to buy a bond AND have enough GP left for basic member gear. Keep grinding F2P money making until you have at least 8-10M.

Q: I got a lucky drop worth 5M. Should I sell it or keep it?

Sell it 90% of the time. Early-game gear upgrades are massive, and a 5M cash stack unlocks 10x more content than holding a single item you don't have the stats to use effectively. Exception: if the item is soulbound or a direct DPS upgrade you can equip now.

Q: What's the best money maker after buying gear with 1M?

Slayer. Always Slayer. It trains combat (which you need anyway) while generating consistent GP through drops. At 55+ Slayer, tasks like Dust Devils, Kurasks, and Gargoyles earn 200-500K/hour with minimal gear requirements.

Q: Should I invest in Construction with my first 1M?

Yes — if you plan to play long-term. 50 Construction (costs ~800K in Oak Planks) unlocks a Portal Nexus and basic altar. This saves thousands in teleport costs over your OSRS career. But get the gear from Tier 1 first.

Q: Can I flip the GE with 1M?

Barely. 1M is enough to start, but your profit per flip will be small (20-50K per cycle). Focus on high-volume items like Rune essence, feathers, or cowhides. Flipping becomes meaningful at 10M+ capital where you can make 300K+ per cycle.

Q: What's the biggest scam to watch out for with 1M in my bank?

The "Doubling Money" scam — nobody doubles GP. The "GE price swap" scam — they offer you a cheaper-looking item that's actually a different (worthless) variant. Always double-check item names and GE prices. Use the RuneLite GE plugin.

Q: Should I buy a Fire Cape if someone offers to sell me the service?

No! Paying someone to complete the Fight Caves for you violates OSRS rules (account sharing). Your Fire Cape only has value if you earned it. Train to 75 Ranged with 43 Prayer and learn the waves — it's easier than you think with modern guides.

Q: I spent all my 1M on Bandos armor and now I'm broke. What do I do?

Sell the Bandos. You can't afford to wear it (70 Defense required) or use it effectively. Buy back to a Tier 1 setup, keep 300K as operating cash, and start Slayer. The Bandos will still be there when you have 50M+ to spend properly.
