2. Sailing — OSRS's 24th Skill

Released November 19, 2025, Sailing remains the defining update of this era. It introduced a complete naval system with 4 training methods, ship upgrades, new resources, and new Slayer monsters. By mid-2026, the community has established efficient training routes and the skill's economy (Dragon Sheets, ship parts) is maturing.

Key confirmed data: 1-99 takes 120-150 hours. Upgrade costs estimated at 20-60M GP. The wind passive adds ~10-15k XP/h. Four training methods confirmed by Jagex.

3. Summer Sweep-Up (June 2026)

The biggest QoL and balancing update of the year. Confirmed changes include:

  • Gauntlet overhaul — Corrupted Gauntlet mechanics refined
  • 15+ gear buffs — Underused weapons and armor adjusted
  • 7 new Agility shortcuts
  • Hunter improvements — Birdhouse and trap mechanics smoothed

The Sweep-Up significantly shifted the PvM gear meta. Some previously "off-meta" items are now competitive, changing boss kill methods across the board.

4. Bank Tags & Trouver Rework

Bank Tags arrived as a permanent QoL feature that lets players organize their banks with custom tags, filter by activity, and save loadouts. Widely considered one of the best QoL additions in OSRS history.

The Trouver (item retrieval) rework simplified death mechanics, making item recovery more consistent across different scenarios — particularly helpful for bossing and wilderness content.

5. Leagues VI (Early 2026)

Leagues VI introduced new relics, an updated task system, and for the first time — integrated Sailing content into a temporary game mode. While temporary, the mode's mechanics have influenced permanent game design discussions at Jagex.

6. What's Coming in H2 2026

Based on official Jagex announcements only — no speculation:

  • Wyrmscraig Island (Summer 2026) — Player-designed island with goat hunting, puppet making, new Slayer master, and solo boss "The Mad Angel"
  • Ship combat rework (Mid 2026) — Improved naval combat mechanics
  • Red Reef quest (2026) — Troubled Tortugans sequel
  • Sea Bosses (2026) — New PvM encounters requiring Sailing

We will update this guide with confirmed details as Jagex releases them.

7. FAQs

What was the best OSRS update in 2026 so far?

Bank Tags and the Trouver rework had the widest positive impact, but the Summer Sweep-Up gear changes are the most impactful for PvM players.

Is Sailing still worth starting in mid-2026?

Yes. Sailing's economy has stabilized, training routes are well-established, and more content is coming in H2 2026 (Wyrmscraig, ship combat rework).

Did the Summer Sweep-Up change my gear?

15+ items were buffed. Check our Summer Sweep-Up guide for a full list of changes to see if your gear setup needs updating.

What's the most anticipated upcoming update?

Wyrmscraig Island (Summer 2026) — it's the first player-designed island and introduces a new Slayer master and solo boss.

Is this guide speculation-free?

Yes. Every update mentioned in this review has been officially released or announced by Jagex. No predictions, rumors, or unconfirmed features are included.
