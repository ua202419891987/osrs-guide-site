The Revenants Caves (also known as the "Revenant Caves" or "Rev Caves") are a sprawling underground dungeon deep in the Wilderness. In 2026, these caves remain one of the most popular — and most dangerous — money-making activities in Old School RuneScape. Whether you are an Ironman hunting for that first Craw's Bow, a main account seeking high GP/hr, or a PKer looking for targets, this guide has everything you need to master the Revenants Caves.

💡 New to Revenants in 2026?

The caves received several drop table adjustments in 2025-2026. Bracelets of Ethereum now work slightly differently, and the Revenant Dragon was added as the highest-tier revenant in a deeper section of the caves. All drop rates in this guide reflect the 2026 post-rework values. If you haven't visited the caves since 2024, the loot is better than ever.

💡 Pro Tip: The Salve Amulet is NON-NEGOTIABLE

Every revenant — from the tiny Imp to the colossal Dragon — is classified as undead. The Salve Amulet (e) provides a 20% multiplicative boost to both accuracy and damage against all undead monsters. This is not additive — it's a straight 1.2× multiplier on your max hit and accuracy roll. No other $1.8M item gives this much DPS increase. Do not enter the caves without it.

If you have completed the Desert Treasure quest, you can use the Edgeville Lever (in the wilderness section of Edgeville) to teleport directly to the Ardougne Lever. From there, run back to Edgeville and enter the caves. This sounds counter-intuitive, but it saves time compared to running the long way if you die and need to re-gear quickly. Some players also use the Wilderness Obelisk network (level 50 Agility recommended) to teleport to level 35+ Wilderness for fast access to the Lava Maze entrance.

① Which Revenants Should You Kill?

Your target priority depends on your combat level and gear:

  • Level 30-50 combat (Budget): Stick to Imps and Goblins in the entrance areas. They have lower HP and drop rates are decent for their level. Expect 1.8M-1.8M GP/hr.
  • Level 60-80 combat (Medium Gear): Kill Hobgoblins, Pyrefiends, and Orks. These drop Ancient Emblems and mid-tier Statuettes. Expect 1.8M-2M GP/hr.
  • Level 90+ combat (Good Gear): Focus on Knights and Orks. Knights drop the most valuable loot per kill (including Statuettes and Medallions). Expect 1.0M-1.8M GP/hr.
  • Maxed Combat (BiS Gear): Go for the Revenant Dragon. It requires careful safe-spotting and anti-dragonfire protection, but the loot table is stacked with high-value items. Expect 3M-2.5M GP/hr.

① Revenant Dragon — The Boss of the Caves

The Revenant Dragon was added in a 2025 update and is the most dangerous revenant in the caves. Key facts:

  • Location: Deepest section of the caves, accessible through the Lava Maze entrance area
  • Dragonfire: It uses both regular dragonfire and a special "ancient dragonfire" that hits through normal anti-dragon shields. You need an Anti-Dragon Shield + Protect from Magic to mitigate damage.
  • HP: 180 — the tankiest revenant by far
  • Max Hit: 22 (dragonfire), 16 (melee)
  • Drop Table: Same base table as Knights but with higher quantities (e.g., 30-50 Rune Arrows instead of 10-20) and a unique Ancient Relic drop worth ~16,000,000 GP (16M)
  • Respawn: 30 seconds (fast respawn — you can camp one spot)
💡 Pro Tip: Use "NPC Aggression Timer" to Your Advantage

Revenants have a 20-minute aggression timer. After 20 minutes, they de-aggress and will not attack you unless you attack them first. This is actually good for safe-spotting — once de-aggressed, you can AFK a single revenant spawn without interruption. Bring a Bracelet of Ethereum to reduce initial aggression, and after 20 minutes, the cave becomes much more relaxed. Just be aware that world-hopping resets the timer!

If you are using the Protect Item prayer (level 25 Prayer), you will keep your most valuable item on death (usually your weapon or Salve Amulet). This is huge for risk management. Always keep Protect Item on when entering the caves, alongside Protect from Missiles or Protect from Magic.

⑦ 🏃 Escape Strategies & PK Awareness

The Revenants Caves are in the Wilderness — PKers are not just possible, they are guaranteed. Learning to escape (or fight back) is essential for long-term profitability. Here is a complete guide to surviving PK attacks.

① Detecting PKers Early

  • Watch the minimap: If you see a white dot moving towards you rapidly, start moving to a safe spot. Most PKers use RuneLite player indicators — they show as a different color on your minimap.
  • Listen for teleport sounds: PKers often teleport into the caves using Revenant Cave Teleports. The teleport sound cue gives you ~2 seconds of warning.
  • Use the "NPC Aggression" indicator: If a revenant suddenly stops attacking you and runs away, a PKer is nearby (revenants have an "aggression detection" mechanic that makes them flee from PKers too).
  • Check world hop frequently: If you see multiple white dots in the cave, hop worlds. A cave with 10+ players is a PK hotspot.

① Fighting Back — Anti-PK Setup

Sometimes running is not an option. If you have decent combat stats and the PKer is alone, you can fight back. Here is a simple anti-PK strategy:

  • Freeze them: If you have Ancient Magicks unlocked, cast Ice Barrage or Ice Burst to freeze the PKer in place. Then either run or kill them with Ranged.
  • Vengeance + Ranged: If you have Lunar Magicks, cast Vengeance (reflects 75% of damage taken). Combined with high Ranged DPS, this can kill a PKer in 3-4 hits.
  • Dragonstone Bolts (e) Spec: The Dragonstone Bolt special attack (e) lowers the target's Ranged level. Combined with high magic defense, this makes PKers miss more often.
  • Always bring a weapon switch: If you carry a Dragon Dagger (p++) or Dragon Claws, you can surprise the PKer with a fast special attack. Most PKers expect revenant hunters to be passive — catching them off guard with a spec weapon can turn the tables.

⚠️ Skull Trick Warning — Do NOT Get Skulled!

If you get skulled in the Revenants Caves, you lose ALL your items on death (except 1 protected item with Protect Item prayer). A skulled death with a mid-tier setup (~1.8M risk) means losing everything. With a BiS setup (~10M+ risk), a skulled death is catastrophic. The most common skull trick involves a player in revenant gear standing near a revenant spawn — they look like an NPC, you attack them, and suddenly you are skulled. Always double-check your target before attacking. Use RuneLite's Player Indicator plugin to highlight player names in a different color from NPC names.

① Advanced Escape Route: The "Agility Gauntlet"

Here is a tested escape route that uses multiple shortcuts to escape PKers in the Revenants Caves:

  1. Exit the caves through the Dark Warriors' Fortress entrance.
  2. Run south-east toward the Wilderness Ditch (Edgeville side).
  3. If PKers are chasing you, squeeze through the Agility Pipe (15 Agility) at the Dark Warriors' Fortress. The pipe is a "one-at-a-time" obstacle — only one player can pass per tick. This splits PKers up or forces them to take the long way around.
  4. Cross the Wilderness Ditch (no Agility requirement — just a click). Once across, you are in level 1-5 Wilderness and can Teleport or Logout safely.
  5. Alternative: If you have 52 Agility, run north to the Agility Course Log Balance and use the agility obstacles to gain distance. PKers without 52 Agility cannot follow you through the course.
⚠️ Critical: Agility Shortcut Warning

Some PKers have higher Agility than you and know these shortcuts too. The pipe squeeze at 15 Agility is the minimum requirement — any PKer with 15+ Agility can use it too. Do not assume shortcuts are 100% safe. Use them to buy time, then logout or teleport when you have a 3-4 tile gap. The best defense is situational awareness + a quick teleport button.

⑨ ❓ FAQ — Revenants Caves (2026 Updated)

Q: What is the best weapon for Revenants in 2026?

A: The Craw's Bow is the best-in-slot weapon for Ranged in the Revenants Caves. It has a built-in +50% accuracy and damage bonus against NPCs in the Wilderness (same mechanic as Viggora's Chainmace). The Magic Shortbow (imbued) with Amethyst Arrows is the best budget option — it outperforms most other weapons at a fraction of the cost. For Magic users, Thammaron's Sceptre with Ancient Magicks (Ice Burst/Barrage) is viable for multi-target areas.

Q: How much GP per hour can I actually make at Revenants?

A: Realistic GP/hr at Revenants in 2026 ranges from 1.8M GP/hr (budget setup) to 2M+ GP/hr (BiS setup with Craw's Bow). The average mid-level player with MSB(i) and Amethyst Arrows should expect 1.8M-2.5M GP/hr. However, PK deaths can reduce this significantly — if you die once per 5 hours, subtract ~100K-1.8M/hr from your profit depending on your risk level. If you survive consistently, revenants are easily top-5 GP/hr for non-maxed accounts.

Q: Is the Salve Amulet worth bringing?

A: Absolutely. The Salve Amulet (e) provides a 20% multiplicative boost to accuracy and damage against all undead monsters. Revenants are undead. This means your max hit and accuracy are multiplied by 1.2x. At 80 Ranged, this effectively adds ~5 levels worth of DPS. At 99 Ranged, it is even more impactful. The Salve Amulet (ei) (imbued version) extends this bonus to all combat styles. Do not enter the Revenants Caves without a Salve Amulet. It costs ~20K GP and is single-handedly the most important item for this content.

Q: How do I avoid getting skulled at Revenants?

A: Follow these rules: (1) Disable Trade in the Wilderness via Settings > Controls. (2) Use RuneLite's Player Indicators plugin to color player names differently from NPC names. (3) Never attack a target unless you are 100% sure it is a revenant. (4) Hold Shift (if configured) to only target NPCs. (5) If a player is wearing revenant gear and standing near a revenant spawn, skip that world. (6) Keep Protect Item prayer active at all times — if you do get skulled, you keep your most valuable item. See Section 7 for a detailed breakdown of skull trick methods and avoidance.

Q: What agility level do I need for the Revenants Caves shortcuts?

A: The minimum useful Agility level is 15 for the pipe squeeze at the Dark Warriors' Fortress. This is the most important shortcut for escaping PKers. At 35 Agility, you unlock the stepping stones near the Lava Maze. At 52 Agility, you can use the Wilderness Agility Course log balance and other obstacles for advanced escape routes. If you plan to camp the caves long-term, aim for 52 Agility — it unlocks the full range of escape options.

Q: Can Ironmen kill Revenants effectively?

A: Yes — Revenants are excellent for Ironman accounts. They drop Rune Arrows, Amethyst Arrows, Rune armor, and valuable artefacts that can be sold for GP (or alched). The Craw's Bow from the rare drop table is game-changing for Ironmen doing Wilderness PvM. However, Ironmen face higher risk because they cannot simply re-buy lost items from the Grand Exchange. Recommended approach: use MSB(i) + Rune Arrows (self-made or dropped), Black d'hide, and Salve Amulet (e). Keep risk under 1.8M GP and expect to die occasionally.

Q: What is the Bracelet of Ethereum and do I need it?

A: The Bracelet of Ethereum is a Revenant Caves-specific item that significantly reduces revenant aggression. When charged with Revenant Ether (bought from the Grand Exchange or obtained from kills), the bracelet makes revenants much less likely to attack you. This means less prayer drain (from Protect from Missiles), less food consumption, and longer trips. Yes, you should bring it — especially for mid-tier and high-tier setups. The bracelet costs ~5K GP and holds up to 1,000 ether. At ~1 ether per minute of reduced aggression, one bracelet charge lasts ~16 hours. Well worth it.

Q: What is the best world for Revenants Caves?

A: There is no single "best" world — it depends on the time of day and activity levels. General advice: Total Level worlds (e.g., World 2, 321, 395) tend to have fewer PKers because the player base is more focused on skilling. Low-population worlds (400-400 players) have less revenant competition but more PKers per capita. High-population worlds have more revenant competition but PKers are spread thinner. Try hopping through worlds 303, 321, 395, and 410 — these are popular for revenants so you will find consistent activity. Use the "World Switcher" plugin in RuneLite to quick-hop between worlds.

Q: Should I use a Looting Bag at Revenants?

A: Always. The Looting Bag is one of the most important items for any Wilderness trip. Here is why: (1) It doubles your effective inventory — you can stuff 28 items inside and still have 28 inventory slots for food/potions. (2) Items in the Looting Bag are kept on death (unless you are skulled or in multi-combat). This means you can store 28 high-value items safely. (3) When your inventory is full, fill the Looting Bag and continue killing until your food runs out. (4) To retrieve items, use the Looting Bag on a bank or die in the Wilderness (items are kept on death in singles).

Q: Are Revenants better GP/hr than Vorkath or Zulrah in 2026?

A: It depends on your stats and risk tolerance. Vorkath: ~2.5-3.5M GP/hr (consistent, low risk, high requirement). Zulrah: ~2.0-3.0M GP/hr (consistent, low risk, high requirement). Revenants (Mid-tier): ~1.8M-3M GP/hr (variable, high risk, mid requirement). So Vorkath and Zulrah are significantly better GP/hr for endgame accounts. However, Revenants require much lower stats (70 Ranged vs 80+ for Vorkath/Zulrah) and much lower gear investment (1.8M vs 10M+). For mid-level accounts (75-85 Ranged), Revenants are more accessible and can actually be better GP/hr relative to your stats. For maxed accounts, Vorkath/Zulrah are superior — but Revenants offer the thrill of Wilderness PvM and the chance at a Craw's Bow drop worth 2-4M.

🏆 Final Tips for Revenant Hunters

Revenants are one of the most dynamic money-making activities in OSRS. The combination of high GP/hr, valuable rare drops, and the constant threat of PKers creates an experience that no other content can replicate. Here is a final checklist before you head into the caves:

  • ✓ Salve Amulet (e) equipped — non-negotiable for maximum DPS
  • ✓ Bracelet of Ethereum charged — reduces aggression, extends trips
  • ✓ Protect Item + Protect from Missiles active — keeps your weapon safe + reduces damage
  • ✓ Looting Bag + Ring of Dueling ready — emergency teleport + extra space
  • ✓ Trade disabled in Wilderness — prevents trade-based skull tricks
  • ✓ RuneLite Player Indicators enabled — distinguish players from NPCs at a glance
  • ✓ Know your Agility shortcuts — practice the pipe squeeze and log balance before PKers arrive
  • ✓ Set a PK death limit — if you die twice in one session, take a break. Tilt leads to more deaths.

Remember: The Revenants Caves are meant to be high-risk, high-reward. Do not bring gear you cannot afford to lose. Stay alert, watch the minimap, and have your teleport hotkey ready. With practice, you will learn to smell PKers before they even see you. Good luck, and may the Ancient Artefacts flow!
