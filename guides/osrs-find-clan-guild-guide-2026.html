OSRS Find Clan Guide 2026: How to Find the Right Community

Finding a good clan is one of the most impactful things you can do in OSRS. The right clan turns boring grinds into fun sessions, teaches you boss mechanics faster than any guide, and opens doors to group content you can't do alone. This guide covers every confirmed method to find and join active OSRS clans in 2026.

⚡ Quick Verdict

The best way to find a clan in 2026 is to start at the Clan Hub in-game — use the Clan Recruitment Boards to browse active listings. Then cross-reference on Discord to get a feel for the community before committing. PvM-focused clans are easiest to find and most active; Skilling and Ironman clans require more searching but offer more specialized communities.

2. Types of Clans: PvM, Skilling, Social & More

Not all clans are created equal. Understanding the different types helps you find one that matches your playstyle:

① ⚔️ PvM/Bossing Clans

The most common and active clan type. These clans focus on group bossing, raids, and PvM content. They typically have minimum combat or gear requirements and organize daily raid teams, boss masses, and learner-friendly events.

  • Best for: Players who want to learn raids, find boss teams, and maximize GP/hour
  • Typical requirements: Base 70-85 combat stats, fire cape, some gear minimums
  • Examples: Raids-focused clans, bossing communities, "social PvM" clans

② 🔨 Skilling/XP Clans

Clans dedicated to efficient skilling, XP optimization, and often competing for ranks on the hiscores. These clans share skilling methods, host group skilling events, and celebrate 99 milestones together.

  • Best for: Skillers, completionists, players going for max cape
  • Typical requirements: Varies — some require minimum total level (1,000-2,000)

③ 💬 Social/General Clans

The "catch-all" clan type. These clans welcome players of all levels and interests. The focus is on community, conversation, and having fun rather than efficiency or progression.

  • Best for: New players, casual players, anyone who wants chat company
  • Typical requirements: None — most are open to all

④ 🛡️ Ironman Clans

Dedicated to the Ironman game mode. These clans share Ironman-specific tips, coordinate boss masses (where Ironmen can participate together), and celebrate unique Ironman achievements.

  • Best for: Ironmen, HCIM, UIM players
  • Typical requirements: Must be an Ironman account type

⑤ ☠️ Wilderness/PKing Clans

Focused on PvP content — Wilderness PK trips, deep wilderness clan wars, and bounty hunting. These clans often have strict gear and Discord voice chat requirements.

  • Best for: PKers, Wilderness enthusiasts
  • Typical requirements: Combat level brackets, gear setups, often voice comms required

🎯 Which Clan Type Is Right for You?

If you're a new or returning player, start with a social clan. You'll get the support and information you need without pressure. As you progress, consider adding a PvM clan for bossing or a skilling clan for XP optimization. There's no limit — many players are in a social clan chat while being part of a PvM clan's Discord server.

3. In-Game Clan Hub & Recruitment Boards

The most direct way to find a clan is inside the game itself. OSRS has a dedicated Clan Hub with Clan Recruitment Boards where clans post their listings:

📍 Where Is the Clan Hub?

The Clan Hub is located in south-eastern Varrock, near the Varrock Museum. The fastest way to get there:

  • Varrock Teleport — run south-east from the Grand Exchange
  • Grand Exchange Teleport — then walk south past the museum
  • Home Teleport (Lumbridge) — run north through Barbarian Village to Varrock

📋 How the Recruitment Boards Work

Confirmed mechanics from the OSRS Wiki:

  1. Finding listings: Click on a Clan Recruitment Board to browse a list of clans currently advertising on that world
  2. Clan profiles: Each listing shows the clan name, brief description, requirements (if any), and contact information
  3. World-specific: Each world has its own recruitment board. If you don't see good options, hop worlds and check again
  4. Contacting clans: Most listings include a clan's chat channel name or a Discord invite link
  5. Active listings: Clans must keep their listings updated — stale listings are removed periodically
💡 World-Hopping Strategy

Don't settle for the first clan you see. Visit the Clan Hub on world 301 (the official clan world) for the most listings. Then check worlds 302, 330, and 500 for different communities. Many PvM clans hang out on total-level worlds; active social clans often gather on the standard F2P and P2P worlds.

🗣️ Clan Chat Channels

Some clans invite you to "guest" in their clan chat channel before requiring membership. This is a great way to test a clan's culture:

  • Open your Clan tab (the two-people icon or type /clan)
  • Click "Join Clan" and enter the clan's chat channel name
  • You can now see their chat and talk to members without joining the clan
  • If you like the vibe, ask for a formal invite to the clan itself

5. Wise Old Man & Clan Directories

Wise Old Man (wiseoldman.net) is a third-party OSRS tracking tool that has become the go-to resource for clan management and discovery. It's widely used by serious OSRS clans for activity tracking, XP competitions, and member management.

📊 How to Use Wise Old Man to Find Clans

  1. Visit wiseoldman.net and create an account
  2. Browse the Groups section to see a directory of registered OSRS clans
  3. Each group profile shows: member count, total XP, average EHP, recent activity, and competition history
  4. Filter by group type, minimum requirements, or activity level
  5. Most group listings include a Discord invite link or in-game clan name

🎯 Step-by-Step: Find a Clan in 20 Minutes

  1. 5 min: Visit the Clan Hub on world 301. Read 5-10 recruitment board listings. Note 2-3 clans that sound interesting.
  2. 5 min: Search those clans on Wise Old Man. Check their member count, activity level, and recent competitions.
  3. 5 min: Join their Discord servers (linked from the recruitment listing or Wise Old Man profile). Read the rules and browse their channels.
  4. 5 min: Guest in their in-game clan chat. Say hello and see how the conversation flows. Active clan chats should have recent messages even during off-peak hours.
  5. Decision: Pick the clan that feels like the best fit. You can always switch later — there's no penalty for leaving a clan.

Reddit and the official OSRS forums remain active sources for clan recruitment, especially for niche communities:

The Old School RuneScape forums have a dedicated Clan Recruitment section where clans can post detailed recruitment threads. While forum traffic has decreased with the rise of Discord, many established clans still maintain forum recruitment posts as their official presence.

  • How to find: Visit the Old School RuneScape website → Community → Forums → Clan Recruitment
  • What to look for: Threads with recent replies, detailed requirements, and clear community descriptions
  • What to avoid: Threads with no replies for 3+ months — those clans may be inactive

📱 OSRS Mobile & Clan Chat

If you play on mobile, joining a clan chat is the same as desktop — open the Clan tab and search for a clan name. Mobile players often prefer text-based clan chats (vs. Discord voice chat), so look for clans that specifically welcome mobile players.

7. How to Choose the Right Clan

Once you've found potential clans, here are the concrete criteria to evaluate before committing:

④ Checklist for Choosing a Clan

1. Check Activity Levels

Guest in the clan chat for 2-3 different times of day. Is there conversation happening at 10 AM? 3 PM? 9 PM? A dead clan chat at any time zone you play in means you'll be talking to yourself.

2. Assess the Culture

Read the chat for 15-20 minutes. Are members helpful to each other? Is there drama or toxicity? Do they welcome questions from new/guest members? The vibe in chat tells you everything about the clan's culture.

3. Verify Requirements

If a clan advertises "base 80 combats required," ask if that's enforced. Some clans are flexible; others will deny your application if you're level 79 Defence. Be honest about your stats to avoid wasting everyone's time.

4. Check Event Frequency

Ask about their event schedule. How often do they do boss masses? Raids? Skilling competitions? A clan that matches your preferred activities will keep you engaged long-term.

5. Evaluate Discord Integration

Most active clans have a Discord server. Join it and look around — how organized are the channels? Is there a dedicated raids team-up channel? Gear advice section? Market/economy discussion? Good organization reflects good clan leadership.

⚠️ Red Flags to Watch For
  • Overly aggressive recruitment — clans that spam you to join are often desperate for members (for good reason)
  • Pay-to-join models — legitimate clans do not charge entry fees. Any clan asking for GP or items to join is a scam
  • No Discord or external communication — modern clans need a Discord or equivalent for coordination. Clans without one are usually inactive
  • Excessive drama in chat — if you see heated arguments or personal attacks in your first visit, it won't improve
  • One-person leadership — clans run by a single person tend to collapse when that person quits or burns out

8. Starting Your Own Clan

If you can't find a clan that fits, you can always create your own. Here's what's required:

⑤ How to Create a Clan in OSRS

  1. Open the Clan tab (the two-people icon) and click "Create Clan"
  2. Choose a clan name — must be unique and follow Jagex naming rules (no offensive language, no impersonation)
  3. Set a clan broadcast message — this appears when your clan is mentioned in chat
  4. Set join requirements — optional, can be left open to all
  5. Start recruiting — use the Clan Recruitment Board at the Clan Hub
  6. Build your community — consistent activity, regular events, and a welcoming culture keep members around
💡 Tips for New Clan Leaders
  • Start with friends and existing connections — a clan of 5 active friends is better than 50 inactive strangers
  • Use a Discord server from day one — even if it's basic, it establishes communication
  • Set clear rules about behavior, recruiting, and clan chat use — ambiguity causes conflict
  • Promote active, trustworthy members to ranks that can invite and moderate
  • Host one event per week minimum — boss masses, skill of the week, or just a drop party keeps engagement

9. Clan Finding FAQs

Where is the best place to find an OSRS clan in 2026?

The Clan Hub in south-eastern Varrock is the best starting point. Visit on world 301 for the most listings. Cross-reference your top candidates on Discord and Wise Old Man for activity levels and culture.

Do I need high stats to join a good clan?

Not at all. Social and skilling clans rarely have stat requirements. Many PvM clans have learner-friendly tiers or mentor programs. Be honest about your level — most clans would rather teach a motivated new player than recruit a maxed account that never logs in.

Can I be in multiple clans?

You can only be a member of one clan at a time. However, you can guest in other clans' chat channels to talk to their members. This is a great way to stay connected with multiple communities.

How do I leave a clan?

Open the Clan tab, click your clan name, and select "Leave Clan". There is no cooldown or penalty. You can join another clan immediately. If you're leaving because of drama or toxicity, consider also blocking affected players.

What's the difference between a clan chat and a clan?

A clan chat (CC) is an informal chat channel anyone can join. A clan is a formal group with ranks, broadcast messages, and the ability to represent the clan name. Most clans use both: the CC for public guests to hang out, and the formal clan for core members.

Are there Ironman-only clans?

Yes. Many active Ironman clans exist for standard Ironmen, Hardcore Ironmen, and Ultimate Ironmen. Search the Clan Hub or Discord for "Ironman clans" to find them. These are great communities for Ironman-specific advice, boss mass coordination, and celebrating account milestones.

Can I find a clan through the OSRS Wiki?

The OSRS Wiki does not host a clan directory, but the Clan recruitment board page explains the mechanics of the in-game system. For finding actual clans, use the in-game boards, Discord, or third-party directories like Wise Old Man.

What if I'm shy about joining a clan?

That's completely normal. Start by guesting in a clan chat — you don't need to say anything. Just read the conversation for a few days. When you feel comfortable, ask a simple question like "How do I get started with X boss?" Most clan members love helping new people. The OSRS community is famously helpful — use that to your advantage.

What's the fastest way to find a raids-ready clan?

Search for "We Do Raids" or similar raiding communities on Discord. These are dedicated to teaching and running raids (CoX, ToA, ToB). They have verified instructors, gear check systems, and scheduled raid teams. Most require base 80-85 combat stats and a willingness to learn.
