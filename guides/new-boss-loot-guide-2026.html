2026 New Bosses Overview

2026 brought several exciting new bosses to Old School RuneScape, expanding the endgame PvM landscape. This guide covers all new boss content released throughout the year, with a focus on the headline boss: Moolord in the Moolrise area.

The new bosses introduced in 2026 include:

  • Moolord — A bovine-themed boss located in the Moolrise (requires completion of the "Blood Moon Rises" quest)
  • Whisperer (Enhanced) — An upgraded version with new mechanics
  • Vault of Ralos Mini-Bosses — New raid encounters
Tip: Moolord is widely considered the most accessible new boss for mid-to-high level players, with relatively simple mechanics but high profit potential.

Moolord — The Bovine Terror

Moolord is the flagship boss of the 2026 updates, located in the Moolrise — a new area accessible after completing the "Blood Moon Rises" quest. The boss is inspired by the community's long-standing "Cow Level" meme, reimagined as a serious endgame challenge.

Location & Access

The Moolrise is accessed through a portal in the Taverley cow field after quest completion. Players need:

  • Completion of "Blood Moon Rises" quest
  • 70 Combat (recommended 90+ for efficient kills)
  • No item requirements to enter (but gear matters hugely)

Kill Mechanics & Phases

Moolord has a three-phase fight with distinct mechanics in each phase. Understanding these is key to consistent kills.

Phase 1: The Graze (100%—66% HP)

In this phase, Moolord uses primarily melee attacks with occasional "Hoof Stomp" AoE. Stay under him and use Protect from Melee.

  • Hoof Stomp: AoE attack dealing up to 25 damage. Move 1 tile away when you see the animation.
  • Attack Speed: 4 ticks (2.4 seconds) between attacks.

Phase 2: The电荷 (66%—33% HP)

Moolord becomes enraged and gains the "Milk Barrage" magic attack. He will alternate between melee and magic attacks.

  • Milk Barrage: Ranged-like magic projectiles that deal up to 35 damage. Protect from Magic helps but doesn't fully block.
  • Enrage: Attack speed increases to 3 ticks (1.8 seconds).
  • Strategy: Prayer flick between Melee and Magic protection.

Phase 3: The Stampede (33%—0% HP)

Moolord enters his final phase with all previous attacks plus "Stampede" — a charge attack that deals 40+ damage if it connects.

  • Stampede: Moolord charges across the arena. Move perpendicular to dodge.
  • Heal Mechanic: If you take too long, Moolord heals 50 HP every 30 seconds.
  • Strategy: Use high DPS gear and overloads/potions. Bring Ruby Bolt (e) for extra damage.

Special Mechanic: The Golden Calf

At 50% HP, Moolord summons two "Calf Minions" with 50 HP each. Kill them quickly or they will heal Moolord for 20 HP every 10 seconds.

Tip: Use AoE abilities (if using Revolution) or quickly switch to AoE weapon (Dragon 2h Sword spec) to clear the calves fast.

Solo vs Team Strategies

Solo Strategy

Soloing Moolord is viable at 90+ Combat stats with decent gear. Expected kill time: 2—3 minutes.

  • Use Preserve prayer to extend Prayer potion effects
  • Bring Sanfew serum to cure poison/venom and restore stats
  • Use Super combat potion + Ranging potion (for ranged switch during Phase 2)
  • Keep Emergency food (Sharks or better) for Phase 3

Team Strategy (2—5 players)

Teaming makes the fight much easier and increases kill speed significantly.

Role Recommended Gear Strategy
Tank High Defence gear, Scythe of Vitur Take all damage, use Protect from Melee/Magic
DPS 1 Scythe of Vitur / Inquisitor's Mace Max DPS, care for calves
DPS 2 Bow of Faerdhinen / Twisted Bow Ranged DPS, switch to magic for minions
Healer (optional) High Prayer bonus gear Augury/ Piety as needed, bring extra food

With a coordinated team of 3-4 players, kill times can be under 1 minute, making this very efficient for GP/hr.

Pet Drops & Drop Rates

The Moolord pet, "Moolord's Hoof", is a cute miniature hoof that follows you around. It has a base drop rate of 1/2,500 per kill.

Pet Drop Rate Modifiers

Modifier Effect on Drop Rate
None (base rate) 1/2,500
Private Kill (solo) 1/2,500 (no change)
With "Loot Share" (team) 1/2,500 per player (each player rolls separately)
Combat Achievement (Kill 50 Moolords) 1/2,000 (20% better)
Combat Achievement (Kill 250 Moolords) 1/1,500 (40% better)

Unlike some other boss pets, Moolord's Hoof does NOT desensitize — you can get it on your first kill (though the odds are very low).

Upon receiving the pet, you'll get the message: "You have a funny feeling like you're being followed..." and the pet will appear next to you.

Warning: If you already have the pet following you and get a second one, it will be sent to your bank. You can right-click the pet to "Drop" it if you want the chat badge but don't want it following you.

Ironman Strategies

Ironman accounts face unique challenges when farming Moolord. Here are tailored strategies:

Gear Progression for Ironmen

As an Ironman, you can't buy gear from the GE. Here's a progression path:

  1. Early Game: Complete "Blood Moon Rises" quest → get Dragon weapons from various bosses → farm Bandos at God Wars Dungeon
  2. Mid Game: Obtain Inquisitor's armor pieces from the Warriors' Guild (requires 400+ Knight Waves) → upgrade to Scythe of Vitur from Theatre of Blood
  3. Late Game: Farm ToB for Scythe → farm Chambers of Xeric for Twisted Bow → attempt high-enrage Moolord kills

Ironman-Specific Tips

  • Farming: Grow Snaps and other herbs for potions. Moolord drops Grimy Snaps which help sustain your supply.
  • Prayer: Moolord drops big bones and Dragon bones rarely. Use Gilded Altar in PoH for faster Prayer training.
  • Combat Achievements: Complete Moolord Combat Achievements for the rewards (explained in our Combat Achievements Guide).
  • Group Ironman: If you're in a Group Ironman, coordination is easier — split roles and share loot according to your group's rules.

Ironman Expected Loot Value

Since Ironmen can't sell drops, focus on useful items:

  • Golden Calf Gem: Use for crafting (makes Golden Calf Amulet, +15 to all combat stats)
  • Bottle of Eternal Milk: Consumable that heals 25 HP over 6 seconds (stacks up to 5), very useful for raids
  • Moolord Armor Set: Best-in-slot Strength bonus for melee (better than Bandos for crush attacks)
