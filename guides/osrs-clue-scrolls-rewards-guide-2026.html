🗺️ Clue Scroll Overview & Mechanics

Clue scrolls are Treasure Trail items that lead players through a chain of clues — digging at specific locations, solving coordinate puzzles, emoting in emote clue locations, or solving cryptic and anagram challenges — culminating in a reward casket containing loot. There are five tiers: Easy, Medium, Hard, Elite, and Master. Each tier requires progressively higher skill levels and quest completion to complete.

① How Clue Tiers Work

Each clue scroll tier has its own unique set of steps, required items, and reward table. You can only hold one clue of each tier at a time (including the casket). If you receive a clue of a tier you already have, it will be dropped on the ground or sent to your bank as a "clue nest" depending on the drop source.

  • Easy: Simple puzzles, basic emote clues, low skill requirements. Dropped by low-level monsters.
  • Medium: Moderate puzzles, beginner coordinate clues. Requires some quest completion and basic skills (30+).
  • Hard: Complex puzzles, coordinate clues, high-tier emote clues. Requires 40-70 skill levels and key quests (Falador Hard Diary recommended).
  • Elite: Demanding puzzles, multiple challenge scrolls, high skill requirements (70-90). Long completion time.
  • Master: Requires a completed hard or elite clue casket to obtain. Combines elements from all tiers with the hardest requirements.

② Stacking & Clue Nest Mechanics

You can have one active clue per tier (including the casket) in your inventory. If you're already holding a clue of that tier and get another drop, it becomes a clue nest (beginner) or drops to the ground. Use the Clue Bottle / Clue Geode system for skilling clues: bird houses (hunter) yield clue nests, and mining yields clue geodes. These stack separately from monster-dropped clues.

③ Reward Casket Mechanics

When you complete the final step of a clue trail, you receive a reward casket that you can open for loot. Each casket contains:

  • Guaranteed items: Rune items, pages, and a quantity of fire/law/nature runes
  • Common rewards: Rune armor/weapons, dragon items (tier-dependent), bolt tips, dragonstone jewelry
  • Rare rewards: God d'hide, blessed d'hide, gilded equipment, ranger boots, Robin Hood hat, 3rd age equipment
  • Master clue uniques: Ornament kits, dragon trophy items, bloodhound pet, dark tuxedo, falo the bard songs

Opening a casket also gives a small amount of clue hunter reputation, which contributes to the Collection Log and broadcasts rare drops to the entire game world.

📦 Reward Casket Mechanics & Loot Table Structure

① How Casket Loot Is Generated

Each reward casket generates loot from a structured table with multiple rolls. The typical casket gives:

  • 1-6 rune item rolls (runes, ammunition, bolt tips, arrowtips)
  • 1-2 main item rolls (armor, weapons, jewelry, or unique rewards)
  • 1 fire/law/nature rune roll (guaranteed, tier-dependent quantity)
  • Occasional tertiary rolls (master clues from hard/elite caskets)

The exact roll structure varies by tier. Higher tiers roll more total items and have higher-value base loot tables. The unique rare table (3rd age, gilded, etc.) is rolled separately from the main item table.

② Rare Reward Table Mechanics

The famous "rare reward table" is shared across all tiers but with different items available per tier. The roll structure works like this:

  • When you open a casket, the game first rolls whether you hit the rare table
  • Easy/Medium tiers roll rare table at approximately 1/1,000
  • Hard/Elite tiers roll rare table at approximately 1/500
  • Master clues roll rare table at approximately 1/200
  • If you hit the rare table, it then rolls for the specific rare item (3rd age is the rarest at ~1/5,000 per casket on elite tier)

Important: The overall 3rd age drop rate is roughly 1/10,000 per casket on elite tier, much rarer on lower tiers. You are statistically more likely to receive any gilded item than a 3rd age piece.

🏆 Master Clue Rewards — The Endgame Chase

① How Master Clues Work

Master clues are unique — they are never dropped by monsters. To obtain a master clue, you must:

  1. Complete a hard or elite clue — obtain a reward casket
  2. Take the casket to Sherlock — located on Fossil Island, near the Museum Camp
  3. Sherlock converts it — turns your hard/elite casket into a master clue
  4. You lose the casket loot — that casket's loot is forfeited

This means every master clue costs you at minimum 60K-120K GP (the hard casket value) plus the time spent on the hard/elite clue. This opportunity cost makes master clues the most expensive tier to attempt.

Generally, no — unless you are specifically hunting the bloodhound pet, 3rd age pickaxe/axe, or dark tuxedo set. The opportunity cost is too high. 🎒 Hidey-Hole System — Save Inventory Space

① What Are Hidey-Holes?

Hidey-holes are storage spaces found at emote clue locations across Gielinor. When you build a hidey-hole at a specific spot, you can store the required emote items there permanently. This means you no longer need to carry those items in your inventory when completing that emote clue — you simply interact with the hidey-hole to equip the stored items.

Hidey-holes are constructed using the Construction skill. There are hidey-holes for easy, medium, hard, elite, and master emote clues. Building all hidey-holes for a given tier gives a permanent quality-of-life improvement for clue scrolls of that tier.

③ How to Build Hidey-Holes Efficiently

To build hidey-holes, you need the required Construction level, a hammer, and the materials listed on the OSRS Wiki for each specific hole. Here's the efficient approach:

  1. Follow a route: Use the OSRS Wiki's hidey-hole location guide — it lists every hole in the optimal order by teleport route
  2. Bring bulk materials: Build all holes of a tier in one trip using a bulking inventory of planks, nails, and steel bars
  3. Use a Construction boost: Crystal saw (+3), tea (+3), or spicy stew (+5) to meet level requirements for higher-tier holes
  4. Complete achievements: The easy-through-hard hidey-holes are prerequisites for the Clue Hunter Outfit tasks

Pro tip: Building all hard hidey-holes is the single best QoL improvement for clue scrolls. Hard clues have the most emote steps (14 total), and each one requires 3-5 items that would otherwise take up your entire inventory. With all hard hidey-holes built, you can complete hard clues with just teleport items and clue scroll.

② Inventory Optimization — The 3+3 Rule

Always carry these items for every clue run:

  • 3 teleport items: Games Necklace (8), Ring of Dueling (8), Skills Necklace (8) — covers 90% of clue locations
  • 3 tools: Spade (always), knife/fletching items (for arrow-shaft-requiring steps), and a light source for underground steps

Pro tip: Use an Open plug-in (RuneLite's Clue Scroll plugin) that shows you the exact item requirements and location for each step. This saves you from having to look up every clue on the wiki.

③ Puzzle Box Solutions — Save 5+ Minutes Per Clue

Puzzle boxes (the sliding tile puzzles) appear in hard, elite, and master clues. They take most players 3-8 minutes to solve manually. Key tips:

  1. Use the RuneLite puzzle solver plugin — it overlays the solution directly onto the puzzle, cutting solve time to 30-60 seconds
  2. Learn the "layered solve" method: Solve the top row first, then the left column, then the remaining 3x3 grid
  3. Store puzzle boxes: You can drop and re-click a puzzle box to reset it — useful if you make a mistake early
  4. Bring multiple puzzle boxes: If you have multiple hard clues banked, solve all puzzle boxes at once to save mental focus

④ Coordinate Clue Shortcuts

Coordinate clues (medium, hard, elite) require you to dig at specific coordinates. Use these shortcuts:

  • Obsidian ring / Teleport crystal: Use for Tirannwn coordinate clues (elite)
  • Fairy rings: Essential for hard and elite coordinate clues — memorize the key codes
  • Fossil Island teleports: Digsite pendant for hard/elite Fossil Island coordinates
  • Mythical Cape: Teleports to Myth's Guild, saves time on certain hard/elite steps

⑤ The 10-Minute Rule — When to Drop vs Complete

A common question is whether to drop a difficult clue. Here's the 10-minute rule: if you've spent more than 10 minutes on a single step (stuck on puzzle, missing an item, can't find the location), drop the clue and get a new one. The opportunity cost of spending 30+ minutes on one clue is higher than dropping it and completing 2-3 easier clues in the same time.

Exceptions: Never drop elite or master clues — the requirement investment to obtain them is too high. For hard clues, it's a judgement call. If you're missing a quest requirement (e.g., Desert Treasure for the Ghost's hut step), it's better to complete the quest permanently than to keep dropping hard clues.

❓ FAQ — Clue Scroll Rewards

Q: Which clue tier is the most profitable in OSRS?

Hard clues are the most consistently profitable tier. They have the best GP/hr (~200K-330K) due to fast drop acquisition (hellhounds at 1/64), moderate completion times (20-35 min), and frequent mid-value uniques (god d'hide pieces at 500K-1.2M each). Elite clues have higher potential per casket but take significantly longer to complete (40-60 min). For most players, hard clues are the sweet spot.

Q: How much GP can I expect from clue scrolls on average?

Easy caskets average ~25K GP, medium ~50K GP, hard ~110K GP, elite ~250K GP, and master ~350K GP. These numbers include the expected value from rare uniques (adjusted for drop rate). Without uniques, the base loot is roughly 30-50% lower. Over a large sample size (500+ caskets), your average will approach these numbers.

Q: What are the drop rates for 3rd age equipment?

3rd age drop rates vary by tier. On easy/medium clues, the chance of any 3rd age item is roughly 1/31,680 per casket. On hard clues, it's ~1/6,528 for a specific 3rd age melee piece. On elite clues, it's ~1/5,920 for 3rd age druidic. On master clues, it's ~1/3,520 for 3rd age tools (pickaxe, axe). Overall, you need approximately 10,000+ elite caskets for a reasonable chance at any 3rd age item.

Q: Are ranger boots worth grinding for in 2026?

Ranger boots are still worth ~35M GP, but they've dropped from their peak of ~50M+. At 1/1,133 per medium casket, the expected grind is ~1,100 caskets (~150-200 hours with eclectic implings). If you enjoy clue scrolls, go for it. If you want GP, you'll make more money bossing (Vorkath, Zulrah) in the same time. The boots are BIS ranged until you can afford Pegasian crystals, so they're a worthwhile long-term goal if you use ranged often.

Q: How do I get master clues?

Complete a hard or elite clue, then take the reward casket to Sherlock on Fossil Island. He will convert your completed casket into a master clue. Important: You forfeit the casket's loot when converting. You also need 50+ in all skills (Sherlock's requirement) and must have completed Bone Voyage to access Fossil Island.

Q: What is the bloodhound pet rate from clue scrolls?

The bloodhound pet is obtained exclusively from master clue caskets at a rate of 1/1,000. There is no other way to get this pet. It is one of the rarest pets in OSRS due to the difficulty of obtaining master clues and the 1/1,000 drop rate. Most estimates suggest ~1,000 master clues for the pet, which takes 500+ hours of dedicated clue grinding.

Q: Should I build hidey-holes before doing clue scrolls?

Yes, absolutely. Building all hidey-holes for a tier saves you 3-5 inventory slots per clue step. For hard clues (14 emote steps), this is a game-changer. Start with medium and hard hidey-holes (Construction 37 and 49 respectively). The material cost is minimal (~500K-1M total for all tiers) and the time saved per clue is massive. Build hard hidey-holes before grinding hard clues — you'll thank yourself.

Q: What is the clue hunter outfit and how do I get it?

The clue hunter outfit is a 5-piece cosmetic set (gloves, boots, cloak, tunic, trousers) that provides a 2.5% boost per piece (12.5% full set) to clue scroll drop rates. All pieces are obtained from hard clue scroll reward caskets. You can also buy pieces from the Grand Exchange. The full set costs ~1M GP and is highly recommended if you grind clues regularly.

Q: Can I get duplicate clue scroll items?

Yes, you can get duplicates of most clue scroll items, including god d'hide, gilded, and trimmed armor. The only exception is 3rd age equipment — you cannot get a duplicate 3rd age item until you've obtained all pieces of that tier's 3rd age table. For example, once you get a 3rd age platebody (hard), you can get another one only after you've also obtained the full helm, platelegs, kiteshield, etc. This makes 3rd age completionist hunting even more challenging.

Q: What is the fastest way to get clues for each tier?

Easy: Pickpocket H.A.M. members (15 Thieving) — 100+ clues/hr. Medium: Catch eclectic implings in Puro-Puro (50 Hunter) — 60-80 clues/hr. Hard: Burst hellhounds in Catacombs of Kourend — ~3 clues/hr from combat + time. Elite: Bird houses (Hunter) for passive clues or kill Lizardman shamans / Wilderness bosses. Master: Complete hard clues quickly, then convert to master via Sherlock — every 2-3 hard clues can yield one master clue.

Q: Are clues still profitable after the 2026 economy changes?

Yes, clue scrolls remain profitable in 2026. While some items have dropped in value (god d'hide, ranger boots), others have held or increased (3rd age, gilded). The base rune/dragon item values are tied to high alch prices, which haven't changed. The main appeal of clues — the thrill of rare drops and the collection log chase — is timeless. If you enjoy the content, you'll make profit. If you're purely GP-focused, PvM is still better GP/hr, but clues are a fun and profitable side activity.
