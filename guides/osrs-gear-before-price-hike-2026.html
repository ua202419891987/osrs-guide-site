9. Gear Investment FAQs

How much GP do I need to start gear investing?

You can start with as little as 500K GP. Buy Sailing materials (nails, planks, tar) in bulk and hold for 2-4 weeks. With 10M GP, you can invest in Nightmare staff, Tormented bracelet, or DHCB. With 50M+, look at Soulreaper Axe, Bow of Faerdhinen, or Rigour scrolls. The key is diversification — don't put all your GP into one item.

What's the best single item to buy before the price hike?

For mid-game players: Bow of Faerdhinen (c) at ~140M. It has consistent 5-10% annual growth, is best-in-slot for most ranged content, and is directly useful for future raid content. For budget players: Tormented bracelet at ~15M — +5% magic damage, widely used, 5-10% growth expected.

When is the best time to buy gear?

Historically, gear prices are lowest in: January (post-holiday lull), after major updates (initial panic selling), and weekdays (Monday-Thursday). Prices peak during: summer (June-August, most players), weekends, and the week before major updates. Buy on Mondays, sell on Fridays for the best margins.

How do I track OSRS gear prices?

Use osrs.exchange for real-time GE prices and historical charts. The OSRS Wiki GE tracker (osrs.ge) also provides 30-day, 90-day, and 180-day price trends. For active traders, the OSRS Flips Discord and the RuneLite "GE Tracker" plugin are essential tools.

Are there any risks to buying gear now?

Yes. Jagex could announce further rebalancing that crashes prices unpredictably. The Summer Sweep-Up changes are already priced in to some extent — the remaining price increases will depend on actual player demand. Never invest more than 50% of your liquid GP in speculative purchases, and never invest GP you need for essential gear upgrades.

Should I buy gear for the new future raid content raid?

If you plan to do the raid soon, buy the gear NOW. Dragon hunter weapons, Masori armor, and Ancestral robes are likely to rise as more players attempt the raid in July-August. If you won't raid for 3+ months, wait — prices may stabilize or correct after the initial hype.

Is Sailing-related investing safe?

Sailing material investing (planks, nails, linen) is relatively safe because these items have intrinsic value — they're used for Construction and Crafting too. Even if Sailing demand drops, the materials retain value through other skills. The margin on Sailing items is also smaller (5-15%), which means less downside risk.

How long should I hold gear investments?

Short-term (2-4 weeks): Sailing materials, potions, consumables. Medium-term (1-3 months): Mid-tier weapons (BoFa, DHCB, Soulreaper Axe). Long-term (6-12 months): High-end gear (Tbow, Shadow, Scythe). The higher the item's price, the longer you typically need to hold for meaningful appreciation.

What's the best way to sell invested gear?

Use the GE's "price check" feature (right-click item in bank) to see current buy/sell prices. Place sell offers slightly below the current lowest seller for quick sales. For items over 50M GP, consider selling through the OSRS Trading Discord for better margins (avoid GE tax).
