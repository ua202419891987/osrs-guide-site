What Are Combat Achievements?

Combat Achievements (CAs) are a system of optional challenges introduced to OSRS in 2022 that reward players for accomplishing specific combat feats. Similar to Achievement Diaries, they are divided into tiers, and each completed tier grants unique rewards.

The system tracks your progress across all bosses and monsters in the game, encouraging you to learn new content and improve your PvM skills.

How to Access Combat Achievements

  • Click the skull icon (⚔️) in your game interface (next to the Quest icon)
  • Alternatively, use the game setting "Combat achievements" to enable the tracker
  • Talk to Ghommal at the Warrior's Guild to claim rewards

Key Features

  • 200+ tasks across all combat tiers
  • Progress tracking for each boss (kill count, personal bests, etc.)
  • Rewards that significantly help with PvM (Ghommal's hilt upgrades, Antique lamps, etc.)
  • Display reel — show off your completed tasks to other players
Tip: You don't need to complete CAs in order. Start with Easy tasks for quick rewards, then work your way up.

All Tiers Explained

Combat Achievements are divided into 6 tiers, each with increasing difficulty:

Tier Tasks Required Difficulty Combat Requirement
Easy ~30 tasks Very Easy — new players can complete most None (accessible at low levels)
Medium ~40 tasks Easy — early mid-game content 70+ Combat recommended
Hard ~50 tasks Medium — mid-game bosses required 90+ Combat recommended
Elite ~45 tasks Hard — high-level bosses, some speed kills 105+ Combat, 80+ stats
Master ~35 tasks Very Hard — endgame bosses, tight DPS/speed requirements 110+ Combat, 90+ stats, endgame gear
Grandmaster ~25 tasks Extreme — hardest challenges in the game Maxed/near-maxed, best gear, high skill

Total: ~225 tasks across all tiers. Some tasks overlap (e.g., a Hard task might also count toward Elite).

Tier Completion Benefits

Completing each tier grants access to the next tier's rewards. You must claim rewards from Ghommal at the Warrior's Guild.

How to Approach Grandmaster Tasks

  1. Gear Up: Max gear is essential. For melee: Scythe of Vitur, Torva, Primordial Boots. For mage: Tumeken's Shadow, Ancestral. For range: Twisted Bow, Pegasian Boots.
  2. Learn the Mechanics: Watch guides on YouTube (e.g., Woox, FPS Ducky). Practice at the boss until you can consistently get kills.
  3. Team Up: Many Grandmaster tasks are easier with a coordinated team. Find a Discord PvM clan.
  4. Use Consumables: Overloads, Brews, Anglerfish, Super Restore (4-dose). Don't skimp on supplies.
  5. Practice Mode: Some bosses have "Practice mode" (e.g., ToB). Use it to learn mechanics without risking supplies.
Warning: Grandmaster tasks can be extremely frustrating. Don't burn out — take breaks and come back later.

Ironman Considerations

Ironman accounts face unique challenges with Combat Achievements. Here's what to consider:

Ironman-Specific Challenges

  • Gear Acquisition: You can't buy best-in-slot gear. Unlocking BIS gear (Scythe, Twisted Bow, Tumeken's Shadow) takes months of farming.
  • Consumables: No buying Sharks/Brews/Overloads from GE. Must farm/skill for all supplies.
  • Lower DPS: Until you get BIS gear, your DPS will be lower, making speed-run tasks harder.

Ironman-Friendly Task Order

Focus on tasks that don't require BIS gear first:

  1. Easy/Medium tasks: Do these early (they don't need good gear).
  2. Collection log tasks: Some CAs require rare drops. Farm these passively while doing other content.
  3. Boss kill count tasks: "Kill X 50 times" tasks — do these while farming for BIS gear.
  4. Heroic tasks last: Speed kills and no-damage tasks should be attempted only after you have BIS gear.

Pro Tips for Efficient CA Completion

Here are some advanced tips to complete Combat Achievements efficiently:

  1. Prioritize High-XP Tasks: Some tasks give more progress toward tier completion than others. Focus on tasks that are quick wins.
  2. Use the Wiki: The OSRS Wiki has a complete list of all tasks with detailed strategies. Reference it often.
  3. Join a PvM Clan: Many CAs are easier with a team. Find a clan that does masses or coordinated raids.
  4. Record Your Attempts: Use OBS or similar to record your kills. You can learn from mistakes by watching the footage.
  5. Don't Burn Out: CAs are a marathon, not a sprint. Do a few tasks per session and mix in other content.
  6. Optimize Your UI: Set up your game interface to show the CA tracker. This helps you keep track of progress.
  7. Use Plugins (Runelite): Runelite has CA tracking plugins that show your progress and highlight requirements.
  8. Learn from the Best: Watch top players (Woox, FPS Ducky, etc.) on YouTube to learn efficient strategies.
Final Tip: Completing all Combat Achievements is a massive accomplishment. Don't rush it — enjoy the journey and the skills you'll learn along the way.
