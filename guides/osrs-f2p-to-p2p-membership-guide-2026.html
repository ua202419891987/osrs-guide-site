💡 The Bottom Line

You CAN reach high combat levels as F2P. Many players have maxed combat stats without ever paying. However, membership gives you 10x more content variety, faster XP rates across most skills, access to money-making methods that are literally impossible in F2P, and the ability to progress beyond "grinding the same 5 monsters forever." Think of F2P as a demo version that lets you learn the basics; membership is the full game.

2. When Should You Get Membership?

This is the #1 question every F2P player asks. Here's the honest answer based on thousands of accounts:

The Three Sweet Spots

Sweet Spot A: After Completing All F2P Quests (~Level 35–40 Combat)

If you've completed Cook's Assistant, Sheep Shearer, Imp Catcher, Rune Mysteries, Restless Ghost, Witch's Potion, Romeo Juliet, Vampire Slayer, Goblin Diplomacy, Pirate's Treasure, Prince Ali Rescue, Ernest the Chicken, Black Knight's Fortress, Dragon Slayer I, and Shield of Arrav... you've exhausted most of what F2P offers. Now is the perfect time to get membership.

Estimated time: 30–50 hours of play
Combat level at this point: ~40–55
Your bank value: ~500K–2M GP from quest rewards and basic money making

Sweet Spot B: When You Hit Level 60+ Combat (Most Popular)

By level 60+ combat, you'll have trained enough in F2P to understand game mechanics well. You've probably made some GP, know how the Grand Exchange works, and have experienced the frustration of being locked out of 85% of the game world. This is when most players subscribe.

Why this timing works: You have enough game knowledge to actually benefit from member content. Getting membership at level 10 combat means you won't be able to use most of it anyway.

Sweet Spot C: Pay for 1 Month Only, Do Key Quests, Then Go Back F2P

A popular strategy among budget-conscious players: buy just ONE month of membership, rush through the highest-value member-only quests and unlocks, then let membership expire. The benefits (quest rewards, unlocked areas) stay with your account permanently even after going back to F2P.

What to accomplish in 1 month: Complete Tree Gnome Village, Horror from the Deep, The Grand Tree, Desert Treasure I (for Ancient Magicks), Monkey Madness I (for Dragon Scimitar), Recipe for Disaster (for Barrows Gloves), Fairy Tale II (for Fairy Rings). These quests give permanent benefits that transform your F2P experience.

⏰ My Recommendation

Get membership once you hit level 50+ combat. At this point you've learned the basics, earned enough to appreciate member content, and have enough stats to actually use what membership unlocks. If you can't afford it yet, stay F2P until you've saved up 2M GP (enough to cover 2 months via bonds).

💰 Can You Afford Membership Just From F2P?

Yes! Here's the math: As a F2P player, you can make roughly 150K–300K GP/hour doing basic methods like killing Hill Giants, collecting cowhides, or running essence. In about 15–25 hours, you'll earn enough for one bond (14 days of membership). That means if you grind for a weekend, you get two weeks of membership essentially for "free."

4. OSRS Bond Guide: Step-by-Step Purchase Process

Method 1: Buying a Bond with Real Money (Cheapest)

📋 Steps:

  1. Login to the OSRS website (oldschool.runescape.com) with your account credentials
  2. Go to "Account" → "Manage Membership"
  3. Select "Subscribe with Bond" OR "Subscribe directly"
  4. Choose your duration (1/3/6/12 months)
  5. Complete payment via credit card, PayPal, or other supported method
  6. Membership activates immediately — no need to re-login

Method 2: Buying a Bond with In-Game Gold (Popular)

📋 Steps:

  1. Open the Grand Exchange (GE) interface anywhere in-game
  2. Search for "Old School bond"
  3. Click the highest available buy offer (current market price)
  4. Wait for the purchase to complete (usually instant or minutes)
  5. Open your inventory and right-click the bond → "Redeem"
  6. Choose "14 days of membership"
  7. Confirm — membership activates immediately!

Method 3: Buying from Another Player (Sometimes Cheaper)

In the Grand Exchange, you can also place a lower-than-market-price offer and wait for someone desperate to sell their bond quickly. This can save you 100K–200K GP per bond but may take hours or days to fill.

💡 Pro Tip: Buy During Updates

When a major OSRS update drops, many players rush back and sell bonds they've been holding. This temporarily floods the market and drives prices down by 100K–400K GP per bond. If you're planning to buy a bond soon, check the news feed and time your purchase around update days!

6. Your First 24 Hours as a Member — Action Plan

So you just got membership. Don't waste time wondering where to start. Follow this optimized plan:

🕐 Hour 0–1: Immediate Setup (Do This First!)

  1. Claim your 7-day membership loyalty reward — Open the Extras tab → Claim reward (free items for new members)
  2. Buy a Dwarf Multi-cannon from any dwarf cannon seller (Nulodion, Grand Exchange) — ~600K GP setup cost
  3. Set your respawn to Lumbridge if not already (talk to Father Aereck)
  4. Check your spellbook — If you haven't done Rune Mysteries, do it NOW (unlocks basic rune crafting and Magic Guild)

🕐 Hour 1–4: High-Impact Quests (Permanent Benefits)

Do these THREE quests immediately — each takes 15–30 minutes and gives permanent game-changing benefits:

  1. The Grand Tree (~30 min) → Spirit Tree teleports everywhere
  2. Tree Gnome Village (~20 min) → Spirit Tree access + agility course
  3. Vampire Slayer was F2P but now do Monkey Madness I prep quests first

🕐 Hour 4–8: Money Making Upgrade

With membership, switch to these better money-making methods:

  1. Start Slayer — Visit any Slayer Master (Turael in Burthrope is easiest). Even Turael tasks earn 10x more than F2P grinding
  2. Try Barrows — With 43 Prayer (Protect prayers) and 50+ combat, you can attempt Barrows for 500K–1.5M per hour
  3. Nature Running via Abyss — If you did Rune Mysteries, run nature runes through the Abyss. 300K–500K/hour, zero risk

🕐 Hour 8–16: Core Skill Unlocks

  1. Get 37 Agility (Gnome Stronghold course) → Unlocks graceful outfit (run forever basically)
  2. Start Construction → Build a Chapel with Gilded Altar (350% prayer XP)
  3. Start Farming → Plant your first herb/trees at any patch (they grow while offline!)
  4. Unlock Fairy Tales Part I → Access to Fairy Ring network (massive travel shortcut)

🕐 Hour 16–24: First Boss Attempt + Long-Term Prep

  1. Try your first Barrows run — Use Protect Prayers (you read Stage 0.11!) against each brother's attack style
  2. Start Desert Treasure I → Takes several hours total but unlocks Ancient Magics (game-changing magic)
  3. Set up daily farm runs — Herb patches, tree patches, bird house runs (passive income while you sleep)

8. Membership FAQs

Is membership worth it for casual players who only play 2–3 hours per week?

Absolutely yes — arguably MORE worthwhile for casual players. Why? Because membership lets you make progress faster during limited playtime. A casual player spending 2 hours doing Slayer with membership gains more XP and GP than 5 hours of F2P grinding. Plus, your membership doesn't expire when you're not playing — those 14 days last whether you log in daily or once a week. So buy a bond, play when you can, and don't feel pressured to "get your money's worth" by grinding.

Can I pay for membership entirely with in-game gold?

Yes, this is extremely common. Many players never spend real money on membership — they earn enough GP to buy bonds indefinitely. Once you're making 500K+/hr (achievable within weeks of getting membership), you only need to grind about 9–10 hours per month to fund another bond. For active players, this is easily sustainable.

What happens to my member items when membership expires?

Member items stay in your bank — you don't lose them. But you cannot wear them, use them, or equip them. They become visually greyed out in your inventory/worn equipment. As soon as you reactivate membership, everything becomes usable again instantly. Quest rewards, skill levels, and unlocked areas remain permanently yours.

Should I buy 1 month or use a bond?

It depends on your situation:

  • Use real money subscription if: You want the cheapest rate ($8.29/month with annual sub), you don't have much GP, you prefer set-and-forget auto-renewal.
  • Use bonds if: You have plenty of GP (2M+), you want flexibility (no recurring charge), you're under 18 and can't pay online, you enjoy earning your membership in-game.

Is there a free trial for membership?

Jagex occasionally offers free 3-day or 7-day trials (usually around major updates or holidays). Check the OSRS website homepage or in-game news feed. These are rare but worth claiming when available. Even 3 days is enough to complete 5–10 key member quests that give permanent benefits.
