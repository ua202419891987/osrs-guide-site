Complete Training Overview

Old School RuneScape features 23 trainable skills, each offering unique gameplay mechanics and rewards. These skills are broadly categorized into four groups: Combat Skills, Gathering Skills, Production Skills, and Support Skills. Understanding these categories is crucial because they interact with each other in the OSRS economy. Gathering skills typically make money while training, Production skills typically cost money to train but create valuable items, Combat skills are essential for PvM and progression, and Support skills provide utility that enhances your overall account progression.

In 2026, the OSRS meta has continued to evolve with several quality-of-life updates and new content releases. The introduction of new training methods and the adjustment of existing mechanics mean that some traditional training paths have been optimized further. This guide reflects all the current meta strategies that experienced players and speedrunners are using. Whether you are following a beginner skills overview or planning an efficient ironman account, the methods outlined here represent the most current and effective approaches available in the game today.

Combat Skills Training

Combat skills form the foundation of any OSRS account. The combat skills include Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Ranged, Magic, and Prayer. Additionally, Slayer is considered both a combat and support skill that provides excellent combat experience while earning profit. Training combat skills efficiently in 2026 requires understanding the combat triangle, utilizing the best weapons and gear for your level, and choosing the right monsters or training methods for your goals.

For melee training, the fastest method remains using the best available weapon with the highest strength bonus. At low levels (1-50), training at cows,阿尔Khazard Warriors, or using the quest rewards to boost your levels quickly is recommended. Mid-level training (50-70) can be done at Hill Giants, Moss Giants, or through controlled Slayer tasks. High-level training (70-99) is best done at Slayer monsters, Nightmares Zone (NMZ), or specific bosses depending on your budget and preferences. The Hitpoints training guide provides detailed methods for efficiently gaining HP levels alongside your primary combat stats.

Ranged training has several excellent options in 2026. From level 1-50, ranging giants or using Wintertodt for early ranged experience (through the reward lamps) is effective. At level 50+, chinning at monkeys in the Ape Atoll dungeon remains one of the fastest methods, though it is expensive. Alternatively, training Ranged through Slayer tasks provides excellent profit while still gaining good experience rates. High-level players can use the Toxic Blowpipe with dragon darts for exceptional DPS and experience rates.

Magic training offers the most diverse training methods among combat skills. From extremely low costs using curse spells andMagic training, to high-speed methods using high-level spells like Ice Barrage, there is an approach for every budget. Splashing was nerfed years ago, so active training methods are now required for reasonable experience rates. The fastest Magic training involves casting high-level combat spells on monsters like Maniacal Monkeys or using the Awakened bosses for experience. Our cheap Magic training guide covers budget-friendly approaches in detail.

Prayer is unique among combat skills as it is trained almost entirely by burying bones or using altars. The Prayer training guide details all the methods from 1-99. The fastest (but most expensive) method involves using Dragon Bones or better yet, Pegasian Crystals at a gilded altar with incenses burning. More budget-conscious players can use Big Bones or Dragon Bones at normal altars. Completing quests also provides substantial Prayer experience, which should always be done first to save millions of GP.

Gathering Skills Training

Gathering skills in OSRS include Mining, Fishing, Woodcutting, Farming, and Hunter. These skills are unique because they typically generate profit while training. In 2026, the Grand Exchange economy remains dynamic, so always check current prices before committing to a training method. The beauty of gathering skills is that you can often recoup your training costs or even make substantial profits while gaining experience.

Mining has seen several updates that have improved lower-level training. From level 1-15, mining copper and tin is standard. From 15-30, consider mining iron ores in the Mining Guild. At higher levels, Amethyst mining (92+) provides excellent experience and profit. For pure speed, 3-tick mining with granite at the Mining Guild (60+) offers some of the fastest rates in the game. Our complete Mining guide covers all methods from 1-99 with detailed GP/hour calculations.

Fishing is one of the most AFK-friendly skills in OSRS. From level 1-20, shrimp and anchovies at any fishing spot work well. At level 20+, trout and salmon provide a good balance of speed and profit. The real money-making begins at level 76 with Anglerfish, and at level 82 with Minnows (which can be cooked for additional Cooking experience). In 2026, the Tempoross boss also provides an excellent alternative Fishing training method with additional rewards.

Woodcutting follows a similar progression pattern. From level 1-15, regular trees. 15-30, oak trees. 30-45, willow trees (especially in Draynor Village). At higher levels, teak and mahogany trees provide excellent experience if you have the Farming patches available. For maximum profit, magic trees and redwood trees are the best options. The Farming guide explains how to integrate tree runs into your Woodcutting training for maximum efficiency.

Farming is perhaps the most unique skill in OSRS because it is time-gated rather than action-gated. The most efficient way to train Farming is through doing tree runs and fruit tree runs every day. This method is incredibly time-efficient because you only need to spend a few minutes per day planting and harvesting. In 2026, the introduction of the Farming Guild and new herbs has made herb running even more profitable. Our Farming guide provides a complete tree run schedule and profit calculator.

Hunter training involves catching various creatures across Gielinor. At low levels, catching rats in the Rat Pits or birds in the Piscatoris Hunter area is effective. At level 60+, Red Chinchompas provide the best experience and profit combination. At level 80+, Maniacal Monkeys offer even faster experience but require accessing the Ape Atoll area. The Hunter skill also integrates well with Slayer training since many Slayer monsters drop valuable loot that offsets training costs.

Production Skills Training

Production skills include Smithing, Crafting, Fletching, Herblore, Cooking, and Firemaking. These skills typically cost money to train but create valuable items that are used throughout the game. In 2026, the most efficient approach to production skills often involves buying materials from the Grand Exchange and converting them into finished products, though some players prefer the "gather and make" approach for a more self-sufficient feeling.

Crafting is notorious for being one of the most expensive skills to train to 99. The fastest method involves making gold jewelry or dragonhide armor, both of which require substantial GP investment. Budget players can use glassblowing or spinning flax into bowstrings for slower but much cheaper experience. Completing quests that provide Crafting experience is highly recommended before starting dedicated training. Our detailed Crafting guide breaks down every method from 1-99 with exact GP costs.

Smithing has been revolutionized by the addition of the Artisans Workshop and Blast Furnace. For pure speed, smelting gold bars at the Blast Furnace (after completing the Family Crest quest) is the fastest Smithing training method, though it requires substantial GP. For a more balanced approach, smithing at the Artisans Workshop provides good experience rates with lower costs. High-level smithing (85+) allows you to create high-level armor and weapons, which can offset some training costs.

Herblore is another expensive but essential skill. Making potions requires herbs and secondary ingredients, both of which must be purchased or gathered. The most cost-effective approach involves buying herbs from the Grand Exchange and cleaning them for initial levels, then making the most efficient potions at each level bracket. Completing quests that provide Herblore experience should always be your first step. In 2026, the herb prices fluctuate based on the current Wintertodt meta and bossing activity, so timing your Herb purchases can save millions.

Fletching is one of the most straightforward production skills. It involves cutting logs into bows and strings them, or making arrows and bolts. The experience rates are decent, and the cost is moderate. Many players train Fletching alongside Woodcutting by using their own logs, though buying materials from the GE is faster. At higher levels, making dragon arrows or amethyst arrows provides the best experience rates.

Cooking is one of the most commonly trained skills and is essential for sustaining through PvM content. The fastest way to train Cooking is at the Hosidius Kitchen in Great Kourend, which provides a reduced burn rate. Alternatively, cooking at the Cooking Guild or using the Myth's Guild provides good rates. Many players combine Cooking training with Farming runs by cooking the fish they catch, creating a nice synergy between gathering and production skills.

Firemaking is typically trained as a byproduct of Woodcutting (by burning the logs you cut) or as a standalone skill using logs purchased from the GE. The fastest method involves burning mahogany logs or maple logs at the Wintertodt. In fact, many players combine Firemaking training with Wintertodt grinding, which provides Firemaking experience, gold, and other valuable rewards simultaneously. This is one of the best examples of efficient multi-skill training in OSRS.

Support Skills Training

Support skills in OSRS include Agility, Thieving, Slayer, Runecraft, and Construction. These skills provide utility that enhances your overall account and makes other training more efficient. Support skills often have unique training methods that don't fit neatly into the gathering/production categories, making them some of the most interesting skills to train.

Agility is trained by completing obstacle courses throughout Gielinor. Each course provides experience and sometimes useful shortcuts that benefit your account permanently. The best courses for each level bracket are: Gnome Stronghold (1-35), Agility Pyramid (30-50), Falador (40-60), Seers' Village (60-70), Feldip Hills (48-60 if hunting), and Ardougne (70-85+). At level 85+, the Hallowed Sepulchre provides by far the fastest Agility experience in the game, along with excellent loot rewards.

Thieving is trained by pickpocketing NPCs or stealing from stalls. The most efficient route involves blackjacking (desert bandits) from level 45-91 for the fastest experience rates. At level 91+, Thieving can be effectively AFK'd at the Ardougne Knights (pickpocketing). Thieving is an excellent moneymaker, especially at higher levels where you can pickpocket valuable NPCs. It also pairs well with questing since many quests provide Thieving experience.

Runecraft is widely considered the most tedious skill in OSRS, but it is also one of the most important. Runes are essential for Magic training and PvM, and having high Runecraft levels allows you to craft your own runes for free. The fastest method involves using the Abyss to craft runes, though this requires completing the Enter the Abyss miniquest. Our all skills overview guide provides a complete Runecraft training roadmap with exact experience rates at each level.

Construction is trained by building and removing objects in your Player-Owned House (POH). The fastest method involves building oak larders or mahogany tables, depending on your budget. Having a high Construction level is extremely valuable because it unlocks useful POH features like jewelry boxes, restoration pools, and teleport portals. Many players consider Construction one of the best quality-of-life investments for an account, as it saves tremendous amounts of time in the long run.

F2P vs P2P Comparison

Free-to-Play (F2P) and Pay-to-Play (P2P) accounts have vastly different training options in OSRS. F2P players have access to only a limited subset of skills and training methods, while P2P players have access to the full game. Understanding these differences is crucial for setting realistic expectations and planning your account progression effectively.

In F2P, the available skills are: Attack, Strength, Defence, Hitpoints, Ranged, Magic, Prayer, Mining, Smithing, Fishing, Cooking, Firemaking, Woodcutting, Crafting, and Runecraft. Noticeably absent are: Agility, Herblore, Thieving, Slayer, Farming, Construction, Hunter, and Fletching. This means F2P accounts cannot access some of the most powerful training methods and items in the game. F2P skill training is generally slower and less diverse than P2P training.

P2P accounts, on the other hand, have access to all 23 skills and significantly more training methods for each skill. For example, F2P Mining caps at level 59 effectively (no better rocks available), while P2P players can mine Amethyst, Runite, and other high-level ores. P2P also provides access to the Blast Furnace, the Artisans Workshop, and numerous other training facilities that dramatically improve experience rates and reduce costs.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1: What is the fastest skill to reach 99 in OSRS?

The fastest skill to reach 99 in OSRS is typically Prayer if you have the GP to burn. With Dragon Bones at a Gilded Altar and incenses burning, you can reach 99 Prayer in as little as 10-15 hours of active playtime, though it will cost you 60-120 million GP depending on current bone prices. Construction is also very fast, taking about 10-20 hours with enough GP. However, if you are looking for the fastest "free" skill (where you don't have to spend GP), Agility at the Hallowed Sepulchre or Cooking at the Hosidius Kitchen can be very fast.

Q2: What is the most expensive skill to train to 99 in OSRS?

The most expensive skill to train to 99 is usually Construction, followed closely by Prayer and Herblore. Construction can cost upwards of 70-130 million GP if you use the fastest methods (oak larders or mahogany tables). Prayer at a Gilded Altar with Dragon Bones costs around 60-120 million GP. Herblore for 99 can cost 80-150 million GP depending on the potions you choose to make and the current Grand Exchange prices. Our all skills overview guide has detailed cost breakdowns for each skill.

Q3: Can I reach 99 in all skills as a F2P player?

No, F2P players cannot reach 99 in all skills because several skills are P2P-exclusive. The F2P skills cap at different levels depending on the available content. For example, F2P Mining effectively caps around level 59 (no higher-level ores available), and F2P Smithing caps around level 50-60. To max all 23 skills to 99, you need a P2P membership. However, F2P players can still achieve "maxed" status within the F2P skill set, which is a notable achievement for F2P-only accounts. Check out our new player guide for more information on starting as F2P.

Q4: What are the best AFK training methods in OSRS for busy players?

OSRS has excellent AFK training options for players who want to multitask or have limited active playtime. Some of the best AFK methods include: Motherlode Mine for Mining (completely AFK with periodic reset), Tempoross for Fishing (semi-AFK), Teak trees for Woodcutting (plant and come back), Ardougne Knights for Thieving (almost completely AFK at high levels), ZMI Altar for Runecraft (semi-AFK), and various Slayer tasks that require minimal clicking. Our beginner skills overview highlights the most AFK-friendly methods for each skill.

Q5: Should I follow a quest order or focus on skilling first?

Following a quest order is almost always more efficient than focusing purely on skilling, especially for new accounts. Quests provide substantial experience rewards in various skills, unlock new training areas and methods, grant access to better gear, and often provide lore that enhances your enjoyment of the game. A well-planned quest order can easily save you dozens of hours of grinding. Many experienced players recommend completing all quest-experience-rewarding content before starting dedicated skill training, as this gives you a massive head start.

Q6: How much does it cost to max all 23 skills in OSRS?

The total cost to max all 23 skills from 1-99 in OSRS (using the fastest methods) is approximately 350-500 million GP as of 2026. This assumes you are paying for the most expensive training methods for skills like Prayer, Construction, Herblore, and Crafting. However, if you use slower but cheaper methods, you can significantly reduce or even eliminate these costs — some skills like Mining, Fishing, and Woodcutting actually make money while training. Completing profitable Slayer tasks can also fund your production skill training.

Final Tips for Efficient Training

Before we conclude this comprehensive guide, here are some final tips to maximize your training efficiency in OSRS. First, always prioritize quests that provide experience rewards — this cannot be overstated. Second, consider your account type: main accounts, ironman accounts, and hardcore ironman accounts all have different optimal paths. Third, don't be afraid to mix methods — you don't have to use the "fastest" method if you find it boring. OSRS is a game, and enjoyment should be your primary goal. Fourth, take advantage of Wintertodt and other activities that train multiple skills simultaneously. Finally, engage with the community — the OSRS bossing community and skilling communities have a wealth of knowledge that can help you optimize your training even further.

Remember that efficiency is not just about experience rates — it is also about cost-effectiveness, enjoyment, and integration with your overall account goals. A well-rounded account that can handle all types of content is more valuable than a specialized account that can only do one thing well. Use this guide as a reference, but don't be afraid to deviate from it based on your personal preferences and in-game circumstances. The beauty of OSRS is that there are many valid ways to play the game, and part of the fun is discovering your own path. Good luck with your training, and we'll see you in Gielinor!
