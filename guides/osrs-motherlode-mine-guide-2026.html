1. What is Motherlode Mine?

The Motherlode Mine (MLM) is a members-only mining training area located in the Dwarven Mountains, accessible via the Mining Guild entrance in Falador. Unlike traditional mining where you mine ore directly from rocks, MLM uses a unique pay-dirt mechanic — you mine pay-dirt from veins, deposit it into a hopper, and collect the processed ore from a sack on the other side.

MLM is widely considered the most popular mining method in OSRS for several reasons:

  • Low effort / semi-AFK — Veins respawn reliably, so you can mine with minimal attention
  • No competition for resources — Each player sees their own ore veins
  • Consistent XP without banking — The hopper-sack system handles ore collection automatically
  • Valuable rewards — Coal, gold, mithril, adamantite, and runite ore, plus unique shop items
  • Good GP/hr for low requirements — Especially from 72 Mining onward

Originally released in July 2014 as part of the Motherlode Mine update, this content has remained a staple of Mining training for over a decade. It was the first skilling content to introduce the instance-based vein system, which Jagex later applied to other skills like Woodcutting (Woodcutting Guild) and Fishing (Fishing Guild). The rewards shop has been expanded over the years, adding the gem bag and superior mining gloves in subsequent updates.

The mine is operated by Prospector Percy, who runs the rewards shop, and Mining dwarf NPCs that offer occasional dialogue about mining techniques. The area also features a bank chest right next to Percy, making it easy to bank your collected ores between sack emptying sessions.

One of the biggest advantages of MLM is its accessibility. Unlike granite mining which requires tick manipulation or volcanic mine which requires group coordination, MLM can be done by any player with level 30 Mining. It's also one of the few training methods that remains viable from level 30 all the way to 99 without switching methods. Many players choose to do MLM from 30 to 99 exclusively because the convenience and consistency outweigh the slightly slower XP rates compared to granite or blast mining.

3. How to Start Motherlode Mine

Getting There

The Motherlode Mine entrance is located inside the Dwarven Mines. Here are the fastest ways to reach it:

  • Mini-game Teleport (Favorites) — After your first visit, you can use the Mini-game Teleport to MLM every 20 minutes
  • Falador Teleport → Mining Guild — Teleport to Falador, run east to the Mining Guild entrance, go down the ladder, and head north-west
  • Necklace of Passage (Wizard's Tower option) — Teleport south-west and run north through the Dwarven Mines
  • Skills Necklace → Mining Guild — Quick access to the Mining Guild entrance, then run north through the mines

Your First Visit

  1. Enter the Dwarven Mines via the Mining Guild entrance in east Falador
  2. Head north-west until you find the wooden structure and NPC named Prospector Percy
  3. Walk through the tunnel into the main mining area — you'll see the ore veins, hopper, and sack
  4. Equip your pickaxe and start mining pay-dirt from the veins in the Lower Level
  5. Deposit your pay-dirt into the hopper (the wooden chute structure)
  6. Pull the lever next to the hopper to process the pay-dirt
  7. Walk around to the sack to collect your processed ore

New Player Tip: Alt-click the hopper lever to "Walk Here" and pull it in one click. This saves a right-click step every time you deposit pay-dirt. Also, you can quickly deposit all pay-dirt by using it on the hopper rather than right-clicking.

4. Pay-Dirt & Sack Mechanics

The Motherlode Mine runs on a simple but unique cycle: Mine → Deposit → Process → Collect. Understanding the mechanics is key to maximizing your efficiency.

When you mine a pay-dirt vein, you receive pay-dirt into your inventory. Unlike regular mining where the ore goes directly into your inventory, pay-dirt is a generic item that must be processed to reveal what ore it contains. Each piece of pay-dirt takes up one inventory slot, so managing your inventory space is important — you can hold a maximum of 28 pay-dirt at a time before you must deposit.

The Mining Cycle

  1. Mine pay-dirt from the veins. Each vein yields 3-6 pay-dirt before collapsing. Veins respawn after 6-12 seconds.
  2. Deposit pay-dirt into the hopper. You can store multiple pay-dirt at once.
  3. Pull the lever to process the pay-dirt into the sack system.
  4. Collect ore from the sack. The sack stores multiple types of ore at once.

Sack Mechanics

The sack at MLM stores processed pay-dirt. Here's what you need to know:

FeatureDetails
Sack Default Capacity81 pay-dirt worth of ore (without upgrades)
Sack Upgrade (Mining level 54)108 pay-dirt capacity — bought from Prospector Percy for 200 golden nuggets
Sack Upgrade (Mining level 72)162 pay-dirt capacity — bought for another 400 golden nuggets
Coal Bag StorageHolds up to 27 coal in your inventory (bought for 120 nuggets)
Gem Bag StorageHolds up to 60 uncut gems (bought for 100 nuggets)

Ore Distribution

When you process pay-dirt, each piece has a chance to produce one of the following ores, based on your Mining level:

Ore TypeMinimum LevelApprox. % Chance
Coal30~41%
Gold30~29%
Mithril30~16%
Adamantite30~10%

Each pay-dirt also has a ~14% chance of rolling no ore at all (you get nothing). The runite ore only becomes available once you reach level 72 Mining and are mining in the upper level.

💡 Pro Tip: The sack upgrade to 162 and 243 capacity is worth getting if you plan to stay at MLM long-term. A bigger sack means fewer trips to empty it and more continuous mining. Prioritize the sack upgrade before the prospector kit if you value convenience.

5. Upper Level vs Lower Level

At Mining level 72 (or with 100% Lovakengj favor), you unlock the Upper Level of the Motherlode Mine. The upper level is accessed by climbing the ladder in the south-east corner of the main mining area.

Key Differences

Runite OreNoYes (~4% chance)
Vein Density6 veins in a cluster8 veins in a tighter cluster

Crowded?More crowded at peak timesLess crowded due to higher requirement
Golden Nugget Rate~1 nugget per 29 pay-dirt~1 nugget per 30 pay-dirt (similar) + runite adds value

Should You Rush Upper Level?

Yes. The upper level is a meaningful upgrade. The veins are packed closer together, so you spend less time walking between them and more time mining. The runite ore you unlock adds a decent GP boost — at current prices (2026), each runite ore is worth around 11K-12K GP. The upper level is also noticeably quieter, with far fewer players per world.

If you're starting from level 30, expect to spend roughly 20-30 hours in the lower level before reaching 72 and graduating to the upper level.

6. Rewards — Prospector Kit, Coal Bag, Gem Bag

Prospector Percy runs the rewards shop at the entrance of the mine. You pay with golden nuggets, which you receive randomly as you process pay-dirt. On average, you get about 1 golden nugget for every 29-30 pay-dirt processed.

In total, unlocking every item from the rewards shop costs 1,180 golden nuggets — a significant investment that requires processing roughly 35,000 pay-dirt. Most players focus on the prospector kit and coal bag first, then decide whether the remaining items are worth the time investment.

Recommended Purchase Order

  1. Full Prospector Kit (180 nuggets) — The 2.5% XP boost applies to all Mining XP you earn, not just MLM. This saves tens of thousands of pay-dirt over 99.
  2. Sack Upgrade to 108 (200 nuggets) — 33% more sack capacity means fewer trips to empty it.
  3. Coal Bag (120 nuggets) — Extremely useful for blast furnace and superheating.
  4. Gem Bag (100 nuggets) — Nice to have but not essential.
  5. Sack Upgrade to 162 (400 nuggets) — Convenience upgrade for long sessions.

💡 Pro Tip: You can buy the Prospector Kit while you train — you don't need all 180 nuggets saved up. Each piece gives a partial XP bonus (e.g., the helmet alone gives 0.5%), so buy pieces as you afford them. The jacket (0.8%) and legs (0.6%) give the biggest individual boosts, so prioritize those first.

7. XP Rates & Efficiency Tips

XP Rates per Hour

Your XP rate at MLM depends on your Mining level, pickaxe, and concentration level. Here are realistic ranges:

72+ (Upper Level, dragon pick)35K-42K45K-55K
85+ (Upper Level, crystal pick)38K-45K48K-58K

Efficiency Tips

  • Use the best pickaxe you can wield. At level 30, use a rune pickaxe. Upgrade to dragon at 61, then infernal at 85 (if you have 85 Smithing). The crystal pickaxe (Song of the Elves) is the fastest option at level 71+.
  • Enable the "Prospector kit" cosmetic override — you can wear your best defensive gear while prospector kit pieces are equipped as an override (if you have the cosmetic slot unlocked).
  • Use the "Run" energy management. MLM involves a lot of walking. Bring stamina potions or wear Graceful pieces to reduce downtime.
  • Bank efficiently. When the sack is full, deposit ores into the bank chest just outside the rewards room. Don't run to the Dwarven Mines bank.
  • Consider the "Mining gloves" for the MLM sack. The superior mining gloves give double ore chance when emptying the sack, which applies to MLM ores too.
  • Use Menu Entry Swapping (Runelite). On Runelite, you can left-click the hopper lever to pull it, and left-click the veins to mine. This saves clicks over time.
  • Timing your sack emptying. In the lower level, empty the sack when it's around 70-80% full so you don't let it become full while you're AFK. Use the Game Timer notification plugin to alert you.

💡 Efficiency Verdict: MLM will never be the fastest Mining XP in the game. At peak efficiency (~55K XP/hr), it still lags behind granite (60K-70K), volcanic mine (70K-80K), and 3-tick granite (90K-100K). But MLM requires 1/10th the attention of those methods. For most players, the trade-off is well worth it.

Golden Nugget Rates & Math

Golden nuggets are the currency of MLM. Here's the math behind how quickly you earn them and what you need for the key rewards:

  • Nugget rate: Approximately 1 nugget per 29-30 pay-dirt processed (roughly 3.4% chance per pay-dirt)
  • Pay-dirt per hour: At level 72+ with a dragon pickaxe, you process about 120-140 pay-dirt per hour
  • Nuggets per hour: Approximately 4-5 nuggets per hour in the upper level
  • Full Prospector Kit: 180 nuggets = ~36-45 hours of mining at MLM to afford the full set
  • Full sack upgrade pathway: 600 total nuggets (200 for first upgrade + 400 for second) = ~120-150 hours
  • All useful rewards: Prospector kit (180) + coal bag (120) + gem bag (100) + sack upgrades (600) = 1,000 nuggets total (~200-250 hours)

Most players will earn enough nuggets for the prospector kit long before they reach level 99. The 2.5% XP boost effectively saves you roughly 250K XP over the course of training from 30 to 99 (based on ~10M total XP at MLM), which translates to about 5-7 hours saved.

When to Choose MLM Over Other Methods

  • You want the best AFK training method from 30-72 — Nothing beats MLM for low-effort consistency at these levels
  • You need the Prospector Kit — The 2.5% XP boost is valuable for any remaining Mining training
  • You want steady GP while training — At level 72+, MLM earns decent money from runite ore
  • You hate banking — MLM has no banking trips. The hopper-sack system handles everything

When to Skip MLM

  • You want maximum XP/hr — If speed matters above all, granite or volcanic mine are better
  • You're already 85+ Mining — At these levels, you can AFK amethyst for better GP, or do blast mining for similar XP with more profit
  • You hate the enclosed mining environment — MLM is underground with limited visibility. Some players prefer the open sky of the desert's granite mine

FAQ — Motherlode Mine Questions

What level do you need for Motherlode Mine?

You need Mining level 30 to enter the mine and mine pay-dirt. For the upper level, you need either Mining level 72 or 100% Lovakengj favor (from the Kebos Lowlands). Level 72 is strongly recommended over grinding favor.

How much XP does Motherlode Mine give per hour?

XP rates range from 15K-55K XP/hr depending on your level, pickaxe, and efficiency. At low levels (30-40), expect around 20K-28K XP/hr. At high levels (85+) with a crystal pickaxe in the upper level, you can reach 48K-58K XP/hr.

How many golden nuggets for full prospector?

The full Prospector Kit costs 180 golden nuggets total — helmet (40), jacket (60), legs (50), and boots (30). On average, you'll earn about 1 nugget per 29-30 pay-dirt, meaning you'll need to process roughly 5,200-5,400 pay-dirt to unlock the full set.

Is Motherlode Mine worth it after 99?

Not really for XP, but it can be for the Coal Bag and Gem Bag if you didn't get them during training. Some ironmen return to MLM for the superior mining gloves. However, at 99 Mining, amethyst (92+) and runite rocks (85+) offer much better GP/hr with similar AFK levels.

What's the difference between upper and lower level MLM?

The upper level unlocks at Mining level 72. It has more veins packed closer together (shorter walking distance), access to runite ore (~4% of pay-dirt), and fewer players per world. The lower level has only coal, gold, mithril, and adamantite, with longer walking distances.

Can you do Motherlode Mine on an Ironman?

Yes, absolutely. MLM is one of the best mining methods for ironmen. The ores you collect stay in your inventory, and the Prospector Kit is highly recommended before doing other Mining content. The Coal Bag is also extremely valuable for ironmen using the Blast Furnace or superheating. Ironmen cannot buy ores from the GE, so MLM provides a self-sufficient source of gold, coal, mithril, and adamantite.

What pickaxe is best for Motherlode Mine?

The Crystal pickaxe (requires Song of the Elves and 71 Mining) is the best pickaxe for MLM. The Infernal pickaxe (85 Mining, 85 Smithing) is slightly slower but provides bonus Smithing XP. The Dragon pickaxe is the best budget option at 61 Mining. At lower levels, use the highest tier pickaxe you can wield — rune at 30, adamant at 30+.

Final Tips — Master the Motherlode Mine

  • Start at level 30 and don't leave until 72 — MLM is the best option from 30-72 by a wide margin
  • Buy the Prospector Kit first — Each piece gives a stacking XP bonus, and the full set is 2.5% more XP for all Mining
  • Upgrade your pickaxe as soon as possible — Every tier upgrade is noticeable at MLM (faster mining = more pay-dirt/hr)
  • Use upper level as soon as you hit 72 — Better vein layout and runite ore make a real difference
  • Don't overthink it — MLM is designed to be low-effort. If you want max XP, do granite. If you want a chill, profitable, semi-AFK method to 99, stick with MLM
