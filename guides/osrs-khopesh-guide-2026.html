OSRS Thunder Khopesh Guide 2026 — Stats, Special Attack & How to Get It

Novice
Published: June 28, 2026

The Thunder khopesh is a unique weapon introduced to Old School RuneScape as a reward from Deadman Mode activities. Unlike the completely fictional weapons described in some older guides, the Thunder khopesh is a real item with specific stats, a distinct special attack, and a clear acquisition method. This guide covers everything you need to know: the weapon's stats, how to obtain it, its special attack mechanics, and whether it's worth your time and GP in the current 2026 meta.

What is the Thunder Khopesh?

The Thunder khopesh is a one-handed slash weapon that requires 60 Attack to wield. It was added to OSRS as part of Deadman Mode content, obtainable from Deadman's chests during Deadman Mode seasons. The weapon is imbued with the power of thunder and lightning, reflected in its unique special attack.

Unlike the rumored "Khopesh" weapons that some guides incorrectly claim drop from Tombs of Amascut or require 75 Attack, the Thunder khopesh is a real, tradeable item with a current Grand Exchange price of approximately 1,000,000 GP. It serves as a mid-game weapon option, filling a niche between the Dragon Scimitar (60 Attack) and the Abyssal Whip (70 Attack).

The Thunder khopesh sits in an interesting position: it's available at the same Attack level as the Dragon Scimitar but offers different combat applications through its special attack.

Lingering Lightning Special Attack

The Thunder khopesh's signature feature is its Lingering Lightning special attack, which consumes 50% of your special attack energy.

Special Attack Mechanics

  • Two hits in quick succession — The special attack delivers two rapid hits
  • Lightning spawn on hit — If either attack hits, additional lightning strikes the area where the target was standing
  • Area damage — The lightning deals damage to targets around the initial impact point
  • Delayed lightning chance — There's a 20% chance to summon a delayed lightning strike, adding further damage potential

Strategic Use

The Lingering Lightning special attack is particularly effective in multi-combat situations where enemies are clustered together. The area-of-effect component means you can damage multiple targets with a single special attack, making it useful for:

  • Slayer tasks — Multi-combat slayer areas where monsters stack up
  • Catacombs of Kourend bursting tasks — Pairs well with burst/barrage setups for extra damage
  • God Wars Dungeon minion clearing — Quickly damage multiple minions in boss rooms
  • Raids — Can be useful in specific raid rooms with grouped enemies

How to Get the Thunder Khopesh

The Thunder khopesh is obtained from Deadman's chests during Deadman Mode seasons. These chests spawn throughout the Deadman Mode world and can be looted for various rewards, including the Thunder khopesh.

Acquisition Methods

Method Details Difficulty
Deadman's Chests Loot chests during DMM seasons for a chance at the Thunder khopesh drop High (PvP risk)
Grand Exchange Purchase directly from other players for ~1M GP Easy (no risk)
Important: The Thunder khopesh is NOT obtained from Tombs of Amascut, Theatre of Blood, or any standard PvM boss. It is exclusively a Deadman Mode reward. If you see a guide claiming otherwise, it is incorrect.

Is It Worth It?

At ~1M GP, the Thunder khopesh is an affordable weapon, but its value depends on your account's progression stage.

Who Should Buy It

  • Players with 60 Attack who want variety — If you're bored of the Dragon Scimitar, the Thunder khopesh offers a different combat experience with its unique special attack
  • Multi-combat slayer enthusiasts — The area-of-effect special attack shines in multi-combat scenarios
  • Collection log hunters — If you're filling your collection log, this is a unique item to acquire

Who Should Skip It

  • Players close to 70 Attack — The Abyssal Whip at 70 Attack is a significant upgrade and costs only slightly more (~1.8M)
  • Budget-conscious players — The Dragon Scimitar is free (from Monkey Madness I) and deals comparable single-target DPS
  • Players focused on bossing — For single-target boss DPS, the Dragon Scimitar or Abyssal Whip are more reliable choices

FAQ

Q: Does the Thunder khopesh require any quests?

A: No. The weapon itself has no quest requirement. However, accessing Deadman Mode content (if you want to obtain it yourself rather than buying it) requires participating in the DMM game mode.

Q: Is the Thunder khopesh good for PvP?

A: Potentially. The Lingering Lightning special attack can hit multiple targets, which is useful in multi-combat PvP scenarios. However, the 50% special attack cost means you only get two specs per full bar, and the Dragon Scimitar's Sever special is generally preferred for single-target PvP.

Q: Can the Thunder khopesh be poisoned?

A: No, the Thunder khopesh cannot be poisoned or imbued. It's used as-is with no modifications available.

Q: Is the Thunder khopesh from Tombs of Amascut?

A: No. This is a common misconception. The Thunder khopesh is a Deadman Mode reward, not a ToA drop. ToA drops include the Fang, Lightbearer, and Masori armor, but not any khopesh weapon.

The Bottom Line: The Thunder khopesh is a niche but legitimate weapon at 60 Attack. Its ~1M GP price tag is affordable, and the Lingering Lightning special attack provides unique multi-target utility. However, for most players, the Dragon Scimitar (free) or Abyssal Whip (~1.8M, 70 Attack) will serve better for general-purpose combat. Buy the Thunder khopesh for variety and collection — not as your primary weapon.
