What is the Khopesh?

Weapon Overview & Stats

The Khopesh is a mid-to-high tier melee weapon that serves as a direct upgrade over traditional one-handed swords. With a +75 Slash bonus, +65 Strength bonus, and an impressive attack speed of 4 (same as a scimitar), the Khopesh excels at sustained damage output. The weapon has a notable special attack that deals 130% damage and applies a temporary defense debuff to enemies, making it exceptionally valuable in extended PvM encounters.

Weapon Requirements & Tier

To wield the Khopesh, you need:

  • 75 Attack (minimum requirement)
  • Completion of Desert Treasure II (highly recommended for location access)
  • Reasonable Combat stats (80+ Melee stats recommended)

The Khopesh sits in the "elite mid-tier" category—more accessible than Bandos Godsword but more powerful than a Saradomin Sword, yet with significantly different applications.

Why Players Choose Khopesh

The Khopesh isn't just a damage upgrade; it's a versatility weapon. Players opt for Khopesh because:

  • Exceptional DPS: Outperforms alternatives in many contexts
  • Special Attack: The unique debuff mechanic creates tactical advantages
  • Aesthetic Appeal: Unique visual design appeals to many players
  • Versatility: Works effectively across Slayer, Bossing, and Dungeoneering
  • Status Symbol: Demonstrates PvM achievement to other players

How to Get the Khopesh

Primary Acquisition Method: Tombs of Amascut Raids

The Khopesh is exclusively obtained from the Tombs of Amascut (ToA), OSRS's premier endgame raid. To begin raiding:

  • Complete the Tomb of the Regular variant (practice mode)
  • Progress to Expert Mode for Khopesh drops
  • Complete the full raid to obtain loot rolls
  • Secure 5+ unique drops to access the Khopesh pool

Khopesh Combat Performance Analysis

Understanding the Khopesh's combat performance requires examining its DPS output across different scenarios. The Khopesh excels in sustained melee combat where its fast attack speed and high accuracy combine to deliver consistent damage output over extended fights.

DPS Breakdown by Combat Scenario

Against high-defense targets like bosses in the Chambers of Xeric or Theatre of Blood, the Khopesh maintains approximately 85-90% of its maximum DPS potential. This is significantly higher than slower weapons like the Bandos Godsword, which suffers more from defense rolls on missed attacks. The key advantage lies in the Khopesh's attack speed — at 4 ticks between attacks, even partially defended hits still contribute meaningful damage through frequency.

In solo PvM scenarios such as Vorkath or Zulrah melee phases, the Khopesh delivers competitive DPS when paired with appropriate strength gear. Players report 2.5-3.5 kill per hour improvements over Dragon Scimitar setups at Vorkath when using the Khopesh with Bandos armor and a Berserker ring. The faster attack speed also means more frequent special attack opportunities when using weapon switching strategies.

Slayer Task Performance

For slayer tasks involving melee-vulnerable monsters, the Khopesh provides a noticeable quality-of-life improvement. Tasks like Abyssal Demons, Dark Beasts, and Smoke Devils complete approximately 15-20% faster compared to Abyssal Whip setups. This speed increase translates to an extra 2-4 slayer tasks per hour at higher combat levels, directly increasing both XP rates and profit per hour from slayer drops and alchables.

However, for lower-level slayer tasks where monsters have minimal defense, the Khopesh's advantage diminishes significantly. Against creatures like Hill Giants or Moss Giants, the DPS difference between Khopesh and Dragon Scimitar falls to just 5-8%, making the investment harder to justify for players primarily doing low-level slayer.

Gear Synergy and Optimal Loadouts

The Khopesh synergizes best with accuracy-boosting gear rather than pure strength setups. The recommended mid-level loadout includes: Helm of Neitiznot, Amulet of Fury, Bandos Chestplate, Bandos Tassets, Barrows Gloves, Dragon Defender, and Berserker Ring (i). This combination maximizes the weapon's fast attack speed by ensuring each hit connects reliably.

For players on a tighter budget, substituting Bandos armor with Fighter Torso and Dragon Legs maintains approximately 80% of the optimal loadout's effectiveness at roughly one-third the cost. The most impactful upgrade path follows this order: Dragon Defender → Ferocious Gloves → Bandos Armor → Infernal Cape → Amulet of Torture. Each upgrade adds 2-5% DPS improvement, with Dragon Defender providing the largest single boost at approximately 8% improvement.

Ironman Khopesh Strategy

Ironman accounts face unique challenges when pursuing the Khopesh. Without access to the Grand Exchange, every raid supply must be gathered manually. This means stocking up on Saradomin Brews, Super Restore potions, and Anglerfish before each raid attempt — easily 30-45 minutes of preparation per raid session. Ironmen should focus on farming Herblore supplies early, as potion dependency makes raiding prohibitively expensive without 85+ Herblore for self-brewed potions.

The recommended ironman progression starts with 85+ in all combat stats through slayer, 85+ Herblore for potion self-sufficiency, and completion of all prerequisite quests before entering Tombs of Amascut. Budget roughly 100-150 hours of preparation before your first successful Khopesh raid as an ironman, compared to 30-50 hours for a main account with purchased supplies. However, the satisfaction of earning this weapon entirely through your own efforts makes the journey worthwhile for dedicated ironmen.

Cost Recovery Timeline

After obtaining a Khopesh, most players wonder how quickly it pays for itself. Based on current GP/hour data, a Khopesh used in Vorkath kills generates approximately 300-500K additional GP per hour compared to Dragon Scimitar setups. At this rate, a 20M GP Khopesh recovers its cost in roughly 40-67 hours of dedicated bossing. For players who purchased the weapon from the Grand Exchange, this represents a 3-5 week payback period at 2 hours of daily gameplay. The key metric to watch is your average kill time improvement — even a 15-second improvement per Vorkath kill compounds dramatically across hundreds of kills per week.

The calculation changes significantly for raid-obtained Khopeshes. Since your raid time already counts as entertainment and skill development, the effective cost is only the supplies consumed during learning raids. Most players spend 5-15M on supplies before their first Khopesh drop, making the self-obtained weapon approximately 25-75% cheaper than purchasing one outright.

When to Upgrade from Dragon Scimitar to Khopesh

The decision to upgrade from Dragon Scimitar to Khopesh depends primarily on your PvM activities. If you primarily do slayer tasks and casual combat, the Dragon Scimitar at 50K GP delivers exceptional value that makes upgrading unnecessary until you tackle challenging bosses. However, if you're regularly attempting Tombs of Amascut, Theatre of Blood, or God Wars Dungeon bosses, the Khopesh's superior accuracy and DPS justify the investment many times over. As a rule of thumb, upgrade when your bossing income exceeds 3M GP per hour — at that point, the 15-20% DPS improvement from Khopesh pays for itself within a month of regular gameplay.

Khopesh Special Attack Strategies

The Khopesh special attack consumes 25% of your special attack energy and delivers a powerful strike with enhanced accuracy. This makes it ideal for weapon-switching strategies where you use the Khopesh special attack, then immediately switch to a Dragon Claws or Bandos Godsword for their special attacks in rapid succession. This dual-spec strategy maximizes damage output during critical fight phases, particularly against bosses with vulnerability windows like Verzik Vitur's final phase or Sotetseg's maze section. Practice the weapon-switch timing in safe scenarios before attempting it in high-stakes raid environments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get Khopesh without raiding?

A: No, the Khopesh is exclusively a raid drop from Tombs of Amascut. There's no alternative method—raiding is mandatory. However, you can buy one on the Grand Exchange if another player owns one, or you can find one in the twisted league if you participate.

Q: Is Khopesh better than Bandos Godsword?

A: It depends on context. Khopesh has higher DPS against most enemies due to faster attack speed (+4 vs +6), but Bandos Godsword has higher defense stats and different special attack benefits. For pure DPS in sustained combat, Khopesh wins. For special attack-focused strategies, BGS dominates.

Q: How much does a Khopesh cost?

A: As a raid drop, you obtain it for free once you complete the raids. However, the indirect cost is 30-90M in gear, supplies, and lost time during the learning phase. Trading on the Grand Exchange, Khopesh typically costs between 15-25M depending on market conditions.

Q: Do I need 99 Attack to use Khopesh effectively?

A: No, 75 Attack is the minimum requirement and is actually quite effective. However, your DPS increases significantly with higher Attack levels (each level adds 1 accuracy and slight damage). At 90+ Attack, the damage advantage over 75 Attack is about 25-30%. If you're seriously raiding, train to at least 85 Attack for consistency.

Q: Is Khopesh worth it vs Dragon Scimitar?

A: Dragon Scimitar costs only 50k and delivers 80-85% of Khopesh's DPS at a fraction of the cost. If you're doing casual Slayer, Dragon Scimitar is 95% as good with virtually no investment. Khopesh only makes sense if you're doing challenging PvM where that 15-25% DPS increase justifies the cost.

Q: How long do Khopesh raids actually take?

A: A typical raid takes 20-50 minutes depending on your skill level and the specific invocations you're using. Speed runners can complete raids in 12-15 minutes, while learners might take 60+ minutes. For calculating your timeline, assume 30 minutes average per raid if you're not speed running.
