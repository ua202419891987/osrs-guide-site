🔐 OSRS Account Security & Anti-Scam Guide 2026

Novice

Every day, thousands of OSRS players lose their accounts to scams and hijacking. This guide covers every common scam type, how to set up 2FA correctly, and the exact safety rules that keep your account safe — even if you're brand new to the game.

🔐 1. Why Account Security Matters

Your OSRS account is valuable — even a "starting" account with 50 total level can take 20+ hours to rebuild. An account with membership and decent stats can be worth hundreds of dollars on black markets.

Scammers target new players because they're less aware of the risks. Here's what you can lose in a single scam:

💀 What Happens After a Scam

  • GP stolen: All your gold gone in seconds
  • Items stolen: Gear, rares, untradeables transferred out
  • Account banned: Scammers often use your account to bot, getting it permanently banned
  • Email changed: You lose access entirely — Jagex support can take weeks to recover it
  • Membership wasted: Scammers drain your membership time or bond

The good news: 99% of scams are preventable. Once you know the patterns, you'll spot them instantly. This guide teaches you everything.

🛡️ The Golden Rule of OSRS Security

Jagex will never ask for your password via email, Discord, in-game chat, or any website other than the official login page. If anyone asks for your password — it's a scam. Period.

🚨 2. 5 Most Common Scam Types

Learn these patterns. Once you recognize them, you'll never fall for a scam again.

① The "Double Your GP" Scam

How it works: A player tells you they can "double your gold" or "flip your GP" if you give them money first. They show "proof" screenshots of other players who supposedly got doubled.

The truth: There is no mechanic in OSRS that doubles GP. This is 100% a scam. Once you trade them your gold, they log out and you never see it again.

How to avoid: Never send GP to another player "to be doubled." Block and report anyone who asks.

② The Phishing Link Scam

How it works: You receive a message (in-game, Discord, or email) saying "You won a giveaway!" or "Your account will be banned unless you verify." The message includes a link that looks like the real OSRS login page.

The truth: The link goes to a fake website that records your email and password. Within minutes, the scammer logs into your real account and cleans it out.

How to avoid: Never click links in DMs. Always type oldschool.runescape.com directly into your browser. Check the URL carefully — fake sites use tricks like "runescap.com" or "oldschool-runescape.com."

③ The "Trust Trade" Scam

How it works: A high-level player offers to lend you an expensive item (Dragon weapon, expensive armor) if you "trust trade" them first by giving them something valuable of your own.

The truth: Once you trade them your item, they log out. They never lend you anything. "Trust trading" is not a real game mechanic — it's just a fancy name for theft.

How to avoid: Never give an item to someone before receiving theirs. Use the official Grand Exchange for all trades, or only trade with people you know in real life.

④ The "Item Switch" Scam

How it works: You agree to buy an item from another player. They show you the real item in trade, then quickly swap it for a nearly worthless look-alike before confirming the trade.

Example: They show a Dragon Scimitar (worth ~100K), then swap it for a Dragon Longsword (worth ~60K) or a fake/untradeable version at the last second.

How to avoid: Always double-check the trade window after both players click "Accept." The second trade screen shows exactly what you're receiving. If it's not the right item, click Decline.

⑤ The "Discord Admin" Scam

How it works: Someone sends you a Discord DM claiming to be an "admin" or "moderator" of an OSRS Discord server. They say you broke a rule and need to verify your account or pay a fine.

The truth: Real moderators never DM users for account verification. They only communicate through official channels. This is a phishing attempt to steal your Discord and OSRS accounts.

How to avoid: Ignore DMs from "admins." Real moderators will @mention you in a public channel, not private message you.

🚩 Red Flags — Spot a Scam in 3 Seconds
  • Anyone promising "free GP" or "double your money" → SCAM
  • Links sent in DMs or Discord → SCAM (unless you requested help)
  • "Trust me, I'm trustworthy" → SCAM
  • Asking for your password/email → SCAM
  • Urgency ("Do this NOW or your account gets banned") → SCAM

🔒 3. How to Set Up 2FA (Step by Step)

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) is the single most important security step. Even if a scammer steals your password, they cannot log in without the 2FA code from your phone.

① Enable Jagex Account (Recommended)

In 2024, Jagex introduced Jagex Accounts — a unified login system that replaces the old login method. It includes built-in 2FA and is much more secure.

🏠 How to Create a Jagex Account

Step 1: Go to account.jagex.com (type it manually, don't click links).

Step 2: Click "Create a Jagex Account." Enter your email and a strong password (12+ characters, mix of letters/numbers/symbols).

Step 3: Jagex sends a verification email. Click the link to verify.

Step 4: In the account settings, enable Authenticator App (not SMS). Download Google Authenticator or Authy on your phone. Scan the QR code shown on screen.

Step 5: Save the backup codes in a safe place (not on your computer). These let you log in if you lose your phone.

② Set a Strong Password

Your password should be:

✅ Good Password❌ Bad Password
My-Dragon-2026!OSRSdragon123
Osrs-Lover#8842password123
Zulrah-Slayer-99!qwerty

Critical: Never reuse a password from another website. If that website gets hacked, scammers will try the same email/password on OSRS.

③ Don't Share Your Account

Jagex's rules prohibit account sharing. Even if someone claims they're "just helping you train," they could steal your account. Only you should ever log in.

🎯 Security Checklist — Do This Today

  1. Create a Jagex Account (if you haven't already)
  2. Enable 2FA via Authenticator App (not SMS)
  3. Set a unique, strong password (12+ characters)
  4. Save backup codes offline (write them on paper)
  5. Never share your password with anyone, ever

🎣 4. How to Spot Phishing Links

Phishing is the #1 way accounts get stolen. Here's how to identify fake websites instantly.

① Check the URL Carefully

The only official OSRS website is:

✅ Official: https://oldschool.runescape.com

✅ Official: https://account.jagex.com

❌ Fake examples:

  • oldscho0l-runescape.com (zero instead of 'o')
  • oldschool-runescape.com (missing '.jagex')
  • jagex-account-verify.com (not official)
  • runescape-osrs.net (fake domain)

② Look for the Padlock Icon

Official Jagex websites always have a padlock icon in the browser address bar (HTTPS). Fake sites may not have this, or may have a fake padlock.

③ Emails: Check the Sender Address

Official Jagex emails always come from @jagex.com or @runescape.com. If the sender is osrs-noreply@gmail.com or anything else — it's fake.

④ Never Enter Your Password on "Giveaway" Sites

Many scam sites pretend to be "OSRS giveaway" or "free bond" sites. They ask you to "log in to claim your prize." This is a trick to steal your credentials.

💡 The Safe Way to Log In

Always type the URL manually: oldschool.runescape.com. Don't click links from emails, DMs, or forum posts. BookMark the official login page and only use that bookmark.

💱 5. Safe Trading Rules

Trading is essential in OSRS, but it's also where many scams happen. Follow these rules to trade safely.

① Always Use the Grand Exchange

The Grand Exchange (GE) is the safest way to buy and sell items. Both players put items/GP into the GE, and the system completes the trade automatically. There's no risk of being scammed.

How to use GE: Go to the GE in Varrock (or Prifddinas for members). Right-click a clerk and select "Exchange." Set your offer and wait for it to complete.

② Face-to-Face Trading: Double-Check Everything

If you must trade directly (some items can't be GE'd), follow this exact process:

Step 1: Initiate Trade

Right-click the player → "Trade with." The trade window opens.

Step 2: Both Players Offer Items

Drag items into the trade window. Do NOT click Accept yet.

Step 3: Check the Second Screen

Both players click "Accept." A second trade screen appears. This shows exactly what you're receiving. Read it carefully. If anything looks wrong, click Decline.

Step 4: Confirm

If the second screen is correct, both players click Accept again. The trade completes.

③ Never Trade Outside the Game

Some scammers ask you to "trade on Discord" or "send GP to my PayPal." Never do this. There's no way to enforce the trade, and you'll almost certainly be scammed.

🎯 Trading Safety Rules

  • Rule 1: Use the Grand Exchange whenever possible
  • Rule 2: Always check the second trade screen
  • Rule 3: Never trade for real money (RWT = ban)
  • Rule 4: Never trade with players who PM you first unsolicited
  • Rule 5: If a trade feels "too good to be true," it is

🖥️ 6. Device & Network Safety

Your account security also depends on the device you use to play. Here's how to stay safe.

① Don't Use Public WiFi

Public WiFi (coffee shops, airports, hotels) is not secure. Hackers can intercept your login data. Always play on a private, password-protected network.

② Don't Install "OSRS Tools" from Unknown Sources

Only use official Jagex-approved clients:

✅ Safe to Use❌ Avoid / Risk
Official OSRS LauncherThird-party "gold generators"
RuneLite (official)"Auto-trainer" software
HDOS (official) Unofficial "RuneLite plugins"

③ Keep Your Email Secure

Your email is the "key" to your OSRS account. If a scammer accesses your email, they can reset your OSRS password. Enable 2FA on your email account (Gmail, Outlook, etc.) as well.

④ Log Out Properly

Always use the in-game "Log Out" button. Don't just close the game window — this can leave your session vulnerable. In internet cafes or shared computers, never save your password.

🆘 7. What to Do If You Get Hacked

If you think your account has been stolen, act immediately.

① Try to Log In

If you can still log in, change your password immediately from the account settings page. Enable 2FA if it's not already on.

② Submit a Recovery Request

If you can't log in, go to Jagex Support and submit an "Account Recovery" request. You'll need to prove you're the original owner.

Information that helps: Original email, approximate date of account creation, first membership purchase date, original ISP (internet provider).

③ Email Recovery

If your email was also hijacked, use your email provider's account recovery process (Gmail: accounts.google.com → "Forgot password").

④ Be Patient

Jagex support can take 1–14 days to respond. There's nothing you can do to speed it up. Don't spam them — it delays the process.

⚠️ Prevention Is Easier Than Recovery

Account recovery is stressful and not guaranteed. Setting up 2FA takes 5 minutes and prevents 99% of hacks. Do it now, not after you've lost everything.

❓ 8. FAQ

Is it safe to buy OSRS GP from third-party sites?

No. Buying GP for real money (RWT = Real World Trading) is against Jagex's rules and can get your account permanently banned. It's also a common scam — many "GP selling" sites either steal your money or your account credentials. Always earn GP in-game.

Someone PM'd me offering free membership. Is this real?

No. This is always a scam. They will either try to phish your account or ask you to pay a "small fee" to receive the membership. Real giveaways are only done through official Jagex social media or Twitch streams, and they never ask for your password.

Can I share my account with my sibling?

Technically this violates Jagex's rules, but many families do it. The risk: if your sibling accidentally gets the account hacked (by falling for a scam), you're both locked out. At minimum, make sure everyone who uses the account knows the security rules in this guide.

What should I do if I see someone scamming in-game?

Right-click their player name → select "Report" → choose "Scamming" as the reason. Jagex reviews reports and bans scammers. You're protecting the community by reporting.

Is the "OSRS Mobile" app safe to download?

Yes — if you download it from the official App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android). Never download OSRS APK files from third-party websites — these often contain malware.
