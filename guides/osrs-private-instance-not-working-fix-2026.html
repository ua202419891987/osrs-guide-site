OSRS Boss Instance Not Working? Fix "Instance Full", Crashes & Lost Progress (2026)

You paid 50k for a Vorkath instance, the client froze, and now the entrance says "This instance is unavailable." Or your GIM mate can't get in. These are the exact failure modes advanced PvMers hit most — and the precise recovery steps that actually work, without wasting a second instance fee.

🛠️ Advanced PvM · Pain-Point Fix — 11 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 "This instance is full/unavailable" = the world can't allocate a new instance or a shared instance hit its player cap — hop worlds, don't re-pay blindly
  • 📌 Your paid instance survives a disconnect — you can usually re-enter the same instance within a few minutes; the hourly timer keeps running
  • 📌 GIM group instances require every member on the same world + in the same group; out-of-sync mates must leave and re-enter
  • 📌 Most boss instances are safe deaths — you keep your items and respawn at the entrance, no gravestone needed
  • 📌 Force a rebuild by leaving, hopping to a low-population world, then re-creating the instance from scratch

1. "This Instance Is Full / Unavailable" — What It Means

This is the single most common instance error, and it does not mean your account is bugged. It means the game server could not place you into an instance. There are two distinct causes, and the fix differs for each.

② Symptom A — Trying to join a shared instance

🔍 Symptom → Cause → Fix

Symptom: You right-click a friend's or clanmate's instance (or a group instance) and get "This instance is full."

Cause: That specific instance has reached its player cap. Boss instances support a limited number of concurrent players (often 1 per owner for solo bosses; group instances allow the group plus any invited players). Someone is already inside.

Fix:

  • Ask the owner to dismiss/remove a player, or wait for a slot to free up.
  • If it's your own instance, nobody else should be in it — see Section 6/7 for a rebuild.
  • For group content, confirm the host actually invited you (right-click player → "Invite to instance" where supported).

③ Symptom B — Creating your own private instance

🔍 Symptom → Cause → Fix

Symptom: You click "Instance" on the entrance (Vorkath coffer, Zulrah altar, GWD boss door, etc.) and get "This instance is unavailable right now" or the option greys out.

Cause: The world has hit its total instance-allocation limit, or the specific instance tile/region is temporarily locked. This happens most on crowded popular worlds (often worlds 1–10 and the official boss worlds).

Fix (do this in order):

  1. Do not keep clicking — you haven't been charged yet for a failed creation.
  2. Hop to a less populated world (US, EU, or AU worlds in the 40s–90s are usually calmer).
  3. Retry the "Instance" option on the new world.
  4. If it still fails, log to the lobby and back in, then hop again.
💡 Don't Double-Pay

A failed "unavailable" creation does not deduct your coins. Only a successful instance creation takes the fee. If you see the fee leave your pouch, the instance exists — the problem is something else (see Section 3).

2. Paid the Fee, Then Disconnected or Crashed

This is the panic moment: you paid for the instance, the client crashed or the wifi dropped, and you're staring at a login screen wondering if your 50k (and your kill streak) just evaporated.

④ What actually happens to your instance

When you successfully create a private boss instance, the game reserves that instance for you for the duration you paid (billed hourly, e.g. ~50,000 GP/hour — the exact amount shows at creation). The instance is tied to your account and the world, not to your live connection.

✅ Good news: a disconnect does NOT instantly delete your instance

  • The instance keeps running on the server — the hourly timer does not pause while you're logged out.
  • You can log back in on the same world and re-enter the same instance through the same entrance.
  • You do not re-pay to get back in, as long as the original instance is still active.

⑤ Step-by-step recovery after a crash

Step 1 — Reconnect fast

Log back in as quickly as you can. The instance stays reserved for a short grace window after disconnect; the faster you return, the safer. Your banked items and unsaved progress are never at risk from a crash.

Step 2 — Return to the same world

Make sure you log into the exact same world you created the instance on. An instance does not follow you across worlds — logging into a different world means you'll see no instance and may be tempted to pay again. Don't.

Step 3 — Re-enter via the entrance

Walk back to the boss entrance and click "Instance" / "Enter instance." If your instance is still active, you'll drop right back in. If it says it no longer exists, the hourly window expired while you were out — you'll simply pay again to make a fresh one.

⚠️ The only real loss: the elapsed instance time. If you disconnect for 20 minutes, that's 20 minutes of paid instance time you can't get back. The kill you were on is reset, but you aren't charged a second fee to re-enter.

3. Reconnect After Disconnect — Instance State

Understanding exactly what persists versus what resets saves you from repeating mistakes. Here is the precise state after you disconnect mid-instance and return:

ThingState after reconnectNotes
Your instanceStill active (if window not expired)Re-enter free on same world
Paid timeKeeps tickingNot refunded for downtime
Current boss fightReset / boss healedYou restart the kill
Items on the groundLost if not picked upLoot you dropped despawns after the timer
Your bank / inventoryUntouchedCrash never deletes items
Prayer / HP / runRestored to pre-fightYou re-enter at the entrance

⑥ If the instance "isn't there" after reconnect

If you log back on the same world and the entrance acts like no instance exists:

  • You likely landed on a different world than you created it on — check the world number top-left.
  • The hourly window expired while you were gone (only if you were out a long time).
  • The instance was a group instance and the owner dismissed it — see Section 4.
💡 Pro Move: Note the World First

Before every paid instance, glance at the world number and mentally note it. After any crash, that single habit prevents 90% of "where did my instance go" confusion.

4. GIM / Group Instance Won't Let Mates In or Sync

Group Ironman (and group boss instances generally) add a layer of permission logic that breaks in predictable ways.

⑦ Symptom — Mate "can't enter the instance"

🔍 Symptom → Cause → Fix

Symptom: You made a GIM group instance; a group member right-clicks the entrance and gets nothing, or "you cannot enter this instance."

Cause: They are on a different world than the instance owner, or they are not actually in the same Group Ironman group (wrong account, or the group name doesn't match).

Fix:

  1. Confirm all members are on the same world as the instance owner.
  2. Confirm they're in the same GIM group (check the group panel).
  3. Owner re-invites: dismiss the instance and re-create it with the group present.
  4. If using manual invites, the owner must invite each player via the instance interface.

⑧ Symptom — "Out of sync" / mate sees different boss state

🔍 Symptom → Cause → Fix

Symptom: One mate is inside fighting, another is stuck at the loading screen, or they see the boss already dead/already spawned that you don't.

Cause: A desync from a mid-instance world hop, a partial disconnect, or one member entering a different instance copy.

Fix:

  • Everyone leaves the instance (walk out / teleport).
  • Owner dismisses the instance entirely.
  • All members hop to the same world together.
  • Owner re-creates the instance; members re-enter in one wave.

⚠️ GIM rule: Group instances are bound to the group, not one player. If the group leader is offline or in another world, the instance can't be joined by the rest until someone with permission re-creates it. Plan who "owns" the instance before you start.

5. Death Inside an Instance — Where Your Items Go

A lot of fear around instances comes from the Wilderness gravestone horror story. For standard boss instances, that fear is misplaced — but there are edges worth knowing.

⑨ Standard boss instances = safe deaths

Private instances for bosses like Vorkath, Zulrah, the God Wars Dungeon bosses, and the Thermonuclear smoke devil are fought in safe areas. If you die:

  • You keep every item — no gravestone, no drop, nothing lost.
  • You respawn at the instance entrance (or the nearest respawn point) fully intact.
  • The only cost is the lost instance time and the interrupted kill.
💡 When a Gravestone DOES Apply

A gravestone only spawns on dangerous deaths. Most instanced PvM is safe, so you won't see one. The gravestone appears outside the dangerous area at your normal respawn — for Wilderness boss instances (e.g. Wilderness bosses), dying drops a gravestone at the Wilderness entrance, and another player can loot it. That's a Wilderness risk, not a standard boss-instance risk.

⑩ Edge case — dangerous instances & the Bank PIN

If you ever instance content in a dangerous zone (certain Wilderness activities), treat death like any Wilderness death: items drop to a gravestone that decays, and a PKer can take them. Keep valuable gear out, and rely on the 3-item Protect Item prayer. For the normal boss instances covered here, this does not apply — death is free.

6. World Too Full — Can't Grab an Instance at All

On the official boss worlds and the lowest-numbered members worlds, instance creation can fail repeatedly because the world's instance slots are saturated by hundreds of other players doing the exact same thing.

⑪ How to tell it's a world-load problem

🔍 Symptom → Cause → Fix

Symptom: "Instance" option fails on world 1–10 but works fine on world 60+. The world shows a high player count in the lobby.

Cause: The world has hit its server-side instance allocation cap. No new instances can be created until others expire.

Fix:

  • Hop to a higher-numbered or regional world (US West/East, EU, AU) where instance load is lower.
  • Avoid the designated "boss worlds" during peak EU evening hours.
  • If you must use a busy world, create the instance during off-peak (early morning UTC).
💡 The "Try a Dead World" Trick

Worlds in the 40s–90s that aren't flagged as skill/boss worlds are routinely near-empty for PvM. You'll almost never hit an instance cap there, and you also dodge the "world too full" lobby queue.

7. How to Force-Refresh / Change World / Rebuild

When an instance is stuck in a broken state — can't enter, says unavailable, or is desynced — a clean rebuild almost always fixes it. Here's the reliable sequence.

⑫ The forced rebuild checklist

Step 1 — Leave the instance cleanly

Walk out the entrance (or use the "Leave instance" / teleport option). Don't just disconnect — a clean exit lets the server release the slot faster.

Step 2 — Hop worlds

Open the world switcher and move to a different, lower-population world. This is the actual "refresh" — it puts you on a fresh instance allocator.

Step 3 — Re-create from scratch

Return to the entrance and pay for a new instance. If the old one was still active, it expires on its own timer — you're not double-charged for the old one, you simply start a new hourly block.

Step 4 — For group: re-invite everyone together

GIM/group: have all members on the new world before the owner creates. Invite in one batch, then enter together to avoid desync (see Section 4).

⚠️ Still failing after a rebuild? Log to the lobby, wait ~30 seconds, and try a third world. If it fails on every world, it's a temporary game-wide instance service hiccup — check the official OSRS status channels and wait it out. You are not being singled out.

8. Instance Failure FAQs

Do I lose my instance fee if the instance says "unavailable"?

No. A failed creation never charges you. The coins are only taken when the instance is successfully created. If you got the "unavailable" message, your money is still in your pouch — just hop worlds and retry.

How long does my instance survive after I disconnect?

The instance stays reserved for a short grace window after disconnect, and the hourly timer keeps running the whole time. Log back on the same world quickly and you'll re-enter for free. If you're gone longer than the paid hour, the instance expires and you pay again.

Why does my GIM mate see "you cannot enter this instance"?

Two causes: they're on a different world than the instance owner, or they're not in the same Group Ironman group. Get everyone on the same world and confirm the group panel matches, then have the owner re-create and re-invite.

Will I drop my items if I die inside a boss instance?

No — standard boss instances (Vorkath, Zulrah, GWD, Thermy, etc.) are safe areas. You keep all items and respawn at the entrance. A gravestone only matters for dangerous/Wilderness deaths, which these are not.

Can I pause my instance timer by logging out?

No. Once created, the hourly instance fee timer runs whether you're online or not. Logging out to "save" instance time doesn't work — only actually being inside fighting uses the time you paid for.

The "Instance" option is greyed out on the entrance — why?

The world's instance allocation is likely saturated, or you're standing in the wrong spot (some entrances require you to be at a specific tile). Hop to a quieter world first; if it's still greyed, walk fully onto the entrance tile and re-right-click.

My whole group got desynced mid-fight. Fastest fix?

Everyone leaves, the owner dismisses the instance, all members hop to the same world together, owner re-creates, and the group enters in one wave. Don't let people trickle in one at a time — that's what causes the desync.

Is there any way to get a refund on a wasted instance?

No. Jagex does not refund instance fees, even for disconnects or crashes. The mitigation is purely operational: reconnect fast on the same world to use the time you paid for, and avoid creating instances on overloaded worlds.

Does using a VPN or proxy cause instance errors?

A VPN itself doesn't break instances, but unstable connections that cause frequent disconnects will repeatedly trigger the "reconnect within the grace window" problem. If you instance often, use the most stable connection you have — a dropped VPN tunnel mid-kill is the classic culprit.

What's the difference between a solo and a group instance for the same boss?

A solo instance is yours alone and can't be joined. A group instance (where supported, e.g. GIM or group content) lets approved group members enter. If you only want to farm solo, make a solo instance — group instances add the permission complexity covered in Section 4.
