Fractured Archive Preparation Guide — Stats, Gear & Team Setup

The Fractured Archive is OSRS's fourth raid (Raids 4), coming Q4 2026 and billed as the "toughest raid yet." Set within Guthix's personal vault, this ToB-style linear raid supports 2-8 players and demands peak combat readiness across all three combat styles. This guide covers everything you need to prepare — confirmed requirements, gear tiers from budget to best-in-slot, team roles, and a complete training checklist.

⚡ Quick Verdict

The Fractured Archive requires While Guthix Sleeps completed and 50 Sailing as a confirmed minimum. For day-one readiness, aim for 90+ in all combat stats, 74+ Prayer (Rigour/Augury), and at least one mega-rare weapon (Tbow/Scythe/Shadow). Team size scales from 2-8, with 4-5 being the recommended starting size. Start your preparation 3-6 months before launch — the While Guthix Sleeps quest alone requires substantial stat grinding and quest pre-requisites. This is not a raid you can rush into unprepared.

🏛️ Raid Overview & Confirmed Details

The Fractured Archive was first announced during the Winter Summit 2026 and is scheduled for release in Q4 2026. It is OSRS's fourth raid and is officially described as the "toughest raid yet" by Jagex.

③ Confirmed Bosses

Boss 1: Guthixian Archive Guardian

A massive, sleeping tree-skeleton horror awakened to protect the archive. Features enormous size, vine tendrils, and glowing red eyes. Likely a mid-raid boss, similar to Verzik's Phase 1 in Theatre of Blood.

Boss 2: Ezekiel

Saradomin's righteous emissary, sent to claim artifacts worthy of the god of order. A mechanically complex humanoid boss fight — any attempt to stop him is met with overwhelming force. Expected to be one of the later encounters.

④ Core Mechanic: Style Switching

The Fractured Archive's defining mechanic is combat style switching. Bosses will have specific phases where they resist certain styles while being vulnerable to others. Players must rapidly identify the boss's current state and swap gear accordingly. A single-style specialist (e.g., ranged-only) will struggle significantly.

⚠️ Don't Neglect Any Style

Unlike previous raids where you could specialize (e.g., mage at CoX, melee at ToB), The Fractured Archive demands competence in all three styles. If your Magic level is 50 while your Ranged is 99, you will be a liability on certain boss phases.

② Why While Guthix Sleeps?

The Fractured Archive is deeply tied to the Guthix / God Wars story arc. While Guthix Sleeps directly sets up this narrative, and the raid serves as the climax of this multi-year storyline. If you haven't completed this quest, you cannot enter the raid.

🎯 While Guthix Sleeps Requirements (Recap)

  • Completion of: The World Wakes, Ritual of the Mahjarrat, The Temple at Senntisten, and several other high-level quests
  • 65+ in multiple skills (Agility, Crafting, Divination, etc.)
  • High combat stats across all styles
  • ~6-10 hours to complete the quest itself

③ Sailing Requirement: 50

A confirmed minimum of 50 Sailing is required to access The Fractured Archive. While 50 Sailing is not particularly high (achievable in ~15-20 hours of training), it's a non-negotiable gate. Jagex has confirmed this as part of integrating the Sailing skill into high-level PvM content.

② Melee BIS Details (Torva + Scythe)

  • Scythe of Vitur — Max hit of 50+ against large targets. Essential for any multi-tile boss phases.
  • Torva Armour — +6 Strength bonus over Bandos. ~1.2B for the set.
  • Ultor Ring — +12 Strength bonus. The single best DPS ring slot upgrade.
  • Infernal Cape — +4 Strength, +8 all attack. Non-negotiable for max DPS.

③ Ranged BIS Details (Masori Fortified + Tbow)

  • Twisted Bow — Scales off target Magic level. If bosses have high Magic stats (likely), the Tbow will hit 80-100+.
  • Masori (Fortified) — +8 Ranged Strength over regular Masori. ~300M for the fortified set.
  • Zaryte Bracers — +2 Ranged Strength over Barrows Gloves.
  • Venator Ring — +6 Ranged Strength, +8 Ranged Attack.

④ Magic BIS Details (Ancestral + Shadow)

  • Shadow of Tumeken — Triples your Magic Strength bonus from equipped gear. With full Ancestral and Torm bracelet, max hit of 50+.
  • Ancestral Robes — +18 Magic Strength total for the set. ~500M for all three pieces.
  • Magus Ring — +8 Magic Strength. ~70M.
  • Imbued Heart — +10 Magic level boost. ~100M.

💰 Cost-Benefit Analysis

The jump from Budget to Mid is ~5-10x DPS improvement for ~3-5x the cost. The jump from Mid to BIS is ~30-50% more DPS for ~5-10x the cost. If you plan to farm 500+ raids, BIS pays for itself. If you just want to clear it a few times, Mid-tier is the sweet spot.

② Recommended Team Roles (4-5 Player Setup)

① Main DPS (2 players)

Primary damage dealers with BIS or near-BIS gear. Responsible for pushing boss phases. One melee-focused, one ranged/mage focused.

② Support / Utility (1-2 players)

Brings supply drops, utility gear (DWH/BGS for defence reduction), and handles any mechanics that require multiple people. Often the "flex" role that switches to whatever style is needed.

③ Backup / Learner (1 player)

In a 5-person setup, the 5th player provides backup DPS and learns mechanics. In larger teams (6-8), this becomes multiple learners or support roles.

③ Key Team Considerations

  • Style coverage: Every team member should be competent in all three styles. A pure melee-ranger who can't mage will fail certain boss phases.
  • Communication: Voice chat (Discord) is strongly recommended. Call out style switches, prayer changes, and positioning.
  • Synergy: Don't stack too many of one style. A balanced team (2 melee, 2 ranged, 1 mage in a 5-man) covers all phases efficiently.
  • Loot distribution: Agree on loot rules (FFA, split, or lootshare) before entering. High-value unique drops will cause friction without clear rules.
🎯 Starting Team Strategy

Start with a 4-person team of equally-geared friends. This gives you enough DPS to clear while keeping the coordination manageable. As you learn the mechanics, you can drop to 3 for better loot shares, or scale up to 6-8 for learner-friendly runs.

📅 Pre-Raid Training Plan

Here's a phased training plan to get your account raid-ready by Q4 2026. Adjust based on your current stats.

① Phase 1: Quest Foundation (Months 6-5 Before Launch)

  • Complete all While Guthix Sleeps prerequisite quests
  • Aim for 50 Sailing (minimum)
  • Get 70 Prayer (for Piety)
  • Start training all combat styles to 80+

② Phase 2: Stat Building (Months 4-3 Before Launch)

  • Push all combat stats to 90+
  • Get 74+ Prayer (Rigour + Augury)
  • Build GP reserves: aim for 500M+ saved
  • Complete while Guthix Sleeps
  • Practice existing raids (CoX, ToB, ToA) for mechanics training

③ Phase 3: Gear Acquisition (Months 2-1 Before Launch)

  • Buy mega-rares (Tbow > Shadow > Scythe priority order)
  • Fill in all gear slots with the best you can afford
  • Stock up on supplies (potions, food, runes, arrows)
  • Practice style-switching at Zulrah, Olm, or Verzik

④ Phase 4: Final Preparation (Month Before Launch)

  • Finalize your team composition
  • Set up voice comms and loot rules
  • Practice with your exact gear setup
  • Read every Jagex blog post about the raid
  • On launch day: go in with 4 people, focus on learning over speed

🎯 Daily Routine (3 Months Out)

  1. 1 hour of combat training (your weakest style)
  2. 1-2 ToB/CoX raids for mechanics practice
  3. 30 min of money making to build GP reserves
  4. 15 min of Sailing training (if below 50)

❓ Fractured Archive FAQs

When does The Fractured Archive release?

Q4 2026 (between October and December). Jagex announced this during the Winter Summit 2026. No specific date has been confirmed yet.

What Sailing level do I need?

The confirmed minimum is 50 Sailing. This is achievable in about 15-20 hours of training through standard Sailing methods such as the Wushanko Isles routes.

Can I solo The Fractured Archive?

Yes, soloing is possible but extremely difficult. The raid scales from 2-8 players, and solo is a harder variant of the 2-player scaling. Expect to need max stats, BIS gear, and perfect execution. Most players should start with a 4-person team.

Is While Guthix Sleeps really required?

Yes, confirmed by Jagex. The raid is the climax of the Guthix/God Wars storyline that While Guthix Sleeps sets up. Without completing this quest, you cannot enter the raid. Don't leave it until the last minute — it has substantial prerequisites.

What's the minimum gear I can bring?

For a budget run, aim for ~300-800M total bank value distributed across all three styles. Minimum viable weapons: Dragon Hunter Lance (melee), Blowpipe with Dragon Darts (ranged), and a Trident or Kodai Wand (magic). You'll need strong teammates to compensate.

Which mega-rare should I buy first?

Twisted Bow first (most versatile, works against high-magic bosses), then Shadow of Tumeken (if the raid has magic phases), then Scythe of Vitur (if there are large multi-tile bosses). This priority order maximizes your coverage.

How does the style-switching mechanic work?

Bosses will have phases where they resist certain combat styles and are vulnerable to others. You must switch gear and weapons mid-fight to match the boss's current state. Single-style specialists will struggle significantly.

Can I practice the raid before launch?

Yes! Jagex has confirmed a Practice Mode that lets you practice individual boss fights without completing the full raid. This is similar to the practice options in Theatre of Blood and Tombs of Amascut.

What are the confirmed unique rewards?

Jagex is keeping reward details deliberately under wraps, revealing items one at a time in small blog posts throughout 2026. Community speculation includes new BIS equipment in slots that haven't been upgraded recently. Expect powerful new weapons and armour.
