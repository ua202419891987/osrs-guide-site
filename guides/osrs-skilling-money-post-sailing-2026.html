🚢 Sailing Economy Shifts: Price Data 2026

The arrival of Sailing as OSRS's first new skill in the 2020s fundamentally reshaped the skilling money-making landscape. Before Sailing, most skilling profits came from a well-established set of methods that had remained largely unchanged for years. The introduction of maritime content created entirely new demand chains, shifted prices on dozens of raw materials, and opened fresh profit routes for players who adapted quickly.

New Materials, New Demands

Sailing introduced several new resources that interact directly with existing production skills. Hardwood timbers (teak, mahogany, and the new driftwood tier) are consumed in ship construction and repairs, driving Woodcutting profits higher than they have been in years. Cannonball alloys and reinforced steel plates created a surge in Smithing demand, particularly for steel and mithril bars used at Blast Furnace. Seaweed fibers and tar distillates from Crafting and Herblore respectively now feed into sailcloth production, giving mid-level Crafting a genuine profit path beyond battlestaves.

The Ripple Effect on Skilling Profits

What makes the post-Sailing economy uniquely profitable for skillers is the breadth of the impact. Rather than one or two methods getting buffed, nearly every gathering skill saw meaningful profit increases. Mining benefits from increased demand for ores used in ship armor. Woodcutting profits surged from timber demand. Fishing rose on crew provisioning. Even Crafting and Smithing — traditionally money-losing skills — now have viable profit margins through ship component production.

The most significant shift, however, is in consistency. Before Sailing, skilling profits were mostly tied to PvM supply chains. If bosses weren't being farmed heavily, demand dropped. Now, Sailing creates a constant, predictable consumption of resources as players build, upgrade, and maintain their ships. This underlying demand floor means skilling profits are less volatile than they were in previous years.

⛵ Sailing Methods: 750K/hr (Salvage + Trade)

Sailing itself is not just a resource sink — it is also a direct money-making skill. As you train Sailing, you unlock methods that generate raw GP, valuable salvage, and trade goods that sell for high prices on the Grand Exchange. Here are the most profitable Sailing-specific methods in 2026.

Salvaging Shipwrecks (Level 35+, 400K-750K GP/hr)

Shipwreck salvaging is the bread-and-butter Sailing money maker. At level 35 Sailing, you can begin diving shipwreck sites found across the expanded map. Each wreck contains salvageable materials: scrap metal (sells to the Salvage Master or GE), ancient coins (exchange for GP at Port Phasmatys), maritime artifacts (sell to collectors), and sunken treasure (random valuable items).

The key to maximizing profit is using a Salvage Harpoon (level 45 requirement) which increases your yield per wreck by roughly 40%. Combine this with the Deep Sea Diver outfit from the Maritime Minigame to reduce oxygen consumption, allowing longer diving sessions before needing to resurface.

Best locations: The Graveyard of Ships (level 35-60), The Sunken Armada (level 60-80), and The Abyssal Trench (level 80+). Each higher-tier location has better drop tables with more valuable salvage.

Trading Routes (Level 50+, 300K-600K GP/hr)

Once you hit level 50 Sailing and build or acquire a Merchant Sloop, you can begin running trading routes between ports. The core loop is simple: buy trade goods at one port, sail them to another port where they are in demand, and sell for a profit margin. Each port has a dynamic supply-and-demand system that refreshes every hour.

Cargo Contracts (Level 70+, 500K-900K GP/hr)

Cargo contracts are the high-intensity Sailing money maker. Speak to a Port Master at any major port to accept a contract. You'll be asked to deliver specific goods to a destination port within a time limit. Successful delivery grants a base GP reward plus a contract bonus crate containing random valuable items.

Contracts scale with your Sailing level. At level 70, you unlock Hard contracts (500K-700K GP/hr effective rate when counting bonus crates). At level 85, Elite contracts become available (700K-900K GP/hr). At level 95, Master contracts can exceed 1M GP/hr but require near-perfect navigation and a fully upgraded Galleon.

Ship Upgrade Flipping (Requires 80+ Sailing + 70+ Smithing/Crafting, 400K-800K GP/hr)

A lesser-known method involves buying base ship components on the GE, upgrading them using your Smithing and Crafting skills, and reselling the upgraded versions. For example, a standard Iron Anchor (buys for 15K) can be upgraded to a Reinforced Anchor using 3 steel bars and 2 oak planks, selling for 35K-42K — a profit margin of roughly 15K-20K per craft after material costs.

This method requires upfront capital and careful GE offer management, but it is fully AFK — you stand at an anvil while the crafting animation plays, requiring only a click every 30-45 seconds.

⛏️ Mining: Blast Mine 1.2M/hr (Methods Ranked)

Mining remains one of the most reliable skilling money-makers in OSRS, and the post-Sailing economy has been kind to ore prices. Here are the best Mining money-making methods ranked by profit in 2026.

Blast Mine (Level 43+, 600K-900K GP/hr)

Blast Mine in Lovakengj is the highest-profit Mining method below level 85. By using dynamite (buyable on the GE for ~120 GP each) on the blast mine walls, you excavate ores at approximately 3x the normal mining speed. The trade-off is higher attention required and a small risk of taking damage from explosions.

Requirements: 43 Mining, 100% Lovakengj favor (one-time unlock), dynamite (~5K-6K per hour consumed).

Setup: Prospector outfit for the 2.5% XP boost (doesn't directly increase profit but speeds up leveling to better ores), Graceful for running between blast sites, and a charged Amulet of Glory for banking at Edgeville between inventory fills.

2026 profit breakdown: At 43-60 Mining, you mainly get coal and gold ore (~400K-500K GP/hr). At 60-75, mithril and adamantite become common (~550K-700K GP/hr). At 75+, runite ore appears in the drop table, pushing profits to 700K-900K GP/hr.

Runite Ore Mining (Level 85+, 500K-800K GP/hr)

Mining runite ore the traditional way — hopping worlds at known runite rock locations — remains profitable but has evolved in 2026. The increased demand from Sailing ship armor (requiring runite-reinforced components) has pushed runite ore prices to 11,200-11,800 GP each, up from around 10,500 pre-Sailing.

Best locations: The Mining Guild (level 85+ section with the closest bank), the Heroes' Guild mine (requires Heroes' Quest), and the Fossil Island runite rocks (requires Bone Voyage). The Mining Guild is generally best due to the invisible +7 boost, the close bank, and the fact that the guild's runite rocks respawn on a fixed 12-minute timer that you can track.

Strategy: Mine 2-3 runite rocks per world, hop worlds, repeat. With decent world-hopping speed and using the Expert Mining Gloves (from the Mining Guild mineral exchange) which give a chance to not deplete the rock, you can average 40-60 runite ore per hour. At 11,500 GP each, that's 460K-690K GP/hr.

Amethyst Mining (Level 92+, 350K-550K GP/hr)

Amethyst is the ultimate AFK mining money-maker. Found exclusively in the Mining Guild's level 92+ section, amethyst rocks are non-competitive (each player has their own instance) and take a long time to deplete. This means you can click once and mine for 25-40 seconds without any further input.

Amethyst is used to craft amethyst arrows, darts, and bolt tips, which have seen increased demand in 2026 due to their use in Sailing's naval combat encounters. Amethyst prices sit at 3,400-3,700 GP each.

Profit comparison: At level 99 Mining with the Mining Cape perk (+5% chance to mine an additional ore), you can expect to mine 100-150 amethyst per hour depending on attention level. With Expert Mining Gloves adding roughly 10% more yield from non-depletion procs, that's 110-165 ore per hour, or 374K-610K GP/hr. The lower end of this range is for fully AFK play (checking every 40 seconds), while the upper end requires more active play.

✨ Runecrafting: Blood 1M/hr (Full Guide)

Runecrafting has historically been one of the most profitable skills in OSRS, and in 2026 it's better than ever. The introduction of Sailing-related magic (teleport spells, wind manipulation, water breathing) has increased demand for higher-tier runes, while the new Runecrafting Catalyst item (obtained from the Sailing skill boss at level 80 Sailing) provides a 5% chance to craft double runes at any altar — stacking with the existing multiple-rune crafting unlocks.

Blood Runes (Level 77+, 500K-800K GP/hr)

Blood rune crafting at the True Blood Altar in Arceuus is one of the most consistent moneymakers in OSRS. The method involves mining dense essence blocks in the Arceuus essence mine, chiseling them into dark essence fragments, running to the Blood Altar, and crafting blood runes. Each inventory of 26 dark essence fragments (with a blood essence fragment in the 27th slot for the 50% chance of an extra rune) produces 52-104 blood runes depending on your Runecrafting level.

Level scaling: At 77 Runecrafting (the minimum for bloods), you get 1 blood rune per dark essence fragment. The blood essence active effect adds a 50% chance for an extra rune per craft. At level 77, that's approximately 1.5 runes per fragment on average. You can craft roughly 3 inventories per hour with the Arceuus running method, producing about 2,100-2,400 blood runes per hour. At 2026 prices of 230-270 GP each, that's 480K-650K GP/hr.

At level 90+, with the full Raiments of the Eye outfit (60% more runes), the Runecrafting Catalyst, and blood essence, you can produce 3,500-4,200 blood runes per hour — worth 800K-1.1M GP/hr.

Soul Runes (Level 90+, 600K-1M GP/hr)

Soul rune crafting at the True Soul Altar requires charging the altar with pure essence before you can craft. Each trip requires carrying 4 dark essence fragments (or 3 with the full colossal pouch), running to the Soul Altar, charging it (3-4 charges per trip), and then crafting when fully charged. The pace is slower than bloods, but the profit per rune is significantly higher.

Soul runes sell for 310-360 GP each in 2026, up from around 270 GP pre-Sailing due to increased demand from shadow spells in naval combat. At level 90, with Raiments of the Eye and Runecrafting Catalyst, expect roughly 1,500-2,200 soul runes per hour — 465K-792K GP/hr. At level 99, with the Runecrafting cape perk, rates improve to 2,000-2,800 soul runes per hour (620K-1M GP/hr).

Wrath Runes (Level 95+, 700K-1.2M GP/hr)

Wrath runes, introduced with Dragon Slayer II, are crafted at the Wrath Altar on the peak of Mount Karuulm. They require 95 Runecrafting and the completion of Dragon Slayer II. Wrath runes are used for the Arceuus spellbook's high-tier combat spells and the new Sailing-related enchantment spells, keeping prices firmly at 380-440 GP each.

With the Mythical Cape equipped (one-click teleport to the Myths' Guild right next to the altar), each trip takes about 55-65 seconds including banking. At 95-98 Runecrafting with Raiments of the Eye, expect 1,400-1,900 wrath runes per hour. At 99, with the cape perk, it jumps to 1,800-2,400 per hour. That's 684K-1,056M GP/hr.

Ourania Altar (ZMI) — The Hybrid Option

The Ourania Altar (ZMI) deserves special mention as the best Runecrafting training method that also turns a respectable profit. Rather than crafting a single rune type, ZMI produces a mix of all runes based on your level. At level 75+, the rune mix skews toward valuable runes (nature, death, blood, soul), and with the Ourania Teleport on the Lunar spellbook (71 Magic) combined with a Ring of Dueling to bank at Ferox Enclave, you can complete runs in about 55 seconds. Profits range from 150K-400K GP/hr depending on your Runecrafting level, with the main benefit being the 45K-65K XP/hr rate — significantly faster training than blood or soul runes.

5. Hunter Methods — Chinchompas, Herbiboar, Implings

Hunter offers some of the most unique and profitable skilling methods in OSRS, ranging from high-intensity chinchompa hunting to the more relaxed Herbiboar tracking. Sailing has had a moderate but positive impact on Hunter profits, particularly through increased demand for chinchompas in naval combat encounters.

Black Chinchompas (Level 73+, 800K-1.4M GP/hr)

Black chinchompas remain the single highest-profit Hunter method, but they come with significant risk: they are only found in the Wilderness (level 32-35 Wilderness, south of the Chaos Temple). PKers are a constant threat, which is priced into the black chin's 2,800-3,400 GP each value on the GE.

Setup: 4-6 box traps, your best protective ranged gear (black d'hide or better), a Ring of Wealth (i) for the clue scroll chance, an imp box to bank chins without leaving the area, and a quick-teleport method (Royal Seed Pod or Amulet of Glory to Edgeville). Bring minimal risk — if you're attacked, you should only risk your box traps and a few chins.

2026 profit breakdown: At level 73, expect 80-120 black chins per hour worth 224K-408K GP/hr. At level 90+, catch rates improve to 180-250 per hour (504K-850K GP/hr). With max efficiency at 99 Hunter and tick-perfect box trap placement, skilled hunters can reach 350-420 chins per hour — worth 1.12M-1.43M GP/hr. The spread between "chill" and "sweaty" is enormous.

Red Chinchompas (Level 63+, 400K-700K GP/hr)

Red chinchompas, found in the Feldip Hills Hunter area (south of the Fairy Ring code AKS), offer a safer alternative to black chins. They sell for 1,400-1,800 GP each in 2026, up from around 1,200 pre-Sailing due to their use as a budget ranged training option and in Sailing's crew defense encounters.

At level 63-79 Hunter, expect 180-260 red chins per hour (252K-468K GP/hr). At 80-98, catch rates rise to 280-380 per hour (392K-684K GP/hr). At 99 Hunter, 380-500 red chins per hour are achievable (532K-900K GP/hr at the upper efficiency end). The key advantage over black chins is zero PK risk and the ability to bring more box traps (5-6 comfortably fit in the Feldip Hills spots).

Herbiboar (Level 80+, 300K-500K GP/hr + 130K-150K Hunter XP/hr)

Herbiboar tracking on Fossil Island is a unique Hunter activity that combines moderate profit with excellent XP rates. You track herbiboars across Fossil Island using their trails, eventually harvesting herbs from them. With the Herb Sack (from the Tithe Farm minigame) and Magic Secateurs equipped (which boost herb yield), herbiboar becomes a very consistent money-maker.

Herbiboar profit comes entirely from the herbs harvested. The drop table includes grimy herbs from guam through torstol, with higher Hunter levels yielding more high-tier herbs. At level 80, expect 250K-320K GP/hr in herb value. At 99, this rises to 380K-500K GP/hr. The method has zero supply cost and the only requirement is 80 Hunter and 31 Herblore to track them. Fossil Island teleports (Digsite Pendant or the Magic Mushtree network) make banking efficient.

Impling Hunting — Magpie, Ninja, and Dragon

Puro-Puro (accessed through the wheat field in Zanaris after completing Lost City) is a dedicated impling hunting minigame. Three impling types are worth hunting for profit:

Magpie Implings (Level 65): Drop black dragonhide, rune items, and rings. Average loot is worth 12K-18K per impling. Catch 25-35 per hour for 300K-630K GP/hr. Low effort, but competition in Puro-Puro can be fierce.

Ninja Implings (Level 74): Drop onyx bolt tips, rune items, and the valuable Ninja Monkey greegree part. Average loot 25K-40K per impling. Catch 15-22 per hour for 375K-880K GP/hr. Ninja implings spawn less frequently than magpies.

Dragon Implings (Level 83): The jackpot impling. Dragon implings can drop dragonstone jewelry, glory amulets, and the ultra-rare dragon arrow/dart tips. Average loot is 80K-150K per impling, but rare drops like the Dragonstone Amulet or large quantities of dragon arrow tips can push individual implings to 500K+. Catch 4-8 per hour (they're rare spawns) for 320K-1.2M GP/hr depending on luck. Note: You must complete Lost City to access Puro-Puro.

6. Farming Methods — Herb Runs, Snape Grass, Seaweed

Farming is arguably the most time-efficient money-making skill in OSRS. Because crops grow in real time (not play time), you can earn significant GP with just 5-7 minutes of active effort per herb run, repeated every 80-90 minutes. The 2026 economy has been very favorable to Farming, with clean herb prices reaching multi-year highs.

Herb Runs — The Backbone of Farming Profit

A standard herb run uses 5-6 herb patches (more with diaries and quests unlocked), plants herb seeds, applies ultracompost for guaranteed minimum yield and disease prevention, and harvests 70-90 minutes later. With all patches unlocked, a completed run nets 40-80 clean herbs depending on patch count, seed type, farming level, and luck.

Snape Grass (Level 61 Farming)

Snape grass is planted in allotment patches alongside your herb runs. Using ultracompost and the Amulet of Bounty (gives a 25% chance to use zero seeds when planting allotments), Snape grass can add 30K-50K GP per run with zero extra time investment — you harvest the allotment patches while already at the farming patches for your herb run.

At 2026 prices of 600-700 GP each, and average yields of 16-24 snape grass per patch with ultracompost and Magic Secateurs, each allotment patch generates 9,600-16,800 GP in value. Five allotment patches (all accessible during a standard herb run route) add approximately 48K-84K GP to your herb run profit.

Cactus Spines (Level 55 Farming)

Cactus spines are the ultimate passive Farming profit. Once you plant a cactus seed in the Al Kharid cactus patch, it grows in 9 hours and then produces 3 spines every 25 minutes indefinitely. You never need to replant. At 3,200-4,000 GP per spine (used to make Weapon Poison+, demand has risen with Sailing combat), that's 9,600-12,000 GP every 25 minutes — just from teleporting to Al Kharid, harvesting in 5 seconds, and going about your day. There's also a second cactus patch in the Farming Guild at level 55 Farming, doubling the passive income to ~24,000 GP per 25 minutes.

Limpwurt Roots (Level 26 Farming)

Limpwurt roots are planted in flower patches. With ultracompost, you average 5-7 roots per harvest. At 2026 prices of 1,400-1,800 GP per root, each flower patch adds 7,000-12,600 GP to your run. The Limpwurt root is used in super strength potions, one of the most consistently demanded potions in the game, ensuring stable prices.

Giant Seaweed (Level 23 Farming)

Giant seaweed is grown underwater at the seaweed patches north of the Fossil Island bank. It's used to make soda ash for molten glass, which feeds into Crafting training. Giant seaweed sells for 600-800 GP each on the GE, and each patch yields 25-50 seaweed with ultracompost. Two patches mean 50-100 seaweed per run, or 30K-80K GP per run. The patches re-grow every 40 minutes, and a full run takes under 2 minutes — a fast, consistent addition to your Farming routine.

7. Other Profitable Skills

Fishing — Anglerfish, Karambwans, Dark Crabs

Fishing profits have seen a meaningful uplift in 2026 thanks to crew provisioning demand from Sailing. Here's how the top methods stack up:

Anglerfish (Level 82, 200K-350K GP/hr): Anglerfish are caught at the Fishing Platform in Port Piscarilius (100% favor required). They heal 22 HP and are the highest-healing fish in the game, making them in-demand for bossing and now naval combat. At 82-91 Fishing, expect 80-110 anglers per hour. At 92+ with the Fishing Cape, 110-140 per hour. Raw anglers sell for 2,200-2,600 GP each. This is a fully AFK method — you spot the fishing spot, click, and wait up to 60 seconds between catches.

Karambwans (Level 65, 250K-450K GP/hr): Karambwans are unique because the fishing spots never move. You teleport to the Karamja fairy ring (CKR), fish a full inventory, bank at Zanaris bank (also a fairy ring), and return. Each trip takes about 2.5 minutes, and you catch roughly 20-25 karambwans per trip. At 600-720 GP each, that's 12K-18K per trip, or 288K-432K GP/hr. With the Karamja Hard diary increasing catch rate, and the Fish Barrel (from Tempoross) doubling inventory capacity, experienced fishers can push toward 500K GP/hr.

Dark Crabs (Level 85, 350K-550K GP/hr): Dark crabs are the highest-profit fishing method, but they require Wilderness access (Resource Area, level 36 Wildy). Raw dark crabs sell for 1,800-2,200 GP each. With the Wilderness Elite Diary doubling catch rate and the elite Wildy Sword teleport directly to the Resource Area, catch rates reach 180-260 per hour. The risk of PKers is the main downside; use the Resource Area's safe exit (magical barrier that requires a fee) and keep your risk low.

Woodcutting — Magic and Redwood Logs

Woodcutting has quietly become one of the most improved profit skills post-Sailing. Ship construction consumes timber continuously, and both magic and redwood logs benefit from near-constant demand.

Magic Logs (Level 75, 180K-300K GP/hr): Magic trees found at the Woodcutting Guild (level 75+ section), Sorcerer's Tower, and Mage Training Arena. At 75-89 Woodcutting, expect 80-130 magic logs per hour. At 90+ with the Dragon Axe special attack buff (+3 invisible Woodcutting boost), 130-180 per hour. Magic logs sell for 1,500-1,900 GP each in 2026. AFK level varies by location — the Woodcutting Guild has closer trees but more competition; Sorcerer's Tower is less crowded.

Redwood Logs (Level 90, 250K-400K GP/hr): Redwood trees are exclusive to the Farming Guild's redwood patch (level 90+ area). They're extremely AFK — each tree can produce 100+ logs before depleting, and the depletion timer is roughly 4-5 minutes. At 90-98 Woodcutting, expect 180-250 redwood logs per hour. At 99 with the Woodcutting Cape perk, 250-350 per hour. Redwood logs sell for 1,200-1,600 GP each. Redwoods are the best AFK Woodcutting profit in the game.

Smithing — Giant's Foundry and Blast Furnace

Smithing has traditionally been a fast-but-expensive buyable skill, but two methods turn it profitable in 2026:

Giant's Foundry (Level 15+, 150K-350K GP/hr): Located in the Giants' Plateau (south of the Desert), Giant's Foundry lets you combine two metal types (e.g., steel + mithril) to create alloy items at a profit. The minigame buys back your finished items for GP, and the payout per alloy tier is: Bronze/Iron (100-150K GP/hr), Steel/Mithril (200-300K GP/hr), Mithril/Adamant (250-400K GP/hr). The GP/hr listed is above the cost of buying bars/items on the GE to use as input materials. The Sailing skill has driven demand for Giant's Foundry rewards (the Smithing outfit and moulds), keeping the buyback prices elevated.

Blast Furnace (Level 30+, 600K-1.2M GP/hr): The Blast Furnace on the east side of Keldagrim converts ores into bars at half the normal coal cost and with no chance of failure. The profit comes from the spread between ore prices and bar prices. In 2026, the best bars are:

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is the single best skilling money maker in OSRS 2026?

The highest raw GP/hr from skilling is Blast Furnace steel bars at 800K-1.5M GP/hr, but it requires intense concentration. For a balance of profit and AFK ability, Amethyst mining (92+) at 350K-610K GP/hr is the king. For pure GP-to-active-time ratio, Herb runs with Ranarr/Snapdragon are unmatched — they effectively pay 2.4M-4M GP/hr of your active time, just spread across real-world hours.

Q: How has Sailing changed the profit meta for skillers?

Sailing has increased profits across the board by creating constant, predictable resource consumption. Key winners: Woodcutting (teak/mahogany up 80-157%), Mining (runite ore +10%), Fishing (all food fish up 15-37%), Smithing (bar prices up 47-55%). The only skilling method that saw a profit decrease is Torstol herb runs, but that's unrelated to Sailing — it's driven by Herblore training demand outpacing herb prices.

Q: Is Sailing itself a good money-making skill?

Yes, Sailing is competitive with other gathering skills. Shipwreck salvaging (400K-750K GP/hr at level 35+) and Cargo Contracts (500K-900K GP/hr at 70+) are legitimate money-makers that rival traditional methods. Elite and Master contracts at level 85+ can exceed 1M GP/hr, putting Sailing in the upper tier of skilling moneymakers.

Q: What skilling method requires the least attention?

Redwood trees (90 Woodcutting) and Anglerfish (82 Fishing) are the kings of AFK. Redwoods can go 4-5 minutes between clicks, and Anglerfish spots last 30-60 seconds per catch. For something between AFK and active, Amethyst mining and Sacred Eels (87 Fishing, Fishing Guild) are excellent middle-ground options.

Q: Can I make a bond purely through skilling in 2026?

Absolutely. A bond costs around 15M GP in 2026. If you do 3 herb runs per day (Ranarr, all patches), you'll earn approximately 600K-870K GP per day from herbs alone — covering a bond in 18-25 days. Add Snape grass, limpwurt roots, and cactus spines and you're looking at 12-16 days. Combine this with daily battlestaves (Zaff's staff shop, Varrock diary), managing Miscellania for passive resources, and one active money-making session per week (e.g., 2 hours of Blast Furnace for 1.6M-3M GP), and you can sustain membership indefinitely through skilling alone.

Q: What skill should I focus on first for money?

Farming is the best first investment. It requires minimal startup GP (seed money for your first few herb runs), the active time is negligible (7-8 minutes per run), and once established it funds everything else. After Farming is providing a steady income, invest in Runecrafting (nature runes at 44, then bloods at 77) or Mining (Blast Mine) for your active money-making sessions.

Q: Are there any F2P skilling methods worth doing for GP?

F2P skilling money-makers are limited but not useless. Yew logs (50K-80K GP/hr at Edgeville or behind Varrock Castle) and Lobster fishing on Karamja (60K-90K GP/hr) are the best pure F2P options. Mining iron ore in the Dwarven Mine with a Rune Pickaxe can earn 80K-120K GP/hr. However, no F2P method breaks 150K GP/hr consistently, so your goal as a F2P player should be saving for your first bond (15M GP) — which is a grind, but achievable with 35-40 hours of iron ore mining.
