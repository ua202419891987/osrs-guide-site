ð¹ 1. How Bosses Work â?Tiers, Scaling & Summoning

Windrose bosses are divided into three tiers plus a final boss. Tier 1 bosses are encountered naturally through exploration. Tier 2 and 3 bosses are summoned using specific items crafted from lower-tier boss drops. This gating ensures you cannot accidentally stumble into a boss fight you are not ready for. Boss health and damage scale with player count (solo = 100%, 2 players = 175%, 3 players = 240%, 4 players = 300%), but drop quality and quantity also scale â?more players means more loot.

Every boss has a unique arena â?a procedurally placed landmark on the map. Tier 1 arenas are marked with a single skull icon. Tier 2 with two skulls. Tier 3 with three. The final boss arena (The Eternal Storm) is visible from anywhere on the map as a permanent hurricane at the center. Bosses do not respawn on their own â?after defeating a boss, you must craft and use their summoning item to fight them again for additional loot.

Captain Blackwood Strategy

Blackwood sails a Sloop with 4 cannons. His signature move is the Broadside Barrage â?he turns perpendicular to you and fires all cannons in rapid succession. When you see him turning broadside, immediately turn your ship into the wind and raise sails â?this minimizes your profile and reduces hits by 70%. After his barrage, he has a 15-second reload window. This is your damage phase. Chain shot his mast first (immobilize him), then switch to round shot and hammer his hull. When his ship durability drops below 30%, he will attempt to flee. Grapple and board at this point for maximum loot.

Q: Can I fight bosses in any order?

Tier 1: yes, freely. Tier 2: you must defeat at least one Tier 1 boss to craft the summoning item. Tier 3: requires Tier 2 boss drops. Final boss: requires all Tier 3 boss drops. The game enforces progression through summoning item recipes.

Q: Which boss drops the best weapon?

Kraken's Fang (Tier 2) is the best value â?obtainable mid-game, S-tier damage, and viable through the final boss. Leviathan Harpoon (Tier 3) has higher damage but slower attack speed. Admiral's Cutlass (Tier 3) is the best all-around weapon but requires the hardest fight (Dread Admiral's duel).

Q: Can I beat the final boss without a Galleon?

Theoretically yes, practically no. A fully upgraded Brigantine can survive the storm's damage, but the Phase 1 naval combat against Storm Barrier ships is balanced for Galleon-level firepower. Attempting with a Brigantine is technically possible but extremely difficult â?expect 10+ attempts.
