📜 1. Windrose Early Access — Current State & Version History

Windrose entered Steam Early Access on November 14, 2025, developed by Windrose Studio (a small independent team of 12 people based in Bordeaux, France). The game launched with a solid foundation: three boss fights, basic shipbuilding, survival mechanics, and approximately 20 hours of content. Seven months into Early Access (as of June 2026), Windrose has received five major content updates, three performance patches, and countless quality-of-life improvements based directly on community feedback.

The current version is 0.7.5 (released June 10, 2026). This update added the long-awaited Merchant Alliance faction, a new trading mini-game, two new boss fights (the Kraken and the Emerald Empress), and significant naval combat improvements. The game now offers approximately 60-80 hours of content for a first-time player, with more on the way. The developers have confirmed that full release (version 1.0) is targeted for Q4 2026, though this may shift depending on the scope of remaining features.

📋 2. Latest Patch (0.7.5) — Full Analysis

Update 0.7.5, released on June 10, 2026, is the largest content update since the addition of co-op in 0.7.0. It introduces two major new systems (the Merchant Alliance faction and the mount system expansion), two new boss encounters, a complete rework of naval combat physics, and approximately 40 new items (weapons, ship parts, cosmetics). Here is the full breakdown of everything changed.

1

New Content: Merchant Alliance Faction

The Merchant Alliance is a new NPC faction that gives players access to a trading mini-game. You can now accept "Trade Contracts" from Merchant Alliance NPCs found in coastal towns on Tropical and Rocky islands. These contracts ask you to deliver specific goods (spices, rare woods, alchemical ingredients) to a destination island within a time limit. Successful deliveries earn you Merchant Reputation (unlocks new ship cosmetics, trading discounts, and a unique mount: the Merchant Caravan Turtle) and Gold Coins (new currency used to buy premium ship parts and cosmetics). This adds approximately 8-10 hours of endgame content.

2

New Boss: The Kraken

The Kraken is a deep-ocean boss encounter triggered by sailing into marked "Kraken Zone" areas (shown as a swirling vortex on the map). The fight has three phases: Phase 1 (tentacles only — destroy 8 tentacles while avoiding ink spray attacks), Phase 2 (Kraken surfaces — target the glowing eyes while avoiding suction attacks that pull your ship in), Phase 3 (enrage — the Kraken gains speed and damage, water becomes rough, fight must be completed within 3 minutes or the vortex collapses and you "drown"). Rewards: Kraken Beak (crafts the highest-DPS piercing weapon in the game), Tentacle Leather (best-in-slot armor material), and the "Tentacle Hat" cosmetic.

3

New Boss: The Emerald Empress

The Emerald Empress is a lore-heavy boss located in a new underground dungeon on Volcanic Islands. She is the leader of the undead pirate fleet and wields ice magic (a unique mechanic — ice in a volcano). The fight requires learning a complex 12-attack pattern and has a "enrage timer" of 8 minutes. This is widely considered the hardest boss in the game currently. Rewards: Empress Circlet (helmet with +15% magic damage), Emerald Blade (sword with life-steal property), and access to the "Empress Vault" (secret treasure room with guaranteed epic loot).

4

Naval Combat Rework

Naval combat has been completely overhauled. Cannons now use realistic ballistics — you must lead your shots based on target distance and wind direction. Cannon types now matter: Round Shot (standard, cheap, moderate damage), Chain Shot (destroys enemy sails — slows them down), Grape Shot (anti-personnel — kills enemy crew on deck), and Fire Bomb (sets enemy ship on fire — damage over time). Ship boarding is now a tactical mini-game: you grapple onto the enemy ship, and combat switches to a "ship deck" arena where you fight the enemy crew in soulslite combat. This replaces the old "instant win if you board" mechanic.

5

Quality of Life Improvements

Update 0.7.5 also includes: Ship cargo UI redesign (you can now see all items across all storage chests on your ship in one screen), auto-sort button for inventory (finally!), map markers (place custom waypoints on your map), торрент weather warning system (a UI indicator 30 seconds before a storm hits), and the ability to rename your ship (talk to any Shipwright NPC). Performance: Reduced memory usage by ~400MB on average, and fixed a bug where ships would occasionally "launch into the sky" after a cannon hit (yes, this was a real bug).

Patch 0.7.5 Pro Tip: The Merchant Alliance reputation grind is the fastest way to get Gold Coins, which buy the best ship cosmetics in the game. Prioritize "Long-Distance Trade Contracts" (deliveries to islands > 2000m away) — they give 3x the reputation of short-distance contracts. Also, the Kraken fight is much easier with a Galleon-class ship (more cannons, more HP) than a Sloop. Bring at least one friend for the Kraken — the tentacles hit much harder in solo mode.

📜 3. Complete Update History — Every Patch Explained

Understanding the full update history helps you appreciate how Windrose has evolved since Early Access launch. Each update was shaped significantly by community feedback — the developers are highly active on the official Discord (15,000+ members) and have implemented over 60 community-requested features since launch. Below is a detailed look at each major update and what it added to the game.

✅ Released

Update 0.5.5 — The Swamp Update (Dec 12, 2025)

Community Influence: Players complained that the launch version had only two island biomes (Tropical and Rocky), making exploration feel repetitive after 10 hours. The Swamp biome was added in response, featuring new resources (poisonous herbs, mushrooms, clay), a new enemy type (Venomous Snakes), and the poison status effect (damage-over-time that requires Antidote items to cure). Also added: 10 new weapons (including the first ranged weapons — muskets and pistols), and the "Campfire Fast Travel" system (connect two campfires with a "Travel Rope" item to teleport between them). This update addressed the #1 complaint on the Steam forums: "not enough biome variety."

✅ Released

Update 0.6.0 — Volcanic Islands & Obsidian Gear (Jan 20, 2026)

Community Influence: Players felt the progression curve was too flat — there was no clear "endgame" gear tier beyond iron weapons. Update 0.6.0 added Volcanic Islands (high-danger, high-reward biome), Obsidian-tier gear (requires defeating the Ash Drake boss to unlock the smithing recipe), and the Ash Drake boss fight itself (a multi-phase dragon boss that remains one of the most visually impressive encounters in the game). Also added: temperature system (Volcanic Islands are hot — you need heat resistance potions), and the first "legendary" weapon (Ash Drake's Tooth — a sword with fire damage). This update doubled the game's content from ~20 hours to ~40 hours.

✅ Released

Update 0.7.0 — Co-op & Ship Expansion (Mar 25, 2026)

Community Influence: The #1 most requested feature since EA launch was 4-player co-op. Update 0.7.0 delivered this, plus shared base building (all players in a co-op session can build/craft at the host's base), and two new ship classes: the Brigantine (mid-game warship with 6 cannons) and the Galleon (endgame behemoth with 12 cannons and 5000 HP). Co-op scaling was carefully balanced: boss HP scales by +40% per additional player (not 100%, which would make 4-player bosses impossible). This update also introduced the "Host Migration" feature — if the host disconnects, a new host is automatically elected and the session continues.

✅ Released

Update 0.7.4 — Coral Reef & Mount System (May 22, 2026)

Community Influence: Players requested more transportation options beyond ships (island traversal on foot is slow). Update 0.7.4 added the Coral Reef biome (beautiful underwater sections you can explore while holding your breath or using a diving helmet), a mount system (rideable turtles that move 2x faster than walking on land, and 1.5x faster in water), and a fishing mini-game (catch 20+ fish species, some of which give buffs when cooked and eaten). The fishing mini-game was added partly as a joke (the community joked that every survival game needs fishing) but turned out to be genuinely fun and rewarding.

Community Impact: Windrose Studio has an unusual level of transparency for an indie developer. They post weekly "Friday Dev Updates" on their Discord and Steam news page, detailing exactly what they are working on, what bugs they are fixing, and what features they are considering. They have a "Community Votes" system where players can suggest features and vote on them. The top 3 voted features are guaranteed to be implemented in the next major update. This has created a very positive relationship between developers and players — the Steam review score has risen from "Mixed" (63%) at EA launch to "Very Positive" (89%) as of June 2026.

📊 4. EA Launch vs. Current State — How Far Windrose Has Come

Windrose at Early Access launch (November 2025) was a playable but incomplete game. It had a solid core loop but lacked content depth, had significant performance issues, and was missing many quality-of-life features that players expect from a modern survival game. Seven months later, the difference is dramatic. This section provides a side-by-side comparison of the EA launch version versus the current version (0.7.5) across all major categories.

The most dramatic improvements have been in performance and content depth. At launch, Windrose had serious memory leak issues that made the game crash every 60-90 minutes — this alone caused many negative reviews. The developers prioritized fixing this, and Updates 0.6.2 and 0.7.3 have reduced crashes to the point where 3-4 hour play sessions are now stable for most players. Content-wise, the game has tripled in size, with five new island biomes, four new boss fights, two new ship classes, and the entire co-op system added post-launch.

Honest Assessment: Despite the massive improvements, Windrose is still clearly an Early Access game. Some textures are placeholder (especially in the Coral Reef biome), the tutorial is minimal (new players often get stuck on "how to build a ship"), and there are still occasional bugs (ship physics can glitch out, saving/loading sometimes resets ship positions). However, the trajectory is unambiguously positive — the game is being actively improved every 4-6 weeks with meaningful content.

Windrose Studio has published a public roadmap on their Steam page and Discord, with quarterly updates showing what they are working on. The roadmap is divided into "Confirmed" (features that are in active development and will ship), "Planned" (features the developers want to add but are not yet in development), and "Community Suggested" (features from player votes that may be added depending on resources). Confirmed Features: NPC pirate hunter fleets that patrol the ocean and attack players with high "Notoriety" (earned by attacking Merchant Alliance ships), a new "Naval Alliance" faction (NPC allies that help you fight pirate hunters), 2 new ship customization options (figureheads and ship paint), and a new endgame dungeon ("The Sunken Crypt") accessible only by diving underwater near Coral Reef islands. Community Requested: The pirate hunter system was the #1 voted feature in the Q1 2026 Community Vote. Players wanted more danger on the open ocean beyond just ghost ships and the occasional Kraken.

Planned Features: Final boss fight ("The Storm Lord" — an elemental boss that controls weather), completion of all 6 planned factions (3 remain: Naval Alliance, Explorer's League, and the mysterious "Order of the Tides"), full game ending cinematic and post-credits scene, New Game+ mode (restart with all gear, enemies scaled +50% HP/damage), Steam Workshop support (mods), and "Completionist" achievement set (100+ achievements). 📋 6. How to Report Bugs & Stay Updated

Windrose Studio relies heavily on player bug reports to identify and fix issues. Because the game has procedural generation (every playthrough has a unique map), certain bugs only appear under specific conditions that the developers' internal testing may not catch. Learning how to report bugs effectively — and where to find the latest news — will help you contribute to the game's improvement and stay informed about upcoming changes.

1

How to Report Bugs Effectively

The most effective bug reports follow a specific format. Include: 1. Steps to Reproduce (exactly what you did before the bug occurred — be precise, e.g., "Equipped Iron Sword, attacked Skeleton Pirate 3 times, then switched to Bow, game crashed"), 2. Expected Behavior (what should have happened), 3. Actual Behavior (what actually happened), 4. System Specs (GPU, CPU, RAM, OS — use "dxdiag" command in Windows Run to generate a system report), 5. Screenshots/Videos (press F12 in Steam to take a screenshot, or record a clip with OBS/ShadowPlay). Submit bug reports on the official Windrose Discord in the #bug-reports channel, or via the in-game bug report tool (Esc → Feedback → Report Bug).

2

Best Ways to Stay Updated on Patches

1. Official Discord (Best): Join the official Windrose Discord server (15,000+ members). The developers post weekly "Friday Dev Updates" every Friday at 5 PM CET. These include patch previews, developer insights, and community highlights. 2. Steam News: Follow Windrose on Steam (click "Follow" on the store page). All patch notes are posted to Steam News within 1 hour of release. 3. Reddit: r/Windrose (community-run, 8,000+ members) has good discussion but is slower than Discord. 4. YouTube: "Windrose Studio" official channel posts video patch trailers for major updates.

3

What Features Are Still Missing from Full Release

Based on the roadmap and community comparisons to the "ideal" survival game, here are the major features still missing: 1. New Game+ Mode (replay with your gear — planned for 1.0), 2. Full Tutorial (current tutorial is minimal — new players frequently get stuck), 3. Mod Support (Steam Workshop — planned for 1.0), 4. Console Ports (PS5/Xbox — planned for 2027), 5. PvP Mode (not confirmed, community is split 50/50 on wanting this), 6. Seasonal Events (Halloween, Winter — planned post-1.0), 7. Steam Achievements (currently none — planned for 1.0), 8. Dedicated Server Tools (for community-hosted servers — high community demand, not yet confirmed).

Community Tip: If you want to influence the roadmap, participate in the quarterly "Community Vote" on the official Discord. Every 3 months, the developers post a list of 10-15 potential features and let the community vote on the top 3. The winners are guaranteed to be implemented in the next major update. Recent Community Vote winners include: co-op (Update 0.7.0), mount system (Update 0.7.4), and pirate hunter fleets (coming in Update 0.8.0). Your vote genuinely matters.

❓ FAQ — Early Access & Updates

Q: Will my save file work with future updates?

Yes, with rare exceptions. Windrose Studio has maintained backward compatibility for all updates so far — you can load a save file from version 0.5.0 in version 0.7.5 without issues. However, major updates (like 0.7.0 which added co-op) may reset some world generation to incorporate new content (new islands won't appear in already-explored areas, but your base and inventory are preserved). The developers recommend backing up your save file before major updates: locate C:\Users\[YourName]\AppData\Local\Windrose\Saved\SaveGames and copy the folder to a safe location.

Q: Is now a good time to buy Windrose, or should I wait for full release?

Now is a good time to buy. The game is ~80% complete, runs well, and has enough content for 60+ hours of gameplay. Buying now supports the developers during the critical end-of-Early-Access phase, and you get to influence the roadmap via Community Votes. If you prefer to wait until all content is finished, wait for version 1.0 (target: November 2026). However, the game will likely cost more at 1.0 than during Early Access (current price: $24.99, expected 1.0 price: $34.99 based on Steam trends for similar games).

Q: How often do updates release?

Major content updates release every 6-8 weeks. Performance/QoL patches release every 3-4 weeks. The developers have maintained this cadence consistently since launch. Each major update is accompanied by a "Patch Trailer" video on YouTube and a detailed written patch note on Steam News. The developers occasionally do "hotfix" updates (small bug fixes) within days of a major update if critical bugs are discovered.

Q: Will there be DLC or paid expansions after 1.0?

The base game (all content up to 1.0) will remain complete and satisfying. The developers have hinted at "expansion passes" post-1.0 (new island regions, new boss tiers, new ship classes) but have not confirmed pricing or content. Based on similar games (Valheim, Ark), expect ~$15-20 per expansion, with the first expansion likely 6-9 months after 1.0. All quality-of-life updates and bug fixes will remain free indefinitely.

Q: Where can I find the full patch notes for older updates?

Full patch notes for every update are available on the Windrose Steam News page (store.steampowered.com/news/app/[WindroseAppID]). You can also find them on the official Windrose website (playwindrose.com/news). The Discord #patch-notes channel has a pinned message with links to every patch note since EA launch. For Update 0.7.5 specifically, the full patch notes are 3,200 words long and cover 140+ individual changes — worth reading if you want the finest details.
