Windrose Front Cannons Dealing No Damage Bug Fix

After a recent patch, many Windrose captains found their bow / front cannons firing but dealing zero damage — balls sail through enemy hulls, the hit marker appears, but the HP bar never moves. This guide breaks down the confirmed causes and gives you fixes you can apply today on PC and console.

⚓ Ship Combat Fix · 8 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Symptom: front/bow cannons fire, show a hit, but deal 0 damage.
  • 📌 Cause 1: wrong ammo type loaded into the bow mount (round shot vs. configured damage profile).
  • 📌 Cause 2: cannon placed at an extreme angle — the ball spawns outside the hitbox.
  • 📌 Cause 3: a known post-patch collision bug where bow hits don't register on large hulls.
  • 📌 Fix now: reload ammo, re-seat the cannon, switch to broadside, or remount the bow gun.

1. What the Bug Looks Like

You line up a perfect bow shot on an enemy ship. The cannon fires, the muzzle flash plays, sometimes a hit marker or "splash" appears — but the enemy HP bar does not move. Reload, fire again, same result. Switch to your broadside cannons and suddenly damage registers fine. That mismatch is the tell-tale sign of the front-cannon bug.

⚠️ Don't Blame Your Build

If your side cannons work but your bow guns don't, it is almost certainly this bug — not your cannon tier, crew, or upgrade tree. Verify with the checklist below before refunding upgrade points.

3. How to Fix It (Step by Step)

Try these in order — most players are back to dealing damage by step 3.

① Reload the Correct Ammo

Walk up to the bow cannon, open the ammo menu, and confirm Round Shot (or your damage round) is selected — not Grapeshot or Chain Shot, which have near-zero vs-hull damage. Manually reload even if it shows full.

② Re-seat the Cannon

Enter build/edit mode, pick the bow cannon up and place it back down. This rewrites its transform and often fixes the "ball spawns outside hitbox" angle problem.

③ Fire From Broadside Instead

As a tactical stopgap, angle the ship so your side battery covers the target. Side cannons are unaffected by the bow bug, so you keep dealing damage while the bow gun is broken.

④ Remount the Bow Gun on a Flatter Spot

If the bow cannon sits on a steeply angled deck piece, move it to a flatter section near the prow. Flatter placement = straighter projectile spawn = reliable hits.

⑤ Restart the Session (Console / Co-op)

On console or in co-op, a desynced "loaded" flag often only clears on a full restart. Save, quit to menu, and reload.

💡 Reality Check

If steps 1–4 don't help and only the bow gun is affected, you're hitting the known collision bug (section 5). Use your broadside until the hotfix lands.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Players who set Grapeshot as their "default ammo" in the loadout screen often forget it carries to the bow mount. Grapeshot shreds crew but does almost nothing to hulls — which looks exactly like the "no damage" bug.

5. The Known Collision Bug

Several players have reported that even with correct ammo and a flat mount, bow hits on large enemy hulls fail to register after the latest patch. This is a server/client collision issue where the forward-facing projectile's hitbox is evaluated a frame late and misses the hull volume. Windrose Studio has acknowledged hull-collision regressions in recent patches.

📋 Workaround Until the Hotfix

  1. Use broadside cannons as your primary damage source.
  2. Keep one bow gun only for finishing low-HP targets at close range.
  3. Report the bug with a clip through the official support channel — reproducible reports speed up the fix.
🔔 Track the Fix

Watch the official Windrose patch notes and the in-game notice board. A collision hotfix is expected in an upcoming update. Avoid "damage modifier" mods — they risk your account and can trigger anti-cheat.

6. FAQs

Do I need to upgrade my bow cannon to fix this?

No. If your side cannons deal damage, your cannon tier is fine. The bug is about ammo/angle/collision, not upgrade level.

Is my save corrupted?

Almost certainly not. This is a combat-state issue, not a save problem. A session restart clears the desynced "loaded" flag.

Does this happen in co-op too?

Yes — and it can appear differently for the host vs. clients. If the host's bow gun works but a client's doesn't, it's the same collision desync. Both should restart.

Will reinstalling the game help?

Unlikely. The bug is in the current build's collision logic, not your install. Reinstalling won't change the patch behavior.
