🛠 1. Crafting System Core Mechanics Overview

Before diving into specific workbench levels and recipes, you need to understand how the crafting system in Windrose actually works. Many new players assume it works like Minecraft or Valheim — place a crafting station and all recipes are immediately available. Windrose is different. Its crafting system has three interlocking mechanics that determine what you can and cannot build: station tier, discovery unlock, and material prerequisite.

Station Tier — The Foundation of Everything

Every crafting station in Windrose has a tier level (Lv1, Lv2, Lv3). Higher-tier stations unlock more advanced recipes. You cannot craft an iron sword at a Lv1 workbench, no matter how many materials you have. The station itself must be upgraded. Upgrading stations requires specific materials and, in some cases, requires you to craft specific "proof of progress" items (like a smelted iron ingot) before the upgrade option appears. This prevents players from rushing advanced recipes before they have explored enough to find the necessary materials.

Discovery Unlock — The Hidden Recipe System

As explained in our Day 1-3 Survival Guide, many Windrose recipes are hidden by default. They only appear after you pick up a specific material for the first time. This "Discovery Crafting" system means that checking a recipe list online and then wondering why the recipe is not in your menu is a universal new player experience. Section 6 of this guide has a complete table of commonly missing recipes and the exact items you need to collect to unlock them. Bookmark that section — you will need it.

Material Prerequisite — Just Having It Is Not Enough

For some advanced recipes, simply having the materials in your inventory is not enough. You must have the materials physically present in a storage container within 20 meters of the workbench. This is called the "local storage" system. It simulates the idea that your character cannot craft a complex item without the parts being nearby. If a recipe is not showing up even though you have the materials in your inventory, try placing the materials in a chest next to the workbench. This fixes the problem 90% of the time.

Crafting Queue — Batch Crafting Saves Time

Windrose allows batch crafting. Hold Shift and click a recipe to queue up to 10 copies. The workbench will automatically craft them one by one as long as you have the materials. This is essential for mass-producing arrows, bolts, and basic building materials. You can also set a workbench to "loop crafting" (right-click recipe → "Repeat") which will continuously craft that item until you run out of materials or manually stop it. This is useful for AFK resource processing — just make sure you are not standing near any aggressive enemies when you AFK.

Pro Tip — Use the Search Bar: The workbench crafting menu can get very long (Lv3 has 80+ recipes). Press Ctrl+F while the workbench menu is open to bring up a search bar. Type part of an item name to filter the list. This saves enormous amounts of time compared to scrolling through dozens of icons looking for one specific recipe.

🔧 2. Workbench Lv1 — Getting Started (Available Recipes + Material List)

The Workbench Lv1 is the first major crafting station you will build, usually within the first 1-2 hours of a new game. It transforms Windrose from a primitive survival game (punch trees, eat berries) into a proper crafting game where you can make tools, armor, and structure components. This section lists every recipe available at Workbench Lv1, organized by category, with material costs and practical notes.

These are the foundation of your entire playthrough. You need all of them. Craft them in the order listed — each one makes getting the materials for the next one easier.

These "intermediate" materials are used in dozens of higher-tier recipes. Always keep a stock of them. Running out of rope in the middle of a building project is frustrating.

Efficiency Tip — Bulk Craft Planks Early: As soon as you have 30 spare wood, convert 20 of it into 10 planks. Planks are used in almost every Lv2 recipe and having a stockpile prevents crafting interruptions later. You can also use planks as fuel for campfires — 1 plank burns for 2 minutes, twice as long as raw wood.

🔥 3. Workbench Lv2 Upgrade Guide (Prerequisites + New Recipes Unlocked)

Workbench Lv2 is the first major "wall" that new players hit. You have basic tools and a small base, but you need better gear to tackle POIs and explore other islands. Lv2 unlocks metal tools, reinforced armor, better building materials, and the crafting stations that enable the rest of the tech tree. The upgrade requires specific materials and a progression milestone.

Upgrade Prerequisites — What You Need Before You Can Upgrade

To upgrade your workbench to Lv2, you must meet ALL of the following conditions:

1. Workbench Lv1 must be placed and used at least 10 times. "Used" means you have crafted at least 10 items from the Lv1 menu. This is a soft gate to ensure you are familiar with basic crafting before advancing.

2. You must have mined at least 1 Iron Ore node. Iron ore nodes are grey rocks with orange/brown speckles, found on rocky outcrop islands (grey on the map). You need a Stone Pickaxe to mine them. Mining your first iron ore also unlocks the "Smelting" recipe (see Section 5).

3. Upgrade cost: 15 Wooden Planks + 10 Iron Ingots + 5 Leather Straps + 3 Rope. Iron ingots are smelted from iron ore at a Smelter (which you can build before upgrading the workbench — see Section 5). Leather straps are crafted from coarse leather (2 leather → 3 straps) at the Lv1 workbench.

New Recipes Unlocked at Lv2 — The Good Stuff

Workbench Lv2 adds approximately 35 new recipes. The most impactful ones are listed below by category:

Iron Tools (Lv2)

Iron tools are 50% faster than stone tools and have 3x the durability. Upgrading your axe and pickaxe to iron should be your first priority after the upgrade.

Reinforced Armor (Lv2)

Reinforced armor is crafted from iron ingots and leather. A full set gives +45 armor rating (vs. +28 for coarse leather) and reduces physical damage by 30%. This is the first armor set that makes you feel "tough".

Improved Structures (Lv2)

Lv2 structures are more durable and have higher weather resistance. Wooden walls (Lv1) have 100 HP and can be destroyed by strong enemies. Reinforced walls (Lv2) have 300 HP and are resistant to fire damage.

Reinforced Wall: 4 Planks + 2 Iron Ingot + 1 Rope. 300 HP. Recommended for perimeter walls.

Reinforced Floor: 3 Planks + 1 Iron Ingot. 300 HP. Use for base floors in high-traffic areas.

Wooden Window: 2 Planks + 1 Glass (glass is smelted from sand at the Smelter). Allows you to see outside while inside. Enemies cannot shoot through it, but you can shoot out. Essential for base defense.

Upgrade Timing Warning: Do NOT upgrade your workbench to Lv2 until you have built all the Lv1 structures you want. Some Lv1-exclusive decorative items and primitive structures are not available at Lv2 (they are "replaced" by better versions). If there is a specific Lv1 cosmetic you like, build it before you upgrade. You can still craft Lv1 items after upgrading — but the menu organization changes and some players find the Lv2 menu harder to navigate.

⚡ 4. Workbench Lv3 Advanced Crafting (Mid-to-Late Game Goal)

Workbench Lv3 is the endgame crafting tier. It unlocks steel tools, enchanting, advanced sailing components, and the boss-summoning items that are required to progress the main story. Most players reach Lv3 around hour 20-30 of playtime. The upgrade prerequisites are significantly more demanding than Lv2, requiring materials from dangerous biomes and defeated mini-bosses.

Upgrade Prerequisites — The Lv3 Gate

1. Workbench Lv2 must be placed and used at least 25 times (craft 25 Lv2 recipes).

2. You must have defeated at least 1 Island Boss (any of the 4 mini-bosses found on volcanic, swamp, rocky, or frozen islands). Defeating a boss drops a "Boss Core" item. You need 1 Boss Core to upgrade to Lv3.

3. You must have mined at least 1 Steel Ingot (smelted from iron ingots + coal at the Smelter Lv2).

4. Upgrade cost: 25 Reinforced Planks + 15 Steel Ingot + 10 Boss Core + 5 Rare Herb + 10 Rope. Reinforced planks are crafted from wooden planks at the Lv2 workbench (2 planks + 1 resin → 1 reinforced plank). Resin is gathered from pine trees on rocky islands.

New Recipes Unlocked at Lv3 — Endgame Crafting

The Lv3 workbench adds approximately 40 new recipes. The most notable are:

Steel Tools: Steel tools are 30% faster than iron and have 5x the durability. A steel pickaxe can mine obsidian (required for endgame upgrades). Steel Axe recipe: 3 Steel Ingot + 2 Wood + 1 Rope.

Steel Armor: Full set gives +80 armor rating, reducing physical damage by 50%. Also gives +20% fire resistance (essential for volcanic island exploration). Steel Chestplate recipe: 8 Steel Ingot + 6 Leather + 2 Resin.

Enchanting Station: Unlocks the enchanting system. You can enchant weapons and armor with buffs like +damage, +defense, +stamina efficiency. Enchanting requires "Arcane Essence" (dropped by ghost ships and certain bosses). The enchanting station itself costs 10 Steel Ingot + 5 Arcane Essence + 5 Wooden Planks to build.

Ship Cannon: Unlocks ship-mounted cannons for naval combat. Essential for late-game sailing. Each cannon costs 5 Steel Ingot + 3 Rope + 2 Arcane Essence. A fully upgraded Galleon can mount 8 cannons.

Boss Summon Items: Lv3 unlocks the items needed to summon the four main story bosses (the "Legendary Captains"). Each summon item requires rare materials from a specific biome. Defeating all four Legendary Captains is required to "beat" the game and unlock the endgame credits sequence.

Lv3 Upgrade Order Tip: When you finally have the materials to upgrade to Lv3, do it at your MAIN base (the one you plan to keep for the rest of the game). The Lv3 workbench has a larger footprint than Lv2 and cannot be placed in small huts. Make sure you have a 4x4 flat area clear before you start the upgrade. Nothing is more annoying than upgrading and then realizing the workbench does not fit in your current base.

⚖ 5. All Crafting Stations Explained (Smelter / Charcoal Kiln / Grindstone / Armor Workbench / Weapon Smithy)

The workbench is the "hub" of your crafting setup, but it cannot craft everything. Windrose uses a network of specialized crafting stations, each handling a specific type of recipe. Understanding what each station does and when to build it is essential for efficient base layout and progression. This section explains every crafting station in the game, its purpose, its material cost, and its upgrade path.

🔥 Smelter (Foundry)

Purpose: Smelts raw ore into ingots. This is where you turn iron ore into iron ingots, copper ore into copper ingots, and (at Lv2) iron ingots into steel ingots. Also smelts sand into glass and clay into bricks.

Lv1 Recipe: 10 Stone + 5 Wood + 3 Clay. Clay is found near riverbanks (look for orange-brown nodes in the ground).

Lv2 Upgrade: Requires 10 Iron Ingot + 5 Fire Bricks + 3 Rope. Fire Bricks are crafted from clay + coal at the Lv1 Smelter. Lv2 Smelter smelts 2x faster and can process steel.

Important Note: The Smelter requires fuel (wood or coal) to operate. 1 wood = 30 seconds of smelting. 1 coal = 90 seconds. Always keep a chest of coal next to your smelter. Running out of fuel mid-smelt pauses the process but does not waste materials.

🔥 Charcoal Kiln

Purpose: Converts wood into coal efficiently. You need coal for smelting, cooking, and some advanced recipes. The Charcoal Kiln is more efficient than burning wood in a campfire (3 wood = 1 coal at the kiln vs. 5 wood = 1 coal at a campfire).

Recipe: 8 Stone + 5 Wood + 2 Clay. Available at Workbench Lv1.

Upgrade Path: Lv2 (available after Workbench Lv2) increases output to 2 coal per 3 wood and reduces processing time by 30%.

Strategy: Build 2 Charcoal Kilns as soon as you have the materials. Coal is the bottleneck for almost all mid-game crafting. Having 2 kilns running in parallel cuts your coal gathering time in half.

⚓ Grindstone

Purpose: Sharpens tools and weapons, restoring their durability. Also crafts sharpening kits (consumable items that temporarily increase weapon damage by 10% for 5 minutes). The Grindstone is the primary tool repair station — do not rely on the Repair Hammer for everything, as the hammer cannot repair high-tier items.

Recipe: 12 Stone + 3 Iron Ingot + 2 Rope. Requires Workbench Lv2.

Repair Cost: Repairing an item at the Grindstone costs 1 Iron Ingot per 25 durability restored. For expensive steel items, this can get pricey. Always carry a Grindstone at your main base AND at any forward operating base you build on other islands.

👚 Armor Workbench

Purpose: Crafts all armor, clothing, and personal equipment. The main workbench handles primitive armor, but the Armor Workbench is required for reinforced, steel, and enchanted armor.

CRITICAL RULE: The Armor Workbench MUST be placed under a roof. If you try to place it in the open, it will not work. Build a 2x2 or 3x3 roof section first.

Lv1 Recipe: 12 Wood + 8 Stone + 4 Fiber. Available at Workbench Lv1 (but requires roof).

Lv2 Upgrade: Requires Workbench Lv2. 10 Iron Ingot + 5 Leather + 3 Rope. Unlocks steel armor recipes.

Lv3 Upgrade: Requires Workbench Lv3. 15 Steel Ingot + 10 Arcane Essence + 5 Resin. Unlocks enchanted armor recipes.

⚔ Weapon Smithy

Purpose: Crafts all weapons (swords, axes, spears, pistols, rifles). The main workbench handles primitive weapons (stone club, flint knife), but the Weapon Smithy is required for iron weapons, firearms, and endgame weapons.

Recipe: 15 Stone + 10 Wood + 5 Iron Ingot + 3 Rope. Requires Workbench Lv2. Can be placed outdoors (unlike the Armor Workbench).

Lv2 Upgrade: Requires Workbench Lv3. 12 Steel Ingot + 8 Arcane Essence + 5 Fire Brick. Unlocks enchanted weapons and boss-summoning weapons.

Note: The Weapon Smithy also repairs weapons (same as the Grindstone). If you have both stations, use the Weapon Smithy for weapons and the Grindstone for tools — this distributes the iron ingot repair cost.

🏠 Tanning Rack

Purpose: Converts raw hides into leather and cloth. Essential for all armor crafting. Without a Tanning Rack, you can only get coarse leather from boars (which is RNG-based). The Tanning Rack lets you convert ANY hide into usable leather.

Recipe: 8 Wood + 4 Rope + 2 Iron Ingot. Available at Workbench Lv1.

Output: 1 Raw Hide → 1 Coarse Leather (30 seconds). 1 Crocodile Hide → 2 Reinforced Leather (60 seconds, requires Lv2 Tanning Rack).

💡 6. Hidden Recipe Unlock Rules (The #1 Beginner Confusion Point)

This section is so important that it appears in both our Day 1-3 Survival Guide and this crafting guide. Why? Because it is the single most common question on Windrose forums, Discord servers, and Reddit: "Why is this recipe not showing up in my crafting menu even though I have all the materials?" If you read only one section of this guide, make it this one.

The Discovery Crafting System — Full Explanation

Windrose uses a "Discovery Crafting" system where certain recipes are HIDDEN from your menu until you meet specific unlock conditions. The game does not tell you this. There is no tutorial popup. The recipe simply does not appear, and you are left wondering if you have a bug or if the guide you are following is wrong.

There are three types of hidden recipe unlocks in Windrose:

Type 1 — Material Discovery: Pick up Material X for the first time, and Recipe Y appears. This is the most common type. Approximately 40% of Lv2 and Lv3 recipes use this unlock method.

Type 2 — Station Proximity: Some recipes only appear when you are standing within 10 meters of a specific station. For example, "Repair Kit" only appears in your menu when you are near a Grindstone. This is intentional — the recipe makes no sense without the station context — but it confuses new players who try to craft everything from their inventory.

Type 3 — Quest Trigger: A small number of recipes (mostly boss-summoning items and endgame gear) are locked behind NPC quests. Talk to the NPC on the rocky outcrop island (the blacksmith) and complete his 3-quest chain to unlock the "Tempered Steel" recipes.

Still Cannot Find the Recipe? If you have tried everything in this section and a recipe still will not appear, check: (1) Is your workbench the right tier? (2) Are you looking in the right menu (some recipes are on the station, not the workbench)? (3) Did you actually PICK UP the material (not just see it on the ground)? If all three are correct, the recipe may be bugged — try restarting the game. A small number of recipes have a known bug where they fail to unlock until a full game restart.

Inventory management is one of the biggest quality-of-life factors in Windrose. The starting inventory (12 slots) is painfully small. Every upgrade adds slots and makes exploration, resource gathering, and combat significantly more convenient. Worn Sailcloth Bag — Your First Priority Upgrade

This is the most important upgrade because it is the earliest and the quality-of-life improvement is immediate. Going from 12 to 20 slots means you can carry: a full tool set (axe, pickaxe, knife, hammer), 2 stacks of wood, 1 stack of stone, food for a full day, and still have room for loot. Without this upgrade, you will be making supply runs back to your base every 10 minutes.

Material farming route for Worn Sailcloth Bag: (1) Cloth: harvest 12 plant fiber (4 fiber = 1 cloth, need 4 cloth = 16 fiber). (2) Rope: craft from fiber (3 fiber = 1 rope, need 3 = 9 fiber). Total fiber needed: 25. Harvest for 10 minutes and you will have enough. (3) Leather: hunt 2 boars. Each boar drops 1-2 coarse leather. You need 2 total.

Reinforced Leather Pack — The Mid-Game Power Spike

32 slots is enough to carry a full combat loadout (weapon, shield, armor, 2 food types, bandages, ammo) AND still have room for significant loot. This is the backpack you will use for most of the mid-game. The iron ingot cost (4) is noticeable but manageable by day 5-7. The leather cost (6) means you need to hunt regularly or build a boar farm (yes, you can farm boars by luring them into a fenced area and breeding them — but that is an advanced topic for another guide).

Canvas Expedition Bag — The Endgame Luxury

52 slots. With this backpack, you can carry enough materials to build a small outpost FROM your inventory. It is expensive (8 canvas, 6 steel ingot, 2 arcane essence) but completely changes how you play the game. With 52 slots, you never need to worry about inventory management again. Canvas is crafted from cloth at the Lv3 workbench (2 cloth + 1 resin = 1 canvas). Arcane Essence is dropped by ghost ships and bosses. Plan ahead — this upgrade requires significant preparation.

Inventory Organization Tip: Use the "label" feature on storage chests (right-click chest → "Set Label") to organize your base inventory. Label chests as "WOOD", "STONE", "ORE", "FOOD", "ARMOR", etc. When your inventory is full, you can quickly dump items into the correct chest without having to search through them. This saves enormous amounts of time over a 30-hour playthrough.

⚠ 8. 5 Pitfalls Beginners Always Fall Into

Even with guides like this one, new Windrose players make predictable mistakes. These are not "skill issues" — they are design decisions in the game that are counterintuitive or poorly explained. Learning these pitfalls before you experience them will save you hours of frustration and possibly a deleted save file.

Pitfall #1: Upgrading the Workbench Before Building All Desired Lv1 Structures

As mentioned in Section 3, upgrading to Lv2 changes the workbench menu organization. Some Lv1-exclusive decorative items and "primitive" structures are buried deeper in the menu after the upgrade, and some players cannot find them. More importantly, if you upgrade before you have a proper base layout planned, you may realize too late that you need more Lv1 materials (which are now inefficient to gather because you have moved on to Lv2 tools and are exploring distant islands). Fix: Before upgrading to Lv2, build ALL Lv1 structures you think you might want. You can always upgrade later — there is no time pressure.

Pitfall #2: Not Knowing About the "Discovery Crafting" System

You read a guide that says "craft a flintlock pistol at the Lv2 workbench". You go to your Lv2 workbench. It is not there. You spend 2 hours mining, crafting, and restarting the game before you find a forum post explaining that you need to pick up a "Rusted Mechanism" first. This is the single most common new player frustration. Fix: Bookmark Section 6 of this guide. Anytime a recipe is missing, check the unlock table before you do anything else.

Pitfall #3: Building the Armor Workbench in the Open (Without a Roof)

The Armor Workbench REQUIRES a roof to function. If you place it in the open, it will appear placed but will not open a crafting menu when you interact with it. New players often waste 15-20 minutes trying to figure out why their Armor Workbench is "broken" before they realize the roof requirement. Fix: Always build a 3x3 roof section BEFORE you place the Armor Workbench. The roof can be as simple as wooden roof tiles on wooden pillars — it does not need to be enclosed walls.

Pitfall #4: Running Out of Coal and Not Knowing How to Get More Efficiently

Coal is required for the Smelter, the Cooking Fire Pit, and several Lv2+ recipes. New players often burn all their coal on smelting and then realize too late that they need coal for other things. They then spend hours mining coal nodes (which are rare) instead of building a Charcoal Kiln. Fix: Build 2 Charcoal Kilns on day 3. Convert your excess wood into coal. 3 wood = 1 coal at the kiln. This is infinitely more sustainable than mining coal nodes. Never use coal nodes as your primary coal source — they are a backup for when your kilns are behind on production.

Pitfall #5: Not Upgrading the Backpack Early Enough

The Worn Sailcloth Bag (Section 7) costs very little and provides a massive quality-of-life improvement. Yet many new players delay crafting it because they are "saving materials for a base" or "want to upgrade their tools first". This is a mistake. Every minute you play with 12 inventory slots is a minute you are being less efficient than you could be. Fix: Craft the Worn Sailcloth Bag on day 2. It costs almost nothing and pays for itself in time saved within the first hour of use. There is no reason to delay this upgrade.

❓ FAQ — Crafting Questions

Q: Why is a recipe not showing up in my crafting menu even though I have the materials?

This is the Discovery Crafting system. Many recipes are hidden until you pick up a specific material for the first time. See Section 6 of this guide for the complete unlock table. The most common missing unlock is "Iron Tools" — you need to mine 1 iron ore node before the recipes appear.

Q: Can I upgrade my workbench directly from Lv1 to Lv3?

No. You must upgrade to Lv2 first, craft Lv2 recipes at least 25 times, and then upgrade to Lv3. The game forces you to progress through tiers sequentially. This prevents rushing and ensures you engage with each tier's content.

Q: How do I get more inventory slots?

Craft backpack upgrades at the workbench. The progression is: Starting Bag (12 slots) → Worn Sailcloth Bag (+8, 20 total) → Reinforced Leather Pack (+12, 32 total) → Canvas Expedition Bag (+20, 52 total). See Section 7 for full details and material costs.

Q: Where do I place the Armor Workbench? It is not opening.

The Armor Workbench MUST be placed under a roof. Build a roof section first (3x3 wooden roof tiles on pillars), then place the workbench underneath. Without a roof, the workbench will appear placed but will not function.

Q: How do I get coal reliably?

Build a Charcoal Kiln (8 stone + 5 wood + 2 clay at Workbench Lv1). 3 wood = 1 coal at the kiln. This is much more efficient than mining coal nodes. Build 2 kilns and keep them running continuously. For smelting, 1 coal = 90 seconds of smelter time.

Q: What is the fastest way to get iron ingots?

Mine iron ore from rocky outcrop islands (grey on the map). 1 iron ore = 1 iron ingot at the Smelter. You need a Stone Pickaxe to mine iron nodes. A single iron node yields 3-5 ore. You need 10 ingots to upgrade to Workbench Lv2, so mine at least 3-4 nodes.

Q: Can I move my workbench after placing it?

Yes. Hit the workbench with a hammer (left-click) to dismantle it. You get 75% of the materials back. This is true for all structures except the campfire (which drops 100% of materials when dismantled). Do not be afraid to reposition your base as you learn what works and what does not.

Q: How do I repair my tools and weapons?

For primitive (stone) tools: use a Repair Hammer (craft: 3 wood + 2 stone). Equip the hammer, right-click the tool in your inventory, and click "Repair". For iron/steel tools: use a Grindstone (costs 1 iron ingot per 25 durability). For weapons: use the Weapon Smithy or Grindstone. Tools and weapons break permanently when durability hits 0 — always repair at 25% durability or higher.
