Windrose Co-op Blocked by ISP / Port 3478 — Player Fix

You can launch Windrose, but every time you try to join a friend's co-op session you get stuck on "Connecting…", a "Strict NAT" warning, or an instant "Failed to join." If your internet works for everything else, the culprit is almost always UDP port 3478 being blocked or filtered by your router or ISP. This guide shows you how to confirm it and fix it from the player side.

🎮 Level: Intermediate · 9 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Symptom: you can't join co-op sessions, get "Strict NAT," or fail to connect while normal browsing is fine.
  • 📌 Root cause: Windrose uses UDP port 3478 for co-op traffic; many routers/ISPs block or don't forward it.
  • 📌 Step 1: test the port to prove it's blocked (not a game bug).
  • 📌 Step 2: enable UPnP on your router, then manually forward 3478 if needed.
  • 📌 Step 3: confirm with a mobile hotspot — if co-op works there, your ISP/home router is the problem.

1. 🔍 What the Block Looks Like

Before you start changing router settings, confirm you're actually looking at a port/network block and not a different problem. A blocked UDP 3478 connection produces a very specific set of symptoms that separate it from server downtime or a corrupted install.

⚠️ Is It Really the Port?

If every online feature fails — you can't even reach the main menu server list — that's a different outage, not your port 3478. This guide is for the case where the game runs fine but co-op joining fails while everything else on your PC/console works.

SymptomWhat you seePoints to port 3478 block?
Stuck connecting"Connecting to session…" forever, then timeoutYes — very typical
Strict / Moderate NATGame shows "Strict NAT" or "Teredo unavailable"Yes
Invite failsFriend's invite never arrives or "Failed to join" instantlyYes
Single-player fineCampaign / solo sailing works with zero issuesConfirms it's network, not game
Hotspot worksSwitching to phone tethering lets you joinDefinitively your home ISP/router

2. 🧠 Why Port 3478 & Your ISP Matter

Windrose's co-op netcode sends real-time ship-state updates over UDP port 3478. Unlike normal web traffic (which rides HTTP/HTTPS on ports 80/443 and is almost never touched by your ISP), game peer-to-peer ports like 3478 are frequently:

  • Closed by default in your router's NAT — the router doesn't know which internal device should receive the incoming packets, so it drops them.
  • Filtered by some ISPs — certain carriers (especially mobile, satellite, and some budget fiber/resellers) block or "throttle" uncommon UDP ranges to cut down on peer-to-peer traffic.
  • Hidden behind CGNAT — if your ISP shares one public IP among many customers, normal port-forwarding on your router can't expose you at all.

The good news: as a player (not the host), you usually don't need the port perfectly open on both sides — you just need your own router to allow the outbound/return traffic and to negotiate a clean NAT type. Enabling UPnP or forwarding 3478 to your machine clears the vast majority of cases.

💡 UDP, not TCP

Windrose co-op uses UDP on 3478. If a guide tells you to forward TCP 3478 only, that will NOT fix co-op. Always set the rule to UDP (or "Both" if your router forces it), never TCP-only.

3. 🧪 How to Test if Port 3478 Is Blocked

You don't have to guess. Run these quick checks in order — by step 3 you'll know for certain whether 3478 is the problem.

① Check the In-Game Network / NAT Indicator

Open Windrose's settings → Network (or the co-op lobby). If it reports "Strict" or "Failed to detect NAT," your router isn't opening the port for the game. An "Open" or "Moderate" result already suggests the issue is on the friend's side instead.

② Try a Direct Invite From a Known-Good Friend

Ask a friend who can play co-op with others to invite you. If you still can't connect but they connect with everyone else, the block is on your end. This isolates "my network" vs. "their network."

③ Test the Port From Your PC

On Windows, open PowerShell and check whether anything is listening / reachable:

Test-NetConnection -ComputerName <friend-or-server-ip> -Port 3478 -InformationLevel Detailed

If TcpTestSucceeded shows False (and recall Windrose uses UDP, so also try a UDP-capable tester like yougetsignal or portchecker aimed at your own public IP while the game is running), the port is not reaching you. That's your confirmation.

⚠️ Don't Trust a Single Tool

Some online "port checkers" only test TCP. Because Windrose uses UDP, a checker may falsely report 3478 as "closed" even when it's fine. Use the in-game NAT indicator + the hotspot test (section 6) as your real proof.

4. ⚙️ Enable UPnP on Your Router

UPnP (Universal Plug and Play) lets Windrose ask your router to open port 3478 automatically. It's the easiest fix and resolves most player-side blocks.

① Log In to Your Router

Find your gateway address (usually 192.168.0.1 or 192.168.1.1 — check ipconfig under "Default Gateway" on Windows, or "Router" in your OS network settings). Open it in a browser and log in with the admin credentials printed on the router or set by you.

② Find the UPnP Setting

Look under Advanced → NAT Forwarding → UPnP (names vary: "ALG," "Internet Sharing," "Device Discovery"). Toggle UPnP / NAT-PMP to On and save. Reboot the router if prompted.

③ Restart Windrose and Re-Test

Fully close the game, relaunch, and check the NAT indicator again. In most home networks this flips "Strict" to "Open/Moderate" and co-op starts working without any manual forwarding.

🔔 Security Note

UPnP has a small security trade-off (any device can request an open port). For a home gaming network it's generally acceptable, but if you run a tightly locked-down network, prefer manual forwarding (next section) and leave UPnP off.

5. 🔓 Manually Port-Forward Port 3478

If UPnP didn't help, set a permanent rule so your router always sends UDP 3478 to your machine. You'll need a static LAN IP for your PC/console first.

① Give Your Device a Static LAN IP

On Windows: Settings → Network → Properties → set IPv4 to "Manual" with a fixed address like 192.168.1.50, gateway 192.168.1.1, and your ISP's DNS. On console: network settings → assign a static IP in the same range as your router.

② Create the Forwarding Rule

In the router, go to Port Forwarding / Virtual Server. Add a rule:

FieldValue
Service nameWindrose-Coop
ProtocolUDP (or Both)
External port3478
Internal port3478
Internal IPyour static LAN IP (e.g. 192.168.1.50)
StatusEnabled

③ Save, Reboot Router, Re-Test

Apply the rule, reboot the router, relaunch Windrose, and re-check the NAT indicator. A manual UDP 3478 forward typically yields a clean "Open" NAT and lets you join any friend whose own NAT is Moderate or Open.

⚠️ Both Sides Usually Need a Usable NAT

Peer-to-peer co-op needs at least one side with an Open/Moderate NAT. If your friend is also Strict, you may both need to forward 3478. Point them to the host-side guide below if they're running a dedicated server.

6. 📱 Use a Mobile Hotspot to Confirm

This is the cleanest "is it my ISP?" test, and it takes two minutes. If co-op works on a hotspot but not on your home Wi-Fi, your home connection is definitively the blocker.

📋 Hotspot Confirmation Steps

  1. On your phone, enable Personal Hotspot / Tethering (USB or Wi-Fi).
  2. Connect your PC/console to the hotspot instead of your home router.
  3. Launch Windrose and try to join the same friend's session.
  4. If you connect instantly → your home ISP/router is blocking 3478. Apply sections 4–5 at home.
  5. If it still fails on the hotspot → the block is on the friend's side, or it's a game/server issue. Have them read the host fix.
💡 Why Hotspots Usually Work

Mobile carriers handle UDP 3478 differently and often present a friendlier NAT for gaming. A successful hotspot join is strong, reproducible proof you can hand to your ISP if you need to argue for an unfiltered connection.

7. 🛡️ When UPnP & Forwarding Aren't Enough

If you've enabled UPnP, forwarded UDP 3478, set a static IP, and co-op still fails at home, you're likely behind CGNAT or your ISP is hard-filtering the port. Options, in order of effort:

  • Ask your ISP for a public IPv4 / disable CGNAT. Many ISPs will move you to a dedicated IP (sometimes for a small fee) — this makes port-forwarding actually work.
  • Request they stop filtering UDP 3478. Cite "I need UDP 3478 open for Windrose co-op" — some residential filters are removable on request.
  • Use a gaming-friendly VPN that supports UDP forwarding. A reputable VPN with port-forwarding can tunnel 3478 around an ISP block. Avoid free VPNs — they often add lag that ruins ship combat.
  • Play via hotspot or a friend's network as a temporary workaround while you sort the home line.
⚠️ Don't Touch Anti-Cheat

Never install "NAT unlocker" or "co-op booster" tools from unknown sources. They can trigger Windrose's anti-cheat and risk your account. Stick to router, ISP, and VPN changes you control.

8. ❓ FAQs

Do I need to forward the port if I'm just joining, not hosting?

Often no — enabling UPnP alone fixes most "joiner" cases because it opens the return path. Manual forwarding is the next step if UPnP doesn't clear your Strict NAT.

TCP or UDP for port 3478?

UDP. Windrose co-op rides UDP 3478. Forwarding TCP-only will not help. Set the rule to UDP, or "Both" if your router gives no choice.

My friend can play with everyone except me — who's blocked?

That points at your side. Run the hotspot test (section 6): if co-op works on the hotspot, your home network is the blocker and sections 4–5 are your fix.

Will reinstalling Windrose fix the port block?

No. This is a network/router issue, not a game file problem. Reinstalling won't open UDP 3478.

Is this the same fix as the dedicated-server host needs?

Similar idea, different device. If you or your friend runs a dedicated co-op server, the port must be forwarded on the server's router/firewall — see the host-side guide linked below.
