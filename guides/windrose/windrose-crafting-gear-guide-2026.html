â?4. Ship Upgrades â?Hull, Cannons, Sails & Figureheads

Ship upgrades go beyond building a higher-tier ship. Each ship has four upgrade categories: Hull (durability + damage resistance), Cannons (damage + reload speed), Sails (speed + turn rate), and Figurehead (unique bonuses). Upgrading a Sloop's cannons from Copper to Iron increases damage per broadside by approximately 35%. A fully upgraded Brigantine with Steel cannons, reinforced hull, and enchanted sails can take on Galleons that outgun it on paper.

Figureheads are special bow-mounted decorations that provide unique passive bonuses. The Shark Figurehead (+15% ramming damage), the Kraken Figurehead (chance to terrify enemy crew, reducing their accuracy by 20%), and the Phoenix Figurehead (ship repairs 5% durability per minute while not in combat) are the three best figureheads. Most figureheads drop from specific bosses â?the Kraken Figurehead drops from the Kraken boss (Tier 2 sea monster).

Q: Should I upgrade my Copper Cutlass or save for Iron?

Upgrade to Copper +3 (cost: 3 additional copper ingots), then save resources for Iron. Do not push Copper past +3 â?the cost per upgrade increases exponentially, and an Iron Cutlass at +0 outdamages a Copper Cutlass at +5.

Q: Where is the best place to farm Iron Ore?

Mountain caves on Rocky Outcrop islands. Bring torches (caves are dark), a pickaxe, and a weapon (caves contain skeleton spawns). One cave run typically yields 20-30 iron ore and 5-10 coal.

Q: Can I craft everything in single-player?

Yes. All crafting recipes are available in single-player. Some recipes require boss drops, but all bosses are soloable. Crafting costs do not change between solo and co-op.
