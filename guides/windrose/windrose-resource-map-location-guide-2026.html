🌍 1. Why Resource Gathering Is the Core Loop of Windrose

Unlike many survival games where resource gathering is a background chore, Windrose places it at the absolute center of the gameplay loop. Everything you build, craft, sail, and fight with traces back to a specific resource node on your map. Your first ship? Requires copper ingots for the fittings. Your first real weapon? Requires copper ore smelted into ingots. Your ability to build a smelter? Requires clay. Your ability to make gunpowder for cannons? Requires sulfur. The entire technological progression of the game is gated by your ability to locate, mine, and process these four core resources.

This guide is designed to be your permanent reference. Because Windrose uses procedurally generated maps, the exact coordinates of resource nodes will differ in every playthrough — but the biome patterns never change. Copper always spawns in starter-region caves and surface veins. Clay always appears along riverbanks and shallow water edges. Iron always appears in the Foothills biome caves. Sulfur always appears in volcanic and geothermal zones. Learn the biome, and you will always find the resource.

The following sections break down each of the four core resources with: visual description (so you recognize the node on sight), exact biome and sub-location guidance, tool requirements, gathering method, primary uses, and associated crafting recipes. All data is based on the current Early Access build (June 2026) and will be updated as the game develops.

🟠 2. Copper Ore — Your First Metal

Copper is the gateway resource of Windrose. It is the first metal you will ever mine, the first ingot you will ever smelt, and the material that unlocks your entire early-game weapon and tool progression. Without copper, you cannot build a Weaponsmithing Station, cannot craft your first real sword, and cannot complete the quest chain that rewards your first free ship. If you are stuck in the early game, the answer is almost always: go mine more copper.

🔍 What Copper Ore Looks Like

Copper ore nodes have a distinctive orange-brown coloration with bright greenish-blue verdigris streaks running through the rock. Surface deposits are small, typically yielding 3-5 ore per node. Cave deposits are larger, yielding 8-12 ore per node. When you approach a copper node, your character will display a small pickaxe icon above their head if they are carrying a pickaxe — this is your visual confirmation that the node is minable.

🗺️ Exact Location Descriptions

Primary Location — Starter Island Northern Cave: Every procedurally generated map places a copper-rich cave in the northern or northwestern quadrant of your starting island. The cave entrance is recognizable by a cluster of 2-3 bats guarding the entrance (they are passive until you enter their aggro range — about 4 meters). Inside the cave, copper nodes line the walls at roughly 10-meter intervals. A full clear of the cave yields approximately 30-45 copper ore. The cave also contains 1-2 flint nodes and occasionally a chest with basic tools.

Secondary Location — Surface Veins (Southern Hills): After you have exhausted the northern cave, look for surface copper veins on the southern hills of your starting island. These appear as small orange rocky outcrops, usually near the tree line but not under full forest canopy. There are typically 4-6 surface veins per island. Each yields only 3-5 ore, but they are safe to mine (no cave bats) and perfect for topping up your supply while exploring.

Tertiary Location — Neighboring Tropical Islands: Once you have a raft, the small tropical islands surrounding your starting island also contain copper caves. These are identical to the starter island cave but may have skeleton pirates instead of bats. Bring a weapon and a shield. The copper yield is the same: 30-45 ore per full cave clear.

Copper Prospecting Tip: Copper nodes respawn on a timer (approximately 20-30 minutes of real time). If you have cleared all copper on your starting island, sail to a neighboring island, mine there, and return to the first island — the nodes will have respawned. This "island hop" method is the fastest way to farm large quantities of copper in the early game.

🛠️ Required Tools & Gathering Method

Minimum Tool: Stone Pickaxe (2 wood + 3 stone, crafted at the Workbench). A stone pickaxe can mine copper but it is slow — each node takes about 12-15 hits and the pickaxe loses 10 durability per node. Recommended Tool: Copper Pickaxe (crafted after you have smelted your first batch of copper ingots). The copper pickaxe mines copper nodes in 6-8 hits and has 3x the durability of a stone pickaxe.

Gathering Method: Equip your pickaxe (assign to a hotbar slot for quick swapping). Approach the copper node. Left-click to swing. Each hit deals tool damage to the node and consumes 5 character stamina. If your stamina reaches zero mid-mine, the node stops taking damage until stamina regenerates. Pro tip: Eat a coconut (restores 30 stamina) immediately before starting a mining session. The stamina regeneration buff lasts 3 minutes and lets you chain-mine 3-4 nodes without stopping.

🟤 3. Clay — The Builder's Essential

Clay is the unsung hero of the Windrose early game. It is not glamorous. It does not make weapons. It does not make armor. But without clay, you cannot build a Smelter, cannot build a Charcoal Kiln, and cannot progress past the stone-tool phase. Clay is the bottleneck that catches many new players off guard — you need it early, it is not obvious where to find it, and you need a surprising amount of it to build all your essential stations.

🔍 What Clay Looks Like

Clay deposits appear as smooth, rounded grey-brown mounds partially embedded in the ground near water. They have a slightly shiny, damp appearance and lack the jagged edges of stone nodes. When you approach a clay deposit with a shovel equipped, your character will display a small shovel icon. Each clay node yields 4-7 clay balls. Clay balls stack to 20 in your inventory.

🗺️ Exact Location Descriptions

Primary Location — Riverbanks: The most reliable clay source is the edges of any freshwater river or stream on your starting island. Walk along the riverbank and look for the smooth grey mounds described above. Clay spawns more frequently on the inner curves of river bends (where water flow deposits sediment). A single river on your starting island typically has 6-10 clay nodes along its banks.

Secondary Location — Shallow Water Edges: Clay also spawns at the exact point where land meets shallow water — the "wet sand" zone of any beach. These nodes are partially submerged and may require you to wade into ankle-deep water to harvest. The yield per node is the same (4-7 clay balls), but these nodes are easier to spot from a distance because the water darkens slightly over the clay mound.

Tertiary Location — Pond and Lake Perimeters: Any standing body of freshwater on your map will have clay deposits around its perimeter. Ponds in forest clearings are particularly rich — they often have 8-12 nodes in a tight circle around the water's edge, making them the most efficient clay farming spot on the starting island.

🛠️ Required Tools & Gathering Method

Required Tool: Shovel (any tier). The Wooden Shovel (2 wood at Workbench) is sufficient for clay gathering. It has 50 durability and each clay node consumes 5 durability. Recommended: Copper Shovel (5 copper ingots at Weaponsmithing Station) — 3x durability and 2x gathering speed.

Gathering Method: Equip the shovel. Approach the clay mound. Right-click (the shovel uses a "dig" action mapped to right-click, not left-click like the pickaxe). Your character will perform a 1.5-second digging animation. The clay balls appear directly in your inventory. Important: Clay nodes do not respawn as quickly as copper nodes. Expect a 45-60 minute respawn timer. Plan ahead and collect all the clay you need in one session rather than making repeated trips.

⚫ 4. Iron Ore — The Core Resource After Reaching the Foothills

Iron is where Windrose's difficulty curve真正 kicks in. Copper gets you through the first 5-8 hours. Iron gets you through the next 20. Iron tools are 2x faster than copper. Iron weapons deal 2x damage. Iron armor reduces incoming damage by 40% compared to copper armor's 15%. But iron is not found on your starting island. To reach iron, you must sail to the Foothills biome — and the Foothills biome is where the game stops holding your hand.

🔍 What Iron Ore Looks Like

Iron ore nodes have a dark grey to black appearance with occasional rusty red streaks. They are larger and more jagged than copper nodes. In caves, iron nodes glow with a very faint reddish ember light — this makes them easier to spot in dark cave sections. Surface iron deposits are rare; 90% of iron is found in caves. Each iron node yields 5-9 ore (more than copper per node, but harder to mine).

🗺️ Exact Location Descriptions

Primary Location — Foothills Mountain Caves: The Foothills biome is a mountainous region typically found 2-3 islands away from your starting island in the "north" or "inland" direction (map generation varies, but Foothills are always farther from the starting area than Tropical islands). The mountain caves in this biome contain dense iron deposits. Entering a Foothills cave without iron-tier or at least copper-tier armor is not recommended — the enemies here hit hard.

Secondary Location — Deep Cave Systems (Foothills): Some Foothills islands have multi-level cave systems. Iron spawns more densely on the lower levels of these caves. You will need a Torch or better yet a Lantern (crafted with copper and clay) to navigate the deeper levels. The deepest level of a Foothills cave typically contains 15-20 iron nodes — a full clear yields 75-180 iron ore.

Tertiary Location — Surface Boulders (Foothills Mountainside): Occasionally, iron spawns as surface boulders on the mountainside of Foothills islands. These are risky to mine because they are often patrolled by mountain wolves or skeleton archers. Only attempt surface iron if you have a ranged weapon (bow or pistol) to deal with patrols first.

Foothills Danger Rating: HIGH. The Foothills biome introduces enemy types that can one-shot an unarmored player. Mountain wolves deal 45-60 damage per bite. Skeleton archers shoot from 20+ meters. Cave trolls (found in the deepest cave levels) deal 80+ damage per slam and have 300 HP. Before sailing to the Foothills, ensure you have: copper armor (full set), copper sword, wooden shield, at least 3 bandages, and a bedroll placed on the beach near your ship. If you die deep in a Foothills cave, you will respawn at your distant starting island — a long sail back.

🛠️ Required Tools & Gathering Method

Minimum Tool: Copper Pickaxe. A stone pickaxe cannot mine iron — this is a common new-player mistake. You must upgrade to at least a copper pickaxe before attempting the Foothills. Recommended Tool: Iron Pickaxe (crafted after your first iron smelting run). The iron pickaxe mines iron nodes in 4-5 hits and has 5x the durability of a copper pickaxe.

Gathering Method: Same as copper — equip pickaxe, left-click to swing. Iron nodes have 2x the hit points of copper nodes, so even with an iron pickaxe, each node takes 4-5 hits. The main challenge is not the mining itself but staying alive while mining. In Foothills caves, mine with your back to a wall and your weapon drawn. Enemies can and will sneak up on you while you are mid-swing on a node.

⚓ The Iron Run: A Step-by-Step First Trip

Step 1: Craft a Copper Pickaxe and at least a Copper Sword. Step 2: Sail your raft or sloop to the nearest Foothills island (look for grey-brown mountainous terrain on the horizon). Step 3: Dock at a beach far from any cave entrance. Place a Bedroll on the sand. Step 4: Scout the cave entrance during daylight. Clear any wolves or archers outside. Step 5: Enter the cave. Mine only the first 5-8 iron nodes (stay near the entrance). Step 6: Retreat to your ship, sail home, smelt your first iron ingots. Step 7: Craft an Iron Pickaxe. Step 8: Return to the Foothills cave with your iron pickaxe — now you can mine the deep cave efficiently and safely.

🟡 5. Sulfur — The Key Ingredient for Gunpowder

Sulfur is the most dangerous resource to gather and the most rewarding. It is the gateway to Windrose's firearms and explosives — the game's late early-game and mid-game power spike. A single sulfur node can be mined in seconds, but getting to that node may require navigating a volcanic biome, dodging environmental damage, and fighting enemies that leverage fire and poison. Sulfur is rare, valuable, and entirely optional — but players who skip sulfur will find themselves unable to progress past the mid-game boss fights.

🔍 What Sulfur Looks Like

Sulfur nodes have a bright yellow to yellowish-green crystalline appearance. They literally glow with a faint yellow light, making them the easiest resource node to spot in poor visibility. The crystals grow in clusters on rock surfaces, similar to quartz in real life. Each sulfur node yields 2-4 sulfur chunks. Sulfur chunks stack to 30 in your inventory.

🗺️ Exact Location Descriptions

Primary Location — Volcanic Biome Caves: The Volcanic biome (red/orange terrain on the map) contains sulfur in its cave systems. These caves are hot — your character's temperature gauge will rise while inside, and staying more than 5 minutes without heat protection will cause heatstroke (drains stamina by 50%). Sulfur nodes line the cave walls, typically near the ceiling. A full Volcanic cave clear yields 20-40 sulfur chunks.

Secondary Location — Hot Springs / Geyser Fields: Some islands have above-ground geothermal features — hot springs, geysers, and steam vents. Sulfur spawns as surface crystals around these features. The yield per node is lower (2-3 chunks), but these are safer to mine than cave nodes. Warning: Geysers erupt on a timer (approximately every 45 seconds). Stand near a geyser only when it is dormant — the eruption deals 30 fire damage and knocks you back.

Tertiary Location — Deep Cave Geothermal Vents: The deepest level of some Volcanic caves contains active geothermal vents. Sulfur spawns in exceptionally high density around these vents (10-15 nodes in a small area). However, the vent area inflicts a heat debuff that drains stamina continuously. Bring Fire Resistance Potions (crafted from alchemical ingredients found in Swamp biomes) before attempting to farm these high-density sulfur nodes.

🛠️ Required Tools & Gathering Method

Required Tool: Any Pickaxe (Stone, Copper, or Iron). Unlike iron, sulfur can be mined with a stone pickaxe. However, by the time you reach sulfur-bearing biomes, you should already have an iron pickaxe. Environmental Protection: Bring a Lantern (caves are dark), Bandages (volcanic biome enemies hit hard), and Heat Resistance (either a Fire Resistance Potion or heat-resistant armor if you have unlocked it).

Gathering Method: Equip pickaxe, left-click to swing. Sulfur nodes are softer than metal ores — they break in 3-4 hits even with a stone pickaxe. The real challenge is positioning. In volcanic caves, sulfur nodes are often on the ceiling or behind steam vents. You may need to build a small wooden platform (costs 4 wood) to reach ceiling nodes.

📍 7. Recommended Farming Routes

Efficient resource farming in Windrose is not about mindlessly running from node to node — it is about planning routes that minimize backtracking, avoid unnecessary combat, and synchronize with resource respawn timers. The following two routes are optimized for the early game (Days 1-7) and mid early-game (Days 8-14) based on current EA gameplay patterns.

🟢 Route A: Early-Game Loop (Days 1-7) — "The Starter Circuit"

This route assumes you have a raft (or can swim short distances) and are focused on accumulating your first 40-60 copper ore and 30-40 clay for your initial Smelter + Charcoal Kiln + first copper tools.

  1. Start at your base. Equip Stone Pickaxe, Stone Axe, and Shovel. Pack 5 bandages and 3 cooked meat.
  2. Mine the northern copper cave. Clear all copper nodes (30-45 ore). Kill the bats with your spear or sword. Place a Bedroll at the cave entrance if you have not already.
  3. Surface copper sweep. Walk the southern hills of your starting island. Mine all 4-6 surface copper veins (12-30 additional ore).
  4. Clay run along the river. Walk the full length of your starting island's river. Mine all 6-10 clay nodes (24-70 clay balls).
  5. Repeat or sail to neighbor island. If your copper nodes have not respawned, sail to the nearest tropical neighbor island. Repeat steps 2-4 there.
  6. Return home. Smelt your first copper ingots. Craft a Copper Pickaxe. This completes the Day 1-3 resource circuit.

Time Investment: One full Starter Circuit takes approximately 45-60 minutes. Yield: 50-100 copper ore + 30-70 clay balls. This is enough to craft your first 2-3 copper tools and still have surplus for a Weaponsmithing Station.

🟡 Route B: Mid Early-Game Loop (Days 8-14) — "The Foothills Expedition"

This route assumes you have copper armor, a copper sword, a Copper Pickaxe, and a Sloop (or at least a repaired Raft). The goal is your first significant iron haul and potentially your first sulfur if you are feeling brave.

  1. Prepare at base. Repair your ship to 100% durability. Pack 15 bandages, 10 cooked meat, 5 coconuts, a bedroll, and a full set of copper tools (pickaxe, axe, shovel).
  2. Sail to the Foothills. Use the wind indicator — sail with the wind. The Foothills island(s) are typically 2-3 islands from your starting area. Look for grey-brown mountainous terrain.
  3. Establish a beachhead. Dock at a safe beach (away from caves). Place a Bedroll. Build a Campfire for saving spawn and cooking.
  4. Scout and clear the Foothills cave entrance. Kill wolves / archers outside. Enter the cave. Clear the first chamber (usually 5-8 iron nodes). Do not go deep yet.
  5. Return to base with your first iron haul. Sail home. Smelt iron ingots. Craft an Iron Pickaxe.
  6. Second Foothills trip (now armed with Iron Pickaxe). Sail back. This time, go deep into the cave. Clear all iron nodes (40-80 iron ore total).
  7. Optional: Volcanic sidetrip. If your ship has cannons and you have iron armor, sail to a Volcanic island next. Mine 10-20 sulfur chunks from the surface geyser fields (safer than caves). This gives you enough sulfur for 5-10 gunpowder — your first firearms experiment.

Time Investment: One full Foothills Expedition (including the return trip home to craft the Iron Pickaxe) takes approximately 2-3 hours. Yield: 40-80 iron ore + optionally 10-20 sulfur. This is enough for a full set of iron tools and 1-2 pieces of iron armor.

Route Optimization Pro Tip: Always do your resource farming in "waves" — farm a full load, return home, process (smelt, craft), then go back out. Do not try to "live off the land" deep in a cave or on a distant island. If you die 20 minutes away from your base with a full inventory of iron ore, you will lose it all (your inventory drops on death based on current EA information — confirm in your version). Safe storage at your base is always better than risking a long-distance carry.

⚡ 8. Resource Gathering Efficiency Tips

The difference between a beginner who struggles for resources and an experienced player who never runs out is not luck — it is technique. The following tips will dramatically increase your resource gathering rate and reduce the number of dangerous trips you need to make.

Tip #1: Tool Durability Management

Your pickaxe losing durability mid-mine is a momentum killer. Always carry a secondary pickaxe in your inventory. When your main pickaxe drops below 20% durability, switch to the backup and repair the main at a Workbench (costs 2 wood + 1 stone per repair). Never let a tool break completely — a broken tool is 100% lost, and you lose all the resources invested in making it.

Tip #2: Inventory Tetris

Resources stack, but not all to the same size. Before a mining trip, empty your inventory completely. Fill only your hotbar with essential tools (pickaxe, weapon, shield, torch) and food (2 slots max). Keep your backpack slots (the 20-slot inventory) completely empty. This lets you mine continuously for 15-20 minutes before needing to return to base. Every trip back to base to empty inventory is 5-10 minutes of lost mining time.

Tip #3: Night Mining vs. Day Mining

Mining at night is 2x more dangerous (enemies spawn more frequently) but 1.5x faster because you can use a Lantern to permanently light cave nodes (no need to hold a torch in your off-hand). The torch occupies your off-hand, preventing shield use — at night, the Lantern (placed on the ground or worn as a helmet attachment) frees your off-hand for a shield. If you are mining in a safe area (cave already cleared of enemies), night mining is actually more efficient. If the area is not cleared, always mine during daylight.

Tip #4: The "Node Flag" System

When you find a high-yield resource node (especially iron or sulfur in dangerous biomes), place a marker on your in-game map. Windrose's map allows custom waypoints. Name the waypoint clearly: "Iron Cave - Foothills Island 2" or "Sulfur - Volcanic Island 2 GEYSER". After 20 hours of playing, you will have forgotten exactly which island had which resource. Your map markers are your most valuable navigation tool.

Tip #5: Multi-Resource Trips

Never sail to a distant island for one resource type. A trip to the Foothills should include: iron mining, wolf hunting (for hides), herb gathering (Foothills has unique alchemical plants), and scouting for your next quest objective. Every long-distance sail should yield 3-4 different resource types. Fuel is free (wind), but time is not.

Tip #6: Repair Before You Depart

This sounds obvious, but 30% of shipwrecks happen because players left base with a ship at 60% durability, took a few hits from rocks or enemies, and sank 15 minutes into the trip. Always repair your ship to 100% before any long-distance resource run. A Repair Hammer costs 3 wood and 2 stone — trivial compared to the cost of losing your entire inventory to the ocean floor.

❓ FAQ — Resource Map Questions

Q: Do resource nodes respawn after I mine them?

Yes. Copper and iron nodes respawn on a timer (approximately 20-30 minutes for copper, 30-45 minutes for iron). Clay nodes have a longer respawn timer (45-60 minutes). Sulfur nodes respawn the slowest (60+ minutes). All respawn timers are approximate and may vary based on server settings if you are playing on a community server.

Q: Can I mine iron with a stone pickaxe?

No. A stone pickaxe will deal zero damage to iron nodes — your pickaxe will bounce off without making any progress. You need at least a Copper Pickaxe to mine iron. This is a common frustration for new players who rush to the Foothills without upgrading their tools first.

Q: Where do I find copper if my starting island cave is empty?

Copper nodes respawn on a timer. If the cave is empty, sail to a neighboring tropical island and mine there. By the time you return to the first island, the nodes will have respawned. This "island hop" method is the fastest early-game copper farming technique.

Q: Is clay really necessary? Can I skip building a Smelter?

Clay is absolutely necessary. Without a Smelter (which requires 15 clay to build), you cannot smelt copper or iron ore into ingots. Without ingots, you cannot craft metal tools, weapons, or ship components. There is no way to progress past the stone-tool phase without clay.

Q: How much sulfur do I need to craft a Flitlock Pistol and enough ammo?

The Flitlock Pistol requires 5 sulfur (plus 10 iron ingots). Each shot consumes 1 gunpowder, which requires 2 sulfur + 1 charcoal. For a decent combat loadout (pistol + 20 shots), you need: 5 sulfur (pistol) + 40 sulfur (20 shots × 2 sulfur each) = 45 sulfur total. One volcanic cave clear (20-40 sulfur) is not quite enough — you will need two clears or a surface sulfur farm from geyser fields.

Q: What is the most efficient use of my first 20 copper ingots?

Priority order: 1) Copper Pickaxe (5 ingots) — speeds up all future mining. 2) Copper Sword (8 ingots) — unlocks Weaponsmithing Station quest completion. 3) Copper Axe (5 ingots) — speeds up wood gathering. Total: 18 ingots. The remaining 2 ingots can become a Copper Shovel. Do not waste your first ingots on decorative items or Copper Armor — the armor provides minimal protection and the ingots are better invested in tools that accelerate your resource gathering.

Q: Are there any resources that do not respawn?

Based on current EA information, all natural resource nodes (copper, iron, clay, sulfur, stone, wood) respawn on timers. However, chest loot in caves and ruins does not respawn — once you loot a chest, it remains empty. Plan your chest runs carefully and always bring a full inventory of empty slots when exploring ruins.

Q: Can I buy resources from NPCs instead of gathering them?

Currently, Windrose does not have a traditional NPC vendor system where you can buy copper ore or iron ore. You can occasionally find resources in chests in ruins, but the primary and intended method of acquiring all resources is gathering them yourself. This is by design — the game is a survival crafting experience, and resource gathering is the core gameplay loop.
