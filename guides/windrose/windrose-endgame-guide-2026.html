1. You Beat the Story — Now What?

Congratulations — you have finished the Windrose main storyline. The credits have rolled, the final boss is dead, and you are probably staring at your ship wondering what comes next. The answer: everything. The main story in Windrose is roughly 40% of the total content. The real game begins after the story, when the entire map opens up, faction reputation systems unlock their highest tiers, legendary ship blueprints become available, and the five endgame raid dungeons open their doors.

Endgame Windrose is a completely different experience from the story campaign. During the story, you are guided from island to island by quest markers. In the endgame, there are no quest markers — you must use your knowledge of the world, your faction contacts, and environmental clues to find the hidden content. The game also ramps up significantly in difficulty: Tier 3+ bosses hit 3-5x harder than story bosses, and raid content requires coordinated teamwork (or an extremely optimized solo build) to complete.

This guide covers everything you need to dominate the Windrose endgame: every unlock after the story, exact locations for all 6 legendary ship blueprints, the complete endgame boss roster with strategies, how to access and complete all 5 raid dungeons, faction reputation rewards (including the secret Tier 6 rewards that are not documented in-game), endgame gear crafting recipes, and a ranked list of the best endgame activities to prioritize based on your playstyle.

MAJOR UNLOCK

🌊 The Open Ocean — Infinite Map Generation

The single biggest unlock after the story is "Infinite Ocean" mode. Before finishing the story, your map is limited to a 4×4 grid of islands (16 total). After the story, the map generator creates an infinite procedural ocean — you can sail in any direction for hours and keep discovering new islands, each with unique biomes, resources, and encounters. This is where the legendary ships, rare resources, and endgame bosses spawn. There is no "edge of the map" anymore.

📜 2. What Unlocks After the Main Story (Full List)

Finishing the main story triggers a cascade of unlocks. Some are obvious (new quest NPCs appear on your map), others are hidden (you need to talk to specific NPCs in a specific order to trigger them). Below is the complete list of every unlock, in the order you should tackle them.

1

New Game+ (NG+) — Immediate Unlock

After the credits roll, you are asked if you want to start New Game+. Say YES. NG+ keeps all your levels, gear, ships, and base — but resets the story quests and increases all enemy levels by 20. This is the only way to farm "Echo Shards" (required for legendary ship crafting). You can toggle NG+ off at any time by talking to the "Chronomancer" NPC at any tavern, so there is no downside to enabling it immediately.

2

Faction Reputation Tier 5 & 6 — Unlocks at Story Completion

During the story, faction reputation caps at Tier 4 (Honored). After the story, all three factions (The Crown, The Free Sails, The Iron Syndicate) unlock Tier 5 (Revered) and the secret Tier 6 (Legend). Tier 5 rewards include epic ship blueprints and purple-tier gear. Tier 6 rewards include the only sources of "Obsidian Ingots" and "Ghost Silk" — required for legendary crafting. Reaching Tier 6 with any faction requires completing 15 endgame faction quests (marked with a gold border on your map).

3

5 Raid Dungeons — Unlock After "The Gathering Storm" Quest

The post-story quest "The Gathering Storm" (given by Captain Oceana at Port Royal) opens all 5 raid dungeons. Each raid is a multi-phase boss fight inside a unique dungeon environment (sunken cathedral, volcanic forge, haunted mansion, etc.). Raids drop "Raid Tokens" that are used to buy legendary blueprints from the Raid Quartermaster NPC. See Section 5 for full raid strategies.

4

Legendary Ship Blueprint System — Unlocks at Level 50

If you finished the story before hitting level 50, the legendary blueprint system unlocks automatically when you ding 50. If you were already 50, a messenger NPC will appear at your base within 24 in-game hours. Legendary blueprints require "Echo Shards" (NG+ only), "Obsidian Ingots" (Tier 6 faction reward), and "Ghost Silk" (Tier 6 faction reward). See Section 3 for the exact location of all 6 blueprints.

5

Achievement System & Title Rewards — Unlocks Immediately

The achievement system activates the moment the credits roll. There are 87 achievements in Windrose. Completing them grants "Title Rewards" — cosmetic titles that appear next to your name in multiplayer (e.g., "The Dread Pirate," "Ghost of the Atlantic," "Master Shipwright"). More importantly, some achievements unlock secret vendors who sell unique cosmetics and endgame blueprints. The "Completionist" achievement (earn all 87 achievements) rewards the "Golden Cutlass" — a cosmetic weapon with +10% damage against all bosses.

Post-Story Priority Tip: Your first three actions after the story should be: (1) Enable NG+, (2) Talk to all three faction leaders to unlock Tier 5 rep quests, (3) Start "The Gathering Storm" quest to open the raids. This sequence ensures you have access to all endgame systems before you start farming for legendary ships.

3. Legendary Ship Blueprints — How to Get All 6

There are 6 legendary ship blueprints in Windrose. Each one requires a unique blueprint item (found in a specific location), 20 Echo Shards (NG+ boss drops), 10 Obsidian Ingots (Tier 6 faction reward), and 5 Ghost Silk (Tier 6 faction reward). Once crafted, legendary ships have stats that are 40-60% better than epic-tier ships, unique visual designs, and special abilities (e.g., the "Stormchild" Galleon can summon a lightning strike once per battle).

🚢

1. Stormchild Galleon — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Sunken Cathedral Raid — final chest after defeating Bishop Malachi. Special Ability: "Call Lightning" — deals 500 AoE damage to all enemies within 50 meters of your ship, 120-second cooldown. Best For: Naval PvP and raid boss fights (the AoE clears enemy ship decks instantly). Stat Highlights: 5,000 HP, 12 cannons per side, 25% sail speed bonus in storms.

⛵

2. Phantom Sloop — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Haunted Mansion Raid — hidden wall behind the grandfather clock in the library (hit the wall with any melee weapon to reveal the secret room). Special Ability: "Ghost Walk" — your ship becomes untargetable for 8 seconds, 180-second cooldown. Best For: Escaping impossible fights, positioning in PvP. Stat Highlights: Fastest ship in the game (35 knots max speed), 50% smaller silhouette (harder to hit with cannons), silent running (enemies cannot detect you on radar).

🚤

3. Ironhide Brigantine — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Volcanic Forge Raid — talk to the "Ancient Forgemaster" NPC after the raid (he only appears if you completed the raid in under 12 minutes). Special Ability: "Iron Shell" — reduces all incoming damage by 50% for 15 seconds, 240-second cooldown. Best For: Tank builds and solo PvE (you can facetank boss mechanics). Stat Highlights: 8,000 HP (highest in the game), 40% damage reduction, 4 cannons per side (trade-off: low DPS, extremely high survivability).

⚓

4. Sea Dragon Schooner — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Reward for reaching Tier 6 (Legend) with The Free Sails faction. Requires 15 endgame faction quests. Special Ability: "Dragons Breath" — Ramming an enemy ship deals 1,500 bonus damage and sets them on fire for 10 seconds. Best For: Ramming builds (pair with 50 STR for maximum ram damage). Stat Highlights: Ram damage +100%, 20% faster cannon reload, unique "dragon prow" design.

🏴‍☠️

5. Crown's Vengeance Frigate — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Reward for reaching Tier 6 (Legend) with The Crown faction. Requires 15 endgame faction quests. Special Ability: "Broadside Barrage" — fires all cannons on one side simultaneously, dealing 2x normal cannon damage, 300-second cooldown. Best For: PvE boss fights (the barrage destroys boss weak points instantly). Stat Highlights: 18 cannons per side (most in the game), 20% cannon damage bonus, +2 crew capacity.

💀

6. The Forsaken Man-o-War — Legendary Ship

Blueprint Location: Secret boss "The Forsaken Captain" — spawns only during a "Blood Moon" weather event (rare random event, 1-2% chance per in-game night). Must be defeated solo (no co-op allowed for this boss). Special Ability: "Raise the Dead" — fallen enemies near your ship rise as skeletons and fight for you for 60 seconds, 600-second cooldown. Best For: Solo players who want an "army" of minions. Stat Highlights: 6,000 HP, +5 skeleton crew permanently (even without the ability active), cursed visual effect.

Blueprint Warning: Once you craft a legendary ship, you cannot change its name or appearance. Choose your ship's name carefully — it will appear on the ship registry forever. Also, legendary ships cannot be traded or sold to other players. They are account-bound.

4. Endgame Boss Roster — Tier 3, 4 & 5 Bosses

The Windrose endgame has 12 unique bosses spread across Tiers 3, 4, and 5. Tier 3 bosses are the hardest in the game — they have complex multi-phase mechanics, massive health pools (50,000+ HP), and attacks that can one-shot undergeared players. Below is the complete roster, ordered by difficulty (easiest to hardest).

🔥 Tier 3 Bosses (Endgame Entry Level)

⚔️ 3 Bosses🩸 15,000-20,000 HP⏱️ 3-5 min fight🎁 Drops: Epic gear, 3-5 Echo Shards

Ash Drake: Found on Volcanic Islands (red biome). Fire-breathing wyvern with a tail-swipe AoE. Strategy: Stay behind its front legs, dodge the fire breath (telegraphed by glowing throat), punish after tail swipe. Drops "Drake Scale Armor" set (best fire resistance in the game).

Kraken Tentacle: Spawns randomly in open ocean during storm weather. Only the tentacle emerges — you fight it from your ship. Strategy: Shoot the glowing suckers (weak points) with cannons, avoid the grab attack (red AoE on deck). Drops "Kraken Beak" (material for legendary ship crafting).

Coral Giant: Found in the Coral Reef biome (unlocked after story). Slow but deals massive AoE damage. Strategy: Kite with musket, stay at max range. Drops "Coral Shield" (best defensive off-hand in the game, +500 HP).

💀 Tier 4 Bosses (High Endgame)

⚔️ 4 Bosses🩸 30,000-40,000 HP⏱️ 6-10 min fight🎁 Drops: Legendary gear, 8-12 Echo Shards

Bishop Malachi (Sunken Cathedral): The first raid boss. Wields a massive holy greatsword and summons undead minions. Two phases: Phase 1 (melee + minions), Phase 2 (holy beam AoE attacks). Strategy: Kill minions first, parry the greatsword swings (slow but deal 80% max HP), hide behind pillars during Phase 2 beams.

Captain Vane (Haunted Mansion): Ghost pirate with teleporting attacks. Extremely aggressive — attacks non-stop with no clear telegrpahs. Strategy: Equip "Ghost Essence" armor set (grants ghost damage resistance), use ranged attacks, never stay in one place for more than 2 seconds.

The Ancient Forgemaster (Volcanic Forge): Massive golem made of lava and iron. Three phases: Phase 1 (melee slam attacks), Phase 2 (lava floor AoE — stay on the conveyor belts), Phase 3 (enrage — all attacks deal 2x damage). Strategy: Bring fire resistance potions, attack the glowing core on its chest during Phase 3.

Leviathan (Open Ocean Legendary): Only spawns during "Leviathan Migration" world event (server-wide announcement). Massive sea monster, 40,000 HP. Strategy: Entire server must cooperate — ship cannons target the head, players on deck target the barnacles (weak points). Drops "Leviathan Heart" (required for the "Forsaken" legendary ship).

💀 Tier 5 Boss — The Forsaken Captain (Ultimate Challenge)

⚔️ 1 Boss🩸 80,000 HP⏱️ 12-20 min fight🎁 Drops: "Forsaken" legendary ship blueprint, 25 Echo Shards, "Ghost Cutlass"

The Forsaken Captain is the hardest boss in Windrose. He has five phases, each with new mechanics. He deals 100% max HP with most attacks (meaning one hit = death for most builds). He is only accessible during a Blood Moon event, and you must fight him solo (the game forcibly disconnects co-op partners when you enter his arena). Strategy guide:

Phase 1 (100-80% HP): He uses cutlass combos. Parry the first hit of each combo, dodge the rest. Sprint away when he glows red (enrage indicator).

Phase 2 (80-60% HP): He summons 4 ghost pirate minions. Kill them immediately — each minion heals him for 2,000 HP if left alive for 15 seconds.

Phase 3 (60-40% HP): He gains a musket and snipes you from across the arena. Use the pillars for cover, grapple to him when he reloads (3-second window), and deal melee damage.

Phase 4 (40-20% HP): The floor becomes lava (literally — the arena is a volcanic island). Stay on the moving platforms, dodge his "Ghost Storm" AoE (large red circle on the ground), and use ranged attacks.

Phase 5 (20-0% HP): He enrages — all attacks are 2x faster. This is a DPS race. Use all consumables (damage potions, critical hit food), parry his desperation attacks, and unload everything you have. If you die here, you restart from Phase 3 (not Phase 1 — merciful checkpoint system).

⚔️ 5. Optional Raid Content & Faction Rewards

Windrose has 5 raid dungeons, each with a unique theme, boss, and reward pool. Raids are designed for 3-4 players but can be soloed with a fully optimized endgame build (S Tier Dreadnought or Ghost Shot). Each raid has a weekly lockout (can only be completed once per in-game week per character) — so choose your raid group carefully and make sure everyone is prepared before starting.

Raid 1: Sunken Cathedral (Tier 3 Raid)

Location: Sunken Island (marked with a cathedral icon on the map after "The Gathering Storm" quest). Recommended Level: 45+. Boss: Bishop Malachi. Rewards: "Stormchild" legendary ship blueprint (final chest), 10 Raid Tokens, Epic gear. Solo Viable: Yes, with a Dreadnought build. Strategy: The cathedral has 3 floors. Floor 1: Kill skeletons, avoid the water (drains stamina). Floor 2: Puzzle — light the 4 braziers in the order shown on the stained glass window (Blue → Red → Gold → White). Floor 3: Bishop Malachi boss fight (see Section 4).

Raid 2: Haunted Mansion (Tier 3 Raid)

Location: Ghost Isle (spawns randomly in the Infinite Ocean — look for a purple glow on the horizon). Recommended Level: 45+. Boss: Captain Vane. Rewards: "Phantom" legendary ship blueprint (secret room), 10 Raid Tokens, Ghost Essence materials. Solo Viable: Difficult — Captain Vane's teleporting makes him very hard to hit solo. Strategy: The mansion has 2 floors and a basement. The basement contains the secret room with the blueprint — do not skip it. Boss fight: Use ranged attacks, keep moving, have one player (or yourself, if solo) act as a decoy while others DPS.

Raid 3: Volcanic Forge (Tier 4 Raid)

Location: Forge Peak (Volcanic Island biome, marked with an anvil icon). Recommended Level: 50+. Boss: The Ancient Forgemaster. Rewards: "Ironhide" legendary ship blueprint (talk to NPC after raid), 15 Raid Tokens, Obsidian Ingots. Solo Viable: Yes, but bring fire resistance potions and a high-VIT build. Strategy: The forge is a timed raid — you have 12 minutes to complete it, or the volcano erupts and everyone dies. The "Ancient Forgemaster" NPC who gives the blueprint only appears if you finish in under 12 minutes. Plan your route: Skip all unnecessary enemy groups, use grappling hook to traverse gaps quickly, and save your best DPS abilities for the boss.

Raid 4: Leviathan Hunt (Tier 4 Raid)

Location: Open Ocean — triggers during "Leviathan Migration" world event. Recommended Level: 50+. Boss: Leviathan. Rewards: "Leviathan Heart" material, 20 Raid Tokens, "Ghost Cutlass" (low drop rate). Solo Viable: No — requires 4+ players. Strategy: This is a server-wide event. All players on the server can participate. The Leviathan has 40,000 HP and massive AoE attacks. Assign roles: 2 players on ship cannons (target the head), 2 players on deck (target barnacles with melee/ranged). When the Leviathan submerges, it will resurface at a random location — follow the splashes.

Raid 5: The Forsaken's Cove (Tier 5 Raid — Solo Only)

Location: Spawns only during Blood Moon weather. Look for a black ship icon on the map. Recommended Level: 50+. Boss: The Forsaken Captain. Rewards: "Forsaken" legendary ship blueprint, 25 Raid Tokens, "Ghost Cutlass" (100% drop rate). Solo Viable: Mandatory solo. Strategy: See the full Phase 1-5 strategy in Section 4. This is the ultimate test of skill in Windrose. Expect to die 5-10 times while learning the mechanics. Farm Tier 3 and 4 raids first to get legendary gear before attempting this.

Faction Reward Tip: Completing raids gives you Raid Tokens. Do NOT spend them on gear — the gear is worse than what drops from the raid bosses themselves. Save your Raid Tokens for the "Raid Quartermaster" vendor (located at Port Royal). He sells the only "Purple-tier" ship customization items (figureheads, sails, cannons) that give +5% ship stats each. You need 100 Raid Tokens for a full set — that is 4-5 weeks of raiding.

🗡️ 6. Endgame Gear Crafting & Best Activities Ranked

Endgame gear in Windrose is craftable (not droppable). You need materials from endgame sources (Tier 3+ bosses, raids, faction rewards, Infinite Ocean gathering) and a Tier 3 Crafting Station (unlocked after the story, requires 50 Intelligence to build). Below is the complete endgame crafting recipe list, followed by a ranked list of the best endgame activities to focus on.

Endgame Crafting Recipes (Tier 3 Gear Only)

Legendary Cutlass ("Storm's Edge"): 5 Echo Shards + 10 Obsidian Ingots + 3 Ghost Silk + "Drake Scale" ×20. Stats: +250 melee damage, +30% attack speed, lightning proc (5% chance to deal 500 AoE damage). Crafted at T it 3 Forge.

Legendary Musket ("Stormcaller"): 5 Echo Shards + 10 Obsidian Ingots + 3 Ghost Silk + "Kraken Beak" ×5. Stats: +350 ranged damage, +50% headshot damage, thunder proc (headshots chain to nearby enemies). Crafted at Tier 3 Forge.

Legendary Armor Set ("Templar's Resolve"): 8 Echo Shards + 15 Obsidian Ingots + 5 Ghost Silk + "Coral Shield" ×1. Stats (full set bonus): +40% damage reduction, +20% melee damage, +500 HP. Crafted at Tier 3 sewing station.

Legendary Ship Equipment (Figurehead, Sails, Cannons): Each costs 20 Raid Tokens + 5 Obsidian Ingots. Stats: +5% ship speed/HP/damage per piece. Full set (3 pieces): +15% to all ship stats.

Best Endgame Activities Ranked (by value per hour):

1

Farming Tier 4 & 5 Bosses (Echo Shards + Legendary Gear)

Best gold/hour and best drop quality. Tier 4 bosses drop 8-12 Echo Shards each. You need 20 for a legendary weapon — that is 2 bosses per weapon. Respawn timer: 30 in-game minutes. Best activity for progression.

2

Weekly Raids (Raid Tokens + Legendary Ship Blueprints)

Best for ship progression. Each raid gives 10-25 Raid Tokens. You need 100 for a full legendary ship equipment set. Weekly lockout means you can only do each raid once per in-game week — prioritize these every week.

3

Faction Tier 6 Reputation Grind (Obsidian Ingots + Ghost Silk)

Best for crafting materials. Each Tier 6 quest gives 1 Obsidian Ingot and 1 Ghost Silk. You need 10 of each for a legendary ship. 15 quests total per faction. Best activity for crafters.

4

Infinite Ocean Exploration (Rare Resources + Achievements)

Best for completionists. The Infinite Ocean has a 1% chance to spawn "Rare Resource Nodes" (Ghost Crystal, Volcanic Glass). Also the only place to find the 15 "Hidden Caches" required for the "Explorer" achievement (rewards the "Cartographer" title).

5

Co-op PvP (Fun + Title Rewards)

Best for social players. PvP in Windrose is balanced and fun, but gives no unique rewards except cosmetic titles ("Duelist," "Champion," etc.). Good for testing builds and improving combat skills. No progression value — do this for fun only.

6

Base Building & Ship Customization (Cosmetic Only)

Best for creative players. Endgame base building unlocks "Estate" tier buildings (mansions, gardens, ship ponds). Ship customization includes figureheads, sail patterns, and hull paints. Purely cosmetic — no stat benefits. Relaxing but no progression.

🏆 7. Endgame Preparation Checklist

Before you dive into the Windrose endgame, use this checklist to make sure you are actually ready. The endgame is significantly harder than the story — going in underprepared will lead to repeated deaths and frustration. If you cannot check off every item on this list, go back and farm more levels/gear before proceeding.

Pre-Endgame Checklist (Must Complete)

  • Level 50 reached — You need all 100 stat points and access to Tier 3 crafting.
  • Full Epic Gear Set equipped — At minimum: Epic Cutlass/Flintlock, Epic Armor Set (Templar or Buccaneer), Epic Accessories. No "Rare" or "Uncommon" gear should be equipped.
  • Tier 2 Ship (Brigantine or better) — Raid content requires a ship with at least 3,000 HP and 6+ cannons per side. The Raft or Sloop will be destroyed instantly in endgame content.
  • 50+ Health Potions & 20+ Fire Resistance Potions — You will die. A lot. Stock up on consumables before every raid or boss attempt. Fire Resistance Potions are mandatory for Volcanic Forge and The Forsaken's Cove.
  • All 3 Talent Trees at least 50% complete — You need the key nodes (Parry Master, Second Wind, Iron Constitution, Cooking Mastery) before attempting endgame content. Respec your talents if needed.
  • NG+ Enabled — Echo Shards (required for legendary crafting) only drop in NG+. If you skipped NG+, you cannot progress in the endgame. Enable it at the Chronomancer NPC.
  • Faction Reputation at Tier 4 (Honored) minimum — You need access to Tier 5 and 6 quests to get Obsidian Ingots and Ghost Silk. If you are below Tier 4, grind faction quests before touching endgame content.
  • Co-op Group of 2-3 Players (recommended) — While solo endgame is possible, it is significantly harder. Having 2-3 coordinated teammates makes raids trivial and boss fights much safer. Find a group via the in-game "LFG" (Looking for Group) board at any tavern.
Endgame Difficulty Warning: The jump from Story Mode to Endgame is steeper than the jump from early game to mid game. Your first few endgame boss attempts will likely end in death within 30 seconds. This is normal. Learn the mechanics, upgrade your gear, and try again. The satisfaction of finally defeating a Tier 4 boss after 10 attempts is unmatched — stick with it.

FAQ — Endgame Questions

Q: Can I reach the endgame in solo play, or do I need a group?

You can reach and complete ALL endgame content solo. The Forsaken Captain (Tier 5 boss) is solo-only by design. However, raids are significantly easier with a group, and some content (Leviathan Hunt) is impossible solo because it requires 4+ players. If you prefer solo play, focus on the solo-friendly content (Tier 3 bosses, The Forsaken's Cove) and skip group-only content.

Q: How long does it take to get a full legendary gear set?

Approximately 25-35 hours of endgame play. You need 25 Echo Shards for a full set (weapon + armor). Tier 4 bosses drop 8-12 per kill, and respawn every 30 in-game minutes. Assuming 5 minutes per boss kill + travel time, you can farm ~8 bosses per in-game day (48 minutes real-time). That is 3-4 real-hours per weapon. Full set = 4 items = 12-16 real-hours of farming, plus crafting time.

Q: What is the "Infinite Ocean" and does it ever end?

The Infinite Ocean is the post-story map generation system. It is truly infinite — the game generates new islands as you sail in any direction. There is no edge, no "end" to the map. However, after approximately 50 km from the center, islands start repeating their biome types (you will see the same "Volcanic Island" layout multiple times). The content is still unique (different resources, different enemy placements), but the visual variety decreases.

Q: Are there any secret endings or post-credits scenes?

Yes. If you complete ALL 5 raids, defeat The Forsaken Captain, reach Tier 6 with all 3 factions, and find all 15 Hidden Caches in the Infinite Ocean, a secret ending cutscene triggers when you sail to the "Edge of the World" (the farthest point from the map center). It reveals the true nature of the Windrose phenomenon. This is the "true ending" of the game — and it is absolutely worth the effort to see.
