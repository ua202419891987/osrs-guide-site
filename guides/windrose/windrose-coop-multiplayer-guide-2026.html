ü§ù 1. Why Co-op Changes Everything in Windrose

Windrose is fully playable solo, but co-op transforms the experience from a solitary survival slog into a dynamic, SOCIAL pirate adventure. When you sail with a crew, the game shifts from "how do I do everything myself" to "how do we work together to dominate the seas." The difference is not just convenience ‚Ä?it is a fundamentally different game. Solo players must manage sailing, navigation, combat, resource gathering, base building, and cooking all at once. A co-op crew divides these tasks, allowing each player to specialize and excel.

More importantly, co-op unlocks tactical depth that simply does not exist in solo play. In solo mode, you are a one-man army ‚Ä?you sail, you fight, you board enemy ships, and you manage your own inventory. In co-op, you can have one player captaining the ship, another manning the cannons, a third repairing hull damage mid-combat, and a fourth preparing to board the enemy vessel. This division of labor creates moments of genuine teamwork that feel like living inside a pirate movie. The boss fights also scale differently ‚Ä?boss health increases by approximately 40% per additional player, meaning a 4-player boss fight is not just "more people to carry you" but a genuinely more challenging and rewarding encounter.

Co-op Advantage: A well-coordinated 4-player crew can clear content 3x faster than a solo player. The key word is "well-coordinated" ‚Ä?a disorganized group will actually be slower because of communication overhead and conflicting priorities. Read this entire guide before inviting your friends.

ü§ù 2. How to Host & Join a Co-op Server

Windrose supports up to 4 players in a co-op session. There are two ways to play together: host a game yourself (your computer runs the server) or join a friend's hosted game. The host's world is the "main" world ‚Ä?if the host disconnects, the session ends and other players are returned to their own single-player worlds. There is currently no dedicated server support, meaning one player must always host.

1

Hosting a Co-op Server (Step-by-Step)

From the main menu, click "Play" ‚Ü?"Host Game." Choose your world seed (or generate a random one). Set your world name ‚Ä?this is what friends will see when they browse for games. Set "Max Players" (1-4). Set a server password if you want to keep strangers out. Click "Start Hosting." Your game will launch and you will be in control of the world. Share your IP address and port number (displayed in the pause menu) with your friends. Alternatively, use Steam's "Invite Friends" feature ‚Ä?right-click Windrose in your Steam library, select "Invite to Game," and your friends can join directly without entering IP addresses.

2

Joining a Friend's Server

From the main menu, click "Play" ‚Ü?"Join Game." You will see a list of "LAN Games" (friends on the same local network) and "Internet Games" (friends hosting online). If your friend's server does not appear in the list, click "Direct Connect" and enter their IP address and port number. If the server is password-protected, you will be prompted to enter the password. Once connected, you will spawn at the host's starting island (or at your own bed/campfire if you have played on this server before).

3

Port Forwarding (If Friends Cannot Connect)

If your friends cannot join your game despite you sharing the correct IP, you likely need to port forward. Log into your router's admin panel (usually 192.168.1.1 in your browser). Navigate to "Port Forwarding" or "Virtual Server." Create a new rule: Protocol = UDP, Port = 7777 (Windrose default), Destination = your computer's local IP (e.g., 192.168.1.50). Save and restart your router. This opens the port that Windrose uses for multiplayer connections. If you cannot port forward (e.g., you are on a university network), use a VPN like Radmin VPN or ZeroTier to create a virtual LAN ‚Ä?your friends can then join as if they were on the same local network.

Server Hosting Warning: The host's computer bears the full processing load of running the server AND playing the game. If the host has a weak CPU or limited RAM (less than 8GB), the game will lag for all players. The host should close all unnecessary background applications (Chrome, Discord in browser mode, etc.) before starting a co-op session. Ideally, the player with the best computer should host.

‚ú?Hosting Yourself (Recommended)

  • You control the world seed and settings
  • No monthly server costs
  • You keep your progress even if friends leave
  • Best for groups of 2-3 friends
  • Requires port forwarding for internet play

‚öÝÔ∏è Joining a Friend's Server

  • You cannot play when the host is offline
  • Your progress is tied to their world
  • If the host's computer crashes, everyone disconnects
  • Best for casual short sessions
  • No configuration needed ‚Ä?just join and play

‚õ?3. Role Division ‚Ä?Captain, Quartermaster, Engineer, Scout

The single biggest mistake co-op groups make is "everyone does everything." Without role division, you will have four players all trying to sail the ship at once, or four players all running in different directions to gather resources. Effective co-op requires specialization. Here are the four core roles that cover all aspects of Windrose gameplay. Not every group needs all four roles ‚Ä?a 2-player group should combine roles (e.g., one player is Captain/Quartermaster, the other is Engineer/Scout).

üéñÔ∏?/div>

Captain (The Leader)

Primary Responsibilities: Sails the ship, navigates to destinations, makes strategic decisions (where to go, what to fight, when to retreat), and coordinates the crew during combat. The Captain should always be at the helm during naval engagements.

Recommended Skills: High sailing skill (reduces fuel consumption by up to 30%), navigation skill (reveals map details at longer range), and leadership skill (passive bonus to all crew members' stamina regeneration).

Best Weapons: Ranged weapons (muskets, pistols) ‚Ä?the Captain should engage enemies from the ship's deck, not board enemy vessels personally unless necessary.

Who Should Play This Role: The most experienced player, or the player with the best grasp of Windrose's game systems. The Captain sets the pace and direction of the entire session.

üì¶

Quartermaster (The Manager)

Primary Responsibilities: Manages the group's inventory and storage, distributes loot fairly, handles base building and crafting, cooks food for the crew, and maintains the ship's supply of ammunition, repair materials, and consumables. The Quartermaster is the logistical backbone of the crew.

Recommended Skills: Inventory management skill (increases carry capacity by 20 slots), cooking skill (cooked food restores 25% more hunger), and crafting skill (reduces material costs by 10%).

Best Weapons: Balanced melee weapons (cutlasses, sabers) ‚Ä?the Quartermaster needs to be able to defend themselves while managing supplies, but should not be on the front lines.

Who Should Play This Role: The most organized player, or the player who enjoys base building and optimization. If you love spreadsheets and inventory management in games, this is your role.

üîß

Engineer (The Fixer)

Primary Responsibilities: Repairs the ship during and after combat, upgrades ship components (cannons, sails, hull), mines resources (especially metal ores), and handles all technical crafting (forging ingots, crafting ammunition). The Engineer ensures the ship stays battle-ready.

Recommended Skills: Repair skill (repairs restore 50% more durability), mining skill (increases ore yield by 25%), and crafting speed skill (crafts items 30% faster).

Best Weapons: Heavy weapons (war hammers, maces) ‚Ä?the Engineer is often in the thick of melee combat while protecting the ship or boarding parties.

Who Should Play This Role: The player who enjoys the technical side of survival games ‚Ä?upgrading gear, optimizing damage output, and keeping machines running. Often the second-most experienced player after the Captain.

üî≠

Scout (The Explorer)

Primary Responsibilities: Scouts ahead on foot (using a separate small boat or by swimming to islands), identifies threats and resources before the main ship arrives, hunts wildlife for food, gathers fiber and wood, and provides sniper cover during ship boarding actions. The Scout is the crew's eyes and ears.

Recommended Skills: Stealth skill (reduces enemy detection range by 40%), movement speed skill (increases running speed by 15%), and ranged damage skill (increases bow/musket damage by 20%).

Best Weapons: Ranged weapons (bows for silent takedowns, muskets for long-range support). The Scout should almost never engage in melee combat ‚Ä?their job is to provide intelligence and cover fire.

Who Should Play This Role: The player who enjoys exploration and reconnaissance. The Scout often plays a more solitary role, which suits players who prefer less direct combat involvement.

Role Flexibility: These roles are not rigid. During a boss fight, the Quartermaster might pick up a cannon, the Engineer might board the enemy ship, and the Scout might heal injured teammates. The roles exist to ensure that ALL necessary tasks are covered ‚Ä?not to restrict what players can do. Good crews adapt their role assignments based on the situation.

ü§ù 4. Voice Chat Setup ‚Ä?The Key to Victory

You can play Windrose co-op using only text chat. But you will be at a massive disadvantage. Windrose's combat is fast-paced and positional ‚Ä?"enemy behind the rock" is useless information if it takes you 10 seconds to type it. Voice chat is not optional for serious co-op play. Here is how to set up voice chat for Windrose, ranked from easiest to most feature-rich.

Option 1: Steam Voice Chat (Easiest, Built-In)

Steam has built-in voice chat that works automatically when you join a friend's game. Press the "Voice Chat" button (default: TAB) to transmit. The audio quality is acceptable but not great (16kHz sample rate, noticeable compression). Latency is typically 100-200ms, which is fine for casual play but noticeable in intense combat. Best for: Casual groups of 2-3 friends who want zero setup.

Setup: Open Steam Settings ‚Ü?Voice ‚Ü?Test your microphone. Set "Voice Input Device" to your headset microphone. Set "Voice Transmission Type" to "Push to Talk" (recommended ‚Ä?avoids transmitting background noise). Choose a keybind that does not conflict with Windrose controls (F5 is a good choice).

Option 2: Discord (Best Overall)

Discord offers superior audio quality (48kHz, low latency ~50ms with proper settings), noise suppression, and the ability to create separate voice channels for different activities. You can also screen-share if you need to show something to a friend. Best for: All co-op groups. This is what 90% of Windrose co-op players use.

Setup: Create a Discord server (or use an existing one). Create a "Windrose Co-op" voice channel. Set your input device to your headset in Discord User Settings ‚Ü?Voice & Video. Enable "Noise Suppression" (Krisp or Standard). Set "Input Sensitivity" manually (disable "Automatically determine sensitivity") ‚Ä?aim for -40dB to -30dB. Use "Push to Talk" with a keybind that does not conflict with Windrose (Numpad 0 works well).

Option 3: TeamSpeak / Mumble (Pro Level)

TeamSpeak and Mumble offer the absolute lowest latency (often under 20ms) and best audio quality. They require more setup (you need a server ‚Ä?either self-hosted or paid). Best for: Competitive PvP groups or players with poor internet connections where every millisecond of latency matters.

Voice Chat Etiquette: Windrose co-op requires communication, but not constant chatter. Establish these rules with your crew: (1) Use push-to-talk, never open mic (background noise from keyboards, fans, and other games is distracting). (2) During combat, only the Captain should be giving orders ‚Ä?other players confirm orders with short responses ("Aye," "On it," "Repairing now"). (3) Call out enemy positions with clear, concise descriptions: "Skeleton pirate, 50 meters, north-northeast" ‚Ä?not "uh there's something over there." (4) No music bot spam in the voice channel. Ever.

‚öîÔ∏è 5. PvE Co-op vs PvP Co-op ‚Ä?Which Mode to Choose

Windrose offers two distinct co-op modes: PvE (Player vs Environment, where you cooperate to fight AI enemies) and PvP (Player vs Player, where you can attack other player crews). Both modes support co-op play, but they offer vastly different experiences. Choosing the wrong mode for your group will ruin the experience.

‚öîÔ∏è PvE Co-op (Recommended for Most Groups)

  • All players work together to fight AI enemies
  • Boss health scales with player count (40% per player)
  • No friendly fire (you cannot accidentally hit teammates)
  • Shared loot from bosses and chests
  • Co-op exclusive events (see Section 8)
  • Best for: Friends who want to cooperate, casual players, first-time co-op groups

üíÄ PvP Co-op (High Risk, High Reward)

  • You can attack other player crews (if playing on a PvP-enabled server)
  • Friendly fire is enabled (accidental team kills are possible)
  • Loot can be stolen from defeated players
  • Requires high trust and coordination
  • Best for: Experienced players, groups that enjoy competition, streaming content
  • Warning: PvP can create real-life friction between friends

If you are playing with close friends and everyone is on the same skill level, PvE co-op is the clear choice. It encourages cooperation, reduces frustration, and lets you experience Windrose's story and exploration at your own pace. PvP co-op should only be enabled if EVERYONE in your group explicitly wants it ‚Ä?one reluctant player will spoil the experience for everyone.

How to Enable/Disable PvP: When hosting a server, go to "World Settings" ‚Ü?"PvP Mode." Set to "Disabled" for pure PvE, "Enabled (Consensual)" for PvP that requires both parties to agree to fight, or "Enabled (Open)" for full PvP where anyone can attack anyone at any time. For most friend groups, "Enabled (Consensual)" is the best compromise ‚Ä?it allows PvP duels for fun without forcing unwanted combat.

üèÜ 6. Shared Base Building & Resource Management

In solo Windrose, your base is your personal sanctuary. In co-op, your base is a SHARED resource that belongs to the entire crew. This requires careful planning to avoid conflicts. Here are the golden rules of co-op base building:

1

Choose a Central Base Location (Don't Build Separate Bases)

The biggest mistake co-op groups make is each player building their own base. This fragments your resources, duplicates your crafting stations (wasting materials), and makes coordination difficult. Instead, ALL players should contribute to a single, large shared base. Choose a location that is central to the resources your group needs most (usually a Tropical Island with nearby Rocky Outcrops for stone/ore). The base should have a large beach for docking multiple ships.

2

Assign Personal Storage Areas Within the Shared Base

Just because the base is shared does not mean all storage should be communal. Assign each player a "personal chest" or a small section of the base with their own storage. This prevents the "who took my iron ingots" arguments. Use colored banners or signs to mark personal storage areas. Communal resources (wood, stone, fiber) should go in clearly marked "COMMUNITY CHEST" containers near the workbench and forge.

3

Build Multiple Crafting Stations to Reduce Bottlenecks

In solo play, one workbench and one forge are enough. In co-op, multiple players will often need to craft simultaneously. Build 2-3 workbenches, 2 forges, and 2 cooking stations. Place them in separate areas of the base to avoid pathing conflicts. The Quartermaster should be the only player allowed to use the "advanced" crafting stations (Alchemy Table, Ship Design Table) ‚Ä?these consume rare materials and should not be used without group consensus.

4

Defensive Walls Should Enclose the Entire Base (Not Just Individual Buildings)

When building base defenses, build a single perimeter wall that encloses ALL buildings and crafting stations. Do not build separate walls around each building ‚Ä?this looks cool but is tactically useless. A single perimeter wall with 2-3 chokepoint gates is far more effective. Assign one player (usually the Engineer) to be responsible for wall repairs after raids.

Resource Management Tip: Establish a "community tax" system. Each player should contribute 10% of all gathered resources to the community chests. This ensures that when someone needs to craft a ship upgrade or repair the base walls, the materials are already available. Groups that do not do this invariably run into "I don't have enough iron to upgrade my sword" arguments.

‚öîÔ∏è 7. Ship Combat Coordination & Loot Distribution

Ship combat is where co-op Windrose shines. A well-coordinated crew can take on ships and bosses that would be impossible solo. Here is the exact combat formation and loot distribution system that experienced crews use.

‚ë?Ship Combat Roles (During Naval Engagements)

When engaging an enemy ship or boss, your crew should adopt these positions:

  • Captain: At the helm. Controls ship movement, positions the ship for broadsides, and decides when to board or disengage.
  • Port Cannon Gunner: Manages the left-side cannons. Focuses on weakening the enemy ship's hull.
  • Starboard Cannon Gunner: Manages the right-side cannons. Focuses on targeting the enemy ship's cannons (to disable their return fire).
  • Engineer/Repair: Stands near the ship's hull health indicator. Monitors durability and repairs damage immediately. Also prepares grappling hooks for boarding actions.

‚ë?Loot Distribution Rules (Prevent Arguments Before They Start)

Nothing destroys a friend group faster than loot drama. Establish these rules BEFORE your first boss fight:

Rule 1: "Need Before Greed" for Gear Drops

If a boss drops a unique weapon or armor piece, the player who can make the best use of it gets priority. "Need" means "this is a clear upgrade for my current gear." "Greed" means "I want this for crafting materials or to sell." If two players both "need" an item, roll a virtual dice (use Discord's dice bot: /roll 100). Highest roll wins.

Rule 2: Resources Go to the Community Chests

All non-unique resources (wood, stone, ore, fiber, leather) go into the community chests. Personal harvesting for personal projects is allowed, but boss loot and chest loot are community property. This prevents one player from hoarding all the iron ore while everyone else struggles to upgrade their gear.

Rule 3: The Quartermaster Has Final Say on Loot

If there is a dispute about loot distribution, the Quartermaster makes the final decision. This is why you appoint a Quartermaster ‚Ä?to have a neutral party who can make fair calls. The Quartermaster should prioritize gear upgrades that benefit the group as a whole (e.g., giving the best sword to the player who does the most melee damage, not to their best friend).

Boarding Action Protocol: When boarding an enemy ship, the Captain stays on your ship (someone must maintain control of the vessel). The other 3 players board. The Engineer and Scout engage enemy crew in melee/ranged combat. The Quartermaster loots the enemy ship's storage (chests, cargo holds) as quickly as possible. Once the enemy crew is dead, ALL players help loot. Then everyone returns to your ship immediately ‚Ä?do not linger on a captured ship, as it may explode or sink unexpectedly.

ü§ù 8. Best Group Size, Cross-Play & Exclusive Events

‚ë?Best Friend Group Size for Windrose Co-op

Windrose supports up to 4 players, but that does not mean 4 is always the best group size. Here is the optimal group size for different playstyles:

  • 2 Players (Duo): Best for close friends or couples. Each player can handle 2 roles (e.g., Captain/Scout + Quartermaster/Engineer). Less coordination overhead. Downside: boss fights are significantly harder because boss health scales by 40% per player but you only have 2 players' worth of damage output.
  • 3 Players (Trio): The "sweet spot" for most groups. Enough players to cover all 4 roles (one player handles 2 roles), but not so many that coordination becomes chaotic. Boss fights are challenging but fair.
  • 4 Players (Full Crew): The "pirate ship" fantasy. Each player has a dedicated role. Boss fights are epic but require serious coordination ‚Ä?a 4-player crew that does not communicate will lose to a boss that a solo player could defeat. Best for experienced groups who use voice chat and have played together before.

‚ë?Cross-Play Limitations (2026 Update)

As of 2026, Windrose does NOT support cross-play between Steam and other platforms. All players must be on Steam to play together. There is no console version (PlayStation, Xbox, Switch) and no mobile version. If you and your friends are all on PC but some bought the game on Epic Games Store, you cannot play together ‚Ä?Epic does not support multiplayer for Windrose. Everyone must own the game on Steam. The developers have stated that cross-play is "on the roadmap" but no release date has been announced.

‚ë?Co-op Exclusive Events

Windrose features several events and content pieces that are exclusive to co-op play (or are significantly easier with a group). These are some of the best reasons to play co-op:

  • The Merchant Fleet Escort (3+ Players Recommended): A timed event where you must protect a NPC merchant fleet from waves of pirate attackers. The fleet moves slowly and cannot defend itself. You must assign players to front-line defense, rear-guard, and flank protection. Completing this event rewards each player with a "Merchant's Charter" (unlocks rare trading recipes).
  • The Ghost Fleet Invasion (4 Players Required): A world event where ghost ships spawn in waves and attack all players' bases simultaneously. You must split your crew ‚Ä?some defend the base, others sail out to destroy the ghost ship spawners. This event cannot be completed solo (the spawners are in different locations and must be destroyed within 5 minutes of each other).
  • Co-op Boss: The Kraken (2+ Players): The Kraken is a world boss that spawns in deep water. It grapples ships with tentacles and pulls them underwater. In solo play, the Kraken is notoriously difficult because you must sail, fight tentacles, and repair your ship simultaneously. In co-op, one player sails, one fights tentacles, and one repairs ‚Ä?making the fight manageable and even fun.
  • Shared Map Exploration (All Group Sizes): In co-op, each player's explored map area is shared with the entire crew in real-time. This means you can split up to explore different islands simultaneously, then share map data. A 4-player crew can explore the entire archipelago 4x faster than a solo player.
Final Co-op Tip: The most successful Windrose co-op groups play together regularly (at least once a week). This builds familiarity with each other's playstyles and reduces the need for constant communication ‚Ä?after a few sessions, you will intuitively know that when the Captain says "hard to port," you should already be loading the port-side cannons. Treat your co-op crew like a real pirate crew: loyalty, clear communication, and shared loot make for a happy (and successful) ship.

‚ù?FAQ ‚Ä?Co-op Multiplayer Questions

Q: Can I play my single-player save in co-op, or do I need to start a new world?

You can play your single-player save in co-op. When you host a game, your existing single-player world is used. Your friends will join you in your world. However, their progress (skills, inventory) is saved to their character, not your world ‚Ä?they keep their progress when they return to their own single-player game.

Q: What happens if the host disconnects or their game crashes?

All other players are returned to the main menu. When the host restarts their game and re-hosts, other players can rejoin. The world state is saved automatically every 5 minutes and when any player sleeps in a bed. You will not lose progress, but you will lose any unsaved loot (pick up loot and deposit it in chests frequently).

Q: Can I invite someone to my co-op game temporarily, then remove them?

Yes. In the pause menu, go to "Player List." You can "Kick" any player (remove them from the session) or change their permissions (e.g., prevent them from building/deconstructing base structures). If you set a server password, only players who know the password can join.

Q: Does Windrose support mods in co-op?

Yes, but all players must have the same mods installed. The host should install mods via the Windrose mod manager (Steam Workshop). When other players join, the game will automatically check if they have the required mods and prompt them to download any missing ones. Not all mods are compatible with co-op ‚Ä?check the mod description for "Multiplayer Compatible" before installing.
