2. How to Find & Join Clans

There are three main ways to find an active OSRS clan in 2026. Each has different pros and cons:

① In-Game Clan Hub (Best for New Players)

The official Clan Hall in Varrock is the best place to start. Right-click the notice board to browse recruiting clans. You can see member count, description, and requirements before applying. Most mid-sized clans recruit here.

② OSRS Official Forums (Best for Established Clans)

The Clan Recruitment section of the official OSRS forums has the most detailed clan listings. Clans post their schedules, requirements, and culture descriptions. The downside: forums are less active than they were in 2020, but the quality of listings is higher.

③ Discord Server Lists (Best for Instant Access)

Websites like DISBOARD and Discadia have searchable OSRS clan directories. Search by tag (PvM, Ironman, social, skilling) and see member counts, online activity, and invite links. Most modern clans do their primary communication on Discord.

④ Reddit r/2007scape (Best for Community-Vetted Clans)

Check the weekly Clan Recruitment threads on r/2007scape. These are community-vetted — clans that repeatedly post with positive feedback from members are usually high quality. Avoid clans that only post once with no member engagement.

💡 The 3-Day Test

Join any clan as a guest for 3 days before committing. A good clan will: (1) greet you within 5 minutes of joining, (2) have active chat during your play hours, (3) offer help without asking for anything. If a clan feels dead after 3 days, move on. There are hundreds of active clans in 2026.

9. Official OSRS Clan Hub & Recruitment

Jagex has improved the in-game clan system significantly in 2025-2026. Here's how to use the official systems:

① In-Game Clan Hall (Varrock)

Located just east of the Grand Exchange. The Clan Hall contains a Clan Notice Board that lists recruiting clans, their activity focus, member count, and a brief description. You can apply directly from the board.

② Clan Recruitment on OSRS Forums

Visit the official OSRS website forums > Clan Discussion > Clan Recruitment. Clans post detailed threads with: activity schedule, requirements, culture description, and contact information. This is the most reliable source for established clans.

③ The "Clan" Tab (In-Game)

Open your clan tab (shield icon in the game UI). You can search for clans by name, view your current clan info, and see pending invitations. Jagex added the ability to browse recommended clans based on your activity in 2025.

✅ How to Spot a Good Clan

Quality indicators: (1) Active Discord with regular conversations, (2) Weekly events posted on a schedule, (3) A mentorship program for new members, (4) Clear rules and code of conduct, (5) No "pay to join" requirements. Red flags: (1) The clan constantly asks for donations, (2) The owner is the only active member, (3) Requirements include "must bring X gear" for social membership.

10. Clan & Discord FAQs

What's the best clan for a new player?

Mercy OSRS or ChillScapers have zero requirements and very helpful communities. For more structured learning, We Do Raids (WDR) is excellent if you're close to raiding-level combat stats. Social clans are better for brand-new accounts that aren't ready for group content yet.

Can I be in multiple OSRS clans at once?

Yes, but with limits. You can be a full member of one clan and a guest in unlimited others (up to the guest clan cap of ~100). Most players join 1 main clan and guest in 2-3 others for different activities (e.g., main clan for social, guest in PvM clan for raids).

What's the difference between a clan and a Discord server?

A clan is an in-game group that appears in your clan tab with shared chat, XP tracking, and activity feed. A Discord server is an external voice/text platform. Most modern clans use BOTH — the Discord for organizing events and voice chat, the in-game clan for XP tracking and afk chat. Some Discords (like OSRS Wiki) are not tied to any specific in-game clan.

How do I find clans that match my time zone?

Check clan recruitment posts for their "active hours" or time zone. Australian/East Asian clans are common and often recruit specifically for their time zone. On the OSRS forums, use the search filters to find clans by region. Discord server listings also show member country/region distribution.

Are there clans for specific activities like Sailing?

Dedicated Sailing clans emerged after the skill's November 2025 release. Search "Sailing CC" or "Sailing OSRS clan" on the forums. Most general PvM clans also have Sailing channels now — the Sailing section of the OSRS Skilling Discord is more active than any standalone Sailing clan currently.

Do I need to pay to join good clans?

No. Legitimate OSRS clans never charge membership fees. The only thing you might be asked to contribute is supply splits for group bossing (which is standard — everyone pays their share of potions/food). If a clan asks for real money or bonds to join, it's a scam.

What should I look for in a clan's Discord?

A well-organized Discord has: (1) Rules channel with clear expectations, (2) Introduction channel for new members, (3) Activity or lfg (looking for group) channels, (4) Bot integrations for XP tracking or loot splitting, (5) Active voice channels during peak hours. Dead Discords with no messages in 24+ hours indicate a dying clan.

Can I start my own clan?

Yes. You need at least 5 members to officially register a clan. Visit the Clan Hall in Varrock and speak to the Clan Registrar. You'll need to choose a clan name, tag (2-4 letters), and set your recruitment preferences. Building an active community takes consistent effort — expect 3-6 months to reach 50+ active members.

Are there OSRS clans focused on the new Vault of Ralos raid?

Yes. We Do Raids (WDR) has dedicated Vault of Ralos channels with gear check requirements and learner-friendly teams. Several smaller clans formed specifically around the new raid in early 2026. Search "Vault of Ralos clan" on the OSRS forums or check the WDR Discord for the most current listings.

How do I avoid clan drama?

Choose medium-sized clans (50-200 active members) — large enough to have active chat, small enough to avoid faction politics. Read the clan's rules carefully before joining. Avoid clans that: regularly have public arguments in clan chat, have inactive/absent leadership, or have "donor ranks" that give paying members special privileges.
