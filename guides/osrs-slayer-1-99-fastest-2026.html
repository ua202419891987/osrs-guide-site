1. Introduction to Fast Slayer Training

Slayer is unique among OSRS skills. Unlike Woodcutting or Fishing where you can grind the same action for 500 hours, Slayer forces you to kill specific monsters assigned by a Slayer Master. This makes it naturally slower than other combat skills — but with the right strategy, you can still reach 99 efficiently.

Before we dive into the level-by-level breakdown, understand the three pillars of fast Slayer:

Three Rules for Maximum Slayer XP

  • Rule 1 — Use the Highest Slayer Master Available: Higher masters assign better tasks with more monsters, more XP, and better drop tables. Upgrade immediately when you meet the requirements.
  • Rule 2 — Skip Bad Tasks Relentlessly: If a task gives under 10K XP/h, skip it. Use Slayer points from Turael boosting to fund skips. Every hour on a bad task is an hour you could be getting 50K+ XP.
  • Rule 3 — Burst and Cannon Whenever Possible: Multi-target attacks are the key to fast Slayer. Ancient Magicks (Ice Burst/Barrage) in multi-combat areas and the Dwarf Cannon double or triple your kills per hour.

Total time to 99 Slayer with optimal methods: approximately 250-350 hours. With suboptimal methods, this balloons to 500+ hours. This guide cuts that down as much as possible.

💡 Start Smart: Prerequisites

Before training Slayer seriously, complete these quests: Smoking Kills (unlocks Slayer helmet and Slayer points), Lost City (Dragon dagger, task requirement), Priest in Peril, and The Grand Tree. If you plan to burst, complete Desert Treasure for Ancient Magicks. See our Slayer Beginner Guide for prerequisites.

2. Level 1-20: Getting Started with Turael

Your first Slayer Master is Turael in Burthorpe. He assigns easy monsters like rats, chickens, cows, and spiders. These tasks give minimal XP, but they teach you the Slayer system and require no combat stats.

Strategy for 1-20

Start at Turael and do tasks until level 15 Slayer. This takes roughly 20-30 tasks (about 2-3 hours). Accept every task and use the fastest killing method available. For rats, spiders, and bats, use rapid-style weapons (scimitars or knives).

💡 Pro Tip

At level 5 Slayer, you unlock access to the Slayer Master in Canifis (Mazchna). However, stay with Turael until level 15 for faster short tasks. The XP difference at these early levels is negligible.

Combat requirement: None, but 20+ Attack/Strength makes this faster. Train your combat stats first if you want smoother tasks.

3. Level 20-40: Vannaka and the Cannon Era

At level 20 Slayer, you unlock Vannaka the Edgeville Dungeon Slayer Master. He assigns better monsters including Hill Giants, Moss Giants, Flesh Crawlers, and Cockatrices. This is where Slayer starts generating meaningful XP.

The Cannon at Low Levels

If you have the budget, buy a Dwarf Cannon (750K GP) and 2,000+ cannonballs. Set up the cannon in multi-combat areas where multiple monsters can be hit simultaneously. For Hobgoblins on the Peninsula and Flesh Crawlers in the Stronghold of Security, the cannon nearly doubles your kills per hour.

Cannon Placement Tips

  • Always place the cannon where it can hit 3+ monsters at once
  • Use Auto-Retaliate off and manually move to stack monsters
  • Pick up cannonballs when a task is almost finished
  • Budget 100-200 cannonballs per 10-minute segment

Priority: Unlock the Slayer Helmet

At level 18 Slayer, complete the Smoking Kills quest if you haven't already. This unlocks Slayer points and allows you to purchase a Slayer helmet. The helmet provides a 15% damage and accuracy boost on Slayer tasks, making it the single most important gear upgrade. See our Slayer Points Guide for the fastest way to earn points.

Expected time from 20-40: 10-15 hours with cannon, 18-25 hours without.

4. Level 40-60: Chaeldar and Mid-Game Tasks

At level 40 Slayer, switch to Chaeldar in Zanaris (requires Lost City quest). She assigns significantly better tasks like Cockatrice, Rockslugs, Pyrefiends, and — most importantly — Dust Devils (level 65).

Key Milestone: Dust Devils at 65

The single biggest XP jump in the mid-game is unlocking Dust Devils at level 65 Slayer. These drop the Dust Battlestaff (worth 450K GP) and are stackable in multi-combat, making them perfect for bursting with Ancient Magicks.

Recommended Chaeldar Tasks

Focus on tasks that are in multi-combat zones or have high HP monsters. Use the Bracelet of Slaughter on good tasks (Dust Devils, Bloodvelds) to extend them, and Expeditious Bracelet on tedious tasks (Basilisks, Pyrefiends) to finish faster.

💡 Budget Bursting Prep

If you plan to burst Dust Devils, you need Desert Treasure completed for Ancient Magicks. You also need 50+ Magic and at least 100 casts of Ice Burst per trip. Each burst costs ~700 GP in runes but kills 8-9 stacked monsters, making the cost per kill very efficient for the XP.

Expected time from 40-60: 20-30 hours with good tasks and skipping.

5. Level 60-75: Nieve/Steve — The Bursting Gateway

At level 60 Slayer (or 85 Combat), unlock Nieve/Steve in the Tree Gnome Stronghold. This master is widely considered the best for XP until level 85. She assigns high-value tasks like Dust Devils, Bloodvelds, Nechryaels, and Abyssal Demons.

Bursting in the Catacombs of Kourend

The Catacombs of Kourend are the best location for burst tasks. Every monster is multi-combat, there are no PKers, and the shard drops add extra profit. The key is stacking: aggro 8-9 monsters, run them into a pile, then Ice Burst or Ice Barrage the stack.

Bursting Rotation for Dust Devils

Step 1: Equip full Magic Damage gear (Ahrim's, Occult, Tormented Bracelet). Bring Ancient Staff and runes for Ice Burst/Barrage.

Step 2: Enter the Dust Devil room in Catacombs. Use Protect from Melee, run to the center, and tag all monsters with auto-cast.

Step 3: Once 8-9 are aggro'd, position them in a tight stack and cast Ice Burst repeatedly.

Step 4: Loot with Area Loot. Restock prayer with altar outside the room. Repeat.

Expected time from 60-75: 25-35 hours with bursting and task skipping.

💡 Bracelet Strategy

Use Bracelet of Slaughter on Dust Devils and Nechryaels to extend these high-XP tasks. Each bracelet costs ~15K GP and lasts roughly 30 procs. For bad tasks, use Expeditious Bracelet (30% chance to count as 2 kills) to finish them faster. See our Block and Skip Guide for optimal task management.

6. Level 75-85: High-Efficiency Mid-Game

Level 75-85 is where Slayer becomes genuinely profitable and fast. You unlock Gargoyles (75), Nechryaels (75), Kurasks (75), Spiritual Creatures (78), and Dark Beasts (85).

Gargoyles (Level 75)

Gargoyles require a Rock Hammer to finish them off. They are excellent money (granite mauls, noted alchables, 200K-400K GP/h) and decent XP. Cannon in the Slayer Tower basement for best efficiency.

Nechryaels (Level 80)

Nechryaels in the Catacombs are the best XP task in the game. They drop noted runes, herbs, and the Rune Boots. With Ice Barrage, you can hit 70K-80K XP/h. Always use Bracelet of Slaughter on these.

7. Level 85-99: Duradel and Endgame Methods

At level 85 Slayer (or 100 Combat), switch to Duradel in Shilo Village. He is the highest-level standard Slayer Master and assigns the most valuable tasks. Combined with Konar for Hydra tasks, this is the endgame.

Barraging Smoke Devils (Level 93)

Smoke Devils are the highest XP task in the game. They require a Slayer helmet (smoke variant, unlocked with 200 Slayer points) and are found in the Catacombs of Kourend. With Ice Barrage, expect 60K-75K XP per hour and massive profits from Occult drops.

Slayer Bosses for Profit

At high levels, you can take boss tasks for enormous profit: Alchemical Hydra (95 Slayer, 2M-3M GP/h), Cerberus (91 Slayer, 1.5M-2.5M GP/h), Kraken (87 Slayer, 800K-1.5M GP/h), Thermonuclear Smoke Devil (93 Slayer, 600K-1M GP/h).

Efficient 85-99 Route: 5-Task Rotation

  1. Assign task from Duradel — skip any task below 25K XP/h potential
  2. Burst/barrage tasks (Nechryael, Dust Devil, Smoke Devil) in Catacombs
  3. Cannon tasks (Abyssal Demons, Bloodvelds, Dagannoth) with optimal placement
  4. Boss tasks (Hydra, Cerberus, Kraken) for profit sessions
  5. Use Turael boosting for points when running low on skip currency

Expected time from 85-99: 80-120 hours with optimal methods. This is the home stretch.

10. Cannon Setup and Turael Skipping

Dwarf Cannon Optimization

The Dwarf Cannon is the single best tool for speeding up Slayer. Here is how to maximize its effectiveness:

Cannon Setup Checklist

Step 1: Cannon placement — always place in multi-combat zones where 3+ monsters spawn. Avoid single-combat areas where the cannon only hits one target.

Step 2: Loot management — bring alch runes or use Area Loot. The cannon generates fast kills and you need to clear drops quickly.

Step 3: Ammo efficiency — average cannonball consumption is 200-400 per 10 minutes. Bring 2K-5K per session and restock from bank when running low.

Step 4: Best cannon tasks — Dagannoth (Waterbirth), Trolls (Death Plateau), Bloodvelds (Catacombs), Ankou (Stronghold), Kalphite (Kalphite Lair), Abyssal Demons (Catacombs/Slayer Tower).

Cannonball cost: approximately 200 GP each. Budget 2M-5M GP for a cannonball supply if you plan to cannon frequently.

Turael Boosting for Points

Turael boosting (also called the 9+1 method) is the fastest way to farm Slayer points. Here is how it works:

Turael Boosting (9+1) Method

Step 1: Complete 9 tasks at Turael — these take 1-3 minutes each (rats, chickens, cows, spiders, bats, bears, goblins, scorpions, wolves).

Step 2: On the 10th task, switch back to your high-level master (Nieve/Duradel/Konar) for a good task.

Step 3: Complete the 10th task for full streak bonus points: 225 points with Konar, 165+ with Duradel.

Step 4: Reset your streak by taking a task from Turael again, then repeat.

This method yields 300-500 Slayer points per hour and is essential for funding skips and unlocks. Check our Slayer Points Guide for the complete strategy.

⚠️ When to NOT Use Turael Boosting

If your goal is pure Slayer XP to 99, do not Turael boost frequently. Those 9 small tasks give almost no Slayer XP. Only boost when you need points for essential unlocks or to fund skips. For pure XP, accept and skip at your high-level master.

11. Slayer Training FAQs

What is the fastest way to train Slayer from 1-99?

Turael tasks (1-15), Vannaka with cannon (15-40), Chaeldar burst/cannon tasks (40-65), Nieve/Steve with barraging (65-85), Duradel with barrage+cannon (85-99). Always unlock Bigger and Badder, Block List, and Slayer Helmet first. Use Bracelet of Slaughter on good tasks and Expeditious Bracelet on bad ones.

How fast is Slayer XP per hour at high levels?

At levels 85-99 with optimal gear and methods, expect 40K-80K XP/h. Bursting Dust Devils and Nechryaels in the Catacombs gives 60K-80K XP/h. Cannon tasks at Ice Trolls or Dagannoth give 50K-65K XP/h. Standard melee tasks with good gear give 20K-35K XP/h.

Should I use a cannon for Slayer training?

Yes, for multi-combat tasks. The Dwarf Cannon adds 30-50% more kills per hour on tasks like Dagannoth, Trolls, Bloodvelds, Ankou, and Kalphite. Cannonballs cost roughly 200 GP each, so budget at least 2-5M GP for the cannonball supply if you plan to cannon frequently.

Is Turael skipping worth it for 99 Slayer?

Turael boosting (9 quick Turael tasks + 1 high-level task) is excellent for accumulating Slayer points for unlocks and skips. However, for pure XP to 99, it is generally not recommended since Turael tasks give minimal Slayer XP. Only use Turael boosting to farm points for specific unlocks, then return to high-level masters for XP.

What quests should I complete for Slayer training?

Key quests: Lost City (level 9 Slayer requirement), Priest in Peril, Smoking Kills (Slayer points + helmet), Fairytale II (Fairy Rings), Lunar Diplomacy, Desert Treasure (Ancient Magicks for bursting), Animal Magnetism (Ava's), Recipe for Disaster (Barrows gloves). These unlock essential Slayer locations, transport, and gear.
