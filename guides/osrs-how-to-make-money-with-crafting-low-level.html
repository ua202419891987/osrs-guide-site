OSRS How to Make Money with Crafting Low Level 2026 -- Best Profit Methods

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 13 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
💎 Money Making -- Updated June 2026

Crafting can be a strong money-maker in OSRS at surprisingly low levels. From gem cutting at level 1 to battlestaves at level 30, dragonhide bodies at level 63, and glassblowing at level 12 -- this guide covers every profitable method with full GP margin breakdowns, XP rates, and the best practices for 2026.

1. Uncut Gem Cutting -- Best Entry-Level Profit (Level 1+)

Cutting uncut gems into cut gems is the simplest Crafting money maker. Buy uncut gems below the cut gem price, cut them, and sell for profit. This requires zero starting capital beyond the first gem, and the Crafting action is instant.

You can cut approximately 3,000 gems per hour. However, GE buy limits cap how many gems you can purchase every 4 hours, so this is best used as a supplementary money maker rather than a continuous grind. The margins fluctuate daily -- always check the GE price gap before buying.

💎 GE Limit Strategy: The GE buy limit for uncut gems is 11,000 per 4 hours for sapphires through diamonds. Use multiple offers across different gem types simultaneously to maximize your cutting volume. Buy 11K sapphires, 11K emeralds, 11K rubies, and 11K diamonds at once -- that's 44,000 gems total, yielding ~15 minutes of cutting and 200K-400K profit.

2. Battlestaves -- Best Consistent Mid-Level Money Maker (Level 30+)

Crafting battlestaves by combining orbs with battlestaves is one of the most reliable money-making methods in OSRS. The core mechanic: buy battlestaves from Zaff at a discount (7K GP each), combine them with charged orbs, and sell the finished battlestaff on the Grand Exchange for a profit.

3. Dragonhide Bodies -- Best XP + Profit Combo (Level 63+)

Crafting dragonhide bodies is the best method for combining Crafting XP with profit. Because bodies use 3 hides per craft, the XP per action is high, and the profit margins on the finished body typically cover input costs with a small profit.

At ~2,000 crafts per hour, Blue D'hide Bodies yield approximately 420,000 Crafting XP/hr AND 1M GP/hr simultaneously. This is the single best method for players who need both Crafting levels and GP. The key is buying hides in bulk when margins are favorable.

4. Sapphire Rings & Gem Jewellery (Level 20+)

Crafting gem jewellery (rings, necklaces, amulets) is accessible from very low levels and provides consistent small profits. The margins per item are thin (50-500 GP), but the craft speed is extremely fast (1,800-2,000 per hour).

Alternatively, use the Portable Forge location on world 308 (Edgeville) or the Al Kharid furnace (south of the bank). Expect ~100K GP/hr profit at low levels from sapphire rings, scaling to ~1M GP/hr at dragonstone rings (level 55+).

5. Glassblowing -- Budget Method (Level 12+)

Glassblowing involves crafting molten glass into orbs, vials, or lenses. The margins are modest when buying materials from the GE, but become much more profitable when you collect seaweed and sand yourself (via Fossil Island seaweed patches and sandstone mining).

Unpowered orbs are the most profitable glassblowing item because they are always in demand for battlestaff crafting. The orb market is stable due to consistent battlestaff demand. For Ironmen, glassblowing orbs from self-collected molten glass is essentially free money -- just time invested.

7. Advanced Tips & Efficiency

  • Daily Battlestaff runs are non-negotiable. Even if you do nothing else, buy battlestaves from Zaff every single day. Over a year, with Varrock Hard Diary, that's ~78M GP in passive profit for about 30 hours of total crafting time.
  • Use the Crafting Guild. At 40 Crafting (with a brown apron), the Crafting Guild provides a bank chest, furnace, and pottery wheel in one location. It's the best place for jewellery crafting and glassblowing until you get the Crafting Cape.
  • Price-check margins daily. Crafting margins change frequently (sometimes hourly). Use a price-checking tool (like OSRS Wiki prices or GE Tracker) before bulk-buying materials. A margin that was 200 GP/profit yesterday might be -50 GP today.
  • Combine with High Alchemy. Some crafted items (rune items, certain dragonhide items, battlestaves) alch for more than their GE value. Bringing nature and fire runes while crafting adds 10-20% to your profit per item.
  • Zenyte Jewellery at 89+ Crafting. At 89 Crafting (boostable from 84 with a Mushroom Pie), you can craft Zenyte jewellery -- the highest-value craftable jewellery in the game. A Zenyte Ring costs ~12M to make and sells for ~13M+, yielding ~1M profit per craft. But the margin is volatile.
  • Use the Edgeville furnace. With Varrock Easy Diary, the Edgeville furnace is the closest furnace to a bank. This single unlock saves 5-10 seconds per inventory of jewellery crafts -- which adds up to serious time over thousands of crafts.

8. Common Mistakes

  1. Buying materials without checking GE prices. Crafting margins are thin and volatile. Always check current GE buy/sell prices. What was profitable yesterday may be a loss today.
  2. Forgetting to buy battlestaves daily. Missing one day of Zaff's battlestaves is ~200K GP of missed profit at Varrock Hard Diary tier. Set a daily reminder.
  3. Crafting at a slow furnace. The Neitiznot furnace, Al Kharid furnace, and Edgeville furnace are the fastest. Avoid the Falador furnace (long run) and Lumbridge furnace (upstairs).
  4. High-alching items at a loss. Some items have an alch value below their GE price. Always check both GE value AND alch value -- never assume alching is always profitable.
  5. Not using the Portable Crafter. On world 308 (Edgeville), players often place Portable Crafters near the bank. These give +10% Crafting XP for all actions near them -- completely free.
  6. Forgetting a ring mould for jewellery. Always check you have the correct mould before starting a jewellery session. Without it, you cannot craft rings/necklaces/amulets.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I make money with Crafting at level 1?

A: Yes! Cutting uncut opals and uncut sapphires requires 1 Crafting. Buy uncut opals for ~500 GP, cut them (worth ~800 GP), and sell. With sapphires at ~200-300 GP profit per gem at 3,000 cuts per hour, you can earn 600K-900K GP/hr from level 1 Crafting. The GE buy limit is your only bottleneck.

Q: What Varrock Diary tier should I aim for?

A: Aim for Varrock Medium (60 staves/day) as a minimum. Varrock Hard (120 staves/day) is ideal. The Easy diary is very accessible (level 30-40 stats) and doubles your staff count. Medium requires level 50 stats. Hard requires level 70+ stats but the jump from 60 to 120 staves doubles your daily profit. Elite is nice but the 120 cap doesn't increase -- you just don't need to switch worlds for more stock.

Q: Are Dragonhide Bodies better than Battlestaves for profit?

A: For XP + profit combined, yes. For pure profit per time invested, Battlestaves are better. Blue D'hide Bodies give ~1M GP/hr + 420K XP/hr. Battlestaves give 200K-600K GP/hr but with less XP. The optimal strategy is to do daily battlestaves every day as a passive profit source, and craft dragonhide bodies when you're actively training Crafting and want profit alongside XP.

💎 Craft Your Way to a Fatter Coin Pouch

Crafting is one of the few OSRS skills where training and profit coexist naturally. Whether you are cutting gems at level 1, crafting sapphire rings at level 20, spinning battlestaves at level 30, or mass-producing dragonhide bodies at level 71 -- there is a profit method at almost every level bracket. The key is consistency: daily battlestaff runs, price-checking margins, and choosing the right method for your current level.

Buy low, craft well, sell high. Your coin pouch will thank you.
