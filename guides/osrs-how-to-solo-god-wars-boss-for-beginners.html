1. God Wars Dungeon — The Basics

God Wars Dungeon is located north of Trollheim. To enter, you need either 60 Strength or 60 Agility to move the boulder at the entrance, plus a rope on your first visit (the rope stays permanently). Inside, four factions are locked in eternal combat: Bandos, Armadyl, Zamorak, and Saradomin.

Each boss room requires 40 kill count (KC) of that faction's followers to enter. KC is earned by killing creatures in the main dungeon area. Once inside the boss room, the boss spawns every 90 seconds along with three minions. KC resets when you leave the dungeon, so plan your trips to maximize kills per entry.

4. Bandos Solo Strategy — Step by Step

  1. Prayer: Activate Protect from Melee BEFORE entering the room. Graardor hits 30+ through melee and can KO you on entry.
  2. Positioning: Stand in the north-east corner of the room. This limits minion damage by keeping them in a cluster.
  3. Attack Graardor: Use Piety (or Ultimate Strength + Incredible Reflexes) for maximum DPS. Spec with Dragon Claws or DDS at the start.
  4. Minion Priority: After Graardor dies, kill Steelwill (mage minion) FIRST — his magic attacks hit through prayer. Then Strongstack (melee), then Grimspike (ranged).
  5. Heal Between Kills: Use Guthan's spear set or Blood Barrage on the minions to heal between Graardor spawns. This extends your trip significantly.
  6. Graardor Respawns: He respawns 90 seconds after death. Be ready with Protect from Melee reactivated.
🩸 Healing Strategy: Bring a Guthan's warspear, body, chainskirt, and helm in your inventory. After Graardor dies, equip Guthan's set and heal on the minions. Guthan's has a 25% chance to heal you for the damage dealt — you can fully restore HP between kills without using any food.

8. Common Mistakes at GWD

  1. Forgetting protection prayer on entry. Bandos can hit 30+ through melee instantly. If you enter without Protect from Melee, you risk a KO before you can react.
  2. Not bringing a teleport out. GWD is deep in the dungeon. Without a teleport tab, you must run through aggressive creatures to escape. Always bring a one-click teleport.
  3. Killing the wrong minion first. At Bandos, the mage minion (Steelwill) hits through prayer. Killing him first saves more HP than any other single action.
  4. Standing in melee range of minions. The minions stack up. If all three attack you simultaneously with Graardor, you can get comboed. Position yourself in a corner to limit how many can reach you.
  5. Not healing between kills. The 90-second respawn window is your healing opportunity. Don't just stand there — use Guthan's or Blood Barrage to top off.
  6. Forgetting KC when world-hopping. If you hop worlds, your KC resets. Only hop inside the boss room if you can survive the boss while waiting for a new world.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What stats do I need to solo Bandos?

A: Minimum recommended stats for solo Bandos: 80+ Attack, 85+ Strength, 80+ Defence, 70+ Prayer (for Piety), and 80+ Hitpoints. You can solo with lower stats but will get fewer kills per trip. A budget setup with Barrows armor and an Abyssal Whip can get 2-3 kills per trip at these stats.

Q: Which GWD boss should a beginner solo first?

A: Bandos (General Graardor) is the best GWD boss for beginners. He uses melee attacks exclusively, which means Protect from Melee negates all of his damage. Bandos gear requirements are lower than Armadyl (you can use Barrows armor), and the fight mechanics are simpler. Start with Bandos, learn the GWD rhythm, then branch out.

Q: How do I extend my trips at GWD?

A: Three key methods: (1) Bring Guthan's set to heal on minions between boss kills, (2) Use Bones to Peaches tabs to convert minion bones into edible peaches (8 HP each), (3) Bring blood barrage runes in a rune pouch for AoE healing on minion clusters. Combining all three methods can extend trips from 3 kills to 15-20+ kills.

Q: How much profit can I expect from solo GWD?

A: Solo Bandos averages 800K-1.5M GP per hour with budget gear, and up to 2M/hr with max gear. Solo Armadyl averages 1M-2M GP/hr but requires higher gear investment (15M+ for budget setup). Unique drops (Bandos armor pieces, hilts) are the biggest payouts — a Bandos Chestplate drop (~8M) covers hours of supplies.

⚔️ The GWD Journey Starts Here

Soloing God Wars bosses is intimidating at first, but once you get your first Bandos kill, the rhythm clicks. Kill count, boss, minions, heal, repeat. Every trip teaches you something new. Start with Bandos in budget gear, focus on survival over speed, and the GP will flow.

Your first unique drop at GWD is a memory you'll never forget. Go get it.
