🏆 What is the Quest Cape?

The Quest Point Cape (commonly called the Quest Cape) is a cape awarded by Varrock Museum's Historian Minas after completing every single quest in Old School RuneScape. As of the 2026 game updates, there are 171 quests available, requiring a total of 385 Quest Points to unlock the cape. It is a members-only item that sits in the pantheon of achievement capes alongside the Diary Cape and Music Cape.

The cape itself offers the following stats and perks:

  • +9 to all Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, and Magic bonuses — respectable hybrid stats
  • Prayer bonus of +2
  • Unlimited teleport to the Legends Guild — directly outside a fairy ring (code B L R), close to the Woodcutting Guild, and near a tree patch
  • Acts as a white lily in your POH Costume Room — can be stored for cosmetic fashionscape

① What Counts as a Quest for the Cape?

The Quest Cape requires completion of every quest visible in the Quest List interface. This includes free-to-play quests, members quests, and all grandmaster-level quests. Mini-quests (such as the Enlightened Journey or the Garden of Tranquillity) are also included. As of 2026, the total count of 171 quests includes all content up to the latest Leagues and post-League quest releases. The cape was first introduced in October 2013 and has remained one of the most sought-after achievement capes ever since.

② Quest Cape Stats Overview

The cape provides +9 to all combat styles (stab, slash, crush, magic, and ranged attack and defence), making it a viable hybrid cape option for players who do not yet have a Fire Cape or Infernal Cape. The +2 Prayer bonus is a welcome addition for extended boss trips. These stats apply to both the trimmed and untrimmed variants — the only visual difference is the gold trim.

③ Do I Need to Complete Novice Quests Too?

Yes — every single quest must be completed, from the simplest novice quests like Cook's Assistant and Sheep Shearer all the way up to Grandmaster behemoths like Dragon Slayer 2 and Desert Treasure 2. There are no shortcuts or skip options. The cape truly represents 100% quest completion.

④ Can I Buy or Boost My Way Through?

No. Every quest requires you to personally complete the dialogue, puzzles, and boss fights. Some skill requirements can be boosted temporarily (e.g., using spices for Stew boosts or Pie boosts), but many quests have unboostable requirements. The Quest Cape is a genuine achievement that cannot be bought or leeched.

📊 Skill Requirements

To unlock every quest in OSRS, you need a broad range of skill levels. While no single skill needs to be maxed, the cumulative requirements are significant. Below is the complete list of skill levels needed to start and complete every quest currently available.

① Combat Skills

  • Attack: 70 (required for Dragon Slayer 2, Night at the Theatre)
  • Strength: 60 (required for Dragon Slayer 2 sub-quests)
  • Defence: 70 (required for Dream Mentor, Dragon Slayer 2)
  • Ranged: 75 (required for Song of the Elves, Desert Treasure 2)
  • Magic: 75 (required for Desert Treasure 2, Song of the Elves)
  • Prayer: 70 (required for King's Ransom, Desert Treasure 2)
  • Hitpoints: No direct requirement, but recommended 80+ for endgame quests

② Gathering Skills

  • Woodcutting: 60 (required for Song of the Elves, Bone Voyage)
  • Fishing: 62 (required for Swan Song, The Great Brain Robbery)
  • Mining: 65 (required for Dragon Slayer 2, Making Friends with My Arm)
  • Hunter: 67 (required for Eagles Peak, The Ascent of Arceuus)

③ Artisan Skills

  • Herblore: 78 (required for Sins of the Father — the single highest skill requirement in the game for a quest)
  • Crafting: 70 (required for Song of the Elves, Recipe for Disaster)
  • Smithing: 65 (required for Dragon Slayer 2, The Knight's Sword)
  • Firemaking: 50 (required for Wintertodt-related quests, Bone Voyage)
  • Cooking: 50 (required for Recipe for Disaster sub-quests)
  • Fletching: 55 (required for Animal Magnetism, Song of the Elves)
  • Runecraft: 50 (required for Enter the Abyss, Desert Treasure 2)

④ Support Skills

  • Agility: 70 (required for Song of the Elves, The Light Within, MePi2 puzzle)
  • Thieving: 62 (required for Desert Treasure 2, The Feud)
  • Farming: 73 (required for Song of the Elves — one of the most time-consuming requirements)
  • Slayer: 65 (required for A Taste of Hope, The Great Brain Robbery)
  • Construction: 65 (required for The Eyes of Glouphrie, Making Friends with My Arm)

⑤ Total Level Requirement Summary

To unlock every quest in OSRS, you need a minimum total level of approximately 1,420 across all skills. The most time-gated requirements are Herblore (78), Farming (73), and Construction (65). Plan to train these skills long before you reach the endgame quests or you will be stuck grinding at the finish line.

🗺️ Recommended Quest Order

Completing quests in the right order saves you dozens of hours. Follow this roadmap organized by tier, from beginner-friendly to endgame challenges.

① Tier 1: Free-to-Play Foundation (1–35 QP)

Start here if you are a new player. These quests teach basic mechanics and require almost no stats.

  • Cook's Assistant, Sheep Shearer, The Restless Ghost, Romeo & Juliet, Goblin Diplomacy, Ernest the Chicken, Vampyre Slayer, Demon Slayer, The Knight's Sword, Witch's Potion, Imp Catcher, Shield of Arrav, Merelin's Mystery, Doric's Quest, Pirate's Treasure, Prince Ali Rescue, Rune Mysteries, Between a Rock...

② Tier 2: Early Members (35–80 QP)

Once you become a member, tackle these to unlock major transportation and quality-of-life features.

  • Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree, Priest in Peril, The Lost Tribe, Death Plateau, The Digsite, Elemental Workshop I, The Feud, The Golem, Tourist Trap, Biohazard, Plague City, Underground Pass, Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, Monkey Madness I, Horror from the Deep

③ Tier 3: Mid-Level Member (80–160 QP)

These quests start requiring base 40–50 stats and introduce more complex boss fights and puzzles.

  • In Aid of the Myreque, Darkness of Hallowvale, A Taste of Hope, Swan Song, One Small Favour, Legends' Quest, Lunar Diplomacy, Dream Mentor, The Great Brain Robbery, Cabin Fever, Rat Catchers, Olaf's Quest, The Hand in the Sand, Cold War, The Chosen Commander

④ Tier 4: High-Level Member (160–280 QP)

This tier covers Quest Cape beta quests and the pre-requisites for Grandmaster challenges. Expect stat requirements of 55+.

  • The Eyes of Glouphrie, The Path of Glouphrie, Making History, The Royal Trouble, Throne of Miscellania, Mourning's End Part I & II, Within the Light, The Prisoner of Glouphrie, The Void Stares Back, While Guthix Sleeps, The Temple at Senntisten

⑤ Tier 5: Grandmaster Gateway (280–340 QP)

Complete all the necessary pre-requisites before tackling the five hardest quests. At this stage you should have base 60+ stats.

  • Sins of the Father, Dragon Slayer 2, Monkey Madness II, Making Friends with My Arm, The Ascent of Arceuus, Song of the Elves, A Night at the Theatre, Desert Treasure 2

⑥ Tier 6: The Final Stretch (340–385 QP)

Finish the remaining quests. Double-check the OSRS Quest List for any you may have missed, especially the mini-quests and odd one-offs like Curse of the Empty Lord, Rag and Bone Man, and the Enlightened Journey.

⚔️ Hardest Quests Breakdown

These five quests are widely considered the hardest in OSRS. They require high stats, mechanical skill, and patience. Master these and the Quest Cape is yours.

① Dragon Slayer 2

Release date: January 2018
Difficulty: Grandmaster
Requirements: 70 Attack, 60 Strength, 70 Defence, 60 Smithing, 65 Mining, 60 Agility, 60 Hitpoints, 50 Crafting, 50 Magic, 43 Prayer
Quest Points: 5

Dragon Slayer 2 is infamous for its Galvek boss fight — a four-phase dragon battle that tests your gear switching, prayer flicking, and positioning. The Vorkath pre-boss is also a challenging mechanical fight. Key strategies include:

  • Use ruby bolts (e) for the first phases of Galvek, then switch to diamond bolts (e)
  • Bring at least 2 Super Restores and a Ranging Potion
  • Wear full Void Knight gear for maximum Ranged damage output
  • Learn the fire bomb special attack — it requires specific tile movement

Estimated time: 4–8 hours for the whole quest. Galvek alone can take 5–20 attempts for first-timers.

② Song of the Elves

Release date: July 2019
Difficulty: Grandmaster
Requirements: 70 Agility, 70 Crafting, 70 Herblore, 70 Mining, 70 Smithing, 70 Farming, 70 Hunter, 70 Woodcutting, 70 Magic, 70 Ranged, 70 Defence, 50 Construction, 50 Fletching
Quest Points: 4

SOTE is the gateway to Prifddinas. Its difficulty comes from the steep skill requirements (the most of any quest) and the Seren boss fight, a mechanically intense fight that requires pixel-perfect movement and prayer prioritization. Key tips:

  • Train Farming early — it is the biggest time gate. Use tree runs and fruit tree runs
  • For Seren, use Blood Barrage or Guthix's Blessing to sustain through the healing phase
  • Bring Phoenix necklaces and a Dark Bow spec to survive the big damage phases
  • Keep your inventory filled with high-healing food like Anglerfish or Manta Rays

Estimated time: 6–12 hours (skill grinding not included). The Seren fight averages 3–10 attempts for new players.

③ A Night at the Theatre

Release date: June 2020
Difficulty: Grandmaster
Requirements: 70 Attack, 60 Strength, 65 Defence, 60 Hitpoints, 55 Magic, 55 Ranged, 55 Prayer, 32 Agility, 35 Mining, 30 Slayer, 35 Farming, 40 Herblore
Quest Points: 2

This quest is a solo Theatre of Blood experience. You must defeat all five bosses: Maiden of Sugadinti, Pestilent Bloat, Nylocas Vasilias, Sotetseg, Xarpus, and finally Verzik Vitur. This is pure mechanical PvM. Key strategies:

  • Learn the Nylocas room — it requires quick gear switching and prioritization
  • For Verzik (phase 3), use the purple crab method to reduce damage taken
  • Bring avernic defender, trident of the seas, whip, blowpipe, and dinh's bulwark
  • Watch Verzik's tornado attack — run in a straight line, not in a circle

Estimated time: 3–10 hours depending on PvM experience. First attempts often wipe at Verzik phase 3 multiple times.

④ Sins of the Father

Release date: June 2020
Difficulty: Master
Requirements: 78 Herblore (unboostable), 65 Slayer, 62 Agility, 60 Attack, 50 Defence, 50 Magic, 50 Ranged, 50 Prayer, 50 Woodcutting, 50 Crafting, 50 Mining, 49 Strength, 40 Firemaking, 38 Smithing, 20 Construction
Quest Points: 2

Sins of the Father takes place in Darkmeyer and culminates in the Vanstrom Klause boss fight. Vanstrom is deceptively hard for a Master-level quest. His fight involves lightning walls, darkness storms, and fast prayer switching. Key tips:

  • The 78 Herblore requirement is the biggest barrier — you cannot boost this level
  • Use a Salve Amulet (e) for bonus damage against Vanstrom (he is a vampyre)
  • Keep run energy high — the lightning wall phase requires constant movement
  • Bring Super Restores and a one-click teleport (home tab) to escape if needed

Estimated time: 3–6 hours. The Vanstrom fight can take 2–10 attempts depending on your prayer flicking skill.

⑤ Desert Treasure 2 — The Fallen Empire

Release date: July 2023
Difficulty: Grandmaster
Requirements: 75 Magic, 75 Ranged, 70 Prayer, 62 Thieving, 60 Runecraft, 60 Hitpoints, 55 Firemaking, 50 Woodcutting, 50 Mining, 50 Herblore, 50 Crafting, 50 Fletching, 50 Smithing, 43 Prayer, 35 Agility, 10 Slayer
Quest Points: 2

Desert Treasure 2 is the newest Grandmaster quest and features four endgame bosses: the Whisperer, Duke Sucellus, Vardorvis, and the Leviathan, plus a final fight against the awakened Stranger. This quest has the most complex boss mechanics of any quest. Key strategies:

  • Use the correct protection prayers for each boss — each uses different attack styles
  • Bring a good spec weapon: Dragon Claws, Voidwaker, or BGS for defence reduction
  • Learn each boss separately — do not attempt them back-to-back without practice
  • Bring plenty of supplies — combo-eat Anglerfish + Karambwan for emergency healing

Estimated time: 8–20 hours. This is the longest and hardest quest in OSRS as of 2026.

⑥ Boss-by-Boss Breakdown for DT2

Desert Treasure 2 features four unique bosses, each with distinct mechanics that require different gear setups and strategies.

  • The Whisperer: A magic-based boss weak to Ranged. Use the venom mechanic to your advantage — step on the poison pools to reduce her special attack frequency. Bring a Twisted Bow or Bow of Faerdhinen for maximum damage output.
  • Duke Sucellus: Weak to crush attacks. Use a Ghrazi Rapier or Inquisitor's Mace for melee damage. The Duke has a unique slam attack that requires you to step away from the central area. Keep Protect from Melee active throughout the fight.
  • Vardorvis: A fast-paced melee boss with a blood orb mechanic. Use a Scythe of Vitur for maximum DPS. The blood orbs spawn in a cross pattern — move to the clear quadrant as soon as they appear. Bring extra Saradomin Brews for sustain.
  • The Leviathan: A Ranged-based boss weak to Magic. Use a Tumeken's Shadow or a Trident of the Swamp with a magic offhand. The Leviathan phases between underwater and airborne states — during the airborne phase, the terrain shifts and you must reposition constantly.
  • The Awakened Stranger (Final Fight): A multi-phase boss that combines elements from all four previous bosses. Bring a diverse inventory with all three attack styles. Spec weapons like Dragon Claws or Voidwaker are essential for the final phase.

Tips for DT2 success: Study each boss individually on YouTube before attempting. Do not rush — the quest instance allows unlimited retries. Bring at least 3 Super Restores, 2 Saradomin Brews, and combo food for each boss encounter. Consider using the Ring of Suffering for the Whisperer and the Lightbearer for sustained special attacks against all bosses.

🌟 Benefits of the Quest Cape

The Quest Cape is more than just a cosmetic item. It offers several meaningful benefits that make the long journey worthwhile.

① Unlimited Legends Guild Teleport

The most practical benefit is the unlimited teleport to the Legends Guild. This location provides access to:

  • Fairy ring code B L R — right outside the guild, giving access to the entire fairy ring network (great for clue scrolls, farming runs, and boss trips)
  • Tree patch — ideal for farming runs (yew/magic trees are nearby in the Woodcutting Guild)
  • Woodcutting Guild — a very short run north-west for redwoods and magic trees
  • The Legends Guild basement — contains a muspah (Slayer monster) and various resources

② Max Cape Requirement

The Quest Cape is one of the required capes to assemble the Max Cape. To create a Max Cape, you must have the Quest Cape, Achievement Diary Cape, and Music Cape alongside 99 in all skills. If your goal is a Max Cape, the Quest Cape is a non-negotiable milestone along the way.

③ Trimmed Variant

Once you complete all 171 quests, the cape becomes trimmed. The trimmed Quest Cape has a distinctive gold trim that sets it apart in fashionscape. It also shows other players that you have truly completed every quest in the game, not just the QP requirement.

④ Respect and Prestige

The Quest Cape is widely recognized in the OSRS community as a mark of a dedicated player. It signals that you have experienced most of the game's storylines, understand its lore, and have the perseverance to see a long-term goal through. Many high-level PvM clans require or strongly prefer the Quest Cape for membership.

⑤ Practical Perks from Quest Unlocks

Completing all quests also gives you:

  • Access to Prifddinas, the elven city with the best skilling spots in the game
  • Access to Myth's Guild (from Dragon Slayer 2) with adamant/rune dragons and a bank
  • Access to Fossil Island for birdhouse runs, herbiboar, and volcanic mine
  • Access to Darkmeyer for vyrewatch sentinels (great prayer/fletching training)
  • Full Barrows gloves from Recipe for Disaster
  • Cannonball smithing at the Myths' Guild
  • Ancient Magicks from Desert Treasure I
  • Lunar Magicks from Lunar Diplomacy
  • Arceuus spellbook from A Kingdom Divided

⏱️ Time Commitment & Efficiency Tips

The Quest Cape journey is a marathon, not a sprint. Here is what to expect in terms of time and how to optimize your progress.

① Total Time Estimate

  • Speedrunner (experienced player, optimal route): 150–200 hours
  • Average player (following guides, moderate stats): 250–350 hours
  • First-time player (learning mechanics, low stats): 350–500 hours (includes skill training time)

The skill training component is the largest variable. Players starting with low Herblore, Farming, and Construction will spend significantly longer than those who have already trained these skills.

② Use Quest Helper Plugins

The Quest Helper plugin on RuneLite is the single most useful tool for efficient questing. It highlights NPC locations, puzzle solutions, dialogue choices, and item requirements in real-time. Combined with the official OSRS Wiki quest guides, you can reduce your quest completion time by 30–50% compared to playing blind.

③ Pre-Gather Quest Items

Use the OSRS Wiki's "Required items" list for each quest before you start. Create a bank tab specifically for quest items. Better yet, use the Quest Helper's item checklist feature to ensure you have everything before traveling to the quest start location. This alone can save 5–10 hours across all quests.

④ Stack Your Quests

Many quests share locations, NPCs, or items. Group them by region to minimize travel time:

  • Morytania quests: Priest in Peril, In Aid of the Myreque, Darkness of Hallowvale, A Taste of Hope, Sins of the Father
  • Feldip Hills / Gnome quests: Tree Gnome Village, The Grand Tree, Monkey Madness I & II, Eyes of Glouphrie, Path of Glouphrie
  • Kandarin quests: Biohazard, Plague City, Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, Regicide, Mourning's End Parts I & II, Within the Light, Song of the Elves
  • Morytania / Theatre quests: A Taste of Hope, Sins of the Father, Night at the Theatre
  • Desert quests: Desert Treasure I & II, The Feud, The Golem, Tourist Trap, Contact!

⑤ Train Skills Efficiently

  • Herblore (78): Use the most cost-effective method — prayer pots at 38, super restores at 63. Consider making potions during double XP weekends or using the Botanical Pie boost for smaller gaps
  • Farming (73): Tree runs and fruit tree runs are the fastest. Combine with farm runs twice a day for consistent gains. Tithe Farm is an alternative for active training
  • Construction (65): Oak larders from 33–65 is the classic method. Mahogany tables if you have the budget. Use the Demon Butler for faster building
  • Agility (70): Seers' Village Rooftop course from 60–70 is profitable with the hard Kandarin diary. Alternatively, use the Werewolf Skullball course for variety
  • Slayer (65): Do Slayer naturally through combat training. Use Nieve/Steve in the Gnome Stronghold for the best tasks

⑥ Know the Speedrun Strategies

For the hardest quests, watch speedrun videos on YouTube before attempting. Key strategies to learn:

  • Galvek (Dragon Slayer 2): Void Knight gear + ruby/diamond bolts (e), walk under method for fire bomb
  • Seren (Song of the Elves): Phoenix necklace + Nightmare staff + Blood Blitz/Barrage
  • Verzik (Night at the Theatre): Purple crab tank method, whip + defender for phase 1 and 2
  • Vanstrom (Sins of the Father): Salve Amulet (e), Dragon Scimitar, keep run energy above 70%
  • DT2 bosses: Learn each boss individually in the quest instance — do not skip the practice runs

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get the Quest Cape without completing all quests?

No. You must complete every single quest in the game. As of 2026, there are 171 quests totaling 385 Quest Points. There are no exceptions or shortcuts.

What is the fastest way to get 78 Herblore?

The fastest method is making the highest-level potion you can afford. Prayer potions (Ranarr + Marrentill) from level 38 are profitable and efficient. For pure speed, use Super Restores from level 63 or Saradomin Brews from level 78.

How many Quest Points do I need for the cape?

You need 385 Quest Points to unlock the trimmed Quest Cape. The untrimmed Quest Cape unlocks at 270 QP, but you need all quests complete for the full trimmed version.

Which quest requires the highest skill level?

Sins of the Father requires 78 Herblore, which is the single highest skill requirement in the game for a quest. Song of the Elves requires 70 in seven different skills, making it the broadest requirement set.

Is Desert Treasure 2 harder than Dragon Slayer 2?

Yes, most players consider Desert Treasure 2 harder because it has four distinct endgame bosses with complex mechanics. Dragon Slayer 2 has two bosses (Vorkath and Galvek), making it mechanically more focused but less varied.

What combat level do I need for the Quest Cape?

There is no official combat level requirement, but to complete all Grandmaster quests, you should be at least Combat Level 100+ with 80+ in your primary combat stats. Night at the Theatre and Desert Treasure 2 are much more manageable at higher combat levels.

Can I use RuneLite Quest Helper for all quests?

Yes, the Quest Helper plugin on RuneLite supports every quest in OSRS. It provides step-by-step navigation, puzzle solutions, and item requirements. It is allowed under Jagex's rules and does not violate the game's terms of service.

What happens if a new quest is released after I get the cape?

When a new quest is released, your Quest Cape becomes untrimmed again. You must complete the new quest to restore the trim. The cape will keep its stats regardless. This is one reason dedicated cape owners stay up-to-date with quest releases.

How much money should I budget for quest skill requirements?

Budget approximately 10–20 million OSRS gold for skill training costs, including Herblore, Construction, and Farming. If you train Herblore with profitable methods (like prayer pots), the cost can be significantly lower, or even net-zero.

Is the Quest Cape worth getting in 2026?

Absolutely. The Quest Cape remains one of the most rewarding achievements in OSRS. The teleport alone is incredibly useful, and the cape is a required component of the Max Cape. Moreover, the content you experience through questing unlocks most of the game's best content: Prifddinas, Myths' Guild, Fossil Island, Barrows gloves, and all spellbooks.

What order should I do the five hardest quests?

Most players recommend this order: 1) Sins of the Father (easiest boss, but highest Herblore requirement), 2) Dragon Slayer 2 (medium difficulty, good gear rewards), 3) Song of the Elves (long puzzles but manageable boss), 4) A Night at the Theatre (harder mechanics, needs practice), 5) Desert Treasure 2 (hardest overall, save for last). This progression lets you build mechanical skill gradually.

Which quests should I skip or leave for last?

Do not skip any quest — you need all 171. However, you can leave the following tedious quests for the end: Rat Catchers (infamous for the terrible stealth section), One Small Favour (long running-around quest), Mourning's End Part II (crystal puzzle hell), and Sheep Herder (frustrating mechanics). Save these for when you are in the completionist mindset.
