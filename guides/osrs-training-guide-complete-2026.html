① 🗺️ Complete Training Roadmap (F2P → Members)

The biggest mistake new players make is training skills in isolation — "I'll get 99 Fishing before touching combat." That's not how OSRS works. The most efficient path is a balanced approach that unlocks content as you go. This section gives you the complete F2P-to-members roadmap.

1.1 🎯 The F2P Training Roadmap (Levels 3–40)

F2P is where you learn the game. You have 15 skills (limited), no quest rewards worth writing home about, and training options are slim. But F2P is also where you build your foundation. Here's the optimal F2P route:

Why this order? Combat first because almost everything in OSRS requires combat stats. Prayer second because protection prayers change how you train forever. The rest are utility skills that make your life easier. For a step-by-step F2P walkthrough, read our F2P Ultimate Beginner Guide.

1.2 🔓 When to Buy Membership (The F2P → P2P Transition)

Don't rush membership. F2P is designed to teach you the basics. But once you have 40+ combat stats, 30 in a few skills, and a few hundred thousand GP saved, it's time. Membership unlocks:

  • 9 additional skills (Farming, Construction, Hunter, etc.)
  • Over 30 new areas (Zeah, Tirannwn, Morytania, etc.)
  • Hundreds of new quests with massive XP rewards
  • Better training methods for every skill
  • Slayer — the best combat training method in the game

1.3 🗺️ The Members Training Roadmap (Post-Purchase)

Once you've bought membership, follow this sequence in your first week:

  1. Day 1: Unlock teleports (Fairy Rings, Spirit Trees, Lodestone network)
  2. Day 2–3: Train combat to 60+ at Sand Crabs or Ammonite Crabs
  3. Day 4: Get 43 Prayer at a Gilded Altar
  4. Day 5: Start Slayer (talk to Turael in Burthorpe)
  5. Day 6–7: Complete high-XP quests (Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village)

This 7-day plan gets you from "lost new member" to "functional mid-game player" with 60+ combat, protection prayers, a Slayer task list, and key teleports unlocked. For the complete post-purchase checklist, read our 10 Things to Do First After Buying Membership guide.

1.4 🎯 Setting Your Long-Term Goal

Before you start grinding, pick a long-term goal. OSRS is a 5,000-hour game — without a goal, you'll quit. Popular goals for new members:

  • "I want to boss" → Priority: Combat 70+, 43 Prayer, 70 Slayer, fire cape
  • "I want to make GP" → Priority: 70+ money-making stats (Woodcutting, Mining, Hunter), 50 Construction for POH
  • "I want to quest everything" → Priority: Quest reqs, 70+ combat, 50+ all stats
  • "I want to be a completionist" → Priority: 99 all skills, all quests, all diaries, all bosses

Your goal determines your training priority. A bosser trains differently than a skiller. Know what you want before you start spending XP lamps and quest rewards.

② 🎯 Combat Training Priority (Melee/Ranged/Magic)

Combat is the heart of OSRS. Even if you want to be a pure skiller, you'll need combat stats to survive certain content. This section breaks down the most efficient combat training priority for 2026.

2.1 ⚔️ Melee Training Priority (Attack/Strength/Defence)

Melee is the most straightforward combat style. Here's your leveling priority:

Phase 1: Levels 1–40 (F2P)

  • 1–20: Cows in Lumbridge (train Attack and Strength equally)
  • 20–30: Al-Kharid Warriors (good drops, decent XP)
  • 30–40: Moss Giants in Varrock Sewers (bury the big bones for Prayer XP)

Phase 2: Levels 40–60 (Early Members)

  • 40–50: Sand Crabs on Zeah (AFK, high XP, no requirements)
  • 50–60: Ammonite Crabs (more XP than Sand Crabs, slightly more effort)

Phase 3: Levels 60–70 (Mid-Game)

  • 60–70: Slayer tasks (starting with Turael, then Mazchna at 20, Vannaka at 40)

Phase 4: Levels 70+ (Late-Game)

  • 70–80: Continue Slayer, boss on the side (Giant Mole, Barrows)
  • 80–99: High-level Slayer (Duradel at 75), endgame bosses (Zulrah, Vorkath, Raids)
💡 Attack vs Strength vs Defence — What to Train First?

The "efficient" route is to get 60 Attack first (unlocks Dragon weapons), then 70 Strength (max hit damage), then 60 Defence (unlocks Barrows armour). But honestly? Train them equally. A balanced account is more fun to play and lets you do more content earlier. The "power-leveling" meta only matters if you're chasing efficiency records.

2.2 🏹 Ranged Training Priority

Ranged is the best ranged attack style and essential for many bosses. Training priority:

  • 1–40: Chickens (collect feathers) → Archery targets in Varrock → Al-Kharid Warriors with Iron arrows
  • 40–60: Flesh Crawlers (Soul Wars) → Sand Crabs with Iron knives or Bronze arrows
  • 60–70: Slayer tasks with Ranged (bring a magic shortbow or rune crossbow)
  • 70+: High-level Slayer with Ranged (Blowpipe, Bursting/Rainbowing at Zulrah or Vorkath)

Key upgrade points: 50 Ranged unlocks the Magic Shortbow (special attack), 61 Ranged unlocks the Rune Crossbow (Dragon bolts), 75 Ranged unlocks the Twisted Bow (endgame). For a full Ranged training guide, check our Ranged Training Guide.

2.3 ✨ Magic Training Priority

Magic is the most versatile combat style — it's also your teleport network. Training priority:

  • 1–25: Cast Wind Strike / Water Strike on cows or Al-Kharid Warriors
  • 25–55: Cast Curse or Vulnerability on high-HP monsters (Camels in Al-Kharid)
  • 55–70: High Alchemy (converts items to GP, also trains Magic) — this is the best dual-purpose training method in the game
  • 70+: Bossing with Magic (Zulrah, Vorkath, Chambers of Xeric)

Don't forget: Every 10 Magic levels unlocks a new teleport. Prioritize getting 25 Magic (Varrock teleport), 31 Magic (Lumbridge teleport), 37 Magic (Falador teleport), and 45 Magic (Camelot teleport). These teleports save you hours of walking over your OSRS career.

2.4 ⚔️ Which Combat Style Should You Train First?

If you're torn between Melee, Ranged, and Magic, here's the honest answer: train Melee first. Melee is the cheapest (you only need food), works everywhere, and most quests require Melee stats. Train Ranged alongside Melee (on Slayer tasks), and train Magic passively through High Alchemy and teleports.

🎯 The "Balanced Account" Build (Recommended for New Players)

Combat 60/60/60 (Melee) + Ranged 60 + Magic 60 + 43 Prayer = You can do 90% of OSRS content. This is the "Swiss Army Knife" build — not optimized for any one thing, but capable of everything. Get these stats first before specializing.

③ 🌾 Skilling Training (Most Efficient Methods)

Skilling is everything that isn't combat. OSRS has 23 skills total — 14 in F2P, 9 additional in members. This section covers the most efficient training methods for each skill in 2026.

You need food to survive. Fishing and Cooking are non-negotiable. Here's the efficient route:

3.2 🪓 Woodcutting & Firemaking (The AFK Skills)

Woodcutting and Firemaking are the most AFK skills in the game. Train them while watching Netflix:

  • 1–15: Trees (normal) → Firemaking: burn the logs
  • 15–30: Oak trees → burn Oak logs
  • 30–45: Willow trees (Draynor is best) → burn Willow logs
  • 45–60: Maple trees (north of Seers' Village) → burn Maple logs
  • 60–75: Yew trees (Edgeville Dungeon or Draynor) → burn Yew logs
  • 75–99: Magic trees (Gnome Stronghold or Woodcutting Guild) → burn Magic logs or bank them for fletching

Efficient Twist: At 50 Firemaking, do Wintertodt for Firemaking XP and GP instead of burning logs. It's 40K–80K Firemaking XP/hour and you get supply crates worth 200K–400K GP/hour. Read our Wintertodt Guide for the full strategy.

3.3 ⛏️ Mining & Smithing (The GP Skills)

Mining is slow but profitable. Smithing is slow but useful. Here's the efficient route:

  • 1–15: Copper/Tin → Bronze bars
  • 15–30: Iron ore (3-tick mining at Dwarven Mine) → Steel bars
  • 30–55: Coal (Dwarven Mine or Mining Guild) → Mithril bars
  • 55–70: Gold ore (Blast Mine after 72 Mining, or Motherlode Mine)
  • 70–99: Amethyst (3-tick at Mining Guild) or Blast Mine (85+)

Motherlode Mine (after 30 Mining) is the best AFK method — you mine ore veins and the machine automatically processes them into ores. It's slower than powermining but requires zero banking. For a complete guide, read our Mining Guide.

3.4 🌱 Farming (The Passive Skill)

Farming is unique — it's passive. You plant seeds, wait (or do other things), then come back and harvest. It's also the most profitable skill in the game (Ranarr weeds, Snapdragon, Torstol).

  • 1–15: Plant potatoes and onions in patches
  • 15–30: Tomatoes and sweetcorn
  • 30–50: Strawberries and watermelons (for birdhouse runs)
  • 50–99: Herb runs (Ranarr, Snapdragon, Torstol) + tree runs (Magic, Redwood)

Herb runs take 5 minutes and give 50K–100K Farming XP and 100K–300K GP per run. Do them daily. It's the single highest GP/hour activity in the game when you count both XP and profit. For the full strategy, read our Farming Guide.

3.5 🏃 Agility (The Mobility Skill)

Agility unlocks shortcuts and increases your run energy regeneration. It's universally hated but essential. Here's the route:

  • 1–10: Gnome Stronghold Agility Course
  • 10–20: Draynor Village Agility Course
  • 20–30: Al-Kharid Roof Tops
  • 30–40: Varrock Roof Tops
  • 40–52: Canifis Roof Tops
  • 52–60: Ape Atoll Agility Course (after Monkey Madness I)
  • 60–70: Seers' Village Agility Course
  • 70–99: Ardougne Agility Course (350 XP per lap, best in game)

Mark of Grace farming on roof top courses gives you Graceful armour — the only armour that reduces your run energy drain. Get the full Graceful set before doing any serious skilling or Quest rushing. It's that important.

3.6 🎯 Hunter (The Members-Only Skill)

Hunter is members-only and unlocks Birdhouse runs (passive Hunter + Herbs) and Chinchompas (Ranged training + GP). Here's the route:

  • 1–9: Catch Crimson Swifts (north of Yanille)
  • 9–15: Catch Copper Longtails (near Entrana)
  • 15–29: Catch Cerulean Twitches (Piscatoris)
  • 29–43: Catch Butterflies (near the Hunting shop)
  • 43–80: Catch Red Chinchompas (Feldip Hills) — 200K–300K GP/hour
  • 80–99: Catch Black Chinchompas (Wilderness) — 400K–600K GP/hour

Birdhouse runs (after 5 Hunter) take 2 minutes and give 2K–5K Hunter XP + 2–5 herbs per run. Do them every 50 minutes while training other skills. It's free XP and free herbs.

④ 📜 Questing Strategy (XP Rewards Optimization)

Quests are the fastest XP in the game. A single quest can give more XP than 10 hours of training. But not all quests are equal — some give massive XP rewards for low requirements. This section gives you the optimal questing strategy.

4.2 🎯 Quest Order for New Members (The "Quest Path")

Don't just pick quests randomly. Follow the "Quest Path" — a sequence that unlocks content as you go:

  1. Novice Quests: Cook's Assistant, Doric's Quest, Ernest the Chicken (unlock basic infrastructure)
  2. Intermediate Quests: Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village (big XP rewards)
  3. Members Quests: Priest in Peril, Fairy Tale I, Lost City (unlock teleports and areas)
  4. Achievement Diary Prep: Fremennik Trials, Tree Gnome Village, Monk's Friend (unlock diary tasks)
  5. Endgame Quests: Monkey Madness I & II, Dragon Slayer II, Sins of the Father (unlock late-game content)

4.3 📊 Using XP Lamps Efficiently

Many quests give XP lamps (free XP you can assign to any skill). Don't waste them on skills you'll train naturally. Use them on:

  • Herblore (slow and expensive to train normally)
  • Construction (extremely expensive to train normally)
  • Agility (tedious to train manually)
  • Prayer (if you don't have GP for bones)

Don't use lamps on: Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic (you'll get these from combat training), Fishing, Cooking, Woodcutting (you'll do these passively), or Slayer (you want the drops).

4.4 🎮 Using the Quest Helper Plugin (RuneLite)

If you're using RuneLite (and you should be), install the Quest Helper plugin. It highlights exactly what items you need, where to go, and what to click. It turns a 2-hour quest into a 30-minute quest. It's the single biggest QoL improvement for questing.

💡 Quest Points Matter

Many members areas and teleports require Quest Points (QP). For example, the Fairy Rings require 2 QP, the Spirit Trees require 3 QP, and the Quest Point Cape requires 150+ QP. Track your QP and prioritize quest series that give multiple QP per completion.

5.2 🎣 Training Methods That Cost GP (Avoid These)

Some training methods have a negative GP/hour — you're paying to train. Avoid these unless you're in a rush:

  • Construction: 1.5M–5M GP to 99 (buy planks from the GT)
  • Prayer (burials): 10M–30M GP to 99 (burying dragon bones)
  • Herblore: 50M–120M GP to 99 (buy secondary ingredients)
  • Smithing (superheating): 20M–50M GP to 99 (unless using Blast Furnace)

The workaround: Train these skills passively. Do Pest Control for Prayer XP (free), do Herblore only with herbs you farm yourself, train Construction by building your POH only with planks from your Fletching training.

5.3 🔄 The "Hybrid" Method (Train Two Skills at Once)

Some methods train two skills simultaneously. These are the most efficient uses of your time:

  • Slayer + Combat styles: You train Slayer and Melee/Ranged/Magic at the same time
  • Farming + Herblore: Grow herbs → make potions (two skills, one harvest)
  • Mining + Smithing: Mine ores → smelt bars → smith items (three skills, one workflow)
  • Fishing + Cooking: Catch fish → cook fish (two skills, one location — Catherby)
  • Agility + Thieving: Ardougne Agility course + pickpocketing Ardougne knights (if you can multitask)

5.4 💎 When to Switch from "Efficient" to "Profitable"

There's a trade-off between XP/hour and GP/hour. Early game, prioritize XP — you need stats to unlock content. Mid-game (after 70+ combat, 50+ skills), start prioritizing GP — you need money for endgame upgrades (Barrows gloves, fire cape, BIS gear).

The rule of thumb: If you have less than 5M GP in the bank, prioritize GP-making methods. If you have more than 10M GP, prioritize XP methods. The GP you make early game pays for the expensive training later.

⑥ 📅 Training Schedule (Week 1 / Month 1 / Long Term)

OSRS is a marathon. Without a schedule, you'll burn out. This section gives you a realistic training schedule for your first week, first month, and long-term progression.

6.2 📅 Month 1 Schedule (The "Momentum" Month)

Month 1 is where you build momentum. You're no longer struggling with basics — now you're working toward mid-game goals. Here's the monthly breakdown:

  • Week 2: Get 70 Combat (Melee), 60 Ranged, 60 Magic, 52 Prayer (unlocks Eagle Eye and Mystic Might)
  • Week 3: Get 70 Slayer (unlocks high-level tasks), 60 in 5+ skills, complete Recipe for Disaster sub-quests
  • Week 4: Get fire cape (70+ combat, 43 Prayer, 60 Ranged), start bossing (Giant Mole, Barrows), aim for 10M+ bank value

By the end of Month 1, you should have: 70+ combat stats, 52+ Prayer, 70 Slayer, fire cape, 10M+ GP, and the ability to do most mid-game content. That's a massive transformation from where you started.

6.3 📅 Long-Term Progression (Month 2–12)

After Month 1, your path depends on your goal (see Section 1.4). But here's a general long-term roadmap:

  • Month 2–3: Max combat stats (99 Attack/Strength/Defence), 85 Slayer, start endgame bossing (Zulrah, Vorkath, Raids)
  • Month 4–6: 99 in 3–5 skills, maxed Slayer (99), completionist goals (all quests, all diaries, all achievements)
  • Month 7–12: 99 in all skills, max cape, quest point cape, high-level PvM (Chambers of Xeric, Theatre of Blood, Tombs of Amascut)

Reality check: Most players never hit 99 in all skills. That's fine. OSRS isn't about "finishing" — it's about the journey. Set goals you enjoy, not goals you think you "should" have.

6.4 ⏰ How Many Hours Per Day Should You Play?

OSRS is designed to be played casually. You don't need to grind 8 hours/day to make progress. Here's a healthy pace:

  • Casual (1–2 hours/day): You'll hit mid-game in 3–6 months, late-game in 12–18 months
  • Moderate (3–5 hours/day): You'll hit mid-game in 1–2 months, late-game in 6–9 months
  • Hardcore (6+ hours/day): You'll hit late-game in 3–6 months, but you'll probably burn out
⚠️ Burnout Is Real

OSRS is a 5,000-hour game. If you try to rush it, you'll hate it. Play at a pace you enjoy. Some of the best OSRS memories come from "wasting time" fishing with friends or exploring a new area you've never visited. Don't optimize the fun out of the game.

⑦ ⚠️ Common Training Mistakes to Avoid

Every OSRS player makes mistakes. The difference between a successful account and a stalled account is learning from other people's mistakes. Here are the most common training mistakes and how to avoid them.

7.1 ❌ Mistake #1: Training Skills in Isolation

The mistake: "I'm going to get 99 Fishing before I touch anything else."
Why it's bad: OSRS skills are interconnected. Fishing feeds Cooking, Cooking feeds combat sustainability, combat sustainability unlocks bossing, bossing unlocks GP, GP unlocks better training methods. Training in isolation is slow and boring.
The fix: Train skills in parallel. Fish while your Agility course is on cooldown. Do a Farming run between Slayer tasks. Play the game like a game, not a spreadsheet.

7.2 ❌ Mistake #2: Ignoring Quests Early On

The mistake: "Quests are boring, I just want to train."
Why it's bad: Quests give massive XP rewards, unlock teleports, unlock areas, and unlock training methods. Ignoring them is leaving free XP on the table.
The fix: Do the "Big XP" quests (Section 4.1) before you manually train those skills. One quest can save you 5–10 hours of grinding.

7.3 ❌ Mistake #3: Not Getting 43 Prayer Early Enough

The mistake: "I'll get Prayer later, it's too expensive."
Why it's bad: Protection prayers (at 43 Prayer) reduce damage to zero. Without them, you'll die constantly, waste food, waste time, and make less GP. The 850K GP investment pays for itself in one day of bossing.
The fix: Get 43 Prayer within your first week of membership. It's the single best GP investment you'll ever make.

7.4 ❌ Mistake #4: Training at Hill Giants in Members

The mistake: Continuing to train at F2P spots after buying membership.
Why it's bad: Members has vastly better training methods. Sand Crabs give 2–3× the XP of Hill Giants. Ammonite Crabs give even more. Don't waste membership time on F2P methods.
The fix: Move to Sand Crabs immediately after buying membership. Read our Combat Training Guide for the best spots.

7.5 ❌ Mistake #5: Not Using RuneLite

The mistake: Playing on the official client because "third-party clients are cheating."
Why it's bad: RuneLite is officially approved by Jagex. It's not cheating — it's QoL. The official client is missing basic features like XP tracking, quest helper, and GPU rendering.
The fix: Download RuneLite from runelite.net and never look back. Your quality of life will improve overnight.

7.6 ❌ Mistake #6: Burning Out by Overefficiency

The mistake: Following a "optimal efficiency" guide and grinding 8 hours/day at the same method.
Why it's bad: OSRS is a marathon. If you optimize the fun out of the game, you'll quit within a month. The "most efficient" path is often the least fun.
The fix: Mix it up. Do a quest, then some Slayer, then a boss attempt, then some skilling. Variety keeps the game fresh. For more on avoiding burnout, read our OSRS Burnout Guide.

7.7 ❌ Mistake #7: Not Having a Bank Tab System

The mistake: Keeping all items in the default bank tab.
Why it's bad: You'll waste hours searching for items. A disorganized bank slows down every activity.
The fix: Set up bank tabs (Section 6.1 in the reference article). Use placeholders. Organize by category (combat gear, skilling tools, quest items, etc.). It takes 30 minutes once, then saves you hundreds of hours over your OSRS career.

  • Enable "Tap to Drop": Settings → Gameplay → Enable "Tap to Drop". Makes dropping items one-tap instead of right-click.
  • Use Presets: Set up inventory presets for commonly used item sets (food + potions, skilling tools, etc.). Saves massive time.
  • Disable Chat: If you find the chat distracting, hide it. You don't need to see every "wtb" spam while training.
  • Play in Short Bursts: Mobile is great for 15–30 minute sessions (commuting, waiting in line, before bed). Don't try to do 3-hour grinds on mobile — it'll drain your battery and your patience.
  • Use "Zoom Out": Pinch to zoom out. Gives you a better view of the area and reduces misclicks.

8.3 🎮 Mobile vs PC: When to Switch

Some content is fine on mobile. Some content needs PC. Here's the breakdown:

  • Mobile-only: AFK skilling (Fishing, Cooking, Woodcutting), Farming runs, birdhouse runs, quest dialogues
  • PC-preferred: Agility courses, Slayer tasks (medium+ difficulty), bossing, PvP
  • PC-only: High-level PvM (Raids, ToB, ToA), tick-perfect content (Vorkath, Zulrah), speedrunning

The hybrid approach: Use mobile for AFK training during the day, then switch to PC for focused content in the evening. Many players maintain both — mobile for convenience, PC for performance.

💡 Mobile Data Usage

OSRS Mobile uses very little data (about 5–10 MB/hour). You can play on mobile data without worrying about your data cap. But for long sessions, WiFi is still recommended to save battery.

⑨ ❓ FAQ — OSRS Training Guide 2026

Q: What's the single most efficient thing a new F2P player can do?

A: Complete every F2P quest (there are only 20–30). The total XP rewards will jump you multiple combat levels and give you a solid foundation for membership. After that, get 40+ combat stats before buying membership.

Q: Should I train combat or do quests first as a new member?

A: Do quests first. Specifically, do Waterfall Quest, Fight Arena, and Tree Gnome Village before you manually train combat. These three quests alone will give you 40,000+ combat XP — that's 5–8 hours of combat training saved. Then go to Sand Crabs with your new 30+ combat stats and train to 60.

Q: What's the best AFK training method for a brand-new member?

A: Sand Crabs. They're level 15, have 60 HP, and are aggressive for 10 minutes at a time. You can literally walk away from your keyboard and come back to 30K–50K XP. They're accessible from day 1 of membership with zero requirements. Bring food and a weapon — that's it.

Q: How much GP should I have before buying membership?

A: At least 500K–1M GP. You'll need GP for gear upgrades, bones for Prayer, teleport runes, and general expenses. If you can't afford 500K GP in F2P, you're not ready for membership yet. Spend another week in F2P farming GP (see our F2P Money-Making Guide).

Q: Is it worth using bonds to stay a member for free?

A: Yes, if you can farm 5.2M GP every 14 days. That's about 370K GP/day — doable with efficient methods (Wintertodt, Slayer, bossing). But for your first membership, just pay the $11. It's not worth the stress of GP farming when you're still learning the game. Once you're established, switch to bonds if you want. Read our Bond Guide for the full strategy.

Q: What should I prioritize: combat stats or quest points?

A: Combat stats first (you need them to survive), then quests (they give XP and unlock content). But there's overlap — many quests give combat XP. The ideal route is: do the "Big XP" quests → train combat to 60 → do more quests → train combat to 70 → do more quests. It's not either/or — it's both/and.

Q: How do I know if I'm training "efficiently"?

A: Use the OSRS Wiki training guides. They have XP/hour tables for every skill at every level. If your XP/hour is within 20% of the wiki's "best method," you're training efficiently. Don't stress about being 100% optimal — "mostly efficient" is good enough for 99% of players.

Q: Should I follow a YouTube "efficient account builder" guide?

A: Maybe. These guides are great for understanding efficiency, but they're often miserable to actually play. They'll have you doing 10-hour grinds of the "most efficient" method, which is a one-way ticket to burnout city. Use efficiency guides for reference, but play the game in a way you enjoy. The "best" training method is the one you'll actually do.

Q: How long does it take to max an OSRS account?

A: 5,000–10,000 hours depending on efficiency. That's 2–5 years of consistent play. But "maxing" isn't the point of OSRS. Most players never max, and that's fine. Set smaller goals (70 combat, fire cape, 99 in one skill) and enjoy the journey. For a realistic timeline, check our Maxing Timeline Guide.
