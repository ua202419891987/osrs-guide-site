OSRS Mobile Menu Entry Swapper Not Working — Fix Guide 2026

One of the most upvoted OSRS mobile complaints in July 2026: Menu Entry Swapper (MES) stops working or behaves oddly after an update. This guide covers why it happens and exactly how to fix it on iOS and Android.

📱 Mobile Fix · 7 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Summary — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Symptom: MES options greyed out, swaps not applying, or settings reset after an update.
  • 📌 Common cause: client version mismatch, post-update settings reset, or Android 32-bit deprecation.
  • 📌 Fix: update the app, clear cache, re-enable MES in Settings → Gameplay, re-log.
  • 📌 Last resort: reinstall the app (your account is safe — it is server-side).

1. What Is Menu Entry Swapper on Mobile

Menu Entry Swapper (MES) lets you customize right-click / long-press menu options — for example, making "Pick-flax" the default instead of "Pick". On mobile it is even more valuable because screen taps are slower than mouse clicks. When MES breaks, everyday tasks like banking, pickpocketing, or bossing become frustrating.

💡 Where to Find It

Settings → Gameplay → Menu Entry Swapper. On mobile, you long-press an option on the action menu, then tap the pin/swap icon.

2. Why MES Stops Working

The July 2026 reports cluster around a few repeatable causes:

① Client Version Mismatch

Your app did not auto-update, so the MES data format from the server no longer matches your local client. Result: swaps silently fail.

② Post-Update Settings Reset

Some updates wipe or partially reset local preferences. Your carefully pinned swaps disappear and the toggle looks off even if it was on.

③ Android 32-bit Deprecation

Jagex dropped 32-bit Android support. Devices stuck on old 32-bit builds may launch an outdated client where MES behaves differently or not at all.

④ Known Mobile Client Bugs

Occasional mobile-only regressions ship with updates. If a specific swap is broken for everyone, it is likely a client bug Jagex will patch.

3. Step-by-Step Fix

✅ The Reliable Fix (do these in order)

  1. Update the app from the App Store / Google Play (or download the latest APK from the official OSRS site if your store is slow).
  2. Clear cache (Android: Settings → Apps → OSRS → Storage → Clear Cache. iOS: offload and reinstall the app).
  3. Re-enable MES: Settings → Gameplay → Menu Entry Swapper → toggle ON.
  4. Re-pin your swaps: long-press the menu option you want, tap the pin icon, confirm.
  5. Log out and back in so the server pushes fresh MES data.
⚠️ Still Broken After That?

If swaps still will not apply, it is probably a client-side bug Jagex is fixing. Wait for the next point release and re-check. Do not keep toggling — it will not help.

4. Android 32-bit Note

If you are on an older Android phone that only runs 32-bit apps, the OSRS mobile client may no longer receive updates. Symptoms include MES and other features acting strangely.

  • Check your device architecture (Settings → About → look for "64-bit" support).
  • If it is 32-bit only, the long-term fix is a 64-bit device. The client will keep degrading on 32-bit.
  • You can still play on the official desktop client or via a 64-bit emulator in the meantime.

6. FAQs

Will reinstalling the app delete my account?

No. Your account lives on Jagex servers. Reinstalling only clears the local app; just log back in.

My swaps disappeared after the update. Are they gone forever?

They are stored locally and can reset on update. Re-pin them following Section 3. They are not lost from your account — just the device.

Is MES a paid feature?

No, Menu Entry Swapper is free for all players. You do not need membership to use it.

When will Jagex fix the mobile bug?

Jagex ships mobile client point releases regularly. Check the official news page for the next mobile update notes.
