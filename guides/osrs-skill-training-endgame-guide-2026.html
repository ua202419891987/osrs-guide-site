1. The Endgame Skill Landscape

Reaching level 70 is a milestone — but 70→99 is where the real commitment begins. The total XP from 70 to 99 is 10,850,428 XP per skill — roughly 83% of the total 1→99 XP. The first 70 levels are the warm-up; the last 29 levels are the marathon.

The gap between a 40K/hour method and 80K/hour method is 135 hours — nearly a full month at 4 hours/day. Method selection matters more at endgame than anywhere else.

🎯 Three Phases of 70→99

Phase 1: Levels 70–80 (Transition)

Unlock your first truly efficient methods. Smithing shines at 70+ with Blast Furnace; Fishing unlocks Infernal Eels at 80. This phase takes ~12% of total 70→99 time. Use it to experiment and find your preferred method.

Phase 2: Levels 80–92 (The Long Middle — "Halfway")

At level 92 you reach exactly half the XP for 99. This is the psychological danger zone — methods are set, routine is established, progress feels glacial. This phase consumes 50% of your total XP. The antidote: train multiple skills in parallel rather than grinding one 99 at a time.

Phase 3: Levels 92–99 (The Final Push)

You can see the finish line. Motivation returns. Some skills have special endgame methods: Soul Runecrafting (90+) reaches peak efficiency at 92+, Prifddinas Agility (75+) is only available after Plague's End. This phase takes ~38% of total time because you're using absolute fastest methods with renewed focus.

Level 92 RC deserves special mention. At 90 you craft Soul Runes, but max efficiency (3.93 runes/essence) only at 92+. Many players rush RC to 92 first because Soul Runecrafting is fast XP AND generates ~3M GP/hour selling runes. It partially pays for itself — unique among buyable 99s.

2. Fastest 99 Methods Per Skill (Complete Rankings)

This is the core reference. Every skill is covered with fastest method, budget alternative, and AFK option. XP/hour numbers assume efficient play with optimal gear.

⚔️ Combat Skills (7)

Attack / Strength / Defence (70→99)

Fastest: Slayer with optimal weapon progression (Whip → Scim → Tentacle). Combined XP: ~50,000/hour per combat skill. Total time each: ~240h (Att/Str), ~310h (Def).

Isolated: Sand Crabs (Ammonite Crabs after Bone Voyage). ~45,000 XP/hour. Free.

Key: Never isolate combat skills unless building a pure. Always train through Slayer for GP + XP synergy.

Hitpoints (70→99)

Fastest: Passive through any combat. ~15,000 HP XP/hour at Crabs. Guthan's set at Ogress Warriors: ~25,000 XP/hour (set effect heals you).

Total time: ~385 hours of combat. Typically reached by level 85–90 combat through normal play.

Ranged (70→99)

Fastest: Chinning (Red Chinchompas) at natural rock formations. ~300,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~45M GP. Time: ~36h.

Budget: Broad bolts + Rune c'bows at Slayer. ~50,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~2M GP.

AFK: Cannon at Slayer tasks. ~40,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~200K GP/hour (cannonballs).

Magic (70→99)

Fastest: High Alchemy (nature runes). ~65,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~28M GP. Time: ~167h.

Profitable: Planking oak logs at Port Phasmatys (after Ghosts Ahoy). ~60,000 XP/hour. Actually makes GP if you sell planks.

Combat: Barraging Slayer (Dust Devils, Nechryaels). ~120,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~75M GP for runes.

Prayer (70→99)

Fastest: Wyrm bones at Gilded Altar (350% XP). ~200,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~28M GP. Time: ~54h.

Budget: Dragon bones at Gilded Altar. ~120,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~13M GP.

Free (Risky): Chaos Altar (Wilderness, 350% XP). ~180,000 XP/hour. Cost: Free. Risk: PKers.

🌲 Gathering Skills (6)

Mining (70→99)

Fastest (82+): Amethyst. ~60,000 XP/hour. Time: ~181h. GP from selling: ~15M GP (partially offsets).

Pre-82: Volcanic Mine. ~70,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~1M GP supplies/hour. Fun minigame format.

AFK: Motherlode Mine (upper, 72+). ~42,000 XP/hour. Very AFK. GP: ~500K/hour from gem rocks.

Fishing (70→99)

Fastest (82+): Minnows. ~68,000 XP/hour. Raw fish can be cooked (synergy with Cooking). Time: ~160h.

Alt Fast (80+): Infernal Eels. ~65,000 XP/hour. Also grants FM XP.

AFK (82+): Anglerfish. ~45,000 XP/hour. Very AFK. Profit: ~50M GP over 99 (~2,500 GP/fish).

Woodcutting (70→99)

Fastest (90+): Redwood trees. ~80,000 XP/hour. Time: ~136h. GP: ~40M GP from logs.

Pre-90 (75+): Magic trees. ~50,000 XP/hour. Profit: ~400 GP/log.

AFK (60+): Yew trees. ~35,000 XP/hour. Profit: ~200K GP/hour.

Firemaking (70→99)

Fastest: Wintertodt (manual). ~120,000 XP/hour. Time: ~90h. Also gives reward crates (GP, seeds, loot).

Alternative: Burning Maples/Magics in sets. ~90,000 XP/hour. Cost: ~5M GP.

Farming (70→99)

Fastest: Palm trees + Calquat + Fruit trees (tree runs). ~50,000–80,000 XP per run (10 min). 2 runs/day = ~120,000 XP/hour effective. Time: ~90h active (spread over weeks).

Cost: ~15M GP for seeds. Harvesting produce offsets 50–70% of cost.

Pro tip: Do Farming last in a skill rotation. XP is passive (plant→wait→harvest) and fits between active grinds.

Hunter (70→99)

Fastest (77+): Maniacal Monkeys (after Monkey Madness II). ~150,000 XP/hour. Time: ~72h. Also gives Construction XP (bury bones at Gilded Altar).

Profitable (73+): Red Chinchompas. ~100,000 XP/hour. Profit: ~100M GP over 99 (~1,200 GP/chin).

AFK: Bird house runs (Fossil Island). ~30,000 XP/hour effective.

Fastest: Block low-XP tasks and prefer high-XP tasks (Gargoyles, Nechryaels, Abyssal Demons). With optimal blocking: ~60,000 Slayer XP/hour.

Synergy: Slayer is the foundation of OSRS economy. A high-level task generates 1–3M GP/hour while training 3 combat skills simultaneously. Never isolate Slayer from your 99 plans.

💡 The "Big 4" Expense Problem

Smithing, Herblore, Construction, and Fletching combined cost ~635M GP to buy to 99. Most players never have this much GP at once. Solution: train profitable skills first, then use the GP to fund expensive ones. Optimal order: Runecrafting → Hunter → Fishing → use GP for Construction → Herblore → Smithing → Fletching.

🎯 Funding Strategy: The Synergy Map

Synergy 1: Slayer → All Buyables

Slayer is the engine of OSRS economy. High-level tasks (Gargoyles, Vorkath, Zulrah) generate 1–3M GP/hour. Over the course of 99 Slayer, you'll accumulate 200–500M GP — enough to buy multiple 99s. Never separate Slayer from your buyable 99 plan.

Synergy 2: Runecrafting (Soul) → Herblore + Construction

Get 90+ RC early. Soul runes sell for ~500 GP each. At 170K XP/hour, you generate ~3M GP/hour profit. In 40 hours of RC, you'll make enough to fully fund 99 Herblore. This is why speedrunners rush RC to 90 within their first 500 total level.

Synergy 3: Hunter (Chins) → Ranged + Fletching

Red Chinchompas sell for ~1,200 GP each. At 100,000 Hunter XP/hour, you catch ~85 chins/hour = ~100K GP/hour. But the real play: use the chins to train Ranged (chinning), then sell remaining chins to fund Fletching. One skill funds two 99s.

Synergy 4: Bossing → All Buyables

With 70+ combat stats, do Vorkath (3–5M GP/hour), Zulrah (2–4M GP/hour), or God Wars (1–3M GP/hour). These bosses drop herbs, ores, seeds, and rares that directly reduce buyable 99 costs. Consistent 2M GP/hour bossing adds up fast: 300 hours = 600M GP = every buyable 99.

4. Recommended 99 Order — Tier System

The order in which you achieve 99s matters. A well-planned order reduces total time, minimizes GP requirements, and keeps motivation high. The tier system below is based on efficiency, account progression, and psychological momentum.

🥇 Tier 1: Foundation 99s (Do These First)

1. Slayer (to 93+ or 99)

Why first: Slayer is the engine that drives your entire account. It gives Combat XP, GP, herbs (Herblore), seeds (Farming), and chins (Ranged/Hunter). By the time you finish Slayer, you'll already have 3–5 other 99s partially complete.

2. Runecrafting (to 90+)

Why second: 90+ RC pays for itself and generates GP for other 99s. XP is among the fastest in the game (170K/hour at Soul altar). Many players rush RC to 90 immediately after hitting 70 in early-game skills.

3. Hunter (to 99)

Why third: Red Chinchompas fund your Ranged 99. Maniacal Monkeys give Construction XP (bury bones at Gilded Altar). Herbiboar gives herbs. Hunter is a "giver" skill — it funds three other 99s.

4. Farming (to 99)

Why fourth: Herbs from Farming directly reduce Herblore costs. Tree runs give Woodcutting/Farming XP. Farming is the most time-efficient skill (plant, wait, harvest). Do tree runs between active grinds of other skills.

5. Magic (to 99)

Why fifth: High Alchemy is fast and generates some GP. 99 Magic unlocks Ancient Magicks (PvM) and Vengeance (PvP). It also completes "all combat to 99" for the Combat Achievements cape.

🥈 Tier 2: High-Value 99s (Medium Priority)

Ranged (99)

By now you have Red Chins from Hunter and GP from Slayer/RC. Chinning to 99 Ranged takes ~36 hours, costs ~45M GP. Unlocks Twisted Bow progression and Rigour prayer (if 74 Prayer).

Prayer (99)

With GP from Slayer/bossing, buy Wyrm bones for Gilded Altar training. ~54 hours. Cost: ~28M GP. Unlocks Piety, Rigour, and Augury — the three prayer upgrades that define high-level PvM.

Cooking (99)

Fast (8 hours) but expensive. If you caught your own Anglerfish from Fishing, cost drops to ~20M GP. 99 Cooking gives Hosidius Kitchen (100% success) and Cooking Cape (teleport).

Fishing + Cooking (Together)

Train with synergy. Minnows → raw Anglerfish → cook at Hosidius. ~160 hours. Free (or profitable if you sell fish).

Agility (99)

Pure time investment (160 hours). But 99 Agility gives Agility Cape (full run energy restore) and Graceful set (70% run energy bonus). Essential for efficient skilling and PvM.

🥉 Tier 3: Expensive/Low-Priority 99s (Save for Last)

Construction (99)

Most expensive and most hated skill. 99 Construction gives Construction Cape (POH teleport) and completes Max Cape requirements. But training is clunky, expensive (~120M GP), and boring. Most players do it last in a single weekend binge.

Herblore (99)

Extremely expensive (180M GP) but unlocks Overloads (PvM) and Brews (survival). If you farmed your own herbs, cost drops to ~80M GP. Prioritize if serious about high-level PvM; otherwise, do alongside Construction as a "buy both at once" project.

Smithing (99)

Single most expensive 99 (230M GP for gold bars at Blast Furnace). Only do if you have a massive bank or are going for Max Cape. Alternative: train through Cannonballs (profitable but 500+ hours). Not recommended unless you love the grind.

Fletching (99)

Fast (27 hours) but expensive (105M GP). Unlocks Dragon bolts/arrows (high-end Ranged ammo). If you plan to use Blowpipe or Twisted Bow extensively, 99 Fletching is valuable. Otherwise, it's a "for the cape" 99.

5. Advanced Tick-Manipulation Methods

Tick manipulation ("tick-perfect" training) uses game tick timing to increase XP rates beyond normal limits. Some methods add 10–30% more XP/hour; others add 100%+ but require extreme APM. This section covers what's worth learning and what isn't.

⏱️ What Is a Game Tick?

OSRS runs on a 0.6-second game tick. Every action takes a whole number of ticks. By understanding exactly when a tick processes, you can queue actions to start on the very next tick — eliminating idle time and maximizing actions per hour.

🎯 Entry-Level 3-Tick Methods (Worth Learning)

3-Tick Mining (Copper/Tin/Iron)

How: Drop ore manually every 3 ticks (1.8s) and immediately click a rock. Normally mining takes 4 ticks/ore; with 3-tick manipulation, you mine 33% faster.

XP: ~55,000 XP/hour (vs ~42,000 normal).

Verdict: Worth it for Ironman accounts. For main accounts, just buy the ore — your time is worth more than the GP saved.

3-Tick Woodcutting (Normal→Oak→Willow)

How: Drop log and immediately click tree. 33% faster XP.

XP: ~45,000 XP/hour (vs ~35,000 on Willows).

Verdict: Only for Ironman accounts going for early Construction XP. For mains, buy the logs.

🔥 Advanced 1-Tick & 2-Tick Methods (High APM)

1-Tick Fletching (Dragon Darts)

How: Click Make-X interface at the exact moment a tick processes. Properly executed: 1,500 darts (2.7M XP) in 12 minutes.

XP: ~400,000 XP/hour (fastest in game).

Verdict: Absolutely worth it. Fletching is the most expensive skill — any XP/hour boost directly saves GP. Even imperfect 1-tick fletching saves 10–20% on total cost.

2-Tick Cooking (Wine)

How: Click grape and jug of water on the same tick. Wine ferments instantly (0 ticks) instead of taking 12 ticks.

XP: ~480,000 XP/hour (vs ~200,000 normal).

Verdict: Worth it if buying to 99 Cooking. Wine method is also profitable (~50K GP/hour) — only profitable buyable training method in game.

⚠️ Tick Manipulation and the "Efficiency" Trap

Tick manipulation can add 10–30% more XP/hour, but it also increases mental fatigue and RSI risk. If it makes the game feel like a second job, it's not worth the gain. Many top players use "sub-optimal" methods because they're more relaxed over hundreds of hours. The "efficient" path is the one you can actually stick to. For most players, learning 1-tick Fletching and 2-tick Cooking is sufficient — skip the rest.

6. Managing Burnout on Long Grinds

The difference between players who achieve Max Cape and those who quit at 1,500 total is rarely skill or GP — it's burnout management. This section covers practical strategies to stay motivated through hundreds of hours of grinding.

📅 Set Milestone Goals (Not Just "99")

The gap between 70 and 99 feels insurmountable when viewed as a whole. Break it into milestones:

  • Every 5 levels: Take a 10-minute break. Stretch. Get water. mentally reset.
  • Level 85: Halfway in XP (not levels). Celebrate.
  • Level 92: Exactly half the XP for 99. Screen capture your XP bar and share it.
  • Level 96: Only 500K XP to 99 (less than 1%). Sprint finish.

🎮 Train Multiple Skills in Parallel

Grinding one skill to 99 at a time is the fastest way to burn out. Instead:

The "Rotation Method"

Train 3–4 skills in a rotating schedule. Example: 2 hours Slayer → 1 hour Farming runs → 1 hour Agility → 2 hours Slayer. Each skill feels fresh when you return to it. This is how most Max Capes are achieved — not by grinding one 99 at a time, but by slowly progressing multiple 99s simultaneously.

📺 Passive Training While Consuming Content

Some skills are compatible with watching videos, streams, or podcasts:

🏆 Track Progress Visually

Use WiseOldMan (wiseoldman.net) or CrystalMathLabs to track your XP gains over time. Seeing a visual graph of your progress is surprisingly motivating. Set weekly XP targets (e.g., "500K XP this week across all skills") and try to beat them.

🤝 Join a Clan

Grinding is more fun with others. Join an OSRS clan (via Clan Chat or Discord). Many clans have "XP goals" competitions with small prizes. Even just having someone to chat with while training Agility makes the hours fly by.

🔄 Take Planned Breaks (Not Unplanned Burnout)

Better to take a 1-week break by choice than to quit for 6 months from burnout. If you feel your motivation dropping:

  1. Switch to a different activity (PvM instead of skilling, or questing instead of training)
  2. Set a "vacation timer" (e.g., "I'll take 3 days off, then come back fresh")
  3. Watch OSRS content creators (Settled, Gnomonkey, etc.) to re-ignite your interest

8. Endgame Training FAQs

What's the absolute fastest way to get my first 99?

Skill-dependent, but generally: Cooking (Anglerfish) takes 8 hours and ~72M GP. Fletching (Dragon darts) takes 27 hours and ~105M GP. Smithing (Blast Furnace gold) takes 36 hours and ~230M GP. If you have the GP, Cooking is the fastest. If you want to make GP while training, Runecrafting (Soul) takes 64 hours and is free/profitable.

Should I buy my way to 99 or earn the GP first?

Buy if you have the GP and value time over GP. Earn if you enjoy the money-making process or need the GP for other 99s. Most players do a hybrid: earn GP through Slayer/bossing, then buy the cheaper 99s (Prayer, Cooking) while saving for the expensive ones (Construction, Herblore, Smithing).

Is 99 Slayer required for Max Cape?

No. Max Cape requires 99 in all skills, not all activities. Slayer is a skill, so yes — you need 99 Slayer for Max Cape. However, you don't need to do "efficient" Slayer. Casual Slayer (picking your own tasks) is fine if you enjoy it more.

What's the best use of my first 100M GP?

Priority order: (1) Prayer 99 (Wyrm bones, ~28M GP) — unlocks Piety/Rigour/Augury for all PvM. (2) Ranged 99 (Red chins, ~45M GP) — massively increases your DPS and GP/hour at bosses. (3) Cooking 99 (Anglerfish, ~72M GP) — useful for PvM sustainability. (4) Save the rest for Herblore + Construction (the two most expensive 99s).

How many hours a day should I play to max efficiently?

There's no "correct" amount. Efficient players often play 4–6 hours/day and max in ~1.5 years. Casual players playing 1–2 hours/day max in ~3–4 years. The key is consistency, not intensity. Playing 1 hour every day for a year (365 hours) accomplishes more than playing 7 hours once a week (364 hours) because you maintain momentum and skill familiarity.

Should I get 99 in one skill before starting another?

Generally no. The "rotation method" (Section 6) is more sustainable and keeps motivation higher. However, for very fast 99s (Cooking: 8h, Fletching: 27h), it's fine to finish them in a single session. For long 99s (Agility: 160h, Mining: 181h), always rotate with other skills.
