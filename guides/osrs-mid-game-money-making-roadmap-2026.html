🎯 Why Mid-Game: 300K-1.5M GP/hr Sweet Spot

The "mid-game" in OSRS is widely considered the most overlooked profit window in the entire game. While new players scramble for 100k GP/hr and endgame players chase 10M+ GP/hr boss kills, mid-game players (combat 60-100, total level 800-1300) occupy a sweet spot where multiple high-profit methods converge — many of which require surprisingly low stats.

The "forgotten middle" exists because most guides either target brand-new accounts (f2p methods, cowhides, elemental runes) or maxed mains (Vorkath, Chambers of Xeric, Nightmare). But the reality is that some mid-game methods rival endgame profits while demanding a fraction of the stats and gear investment.

What makes mid-game especially powerful is diversification. At this stage you can combine combat money making, skilling for profit, daily passive income, and light flipping into a portfolio that generates consistent GP whether you have 30 minutes or 3 hours to play. This guide breaks down every viable method and ranks them so you can choose what fits your account and playstyle.

Key Insight: The mid-game is where you should stop grinding for GP and start investing in GP-generating infrastructure — gear that unlocks higher GP/hr methods, quest unlocks that open new money makers, and stats that compound your hourly rate.

⚔️ Combat 60-80: 400K-1.5M GP/hr

With combat stats in the 60-80 range, you have access to several reliable money-making methods that require minimal risk and offer solid returns. These methods are perfect for building your first 10-30M GP bank.

1️⃣ Green Dragons (400K-700K/hr)

Green Dragons remain one of the most accessible mid-game money makers in OSRS. Located in the Wilderness (level 13-18), they drop Dragon bones (≈4.8k GP each) and Green dragonhide (≈2.1k GP each), with 2 AMLR herbs as a common drop.

  • Requirements: 70 Combat (recommended), 43 Prayer for Protect from Magic, anti-dragon shield
  • GP/hr: 400k - 700k (solo, depending on luck and kills/hr)
  • Effort Level: Medium — requires attention for prayer flicking and PK awareness
  • Risk: Medium — Wilderness location means PK risk. Bring 3-4 combat potions, food, and minimal gear (≈200k risk)
  • Gear Setup: Rune or Dragon armor, Dragon Scimitar, Amulet of Glory, Obsidian cape

2️⃣ Blue Dragons (350K-600K/hr)

Blue Dragons in Taverley Dungeon offer a safer alternative to Greens. The drop table is similar but Blue dragonhide sells for ≈2.4k GP each. With 74 Mage you can use Bones to Peaches for sustainable kills.

  • Requirements: 65 Combat, 74 Magic (optional for B2P), dusty key or 70 Agility for shortcut
  • GP/hr: 350k - 600k
  • Effort Level: Medium — need to bank for hides/bones
  • Risk: None — safe dungeon

3️⃣ Barrows (500K-1.2M/hr)

Barrows is the first "real" bossing method accessible to mid-game accounts. Each brother killed gives a 1/450 chance at a unique reward (worth 70k to 48M GP). Even without uniques, the runes, bolt racks, and half-key drops generate steady income.

  • Requirements: 70 Combat, 43 Prayer, Iban's Blast or Trident, 60+ Defense recommended
  • GP/hr: 500k - 1.2M (heavily dependent on unique drops)
  • Effort Level: High — each run takes 4-7 minutes
  • Risk: Low — safe minigame, but death costs runes and potential loot
  • Gear: Verac's set (≈2M total), Dragon Scimitar, Amulet of Fury or Glory

4️⃣ Scurrius (600K-1M/hr)

Scurrius is a newer mid-game boss located in the Varlamore region (requires Children of the Sun quest). It's designed as an introductory boss with a simple mechanic and very accessible requirements.

  • Requirements: 75 Combat recommended, 50 Prayer, melee gear
  • GP/hr: 600k - 1M
  • Effort Level: Medium — learn the attack patterns
  • Risk: Low — safe instance available
  • Notable Drops: Scurrius' spine (≈15k GP), various seeds, rune items

5️⃣ Hill Giants / Moss Giants (200K-400K/hr)

Less glamorous but reliable — Giant keys (from Hill Giants in Edgeville Dungeon) open chests that drop Big bones and various herbs. Moss Giants in the Crandor Dungeon drop Limpwurt roots (≈1.1k GP) and herbs consistently.

  • GP/hr: 200k - 400k
  • Effort Level: Low — very AFK

💰 Combat 80-100: 800K-2.5M GP/hr

Once your combat stats reach the 80+ range, a whole new tier of money-making methods opens up. These methods offer significantly higher GP/hr but require better gear and more mechanical knowledge.

6️⃣ Demonic Gorillas (800K-1.5M/hr)

Demonic Gorillas in the Crash Site Cavern (requires Monkey Madness II) are widely considered the best mid-game money maker in OSRS. They drop Zenyte shards (≈11.5M GP), Ballista pieces, and Dragon scimitar offhand, along with consistent herb and seed drops.

  • Requirements: 85 Combat recommended, 70 Prayer, 75+ Ranged or Mage, Monkey Madness II completed
  • GP/hr: 800k - 1.5M (without Zenyte); 3M+ with Zenyte drops
  • Effort Level: High — requires prayer switching and movement
  • Risk: Low — safe cave, but expensive deaths
  • Gear: Bandos or Dragon gear, Armadyl crossbow or Trident, Prayer potions

7️⃣ Zulrah Entry (1M-2.5M/hr)

Zulrah is the first "real" high-end boss many mid-game players attempt. With 75 Prayer, 75 Ranged, 75 Magic, and 70+ Defense, you can learn Zulrah and start earning 1M+ GP/hr. The learning curve is steep but the rewards are exceptional.

  • Requirements: 75 Ranged, 75 Magic, 75 Hitpoints, 70 Defense, 43 Prayer (75+ recommended), Regicide quest
  • GP/hr: 1M - 2.5M (once proficient, 5-6 kills per hour)
  • Effort Level: Very High — unforgiving mechanics
  • Risk: Medium — death loses expensive gear
  • Gear Cost: 15M - 30M for a basic setup (Trident, Armadyl, Barrows gloves, etc.)
  • Notable Drops: Tanzanite fang (≈9.1M), Magic fang (≈3.5M), Serpentine visage (≈3.5M), Uncut onyx (≈1.2M), various seeds and herbs

8️⃣ Vorkath Entry (1.5M-2.5M/hr)

Vorkath is accessible after Dragon Slayer II and offers some of the most consistent high-tier profits in the game. Entry-level Vorkath can be done with a crossbow setup (cheaper than Zulrah) and earns 1.5M - 2.5M GP/hr.

  • Requirements: Dragon Slayer II completed, 75 Ranged, 70 Defense, 43 Prayer (75+ recommended)
  • GP/hr: 1.5M - 2.5M (2-3 kills per hour at entry level)
  • Effort Level: High — must learn acid phase and prayer switching
  • Gear: Armadyl crossbow, Dragon bolts (rune or diamond), Barrows armor, Dragon defender
  • Notable Drops: Vorkath's head (≈11M), Dragonbone necklace (≈100k), Superior dragon bones, Wrath runes

9️⃣ Lizardman Shamans (500K-1M/hr)

Lizardman Shamans in the Lizardman Settlement (requires House on the Hill quest) drop Lizardman fangs (≈2.8k GP each) and the Dragon warhammer (≈45M GP). Even without the warhammer, the consistent fang drops make this a solid 500k-800k GP/hr method.

  • Requirements: 80 Combat, 65 Prayer, House on the Hill completed
  • GP/hr: 500k - 1M (without Dragon warhammer)
  • Effort Level: Medium — watch for area-of-effect attacks
  • Risk: Low-Medium

🔟 Slayer Money Makers (300K-700K/hr)

Many mid-game Slayer tasks are profitable on their own. Key high-value tasks include:

  • Abyssal Demons (85 Slayer): Abyssal whip (≈1.2M) and Abyssal dagger (≈1.5M) drops. 400k-700k GP/hr.
  • Gargoyles (75 Slayer): Granite maul (≈45k) and consistent alchables. 300k-500k GP/hr.
  • Nechryaels (80 Slayer): Death runes and herbs. 400k-600k GP/hr.
  • Greater Demons (75 Slayer): Summoning scrolls and herbs. 300k-500k GP/hr.

📋 Skilling: 200K-1.2M GP/hr

Not everyone enjoys combat. The good news is that several skilling methods are highly profitable at mid-game levels and require minimal stats. These methods are often more relaxing and can be done while doing other things.

🏭 Blast Furnace (400K-1.2M/hr)

Blast Furnace is the fastest Smithing XP method and also one of the most profitable skilling methods in the game. At 60 Smithing you can smelt Steel bars (≈550 GP profit per bar), at 70 you can smelt Mithril bars (≈400 GP profit), and at 85 you can smelt Adamant bars (≈1.2k GP profit).

  • Requirements: 30 Smithing (60+ recommended for profit), 42 Mining (to use coal bag efficiently)
  • GP/hr: 400k - 1.2M (depending on bar type and Smithing level)
  • Effort Level: Medium-High — must keep furnace fueled and bars collected
  • Notes: Join the Blast Furnace world (358). Use a coal bag (from Digsite Quest) to speed up trips. Gold bars (after Family Crest) give 300k-400k GP/hr with goldsmith gauntlets.

🧵 Tan Leather (300K-800K/hr)

Using the Tan Leather spell (Magic 78) or the portable tanner in Al Kharid, you can tan Dragonhide and other hides for profit. Green dragonhide bodies and Blue dragonhide bodies are especially profitable.

  • Requirements: 78 Magic for Tan Leather spell, or use Al Kharid tanner (Crafting 1+)
  • GP/hr: 300k - 800k (depending on hide type and market prices)
  • Effort Level: Medium — requires buying hides, tanning, and selling bodies
  • Notes: Tan Leather spell requires 2 Nature runes and 1 Astral rune per cast. Buy hides on GE, tan them, craft into bodies (Crafting 63+ for Green, 71+ for Blue), sell for profit.

🧪 Making Unfinished Potions (200K-500K/hr)

Unfinished potions are a consistent low-risk money maker. Buy herbs and vials of water on the GE, use the herb on the vial to create unfinished potions, and sell them. The margin is typically 200-800 GP per potion.

  • Requirements: None (1 Herblore)
  • GP/hr: 200k - 500k (depending on which unf potions are in demand)
  • Effort Level: Low — very click-intensive but simple
  • Best Options: Toadflax unf (≈1.2k buy, ≈1.8k sell), Avantoe unf, Irit unf. Check GE margins daily as they fluctuate.

🌱 Herb Runs (80K-400K/run)

Herb runs are the single most profitable Farming activity and require very low stats. At 32 Farming you can plant ranarr seeds (≈28k GP each), at 65 you can plant snapdragon seeds (≈55k GP each), and at 79 you can plant torstol seeds (≈85k GP each).

  • Requirements: 32 Farming (Ranarr), 65 (Snapdragon), 79 (Torstol), 50+ recommended for ultracompost
  • Profit per Run: 80k - 400k (depending on herb and patches used)
  • Effort Level: Very Low — takes 5-10 minutes per run, harvest after 80-90 minutes
  • Notes: Use ultracompost and pay the farmer to protect your crops. 7-9 patches available depending on quest progress. Do runs 2-3 times per day for 200k-1M+ daily passive income.

🍷 Wine Making (100K-250K/hr)

Making unfermented wine (use Grapes on a Jug of water) and waiting for it to ferment yields 200-400 GP profit per wine. It's slow but requires almost zero attention.

  • GP/hr: 100k - 250k
  • Effort Level: Very Low

🔄 Flipping: 200K-1M GP/day

Flipping items on the Grand Exchange is one of the few money-making methods that scales with your available capital rather than your stats. With a 5M-20M GP bank, you can start learning the fundamentals of flipping and earn 200k-1M+ GP per day with just a few minutes of active play.

🔄 What Is Flipping?

Flipping is the practice of buying an item at a low price (instant buy or low offer) and selling it at a higher price (instant sell or high offer). The difference between your buy and sell price is your profit per item. Flip 100 items with a 1k GP margin and you've made 100k GP.

🔄 Flipping Strategy for Mid-Game

  • Start with runes: Nature runes and Law runes have high volume and small but consistent margins. You can flip 10k Nature runes in a day with 5M capital for 50k-100k GP profit.
  • Diversify across 3-5 items: Don't put all your capital into one item. Spread it across runes, ores, and herbs to reduce risk.
  • Use RuneLite's GE Tracker plugin: This shows you the current buy/sell prices and margin for every item. It's essential for efficient flipping.
  • Flip in cycles: Check your offers every 1-2 hours. Re-buy when your sell offers complete. The key to high GP/hr is velocity — the more times your capital cycles, the more you earn.
  • Avoid "merching" (pump and dump): Don't try to manipulate prices or hold items hoping they go up. Stick to items with natural high volume.

📈 Expected Profits by Capital

  • 5M GP capital: 100k - 300k GP/day (2-6% daily return)
  • 10M GP capital: 200k - 600k GP/day (2-6% daily return)
  • 20M GP capital: 400k - 1.2M GP/day (2-6% daily return)

Note: Flipping profits are highly variable and depend on market conditions, competition, and your skill. These are realistic targets for a dedicated flipper.

💰 Passive Income: 660K-1.89M/day

One of the most powerful wealth-building strategies in OSRS is stacking multiple passive income sources. None of these individually will make you rich overnight, but combined they can generate 500k - 2M+ GP per day with minimal active play time. This is the foundation of "passive wealth" that compounds over time.

🌿 Herb Runs (2-3 per day)

As covered in Section 4, herb runs are the backbone of passive income. With 7-9 patches and high-value seeds, each run earns 150k-400k GP.

  • Frequency: 2-3 times per day (every 80-90 minutes after planting)
  • Daily Profit: 300k - 1M GP
  • Time Investment: 15-20 minutes total per day

🏠 Birdhouse Runs (Hunter 5+)

Birdhouse runs on Fossil Island are a Hunter training method that also generates consistent GP. You place 4 birdhouses (requiring clockwork mechanisms and logs), wait 50 minutes, and collect the nests which contain herbs, seeds, and rings.

  • Requirements: 5 Hunter (for regular bird houses), 15 Construction, Big Chompy Bird Hunting and The Dig Site quests
  • Profit per Run: 40k - 120k GP (depending on Hunter level and house type)
  • Daily Profit: 80k - 360k GP (2-3 runs per day)
  • Time Investment: 5 minutes per run

🏰 Managing Miscellania (Kingdom)

After completing The Fremennik Trials, you can manage your own kingdom on Miscellania. By investing 75k GP per day and allocating workers correctly, you can earn 150k-400k GP per day in resources (mahogany logs, herbs, flax, etc.).

  • Requirements: The Fremennik Trials completed, 75k GP/day to invest
  • Daily Profit: 150k - 400k GP (after subtracting the 75k investment)
  • Optimal Allocation: 10 on Herbs, 5 on Flax (for banknotes) — this maximizes GP return. Alternatively, 15 on Mahogany logs for Construction training with profit.
  • Time Investment: 2 minutes per day to collect and re-invest

📊 Battlestaves (130K/day)

After The Hand in the Sand quest, you can buy 100 Battlestaves per day from the Wizard's Guild for ≈7.2k GP each and sell them on the GE for ≈8.5k GP each. This is a guaranteed 130k GP per day profit with zero risk.

  • Requirements: The Hand in the Sand completed, 54 Crafting, 66 Magic
  • Daily Profit: ≈130k GP (100 staves × ≈1.3k margin)
  • Time Investment: 2 minutes per day

⚔️ Gear Priority: Highest ROI First

As you accumulate GP through the methods in this guide, you'll face a critical question: what should I buy first? The answer depends on your goals, but the principle is always the same — buy gear that unlocks higher GP/hr methods (highest ROI first).

📊 The ROI Hierarchy

  1. Quest-unlocking gear: Items that let you complete quests which unlock new money makers (e.g., Iban's Blast for Barrows, Dragon Slayer II requirements for Vorkath)
  2. Boss-unlocking gear: Gear that lets you attempt your first high-tier boss (e.g., Trident for Zulrah, Armadyl crossbow for Vorkath)
  3. Efficiency upgrades: Gear that increases your kills/hr or reduces your supply costs at existing methods (e.g., Barrows gloves, Amulet of Fury, Dragon boots)
  4. Prestige/Speed upgrades: Gear that saves time but has lower ROI (e.g., upgrading from Dragon to Barrows armor, buying a Twisted bow)

⚠️ What NOT to Buy (Mid-Game Traps)

  • Third-age gear: Purely cosmetic, zero ROI for money making.
  • Twisted Bow (too early): At 1.2B GP, this is an endgame purchase. Don't buy it until you have 100M+ bank and 90+ stats.
  • Full Dragon armor: Dragon platebody is 100k GP and only slightly better than Rune. Skip it and save for Barrows.
  • Skillcapes (too early): Don't buy 99 skillcapes until you actually have 99 in that skill. The stats aren't worth the 99k GP at mid-game.

❓ FAQ + Endgame Transition

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

❓ Q: Fastest way to 10M GP at combat 75?

A: Barrows. With 75 Combat and 43+ Prayer, you can do Barrows runs in 5-7 minutes each. Even without uniques, the consistent rune and key drop value adds up. Expect 500k-800k GP/hr reliably, with unique drops pushing that much higher. Combine with herb runs for passive income.

❓ Q: Combat or skilling for mid-game?

A: Both. Combat methods typically offer higher GP/hr but require more attention and gear. Skilling methods (Blast Furnace, herb runs) are more relaxing and passive. The optimal approach is to do combat money making when you're focused, and passive skilling/daily runs when you're tired or busy.

❓ Q: How much GP before endgame?

A: You should have at least 30M-50M GP banked before attempting endgame bossing (Vorkath, Zulrah at high efficiency, Chambers of Xeric). This covers gear, supplies, and deaths. Trying to rush endgame with 5M GP usually results in dying, losing your gear, and quitting.

❓ Q: Is flipping worth it mid-game?

A: Flipping is absolutely worth learning at mid-game. It teaches you the GE economy, generates passive income with minimal play time, and the skills transfer to endgame merching (where margins are larger). Even 30 minutes of flipping per day can add 200k-500k GP to your income.

❓ Q: Best use of first 5M GP?

A: Priority order: (1) Complete all quest requirements for Recipe for Disaster (unlocks Barrows gloves — free but takes time), (2) Buy Amulet of Fury (≈2.5M), (3) Buy Dragon Boots and Dragon Defender, (4) Save the rest for boss gear (Trident or Armadyl crossbow).

Final Tip: The mid-game is the best time to enjoy OSRS. You have access to diverse content, the profit rates are meaningful but not stressful, and you're constantly unlocking new areas and bosses. Don't rush to endgame — savor the journey, and the GP will follow.
