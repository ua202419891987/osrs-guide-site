1.3 🗺️ How to Get to Pyramid Plunder

📍 Getting to Sophanem

  1. Complete Icthlarin's Little Helper — This quest gives you access to Sophanem. Complete it before attempting to enter the Pyramid Plunder minigame.
  2. Travel to Sophanem — Use the Carpet Ride from the Shantay Pass (south of Al Kharid) to Menaphos, then walk east to Sophanem. Alternatively, use the Pharaoh's Sceptre teleport (if you have one) for instant access.
  3. Enter the Pyramid — Once in Sophanem, head to the large pyramid in the center of the city. Talk to the High Priest to enter. You'll be prompted to start a run.
  4. Start the Minigame — Click the "Enter the pyramid" option. You'll be transported inside with no gear. Your inventory is temporarily stored outside.

Travel tip: If you plan to do long sessions, bring multiple Sophanem teleport scrolls (available from the Grand Exchange for ~2K GP each). They teleport you directly to Sophanem, saving the carpet ride time.

② 🏛️ How Pyramid Plunder Works (Minigame Mechanics)

Pyramid Plunder is a timed thieving minigame set inside a massive pyramid in Sophanem. Your goal is to navigate through a series of rooms, loot as many urns, chests, and doors as possible, and reach the final room before time runs out. Here is a full breakdown of how the minigame works.

2.1 ⏱️ Timer & Room Structure

  • Time limit: Each run gives you a fixed amount of time based on your Thieving level. Higher levels = more time. At level 41 you get ~5 minutes; at 91+ you get ~10 minutes.
  • Room progression: The pyramid has 9 rooms (Room 1 through Room 9). You start in Room 1 and progress to higher rooms as you open doors. Each room contains urns, a gold chest, doors, and spear traps.
  • Floor mechanic: You start on Floor 1 (Room 1). Each room you advance through moves you to a higher floor. The deeper you go, the better the XP and loot.
  • Exit: You can leave the pyramid at any time by clicking the exit. If you run out of time, you are automatically ejected.

2.3 🌀 Room Flow — How a Run Looks

  1. Start in Room 1 — You appear with an empty inventory. Survey the room for urns, the gold chest, and the door to the next room.
  2. Loot everything — Search all urns in the room, open the gold chest, then picklock the door to advance.
  3. Avoid spear traps — Walk carefully around the floor tiles that trigger spear traps (they glow slightly).
  4. Repeat for each room — Rooms 2 through 9 follow the same pattern but with higher requirements and better rewards.
  5. Time management — Don't loot everything in early rooms if you're after the deepest rooms. Skip some urns to conserve time and push deeper.
💡 Key Insight — The Strategy Shift at Level 71

Below level 71, you can only access Rooms 1-5. At level 71, you unlock Rooms 6+ and the minigame becomes much more viable for XP. Level 91 is the sweet spot — you unlock all 9 rooms and can consistently hit 270K-320K XP/hr. The deeper rooms give dramatically better XP per urn/gold chest, so pushing deep is always the priority once you have the level.

③ 🚪 Room-by-Room Strategy Walkthrough

Each room in Pyramid Plunder has different Thieving requirements, different XP rates, and different loot tables. Here is a complete breakdown of each room and the optimal strategy for each.

① Room 1 (Floor 1) — Entrance Chamber

  • Thieving requirement: 41 (minimum entry)
  • Urns: 2-3 low-level urns (~200 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Low-level (~500 XP)
  • Door requirement: 41 Thieving to pick
  • Strategy: Skip all urns. Open the gold chest quickly, then rush the door. The XP here is too low to justify the time spent.

② Room 2 (Floor 2) — Scorpion Passage

  • Thieving requirement: 41 (same as Room 1)
  • Urns: 2-3 low-level urns (~300 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Low-level (~700 XP)
  • Door requirement: 41 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest only, then advance. Scorpion traps are easy to avoid — just walk around the glowing tiles.

③ Room 3 (Floor 3) — Guardian's Hall

  • Thieving requirement: 41 (still accessible)
  • Urns: 3-4 urns (~400 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Medium (~1,000 XP)
  • Door requirement: 41 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + 1-2 urns if you have spare time. Otherwise rush the door. Room 3 is still not worth lingering in.

④ Room 4 (Floor 4) — Tomb of the Ancients

  • Thieving requirement: 51 Thieving to open door
  • Urns: 3-4 urns (~600 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Medium-high (~1,500 XP)
  • Door requirement: 51 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + 2-3 urns. The XP is starting to become reasonable. This room is worth looting semi-thoroughly if you're below level 71.

⑤ Room 5 (Floor 5) — Chamber of Secrets

  • Thieving requirement: 61 Thieving to open door
  • Urns: 4-5 urns (~900 XP each)
  • Gold chest: High (~2,500 XP)
  • Door requirement: 61 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + all urns if you have time. At lower levels (61-70), this is the deepest room you can reach. Loot it fully.

⑥ Room 6 (Floor 6) — The Grand Hall

  • Thieving requirement: 71 Thieving to open door
  • Urns: 4-5 urns (~1,200 XP each)
  • Gold chest: High (~3,500 XP)
  • Door requirement: 71 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + all urns. This is where XP starts to get good (~180K XP/hr). Loot thoroughly before advancing.

⑦ Room 7 (Floor 7) — Sanctum of Souls

  • Thieving requirement: 81 Thieving to open door
  • Urns: 5-6 urns (~1,600 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Very High (~5,000 XP)
  • Door requirement: 81 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + all urns. XP rate jumps to ~220K-250K/hr. Start skipping earlier rooms to save time for Room 7.

⑧ Room 8 (Floor 8) — Pharaoh's Treasury

  • Thieving requirement: 91 Thieving to open door
  • Urns: 5-6 urns (~2,000 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Elite (~7,000 XP)
  • Door requirement: 91 Thieving
  • Strategy: Gold chest + all urns. XP rate hits ~270K-290K/hr. This is the optimal stopping point for most players.

⑨ Room 9 (Floor 9) — The Final Chamber

  • Thieving requirement: Only reachable if you have enough time (no extra Thieving requirement beyond Room 8)
  • Urns: 6-7 urns (~2,500 XP each)
  • Gold chest: Legendary (~10,000 XP) — best chest for Pharaoh's Sceptre chance
  • Door requirement: None — automatically accessible once you enter Room 9
  • Strategy: Max XP room — loot everything. If you reach Room 9 consistently, you're hitting ~300K-320K XP/hr.
💡 Pro Tip — The Faster Route vs The Greedy Route

You have two strategies: Fast route (skip early rooms entirely, rush to Rooms 7-9) or Greedy route (loot every room thoroughly). The fast route is better for XP. The greedy route gives more loot but lower XP/hr. At high levels (91+), always take the fast route — rush to Room 8/9, loot everything there, and don't waste time on Rooms 1-4.

④ 🔐 Door, Urn & Chest Mechanics Explained

Understanding exactly how doors, urns, and chests work in Pyramid Plunder is essential to maximizing your XP and loot. Here is a detailed breakdown of each mechanic.

4.1 🚪 Door Picklocking Mechanics

  • Success rate: Scales with your Thieving level relative to the room's requirement. Higher Thieving = fewer fails.
  • Failure penalty: A mummy appears and hits you (max 10 damage). You also get stunned for 1-2 ticks.
  • Door XP: Very low (~50 XP per success). Doors are NOT for XP — they're for access.
  • Strategy: Always pick the door last (after looting urns and chest). If you're low on HP, eat food before attempting the door.
  • Key tip: Use Dodgy Necklaces at lower levels (41-61) to reduce the stun penalty on failure. The stun can cost you valuable seconds.

4.2 🏺 Urn Searching Mechanics

  • How it works: Click an urn to search it. You'll receive XP and loot instantly. The urn disappears after being searched once.
  • XP per urn: Scales with room depth — Room 1 urns give ~200 XP; Room 9 urns give ~2,500 XP.
  • Loot from urns: Coins (common), runes (fire, air, mind, chaos), uncut gems (sapphire, emerald, ruby, diamond), and rare sceptre pieces.
  • Failure penalty: A snake bites you (1-3 damage). Rarely triggers — maybe 1 in 20 urns.
  • How many urns per room: 2-7 urns depending on the room and random layout. Rooms 7-9 consistently have 5-7 urns.

4.3 👑 Gold Chest Mechanics

  • How it works: Each room has exactly one gold chest. Open it once per room for a single large XP drop and loot roll.
  • XP per chest: Room 1 gives ~500 XP; Room 9 gives ~10,000 XP. This is the single best XP source in each room.
  • Loot from chest: Higher-value items — runes in larger quantities, gems, sceptre pieces (including the complete sceptre from Room 9 chest), and coin pouches.
  • Failure penalty: A mummy curse hits you (2-5 damage). Almost no risk at high levels.
  • Best chest for sceptre: Room 8 and Room 9 gold chests have the highest chance of dropping a full Pharaoh's Sceptre (~1/1,000 vs ~1/10,000 from urns).

4.4 🧠 Optimal Looting Order Per Room

Optimal Flow Per Room (Fast Route)

  1. Enter room — Quickly scan for gold chest location. It's usually near the center or along the wall.
  2. Open gold chest first — This gives the largest XP drop. Do it while you're still fresh and have the full time remaining.
  3. Search urns — Hit all urns in the room. Skip low-room urns (Rooms 1-4) if you need to conserve time.
  4. Picklock the door — Last step before leaving. Make sure you've looted everything valuable before attempting the door.
  5. Avoid traps — Walk carefully around spear trap tiles. They're visible as slightly glowing floor panels.

⑥ ⚠️ Spear Trap Avoidance — Complete Guide

Spear traps are one of the most frustrating mechanics in Pyramid Plunder for new players. They deal 8-15 damage and reset your progress if you're not careful. Here is everything you need to know to avoid them.

6.1 🔍 How to Spot Spear Traps

  • Visual cue: The floor tile of a spear trap has a slightly different shade than the surrounding tiles. They look like they have a faint glowing or darker stone pattern.
  • Positioning: Traps are typically placed in doorways, near the gold chest, and along common walking paths between urns.
  • Sound cue: When you step on a trap, you'll hear a metallic "shink" sound followed by damage. If you hear this, you stepped on one.
  • RuneLite plugin: Use the "Pyramid Plunder" plugin on RuneLite. It highlights spear trap tiles in red/yellow, making them impossible to miss.

6.2 🚶‍♂️ How to Walk Around Traps

  1. Slow down — Don't spam-click through rooms. Walk deliberately, looking at the floor tiles as you move.
  2. Walk around the trap tile — Traps are single tiles. You can walk around them in 1-2 extra steps. The time lost is minimal.
  3. Use the edge of the room — Most trap tiles are in the center or near objects. Walking along the walls is usually safe.
  4. Watch for trap patterns — Traps tend to repeat in the same positions across different runs within the same room. After a few runs, you'll memorize the common trap locations.
  5. Don't run over traps — Running doesn't avoid the trap. You trigger it regardless of your movement speed.

6.3 ⚡ What Happens When You Trigger a Trap

  • Damage: 8-15 HP per trap (depends on room depth — deeper rooms = higher trap damage)
  • Reset: You are pushed back to the entrance of the current room. You must walk back to where you were.
  • Time loss: 3-5 seconds per trap (walk back + reorient). Over a full run, multiple trap triggers can cost you 15-30 seconds.
  • Death risk: If you're low HP and trigger a trap, you can die. Bring 1-2 pieces of food as insurance.
💡 Pro Tip — Trap-Fast Route for Experienced Players

Once you've done 50+ runs, you'll have the trap locations memorized. At that point, you can run through rooms without thinking, instinctively avoiding traps. The first 10-20 runs require conscious effort; after that, it becomes muscle memory. Stick with it — the XP is worth the initial frustration.

⑦ ⚖️ Urns vs Gold Chest vs Doors — Which Is Best?

A common question among Pyramid Plunder players: should you prioritize urns, the gold chest, or doors? Here is a detailed comparison to help you decide.

7.2 🏆 Which Should You Prioritize?

  • Gold chest is king — It gives the most XP per second of any action in the minigame. Always open the gold chest in every room you enter, including deep rooms.
  • Urns are excellent — They give high XP per second and also provide loot. In deep rooms (7-9), always search ALL urns before moving on.
  • Doors are a tax — You must open doors to advance, but they give negligible XP. Pick the door only after you've looted everything valuable in the room.
  • Skip early rooms — Room 1-4 urns give very poor XP (200-600 XP each). If you're level 71+, skip these rooms entirely and rush to Room 6+.

7.3 🎯 Optimal Priority List Per Room

Priority Order (Any Room)

  1. Gold chest — Always first. Highest XP per second.
  2. All urns in the room — Search every urn. In deep rooms, this is your primary XP source.
  3. Door — Last step, only after the room is fully looted.

Exception: If you're rushing and low on time, skip Room 1-4 urns entirely. Only loot Room 5+ urns. The deeper the room, the more valuable each urn becomes.

💡 XP Optimization — Why You Never Skip the Gold Chest

The gold chest in Room 9 gives ~10,000 XP in 2 seconds. That's the equivalent of searching 4-5 urns (8-10 seconds) or picking 200 doors (10+ minutes). Never, ever skip the gold chest in any room you have access to. It is by far the most efficient XP action in the minigame.

⑨ 👑 Loot & Pharaoh's Sceptre

9.1 👑 Pharaoh's Sceptre — The Ultimate Prize

  • What it is: A rare sceptre that provides teleports to Jalsavrah (Pyramid Plunder), Jaleustrophos (Agility Pyramid), and Jaldagaot (Sophanem Dungeon). Also counts as a master clue step item.
  • Drop rate: ~1/1,000 from gold chests in Rooms 8-9. ~1/10,000 from urns. The Room 9 gold chest has the highest chance.
  • GE value: ~4M GP (June 2026). It fluctuates but has held value well over the years.
  • How to check if you got it: The sceptre appears in your inventory as a glowing golden sceptre. It's untradeable when first obtained but can be sold on the GE.
  • Is it worth grinding for? If you're going for 99 Thieving, you'll almost certainly get at least one sceptre along the way. The expected hit rate is ~1 sceptre per 100 hours of Pyramid Plunder at 91+.

  • Profitable with a lottery ticket — The Pharaoh's Sceptre adds excitement. Even without it, you make positive GP.

10.3 ⚠️ When to NOT Use Pyramid Plunder

  • Below level 71: XP rates are too low. Use blackjacking (45+) or ardy knights (55+) to reach 71 first.
  • Max XP/hr (tick-perfect): Dorgesh-Kaan chests (level 78+) can reach 300-350K XP/hr with perfect execution. If you want absolute max XP and can handle high intensity, choose Dorgesh-Kaan.
  • Ironman banking: Master Farmers are better for seeds. Pyramid Plunder doesn't give herb seeds.
  • GP-focused training: Blackjacking and ardy knights give more GP per hour from coin pouches and loot.

10.4 📈 Verdict — The Best Thieving Method for Most Players

🏆 Overall Verdict

Pyramid Plunder is the best thieving method for 90% of players training from 71 to 99. It offers the best balance of XP rate (270-320K/hr), AFK level, and profitability. Blackjacking is faster at lower levels but significantly more click-intensive. Dorgesh-Kaan chests offer higher peak XP but require much more effort. For the average player who wants to get 99 Thieving without carpal tunnel, Pyramid Plunder is the clear winner.

Recommended path: Blackjacking (45-71) → Pyramid Plunder (71-99). This route takes ~55-80 hours total and is the most time-efficient for most players.

⑪ ❓ FAQs — Pyramid Plunder (2026 Updated)

Q: What level should I start Pyramid Plunder?

A: You can start at 41 Thieving (minimum requirement), but we strongly recommend waiting until level 71. At 41-70, the XP rates are 25-100K/hr, which is significantly slower than blackjacking or ardy knights. At 71+, you unlock Room 6 and the XP jumps to 150K+/hr. For the best experience, reach 71 via blackjacking or ardy knights first.

Q: Is Pyramid Plunder better than blackjacking?

A: Yes, from level 71 onward. Pyramid Plunder gives 270-320K XP/hr at 91+ vs 150-250K XP/hr for blackjacking. More importantly, Pyramid Plunder is significantly more AFK — you click every 3-5 seconds compared to blackjacking's 1-2 clicks per tick. Blackjacking is better at low levels (45-70) where Pyramid Plunder XP is poor.

Q: What is the Pharaoh's Sceptre drop rate?

A: The drop rate is approximately 1/1,000 from gold chests in Rooms 8-9, and 1/10,000 from urns. The Room 9 gold chest has the highest probability. If you're training from 91 to 99 Thieving at Pyramid Plunder (8-10 hours), you have roughly a 10-15% chance of getting one sceptre.

Q: Can you die in Pyramid Plunder?

A: Yes, but it's very rare. Spear traps deal 8-15 damage, and mummies from failed door picks deal up to 10 damage. If you're low HP and trigger multiple traps, you can die. Bring 1-2 pieces of food as insurance. If you die, you lose nothing — your items are stored outside the pyramid.

Q: Should I use the Rogues' Outfit?

A: Yes, absolutely. The full Rogues' Outfit gives a 10% bonus to loot from urns and chests. This means more runes, more gems, and a slightly higher chance at sceptre pieces. The outfit requires completing the Rogues' Den minigame (level 50 Thieving, 50 Agility). It's well worth the 30-60 minute grind.

Q: How do I avoid spear traps consistently?

A: Use the RuneLite "Pyramid Plunder" plugin — it highlights trap tiles in red/yellow. With the plugin active, traps are impossible to miss. Without the plugin, look for slightly different-colored floor tiles (darker or with a faint glow). Walk slowly through rooms and watch your path.

Q: Is Pyramid Plunder worth it for ironmen?

A: Absolutely. It's a fantastic source of runes (fire, air, mind, chaos) and uncut gems for crafting. The Pharaoh's Sceptre is also incredibly useful for ironmen — the teleport to Sophanem saves a lot of travel time. Plus, the XP rates are excellent. It's arguably the best thieving method for ironmen from 71 onward.

Q: How many rooms can I access at each level?

A: Room 1-3: 41 Thieving. Room 4: 51 Thieving. Room 5: 61 Thieving. Room 6: 71 Thieving. Room 7: 81 Thieving. Room 8: 91 Thieving. Room 9: Reachable after Room 8 (no extra requirement, but you need enough time on the clock). Each room milestone significantly improves XP rates.

Q: Is Pyramid Plunder good GP per hour?

A: Moderate GP — expect 50-150K GP/hr from consistent loot (coins, runes, gems). If you factor in the Pharaoh's Sceptre chance (~4M GP every ~15 hours), it averages out to ~300-400K GP/hr. However, if GP is your primary goal, other methods like Vorkath or Zulrah are much better. Pyramid Plunder is primarily for XP.

Q: Does the Rogues' Outfit affect XP rates?

A: No. The Rogues' Outfit only affects loot quantity (+10% more items), not XP. It does not increase the XP per urn or gold chest. However, more loot means more GP, which is still valuable. If you're purely focused on XP, the Rogues' Outfit is optional. If you want GP alongside your XP, wear it.

⑫ 🏆 Final Tips & Verdict

🎯 Final Tips for Pyramid Plunder

Pyramid Plunder is the crown jewel of Thieving training in OSRS. Here are our final recommendations to make your experience as smooth as possible:

  • Wait until 71 Thieving — Don't torture yourself with low-level runs. Get to 71 via blackjacking or ardy knights first.
  • Use RuneLite's Pyramid Plunder plugin — Highlights traps, shows room layouts, and tracks your timer. It's essential for consistent runs.
  • Rush to the deepest room you can access — Skip Rooms 1-4 entirely at high levels. The deepest 2-3 rooms give 80% of your total XP.
  • Always open the gold chest — It's the single best XP action in the minigame. Never skip it in any room.
  • Watch your timer — If you're about to run out of time and haven't reached your target room, skip the current room's urns and rush the door instead.
  • Bring a Gem Bag — Uncapped gem storage makes long sessions much more efficient.
  • Get the Rogues' Outfit — The 10% loot bonus adds up over thousands of chests and urns.

🏆 Final Verdict — Should You Pyramid Plunder?

Yes, absolutely — from level 71 Thieving onward. Pyramid Plunder offers the best balance of XP, AFK, and profit of any Thieving method in OSRS. At 91-99, it gives 270-320K XP/hr with minimal effort compared to blackjacking or Dorgesh-Kaan chests. The potential for a Pharaoh's Sceptre drop (~4M GP) adds a nice lottery element that keeps training exciting. If you're looking for a relaxed path to 99 Thieving, this is it.

Recommended path: Blackjacking (45 Thieving) → Ardy Knights (55-71) → Pyramid Plunder (71-99). Total time: ~55-80 hours. Total profit: ~2-5M GP. Enjoy the grind.
