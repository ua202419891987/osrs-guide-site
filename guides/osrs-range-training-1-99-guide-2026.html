OSRS Range Training 1-99 Fastest Guide 2026

By OSRSGuru Updated: January 15, 2026 25 min read

Welcome to the most comprehensive OSRS Ranged training guide for 2026. Whether you're a brand-new account or pushing for that coveted 99 Ranged cape, this guide covers every method you need to know — from shooting cows outside Lumbridge to slinging Red Chinchompas at 99.

Ranged is arguably the most popular combat style in Old School RuneScape, and for good reason. It's the safest way to train combat — you can attack from a distance while enemies struggle to reach you. At higher levels, Ranged delivers some of the highest DPS in the game, especially with endgame weapons like the Twisted Bow. And unlike Melee or Magic, Ranged can actually make you money at high levels through efficient Slayer training or by using profitable ammo.

In this guide, you'll learn the fastest methods for each level bracket, the exact gear upgrades to prioritize, cost breakdowns for every method, and whether you should use Slayer or Chinchompas for your final push to 99. Let's get started.

Why Train Ranged? Key Benefits

Before diving into the training methods, let's understand why Ranged is such a powerful skill in OSRS:

Safety

Ranged allows you to attack from a distance. Most enemies can't reach you, meaning you take zero damage. This saves massive amounts on food and prayer potions.

High DPS

With high-tier gear, Ranged delivers the highest damage per second in the game. Twisted Bow users can hit 50+ on dragons and 90+ on cavalry.

Profit Potential

At high levels, Ranged training can actually make you money through Slayer. High-level Slayer monsters drop valuable loot while giving excellent Ranged XP.

Bossing Power

Nearly every endgame boss in OSRS is weak to Ranged. From Vorkath to Chambers of Xeric to Theatre of Blood, Ranged is the meta.

Chapter 1: Levels 1-30 — The Beginner Phase

Easy

Getting from level 1 to 30 Ranged is straightforward and cheap. You have two main options: combat training on low-level monsters or using the optimal quest path.

Method 1: Cows and Chickens (Levels 1-20)

The classic OSRS experience. Head to the cow field north of Lumbridge or the chicken coop west of Lumbridge. Equip an Iron Crossbow or Standard Bow with bronze/iron arrows.

Recommended Setup (Levels 1-20)

  • Weapon: Iron Crossbow (cheap, decent accuracy) or Standard Bow with Iron Arrows
  • Armor: Leather armor set (available from Lowe's Archery Store in Varrock)
  • Inventory: 10-15 food (shrimp, anchovies, or bread)
  • Location: Cow field north of Lumbridge (safe spots available)

At level 20 Ranged, you unlock Maple Shortbows and Green Dragonhide armor. This is a significant power spike. Green D'hide provides excellent ranged attack bonus (+10) and decent defense. It's inexpensive and should be your go-to armor until level 60.

Method 2: Rock Crabs (Levels 20-30)

<

Rock Crabs are the best AFK training option for low levels. They have low Defense, 1 HP, and regenerate after being killed, meaning you don't need to constantly find new monsters.

Rock Crab Locations

  • Rellekka (easiest access: fairy ring DKS, then run north)
  • Waterbirth Island (requires started Fremennik Trials quest)
  • Zeah — Lovakengj (requires 50% Hosidius favor for quick access)

XP Rate: ~15,000-20,000 XP/hour at level 20-30

Cost: Free (drops cover ammo costs)

Alternative: Questing to 30 Ranged

If you're following an optimal quest guide, several quests give Ranged XP rewards:

  • Animal Magnetism: 1,000 Ranged XP (unlocks Ava's accumulator)
  • Horror from the Deep: 900 Ranged XP (unlocks CED
  • Terror from the Deep (2025 update): 5,000 Ranged XP
  • The Path of Glouphrie: 8,000 Ranged XP

Combined, these quests can get you very close to level 30 without any traditional training.

Chapter 2: Levels 30-60 — The Mid-Game Grind

Medium

Levels 30-60 are where Ranged training starts to get more interesting. You have access to better weapons, more locations, and the option to start Slayer for profit.

Sand Crabs (Levels 30-50)

Sand Crabs in Zeah remain the best AFK training method for this level range. They have 60 HP (scaled to your combat level), regenerate after 3 minutes, and the area is multi-combat safe.

Optimal Sand Crab Setup (Levels 30-50)

  • Weapon: Maple Shortbow (until 50) → Willow Composite Bow or Steel Crossbow
  • Ammo: Steel arrows (cheap) then Iron arrows (better DPS)
  • Armor: Green Dragonhide set (full set recommended)
  • Ring: Ring of Recoil (cheap, gives extra damage) or Ring of Life (safety)
  • Amulet: Amulet of Accuracy (cheap +3 ranged bonus) or Amulet of Glory

XP Rate: ~25,000-35,000 XP/hour

Time to 50: ~3-4 hours from level 30

Flesh Crawlers (Levels 50-60)

Located in the Stronghold of Security (death to 100), Flesh Crawlers are an excellent semi-AFK option. They have low Defense and drop reasonable loot.

Pro Tip: Stronghold of Security Rewards

While training at Flesh Crawlers, make sure to complete each floor of the Stronghold of Security. The rewards include 10,000 GP per floor and the useful warhammer +2.

Mystery Island (Player-Owned House) (Levels 50-60)

If you have access to a Player-Owned House with a Combat Ring, you can train on the dummies there. This method is completely AFK and free, but requires 50 Construction and a POH.

XP Rate: ~20,000 XP/hour (AFK, no cost)

This is ideal if you want to train while doing something else. The dummies give XP every 3 seconds and never run out of HP.

Starting Slayer (Levels 50-60)

At level 50 Ranged, you can start unlocking Slayer masters. While not the fastest XP, Slayer gives variety and profit. Recommended Slayer masters for this level:

  • Turael (Burthorpe): Easy tasks, good for unlocking points
  • Krystilia (Wilderness): Good tasks but risky (Wilderness)
  • Mazchna (Canifis): Low-requirement tasks

Chapter 3: Levels 60-80 — Maniacal Monkeys (Best XP)

Medium
~400,000 XP/hr

Welcome to the single best Ranged training method in the entire game. Maniacal Monkeys in the Monkey Madness II dungeon offer the fastest Ranged XP in OSRS, bar none. If you want 99 as fast as possible, this is where you'll spend most of your time.

Requirements for Maniacal Monkeys

  • Quest: Monkey Madness II (required to access the island)
  • Ranged Level: 60+ (recommended 70+ for efficiency)
  • Agility: 60+ (recommended for faster movement)
  • Items: Gorilla greegree (unlocks after Monkey Madness II)

Optimal Maniacal Monkey Setup

Weapon & Ammo

  • Best-in-Slot: Twisted Bow with Dragon Arrows (500,000+ XP/hour)
  • High-Efficiency: Rune Crossbow with Broad Bolts or Diamond Bolts (e300,000+ XP/hour)
  • Budget: Magic Shortbow with Rune Arrows (250,000+ XP/hour)

Armor & Equipment

  • Helm: Slayer Helm (recommended) or Armadyl Helmet
  • Cape: Ava's Assembler (best) or Ava's Accumulator
  • Amulet: Amulet of Torture or Amulet of Fury
  • Body: Armadyl Chestplate or Black Dragonhide Body
  • Legs: Armadyl Chainskirt or Black Dragonhide Chaps
  • Boots: Pegasian Boots (best) or Dragon Boots
  • Ring: Archers Ring (i) or Ring of Suffering (i)
  • Shield: Twisted Buckler or Dragonfire Ward

How to Train at Maniacal Monkeys

  1. Teleport to Ape Atoll using the Ape Atoll Teleport (unlocked after Monkey Madness II)
  2. Equip your Gorilla greegree to access the dungeon
  3. Run south to the Maniacal Monkey area (marked on the mini-map)
  4. Position yourself in a 3-tile safe spot (use the pillars)
  5. Turn on Protect from Missiles prayer (optional but recommended)
  6. Attack monkeys with long-range style for extra XP per kill

Pro Tip: Long Range Attack Style

Always use the "Long Range" attack style when training at Maniacal Monkeys. It gives +1 range to your attack distance (allowing safer positioning) AND gives extra XP per damage dealt. This is the single biggest XP boost you can get.

Chapter 4: Levels 80-99 — Red Chins vs Slayer

Advanced
565,000 XP/hr

At level 80 Ranged, you have two excellent options for the final push to 99. The choice depends on your goals: speed (Red Chinchompas) or profit (High-level Slayer).

Method 1: Red Chinchompas (Fastest XP)

Red Chinchompas are the fastest Ranged training method in OSRS when used correctly. They have an area-of-effect (AOE) effect that damages multiple enemies, making them incredible for multi-combat areas.

Best Locations for Red Chins

  1. Chinchompa Hunting (Hunter level 63+): Catch your own Red Chins in the Feldip Hunter area (south of Yanille). This gives Hunter XP while providing free ammo.
  2. Castle Wars (AFK): Safe spot behind the wall, attack enemies through the gate. 200,000-250,000 XP/hour.
  3. Muscle Wizard (AFK): Located in the(alch)Fight Arena, low-level wizards that respawn quickly. 250,000-300,000 XP/hour.
  4. Experiments (Slayer Tower): Safe spot in the basement. 350,000-400,000 XP/hour.
  5. Kalphite Queen (Multi-combat): High risk but 400,000+ XP/hour.

Optimal Red Chin Setup

  • Weapon: Dragon Crossbow or Twisted Bow (with Dragon Arrows for Twisted Bow)
  • Ammo: Red Chinchompas (284 GP each as of January 2026)
  • Attack Style: Long Range (for distance and extra XP)
  • Prayer: Eagle Eye (level 44) or Rigour (level 74) for accuracy/damage boost

Red Chin Cost Calculation (2026 Prices)

Red Chinchompas cost approximately 284 GP each (GE price, January 2026).

Each Chinchompa gives ~270 Ranged XP on average (including AOE damage).

Cost per XP: 284 / 270 = 1.05 GP per XP

Total cost for 80-99 (约 5.5M XP): ~5,775,000 GP

Total cost for 90-99 (约 2.8M XP): ~2,940,000 GP

Chapter 5: Gear Progression — From MSB to Twisted Bow

Your gear makes a massive difference in Ranged training efficiency. Here's the complete progression path from level 1 to endgame.

Early Game (Levels 1-50)

Budget Setup (Total cost: ~50,000 GP)

  • Weapon: Iron Crossbow (100 GP) → Maple Shortbow (free from stringing) → Willow Composite Bow (400 GP)
  • Ammo: Bronze Arrows → Iron Arrows → Steel Arrows (all cheap)
  • Armor: Leather set (1,000 GP) → Green Dragonhide set (20,000 GP)
  • Boots: Leather Boots (100 GP) → Snakeskin Boots (5,000 GP)
  • Gloves: Leather Gloves (free) → Green D'hide Vambraces (2,000 GP)

Mid Game (Levels 50-80)

Mid-Tier Setup (Total cost: ~2,000,000 GP)

  • Weapon: Magic Shortbow (MSB) (1,200 GP) → Magic Shortbow (i) (750,000 GP) → Rune Crossbow (25,000 GP) → Dragon Crossbow (1,500,000 GP)
  • Ammo: Rune Arrows (200 GP/ea) → Broad Bolts (unlocked at 55 Slayer) → Diamond Bolts (e) (500 GP/ea)
  • Armor: Green D'hide → Blue D'hide (80,000 GP) → Red D'hide (150,000 GP) → Black D'hide (300,000 GP)
  • Cape: Ava's Accumulator (quest reward) → Ava's Assembler (1,200,000 GP)
  • Boots: Snakeskin Boots → Dragon Boots (100,000 GP)
  • Ring: Ring of Recoil → Archers Ring (200,000 GP)

End Game (Levels 80-99+)

High-End Setup (Total cost: ~1,500,000,000+ GP)

  • Weapon: Dragon Crossbow (1.5M GP) → Heavy Ballista (8M GP) → Twisted Bow (1.2B GP)
  • Ammo: Dragon Bolts (e) (1,000 GP/ea) → Dragon Arrows (800 GP/ea) → Amethyst Arrows (cheaper alternative)
  • Armor: Armadyl Kit (Chestplate + Chainskirt) (40M GP total) or Obsidian Armor (cheaper alternative)
  • Helm: Slayer Helmet (i) (500,000 GP) → Armadyl Helmet (15M GP)
  • Cape: Ava's Assembler (1.2M GP) → Infernal Cape (150M GP for Fire Cape required)
  • Boots: Dragon Boots → Pegasian Boots (20M GP)
  • Ring: Archers Ring → Archers Ring (i) (10M GP)
  • Necklace: Amulet of Fury (2M GP) → Amulet of Torture (15M GP) → Necklace of Anguish (35M GP)

Is It Worth It? (Cost-Benefit Analysis)

The answer depends on your account goals and budget:

Red Chins — Pros

  • Fastest Ranged XP in the game (565K XP/hour possible)
  • 80-99 in just ~13 hours of active training
  • No requirements besides 80 Ranged and money
  • Can be done at safely (low risk of dying)
  • Great for hardcore ironmen who want 99 quickly

Red Chins — Cons

  • Expensive (7.8M GP for 80-99 as of January 2026)
  • Boring (repetitive, no variety)
  • No profit (you're spending money, not making it)
  • Can be banned on Ironman accounts if buying massive quantities

Slayer Alternative — Cost & Profit

If you choose high-level Slayer instead of Chins, here's what you can expect:

  • XP Rate: 150,000-250,000 XP/hour (slower than Chins)
  • Profit: 200,000-500,000 GP/hour (varies by task)
  • Time for 80-99: ~30-50 hours (vs 13 hours with Chins)
  • Total Profit: 6,000,000 - 25,000,000 GP over the course of 80-99

Break-even point: If you value your time at more than ~400,000 GP/hour, Red Chins are worth it. If you enjoy Slayer and don't mind the slower XP, Slayer is more fun AND profitable.

OSRSGuru Recommendation

For Main Accounts: Use Slayer from 80-90 (enjoy the variety and make money), then use Red Chins for the final 90-99 push if you want the cape ASAP.

For Ironman Accounts: Hunt your own Red Chins (Hunter 63+). This gives Hunter XP and free Ranged training ammo. It's slower but completely free.

For Hardcore Accounts: Red Chins are the safest 99 method. You can do it in complete safety with minimal risk.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Ranged the best combat style for training?

Yes, for most players. Ranged is the safest combat style (you attack from a distance), has the highest DPS at endgame (with Twisted Bow), and can be profitable at high levels through Slayer. However, Melee is cheaper to train, and Magic has useful utility (teleports, alchemy).

How much does 99 Ranged cost?

It depends on your method:

  • Free (Slayer only): 0 GP (but takes 40-60 hours)
  • Red Chins (80-99): ~7,800,000 GP (takes ~13 hours)
  • Maniacal Monkeys (60-99): ~2,000,000 GP (arrow/bolt costs, takes ~20-30 hours)
  • Mixed (Slayer + Chins): ~4,000,000 GP (takes ~25 hours)

What's the fastest way to get 99 Ranged?

The absolute fastest method is:

  1. 1-30: Cows/Chickens (1 hour)
  2. 30-60: Sand Crabs (3-4 hours)
  3. 60-80: Maniacal Monkeys with Twisted Bow (10-15 hours)
  4. 80-99: Red Chinchompas (13 hours)

Total time: ~27-33 hours of active training.

Can I train Ranged for free?

Yes! Here's a completely free 1-99 Ranged route:

  • 1-30: Cows/Chickens (free, safe)
  • 30-60: Sand Crabs (free, semi-AFK)
  • 60-99: Slayer only (free, profitable)

You won't be as fast as someone using Red Chins, but you'll make money while training.

When should I buy a Twisted Bow?

Twisted Bow is the best Ranged weapon in the game, but it costs over 1.2 billion GP. You should only buy it if:

  • You have maxed or near-maxed combat stats
  • You plan to do high-level bossing (CoX, ToB, ToA, Nex)
  • You have at least 200M GP saved after buying it (for gear/consumables)
  • You enjoy Ranged-based PvM

For most players, Dragon Crossbow or Heavy Ballista are better-value weapons until you're very rich.

What's the best Ranged armor for training?

For training (not bossing), you want maximum Ranged Attack bonus:

  • Early game: Green Dragonhide set (+10 Ranged Attack)
  • Mid game: Black Dragonhide set (+20 Ranged Attack)
  • Late game: Armadyl set (+30 Ranged Attack, +15 Ranged Strength)
  • Budget endgame: Obsidian armor set (cheaper than Armadyl, slightly less accurate)

Final Tips & Recommendations

Tip #1: Use Slayer for Profit

Don't just grind XP — make money while training. High-level Slayer tasks (Nechryaels, Abyssal Demons, Alchemical Hydra) give excellent Ranged XP AND valuable loot. You'll get 99 Ranged AND a bank full of wealth.

Tip #2: Save Chins for the Final Push

Red Chinchompas are expensive. Use Slayer or Maniacal Monkeys until 90-95 Ranged, then use Chins for the last 5 levels. This saves millions of GP while still getting 99 relatively quickly.

Tip #3: Always Use Long Range

The "Long Range" attack style gives +1 attack distance AND extra XP per damage dealt. Always use it when training Ranged. This single tip can save you hours of training time over the course of 1-99.

Tip #4: Unlock Rigour as Soon as Possible

Rigour (level 74 Prayer) gives +20% Ranged accuracy and +23% Ranged damage. It's the single biggest DPS boost for Ranged. If you don't have 74 Prayer, prioritize getting it.

Tip #5: Get Ava's Assembler

Ava's Assembler (unlocked after completing Dragon Slayer II) automatically picks up your arrows/bolts. This saves massive amounts of money on ammo costs. It's essential for efficient Ranged training.

Tip #6: Consider an Ironman Approach

If you're an Ironman, hunt your own Red Chinchompas (Hunter 63+). Each Chin gives 265 Hunter XP and provides free Ranged training ammo. It's slower than buying Chins, but completely free and gives two 99s for the price of one.
