OSRS Combat Triangle Guide 2026 — Melee vs Ranged vs Magic Explained

The combat triangle is the foundation of OSRS combat. Melee beats Ranged, Ranged beats Magic, Magic beats Melee. This guide explains exactly how it works, when to switch styles, and how to train each combat skill efficiently.

⚡ Quick Verdict

Master at least two combat styles to be effective. The combat triangle dictates that no single style works everywhere. For beginners: train Melee first (best for quests and slayer), then Ranged (bossing and safe spots), then Magic (utility and high-level PvM). Each style has unique advantages — use them together, not just one.

1. What Is the Combat Triangle?

The combat triangle is OSRS's core balance mechanic. Each combat style has a built-in advantage and disadvantage against the other two:

🔺 The Triangle

  • ⚔️ Melee beats 🏹 Ranged — Melee armor has high Ranged defense. Ranged users in leather/d'hide are vulnerable to melee hits.
  • 🏹 Ranged beats 🔮 Magic — Ranged armor (d'hide) has high Magic defense. Mages in robes take heavy hits from arrows.
  • 🔮 Magic beats ⚔️ Melee — Magic attacks target Magic level (which plate armor doesn't help). Melee users in heavy armor are slow and vulnerable to magic.

This isn't a strict rule — it's a trend based on armor types and accuracy mechanics. In practice, the triangle has blurred edges at high levels where gear quality matters more than the triangle. But for beginners, understanding the triangle helps you choose the right weapon, armor, and strategy for every encounter.

💡 The Golden Rule of the Combat Triangle: If you're taking too much damage from a monster, switch to the style that resists it. A dragon wearing plate (melee-style) takes more damage from arrows (ranged) than from swords (melee). A mage in robes takes more damage from arrows than from spells.

2. Melee: Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Gear

Melee is the most straightforward combat style and the one most beginners should train first. It deals damage through three skills: Attack (accuracy + weapon access), Strength (max hit), and Defense (armor access + HP).

① Melee Strengths

  • Highest DPS potential at low-to-mid levels — melee weapons have the fastest attack speed and highest strength bonus
  • Best for quests — most quest bosses are weak to melee (Dragon Slayer, Monkey Madness, etc.)
  • Cheapest to train — melee weapons cost little to nothing. A Dragon Scimitar at 95K lasts you 100+ hours
  • Best for AFK training — Nightmare Zone, Sand Crabs, Ammonite Crabs all favor melee

② Melee Weaknesses

  • No safe spots — you must get close to the target, taking damage
  • Weak against Magic — heavy plate armor has low Magic defense
  • Higher supply cost — you take more damage than ranged/mage if not using Protect prayers
  • Ineffective against flying/fast monsters — some targets are hard to reach with melee

3. Ranged: Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Gear

Ranged is the safest combat style — you attack from distance, often without taking damage. It's essential for bossing from mid-game onward.

① Ranged Strengths

  • Safe spots — attack monsters from behind obstacles (no damage taken)
  • High Magic defense — dragonhide armor gives excellent Magic protection (up to +250 Magic defense)
  • Best for slayer profit — most high-profit slayer monsters are weak to Ranged (Dust Devils, Smoke Devils, Wyverns)
  • Essential for bossing — Vorkath, Zulrah, Kree'arra, all require Ranged
  • Cheap ammunition — Broad Bolts at 2 GP each after 55 Slayer

② Ranged Weaknesses

  • Low defensive stats — d'hide armor has low Melee defense. Monsters that reach you hit hard
  • Ammunition cost — before Broad Bolts (55 Slayer), you spend 50-100 GP per shot
  • Slower early training — Ranged is slow below level 40. Knives/Darts help but cost GP
  • Less effective in multi-target areas — chinchompas (multi-Ranged) are expensive

4. Magic: Strengths, Weaknesses & Best Gear

Magic is the most versatile combat style — it provides utility (teleports, alchemy, enchantments) in addition to combat damage.

① Magic Strengths

  • Best against Melee enemies — Magic attacks ignore melee armor. Cast on plate-armored targets for full damage
  • Utility outside combat — teleports, High Alchemy (money), enchantments, teleport blocks in PvP
  • Multi-target damage — Burst/Barrage spells from Ancient Magicks hit up to 9 targets at once, great for slayer
  • Safe spells — many spells can be cast from distance with no retaliation from the target
  • Required for quests — Desert Treasure (Ancient Magicks), Lunar Diplomacy (Lunar spells)

② Magic Weaknesses

  • Very expensive — runes cost GP. Burst/Barrage tasks cost 200-500K/hour in runes
  • Low defense — magic robes provide almost no physical defense. You're vulnerable to Ranged and Melee
  • Slow XP at low levels — curse/bolt spells give minimal XP. Splash training is slow
  • Requires quest progress — best spells need quests (Desert Treasure, Lunar Diplomacy)

6. Style Switching & Hybrid Combat

High-level PvM and PvP require switching between combat styles mid-fight. Here's what you need to know about hybrid combat.

① Why Switch Styles?

Many bosses change their attack style or defensive weakness during the fight: Zulrah switches between mage and ranged forms every ~45 seconds. Vorkath alternates between dragonfire (use protect from magic) and melee attacks. Demonic Gorillas change their prayer based on your attack style — you must switch between Melee, Ranged, and Magic to damage them.

② Gear Switching Basics

To switch styles effectively: Use RuneLite's Inventory Tags plugin (color-code your gear sets). Arrange your inventory with weapon switch in slot 1, body in slot 2, legs in slot 3, shield in slot 4. Practice the 4-way switch (weapon + body + legs + shield) — this covers the majority of DPS difference. Start with 3-way switches (weapon + body + legs), then add more items as you improve.

③ F-Key Setup for Switching

Configure your F-keys for rapid style switches: F1 = Inventory (gear switch), F2 = Prayer (prayer switch), F3 = Spellbook (spell switch). Practice the combination: F1 → right-click gear → equip → F2 → click protection prayer → F3 → click attack spell. With practice, this takes 1-2 game ticks (0.6-1.2 seconds).

⚡ Beginner Hybrid Checklist

  • ✅ Train all three combat skills to at least 70 before attempting hybrid bosses
  • ✅ Complete Desert Treasure (Ancient Magicks) and Lunar Diplomacy (Lunar spells)
  • ✅ Buy one good weapon per style (Whip, RCB, Trident)
  • ✅ Practice 3-way switches at the Nightmare Zone or against slayer monsters
  • ✅ Set up F-keys before entering the boss room
  • ✅ Bring extra food for your first attempts — switching styles takes practice

🔥 Pro Tip: Don't try to 6-way switch (full inventory swap) until you're comfortable with 3-way and 4-way. Each extra item adds ~0.3 seconds to your switch time. A clean 4-way switch in 1 tick is better than a messy 6-way switch that takes 3 ticks.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Which combat style should a beginner train first?

Melee. Train Attack + Strength to 60-70 first. Melee is cheapest, fastest to train at low levels, and required for most quests (Dragon Slayer, Monkey Madness). Get Ranged to 50 next (for quests like Animal Magnetism), then Magic to 55 (for High Alchemy and teleports).

Q: What's the best combat style for slayer?

It depends on the task. Roughly: Melee for 50% of tasks (hellhounds, bloodvelds, gargoyles), Ranged for 30% (fire giants, dragons, wyverns), Magic for 20% (dust devils, nechs, smoke devils). Level them all — slayer naturally trains all three if you use the right style for each task.

Q: Does the combat triangle matter in PvP?

Yes, but less than in PvM. PvP players use hybrid/tribrid setups and switch prayers much faster than monsters. The triangle matters for gear choices (d'hide vs robes vs plate) but the prayer system matters more — if you pray correctly, the combat triangle becomes secondary.

Q: What is "splash" training for Magic?

"Splash training" uses a low-accuracy spell on a monster with high Magic defense, so the spell "splashes" (misses) every time. Each splash still gives Magic XP. This allows AFK Magic training if you have negative Magic attack bonus. Not recommended for active play — it's very slow (13-18K XP/hour) but requires zero attention.

Q: What combat level should I train to for bossing?

Minimum stats for entry-level bossing: 70 Attack, 70 Strength, 70 Defense, 70 Ranged, 70 Magic, 43 Prayer. This lets you equip Dragon gear, Barrows armor, and use Protect Prayers. For mid-game bosses (Vorkath, Zulrah): aim for 85+ in your main combat style and 75+ in the secondary style.

Q: Is Melee, Ranged, or Magic best for making money?

Ranged is generally the best profit-per-hour style for mid-game players. Vorkath (Ranged, 3-4M GP/hour), Zulrah (Ranged/Magic hybrid, 2-3M/hour), and GWD bosses all favor Ranged. Magic is second-best for slayer burst tasks. Melee is the cheapest to train but has lower profit/hour at most bosses.

Q: Can I use all three combat styles at the same time?

No — you can only attack with one weapon at a time. You CAN bring gear for all three styles in your inventory (called "tribridding") and switch between them mid-fight. This is common at Zulrah, Demonic Gorillas, and PvP. But you'll need 70+ in all three styles and F-key muscle memory.

Q: What equipment gives the best Magic defense?

Dragonhide armor (Ranged armor) gives the highest Magic defense bonus per slot. Black D'hide Body: +40 Magic defense. Karil's Leathertop: +65. For Magic attack (damage): Ahrim's Robe Top: +35 Magic attack but much lower Magic defense. Choose based on whether you're attacking or defending against magic.

Q: What is "void" and is it good for the combat triangle?

Void Knight equipment from Pest Control gives a damage bonus (+10% for all styles with Melee Helm, +10% Ranged with Ranged Helm, +5% Magic with Magic Helm). Void is unique because it boosts ALL combat styles equally — making it excellent for hybrid/tribrid combat. However, the defense is lower than Barrows or dragonhide.

Q: Should I level all three combat types equally?

No. Focus one style at a time to 70-80. Suggested order: Melee (Attack+Strength) → Ranged → Magic → Defense. Balanced levels are needed for quests (Song of the Elves requires 70 in all skills), but for combat efficiency, specializing one style at a time is faster. Your combat level increases from any style, so you unlock content sooner.
