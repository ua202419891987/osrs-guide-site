🏆 OSRS Achievement Diary Guide 2026

Achievement Diaries (also called "Diaries") are sets of tasks in each region of Gielinor. Completing them gives permanent rewards: unlock teleports, extra GP from NPCs, useful items, and even stat boosts. This guide covers Easy and Medium diaries — the ones you should complete first for maximum benefit.

🏆 1. What Are Achievement Diaries? (Why Do Them)

Each region in OSRS has an Achievement Diary — a list of 10–20 tasks you can complete to earn a reward. Tasks are simple things like:

  • "Kill 5 cows in Lumbridge"
  • "Travel from Varrock to Falador via the shuttle"
  • "Pickpocket a H.A.M. member"
  • "Complete a lap of the Barbarian Agility Course"

Once you complete all tasks in a diary, talk to the Diary NPC (usually in a central location) to claim your reward. Rewards include:

🎁 Why Achievement Diaries Are Worth Doing

  • Teleport unlocks: Some diaries unlock useful teleports (e.g., Ardougne Diary → Kandarin head teleport)
  • GP bonuses: NPCs give you extra GP when you show them your completed diary (e.g., 20% more GP from H.A.M. loot)
  • Item unlocks: Free useful items (explorer ring, lumbridge ring, etc.)
  • Stat boosts: Some rewards give temporary stat boosts (e.g., Explorer ring → 27 Super energy doses per day)
  • Access unlocks: Some areas/features require a completed diary (e.g., Fremennik Diary → access to some areas)

The best part: Most diary tasks are things you'll do anyway (killing cows, traveling, etc.). You can complete many diaries "passively" while playing normally.

💡 Achievement Diaries = Free Permanent Upgrades

Unlike quest rewards (one-time XP), diary rewards are permanent buffs that help you every day. An extra 20% GP from all sources adds up to hundreds of thousands of GP per week. That's why diaries are a top priority for efficient players.

🎯 3. Which Diaries to Complete First (Priority Order)

Not all diaries are equal. Here's the priority order for beginners:

🏅 Priority 1: Lumbridge & Draynor (Easy + Medium)

Why first: Gives the Explorer's Ring (useful for run energy + alchemy) and Lumbridge Ring (teleport to Lumbridge). Tasks are very easy (all in starting area). Complete this in your first week of playing.

🥈 Priority 2: Varrock (Easy + Medium)

Why second: Gives Varrock Armor (double ore smithing) and Varrock teleport (very useful). Tasks are in Varrock (easy to reach). Complete after 30 Combat.

🥉 Priority 3: Falador (Easy + Medium)

Why third: Gives Falador Shield (teleport to Falador + prayer restore) and Falador teleport. Tasks are in Falador/Draynor area. Complete after 40 Combat.

Priority 4: Ardougne/Kandarin (Easy + Medium)

Why fourth: Gives Kandarin Head (teleport to Ardougne) and Ardougne cloak (double herbs from patches). Very useful for mid-game. Complete after 60 Combat.

💡 Do Diaries WHILE Leveling Stats

Don't specifically "grind" diaries. Instead, look at the diary tasks for your current region, and try to complete them while you're already there. Example: If you're training Combat in Edgeville Dungeon (near Varrock), check the Varrock Diary tasks and see if you can complete any.

📋 6. How to Track Your Diary Progress

You can track diary progress in two ways:

① In-Game Quest List (Official)

Press Alt + Q (or click the Quest List icon in the game interface). Click the "Diaries" tab. Select a region (e.g., "Lumbridge & Draynor") to see all tasks. Completed tasks show a green checkmark. In-progress tasks show yellow. Locked tasks show red.

② RuneLite Diary Plugin (Recommended)

If you use RuneLite, the Achievement Diary plugin is much better than the in-game tracker. It shows:

  • All tasks for all diaries in a clean list
  • Exactly where to go (map markers)
  • One-click task filtering (show only incomplete tasks)
  • Task requirements (what stats/quests you need)

How to Enable the Diary Plugin (RuneLite)

Step 1: Open RuneLite settings (wrench icon).

Step 2: Click "Plugins" on the left sidebar.

Step 3: Search for "Achievement Diary" in the plugin list.

Step 4: Click the toggle switch to enable it (turns green).

Step 5: The diary overlay appears on your game screen. Click any task to see details.

🎁 7. Most Useful Diary Reward Items

Some diary rewards are game-changing. Here are the top 5 most useful:

① Explorer's Ring (Lumbridge & Draynor)

Reward: 30% run energy restore (5–15x per day) + Low alchemy (5–15x per day). Why it's amazing: Saves thousands of GP on energy potions and lets you alch items without runes. Get this ASAP.

② Kandarin Head / Ardougne Cloak (Ardougne Diary)

Reward: Teleport to Ardougne (unlimited) + 10% extra herbs from Farming patches. Why it's amazing: Farming is the best passive income, and 10% extra herbs = 10% more GP per run. Pays for itself quickly.

③ Falador Shield (Falador Diary)

Reward: Teleport to Falador (unlimited) + 1–3 prayer restore per day. Why it's amazing: Free teleport to a central location + free prayer restore (saves prayer potions).

④ Varrock Armor (Varrock Diary)

Reward: 10% chance double ore when smithing + 10% extra GP from museum. Why it's amazing: If you train Smithing, this saves millions of GP on ores. The GP bonus is also nice passive income.

⑤ Fremennik Sea Boots (Fremennik Diary)

Reward: Teleport to Rellekka (unlimited) + 5% extra GP from slayer tasks. Why it's amazing: Slayer is the best mid-game money maker, and 5% extra GP adds up.

❓ 8. FAQ

Can F2P players complete Achievement Diaries?

Yes, but only partially. F2P players can complete some Easy diary tasks (the ones in F2P areas), but many tasks require membership. The diary reward is also members-only. Get membership to get full value from diaries.

Do I need to complete diaries in order (Easy → Medium → Hard → Elite)?

No. You can complete them in any order. However, Higher tiers have higher stat requirements, so it's natural to do Easy first, then Medium, etc. You can work on multiple diaries simultaneously (e.g., do Lumbridge Easy tasks while training in that area, then switch to Varrock Easy tasks when you're in Varrock).

What happens if I lose my diary reward item?

If you lose a diary reward item (e.g., Explorer's Ring), you can get it back for free by talking to the Diary NPC again. No need to re-complete the diary. This is very useful — you can safely drop/lose the item and get a free replacement.

How long does it take to complete all Easy + Medium diaries?

About 20–30 hours total (spread across many play sessions). But you don't need to "grind" them — complete tasks passively while playing normally. After 2–3 weeks of playing, you'll have most Easy diaries done without specifically trying.

Are Achievement Diary rewards worth the effort?

100% yes. Diary rewards are permanent passive bonuses that help you every single day. The Explorer's Ring alone saves you hundreds of thousands of GP on run energy and alchs. Completing diaries is one of the highest-ROI activities in OSRS.
