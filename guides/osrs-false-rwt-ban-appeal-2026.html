OSRS False RWT Ban Appeal Guide 2026

A Real-World-Trading (RWT) ban is one of the most frightening messages you can receive in Old School RuneScape — but not every RWT ban is correct. This guide explains what a false-positive RWT ban looks like, how to tell it apart from a macro/bot ban, exactly how to appeal through Jagex Support, and what to do if your account is recovered.

🎯 Level: Intermediate — Account Safety Series · 12 min read · Updated July 2026

⏱️ Quick Verdict — 30-Second Read

  • 📌 Most "false" RWT bans are behavioral matches — huge trade volume, bond-flipping, or GP moved between your own accounts can trip Jagex's trade monitors.
  • 📌 RWT bans and macro bans are different systems — RWT is detected from transactions, macro from input/automation. Appeal each differently.
  • 📌 Appeal through the Jagex Support Centre (support.runescape.com) or the "Appeal" button on your lockout screen — never pay a "unban" scammer.
  • 📌 Evidence wins appeals: dates, known trade partners, screenshots, and a clear explanation of legitimate activity.
  • 📌 If reversed, secure the account immediately — enable a Jagex Account, 2FA, and stop the behavior that triggered the flag.

1. 🛡️ What a "False RWT Ban" Actually Means

RWT (Real-World Trading) is Jagex's term for exchanging in-game wealth — GP, items, or accounts — for real money (or other out-of-game value). Buying gold from a third-party site, selling your account, or paying someone to "boost" you are all RWT. These are permanently bannable offences under the OSRS rules.

A false-positive RWT ban happens when Jagex's automated systems flag legitimate play as RWT. You never paid for gold, never sold anything — yet the trade pattern looked suspicious enough to trigger a ban. The good news: Jagex does review appeals, and genuine false positives can be overturned.

💡 First, Confirm the Ban Reason

Log in and read the exact message. It will state the rule broken (e.g., "Real World Trading" or "Macroing / use of unauthorised third-party software"). The wording determines everything below — so screenshot it before you do anything else.

It is also worth being honest with yourself: if you did buy gold or use a bot, this guide won't help reverse that — but understanding the process still protects you from scammers who pretend they can "unban" you for a fee.

2. 🔍 What RWT False-Positives Look Like

Jagex's trade monitoring looks for patterns, not intentions. Several perfectly legal activities create the same fingerprints as a gold-seller. The most common triggers are below.

Behavior That Trips the SystemWhy It Looks Like RWTUsually Legitimate?
Very high trade volumeMoving millions of GP through trades in a short window mimics a gold seller's payout flowYes — merching, boss loot splitting, max-cape pooling
Grand Exchange "dump" patternsBuying a commodity in bulk then selling all at once resembles price manipulation / gold-launderingYes — flip trading, price crashes
Bond buying & resellingBonds are bought with real money then converted to GP; large bond-to-GP conversions flag as "cash injection"Yes — completely official, but monitored
Wealth transfer between your own accountsMoving GP/items from an alt to a main looks like account-selling or mulingYes — family of accounts is allowed
Shared IP / shared deviceMultiple accounts logging from one house looks like an RWT "operation"Yes — siblings, roommates, shared Wi-Fi
Lending / fronting itemsHanding a stranger a valuable item "to be returned" reads like a collateral tradeRisky — often against the spirit of the rules

🎯 The "Innocent But Flagged" Checklist

  • You recently cashed out a large bond stack into GP
  • You split a big boss drop (e.g., Torva, twists) with a team
  • You "muled" GP from an ironman-helper or alt account
  • You bought a rare from the GE and instantly relisted it higher
  • Several of your accounts share one internet connection

If two or more of these are true, you are in classic false-positive territory.

3. ⚖️ RWT Bans vs Macro Bans — How to Tell Them Apart

This is the single most important distinction, and the one players confuse most often. A macro ban (botting) and an RWT ban are detected by completely different systems and reviewed through different lenses.

⚠️ Don't Mix Up the Appeal

If your lockout says Macroing, arguing "but I never bought gold" is irrelevant — the system flagged how you played, not what you traded. Build your appeal around the correct reason. (We cover the macro side in detail in our companion guide on bot-client crackdowns — see Related Guides below.)

4. 📝 The Jagex Support Appeal Process (Step by Step)

All appeals go through the official Jagex Support Centre. There is no email, no Discord, and no "moderator" who will unban you in DMs. Follow this path:

① Read the Lockout Screen

When you try to log in, you'll see the offence and a "Appeal" button (or a link to the Support Centre). Note the offence type and any reference/ban ID. Screenshot it.

② Open a Ticket at support.runescape.com

Go to the Jagex Support Centre, sign in with the banned account, and choose the option to appeal a sanction. Fill in the offence reference and a written explanation.

③ Write a Clear, Factual Appeal

Keep it short and specific. State: the account name, that you believe the ban is a false positive, and a plain-English explanation of your legitimate activity (e.g., "I bond-flipped 40 bonds to fund my main; the GP came from official bonds, not a gold site").

④ Attach Evidence (see next section)

Upload or reference anything that proves the GP/items were earned or officially purchased. Don't paste huge logs — summarize and point to the key dates.

⑤ Wait for the Review

Response times vary (often several days to a couple of weeks during ban waves). You'll get the outcome by email and on the ticket. Do not spam tickets — it can slow the review.

💡 One Account, One Honest Story

Jagex cross-references your whole account family. Pick the truthful version of events and stick to it. Contradicting yourself across tickets is the fastest way to get an appeal denied.

You don't need a lawyer — you need consistency. The strongest appeals pair a clear story with verifiable facts.

🚫 What Hurts Your Appeal

  • Arguing the rule is unfair instead of proving you didn't break it
  • Claiming you "never traded" when logs show you did (be precise)
  • Threats, spam, or demanding escalation to a "human mod"
  • Paying a third-party "unban service" — these are scams that steal your account

6. 🔄 Account Recovery After a Reversal

If Jagex upholds your appeal, the sanction is lifted and you regain access. But recovery isn't finished — you should harden the account so it never trips again.

Step 1: Regain Access & Scan for Damage

Log in and check for missing GP/items. If the ban was a false positive, your wealth should be intact. If something is gone, note it — but understand Jagex rarely reimburses items lost during a legitimate ban period.

Step 2: Migrate to a Jagex Account

Convert the login to a full Jagex Account with 2-step verification. This both protects against hijackers and clearly separates your account family under one verified identity — reducing "shared IP" confusion.

Step 3: Change the Behavior That Triggered the Flag

If bond-flipping or muling caused the flag, spread activity out, keep trades between accounts modest, and avoid "collateral lending." You were forgiven once — a second review is far less likely to go your way.

⚠️ If the Appeal Is Denied

RWT bans are typically permanent, and a denial is usually final. Don't create a replacement account to "get around" it — that can broaden the sanction. Start fresh only on a genuinely new, clean account, and read our account-security guide first.

7. 💡 How to Avoid Future False-Positives

Prevention is easier than appeal. Adopt these habits to stay under the radar while playing normally:

  • Keep bond-to-GP conversions reasonable. Cashing a few bonds at a time looks far more natural than dumping 100 at once.
  • Document your account family. Link alts under one Jagex Account so wealth transfers read as "yours," not "muling for a stranger."
  • Avoid collateral lending and "item fronting." These sit in a gray area and are easy to misinterpret.
  • Don't trade with known RWT accounts. Associating with flagged players can pull your account into an investigation.
  • Enable 2FA and recoveries. A hijacked account that gets used for RWT will be banned under your name — security prevents that.
🔒 Security First

Most "RWT bans" that turn out to be someone else's fault start with an account hijack. Lock down every account before worrying about trade patterns — see our Account Security guide in Related Guides.

8. ❓ False RWT Ban FAQs

Can I really get banned for RWT if I only bought official bonds?

Bonds themselves are 100% legitimate — Jagex sells them. The risk is pattern: converting huge bond volumes to GP and then moving that GP around can resemble a cash-laundering flow. If your bonds were purchased through the official Member Centre, say so in your appeal; Jagex can verify the purchases internally.

How long does a Jagex appeal take?

Most appeals are reviewed within a few days, but during large ban waves (such as post bot-client crackdowns) it can stretch to one or two weeks. You'll be notified by email and on the Support Centre ticket. Opening duplicate tickets does not speed this up.

Will a "unban service" on Discord or a website help me?

No — these are scams. Jagex does not use Discord DMs, paid "moderators," or third-party unban sites. Anyone offering to lift your ban for crypto or GP is trying to steal your login or your money. Use only the official Support Centre.

My ban says "Macroing" but I never botted — is that the same as RWT?

No. Macroing (botting) is detected from how you play — input timing and unauthorized clients — not from trades. If your message says Macroing, build your appeal around proving your activity was manual. The two offences use different detection systems and different evidence.

If my appeal is denied, is there any second chance?

For RWT, a denial is generally final and the ban permanent. You can sometimes submit a follow-up with new evidence, but repeatedly appealing the same facts rarely helps. The safest move is to secure a brand-new account and follow the prevention steps above from day one.

🎯 What To Do Next

A false RWT ban is stressful, but the path is clear: appeal through the official Support Centre, prove your activity was legitimate, and then lock the account down so it never happens again.
