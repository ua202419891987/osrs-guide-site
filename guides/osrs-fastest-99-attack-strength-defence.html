Start every account with Waterfall Quest — it requires zero combat, takes 30 minutes, and instantly boosts you from level 1 to 30 Attack and Strength. Follow up with Fight Arena, Tree Gnome Village, and The Grand Tree for easy Attack XP. You'll hit 50+ Attack before killing a single monster.

Sand Crabs (Levels 30–60)

Head to Hosidius Beach (south of Kourend Castle) with your best weapon. Sand crabs have 60 HP and are aggressive for 10 minutes. Find a 3-crab spot (southern beach has multiple), click to attack, and AFK for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, run 15+ tiles away and run back to reset aggression. At 40K-60K XP/hr with a rune scimitar, you'll reach 60 in about 4-6 hours of total AFKing.

3. Levels 60–85: Mid Game — NMZ & Ammonite Crabs

Ammonite Crabs (60–70)

After Bone Voyage, head to the Ammonite Crab spots on Fossil Island (north-central beach). Ammonite crabs are superior to sand crabs: 100 HP, same aggression mechanics, 60K-80K XP/hr with a dragon scimitar. The northeastern spot has 3 crabs clustered on one tile — the best melee training spot for mid-levels. Use the RuneLite NPC Aggression Timer plugin to track exactly when aggro expires.

Nightmare Zone Setup (Unlock at 60+)

🛡️ NMZ Boss Selection for Max AFK: Choose only these 5 bosses for minimal attention: Count Draynor, King Roald, Tree Spirit, Kendal, and Khazard Warlord. Avoid bosses that require special mechanics: Elvarg (dragonfire), Moss Giant (prayer drain), Slagilith (pickaxe required), Dad (knockback), Arrg (ranged). These 5 have zero special mechanics and never hit above 3 damage at 1 HP.

NMZ Normal Mode — Obsidian AFK (80K–100K XP/hr)

Requirements: 60 Defence, ~3M GP for Obsidian armor set. Wear full Obsidian armor + Berserker Necklace + Obsidian Sword (Toktz-xil-ak). The set effect provides +10% melee accuracy and +10% melee strength. Enter custom rumble (easy bosses), drink Absorption potions to 1,000 HP, rock cake to 1 HP, and AFK for 15-20 minutes. Re-pot absorptions and rock cake when you check back. This is the best balance of AFK-ness and XP in OSRS.

NMZ Dharok Method (100K–130K XP/hr)

Requirements: 70+ Hitpoints, 70+ Strength, full Dharok's set (~5M GP). Dharok's set deals more damage at lower HP. At 1 HP, you regularly hit 70+ damage (400+ XP per hit). Same NMZ setup: absorptions + rock cake to 1 HP. The catch: you must maintain 1 HP, requiring a rock cake click every 50-60 seconds. Not truly AFK, but absurd XP rates for the minimal attention required. This is the meta for efficient melee training from 70–99.

4. Levels 85–99: Fastest Methods

Sulphur Nagua (Varlamore Part 2) — 130K–150K XP/hr

Sulphur Nagua are 2024/2026 additions that became the best melee XP in OSRS. Located in the Varlamore expansion area, these aggressive creatures have low Defence and drop valuable loot (Sulphur blades, noted resources). Requirements: completion of the Varlamore Part 2 introductory quest, 85+ combat stats, and protection from their sulphur gas (face mask or Slayer helmet).

Kill them with your best melee gear — they're weak to slash. Use Piety/Rigour constantly (they drop enough loot to cover prayer potion costs). With max strength gear (Torva + Soulreaper Axe), you'll see 150K+ XP/hr. With mid gear (Bandos + whip), expect 110K-130K XP/hr. This is active training — you'll be clicking constantly — but the combination of record XP and 300K-500K GP/hr in loot makes it the best overall melee training in the game.

🔥 Nagua vs NMZ Decision Guide: Choose Sulphur Nagua if: you want the absolute fastest 99, you're playing actively, and you like making GP while training. Choose NMZ Dharok if: you want to AFK, you're working/watching shows on the side, or you value low-attention training over speed. Both are valid — Nagua shaves ~50 hours off each 99, but requires full attention. NMZ takes longer but lets you train while doing other things.

7. Common Melee Training Mistakes

  1. Training Defence first. Higher Defence doesn't speed up training. Higher Strength does (bigger hits = more XP per attack). Always train Strength first, then Attack, then Defence.
  2. Using the wrong weapon for Strength training. The Abyssal Whip has a "controlled" option but no "strength-only" option. For pure Strength training, use a Saradomin Sword, Abyssal Dagger, or Dragon Scimitar (budget). The Whip is for Attack and Defence training only.
  3. Not using Super Combat Potions. Super Combat Potions boost all melee stats by ~15% and cost 5K-8K per dose. That's 1-2K GP per kill of extra DPS. Over a 100-hour grind to 99, the boost from Super Combats saves 10+ hours. Don't cheap out on potions.
  4. Ignoring Piety/Rigour. Piety provides +25% Defence, +23% Strength, and +20% Attack. At 70 Prayer, you should use Piety for ALL melee training. The prayer potion cost (50K-80K/hr) is worth the 23% DPS increase. Without Piety, you're training at 77% efficiency.
  5. Training at over-crowded crab spots. Sand crab and ammonite crab spots are competitive. If you're sharing a 3-crab spot with 2 other players, your XP drops by 60%+. World hop until you find an empty spot. Worth the 2 minutes of hopping.

Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

  1. Using the wrong method for your level. NMZ (Dharok/melee) and the Slayer Cave are strongest at higher levels; below ~70 you're usually better on controlled tasks or sand crabs. Match method to level.
  2. Skipping the defender for Defence. Train Defence at the Warriors' Guild with a defender equipped — without it you forgo the accuracy and XP edge.
  3. Over-estimating real XP/hr. Guides quote ideal rates; latency, food runs, and deaths cut actual gains 10-20%. Budget time accordingly.
  4. Not using the best weapon and stance. For Attack/Strength use the strongest scimitar available; for controlled XP pick the "Attack" or "Defence" stance deliberately.
  5. Forgetting prayer and gear bonuses. A Strength amulet, best-in-slot gloves, and a Protect from Melee setup materially raise kills/hr. Don't train naked.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Slayer the best way to train melee?

A: For overall account progression, yes. For pure melee XP speed, no. Slayer averages 40K-60K melee XP/hr depending on tasks, which is 50%+ slower than NMZ or Nagua. However, Slayer trains a separate skill simultaneously, generates profit, and unlocks essential PvM content (Abyssal Demons, Cerberus, Hydra). The efficient path: train melee with Slayer until 85-90 in combat stats, then max the remaining levels at NMZ/Nagua.

Q: Is full Dharok's worth it for NMZ if my HP is only 70?

A: No. Wait until 85+ Hitpoints. Dharok's set damage scales with the percentage of HP missing. At 70 HP, your max hit at 1 HP is significantly lower than at 99 HP. At 70 HP, Obsidian armor + Berserker Necklace outperforms Dharok's. At 85+ HP, Dharok's becomes better. At 92+ HP, Dharok's is decisively the best NMZ XP method. Use Obsidian until your HP is 85+.

Q: How do I train Defence quickly if my Attack and Strength are already 99?

A: Use the best Strength gear even though you're training Defence. Set your weapon to "Defence" attack style (or "Block" for whip). You'll use your 99 Strength to hit hard (since max hit is based on Strength, not the skill you're training) while earning Defence XP. This is the fastest way. Use NMZ Dharok on "Defensive" or whip on "Defence" at Sulphur Nagua. Either way, your 99 Strength carries the DPS.

⚔️ Max Melee Is Worth Every Hour

Max combat stats are the single biggest account upgrade in OSRS. Every boss kill gets faster. Every Slayer task becomes trivial. Every piece of PvM content opens up. The 250-500 hour grind is substantial, but the difference between 70 melee stats and 99 is the difference between struggling at Vorkath and casually farming it for 3M GP/hr.

Whether you AFK NMZ for months or grind Sulphur Nagua for a few dedicated weeks, the destination is the same. Pick the method that fits your playstyle, stay consistent, and those three 99 capes will be yours before you know it.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ How long does 1-99 in Attack, Strength, and Defence take?

Each skill from 1-99 requires ~13M XP. Using NMZ Dharok method (100K-130K XP/hr): roughly 100 hours per skill, or 300 hours for all three. With Sulphur Nagua (130K-150K XP/hr active): about 87 hours per skill. AFK methods at 60K-80K XP/hr: 165-215 hours per skill.

❓ What is the best AFK method for melee training?

Nightmare Zone (NMZ) normal mode with Absorption potions is the best pure AFK melee training. Customize a rumble with 5 weak bosses, drink Absorption potions to stack 1,000 HP of damage absorption, and use a rock cake to stay at 1 HP. Click once every 15-20 minutes to re-pot. This yields 80K-100K XP/hr.

❓ Should I train Attack, Strength, or Defence first?

Train Strength first (bigger max hits = faster XP). Then Attack (more accuracy). Defence last — it provides the least DPS benefit and you'll have higher Strength/Attack by then. Optimal order: Strength → Attack → Defence.

❓ What gear should I use for fastest melee XP?

Best DPS gear by budget: Budget (5M): Obsidian armor set + Berserker Necklace + Obsidian Sword. Mid (30M): Full Bandos, Amulet of Torture, Abyssal Whip, Dragon Defender. Max (500M+): Torva set, Soulreaper Axe (Strength), Avernic Defender, Ultor Ring, Infernal Cape.

❓ Is full Dharok's worth it for NMZ at 70 HP?

No. Wait until 85+ Hitpoints. Dharok's damage scales with % HP missing. At 70 HP, Obsidian armor outperforms Dharok's. At 85+ HP, Dharok's becomes better. At 92+ HP, it's decisively the best NMZ XP method.

❓ How do I train Defence if Attack and Strength are already 99?

Use the best Strength gear even while training Defence. Set your weapon to Defence attack style. Your 99 Strength carries the DPS. Use NMZ Dharok on Defensive or whip on Defence at Sulphur Nagua.
