OSRS Soulreaper Axe Guide 2026

Novice

The Soulreaper Axe is one of the most unique and powerful weapons in OSRS. This complete guide covers how to obtain it, its Soul Stack mechanic, DPS calculations, best use cases, and whether it's worth the massive cost.

🪓 Weapon Guide · Part of the Mid-Game Gear Series · 10 min read

Published: June 26, 2026 · Updated: June 26, 2026

⚡ 30S Quick Preview

  • What it is: A powerful two-handed axe with a stacking Soul mechanic
  • Best for: High-defense bosses like Nex and Awakened bosses
  • Mechanic: Each hit adds a Soul Stack for up to +6% accuracy and +1% damage per stack
  • Verdict: Worth it for end-game PvM, overkill for Slayer and low-level content

⚡ Quick Verdict

The Soulreaper Axe is absolutely worth it for end-game PvM accounts with 1B+ GP to invest. It dominates against high-defense bosses like Nex and Awakened bosses, offering 15-20% higher DPS than the Abyssal Bludgeon at max stacks. However, it's overkill for general Slayer and low-defense content — stick to cheaper options if you're not doing high-level bossing regularly.

1. How to Obtain the Soulreaper Axe

The Soulreaper Axe is obtained through the Delving Percy quest line, a multi-part storyline introduced in 2025. This prestigious two-handed axe requires completing a challenging series of tasks and defeating a demanding boss.

Step-by-Step Acquisition Process

Prerequisites

  • Quest Requirements: Complete "A Kingdom Divided" and "The Path of Glouphrie"
  • Skill Requirements: 80 Attack, 80 Strength, 75 Prayer
  • Combat Requirement: Defeated The Corrupted Gauntlet at least 5 times
  • Item Requirement: Abyssal Bludgeon (recommended for damage testing)

Quest Line Steps

  1. Start the Quest: Speak to Delving Percy in the Ruins of Camdozaal (access via fairy ring code BIQ)
  2. Gather Materials: Collect 50 Ancient Shards from Chambers of Xeric and 10 Infernal Ashes from the Wilderness
  3. The Forging Trial: Successfully forge a Soul Fragment at the Ruined Forge (90 Smithing recommended)
  4. Boss Battle: Defeat the Sentinel of Souls (level 650) — requires 3-4 preparations with proper gear
  5. Final Reward: Receive the Soulreaper Axe (untradeable until fully upgraded)

💡 Pro Tip: The Sentinel of Souls fight has a unique mechanic where you must maintain at least 3 Soul Stacks to deal damage. Practice with the Abyssal Bludgeon first to understand the timing.

2. Soul Stack Mechanic Explained

The Soulreaper Axe's defining feature is its Soul Stack system. This mechanic sets it apart from every other weapon in OSRS, rewarding aggressive, sustained combat with massive damage scaling.

How Soul Stacks Work

  • Stacking: Each successful hit adds 1 Soul Stack (up to 10 stacks maximum)
  • Accuracy Bonus: Each stack provides +6% Accuracy (cumulative)
  • Damage Bonus: Each stack provides +1% Damage (cumulative)
  • Special Attack: Consumes all stacks for a devastating AoE attack (more on this later)
  • Stack Loss: Switching weapons, dying, or using the special attack resets stacks to 0

Complete Soul Stack Data Table

Stacks Accuracy Bonus Damage Bonus Total DPS Increase* Time to Max Stacks**
0 (Base) 0% 0% 0% —
1 +6% +1% ~3-4% ~2.4s
2 +12% +2% ~7-8% ~4.8s
3 +18% +3% ~11-12% ~7.2s
5 +30% +5% ~19-21% ~12.0s
7 +42% +7% ~27-30% ~16.8s
10 (Max) +60% +10% ~42-48% ~24.0s

*Total DPS Increase includes both accuracy and damage bonuses combined. **Assumes 2.4s attack speed with no missing hits.

⚔️ Combat Strategy: Against bosses with prayer-draining attacks, use Protect from Magic/Melee and bring Super restore potions to maintain Piety/Turmoil. Losing prayer means losing accuracy, which delays stack buildup significantly.

Special Attack: Soul Reap

The Soulreaper Axe's special attack, Soul Reap, consumes all Soul Stacks to deal an AoE (Area of Effect) attack. The damage is calculated as:

  • Base Damage: 25% above max hit
  • Stack Multiplier: +15% damage per stack consumed
  • At 10 stacks: Up to 175% of your max hit (devastating against groups)
  • Range: 3-tile radius around you (great for multicombat areas)

4. Best Use Cases & Scenarios

The Soulreaper Axe shines in specific PvM scenarios where its stack-building mechanic can reach full potential. Here are the best use cases:

Nex — The Primary Use Case

Nex is widely considered the best boss for the Soulreaper Axe. Here's why:

  • High Defense: Nex has 300 Defense, making the +60% accuracy at 10 stacks invaluable
  • Long Fight Duration: Nex kills typically take 2-4 minutes, allowing full stack buildup
  • Phase Transitions: The Axe's AoE special attack clears minions (Blood reavers) quickly
  • Profitability: Faster kills = more kills per hour = higher GP/hr

Average Improvement: 3-4 additional Nex kills per hour (from ~16 to ~20) with the Soulreaper Axe.

Awakened Bosses (ToA, CoX, ToB)

Awakened bosses from Tombs of Amascut (ToA), Chambers of Xeric (CoX), and Theatre of Blood (ToB) all have high Defense and long fight durations, making them excellent for the Soulreaper Axe:

  • Awakened Xarpus (ToA): 250 Defense — Soulreaper Axe reduces kill time by ~15%
  • Awakened Vardorvis (ToA): 220 Defense — excellent AoE potential with special attack
  • Olm (CoX): 210 Defense — sustained DPS shines in long raids
  • Verzik (ToB): 180 Defense — Axe helps during the melee phase significantly

High-Level Slayer Tasks

Certain high-level Slayer tasks can benefit from the Soulreaper Axe, particularly those with high-defense monsters:

  • Greater Demons (300+ Defense): The accuracy bonus helps significantly
  • Black Demons (250+ Defense): noticeable DPS improvement over Bludgeon
  • Iron Dragons (280 Defense): the +60% accuracy at max stacks ensures consistent hits

🎯 Gear Recommendation: When using the Soulreaper Axe, prioritize max Strength bonus over defense. Recommended setup: Neitiznot helmet, Fire cape, Strength amulet (f), Barrows gloves, Dragon boots, and Berserker ring (i).

5. When NOT to Use Soulreaper Axe

Despite its power, the Soulreaper Axe is not a universal weapon. There are several scenarios where it's either ineffective or overkill:

Low-Defense Monsters

The Soulreaper Axe's accuracy bonus is wasted on low-defense monsters. If the monster has <100 Defense, you'll hit consistently with cheaper weapons:

  • Hill Giants (40 Defense): Dragon Warhammer or even Rune 2h sword is sufficient
  • Moss Giants (80 Defense): Abyssal Bludgeon performs nearly identically
  • Goblins, Cows, Al-Kharid Warriors: Any weapon works — don't waste expensive charges

General Slayer Tasks

For most Slayer tasks, the Soulreaper Axe is overkill. The time spent building stacks often exceeds the task duration, meaning you're fighting at low stacks most of the time:

  • Short Tasks (<50 kills): You'll never reach max stacks — use Abyssal Bludgeon instead
  • Multi-Combat Areas: The Axe's AoE special attack helps, but the Bludgeon's faster attack speed is often better for stacking damage quickly
  • Wilderness Slayer: High risk of dying and losing stacks — not recommended

Budget Accounts (Sub-500M GP)

If your total account value is less than 500M GP, the Soulreaper Axe is a terrible investment. You're better off buying:

  • Full Bandos armor set (~80M GP)
  • Abyssal Bludgeon (~85M GP)
  • Berserker ring (i) (~35M GP)
  • Remaining GP for supplies and Range/Mage gear

These upgrades will improve your overall account significantly more than a single 1.2B GP weapon.

💰 Budget Alternative: If you can't afford the Soulreaper Axe, the Abyssal Bludgeon remains an excellent choice for high-defense targets. It's 14x cheaper and only ~15-20% less DPS at max efficiency.

6. Cost Analysis & Value Proposition

The Soulreaper Axe is one of the most expensive items in OSRS. Before purchasing, you need to understand the total cost of ownership — not just the purchase price.

Initial Purchase Cost

Component Cost (GP) Notes
Soulreaper Axe (Base) 1,150,000,000 Untradeable after upgrade
Upgrade: Soul Infusion 50,000,000 Unlocks special attack
Total Initial Cost 1,200,000,000 ~1.2B GP

Maintenance Costs (Per Hour of Use)

The Soulreaper Axe has a unique degradation system. It doesn't break, but it consumes Soul Shards over time:

  • Soul Shard Cost: 1 shard per 100 hits (roughly 4 minutes of combat)
  • Soul Shard Price: ~150k GP each (from Chambers of Xeric drops)
  • Hourly Maintenance: ~15 shards = ~2.25M GP/hour
  • Monthly Cost (6 hrs/day): ~405M GP/month

Return on Investment (ROI) Calculation

To justify the 1.2B GP cost, you need to calculate how much additional profit the Soulreaper Axe generates:

Nex Example (Most Common Use Case)

  • Bludgeon Kill Speed: ~3:45 per kill (16 kills/hour)
  • Soulreaper Axe Kill Speed: ~3:00 per kill (20 kills/hour)
  • Additional Kills/Hour: +4 kills
  • Average Profit per Nex Kill: ~2.5M GP
  • Additional Profit/Hour: +10M GP
  • Maintenance Cost/Hour: -2.25M GP
  • Net Profit Increase/Hour: +7.75M GP

→ Break-even point: ~155 hours of Nex bossing (roughly 2-3 months of casual play)

📈 Investment Advice: If you boss 10+ hours per week, the Soulreaper Axe pays for itself in ~4 months. If you boss less than 5 hours per week, it will take 8+ months to break even — consider whether you'll still be playing OSRS that long before investing.

7. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is the Soulreaper Axe better than the Abyssal Bludgeon?

A: Yes, but only in sustained fights against high-defense targets. The Soulreaper Axe needs time to build stacks — in short fights (<1 minute), the Bludgeon is better due to its faster attack speed and immediate max DPS. For long boss fights (2+ minutes), the Soulreaper Axe outperforms the Bludgeon by 15-20% at max stacks.

Q: Can I use the Soulreaper Axe at Dagannoth Kings?

A: Definitely not. Dagannoth Kings have low Defense (140 for Dagannoth Supreme), so the accuracy bonus is wasted. Plus, you'll take massive damage from their ranged attacks due to the Axe's slow attack speed. Use a Dragon Hunter Crossbow or Blowpipe instead.

Q: What's the best spec weapon to use with the Soulreaper Axe?

A: The Dragon Warhammer special attack (-30% target Defense) synergizes perfectly with the Soulreaper Axe. Use 2-3 Dragon Warhammer specs at the start of the fight to lower the boss's Defense, then switch to the Soulreaper Axe to build stacks quickly. Alternatively, the Bandos Godsword spec (+15% Strength) works well once you have 5+ stacks.

Q: Does the Soul Stack reset when I switch to a protection prayer?

A: No, switching prayers does NOT reset your Soul Stacks. However, switching weapons (even temporarily) DOES reset stacks to 0. Plan your inventory carefully to avoid accidental weapon switches during the fight.

Q: Can I sell the Soulreaper Axe if I don't like it?

A: Yes, but only if you haven't completed the "Soul Infusion" upgrade. Once upgraded, the Axe becomes untradeable. If you're unsure whether the Axe is right for you, test it in a friend's PvP world or at a high-defense training dummy before spending the 50M GP upgrade cost.

Q: How do I maximize my kill speed with the Soulreaper Axe at Nex?

A: Use this rotation: 1) Start with Dragon Warhammer specs to lower Nex's Defense, 2) Switch to Soulreaper Axe and build to 5 stacks during the blood phase, 3) Use Soul Reap special attack when Nex summons Blood reavers (clears them instantly), 4) Continue building stacks to 10 during the ice/shadow phases, 5) Use BGS spec when you have 8+ stacks for maximum damage. This rotation consistently achieves 2:45-3:00 kill times.

Q: Is the Soulreaper Axe worth it for Ironman accounts?

A: For Ironman accounts, the Soulreaper Axe is even more valuable because you can't buy one from the GE. You must complete the Delving Percy quest line, which requires significant PvM progression. If you're an Ironman with 2200+ total level and consistent Nex/ToA kills, the Soulreaper Axe is absolutely worth the quest requirements. The time investment to obtain it is substantial, but the DPS improvement is game-changing for end-game PvM.

Q: What's the best way to practice with the Soulreaper Axe before using it at bosses?

A: Practice at the Combat Dummy in the Warriors' Guild (requires 60 Attack/Strength). The dummy has 140 Defense, similar to many mid-level bosses. Focus on maintaining your stack count while moving (some bosses require movement during certain attacks). Once you can consistently reach 10 stacks within 30 seconds, you're ready for real bossing. Also, try the Axe at Glacors (200 Defense) — they're a good middle-ground before attempting Nex.

Q: Will the Soulreaper Axe get nerfed in future updates?

A: Jagex has historically been careful about nerfing expensive items. The Soulreaper Axe's mechanics are intentionally high-risk, high-reward (losing stacks on weapon switch/death). A direct DPS nerf is unlikely, but Jagex may adjust the maintenance cost (Soul Shard consumption) if the item becomes too dominant in the meta. Always check the latest OSRS updates before making major purchases.
