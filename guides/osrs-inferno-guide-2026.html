💡 Inventory Pro Tip: The "Brew-Restore" Ratio

The most common early mistake is bringing too many brews and not enough restores. Brews drain your stats — every time you drink a brew, you need a restore dose to bring your Ranged/Magic back up. ⑩ 🗺️ The Central Rock — Your Best Friend

The central rock formation in the Inferno arena is the single most important terrain feature. Every top player uses it to "stack" monsters — positioning yourself so that multiple monsters line up behind the rock, allowing your Tbow to hit all of them while only one can attack you at a time.

🎯 How to Stack Waves 1-32

  1. Enter the wave. Immediately run to the south side of the central rock.
  2. Wait 1-2 ticks. Monsters will path toward you. They naturally line up as they approach the rock.
  3. Peek around the east side. Attack the first monster in the line — your Tbow/ACB will hit it.
  4. Step back. The monsters behind the first one cannot attack you because the rock blocks LoS.
  5. Repeat. Kill the first monster, then the second, then the third — all without taking damage from the ones behind.

⑪ ⏱️ Wave 31-32 — The Real Warm-Up Ends

By wave 32, you should have a full set of supplies (you should be using very few brews/restores in these early waves — ideally 1-2 doses max). If you're chugging brews before wave 30, you are either not flicking properly or not using the central rock effectively. Consider practicing earlier waves again before pushing forward.

⑤ Waves 33-50 — Blobs & Rangers

Waves 33-50 introduce heavier blob + ranger + mage combinations. This is where the Inferno separates casual PvMers from serious cape-hunters. Two new key mechanics become critical: blob management and wave planning.

⑫ 👾 Blob Behavior — What You Need to Know

Blobs (Jal-AkRek-Mej) are the most misunderstood monster in the Inferno. Here is exactly how they work:

  • HP: 30 (low) — They die quickly to a single Tbow hit or two ACB hits.
  • Attack type: They attack with whatever prayer you do NOT have active. If you have Protect from Range on, they attack with Magic. If you have Protect from Magic, they attack with Range. If you have neither, they attack with both simultaneously.
  • Damage: ~15-25 per hit. With both attacks (no prayer), they can hit 35+ damage in a single tick — enough to one-shot you.
  • Attack speed: Every 4 ticks (2.4 seconds). This is the same as the player attack cycle — meaning you can "flick" your prayer at the same rhythm as attacking.
  • Special: Blobs "transform" after ~20 seconds. Their colour changes and they regenerate HP. Kill them quickly.

⑬ ⚡ Blob + Ranger + Mage Trios (Waves 35-45)

This is the most common triple-threat combination in the mid-waves. The correct approach is:

Step 1: Freeze the Blob

Cast Ice Barrage on the blob as soon as the wave starts. This gives you 4 ticks (~2.4 seconds) before it unfreezes. During that window, you can kill other mobs without worrying about prayer flicking.

Step 2: Kill the Mage First

The mage (Jal-AkRek-Xil) deals the highest consistent damage of any Inferno mob (~25 per hit). Always prioritize the mage. With Tbow, a mage dies in 4-6 hits. With ACB, it takes 8-12 hits.

Step 3: Kill the Ranger Second

Rangers (Jal-AkRek-Ket) also hit hard (~20 per hit) but are slightly lower priority than mages. After the mage is dead, switch to the ranger.

Step 4: Kill the Blob Last

With the mage and ranger dead, the blob is just a single-target flick. Keep Protect from Magic on (blob will attack with Range) and finish it off.

⑭ 🧊 Freeze Timings — The Blob Freeze Cycle

SpellFreeze DurationCooldownEffective "Safe" Window
Ice Burst4 ticks (2.4s)10 ticks (6s)~2 ticks after freeze drops (can refreeze)
Ice Barrage4 ticks (2.4s)10 ticks (6s)Same as Burst — more damage + AoE is the advantage

Key insight: You cannot re-freeze the same blob until 10 ticks after the freeze expires. So the cycle is: Freeze (4 ticks safe) → Unfreeze → Wait 6 ticks → Refreeze. During those 6 unfrozen ticks, you must prayer flick. This is THE core mechanic of the Inferno.

⑥ Waves 51-66 — Mages, Blobs & The Gauntlet

Waves 51-66 are the "gauntlet" — the hardest pre-Zuk content in the Inferno. These waves feature 6-9 mobs simultaneously, including multiple mages and rangers with blobs. One mistake and you explode.

⑮ ☠️ Wave 56 — The Notorious "Double Mage + Double Ranger + Blob"

This is widely considered the hardest non-boss wave in the Inferno. Two mages, two rangers, and a blob — all attacking simultaneously. The strategy:

🎯 Surviving Wave 56

  1. PRE-WAVE PREPARATION: Before wave 55 ends, position yourself behind the central rock on the south side. Make sure your Prayer is at 80%+ and your HP is 90+.
  2. WAVE START: Immediately cast Ice Barrage on the blob to freeze it. Then cast Blood Barrage on the mage stack (they cluster together if you're positioned correctly).
  3. KILL ORDER: Mage A → Mage B → Ranger A → Ranger B → Blob. Do NOT deviate from this order.
  4. PRAYER FLICKING: With multiple mages and rangers alive, you must flick Protect from Magic + Protect from Range simultaneously (see Section 7 for flicking technique).
  5. BLOOD BARRIER: Use Blood Barrage on the pile when your HP drops below 60. This heals you for 25% of damage dealt and creates extra survivability buffer.

⑯ 🚨 Wave 61-65 — Healing & Resource Management

By wave 61, you should have: 50%+ Prayer remaining, 3+ brews, 2+ restores, and at least 1 ranging pot dose. If you have less than this, you are in danger. Consider using Eldritch staff spec now to top up prayer.

💡 Wave 66: The "Resource Drain" Trap

Wave 66 is intentionally designed by Jagex to drain your remaining supplies before Triple Jad. Don't fall for it. If you enter wave 66 with 6+ brews, consider tanking some hits (drink a brew if needed) rather than burning prayer flicking effort. You want to enter Triple Jad with 4+ brews and 2+ restores. ⑦ Prayer Flicking — Master the Tick

Prayer flicking is not optional in the Inferno. You must be able to flick at least Protect from Magic and Protect from Range on alternating ticks while simultaneously attacking and moving. Below is the complete flicking system used by Inferno speed-runners.

⑰ 🎯 Single-Target Flicking (One Mob)

When facing a single blob (the most common scenario), the flick pattern is straightforward:

Single Blob Flick Cycle

  1. Tick 1: Activate Protect from Magic → blob attacks with Range → you take 0 damage
  2. Tick 2: Deactivate Protect from Magic, activate Protect from Range → blob attacks with Magic → you take 0 damage
  3. Tick 3: Deactivate Protect from Range, activate Protect from Magic → cycle repeats
  4. Attack timing: On tick 2 (while Protect from Range is active), click your attack on the blob. Your attack goes off on tick 4, which is the same tick you switch back to Mage pray.

⑱ 🔄 Double Blob Flicking — The "No Prayer" Method

When TWO blobs attack simultaneously, you cannot protect against both. The solution: prayer flick with NO prayer active between their attacks.

  • Blob A attacks on tick 0 and tick 4 (every 4 ticks)
  • Blob B attacks on tick 2 and tick 6 (offset 2 ticks from Blob A)
  • Your prayer: Activate Range pray on tick 0 (blocks Blob A's Range hit) → Deactivate on tick 1 → Activate Mage pray on tick 2 (blocks Blob B's Mage hit) → Deactivate on tick 3 → Repeat
  • You take 0 damage from both blobs permanently, with only 2 prayer points drained every 4 ticks (instead of 4 points per tick if you kept both on).

⑲ 🧮 The "1-Tick Flick" for Mages + Rangers

When both a mage and a ranger target you simultaneously, you MUST flick both prayers within the same tick:

Step 1: Set Up Your Prayers

Click the Protect from Magic icon so it's highlighted. Click the Protect from Range icon right after. Both should be glowing.

Step 2: The One-Tick Switch

Right-click the Protect from Magic icon (turning it off) → immediately right-click the Protect from Range icon (keeping it on). This switches from "both on" to "Range only" in one tick. The mage's attack lands on this tick and you take 0 damage (because Protect from Magic was on for that tick).

Step 3: Back to Both

In the next tick, click Protect from Magic back on. Now both are active again. Rinse and repeat.

Practice this at the Fight Caves before entering the Inferno. It takes 2-3 hours of deliberate practice before it becomes second nature.

💡 Flicking With F-Key Shortcuts

Use F-Key shortcuts (enable in Settings → Controls): F1 = Combat Options (prayer tab), F2 = Inventory, F4 = Spellbook. Most top Inferno runners use F1 for prayer flicking. Set your quick prayer (default: `) to the most common flick set (Protect from Range + Rigour for mage-priority waves).

Blob management is the #1 reason players die in the Inferno — even more than Triple Jad or Zuk. Here is everything you need to know about controlling these pink demons.

㉑ 📏 Blob Positioning — Pathing Manipulation

Blobs have predictable pathing. You can manipulate where they move by controlling your own position:

  • Blobs always path toward the closest player. If you stand on the west side of the central rock, the blob will path west — forcing it to walk around the rock (giving you more time).
  • Blobs can be "stacked" behind each other. When two blobs are on the same tile, your Tbow/ACB can hit both with a single attack. This is called "blob stacking" — a key speed-run technique.
  • Never let a blob reach melee range. Blobs melee attack is a 1-tick (0.6s) attack that deals 25+ damage. If a blob reaches you, freeze it immediately and back up.

㉒ 🚫 Blob Regeneration — How to Prevent It

Blobs regenerate to full HP after ~20 seconds of not being attacked. This is devastating because it undoes all your progress. Prevention is simple:

🎯 Blob Regeneration Prevention

  • Always keep at least 1 damage on every blob. If you freeze a blob and kill other mobs, tag the frozen blob with a single ranged attack before it unfreezes. This resets its "time since last damage" counter.
  • If a blob does regenerate: Don't panic. It's only 30 HP. Freeze it again and kill it properly this time. The wasted time is annoying but not run-ending.
  • When 2+ blobs are alive: Freeze both, then kill one completely. Tag the other every 15 seconds with a ranged attack to prevent regeneration.
💡 The "Blob Rule" — Never Let Blobs Live Longer Than 15 Seconds

If a blob has been alive for more than 15 seconds and you haven't attacked it, you are playing incorrectly. Blobs are the lowest HP mob in the Inferno (30 HP). A single Tbow hit kills them. A single ACB hit + one more bolt kills them. Prioritize tagging blobs the moment they spawn. A dead blob does zero damage.

⑨ Wave 67 — Triple Jad Strategy

Wave 67 is the most famous moment in any Inferno run. Three TzTok-Jads simultaneously — each with their own attack pattern, each capable of one-shotting you. This is the "make or break" wave that ends most runs.

㉓ 🎯 The Setup — Entering Wave 67 Correctly

Before wave 67 begins, you must be in the right mental and inventory state:

✅ Pre-Triple Jad Checklist

  • ✓ HP: 95+ (brew up if below 90)
  • ✓ Prayer: 60+ points remaining
  • ✓ Position: Standing on the south-east tile of the central rock (the "Jad safespot" tile)
  • ✓ Supplies: 4+ brews, 2+ restores remaining
  • ✓ Range pot: Active (re-dose if needed)
  • ✓ Mentally ready: Say it out loud — "I will not panic. I will watch all three Jads. I will wait for safe windows to attack."

㉔ 👁️ Reading All Three Jads — The 3-2-1 Method

The most reliable technique for Triple Jad is the 3-2-1 method developed by the OSRS Inferno speedrunning community:

Step 1: Scan All Three

When the wave starts, immediately look at all three Jads. Do not attack yet. Just watch their animations for 3-4 seconds. Identify which one attacks first (their attacks are offset by ~2 ticks each).

Step 2: Prioritize by Attack Order

  • Jad A (first attacker) — This is your primary focus. Change prayer based on its animation.
  • Jad B (second attacker) — Immediately after switching prayer for Jad A, look at Jad B. If its attack is different from Jad A, you have 2 ticks to switch again.
  • Jad C (third attacker) — Same process. After handling Jad B, check Jad C.

Step 3: Attack in the Safe Window

After all three Jads have attacked and your prayer is correctly set, you have a 6-tick window (3.6 seconds) where ALL three Jads are in their attack cooldown. This is your attack window. Click one Jad, shoot it, then immediately return to scanning.

㉖ ⚔️ Sub-Strategy: Killing Jads One at a Time

Most first-time clearers try to kill all three Jads simultaneously. This is a mistake. The correct strategy is kill one Jad completely, then the second, then the third:

  • Jad 1: Kill it first (regardless of position). Fewer Jads = less to scan = easier.
  • Jad 2: Now you only have 2 Jads to watch. Much more manageable.
  • Jad 3: Single Jad — you have done this in the Fight Caves. Easy.

Important: When you kill a Jad, the healers from the dead Jad despawn. This means you only need to deal with the healers from the remaining Jads. Kill the healers on Jad 2 quickly before focusing on the kill.

💡 Triple Jad Audio Cue Hack

Turn up your game audio. Each Jad attack has a distinct sound queue — the ranged attack sounds like a "whoosh," the magic attack sounds like a "crackle." When multiple Jads attack simultaneously, listen for the sound rather than watching the animations. Many players find audio cues faster than visual ones for prayer switching.

⑩ TzKal-Zuk — Shields, Healers & Final Phase

You have survived Triple Jad. Now you face TzKal-Zuk — a massive, shield-wielding beast that is the final boss of the Inferno. The Zuk fight has three phases: Shield Phase, Healer Phase, and Final Phase. Understanding each phase is critical.

㉗ 🛡️ Phase 1: The Shield Phase (Zuk at 100%-80% HP)

Zuk starts behind a massive shield that rotates around him. You cannot damage Zuk directly until the shield moves away.

㉘ 🏃 Shield Movement Strategy

🎯 How to Attack Zuk Safely

  1. Identify shield position. When the wave starts, note where the shield is relative to Zuk (north, south, east, west).
  2. Move to the open side. Run to the side opposite the shield. For example, if the shield is on the east side, stand on the west side.
  3. Attack Zuk. You have ~6 ticks (3.6s) before the shield rotates to cover Zuk again. Shoot him 2-3 times.
  4. Move ahead of the shield. After your 3 shots, run to the next open position (e.g., if shield was east, it's now rotating north → you move south).
  5. Repeat. Keep rotating around Zuk, staying opposite the shield at all times.

㉙ 💊 Phase 2: The Healer Phase (Zuk at 80%-40% HP)

At 80% HP, Zuk spawns 3 Healers (similar to Jad's healers, but tougher). These healers will heal Zuk for ~15 HP per tick if they reach him.

  • Healer 1: Spawns north-east of Zuk. Paths directly toward him.
  • Healer 2: Spawns south-west of Zuk. Paths directly toward him.
  • Healer 3: Spawns east of Zuk. Takes a slight detour.

Critical rule: Kill ALL three healers before damaging Zuk again. If even one healer reaches Zuk, he will heal back above 80% HP and reset the phase — forcing you to deal with the shield again.

How to Kill Zuk Healers

Strategy A (Ranged): With Tbow, each healer takes 2-3 hits. Kill them in order of proximity: Healer 3 (east) → Healer 1 (north-east) → Healer 2 (south-west).

Strategy B (Melee): Some players use Dragon Claws or a Tentacle Whip to melee the healers. This is faster but riskier (you must step into Zuk's attack range). Only do this if you have high Defence and are confident in your prayer flicking.

Strategy C (Switch to ACB): If you are using Tbow, switch to Armadyl Crossbow + Ruby bolts for the healers. The faster attack speed (5 ticks vs 6 ticks) lets you kill them 20% faster.

㉚ 🔥 Phase 3: Final Phase (Zuk at 40%-0% HP)

At 40% HP, Zuk enrages. The shield disappears permanently. This is a burn phase — all defence, all offence.

  • Attack speed increases: Zuk attacks every 6 ticks instead of every 8 ticks.
  • Extra fire trails: Sets spawn twice as often — you must kill them within 2 ticks or they stack up and become unmanageable.
  • Special attack: Zuk spews lava across the southern half of the arena. Run north to avoid it.
  • Prayer: Keep Protect from Magic on (blocks fireballs). Switch to Protect from Range only when you see the spike attack animation (distinct 3-tick wind-up).
💡 Zuk Final Phase — The "No Mercy" Rule

In the final phase, do not stop attacking. Even if you need to eat or restore prayer, do it between attacks — never sacrifice a tick of DPS on Zuk when he's below 200 HP. The longer the fight goes, the more Sets spawn, the more healing Zuk gets, and the more likely you are to run out of supplies. Staying on Zuk and dodging his attacks is the winning strategy.

⑪ One-Tick Alternate — Advanced Movement

One-tick alternate (1t-alt) is the most advanced technique in the Inferno. It involves swapping between two sets of gear every single tick to maximise DPS while maintaining defensive stats. This technique is not required for a first Inferno cape — but it will dramatically improve your clear speed and resource efficiency.

㉛ 🔄 How 1t-Alt Works

The fundamental principle: In OSRS, you can change gear between attacks without losing a tick of DPS. By alternating between a "high defence" set (Justiciar) and a "high DPS" set (Masori/Blessed D'Hide), you effectively get both stats simultaneously.

TickActionGear Set Active

2Swap to JusticiarHigh Defence (Justiciar Faceguard/Chest/Legs)
3Monster attack landsDefence & Prayer active (you take minimal damage)
4Swap to DPS gearHigh DPS again

6Swap to JusticiarHigh Defence again

Net effect: You deal Tbow damage 2 out of every 5 ticks (40% attack uptime) while taking monster hits at Justiciar-level defence. Compared to wearing Justiciar full-time (attack uptime ~25%), this is a significant DPS increase.

💡 Should You Learn 1t-Alt for Your First Cape?

No. One-tick alternate is a speed-running technique for players aiming for sub-65 minute clears. For your first cape, wear JUST Justiciar full-time. The ~15% DPS loss is worth the 50% reduction in damage taken. Once you have the cape, you can learn 1t-alt for faster subsequent runs. First cape = survive. Second cape = speed.

㉝ 🎟️ What Are Item Bonds?

㉟ 🤝 Safety Tips for Item Bonding

⚠️ Never Get Scammed

  • Use reputable lenders only. Check the OSRS Subreddit's "Trusted Lenders" list (updated monthly). Do not trust random Discord DMs offering cheap bonds.

💡 Bond vs Buy: Which is Better?

Bond if: You have less than 2B GP total net worth and want to try Inferno without risking your bank. Bonding Tbow for 4-6 weeks (~70-100M GP) is cheaper than buying it (~1.1B GP) if you sell it back later.

Buy if: You have 2B+ GP net worth and will do multiple Inferno capes (main + alts) or want the item permanently. The Inferno items (Tbow, Kodai, Ancestral) hold their value well over time.

⑭ Inferno FAQs

What are the minimum requirements for the Inferno?

Fire Cape (required to enter), 90+ Ranged, 90+ Magic, 75+ Prayer (77+ for Rigour), 80+ Defence, 80+ Hitpoints. These are the absolute minimums — you will have a much harder time at these levels than at 95+.

How many attempts does the average player take?

The OSRS community estimates the average first cape takes 50-100 attempts. Players with extensive PvM experience (Inferno at other bosses, high-tier Raids) typically take 20-40 attempts. Players learning PvM from scratch can take 150+ attempts. Be patient — every attempt teaches you something.

Is Twisted Bow mandatory?

No, but it makes the Inferno significantly easier. Tbow is 30-40% more DPS than Armadyl Crossbow against Inferno mobs (which have very high Magic levels). Bow of Faerdhinen with full crystal armour is a viable middle-ground option, dealing ~85% of Tbow's DPS for ~20% of the cost.

Should I use Justiciar or Masori for Inferno?

For your first cape, use Justiciar. The extra Defence saves you from random one-shots and reduces chip damage by ~40%. For subsequent capes (speed runs), Masori + 1t-alt is better. If you can afford both, bring a 3-piece Justiciar swap for specific waves (56, 63, Zuk healers).

Can I practice Triple Jad before attempting the Inferno?

Yes! The "Jad Simulator" plugin on RuneLite lets you practice Triple Jad mechanics without risking any supplies. You can also practice in the Fight Caves by intentionally letting Jad healers stack (simulating multiple Jads). Many players also recommend "Inferno Trainer" — a third-party practice tool for waves 60-67.

What is the best prayer to use during Zuk?

Protect from Magic is the default (blocks Zuk's fireball attack, which hits 70 damage). Flick to Protect from Range only when you see Zuk's spike attack wind-up animation (a distinct 3-tick telegraph). Between Zuk's attacks (every 6-8 ticks), you can flash Rigour for extra DPS.

How do I deal with Zuk's healers?

Kill them before they reach Zuk. Three healers spawn at 80% HP. Use Tbow (2-3 hits each) or switch to ACB with Ruby bolts (faster attack speed). If a healer reaches Zuk, he heals to 81%+ and you must re-do the shield phase. Prioritize the eastern healer first (it has the furthest path but arrives fastest).

Is Inferno worth doing in 2026?

Yes. The Infernal Cape is BiS melee cape forever — no replacement has been announced or is expected. It provides +12 Strength, +12 Prayer, +8 all Defence. For melee-focused accounts, it is the single biggest DPS upgrade available after 99 Strength. Beyond the stats, the cape is a prestige symbol — wearing it in the GE or at any boss instantly signals your PvM competence.

What food should I bring? Brews or Sharks?

Saradomin Brews only. Do not bring Sharks or Dark Crabs. Brews provide +2 Defence per dose (stacks up to +12), which is critical for surviving mage/ranger combo attacks. The stat drain from brews is offset by Super Restore. A standard inventory has 8-10 brews and 4-5 restores — the 2:1 ratio gives you sustainable healing for 67 waves.

What happens if I die during the Inferno?

You lose everything in your inventory (brews, restores, ammo). Your equipped items are protected by normal death mechanics (3-5 items protected, depending on Skull status). If you have a Ring of Suffering (i), it will protect itself over other items. Death fees are ~100K GP to retrieve items from Death's Office. Your Inferno progress resets to wave 1 — there are no checkpoints.

Can I use the Inferno for money making?

Not reliably. The Inferno is not a money-making activity — it costs ~500K-800K GP per attempt in supplies. Even with perfect play and fast clears (sub-60 minutes), the cape itself is untradeable. There are no profitable drops during the waves. The Inferno is a prestige and gear achievement, not a money maker. However, once you have the cape, you unlock the Infernal Cape (saves you from ever buying a Fire Cape again).
