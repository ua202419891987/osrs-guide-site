OSRS PvM Guide 2026

① What Is PvM & Why Start Now

PvM (Player vs Monster) means fighting bosses — high-HP monsters with special attacks. Unlike safe-spotting (where you take zero damage), bossing requires active prayer management and understanding mechanics.

① Why PvM Is the Best Content in OSRS

🎮 5 Reasons to Start PvM Early

  • High GP per hour: Bosses drop valuable loot (2-5x more than skilling)
  • Unique rewards: Many boss drops are unobtainable elsewhere
  • Adrenaline: Real-time combat is way more exciting than AFK skilling
  • Progression: Each boss teaches skills you'll use at harder bosses
  • Community respect: Being a "boss man" is a OSRS achievement
💡 You're Ready for PvM When...

You have 43 Prayer (all Protect prayers) + 50+ Combat + 500K+ GP for gear. That's it! Don't wait until "end-game" — start with easy bosses NOW.

② Your First Boss: Obor (F2P) or Bryophyta (F2P)

If you're F2P (Free-to-Play), your first boss is Obor or Bryophyta. These are specifically designed for beginners!

② Obor — The #1 First Boss (F2P)

🧌 Obor Quick Facts

  • Location: Edgeville Dungeon (Wilderness section, need key)
  • Requirement: Kill 40 Hill Giants (they drop key halves)
  • Combat Level: 106 (but can be safespotted at low level!)
  • Drops: Rune armor, Big Bones, occassionally Obor's Club (50M+ GP!)

③ Bryophyta — Magic-Only Boss (F2P)

🧙 Bryophyta Quick Facts

  • Location: Forthard Jungle (Wilderness, need key)
  • Requirement: Kill 40 Moss Giants (key halves)
  • Combat Level: 123 (magic-only attacks — safespot with ranged!)
  • Drops: Rune items, occassionally Bryophyta's Essence (500K+ GP)

④ How to Get a Boss Key (Step-by-Step)

🔑 Key Half Mechanism

  1. Kill Hill Giants (for Obor) or Moss Giants (for Bryophyta)
  2. They drop "Giant Key (left half)" or "Giant Key (right half)"
  3. Use the two halves together → "Giant Key" (full key)
  4. Enter the Wilderness portion of Edgeville Dungeon → click Obor's Lair
💡 Wilderness Warning!

Obor/Bryophyta are in Wilderness (Level 7). PKers (player killers) CAN attack you. Bring ONLY what you can afford to lose (3-item protection with Protect Item prayer).

⑥ Ranged Setup (Safespotting Obor)

🏹 Budget Ranged Setup

  • Weapon: Magic Shortbow (~5K GP) + Rune arrows (~50 GP/arrow)
  • Armor: Green Dragonhide set (~80K GP)
  • Prayer: Protect from Missiles (40 Prayer required)
  • Strategy: Safespot Obor (stand behind an obstacle where he can't reach you)

⑦ What NOT to Bring (Beginner Mistakes)

❌ Don't Bring These to Your First Boss:

  • Expensive gear (>1M GP per item): You might die and lose it!
  • Full inventory of expensive food: 8-10 Shark is enough for Obor
  • No teleport: ALWAYS bring a Teleport tablet or runes for emergency escape

④ Boss Mechanics 101 — What You Need to Know

Every boss has special attacks and mechanics. Here's the 101 for beginners:

⑧ Understanding Attack Styles

⚔️ 3 Attack Styles (and How to Counter)

  • Melee: Boss attacks with melee → Protect from Melee prayer (43 Prayer)
  • Ranged: Boss attacks with projectiles → Protect from Missiles prayer (40 Prayer)
  • Magic: Boss attacks with magic → Protect from Magic prayer (43 Prayer)

⑨ Prayer Switching (Essential Skill!)

🛡️ How to Prayer Switch

Bosses often switch attack styles during the fight. You need to switch your Protect prayer to match! Example:

  1. Obor starts with Melee → Activate Protect from Melee
  2. Obor switches to Ranged (throws rocks) → QUICK! Switch to Protect from Missiles
  3. Obor switches back to Melee → Switch back to Protect from Melee

⑩ Recognizing Special Attacks

🎯 Common Boss Special Attacks

  • Area-of-Effect (AoE): Boss damages everything nearby → run away!
  • Charge-up: Boss stands still and charges → hit them to interrupt!
  • Summon minions: Boss spawns helpers → kill minions first (they hit hard!)
  • Enrage: Boss gets stronger as HP drops → bring more food for last 25% HP!

⑫ Pro Tips for Inventory Management

💡 Tip #1: Use Noted Food (if possible)

Some bosses allow you to use noted food on a bank chest nearby. This lets you stay longer without banking.

💡 Tip #2: Pre-pot before entering

Drink Super Combat potion BEFORE entering the boss room. This saves inventory space!

💡 Tip #3: Bring a spare weapon

If your weapon breaks (rare, but some bosses can disarm you), you'll need a backup!

⑥ Boss Progression Path — 5 Milestones for Beginners

Don't jump into hard bosses immediately! Follow this progression path:

⑬ Milestone 1: Obor / Bryophyta (F2P)

🧌 Your First Boss

Why start here: Easist bosses in the game, guaranteed loot, NO complex mechanics.
Goal: Kill 10 Obor / 10 Bryophyta to learn prayer switching.

⑭ Milestone 2: Chaos Elemental (Wilderness)

🔥 Slightly Harder

Why next: Teaches you to deal with random attack styles (Chaos Elemental switches EVERY attack style randomly).
Goal: Kill 5 Chaos Elementals (bring anti-poison!).

⑮ Milestone 3: Moss Giants / Greater Demons (Slayer)

🗡️ Slayer Bosses

Why next: These are "mini-bosses" you'll encounter during Slayer tasks. Good practice for multi-monster fights.
Goal: Complete 20 Slayer tasks (you'll naturally fight these).

⑯ Milestone 4: Sarachnis (Members)

🕷️ Your First "Real" Boss

Why next: Sarachnis is specifically designed as a beginner boss. Only 30 seconds away from a bank, telegroups, and drops Webweaver bow (great starter ranged weapon).
Goal: Kill 25 Sarachnis (learn to manage prayer + eat food efficiently).

⑰ Milestone 5: Barrows Brothers (Members)

💀 The Ultimate Beginner Challenge

Why last in beginner list: Barrows requires multiple kills per trip (6 brothers + chest run). Teaches resource management and multi-boss strategy.
Goal: Complete 50 Barrows runs (aim for 1 Barrows piece per 10 runs!).

💡 Don't Rush!

Each milestone teaches specific skills. Obor teaches prayer switching, Chaos Elemental teaches adaptability, Sarachnis teaches efficient resource usage. Master each before moving to the next!

⑦ What Happens When You Die at a Boss

Dying is NOT the end of the world in OSRS (unless you're in Hardcore Ironman mode!). Here's what happens:

⑱ Death Mechanics (Standard Ironman / Main Account)

🏠 What You Keep on Death (Standard Rules)

LocationItems KeptItems Lost
Non-Wilderness (Barrows, Sarachnis, etc.)3 most valuable items (with Protect Item prayer = 4 items)Everything else
Wilderness (Obor, Chaos Elemental)1 most valuable item (with Protect Item prayer = 2 items)Everything else

⑲ How to Recover Lost Items (Gravestone Mechanic)

⚰️ Gravestone System

  1. When you die, a gravestone appears where you died.
  2. You have 2 minutes to run back and "repair" it (pay 50K-200K GP depending on items lost).
  3. If you repair in time → get ALL items back!
  4. If you fail to repair → items go to Death's Office (can retrieve later for a fee).

⑳ Death's Office — Retrieving Lost Items

💡 Don't Panic If You Die!

Even if you don't make it back in 2 minutes, your items go to Death's Office (NPC in Lumbridge). You can retrieve them later for a fee (starting at 50K GP). You WON'T lose items permanently unless you wait MONTHS.

⑧ PvM FAQ — Common Beginner Questions

I keep dying at Obor. What am I doing wrong?

Most likely: You're not using Protect prayers consistently. Obor switches between Melee and Ranged — you MUST switch your prayer. Also, bring more food (10-12 Shark minimum for first attempts).

Do I need 70 Attack/Strength to start PvM?

NO! You can start PvM at 50 Combat with Dragon weapons. Attack/Strength levels help you kill faster, but they're NOT required to start learning bosses.

What's the best boss for earning my first 10M GP?

Barrows (after you learn it) gives ~300-800K GP per hour depending on loot. Sarachnis is safer but slower (~200-400K GP/hour). Start with Barrows once you're comfortable with prayer switching!

Can I PvM on an Ironman account?

YES! In fact, Ironman must PvM to get boss drops (can't buy them from GE). Start with Obor/Bryophyta — they're Ironman-friendly because keys are untradeable (no one can steal your key!).

How do I learn boss mechanics without dying repeatedly?

Watch YouTube tutorials FIRST! Search " beginner guide" before attempting. Also, use lower-tier gear for your first 5 kills (if you die, you lose less GP). Once you understand the mechanics, switch to better gear for faster kills.
