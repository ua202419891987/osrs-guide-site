1. Welcome Back — What You Missed (2025→2026)

Coming back to OSRS after a long break can feel overwhelming. The game you remember is still there, but a lot has changed. This section gives you a complete overview of everything that's new, so you can prioritize what matters most to your account.

🚢 Sailing — The First New Skill Since 2007

The biggest addition to OSRS in nearly two decades is Sailing, a brand-new skill that launched in early 2026 after years of community polls and development. Sailing is a non-combat Artisan skill that lets you build and captain your own ship, explore uncharted waters, and access new areas of the map that were previously unreachable.

What Sailing Actually Does

  • Ship Building: Construct your own ship at the Port of your choice, customizing hull, sails, and equipment
  • Navigation: Use charts and instruments to navigate to new islands and coastal destinations
  • Resource Gathering: Fish, mine, and chop trees on remote islands inaccessible by other means
  • Combat at Sea: Engage in naval combat with pirate ships and sea monsters
  • New Transportation: Fast travel between distant ports once you've charted the routes
  • Unique Rewards: Unlock the ability to teleport your ship to any discovered port, access members-only islands, and gain combat advantages from ship-based buffs

Sailing integrates with existing skills in meaningful ways: higher Construction levels help you build better ships, Fishing pairs with Sailing for offshore fishing spots, and Agility helps with ship maneuvers. At level 70 Sailing, you unlock the ability to use your ship as a Player-Owned House on water — essentially a mobile POH that can dock at any port.

💡 Do You Need to Train Sailing Immediately?

Not necessarily. If you're coming back to OSRS primarily for PvM, bossing, or PvP, Sailing is optional for now. However, if you want to access new content, complete new quests, or use the new fast-travel system, you'll need at least level 30-50 Sailing. Consider it a medium-priority skill for returning players.

🗺️ Varlamore — The Third Major Region Expansion

Following the successful launches of Zeah (2016) and Great Kourend (2018), Varlamore represents the largest geographical expansion since the game's 2007 revival. This new continent, located far to the west of the main map, introduced entirely new cities, questlines, bosses, and training methods.

Varlamore is divided into three main regions, each with its own unique theme and content:

① The Capital City — Civitas illa Fortis

The sprawling capital of Varlamore, featuring a Roman-inspired aesthetic. Here you'll find new shops, a colosseum-style mini-game, and the Varlamore Museum which offers XP lamps for identifying artifacts (similar to Fossil Island). The city also hosts the new mastery training system for combat — more on that in the Combat Achievements section.

② The Southern Wildlands

A dangerous, untamed region perfect for mid-to-high-level Slayer training. New monsters here drop unique loot including the Ultor pendant (melee ring upgrade) and materials for the new Varlamore armor sets. The area also features the new herb patches that are more efficient than traditional farming.

③ The Coastal Provinces

These areas integrate with the Sailing skill, serving as ports for your ship. You can complete new quests here that unlock Sailing experience rewards and access to exclusive fishing spots that yield higher-tier fish than anywhere else in the game.

🌙 Blood Moon Rises — Grandmaster Quest (June 30, 2026)

Mark your calendar: June 30, 2026 marks the release of Blood Moon Rises, the sixth and final Grandmaster quest in the Elder God Wars storyline. This quest concludes the narrative arc that began with Desperate Times and continued through City of Senntisten, Azzanadra's Quest, Extinction, and Eye of Het.

🔓 Prerequisites (Start Preparing Now)

Blood Moon Rises has extremely high requirements. If you want to experience the conclusion on release day, you need to prepare in advance:

  • Quests: Completion of all previous Elder God Wars quests (5 Grandmaster + several Master quests)
  • Skills: 80+ in most combat skills, 70+ in key support skills (Construction, Prayer, Herblore)
  • Quest Points: 280+ required (you need to have completed almost every quest in the game)
  • Combat Achievements: Some requirements tie into the new Combat Achievement system (see Section 4)
  • Gear: High-tier gear recommended — at minimum, Barrows equipment with dragon weapons

The quest is expected to take 4-6 hours for an experienced player and unlocks access to the Blood Moon Temple — a new high-level PvM area with unique rewards including the Blood Moon armor set and a powerful new melee weapon that rivals the Scythe of Vitur in certain situations.

🏆 Combat Achievements System

Combat Achievements (CA) is a new progression system that rewards you for accomplishing specific combat feats. Think of it like Diary tasks but for PvM. Completing tasks earns you Combat Achievement Points which can be spent on unique rewards including upgrade kits for existing gear, new teleport unlock, and prestige titles.

The system is divided into tiers:

  • Easy: Kill 50 of each common monster (completed naturally through Slayer)
  • Medium: Kill bosses with specific restrictions (e.g., "Kill Zulrah without taking damage from snakelings")
  • Hard: More demanding challenges (e.g., "Kill Telos at 500% enrage")
  • Elite: Endgame challenges for the most dedicated PvMers
  • Master: The ultimate tests — only a few hundred players worldwide have completed these
💡 Combat Achievements Are Optional But Rewarding

You don't need to engage with CAs to enjoy OSRS. However, the rewards are genuinely useful — the upgrade kits let you customize gear appearance and add small stat boosts. If you're returning and planning to get into bossing, CAs provide a structured way to improve your PvM skills.

👹 New Bosses Since You've Been Gone

If you haven't played since before 2025, here's a quick rundown of the new bosses you can expect to encounter:

① Amoxliatl (Mid-Level Boss)

Located in the Varlamore Wildlands. This serpent boss is perfect for mid-level players (90+ combat, 70+ gear). Drops the Amoxliatl tail used to craft a powerful ranged weapon. Estimated kill time: 2-3 minutes for experienced players.

② The Leviathan (High-Level Boss)

A massive sea creature encountered through the Sailing skill. Requires 65+ Sailing to access. This boss introduced a new mechanic-heavy fight that rewards good positioning and timing over raw DPS. Drops the Tidal Eel used for Sailing upgrades.

③ Hueycoatl (Solo/Group Boss)

A raid-style boss that can be fought solo or in groups of up to 5 players. Introduced in late 2025, this boss features a multi-phase fight with distinct mechanics in each phase. The Hueycoatl scales drop is used to craft the new draconic armor set.

🎯 What Category Are You In?

  • Rusty: You haven't played in 3+ years, your stats are low, and you're essentially starting from scratch. Don't worry — this guide has a track specifically for you.
  • Mid-Game: You have decent stats but your gear and quest completion are outdated. You remember the basics but need to relearn new systems. This is the most common returning player profile.

📊 Step 2: Check Your Adventure Log

Your Adventure Log (accessed via the game interface) shows your play history and recent activity. This is incredibly useful for returning players because it tells you:

  • When you last logged in (helps you figure out what updates you missed)
  • Which quests you've completed (compare against the current quest list)
  • Your Diary completion status (very important — Diaries unlock teleports and gear upgrades)
  • Your Combat Achievement progress (if you've logged in since late 2025)

To access your Adventure Log: Click the floppy disk icon (bottom-right of game interface) → Select Adventure Log. Alternatively, use the command ::adventure in the chat box.

🎯 Step 3: Identify Your Priority Gaps

Based on your self-assessment, here are the most common gaps for returning players and how to prioritize filling them:

Priority 1: Quest Completion (Highest ROI)

Quests unlock everything — gear, areas, transportation, and XP rewards. If you have < 150 Quest Points, drop everything and quest. Use the Optimal Quest Guide on the OSRS Wiki to efficiently reach 150+ QP. This alone can boost your combat stats by 20-30 levels through quest rewards.

Priority 2: Diary Completion (Huge QoL Unlocks)

Achievement Diaries unlock teleport items, XP rewards, and gear upgrades. The Kandarin Diary (Seers' Village) gives a teleport to Camelot. The Desert Diary gives access to the Nardah teleport. The Fremennik Diary gives a teleport to Rellekka. These save hours of travel time over your play session.

Priority 3: Gear Upgrade (Immediate Power Boost)

Gear that was "high-tier" in 2020-2022 might be medium-tier now. Check the current BIS (Best In Slot) guides for your combat style. Often, you can upgrade your entire gear set for < 10M GP using current Grand Exchange prices. The meta shifts over time as new items are released, so what you remember as "best" might not be anymore.

Priority 4: New Skill Training (Sailing)

If you're Mid-Game or High-Level, you can start Sailing at any time. There's no "required" level for most content yet, but having 30-50 Sailing makes accessing new areas much easier. Training Sailing also provides passive XP in other skills (Fishing, Construction, Agility) through the ship-building process.

3. 30-Day Catch-Up Roadmap

This section provides a week-by-week plan to get your account from "I have no idea what I'm doing" to "I'm ready for endgame content." The roadmap assumes you can play 1-2 hours per day on average, with more on weekends.

🎯 Roadmap Overview

  • Week 1: Foundation — Relearn the interface, complete key quests, set up your account
  • Week 2: Money Making — Establish a reliable GP/hour income to fund your progression
  • Week 3: Skill Gaps — Fill in missing levels for quests and gear requirements
  • Week 4: Endgame Prep — Prepare for new content (Blood Moon Rises, Sailing, bosses)

📅 Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-7)

Goal: Reacquire basic competence and complete the "Gateway Quests" that unlock everything else.

Day 1-2: Interface Re-familiarization

Spend your first session just clicking around. OSRS's interface hasn't changed dramatically, but there are new panels (Sailing, Combat Achievements). Make sure you can:

  • Navigate to the Grand Exchange (use the GE teleport in your house if you have one, or walk from Varrock)
  • Access your Bank efficiently (learn the new Bank tutorial if prompted)
  • Set up Action Bars (if you use Revolution/EOC-style combat — note: OSRS is tick-based, not action-bar-based like RS3)
  • Configure your Settings (turn off "FPS limit" if you have a good PC, enable "Entity Hider" to reduce clutter)

Day 3-5: Complete "Gateway Quests"

These quests are mandatory for any progressing account. They unlock teleports, gear, and access to training areas:

  1. Tree Gnome Village — Unlocks Spirit Tree network (fast travel)
  2. Grand Tree — Unlocks Gnome Agility Course (early Agility XP)
  3. Watchtower — Unlocks Watchtower teleport (Yanille)
  4. Fairy Tale Part I & II — Unlocks Fairy Ring network (best fast-travel in the game)
  5. Lost City — Required for Fairy Rings
  6. Underground Pass — Required for Regicide (unlocks Elf area)

Pro tip: Use the OSRS Wiki Quest Planner (external tool) to optimize your quest order. It tells you the most efficient path to unlock whatever you need.

Day 6-7: First Major Gear Upgrade

Use your existing GP (or make a quick 500K-1M) to buy Barrows equipment. A full set of Ahrim's (Magic), Karil's (Ranged), or Dharok's/Guthan's (Melee) will immediately make you 2-3x more effective in combat. Barrows gear is cheap now — a full set costs < 5M GP total.

Goal: Establish a reliable income stream of 500K-1M GP per hour. You'll need GP for gear upgrades, supplies, and Sailing ship construction.

📈 Week 3: Skill Gaps (Days 15-21)

Goal: Get your skills to the point where you can attempt mid-to-high-level content. Most quests and Diaries have skill requirements — this week fills those gaps.

Priority Skills to Train

  1. Prayer (43 → 70+): If you don't have Piety yet, get 70 Prayer. Piety adds +23% Melee accuracy and damage. Use Dragon Bones at a Gilded Altar (see Prayer guide).
  2. Herblore (50+): Unlocks Super combat potions (boost Atk/Str/Def by 5+ levels) and Prayer potions. Buy potions from GE if training is too expensive.
  3. Agility (50+): Unlocks Agility shortcuts and the Agility Pyramid (good XP). 70 Agility unlocks the Werewolf Agility Course (fastest XP until 80).
  4. Magic (75+): Required for teleport spells (Varrock, Lumbridge, Falador, etc.) and High Alchemy (turn junk items into GP).
  5. Construction (50+): Unlocks the mounted Glory (infinite teleport to Edgeville) and a resettable POH teleport.
💡 The "Lazy" Way to Train Skills

If you have GP but not much time, buy your way to higher levels. Herblore, Construction, and Prayer can all be power-leveled by buying supplies from the GE and grinding them out in one session. 70 Prayer costs ~10M GP but takes only 4-6 hours at a Gilded Altar. 75 Herblore costs ~15M GP but unlocks every potion in the game. Prioritize buying time over saving GP if you're a returning player with limited play time.

🚀 Week 4: Endgame Prep (Days 22-30)

Goal: Prepare for Blood Moon Rises (June 30) and other new content. By now, you should have:

  • Combat stats 80+ (ideally 90+)
  • Quest Points 200+ (working toward 280 for Blood Moon Rises)
  • GP buffer of 10M+ (for supplies and gear upgrades)
  • Barrows gear or better on all combat styles

Week 4 Action Plan

  1. Complete Remaining Quests: Focus on Grandmaster and Master quests. Use the Wiki's quest list sorted by difficulty.
  2. Start Sailing: Begin training Sailing by building your first ship. Even 20-30 Sailing is enough to access new coastal areas.
  3. Attempt Mid-Level Bosses: Try Amoxliatl (Varlamore) or God Wars Dungeon (GWD). These are the "gateway bosses" to endgame PvM.
  4. Join a Clan: Returning players often feel isolated. Joining an active OSRS clan gives you access to clan wars, group bossing, and advice from experienced players. Look for clans recruiting in the "Clan Recruitment" forum on the OSRS official site.
  5. Set Up Hotkeys: If you haven't already, configure F-keys (F1-F12) to switch between game tabs quickly. This is standard practice for efficient players.

4. Key Systems That Didn't Exist Before

If you've been gone since before 2025, several entirely new systems have been added to the game. This section explains each one in detail so you're not confused when you encounter them.

🏆 Combat Achievements (CA) — Deep Dive

We mentioned CAs briefly in Section 1, but they deserve a full explanation because they're now a core part of the PvM progression system.

How Combat Achievements Work

  • Access: Talk to Konar或半段 (your Slayer master in the Mountains) or check the Combat Achievements tab (new interface button).
  • Tasks: Each boss and monster has a set of tasks. Example for Zulrah: "Kill Zulrah 10 times" (Easy), "Kill Zulrah without taking damage from snakelings" (Medium), "Kill Zulrah in under 1:30" (Hard).
  • Rewards: Earn Combat Achievement Points for each completed task. Spend points at the Combat Achievements Rewards Shop (located in Falador, near the castle).
  • Tiers: As you complete tasks, you progress through tiers (Easy → Medium → Hard → Elite → Master). Each tier unlocks new rewards.

Key Rewards Worth Grinding For:

  • Upgrade Kits: Cosmetic upgrades for gear (e.g., make your Dragon boots look like Rune boots). Purely aesthetic but cool for flexing.
  • Teleport Unlocks: Unlock direct teleports to boss lairs (e.g., teleport directly to Zulrah's shrine). Saves inventory space (no need for teleport tablets).
  • Prestige Titles: Show off your PvM skill with titles like "the Untouchable" (completed all Hard CAs) or "the Godlike" (completed all Master CAs).
  • Gear Upgrades: Some rewards are actual stat upgrades — e.g., the Berserker ring (i) upgrade requires specific Combat Achievements.
💡 Combat Achievements Are a Great Way to Learn PvM

If you're returning and want to get better at bossing, follow the CA tasks in order. They're designed to teach you boss mechanics progressively. By the time you finish all Easy and Medium tasks for a boss, you'll be competent enough to attempt the Hard tasks. It's like having a built-in PvM tutorial.

⛪ Plague's End Revisited & New Area Content

Plague's End (released in 2014) was the capstone quest that unlocked Prifddinas, the Elven capital city. Since then, multiple updates have expanded the Prifddinas content:

New in Prifddinas (2025-2026)

  • Enhanced Crystal Keys: Dropped by enhanced bosses in the Elven lands. Used to open Crystal Chests that contain high-tier loot.
  • Crystal Skill Training: New training methods for Agility, Woodcutting, and Mining in the Prifddinas area. These are AFK-friendly and offer competitive XP rates.
  • The Killing of a King (Mini-Quest): A new Master-level quest that continues the Elven storyline. Unlocks access to new crystal gear that rivals Barrows equipment.

5. What Stayed The Same

With all this talk of new content, you might be worried that everything has changed and you need to relearn the entire game. Good news: The core OSRS experience is largely unchanged. This section will reassure you that the game you loved is still there.

✅ The Core Gameplay Loop Is Identical

At its heart, OSRS is still:

  • Click to move, click to attack, click to interact. The point-and-click interface hasn't changed.
  • Talk to NPCs to start quests. No quest markers, no guiding arrows — you read dialogue and figure it out (just like old times).
  • Grind skills to level up. The XP curves are the same. 99 in any skill still requires the same amount of XP (13,034,431 XP from level 1).
  • Die and keep your stuff (mostly). The "three-item protect" rule in Dangerous Death still applies (with some new exceptions for Sailing).
  • Trade with other players at the GE. The Grand Exchange works exactly the same way.

🎮 What Still Works (Your Old Strategies Are Still Valid)

  • Melee Training: Killing cows for hides, Hill Giants for Big Bones, and Slayer for XP are still optimal methods.
  • Ranged Training: Cannoning at Rock Crabs or Sand Crabs is still the fastest Ranged XP for mid-levels.
  • Magic Training: High Alching junk items and bursting Dust Devils are still top-tier methods.
  • Money Making: Flipping items at the GE, farming herbs, and bossing are still reliable income sources.
  • Bossing: The "big three" bosses (Zulrah, Vorkath, GWD) are still meta for mid-to-high-level players.
  • Quest Order: The "Optimal Quest Guide" on the Wiki is still accurate — quest requirements haven't changed.

🛡️ The "Old School" Feel Is Preserved

Jagex has been very careful not to change the fundamental OSRS experience. Unlike RuneScape 3 (which introduced action bars, Revolution combat, and modern MMO features), OSRS remains:

  • Tick-based: Everything happens in 0.6-second game ticks. Timing your actions to the tick is still a core skill.
  • Manual: You control every action. There's no "auto-attack" or "assist mode." You eat food manually, you drink potions manually, you activate prayers manually.
  • Punishing: Death still has consequences. High-level PvM still requires learning mechanics. There are no "easy mode" shortcuts.
  • Community-Driven: Major updates still go through player polls. If the community votes "No," Jagex doesn't implement it. This is why OSRS hasn't "changed too much."
💡 You'll Feel at Home Faster Than You Think

Most returning players report that after 2-3 play sessions, their muscle memory kicks in and OSRS feels like "riding a bicycle." The new content (Sailing, Varlamore, CAs) is optional — you can engage with it at your own pace. If you want to ignore Sailing and just do Slayer, you absolutely can. The game accommodates both playstyles.

🗺️ The Map Is Mostly the Same (With Nice Additions)

The "old world" (Misthalin, Asgarnia, Kandarin, etc.) is unchanged. Varrock, Falador, Lumbridge, Draynor, Edgeville — they're all exactly where you remember them. The new content (Varlamore, Sailing islands) is added as "new areas" rather than replacing old areas.

This means:

  • Your mental map of Gielinor is still accurate.
  • You can still use old teleport strategies (Edgeville for Wilderness access, Varrock for GE, Falador for Mining).
  • Nostalgia spots (your favorite training area, your first quest location) are still there, unchanged.

The only "disorienting" change is that the world map is bigger now (due to Varlamore and Sailing islands). But you can simply ignore the new areas until you're ready to explore them. The game doesn't force you to go to Varlamore.

6. FAQ for Returning Players

This section answers the most common questions returning players have. If your question isn't here, ask in the comments or check the OSRS subreddit (/r/2007scape).

I haven't played in 5+ years. Is it worth coming back?

Yes, absolutely. OSRS in 2026 is the best version of the game ever. The new content (Sailing, Varlamore, Blood Moon Rises) is high-quality and respects the "old school" philosophy. The community is more active than ever (averaging 80K-120K concurrent players). And because OSRS is subscription-based ($11/month), the player base is committed and friendly. Your old account is still there, exactly as you left it.

Do I need to pay for membership to enjoy the new content?

Yes. Sailing, Varlamore, Blood Moon Rises, and Combat Achievements are all members-only content. However, F2P has also received some updates (new tutorial, improved UI). If you're on the fence, subscribe for one month ($11) and see how you feel. Most returning players find that one month isn't enough and happily resubscribe.

My account was hacked / I forgot my password. Can I recover it?

Probably. Jagex has a good account recovery system. Go to the Jagex website → "Recover Account" → fill out as much information as you can (original email, creation date, past passwords, etc.). If you can prove the account is yours, they'll help you recover it. Tip: Link your account to a Jagex Account (new in 2025) for better security. Jagex Accounts use 2FA and are much harder to hack.

What's the fastest way to catch up if I only have 1 hour per day?

Prioritize Quests and Diaries. These give instant rewards (XP lamps, teleports, gear) that would take hours to obtain through grinding. With 1 hour/day, you can complete 1-2 quests per day. In 2 weeks, you'll have unlocked most quality-of-life features. Also, focus on passive money makers (Herb runs, Farming) that don't require active play time.

Should I start a new account or continue my old one?

Continue your old account. The "catch-up" time is much shorter than you think — most returning players are "mid-game ready" within 2-3 weeks. Starting a new account means redoing Early Game (which can be slow and boring). However, if your old account was very low-level (total level < 500) and you don't have emotional attachment to it, a fresh start can be fun and nostalgic. The choice is yours.

Is OSRS pay-to-win (P2W) now?

No. OSRS has no pay-to-win mechanics. You can't buy XP, items, or advantages with real money. The only "microtransaction" is the membership subscription (required for members' content). Bonds (in-game items that grant membership) can be bought with GP, but they're traded on the GE like any other item — no real-money advantage. OSRS is widely considered one of the least P2W MMOs on the market.

What's the "meta" for [Combat Style] in 2026?

The meta shifts with each update, so check current guides (like the ones on this site!). As of early 2026:

  • Melee: Abyssal Whip → Dragon Defender → Barrows gloves is still the standard mid-game setup. For endgame, Scythe of Vitur (raid drop) or the new Blood Moon melee weapon (post-June 30).
  • Ranged: Twisted Bow (raid drop) is still BIS for high-level content. For mid-game, Dragon Crossbow + Rune arrows or Blowpipe (Zulrah drop).
  • Magic: Trident of the Swamp (Barrows) or Sanguinesti Staff (raid drop). The new Blood Moon magic weapon (post-June 30) might challenge these.

How do I meet other returning players?

Join a clan. Clans are the best way to make friends in OSRS. Look for clans that specify "Returning Players Welcome" or "Casual/Social." You can also post on the OSRS subreddit (/r/2007scape) — there's a weekly "Returning Player" thread where you can find people to play with. Finally, in-game chat (especially in popular areas like GE, Fort Forinthry (new), and Varlamore) is surprisingly friendly.

I'm overwhelmed by all the new content. Where do I start?

Start with what you know. Do a quest you remember enjoying. Kill a monster you used to farm. Visit a location you have fond memories of. Once you feel comfortable, try one new thing at a time. Maybe do one Varlamore quest. Maybe try building a Sailing ship. Don't pressure yourself to "experience everything" in your first week back. OSRS is a marathon, not a sprint. Enjoy the journey of rediscovery.
