📱 OSRS Android 32-Bit Support Is Ending 2026 — What It Means & How to Keep Playing

Jagex is dropping 32-bit Android support for Old School RuneScape Mobile. If you play on an older phone, the app may stop updating — or stop opening — entirely. This beginner-friendly guide explains exactly what changed, how to check whether your device is affected, and every realistic way to keep playing Gielinor.

1. 📱 What the 32-Bit Deprecation Actually Means

Every Android app is built for a specific processor architecture. Think of "32-bit" and "64-bit" as two different "languages" a phone's brain understands. Older phones speak only 32-bit (technically called armeabi-v7a); newer phones speak 64-bit (arm64-v8a / aarch64) and can also understand 32-bit apps for backwards compatibility.

Jagex has announced that OSRS Mobile will no longer ship a 32-bit version. From the cut-off date forward, the app on the Google Play Store is 64-bit only. If your phone can only run 32-bit software, one of two things happens:

  • The Play Store hides the update — you simply can't install new versions, so the app slowly falls behind and eventually won't launch or connect.
  • The app refuses to open — on some devices it crashes on startup or shows a "your device isn't compatible" message.

Why would Jagex do this? Two reasons. First, Google itself requires 64-bit apps on the Play Store, so 32-bit builds are dead weight. Second, maintaining two versions doubles testing work for almost no audience. Dropping 32-bit lets the mobile team focus on the 64-bit experience most players use.

💡 The One Thing to Remember

This is a device limitation, not an account problem. Your login, your bank, your quest progress, your membership — all of it lives on Jagex's servers, not on your phone. Nothing is deleted and you are not banned. You just need a compatible device (or another way to play) to reach your account.

2. 🔍 Who Is Affected (Older Phones)

In plain terms: if your phone is from roughly 2020 or later, you are almost certainly fine. The 32-bit-only era ended years ago for mainstream devices. The players who hit this wall are mostly on older or low-cost handsets.

3. 🛠️ How to Check Your Device Architecture (64 vs 32 Bit)

You do not need to be a tech expert. Pick the method that is easiest for you — any one of them gives you a definitive answer.

③ Method Comparison

MethodWhat You DoWhat "Safe" Looks LikeEffort
Devcheck / CPU-Z / AIDA64 (free app)Install one, open it, tap "CPU" or "System"Shows ARM64 or aarch64⭐ Easiest — 60 seconds
Play Store listingSearch "Old School RuneScape", open the app pageSays "Install" or "Update" (not "not compatible")⭐ Easiest — 30 seconds
Android "About phone"Settings → About phone → look for Processor / KernelMentions 64-bit or an arm64 chip⭐⭐ Varies by brand
Try to launch the appOpen OSRS Mobile after the cut-offGame loads to the login screen⭐ Only confirms after the fact

④ Step-by-Step: The Free-App Method (Recommended)

Step 1 — Install a hardware-info app

On your phone, open the Google Play Store and install Devcheck (or CPU-Z / AIDA64 — any of them work). These are free and read-only; they do not change anything on your device.

Step 2 — Open it and find the CPU section

In Devcheck, tap the System or Hardware tab. Look for a line labeled Instruction Set, CPU Architecture, or ABI.

Step 3 — Read the result

If it says arm64, ARM64-v8a, or aarch64 → 🟢 you are 64-bit and safe. If it says armeabi-v7a or ARMv7 → 🔴 you are 32-bit only and affected.

💡 Don't See "64-bit" Anywhere?

Some phone makers hide the architecture in Settings. If "About phone" only shows a model name, just use the Devcheck method above — it reads the real chip directly and removes all doubt. When in doubt, the Play Store "not compatible" message is also a perfectly valid yes/no answer.

5. 📱 Option 1 — Get a 64-Bit Android Device

If you love playing OSRS on the go and your current phone is 32-bit-only, the cleanest fix is a 64-bit handset. You do not need a flagship.

  • New budget phones are fine: Almost any new Android phone sold in 2024–2026 (Samsung A-series, Moto G, Pixel "a", Redmi/POCO, OnePlus Nord) is 64-bit. Even the cheapest current models qualify.
  • Used is great value: A second-hand 2020+ phone from a reputable seller is often under $100–$150 and runs OSRS Mobile perfectly.
  • Check before you buy: Use the Devcheck method (Section 3) on the device, or confirm the model supports 64-bit ARM online.
💡 Minimum Specs That Feel Good

OSRS is light, but for smooth gameplay aim for at least 3–4 GB RAM and a phone from 2019 or newer. You do not need a gaming phone — a typical mid-range device runs the mobile client at a stable frame rate.

6. 🍎 Option 2 — Play on iPhone, iPad, or Another Device

iOS is completely unaffected by the Android 32-bit change. If you (or a family member) have an iPhone or iPad, just install Old School RuneScape from the App Store and log in. The iOS app is 64-bit and fully supported.

  • iPhone / iPad: Download from the App Store, sign in with your Jagex account, and you are back in Gielinor. No architecture check needed — all modern iOS devices are 64-bit.
  • Another Android phone: Borrow or use a spare 64-bit Android phone (verify with Devcheck). Install from the Play Store and log in.
  • A shared family tablet: Many Android tablets are 64-bit; confirm with the method in Section 3, then install and play.

This is the fastest $0 solution if a compatible device is already in your home. Your account is cloud-based, so switching devices is instant — no transfer, no wait.

7. 💻 Option 3 — Emulators & Cloud Gaming on PC

If you have a Windows, Mac, or Linux computer, you can play OSRS on a big screen for free — no phone required.

⑦ A. The Official Desktop Client & RuneLite (Best for PC)

The simplest path: download the official OSRS client (or RuneLite, the community-favorite launcher) on your computer. Both are 64-bit, free, and run the full game with keyboard + mouse. This is what most "desktop" players use every day.

⑧ B. Android Emulators (Run the Mobile App on Your Computer)

Want the mobile experience on a computer? An Android emulator creates a 64-bit virtual phone on your PC, then you install OSRS Mobile from the Play Store inside it:

  • BlueStacks — the most beginner-friendly emulator; one-click install, then search OSRS in its Play Store.
  • LDPlayer / MuMu Player — lightweight emulators popular for mobile games; good performance on modest PCs.
  • Android Studio (AVD) — the official Google emulator; more technical, but rock-solid if you are comfortable with dev tools.

⚠️ Emulator Setup in 4 Steps

  1. Download and install an emulator (BlueStacks recommended for beginners).
  2. Sign in with a Google account inside the emulator.
  3. Open the Play Store, install Old School RuneScape.
  4. Launch it and log in with your Jagex account.

⑨ C. Cloud Gaming & Remote Play

If your own computer is weak, cloud gaming services can run the game on a remote server and stream it to you. Some players also use remote desktop apps to control a home PC from a tablet. These need a stable internet connection but eliminate the device problem entirely — the game runs "in the cloud" on 64-bit hardware.

🔒 Stay Safe When Switching Devices

Whenever you log in on a new phone, emulator, or PC, enable Jagex Account 2-step verification (2SV) first. Moving between devices is exactly when account thieves strike. See our Account Security Guide for a step-by-step setup.

9. 💡 Beginner Tips to Keep Playing Smoothly

Whichever option you choose, a few habits make the transition painless:

  • Link your Jagex Account (not a character password): Modern Jagex Accounts sync across every device. Once linked, you log in the same way on phone, tablet, or PC.
  • Turn on 2-step verification before you switch devices: It takes five minutes and stops almost all hijacking attempts. Our Anti-Scam Guide shows the common traps to avoid.
  • Keep your membership wherever you bought it: Membership is tied to your account, not your device. No need to re-buy when you change phones.
  • Try the desktop client free: Even if you plan to buy a new phone later, install RuneLite now so you never miss a day of progress.
🤝 You Are Not Losing Anything

We cannot say this enough: the deprecation affects hardware, not your account. Your stats, GP, items, and pets are safe on Jagex's servers. Pick any working device and you will pick up exactly where you left off.

10. ❓ FAQs

Will I lose my account, bank, or membership if my phone is 32-bit?

No. Absolutely not. Your account data lives on Jagex's servers, not on your phone. The 32-bit change only stops the app from running on that specific device. Log in from any supported device (64-bit Android, iPhone, PC) and everything is intact. Your membership is attached to the account and keeps working too.

How do I know for sure if my phone is 32-bit or 64-bit?

Install a free app like Devcheck or CPU-Z, open it, and check the CPU / System tab for the "Instruction Set" or "ABI" line. arm64 / aarch64 = 64-bit (safe). armeabi-v7a / ARMv7 = 32-bit (affected). Alternatively, just search "Old School RuneScape" in the Play Store — if it says your device "isn't compatible," that is your answer.

My phone is affected — what's the cheapest way to keep playing?

The $0 options are: (1) play on an iPhone/iPad you already own, (2) use a 64-bit Android phone/tablet already in the house, or (3) install the free official desktop client or RuneLite on a computer you have. If you must buy something, a used 2020+ phone often costs under $150 and runs the mobile app perfectly. You do not need anything expensive.

Can I use an emulator like BlueStacks to play the mobile version on my PC?

Yes. BlueStacks, LDPlayer, and MuMu Player all create a 64-bit virtual Android device on your computer. Install OSRS Mobile from the Play Store inside the emulator, log in, and play with mouse and keyboard. Performance is usually excellent. If you would rather play the "real" PC version, the official client and RuneLite are even simpler and also free.

Is iOS (iPhone/iPad) affected by this change at all?

No. This deprecation is specific to 32-bit Android. All modern iPhones and iPads are 64-bit and the iOS app is fully supported. If you have an Apple device, just download Old School RuneScape from the App Store and log in — no checks, no restrictions.
