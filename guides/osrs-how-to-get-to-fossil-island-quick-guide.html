OSRS How to Get to Fossil Island Quick Guide 2026 -- Unlock, Transport & Activities

📅 Published: June 7, 2026  |  ⏱️ 12 min read  |  ✍️ OSRS Guru
🪴 Transport Guide -- Updated June 2026

Fossil Island is one of the most useful areas in OSRS -- home to birdhouse trapping, hardwood trees, the Mushroom teleport network, seaweed farming, and the Varrock Museum fossil displays. This quick guide covers how to unlock Fossil Island via the Bone Voyage quest, every transport method to reach it, and all the key activities you should do once there.

If you have not done these, expect 2-3 hours total for the prerequisite quests + Bone Voyage.

2. Bone Voyage Quest -- Quick Walkthrough

Bone Voyage is a short novice quest (15-30 minutes) that permanently unlocks Fossil Island. Here is the complete walkthrough:

  1. Speak to the Curator in the Varrock Museum (ground floor, east wing). He asks you to investigate a barge crew at the Dig Site.
  2. Travel to the Dig Site (east of Varrock). Go north past the exam centre to find the barge. Speak to the Barge Foreman.
  3. Speak to the Lead Navigator (nearby). He needs help completing the barge.
  4. Gather these items: 3 oak planks (buy from sawmill or GE), 1 rope (buy from general store or GE), 1 copper wire (buy from Al Kharid general store or make with 1 copper bar).
  5. Use the items on the barge to repair it. Speak to the Lead Navigator again.
  6. Set sail! You arrive at Fossil Island's camp.
  7. On the island: help the museum staff set up the camp. Build a crate (planks), make a fire (logs + tinderbox), and speak to the people around the camp.
  8. After the camp is established, the quest completes automatically. You now have permanent access to Fossil Island.
🛒 Buy Items Before You Start: Purchase 3 oak planks, 1 rope, and 1 copper wire from the Grand Exchange before starting Bone Voyage. This saves you running around mid-quest. Total cost: under 2,000 GP. Keep these in your inventory before speaking to the Lead Navigator.

4. Mushtree Network -- The Most Important First Step

The Mushtree Network is Fossil Island's internal teleport system. Activating it should be your FIRST action upon arriving at the island. There are 5 mushtrees:

MushtreeLocationKey Nearby Features
HilltopNorth-central area, near the volcanoBank + General Store (most useful mushtree!)
Mushroom MeadowCentral area, near the campBirdhouse locations (south-east and south-west)
Verdant ValleySouth-western areaHardwood trees (teak + mahogany), herb patch
Seaweed SwampSouth-eastern areaSeaweed farming patches, anglerfish spot
ForestEastern areaAncient Cavern entrance, ammonite crabs, drift net fishing

To activate each mushtree, simply visit it once and click "Activate". After that, you can right-click any mushtree and select "Teleport" to instantly travel to any other activated mushtree. The Hilltop mushtree is the most important because it is adjacent to a bank chest and general store.

5. All Fossil Island Activities -- What to Do

There are 4 birdhouse spots on Fossil Island (2 near Mushroom Meadow mushtree, 2 near Verdant Valley). Set them up with a birdhouse + 10 hop seeds (or 5 herb seeds), wait 50 minutes, and return for Hunter XP + bird nests. This is widely considered one of the best passive training methods in OSRS.

The hardwood grove on Fossil Island is the only non-quest alternative to Ape Atoll for teak and mahogany trees. It's safer (no aggressive monkeys) and can be accessed via the Verdant Valley mushtree -- just steps from the trees. The logs are excellent for Construction training (via the Sawmill or Plank Make spell).

Seaweed Farming (Crafting -- Ironman Essential)

The two seaweed patches on Fossil Island (near the Seaweed Swamp mushtree) grow Giant Seaweed. When harvested and cooked into soda ash, combined with buckets of sand (from the Sandstone Grinder in the desert), this becomes molten glass -- one of the best Ironman Crafting training methods.

Fossil Displays (Prayer + Slayer XP)

Unidentified fossils are commonly found while doing any activity on Fossil Island (Woodcutting, Hunter, underwater activities, etc.). Clean them by using a chisel on them (requires the Fossil Island Fossil Cleaner, built during the quest), then take the cleaned fossils to the Varrock Museum basement to add to displays. Each display completed gives a one-time XP reward in either Prayer or Slayer (your choice). Completing all displays yields approximately 120K Prayer XP or 120K Slayer XP.

6. Pro Tips for Fossil Island

  • Activate ALL mushtrees immediately. This is the #1 Fossil Island tip. Run to each of the 5 mushtrees and click "Activate." This takes 10-15 minutes and forever changes your Fossil Island experience. The Hilltop mushtree next to the bank is the single most useful teleport on the island.
  • Set up birdhouse runs as part of your daily rotation. Every 50 minutes, spend 2-3 minutes checking 4 birdhouses. This nets 4K-5K Hunter XP and 4-12 bird nests per run. Over a month of casual play, that's 300K-500K+ passive Hunter XP and millions in seeds.
  • Use the Digsite Pendant for fast barge access. After completing The Dig Site quest, you can make a Digsite Pendant (enchanted ruby necklace). Use it to teleport directly to the Dig Site, then run north 20 seconds to the barge. This is faster than walking from Varrock.
  • Combine birdhouse runs with seaweed runs. Since birdhouses take 50 minutes and seaweed takes 40 minutes, you can alternate them: do a birdhouse run, wait 10 minutes, do a seaweed run, wait 30 minutes, and repeat. This maximizes your Fossil Island time efficiency.
  • Ammonite Crabs are the best AFK combat training for mid-level accounts. With 100 HP and extremely low defence, Ammonite Crabs let you AFK for 10 minutes at a time with minimal attention. Located on the eastern beach (near the Forest mushtree). Bring a rock crab teleport or run from the barge.
  • Herbiboar is excellent for Ironmen. At 80 Hunter, Herbiboar tracking yields consistent herb drops (ranarr, snapdragon, torstol) and takes about 45-60 seconds per boar. It's one of the most reliable Ironman herb sources in the game alongside farming contracts.

7. Common Mistakes

  1. Skipping Mushtree activation. Players often land on Fossil Island, do Birdhouses once, and leave without activating the mushtrees. This makes every future visit 5x slower. Always activate mushtrees on your first visit.
  2. Not unlocking Fossil Island until late-game. Fossil Island is accessible with VERY low requirements (10 in most skills + Dragon Slayer I). Birdhouse runs alone make it worth unlocking as early as possible -- Ironmen who delay this lose months of passive Hunter XP.
  3. Forgetting to bring a chisel for fossils. Unidentified fossils pile up in your bank if you don't clean them. Bring a chisel and clean them immediately, then head to Varrock Museum for free Prayer or Slayer XP.
  4. Ignoring giant seaweed. For Ironmen, Giant Seaweed is essential for Crafting progression. Even mains benefit from cheap molten glass materials. The seaweed patches take 40 minutes to grow and require almost no attention.
  5. Walking everywhere instead of using Fairy Rings. Once you unlock Fairy Ring AKQ, you can teleport to the island instantly from any fairy ring. Get Fairy Tale II started (you don't need the skill requirements -- just begin the quest for fairy ring access).
  6. Not using the Fossil Island bank. The Hilltop mushtree is right next to a bank chest. Use it for banking hardwood logs, bird nests, seaweed, and fossils. Don't run back to the mainland to bank.

8. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I access Fossil Island without completing Bone Voyage?

A: No. Bone Voyage is the ONLY way to unlock Fossil Island. There is no alternative quest, no teleport that bypasses it, and no method to reach the island before completing the quest. The bright side: the quest is short (15-30 minutes), has very low skill requirements, and the prerequisite quests (Dragon Slayer I + The Dig Site) are quests you should complete early anyway for their own rewards.

Q: How often should I do birdhouse runs?

A: Every 50 minutes is optimal, but even 2-3 times per play session is worth it. Birdhouses take 50 minutes to fill. Each run takes ~2 minutes for 4 birdhouses and yields 4K-5K Hunter XP + 4-12 bird nests. Bird nests average ~6K-8K GP each (with seed contents). So a single run is worth ~25K-100K GP in seeds + 4K Hunter XP. Over 4-5 runs per day (casual), that's 100K-500K GP and 20K Hunter XP -- entirely passive.

Q: What's the best birdhouse type to use?

A: Always use the highest-level birdhouse your Hunter level allows. The tiers: Regular (5 Hunter), Oak (14), Willow (24), Teak (34), Maple (44), Mahogany (49), Yew (59), Magic (74), Redwood (89). Higher-tier birdhouses yield more Hunter XP and have a better chance at higher-tier seeds in nests. Use hop seeds (cheapest) to bait them -- the seed type does NOT affect rewards. Buy hop seeds from the Draynor seed shop for 1 GP each.

🪴 Fossil Island -- One of OSRS's Most Valuable Unlocks

Fossil Island packs an incredible amount of utility into a single area. Birdhouses, hardwood trees, seaweed farming, fossil displays, Ammonite Crabs, Herbiboar, and the Mushtree network -- all accessible with a 15-minute quest and level 10 skills. Whether you are a brand-new member looking for passive income, an Ironman building up supplies, or a main account chasing Hunter XP, Fossil Island delivers.

Complete Bone Voyage, activate the mushtrees, and start your birdhouse runs today. Your future self will thank you.
