2. Hunllef Boss — Complete Mechanic Breakdown

The Crystalline Hunllef (and its Corrupted variant) is a three-phase boss with a hybrid attack cycle. Here is every mechanic you need to know:

2.1 The Core Attack Cycle

Hunllef uses a strict 6-attack cycle before switching combat style:

Attack #Attack TypeWhat It Looks LikePrayer
1-6Melee OR Ranged OR MagicSee visual cues belowPray the style that hits first
After 6 attacksStyle switchHunllef changes color + animationSWITCH prayer IMMEDIATELY

How to recognize each attack type visually:

  • Magic: Purple/blue orb animation projectile
  • Ranged: Green crystalline shards — looks like an arrow projectile
  • Melee: Hunllef steps toward you and swings (only happens when adjacent)
⚠️ Critical Rule: Hunllef NEVER uses melee while you are more than 1 tile away. If you keep 2+ tiles of distance during the fight, you will never take melee damage. This completely removes one attack style from the fight. Always maintain distance — dance around the tornado obstacles to stay 2+ tiles away.

2.2 Stomp Attack (Special Mechanic)

Every 12 attacks, Hunllef performs a stomp attack that creates a 3x3 tile area of floor tiles that turn red and then slam downward. Standing on a red tile takes 30-50 damage regardless of prayer.

🦶 Stomp Attack — How to Dodge It

Timing: The tile turns red 1 game tick before the slam. You have exactly 1 tick to move.

Tell: Look for the 3x3 tile area starting to glow red around your position — this is your cue to move 1 tile in any direction.

Common mistake: Moving too far away. One tile to the side is enough. Moving too much wastes movement ticks and can put you out of position for the next prayer switch.

Advanced: Track your kill count — the stomp occurs exactly every 12 attacks. Some experienced players count attacks to anticipate it rather than react to it.

2.3 Tornado Obstacles

Throughout the fight, crystalline tornadoes spawn and move randomly around the arena. Contact with a tornado deals damage.

🌀 Tornadoes — Key Rules

  • Tornadoes move in predictable paths — they travel in straight lines and bounce off walls
  • They cannot change direction mid-movement, which means you can predict where they'll be 2-3 tiles ahead
  • More tornadoes spawn as the boss loses HP (Phase 2 = 2 tornadoes; Phase 3 = 3 tornadoes)
  • Use tornadoes to your advantage: Position Hunllef on the opposite side of a tornado from you — the tornado acts as a barrier, limiting Hunllef's melee opportunities

2.4 Phase Transitions (HP Thresholds)

PhaseHP RemainingWhat ChangesKey Response
Phase 1100% → 67%Normal attack cycle, 1 tornadoGet comfortable with 6-attack cycle
Phase 267% → 33%Attack speed increases; 2 tornadoes spawnTighten prayer switches — less time between attacks
Phase 333% → 0%Attack speed increases again; 3 tornadoes; stomp more frequentFull focus — slowest reaction time is now dangerous
⚠️ Phase transitions are when most deaths occur. The attack speed increase is subtle but significant — players who were prayer switching reactively suddenly find themselves taking off-prayer hits they didn't expect. At Phase 2, start anticipating the switch rather than reacting to the animation.

3. Attack Patterns & Prayer Switching

3.1 The "4-Tick" Attack Pattern

In Phase 1, Hunllef attacks every 4 game ticks (~2.4 seconds). This is your window to switch prayers.

The visual sequence when a style switch occurs:

  1. Hunllef fires its 6th attack (you're praying Style A)
  2. Hunllef's body changes color/animation (switch visible)
  3. Hunllef begins the 7th attack motion (switch to Style B prayer NOW)
  4. Projectile lands — you take 0 damage if you switched in time

The key is recognizing the body animation, not the projectile. By the time the projectile is visible, you're already in the wrong prayer timing window if you reacted to it.

3.2 What to Pray During the Fight

SituationPrayerNotes
Hunllef starts fight (Magic)Protect from MagicAlways opens with Magic in Phase 1
After 6 attacks (switches to Ranged)Protect from MissilesSwap immediately on the color change
After another 6 attacks (switches to Magic)Protect from MagicCycle repeats throughout Phase 1
Offensive PrayerPiety (melee) / Rigour (ranged) / Augury (magic)Always have one active — stacks with Protect prayers
💡 Two-Prayer Hotkey Setup: Bind Protect from Magic and Protect from Missiles to your hotkeys (F1/F2 or your preferred layout). When you recognize the style switch, tap the opposing hotkey. Don't try to click manually — hotkeys are 2-3x faster and reduce mental load.

3.3 Phase 2 & 3 Prayer Switching Adjustments

In Phase 2 and 3, the attack speed increases to 3 ticks between attacks (~1.8 seconds). This means:

  • You have 0.6 seconds less per prayer window
  • You must switch on the animation, not the projectile — there is no longer enough time to react to the projectile
  • Some players find it helpful to reduce their mouse-clicking frequency on Hunllef to focus purely on prayer watching

4. The 7 Most Common Death Causes — And How to Fix Each

💀 Death Cause #1: Missing the Phase Transition Prayer Switch

What happens: At 67% HP, Hunllef's attack speed increases but players are still operating at Phase 1 reaction speed. The next attack lands before the prayer is switched.

Fix: When you see Hunllef's HP cross 67%, consciously shift your focus from "reacting to projectile" to "watching for body animation." The transition is your mental cue to tighten up.

💀 Death Cause #2: Standing on Stomp Tiles

What happens: Players focused on prayer switching don't notice the red tile appearing beneath them. 30-50 unavoidable damage hits through prayer.

Fix: Keep peripheral awareness of your tile. The red glow is visible even in your peripheral vision during prayer switching. One step to the side is all that's needed — don't overthink the movement direction.

💀 Death Cause #3: Walking Into Tornadoes

What happens: During phase 3 with 3 tornadoes active, players back-pedal into a tornado they weren't tracking.

Fix: Never move backwards without checking. When repositioning away from Hunllef's stomp, click to the left or right rather than directly backward. Tornadoes generally spawn from the corners — be most cautious of the area behind you and to your sides.

💀 Death Cause #4: Poor Inventory Management — Running Out of Food

What happens: Players spend too long in the prep phase, use too much food before entering the boss, or tank hits that should have been prayed.

Fix: Enter the boss with at least 10-12 food items minimum. If your prep gives you fewer than 8 food items, consider that a "poor prep" run — extending prep slightly for more resources is worth it.

💀 Death Cause #5: Attacking with the Wrong Combat Style

What happens: Players switch weapons but don't notice Hunllef's current weakness. Hunllef takes 1 damage from any attack that matches its current defense style.

Fix: Hunllef's weakness corresponds to the opposite of what it's currently attacking with. If it's attacking with Magic → use Melee or Ranged. The body color also indicates its current defense: blue = magic defense (don't use magic), green = ranged defense, red/orange = melee defense.

💀 Death Cause #6: Getting Cornered by Tornadoes and Hunllef Simultaneously

What happens: Tornadoes cut off retreat options, forcing the player into melee range of Hunllef during a phase when they're also managing stomp tiles.

Fix: Never let yourself be pushed toward a wall. Fight in the center of the arena where possible. If tornadoes cut off your path, move laterally (sideways) rather than backward — this is almost always a viable escape route.

💀 Death Cause #7: Starting Phase 2 Under 40 HP

What happens: Players tank a few hits in Phase 1 and don't eat. They enter Phase 2 low on HP and the first off-prayer Phase 2 hit (which hits harder due to higher base damage) finishes them.

Fix: Set a personal HP floor: never let your HP drop below 40 at any point in the fight. Eat proactively, not reactively. If you're at 50 HP and Hunllef is about to Phase 2, eat first and then deal damage — the phase transition is predictable based on HP.

5.3 Armor Protection Priority

Your crystal armor must match Hunllef's attack style — not your attack style. Always prioritize matching your defense prayer with the correct armor:

This is counterintuitive — ranged armor protects you from magic attacks, and magic armor protects you from ranged attacks. This is how OSRS's defense system works (Ranged armor has high Magic Defense bonus; Magic armor has high Ranged Defense bonus). If you're getting hit hard through prayer, check if you're wearing the wrong armor type.

6. Corrupted Gauntlet — Complete Walkthrough

Corrupted Gauntlet is the harder variant. The layout is identical, but the enemies are stronger, the crystal resources are different (dark crystals), and Hunllef hits significantly harder. GP/hr is ~14-16M (post-update) vs ~6-8M for regular.

6.1 Prep Phase — Corrupted Gauntlet Specifics

Phase 1 — First room (North semi-boss, now fixed location):
Enter and go North immediately. The Corrupted variants of semi-bosses have more HP but the same attack patterns. Defeat it, collect the Corrupted Crystal Dust and resources. Remember: first enemy you kill guarantees a Weapon Frame (this applies to Corrupted too).
Phase 2 — Resource gathering:
Corrupted Gauntlet resources (Dark Crystal Ore, Corrupted Bark, Corrupted Linenium) stack now — a huge QoL improvement. Gather until you have enough for your target gear tier. For T1 weapon + T1/T2 armor, you need approximately 15-20 enemy kills.
Phase 3 — Crafting:
Craft your Weapon Frame into a T1 Crystal Weapon (0 shards, just the Frame). Then craft armor as time/shards allow. Corrupted weapons/armor provide higher stats but the crafting costs are proportionally higher — the value formula hasn't changed.
Phase 4 — Food/potions:
Corrupted Gauntlet requires more food than regular. Target 12-15 food items minimum before entering the boss. If you have fewer than 10, continue the prep phase rather than entering underprepared — a death in Corrupted Gauntlet costs you the entire run.

6.2 Boss Fight — Corrupted vs Regular Differences

MechanicRegular GauntletCorrupted Gauntlet
Hunllef max hit (through pray)~12-15~18-22
Stomp damage~25-35~35-50
Attack speed (Phase 1)4 ticks3.5 ticks
Attack speed (Phase 3)3 ticks2.5 ticks
Tornado count (Phase 3)34

What this means practically: In Corrupted Gauntlet, an off-prayer Phase 3 hit can easily KO you from 50+ HP in a few ticks. Sloppy prayer switching that's "okay" in regular Gauntlet will get you killed consistently in Corrupted. The mechanics are identical — the margin for error is just smaller.

6.3 Corrupted Gauntlet Completion Checklist

  • ☐ Entered fight with 12+ food items
  • ☐ Weapon matches Hunllef's current weakness style
  • ☐ Armor matches Hunllef's current attack style (counterintuitive — see Section 5)
  • ☐ Offensive prayers active throughout (Piety/Rigour/Augury)
  • ☐ HP stays above 40 at all times — eat proactively
  • ☐ Prayer hotkeys ready — don't click the prayer book manually
  • ☐ Phase transition awareness at 67% and 33% HP
  • ☐ Stomp tiles: move one tile sideways, not backward

7. Advanced Techniques for Sub-4-Minute Kills

Once you're consistently completing Corrupted Gauntlet without dying, the next goal is improving your kill time. Sub-4-minute kills are achievable and significantly improve your GP/hr from ~14M toward 16-18M.

7.1 DPS Optimization: Which Attack Style to Lead With

When Hunllef switches to a style that your weapon is already strong against, you gain free damage ticks. The optimal weapon switching sequence:

  1. Keep your Magic weapon (Crystal Staff) active during Hunllef's Ranged phase — magic always exploits the weakest defense in this matchup
  2. Switch to Melee (Crystal Halberd) when Hunllef switches to Magic phase
  3. Use Ranged (Crystal Bow) as a versatile gap-filler when positioning makes melee difficult

7.2 Tornado Exploitation for DPS Uptime

Instead of always moving away from tornadoes, experienced players deliberately position Hunllef between themselves and a tornado. This limits Hunllef's movement options and reduces the amount of reposition clicks needed — more time attacking, less time moving.

How to do it: When a tornado moves between you and Hunllef, step to the side to keep Hunllef "in front" of the tornado relative to your position. Hunllef will spend ticks navigating around the tornado, giving you free attack windows.

7.3 The Sub-4-Minute Prayer Pattern

Elite players don't switch prayers reactively — they switch predictively. After your 30th+ Corrupted completion, you should know the 6-attack count well enough to anticipate the switch:

  • Count attacks mentally: 1-2-3-4-5-6-SWITCH
  • On "5," your finger should already be on the alternate prayer hotkey
  • On "6," the attack launches — your prayer switches before the projectile lands
  • This eliminates the reaction window entirely — you're 1 full game tick ahead of the attack

7.4 Food Conservation for Longer Sessions

Every food item you don't use is time you don't need to spend regenerating in the prep phase. To minimize food usage:

  • Never let HP drop below 50 without eating — proactive healing takes you from 50 → 70 HP using 1 food item rather than from 20 → 70 using 2-3
  • Stomp dodges are worth learning to save food — each stomp hit you avoid is ~35-50 HP of food conservation
  • Prayer switching improvements compound into food savings — fewer off-prayer hits = less food spent per kill

8. FAQ

Q: What's the minimum gear tier to attempt Corrupted Gauntlet?

T1 weapon + T1 armor from the prep phase is technically sufficient if your prayer switching is excellent. However, we recommend T1 weapon + T2 armor for your first 20-30 Corrupted completions. The extra Defense bonus from T2 armor means off-prayer hits and stomp hits don't hit quite as hard, giving you more room to learn.

Q: How long should my Corrupted Gauntlet prep phase be?

Post-Summer Sweep-Up, target 3:00-4:00 prep time. Faster than 3 minutes usually means you're cutting corners on resources. Slower than 5 minutes means you're over-preparing, which hurts your overall GP/hr. The sweet spot is a relaxed, efficient prep that gives you T1/T2 gear and 12+ food.

Q: Is the Blade of Saeldor drop worth farming Corrupted Gauntlet for?

At approximately 1/400 drop rate, expect 400+ Corrupted completions for the Blade of Saeldor. At 14-16M GP/hr and ~4-5 minute kills, you're looking at ~12-15 completions per hour. On average, the drop comes after ~30-35 hours of Corrupted Gauntlet. This is excellent for an item that sells for ~600-700M. Yes, it's worth it — and the GP income while grinding is itself excellent.

Q: What's the fastest way to improve from 10-minute Corrupted kills to 5-minute kills?

The single biggest improvement factor is prayer switching speed. Film 3 of your kills and watch the footage — count how many off-prayer hits you take per kill. Every off-prayer hit represents wasted food and wasted DPS (you're taking damage instead of killing). Focus entirely on prayer switching for your next 20 kills, even if it means attacking less. As the prayer switching becomes automatic, your attack time increases naturally.

Q: Should I use T3 gear or is T1/T2 better now?

After Summer Sweep-Up, T3 armor costs 25% more shards than before. The boss doesn't hit harder than the extra Defense provides — meaning the trade-off of longer prep time for T3 armor doesn't pay off in kills-per-hour. T1 weapon + T1-T2 armor with fast prep beats T3 gear with long prep every time in the current meta.

Q: I keep dying at Phase 3 with 3-4 tornadoes. What should I do differently?

Phase 3 deaths with multiple tornadoes are almost always a positioning problem, not a prayer problem. Before Phase 3 begins (when Hunllef hits 33% HP), deliberately position yourself in the center of the arena. With the center position, you have movement options in all 4 directions when tornadoes approach. Corner or wall positions give you only 1-2 escape directions — that's why players die there.
