OSRS Safe Spots Guide for Beginners 2026

Safe spots let you fight monsters using Ranged or Magic without taking ANY damage — no food needed, no banking, almost fully AFK. This guide shows you exactly how to find and use them.

1. What Is a Safe Spot?

A safe spot is a position where you can attack a monster with Ranged or Magic, but the monster cannot reach you because an obstacle (rock, corner, tree, furniture) is in the way.

The monster tries to walk around the obstacle to reach you, but if the pathfinding keeps them stuck, you take zero damage for the entire fight.

🎯 Why Safe Spots Are Game-Changing

  • Zero food cost — train for hours with no banking
  • Safe spot training = 100% of your XP without HP drain
  • Perfect for AFK sessions — let it run while doing something else
  • Lets low-level players kill higher-level monsters far above their combat level

2. How to Use a Safe Spot — Step by Step

  1. Have Ranged or Magic equipped — Safe spots only work with Ranged/Magic. Melee requires you to be standing next to the monster.
  2. Identify the obstacle — A rock, a corner of wall, furniture, or any tile the monster cannot path through.
  3. Stand on one side of the obstacle — Position yourself so the obstacle is directly between you and the monster.
  4. Attack the monster from range — Click the monster. Your character will shoot/cast at it.
  5. Watch the monster's pathing — It should try to walk toward you, but the obstacle blocks it. If it reaches you, reposition.
  6. Confirm you're not taking damage — Your HP should stay at 100% for the whole fight. If not, your safe spot is broken — find a new angle.
💡 Corners Work Best

The most reliable safe spots are corners — stand behind a corner tile so the monster can't navigate around it. Dungeons and cave interiors are full of these.

📍 Detailed Safe Spot Setups

🗺️ Goblins — Lumbridge Swamp Caves

How to set up: Enter the cave and find the northeastern corner where two cave walls meet at a 90° angle. Stand in the corner tile so your character is against both walls. Attack a Goblin from 4-5 tiles away — it will path toward you but get stuck on the uneven cave floor tiles. If the Goblin reaches you, move one tile north/south and try again.

Pro tip: This is the best monster to practice safe-spotting on. If you mess up, Goblins only hit 1s.

🗺️ Hill Giants — Edgeville Dungeon

How to set up: Enter Edgeville Dungeon (requires a brass key or 10 GP to enter from the manhole). Navigate to the southern area with large bone piles and rocks. Stand behind the large bone pile in the southwest corner — position yourself so the bone pile is between you and the Hill Giant. The giant will try to walk around but the pile blocks pathfinding. Attack from 5+ tiles away with Ranged.

Exact tile: Stand at the bone pile cluster against the south wall, 3 tiles away from the nearest giant spawn. The giant will be stuck on the east side of the bones.

Alternative spot: Use the large rock formation in the northwest part of the giant area. Stand on the north side of the rock, attack giants on the south side.

🗺️ Moss Giants — Varrock Sewer

How to set up: Enter Varrock Sewer through the manhole in Varrock East; run south to the Moss Giant area. There is a large pillar near the southern wall. Stand on the north side of the pillar, attack Moss Giants on the south side. The pillar completely blocks line of sight for melee pathing.

Crandor alternative: After Dragon Slayer I, go to Crandor Island. In the dungeon under the island, use the narrow hallway entrance — stand on one side of the doorway, attack giants on the other side. The door frame acts as the obstacle.

🗺️ Lesser Demons — Karamja Dungeon (F2P)

How to set up: Take the boat from Port Sarim to Karamja (30 GP). Enter the dungeon south of the volcano. Inside, there is a caged area with Lesser Demons. Stand OUTSIDE the cage, against the bars. Attack the demons through the cage bars — they cannot pass through the bars to reach you. You can safe spot all 4 demons in the cage from the same tile.

Exact position: Stand 1 tile away from the cage bars, directly facing a demon. Your auto-attack will fire through the bars. The demons will run at you but stop at the cage boundary.

🗺️ Greater Demons — Brimhaven Dungeon (P2P)

How to set up: Take the boat to Brimhaven (10 GP or free with Karamja gloves). Enter the dungeon (requires 875 GP entrance fee). In the main chamber, use the pillars and rock formations to safe spot. The best spot is the northwestern corner behind a large rock — stand so the rock is between you and the demon. Alternatively, use the narrow corridor in the western section.

Note: Bring Antifire potions or an Antifire shield if you're not using a safe spot — Greater Demons use fire attacks.

🗺️ Black Demons — Edgeville Dungeon (P2P, after Demon Slayer)

How to set up: In the Edgeville Dungeon's deep southern area (past the Hill Giants), there are Black Demons in a multi-celled area with iron bars. Stand outside the cell, shoot through the bars. Like the Lesser Demon cage, the bars block the demons completely. You can safe spot 2-3 demons simultaneously from the same position.

Alternative: There's also a corner safe spot in the open area — use the large rock formations and stand on the opposite side from the demon's patrol path.

🗺️ Jellies — Fremennik Slayer Dungeon (P2P, 52 Slayer)

How to set up: After starting the Fremennik Slayer Dungeon miniquest, enter the dungeon. Jellies patrol around pillars in the main chamber. Stand on the opposite side of a pillar from a Jelly. Attack it — the Jelly will try to path around the pillar but the narrow gaps often confuse the pathfinding. For a guaranteed spot, use the corner where two walls meet in the southeastern part of the Jelly area.

Multi-spot trick: You can line up 2-3 Jellies around a single pillar. Attack one, and if positioned correctly, the others will also be unable to reach you.

🏆 Best Beginner Safe Spot: Hill Giants in Edgeville Dungeon

At level 30+ Ranged, Hill Giants in the Edgeville Dungeon are safe-spottable behind the large bones and rocks. Stand behind the bone pile in the southwest corner. They drop Big Bones (250 GP each), coins, and limpwurt roots. You can earn 40,000–80,000 GP/hr with zero food cost.

6. AFK Safe Spot Training Locations

These spots let you click once and go semi-AFK for 10–20 minutes without dying or banking:

🏆 Top AFK Safe Spots (Members)

  • Rock/Sand/Ammonite Crabs — Not technically safe spots, but they're very low damage and respawn in 30 seconds. AFK up to 20 minutes. Best for Melee/Ranged/Magic training without food.
  • Ankous in Stronghold — Floor 4, safe spot behind tomb tiles. Low Defence, decent Slayer XP. Use Ranged.
  • Dagannoth in Waterbirth Island — Cave entrance area, safe spot behind rocks. Good XP, unique drops.

🔓 Best AFK Safe Spots (F2P)

  • Moss Giants (Crandor) — After Dragon Slayer I, use the narrow entrance hallway to safe spot safely. Big Bones are great for Prayer XP.
  • Lesser Demons (Karamja) — Behind the cage bars. Shoot through the cage. Rune Med Helm drop is worth 11k GP.
  • Hill Giants (Edgeville) — Best F2P AFK option. See Section 3.

7. Advanced Safe Spot Tips

  • 🔄 Multi-monster safe spots — Some spots let you safe spot multiple monsters at once. Jellies in the Fremennik Slayer Dungeon can be lined up in clusters.
  • 🚪 Doors as safe spots — In some locations, a closed door acts as an obstacle. Close the door and shoot through it at monsters on the other side.
  • 🧊 Freeze + safe spot — Cast Ice Burst/Barrage (high level Magic) to freeze a monster in place, then safe spot it from any angle. Used for nightmare Slayer tasks.
  • 🗺️ Use RuneLite's safe spot plugin — RuneLite has a Monster Highlight plugin that shows you established safe spot tiles highlighted in green.
  • ⚠️ Safe spots can be patched — Jagex sometimes removes safe spots in game updates. Always verify a safe spot still works before committing a long AFK session.

8. Safe Spot FAQs

Do safe spots work in PvP (Wilderness)?

Yes, but other players can navigate around obstacles in ways monsters can't (players can solve pathfinding better). Safe spots are most reliable against NPCs, not players.

Will monsters ever figure out how to reach me in a safe spot?

Sometimes. If a safe spot is partially blocked, the monster may find an alternate path. Always monitor your HP for the first minute to confirm the spot is solid.

Can I safe spot with Melee?

No. Melee requires you to be directly adjacent to the monster. You can position in a corner to only take hits from one direction, but that's a "tank spot" not a true safe spot.

Is safe spotting considered exploiting or bannable?

No. Safe spotting is a completely legitimate and intentional game mechanic that Jagex has never punished players for using. It's a fundamental part of OSRS strategy.

What's the best cannon spot for beginners?

Ogress Warriors (Corsair Cove Dungeon) after Dragon Slayer I. Set up Dwarf Cannon (Dwarf Cannon quest required), safe spot the Ogresses with Ranged while cannon hits. Very fast XP and decent GP.

How do I know if I've found a valid safe spot?

After attacking, watch your HP bar for 30-60 seconds. If you take zero damage during that time and the monster is visibly stuck (running in circles or against a wall), you've found a valid safe spot. If the monster reaches you, reposition immediately.

Can I safe spot multiple monsters at the same time?

Yes! Multi-monster safe spots exist. Jellies in Fremennik Slayer Dungeon can be lined up around a single pillar. Hill Giants in Edgeville can sometimes be safespotted in pairs if they both path to the same obstacle. Use RuneLite's Monster Highlight plugin to find verified multi-spots.

What's the difference between a safe spot and a cannon spot?

A safe spot uses obstacles to prevent a monster from reaching you. A cannon spot is where you set up a Dwarf Cannon to hit monsters while you safe spot or tank. The best cannon spots are also safe spots — you attack with Ranged/Magic while your cannon deals automatic damage.

Do I need a specific quest completed to use safe spots?

No quests are required to use safe spots — it's a base game mechanic. However, some of the best safe spot locations are in dungeons that require quest progress (Dragon Slayer I for Crandor, Lost City for Taverley Dungeon, etc.).

Why does my safe spot sometimes break when I log out and back in?

When you log out mid-combat, the monster may have time to pathfind a new route to your last position. Always re-verify your safe spot after logging back in. Also, if another player walks through your tile while you're logged out, it can reset the monster's pathfinding.

Can I use safe spots for Slayer tasks?

Absolutely — this is one of the best uses of safe spots. High-Defence Slayer monsters like Jellies, Turoths, and Kurasks can be safely safespotted. This saves massive amounts of food and lets you complete tasks much faster. Check the Slayer master's advice for known safe spots.

What's the best F2P safe spot for Ranged training?

Lesser Demons in the Karamja Dungeon cage are the best F2P Ranged training spot. At level 30-60 Ranged, you can gain 25,000-40,000 Ranged XP/hr with zero food cost. Bring 1,000+ iron or steel arrows and High Alch the Rune Med Helms (11,000 GP each).

How far away can I be from a monster and still safe spot it?

Your maximum distance depends on your attack style's range: Ranged with a shortbow is 7 tiles; with a longbow is 8 tiles; Magic is 8 tiles (10 with a staff of fire/air). The obstacle must stay between you and the monster at all times within this range.

Can other players ruin my safe spot?

Yes, unfortunately. In multi-combat areas, another player can attack "your" monster and cause it to move, breaking your safe spot. In P2P worlds, this is less common because there are more monsters. In F2P, popular safe spots (like the Karamja Lesser Demon cage) can get crowded.

What's the most GP per hour I can make using safe spots?

Black Demons in Edgeville Dungeon (P2P) can yield 100,000-200,000 GP/hr when you alch your drops. Hill Giants are steadier for low levels at 40,000-80,000 GP/hr. The key is using High Alchemy (55 Magic) to convert drops to GP instantly without banking.
