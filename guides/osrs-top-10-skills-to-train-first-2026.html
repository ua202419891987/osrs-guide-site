1. The ROI Concept for Skill Training

In OSRS, you have a finite amount of time to play. Every hour you spend training a skill is an hour you're NOT spending doing something else. The question is: which skills give you the most return for every hour invested?

This isn't about which skills are "fun" or "cool" — it's about account value efficiency. If you only have 100 hours to play this month, which skills should you train to get the most powerful account possible?

📊 What "Account Value" Means (Our Scoring System)

To rank skills objectively, we scored each one across 4 dimensions:

① Content Unlocks (40% of score)

How many quests, areas, bosses, and activities does this skill unlock? A skill that unlocks 10 quests and 5 bosses is more valuable than one that only unlocks 1 quest.

Example: 70 Agility unlocks the Ardougne Cloak 4 teleport (shortcut to Ardougne). That's a massive time-saver for many activities. Agility scores high on content unlocks.

② Money-Making Potential (30% of score)

Can this skill make you GP? And if so, how much per hour? A skill that lets you make 1M GP/h is more valuable than one that makes 100K GP/h (because GP lets you buy gear, supplies, and membership).

Example: 99 Fishing at Minnows gives ~300K GP/h. That's useful, but 99 Slayer gives ~800K-1.2M GP/h. Slayer scores higher on money-making potential.

③ Time Savings (20% of score)

Does this skill save you time in other activities? Teleports, shortcuts, and utility effects count here.

Example: 66 Magic unlocks the Camelot Teleport. If you do a lot of activities in the Seers' Village area, this saves you ~30 seconds per trip. Over 100 trips, that's ~50 minutes saved. Magic scores high on time savings.

④ Combat Power (10% of score)

Does this skill make your character hit harder, take less damage, or kill monsters faster? This is especially important for PvM-focused players.

Example: 99 Strength gives +12 max hit compared to 1 Strength. That's a ~40% damage increase. Strength scores high on combat power.

🧮 The ROI Formula

Our ranking uses this formula:

📐 ROI Score = (Content Unlocks × 0.4) + (Money-Making × 0.3) + (Time Savings × 0.2) + (Combat Power × 0.1) ÷ Hours to Train

Skills with a high ROI score give lots of value and take relatively few hours to train. Skills with a low ROI score either give little value or take hundreds of hours to train.

💡 Key Insight

The most important factor isn't how "fast" a skill is to train — it's how much account power you get per hour. A skill that takes 10 hours to reach "useful" levels and unlocks massive content is higher ROI than a skill that takes 200 hours to reach 99 and only gives marginal benefits.

2. Top 10 Skills Ranked (Detailed Analysis)

Here are the 10 skills that give the highest return on your time investment. Each one is analyzed in detail so you understand exactly why it's ranked here and how to train it efficiently.

🥇 #1: Prayer — ROI Score: 9.8/10

Why it's #1: Prayer is the only skill where a single level (43) gives you 100% damage reduction against an entire attack style. No other skill has such a dramatic, immediate impact on your combat effectiveness.

Key Milestones & Unlocks

70Piety+23% Melee offensive stats — best melee prayer
74Rigour+23% Ranged offensive stats — best ranged prayer
74Augury+25% Magic damage — best magic prayer

Training Time to "Useful"

  • To Level 43 (all 3 Protect prayers): ~2-4 hours (if burying bones while doing other activities) or ~1 hour (if power-buying Dragon Bones for Gilded Altar)
  • To Level 70 (Piety): ~6-10 hours (Dragon Bones at Gilded Altar)
  • To Level 99: ~8-20 hours depending on budget (Dragon Bones vs Superior Dragon Bones)

Recommended Training Method

Fastest: Dragon Bones at a Gilded Altar (350% XP) — ~300 XP/bone, ~52,000 bones to 99 = ~9M GP.

Budget: Bury Big Bones from Hill Giants while training Combat — slow but free.

Riskier but free: Offer bones at the Chaos Altar in Wilderness (350% XP, no cost, but risk of PKers).

What You Miss If You Skip It

If you don't train Prayer, you'll take full damage from every monster. This means more food costs, more deaths, slower kills, and less profit per hour in EVERY PvM activity. Not training Prayer is the single biggest mistake new players make.

🥈 #2: Magic — ROI Score: 9.3/10

Why it's #2: Magic unlocks teleportation (massive time savings), high-DPS combat spells, and utility spells (High Alchemy, for GP). It also unlocks the entire Ancient Magicks spellbook after Desert Treasure, which is essential for many bosses.

  • To Level 75 (Fire Surge): ~10-15 hours (F2P or P2P methods)
  • To Level 99: ~25-40 hours (Bursting/Barraging Dust Devils or NMZ)

Recommended Training Method

Fastest (P2P): Cast Fire Wave or Fire Surge on monsters in the Slayer Tower or Chaos Temple (Wilderness). ~40-60K XP/h.

AFK: Curse splashing (illegal on some worlds, check rules) or combat training with Magic.

Profitable: High Alchemy while doing other activities — doesn't train Magic but makes GP to fund other training.

What You Miss If You Skip It

No teleports = wasting hours running around Gielinor. No High Alchemy = leaving GP on the table (alch everything you don't need!). NoAncient Magicks = can't efficiently boss or PvP. Magic is arguably the most versatile skill in the game.

🥉 #3: Hitpoints — ROI Score: 8.7/10

Why it's #3: Higher Hitpoints = more survivability = less food needed = more profit per trip. It also unlocks better gear (many pieces require 40+ HP) and makes Slayer much less dangerous.

  • To Level 70: Passively through Slayer — ~40-60 hours

What You Miss If You Skip It

Low HP means you die more often, eat more food (reducing profit per hour), and can't access high-level content. However, since HP trains passively, "skipping" it isn't really possible — just don't neglect Combat training and your HP will follow.

#4: Slayer — ROI Score: 8.2/10

Why it's #4: Slayer is the best way to train Combat skills while making GP. It unlocks superior versions of monsters (bigger drops), grants access to boss tasks (Abyssal Sire, Alchemical Hydra, etc.), and gives XP in Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, and Hitpoints simultaneously.

  • To Level 50 (unlock Blocks): ~15-25 hours of Slayer tasks
  • To Level 75 (unlock Extensions): ~40-60 hours
  • To Level 99: ~150-250 hours (depending on task efficiency)

Recommended Training Method

Early (1-50): Do tasks from Turael (Burthorpe) for fast points, then switch to Mazchna (Canifis) for tasks in the same area.

Mid (50-75): Vannaka (Edgeville Dungeon) or Chaeldar (Zanaris). Block slow tasks (like Cave Horrors or Turoths).

Late (75-99): Duradel (Shilo Village). He gives the best XP/h and best drop tables. This is where Slayer becomes very profitable (500K-1.2M GP/h depending on task).

What You Miss If You Skip It

No Slayer = no boss tasks = no rare drops = slower Combat XP = less GP. Slayer is the "engine" that drives mid-to-late game progression. Every maxed player has 99 Slayer because it's the most efficient way to train Combat while making money.

#5: Agility — ROI Score: 7.8/10

Why it's #5: Agility gives shortcuts that save time every single day you play. It also unlocks the Agility cape (restores run energy to 100% instantly) and gives +5% flat XP boost to all other skills when wearing the full Graceful outfit.

No Graceful = no XP boost = slower training in ALL other skills. No shortcuts = wasting time running everywhere. Agility is annoying to train, but the time savings compound over hundreds of hours of gameplay. It's one of the best "investment" skills.

📊 Skills #6 Through #10 (Summary Analysis)

#6: Thieving — ROI Score: 7.3/10

Key unlock: 53 Thieving = Ardougne Diary (easy) and ability to steal from Ardougne Knights (200K GP/h). 72 Thieving = access to Pyramid Plunder (300-500K GP/h). Training time: ~20-30 hours to 72. Why ranked here: Profitable at multiple levels + unlocks important quest requirements.

#7: Hunter — ROI Score: 6.9/10

Key unlock: 63 Hunter = Red Chinchompas (ranging training ammo). 83 Hunter = Black Chinchompas (PvP ammo, very profitable). Training time: ~30-50 hours to 83 (faster in 2026 due to new methods). Why ranked here: Chinchompas are essential for efficient Ranged training, and Black Chins are a massive money-maker (~1-2M GP/h at 83+).

#8: Construction — ROI Score: 6.5/10

Key unlock: 50 Construction = Spirit Trees + Jewelry Box in POH. 75 Construction = max POH (Ornate rejuvenation pool, Spellbook Swap altar, etc.). Training time: ~10-20 hours to 75 (expensive: ~50-80M GP to 75). Why ranked here: POH teleports save massive time, but the GP cost keeps it from ranking higher.

#9: Mining — ROI Score: 6.0/10

Key unlock: 60 Mining = Motherlode Mine (AFK mining, 30-40K XP/h). 85 Mining = Runite ore (500K+ GP/h at Runite rocks). Training time: ~40-60 hours to 85. Why ranked here: Useful forSmithing supplies and GP at high levels, but most gear is cheaper to buy than toSmith yourself.

#10: Fishing — ROI Score: 5.7/10

Key unlock: 76 Fishing = Minnows (300K GP/h). 82 Fishing = Anglerfish (essential for high-level PvM — heals 3-22 HP per fish). Training time: ~30-50 hours to 82. Why ranked here: Food is essential for bossing, but most food can be bought from GE. Fishing is only high-priority if you're an Ironman (where you can't buy food).

💡 The "Main Account" vs "Ironman" Difference

If you're playing a Main account (can use GE freely), you can skip or delay many gathering/production skills (Fishing, Cooking, Crafting, Fletching, Smithing) because you can just buy the products from other players. If you're an Ironman (no GE access), all these skills become much higher priority because you have to gather/make everything yourself.

4. Optimal Training Order Roadmap

Based on the ROI rankings, here's the optimal order to train skills for a new account in 2026. This roadmap assumes you're a Main account (not Ironman) and want to reach "mid-game ready" (able to do most bosses and make good GP/h) as fast as possible.

📅 Phase 1: The Foundation (Levels 3-50 Combat)

Skills to Train

  1. Prayer to 43 (Protect prayers) — Do this FIRST before anything else
  2. Magic to 55 (High Alchemy + teleports) — Splashing or combat spells
  3. Combat to 40 Attack/Strength/Defence (via Slayer or training)
  4. Hitpoints to 40 (passive through Combat)
  5. Agility to 50 (Graceful outfit pieces start dropping)

Estimated time: ~20-40 hours (depending on playstyle)

What you can do after: Turael Slayer, medium-difficulty quests, member skills access

📅 Phase 2: The Mid-Game Builder (Levels 50-70 Combat)

Skills to Train

  1. Slayer to 50 (unlock Blocks) — Do tasks from Mazchna/Vannaka
  2. Agility to 60 (full Graceful) — Massive XP boost to all skills
  3. Thieving to 53-72 (Ardougne Knights + Pyramid Plunder) — Makes GP while training
  4. Prayer to 70 (Piety) — Best melee prayer
  5. Magic to 75 (Fire Surge) — Better combat + high-level spellbook access
  6. Hunter to 63 (Red Chinchompas) — Ammo for Ranged training

Estimated time: ~60-100 hours

What you can do after: Barrows, medium bosses, Duradel Slayer, make 500K+ GP/h

📅 Phase 3: The Late-Game Push (Levels 70-99 Combat)

Skills to Train

  1. Slayer to 85-99 (Duradel tasks) — Best Combat XP + GP
  2. Agility to 85-99 (TzHaar course) — Faster runs, Agility cape
  3. Construction to 75 (max POH) — Teleport convenience
  4. Hunter to 83 (Black Chinchompas) — 1-2M GP/h money-maker
  5. Fishing to 82 (Anglerfish) — Food for high-level PvM
  6. Herblore to 75-90 (Super combat + Sara brew) — Essential for bossing

Estimated time: ~150-300 hours

What you can do after: All bosses in the game, 1M+ GP/h money-makers, maxed account

6. FAQ — Skill Priority Questions

I only have 1 hour per day to play. Which skills should I focus on?

With limited time, focus on high-impact, low-time-investment skills: Prayer (1-2 hours to 43), Magic (3-5 hours to 55 for High Alchemy), and Agility (12-18 hours to 60 for Graceful). These givemassive account value for relatively few hours invested. Avoid long grinds like 99 Slayer (150-250 hours) until you have more playtime available.

Should I follow this ranking exactly, or can I train skills in any order?

This ranking is a guideline, not a strict rule. If you enjoy trainingFishing, go ahead and trainFishing! The point of this guide is to help you avoid wasting time on low-ROI skills when you have limited time. If you have unlimited time and just want to enjoy the game, train skills in any order you like.

Why is Defence ranked so low? Isn't higher Defence essential for surviving?

Defence is ranked low because it's usually more efficient to train Attack and Strength first, then add Defence later. A player with 75 Attack / 75 Strength / 40 Defence hits much harder (and kills monsters faster) than a player with 40 Attack / 40 Strength / 75 Defence. Since time = GP in OSRS, faster kills = more GP/h. Defence is still important, but it's usually the last Combat skill you should max out.

I'm an Ironman. Should I follow the Main ranking or the Ironman-adjusted one?

Follow the Ironman-adjusted ranking (Section 5 of this guide). As an Ironman, you can't buy supplies from the GE, so gathering/production skills (Farming,Fishing, Cooking, Herblore, Mining, Smithing) become much higher priority. The Main ranking assumes you can just buy whatever you need, which isn't an option for you.

How often does the "optimal" training order change? Should I worry about updates making this guide obsolete?

The core principles (Prayer is #1, Magic is #2, etc.) have been stable for years. Major updates like the Summer Sweep-Up 2026 can shift some rankings (like Agility moving up due to new courses), but the changes are usually incremental, not revolutionary. This guide will be updated after any major balance changes — check back if a large update drops.

Can I "skip" low-ROI skills entirely? Never train them?

You can skip them for a long time, but most skills eventually become useful or required. For example, you might skip Construction for 100 hours of gameplay, but eventually you'll want POH teleports for convenience. The point of the ROI ranking isn't to never train low-ROI skills — it's to delay them until after you've trained the high-ROI ones.
