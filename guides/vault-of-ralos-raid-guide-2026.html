1. What Is The Fractured Archive?

The Fractured Archive is Old School RuneScape's fourth raid (Raids 4), first announced during the Winter Summit 2026 on January 25, 2026. It is described by Jagex as "the hardest raid we've ever designed."

Lore Background: The Archive is a vault created by the god Guthix himself, built over millennia to house his most prized artifacts, ancient knowledge, and lost relics. Located deep beneath Gielinor at the bottom of the Chasm of Tears, the Archive has remained sealed for thousands of years — until now. News travels fast in Gielinor, and you are not the first to find the Archive. Other gods' agents, curious creatures, and Guthix's own guardians now vie for control of its contents. Your mission: prevent the Archive's treasures from falling into the wrong hands.

Key Fact: This is the first new OSRS raid since 2022 (Tombs of Amascut), and the first raid designed with the new Sailing skill ecosystem in mind. The raid poll passed with 94.5% yes votes on February 2, 2026 — the highest approval rating of any OSRS raid content poll.

4. Raid Structure & Design Philosophy

The Fractured Archive's design is a deliberate departure from both CoX and ToA. Jagex described the philosophy as: "simple mechanics, but a huge amount of room to optimise — a pure test of you, your teammates, and the fight."

Key Design Pillars

  • Linear Boss Rush: Similar to Theatre of Blood. No branching paths, no puzzle rooms, no skilling sections. You fight a sequence of demi-bosses leading to a final boss.
  • Fixed Difficulty: Unlike ToA's invocation system, The Fractured Archive will launch with a single difficulty setting. The developers want a "maximum excitement" world-first race where everyone competes on equal footing.
  • 8-Player Capacity: This is the first OSRS raid to support up to 8 players (ToB caps at 5, CoX at 100 but effectively 3-5, ToA at 8 but designed around smaller teams). The difficulty will scale dynamically for team sizes between 2-8.
  • No Infinite Supplies: Unlike ToA (which had infinite "honey locust" food at launch), The Fractured Archive will not provide unlimited resources. Supply management will be critical.
  • High Skill Ceiling: The mechanics are designed to be simple to understand but extremely difficult to execute perfectly — especially under pressure. This rewards players who invest time in mastering the raid.

Raid Flow

Based on the First Look blog and developer commentary:

  1. Enter the Archive through the Chasm of Tears (exact entry method TBD)
  2. Demi-boss gauntlet — face 3-5 demi-bosses (exact number unconfirmed, community estimates based on the number of concept art pieces revealed)
  3. Final boss encounter — a multi-phase battle against the Archive's ultimate guardian (identity unconfirmed)
  4. Loot chamber — rewards based on performance and survival (exact mechanics TBD)

5. Confirmed Bosses

As of June 2026, Jagex has officially revealed two bosses via concept art and descriptions. More will be revealed throughout 2026.

The Archive Guardian

🌳 The Archive Guardian — Status: Confirmed (Official Concept Art)

  • Description: A massive, dormant tree-skeleton creature awakened to protect the Archive. Wrapped in vines with glowing red eyes.
  • Scale: Concept art depicts a human figure standing at the Guardian's feet, implying enormous size (room-filling encounter).
  • Role: Likely a mid-raid boss, comparable to Maiden of Sugadinti in ToB or the first demi-boss of a gauntlet.
  • Theme: Guthixian natural magic — vines, nature-based attacks, regenerative abilities.
  • Speculated mechanics (community): AOE vine sweep attacks, summoning smaller guardians, nature-based prayer denial.

Source: Official OSRS Winter Summit 2026 First Look blog — concept art revealed January 25, 2026

Ezekiel — Saradomin's Executor

⚔️ Ezekiel — Status: Confirmed (Official Concept Art & Lore)

  • Description: An armored knight wielding a massive weapon, clad in Saradomin's iconography. Ezekiel is described as Saradomin's "executor of justice" — a zealot sent to claim the Archive's most powerful artifacts for Saradomin.
  • Role: Likely a challenging mid-to-late raid boss, possibly the penultimate encounter before the final boss.
  • Theme: Divine justice — holy attacks, smite mechanics, possibly prayer-draining abilities.
  • Lore context: Unlike the peaceful Saradominist priests players encounter during quests, Ezekiel represents the "old way" of Saradomin — crusader-style enforcement of divine will.
  • Speculated mechanics (community): Heavy-hitting melee attacks with AOE holy shockwaves, temporary invulnerability phases, special attack that requires prayer switching.

Source: Official OSRS Winter Summit 2026 First Look blog — concept art and lore revealed January 25, 2026

6. Rewards — What We Know (And Don't Know)

Officially: Jagex has not announced any specific rewards yet. This is intentional — the developers are taking a completely different approach to reward design compared to previous raids.

Jagex's Reward Strategy

  • Incremental reveals: Instead of revealing all rewards at once (which led to issues where one item dominated discussion, like Dragon Claws at ToA's reveal), Jagex will reveal rewards 1-2 at a time throughout 2026.
  • Community polling: Each reward item will be polled individually. The 94.5% approval on the content poll suggests the community trusts Jagex with this approach.
  • Holistic evaluation: After all rewards are revealed, the full set will be evaluated as a package before final implementation.

Community Speculation (Not Confirmed)

The following are community-driven predictions from Reddit r/2007scape, the OSRS Discord, and content creator discussions. None of these are confirmed by Jagex.

  • Mega Rare weapon: Every raid since CoX has introduced a "mega rare" weapon (Twisted Bow, Scythe of Vitur, Tumeken's Shadow). The community widely expects The Fractured Archive to introduce a new mega rare, speculation includes a crush-style weapon or a new damage type to fill gaps in the current mega-rare triangle.
  • Guthixian armor set: Concept art suggests a nature-themed armor set — potentially a new bis option for certain combat styles or a hybrid set.
  • Prayer-related rewards: Given the Guthix theme (balance and nature), a new prayer book or prayer scrolls are a recurring community theory.
  • New BIS items: Previous raids have targeted underused equipment slots. Expect The Fractured Archive to continue this trend.
  • Cosmetic rewards: Pets, transmogs, and ornament kits are standard for OSRS raids and widely expected.

🚨 REWARD SPECULATION WARNING

All reward speculation is based on community discussion and historical precedent. Jagex has explicitly stated they are "not ready to talk about rewards" and will reveal them through a new process. Do not make financial decisions or gear purchases based on unconfirmed reward speculation.

7. How to Prepare — Gear, Stats & Supplies

Since The Fractured Archive hasn't released, this preparation guide is based on community analysis of the raid's confirmed design (ToB-style pure combat, multiple demi-bosses + final boss, fixed difficulty, 2-8 players).

Immediate Must-Do Actions

  1. Complete While Guthix Sleeps. This is the only confirmed requirement. If you haven't done this quest, start the quest chain NOW — it requires multiple Grandmaster quests.
  2. Max your combat stats. 99 Attack, Strength, Defence, Ranged, Magic, and Hitpoints are the realistic minimum for day-one raiding based on community expectations.
  3. Learn Theatre of Blood. The Fractured Archive will share ToB's design philosophy. ToB experience will directly translate to faster learning in the new raid.
  4. Build a gear bank. The community consensus recommends owning at least the "big three" mega rares if possible — Scythe of Vitur, Twisted Bow, and Tumeken's Shadow — as the new raid is likely to require all three combat styles.
  5. Stockpile supplies. Brews, restores, prayer potions, anglerfish, and runes for all combat styles. Day-one supply prices will spike.
  6. Save GP. If the raid follows historical patterns, unique drops on day one will trade at extreme premiums (100M+). Having liquid GP ready lets you buy items before the market stabilizes.

9. Community Speculation & Discussion

The OSRS community has been actively discussing The Fractured Archive since the Winter Summit 2026 announcement. Here are the most notable discussion threads and common theories:

Key Discussion Topics (from Reddit r/2007scape & OSRS Discord)

  • Reward speculation: The most active discussion topic. Common themes include: a crush-style mega rare to complete the combat triangle, a new prayer book themed around Guthix (balance/neutrality), and nature-themed armor.
  • Difficult vs ToB: Jagex stated this is "the hardest raid we've ever designed." Community consensus is that it will surpass ToB in mechanical difficulty, but the discussion is divided on whether this refers to individual mechanics complexity or overall execution difficulty.
  • 8-player scaling concerns: Some players worry that 8-player scaling will make the raid too easy in large groups or too difficult in small groups. Others welcome the social raiding aspect.
  • Restricted account access: Pures and skillers are concerned about the While Guthix Sleeps requirement. Jagex has acknowledged this and stated they will discuss accommodations in future blogs.
  • Sailing integration: While Jagex hasn't confirmed a Sailing requirement, many players expect the Chasm of Tears location to require some Sailing navigation. Theories range from a simple Sailing checkpoint to a full Sailing-based entry sequence.
  • Release date: Late 2026 (Q4) is a broad window. Community estimates range from October (if development is on track) to December (if it slips, similar to ToA's original timeline).

10. FAQ — The Fractured Archive

Q: When does The Fractured Archive release?

A: The raid was announced at the Winter Summit 2026 (January 25) and is scheduled for release in late 2026 (Q4). The content poll passed with 94.5% approval on February 2, 2026.

Q: What quests do I need to complete?

A: The only confirmed requirement is While Guthix Sleeps. Jagex is discussing accommodations for restricted accounts in future blogs.

Q: Do I need Sailing for this raid?

A: Jagex has not officially confirmed a Sailing requirement. Some community members speculate that level 50 Sailing may be required to navigate the Chasm of Tears. We recommend training Sailing to at least 50 as a precaution, but this remains unconfirmed. Check our Sailing guide for optimal training routes.

Q: Can I solo this raid?

A: Jagex has stated that solo play is possible but extremely difficult. The raid is designed around 2-8 players with dynamic scaling. Solo raiding will likely require max stats, top-tier gear, and exceptional mechanical skill.

Q: How many bosses are in the raid?

A: Two bosses have been officially revealed: The Archive Guardian and Ezekiel. The total number is unconfirmed, but community estimates suggest 4-6 encounters (3-5 demi-bosses + 1 final boss). More will be revealed through 2026.

Q: What are the rewards?

A: Jagex has not yet announced any specific rewards. They plan to reveal rewards one or two at a time through dedicated blog posts with community polling. This is the first raid where Jagex is using this incremental reveal strategy.

Q: How does the wipe system work?

A: Two phases: Launch period — full team wipe resets the entire raid (no second chances). This creates a high-stakes world-first race. Post-launch (weeks later) — transitions to a ToA-style system with unlimited attempts but loot penalty per death and finite supplies.

Q: Will there be a practice mode?

A: Yes, a practice mode is confirmed. It will allow you to practice individual bosses at normal difficulty. However, it will not be available at launch — it will be added "a few weeks" after the raid releases to preserve the competitive world-first race atmosphere.

Q: Is this raid harder than Theatre of Blood?

A: Jagex has described The Fractured Archive as "the hardest raid we've ever designed." The fixed difficulty (no invocation scaling) and hard wipe at launch reinforce this. The community consensus expects it to surpass ToB in overall difficulty, but exact difficulty comparisons won't be possible until the raid releases.

Q: Where can I follow official updates?

A: Official sources: oldschool.runescape.com for news posts, OSRS Wiki for consolidated information, and r/2007scape for community discussion. Jagex has confirmed they will release more details through blog posts throughout 2026.
