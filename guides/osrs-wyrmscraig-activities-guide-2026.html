Wyrmscraig Activities Guide 2026 — Boss, Quests, Slayer & More

Wyrmscraig is the latest Sailing expansion island hidden deep within the Unquiet Ocean. From the Fallen From Grace quest to Mortimer's innovative Slayer Master system, Golem Crafting, Goat Hunting, and a brand-new boss — this guide covers everything you need to know about 2026's most exciting island content.

⚡ Quick Verdict

Wyrmscraig is a high-value Sailing island requiring 62 Sailing to access. It offers a significant content package: a short quest (Fallen From Grace), a repeatable Fire Cape-tier boss, an innovative Slayer Master (Mortimer) with a task-choice + modifier system, two new training activities (Golem Crafting and Goat Hunting), and Sunstone gathering. Mortimer's system is the standout feature — it lets you pick your tasks and earn powerful modifiers instead of Slayer points. If you're 62+ Sailing and 85+ Combat, this island should be your next destination.

🗺️ Wyrmscraig Island Overview & Access

Wyrmscraig is a rough, rugged island hidden deep within the Unquiet Ocean, designed by community member RoseNMcgeet (aka ScreteMonge / Gnome!) as the winner of the 2026 Player-Designed Island competition. It was officially polled and locked in via the Wyrmscraig Activities & Lock In Poll on May 29, 2026.

② Auchrie Village

The main settlement on Wyrmscraig is Auchrie, a grim little village where the locals are not exactly thrilled to see outsiders. The villagers have survived harsh conditions for generations and have long since discovered the island's unique resource: Sunstones. They use these sunstones to craft golems for protection. Some facilities are available immediately upon arrival, but the full island content unlocks after completing the Fallen From Grace quest.

③ Teleport Access

After completing Fallen From Grace, you unlock a dedicated teleport method to the island — no need to sail back and forth every time. Additional teleport options may come from the repeatable boss and Golem Crafting rewards.

📜 Fallen From Grace Quest Walkthrough

Fallen From Grace is a short Experienced-tier quest that serves as your introduction to Wyrmscraig. Players familiar with the OSRS vampire questline may notice intriguing connections to the broader story arc.

② Quest Walkthrough

① Arrival at Auchrie

Sail to Wyrmscraig (requires 62 Sailing) and dock at Auchrie village. Speak with the locals to learn about the island's secrets and the source of the disturbances plaguing the settlement.

② Investigate the Island

Explore Wyrmscraig to uncover the mystery behind the villagers' fear. The quest progresses through dialogue and exploration, gradually revealing the threat lurking beneath the island's surface.

③ Face the Boss

The quest culminates in a boss fight against a dangerous enemy. This is a genuine challenge — bring your best combat gear, prayer potions, and food. The boss is rated at Fire Cape difficulty level. At 75 Combat, expect to use most or all of your supplies just to secure a single kill.

④ Claim Your Rewards

After defeating the boss, you unlock full access to all of Wyrmscraig's activities, including the repeatable boss fight and Golem Crafting.

🎯 Quest Rewards

  • 10,000 Crafting XP
  • 12,500 Sailing XP
  • 5,000 Mining XP
  • 5,000 Runecraft XP
  • Unlocks repeatable boss fight
  • Unlocks Golem Crafting activity
💡 Vampire Questline Connection

Fallen From Grace has been confirmed to connect to the broader vampire storyline, potentially tying into Blood Moon Rises and future quests. Lore enthusiasts should pay close attention to the dialogue.

⚔️ Repeatable Boss Fight — Mechanics & Drops

The boss you face during Fallen From Grace becomes a repeatable boss fight after the quest. Originally conceived as a "mini-boss," the prototype proved anything but mini — it's a full Fire Cape-tier encounter with real mechanics.

① Boss Overview

AttributeDetails
DifficultyFire Cape-tier (Post-Fire Cape recommended)
Minimum Viable Combat~75 (one clear possible, heavy supply usage)
Efficient Farming Combat80-95+
RepeatableYes — identical mechanics to quest version

② Combat Strategy

The boss mechanics remain the same as the quest encounter. At ~75 Combat, you can complete a kill but will likely consume most or all of your supplies — suitable for a clear, not for farming. At 80-95 Combat, efficient, extended trips become viable. Players with Fire Capes will find the fight manageable.

⚔️ Recommended Setup

Bring your standard Prayer-based PvM gear. Expect to use Protect from Melee/Magic depending on the boss's current phase. Prayer potions and high-healing food are essential. If you can handle Jad, you can handle this boss.

③ Confirmed Drops

  • Updated Sword Design — A redesigned sword cosmetic, details to be discussed in future development blogs.
  • Island Teleport — A method to teleport directly back to Wyrmscraig.
  • New Pet — A brand-new pet drop from the boss.

④ Angelic Heart — Vetoed

The original "Angelic Heart" reward proposal was removed from the boss's drop table. Jagex explained this decision clearly:

  • The boss and its requirements do not warrant such a powerful reward
  • It would compete with Forgotten/Ancient Brews (from Muspah and Nex)
  • If weaker, it would feel unsatisfying to use
  • If stronger, it would devalue the Imbued Heart
  • The "mini-heart" concept has been repurposed for Mortimer's Slayer system

👹 Mortimer — The New Slayer Master System

Mortimer is Wyrmscraig's Slayer Master, and he changes the Slayer game fundamentally. Unlike every other Slayer Master who assigns a single random task, Mortimer offers you choices.

① Unlocking Mortimer

RequirementDetails
AccessVisit Wyrmscraig (62 Sailing)
Combat Level100
Slayer Level70 (or 99 Slayer as alternative)

② How Mortimer Works

Mortimer presents you with 2 task choices initially — you pick the one you prefer. After completing 100 tasks, you unlock a third choice. This lets you choose between a fast task, a profitable task, or a specific Superior monster hunt.

⚠️ Important Restrictions
  • Skip cost is steep: 100 Slayer points (vs 30 for Duradel)
  • Cannot Turael skip: Mortimer tasks cannot be reset via Turael
  • Only 2 block slots (costing 120 points each)
  • "Like a Boss" is disabled — Duradel remains the best boss-task master

③ The Modifier System

Instead of Slayer points, completing Mortimer tasks unlocks Modifiers — powerful passive bonuses that appear on your task choices. At least one task option always comes with a modifier. Each other option has a 10% chance to also roll a modifier.

Tasks CompletedUnlock

0Modifier 2: Task quantity adjustment (% change)

50Modifier 4: Slayer XP +X% from kills

④ Task List

Mortimer can assign every Slayer monster that has a Superior variant. Task weights are currently planned to be equal. Notable tasks include:

  • Smoke Devils, Abyssal Demons, Nechryaels, Dust Devils
  • Hydras, Dark Beasts, Gargoyles, Wyrms, Bloodvelds
  • Araxyte, Custodian Stalkers, Gryphons, Aquanites
  • Drakes, Kurasks, Basilisk variants, and more
  • Low-level options: Banshees, Crawling Hands, Cave Crawlers, etc.

🔨 Golem Crafting — New Crafting Training Method

Golem Crafting is a brand-new Crafting training method designed to be a viable alternative to Glassblowing, especially at higher levels. It combines Mining and Crafting XP in a multi-step process and introduces the Momentum mechanic.

① Requirements

RequirementDetails
Crafting60
Mining53
QuestFallen From Grace (completed)

② Step 1: Sunstone Mining

Mine Sunstone from Sunstone rocks on Wyrmscraig. This is the core material for Golem Crafting.

  • Momentum Mechanic: After a successful mine, your next mining attempt within 5 ticks has a higher success rate — starting at 75% at level 50 Mining and reaching 100% at level 92.
  • Bonus: While Momentum is active, 20% chance to gain an extra Sunstone shard.
  • Bonuses: Benefits from Expert Mining Gloves and other Mining upgrades.
  • Passive option: You can also mine from Sunstone Monoliths for slower but more AFK gathering.

③ Step 2: Craft the Golem Base

Use 4 Sunstone blocks at a crafting station to create a Golem base. The base then needs to be carved on four sides (North, East, West, South). Each side takes 15 ticks (60 total + movement time). Carving can be sped up by clicking at the correct tick.

④ Step 3: Insert the Golem Core

Use a Chisel on a Sunstone to craft a Golem Core (can be pre-made). Insert the core into the carved golem at the front face. With efficient pathing, you can insert the core immediately after carving.

⑤ Step 4: Clothe the Golem

This is the Crafting XP step. Use Hunter furs to "clothe" the golem. This gives Golem Crafting a non-self-sufficient resource loop and gives Hunter furs a valuable sink.

  • Higher quality furs yield more Crafting XP
  • Low-tier furs (including island-sourced) work fine
  • Rare furs from across Gielinor offer the best XP rates

🎯 Design Goals

  • XP-focused — Designed as a competitor to Glassblowing at high Crafting levels
  • Multi-skill synergy — Combines Mining, Crafting, and Hunter
  • Hunter fur sink — Gives purpose to a previously underused resource

🏹 Goat Hunting — Active Hunter Training

Goat Hunting is a new Hunter training activity on Wyrmscraig, designed as an active, engaging alternative to Red/Black Salamanders. It's the fastest way to train Hunter on the island and provides materials for Golem Crafting.

① Requirements

RequirementDetails
Hunter60
QuestSheep Herder (completed)

② Game Loop

① Arm the Pit

Use spike sets (found on the island) to arm a pit trap.

② Drive Goats into the Pit

Use a cattleprod to herd goats into the armed pit. Alternatively, use Telekinetic Grab or Dark Lure spells for Magic XP — giving these spells additional utility. Success grants a small amount of Hunter XP. At level 60 Hunter, you have approximately 15% failure rate.

③ Process the Pit

Once the pit is full (number of goats depends on your Hunter level), interact with it to process the goats. This awards the bulk of the XP and materials.

④ Repeat

Re-arm the pit with new spike sets and begin again.

③ XP Rates

Hunter LevelXP/Hour
60~80,000 (slightly below Red Salamanders)
80~140,000

④ Drops & Rewards

  • Goat Fur — Used in Golem Crafting to clothe golems
  • Goat Horns — Additional material drop
  • Pet: Mr McGroot — A unique pet from the activity
💡 Efficient Training Strategy

Use Magic (Telekinetic Grab / Dark Lure) to herd goats — this trains Magic alongside Hunter. Pre-craft spike sets before starting. The loop is active but offers good XP rates at higher levels.

💎 Sunstone Gathering Mechanics

Sunstones are Wyrmscraig's unique resource, essential for Golem Crafting. There are two ways to gather them:

① Active Mining (Recommended)

Mine Sunstone rocks like standard ores. The Momentum mechanic makes this increasingly efficient as your Mining level rises:

  • Level 50: 75% success rate per tick with Momentum
  • Level 92: 100% success rate — every attempt succeeds
  • 20% bonus: Extra Sunstone shard while Momentum is active
  • Synergizes with: Expert Mining Gloves and other Mining gear

② Passive Mining (AFK Option)

Mine from Sunstone Monoliths for a slower but highly AFK gathering method. Perfect for players who want to collect Sunstones while watching something on the side.

❓ Wyrmscraig FAQs

What Sailing level do I need to visit Wyrmscraig?

62 Sailing is required to sail to Wyrmscraig. This is a mid-to-high level Sailing requirement, meaning you'll need to invest some time in the Sailing skill before exploring the Unquiet Ocean.

Is Fallen From Grace a hard quest?

It's rated as Experienced-tier. The quest itself is short with straightforward exploration and dialogue. The boss fight at the end is the real challenge — it's Fire Cape-tier difficulty. You need at least 85-90 Combat for a comfortable clear. Bring prayer potions and good food.

Can I farm the boss for profit?

Yes, but not until you're 80-95+ Combat. At lower levels (around 75 Combat), you'll spend most of your supplies on a single kill. The boss drops include an updated sword design, a teleport method, and a pet. More rewards are expected in future updates.

What makes Mortimer different from Duradel?

Mortimer does not give Slayer points. Instead, he offers 2-3 task choices and attaches Modifiers (bonus XP, clue rate, Superior spawn rate, etc.) to tasks. He's ideal for hunting specific Superior monsters or chasing rare drops. Duradel remains better for general XP/hour farming.

Is Golem Crafting better than Glassblowing for XP?

At high Crafting levels, Golem Crafting is designed to be a viable competitor to Glassblowing. It also awards Mining XP alongside Crafting XP, making it more efficient for overall account progress. However, it requires Hunter furs as a resource input, so it's not self-sufficient like Glassblowing.

What's the best way to train Hunter on Wyrmscraig?

Goat Hunting is the dedicated Hunter activity. At level 60 Hunter, you'll get ~80K XP/hour. At level 80, this rises to ~140K XP/hour. Use Telekinetic Grab or Dark Lure to also train Magic while herding goats.

What is the Momentum mechanic?

Momentum is a new mechanic introduced for Sunstone mining. After a successful mine, your next attempt within 5 ticks has a higher success rate (75% at level 50, up to 100% at level 92). It also gives a 20% chance for an extra Sunstone shard. Think of it as a rhythm-based mining bonus.

Was the Angelic Heart removed?

Yes. The Angelic Heart drop was vetoed by Jagex due to balancing concerns — it would have competed with the Imbued Heart and Forgotten/Ancient Brews. The concept has been repurposed and may appear in future Slayer content tied to Mortimer.

Do I need to complete Pandemonium first?

Yes, Pandemonium is a prerequisite quest for Fallen From Grace. Make sure you've completed it before sailing to Wyrmscraig, along with having 62 Sailing, 60 Crafting, 53 Mining, and 47 Runecraft.

🎯 What To Do After Unlocking Wyrmscraig

Once you've completed Fallen From Grace and unlocked all activities, here's your recommended priority order:

→ Step 1: Try the repeatable boss a few times — learn the mechanics and aim for the pet
→ Step 2: Start working on Mortimer tasks — the modifier system gets valuable after 25-50 tasks
→ Step 3: Gather Sunstones and try Golem Crafting — a great alternative to Glassblowing
→ Step 4: Train Hunter through Goat Hunting — aim for 80+ for the best XP rates
