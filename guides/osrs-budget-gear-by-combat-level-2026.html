🎽 OSRS Budget Gear by Combat Level 2026

You don't need 10M GP in gear to be effective in OSRS. This guide shows the best budget gear for 40, 50, 60, and 70 combat stats — with exact GP costs, upgrade priorities, and budget alternatives. Whether you're F2P or a new member, here's how to gear up without breaking the bank.

📅 Updated: June 2026 — Budget gear for every combat bracket · 11 min read
💡 Quick Verdict — Budget Gear 2026

At 40 Combat: spend 150K GP on rune gear + amulet of power. At 50 Combat: upgrade to Dragon scimitar + Dragon chainbody (500K-800K GP total). At 60 Combat: get Abyssal Whip (from Barrows) or Dragon scimitar + Dragon Defender (1M-3M GP). At 70 Combat: Barrows weapons + Obsidian platebody (3M-8M GP). Always upgrade your weapon before your armour — a Dragon scimitar is a bigger DPS upgrade than Dragon platebody.

Most OSRS gear guides assume you have 10M+ GP and 80+ combat stats. But the reality is: you can be effective with 150K GP and 40 Combat. This guide is for players who want to gear up on a budget — whether you're F2P, a new member, or just don't want to spend 100 hours grinding for BiS gear.

Each combat bracket has a minimum gear setup (cheapest effective option) and a recommended gear setup (best value for performance). We'll cover both — so you can gear up at your own pace. For a complete F2P-to-members gear transition guide, also check our F2P-to-P2P guide.

① 🎯 What "Budget Gear" Means — Definitions

"Budget gear" doesn't mean "bad gear." It means best value for performance — the gear that gives you the biggest power boost per GP spent. Here's the philosophy:

PrincipleExplanationExample
Upgrade Weapon FirstWeapon DPS > Armour Defence. A Dragon scimitar (+12 max hit) is a bigger upgrade than Dragon platebody (+6 defence).Dragon scimitar (120K GP) > Dragon chainbody (150K GP)
Don't Overupgrade DefenceDefence only reduces max hit (doesn't reduce hit frequency). At low combat, offence > defence.60 Attack + 40 Defence > 50 Attack + 60 Defence
Use What You GetBarrows drops (free!) are better than anything you can buy for 500K GP. Always check your loot before buying gear.Abyssal Whip (Barrows drop) > Dragon scimitar (120K GP)
💡 The "Power Per GP" Metric

When buying gear, calculate Power Per GP = (DPS Increase / GP Cost). A Dragon scimitar costs 120K GP and increases your max hit by 12 (vs. rune scimitar) = 0.1 DPS/GP. A Dragon chainbody costs 150K GP and reduces damage taken by 6 per hit = ~0.04 DPS/GP. Weapon always wins. For the complete DPS calculation guide, see our DPS calculator guide.

3.2 🔼 What to Skip at 50 Combat (Don't Buy These)

  • Amulet of Fury (2.5M GP): Too expensive. Wait until you have 5M+ GP bank.
  • Dragon Platebody + Platelegs (330K GP total): The defence upgrade is nice, but weapon DPS is more important. Skip until after you have Dragon scimitar + Defender.
  • Barrows weapons (variable, 500K-5M GP): You can't use Barrows weapons yet (most require 70+ stats). Skip until 60-70 Combat.

3.3 📊 What This Gear Can Do

  • Giant Mole (60 Attack/Strength recommended): With Dragon scimitar, kills in 3-5 minutes. 500K-800K GP/hour.
  • Barrows (50 Combat + 43 Prayer): Kills brothers in 2-4 minutes each. 200K-400K GP/hour.
  • Ogress Warriors: Kills in 5-8 seconds. 150K-250K GP/hour (base).
💡 Dragon Defender is FREE and BIS — Get it at 60 Attack/60 Defence

The Dragon Defender is the biggest DPS upgrade for 50-70 Combat accounts. It requires 65 Attack + 65 Defence (or 60 + 60 if you train at Warriors' Guild) and is obtained for free from the Warriors' Guild. If you're 50 Combat but have 60 Attack — go to Warriors' Guild immediately. The Defender is better than any 500K GP weapon upgrade. For the complete Warriors' Guild guide, see our Dragon Defender guide.

4.2 🔼 The "No Whip Yet" Budget Path (120K GP Total)

If RNG hasn't blessed you with a Barrows Whip drop yet — don't worry. Your Dragon scimitar is still BIS for 60 Combat among buyable weapons. Here's the absolute minimum gear for 60 Combat:

  • Weapon: Dragon scimitar (120K GP) — still amazing
  • Shield: Dragon Defender (free) — massive upgrade over kiteshield
  • Body: Dragon chainbody (150K GP) — or keep rune platebody if on extreme budget
  • Amulet: Amulet of Power (1.2K GP) — until Fury
  • Total: ~270K GP. You can earn this in 2-4 hours of Giant Mole.

4.3 📊 What This Gear Can Do

  • Giant Mole: Kills in 1.5-2.5 minutes (with Whip) or 2-4 minutes (with Dragon scimitar). 500K-1M GP/hour.
  • Barrows: Full runs in 6-10 minutes. 400K-800K GP/hour.
  • God Wars Dungeon (team): With 70 Combat + this gear, you can join GWD teams. 1M-2M GP/hour (split loot).
  • Zulrah (with 75 Magic + 70 Ranged): Possible but extremely difficult. 2M+ GP/hour if you can survive the mechanics.

💡 The "Barrows Drop" Priority

If you're doing Barrows at 70 Combat, your priority drop is Abyssal Whip. It's untradeable, so you MUST farm it. Once you have a Whip, your next priority is Barrows armour sets (Karil's, Ahrim's, Dharok's, etc.) for their set effects. They're tradeable — but farming them is faster than buying (unless you have 10M+ GP bank).

💡 Don't Buy What You Can Farm — The Barrows Rule

Abyssal Whip, Barrows armour sets, and most "high-end" melee gear is farmable for free from Barrows. If you're spending 5M GP on gear that you could get for free in 10-15 hours of Barrows — you're wasting GP. The only things you should buy are: Dragon scimitar, Amulet of Fury, and Consumables (food, potions, runes). Gear? Farm it. For the complete Barrows farming guide, see our Barrows farming guide.

⑦ ❓ FAQ — Budget Gear by Combat Level

Q: What's the absolute minimum gear to start bossing?

A: 43 Prayer + Rune scimitar + Rune platebody + Amulet of Power (total: ~150K GP). With this gear and 50 Combat, you can solo Barrows. The runs will be slow (15-20 minutes), but the loot is real. Upgrade to Dragon scimitar as soon as you have 120K GP.

Q: When should I stop using budget gear?

A: Stop using budget gear when you have 10M+ GP bank and 80+ combat stats. At that point, you can afford Bandos chestplate (4.5M GP) and Barrows weapons (farmed). "Budget gear" for 80+ Combat means: Bandos chest + Tassets + Abyssal Whip + Amulet of Fury + Dragon boots. That's ~15M GP total — a far cry from the 150K GP starter gear, but still "budget" compared to 100M GP BiS setups.

Q: Is Dragon chainbody worth 150K GP?

A: Barely. The defence upgrade from rune platebody to Dragon chainbody is only +6. For 150K GP, you could buy 1.2x Dragon scimitar (if you lose yours) or 3,000 lobsters. Spend the GP on combat training instead. Only buy Dragon chainbody if you have surplus GP and want the +3 Prayer bonus (helps with Protect prayers duration).

Q: What about Ranged/Magic budget gear?

A: This guide focuses on melee (the most popular combat style for budget players). For Ranged: Green d'hide (40 Ranged, cheap) → Blue d'hide (50 Ranged) → Red d'hide (60 Ranged). For Magic: Staff of Air (free) → Mystc staff (40 Magic, 60K GP) → Lava staff (60 Magic, 300K GP). The principles are the same: upgrade weapon first, don't overupgrade armour. For complete Ranged/Magic budget guides, see our Ranged budget gear guide and Magic budget gear guide.

Q: Can I PvP with budget gear?

A: In F2P: yes, rune gear is the PvP meta (everyone has it, so you're not at a disadvantage). In P2P: budget gear (Dragon scimitar + Obby) is viable for "pure" accounts (e.g., 60 Attack / 40 Defence pures). But for main accounts, you'll get destroyed by players with Bandos + Abyssal Whip in P2P PvP. Stick to PvM (bossing) with budget gear — it's designed for PvM, not PvP. For PvP-specific gear guides, see our PvP budget gear guide.

Q: How do I get Dragon Defender without 65 Attack/Defence?

A: You don't — the Warriors' Guild requires 65 Attack + 65 Defence (or 60 + 60 if you train at the Guild). If you're 50 Attack / 50 Defence, you can't enter. Train to 60/60 first (takes 10-20 hours of combat training), then go to the Guild. The Defender is worth the grind — it's free and gives +24 Attack bonus.

Q: What's the best budget amulet before Fury?

A: Amulet of Power (1,200 GP) — +10% melee damage. Alternatively, Amulet of Strength (1,050 GP) — +10% melee strength. The difference is minimal — use whichever is cheaper on the GE. At 50+ Magic, you can also use Amulet of Magic (2,500 GP) for Magic training. But for melee bossing, Power is BIS budget.

Q: Should I buy a Bond or buy gear first?

A: Gear first. If you have 1M GP, spend 150K GP on rune gear + Power amulet, then use the remaining 850K GP to work towards a bond (5.2M GP total). Having good gear makes money making faster — which means you reach 5.2M GP sooner. The only exception: if you're 40 Combat and have 0 GP, consider buying a bond with real money (£5-£8) to unlock members methods (which are 3-5× faster than F2P).

⑧ 💡 Final Tips for Budget Gearing

Budget gearing in OSRS is all about patience and prioritisation. You don't need BiS gear to enjoy the game — you need smart gear choices.

  • 43 Prayer before anything else. It's the biggest combat upgrade in the game, and it costs almost nothing (just time to bury big bones or run to Wilderness altar).
  • Farm, don't buy. Abyssal Whip, Barrows armour, and most high-end gear is farmable for free. Only buy: weapons (Dragon scimitar), amulets (Fury), and consumables.
  • Weapon > Armour. A Dragon scimitar is a bigger upgrade than Dragon chainbody. Always upgrade weapon first.
  • Use your loot. Got a Barrows drop? Equip it. Don't sell it for 50K GP if it's a 200K GP upgrade to your gear.
  • Track your DPS. If your kill speed isn't improving as you upgrade gear — something's wrong. Check your Attack style (choose "Aggressive" for Strength XP + max hit), check your inventory (are you using potions?), check your ping (RuneLite shows your DPS).
  • Join a clan chat for gear advice. "OSRS Advice" and "Reddit CC" have experienced players who can review your gear and suggest upgrades. Don't guess — ask.
🎯 The Budget Gearing Mindset

OSRS has a "pay-to-win" reputation — but it's not true. You can do 99% of the game content with 1M GP in gear. The other 1% (Chambers of Xeric, Theatre of Blood, Tombs of Amascut) requires 10M+ GP — but you don't need to do that content to enjoy OSRS. Budget gear is viable, fun, and teaches you the game mechanics without relying on overpowered gear. Embrace the budget — the GRIND is the game.
