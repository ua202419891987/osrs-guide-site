1. 🌱 Overview & Why F2P Beginners Start Here

Free-to-play OSRS is the most honest economy in the game: you have a tiny GE limit (only a few buy/sell slots), no Farming or Herblore money, and no high-level bosses. That sounds restrictive, but it means the beginner methods are stable, bot-resistant enough, and genuinely learnable. The four methods in this guide — cowhides, flax, lobsters, and essence — are the ones veterans name when asked "how did you get your first bond?"

🎯 The F2P Beginner Loop

  • Zero risk: All four are safe (no Wilderness, no PvP). You can never lose your items doing them.
  • Low floor: Cowhide collecting needs literally no stats. Flax needs 10 Crafting (≈30 min to get). Lobsters need 40 Fishing.
  • Real progress: Each method also trains a skill (Crafting, Fishing, Runecraft), so your time compounds into account progress.
  • Capital builder: Stack ~1–3M GP and you can self-fund your first bond — see the F2P-to-bond guides linked at the bottom.
💡 Read Prices Before You Commit

The OSRS Wiki prints a single "hourly profit" number, but every figure here depends on live Grand Exchange prices. Before any bulk buy, check the current buy/sell of the item on the GE. Margins move daily — a method "dead" today can be golden tomorrow.

4. 🐄 Method 1: Collecting Cowhides

The cow field north-east of Lumbridge Castle is the single best 0-requirement money maker in F2P. Cows are level 2, harmless, and drop a cowhide (100% rate) plus bones. The trick most new players miss: you don't even have to kill them — many players train combat there and leave hides on the ground. Walk the field picking up ground hides and you fill an inventory in 2–3 minutes with zero fighting.

Step 1 — Get to the field

From Lumbridge Castle, run north and slightly east to the cow pen. The north-east pen has the shortest run to the castle basement bank.

Step 2 — Collect & bank

Grab every cowhide (and bones) you can carry (28 slots). Run to the Lumbridge Castle basement bank. Sell hides + bones on the GE. Repeat. ~180K–250K GP/hr at current hide prices.

Step 3 — Optional: tan for more (P2P bridge)

Members (or F2P who finished Prince Ali Rescue for free Al Kharid entry) can tan hides at Ellis for 1–3 GP each and sell leather for a markup. See the F2P-to-P2P guide linked below.

💡 Prince Ali Rescue Saves Tolls

Completing Prince Ali Rescue (a short, no-combat quest) removes the 10-GP Al Kharid gate toll forever. If you ever tan in Al Kharid, do this first — it pays for itself in one session.

5. 🧵 Method 2: Spinning Flax → Bowstrings

Flax is one of the cheapest tradeable items in the game, and bowstrings are always in demand (every Fletching training session needs them). The margin is simple: buy flax cheap, spin it, sell the bowstring for more. You need 10 Crafting (reachable in ~30 minutes by spinning flax itself or killing chickens for bones).

🏆 Best F2P Setup: Lumbridge Castle

Buy flax on the GE, withdraw 28 at the Lumbridge bank, run upstairs to the spinning wheel on the same building's 2nd floor, spin all 28 into bowstrings, bank, repeat. The bank and wheel are in one building — minimal running. ~1,500–2,500 flax/hr processed → ~150K–250K GP/hr before GE tax.

💡 Watch the Buy Limit

Both flax and bowstrings have Grand Exchange buy limits. You cannot process unlimited volume — once you hit the limit you wait 4 hours. Treat spinning as a steady supplementary method, not a 700K/hr fantasy. The Wiki's processing estimates assume you can buy freely; reality is lower.

6. 🦞 Method 3: Fishing Lobsters (F2P)

At 40 Fishing you unlock lobsters — a F2P staple food with steady demand. The F2P spot is Musa Point, Karamja. Bank at Port Sarim (take the boat from Musa Point for 30 GP, or use the Port Sarim teleport if unlocked). Lobster fishing is semi-AFK: click the fishing spot, wait, bank, repeat.

7. ✨ Method 4: Runecrafting Essence

Runecrafting is the classic "slow but steady" skill. In F2P the honest truth is: you cannot make real GP from essence until you're a member (nature/law runes need members areas or the Abyss). But air runes are a perfect 0-requirement starting point that doubles as RC training.

🪨 Air Runes (F2P intro, 1 Runecraft)

Buy pure essence from a rune shop (~30 GP) or mine it, enter the air altar south of Falador, craft air runes (1 rune per essence at level 1). Air runes sell for ~5–7 GP. Profit is small (~10K–30K/hr) but it's free XP and teaches the altar loop.

🔥 Nature Runes via the Abyss (P2P, 44 Runecraft)

Once a member, the jump is enormous: the OSRS Wiki lists "Crafting nature runes through the Abyss" at 1,132,000 GP/hr (91 RC recommended, 44 required). The Abyss is in the Wilderness edge, so bring minimal gear. This is the bridge from F2P beginner to real money.

💡 Essence Is a Patience Skill

Don't grind air runes expecting bond money in F2P — you won't get it. Use air runes to reach ~20–30 Runecraft, then let membership unlock the real rune money. The F2P essence method is about learning the loop and banking XP, not GP.

9. ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Myth-Busting)

❌ Myth 1: "I'll just buy GP from a bot site — faster."

Never. Buying GP from RWT/bot sellers is a direct breach of Jagex's rules and gets accounts banned or rolled back. It also funds the bots that inflate hide/flax prices and hurt the F2P economy you're trying to use. Earn it the safe way — the methods here genuinely work.

❌ Myth 2: "Spinning flax makes 700K/hr no matter what."

No. That number assumes infinite flax supply. The GE buy limit on flax (and the sell limit on bowstrings) caps you. Real sustained F2P spinning is ~150K–250K/hr for most players.

❌ Myth 3: "Air runes will fund my bond."

They won't in F2P. Air runes are ~5–7 GP each — profit is tiny and mostly XP. Real rune money needs membership (nature runes via the Abyss, ~1.13M/hr Wiki).

❌ Myth 4: "Cooking lobsters always beats selling raw."

Only above ~74 Cooking (no burn). Below that your burn rate eats the margin. For a beginner, sell raw lobsters and skip the cooking grind until later.

❌ Myth 5: "Cowhides are only from killing cows."

You can pick up ground hides other players leave while combat-training. That's often faster than killing your own cows — and zero combat risk.

10. ❓ FAQ

What's the very first method I should do at level 3?

Collecting cowhides at the Lumbridge cow field. Zero requirements, zero risk, and ~180K–250K GP/hr. Pair it with the one-time Stronghold of Security (10K GP, no requirements) for a quick starter stack.

Do I need membership to make my first bond?

No. Thousands of players bond out in pure F2P using cowhides, flax, lobsters and shop methods. Membership speeds it up massively, but it's optional. See the F2P-to-bond guides below.

Is spinning flax better than collecting cowhides?

They're similar GP/hr (~150K–250K), but cowhides need no stats while flax needs 10 Crafting. Do cowhides first; add flax once you have the level (or just buy flax and spin while watching a video).

Why are my GP/hr numbers lower than the Wiki's?

The Wiki assumes perfect efficiency and ignores GE buy limits and tax. Real players bank slower and hit limits. Treat the Wiki number as a ceiling, not a promise.

When should I stop F2P methods and go member?

Once you have ~1–3M GP banked and want faster progress. Membership unlocks nature runes (~1.13M/hr), birdhouses, herb runs, and bossing — see the member guide linked below.

11. 🏁 Final Tips

  • Sequence: cowhides → flax (after 10 Crafting) → lobsters (after 40 Fishing) → air runes for RC XP. Stack ~1–3M before considering a bond.
  • Check GE first: margins flip daily. A 2-minute price check saves a wasted bulk buy.
  • Stay safe: all four methods are non-Wilderness and risk-free. Never "upgrade" into a Wilderness method as a beginner.
  • Skill while you earn: each method trains a skill — your bank and your account grow together.
  • Next step: once you have a few million, read the 0-Requirement Money Making guide for shop methods that scale with capital, then the Member Money Making guide when you subscribe.
