OSRS Cerberus Boss Guide 2026 — Mid-Game Slayer Boss

By OSRSGuru Updated: January 15, 2026 22 min read
Combat Level: 318 Hitpoints: 300 Slayer Requirement: 91 Avg Kill Time: 45-60 seconds

Welcome to the definitive OSRS Cerberus guide for 2026. Cerberus is a mid-game Slayer boss that represents a major milestone for any OSRS account. Why? Because she drops the three most important boot upgrades in the game: Primordial Boots, Pegasian Boots, and Eternal Boots.

These boots are best-in-slot for melee, ranged, and magic respectively, and they don't degrade. Getting all three is a massive power spike that will significantly improve your PvM performance across the board. But Cerberus is no pushover — she requires precise prayer switching, good gear, and the ability to survive a challenging 3-phase fight.

In this comprehensive guide, you'll learn everything you need to efficiently farm Cerberus: the exact requirements, optimal gear setups for every budget, detailed fight mechanics (including the ghost phase), step-by-step strategies for all three phases, the complete loot table with expected KP/hour, pet chances, and pro tips to maximize your efficiency.

Chapter 1: Requirements — 91 Slayer & Gear Check

Cerberus is a Slayer-exclusive boss, meaning you must meet specific requirements before you can fight her. Here's everything you need:

Slayer Requirements

  • 91 Slayer: This is the only Slayer level requirement. However, reaching 91 Slayer takes significant time (~150-200 tasks).
  • Cerberus Unlocked: You must unlock Cerberus as a Slayer task from Duradel, Nieve, or Steve (after 80 Slayer).
  • Task Amount: Cerberus tasks are typically 50-75 kills. You can extend the task once with a Slayer VIP Coupon or by paying 100 Slayer points.

Combat Stats Recommendation

While there are no hard stat requirements, here are the recommended minimums for efficiently killing Cerberus:

Stat Entry Level Recommended Efficient
Attack 70 80 90+
Strength 70 85 99
Defence 70 80 90+
Ranged 75 85 99
Magic 75 85 99
Prayer 70 (Piety) 74 (Rigour) 77+ (Augury)
Hitpoints 80 90 99

Essential Quests

  • Priest in Peril: Unlocks the ability to enter Morytania (required to reach Cerberus)
  • Nature Spirit: Unlocks some Prayer rewards
  • Dream Mentor: Unlocks the Lunar Spellbook (optional but useful for Vengeance)
  • Dragon Slayer II: Unlocks Ava's Assembler (highly recommended for Ranged setups)

Recommended Stats for Efficiency

Pro Tip: Don't Rush Cerberus at 91 Slayer

While you CAN fight Cerberus at 91 Slayer with 70 combat stats, you'll struggle. Your kill times will be slow (2-3 minutes per kill), and you'll die frequently. We recommend at least 80+ in all combat stats and 70+ Prayer (for Piety/Rigour) before attempting Cerberus. This will make the grind much more enjoyable.

Chapter 3: Fight Mechanics — 3 Ghosts & Prayer Switching

Cerberus has a complex 3-phase fight mechanic that makes her unique among OSRS bosses. Understanding these mechanics is crucial for survival and efficiency.

Phase 1: The Three Ghosts

At the start of the fight (and every 100 HP she loses), Cerberus summons three ghosts: Summoning Mage, Summoning Range, and Summoning Melee. These ghosts attack with their respective combat styles and can quickly kill you if you don't pray correctly.

Ghost Attack Patterns

Ghost Type Attack Style Damage Correct Prayer
Summoning Mage (Blue) Magic (Air spells) 18-24 per hit Protect from Magic
Summoning Range (Red) Ranged (Arrows) 20-28 per hit Protect from Missiles
Summoning Melee (Green) Melee (Slash) 22-30 per hit Protect from Melee

Pro Tip: Prayer Switching order

The ghosts always appear in the same order: Mage → Range → Melee. Memorize this order to switch prayers quickly. Also, the ghosts have low HP (~10 each), so you can kill them quickly to avoid prayer switching entirely.

Phase 2: Cerberus Appears

After the ghosts are dealt with, Cerberus becomes attackable. She has three attack styles that she rotates through:

  • Magic Attack: She shoots a blue ball of magic. Pray Protect from Magic.
  • Ranged Attack: She shoots a red projectile. Pray Protect from Missiles.
  • Melee Attack: She bites or claws you. Pray Protect from Melee OR use Venom (Serpentine Helm) to negate most melee damage.

Important: Cerberus's attacks are predictable. She always uses the attack style you're NOT praying against. If you're praying Magic, she'll attack with Ranged or Melee next. Learn this pattern to efficiently switch prayers.

Phase 3: All Together (100 HP & 200 HP)

When Cerberus reaches 200 HP and 100 HP, she summons the three ghosts again WHILE continuing to attack. This is the most dangerous part of the fight. You must:

  1. Kill or ignore the ghosts (depending on your DPS)
  2. Continue praying correctly against Cerberus
  3. Manage your food and prayer

Special Attacks

Lava Attack (Rare)

Occasionally, Cerberus will shoot lava at you that deals damage over time. This can't be prayed against. Eat immediately if you get hit by this.

Triple Attack (Rare)

Cerberus will sometimes attack three times in rapid succession with different combat styles. This can deal massive damage if you're not careful. Keep your HP above 60 to survive these bursts.

When to Use Purple Sweets

Purple Sweets are a unique item that heals 1 HP each and can be eaten while moving or attacking. They're incredibly useful at Cerberus for:

  • Conserving food during longer kills
  • Healing small amounts between ghost phases
  • Keeping your HP above 60 without wasting sharks

How to get Purple Sweets: They're a reward from the Halloween event (trade with other players) or buy from the Grand Exchange (~1,500 GP each as of January 2026).

Chapter 4: Step-by-Step Strategy — All 3 Phases

Now let's put it all together. Here's the exact step-by-step strategy for killing Cerberus efficiently.

Pre-Fight Preparation

  1. Teleport to Cerberus using a Slayer Ring (recommended) or run from the Taverley Dungeon entrance.
  2. Drink a Super Combat Potion (or Ranging Potion if using Ranged).
  3. Activate Piety (Melee) or Rigour (Ranged).
  4. Enter the cave and attack Cerberus to start the fight.

Phase 1 Strategy (Ghosts Appear)

Step 1: Identify the First Ghost

The first ghost is always the Summoning Mage (Blue). Immediately switch to Protect from Magic.

Step 2: Kill or Tank the Mage Ghost

Option A (Efficient): Attack and kill the Mage ghost quickly (1-2 hits). This eliminates the need to pray against it.
Option B (Tank): Keep praying Magic and let your protection reduce damage. Consume 1-2 damage per hit.

Step 3: Switch to Protect from Missiles

The second ghost is the Summoning Range (Red). Switch to Protect from Missiles immediately.

Step 4: Kill or Tank the Range Ghost

Same as Step 2. Killing is faster but uses more food/prayer. Tank if you're low on supplies.

Step 5: Switch to Protect from Melee

The third ghost is the Summoning Melee (Green). Switch to Protect from Melee.

Step 6: Kill or Tank the Melee Ghost

By now, Cerberus should be almost ready to attack. Kill the Melee ghost quickly if possible.

Phase 2 Strategy (Cerberus is Attackable)

Step 1: Attack Cerberus

Once the ghosts are dead or almost dead, start attacking Cerberus. She will now attack you with her three styles.

Step 2: Prayer Switch Against Cerberus

Cerberus always uses the attack style you're NOT praying against. The pattern is:

  • You pray Magic → She attacks with Ranged or Melee next
  • You pray Ranged → She attacks with Magic or Melee next
  • You pray Melee → She attacks with Magic or Ranged next

Efficient strategy: Keep praying the style she used LAST. This way, you're always protected against 2/3 of her attacks.

Step 3: Deal Damage Quickly

Use your best DPS abilities and attacks. If using Melee, use specs (Dragon Warhammer special, Bandos Godsword special) to boost damage. If using Ranged, use Dragon Bolts (e) for extra DPS.

Phase 3 Strategy (200 HP & 100 HP — Ghosts Return)

Step 1: Ghosts Appear Again

When Cerberus reaches 200 HP or 100 HP, she summons the three ghosts again. This happens DURING the fight, so you must manage both Cerberus and the ghosts.

Step 2: Prioritize Ghosts or Cerberus?

If you have high DPS (Twisted Bow / Inquisitor's Mace): Ignore the ghosts and keep attacking Cerberus. Your DPS is high enough that she'll die before the ghosts become a problem.

If you have low-mid DPS: Kill the ghosts quickly (especially the Mage ghost, which deals the most damage per hit). Use AOE attacks if possible (Dragon Claws, Dragon Dagger specs).

Step 3: Manage Prayer and Food

This is the most prayer-intensive part of the fight. You'll be switching prayers frequently. Drink Prayer Potions as needed. Don't let your prayer drop to 0.

Step 4: Finish Cerberus

Once Cerberus reaches 0 HP, she dies. Loot the ground (check for Pegasian Boots, Primordial Boots, Eternal Boots, or the Cerberus Pet). Then, reset the instance and start again.

Example Kill (Step-by-Step Timeline)

0:00 — Enter cave, drink Super Combat, activate Piety
0:05 — Attack Cerberus, Mage ghost appears, switch to Protect from Magic
0:10 — Kill Mage ghost (1 hit), Range ghost appears, switch to Protect from Missiles
0:15 — Kill Range ghost (1 hit), Melee ghost appears, switch to Protect from Melee
0:20 — Kill Melee ghost (1 hit), start attacking Cerberus
0:25 — Cerberus attacks with Magic, switch to Protect from Magic
0:30 — Cerberus attacks with Ranged, switch to Protect from Missiles
0:35 — Cerberus attacks with Melee, switch to Protect from Melee
0:40 — Cerberus at 200 HP, ghosts appear again
0:45 — Quickly kill Mage ghost, switch prayers as needed
0:55 — Cerberus at 100 HP, ghosts appear AGAIN
1:00 — Cerberus dies! Loot and reset

Average kill time: 45-60 seconds with good gear, 90-120 seconds with budget gear.

How Many KC for Full Log?

The Completionist Log for Cerberus requires getting all three boot upgrades. Here's the math:

  • Expected KC for 1 boot: 128 KC (1/128 drop rate)
  • Expected KC for 2 boots: ~256 KC (you might get the same boot twice)
  • Expected KC for 3 boots: ~384 KC (statistically)
  • Average KC for full log: 323 KC (based on community data)

  • Lucky players: 150-200 KC (get all three quickly)

Time to full log: At 70 kills/hour, 323 KC = ~4.6 hours of grinding. But with bad RNG, it could take 10+ hours.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I safespot Cerberus?

No. Cerberus cannot be safespotted. She always walks directly toward you, and there are no obstacles in her arena that you can hide behind. You must tank her attacks and prayer switch correctly. This is what makes Cerberus a true test of PvM skill.

How much GP per hour does Cerberus make?

Cerberus profit varies wildly based on RNG:

  • Unlucky (no boots): ~8,000,000 - 10,000,000 GP/hour
  • Average (no boots): ~10,000,000 - 13,000,000 GP/hour
  • Lucky (get a boot): ~25,000,000 - 35,000,000 GP/hour
  • Super Lucky (get 2 boots in 1 hour): ~50,000,000+ GP/hour

Comparison: Cerberus is among the best mid-game money makers in OSRS, rivaling Vorkath (after you have the boots).

What's the best combat style for Cerberus?

Melee (Crush) is generally considered the best style for Cerberus because:

  • Cerberus has lower defence against Crush attacks
  • Melee allows you to use Venom (Serpentine Helm) to negate most melee damage
  • Melee DPS is higher than Ranged with equivalent gear
  • You can use Dragon Warhammer special to lower her defence

However, Ranged (Twisted Bow) is also excellent if you have the budget. It allows you to stay at a distance and reduces the need for precise prayer switching.

How do I get to Cerberus quickly?

The fastest way to reach Cerberus is:

  1. Slayer Ring (recommended): Teleport directly to Cerberus's cave (unlocked after 75 Slayer and completing the quest "A Porcine of Interest").
  2. Fairy Ring: Use code DKS (relocated to north of Taverley), then run south.
  3. Home Teleport: Taverley, then run through the Taverley Dungeon (requires 70 Agility or a key).

Should I use a Cannon at Cerberus?

No. Cannons are not allowed in Cerberus's arena. Don't bring one. You'll just waste time setting it up and then have to pick it back up.

Can I duo Cerberus?

Yes! Cerberus can be duoed. In fact, duoing is often easier because you can split the ghost phases (one person handles Mage/Range ghosts, the other handles Melee). However, loot is rolled separately, so you don't get extra chances at boots by duoing.

What should I do if I keep dying at Cerberus?

If you're struggling with Cerberus, try these fixes:

  • Upgrade your gear: Get Bandos armor, Avernic Defender, and better potions.
  • Practice prayer switching: Use a practice account or watch YouTube tutorials.
  • Bring more food: Don't be greedy with inventory space. Bring 14-16 sharks instead of 10.
  • Use Venom: Serpentine Helm (uncharged) is cheap and negates most melee damage.
  • Learn the ghost order: Mage → Range → Melee. Memorize it!

Final Tips & Common Mistakes

Tip #1: Bring Teleport Tabs

Always bring 2-3 teleport tablets (House, Camelot, or Barrows). If you make a mistake and your HP drops below 10, teleport out immediately. Don't risk dying and losing your hard-earned loot.

Tip #2: Expect to Die a Few Times

Cerberus has a steep learning curve. Your first 10-20 kills will likely result in several deaths. This is normal! Don't get discouraged. Once you learn the prayer switching pattern, you'll rarely die.

Tip #3: Use the Serpentine Helm

The Serpentine Helm (even uncharged) applies venom to Cerberus, which deals 6 damage every 18 seconds. This adds up to significant DPS over time and reduces the healing you need to do.

Tip #4: Don't Get Discouraged by Bad RNG

Cerberus boot upgrades have a 1/128 drop rate. You might go 300-400 kills without a single boot. This is just RNG. Take breaks, don't let it frustrate you, and remember that every kill is profit (even without boots).

Tip #5: Consider Using a Ring of Wealth (i)

The Ring of Wealth (i) slightly improves drop rates for all drops (including boots). It's not a huge difference, but every little bit helps. Plus, it's relatively cheap (~2M GP).

Tip #6: Record Your KC

Use a notepad or spreadsheet to track your KC (kill count) and drops. This helps you know when you're due for a boot drop and makes the grind feel more manageable. Plus, you can brag to your friends when you finally get that Pegasian Boot at 600 KC!
