Overall Strategy & Philosophy

The fastest route to Max Cape is NOT training each skill from 1-99 in isolation. The efficient approach is:

  1. Quests First: Quests give massive lump sums of XP. Do them before grinding.
  2. Chain XP Sources: Use one activity to train multiple skills (e.g., Slayer trains Combat, Hitpoints, Slayer, and sometimes Prayer/Ranged/Magic).
  3. Leverage Expensive Methods: GP is easier to make than time is to spend. Buy Dragon bones for Prayer, spend on blaze powder for Construction.
  4. Quest-Locked Skills: Some skills (Construction, Farming, Herblore) have quest gates. Plan your quest order to unlock these early.
  5. Max Cape is 23×99: You need all 23 skills at 99. Plan for the long haul — burnout is the #1 killer of Max Cape attempts.
⚠️ Important: This guide assumes a Members (P2P) account with a Bond or paid membership. F2P Max Cape is not possible (only 15 skills available).

Prerequisites — What You Need Before Starting

Account Setup

  • Membership: Continuous P2P is ~$11/month. One Bond (~14 days) costs ~7M GP (fluctuates).
  • Bank Space: Upgrade to Members bank preset (more tabs). Loot everything — you'll need herbs, seeds, bars later.
  • Quest Requirements: Some quests require stats. Plan your early levels to unlock key quests.

Essential Unlocks (Do These First)

  • Fairy Rings (Fairytale I + II): Unlocks 40+ teleport locations.
  • Spirit Trees (Tree Gnome Village + Grand Tree): Fast travel network.
  • House Teleport (Construction 1+): Build a basic house for teleportation.
  • Duel Arena (not available anymore — use Clan Wars): Safe PvP practice and quick respawn.
  • Spellbook Swap (Lunar diplomacy): Unlocks useful teleports (Catherby, Barber, etc.).

Skill Priority Order — Which Skills to Train When

The optimal order trains skills that UNLOCK other content first, then chains skills together.

Recommended Priority Order

  1. Combat Stats (Attack/Strength/Defence): Unlocks better weapons and armor. Train to 60/60/60 first (Dragon weapons + Rune armor).
  2. Ranged & Magic: Get to 43+ Prayer and 70+ Ranged/Magic for safe bossing (Vorkath, Zulrah).
  3. Prayer: 43 (Protect from Missiles) is essential. 70+ (Piety) is optimal for mid-game.
  4. Hitpoints: Trains passively from combat. Don't grind it directly.
  5. Slayer: Use Slayer as your primary combat training from ~50 Combat onwards. It trains Combat + Slayer simultaneously.
  6. Agility: 60 (Wilderness Agility Course access) → 70 (Seers' Village → Fenkenstrain) → 80+ (Prifddinas if available). Agility shortcuts save massive time.
  7. Herblore: Train in bursts when you have GP. Unlock key potions (Super combat, Prayer potions, Ranging potion).
  8. Farming: Do tree runs daily from early game. Crop runs when you have GP for seeds.
  9. Construction: 50+ for Ornate pool (health/freeze respawn). 83+ for Ornate jewelry box. 90+ for Portal chamber.
  10. Cooking/Fishing/Firemaking: Do these early (they're fast and cheap). Cooking 99 is one of the easiest 99s.
  11. Other Buyables (Prayer, Construction, Herblore): Save GP and buy these 99s last (most expensive but fastest).

AFK vs Active Training Balance

Max Cape is a marathon. You cannot actively train 8 hours/day for months without burning out. Balance AFK and active methods.

AFK-Friendly Skills (Train While Doing Other Things)

  • Cooking: Afk at Catherby range with lobsters/swordfish. ~200k XP/hr.
  • Fishing: Afk at Minnows (82+ Fishing) or Trawler (levels 15-99). ~40k-80k XP/hr.
  • Woodcutting: Afk at Teaks (35+) or Redwoods (90+). ~40k-70k XP/hr.
  • Mining: Afk at Motherlode Mine (30+). ~20k-40k XP/hr.
  • Slayer (certain tasks): Some Slayer tasks (e.g., Suqahs, Trolls) are AFK-friendly.

Active-Focus Skills (Require Attention)

  • Agility: Click-intensive. Use Hallowed Sepulchre for best XP/hr (very active).
  • Prayer (Chaos altar): Very click-intensive but fast.
  • Construction (larders): Very click-intensive, consider Mahogany Homes for semi-AFK.
  • Herblore: Making potions is click-intensive (unless using Python scripts or mouse keys).

Balanced Daily Schedule (Example)

  • Morning (Active): 2 hours Agility or Slayer (high-focus content).
  • Afternoon (Semi-AFK): 2 hours Cooking/Fishing/Woodcutting while watching Netflix/YouTube.
  • Evening (Active): 2 hours bossing (Vorkath, Zulrah) or Slayer.
  • Total: ~6 hours/day sustainable for months.

Money-Making Interspersed Strategy

You'll need ~200-500M GP to buy your way through the expensive buyable skills (Prayer, Construction, Herblore). Here's when and how to make that GP.

Early Game Money (First 200 Hours)

  • Slayer (40-75): 300k-800k GP/hr depending on task. Also trains Combat + Slayer.
  • Wintertodt (Firemaking 50+): 300k-600k GP/hr. Also trains Firemaking.
  • Giants (Hill/Killer): 200k-400k GP/hr. Drops Limpwurt roots, big bones, herbs.

Mid Game Money (200-1000 Hours in)

  • Vorkath (Combat 90+, Ranged 80+): 1.5M-2.5M GP/hr. Best consistent money in the game.
  • Zulrah (Combat 90+, Magic/Ranged 75+): 1.5M-3M GP/hr. High difficulty, high reward.
  • Raids (CoX / ToB): 2M-5M GP/hr (team-dependent). Best money but requires skill.
  • Herb runs (Farming 70+): ~200k-500k GP per run (every 80 mins). Passive income.

Late Game Money (1000+ Hours in)

  • Raids (CoX / ToB High Level): 3M-10M GP/hr for top teams.
  • Flip Items on GE: 1M-10M+ GP/hr (merchant skills required).
  • High-level Slayer (Boss tasks): 1M-3M GP/hr. Duke, Vardorvis, etc.
⚠️ Warning: Don't stop training to farm GP unless you NEED it for a specific buyable skill. The most efficient path is to train Slayer continuously — it generates GP naturally while training.

Ironman Differences — What Changes

Ironman mode restricts trading with other players. This changes the strategy significantly.

Key Differences

  • No Buying Supplies: You must gather herbs, ores, logs, bones yourself. Farming is essential.
  • Slower Buyable Skills: Prayer, Construction, Herblore take 2-3x longer because you must gather resources.
  • Farming is Mandatory: You need herb runs for Herblore. Tree runs for Construction/Woodcutting. Do every 4 hours.
  • Bossing for Drops: You need to kill bosses for rare drops (Dragon defender, trident, etc.) instead of buying them.
  • Group Content Helps: Raids (CoX, ToB) in groups is easier for Irons to get gear.

Ironman-Specific Strategy Changes

  • Prioritize Farming early (do tree runs from level 15+).
  • Do Herb runs every 80 minutes (Toadflax → Ranarr → Snapdragon → Torstol).
  • Kill Barrows for gear (tank gear, weapons).
  • Complete Recipe for Disaster ASAP for God clocks and access to Culinaromancer's chest.
  • Use Giant's Foundry (Smithing 60+) for Smithing training + mMolten glass for Crafting.

Daily & Weekly Routines for Maximum Efficiency

Daily Routines (Do Every Day)

  • Farming Runs (Every 4 hours): Trees, fruit trees, herb patches. Set phone alarms.
  • Birdhouse Runs (Hunter 5+): Every 50 minutes. Gives Hunter XP + seeds + herbs.
  • Daily Tasks (if any event is active): Check for daily challenges (bonus XP).
  • God Wars + Barrows (if geared): 1-2 hours of bossing for drops.

Weekly Routines (Do Every Week)

  • Completing Achievement Diaries: Unlocks useful items (explorer's ring, ardougne cloak, etc.).
  • Group Raiding (CoX/ToB): Find a consistent team. Raids are best GP and best loot.
  • Farming Contracts (if available): Hunter + Farming contracts for bonus XP.
  • Reviewing Goals: Check your skill progress. Adjust training methods if a method is outdated.
