First 5M GP for New OSRS Members 2026

1. The Quick Answer — Your GP Roadmap

If you follow this guide for 2 weeks, you will hit 5M GP. Here's the breakdown:

The 5M GP Blueprint (2-Week Plan)

  • Bird House Runs (4x/day) — ~400K GP/day = 5.6M over 2 weeks
  • Herb Farming (2x/day) — ~600K/day = 8.4M over 2 weeks
  • Blast Furnace (1-2 hrs/day) — ~400K/hr = 5.6M-11.2M over 2 weeks
  • Early Slayer (1-2 hrs/day) — ~200K/hr = 2.8M-5.6M over 2 weeks

Even doing just bird houses + herb farming alone gets you to 5M in under a week. The other methods accelerate it further if you have playtime.

Key insight: Membership money-making is fundamentally different from F2P. You don't grind one method for hours — you stack multiple passive + semi-active methods and let them compound.

2. Why 5M GP Matters

5 million GP isn't a random number. It's the threshold where your account transforms from "surviving" to "progressing":

Gear Upgrades

5M buys you a full mid-game melee setup: Dragon Scimitar (100K), Fighter Torso (free but time), Barrows Gloves (free via quest), Berserker Ring (1.5M), and Obsidian Platelegs (350K). This gear carries you to 80+ combat.

Prayer Training

43 Prayer costs ~500K at the Chaos Altar. 70 Prayer (Piety) costs ~3M. These are massive power spikes that make every boss and quest easier.

Skill Buyables

Construction (player-owned house), Herblore (potions for bosses), and Crafting (jewelry, Zenyte) all require GP investment. 5M funds your early Construction and key Herblore levels.

Bond Freedom

Once you can earn 5-6M GP consistently per 2 weeks, you can pay for membership with in-game Bonds — making OSRS genuinely free-to-play at the members level.

3. Method 1: Bird House Runs — Passive 400K+/Day

This is the single most important money-making method for new members. It requires almost no stats, takes 5 minutes per run, and generates consistent passive income every single day.

GP Per Run

~100K per run at 5 Hunter. Up to ~150K per run at 50+ Hunter. With 4 runs/day: 400K-600K GP/day.

Requirements

5 Hunter (start), Bone Voyage quest (for Fossil Island access). No other skills needed. Steel bar for clockwork if making your own.

Time Per Run

3-5 minutes. You set 4 bird houses, leave, and come back 50 minutes later. Zero active time while they fill up.

Why It's #1

It's pure passive income that also trains Hunter. You do 4 runs a day while doing other activities. Over a month, that's 12M-18M GP from bird houses alone — enough for a Bond.

Step-by-Step Bird House Run

  1. Get to Fossil Island — Complete Bone Voyage quest (no requirements). Use the barge north of Digsite.
  2. Bring 4 clockwork + 4 hop seeds (barley, hammerstone, asgarnian, jute — any hop seeds work). Buy clockwork from the GE for ~200 GP each.
  3. Run the 4 bird house spots on Fossil Island: Mushroom Meadow (2), Verdant Valley (1), and Swamp (1). Set each one.
  4. Wait 50 minutes — Go do other activities. Quest, train combat, do farm runs.
  5. Return and harvest — Dismantle each bird house. You get bird nests (valuable: ring seeds, tree seeds), raw bird meat, and feathers.
  6. Repeat — Set 4 new bird houses immediately after harvesting. That's it.

Pro move: Always keep hop seeds and clockwork in your inventory. Never leave the island without resetting your bird houses.

Pro Tip — Bird House Upgrades

As your Hunter level increases, upgrade your bird houses: Regular (5) → Oak (14) → Willow (24) → Teak (34) → Maple (44) → Mahogany (54) → Redwood (74). Higher-tier houses = more valuable nests. Always use the best house you can.

4. Method 2: Herb Farming — 500K-1M Per Farm Run

Herb farming is the second pillar of new-member income. Like bird houses, it's semi-passive: you plant, leave, and harvest later. Unlike bird houses, the profit scales dramatically with your Farming level.

GP Per Run

~150K at 9 Farming (Guam). ~300K at 26 Farming (Harralander). ~500K-1M at 38+ Farming (Toadflax, Snapdragon, Ranarr). 2 runs/day = 300K-2M/day.

Requirements

9 Farming (minimum). Farming skill, seed dibber, rake, spade, and herb seeds. Completion of relevant farming quests helps.

Growth Time

80 minutes per growth cycle. You can do 2 full runs per hour if you're dedicated, or 2-3 runs per day casually.

Profit per Seed

Ranarr seed: ~60K profit/herb. Toadflax: ~40K. Snapdragon: ~80K. Always check GE prices before planting — herb prices fluctuate weekly.

Step-by-Step Herb Farm Run

  1. Get herb patches unlocked — Start with the 4 standard patches: Ardougne, Catherby, Falador, and Port Phasmatys. Each needs raking + compost before planting.
  2. Buy seeds from the GE — Start with Guam seeds (9 Farming, ~100 GP each). Graduate to Ranarr (32 Farming, ~30K seed) or Toadflax (38 Farming, ~5K seed) ASAP.
  3. Use ultracompost always — It costs ~500 GP/dose but dramatically increases your yield. From 4-6 herbs to 6-9 herbs per patch. Always worth it.
  4. Plant, wait 80 minutes, harvest — Teleport between patches using Ardougne Cloak, Catherby tele, Falador tele, and Ectophial.
  5. Sell herbs on the GE — Clean herbs sell for more than grimy in most cases. Check both prices.
Pro Tip — Farming Profit Calculator

The most profitable herb changes weekly based on GE prices. Before each run, check the OSRS Wiki Farming profit calculator to see which herb has the best GP/seed ratio. Toadflax is usually the sweet spot for new members — cheap seeds, decent herb value, low risk.

Extra Farming Patches (More Patches = More Profit)

  • Trollheim patch — Unlocked after My Arm's Big Adventure quest. Never gets diseased. Free profit.
  • Hosidius patch — Unlocked with 50% Hosidius favor. +5% yield bonus.
  • Farming Guild patch — Requires 65 Farming and 60% Hosidius favor. Another free patch.

Each additional patch is pure profit. Unlock them as soon as you can — they pay for themselves within days.

5. Method 3: Blast Furnace — 300K-600K GP/Hr

Blast Furnace is the best active money-maker available to new members with moderate stats. You smelt bars at double speed for half the coal cost — and the margin between ore + coal and bar prices is consistently profitable.

GP Per Hour

Steel bars: 300-400K/hr (30 Smithing). Gold bars: 400-600K/hr (40 Smithing + Goldsmith gauntlets). Mithril bars: 400-550K/hr (50 Smithing).

Requirements

30 Smithing for steel bars. 40 Smithing for gold bars. Start The Giant Dwarf quest for Keldagrim access (free boat from Rellekka after starting).

Input Cost

Steel bars: Iron ore (120 GP) + Coal (160 GP) → Steel bar (560 GP) = ~280 GP profit/bar. Gold bars: Gold ore (170 GP) → Gold bar (230 GP) + Smithing XP = profit with Goldsmith Gauntlets.

Why It's Great

It simultaneously makes money AND trains Smithing. Smithing is one of the most useful member skills — you need it for Dragon Slayer 2, quest requirements, and money-making later.

Step-by-Step Blast Furnace

  1. Get to Keldagrim — Take the minecart from the Grand Exchange (after starting The Giant Dwarf) or boat from Rellekka.
  2. Pay the foreman — 1,250 GP to use the furnace (free if under 60 Smithing). Always pay — the time saved is worth 10x the cost.
  3. Bank at the nearby NPC — Use the bank chest next to the conveyor belt. Deposit coins for the foreman.
  4. Load ores on the conveyor belt — Iron ore + Coal for steel. The furnace automatically smelts them. You need 1 iron + 2 coal per steel bar (half the normal coal requirement).
  5. Collect bars from the bar dispenser — They appear after a few seconds. Grab them and bank.
  6. Repeat — A full inventory takes ~90 seconds. You can do 30-35 inventories per hour.
Pro Tip — Gold Bars for XP, Steel for Profit

Gold bars with Goldsmith Gauntlets give insane Smithing XP (56.2 XP/bar) but lower GP/hr. Steel bars give better profit but slower XP. Do gold bars until 50 Smithing, then switch to mithril for the best balance of XP and GP.

6. Method 4: Early Slayer — 100K-300K GP/Hr + Combat XP

Slayer isn't the fastest GP/hr at low levels, but it's the only method that makes money while training all three combat stats simultaneously. It's also the gateway to high-level money-makers like Kraken, Cerberus, and Hydra.

GP Per Hour

Early (1-40 Slayer): 50-150K/hr. Mid (40-70): 150-300K/hr. Alch drops + emblems add 50-100K/hr on top.

Requirements

Slayer skill (unlocked with membership). No minimum combat level. Talk to Turael in Burthorpe for easy tasks.

Hidden Value

Slayer gives Slayer Points (unlock Rings of Wealth, Bigger Badder, and Superior spawns). It also trains combat for free while earning GP — something no other method does.

Best Early Tasks

Crawling Hands, Cave Bugs, Banshees, Cockatrices, Pyrefiends. These tasks are fast, safe, and drop alchable items worth 50-200K per task.

Slayer Money-Making Tips

  • Always use the Slayer Ring — Unlocked at 75 Crafting (or buy from GE). Teleports to task locations save enormous time.
  • Alch drops while fighting — Keep nature runes and a fire staff. Alch any drop worth 500+ GP. This adds 50-100K/hr to any task.
  • Do every 10th task with Konar — She gives bonus points and brimstone keys (extra loot). Use Turael for the 9 tasks in between for fast completions.
  • Unlock "Bigger and Badder" — 150 Slayer Points. Superior monsters drop valuable loot and give bonus XP. Pays for itself within days.
  • Block bad tasks early — Use your Slayer Points to block tasks you hate. More time on good tasks = more GP/hr overall.

7. Method 5: Fossil Island Nepotrophe — 200K-400K GP/Hr

If you've completed Bone Voyage (which you should have for bird houses), the Fossil Island underwater area offers one of the best low-requirement money-makers in the game.

What You Kill

Ammonite Crabs — Low defense, 100 HP, aggressive, and they respawn instantly. Zero effort combat with consistent alchable drops.

GP Per Hour

200-400K/hr depending on your combat level and alching efficiency. Higher combat = faster kills = more drops.

Requirements

Bone Voyage quest. Any combat level. Bring food if under 50 combat. No special gear needed.

Why It's Great

Ammonite Crabs are the #1 AFK combat training method for new members. You earn GP while getting 40K-80K combat XP/hr. Double value: money + levels at the same time.

Pro Tip — Crab Spot Etiquette

Ammonite Crab spots are shared. If someone is already at a spot, hop worlds. There are 3 crab spawns per spot — standing in the middle aggros all 3. Use a Dwarf Cannon for maximum XP (costs ~400K/hr in cannonballs but dramatically speeds up kills).

8. Method 6: Plank Making — 200K-350K GP/Hr

This is a simple buy-low-sell-high method that requires zero combat and zero stats. You buy logs, turn them into planks, and sell the planks for profit. The Sawmill operator does the work — you just run back and forth.

GP Per Hour

Oak planks: 200-300K/hr. Teak planks: 250-350K/hr. Mahogany planks: 200-300K/hr. Profits fluctuate with plank prices.

Requirements

None. Just GP to buy logs and pay the Sawmill. The Sawmill is north-east of Varrock.

How It Works

Buy oak logs (50 GP) → Pay Sawmill 250 GP/plank → Oak plank sells for ~450 GP. Profit: ~150 GP per plank. 1,200 planks/hr = 180K/hr minimum.

When to Do This

When you need guaranteed, combat-free income. Best for players who are waiting for farm runs to grow or bird houses to fill. Good "filler" method.

Plank-Making Route (Fastest)

  1. Start at Varrock GE — Withdraw 27 oak logs + GP for planks (27 x 250 = 6,750 GP).
  2. Run to Sawmill — North-east of Varrock. ~30 seconds from GE.
  3. Convert all logs to planks — Talk to the Sawmill operator, choose "Buy planks," select oak.
  4. Run back to GE — Sell planks. Repeat.
  5. With Varrock Diary 2+ — Use the Varrock teleport to the GE for faster banking. Cuts trip time in half.

9. Method 7: Wintertodt Supplies — 150K-300K GP/Hr

You might have done Wintertodt in F2P, but as a member, it's dramatically more profitable. Members get access to better food, warm clothing, and higher-level rewards from supply crates.

GP Per Hour

150-300K/hr from supply crates. The real value is in seed drops (Snapdragon, Ranarr, Torstol seeds worth 10K-50K each) and the Pyromancer outfit pieces.

Requirements

50 Firemaking (recommended). No combat. Bring warm clothing (Pyromancer, Scarf, etc.) and food (Saradomin brews for members).

Member Advantage

Members can use better food (brews, anglers), wear warm clothing that reduces damage, and get higher-level supply crates with better loot tables.

Why It's Still Worth It

Wintertodt gives Firemaking XP + Construction supplies + seeds + GP all at once. It's not the best GP/hr, but the multi-skill value makes it time-efficient.

10. Your Daily GP Routine — The 5M Blueprint

Here's the exact daily routine that will get you to 5M GP in under 2 weeks. No guessing, no wasted time — just follow this loop:

Morning Session (30 minutes)

  1. Harvest bird houses (3 min) — Collect nests, reset all 4 houses
  2. Do a herb farm run (5 min) — Harvest, replant, apply compost
  3. Check GE offers (2 min) — Sell herbs, nests, and bird house loot
  4. Start another activity — Blast Furnace, Slayer, or Ammonite Crabs for 20 min

Earned: ~200K from bird houses + ~300K from herbs = 500K in 10 min active time

Midday Session (1-2 hours)

  1. Harvest bird houses again (3 min) — It's been 50+ minutes
  2. Blast Furnace or Slayer (1-2 hrs) — Active money-making of your choice
  3. Second herb farm run (5 min) — If herbs have grown

Earned: ~200K bird houses + ~400K-800K from active method = 600K-1M

Evening Session (30 minutes)

  1. Final bird house run (3 min) — Reset before bed
  2. Final herb farm run (5 min) — Plant before sleeping
  3. Bank everything — Sell on GE, check profits

Earned: ~200K bird houses + ~300K herbs = 500K

Daily Total: 1.6M - 2M GP/Day

At 1.6M/day, you hit 5M GP in 3.1 days. At 2M/day, you hit it in 2.5 days. Even doing just bird houses + herbs (no active methods), you still reach 5M in about a week.

The secret is consistency. Missing bird house runs is like throwing away 100K. Missing farm runs is like throwing away 300K. Set a timer, do your runs, and the GP flows in automatically.

11. GP Milestones — What to Buy at Each Stage

Don't just hoard GP — spend it on upgrades that accelerate your progress. Here's what to buy at each milestone:

500K GP — The Essentials

  • 43 Prayer (Chaos Altar, ~500K) — Protect from Melee changes everything
  • Glory Amulet (4) (50K) — Best general-purpose teleport jewelry
  • Combat bracelet (4) (30K) — Edgeville/Fishing Guild teleports
  • Stamina potions (50K for a stock) — Save time running everywhere

1M GP — The Upgrades

  • Dragon Scimitar (100K) — Best 60 Attack weapon (after Monkey Madness)
  • Obsidian platelegs/skirt (350K) — Best mid-game leg slot
  • Herb seeds for farming (50-100K restock) — Keep the passive income flowing
  • Player-Owned House (100K for basic setup) — Ornate jewelry box, portal nexus

2M GP — The Power Spike

  • Berserker Ring (1.5M) — +4 Strength bonus. The biggest melee DPS upgrade at this level
  • Fighter Torso (free, but invest time) — +4 Strength from Barbarian Assault
  • Dragon Boots (300K, after 60 Slayer) — +2 Strength, best melee boots

5M GP — The Full Setup

  • Complete mid-game melee setup — You now have everything you need for Slayer, Barrows, and early bossing
  • Start saving for a Bond (5-6M) — Membership pays for itself from here
  • Upgrade to Iban's Staff (free after Underground Pass) — Best budget magic weapon for Barrows
  • Consider Barrows runs — With 43 Prayer + Iban's, you can do Barrows for 1-2M GP/hr
Pro Tip — The Compound Effect

Every GP you spend on upgrades should increase your GP/hr. Berserker Ring → faster kills → more Slayer drops. 43 Prayer → no food costs → pure profit. Herb seeds → more farm income. Think of spending as investing, not consuming.

12. FAQ

What if I don't have time for 4 bird house runs per day?

Do as many as you can. Even 2 runs per day (morning + evening) still generates 200K/day. The beauty of bird houses is that they're flexible — you can do them during bathroom breaks, between activities, or while watching YouTube. No other method is this time-efficient.

Should I do Blast Furnace or Slayer for active money-making?

Blast Furnace if you want pure GP/hr. Slayer if you want combat XP + GP + long-term progression. The answer is probably "both" — alternate between them. Do Slayer until you get a slow/bad task, then swap to Blast Furnace until your next task.

What about flipping on the GE?

Flipping is profitable but requires capital (1M+ GP) and market knowledge. It's not ideal for your first 5M because you need that GP for gear upgrades. After 5M, you can set aside 1-2M for flipping. See our Cheap Flipping Methods for New Players guide.

Are these methods Ironman-friendly?

Bird houses: Yes, fully Ironman-friendly (gather your own seeds). Herb farming: Yes, but you must get seeds from drops/bird nests. Blast Furnace: Yes, you mine your own ores. Slayer: Absolutely. Plank making: No (requires buying logs from GE). Ironmen should focus on bird houses + herb farming + Slayer as their core trio.

How do I track my GP/hr?

Use the RS Pocket Book or GE Tracker apps/websites. They log your loot, calculate GP/hr per method, and help you find the most profitable activities. Also check the OSRS Wiki's money-making guide for updated rates.

When can I start doing Barrows for money?

With 43 Prayer + Iban's Staff + Mystic Robes + 50+ Magic, you can do Barrows. That's achievable within your first 1-2 weeks of membership. Barrows generates 1-2M GP/hr on average and is the natural next step after your first 5M. We'll cover it in the next article in this series.

Should I sell everything on the GE or alch it?

Rule of thumb: If the GE price is higher than the high alch value, sell on GE. If the GE price is lower, alch it. For items worth <500 GP, drop them or alch — they're not worth a GE slot. Nature runes cost ~100 GP each, so only alch items worth 768+ GP (the break-even point).
