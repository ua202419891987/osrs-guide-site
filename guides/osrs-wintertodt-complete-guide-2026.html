OSRS Wintertodt Complete Guide 2026 — Fast 99 Firemaking, Solo & Mass Strategy

1. How Wintertodt Works

Wintertodt is a skilling boss in the snowy north of Zeah. You enter the boss chamber, and for ~5 minutes (mass) or up to 15 minutes (solo), you fend off the Wintertodt's cold by feeding the braziers with kindling. The core loop:

  1. Chop Bruma Roots from the trees inside the boss room
  2. Use the roots on the brazier to create kindling (Fletching XP)
  3. Feed the kindling to the brazier (Firemaking XP)
  4. Occasionally fix broken braziers (Construction XP) or light unlit braziers (Firemaking XP)
  5. Eat food to survive the cold — Wintertodt damages everyone periodically
  6. When Wintertodt's energy reaches 0%, you win and earn supply crates

5. Mass World Strategy — Fastest XP

Mass worlds (309 and 311 are the dedicated WT worlds) have 40+ players per game. Games last ~4-5 minutes.

  1. Teleport in with a Games Necklace → Wintertodt Camp. Light the brazier at the entrance door (use Tinderbox on unlit brazier).
  2. Chop Bruma Roots from the 4 trees. The roots are stackable — chop until your inventory is ~60% full.
  3. Fletch the roots into kindling. Each root → 1 kindling. Do this next to the brazier — you can fletch while standing near the brazier to avoid cold damage.
  4. Feed the brazier — click the brazier with kindling. This gives the most Firemaking XP. Keep an eye on the brazier's health bar.
  5. When brazier breaks: Fix it with Hammer (Construction XP). When brazier goes out: Relight with Tinderbox (Firemaking XP).
  6. Keep 500+ points minimum per game to qualify for supply crates. At 90+ FM, aim for 750-1000 points per game for 3+ crates.
🔥 Point Formula: Each kindling fed to the brazier = 10 points. Each fletched root = ~25 FM XP at level 90. Each fed kindling = ~90 FM XP. At 90+ FM on mass worlds, shoot for 80-120 fed kindling per game for 750-1000 points (= 3-4 supply crates).

6. Solo Strategy — Maximum Profit & Construction XP

Solo Wintertodt is slower Firemaking XP but gives significantly more Construction XP and better crate loot because you control the pace and get more points per game.

  1. Enter and light all 4 braziers. Wintertodt's energy starts at 100%.
  2. Phase 1 (100%-60% energy): Chop and fletch. Don't feed the brazier yet — let Wintertodt break braziers. Fix them for Construction XP. Repeat until you have a full inventory of kindling.
  3. Phase 2 (60%-5% energy): Keep one brazier lit. Feed kindling steadily. Continue fixing broken braziers. The goal is to stretch this phase for maximum points and Construction XP.
  4. Phase 3 (0% energy): Wintertodt dies. Collect crates based on your point total. Solo games last 12-15 minutes and reward 2,500-3,500 points per game (= 8-12 crates).

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Should I fletch the roots or feed them raw?

A: Always fletch. Fletched kindling gives significantly more points (10 vs 5 per root) and more Firemaking XP when fed. The only exception is if the game is about to end and you need to dump points quickly — then feed raw.

Q: Do I lose items if I die at Wintertodt?

A: No. Death at Wintertodt is safe. You respawn outside the boss room with all your items. You only lose the supplies used during that game.

Q: What's the best world for Wintertodt?

A: World 309 (official Wintertodt world). Always packed with 30-50 players. World 311 is the backup if 309 is full. Both are official themed worlds for Wintertodt.

Q: When should I open my supply crates?

A: Immediately — they scale to your CURRENT levels. Crates roll loot based on your Fishing, Herblore, Mining, Crafting, etc. levels AT THE TIME OF OPENING. If you plan to train those skills, opening crates at higher levels yields better resources. But if you need the GP now, just open them.
