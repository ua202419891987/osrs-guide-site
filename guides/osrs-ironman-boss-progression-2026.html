OSRS Ironman Boss Progression 2026 — The Unlock Order That Actually Works

Intermediate

An Ironman cannot buy their way past a gate. Every boss unlock is earned by a quest, a Slayer level, or a grind. This 2026 roadmap lays out the correct boss order, the exact gating behind each one, tiered gear, and verified supply-value GP/hr so you always know what to chase next — and what you are permanently blocked from until you fix the gate.

1. 🗺️ Why Progression Order Matters for Ironmen

On a main account, boss order is a suggestion — you buy gear and skip ahead. On an Ironman, boss order is a chain of dependencies. You cannot camp Vorkath until you finish Dragon Slayer II; you cannot step into the Theatre of Blood until Sins of the Father is done; you cannot reliably farm the Alchemical Hydra until you hit 95 Slayer. Skipping the right prerequisite does not save time — it wastes it.

This guide gives you the proven 2026 order: start with the zero-gate bosses that build your bank and stats, push Slayer for the gated Slayer bosses, then clear the two pivotal quests that open the best money and the raids. Every fact below was verified against the OSRS Wiki in July 2026.

🎯 The Three Pillars of Ironman Gating

  • Slayer level gates: the single biggest source of "why can't I damage this boss" tickets. 87 / 91 / 93 / 95 are the four numbers that matter.
  • Quest gates: Dragon Slayer II, Sins of the Father, and Beneath Cursed Sands each unlock a whole tier of content.
  • Stat gates: rarely a hard block, but 70+ combat stats are the soft floor for every endgame boss.
💡 Rule of Thumb

If a boss feels impossible, check the gate before the gear. Nine times out of ten an Ironman stuck on a boss is missing a quest or 4 Slayer levels, not a weapon. The Ironman Early-Game Guide covers how to reach these gates fast.

💡 CoX Has No Quest Gate

Unlike ToB and ToA, the Chambers of Xeric requires no quest — only access to Great Kourend (free) and a team. That is why most Ironmen learn raiding at CoX first. See the Ironman Raid Path for the CoX → ToB → ToA route.

7. ⚠️ Common Mistakes (Myth-Busting)

❌ Myth 1: "I can just buy Vorkath gear and go."

Wrong. Vorkath is locked behind Dragon Slayer II (200 QP + several stats). No gear in the world opens the boat to Ungael for an Ironman.

❌ Myth 2: "CoX needs a quest like the other raids."

Wrong. Chambers of Xeric has no quest requirement — only Great Kourend access. ToB and ToA do require Sins of the Father and Beneath Cursed Sands respectively. Mixing these up wastes weeks.

❌ Myth 3: "Higher combat stats let me skip Slayer gates."

Wrong. Kraken (87), Cerberus (91), TSD (93) and Hydra (95) Slayer levels cannot be boosted. You must actually train Slayer — there is no shortcut.

❌ Myth 4: "Zulrah needs a quest."

Mostly wrong. Zulrah itself has no quest wall; you only need a way to reach Zul-Andra (76 Magic teleport or a paid boat). It is one of the few top money bosses an Ironman can reach with zero quests.

❌ Myth 5: "GP/hr on the Wiki applies to my Ironman."

The Wiki's GP/hr assumes GE selling. As an Ironman your "GP" is supply value — the same drops, but you keep and use them. Treat the numbers as a relative ranking, not liquid cash.

8. ❓ FAQ

What is the very first boss an Ironman should do?

The Giant Mole or Barrows — both have zero gates and drop useful early gear (Barrows sets, Dragon items, seeds). They build the bank and stats you need for everything after.

Which quest should I rush first?

Dragon Slayer II is the highest-leverage quest: it opens Vorkath (the best consistent money boss) and is also required for the Alchemical Hydra. After that, Sins of the Father for ToB.

Can an Ironman raid solo?

CoX and ToA are soloable; ToB is designed for groups of up to 5. Ironmen are eligible for raid rewards when raiding alongside other players (group ironmen lose prestige if they raid with outsiders).

Why am I not getting Hydra kills?

You need 95 Slayer (unboostable) and Dragon Slayer II completed to reach Lithkren. If either is missing, you cannot damage or reach the Alchemical Hydra.

What GP/hr can I expect as a mid-game Ironman?

Once Zulrah + Vorkath are open, ~3–5M supply-value per hour is realistic. Before those gates, Kraken/Cerberus sit around 1–2.5M. See the verified table above.

9. 🏁 Final Tips

  • Train Slayer in parallel: the 87→95 ladder is your real progression bar. Block what you cannot yet damage.
  • Do Dragon Slayer II early: it is a long quest but unlocks two of the three best money bosses.
  • CoX first, then ToB/ToA: no quest gate on CoX makes it the natural raiding teacher.
  • Track gates, not gear: if stuck, re-read Section 2 before buying anything.
