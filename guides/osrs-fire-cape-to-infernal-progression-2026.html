From Jad to Zuk — The Complete PvM Milestone Path Every Mid-Level Player Must Walk

📅 June 11, 2026📖 10 min read⚔️ Mid to High Level PvM

Every OSRS account has two capes that define it: the Fire Cape and the Infernal Cape. One says "I'm not a noob anymore." The other says "I've mastered this game." But between those two capes lies a gauntlet of bosses that most players never properly prepare for. They jump from Jad to Vorkath without learning the fundamentals, wipe 20 times, and quit. This guide lays out the exact PvM progression path — every boss, every skill check, every gear upgrade — from your first Fire Cape to your first Infernal Cape.

1. Stage 1: Getting Your Fire Cape (Jad)

The Fight Caves is your PvM graduation. Before you attempt it, make sure you have:

  • 75+ Range — Blowpipe makes this trivial. At 75, it's a 60-90 minute run.
  • 43+ Prayer — Protect from Missiles, Protect from Magic, and Eagle Eye.
  • 70+ Defense — Blessed d'hide or Karil's. You'll tank ranger hits on later waves.
  • 60+ HP — Jad maxes at 97 with melee. You need to survive one mistake.

Key Skill Learned: Prayer Flicking Under Pressure

Jad teaches you the core OSRS PvM skill: switching prayers based on animation, not sound. His ranged attack (stomp) and magic attack (rear up) are telegraphed. Practice on YouTube Jad simulators before attempting — it costs nothing and saves hours.

2. Stage 2: Barrows — Prayer Switching 101

After Fire Cape, Barrows is your first taste of multi-style PvM. You fight 6 brothers, each with different combat styles, then a tunnel. Skills learned:

  • Prayer management across multiple fights — You have limited prayer for 6+ kills.
  • Gear switching between styles — Melee for melee brothers, ranged/magic for the others.
  • Supply management — How many prayer pots per run? When to bank?

Requirements: 43 Prayer, 60+ Magic (Iban's Blast), 50+ Range. Complete In Search of the Myreque and In Aid of the Myreque for Burgh de Rott teleport access.

Barrows Profit: 600K-1M/hr

With Morytania Hard Diary (70 Agility + 71 Prayer req), Barrows becomes ~1M/hr from doubled rune rewards. Each brother killed gives 2x runes instead of 1x. Complete this diary before farming Barrows seriously.

3. Stage 3: Zulrah — Movement Under Pressure

Zulrah is the first boss that demands simultaneous prayer switching AND movement. You can't just stand still and flick — you must reposition between phases while tracking Zulrah's color (green = ranged, cyan = magic, orange = melee).

4. Stage 4: Vorkath — Precision & Consistency

Vorkath teaches you movement precision. The acid phase and fireball spawn demand exact clicks under pressure. There's minimal prayer flicking — Vorkath uses one style per fight — but the movement requirements are tighter than anything before it.

Vorkath Mechanics You Must Master

  • Acid Phase: Walk in a straight line continuously. Stop = dead. Practice on an empty world first.
  • Zombified Spawn: Cast Crumble Undead immediately. If you miss, it explodes for 60 damage. Use the RuneLite spawn timer.
  • Fireball Barrage: Rapid-fire fireballs you must dodge. Keep moving.
  • Prayer Disable + Dragonfire: Vorkath disables prayer then hits with dragonfire. Super antifire + shield is mandatory.

Vorkath Profit: 2M-3M/hr

With Dragon Hunter Crossbow or Dragon Hunter Lance, Vorkath is consistent 2-3M/hr. Without them, budget setup still nets 1.5M/hr. Complete Dragon Slayer II first. Vorkath is where you farm GP for all your endgame gear.

5. Stage 5: Corrupted Gauntlet — No Gear, Pure Skill

The Corrupted Gauntlet is the great equalizer. No gear from outside matters. You enter with nothing, craft everything in 7.5 minutes, and fight Hunllef. This is where you prove you're a PvMer, not a gear-carried player.

Why CG Matters For Your Inferno Progression

  • Teaches fast decision-making — 7.5 minute prep window. You must gather, craft, and cook efficiently.
  • Multi-tasking under pressure — Hunllef has 4 prayer-disabling tornados + floor tiles to dodge simultaneously.
  • Resource management — Limited food, limited time. Every mistake costs you the run.
  • No gear excuse — If you die at CG, it's 100% your skill. This mental shift is critical for Inferno.

Requirements: Song of the Elves quest (70 in 8 skills). Recommended: 80+ Range, 80+ Magic, 80+ Defense, 70+ Prayer with Rigour/Augury.

⚡ CG BENCHMARK

If you can consistently complete Corrupted Gauntlet with 80%+ success rate, you are mechanically ready for Inferno. If you're below 50%, go back and practice until CG feels "easy." Inferno is CG multiplied by 10.

6. Stage 6: Inferno Prep — The Final Ascent

The Inferno is a 2-3 hour gauntlet of 69 waves ending with TzKal-Zuk. It demands everything you've learned:

Minimum Inferno Gear (~130M)

  • Bow of Faerdhinen (130M) — Non-negotiable. Ranged is your primary DPS for most waves.
  • Crystal Armor Set — Required with BowFa for the set effect. Body + Legs minimum.
  • Ancient Staff / Kodai Wand — For blood barrage healing on nibblers and blobs.
  • Occult Necklace + Tormented Bracelet — Magic damage for blood barrage healing.
  • Suffering (ri) — Best defensive ring. Recoil + prayer + defense.

FAQ

Q: How many attempts does Inferno take on average?

Most first capers take 15-30 attempts over 2-4 weeks. The record is 1 (first try), but expect a grind. Each attempt is 2-3 hours. Use the Inferno plugin on RuneLite to mark tile safespots and track wave spawns.

Q: Is Rigour required for Inferno?

Not strictly required, but strongly recommended. Rigour costs ~15M and gives +26% ranged accuracy and +25% ranged strength. It's the single most impactful ranged upgrade in the game. Buy it before BowFa if you must choose.

Q: Can I skip Zulrah/Vorkath and go straight to CG?

You can, but I don't recommend it. Zulrah teaches gear switching + movement. Vorkath teaches movement precision. Skipping them means you'll learn those lessons the hard way at CG, where one mistake ends the run. Walk before you run.

Q: What if I just want the Fire Cape and stop?

That's fine. Fire Cape is the most important single item for melee DPS. But every boss after Jad teaches you skills that make the entire game more fun. Even if you never attempt Inferno, mastering Zulrah, Vorkath, and CG makes you a dramatically better player in every activity.

Where Are You on the Path?

Got your Fire Cape? Next stop: Barrows → Zulrah → Vorkath → CG → Inferno. Each one teaches you a skill you need for the next. Walk the path in order.
