① Requirements & Getting to the God Wars Dungeon

Before you can fight any of the four generals, you need to meet the requirements to enter the God Wars Dungeon. The dungeon entrance is located in Trollheim (the snowy mountain area east of Burthorpe). This is a safe area (no PvP — it is NOT in the Wilderness). Here's everything you need:

1.1 📜 Minimum Requirements

RequirementDetailsWhy Needed
70 AgilityRequired to cross the narrow ice ledgeWithout this, you cannot enter the dungeon at all
70 StrengthRequired to swing across the rope shortcutOptional but highly recommended — saves 2-3 minutes per trip
Combat LevelRecommended 100+ (solo), 80+ (group)Bosses hit hard; lower levels can go in teams
Prayer Level43+ (70+ recommended for Piety/Rigour/Augury)Protect prayers and offensive prayers are essential

1.2 🗺️ How to Get to GWD

There are several ways to reach the God Wars Dungeon entrance. Here are the most common methods:

① Route via Trollheim (Recommended)

Complete Troll Stronghold quest. Use the Trollheim teleport (standard spellbook) to arrive at the mountain. Run west and down the path to the GWD entrance. This is the most common route. Bring climbing boots for the rocky path.

② Route via GWD Teleport (POH)

With 90+ Construction, build a GWD Portal in your Player-Owned House. This teleports you directly to the dungeon entrance. Requires the Ancient spellbook for the portal focus. Alternatively, use a GWD Tab from the Grand Exchange (~8K GP each).

③ Route via Burgh de Rott (F2P Alternative)

Use the Wizard's Tower teleport → run north to Draynor → charter ship to Port Sarim → walk to Trollheim → climb to GWD. This is very slow and not recommended for regular trips.

1.3 🔑 Kill Count (KC) — How to Enter Each Boss Room

To enter each boss's chamber, you need to kill 40 of that god's followers inside the dungeon. The KC is tracked per session (resets when you leave the dungeon). Here's which monsters to kill for each boss:

💡 Pro Tip: Save KC with Altar Prayers

Before entering a boss room, restore your prayer points at the altar in the dungeon's central area. Each camp has a small altar that restores prayer. Use it after getting your 40 KC so you enter the boss room with full prayer. Also consider bringing a Prayer potion for emergencies — nothing wastes a good trip faster than running out of prayer mid-fight.

② General Graardor (Bandos) — The War God's Champion

⑥ Minion Management & Safe Spots

Every GWD boss spawns with 3 minions that accompany the boss. These minions are not just decoration — they can heal the boss, hit you hard, or drain your supplies. Managing them is essential to long trips. Safe spots also exist for some encounters, though most require you to fight fairly.

6.2 🧹 Minion Stacking Strategy

The most efficient way to handle minions is to stack them using positioning tricks, then AoE them down with chinchompas (Grey or Red) or burst/barrage spells. Here's how:

① Bandos Minion Stack

Run to the south-east corner of Graardor's room. Graardor will follow you, and his minions will converge on the same spot. Once stacked, use Red Chinchompas (if Ranged) or Ice Burst/Barrage (if Mage) to damage all four targets at once. This method effectively quadruples your damage output per tick.

② General Minion Control

For all bosses, the golden rule is: kill the healing minion first, then kill the DPS minion, then finish the boss. If you kill the boss too fast without handling minions, they'll heal the boss during your next fight. A common mistake is ignoring minions to focus on the boss — this doubles your kill time.

7.3 👥 Solo, Duo, and Mass — Detailed Breakdown

① Solo GWD (Advanced Players Only)

Soloing GWD bosses is viable but requires high stats (90+ combat stats, 70+ prayer) and good gear (Bandos/Armadyl/Torva). Solo trips typically last 5-10 kills (fewer for Kree'arra) before needing to restock. The advantage: you keep 100% of the loot. The disadvantage: slow kills, high supply usage, and high risk of death. Recommended for experienced players who want to maximize profit from a single drop.

② Duo GWD (Recommended)

Duo is the most efficient team size for most players. Here's a typical duo setup:

  • Player 1 (Tank): High Defence + high Prayer. Brings a Bulwark or tank gear to absorb damage. Focuses on maintaining Protect prayers while the DPS player kills the boss.
  • Player 2 (DPS): Max strength gear, brings spec weapons (Dragon Claws, Dragon Dagger). Focuses on dealing damage. Also handles chinchompa minion clearing.
Duo trips can last 15-25 kills (Bandos) or 10-18 kills (Armadyl/Zamorak) before restocking. Loot is typically split 50/50 or by drop value.

③ Mass GWD (10+ Players)

Mass kills are common in mass worlds (look for "GWD Mass" in the official OSRS world list). In a mass, the boss dies in 10-20 seconds, but your share of the loot is divided among far more players. Masses are useful for:

  • Practice: Learn boss mechanics without the pressure of solo/duo
  • KC farming: Build kill count for collection log entries
  • Fun: Social experience with other players
Profit per person is typically 1-2M GP/hr — lower than duo, but you don't need to coordinate gear or roles. Just show up with decent gear and DPS.

⑧ GWD FAQs

Q: What's the minimum combat level for GWD?

A: The minimum level to enter the God Wars Dungeon is 70 Combat (required to survive the Agility obstacle). However, for actually killing bosses, 100+ Combat is recommended for solo, 85+ Combat for group kills. Below these levels, you'll struggle to get 5+ kills per trip and the bosses will hit you hard. Focus on leveling your combat stats and prayer first.

Q: Do I need 70 Agility to enter GWD?

A: Not exactly. You need 70 Agility to cross the narrow ice ledge that leads into the dungeon. There's no alternative route that bypasses this requirement. However, the 70 Strength requirement for the rope shortcut is optional — you can take the longer path (adds 2-3 minutes per trip). If you don't have 70 Strength, expect slower trips.

Q: Which GWD boss is best for profit in 2026?

A: General Graardor (Bandos) is the most profitable GWD boss in 2026, with an average of 3-6M GP/hr for a mid-level player in a duo. Bandos Tassets (32M GP) and Chestplate (27M GP) are the most valuable individual drops. Kree'arra is second place for profit if you can handle the difficulty (Armadyl Chestplate at 28M GP). Zilyana and K'ril are lower but still profitable, especially for Ironmen who need specific items.

Q: Is Kree'arra immune to Ranged? Why?

A: Yes, Kree'arra is immune to Ranged damage. This is a unique mechanic among GWD bosses. The lore reason is that Kree'arra is the avatar of Armadyl — the god of justice associated with air and Ranged combat. Using Ranged against him is like fighting fire with fire (and the fire wins). Always bring a melee weapon (preferably stab) when fighting Kree'arra. Many new players make the mistake of bringing a crossbow and wonder why they splash for 0 damage!

Q: How do I get a Zamorakian Hasta?

A: First, get a Zamorakian Spear from K'ril Tsutsaroth (1/128 drop rate, ~8M GP on GE). Then, take the spear to the Entrana monastery (requires Priest in Peril quest) and use it on the Altar of the God of War while wearing a Blessed Saradomin item (e.g., a Saradomin stole). This "purifies" the spear into a Zamorakian Hasta. The hasta has identical stats to the spear but can be used on stab, slash, or crush — making it far more versatile. It costs ~600K GP for the blessing process.

Q: Can I safespot any GWD boss?

A: Reliable safe spots are extremely limited after Jagex's patches. The following partial safe spots exist but are inconsistent:

  • K'ril Tsutsaroth: Behind the altar in the north-west corner — blocks melee, but magic and prayer drain still hit you
  • General Graardor: Behind the rocks near the entrance — blocks melee, but his ranged boulder attack still works
For all other bosses, no safe spots exist. Focus on learning proper mechanics rather than relying on safe spots.

Q: What's the average GWD trip length?

A: Trip length varies significantly by boss and team size:

  • Solo: 5-10 kills (Bandos/Zilyana), 3-6 kills (Kree'arra), 4-8 kills (K'ril)
  • Duo: 15-25 kills (Bandos), 10-18 kills (others)
  • Mass: 30-50+ kills (limited only by your supplies and KC)
The limiting factor is usually prayer potions and food. Bringing a Prayer flask can extend trips by 30-50%. Using the Altar in the dungeon before each boss room entry also helps.

Q: Should I use Godswords at GWD?

A: Yes, Godswords are excellent at GWD for specific reasons:

  • Saradomin Godsword (SGS): The special attack heals HP and restores prayer — ideal for sustaining long trips. Excellent for solo GWD.
  • Bandos Godsword (BGS): The special attack reduces Defence — useful for accuracy. Works well in teams where one player specs Defence down.
  • Armadyl Godsword (AGS): High single-hit damage — good for finishing off the boss at low HP.
  • Zamorak Godsword (ZGS): Freezes the target — niche use for controlling boss movement.
Most players recommend bringing a SGS as a secondary weapon for healing, then switching to your primary DPS weapon (Tentacle/Scythe/Hasta) for the majority of the fight.

🏆 Final Tips for GWD Success

The God Wars Dungeon is one of OSRS's most iconic bossing locations, and mastering all four generals is a rite of passage for any PvMer. Here are three final pieces of advice:

  1. Start with Zilyana. She's the easiest GWD boss. Learn the mechanics, minion handling, and prayer management there before moving to Graardor (profit) and K'ril (utility). Save Kree'arra for last — he's the hardest.
  2. Prioritize prayer potions over food. In GWD, running out of prayer kills you faster than running out of food. Bring at least 4-6 Super Restores per trip for solo, 2-3 for duo. A dead player with full food is worth nothing.
  3. Track your drops. The unique drop rate for most GWD items is 1/128. On average, you'll see a unique every 32 kills (with 28 kills per boss slot). Don't get discouraged by dry streaks — they're normal. The collection log entries for GWD are some of the most rewarding in the game.

Good luck, and may RNG be on your side! 🎲
