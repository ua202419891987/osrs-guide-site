OSRS Ironman 1-99 Smithing Guide 2026

Ironman Smithing Skill Training Updated 2026

Smithing is one of the most useful skills for an Ironman account in OSRS — it lets you craft your own armour and weapons, and the Blast Furnace is the fastest XP method available. This guide covers every stage from level 1 to 99 specifically for Ironmen, including how to obtain ores, the best Blast Furnace strategy, and gold bar smiting for the final push to 99.

Ironman vs Regular Smithing — Key Differences

For regular players, Smithing typically involves buying ores on the Grand Exchange and processing them. Ironmen cannot do this — every ore must be obtained through Mining, monster drops, or other in-game sources. This makes planning your Smithing training route critically important for resource management.

  • Mine ores yourself (time-intensive but sustainable).
  • Get bars from monster drops (limited supply).
  • Blast Furnace still works for Ironmen — you just need to bring your own ores.
  • Gold bars are the fastest XP method at high levels — gold ore is abundant via mining.
  • Completing Elemental Workshop quests gives Smithing XP without consuming materials.

Early Levels: 1–40

The early levels of Smithing can be knocked out quickly with a combination of quest rewards and basic smelting. Here is the recommended approach:

Quest XP Rewards for Early Smithing

  • Doric's Quest: 1,300 Smithing XP — unlocks at level 1, requires just some ores. Get to level 10 instantly.
  • The Knight's Sword: 12,725 Smithing XP — gets you from level 1 to approximately level 29 in one quest.
  • Elemental Workshop I: 5,000 Smithing XP
  • Elemental Workshop II: 7,500 Smithing XP

By completing The Knight's Sword and Doric's Quest, you skip to approximately level 30 without smelting a single ore. This is the single most time-efficient start to Ironman Smithing.

  1. Level 1–15 (or after quests): Smelt bronze or iron bars. Mine copper and tin from Lumbridge mines.
  2. Level 15–30: Smelt iron bars. Iron ore is abundant and iron bars can be used to make useful items.
  3. Level 30–40: Smelt steel bars (requires 1 iron ore + 2 coal). Steel equipment is useful for mid-game combat.
  4. Level 40+: Begin considering Blast Furnace for mithril, adamant, and rune bars.

Blast Furnace — Best Ironman XP Method

The Blast Furnace in Keldagrim is the best Smithing XP method available in OSRS. It halves the coal required to smelt bars and processes bars faster than a regular furnace. For Ironmen, this means significantly less coal needs to be mined compared to using a standard furnace.

Blast Furnace Requirements

  • 30 Smithing (or higher for better bars)
  • Starting Keldagrim quest complete (The Giant Dwarf, or simply enter Keldagrim by boat)
  • Ore stockpiled from Mining
  • 1,000–2,500 GP for the hourly Blast Furnace fee (negligible for Ironmen)
  • Recommended: Ice Gloves or a bucket of water (to handle hot bars efficiently)

Gold Bars — The Fastest XP Method

Smelting gold bars at the Blast Furnace with Goldsmith Gauntlets equipped (obtained from Family Crest quest) gives 56.2 XP per bar instead of the normal 22.5 XP. This results in XP rates of 300,000+ per hour — far higher than any other Smithing method.

Gold Bar Method Summary

  • Requirements: 40 Smithing, Goldsmith Gauntlets from Family Crest quest
  • XP per bar: 56.2 (with gauntlets)
  • XP/hr: ~250,000 – 380,000
  • Gold ore source: Arzinian Mine (after Between a Rock quest), Brimhaven Agility Dungeon resource area, Mining Guild
  • Best use: Level 40–99 for fastest possible XP

Where Ironmen Get Gold Ore

Gold ore is the main bottleneck for Ironmen using this method. Here are the best sources:

  1. Arzinian Mine (Between a Rock quest required) — After completing the quest, you can mine from 180 gold ore rocks simultaneously. This is by far the best gold ore source for Ironmen.
  2. Crafting Guild — Requires level 40 Crafting and a brown apron. Contains several gold rocks.
  3. Brimhaven Resource Area — Accessible with a fee (GP or ticket). Contains several gold rocks.
  4. Shilo Village Mine — Good density of gold rocks, requires Shilo Village quest.

Anvil Methods — Alternative Smithing XP

While the Blast Furnace is generally superior, anvil methods are useful for Ironmen who want to create useful equipment rather than just bars. Smithing items at an anvil gives additional XP on top of the smelting XP.

Cannonballs deserve special mention — they are extremely useful for Ironmen who use the dwarf multicannon for Slayer tasks. Smithing cannonballs uses steel bars (2 bars = 4 cannonballs) and gives modest XP while supplying cannon ammunition you would otherwise need to buy.

Ironman Smithing Tips

  • Complete The Knight's Sword quest first — it saves hours of early Smithing training.
  • Always use Goldsmith Gauntlets at the Blast Furnace for gold bars — the XP difference is enormous.
  • The Arzinian Mine from Between a Rock quest is the best gold ore source in the game for Ironmen.
  • Smith cannonballs alongside other Smithing training to maintain a supply of cannon ammo.
  • Use the Varrock Armour (Hard diary reward) to occasionally smelt two bars at once — this is a passive XP boost.
  • The Smithing cape perk allows you to smelt runite ore without needing any coal — excellent for late game.
  • If stuck on ores, consider fighting monsters that drop bars directly — Fire Giants, Dark Beasts, and others drop various metal bars.
