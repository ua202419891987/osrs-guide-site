OSRS Combat Triangle Explained 2026

Novice

OSRS combat isn't just 'click monster, deal damage.' There's a rock-paper-scissors system called the Combat Triangle that determines which style beats which. Understanding this before you train will save you from making expensive mistakes.

1. What Is the Combat Triangle?

The Combat Triangle is the core system that determines which combat style has the advantage over others. It works like Rock-Paper-Scissors:

⚔️ The Three-Way Relationship

  • 🗡️ Melee beats 🏹 Ranged (Melee has an advantage against ranged users)
  • 🏹 Ranged beats ✨ Magic (Ranged has an advantage against mages)
  • ✨ Magic beats 🗡️ Melee (Magic has an advantage against melee users)

What "advantage" means in practice: If you use a style that has the advantage, you'll hit more often and take less damage. If you're at a disadvantage, you'll miss more and get hit harder.

💡 Important: The Triangle Only Matters in PvP and Some PvM

Against most NPCs (monsters), the combat triangle doesn't matter much. NPCs have fixed weaknesses. A general rule: Melee for most things, Magic for monsters with high defence, Ranged for safety (attack from distance). The triangle matters most when fighting other players.

2. Melee — The Starting Point for Everyone

Melee is fighting up close with swords, axes, maces, or fists. Every new player starts with melee because it's the simplest and requires no ammunition (unlike ranged) or runes (unlike magic).

The 3 Melee Stats

SkillWhat It DoesHow to Train
AttackDetermines which weapons you can wield and your accuracy (hit rate)Combat training with "Accurate" style
StrengthDetermines your max hit (how hard you hit)Combat training with "Aggressive" style
DefenceDetermines how often you avoid damageCombat training with "Defensive" style

Melee Combat Styles (Weapon Types)

Weapon TypeAttack Types (Styles)Best For
Sword / ScimitarSlash (most common)General training, fast attacks
AxeSlash / CrushStrength training (slow but hard hits)
Mace / MaulCrushAgainst monsters with slash defence weakness
Spear / HailberdStab / SlashSafe spotting (attacks from 2 tiles away)

🛡️ Beginner Melee Gear (F2P)

  • Levels 1-10: Bronze → Iron → Steel equipment (Tutorial Island gives you iron gear)
  • Levels 10-30: Black / Mithril equipment (buy from general stores or other players)
  • Levels 30-50: Adamant / Rune equipment (Rune is the best F2P gear!)
  • Weapon: Scimitar is the best F2P melee weapon (fast, good damage)

3. Ranged — Attack From a Distance

Ranged lets you attack monsters from a distance using bows, crossbows, or thrown weapons. The big advantage: you can often attack without taking damage (the monster can't reach you).

The 2 Ranged Stats

SkillWhat It Does
RangedDetermines your accuracy and what bows/crossbows you can use
Defence(Same as melee) Helps you take less damage

Ranged Ammunition — You Need These!

Unlike melee (which needs no ammo), ranged requires arrows (for bows) or bolts (for crossbows). If you run out, you can't attack.

  • Bows: Shortbow (fast, less accurate) / Longbow (slower, more accurate) / Composite bow (members)
  • Crossbows: Slower but hit harder. Need bolts.
  • Ammunition costs: Bronze arrows are cheap. Higher-tier arrows get expensive fast.
💰 Ranged Is More Expensive Than Melee

You'll constantly spend money on arrows/bolts. As a beginner with little gold, melee is cheaper. Consider ranged only after you have a steady income source.

4. Magic — Spells, Runes, and Ancient Magicks

Magic uses spells that consume runes (consumable items). Magic is extremely powerful for certain tasks (high-damage spells, teleportation, utility spells) but requires constant rune purchases.

The 2 Magic Stats

SkillWhat It Does
MagicDetermines spell accuracy, what spells you can cast, and magic damage
Defence(Same as above)

Important Magic Concepts

  • Spellbook: You start with the Standard spellbook. Later you can unlock Ancient Magicks (powerful multi-target spells) and Lunar spellbook (utility spells).
  • Runes: Each spell consumes specific runes. You need to buy them from the Varrock rune shop or other players.
  • Auto-casting: Set a spell in your spellbook → it automatically uses that spell when you attack with the "Magic" combat style.
  • Magic weapons: Staves (like the Air Staff) provide infinite runes of one type. Save you money!
⚠️ Magic Is Expensive for Beginners

Every magic attack consumes runes. As a new player with 0 gold, magic is the most expensive combat style to maintain. Most players train Magic last (or use Magic only for teleporting).

5. How the Triangle Actually Works (Damage Bonuses)

Here's the math (simplified) of how the combat triangle affects your damage:

Your StyleOpponent StyleEffect
MeleeRanged+10% accuracy, -5% damage taken
RangedMagic+10% accuracy, -5% damage taken
MagicMelee+10% accuracy, -5% damage taken
If neither has advantage: normal accuracy

For NPCs (monsters): Most monsters have a specific weakness (shown in the combat interface when you right-click them → "Show Combat Info"). Hit them with their weakness style for best results.

🔍 How to Check an NPC's Weakness

  1. Right-click the NPC → "Show Combat Info"
  2. Look at the Weakness line
  3. Equip the corresponding style and attack!

Example: Most dragons are weak to Stab (use a spear or stab weapon). Giants are weak to Crush (use a maul or mace).

6. Which Combat Style Should YOU Train First?

As a complete beginner, here's my recommendation:

🥇 Start With Melee — Here's Why

  1. No ammo cost: You never run out of "ammunition" with melee
  2. Simple: Walk up to monster, click it, done
  3. High HP XP: Melee training also gives you Hitpoints XP (you take damage while training)
  4. Best F2P gear is accessible: Rune armor (level 40 Defence) is cheap and effective
  5. Foundation for everything: Most PvM content expects decent melee stats

Recommended Stats to Train First (F2P Route)

2ndDefence40Rune armor requires 40 Defence. HUGE defence boost
3rdHitpoints (passive)40+Comes naturally from combat. More HP = survive longer
4thRanged (optional)30+Ogres (Cannon) or safe training spots

7. Understanding Your Combat Stats (The Combat Level Formula)

Your Combat Level determines which monsters you can fight and which players you get matched with in PvP. Here's the formula (simplified):

⚔️ Combat Level Formula

Combat Level =

Base: (Defence + Hitpoints + Prayer/2 + Magic/2 + Range/2) / 4

Plus: Max(Attack+Strength, 2×Magic, 2×Range) / 4

(Don't worry — you don't need to calculate this manually. The game shows your combat level in the skill tab.)

What Combat Level Means in Practice

  • Level 3: Fresh account. Can't fight much. Tutorial Island gets you to about level 10.
  • Level 30-40: Can wear Rune armor. Can fight most F2P monsters comfortably.
  • Level 60-70: Mid-game. Can do most member quests. Ready for early bossing.
  • Level 100+: High-level content. Endgame bossing, raids, PvP.

🛡️ Always Upgrade Your Weapon First

A better weapon gives you more damage than better armor does. As a rule: upgrade weapon → then upgrade armor. A Rune scimitar with Iron armor is better than an Iron sword with Rune armor.

9. Common Combat Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️ Top 5 Combat Mistakes Beginners Make
  1. Never changing attack style: You've been training "Accurate" (Attack XP) for 20 levels and wonder why your Strength is still level 1. Check your combat tab!
  2. Not bringing food: You run out of food in the middle of a fight and die, losing all your items. Always bring more food than you think you need.
  3. Not checking NPC weakness: Hitting a monster with the wrong style (e.g., slashing a stab-weak monster) means you miss 50% of your hits. Check Weakness first!
  4. Spending all gold on gear too early: You buy Rune armor at level 35 Defence (can't wear it!) or buy gear before having a money-making method. Build income first!
  5. AFK combat without checking: OSRS has an AFK logout timer (about 5 minutes). You walk away and come back to a logged-out character. Set a timer!

10. Your First Combat Training Plan

Here's your exact step-by-step combat training plan for your first week:

📅 Week 1 Combat Plan (F2P)

  1. Day 1: Finish Tutorial Island → kill cows near Lumbridge for Attack/Strength (use "Accurate" for Attack, "Aggressive" for Strength)
  2. Day 2-3: Reach 20 Attack + 20 Strength → upgrade to Steel scimitar → kill Al-Kharid warriors (bring food!)
  3. Day 4-5: Reach 30 Attack + 30 Strength → Mithril scimitar → kill Moss Giants (Edgeville Dungeon, bring lots of food)
  4. Day 6-7: Reach 40 Attack + 40 Strength + 40 Defence → Rune gear! → You're now ready for Member content
