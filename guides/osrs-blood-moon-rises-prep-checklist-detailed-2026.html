1. Blood Moon Rises — What We Know

The Blood Moon Rises is an upcoming Grandmaster-level quest scheduled for release on June 30, 2026. It's the third and final entry in the "Moon" quest series, following The Lost City of the Elnat (2024) and Echoes of the Blood Moon (2025).

📖 Story Background (No Spoilers)

The Moon quest series explores the ancient history of Gielinor and the mysterious "Blood Moon" entity that influenced many of the game's factions. Without spoiling the previous quests, here's what you need to know:

📚 Lore Recap (Safe to Read)

The Blood Moon is an ancient force that once threatened Gielinor thousands of years ago. The previous two quests in the series dealt with discovering its remnants and preventing its partial return. The Blood Moon Rises is the culmination — the Blood Moon itself is awakening, and you must stop it (or perhaps... harness it?).

If you haven't played the first two quests: You must complete them before attempting Blood Moon Rises. See the requirements checklist below.

3. Preparation Timeline (4-Week Plan)

If you're reading this before June 2026, here's a week-by-week preparation plan to ensure you're 100% ready by June 30. Adjust based on how many hours per day you can play.

📅 Week 1: Quests That Take Longest

Goal: Complete the 3 longest prerequisite quests

  1. Song of the Elves (4-7 hrs) — Start with this, it's the longest and requires the most stats
  2. Desert Treasure II (4-6 hrs) — Second longest, requires 70 Magic
  3. Dragon Slayer II (3-5 hrs) — Third longest, requires 75 Combat

Time needed: ~11-18 hours (spread across the week)

Tip: Use a quest guide (not just the in-game hint book) for these — some puzzles are very complex and you don't want to waste time figuring them out yourself.

📅 Week 2: Stat Gaps & Medium-Length Quests

Goal: Get your stats to recommended levels + complete medium quests

  1. Train Prayer to 70 (if not already) — Gilded Altar with Dragon Bones (~6-10 hrs)
  2. Train Agility to 70-75 — Oo'glog course (new in 2026, faster!)
  3. Complete Dream Mentor (2-3 hrs)
  4. Complete Monkey Madness II (2-3 hrs)
  5. Complete The Path of Glouphrie (2-3 hrs)

Time needed: ~15-25 hours

Tip: Train Prayer and Agility while doing other afk activities (like watching Netflix). Both are annoying grinds but essential for the quest.

📅 Week 3: Items, Gear, & Short Quests

Goal: Obtain all required items + finish remaining short quests

  1. Complete all short quests (Waterfall Quest, Undergound Pass, Regicide, A Taste of Hope, etc.) — ~6-10 hrs total
  2. Buy or obtain all items on the shopping list (budget ~600K-2.2M GP)
  3. Upgrade your combat gear to mid-tier (see Gear Setup section)
  4. Complete Client of Kourend + any Kourend favor requirements
  5. Make sure you have teleportation unlocked (all standard spellbook teleports + POH teleports if possible)

Time needed: ~10-15 hours + GP farming time if you need GP

Tip: If you need GP for the shopping list, spend a few days doing money-making methods (Slayer, Thieving, or bossing) to fund the preps.

📅 Week 4: Final Prep & Review

Goal: Double-check everything + do a "dry run"

  1. Review the quest requirements list — Did you miss anything? (Use the checklist table in Section 2)
  2. Do a "dry run" of the first two Moon quests — If you haven't played them in a while, replay them to refresh the story
  3. Stock your bank — 10+ Sharks, 5+ Sara brews, 5+ Super Restores, teleport tablets, etc.
  4. Set up your hotkeys/interface — Make sure your prayer tab, inventory, and equipment tabs are easily accessible
  5. Clear your quest log — Complete any partially-started quests so you don't get confused
  6. Mark June 30 on your calendar — Don't miss the release!

Time needed: ~5-10 hours (mostly reviewing and double-checking)

⚠️ If You're Starting Late (Less Than 4 Weeks Before June 30)

If you're reading this on June 15 or later, you still have time but you'll need to prioritize. Focus on:

  1. Complete the 3 longest quests FIRST (Song of the Elves, DT2, DS2) — these are the biggest time sinks
  2. Get Prayer to 43 minimum (Protect prayers) — don't worry about 70 if you're short on time
  3. Skip low-priority prereq quests if possible (check the official requirements when the quest releases — some may be "recommended" not "required")
  4. Consider paying for quest services (some players offer quest completion services for GP) — controversial but an option if you're desperate

5. What Happens If You're Not Fully Prepared

Let's be real — not everyone will be 100% prepared by June 30. Here's what happens if you're missing certain requirements, and what you can skip (if anything).

❌ Can't Start the Quest (Missing REQUIRED Items)

You MUST have these before June 30:

  • All quest prerequisites completed — If you're missing even ONE required quest, you can't start Blood Moon Rises. No exceptions.
  • Required stat levels — The quest will literally not let you start if you don't meet the minimum stats. Train them beforehand.
  • Required quest items (from previous quests) — If you lost a quest item, you may need to re-obtain it (check the official quest guide when released).

Consequence: You'll be locked out of the quest until you complete the missing requirements. You'll miss the "day one" completion and all the bragging rights / early boss access that comes with it.

⚠️ Can Start But Will Struggle (Missing RECOMMENDED Preps)

You CAN start the quest, but it will be harder:

  • Low Combat stats (below 70 Attack/Strength/Defence): Quest fights will take longer and you'll take more damage.Solution: Bring more food and use Protect prayers constantly.
  • Low Prayer (below 43): You don't have Protect prayers. This makes quest fights MUCH harder. Highly recommend getting 43 Prayer before attempting.
  • No high-tier gear: You can use low-tier gear, but you'll deal less DPS and take more damage. Budget gear (Dragon set) is fine — you don't need BnadOS armor to complete the quest.
  • Missing teleportation: You'll have to run everywhere or use slower teleports. Annoying but not run-breaking.

✅ What You Can Safely Skip (LOW Priority)

These are NOT essential for completing the quest:

  • Maxed stats (99 in everything): Overkill. 70-80 in Combat stats is plenty.
  • Best-in-slot gear: mid-tier gear is fine. Don't delay your quest start because you can't afford a Twisted Bow.
  • Full Graceful outfit: Nice to have for running, but not required.
  • Construction 75 (POH max): Useful for teleports, but you can use regular teleport spells instead.
💡 The "Minimum Viable Preparation" Checklist

If you're short on time, here's the absolute minimum you need to complete before June 30:

  1. All required quests completed (see Section 2 checklist)
  2. 43 Prayer (Protect prayers — non-negotiable for high-level content)
  3. 70 Combat stats (Attack/Strength/Defence/Hitpoints)
  4. Budget gear set (~500K-1M GP investment — Dragon gear is fine)
  5. 10 Sharks + 3 Sara Brews + 3 Super Restores in bank

Everything else is "nice to have" but not essential. If you have these 5 things, you can start and complete the quest (might be challenging, but doable).

7. Quick Checklist Summary & FAQ

What's the ABSOLUTE minimum I need to start the Blood Moon Rises on June 30?

See the "Minimum Viable Preparation" checklist in Section 5. In short: all required quests done, 43 Prayer, 70 Combat stats, budget gear, and basic supplies (food + potions).

How long will it take to complete all the prerequisite quests if I haven't started any?

~40-60 hours for an experienced player. If you're new to these quests, add 50% (so ~60-90 hours). Start NOW if you want to be ready by June 30.

Can I skip any of the prerequisite quests?

No. If a quest is listed as "required" in the official requirements (when released), you must complete it. However, some requirements may be "recommended" not "required" — we'll update this guide after the official requirements are announced.

Do I need to be maxed Combat (99 Attack/Strength/Defence) to complete the quest?

No. 70-80 Combat stats are plenty. Maxed Combat makes the quest easier/faster, but it's not required.

Will the quest be too hard for a mid-game account (combat levels ~90-110)?

It might be challenging, but doable. Use Protect prayers, bring plenty of food, and don't be afraid to look up a walkthrough if you get stuck. The quest is designed to be completable by mid-game accounts (otherwise Jagex would require 99 stats).

Where can I find a walkthrough when the quest releases?

We'll publish a full walkthrough on OSRSGuru within 24 hours of the quest's release. In the meantime, you can use the official OSRS wiki or streamer walkthroughs.

Is the Blood Moon Rises worth doing on an Ironman account?

Yes! The quest rewards (XP lamps + new area access + new boss) are valuable for any account type. Ironmen may have a harder time with prerequisite quests (since they can't buy supplies), but the effort is worth it for the rewards.
