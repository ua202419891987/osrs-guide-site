How Does the Gear Recommender Work?

Data Collection and Filtering

First, the tool loads our complete OSRS equipment database — over 2,000 items, each tagged with hundreds of attributes. When you enter your stats, the tool immediately filters the database to exclude any items you can't equip.

Scoring Algorithm

For each remaining item, the tool calculates a composite score based on your combat style. If you selected Melee, the algorithm weights strength bonus and accuracy higher than magic defence. For Ranged, it prioritises ranged attack and ranged strength. For Magic, it looks at magic attack bonus and damage percentage.

Budget Optimisation

The OSRS budget gear recommender uses a greedy optimisation approach: it first allocates your budget to the highest-impact slots (weapon first, then amulet, then torso/legs), because a good weapon provides more DPS improvement than any other single piece.

Slot-by-Slot Assembly

Once the algorithm finishes scoring and budget allocation, it assembles a complete 10-slot setup. For each slot, the tool identifies: the best overall item (regardless of cost), the best value item within your budget, and a budget-friendly alternative.

Continuous Updates

Our database syncs with the OSRS Wiki and Grand Exchange API every 24 hours, ensuring your recommendations reflect the latest game updates and market prices.

OSRS Gear Progression Guide

Melee Gear Progression

Level 1-20 (Bronze to Iron): Every OSRS player starts with bronze equipment from Tutorial Island. Upgrade to iron at level 10 Attack and Defence — it's cheap (under 5K GP for a full set) and noticeably improves your accuracy and damage.

Level 20-40 (Steel to Mithril): At level 20 Attack, the steel scimitar becomes available. At level 30, unlock the mithril scimitar. Complete Waterfall Quest (no level requirements, grants 30 Attack and 30 Strength XP).

Level 40-60 (Rune to Dragon): The Rune Scimitar (approximately 35K GP) is your best weapon until level 60 Attack. Pair it with an Amulet of Glory (30K GP) and Rune armour. At level 60 Attack, upgrade to the Dragon Scimitar (100K GP, requires Monkey Madness I quest).

Level 60-75 (Dragon to Whip): At level 70 Attack, the Abyssal Whip (approximately 1.5M GP) becomes available. Upgrade armour to Fighter Torso (free from Barbarian Assault), Dragon Legs (200K GP), Dragon Boots (200K GP), and Berserker Ring (2.5M GP).

Level 75-99 (End-Game Melee): At 80 Attack, the Blade of Saeldor (100M GP). At 90 Attack, eventually the Scythe of Vitur (400M+ GP). For budget-conscious players, the Abyssal Whip remains effective even at high levels.

Ranged Gear Progression

Level 1-30: Start with bronze arrows and a training bow. Willow shortbows become available at level 20, Maple shortbows at level 30. For armour, leather gear and studded leather are your early options.

Level 40-50: Level 50 Ranged unlocks the Magic Shortbow (15K GP), one of the best training weapons until level 75. Use the best arrow you can afford.

Level 50-70: Level 61 Ranged and 50 Defence unlock the Rune Crossbow (30K GP), the most versatile ranged weapon in OSRS. Use broad bolts for cheap, effective training.

Level 70-99: Level 75 Ranged unlocks the Toxic Blowpipe (2M GP). For budget recommendations, stick with the Rune Crossbow + broad bolts.

Magic Gear Progression

Level 1-40: An Air Staff (8K GP) is sufficient for early training. Wizard robes and Xerician robes are your best early armour options.

Level 40-55: Level 40 Magic unlocks Mystic robes (approximately 500K GP for the full set). Complete Underground Pass quest to unlock Iban's Staff — a critical milestone for any new mage.

Level 55-75: Level 75 Magic unlocks the Trident of the Seas (600K GP). Use it with Ahrim's robes (level 70 Defence and Magic) or Mystic robes as a budget alternative.

Level 75-99: The Trident of the Swamp (1.2M GP). For budget OSRS magic gear guide recommendations, the Trident of the Seas + Mystic robes + Occult Necklace combo is unbeatable for its price point.

Money-Saving Tips for OSRS Gear

Weapon First, Armour Second

This is the golden rule of OSRS budget gear planning. A weapon upgrade provides exponentially more DPS than any armour upgrade. Prioritise your weapon first, then amulet/necklace, then body and legs.

Use Degradable Items Wisely

Use degradable items only for content that generates profit (bossing, Slayer tasks with good drops) and switch to non-degradable alternatives for general training.

Quest Rewards Save Millions

Many of the best beginner items are FREE if you complete the right quests. The Fighter Torso (Barbarian Assault), Dragon Scimitar (Monkey Madness I), Barrows Gloves (Recipe for Disaster), and Fire Cape (Fight Caves) are all free and better than gear costing millions.

Buy "Unpopular" Alternatives

Not every slot needs BiS equipment. Snakeskin Boots (12K GP) provide almost identical stats to Ranger Boots (35M GP). Mystic Boots (50K GP) are 99% as effective as Eternal Boots (8M GP).
