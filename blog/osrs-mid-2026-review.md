## ✅ What Actually Shipped (And Whether It Was Worth It)

### 1. Sailing — The Real Deal

Sailing is the first new OSRS skill in over 20 years. That is not hype — that is fact. And the important thing: **it actually shipped**, and it shipped in Q2 2026 as promised.

Is it perfect? No. The skill is still finding its meta — early training methods are a bit repetitive, and the endgame content (ship PvP, high-level voyages) is still being tuned. But the foundation is solid:

- **Combat integration**: Sailing is not just a standalone skill. It unlocks new boss access, new resource nodes, and new quest areas.
- **Pay-to-play divider**: Sailing is P2P only, which means F2P players are not affected — but it gives members a meaningful new progression vector.
- **The numbers**: Early data suggests Sailing training costs are high (similar to Construction), but the profit methods (high-level voyages, rare loot) are competitive with top GP/hr methods.

> **Bottom line**: Sailing shipped on time and it is good. Not perfect, but good. If you have not started training it, the [OSRS Guru Sailing Guide](https://osrsguru.com/guides/sailing-complete-guide-2026.html) has the current optimal routes.

### 2. Vault of Ralos — A Solid Raid, Not a Game-Changer

Vault of Ralos launched on April 10, 2026. It is the first new raid in OSRS since Chambers of Xeric in 2017. That alone makes it significant.

The raid itself is well-designed: multi-path layout, mechanic variety, and loot that actually matters (new armour tier, unique weapons). But there are some real issues:

- **Entry requirements are steep**: You need 80+ in multiple combat stats, completed Dragon Slayer II, and the new Myreque finale quest. Casual players are locked out.
- **Loot inflation concerns**: The unique drops are valuable (5-20M GP), which is great for raiders but has some players worried about GP inflation.
- **Learning curve is brutal**: Unlike CoX, which eases you in, Vault of Ralos throws you into complex mechanics immediately. Expect deaths.

> **Worth doing?** Yes — if you have the stats and the team. The [Vault of Ralos Strategy Guide](https://osrsguru.com/guides/vault-of-ralos-raid-guide-2026.html) breaks down every room and mechanic.

## ⚠️ What Got Delayed (And Why It Matters)

### Blood Moon Rises — The Quest That Won't End

Blood Moon Rises is the finale of the Myreque quest line. It was originally promised for Q2 2026. It is now delayed to Q3 — no firm date yet.

Why does this matter? Because Blood Moon Rises is not just a quest. It:
- Unlocks the endgame area needed for Vault of Ralos entry
- Completes the Myreque storyline that has been running since 2014
- Unlocks new teleport locations and resource nodes

If you are waiting on the quest finale to access late-game content, you are stuck until Q3. Jagex has not explained the delay in detail — just a brief note in a mid-year update post about "additional polish needed for the finale sequence."

Frustrating, but not surprising. Quest finales are historically where Jagex hits scope creep.

### League 6 — Still in the Dark

League 6 was supposed to get a teaser in Q2. We are at the end of June. Nothing.

The community is anxious. League 5 (Twisted) was huge — player counts hit all-time highs during the League window. Everyone expects League 6 to be similarly massive. But with no details, players cannot plan their accounts, and content creators cannot prepare.

Jagex's silence could mean:
1. **It is bigger than expected** and they are still scoping it (good sign)
2. **It slipped** and they do not want to announce a delay (bad sign)
3. **They are changing the League formula** and want to get it right (unclear)

Either way, if you are an account builder, do not bank on League 6 specifics until Jagex actually announces.

## 🔶 What Is Still In Progress

### Wilderness Rejuvenation — Slow but Steady

The Wilderness Rejuvenation project was announced with a lot of fanfare — PvP balance changes, new Wilderness content, and incentives to actually go into the Wildy without getting instantly PKed.

What actually shipped:
- A few PvP weapon balance tweaks (good)
- One new Wilderness boss (Rev Cave changes, mixed reception)
- Loot table adjustments for existing Wildy content

What did not ship:
- The promised "safe-ish" Wilderness skilling content
- Major anti-PK mechanics changes
- New Wilderness quest content

If you are a PvPer, you have probably noticed that the Wildy is still... the Wildy. Dangerous, unforgiving, and not exactly teeming with casual players. The rejuvenation is happening, but slowly.

## The Honest Assessment

If you asked me in January 2026 whether OSRS would have a good year, I would have said yes. Halfway through, the answer is: **yes, but with caveats**.

- **Sailing shipped** and it is a real skill, not a gimmick. That alone makes 2026 a success.
- **Vault of Ralos shipped** and it is a good raid. Not the best, but good.
- **Delays happen** — Blood Moon Rises slipping to Q3 is annoying but not a disaster.
- **The big unknown** is League 6. If that delivers, 2026 is a top-tier year for OSRS. If it does not, the year feels unfinished.
